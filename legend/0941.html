The group, clad entirely in black robes, ran through the darkness of night toward their destination.

The moon, which normally casts its soft light upon the earth, was now hidden behind clouds.

It was winter, and the temperature was low. Still, the lack of snow was fortunate for the group moving at this hour.

"No snow is good, but this cold is unbearable."

"Hah! Then let's kill that guy Eland and burn him. And while we're at it, burn down that so-called officer academy too. That should warm us up a bit."

"The academy? Are you sure that's wise? Those strange fellows claim they'll help us, but I can't trust them for a second."

"They probably feel the same way. In the end, we're just using each other for our own purposes."

"Honestly, it would've been easier if the ones who started this had finished him off back then. Because they failed, he holed up in that academy. Damn it, what a pain. It's an academy, for crying out loud. Of course it's full of skilled bastards."

"Oh? An academy? Isn't that just a bunch of greenhorns who aren't knights or adventurers yet? Well, they might be somewhat strong since they train in combat and all."

The men moved through the night's darkness, exchanging words, but their voices were very low.

Even if someone passed by them, they wouldn't be able to make out the conversation.

...Though, before anyone could overhear their conversation, the men had no intention of leaving any witnesses alive.

Continuing to run, the officers' academy, their destination, soon came into view.

But when they looked toward the entrance, two soldiers armed with spears stood guard. Seeing this, the man leading the way clicked his tongue and signaled for them to stop.

Almost instantly, everyone stopped and hid behind a nearby building.

"Hm?"

It wasn't as if he'd noticed their movement, but one of the soldiers glanced toward the darkness.

"What is it?"

"Nothing. I just thought I saw something move..."

"Just your imagination, man. Just your imagination. ...Still, is it shift change yet? Even with the fire, this crap doesn't keep us warm at all. Times like this, you just need some strong liquor and a good night's sleep."

Hearing his partner's voice, the soldier who had been staring into the darkness must have decided his own perception was just that—a trick of the mind. He averted his gaze from where he'd been looking and agreed with the remark.

"Yeah, I'd love to drink some hot wine or something and get some sleep."

"Man, you've got no ambition. At least let's hit the brothel. Found a good girl recently. Face's just okay, but she's got huge tits."

"Eh? I'd rather have a beauty, even if her chest is smaller."

"You idiot, you can only say that because you don't know the bliss of being enveloped by that overwhelming softness. Listen..."

The two soldiers began chatting casually, completely lacking any sense of tension... or more accurately, started making small talk.

The men in black watched the two soldiers intently.

"I thought it might be a trap to lull us into complacency... but apparently that's not the case."

"Yeah. Did they not expect us to show up?"

"Does it even matter? Let's just finish them off and take care of the target. Those soldiers were idiots, but they did say something good. Nothing beats hot wine on a cold day like this."

"What are you doing, spouting nonsense too? Got it? I'll take the one on the right."

"Then I'll take the left one."

Quickly deciding how to dispose of the two soldiers, the men in black moved immediately into action.

Timing their moves, they simultaneously hurled their black-painted daggers.

If the moon had been out, perhaps the glint might have alerted them to the daggers' presence. Or if the bonfire had been brighter.

But in the end, the two soldiers didn't even realize daggers had been thrown at them. The blades buried cleanly into their foreheads, taking both soldiers' lives.

The only solace, perhaps, was that the two soldiers lost consciousness without realizing they were dying.

To die without fearing the approaching terror of death, to die knowing nothing, to simply wake up dead—that was perhaps a fortunate way to go.

"Cover them up."

With a brief command, the men stormed into the military academy.

They dragged the fallen gate guard soldier into the shadow of the building.

If someone came and found a corpse on the ground, it would cause a commotion. But if no one was there, they might just be suspected of slacking off.

Of course, a commotion would be certain tomorrow, but for the men, it was enough that no trouble arose while they were inside the academy hunting down their target, Eland.

A pool of blood on the ground would be hard to hide completely, but that was precisely why the thrown dagger aimed for the forehead, causing minimal bleeding. Furthermore, to avoid leaving even the slightest trace of blood scent, they didn't pull out the dagger stuck in the forehead.

Of course, it was a winter night, and the wind was cold and strong.

Even a slight scent of blood would likely be scattered away quickly.

In any case, the men quickly cleaned up the traces of their attack and entered the military academy grounds, scanning their surroundings.

Their intelligence network had naturally confirmed exactly which dormitory the target was in. They ran straight through the grounds for about five minutes, and just as the target's dormitory finally came into view...

"Gah!"

"Gyah!"

"Guh!"

Sudden screams erupted.

From the men who had undergone rigorous training as assassins.

Something unexpected must have happened, and the men instantly scattered in different directions.

Several glanced behind them, only to find three figures collapsed on the ground.

The cause of their collapse was obvious from the arrows piercing their foreheads.

But...

(Damn it! I didn't hear the arrows whistling at all!?)

One of the men in black robes screamed inwardly.

The others felt the same way.

These men had undergone rigorous training as assassins.

So much so that hearing an arrow whistling through the wind should have been easy.

But during this attack, they hadn't heard a single whistling sound of arrows cutting through the air.

If only one arrow had flown, it might have been possible to attribute it to a chance failure to hear. But three arrows had flown now.

It was absolutely impossible to miss hearing all of them. ...Well, normally, that is.

Had an ordinary archer fired these arrows, the men would never have failed to hear their whistling through the wind.

But with this attack, they felt absolutely no whistling sound at all.

The men in black robes couldn't comprehend what had happened, yet they didn't stop here. Each vanished into the darkness to pursue their target.

But... regardless of their actions, arrows sliced through the night's darkness, flying without a sound, and one after another, they struck down the men.

Even if one man fell to enemy fire, the others would secure their target, Eland.

The men had made that decision with death-defying resolve... yet the flying arrows pierced their heads as if drawn to them.

"Well, that should take care of most of the intruders. ...I suppose I've done a rather unfortunate thing to the guard soldiers."

Sardat, standing atop a building within the academy grounds, murmured with a sorrowful gleam in his eyes.

The deaths of the academy's guards were a sad occurrence.

Sardat could have prevented it under normal circumstances, but there was a reason he couldn't do so now.

"If the attack had happened after they entered the school grounds, I could have stopped it,"

He muttered, reaching for the now-empty quiver.

If anyone else had been there, they might have wondered if he was removing the empty quiver.

But what Sardat did was channel magic into the quiver.

The quiver began to glow faintly... but in the next instant, Sardat stopped channeling magic into it and leapt away.

Simultaneously, the sound of shattering echoed around him.

"...Did he remove it?"

Where Sardat had been standing just an instant before, a man now stood.

The longsword in his hand was driven into the rooftop floor, carving a large crater-like gash into the stone structure.

An attack powerful enough to easily gouge into stone... sturdy stone like that used in military academy buildings. It wasn't hard to imagine what would have happened if he'd taken that blow.

Yet, even witnessing such an attack, Saldat showed no particular sign of fear.

For Saldat, a Rank S adventurer who had survived countless battles, facing an enemy with such destructive power at close range was by no means an unfamiliar experience.

He grasped his bow and focused his attention on his quiver.

(No good. If I'd had a few more seconds, I could've made a few arrows, even if they were a bit shoddy.)

When wielding a bow, the most crucial consideration is naturally the number of arrows.

Without arrows, one cannot attack, making the number of arrows critically important for any archer.

However, the quiver Sardat carried was a magic item known as the Trajectory of Abundance. By channeling magic power into it, he could produce arrows without limit.

On its own, it was similar to the Nebula's Eye Ray Rei had acquired in the Bestir Empire, but this one could produce a wider variety of arrows.

It could enhance the arrows' inherent strength or imbue them with various attributes.

Furthermore, unlike Nebula's Eye, the arrows did not vanish over time.

Combining this with Saldart's unique skills and firing them from the Heavenly Bow, another magic item, defined the combat style of the rank S adventurer known as the Heavenly Bow.

...However, this combat style had its weaknesses.

Once all the arrows stored within the Trajectory of Plenty were fired, it required channeling magic again to create new ones.

While creating a crude arrow takes less than ten seconds, conversely, that means the man known as the Heavenly Bow can go ten seconds without using his bow.

"They're surprisingly useful after all."

Sardat grasped the truth behind this turn of events from the man's words.

"I see. So they were decoys meant to exhaust my arrows. If that's the case, does that mean someone from the Holy Light Church is involved with that noble child, Eland?"

Despite having his greatest weapon—his bow—neutralized, Sardat showed no particular sign of agitation... On the contrary, with a composed air, he ran a hand through his hair and addressed the man before him.

The man, too, showed no sign of letting his guard down against Sardat.

He understood that facing a Rank S adventurer meant this was no easy foe to defeat.

(If only I'd landed damage with that first strike...)

Though inwardly bitter at the sight of Sardat standing before him, the man concealed his feelings and continued speaking.

"Regardless of the truth, I have no intention of giving information to the enemy. I'm afraid you'll have to stay here for a while and... no, help us buy time."

As soon as he finished speaking, several figures materialized behind the man.

All were cloaked in robes, each holding a weapon.

Despite facing one of the world's three Rank S adventurers, they showed no sign of nervousness.

On the contrary, their extremely relaxed demeanor made it clear they weren't cowering in fear, unable to perform at their best.

Seeing this, Sardat twisted his handsome face in pained expression.

"You guys really do love brainwashing, don't you? ...But do you truly believe Stigma alone can defeat me? I am, after all, a Rank S adventurer. And if anyone knows that power best lately, it's probably you lot."

"Yeah, of course I don't think I can win. But my job is to stall. That much, even I can manage..."

...can manage. Before he could finish, Sardat fixed him with a piercing stare.

Caught off guard by the intensity of that gaze, the man faltered for a moment... Not missing it, Saldat smiled and spoke.

However, the smile playing on his lips was not the one Saldat always wore when courting women. It was the savage grin of a predator that had spotted its prey and was about to strike.

"Huh!? ...I see. You truly live up to the name Heavenly Bow. As I am now, winning against you is impossible, and even buying time would be difficult. But have you forgotten? I am Stigma. I bear the Sacred Scar upon my body!"

With those words, the tattoo-like mark on the back of the man's left hand glowed, filling the surrounding area with some kind of power. It resembled magic, yet possessed a decisive difference.

It was a power unknown even to Sardat, who had lived as an elf for many years.

And yet, it was the same power he had seen several times before when fighting Stigma.

Emitting that strange power, the man charged toward Sardat.
