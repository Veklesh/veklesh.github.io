The morning after camping in the desert, Rei finished getting ready and stepped out of the magic tent, just as the four members of Sand Claw were disembarking from their sand ship.

...Yes, while Rei had his magic tent, the Sand Claw members had abandoned some of their belongings, including tents, while fleeing the Sand Lizardmen, leaving them without a place to sleep.

Rei considered suggesting they go back to retrieve it, but when told the tent was ruined from being used as a shield when impaled by the trident and they didn't even know where it was lost, Rei lost all motivation to search.

At first, Rei thought staying in the magic tent might be okay, but before he could say anything, Howl apologetically asked if they could borrow a room on the sand ship. Rei readily agreed.

Thus, the four members of the Sand Claw spent the night aboard the Sand Ship.

Of course, there was concern that the Sand Ship might be stolen. However, none of the Sand Claw members were wizards. While they might have been able to handle common magic items, none possessed the magical power to move something as large and significant as the Sand Ship.

It might have been different if they had magic stones, but they couldn't do that either since they'd left their belongings behind.

The biggest factor, though, was that after seeing Rei's strength, and with Set there too, he couldn't imagine anyone daring to steal the Sand Ship.

"Rei, good morning."

The first to greet Rei was Cardina, the sole female member of the Sand Claw.

She was a spear user like Rei, and having witnessed his strength up close, coupled with his status as a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname, she harbored a certain fondness for him.

Though it was more a sense of respect for someone stronger than herself than romantic interest.

"Ah, good morning. Inside the sand ship, but did you sleep okay?"

"Yes. As expected of a high-performance magic item, it was much better than sleeping in a tent. Well, there was nothing inside the room, so it was almost like staying in a bare room, but it was far more comfortable than sleeping outside."

For a moment, Rei wondered if it was some kind of flattery towards him for lending the Sand Ship. But the other three looked even more energetic and full of vitality than yesterday.

As Cardina had said, they must have gotten plenty of rest.

Hearing this, another potential use for the Sand Ship flashed through Rei's mind.

(I see. Could it work as a simple lodging? It has several rooms, and if you set up beds or something there...)

The Sand Ship, which had seemed like a useless magic item for Rei, who operated in Gilm and couldn't move outside the sandy desert, might actually be usable purely as a substitute for tents.

Unlike ordinary tents, it was solidly constructed, so there was no need to worry about drafts.

Of course, it wouldn't be as comfortable as the magic tent Rei used, but it was clearly a more comfortable living space than a regular tent.

Of course, it has several drawbacks.

The biggest drawback is undoubtedly the Sand Ship's sheer size.

It's large enough to comfortably hold nearly fifty people, meaning it can't be deployed just anywhere.

Pulling up a sand ship in the middle of town or beside a highway would definitely attract attention.

And standing out means there's no guarantee people with strange ideas, including bandits, won't attack...

(It seems functional enough, but it'll probably bring a lot of trouble. Plus, there's the issue of keeping watch...)

With a tent, if something... or someone attacked, you could immediately go outside and intercept them.

But with the sand ship, you'd have to leave your room, then go out onto the deck, and then descend to the ground.

(Should I think of it more like a portable fortress? ...Nah, probably not a fortress. Still, it's definitely sturdier than a tent and eliminates nighttime worries. ...Maybe more like a mobile hotel?)

The thought crossed his mind that perhaps he should hold off on selling the sand ship, considering its new value.

At the very least, he'd need to consider all that once he got back to Gilm.

(Well, I'm basically traveling solo anyway, so I doubt I'll have many chances to use this sand ship as a hotel substitute.)

If only Rei is staying overnight, a magic tent would suffice, and Set is there for defense.

Besides, using the sand ship as a mobile hotel would obviously require Rei, who possesses the Misty Ring.

Rei operating solo versus Rei possessing a mobile hotel. Considering the sand ship's utility, it remained inconvenient for Rei to own one regardless.

Still, the reason Rei started thinking about not letting go of the Sand Ship was the belief that it could be useful when acting with others besides himself, like this time.

...Though, even now that Rei was operating solo, once he returned to Gilm and Vihera and Byune came back, they would be acting together.

(Two people should fit comfortably in the magic tent, though.)

While pondering this, he also felt it might be time to properly form a party.

Currently, they were traveling together, with solo adventurers cooperating individually. But considering the future, it was undeniably more convenient to form a proper party.

"Rei, shouldn't we eat soon? We'll probably reach Gosh by midday, but I want to make sure we have a proper meal."

Kaldina's words snapped Rei back to reality. He took out food from the Misty Ring beside the sand ship and prepared a meal.

Similar to last night's dinner, the four Sand Claw members drank water produced by the Waterflow Dagger. They marveled at the taste of the water and were amazed by the dishes, which were practically unattainable while traveling through the desert. To Rei, however, this was almost routine.

"What's most important when fighting with dual blades?"

Rei asked Haul, who was eating a skewer of roasted oak meat.

Last night, he had tried the dual spear style with Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, but he still couldn't say he had mastered it fully.

If asked which Rei was stronger—the dual-spear Rei or Rei wielding only either Death Scythe or Twilight Spear—even Rei himself could instantly declare the latter.

Still, Rei strongly believed this was only temporary.

Two long weapons with distinctly different handling: a large scythe and a spear. Mastering them is difficult, but conversely, he believed that if he could master them, they would become an extremely powerful means of attack.

(Though, I've heard of dual-wielding swords or blades, but never dual-wielding spears. Well, it's probably inevitable since wielding a spear heavier than a sword in one hand is difficult to begin with.)

Many practitioners wield dual short swords.

In fact, Rei had heard several thieves in Gilm's guild boast about it.

But with a longsword, the weight is incomparable to a dagger.

You're holding a weapon weighing around two kilograms in one hand and swinging it freely.

...And spears or large scythes? Their weight is even heavier than a longsword.

Not only must you hold them with one hand, but you must also move them freely, separately, left and right.

The sheer difficulty of this was something Rei, who was trying out the two-spear style, understood best.

That's precisely why he asked Howl, also a dual-wielding swordsman, if there was any trick to it...

"The important thing? Just go with your flow."

The response that came back was a phrase whose meaning she couldn't grasp.

"Flow?"

"Yeah. The flow that comes from within you... You could call it music. Ride that flow."

"...I don't really get what you're saying."

The words that came out of Howl's mouth were completely incomprehensible.

Seeing Rei's expression, Cardina gave a wry smile and spoke.

"You shouldn't worry too much about it. In Howl's case, he fights more by instinct than anything else. Trying to make someone else understand that is a bit difficult."

"Hmm. That's not entirely true, though."

Howl muttered discontentedly at Cardina's words, but unfortunately, the other two seemed to agree with her, merely nodding silently.

"I was hoping to hear some tips on dual-wielding. Guess I'll just have to figure it out on my own after all."

"You're not the only dual-wielder out there, right? If anything, Gilm, where Rei belongs, has plenty of skilled adventurers. Surely there are others who dual-wield?"

"I wonder..."

To be precise, he knew there were.

But the biggest problem was that Rei's existence within the Gilm was special in many ways.

Even if Rei approached a dual-wielding adventurer, it was doubtful they'd willingly share their secrets.

Besides, Haul taught him based on his own instincts. Normally, adventurers don't just give away the core of their own advantages to others.

(Guess I'll just have to learn on my own. ...Though if there were a dojo or something, that might be different.)

Rei hadn't received any formal training anywhere.

Even back in Japan, as a high schooler in the countryside, he'd led a pretty ordinary life, only doing a bit of judo in class.

The only difference from a typical high schooler was that he'd been in the mountains since childhood, so he was accustomed to physical exertion.

That was the extent of Rei's advantage. Yet, it was this, combined with the inhuman physical abilities of his current body and the combat skills honed through countless battles, that forged the Rei now known as Crimson.

Moreover, he never skipped his daily training. It wasn't unusual to see him practicing with the Death Scythe at odd hours like dawn or dusk.

Even so, it seemed he would need more than enough time to truly master the Two-Spear Style.

"Ah, speaking of which... changing the subject, do you know this monster?"

Changing the subject, Rei pulled the corpse of a coconut crab monster out of his Misty Ring.

While it was known by now that Rei carried an item box, suddenly seeing a giant monster corpse appear before them was still bound to be startling.

"Th-this monster... Pickrum, is it?"

Odilon muttered timidly, and the other three who heard him nodded in agreement.

"Picklemon? Is that what this monster's called?"

"I guess it goes without saying, but the one who defeated this Picklemu was..."

Rei shook his head slightly at the gaze Odilon directed at him.

"I didn't defeat it. ...It was Set."

When Rei answered Odilon, the gaze of everyone present shifted to Set, who was lying down beside Rei.

And all four members of the Sand Claw group couldn't help but look convinced.

This monster, known as Picklemu, was feared in the desert for its formidable defense and diverse attack methods, including powerful scissor strikes and magic.

But even so, if asked whether they could fight and defeat a Griffin, everyone would instantly shake their heads.

"So, my question is, is this monster edible?"

"Ah... yeah. You can eat it. Actually, I've heard it's pretty tasty. ...Unfortunately, I've never tried it myself, so it's purely hearsay."

"What about poison?"

"I've never heard of it being poisonous, though I've heard stories of people getting sick from eating too much."

"I see. So this is edible then. ...Perfect timing. Let's have this for breakfast. Luckily, a few spots are already charred."

The source of that savory aroma was, needless to say, Set's fire breath.

The heated shell sections from the battle with Picklum had turned red, releasing an appetizing aroma into the air.

Even though his stomach was somewhat full from eating the roasted oak meat skewers, he still felt hungry.

This scene was only possible because the Misty Ring stopped the flow of time while stored.

"Can we eat too?"

Like the others, Howl had likely never eaten Pickle before.

That's why, when Rei said it was okay to eat, he asked that question.

"Yeah, no problem. Come on, let's get ready."

Saying that, Rei ordered the campfire's flames to be stoked higher, placing the Pickle's legs, claws, and torso over the fire... and everyone ended up eating a breakfast that couldn't have been more luxurious.

"I see it! I see it! It's Goush!"

A few hours after eating pickle for breakfast. On the deck of the sand ship moving across the sand, Howl shouted loudly.

Rei, who was operating the sand ship, saw Gosh clearly projected onto the panel in the control room and finally breathed a sigh of relief... knowing he had returned.
