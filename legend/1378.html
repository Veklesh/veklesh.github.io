"What the hell are you bastards thinking?!"

The angry shout echoed through the guild.

With the sun already fully set and night fallen, there were hardly any people left inside the guild.

...Though, the adjoining tavern was extremely noisy, filled with adventurers celebrating today's successful jobs or drowning their sorrows in drink over their mistakes.

The fact that the shout could be heard all the way over to the noisy tavern showed just how loud it was.

In fact, several of the drinkers in the tavern turned their gaze toward the guild... or more precisely, toward the group of woodcutters gathered there.

"Um, we're still open for business, so if you could please lower your voices a bit, or move somewhere else, that would be appreciated..."

Rei had seen the receptionist several times before, and she addressed the man who had shouted—the person in charge of the woodcutters at the Gilm guild.

His words seemed to snap the shouting man back to his senses. He bowed hurriedly to the receptionist, then to Rei.

"Sorry about that. I'll move somewhere else. ...Rei, I'm sorry for all the trouble these idiots have caused you."

The sight of a middle-aged man with a stern, lumberjack-like face bowing deeply wasn't exactly... unusual.

After all, this was the Gilm Guild.

Naturally, the people here were adventurers, and among adventurers, warriors were the most common.

Even among warriors, some fought using speed and technique, but that naturally required talent.

Of course, that doesn't mean brute-force fighters don't need talent either... but while there are differences in how easily or hard it is to build muscle, training your body will definitely build it.

In that sense, warriors who rely on brute force to fight, requiring less innate talent, are numerous.

...Though it's difficult for that type to become high-rank adventurers.

Elk, an A-rank adventurer, is one such type, though he possesses a keen combat sense and wields the magic item known as the Axe of the Thunder God.

In any case, men with such well-developed muscles weren't uncommon in the guild.

Though it would be a different story in town.

Incidentally, this was one reason Rei avoided coming to the guild early in the morning.

The adventurers gathered before the request board looking for jobs... among them, there were certainly no shortage of those sporting muscles so bulky they were almost suffocating.

"No, don't worry about it. It's just that if the same thing keeps happening, it gets troublesome for us too. Just understand that much. Also, I brought the tree they cut down, so I'll hand it over to the guild later."

"Ah, sorry about that. Regarding the wood, could you take it as compensation for the trouble this time, Rei?"

"...Nah, I'll pass. Honestly, I've got no use for wood like that anyway."

Actually, Rei does cut down a few trees himself and sells them to the guild, so it's not like he has absolutely no use for them.

If push came to shove, he could always chop them up for firewood.

Still, he refused because he was thinking about the Fekts, at least.

While no one died, many suffered minor injuries.

Plus, these were trees felled in the nighttime frontier—a place generally considered extremely dangerous.

Rei wasn't too keen on snatching them away like that.

"Are you sure? You don't need to hold back for Fekts and the others, you know?"

Rei nodded back at the woodcutter who asked again.

"Yeah, don't worry about that."

"...Well, if you say so, Rei, I guess it's fine with me."

"Besides, this request isn't exactly unpaid labor. We're getting paid properly for it, so don't worry about it."

When Rei reiterated this, the other man finally seemed satisfied.

Looking at Fekts, he let out a deep sigh.

"Anyway, you two rest today. We've got work tomorrow. ...Just be prepared, though. Your pay's gonna be cut for a while."

"Boss?"

He probably hadn't expected to be let off so easily.

Fekts looked at the woodcutter before him... the foreman who oversaw them, and voiced his puzzlement.

Only now did it dawn on them how incredibly dangerous their actions had been.

Precisely because they understood that, they probably never imagined they'd get off with almost no punishment—though their pay would be cut.

"Shut up! Anyway, you lot get back to your quarters and sleep! We've got an early start tomorrow!"

Fekts and the others bowed to the shouting foreman before leaving.

The foreman watched them go, but then noticed Rei and Vihera staring at him. He opened his mouth.

"Do you think it's too lenient?"

"Yeah. Compared to what they did, it's pretty lenient, don't you think?"

It was Vihera, not Rei, who murmured this.

A short distance away from Vihera, Marina—who had been discussing the reward for this job with the receptionist—glanced briefly in their direction before returning to the negotiation.

Only the receptionist and the boss present understood what Marina's action signified.

"This job requires a lot of woodcutters to begin with. If the Fekts pull out now, we'll be short-handed. ...Gilm doesn't have that many woodcutters to begin with."

The boss's words were true.

To fell trees, one must go into the forest, and given this was the frontier, it wouldn't be surprising if powerful monsters lurked there.

Moreover, unlike the trees in the Treant Forest, ordinary forests required a certain amount of time to pass after felling.

And while the wood is left lying around, it's not uncommon for other monsters, including goblins, to help themselves to it.

Of course, with fewer people, the pay was better.

This was likely influenced by Lord Daskar's preferential treatment, aimed at preventing the dwindling number of woodcutters and ensuring Gilm didn't lose its last ones.

"But I won't let this happen again. ...I promise you that much."

The foreman's powerful declaration made Rei and Vihera exchange glances before nodding in response.

"Well, it wasn't that tough for me, personally."

"If stronger monsters had shown up, it might've been more fun... but it was just Treant."

Unlike Rei, Vihera's words surprised the master.

The boss looked surprised, but Rei, knowing Vihera's personality, didn't seem particularly bothered.

"Well, we'll leave it at that. You're heading back to Treant Forest tomorrow, right?"

"...Of course. But you saw a Treant, right?"

The master asked, prompted by Vihera's words, but Rei nodded as if it were obvious.

"Yeah. But it didn't show up at noon today, and if you're an adventurer hired as an escort, I think you can handle Treant however you want."

"I see."

The master didn't entirely believe Rei's words, but still... if a high-ranking adventurer with a reputation said so... he forced himself to accept it.

Besides, even if the adventurers hired as guards couldn't handle the situation, he figured if Rei showed up, he'd find a way to deal with it.

"Alright then, we'll call it a day around here. ...Just so you know, and I'll say it again, I won't put up with this kind of foolishness happening over and over."

"I know. That Fekts guy is usually a serious woodcutter. It's just... well..."

With those words, Rei understood that Fekts had some kind of situation going on.

But he didn't press for more details.

After all, Rei and Fekts weren't exactly close friends.

They were merely connected by this particular job.

After finishing their talk with the woodcutter's boss, only Rei and the others remained at the guild.

That was because Rei and the others still had things to do at the guild.

Rei looked around, but as expected, Kenny was nowhere to be seen.

He had noticed her when they entered the guild, but he had hoped she might be here.

Rei had never been particularly skilled at socializing.

Still, he'd managed to make it as an adventurer thanks to the two receptionists, Lenora and Kenny, who'd been genuinely kind to him.

That's precisely why he wanted to talk to one of them about this matter if possible, but unfortunately, it seemed their working hours had already ended.

Kenny bringing Rei a request was probably her last job.

"Rei, if you need to talk, Ruch over here will listen."

Marina must have understood what Rei's attitude meant.

Marina called out the name of the receptionist who had been discussing the reward.

"Well, I've taken over from Kenny, so I'll be the one to hear your story."

"...Understood. When we went to help the Fekts in Trent Forest this time, we ended up fighting Treant himself, just like his name suggests."

"Is that so? So monsters do appear at night, after all."

"Ah... seems that's not quite right."

Rei recalled information he'd heard from one of the woodcutters while traveling.

"One of the woodcutters confirmed there was a Treant among them bearing a wound he'd inflicted as a mark."

"Couldn't it just be that they didn't realize it was a Treant?"

"...Do you really think a professional woodcutter would mistake a regular tree for a Treant just by touching it? Well, of course, it would be different if it were a higher-tier or rare species of Treant, but the one we fought was a regular Treant."

"Well then... what does that mean?"

"Probably the Treant we fought was a Treant, yet not a Treant. It's not exactly proof, but I brought back a few Treant corpses."

"No, what proof does that provide?"

Just bringing corpses here—what are you going to do with them?

Facing the receptionist's questioning gaze, Rei smiled faintly and spoke.

"Even if there's no magic stone inside that monster's body?"

"...Huh?"

The receptionist let out a dumbfounded sound.

That was only natural. Since it was a monster, there should be a magic stone inside its body.

But what did it mean to say there was no magic stone?

A questioning gaze, filled with that very doubt, turned toward Rei.

Facing the receptionist's gaze, Rei silently shook his head.

Even Rei didn't have a clear understanding of why the magic stone was missing.

Still, since it was certain the magic stone didn't exist, pretending it was there was out of the question.

"I did bring the corpse without the magic stone. I'd like to hand it over. ...Well, considering the materials involved, I suppose it'll actually be a matter of selling it to you."

Treant's body could be used for various things.

The most famous use is probably for a staff.

Without magic stones... meaning it might not even be a monster, so whether it can actually be used as a staff is uncertain.

To investigate that, the guild will purchase the Treant corpse brought by Rei.

"Understood. If monsters truly exist without magic stones, this is an extremely significant matter. I will report this to the Guildmaster."

From Rei's demeanor, she must have understood this was dangerous business, no joke.

The receptionist nodded seriously at Rei's words and stated this.

"Yes, please do. ...To be honest, I might have underestimated that Treant Forest. Of course, the monsters that appear there aren't particularly strong... but there's a different kind of terror there."

Purely as monsters, they wouldn't be any kind of threat.

At least, that was certain for someone like Rei or the adventurers hired as his guards.

But a different kind of fear, born of the unknown, assailed Rei.

No, in this case, it was precisely because it was unknown that it was terrifying.

What kind of monsters lurked in Treant Forest? How exactly did they manifest?

If she knew that, she wouldn't feel such a threat in the first place.

(Treant Forest, huh. Seems it got its name because Slesha was the first to encounter it... But the enemies we fought today were mostly Treants too. Of course, without magic stones, strictly speaking they weren't true Treants, so calling them Treant-like monster imitations would be more accurate.)

Pondering such things, Rei placed Treant's corpse in the storeroom before leaving the guild with Marina and Vihera.

He wanted to return to the inn as quickly as possible to put Byune at ease, since she was still waiting there.

...Though, to be honest, Byune's expression rarely changed, making it impossible for anyone besides Vihera to truly discern her feelings.
