Several days into their journey after the oak incident...

What it meant was obvious to anyone.

"Finally, a town. ...What was the name of that town again?"

"It should be called Dorgana, I believe. It's a fairly large city within the Kingdom of Reburina."

Marina replied to Vihera's words.

As a former guildmaster—and before that, a renowned adventurer—Marina possessed a wealth of information.

Of course, determining a town's size was the kind of information one could easily gather with just a little effort.

Furthermore, traveling along the highways, they frequently encountered caravans and peddlers. For Rei and his companions, buying goods from such people and casually obtaining information in the process was hardly an unusual occurrence.

The merchants, recognizing Rei and his companions as generous customers who paid well, readily shared common knowledge that anyone would know.

...Of course, some tried to rip them off, seeing that most of the group were Rei. What fate befell such individuals is probably obvious.

"First time in this town?"

Rei murmured, though considering Mejogo and Rossi, it wasn't strictly true that this was their first city.

Still, in the sense that it was a place they could actually stop at, calling it Rei's first town was by no means wrong.

"Rei, what are we doing in town? Are we free to go our own ways?"

"Yeah. But if you don't get back before the evening bell rings when we leave, we'll have to leave you behind."

Since there might be those who wished to stay in Dolgana, Rei's understanding was that if they chose to remain without being told, then so be it.

There was no need for anyone to specifically tell them if they wanted to leave the group.

(Though I'd prefer the ones grouped in tens to at least say something beforehand.)

Even if it wasn't for a very long time, we've traveled together this far.

We've clashed a bit, and even become friends in some cases. So, regardless of myself, I'd like them to properly say goodbye to those people.

Thinking that... but then again, it really depended on the person. He let out a small sigh.

"So, Rei. Leaving people is one thing, but what if someone wants to come with us?"

"Oh, that's another problem, huh?"

"What a hassle," Rei thought, looking up at the sky in response to Marina's words.

Blue sky, white clouds, and a sun asserting itself fiercely.

It was a scene worthy of being called "Summer!" but at the same time, it was a cause of exhaustion for Rei and his group.

For Rei, wearing a Dragon Robe with its makeshift air conditioning, the weather was no problem at all. But the majority of the group were simply enduring the heat.

Even the women who had previously worked as prostitutes and had never seen a sunburn were now inevitably getting tanned, walking under the midsummer sun.

Some women disliked this, while others welcomed it as a sign of health.

They walked while carefully hydrating, but even so, some reached their physical limits and collapsed.

Those individuals were transported by carriage, but being jostled around in the back of a carriage while suffering symptoms like heatstroke must have been a tremendous physical strain.

(Not that it's guaranteed to happen, but I can't really imagine anyone crazy enough to come with us knowing that risk...)

Rei explained his honest thoughts to Marina, but the look she gave him was one of bewilderment.

"Listen. People from a country like Reburina rarely get the chance to visit a place like the Kingdom of Mireana, especially a fairly developed city like Gilm, you know? Plus, even though we're camping out, with the sand ships, it's not really camping in the true sense."

"So, Marina, you think our numbers will increase?"

"Yes. Definitely."

Rei's decisive tone made him pause, thinking, Is that all there is to it? But he concluded that if Marina said so, she was probably right.

Honestly, it's not surprising that someone would rather go make a name for themselves somewhere more prosperous than languish in a small country like Reburina.

It wouldn't be strange for people to feel that way.

(Like how someone from the countryside gets a job at a Tokyo company after graduating high school or college...?)

Thinking that far, Rei suddenly wondered what he would have done if he hadn't come to this world of Elgin and had just graduated from a normal high school.

Go to university... He'd never been interested in studying, but it would mean being a student for a few years. A college degree carries weight in the countryside, no matter what.

Getting a job at a local company... Considering his personality, he wasn't sure what kind of work would suit him. Since he loved books, maybe working at a bookstore? Or perhaps taking over his family's farm.

In any case, Rei never really had any desire to go to Tokyo.

He had this strange confidence that if he went to Tokyo, the place he saw on TV, he definitely wouldn't be able to handle it. After all, he even got overwhelmed by the crowds at the local summer festival.

(Well, that's irrelevant now.)

I do want to see my parents and friends again, and I want to read the sequels to the manga and novels I was reading.

But even so, the person I am now isn't Saeki Reiji. I'm Rank B adventurer Rei of Crimson, the one with the alias.

Even if I could return to Japan in my current state, my face and physique are vastly different from what they were before... though I'm not sure if "before" is the right way to put it.

Even if I appeared before my family or friends, I was certain... no, absolutely certain they wouldn't recognize me as myself.

"Hey, Rei, that thing..."

Rei had been lost in thoughts about returning to Japan when Marina's startled voice snapped him back to reality.

It was a voice filled with genuine surprise, coming from Marina, who was usually so calm and possessed an almost exaggerated air of mature composure.

That voice—could it be that pursuers sent by Jaya or Rossi were lying in wait? But when Rei followed Marina's gaze, he saw an unexpected figure ahead.

"That's... Gimekara?"

Yes, riding a horse and approaching Rei and the others was undoubtedly Gime Kara of the Zolge Trading Company.

If they remained in the Kingdom of Reburina, the Zolgei Trading Company would suffer significant damage.

To avoid that, they had approached someone to discuss relocating their headquarters to the Kingdom of Mireana... specifically to Gilm, the hottest spot right now—not in terms of temperature, but economically.

In response, Rei and the others had presented their conditions: horses and carriages for a thousand people, fodder, clothing, cloth, water... and escorts.

They presented terms that, by any reasonable standard, would be utterly unacceptable: fulfill Rei's demands, and he would intercede with the Daskar.

Because what Rei had guaranteed was merely an audience with Daskar.

If things went well, that would be ideal, but even if it failed, Rei and his group had no intention of returning the supplies they'd received.

Of course, they anticipated that in that case, it would be presented as Daskar having purchased them from Gimechara.

"Long time no see, Rei."

Approaching Rei and the others, Gime Kara greeted them with a smile.

There was no trace of a grim expression on his face; on the contrary, he wore a confident smile.

Seeing Gimekara like this, it was easy for Rei to understand why he had come here.

"Are you going to fully accept our terms?"

"Yes. Some of the higher-ups were reluctant, but it's certain that things will become hopeless if we continue as we are."

Seeing Gimekara explain that persuading them had been difficult due to the significant expense, Rei regarded him with a little more respect.

The terms had been set knowing they were unreasonable from the start.

As a trading company, he'd assumed they'd likely negotiate for more favorable terms...

(Who'd have thought they'd swallow it whole? That was unexpected. ...But unexpected or not, it's unexpected in a good way.)

If they managed to secure the conditions Rei and the others demanded, the journey ahead would be considerably easier.

And since everyone would be traveling by carriage, their speed would undoubtedly increase significantly compared to before.

That meant we'd reach the envoy sent from Gilm much sooner.

"I see. Then, I suppose we'll camp near this town tonight. Originally, I planned to leave immediately after finishing our business here."

There were adjustments to make regarding who rode in which carriage, stowing the prepared fodder and provisions, meeting the guards... and numerous other tasks besides.

Since it was impossible to finish everything in just a few hours, Rei had no choice but to scrap his original schedule entirely.

The only silver lining was that he hadn't informed everyone of the schedule yet.

Changing plans after informing them would have undoubtedly caused chaos.

"That would be a great help to us as well. We've already informed Dolgana, so it shouldn't be a major issue."

"You're well-connected."

Dorgana, where Rei's gaze was fixed, was often considered one of the foremost cities within the Kingdom of Reburina.

Having already informed Dorgana's upper echelons demonstrated just how much influence the Zolge Trading Company held within the Kingdom of Reburina.

"Yes. The future of the Zolgei Trading Company is at stake. So... is the meeting with Lord Daskar still on?"

"Yes. I'll arrange it for tonight. Also... would it be acceptable to take the sand ship outside the city? If that's not possible, we'll need to travel a considerable distance from here."

"That won't be a problem either. Permission has been properly secured. The city has also agreed to provide guards for surveillance, so please rest easy tonight."

This truly meticulous arrangement revealed Gimekara's serious intent.

Seeing him go to such lengths, Rei concluded he wouldn't betray him from here and nodded.

"Understood. I'm leaving all negotiations, coordination, and everything else with the town entirely to you. ...Just to confirm, we're all allowed into the town, right?"

If it were a village, negotiations could have worked out somehow.

But what lay ahead was the city.

Given that, there was a very real possibility they would be asked for identification.

Rei had intended to negotiate using the names Crimson Wings or Elena, but if the Zolge Trading Company could handle that for him, it would save him the trouble.

As if understanding Rei's thoughts, Gimekara nodded with a gentle smile.

"Sorry about that. Well, we'll need to discuss various things. Marina, you handle it."

"Oh? Is it really okay for me?"

A smile different from Gime Kara's, one with a certain allure.

Yet, Marina's smile carried a hint of provocation as she asked. Rei immediately nodded in agreement.

"You're the best negotiator among us, Marina. So I'm counting on you."

"It's nice to be trusted like that. Well then, I suppose I'll live up to those expectations now. ...Right?"

"Go easy on me."

Though Gimekara was struck by Marina's sidelong glance—a weapon of devastating potency—he managed to respond without showing his unsettlement.

Though, contrary to his composed words, his cheeks were faintly flushed.

Leaving the two there, Rei searched for Sura... though he didn't need to look far. Sura, thinking she had business with him, had already approached.

It was only natural, given that the group had halted before the city of Dolgana.

And seeing Gimekara arrive and exchange words with Rei, it wasn't unreasonable for her to assume some kind of instruction was forthcoming.

"What's up?"

"Gimekara accepted the terms I set earlier and prepared supplies, including a carriage."

"No way!?"

Sura had heard about the conditions too, so actually fulfilling them was completely unexpected.

Perhaps because of that, she cried out in a way that seemed involuntary.

"It's true. So... well. First, we'll need to divide everyone into groups for the carriages. Ideally, I'd like each group of ten to ride together, but if that's impossible, I'll leave it to you to figure out how to split them up."

"...Understood."

Sura still harbored feelings toward the Zolgei Trading Company, which had continued to cooperate with Jaya, and toward Gimekara, who belonged to that company. Still, if everyone could truly fit into the carriages, they could leave the Kingdom of Reburina sooner.

With that calculation in mind, she managed to suppress the irritation within her and gave that reply.
