"Alright, what a haul!"

Rei smiled happily as he watched the spear he had thrown pierce the body of a large fish, which then floated to the surface, and he immediately stowed it away in his Misty Ring.

It wasn’t the kind of tuna Rei had been aiming for, but since it was a large fish, it should still be quite filling.

Since Rei didn’t know anything about this fish, he’d have to ask a fisherman or do some research to find out exactly what it tasted like… or even if it was safe to eat.

“If it turned out to have poison like a pufferfish… well, that wouldn’t be a laughing matter. But I just have a feeling it’ll be fine.”

It wasn’t based on any concrete evidence; it was really just a hunch.

But still, for some reason, Rei had a feeling it would be okay.

“Growl!”

Set let out a growl, eager to eat the fish.

Rei stroked Set’s neck, petting him as if to calm him down.

“For now, let’s focus on catching the fish instead of eating them. In return, I’ll make sure you get a good meal tonight. …Come to think of it, I wish we had a net, but that’s not going to happen.”

As he spoke to Set, Rei thought of how fishing boats haul in nets to catch fish.

With just Set and Rei—one person and one dog—they could undoubtedly do the same kind of fishing if only they had a huge net.

But the problem was how to get that net.

If they went to a fishing village or a port town, they might find a net.

But, conversely, there was also the possibility that there wouldn’t be any nets at all.

After all, Rei didn’t know if they even used nets for fishing.

(It seems they did use nets for fishing in Emoshion, so it’s not as if that kind of fishing hasn’t developed at all… but the question is whether they practice that kind of fishing around here.)

Rei’s assumption was that if they did fish with nets, they would naturally need boats of a certain size.

And since there weren’t any particularly large fishing villages or port towns in this area, Rei judged that it was doubtful whether they’d be able to fish using boats of that size.

...Though, since Rei had chosen this area as a fishing spot precisely because it was sparsely populated to begin with, that’s only to be expected.

“Grr!”

Just as Rei was deep in thought, he heard Set’s growl.

“What’s wrong?” Rei looked over at Set and noticed that he was staring at a specific spot on the water’s surface.

What on earth is Set looking at? Curious, Rei looked where Set was staring and spotted what appeared to be several fish shadows beneath the surface.

They were about seventy percent the size of the fish Rei had caught earlier—slightly larger than half—but unlike the solitary fish from before, these were swimming in a school.

Seeing this, Rei once again wished he had a net, but deciding that catching fish was the priority for now, he reached for his spear from the Misty Ring… or rather, he activated the Nebula’s Eye on his waist.

He decided it would be quicker to use Nebula’s Eye than to pull out his spear and throw it every time.

However, as expected, what Nebula’s Eye produced was an arrowhead, and its attack power was by no means high.

In fact, if asked whether a single arrow could take down a fish… the honest truth is, he wouldn’t know until he tried.

However, the projectiles generated by Nebula’s Eye can be produced endlessly, as long as Rei’s magical power holds out.

While the power of a single shot doesn’t match that of a thrown spear, in terms of rate of fire, Nebula’s Eye has an overwhelming advantage.

“I guess I’ll just have to give it a try. …For the sake of the natural environment, this is probably the better option, though!”

Shouting that, Rei hurled an arrow created by Nebula’s Eye from atop Set’s back as he approached the school of fish.

Normally, he would use the arrowheads created by Nebra’s Eye by flicking them with his fingers, but this time, to increase their power even slightly, he threw them with a snap of his wrist.

The arrow flew through the air as if slicing through it, pierced the seawater, and struck the body of a fish below.

Unlike a spear, simply piercing the seawater did not reduce its power enough for the arrowhead to penetrate the fish’s body.

However, even if it didn’t pierce the fish’s body, its power was still sufficient to kill it.

……No, if I were to throw a discarded spear—assuming the shaft isn’t already on the verge of breaking—but if I were to pierce a fish with a spear that has a chipped tip, there’s a possibility that fragments of the tip could remain inside the fish’s body.

When you think about it that way, since the arrowhead created by magic vanishes immediately, there’s no need to worry about blade fragments.

In that sense, it was even safer than throwing a spear.

The fish, pierced by several arrowheads, floated to the surface.

However, rather than retrieving the fish that had surfaced, Rei—thinking that since it was such a rare opportunity to encounter a school of fish—continued to conjure and throw arrowheads one after another.

Fish that form schools generally do so based on a survival strategy where, when attacked, they sacrifice their companions to ensure their own escape.

In fact, even if a predator or monster were to attack the school, only the unlucky few that got hit a few times would die, and the school itself would likely escape unscathed.

However... in this particular case, I can only say they picked the wrong opponent.

After all, the survival instinct ingrained in the fish’s nature was completely useless.

The main reason for this was likely that Rei and Set, who were currently attacking the school, didn’t eat the fish they caught on the spot but left them be and continued to catch more one after another.

Of course, since the fish that were brought to the surface would eventually sink, they couldn’t keep attacking them forever, but as far as Rei could tell, they didn’t seem to be sinking right away, so the fact that they kept attacking one after another was a major factor.

Moreover, Rei wasn’t the only one attacking—he wasn’t the only one using the arrowheads created by Nebula’s eyes.

Set, whom Rei was riding, was also firing Ice Arrows in rapid succession, driving one after another into the sea.

The relentless barrage of attacks naturally caused the school’s numbers to dwindle at an accelerating rate.

This went on for a few minutes... and when the number of fish floating in the sea had dwindled to nearly a hundred, Rei and Set’s rampage—if you could call it that—finally came to an end.

“Well, if we reduce the fish population too much here, it might have a negative impact on the surrounding waters… though with just one school of fish, the impact probably won’t be that significant. Anyway, Set. Let’s go collect the fish.”

“Grrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled happily.

He was thinking that he’d probably be allowed to snack on a few fish while they were being collected.

Rei understood Set’s intentions, but since Set had worked so hard this time, he didn’t intend to say anything about a few nibbles.

Rei had Set descend close to the surface and tilt his body, then began stowing the fish floating in the sea into the Misty Ring one after another.

Normally, it would be quite difficult to keep holding onto Set’s torso using only leg strength.

But with Rei’s physical abilities, it was a breeze.

As she continued to collect the fish, whenever one looked like it was about to sink, Set would plunge his face into the water, grab it with his beak, and swallow it whole.

(There are quite a few fish sinking into the sea—more than I expected.)

Rei seemed a little disappointed by this, but since the fish that couldn’t be stored in the Misty Ring would end up in the stomachs of the crabs and other creatures in the sea, it wasn’t a complete waste.

In the end, they managed to recover about eighty to ninety percent of the fish, and Set also succeeded in snacking on a fair number of the fish that were about to sink into the sea.

“Well then, Set. Shall we go for a bigger fish next?”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a happy growl.

For Set, a vacation at sea—or, more accurately, fishing—means he gets to spend the whole day with Rei and eat as much delicious fish as he wants, so there’s no way he could be in a bad mood.

And so, Set flew over the sea once more in search of delicious fish.

Along the way, he spotted what appeared to be monsters in the water several times, but whether they sensed Set’s presence or simply found bait and headed that way...

Whatever the reason, once they dive deep into the ocean, even Rei and Set can’t attack them.

Well, maybe they could attack, but it would be more accurate to say they couldn’t finish them off.

“Hmm… There are quite a few small fish, but I can’t find any tuna or anything like that.”

The number of fish visible from the surface is by no means small.

However, most of the fish visible from the surface were small, consisting mainly of small fish like horse mackerel and sardines.

“Ah, but I’d love some fried horse mackerel. Still, since they’re small, they’re probably hard to catch. …Well, I don’t even know if they’re actually horse mackerel.”

As for something like fried horse mackerel, Rei knows the basic method.

It’s not that difficult—you just coat the fish in flour, dip it in beaten egg, coat it in breadcrumbs, and fry it.

...That said, Rei has never even filleted a horse mackerel before, let alone scaled one.

Even if she managed to catch a horse mackerel, there was practically no chance she’d be able to cook it properly.

That said, since they say the way to a man’s heart is through his stomach, and Bistol is a skilled cook, there’s absolutely no need to worry about the cooking.

“I guess I’ll need a net or something. …Maybe I’ll look for a nearby fishing village or port town tomorrow. They might be fishing with nets there.”

“Growl!”

“We might even be able to buy some fish!” Set’s throat growled, and Rei nodded in agreement.

If it’s tuna or a fish of similar size, Rei could easily catch it herself using a spear or Nebra’s eyes.

But when it comes to small fish—like the horse mackerel Rei is craving—there’s no doubt it would be far easier to buy them from a professional fisherman than to catch them himself.

Of course, since it’s the summer sea, things would be different if they were fishing as part of a vacation.

And in fact, while Rei and the others came to the sea partly to catch as many fish as possible, they also wanted to enjoy a vacation since they’ve been working too hard lately.

In fact, since the incident in Treant Forest, they’ve been dealing with the expansion of Gilm, eliminating interference from other nobles, retaliating against a foreign criminal organization that took advantage of the crowds to cause trouble, defeating the massive number of giants that organization had prepared,they’ve been doing all sorts of things: accompanying those who’d been captured and brainwashed by the criminal organization into prostitution back to Gilm with the Resistance, returning to Gilm along the way to help with the expansion work, defeating the large number of bandits around Sabrusta… and so on.

Moreover, these were just the major events; if you count the minor incidents as well, the number would be far greater.

Given how busy I’d been, it was only natural that I’d want to come on vacation to clear my head.

(Well, in this world, people might swim in rivers, but apparently, swimming and playing in the ocean is basically unheard of.)

This is purely because there are more vicious monsters in the ocean than in places like rivers and lakes.

Of course, it’s not as if there are no monsters in rivers, lakes, swamps, or ponds… but when it comes to the number and variety of monsters, the ocean simply wins out.

“Well, even so… I guess I’ll catch enough for tonight’s dinner.”

In this case, what’s effective isn’t an attack designed to reliably take down a giant fish, but a method that can strike a wide area.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei pulls out the Death Scythe—known as his symbol—from his Misty Ring.

“Flying Slash!”

The slashing blade released from the Death Scythe flies straight toward the surface of the sea where the school of fish is swimming.

And the moment the blade struck the water’s surface, it unleashed its full power upon the surrounding area.

Cutting through the sea itself, the blade traveled a certain distance underwater.

Of course, the severed seawater immediately returned to its original state.

But... due to the shockwave generated the moment the Flying Slash touched the water, nearly ten fish swimming near the surface were knocked unconscious and floated to the surface.

(Could Power Slash have delivered a bigger impact? No, but if I’d used that, I would’ve fallen straight into the sea myself. Thinking about it that way, Hishin was definitely the right choice.)

Watching the school of fish hastily swim away from Rei and Set, Rei thought to himself.
