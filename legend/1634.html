"Setou-chan! Take care!"

Expressing sadness with his whole face, Mirei waved vigorously at the departing carriage—or, more precisely, at the back of Set, who was walking alongside it.

Even though it was only the day after Rei and the others had arrived in Asura, he had already left.

Milein would have liked to spend a little more time with Set, but she couldn’t possibly let her own convenience disrupt Rei and the others’ tight schedule.

If she tried anything like that, she would undoubtedly be scolded by Sulunin, and above all, considering the possibility that Set might come to dislike her, there was absolutely no way she could do such a thing.

In reality, even if she had tried to delay Rei and the others’ departure, there was probably nothing Milein could have done anyway.

“Um… hey, is this okay?”

Watching Milein from a short distance away, Ryutas offered his usual smile, yet muttered as if still confused.

As far as Ryutas was concerned, he’d heard that Milein was a skilled adventurer.

In fact, his own bodyguards had told him that Milein was incredibly skilled, and they’d flat-out declared that if they fought her head-on, they wouldn’t stand a chance.

But… the fact remained that the Milein now, grieving over her parting with Set, certainly didn’t look like such a formidable fighter.

“Don’t worry. As long as Set isn’t involved, Milein is a capable adventurer. I can guarantee she’s one of the most skilled young adventurers in Gilm, and she has no character flaws. …Though, seeing Milein like this right now, it wouldn’t be strange if you felt uneasy.”

“That’s true. Normally, she’d be a capable and reliable adventurer… but that’s only ‘normally.’”

In response to Sulunin’s words, Ekril said this.

Watching the two of them, Ryutas wondered if Milein could really be counted on… but a few minutes later, his doubts would be proven wrong.

“Hmm… This fruit is pretty sour.”

Rei, who had put a fruit the size of a fingertip into his mouth, muttered as if caught off guard.

It looked similar to a grape, but instead of the typical grape colors like blue-purple or green, it was Crimson—a color truly befitting Rei’s nickname.

They had bought the fruit in Earthra, but its tartness overpowered any sweetness… it was a fruit that somehow brought to mind the taste of a lemon.

“You bought them to stay awake, right? In that case, wouldn’t it make sense to prioritize their ability to keep you awake over how they taste?”

Inside the carriage, Vihera spoke to Rei, who had taken off her hood and was grimacing at the sourness of the fruit she’d eaten, with an amused expression.

Seeing Rei’s expression like that was probably a rare sight for the others as well. Even Gimekara was watching Rei with interest.

“It’s a Lana fruit. Since it’s usually used in cooking or fruit wine, people don’t normally eat it raw like that…”

Marina, true to her Dark Elf nature, demonstrated her knowledge of plants.

In fact, fruit wine made from Lana berries is quite popular in taverns because it’s delicious for the price.

Rei, who couldn’t exactly call it delicious even though he could drink it, was unaware of this fact.

“Huh. Well, wine aside, it looks like it’d be pretty useful for cooking and stuff.”

Once the sourness on Rei's tongue finally faded, Rei stowed the Lana fruit away in his Misty Ring.

All in all, last night was spent with a large group, exchanging information, negotiating, and debating for a long time.

Naturally, this meant they went to bed late, and on top of that, Rei had the task of stowing the various supplies prepared the night before into his Misty Ring, so he ended up waking up earlier than the others.

Rei isn’t exactly a morning person to begin with.

If he were in the middle of a mission, he could somehow manage to overcome it… but this time, having slept in a bed at a luxury inn, and above all, with Elena and the other women around him, it was difficult to fight off the drowsiness.

As a result, to wake himself up, he bought some Lana fruit at a shop he’d stopped at before leaving Earthra.

“That’s right. When I want to add a bit of tartness to a dish, I use the juice from a Lana fruit. Or I’ll chop it up and add it to the dish.”

At Marina’s words, Byune turned her gaze toward her.

As someone who loves to eat, Byune naturally finds herself drawn to any conversation about food.

For a while after that, Rei and the others continued talking about food.

Gimekara talked about dishes that could be considered the local cuisine of the Kingdom of Reburina; Elena talked about the delicious food she’d eaten at a party; Marina talked about the food she’d eaten back when she was an adventurer before becoming a guild master; and Vihera talked about the cuisine of the Bestir Empire… and so on.

And naturally, whenever the conversation turned to food, the focus inevitably shifted to Rei.

After all, he was the one who had invented various dishes that didn’t exist before—like udon and meat buns.

Gimekara, who had been gathering information on Rei and the others, apparently hadn’t known that Rei was so knowledgeable about food.

She was looking at Rei with a gaze that was a bit too intense to simply call “interested.”

New dishes that had never existed before must have seemed like a huge business opportunity to the Zolgue Trading Company as well.

In fact, that wasn’t wrong, and in Gilm, many people made a fortune thanks to the dishes Rei created, starting with udon.

That said, the people Rei taught cooking to didn’t keep the recipes to themselves; they shared the information widely.

It’s likely that this is one of the reasons why udon and various other dishes spread so explosively in Gilm.

If Full Belly Inn, the restaurant where Rei taught udon, had kept the recipe to themselves, they would have made a profit, but udon would never have become as widespread as it is today.

“Rei, if you wouldn’t mind, could you teach the Zolgue Trading Company a recipe or two? Since we’re going to be operating in Gilm, it would be helpful to have something that stands out.”

“……Well, that’s a tough one.”

Although Rei had taught cooking to a few people before, starting with udon, he wasn’t exactly an expert on the subject.

In fact, even with dishes like udon and steamed buns that Rei had taught, she didn’t remember every single detail of how to make them.

She’d explain a rough idea and the basic steps Rei remembered, but it was the chefs who actually turned those ideas into proper dishes.

When it came to dishes Rei wanted to try that he could recall the basic method for… well, the number was extremely limited.

(Pasta? No, I don’t even know how to make pasta in the first place. Since it uses flour and such, I suppose it’s similar to udon. Ah, I think it’s only called pasta if you use a specific type of flour… or was that it?)

In Italy, the home of pasta, even the ingredients for pasta are specified by law.

Rei recalled seeing a bit about that in a cooking manga he’d read before, but since he didn’t know what the essential ingredients were, there was no way he could make pasta.

Even if he tried to tackle that, what he’d end up with would almost certainly be udon… or something similar to udon.

(In that case, soba? I have a vague idea of how to make soba...)

Living in Japan, you have plenty of opportunities to see scenes of people making soba on TV or in manga.

In fact, if you asked which is more widespread—udon or soba—it would undoubtedly be soba.

However... soba has a few fatal flaws as well.

(I’ve never actually seen a buckwheat flower or seed, though.)

When Rei was in Japan, there was a farmer who grew buckwheat.

That’s why I’m confident I’d recognize a place where buckwheat grows if I saw it.

But since there wasn’t a single buckwheat plant in sight, eating soba was out of the question.

(I suppose you could make soba just by grinding the seeds into flour and adding hot water... but wait, doesn’t it take some skill to shape it into noodles?)

Rei recalled the time in junior high school when she made New Year’s soba using buckwheat flour her father had brought home.

It had sort of taken the shape of noodles, but the moment she picked them up with chopsticks, they would snap into pieces—it was simply impossible to call them soba.

Based on that memory, she thinks that even if buckwheat grew wild, it might be difficult to eat it as noodles...

Of course, Rei’s father was, after all, an amateur. If it were the chefs in Elgin—let alone the people making udon in Gilm—they might have been able to make proper soba.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“Hm? Oh, nothing. I was just wondering if there’s a dish I could teach you…”

“Oh! So, how’s it going? Did you come up with anything?”

“You probably don’t know this, but most of the dishes I know are ones I memorized from a book I had while training under my master.”

It’s been a while… Recalling a setting he hadn’t used in a very long time, Rei continued.

“That’s exactly why I’m not that knowledgeable about cooking. Plus, even if I know a dish, there are times when I have almost no idea how to make it.”

That said, when it comes to dishes I’m familiar with—having read quite a few cooking manga—there are actually quite a few I know.

But just because I know the names of the dishes doesn’t mean I can actually make them… the answer is no.

That means I really need to teach Rei the dishes I actually know how to make, but there are very few of those.

(If we don’t have rice or soy sauce, dishes like Kiritanpo hot pot or Damako hot pot are out of the question… If we were near the ocean, maybe something like Hatahata or Jotturu hot pot… No, wait, I don’t even know if we have Hatahata to begin with.)

I ponder this while talking with Gimekara, but I still can’t think of any dishes off the top of my head.

Since we’re by the sea, I remember teaching her how to make seafood okonomiyaki before, but that’s not something I can just whip up easily either.

“Hmm, I see. …Well, if you think of anything, could you let me know?”

“If the opportunity arises.”

Rei answers that way for now, thinking that if he rashly promises to teach her here, it might lead to all sorts of trouble later on.

Gimekara probably understood that, but she nodded without any particular objection to Rei’s words.

“Rei, the checkpoint is coming into view!”

Just then, the coachman’s voice rang out.

Even though they had only just left Earthra this morning, the fact that they had already arrived at the checkpoint showed just how fast travel by carriage was.

In fact, if everyone had been traveling on foot like they did when they left Mejogo, it would likely have taken several days to reach the checkpoint.

(There’s no checkpoint between us and the hostile Bestir Empire, yet there’s one at the border with the vassal state of Reburina. …Well, I suppose there is the Selemus Plains between us and the Bestir Empire, which could be called a natural border.)

The Selemus Plains, where undead roam at night—or even during the day if you’re unlucky—are truly a place that deserves to be called a natural border.

If there were a similar place along the border with the Kingdom of Reburina, there might not have been any kind of checkpoint there.

“Got it. I should have already made the necessary arrangements, so I don’t think we’ll be stopped for inspection. If they say anything, feel free to send them over here.”

“Yes, I’ll do that.”

The coachman nodded at Rei’s words and began to gradually slow the carriage’s speed.

Since the carriage carrying Rei and the others, leading the procession, was slowing down, the other carriages naturally followed suit.

The soldiers at the checkpoint could only watch in stunned silence.

…And no wonder. In a rural area like this, they never expected to see such a large group of carriages traveling together.

On top of that, there were covered wagons, box wagons, and even some freight wagons… as far as they could tell, the types of carriages were all different.

If they hadn’t been briefed at all, it wouldn’t be strange for them to think that the Kingdom of Reburina was invading.

“Hey, that’s Crimson Rei in that carriage! There are other important figures on board too, so don’t do anything stupid! Just as the delegation told us, don’t inspect them—just let them pass!”

“Y-yeah, I know!”

The man in charge of this checkpoint shouted at the soldiers on the spot, warning them not to do anything strange.

Given that he’s in charge of such a rural area, he’s not exactly a capable man… but at the same time, since he’s entrusted with a border checkpoint—albeit a small one—he’s not exactly incompetent either.

In terms of carrying out the orders he’s given, he’s a reliable man.

As a result, Rei and the others passed through the checkpoint without any trouble… and successfully entered the Kingdom of Mireana.
