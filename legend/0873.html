The day after conquering the dungeon, Rei was walking through town around mid-morning.

In his hands, he clutched skewered meats, jerky, and sandwiches bought or given to him at stalls, but this wasn't a stroll specifically for snacking.

...Just a little while ago, as usual, those who wanted to play with Set had gathered.

But today, he had other business, so he cut the playtime short and was now walking through town.

Still, he had spent a little time with the children, probably because Set also enjoyed playing with them.

The adults, of course, understood what Rei meant and only gave Set a light pat.

And where Rei was headed... wasn't the guild.

While the Gamelion matter was a factor, it was obvious that since it had only happened yesterday, the stripping of materials and dismantling would hardly have progressed much by today.

Of course, he could have gone to the guild to see if there were any interesting requests or to check how far along the Gamelion's dismantling was, but that would only be if he had no other business.

Today, he had business, which was why he was walking through the city streets with Set.

And what came into view was the library... but I turned before reaching it, heading toward the back alleys.

Perhaps it was due to winter preparations, but most of the people walking the streets were dressed warmly.

The quality of those clothes, however, dropped by a grade or two among the people in the back alleys.

Amidst these people, Rei and Set walked on, drawing attention.

That said, the one person and one dog receiving the attention were already thoroughly accustomed to it. They walked down the back alley without seeming particularly bothered.

Soon, a familiar shop came into view in Rei's sight.

Pamidor's shop.

In front of the shop, a small child was waving something around.

The child spotted Rei and Set—or more accurately, Set—and broke into a run, a delighted smile lighting up their face.

"It's Set! Set! Hey, hey, can we play? Is now okay?"

The rapid-fire words conveyed the child's pure joy at seeing Set, a feeling that reached Rei honestly too.

"Grrrrr."

"Kumito, it's been a while."

"Yeah, it's been a while! You haven't been around lately. Where've you been?"

The innocent smile he directed at Rei made it hard to believe he was the son of Pamidor, his biological father.

In fact, few who saw Kumito for the first time would believe he and Pamidor—who looked like nothing but a vicious bandit—were father and son.

In fact, there had even been an incident before where a passerby mistook Pamidor and Kumito walking together for a kidnapping and went to call the guards.

Every time Rei saw Kumito, he couldn't help but wonder at the mysteries of genetics.

(Come to think of it, I've never met Kumito's mother.)

Given that she was marrying Pamidor, Rei assumed she must be quite gutsy inside. At the same time, since she had produced such an adorable child as Kumito, it was clear she must also be quite attractive on the outside.

Though he wanted to meet her, Rei called out to Kumito instead.

"So, Kumito. Is Pamidor in the shop?"

"Ah, yeah. He said something about final adjustments for a job he was asked to do a little while ago."

"I see. Well, I need to talk to Pamidor, so could you hang out with Set for a bit?"

"Sure! Leave it to me! Hey, Set. Want to play with me?"

"Grrr."

Set purred happily at Kumi's words.

He rubbed his face against Kumito's, laughter echoing around them.

(Kids are like the wind, aren't they? ...I just hope they don't catch a cold.)

Glancing at Kumito, who was dressed fairly warmly, Rei opened the shop door.

"Pamidor, are you there?"

He called out, but there was no reply.

Though there was a presence inside... Rei tilted his head, then stepped inside. The first thing he saw was Pamidor, staring intently at a dagger.

Strangely, his fierce face didn't seem all that frightening now that he was looking at it like this.

It was the face of a man truly focused on his work, he thought.

(Better not disturb him.)

Seeing Pamidor like this, Ray entered the shop but leaned against the wall.

"......"

Inside the shop hung a tense atmosphere.

That must mean Pamidor was working with such seriousness.

Several minutes passed like that...

"Well, that'll do."

Finally satisfied, Pamidor sheathed the dagger he had been holding.

Having watched the entire action, Rei finally spoke up.

"You're done?"

"Yeah. ...Huh? Whoa! Rei!? When did you get here!?"

"So you really didn't notice. Well, I guess it's only natural when you're that focused. It wasn't that long ago. A few minutes at most."

Smiling slightly at Pamidor's predictable reaction, Rei's gaze shifted to the dagger in Pamidor's hand.

"What about that dagger?"

"Huh? Oh, it's for a job I was asked to do. Apparently, someone was caught off guard by a Gamelion and had to parry its fang attack with a dagger in the heat of the moment. They survived, but the blade chipped in the process. So, I was asked to repair it."

"Then I think you should just buy a new one."

With the blade chipped off like that, repairing it would cost a pretty penny.

And if it's the work of a skilled smith like Pamidor, the price will likely go up, not down.

Hearing Rei's words, spoken with that thought in mind, Pamidor let out a deep sigh.

"Logically speaking, that's the right call. But it saved your life, didn't it? Attachment... maybe that's the wrong word. It's only natural to feel something for it."

"Is that how it is?"

"Take your Death Scythe, for example. If it got damaged, wouldn't you want to use a replacement?"

Pamidor's words made perfect sense to Rei.

The Death Scythe was a magic item forged through demonic beast magic, and it was true that he felt a strong attachment to it, having created it using his own magical power as fuel.

If something went wrong with that Death Scythe, and he was asked if he could use a replacement weapon... the answer was no.

"Yeah, Pamidor's right. ...But still, Gamelion?"

Changing the subject, Rei voiced Gamelion's name.

The previous day's dungeon incident had left him genuinely concerned.

Pamidor nodded at Rei's words and spoke.

"Ah. I heard Gamelion hasn't been caught this year, but that doesn't mean there are none. Skilled adventurers and those with good luck seem to be managing to bring Gamelion back. The Gamelion meat being sold is apparently from those who've managed to get their hands on it."

"Yeah, probably. ...Well, I think Gamelion meat will become more plentiful and cheaper eventually, so it's probably fine not to worry too much about it."

"Huh? What do you mean? Have Gamelions become easier to catch?"

Pamidor couldn't let Rei's words go unquestioned.

Pamidor had only come to Gilm last year, and had only eaten Gamelion meat last autumn.

But after just one taste, Pamidor had become utterly captivated by the flavor of Gamelion.

That was precisely why he absolutely needed to know the true meaning behind Rei's words now.

"Well, I suppose I could say 'probably' or 'likely,' but it's fairly reliable information."

"...What are you hiding? We haven't known each other that long, but even I can tell you're hiding something?"

"There's a lot going on. You get it, right?"

"I see. ...Understood. So, Gamelion will start circulating more widely, then?"

"Yeah, probably."

"Fine. I'll take your word for it. ...So, what brought you here today? As you can see, I'm not exactly free."

Rei flashed a momentarily taken aback expression at Pamidor, who asked while inspecting the hilt of his dagger.

After a moment, he opened his mouth, looking exasperated.

"Didn't you say you were going to the alchemist about the magic sword I brought? That's why I came... Did you forget?"

"Huh? Oh, right, that was something I needed to do. Sorry."

Rei's words seemed to finally jog his memory. He placed the dagger on the counter and immediately headed toward the back of the shop.

"Wait here a moment. I'll get everything ready."

That was all he said.

"It's good you're dedicated to your work, but maybe you focus a little too much. Well, no, it's probably that intense focus that made you such a skilled blacksmith in the first place. ...Though looking at your face, you definitely look like a badass."

"Shut up. This face is how I was born. What can I do about it?"

Pamidor, dressed in cold-weather gear for going out, emerged from the back of the shop, grumbling.

"Come on, let's go. That guy's pretty moody. If he's in the mood, he'll help us right away, but if he's not, it'll take forever."

Though he grumbled, the tone in Pamidor's voice carried affection.
