A massive ship suddenly appeared right next to Ursula.

Those unfamiliar with the magic item known as the "sand-walking ship," or who didn’t know much about Rei and his group, were naturally startled by the sight of the ship—some were so shocked they not only lost their composure but even fainted.

However, the guards quickly provided an explanation, and most people regained their composure.

Once everyone had calmed down, it was only natural that some began to show a keen interest in the existence of sand ships.

This town of Earthra is the closest to the Kingdom of Mireana within the nation of Reburina, and as such, it is home to many who could be called wealthy merchants.

Naturally, such people sought to gather information about magic items they did not possess, hoping to purchase them if possible—some even considered using coercion to do so. However, upon learning that the sand ship was connected to the Zolge Trading Company, they became hesitant to act rashly.

...Though, considering the fate that befell the Azott Trading Company—which had previously tried to meddle with Rei—it was actually fortunate that they backed off.

“Well then, Ronitas. I’ll be relying on you today.”

“Ah, don’t worry about it. Just relax. We’ll send someone over to guard the sand ship.”

With that, Ronitas left Rei’s room.

Rei is currently staying in a room at the Earth’s Bounty Inn, renowned as one of the finest inns in Earthra.

Thanks to Ronitas’s kindness, Rei’s party—the Crimson Wings, Elena, Ryutas, Gimekara, and Sura—would be staying at the Earth’s Bounty Inn tonight.

Once inside, Rei’s magic tent is just like the Earth’s Bounty Inn—no, it’s actually even more comfortable.

Even so, Rei and the others decided to accept Ronitas’s kindness.

For Ronitas, having Rei and the others stay at their inn also served the purpose of ensuring they could contact them immediately if anything happened.

“I suppose we’ll need to exchange information and discuss various matters with Lord Daskar, but for now, I think I’ll just relax.”

He muttered, lying down on the bed.

It wasn’t that he didn’t want to play with Set, but from what Rei had seen before entering Earthra, it seemed Set was playing with Milein.

He worried that if he clumsily interrupted Milein here, in the worst-case scenario, she might leave Ronitas’s escort and come with them instead.

Therefore, Rei’s thinking was that it would be better to let Set and Milein play to their hearts’ content here, so they wouldn’t have any regrets.

In reality, though, just as she was glaring at the women who were jealous of her playing with Set, Sulunin intervened and forcibly dragged her back.

Unaware of this, Rei still believed that Milein was playing with Set.

Sitting on the bed, she looked out the window.

In the sky, the moon was pouring brilliant moonlight down onto the earth.

The cloudless night sky hinted at another clear day tomorrow.

Well, since we’re traveling by carriage, it’s only natural to hope for clear weather, I suppose.

Muttering to herself, she enjoyed some time alone for the first time in a long while… a very long while.

Lately, I’ve been spending most nights in the magic tent with Elena and the others, so I haven’t had much time to truly be alone.

Of course, it’s not that I feel uneasy being with Elena and the others, but still, I think it’s nice to have a little time to myself like this every once in a while.

Just like that, time passes as I gaze at the moon in the sky without thinking about anything in particular.

All I could hear were the sounds of people making noise outside the inn and laughter from the dining hall on the first floor, where they were probably having a party.

I wonder how much time had passed... Suddenly, the sound of a knock on the door echoed, and Rei snapped back to reality.

“Excuse me, Rei. May I have a moment?”

Perhaps because the voice coming from the other side of the door sounded familiar, Rei replied without giving it much thought.

“Gimekara? Sure, come on in.”

As Rei spoke, the door opened, and there stood Gimekara.

“I’m sorry to disturb you while you’re resting. I’d like to talk to you briefly about the bodyguard matter… Would you have a moment?”

“Sure, no problem. I didn’t have anything else to do anyway.”

People might call that resting, but Gimekara didn’t say anything in response to Rei’s words; she simply walked into the room and sat down on the sofa.

“Regarding the bodyguards, fortunately, since Earthra is a large city, we were able to assemble a decent number of people. …Though, just as before, I selected them based on personality rather than skill.”

“That’s okay, right?” Gimekara asked with a look, and Rei nodded in response.

Since the group was composed almost entirely of women, bringing in a rough-and-tumble man would undoubtedly cause problems.

To prevent that, it was only natural to prioritize character over skill.

“That’s fine in itself. …But aren’t there any adventurers who are both skilled and have a good personality? It wasn’t a problem before since we were in the Kingdom of Reburina, but once we enter the Kingdom of Mireana, we won’t be able to get away with that anymore.”

It might be fine near the border, but if they entered the Kingdom of Mireana as they were, the likelihood of being attacked by bandits—not to mention monsters—was high.

Since Rei’s group consists almost entirely of women, they’re nothing but sitting ducks from any angle.

Rei’s words were only natural, but Gimekara shook her head with a troubled expression.

“I’m sorry, but things aren’t that simple… I’ve been looking into it thoroughly myself, though.”

“Difficult, is it?”

“Yes. However… as we get closer to Gilm, I expect there will naturally be more skilled adventurers, so I suppose we’ll just have to hope for the best in that regard.”

“I suppose so. But the problem in that case is whether we can actually make it to the towns and villages where those adventurers are.”

“That’s right.”

As Gimekara muttered thoughtfully, Rei wondered, Should I ask Milein and the others to come over here after all?

But he immediately shook his head, dismissing the idea.

There was no doubt he’d prefer they came, but the reason the members of Scorching Wind were heading to the Kingdom of Reburina alongside Ronitas’s delegation was partly for symbolic purposes.

Being a Rank C party at such a young age naturally draws a lot of attention.

…Though, even if I say “young,” Sulunin is actually of a certain age.

“I see. In that case, we’ll need to make the combat training we’re conducting before we leave a little more rigorous.”

If they can’t rely on others to protect them, they’ll have to protect themselves.

At Rei’s words, which implied just that, Gimekara wore a complex expression but nodded.

Even from Gimekara’s perspective, the combat training being conducted recently is quite intense.

Since everyone is traveling by carriage, those undergoing training are still managing, but if they had to travel on foot as they did in the past, it wouldn’t be surprising if many had already dropped out.

...And yet, in Rei’s mind, this is still considered light training.

Watching such training, Gimekara couldn’t help but wonder: Is it really safe to make it any harder than this?

At the same time, training conducted by high-ranking adventurers with nicknames is something that many people—especially those whose livelihood depends on combat—would be willing to pay to experience themselves.

(It’s like a shrewd merchant teaching other merchants how to do business… or something like that.)

Gimekara thought this as she watched Rei, but while it seemed accurate, it was slightly off the mark.

In any case, to someone who knows nothing about it, there’s no doubt that the women’s position seems incredibly enviable.

“Anyway, we still have until tomorrow, so I’ll try a little harder. If that doesn’t work, I suppose I’ll have to look around once we enter the Kingdom of Mireana…”

“Is that okay?” Gimekara asked, and Rei nodded.

If that’s the only option, then there’s no choice but to do so.

“Understood. …Oh, right. And about that conversation with Lord Daskar we were supposed to have soon—what should we do?”

“What do you mean, ‘what should we do’? There’s no way I’m not going.”

In reality, he had left almost all the information gathering and negotiations with Daskar entirely up to Marina.

Still, as the party leader of the Crimson Wings, he couldn’t be so irresponsible as to not be present.

It would be a different story if I were so busy I couldn’t spare a moment, but since I was taking a leisurely break like this, I certainly couldn’t claim to be busy.

(Wait, is it even possible to be too busy to take a break?)

Even though he thought that, Rei understood that such reasoning wouldn’t hold water.

“Well then, shall we go?”

Nodding at Gimekara’s words, Rei left the room.

“Oh, you could have stayed a little longer.”

Seeing Rei enter the lounge they’d used earlier that day, Marina smiled and called out to him.

Marina, too, must have thought that Rei—who was always with them—needed some time to rest alone.

She didn’t think Rei found it difficult to be with them, but she still judged that having just one man among a group of women would likely cause various mental issues.

(It’s a bit presumptuous of me to say so, but our party is full of beauties, including Elena.)

Marina used the hot water provided in the lounge to brew some tea and placed it in front of Rei and Gimekara.

“Oh, Marina-san, going to the trouble of making us tea. If the others found out, they’d probably be green with envy.”

Gimekara said this jokingly, but it was by no means an exaggeration.

Marina, Elena, Vihera... These three possess beauty worthy of being called peerless beauties, and among them, Marina clearly surpasses the other two in terms of feminine allure.

For that very reason, if word got out that Marina had made someone tea, it would certainly be no surprise if people grew jealous.

“Hehe, thank you. …Well, once Ryutas and Ronitas arrive, we’ll be all set…”

The moment Marina said that, the door opened as if someone had timed it perfectly.

“Huh? Everyone’s here already? You’re a little late.”

Ronitas said this as he entered the lounge with Ryutas.

It seemed they had exchanged quite a bit of information with each other before coming here. Their attitudes toward one another had become much more relaxed compared to when they first met.

For Rei, who had brought Ryutas along, it was a relief to see that they seemed to be building a good relationship.

“Well then, since everyone’s here… Elena, please.”

“Mm-hmm.”

Nodding at Marina’s words, Elena takes out the Paired Orb.

Since no one present was seeing the Paired Orb for the first time, no one was particularly surprised when the figure of Ara appeared on the other side of it.

“Lady Elena, we’ve been waiting for you… and Lord Daskar.”

While Ara ordered someone nearby to go fetch Daskar, Elena and Ara exchanged various remarks.

Until Daskar arrives, those around them do not interrupt their conversation.

“So, where are you staying tonight, Lady Elena?”

“In a town called Asura, near the border between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Kingdom of Reburina.”

“Ah, I know that town. I heard it was attacked by a group of goblins a few years ago.”

“I see.”

Elena murmured, seeming slightly impressed by the information that came from Ara’s mouth.

Goblins are, after all, among the weakest of monsters.

But even so, numbers are power, and goblins are quite formidable when it comes to multiplying their numbers.

Moreover, this wasn’t exactly the frontier; it was a place that could more appropriately be called the countryside.

Given that a town in such a place had been attacked by a group of goblins and managed to fend them off, it was only natural that Elena would find it surprising.

(Though, if these were the kind of goblins that appear in a place like this, it’s possible that even as a group, their numbers weren’t all that large.)

As I thought this and enjoyed my conversation with Ara...

“I heard you received a message from Elena?”

A voice echoed from the other side of the Paired Orb.

And the figure who appeared was the very person Rei and the others had been waiting for.

Daskar, the lord of Gilm.

“Lord Daskar, I apologize for the trouble.”

“No problem. More importantly… hmm, it seems everyone is here. Well then, let’s get right to exchanging information and discussing matters.”

With that said... the discussion between Rei’s group, the diplomatic delegation, and Daskar began.
