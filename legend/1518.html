Rei walked down Mejougo's main street, disappointed by how little information he'd managed to extract from the man.

(Well, he was a former adventurer turned thug... or was he still an adventurer? Either way, that's the kind of guy he was. I didn't expect him to have any crucial information... but still, he had less than I anticipated.)

He would have preferred to get something more substantial.

That's what Rei thought. If someone knew something and wouldn't talk, he could try every trick in the book to get it out of them. But if they didn't have the information to begin with, there was nothing he could do.

Feeling disappointed, he walked the street, wondering if anyone else would come bother him...

But those who came here in the first place were here to embrace prostitutes.

Some might be after the drink, but they were undoubtedly an overwhelming minority.

With their ultimate pleasure awaiting them, few would deliberately bother with Rei.

Unless it's for a reason like that man earlier, who couldn't find his intended prostitute, such people simply don't exist.

"Hey, mister. Want to play for a bit?"

"No, sorry, but I still want to look around the town of Mejougo, so I'll pass."

He replied to the prostitute wearing Ren's mask instead of Rei's, her shoulder-length brown hair cut straight across.

(For now, I should check out that... police box? Where the organization... Jaya gathers. They say it's a hideout, so there might be some information there.)

Having decided his next move, Rei continued down the street, occasionally being approached by several prostitutes.

Along the way, he passed several men happily clutching prostitutes' waists, but such men naturally paid Rei no mind.

They were smiling, noses twitching, imagining the fun to come.

(Still... while some might be content with their work as prostitutes, surely there are others who were forcibly brought here, like what happened in Gilm with Ajax? Yet, none of the prostitutes seem to be wearing unhappy expressions. ...What's going on?)

It was a question that had struck him when he first came to Mejougo, but seeing more prostitutes only intensified it.

Why did none of them show negative emotions like sadness, anger, or despair?

Something is definitely going on, but I can't figure out what it is.

(The hideout... no, the headquarters. Could the headquarters hold the secret to that something? ...Probably not.)

Rei felt a momentary flicker of hope, but immediately dismissed it himself.

Fundamentally, even if an organization like Jāya had secrets—and Rei was certain it did—would they really hide such secrets in a station that anyone could easily enter? The answer was undoubtedly no.

Even Rei couldn't imagine wanting to hide something like that in such a place.

(So, it must be somewhere else... Wait, even if there is a secret, it doesn't necessarily have to be in Mejougo, does it? It would be safer hidden somewhere deep in the mountains where no one goes. ...Well, that is, if there's no need to access it frequently.)

Thinking like that as I walked, I headed toward the office the man had mentioned earlier... and eventually found it.

It looked slightly different from what Rei had imagined when she heard the word "office."

The one Rei had pictured was, naturally, the Gilm headquarters he'd visited countless times before.

A plain, unremarkable building.

But... the headquarters in Mejougo was vastly different from the one Rei knew.

Of course, Rei understood there were differences—the guards versus the underground organization, the frontier town of Gilm versus the entertainment district of Mejougo.

But what now lay before Rei's eyes was a far more flamboyant headquarters than he had imagined.

Using magic items for lighting was a characteristic of Mejougo, so that made sense.

But the meaning behind decorating it to such an extravagant degree... he wondered if it was really necessary, then quickly understood.

After all, Mejougo was a pleasure district visited by countless customers daily.

Moreover, since the brothels themselves were decked out to compete with each other, a plain headquarters would simply get lost among the other establishments.

If that happened, it would be entirely possible for those seeking the headquarters to have no idea where to go.

Given that, Rei figured the headquarters simply had to stand out as a headquarters.

Though it was entirely possible the members of the organization stationed there simply wanted their location to be conspicuous...

Anyway, having found the target building, Rei hid in an alleyway where he could observe the headquarters.

Being an entertainment district, it was no surprise that various troubles arose.

Less than ten minutes into his observation, a man dashed into the headquarters.

A few minutes passed...

"Huh?"

A surprised murmur escaped Rei's lips.

Because the man who emerged from the office alongside the one who had rushed in earlier didn't look like a serious guy... at least not like a low-level member of an underground organization.

It might have been natural if he were fulfilling a guard-like role, but this man looked serious.

He looked like he could easily pass for a regular security guard, not someone from the underground organization.

(Well, if he's fulfilling the same role as a security guard, I suppose he couldn't exactly dress like a thug?)

As Rei came to that conclusion, his gaze followed two men carrying a man who must have passed out drunk into the guardhouse.

(There sure is a lot of coming and going here. ...I'd really like to go inside and check it out once... but that might be a bit tricky. No, actually, maybe I should just act troubled and go into that guardhouse myself. ...But I really want to avoid doing anything that might make them remember my face for now.)

As Rei pondered this, he suddenly noticed a woman heading toward the office.

If she had been an ordinary woman, he wouldn't have paid her much mind.

Or even if she were a prostitute, he could understand her going to the guardhouse for help if a client caused trouble and she needed it resolved.

But the woman approaching was far from what one would call a prostitute.

The clothes she wore were instantly recognizable as extravagant, made with copious amounts of gold and silver thread and studded with jewels. But the biggest problem was the woman wearing them.

She was nearly two meters tall, and her body was so large it seemed her weight easily exceeded a hundred kilograms.

(People often use the phrase "busty, curvy, voluptuous," but this was more like "busty, busty, busty.")

Her face was far from beautiful, reminiscent of an orc.

Given that she was such a woman, there was no way Rei could see her as a prostitute.

Of course, given that people have all sorts of tastes in this world, he did consider that such a prostitute might exist.

But seeing the men standing guard in front of the guardhouse, and those coming out of it, all bowing their heads to her in unison, made it impossible to think of her as a prostitute.

Moreover, the expression on the men's faces as they bowed was one of awe.

If the woman were forcing the men to bow through some kind of influence, they wouldn't be wearing expressions like that.

It was clear that the men felt awe and fear toward the woman herself, not the person behind her.

Given such a figure, it was easy for Rei to guess what kind of position this woman held.

(Someone from Jaya's upper echelons? ...Though called a vassal state, a minor kingdom, Reburina is quite something...)

Even to Rei's eyes, she seemed to possess a decent level of skill.

Her exact strength was unclear, but she seemed to possess the power of a Rank D... no, perhaps even a Rank C adventurer.

Based on the information gathered beforehand about the Kingdom of Reburina, the existence of someone this strong was almost unconfirmed...

(Guess they're out there where they're needed. Besides, it's a fact that Jaya's raking in a fortune. It wouldn't be strange for someone to be after that, or hoping to get a piece of the scraps, huh?)

Rei was observing the orc-like woman, but she must have sensed his gaze, for she suddenly stopped in her tracks.

Then she touched the handle of the mace at her waist... that vicious mace, the kind with spikes on the tip.

But probably because Rei quickly looked away. As if to say, that sensation must have been a mistake... she glanced around.

"Hey, sis, what's up?"

"...Nothing. More importantly, no major problems, right?"

"Yeah. A few fights broke out, some drunks got carried in, but that's all. ...But..."

The man hesitated slightly, unsure if he should say it.

But the woman, glancing at him, opened her mouth with a hint of irritation.

"Just tell me. Even if you don't understand it, it might be something that concerns me."

"Uh, right. Well... actually, the coachman who brought tonight's guest from Rossi mentioned something. Apparently, there was a guy who seemed to be snooping around us. ...But, it wasn't so much snooping as he just looked like a total country bumpkin, so they left him alone."

"...Hmm, I see. From what you're saying, it doesn't sound like a problem..."

Overhearing the conversation, Rei frowned slightly, likely realizing she was the topic.

He had intended to wear the mask of Ren, the naive outsider, and act as if he knew nothing. Yet, it seemed he had still caught the driver's eye.

(Come to think of it, the carriage carries the passengers, and the coachman drives it. Assigning someone with keen observation or sharp intuition to spot suspicious types is probably only natural.)

Maybe I should have been a little quieter.

Even as Rei pondered this, the conversation continued.

"If you order it, Sister, I could go catch him right away. What do you say?"

"Hmm. I suppose I'd like to see the guy once, if possible... but things are about to get busy. If he's only slightly suspicious, maybe it's better to let him swim for now."

"Huh? Busy? I haven't heard anything specific coming up."

"Ah, it's none of your concern. Stop worrying about unnecessary things. Right now, just make sure no trouble arises with this Mejougo."

Hearing this, the man briefly flashed an expression that seemed to want to ask something... But he knew saying more wouldn't get an answer, and above all, he feared that learning information he shouldn't know could put his own life in danger. So he simply nodded obediently at the woman's words.

"Understood. Well... 'fortunately' might be the wrong word, but we have enough prostitutes. I doubt we'll have another shortage like before."

"Ah, that idiot's business. ...Damn, why are men always so... Never mind that. So, if no other major problems arise, I'm heading out. Is that alright?"

Recalling the man she'd encountered during the previous incident, the woman asked with a displeased expression. The man could only nod in response.

"Yes. Thank you for coming all this way. Please use this to buy yourself something nice to eat."

Saying this, the man handed her several silver coins.

Normally, there would be no need to give money here, but the woman before him was known as one of the most skilled individuals in Jāya.

And yet, as you can see, her appearance wasn't exactly what you'd call attractive, and she harbored a strong complex about her looks.

This had made her quite neurotic and twisted in personality.

Knowing this, the man offered the money to try and win her favor.

Fortunately, the woman smiled happily as intended, took the silver coins, and walked away.

The man bowed his head to her... and when she was finally out of sight, he raised his face, relieved that he had managed to get through it safely.
