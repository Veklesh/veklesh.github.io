"Well, that’s all for today! Thanks for your hard work, everyone!"

It was unusually clear for winter, and though the day was far shorter than in summer, as the guild staff made their announcement during the twilight hour when the surroundings were bathed in red, those who had participated in the Giganto Turtle butchering carefully cradled the meat they had received and made their way back to Gilm.

While many would likely head to the guild to collect at least some reward, those not among the majority would probably prioritize either eating the Giganto Turtle meat they received from Rei themselves or selling it for a fair price, rather than going to the guild.

……Of course, if this many people all headed to the guild at once, it would naturally get crowded.

Also, adventurers who accepted the Kobold extermination quest should be returning to the guild as well, so considering the prospect of avoiding that crowd, the choice to wait a little while before going to the guild is certainly not a bad one.

“So, Rei. What are we going to do?”

Unlike those who’ve returned to Gilm, Suchi and the others from the slums were told to stay here.

We’ve already received our rewards, aside from the Giganto Turtle meat.

“For now, you guys should go back to the slums and bring your parents, siblings, friends, lovers, wives… people like that. Basically, the people you live with. Like I said earlier today, there’s a chance there are people out there who might take you hostage to get their hands on the money you’ve earned.”

“Huh? Really?”

At Rei’s words, Suchi let out a cry of surprise.

It was only natural. She had heard about the plan to hide them in the warehouse, but Suchi had refused, explaining that the people who came with her all had families of their own.

It was for their own convenience, but since Rei had turned down the offer, she’d assumed the plan to hide them in the warehouse was off the table.

But she never imagined that idea was still on the table... Seeing Suchi’s astonished reaction, Rei turned his gaze toward the guild official.

She had assumed he had already told them about her going to the Guild to negotiate the matter directly... that was what she thought.

“Huh? I’m sure I told the subordinate who brought that news to pass it on… Really.”

Realizing from their exchange that communication had broken down, the guild official bowed his head.

“I’m sorry. It seems this was our mistake.”

“Eh, no, wait a minute. I’m just grateful you let me take this job, so please don’t apologize!”

Suchi said this in a flustered manner, and for the time being, the matter didn’t seem to become an issue.

“Well, assuming that issue is settled for now… so, what do we do? Can you bring the person you’re living with here without them being noticed?”

“Hmm… well, I’m not sure. I think it might be a little difficult.”

“Is that so? The fact that Suchi and the others are participating in the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle today isn’t exactly common knowledge in the slums, is it? If that’s the case, don’t you think it would be fine to bring your family or something over now?”

“I’m not sure. People in the slum gangs get information pretty fast. They’ve probably already heard that we…”

“In that case, we’ll handle it here.”

Hearing Suchi’s words, the guild official who had been listening to the conversation said this.

Perhaps not expecting such words to come from a Guild official’s mouth, Suchi—and her companions listening behind her—all turned to look at him in surprise.

“That’s very kind of you, but why would you go to such lengths?”

That was the genuine question on Suchi’s mind.

Suchi genuinely couldn’t understand why they were going to such lengths for her.

The guild official smiled at Suchi and spoke.

“Well, there are various reasons… but the biggest one is seeing how you worked today. You seemed a bit hesitant about the demolition at first, but you quickly got used to it and worked diligently. Plus, even those not participating in the demolition found tasks they could handle and got to work on them before anyone had to tell them to.”

To Suchi, the guild official’s expression seemed to show genuine admiration.

“To be honest, since you’re from the slums, I fully expected a bunch of hopeless people to show up, but all of Suchi’s companions are working diligently, and they haven’t done anything like stealing Giganto Turtle meat or materials.”

Hearing the guild official’s words, Suchi… and the other slum dwellers nodded in agreement.

In fact, if they had brought in random slum dwellers to do the butchering work, they probably wouldn’t have done the job properly and would have tried to steal the Giganto Turtle’s meat and materials.

The reason Suchi’s companions didn’t do such things was because they knew it would bring shame upon Suchi.

That was proof of just how much Suchi was loved by her companions.

This is exactly what they mean when they say that hard work pays off.

That must have made him happy. A broad smile spread across Suchi’s face.

“And if Suchi has other acquaintances, the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle will progress even further than it has now. Today’s progress was undoubtedly the greatest we’ve made in the past few days.”

The guild official’s words weren’t a lie, but it was also true that not all of it was thanks to Suchi and her team.

There was no doubt that Suchi and her group had been a powerful asset during the dismantling.

But more than that, the adventurers and others who had been working on dismantling the Giganto Turtle up until today had also grown accustomed to the process and were now able to dismantle it more efficiently… and in this case, that was a significant factor.

“That’s precisely why we decided to take action—because we judged that even if we gave Suchi and her group somewhat special treatment, they would still deliver results worthy of it. Besides, we’ve been instructed to accommodate Rei’s requests as much as possible regarding this dismantling.”

While Suchi and the others’ performance certainly played a role, the latter reason was likely the most significant factor for the Guild.

Suchi, who had been listening to this, seemed to understand. She stared intently at the guild official... and eventually spoke up.

“Thank you for treating us so generously. But when you say you’ll help us return to the slums, exactly what kind of assistance are you offering?”

“The Guild will hire some available adventurers to accompany you as escorts. We don’t have anyone particularly skilled, but dealing with opponents in the slums—especially those not affiliated with any organization—shouldn’t be a problem.”

If this underground organization were truly serious about this, they would have needed to send some of the most skilled adventurers.

But within the Gilm’s underground organization, Rei is considered a jinx… no, a veritable disaster.

Given that several underground organizations have already suffered catastrophic losses from meddling with Rei, the idea of them targeting Suchi and the others—who are assisting with the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle under Rei’s commission—was… well, I wouldn’t say it was absolutely impossible, but the likelihood was extremely low.

That’s likely why they decided that a reasonably skilled adventurer would suffice.

Furthermore, such adventurers are often short on money or owe the Guild a favor, making them easy to hire on short notice.

...Above all, the fact that they didn’t have to offer the high rewards typically given to high-rank adventurers—since these were low-rank adventurers—was a major factor for the guild.

However, upon hearing that they would be provided with an escort—even for low-rank adventurers—Suchi… no, the other slum dwellers as well, wore expressions of astonishment.

She didn’t know how many times she’d looked surprised just today, but there was no doubt that this was the biggest surprise of them all.

“Really? Is it okay for you to go to such lengths for us?”

“Yes. As I’ve mentioned several times already, you and your group have worked hard enough to deserve it. Given that, if you’ll continue to be as valuable a force tomorrow as you were today—no, even more so—then this level of assistance is a small price for the Guild to pay.”

The guild official said this, but there was no doubt that giving Suchi and the others preferential treatment was, in fact, a small price to pay.

To begin with, the reward for dismantling the Giganto Turtle is split equally between the Guild and… Daskar.

The problem is that requests to hunt Kobolds will likely decrease due to the Earth Wall, and as a result, the dismantling rewards—which had been lowered—will undoubtedly rise.

Since it was certain that many of the kobolds who could no longer enter Gilm due to the earthen wall would be redirected here, this outcome was only natural.

However, the slum dwellers like Suchi can be hired to participate in the dismantling for a lower fee than others.

From the guild’s perspective, Suchi and the others—who worked diligently for low pay—were a tremendous asset.

They did not hesitate to spend a little extra to hire such people.

Looking at the final expenses, it was also a major factor that hiring more slum dwellers was expected to result in lower overall costs.

And above all, there was no doubt that the slum dwellers—who possessed a certain level of demolition skill and worked diligently—were the kind of talent the guild wanted to secure.

That is, provided they didn’t try to run away once the work of dismantling the Giganto Turtle was finished come spring. We even felt they were worthy of being hired as guild staff—though not to work at the counter, but rather in the warehouse.

Above all, Gilm faces another major task in the fall: the dismantling of the Gamelion.

If we can hire these slum dwellers for that, it would undoubtedly be a huge help to the Guild.

After all, there are plenty of people who, even if they manage to defeat a Gamelion, simply load it onto a cart or similar vehicle and return to Gilm without dismantling it, leaving the dismantling to the Guild…

Naturally, if they pull a stunt like that, the dismantling fee will be deducted from the purchase price of the Gamelion, but if they have the skill to hunt Gamelions with ease, it would be a valid strategy to leave the dismantling to the Guild and focus on hunting as many Gamelions as possible themselves.

And it is a fact that Gilm, being home to a gathering of skilled adventurers, has a fair number of people who, if they push themselves a little, are capable of pulling off such a stunt.

The guild clerk was thinking along these lines, but naturally, he didn’t let it show on his face… and to drive the point home, he spoke up.

“Also, this isn’t set in stone yet—it’s based on what I saw of your work today—but… while it might be difficult to include everyone, I might recommend you to the guild staff. In this case, I’m not referring to the guild staff in the general sense, but rather the support staff.”

A murmur rippled through the crowd.

Suchi and many of the people from the slums standing behind her murmured in response to the guild official’s words.

It was only natural. After all, even if it meant working as a low-level staff member, being hired by the Guild would mean they could finally escape the slums.

There are some in the slums who choose to live there of their own free will.

But the vast majority are there because they have no other choice.

That’s exactly why so many would welcome the chance to leave the slums—not to become adventurers or prostitutes, but to take on ordinary jobs.

“So, everyone, please do your best with the Giganto Turtle dissection until spring. Depending on how things progress, there’s a possibility we’ll be able to hire more people at the Guild.”

Those words from the guild official were undoubtedly a very effective final push.

(Well, if the population of the slums decreases, that’s probably good for Gilm too… Lord Daskar will be happy with the increased tax revenue, and the Guild will have more capable workers—so in the end, is there really anyone who stands to lose?)

To be precise, there were people who would be displeased simply because they were from the slums, or those who had previously worked as laborers might find fewer jobs available… and so on—there were certainly those who would suffer disadvantages. But Rei hadn’t thought that far ahead at the moment.

“Hmm… quite the shrewd one.”

Vihera, who had been watching the exchange from beside Rei, muttered, seemingly impressed by the guild official’s shrewdness.

...Meanwhile, Byune was too busy extracting the proof of the Kobold’s defeat and its magic stones to pay any attention to the conversation.
