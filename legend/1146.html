The day after failing to learn a skill from Sand Lizardman's magic stone... Rei was once again in Gosh.

After failing to acquire the skill, he spent the night with Set, feeling a little sulky, and upon seeing Zarust and Narsus wake up in the morning, he returned to Gosh like this.

For breakfast, he had simply placed some dishes from Rei's Misty Ring.

Of course, there was food in the Hundred-Faced Ones' village, but it was only natural that he didn't want to eat food left in the assassin group's village.

It would be one thing if there were absolutely no food available, but Rei had plenty stored in his Misty Ring.

Furthermore, there were rumors that assassins routinely ingested trace amounts of poison to build resistance, so it was understandable that they'd prefer to avoid it for that reason too.

As a result, Rei just handed over a few sandwiches...

(I never imagined they'd be that happy about it.)

Walking through the outskirts of Gosh, toward the direction of Augusto's mansion, he recalled how delighted Zarust and Narsus had been when they saw the sandwiches.

Even though they were sandwiches, they were quite different from what was eaten in Gosh.

Compared to the sandwiches eaten in Gosh, everything was different, from the quality of the bread to the ingredients inside.

Furthermore, sandwiches in Gosh tended to use a lot of spices.

For Zarust and Narsus, accustomed to such sandwiches, the ones sold in Gilm must have felt like an entirely different dish.

The sandwiches sold normally in Gilm... though "normally" meant Rei bought them in bulk and stored them in the Misty Ring, so naturally they were several levels above what other shops offered.

In any case, Rei was genuinely surprised that such an ordinary sandwich could bring them so much joy.

To Rei, the dishes sold at Gosh were also quite unique, and he found them all delicious.

Of course, Zarust and Narsus were probably just delighted because they were unusual sandwiches.

After all, most people prefer familiar dishes for regular meals, even if they might occasionally enjoy something different.

As she walked with Set, pondering such things, they soon arrived at their destination mansion.

Compared to when they had come the previous night, the number of guards had decreased.

That was likely not unrelated to Rei and the others destroying the village of the Hundred-Faced Ones.

Still, they'd left a certain number of guards behind, just in case there were other Hundred-Faced ones within Gosh... or so it seemed.

"Oh, Rei. And Set too. You're quite early."

Gunter, the gatekeeper at Augusto's mansion, gave a light greeting upon seeing Rei and Set.

It was still early morning... not long after the six o'clock bell had rung, so it was no wonder Günter spoke up like that.

But to Günter, Rei shrugged his shoulders slightly before speaking.

"Is that so? The residents of Gosh are early risers, aren't they? I think this is perfectly normal."

Rei's words were undeniably true.

The desert where Gosh exists is hot during the day and cold at night.

In other words, it's more efficient to act during the cool morning hours than to be active during the day.

Therefore, it was only natural that activity became more intense in the cooler morning hours when temperatures were still mild.

Though time was irrelevant to Rei, who wore the Dragon Robe and was unaffected by heat or cold.

After exchanging those brief words, Rei passed through the gate with Set.

Without needing to be told, Set headed for the stables, while Rei entered the mansion.

Guards were present within the mansion too, but compared to when he had arrived last night, their numbers seemed reduced.

After speaking with the guards, Rei headed for the dining hall.

At first, he thought it best to wait until the meal was over, but as he spoke in front of the dining hall, Augusto appeared and pulled Rei inside.

There, Augusto, Kathy, Daldora, and Daldora's guards were all having breakfast together.

"Oh? You're quite early."

The first to notice Rei's presence was, unsurprisingly, not the guards... but Daldora.

She glanced at Rei and showed a hint of surprise.

Her usually nervous expression didn't seem quite so harsh now—perhaps thanks to Cathy's cooking.

Still, Daldora was far from a welcome presence to Cathy.

They were currently forced to work together regarding the matter of the Hundred-Faced One, but Cathy had never forgotten that Daldora had used sand pirates to target Augusto.

Daldora must understand this too. He had been staying at this mansion, mindful of Augusto and Cathy.

"Ah, with Set, it's only a few minutes from the village to Gosh. Actually, walking here from Gosh takes longer."

"How enviable."

As they chatted like this, Rei was naturally pulled over by Kathy and seated in a chair.

"Come on, since you're here, you should eat. You must have been exhausted yesterday, right?"

Breakfast was prepared for him, almost forcefully, and Rei couldn't refuse it.

Then again, Rei had only had a light sandwich with Zarust and Narsus for breakfast, so if he wanted to, he could easily eat this much.

"So, Rei. As I mentioned yesterday, after this..."

"I understand. I'm ready whenever you are. How about your preparations?"

"Yes, no problem. It's been arranged as a joint operation with Mr. Augusto."

Daldora set down her stew spoon and turned her gaze toward Augusto.

With just that, Augusto seemed to understand what Daldora meant. He smiled and spoke.

"I'm deeply involved in this matter, after all. And then there's the matter of the Tira tree. It's only natural we send people to that village."

"...I see."

Come to think of it, while the matter of the Hundred-Faced One had been swept under the rug, there were still unresolved issues like the Tira Tree and the walls of Gosh Castle... Rei pondered.

But since those matters were already being handled in Gosh, he decided it wasn't his place to speak up about them.

"Well, I understand. Then, once preparations are complete, we'll head out immediately. Fortunately..."

Rei glanced briefly toward Daldora, then continued with a slightly mischievous smile.

"We have the Sand Ship. It's faster than traveling by carriage, and it can handle a decent number of people, right?"

At the mention of the sand ship, Daldora's face twisted in a brief frown.

Originally hers, it had been taken by the Sand Pirates—nominally, at least—and Rei had reclaimed it. Ownership had already shifted from Daldora to Rei.

Given that, there was nothing he could say... Moreover, speaking up now would ruin the improved relationship with Rei he'd managed to build through Augusto.

Therefore, there was nothing he could say here.

To break the slightly awkward silence, Daldora spoke up.

"I'd like to visit the village of the Hundred-Faced One myself, but I need to finalize arrangements with Ryubrand regarding that matter. So, unfortunately, it will be a little while longer before I get to see that village."

Daldora's words, delivered with a hint of jest, lightened the slightly soured atmosphere.

After finishing their meal, the group began making their respective preparations.

Augusto and Daldora each had their subordinates prepare for the journey to the Village of the Hundred-Faced Ones. Kathy prepared lunch boxes, while Rei and Set wandered aimlessly around town before heading to the main gate.

Despite having just eaten breakfast, Rei bought food from stalls and shared it with Set, purchasing more of the delicious items to store in his Misty Ring.

Until recently, many vendors had been reluctant to sell to them because of their hostility towards Daldora. But now, with Rei... or more accurately, with Augusto, recognized as his employer, acting alongside them, that reluctance had vanished.

(It's only been since yesterday that they started cooperating, yet the information spread remarkably fast. Well, I suppose this is only natural for merchants.)

Munching on skewered sand sheep, Rei marveled at the merchants' extensive and swift information network.

"Give me another thirty of these skewers."

"...Huh?"

"It's fine, no problem."

"H-huh. Well, if you're paying for it, I won't complain."

After paying the shopkeeper the full price for thirty skewers, Rei stored the received skewers into the Misty Ring.

Leaving the shopkeeper frozen in shock, Rei finished storing them and walked away.

The skewers had a slightly noticeable meatiness, but they were skillfully seasoned to minimize it.

The meat itself might not be preferred by those who like tender meat, but Rei didn't dislike meat with a bit of chew.

(If I had a bit more skill in cooking, maybe I could use spices not just to mask the meat's gaminess, but to transform it into something like a meat flavor...)

She brought the last piece of meat from the skewer she held to her mouth.

Set seemed to like the sand sheep meat too, rubbing his head against Rei as if asking for more.

"Alright, alright. Just one more, okay?"

He retrieved a skewer from the Misty Ring he'd just stored it in and handed it to Set.

Deftly manipulating his beak, he stuffed the entire skewer into his mouth in one go. Set happily purred, savoring the taste of the meat.

Just as Rei had eaten breakfast at Augusto's mansion, Set naturally had his meal brought to the stables.

Even so, it was no match for Set's appetite—after breakfast, mind you—and so he enjoyed these little treats.

Such pleasant moments came to an end when the food stalls and shops around them disappeared.

As they approached the main gate, they saw merchants who had finished their business in Gosh and were heading to other villages and towns, as well as adventurers heading out to seek work.

Upon arriving near the main gate, a certain group was gathered there.

Rei didn't need to think twice about what kind of group it was.

After all, he had returned to Gosh specifically to lead this group to the village of the Hundred-Faced Ones.

"Rei-san, over here!"

One of Augusto's subordinates shouted this the moment he spotted Rei.

He was the man from the caravan Rei had rescued when they were attacked by Sand Serpents, so in that sense, he was one of the longer acquaintances Rei had made since arriving in Gosh.

...Though, since it hadn't even been a month since Rei arrived in Gosh, it wasn't much different from the others.

Ray approached him alongside Set.

"Is everyone here already?"

"Yes. We can depart immediately. The carriage is ready too."

The man who answered pointed toward the sight of a carriage being pulled by camels.

Five of them.

"...That's quite a lot."

Seeing the many carriages, Rei murmured. A woman in her twenties, standing beside the man who had first spoken, opened her mouth.

"It can't be helped. We're heading to a place where we don't know what lies ahead. We need to have a certain margin, including food supplies. ...Oh, I'm sorry. My apologies for the late introduction. I work under Lord Daldora. My name is Nena."

Nena bowed her head with a smile.

Her jet-black, lustrous hair shimmered in the sunlight.

Any ordinary man would likely be captivated by Nena's beauty.

She was a woman Daldora had arranged to try and raise Rei's favorability even slightly.

But... unfortunately for him, Rei knew three exceptionally beautiful women: Elena, Vihera, and Marina.

Compared to them, Nena was beautiful, but not to the point of being breathtaking.

Her feminine pride must have been hurt by Rei's attitude. She revealed a slightly dissatisfied expression.

Even so, that expression vanished quickly, and the group stepped outside Gosh.

The group of nearly thirty people, who must have completed their procedures while Rei was arriving, quickly exited.

Rei and Set swiftly completed their own procedures and headed outside Gosh.

They headed to the gathering place for those going to the village of the Hundred-Faced Ones... and, drawing everyone's attention, the Misty Ring pulled the sand ship out.
