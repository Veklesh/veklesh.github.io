"I'm opening it."

Rei calls out to Elena and Ara behind him and reaches for the door.

“Grrrrr!”

From above, Set let out a cry as if cheering Rei on.

……That said, only those who had known Set for some time—like Rei, Elena, and Ara—could interpret that as a cheer; to everyone else, it just sounded like a griffin letting out a threatening growl.

In any case, Rei reached for the door while listening to Set’s cry.

Judging by the expressions of the soldiers and knights of the Seisor Marquis family, it was easy to imagine that countless traps lay beyond this door as well.

(It would be so much easier if I could just use magic to deal with it, like I did with the ground.)

Even as he thought that, he knew this house must contain a vast amount of documents that the Duke of Kerebel’s family absolutely needed to obtain. Furthermore, having heard that this was the home of the leader of the most powerful organization in Anesis, Rei also harbored hopes of finding some kind of magical item.

That said, ultimately, his primary motivation was to obtain information regarding the Black Wolf.

“Well, with that in mind…”

Having said that, Rei opened the door.

Instantly, sensing something slicing through the air and flying toward him, he immediately swung his Death Scythe.

“There were arrows flying outside the house too, weren’t there? I think using the same tactic twice is a bit much.”

“...Given the situation, the fact that you’re swinging the Death Scythe like that makes me think something’s off...”

It was Ara who muttered this, looking dumbfounded.

With the door open—meaning he was standing right next to it—Rei swung the Death Scythe, which has a handle over two meters long, and sliced through the arrow that was flying toward him.

Just how much skill does it take to do something like that… well, that goes without saying.

But Rei, who had received Ara’s words of admiration, didn’t seem particularly bothered by them as he stepped inside the house.

At that moment, a thick, pungent smell of blood wafted through the air.

“What’s this…”

Rei’s gaze was fixed on the floor just inside the doorway.

Spread out before him was blood, blood, and more blood.

It was likely the blood spilled by those who—fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately—had managed to break through the traps set outside this house.

But... Rei felt something off about the lingering scent of blood.

He has no concrete proof. None at all...

“The smell of this blood… isn’t it drifting in from somewhere other than this hallway?”

Before Rei could voice his unease, Elena murmured those words.

Hearing those words, Rei realized what was causing his unease.

“That’s right. This smell of blood is drifting in from somewhere else, too. And it doesn’t seem like fresh blood that’s just been spilled…”

“Huh? Is that so?”

Hearing Elena and Rei’s words, Ara replied with a puzzled look.

As far as Ara was concerned, she had no way of knowing whether the smell of blood wafting through the air was coming from somewhere other than the blood right in front of her, nor could she tell if the blood was fresh or not.

If the smell of blood were mixed with a putrid odor, she might be able to tell it was old blood… but at least, Ara couldn’t detect any such smell.

However, Ara didn’t doubt Elena’s words.

It wasn’t that she blindly trusted Elena, but she simply couldn’t imagine Elena lying in a situation like this.

Besides, since not only Elena but even Rei was saying the same thing, she couldn’t bring herself to deny it.

“Ah… The problem is, where exactly is this smell of blood coming from? Since it’s not fresh blood… I have nothing but a bad feeling about this.”

“Hmm. That’s an exceptionally bad feeling.”

The fact that these two, with their keen senses, were saying this made Ara feel certain that there was undoubtedly something utterly terrifying in this house.

“Then we’ll need to proceed with even greater caution than before, ready to respond immediately to whatever happens. I suppose the one silver lining is that this house isn’t all that big.”

At Ara’s words, Rei and Elena nodded in unison.

While the house was reasonably spacious, its size still fell within the range of what could be considered an ordinary home.

It was clearly smaller than the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, and even compared to other noble mansions in Anesis.

For that very reason, they didn’t think it would take that long to search the house… or rather, to investigate the source of whatever sense of unease Rei and Elena had sensed.

If this had been a mansion as vast as the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, exploring the interior would have been quite a struggle.

In that sense, finding a place like this wasn’t a bad thing at all.

“Well, there’ll probably be traps of some sort, so we’ll need to be careful about that,”

Muttering to himself, and realizing that lingering near the entrance might trigger another trap, Rei turned his gaze toward Elena and Ara and moved further inside.

In an instant, Rei pulled the Death Scythe toward him, bringing the hilt in front of him.

Elena and Ara, who were behind him, heard a faint metallic sound.

「Rei？」

In response to Elena’s question, Rei shook his head to indicate everything was fine.

As he did so, Rei looked down at the needle lying on the floor.

Rei leads a Rank B party called the Crimson Wings. One of his comrades, Byune, uses a weapon called a long needle, but what Rei blocked with the hilt of the Death Scythe wasn’t a long needle or anything of the sort—it was just a perfectly ordinary needle.

It was the kind of needle that wouldn’t look out of place in a sewing kit.

If this were a dungeon, or a noble’s mansion as spacious as the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, he could have easily dodged it without going to the trouble of blocking it.

But this was, after all, just an ordinary house, and the hallway didn’t have that much room to spare.

And above all, Elena and Ara were currently standing right behind Rei.

Setting Ara aside, if it were Elena right behind Rei, she probably wouldn’t have had much trouble dealing with a needle flying at her on the spot… but even so, considering the possibility of an accident, it was still the best option for Rei to get rid of the needle here.

(Ah, but since it wasn’t aimed at my face or anything, maybe using the Dragon Robe would have been fine?)

Rei’s Dragon Robe is, as the name suggests, an item crafted from dragon materials.

Moreover, it wasn’t crafted by just any alchemist or artisan, but by Esta Nord, who is often hailed as the greatest alchemist of all time.

Lately, he’s been making full use of its simple air-conditioning-like functions that allow him to stay comfortable even in winter or summer, but purely as armor, it’s top-tier equipment—superlative, even.

So much so that Rei can state with absolute certainty that there’s no way a single strike—even one as small as a needle—could pierce the robe.

But that’s something only Rei, who wears the Dragon Robe, can say.

“Watch out—it’s not just arrows; a needle just flew by. I think it’s coated with some kind of substance… probably poison.”

It was Ara, rather than Elena, who grimaced at Rei’s warning.

Elena is equipped with excellent gear, but Ara’s equipment is a notch or two below that.

Even so, as the captain of Elena’s escort knights, her gear is top-notch… but even so, it’s difficult to watch out for needles flying in from who knows where.

“Are you all right?”

“Yes. I’ll handle an attack like this.”

Ara replied immediately to Elena’s words.

Elena noticed that there was a hint of bravado mixed in with those words.

However, considering Ara’s abilities, Elena believed that if it actually came down to it, she would certainly be able to handle the situation.

Perhaps because she spends so much time with Elena, Ara often finds herself unconsciously using Elena as her standard.

Naturally, this made her feel as though she was inferior.

...Though, to begin with, comparing her abilities to Elena’s is a mistake.

That said, not everything about Ara is inferior to Elena’s.

For example, when it comes to brewing tea, there’s no doubt that Ara is overwhelmingly better than Elena.

“Alright, let’s go.”

Rei announced this, then led Elena and Ara down the hallway.

Of course, simply walking down the hallway triggers traps, but instead of disarming them, Rei forces his way through.

Fortunately, since the house isn’t large enough to be called a mansion, they’re able to find a door almost immediately just by walking down the hallway.

“What do you think? There are a few doors here, but you can be sure every single one has a trap.”

If the traps were set on the floor, Rei could somehow sense them. …Though, of course, that was limited to simple ones.

However, there was no way Rei could possibly see through the traps set on the doors.

In other words, to open a door, you have to assume you’ll trigger a trap.

...That role falls to Rei, who is wearing a Dragon Robe with high defense, but that doesn’t mean he wants to willingly fall into traps over and over again.

After all, even if the Dragon Robe were inside the trap, it might not be able to protect him… It wouldn’t be surprising if some kind of liquid or gas were to be sprayed out.

“In a situation like this, I think the one at the very end is the most suspicious…”

Saying this, Elena looks at the door at the very end of the hallway.

Rei followed her gaze and nodded in agreement with Elena’s words.

In fact, in the manga and novels Rei had read back in Japan, most bosses were often depicted as being at the very end.

He couldn’t exactly equate this world with the manga and novels he knew, but still, he had a vague feeling that Elena’s point was correct.

So, since Rei agreed with Elena’s opinion, and Ara didn’t have any particular thoughts on the matter, she had no intention of objecting to what Elena said.

“Then it’s that door. Let’s go.”

Rei muttered, and began walking down the hallway again...

“Huh? This… there’s no trap?”

Just as he started walking toward the door, he suddenly realized the traps had completely disappeared, and a strange sound escaped Rei’s mouth.

Up until now, the traps would have been triggered—and attacked Rei—within just a few steps… or even, depending on the situation, after taking just a single step. But for some reason, the traps had suddenly stopped triggering.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

Perhaps puzzled by Rei’s behavior, Elena asked him that.

“It looks like there aren’t any traps beyond this point. …I don’t know if there weren’t any set up here to begin with, or if the traps were deactivated because we decided to go through that door.”

Under normal circumstances, one might wonder if such a clever trick was even possible. It wouldn’t be strange to think that.

But the moment a trap was triggered or deactivated, it would instantly transform into a different trap and reappear right there.

Even Rei doesn’t know how such a thing is possible.

He could guess it’s probably the power of a magic item or something, but conversely, that also means that’s the only thing he can guess.

Because of that, no matter how absurd the events in this house may be, he can only accept them as they are.

“Are there no traps? …If that’s the case, I’d like to say there’s no need to worry… but I suppose that’s not quite right.”

Could it be that there are no traps because there’s no need to set them?

Rei and the others harbored this doubt, but that didn’t mean they could just stay here.

Their mission was to find the owner of this mansion, obtain documents related to the Black Wolf incident, and also to exact retribution for the insult of laying a hand on Rei—a guest of the Duke of Kerebel—and thus ruining their reputation.

“You’re right. …Well then, let’s go. Be sure not to let your guard down.”

It was a needless reminder, but he said it just to be safe. Rei walked down the hallway… and eventually reached the door at the end.

As expected, no traps were triggered as he walked down the hallway.

Upon reaching the door, Rei turned his gaze toward Elena and Ara, and after seeing them nod, he reached out to open it.

Rei opened the door while keeping his guard up, ready to deal with any traps immediately, but—as expected, or perhaps inevitably—no traps were triggered here either, and the door swung open.

What they saw inside was... a magnificent room, filled with several pieces of furniture that looked incredibly expensive, yet it was completely empty—no one was there.
