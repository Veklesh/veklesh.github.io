"We've confirmed it. It seems the Ajax group will depart Gilm immediately after completing the transaction tonight."

"...I figured."

Dahl's words as he entered the room weren't particularly surprising to Rei.

After all, aside from Ilse, Rei had also caught their attention.

There was no way he'd stay in Gilm under those circumstances.

(Well, things might have been different if they had some powerful backing. Unfortunately for them... and fortunately for us, they don't have that kind of backing.)

Feeling relieved, Rei stood up from the chair he had been sitting in.

Following suit, the others stood up as well.

Even Byune, who had been asleep until just moments ago, had already been roused by Vihera and was fully prepared.

Led by Dahl, they exited the tavern.

Though, they didn't exit through the front door, but the back.

(Then, couldn't they have just come in through the back door too?)

Rei briefly wondered about that, but shrugged it off, figuring it must be for some reason.

As Rei and the others stepped outside the tavern, Set approached them as if he had been waiting.

If that were all, it would have been business as usual, and Rei wouldn't have given it a second thought.

But seeing two familiar faces and one familiar creature beside him changed everything.

"...Hey, what are you doing here?"

The person addressed—Elena—smiled as she spoke, brushing back her golden hair that sparkled in the moonlight.

"What? Is Rei in danger? If so, I don't see anything wrong with me helping out, do you?"

"No, I want to know why Elena and the others, who were at Marina's house, are here in the first place. And besides, I'm not in any danger."

Even as he said this, Rei somehow understood who had orchestrated this whole affair.

"Marina, is there something you want to tell me?"

Yes, the only one capable of pulling this off, in terms of versatility surpassing even Rei, was Marina, a master of spirit magic.

Moreover, the fact that Elena and the others were currently living in Marina's house only reinforced Rei's suspicion.

"Oh? What is it? Did I do something? Besides, aren't you going to capture Ajax now? Setting Ajax and his crew aside, the organization they're dealing with must have a lot of people, right? No matter how good Rei is at handling large numbers, he can't exactly use powerful magic in Gilm, can he?"

"Guh..."

Marina's words left Rei speechless.

In truth, to ensure none of Ajax, his associates, or the members of the organization responsible for abducting numerous women escaped during the upcoming transaction, the more combat power, the better.

And if that force consisted solely of Elena and Ara, they would undoubtedly be top-tier combatants.

Rei understood this perfectly well, which was precisely why he couldn't respond to Marina's words.

"Heh heh, you needn't worry about me so much. The reason I'm holed up in Marina's house is because too many people come visiting for no good reason. So, if I go out at this hour, how many people would even bother trying to get involved with me... Hmm, I wonder how many there would be?"

"Now that you mention it, you're right."

Indeed, the likelihood of someone seeking a meeting with Elena at midnight was extremely low.

Therefore, it was also true that Elena being out at this hour posed no problem.

Still, that wasn't an absolute guarantee. It wasn't entirely impossible that some member of the Noble Faction might be out walking the streets of Gilm at night for some reason.

(Like brothels or something)

That's what Rei thought, but he possessed enough composure not to voice it.

"Understood. If we can secure Elena, Ara, and Yellow's cooperation, it would be a great help to us. ...Dahl, you'll guide us."

"Yes."

Though he nodded at Rei's words, Dahl couldn't help but wonder: Is this really okay?

Being part of the intelligence unit, he naturally knew what kind of person Elena was.

And since their superior, Daskar, was a central figure in the Neutral Faction, he understood that if anything happened to Elena, it would cause a major problem.

Still, it was also true that whatever Dahl said here would be futile.

After all, Dahl didn't hold any particular position of authority.

Given that, it was difficult for him to voice an opinion in the current situation.

He also felt that if Rei was present, things would probably work out somehow, even if something did happen.

Almost as if talking himself into it, Dahl led the others present—though "guided" might be a more accurate description—as they made their way down the path.

They encountered several guards along the way, but upon seeing Rei and Set, the guards merely waved a light greeting without saying anything further.

Of course, some of the guards probably recognized Elena.

But seeing her with Rei, they said nothing more.

This was likely because the circumstances of Elena's arrival were well understood around here.

Dahl was relieved not to have to explain everything to the guards each time, so he continued on without saying anything in particular.

They kept walking like this. Soon, the main street narrowed, and the buildings around them grew older and grungier.

"We're getting close to the slums,"

Rei, who had been to the slums many times without really meaning to, muttered this as he surveyed the surroundings.

At those words, everyone's awareness tightened involuntarily...

"Calm down."

It was Rei who said it.

However, his words weren't directed at Elena or the others who'd seen battle many times, but at the one person present with almost no combat experience.

"Huh!? ...Sorry, I'm fine."

Ilse, addressed by Rei, gasped for a moment but quickly replied.

But everyone present—even Ilse herself—understood that she wasn't truly fine. She was only saying it to sound calm.

What was about to happen was undoubtedly combat. Normally, bringing someone like Ilse, with no combat experience, would be a mistake.

In fact, while waiting at the tavern, Rei had even asked Ilse if she shouldn't stay behind.

But Ilse had shaken her head firmly at those words.

She insisted she was absolutely going with them. She might not be able to avenge her family herself, but she still wanted to see the outcome of this with her own eyes until the very end.

Faced with Ilse's declaration, Rei said nothing more.

"Alright, since Ilse seems a bit less tense now, let's get going. Dahl, you take the lead."

"Yes. We've already positioned our people around the area where Ajax and the organization are scheduled to make the deal. We just need to move in at the decisive moment and capture Ajax and the others. ...But..."

Dahl paused there, shifting his gaze to Ilse.

"As for Ajax, Ilse's enemy, that's one thing. But please capture the two individuals acting alongside Ajax alive, if at all possible. This incident... it's simply impossible that it was committed by individuals alone. If that's the case, it's safe to assume someone is behind Ajax and the others."

"Oh? So Ajax is fine?"

Marina asked in response to Dahl's words.

Though it wasn't really a question born of genuine doubt, but rather one meant to confirm something.

"Yes, it's fine. ...Or rather, we have no choice, do we?"

Of course, from Darl's perspective, securing Ajax's custody was absolutely essential.

Ajax was seen as the leader among the three men, and if that was the case, he might hold more critical information than the other two.

But that was merely a possibility, not a certainty.

If it were certain, things might be slightly different... but it wasn't certain.

Therefore, for an intelligence unit already short-handed, Egg judged it better to secure the cooperation of these top-tier operatives—some of whom hadn't quite reached that level yet—in exchange for Ajax's custody: Rei, Marina, Vihera, Byune, Set... and, completely unexpectedly, Elena, Ara, and Yellow.

These were top-tier operatives—though some hadn't quite reached that level yet—and Egg judged it better to secure their cooperation.

Dahl was somewhat perplexed by this... but he concluded it was unavoidable given the intelligence unit's manpower shortage, leading to the current situation.

"Still... before Ilse takes her revenge, I'd prefer to hear her side of the story first. After that, we won't say another word."

Hearing this, Rei turned his gaze to Ilse, asking what she would do.

For Rei, there was logic in Dahl's words.

Besides, since Dahl had helped them so much with this whole affair, he thought it was only fair to give him a chance to extract information from Ajax.

But Ilse was the main protagonist in this situation.

If Ilse said no, it would be difficult to try prying information out of Ajax. That's what he thought.

Yet, contrary to Rei's expectations, Ilse nodded in agreement with Dahl's words.

"I want to know what Ajax was planning too. If we can stop a scheme Ajax was involved in, it would be close to the worst possible outcome for him."

"Ah, ahahaha. Well, hearing you say that is a relief."

Ilse's words made Dahl sweat a little as he laughed.

He understood that her hatred for Ajax drove her to want to destroy even the schemes Ajax himself was involved in.

That said, Dahl had no objection to this.

Realistically, considering the damage this incident would cause to Gilm, he couldn't let Ajax have his way.

...It was absolutely impossible, but if Ajax had actually reformed and was planning something beneficial for Gilm, Dahl might have stopped Ilse from crushing Ajax's scheme.

But what Ajax was currently doing was something like abducting a large number of women from Gilm.

From the perspective of Gilm as a whole, it was a truly small number of women, hardly even a problem.

Even so, rumors couldn't be stopped. If things went badly, rumors could spread that every woman who went to Gilm disappeared.

To prevent such rumors from spreading, this matter absolutely had to be resolved within Gilm.

Of course, the fact that they were taken away probably means the women have mixed feelings about Gilm... Still, if it's settled internally, even if strange rumors spread, they can be denied.

"Well then, since we have Ilse's consent, shall we proceed? According to information we obtained here, it seems Ajax and the others will leave Gilm immediately after completing the transaction. ...I'd very much like to hear about the methods used around here as well."

Everyone present nodded at Darl's words. ...Though a few seemed to nod simply because others were nodding.

As the group entered the slums, a figure approached them.

The moment Ilse saw that figure, she stiffened, wondering, Could it be an enemy? But... Darl, seeing the figure, showed no particular caution. On the contrary, he smiled and addressed the person.

"Rudos, how did it go?"

"Yeah, no problem. We've got a solid lock on where Ajax and the others are. ...But they seem pretty sharp over there. There's a chance they might sense we're trying to surround them."

"...Troublesome."

Even as he said this, Dahl had half-suspected as much.

After all, they were the kind of people who'd been operating as adventurers while engaging in semi-bandit-like activities.

And it wasn't just once or twice—hearing the details from Ilse would make that abundantly clear.

Given that kind of life, it wasn't surprising they were skilled at sensing unusual activity around them.

However... even possessing the ability to sense the surrounding situation is one thing; whether he could actually make use of it was quite another matter.

The biggest factor, after all, was the outstanding deal with the organization.

As long as they had to fulfill that deal, there wasn't much Ajax and the others could do, regardless of the current situation.

They might be able to eliminate those watching them, but while they could sense something was amiss, actually pinpointing everyone involved in that disturbance was bound to be difficult, no matter what.

Moreover, since this was a major deal for the organization too, they undoubtedly had people stationed around them as well.

"...Shall we go?"

At Darl's words, everyone nodded. Guided by the man called Rudos, the group made their way through the slum district.
