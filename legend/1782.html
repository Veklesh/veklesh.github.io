After Elena left, only Rei and a single maid remained in the room.

The maid had entered just as Elena left, so she didn’t know exactly what had transpired between Rei and Elena… but even so, she could guess what kind of relationship the two shared.

That said, even though she could guess, the maid didn’t dare to ask Rei about it.

As maids in the service of the Duke of Kerebel, many of them were the daughters of other nobles.

This maid assigned to Rei was the third daughter of a certain baron who belonged to the Noble Faction.

Fortunately, this maid did not let her noble status go to her head and simply worked as a maid.

If she had been the type to give high-handed orders to Rei, who was not of noble birth, she would undoubtedly have incurred Elena’s wrath.

That said, it was the butler of the Duke of Kerebel’s household—who knew Rei’s personality and his relationship with Elena—who had arranged for her.

The maid approached Rei, who was drinking tea to pass the time until dinner.

“Here you go.”

Without waiting for Rei to say a word, the maid brewed a fresh pot of tea.

“Hm? Oh, sorry. …By the way, can I ask you something?”

“What is it?”

The maid, who asks this in response to Rei’s words, has features that, while perhaps not quite on par with Elena’s, can certainly be described as beautiful.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange for someone to be captivated by such a maid, but for Rei—who spends his days with Elena, Marina, and Vihera, three women whose beauty, though of different natures, is legendary enough to have been recorded in history—she was nothing to be captivated by.

……The maid, who had a faint confidence in her own beauty, couldn’t help but feel a little dissatisfied with Rei’s attitude, but she didn’t let it show and simply asked what he wanted.

“Could you tell me what kind of person Elena’s mother, the Duchess of Kerebel, is?”

“Lady Arcadia? Well… I suppose she’s someone who combines kindness with a certain fearsome quality.”

“Kindness and fear… In other words, can I assume she has a personality typical of the nobility?”

Unable to think of any specific examples based on the words “kindness” and “fear,” Rei decided to accept that description for the time being.

Considering she’s Elena’s mother, I can’t really picture a personality that’s quintessentially aristocratic…

“That’s right. She’s certainly a proud woman. And, as Elena’s biological mother, she’s also very beautiful.”

“I suppose so.”

Since she was Elena’s mother, Rei understood she was beautiful without the maid even having to say so.

(Well, nobles have basically been marrying beautiful people—whether as brides or grooms—for generations. Given that, it’s only natural that there would be so many beautiful people among them, I suppose.)

Of course, it wouldn’t be strange for there to be people who love commoners rather than nobles, but they’re in the minority.

Given that their ancestors were all good-looking, it was an undeniable fact that their descendants would also tend to be good-looking.

In fact, most of the nobles Rei had met so far had features that were above average in their refinement.

……Though I can’t deny that there were a few who had become either too fat or too thin due to poor lifestyle habits.

“It seems Lord Arcadia was also looking forward to meeting you, Rei.”

“Me? Why on earth?”

Rei had considered the possibility that he might be disliked for leading Elena down the wrong path—at least from a noble’s perspective—but he never expected to be told that she was looking forward to meeting him.

For a moment, he wondered if she meant she was looking forward to seeing him in a negative sense, but judging by the maid’s demeanor, it was clear that wasn’t the case.

“That is…”

Just as the maid was about to respond to Rei’s words, there was a knock at the door.

The maid looked at Rei, and he nodded in response, indicating it was fine for her to answer.

When the maid opened the door, there stood an elderly man dressed in a butler’s uniform.

He was an elderly man who looked every inch the gentleman, but the maid froze for a moment at the sight of him.

It was only natural. Standing before her was the head butler, who oversaw all the maids and butlers serving the Duke of Kerebel.

The maid must have frozen because she never expected the head butler to appear.

Still, she quickly regained her composure and spoke up.

“Um, Head Butler. What can I do for you?”

“Dinner is ready, so I’ve come to fetch Rei.”

“……You’re coming personally, Head Butler?”

Although she had expected it, the maid couldn’t hide her surprise that the Head Butler would actually go so far as to do this himself.

But from the Head Butler’s perspective, Rei was someone who was highly likely to become deeply involved with the Duke of Kerebel’s household in the future.

Given that, it was absolutely essential for him to see Rei with his own eyes.

For the Head Butler, Elena was someone he had watched over since childhood… no, since the day she was born.

To put it bluntly, he even thinks of her as half his own daughter, or perhaps even his granddaughter.

For that very reason, if someone was considered to be the lover of such a person, the head butler simply had to verify that person’s character.

“Yes. Since it’s a guest with a reputation, we must ensure we treat them with the utmost respect.”

Seeing the Head Butler speak without revealing a single trace of his true feelings, the maid nodded in understanding.

“Understood. Please wait a moment.”

Saying that, the maid headed toward the sofa where Rei was sitting.

Watching the maid’s movements, the head butler looked at Rei.

Gently, so as not to be rude. But clearly enough to make out his face.

(Hmm.)

Upon seeing Rei’s face, the head butler let out a small internal gasp of admiration.

According to what he’d heard, Rei was someone who wore a hooded robe and usually kept the hood up.

But seeing him now, dressed in proper clothes with his face uncovered, his features are clearly visible.

His features weren’t exactly masculine; in fact, one might even describe them as feminine.

Yet there was no trace of frailty about him—perhaps because his eyes held a strong, even intense, gleam of determination.

The head butler was impressed not by Rei’s face, but by the light in his eyes.

At the very least, she’s passed the first stage.

Reaching that conclusion inwardly, the head butler elegantly bowed to Rei, who had risen at the maid’s words and was approaching him.

“It is a pleasure to meet you, Rei. I am Remnes, the head butler of the House of Kerebel.”

“……Isn’t it Sebastian?”

As Head Butler Remnes bowed his head, he felt as though Rei had said something to him, but when he looked up, Rei showed no sign of having said anything in particular.

Although Remnes was quite elderly, his hearing had not yet grown faint.

Rei had undoubtedly muttered something, but since Rei was acting as if nothing had happened, he couldn’t very well press the matter.

…That said, what Rei had muttered was that, despite being a butler who looked every bit the part, his name wasn’t Sebastian.

Thinking back to another butler he’d met before, he’d actually suspected that might be the case.

“Well then, dinner is ready, so please follow me.”

“Please do.”

With that, Remnes and Rei left the room and walked down the hallway.

As befitted the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, the hallway was adorned with paintings and vases that were likely works of art.

Rei, who didn’t possess any particular eye for art, had absolutely no idea whether the paintings, vases, and other various art objects were genuine or fake, or, if genuine, how much they were worth.

Remnes, judging from Rei’s attitude that he didn’t understand the value of the displayed items, silently lowered his opinion of Rei.

If the paintings and art objects had been magical items, Rei might have taken at least some interest in them.

As they walked down the hallway, Rei watched Remnes’s back while enduring the uncomfortable sensation of something tightening around his neck.

He was a bit older than middle-aged, but even so, there was no sign of unsteadiness in his gait.

In other words, it was proof of just how well-trained he was.

(Even as the head butler for Duke Kerebel, who leads the Noble Faction, he must be quite strong. ...Actually, could it be that butlers are required to have high combat prowess?)

The fact that Rei thought this way was, naturally, due to the influence of subculture—starting with the manga he’d read back in Japan.

It’s not uncommon for butlers and maids to possess high combat prowess.

Of course, that’s only because it makes for a more interesting story; in reality, here in Elgin, butlers and maids aren’t expected to have high combat prowess.

……In reality, while there are certainly some butlers and maids with such abilities for various reasons, they are truly only a tiny minority.

There was no conversation between Remnes and Rei; they continued walking in silence for over ten minutes.

The fact that they walked for over ten minutes within a single mansion demonstrated just how vast the Duke of Kerebel’s residence was.

Since they had been looking down from above while riding Set, they had a general idea of its size.

Still, walking through it myself made me realize just how vast it truly was.

“We’ve arrived.”

At Remnes’s words, Rei finally realized they had arrived at their destination—the venue where the banquet was to be held.

Spread out before him was a door larger than he had anticipated.

Rei could guess that the reason it was larger than a normal door was likely to accommodate the transport of massive dishes.

As Rei stared at the door, Remnes eventually opened it.

Instantly, Rei reflexively gasped at the appetizing aroma wafting out from inside the dining hall.

The savory aroma suggested that the food hadn’t simply been grilled or stir-fried, but prepared using some special cooking method.

“Excuse me. I’ve brought Rei.”

Listening to Remnes’s words, Rei entered the dining hall.

The first thing that caught his eye was a massive table.

Seated at that table was Elena, dressed in a blue party dress.

She was so beautiful that even Rei, who was accustomed to Elena’s beauty, couldn’t help but be captivated.

Elena, perhaps noticing Rei looking at her, gave a faint smile.

“Thank you for coming, Rei. …Or perhaps, in this case, I should call you Lord Crimson Rei?”

At the sound of that voice, Rei turned his gaze in the direction it came from.

There stood a figure who was clearly smaller in stature than Daskar.

But... in terms of the aura and presence they exuded, they undoubtedly seemed superior to Daskar.

Given that he was here, there was no need to wonder who he was.

“...Nice to meet you, Duke Kerebel. You can just call me Rei.”

For a moment—just a fleeting moment—Rei was undeniably intimidated by the man standing before him.

If this were a battlefield, Rei would never have allowed himself to be intimidated by Kerebel Duke.

But that was only true on the battlefield; here and now, he had no choice but to acknowledge that the man standing before him was a man of great stature.

Duke Kerebel, too, was speaking while intentionally intimidating Rei.

It was a tactic to gauge the extent of Rei’s character.

He had gathered information from various sources, but that was merely information.

To truly understand what kind of person Rei was, he needed to meet him in person and speak with him directly.

As the leader of the Noble Faction in the labyrinth that is aristocratic society, Duke Kerebel was confident in his ability to judge character.

That is precisely why he was surprised by Rei, who, though momentarily startled by the pressure he exerted, responded immediately.

How many people could react this quickly to the pressure I just exerted?

Even among the Noble Faction, there surely weren’t that many.

(Passed the test, for now, I suppose.)

Having made that judgment, Kerebel Duke eased the pressure he had been exerting.

Since he’d done all this while even wearing a friendly smile, one could say he was acting like a true noble.

“Hehe, you. Why don’t you step aside a bit? He’s having trouble, isn’t he?”

The one who spoke to Duke Kerebel was the only woman at the table besides Elena.

As for who she was, Rei didn’t even need to think about it to understand.

After all, he had heard all about that person from Elena and the others.

In other words, that person was none other than Arcadia Kerebel.

She was the Duchess of Kerebel—and Elena’s mother.
