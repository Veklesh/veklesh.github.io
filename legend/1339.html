The running Vihera had already overtaken Set and was now leading the group.

The group ran through the narrow, winding back alleys.

Though they nearly collided with several people, Rei, Vihera, and Marina each possessed first-rate agility.

They effortlessly dodged those they nearly collided with, while Set propelled himself off walls to avoid others as they made their way through the back alleys.

By any normal standards, their movement was unbelievable, yet it proved effective this time.

"Grrr!"

Set, running behind Vihera, growled sharply.

From Set's reaction, he knew the enemy was still fighting Byune.

Within seconds... less than ten seconds, he emerged into a relatively open space.

In that open space, the woman they had seen earlier in front of the Spier Trading Company branch stood facing Byune.

The woman... Keena held a rapier, while Byune wielded the White Cloud.

Both were watching for an opening, but naturally, they noticed when Rei and the others arrived.

"Damn it, you're fast!"

Realizing it was Rei and the others, Keena tried to reach into her bosom with her left hand... but that left hand was where Byune's long needle had pierced her earlier.

The long needle, a hindrance in battle, had already been discarded on the ground.

Byune also bore a slash across her cheek, but the wound posed no hindrance to combat.

However, Kena, whose left arm had been pierced by the long needle, felt the pain for an instant... just an instant, but it slowed her movements.

And for Vihera, who was leading the charge ahead of Rei and the others—not Byune—closing the distance in that split second was no difficult feat.

Before Kena's left hand, reaching into her bosom, could be withdrawn, that arm was seized by Vihera's hand, now mere inches away, and forcibly stopped.

Kena specialized in assassination, relying on speed and stealth.

Facing Vihera head-on, the inherent difference in raw power meant victory was impossible.

"Be a good girl. I need to hear a few things from you. ...And I'd like to repay you for the wound on Byune's cheek, too."

"Hmph... Hmph... If you call that a wound, then my left arm is quite wounded too, you know?"

"Oh? Is that so? But for someone who opposed us... the Crimson Wings, your losses seem... surprisingly light, don't they!?"

He forcibly lifted her restrained left arm.

Kena, though a woman, had trained her body thoroughly as an assassin.

Her weight, due to her muscle mass, was heavier than that of an average woman.

Despite that, Kena's body was easily lifted with one hand... and the next moment, slammed onto the ground, back first.

(A one-armed shoulder throw... though that strike was a bit too rough for that technique.)

Watching the sequence of events, Rei couldn't help but think that.

Despite facing an assassin, he showed no concern for Vihera. He understood her true strength.

He knew Vihera wouldn't be defeated by an opponent of this caliber.

"Vihera, I want to hear what you have to say, so keep yourself in a condition where you can talk."

Marina, too, called out to Vihera without any sign of worry.

Having witnessed Vihera's combat training sessions numerous times over the winter, Marina knew exactly how strong he was.

Considering they hadn't been able to defeat Byune in the time it took them to arrive here, there was no way he could be a match for Vihera.

"I know."

Vihera said curtly, watching Keena pinned face-down on the ground.

Even that last blow had likely been somewhat restrained.

That was precisely why Kena was still alive, despite the pain and shock of being slammed onto her back, unable to breathe. And without a single bone broken.

She was a valuable source of information leading to Precious.

She couldn't let him escape or kill him here.

"Rei, give me the rope."

Following Vihera's request, Rei retrieved a rope from the Misty Ring.

Taking it, Vihera began tying Kena's hands and feet with the rope, though he was still unable to move from the pain and shock.

Though Kena was still conscious, her current state—her back slammed hard against the ground—made resistance impossible.

And so, ultimately unable to do anything, Kena was swiftly bound hand and foot with rope, rendered immobile.

If it had been tied by an amateur, Kena, a member of the underworld as an assassin, could have easily freed herself.

But Vihera had tied the knots in a way that made escape impossible, leaving Kena completely helpless.

Well, perhaps with more skilled technique he could have escaped, but unfortunately, it was impossible with Kena's abilities.

Ignoring Kena's predicament, Marina picked up the rapier that had fallen from Kena's hand when he was slammed to the ground.

"Hmm... quite the piece. Not exactly a magic item, but still a top-tier weapon."

Marina murmured with admiration.

In truth, that rapier had been Kena's most important partner throughout her career as an assassin.

That was precisely why Kena couldn't bear the thought of anyone else touching it. Though she still couldn't speak properly, she forced out a shout.

"Don't... touch it... okay?"

"Huh. You can speak after that impact? Did I go a little too easy on you?"

Watching Keena, Vihera remarked with a hint of surprise.

The reason she didn't seem all that angry was likely because Rei had used a potion to heal the wound on Byune's cheek.

Since it was only a superficial scratch to begin with, even a cheap potion was sufficient for healing.

At first, Rei had been wary of poisonous substances, given that it was a weapon used by underworld figures. Fortunately, it seemed there was no need to worry about that aspect.

In any case, Marina decided it wasn't wise to provoke Kena now and placed the rapier she was holding on the ground.

Still, she placed it at a distance from the rope-bound Kena, likely out of lingering caution.

"Well, it seems we can talk now. Let's hear everything. First, who hired you?"

"Cheval."

"...What?"

"Oh, it's the name of the person who hired me, isn't it? So, Shuval."

Having recovered from the impact of being slammed to the ground, Kena stated this without coughing or showing any signs of distress.

It was convenient for interrogation, but the name actually spoken was one Rei had never heard before.

He had been certain the name Precious would come up...

"Cheval? Who's that?"

"I don't know. Sorry, but I don't verify the client's background. As long as the conditions—including the payment—are right, I take the job."

"Yet here you are, so readily spilling their information?"

"Well, you're Rei, right? There's no one else who acts alongside the Griffin. I've no intention of putting up any strange resistance here against an adventurer with a nickname."

"...So you really were hired by this guy Cheval?"

"Yes. That's what I'm saying, isn't it?"

Kena declared this with a hint of a smile. In terms of nerve, she could be considered a full-fledged adventurer.

But in this case, it was simply bad luck who she picked a fight with.

"I see. Then this is completely irrelevant to us. In that case, we don't have time to waste on you... Let's just get this over with."

Rei muttered, glancing at Marina before drawing the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

The massive blade was thrust directly against Keena's neck.

All Rei needed to do was tighten his grip on the Death Scythe... and in the next instant, Keena's head would be cleanly severed from her body.

As if understanding this, a bead of sweat trickled down Keena's cheek.

She understood that the killing intent emanating from Rei was no joke—he truly intended to take her life.

"Well then."

With that muttered farewell, power surged into Rei's hand... and Kena reflexively closed her eyes.

Then the blade of the Death Scythe touched Kena's neck, slicing through skin as a trickle of blood flowed...

"Wait!"

Just as the blade of the Death Scythe was about to cut through the flesh beneath the skin, Marina's voice cried out a halt.

"What's wrong?"

"It might not concern us directly, but it's clear this person is involved in this matter. So instead of us disposing of them here, shouldn't we hand them over to the guards?"

"...Are you serious? Like he says, we can't be sure he's not involved in this case, right?"

"Yes. But it's also possible he is involved, right? So, the best course is to leave this matter to the security squad handling the Spier Trading Company case."

"You insist?"

"Yes. Even from the perspective of a former guildmaster, I think that's the better course."

As he listened to Marina's words, Keena's expression softened just a little.

He had been certain he would die here. Precisely because he had thought that, the mere possibility of surviving made it impossible for him to simply dismiss it.

...Unbeknownst to Kena, bound and unable to see behind him, Vihera watched the exchange between Rei and Marina from behind him, a smile playing on his lips.

"Alright. I'll go with Marina's suggestion for now. Then, instead of taking this guy with us... it'd be better to have the guards brought here. Should I go get them?"

No one voiced any objection to Rei's question.

Kena, in particular, silently agreed—she genuinely wanted Rei gone, especially since he'd so readily tried to kill her.

"Yeah, Rei's fine. But just to be safe, could you leave Set here?"

"Yeah. ...Set, I'll go get the guards. Can you stay here and watch over Marina and the others for a bit? Well, I doubt anyone would be foolish enough to attack us."

With Set here, it was obvious everyone present was connected to Rei.

Anyone who attacked without knowing that would regret it, and anyone who did know wouldn't dare.

"Grrrr!"

Laying a light hand on Set, who growled "Leave it to me," Rei departed.

Once Rei's figure vanished, Keena let out a sigh of relief.

She knew she was an assassin, someone who could never live in the open society.

But even so... she had never felt death so directly before.

For that very reason, it was only natural that Kena felt a sense of relief welling up inside her when Rei—the very symbol of death to her—disappeared.

Marina and Vihera, who had been watching Kena, quietly exchanged glances.

Byune leaned her weight against Set's body, as if to heal the fatigue from the battle that had just ended.

Silence filled the air around them.

It was a back alley, so there were people passing by, but those who had witnessed the battle moments before had quickly left the scene.

Even though the fighting was over, the air remained thick with tension. No one seemed inclined to step forward into the scene.

Amidst this, Marina finally broke the silence.

"You did something reckless too, didn't you?"

"...What are you talking about? There shouldn't have been any problems until you lot showed up."

"Didn't you know we were involved in this matter too, including Rei? Given the current situation, if you mess with Spier Trading Company, it's only natural we'd show up."

"And why were you lingering in front of the branch after your work was done? Why didn't you just leave promptly?"

Vihera chimed in, agreeing with Marina's words.

There had been ample time between Keena attacking the Spier Trading Company branch and Rei's group arriving.

Despite that, Keena was still lingering around the branch.

He had blended into the surrounding crowd, but it was still nothing short of careless.

"What could I do? I never thought you guys would show up this quickly."

"Oh? I don't think it was that fast."

"It was fast. Based on my past experience, I should've had a bit more time."

Kena replied to Marina's words.

With Rei, who had pressured her with death, gone, and feeling relieved, Marina, who had shielded her, spoke up. She couldn't help but engage in conversation.

Seeing things unfold as she'd expected, Marina smiled as she continued her conversation with Keena.
