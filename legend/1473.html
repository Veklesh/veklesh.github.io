"Gurrrrrrrrr!"

Set growled as he descended into Treant Forest.

Hearing Set's growl, the woodcutters and adventurers guarding them in Treant Forest became alert.

Several adventurers stepped outside the Treant forest, waving their arms wide to indicate where Set should land.

(Honestly, they didn't need to go to such lengths.)

If Rei had been riding in an airplane, such gestures might have made sense, but this time it was Set flying through the sky.

Set could decide for himself where to land.

Still, since the opportunity presented itself, Rei stroked Set and told him to follow the instructions of the adventurer guiding him from the ground.

"Set, land where he's pointing."

"Grrrr."

At Rei's words, Set growled and began descending toward the ground.

The adventurer greeted the landed Set... and Rei, with a delighted smile.

"Rei-san, Set. Thank you for coming."

"...Ruguruno, why are you so happy? I was here yesterday too, right?"

Yesterday, he had spent a long time in Treant Forest.

So why, specifically today, was he being welcomed so warmly upon his arrival? He couldn't help but wonder.

To such a Rei, Rugluno smiled, his joy tinged with a hint of trouble.

"Actually, well... someone made a bit of a mistake cutting down a tree, and it's getting in the way of the other woodcutters. They felled a tree right where people walk through."

"Ah... I see."

Seeing Ruguruno's expression, Rei understood the situation.

Judging by Ruguruno's expression, it was probably not a woodcutter who caused it, but an adventurer.

(And not the adventurer who'd been guarding the woodcutters from before, but the one assigned here yesterday. The one who charged straight into the forest first.)

They barely listen to others, only caring about their own profit.

If that's how those adventurers think, then it wouldn't be surprising if something like what Ruguruno described happened to Rei either.

After all, the woodcutter's job is to fell trees.

They carefully consider where a tree will fall when cutting it down.

"Those guys from yesterday?"

When Rei asked this, Rugluno flashed a smile that seemed to hide something.

Just seeing that, Rei knew it was definitive.

"Actually, it seems yesterday's payment was more than expected. So, they were apparently trying to cut more today to increase their earnings."

"Blinded by greed, huh?"

Muttering this, Rei felt a slight pang of guilt toward Rugluno and the woodcutters.

After all, sending adventurers into Treant Forest yesterday was meant to probe for information about Ajas.

But focusing solely on Ajas... well, that wasn't feasible. The other adventurers sent to Treant Forest served more as a camouflage.

That meant if the ones sent yesterday caused trouble, responsibility would fall on Rei too, since he'd proposed the plan.

Unlike before, monsters were now appearing in Treant Forest. Increasing the number of adventurers was something the guild had considered, and indeed, requests had come from the woodcutters themselves. Instructions regarding this had also been issued from the lord's manor.

All Rei did was jump on the bandwagon.

Normally, he wouldn't need to feel that burdened by responsibility. Still, since his proposal was the catalyst, he couldn't help but feel at least some degree of accountability.

"I understand. Anyway, the felled trees are in the way, right? Then, let's deal with those trees first."

"Thank you."

Hearing Rei's words, Rugluno led them into the forest with a sigh of relief.

"...Wasn't the plan basically to cut the trees from the outside in?"

A few minutes into the forest. Seeing Ruguruno continue walking without stopping, Rei voiced his confusion.

But to Rei, Ruguruno opened his mouth apologetically.

"Actually, well... cutting down trees in better condition brings in more money..."

"Ah, yeah. No need to say more. I get the gist."

Rei understood, even without hearing the end of it, what those who caused this commotion were thinking when they did such a thing.

(No wonder the woodcutters look so unhappy.)

This mess must have stopped their work. Watching several lumberjacks looking displeased, Rei sighed.

Should he have the guild properly address the troublemakers, or should he step in himself to stop them from acting recklessly?

Even as these thoughts occupied him, he kept moving deeper into the forest. Soon, the problematic area came into view.

Just as Rugurno had said, felled trees lay across the path, blocking the way that had been trodden down by woodcutters and adventurers.

Of course, the trees weren't thick enough to completely block movement.

Still, if you wanted to move normally, the felled trees would be a nuisance.

"Hey, it's Rei. Rei's here."

"Finally. ...Damn it, what a nuisance. When amateurs do stupid things, it causes trouble for us."

"Anyway, this means we can come and go freely now. ...Phew, it's perfect timing that Rei showed up. What a relief."

Overhearing this exchange, Rei glanced around at the surroundings.

People gathered around the felled tree.

The gaze of those people—a mix of lumberjacks and adventurers—was fixed on several adventurers who looked downcast, perhaps realizing their own inadequacy after causing this situation.

"So it was you lot who messed this up?"

"Hii!"

As if expecting to be killed by Rei's words, the men let out frightened cries.

Watching them like this, Rei found dealing with them ridiculous.

If they regret it now, they shouldn't have done such a thing in the first place, he thought.

"Well, fine. You seem remorseful, so I won't say anything this time. From now on, listen carefully to what Rugluno says and do your jobs properly. ...If you pull something like this again, I'll have to report it to the higher-ups."

"Superiors" in this case would mean the Guild.

And if this incident were reported to the guild, the reputation of the adventurers who caused this commotion would naturally suffer.

On top of that, the guild will interrogate them in detail, and they'll likely be closely monitored from now on.

They risk missing out on the boom from the expansion project at the thriving Gilm.

It's precisely because they understand this that the adventurers seem relieved by Rei's words.

(If they think they can just get through the present moment and everything will be fine... well, they'll probably regret it later. That's their own fault. No need for me to get involved.)

I won't report what Rei said, but that's only because it came from Rei.

The other woodcutters and adventurers here might very well report it to the guild.

After all, Rei only said he wouldn't report it himself.

Of course, even if someone else reports it, would it have the same effect as if Rei had reported it? The answer is no.

A high-ranking adventurer with a nickname, who's also provided significant assistance with this expansion project. Just having Rei here has cut the construction time considerably.

It's precisely because Rei possesses that level of ability that he wields such strong influence.

...Of course, if someone other than Rei reported it, the guild's scrutiny would likely be somewhat harsher.

"Alright, move aside for now. I'm going to stow that tree away."

At Rei's words, those gathered around the tree dispersed.

As they dispersed, Rei spotted Ajas among them and felt a slight sense of relief.

Since Ajas was pretending to be a virtuous adventurer, Ray hadn't expected him to head off to another job so soon after yesterday. Still, he hadn't had absolute certainty.

That's precisely why seeing him here in person brought such relief.

(Once this is settled, I'll try to get something out of him. ...If he reacts at all, it'd help me out.)

Thinking this, Rei confirmed everyone had moved away from the fallen tree, touched it, and stored it in his Misty Ring.

"Whoa!"

The woodcutters and adventurers let out cries of astonishment.

Seeing the Misty Ring stow away felled trees almost daily, Rei couldn't understand why they were so shocked.

But regardless, Rei finished storing the tree without paying much attention to his surroundings. The woodcutters and adventurers who had been watching nearby returned to their respective tasks.

Glancing sideways at Rugluno talking to the failed adventurers, Rei addressed his intended target... Ajash.

"Hey, long time no see."

"Long time no see... What kind of greeting is that?"

Ajas said, sounding exasperated.

His expression didn't seem to show any particular sense of guilt.

(Hmm. It feels odd, but I have no proof. ...Well, what should I do?)

Thanks to the hood of his Dragon Robe, Ajas couldn't tell what kind of look Rei was giving him.

He couldn't tell... but even so, he understood from the atmosphere that Rei suspected something about him.

That's precisely why Ajas answered carefully, mindful not to arouse suspicion.

To show he was a model adventurer.

Rei suspected Ajas might be Ilse's killer, while Ajas suspected Rei might know something about his hidden side. They were both suspicious of each other, yet to an outsider, they appeared to be having a perfectly friendly conversation.

"You showed up for work properly again today, huh?"

"Yeah. This job pays reasonably well. ...Though it's a pain when people act selfishly like they did earlier."

"True. When it comes to work, taking the easy way out and trying to make money without effort... I don't think that's right."

Rei was implicitly questioning whether attacking peddlers to steal their money was right... but to Ajas, it felt like he was being asked, "You're not kidnapping people and selling them as slaves, are you?"

The two were slightly out of sync, yet somehow their underlying conversation still managed to connect. You could almost call it miraculous.

Of course, neither of them could possibly be happy about that.

"Ahahaha. Yeah, making easy money probably isn't exactly what you'd call proper for an adventurer. But people do seek ease, don't they?"

"I won't deny that."

Making easy money isn't right for an adventurer.

That's what Rei said, but in reality, he'd destroyed numerous bandit groups and acquired much of the treasure they'd hoarded.

He'd even earned the nickname "Bandit Eater" among the bandits themselves, so his words held little persuasive power.

Of course, he was doing the work of defeating the thieves, so it wasn't truly easy in the strictest sense.

In truth, it's not something just anyone could do.

...And precisely because Rei possessed that level of power, Ajas couldn't help but be wary of Rei drawing closer to him.

"Well, let's leave that topic for now. What's up with you today, Rei?"

He must have judged that continuing this conversation would be inconvenient for him. Ajas changed the subject to evade the topic.

Rei noticed Ajas' abrupt change of subject, but he judged that pushing the conversation further might provoke Ajas into rash action. He went along with the new topic.

"What do you mean 'what's up'? I've been coming to this Treant Forest every day since the start. You saw me yesterday, right? If I don't store the lumber I cut down, it'll be a huge hassle hauling it all the way to Gilm."

"...An item box, huh. I'm honestly jealous you have such a rare magic item."

"Yeah. Though..."

Rei paused there, fixing Ajash with a sharp gaze.

"Many have coveted this. Yet it remains in my possession... Now then, I wonder what became of those who tried to take it from me?"

Rei's gaze held a hint of murderous intent.

Seeing it, Ajas could easily imagine the pitiful end that awaited those fools.

At the same time, he understood that no matter how things turned out, his own skill was absolutely no match for Rei's.

As a Rank D adventurer, Ajas possessed a reasonable confidence in his own abilities.

But just that one glance was enough for him to grasp that the man named Rei standing before him was of an entirely different caliber.

(But then again, I'm not trying to fight Rei. If I can't win, I just won't fight.)

Shifting his mindset with surprising ease, Ajas opened his mouth as if to placate Rei.

"Hey, hey, don't get so worked up. I'm not after your item box or anything."

He said this and changed the subject.
