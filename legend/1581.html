While Rei and Byune passed the time in a slightly relaxed atmosphere with Yellow, a fierce battle raged on the other side of the door.

Though it was a fierce battle, it was more accurately described as a one-sided fight.

"Haah!"

Elena's whip-like mirage lashed out, coiling around the giant's neck... She yanked it back swiftly, and in the next instant, the giant's head rolled to the ground.

Seizing the opening, the giant lunged at Elena.

Giants who had been touching the door suddenly lost consciousness or life, collapsing to the ground. Twilight Spears hurled through the door. Flames flying in countless numbers from beyond the door.

The flames consumed many giants, leaving the fortunate survivors in chaos.

But when two women appeared from beyond the door, the giants' consciousnesses became one.

Namely... appetite.

They likely sensed, almost instinctively, that a woman's flesh was softer than a man's.

Or perhaps they remembered from consuming their mothers' bodies at birth.

Regardless, with the black crystal's influence gone, the starving giants now had the finest prey leaping right into their grasp.

Naturally, they would never dream of letting such a perfect opportunity slip away.

But... even if it was perfect prey for the giants, that prey concealed sharp fangs.

No, in this case, it would be more accurate to say it possessed not just fangs, but claws as well.

The two women, who should have been the giants' prey, were instead systematically reducing their numbers.

Miracle, held in Elena's hand, severed their necks, limbs, and torsos one after another. When struck by Vihera's fists and kicks, the giants' bones shattered and their internal organs ruptured.

For the giants, these were no easy prey whose soft flesh they could simply devour.

Even understanding this, driven by their hunger, the giants charged at Elena and Vihera.

Another disadvantage for the giants was the presence of several still-scorching spaces.

Most of the heat didn't escape outward, so being near them only felt moderately hot—but only if you stayed close.

Stepping into one of those scorching spaces would instantly incinerate a body.

The giant burned by that magic earlier had not only its skin and flesh, but even its bones completely charred.

It was as if the giants had never been there at all, leaving not a single trace behind.

Even in such a hellish inferno, the giants must have retained enough instinct to sense the danger. But...

"Haahhh!"

Vihera's kick, unleashed with a battle cry.

Though it caught the blow, the sheer force of the strike—unlike anything expected from such a size difference—sent the giant staggering back several steps...

"GAAAAAAHHHH!"

The next instant, one of its feet stepped into one of the remaining scorching hells, and before anyone could look away, the foot began to char and burn.

The giant screamed in agony as his foot was burned alive, but Vihera showed no mercy.

A follow-up strike drove a fist into the giant's torso, sending it crashing into the searing hell below to be burned alive.

But even seeing their comrade's fate, the other giants didn't pause.

Consumed by hunger, their minds held only one thought: to devour the flesh and organs of the woman before them, no matter what.

The amount of flesh was nowhere near enough to satisfy the stomachs of nearly a thousand giants—though their numbers had already been significantly reduced. Precisely because of that, it was only natural each giant wanted to devour that flesh as quickly as possible.

Though Elena and Vihera were accustomed to being the object of lustful or passionate gazes, being the target of such ravenous stares was rare.

Though not exactly a pleasant experience, it was a novel one. Yet, without pausing their movements, the two continued to bring death to the giants one after another.

(If they're that hungry, they could just eat the flesh of the giants already dead... or have they stopped cannibalizing?)

Dodging a giant's strike aimed at her, Elena cut through its neck with a Mirage as she slipped past, her mind filled with questions.

If they were truly overcome by extreme hunger, it wouldn't be strange for them to eat anyone, even their comrades.

But since they weren't in such a situation, Elena wondered if they weren't truly overcome by hunger in the real sense.

Even as these thoughts crossed her mind, the battle against the giants continued unabated.

She kept swinging Mirage swiftly, slicing and dicing the giants one after another.

It wasn't just Elena; Vihera was doing the same.

The two of them... truly, only the two of them were challenging the battle.

Facing them were hordes of giants.

Though their numbers had been significantly reduced by attacks through the door and magic, hundreds of giants still remained.

Against those hundreds, two were fighting.

But... even so, it was the two of them who were overwhelmingly pushing the giants back.

If someone unaware of the situation were watching this scene, it wouldn't be strange if they couldn't comprehend what was happening.

"GRAAAAAAAH!"

Too much trouble to grab with my hands, it seemed.

The giant turned its face directly toward Vihera, intent on tearing flesh from her body with its own teeth.

Facing such a giant, Vihera dodged the attack with dance-like footwork, then slashed the giant's throat with the magical claws generated on his gauntlets.

Blood spurted from the giant's neck, but Vihera was already nowhere near that giant.

Before anyone realized it, she had slipped past another giant standing a short distance away, slicing open its side with her magic claws.

Normally, it wouldn't be strange for her to pull back and create some distance from the giants.

But... Vihera was far from ordinary.

Instead, she advanced toward the gathering of giants.

Moving his two arms and two legs with perfect freedom, he used the blades protruding from the heels of his greaves to slash through one giant's body after another.

Since these were attacks relying on sheer luck to land, a single strike rarely proved fatal.

Still, blood flow slowed the giants' movements, and severed tendons prevented them from moving limbs freely.

Some giants tried to move around forcefully even in that state, but giants whose movements had slowed were no longer enemies to Vihera and Elena, but targets.

Vihera gently touched the giant's body and unleashed the Magic Palm.

It destroyed some internal organ, including the heart. Sometimes, when a giant was kneeling, she used the Magic Palm on its head, destroying its brain.

"Whoa... This feels like a one-sided fight. No, not a fight—more like annihilation or slaughter, that kind of thing?"

Watching Elena and Vihera fight, Rei muttered this while peering inside through the door's crack.

He had been keeping watch outside the door for any abnormalities, but finding absolutely nothing unusual, he had grown bored and started peering through the crack.

The door was already riddled with holes from the previous exchanges... It was more accurate to call it the remains of a door than an actual door, so peering through the gap wasn't difficult.

"Hmm."

"Kyu"

Beside Rei, Byune and Yellow—one person and one creature—were also peering inside through another gap.

Yellow sat atop Byune's head, watching his master fight.

To an outsider, it might have been a rather ridiculous sight... or perhaps not. Regardless, Rei completely dismissed the idea that anyone else might be watching and focused solely on the space where the giant stood.

"Looks like I won't need to lend a hand at all. Judging by this overwhelming difference in strength... Well, I never expected it to turn out like this."

For Rei, Elena and Vihera's abilities were nothing to worry about.

Especially for Rei, who had experience fighting giants, the idea that the two in his line of sight could lose to a giant was utterly inconceivable.

...If Elena and Vihera were ordinary humans, there might have been a chance they'd run out of stamina mid-fight.

But Rei could state with certainty that Elena and Vihera, specifically, would not lose here due to exhaustion.

After all, Elena had inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, and Vihera had absorbed Ambris.

Still, even while thinking that, he would undoubtedly rush to help them immediately if anything happened.

"Oh, they've come this way."

If they could see through the door's crack, it meant the giants on the other side could also see Rei and the others peering through the crack.

Given that, it was only natural that some of the giants, who were currently only dealing with the two women, would take an interest in Rei and his group.

To begin with, two women alone—Elena and Vihera—could never possibly satisfy all the giants.

Moreover, the number of giants currently engaged in combat with those two was extremely limited.

In reality, many giants hadn't even engaged with those two yet.

What would those giants do if they spotted Rei and the others...? That was obvious.

Watching the giants approaching the door, Rei spoke to Byune, who was nearby.

"For now, I'll fight the giants here. Byune, keep a close watch from behind to make sure no one else comes."

"Mm."

Though Byune flashed a slightly displeased expression at Rei's words, she still obediently moved back to the rear.

Of course, Byune knew just how strong Rei was.

In fact, she probably judged that Rei was in no way inferior to Elena or Vihera, so there was no need to worry about letting him join the fight here.

...Though, to be honest, Byune wasn't entirely without the desire to join the fight himself.

This wasn't because Byune shared Vihera's penchant for enjoying combat... It was simply because he couldn't possibly imagine enemy reinforcements arriving from behind.

Of course, that wasn't necessarily wrong.

The Jaya members in this cave were already nearly incapacitated.

If anyone did attack, it would likely be soldiers Rei and the others had defeated along the way, ones who had only been knocked unconscious.

Even such individuals would likely have a broken bone or two.

Byune's power, while taking down a giant alone might be a poor match and impossible, dealing with such individuals was undoubtedly within her capabilities.

That was precisely why Byune, though dissatisfied, followed Rei's orders.

"Well then, this door is getting in the way."

The door's remains were already riddled with holes... worse, in some places, the heat had even melted it red.

If the giant blew that door toward where they were, it would be dangerous.

(Then we just need to act before they do anything.)

Rei made that decision without hesitation and swung the Death Scythe in his right hand with a wide arc.

"Power Slash!"

The single blow he delivered possessed such staggering power that it was hard to believe the target was a massive metal door. It blew the door away effortlessly.

Hinges or the like were meaningless against such force.

Several giants who had approached the door, intending to devour Rei, were struck by the door itself as it flew back from the Power Slash.

No, in this case, it wasn't a collision—it was more like a collision.

The power of a single Power Slash unleashed by Death Scythe was so immense it even strained the Rei wielding it.

Consequently, the few giants that collided with the blown-away door suffered severe injuries, their bones shattered and internal organs destroyed.

Had the black crystal Rei destroyed been here, the giants might have been able to heal their wounds.

Just as Rei had witnessed in Mejogo's underground facility.

But there was no black crystal here.

To be precise, fragments of black crystal had been embedded within the giants themselves. Yet, with the destruction of the main crystal, those fragments too vanished.

That is precisely why the giants went berserk... though no one understands that yet.

"They didn't seem to care at all about their comrades' sacrifices... Well, I guess that's just how giants are."

Several giants lay collapsed on the ground, their bones broken and internal organs destroyed by the door.

But whether they lacked the concept of comradeship or were too consumed by hunger to care... either way, the other giants spotted Rei and charged at him without hesitation.

Watching them, Rei stepped forward, maintaining his usual stance: Death Scythe in his right hand, Twilight Spear in his left.
