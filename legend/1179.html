Rei killed the kobold leader... or rather, created a corpse that could easily be called blown to bits, picked up the magic stone, and looked around.

A little way off stood the caravans' wagons, and behind them, another battle had already ended.

With just one person and one beast—Vihera and Set—attacking the goblin horde, it was only natural the fight ended swiftly.

Holding the magic stone, he glanced at the scattered fragments of flesh that could be called the remains of the kobold leader.

Scattered so thoroughly like this, it seemed unlikely they could become undead.

With their bodies so scattered, they couldn't become zombies or similar undead. Likewise, with their bones scattered, they couldn't become skeletons either.

Thinking that, there was probably nothing to worry about.

For now, he retrieved only the head, which had been spared thanks to his attack on the torso. He stored it along with the Death Scythe, Twilight Spear, and magic stone in the Misty Ring and headed back toward the caravan.

The distance had been covered in an instant when chasing the kobold leader, but actually walking it took a fair amount of time.

During the walk, I touched the wrist holding the Death Scythe to check its condition.

I felt some pain from the recoil when I used Power Slash.

(I thought I'd managed to absorb the recoil well... No, maybe it's because I used Death Scythe with one hand this time, whereas before I used both hands.)

If I'm going to use Power Slash while employing the Two-Spear Style, I need to absorb the recoil even better than I am now.

He kept walking while thinking these thoughts, eventually arriving beneath the caravan.

The merchants and Vihera were already engaged in conversation.

The adventurers who had been fighting ahead of the caravan just stared at Rei in return, utterly dumbfounded.

(Don't they know me? ...Well, not to brag, but I'm pretty famous. It's hard to imagine adventurers active around here not knowing me.)

Feeling slightly puzzled by the stares directed at him, Rei addressed the adventurers.

"Are you all right?"

"Huh? Ah, yeah. We got some injuries, but thanks to Rei, they were minor."

Rei concluded they must have known him after all, since his name came up immediately.

Still, he couldn't help wondering why they seemed so utterly shocked.

(Well, asking about that probably wouldn't get a straightforward answer anyway.)

Even if someone was scared of him, asking the very person causing that fear why they were scared wouldn't get an honest answer.

In any case, Rei decided staying here would only unnecessarily frighten the adventurer guards. He started walking toward Vihera and the others.

The adventurers guarding Rei tried to call out to Rei... but in the end, they couldn't bring themselves to do it.

"Hey, you! If you hadn't been so slow to speak up..."

"Well, then you should've spoken up first instead of me!"

"Well... yeah, but that was impressive. So that's the one with the nickname."

The adventurers guarding Rei had mistakenly thought Rei was afraid of them, but in reality, they were mostly overwhelmed by his sheer strength.

They knew that with proper preparation, defeating a kobold horde wouldn't be difficult.

But even so, if asked whether they could fight like that... in a way truly worthy of being called a rout, the answer would be no.

His swift movements when chasing the fleeing kobold leader, and the way he didn't just slash kobolds with his scythe but crushed them with a single blow—that left a powerful impression on the adventurers.

...Yes. These adventurers weren't afraid of Rei; they were filled with admiration.

All the men were in their twenties, but the skill displayed by an adventurer younger than themselves was more than enough to make them feel admiration.

Of course, admiration wasn't the only emotion stirring within them.

Some harbored feelings bordering on jealousy.

But... even so, it was an undeniable fact that admiration was the strongest emotion.

Unaware that others felt this way about him, Rei headed toward Vihera.

Vihera was talking with a merchant who seemed to be leading the caravan, while Set lay sprawled out a short distance away, keeping watch as usual.

Of course, only Rei and Vihera knew that Set was actually keeping watch in that state; to everyone else, he must have just looked like he was sleeping.

"Rei, have you finished over there?"

Vihera calls out to Rei as he approaches.

Rei nodded back at Vihera before speaking.

"Yeah. Got the kobold leader good and proper. As for you... nah, no need to ask."

Rei looked away, his gaze falling on the pile of goblin corpses.

The kobolds seemed to have been trampled by Rei, but the goblins were no different.

No, given that the attack came from just one person and one beast—Set and Vihera—the scale was likely even greater.

While Vihera's attacks often caused internal destruction like the Demon Claw, naturally, a straightforward attack consumed less magic power and was quicker.

Furthermore, when attacking with magic items that generated claws on gauntlets and blades on greaves, the targets naturally suffered external wounds and died.

That would inevitably scatter blood, flesh fragments, and internal organs across the ground.

As Rei's gaze shifted from the adventurer guards gathering the bodies, it landed next on the merchant who had been speaking with Vihera moments before.

A man in his forties, well-built... or rather, it would be more accurate to say he was fat.

Despite being attacked by a horde of goblins and kobolds, he showed no sign of fear whatsoever.

He must possess considerable courage to send out a caravan despite the diverse monsters that roamed the area.

"You said your name was Rei? I am Lucho, leader of this caravan. Thank you for helping us this time. Honestly, without you and your group, we would have been wiped out... or at the very least, suffered heavy losses."

Rei shook his head at Lucho's words, delivered with a deep bow.

"Don't worry about it. Few caravans come to Gilm these days. In that sense, it's only natural for us who live here to help. More importantly, you were attacked by kobold and goblin hordes. Do you think that was just coincidence?"

"No. They were clearly cooperating. It wasn't exactly coordinated—it wasn't that sophisticated—but it felt like the goblin horde attacked us from behind, and then the kobold horde attacked us from the front as we fled."

"...I see. Any information like that coming through the merchant network?"

Rei asked tentatively, but Lucho silently shook his head.

He probably never imagined different species of mobs would cooperate like that to attack.

"We should probably inform the guild about this. Is that alright with you?"

"Yes. Absolutely. To prevent other merchant caravans from suffering the same fate, I'd actually prefer we be the ones to request it."

Contrary to his appearance, Lucio nodded quickly.

Then, turning his gaze toward the goblin horde, he spoke.

"So, I was discussing with Vihera-san that we'd handle the goblin corpses ourselves, but what about the kobolds?"

Goblins only fetch bounty parts and magic stones, but even so, a large number can add up to a decent sum.

Compared to that, kobolds definitely fetch a higher price than goblins.

Honestly, his desire was to compensate for the losses the caravan suffered in this raid using goblin and kobold proof-of-kill parts, magic stones, materials, and meat.

He managed to get the goblins handed over easily through negotiations with Vihera.

For Vihera, dealing with goblins was nothing but a hassle and a pain, so if someone else would handle the cleanup for him, it was absolutely no problem.

That's precisely why... it's only natural that Lucho would think the same about the kobolds.

"Fine by me."

Rei replied simply to Lucho's words.

Since he had already obtained the magic stone of the Kobold Leader, the only one he needed, there was nothing else he particularly wanted.

"Alright then, I'll give you this too."

He handed Lucho the severed head of the kobold leader, retrieved from the Misty Ring.

"Whoa!"

Lucio, suddenly handed the severed head of the Kobold Leader, let out a startled scream.

Even a merchant coming to the frontier town of Gilm would cry out in surprise if suddenly handed a cobalt's severed head like this.

Especially if they'd only heard stories about the Misty Ring before, seeing the Misty Ring with their own eyes for the first time made it all the more shocking.

"Along with the proof of slaying parts, having this will benefit you too, right?"

Lucio, the one who had requested it, wore an expression of astonishment at Rei's unexpectedly generous action.

He'd hoped for that outcome, but figured it was probably impossible. So he'd been thinking he might get at least a little something... He never imagined he'd be handed the entire monster carcass.

But for Rei, the money he could make from the kobold carcasses now wasn't significant. He also had kobold meat from defeating a kobold leader a few days prior.

Honestly, he didn't need any more kobold meat.

(Asking the guild to butcher or skin them would surely be met with reluctance if the quantity was too large.)

Someone like Lenora or Kenny, who were favorably disposed towards Rei, might have accepted the request even if it meant going out of their way.

But once the current situation with the massive monster swarms was dealt with, what awaited was undoubtedly a huge amount of monster butchering.

Plus, being summer, the monsters would rot easily unless Rei had a Misty Ring. Considering disposal, it might even be better to just burn them without stripping much material.

In other words, even if you want to place a dismemberment request, you'll have to wait until other adventurers have some free time.

Of course, during harsh winters when quests requiring travel outside town become difficult, disassembly requests—though less profitable than hunting, gathering, or escorting—are actually welcomed for being easier work.

(Ah, thinking about it that way, maybe I should just keep those kobolds in the Misty Ring for a while? ...Well, it's not like I want the meat that badly anyway, so whatever.)

He quickly dismissed the thought and turned his attention back to Lucho.

"About this incident... I think we should report to the guild that goblins and kobolds were cooperating. We're heading to the guild to report this anyway, but it's better to gather information from multiple sources."

"Yes, of course. We also want this pack issue resolved as soon as possible, so we'll cooperate as much as we can. We owe you and Vihera a debt of gratitude. ...Here, please take this."

Saying that, she handed over a basket containing sandwiches.

"...What's this?"

"Well, I heard Rei enjoys eating, and I also heard that griffin over there... Set, enjoys eating as well. So, as a small token of our gratitude..."

Rei looked slightly surprised at Lucho's explanation.

He knew his information was widely known.

Things like being skilled in wide-area annihilation using fire magic, wielding a scythe far larger than himself, and having a griffin as a familiar.

Lately, there was even talk of him having an exceptionally beautiful woman as a companion.

...Regarding this "peerless beauty," there were three distinct theories: Elena, Vihera, and Marina.

Since all three women are indeed worthy of being called "exceptionally beautiful," this has led to various conflicting pieces of information circulating.

"Sorry, but I'll gladly take it."

Feeling it would be rude to ignore Lucio's thoughtfulness, Rei accepted the sandwich.

"Well, we should be heading out soon. It should be fine, but you should try to get to Gilm as quickly as possible."

After exchanging only those brief words and a quick farewell, Rei climbed onto Set's back.
