'Flame, your power is my power. Burn with magic at my command and scorch the enemy. Your nature is spreading fire, hellfire. Use my magic as fuel to increase your firepower.'

Rei, holding the Death Scythe, chants the spell.

Ten fireballs were born from that incantation.

This magic was only possible because the spiral staircase was wide enough for even a giant to move through.

Had the staircase Rei and his companions stood upon been ordinary, casting this spell would likely have been impossible.

'Ten Fireballs'

As Rei swung his Death Scythe, the ten fireballs shot straight downward.

However, precisely controlling all ten fireballs was far from easy.

That's precisely why the ten fireballs that were released struck not the giant near the black crystal... but rather the outer edges of the giant horde.

Upon impact, they unleashed violent explosions around them.

But that wasn't all that was unleashed.

Elena unleashed multiple wind blades, while Marina used spirit magic to transform the floor where the giants had been sleeping into stone spears that pierced the giants above.

However, only the attacks from those three landed properly.

Byune threw her long needle, determined not to be outdone by the other three, but it barely pierced the giant's skin.

As for Vihera, lacking any means of ranged attack, she could only watch silently.

Of course, if Rei had thrown some weapon stored within the Misty Ring—a dagger, for instance—it wouldn't have been difficult to damage the giants.

However, rather than risking the lives of Rei and the others by attempting to kill or wound one or two giants, she judged it wiser to remain focused on guarding them, ready to respond immediately if anything happened. Thus, Vihera resolved to concentrate solely on her role as their escort for the critical moment.

...For Vihera, who found pleasure in combat, it was a painful decision.

Regardless, the attacks unleashed by Rei and the others dealt damage one after another to the giants gathered around the black crystal.

Some giants died from a single blow, while many others sustained injuries, varying in severity, though none were fatal.

There were about fifty giants gathered underground.

Nearly half of those fifty giants had been killed outright by this single strike.

Many of the remaining giants were also wounded to varying degrees.

"Well then, what will they do?"

If they remained motionless, it would be possible to continue launching long-range attacks from their current position and simply annihilate them.

But... if they pursue us... As Rei contemplated this, the giants who had been curled up asleep began to stir.

Despite some being injured, the giants rose as if completely unaffected... and scanned their surroundings.

Eventually, their gazes shifted away, as if they'd determined there were no enemies near them.

Once that happened, it was only natural that spotting Rei and the others wouldn't be difficult, and the giants easily discovered Rei's figure.

'GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR

In that instant, a unified roar erupted from the mouths of the surviving giants.

That roar was filled with anger beyond measure.

It was pure hatred—the kind that screamed, "If Rei and his crew were right in front of us, we'd kill them right now, slaughter them, grind them into dust."

Anyone hearing the roar could understand that such hatred was contained within it.

"Looks like we went a bit too far. Seems we've finally pushed them over the edge."

Rei muttered, but before anyone could respond, the giants moved.

Driven by raw frustration, and intent on crushing its source, the giants charged toward the spiral staircase where Rei and the others stood.

Over twenty giants charging toward Rei at once created an overwhelming force beyond imagination.

Giants' bodies were enormous to begin with, as their name implied.

Seeing them charge together, filled with rage and murderous intent, would normally cause anyone to panic.

But... unfortunately for them, Rei and the others were far from ordinary.

Upon confirming the giant horde charging toward them, they immediately retreated up the stairs to the fourth basement level.

They reasoned that fighting the giants on the fourth basement level, where nothing existed, was preferable to fighting them on the fifth basement level, where they might destroy the black crystal.

This was the reasoning behind their actions, though ordinarily, it wouldn't be unreasonable to call it a suicidal act.

After all, the giants possessed strength that would be difficult for ordinary humans to handle.

Still, for Rei and the others, they weren't opponents that were absolutely impossible to handle... and above all, fighting on the fourth basement level had the advantage of not having to face a large number of giants at once.

The spiral staircase was sturdy enough to handle giants moving through it without issue, but even so, the physical limit for movement at once was two at best.

Even those two required the precondition that they were properly anticipating each other's movements. In a completely enraged, rampaging state like now, it was hopeless.

If two giants tried to ascend the stairs together in that state, there was a very real possibility one would be blown off the stairs.

And a giant knocked off the stairs would, of course, be slammed onto the fifth basement floor.

Worse still, it would fall onto the other giants climbing the stairs, so sustaining some injury would be inevitable.

Yet, driven by rage, the giants would attempt to climb the spiral staircase again, convinced such injuries were insignificant.

It didn't matter at all whether the giant was knocked off the stairs or crushed by comrades falling from above.

Such was the intensity of the rage burning within the giants.

It was a fury so fierce that merely killing those who had provoked it would not suffice. They would not rest until they had devoured every scrap of flesh, every single eyeball, every bone fragment, and every drop of blood.

Driven by that rage, the giants charged up the spiral staircase.

The first giant to reach the fourth basement level, not even realizing he was the first to arrive, spotted Rei and the others a short distance away.

Roaring with rage, he charged toward them, intent on crushing them.

Even the Jaya people must have deemed it unsightly to leave the giants completely naked. Giants wrapped in some sort of fur around their waists weren't quite unbearable to look at.

"Well, even so... with blood rushing to his head, he's nothing but a sitting duck... huh!"

With that mutter, Rei took a few paces back and hurled the Crimson spear he held—the Twilight Spear.

The Twilight Spear, retrieved from the Misty Ring instead of the Death Scythe the moment they returned to the fourth basement level, pierced the air in a straight line toward the giant, propelled by Rei's inhuman physical prowess and, above all, the magic power infused within it.

Among the giants, the one leading the charge never imagined such a thing could be hurled at him.

No, consumed entirely by rage, it likely couldn't even comprehend that something was flying toward it.

Something was flying at it. The instant it grasped that, the Twilight Spear pierced the giant's skull, shattering it, and its consciousness was completely extinguished.

The Twilight Spear's damage wasn't limited to the leading giant.

The giant immediately behind it also had its head pierced by the Twilight Spear... The skull of the second giant caused the spear's trajectory to deviate slightly.

This deflection meant the third giant avoided having its head pierced clean through the center, but instead suffered the fate of having roughly half its skull crushed.

The first three giants to ascend to the fourth basement floor thus had their heads shattered cleanly and lost their lives.

The spear that took the lives of the three Titans then embedded itself halfway... no, about eighty percent of its length into the wall of the fourth basement level.

Under normal circumstances, pulling it out would undoubtedly be a struggle. But when Rei focused her will slightly, the Twilight Spear vanished the next instant, as if it had never been stuck in the wall at all... and now it was in Rei's hand.

"Impressive. ...I'd like to say that, but what's up with Rei taking out every single giant that showed up right off the bat?"

Vihera remarked with apparent admiration, but her expression quickly turned dissatisfied.

She was probably asserting that she wanted to fight them herself.

"Well, even so. There are a lot of enemies, so Vihera should get her share too, right?"

"Well then, I'll go next. If it gets too much for me, I'll ask for your help."

Saying this, Vihera ran up the stairs toward the most energetic-looking giant among those that had reached this fourth basement level.

While Rei couldn't say he didn't have some reservations about Vihera's actions, considering her personality, he decided it couldn't be helped. He then gave instructions to the others.

"The giants spawn at those stairs, and there are usually no more than two at a time. Basically, leave the front lines to Vihera. We'll provide support from the rear."

"Given Vihera's personality, I suppose it can't be helped."

"I suppose so."

"Mm."

Elena, Marina, and Byune each nodded and moved to support Vihera.

Elena attacked from the center, wielding Mirage like a whip, while Marina attacked from the rear, using both spirit magic and her bow.

Byune attacked the giant with her long needle, but her strength could only pierce the giant's skin—not sever flesh or bone.

As a result, Byune aimed for the giant's eyes and mouth—places the enemy would find unpleasant.

While Byune found this attack method deeply unsatisfying, as it forced her to confront her own limitations, she judged it the only option available to her now and continued hurling the long needles one after another.

...Despite Byune's own dissatisfaction, even a giant that could be described as half-out-of-control clearly didn't enjoy having long needles thrown at its eyeballs, mouth, or nose.

It tried to block the long needles flying toward it with irritation... but unfortunately, there was no way it could easily defend against the needles Byune was hurling.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

Shouting in frustration, he shifted his target from Vihera to Byune, intent on turning the annoying little attacker into a pile of flesh...

"Do you think I'll let you pull off something like that so easily?!"

"GRAAAAAAAH!"

With a sharp cry, the swung Mirage transformed into a whip-like blade, coiling around the giant's neck... The next instant, the blade, drawn by magic, easily severed the giant's head.

The severed head flew through the air, landing with a dull thud on the floor.

Without waiting to see the end of it, Elena swung the Mirage again, wounding the giant repeatedly.

And not just indiscriminately inflicting wounds, but targeting tendons in the legs and hands, joints... launching attacks designed to immobilize the giant's movements one after another.

Naturally, the giants counterattacked when struck, even if it wasn't Vihera.

But the way she danced around their attacks while wielding the whip-like Mirage was nothing short of a performance worthy of the name.

At first glance, Elena's limbs could easily be described as slender, though her ample bosom was another matter.

Yet, the whip-like mirage Elena wielded sliced through the giant's body again and again.

Severing limbs was commonplace, and decapitating giants attempting to attack Byune was no rarity.

The sheer power she wielded was largely due to her inheriting the Ancient Dragon's magic stone.

Possessing such unexpected power, it wouldn't be strange if someone unaware were completely captivated by her beauty and the sheer mystery of it all.

"Honestly, it feels like she's doing my job for me!"

Marina shot arrows while watching Elena fight.

Marina is top-notch not only in spirit magic but also in archery.

This is truly the Dark Elf's true potential shining through.

Arrow after arrow pierced the giants' bodies.

...Where Byune's long needles could barely scratch their skin, Marina's arrows pierced the giants' bodies with ease.

This was undoubtedly the skill of a seasoned high-rank adventurer.

Throwing and catching the Twilight Spear, Rei thought to himself as he did so.
