Thanks to Rei's work, the wall construction that day—or rather, the preparatory work needed for construction—progressed unexpectedly smoothly.

This was excellent news for Gilm, but it wasn't necessarily good news for everyone else.

"Dammit! Just because the schedule got moved up, what the hell is going on?!"

"Shut up! Just focus on the job right now!"

"But it's almost dark already!?"

One of the shouting carpenters looked up at the sky.

There, the crimson sunset hung low, ready to dip below the horizon.

But even in that situation, the men present couldn't enjoy a drink after finishing work.

Right now, they had to get their work done as quickly as possible, yet they couldn't rush and risk delivering defective goods.

If they completed the job perfectly here, it could mean being entrusted with major projects for Gilm in the future.

Conversely, if they failed to meet the deadline here, or worse, delivered shoddy work even if they did meet it, the reputation of the carpenters present would be utterly ruined.

They were known as some of the most skilled carpenters in the Gilm, so such a mistake was absolutely unthinkable.

"Damn it, what the hell is this... Today was supposed to be the first day back at work, so we should've been drinking and eating our fill at the tavern!"

"Shut up and get to work, I said! Once this job's done, I'll let you eat and drink as much as you want!"

The foreman leading the carpenters shouted at the others.

True, they'd been asked to take on this sudden job, but since they were pushing the carpenters hard, the pay was higher than usual.

The extra pay promised was substantial enough to cover treating every carpenter present here for several days straight without issue.

It wasn't just these carpenters who were busy in the current Gilm. Various other craftsmen were also crying out—including cries of joy—at the sudden surge of extra work.

Yet, the craftsmen weren't complaining seriously. This was partly because they took pride in their work, and partly because the extra pay offered was commensurate with the effort.

And while the craftsmen worked desperately, many adventurers had also gathered around the destroyed wall.

"Whoa, what the hell is this... Did they get this far in just one day?"

An adventurer who'd come to Gilm hoping to make a killing on this expansion project stepped outside where the wall had been destroyed. Seeing the scene illuminated by the fading evening sun, he let out a gasp of astonishment.

Having worked on expansion projects in other villages and towns before, he likely never imagined such progress could be made in a single day.

This was partly due to the presence of Rei, who could manipulate terrain, and partly because Gilm, being a frontier town, had a higher concentration of skilled mages compared to other villages, towns, or even cities. It was also related to Gilm's abundant wealth, which allowed them to pay high sums to hire mages.

Whatever the reason, the man could see the expansion work progressing at an unbelievable pace.

But the adventurers who had been active in Gilm from the start, seeing the man's reaction, smiled.

It was a resigned smile, as if to say, "Well, what can you do?" It was a proud smile, showing off the true strength of Gilm's adventurers. And it was also an exasperated smile, as if to say, "Well, it's Rei, after all."

Seeing the adventurers wearing these various expressions, the man decided against saying anything further.

He felt that, rather than saying anything here, it was more important to just get the job done properly for now.

"Tch! Not even fully dark yet, and you're already showing up! We'll throw you a grand welcome feast!"

A thief shouted, sensing the approach of something—a monster, perhaps, or an animal... whatever it was.

Hearing the voice, the adventurers realized there was no time left for conversation. Each prepared for battle.

They split into prearranged groups, waiting to confront the approaching foe.

And then, finally revealing itself...

"Goblins, damn it! If it had to be something, couldn't it have been a monster worth more gold?!"

The male adventurer shouted in frustration as he swung his longsword, severing a goblin's head.

This marked the start as several goblins charged through the hole in the wall.

They must have been trying to find a way inside Gilm... Fortunately, the number of goblins was far from large.

"Still, how did these goblins find this place so quickly?"

Perhaps because they were only facing goblins, one of the adventurers, having regained some composure, muttered this.

"Yeah. Logically, wouldn't it make sense that these goblins come around here every day at this time?"

"Even though it's near Gilm?"

The man who voiced the question opened his mouth in disbelief at the words of the female adventurer with the bow.

But the woman nodded back, as if to say it wasn't that surprising.

"Exactly. After all, the walls of Gilm are just there; they don't patrol the top looking out. ...Well, even if they did patrol, if goblins were near the walls, they'd likely decide attacking them would be a waste of effort and leave them alone. This is Gilm, after all."

Around Gilm, goblins are one of the lowest-tier monsters.

Naturally, if spotted by other monsters, they'd be killed without a second thought.

...No, not just monsters—they probably couldn't even beat slightly ferocious animals.

That's precisely why, unless they gather in groups of dozens or hundreds, it's not unusual for Gilm adventurers to deliberately avoid bothering with numbers like the less than ten that arrived here now.

Of course, if the goblins attacked first, that would be a different story.

Moreover, for new adventurers raised in Gilm or those who'd arrived here through various strokes of luck, this spot had even become something of a hidden gem. If things went well, they could obtain proof-of-kill parts and magic stones without even engaging in combat.

"Anyway, let's just be thankful the first ones to show up were goblins. If some high-rank monster had appeared right off the bat, things would have gotten messy, wouldn't they?"

"...Can't deny that."

If a Rank C monster had shown up, someone might have died on the very first day.

Thinking about it that way, it really was fortunate they were goblins... or so the adventurer forced himself to believe.

But the goblin that did appear ended up suffering heavy damage from the earth mage still lingering on the scene.

"Well then, to safely completing our first day... and to making more progress on the construction than we expected, cheers!"

"Cheers!"

The craftsmen in the tavern raised their cups and shouted joyfully.

The clinking of cups echoed, and in the next instant, they were raised to their lips.

"Ahhh! This drink after work is the best, isn't it?! You could say this is what I live for!"

"That's a bit much. ...Well, I definitely enjoy this time every day, though."

After downing about half their ale, the men reach for the food on the table.

Boiled sausages, skewered meats, stew, stir-fries, simmered dishes... Various dishes lined the plates, but they disappeared one after another.

They did eat something light during the day, but it was only light.

The sense of liberation after work... Above all, finishing their first day's work was huge, and the craftsmen drank and ate to their hearts' content.

They knew some were still working, but the craftsmen in this tavern had finished their share for the day. They drank, ate, and made noise to soothe their fatigue and recharge for tomorrow.

"You lot, drinking's fine, but don't get so stupidly drunk it affects tomorrow! We've got work to do tomorrow!"

Working with a hangover under the scorching summer sun is hell.

That hell isn't just for the drunkard, but also for those forced to cover their work.

"I won't tolerate that!" the man shouted.

"We know, boss! We've got it all figured out, so it'll be fine!"

"You idiot! Have you forgotten what happened two months ago? You were so confident saying it'd be fine, yet you were completely wasted the next day too!"

"Ah, that... well..."

The man who had boldly declared it would be fine was now being told he couldn't be trusted because he'd made the same mistake again.

The people around them watched the exchange with amusement, enjoying it as a side dish to their drinks.

"Hey, sis! Another plate of this dried meat, please!"

"Ah, another ale over here!"

Around the tavern, others placed various additional orders.

With the expansion work on Gilm finally starting today, it was only natural everyone was making a fuss.

Taverns, eateries, brothels... countless shops were working hard not to miss this business opportunity.

Being the first day, many were shouting themselves hoarse trying to attract customers.

In such a situation, fights naturally broke out frequently, with guards running around inside Gilm.

The noisy night had only just begun.

The guards would be running around all night without a moment's rest.

"...I see. That was fortunate for us. I never imagined Rei would be so skilled with earth-based magic."

"Yes. Thanks to Rei, the wall construction will undoubtedly finish sooner than anticipated. ...Though exactly how much sooner remains unclear."

In the office of the lord's mansion, Daskar looked satisfied as he read the documents regarding today's construction work that had been brought up.

Naturally. The sooner an extension project is finished, the better.

Of course, no matter how fast the work is done, if the finish is shoddy, it's practically harmful.

But... the craftsmen Daskar had gathered this time were all trustworthy individuals, and at the same time, skilled professionals.

He was confident enough to leave it entirely to them.

Even if his subordinates or day laborers made some strange mistakes, these craftsmen could cover for them.

Admittedly, gathering such skilled craftsmen meant their opinions clashed, and it took a fair amount of time before actual construction could begin.

"That part should be fine. It's true the construction speed has increased, but that's strictly for the preparatory work involved in building the walls. Once Rei finishes what she needs to do, the construction should proceed at our planned pace from there."

"...I hope so."

Daskar's subordinate hesitated at his words.

Having witnessed firsthand how many times Rei had defied their expectations... precisely because he'd seen it from Daskar's side, he couldn't bring himself to fully trust Daskar's words.

In terms of what Rei had accomplished, Daskar surely knew more than his subordinate... yet from the subordinate's perspective, Daskar showed absolutely no sign of worrying about that aspect.

(Why does Lord Daskar place so much trust in Rei? I know Rei has done various things for Gilm, but ultimately, he's just an adventurer. Isn't trusting him that deeply dangerous?)

It wasn't that he had anything against Rei personally, but still, the level of trust Daskar placed in Rei seemed excessive.

But he judged that voicing this would only upset Daskar, so he spoke of something else instead.

"I hear monsters have already appeared at the site where the wall was breached."

"...What kind of monster?"

"Reports indicate it was a goblin."

"I see. A goblin shouldn't be a problem."

Daskar had briefly felt a bad premonition—what if a more powerful monster had appeared?—but if it was only a goblin, there was no cause for concern.

Relieved by this, he looked out the window.

There, in the cloudless night sky, a large moon hung.

Seeing that moonlight, as if blessing Gilm who had begun the expansion work, pouring down upon the earth, Daskar somehow felt like taking a moonbath himself.

...Of course, on the very day Gilm's expansion project began, he had no such luxury. After hearing various reports from his subordinates, he promptly returned to his paperwork.
