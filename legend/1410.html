"Hey, pack these supplies over there! We can't fit any more in this cave!"

"Hold on, this one's pretty full too. If possible, take it somewhere else."

"Don't be ridiculous. Mountains and forests are one thing, but this is a forest! You know caves aren't just lying around everywhere, right?!"

"Hey, you're the first to pick up those stones over there, so make sure they're ready to go!"

"Captain, here's the damage we sustained in this battle."

"...Damn. They were a troublesome bunch, no matter how you look at it. Our losses are significant too. When will reinforcements arrive?"

"I don't know. The fact we're even operating around Gilm like this is dangerous if we're spotted. That makes it hard for headquarters to send reinforcements easily."

"Then what do we do? At this rate, our forces are depleting faster than planned."

"True. ...Why not hire some real bandits?"

"Exactly. We have them take the hits while we carry out our mission."

"Damn it. That merchant should've just handed over the supplies quietly. ...Next time I see him, I'll make sure to finish him off."

"Calm down. We're supposed to be thieves, after all."

Deep within the forest.

Currently, Rei and Byune were eavesdropping on the conversation coming from there.

Normally, this kind of job should be Byune's responsibility, but unfortunately, she's not suited for this sort of work.

Well, just looking at her abilities, it wasn't all that bad, but it was more accurate to say she had limitations in her ability to convey information.

That's precisely why Rei, who wasn't averse to this sort of stealth operation, had infiltrated the enemy's hideout with Byune to gather intel...

(So they weren't just common thieves after all.)

Even just the snippets of conversation they overheard contained words that were concerning in various ways: Captain, reinforcements, higher-ups, additional troops, mission...

Still, the one thing that gave Rei a slight sense of relief was discovering that the attacked merchant hadn't actually been working with them.

A merchant who viewed hired adventurers as disposable pawns would be dangerous in many ways.

And if that merchant was openly allied with someone like Gilm... someone openly hostile to the lord, Daskar, then involving them in the expansion project would have been downright dangerous.

(Well, this particular case aside, I doubt there are any merchants completely untouched by other factions' influence.)

Merchants will do anything that benefits them.

Of course, what constitutes "anything" varies from merchant to merchant.

It depends greatly on their sense of ethics, their business philosophy, and such things.

That's precisely why it was actually quite common for merchants doing business in Gilm to maintain friendly relations with nobles other than the Daskar—specifically, those aligned with the Royal Faction or the Noble Faction.

(Though, this isn't something we can just brush off as minor. Who knows who's behind these guys, but this clearly isn't about watching and waiting—it's practically an attack.)

Bandits... no, judging by what they were saying, they were likely private soldiers sent by some noble, merchant, or other faction.

If they're raiding merchants and stealing supplies, that's way beyond anything I could laugh off and forgive.

(Well, that's just my take on it. I have no idea what Daskar would decide if he found out.)

Daskar is a Neutral Faction... the central figure of one of the three major factions said to exist within the Kingdom of Mireana.

Given the nature of such a faction, it naturally has many enemies.

But acting rashly here could provoke the Noble Faction or the Royal Faction.

Even as one of the three major factions, the Neutral Faction itself is considerably smaller in scale compared to the Royal Faction or the Noble Faction.

A misstep could provoke both of those factions.

The Noble Faction was friendly due to the relationship between Rei and Elena, but factions are factions. They wouldn't dare bring personal friendships into faction relations.

As these thoughts crossed his mind, he must have decided the reconnaissance was sufficient. Byune gave the Dragon Robe a light tug.

Rei gave Byune a small nod and then quietly retreated from their hiding spot, careful not to be seen.

(Huh? If Byune couldn't convey her own will to others, then maybe she didn't need to come at all? I could have handled this alone...)

He pondered this while moving through the woods, watching Byune. But he quickly changed his mind, reasoning that if he considered her an advisor, then it wasn't a problem.

After moving through the woods for about ten minutes, the grassland outside came into view.

The fact they could move at such an unimaginable speed was undoubtedly because Rei was there.

With Rei leading the way, he could clear the underbrush, identify where branches obstructed the path, and pinpoint tree roots—essentially, he could anticipate and warn of obstacles that would normally hinder Byune's swift movement.

Thanks to this, simply following Rei allowed them to move swiftly.

Emerging from the woods, they headed straight for a tree a short distance away.

"Well, well, you're quite early. Are you done already?"

Marina emerged from behind the tree, her voice carrying the question.

A little further away, they could also see Vihera petting Set.

Marina's tone expressed surprise that Rei and Byune had returned so quickly, showing no sign of anxiety about being discovered.

She understood that with Rei's skill, there was absolutely no way they would be found by the thieves.

That she was right was proven by the fact that Rei and Byune were safely here now.

Rei understood Marina's reasoning, so he didn't get angry.

"Yeah. Looks like my bad feeling was right. ...They're not just common thieves. More like a private militia sent by some faction."

Though Rei had said private militia, the image that came to mind after hearing that conversation was more like a special forces unit.

"Right."

Even after hearing Rei's words, Marina didn't seem particularly surprised.

No, it was more like she accepted it.

This whole operation—attacking merchants transporting supplies, especially near Gilm—had been suspicious from the start.

If someone's private militia had planned this, it actually made more sense.

Because she had thought that, she only gave a brief reply to Rei's report.

Unlike Marina, who seemed convinced, Vihera, who had been stroking Set while listening to Byune's story, smiled with amusement.

Bandits and private soldiers.

If asked which would be tougher opponents, most would probably think of the latter.

Of course, there are strong opponents among thieves, and weak ones among private armies.

But considering this private army group was entrusted with such a special mission, it was almost certain they were formidable opponents.

That very thought filled Vihera's eyes with a hunger for battle.

She longs to fight a worthy opponent.

"Ah, come on. Calm down, Vihera. This request is ultimately about retrieving the supplies. That's the top priority."

"...I know."

Even as she said this, Vihera's gaze remained fixed on the depths of the forest.

Vihera had found some satisfaction in the incident at Treant Forest, but since then, she hadn't experienced any major battles.

There had been instances where someone new to Gilm, unaware of the situation, tried to hit on Vihera, leading to a fight. But few could stand a chance against the Vihera of today.

As a result, such individuals ended up losing consciousness and collapsing to the ground without much fuss.

For someone like Vihera, a private militia operating deep within the frontier was an intriguing prospect.

"More importantly, let's hurry. If we waste any more time like this, there's a real chance they could escape."

"Nah, I don't think so."

They'd only abandon all that material and flee if they realized danger was closing in on them.

If they knew Rei and the others were nearby, that might be another story. But since their existence was still unknown, there was no need to worry about them escaping.

"Do you have any proof?"

"Yeah. They mentioned wanting reinforcements. If they want reinforcements, that obviously means they still have business here, right?"

"...Well, I suppose so."

Vihera seemed convinced by Rei's words. Reluctantly, he nodded in agreement.

(The fact they were waiting for reinforcements means they might just retreat if none come... but well, I guess I don't need to say that part.)

If he did say it, Vihera might just charge headlong into the enemy with renewed vigor.

Thinking this, she reported the information she'd overheard inside the enemy hideout.

Upon hearing that they had infiltrated not just the immediate vicinity but inside the hideout itself to eavesdrop, Marina was torn between anger and praise.

It was gratifying that both Rei and Byune possessed such capability, but she wondered if it was truly necessary to push that far in the current situation. That was the reason for her hesitation.

Of course, more information is always better...

In the end, Marina only let out a small sigh and didn't go so far as to blame Rei any further.

"I understand the situation. ...That being the case, I'd like to strike back immediately, but... being in the woods makes things difficult. If they escape, it'll be hard to find them."

Marina murmured, glancing at the woods where they were hiding.

Unlike the trees in Treant Forest, the trees here were a mix of thick and thin trunks.

But even the thickest trunks could barely conceal a single person.

Even so, a forest remained the ideal place for escape.

This was especially true for an elite unit tasked with infiltrating enemy territory to launch an attack.

"Over there... Set, can you handle it?"

"Guru?"

At Marina's words, Set, still being stroked by the expressionless Byune, let out a growl.

"Set, hold position above this forest. If anyone tries to escape, capture them if possible. ...Set, you can handle it, right? Even if they scatter in all directions, you can either capture them all or take them down?"

"Gururu!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a cry that seemed to say, "Leave it to me!"

...Though, lying on the ground being petted by Byune, he lacked a certain intensity.

"Alright, that settles it then. Let's go. I want to eat dinner at Gilm."

"What exactly were you eating at the Dusk Wheat inn before coming here?"

Vihera muttered, sounding slightly exasperated, but Rei pretended not to hear and turned his gaze toward the forest... where the enemy's hideout lay.

Seeing Rei like that, Vihera's exasperation turned into a small smile, and Marina also shifted her mood.

Vihera checked the condition of her gauntlets, while Marina inspected her bow.

Byune, too, checked her weapon... the White Cloud, forged from the Silver Lion.

This spear, too, was a splendidly practical tool.

Rei murmured, looking slightly surprised at the deep green spear he held.

Originally, this spear was a reward Rei received from Marina for completing a request.

That was probably why Marina was curious about it.

"We're in the woods, after all. The Death Scythe is awkward to use in various ways, and the Twilight Spear has too much attack power. Compared to those, this Thorn Spear is superior to the Twilight Spear in terms of capturing opponents."

Marina's sympathetic words made Rei nod in agreement.

True to its name, thorns sprouted from where the tip pierced, wrapping around the target.

It was undeniably a rather extreme ability to call mere capture.
