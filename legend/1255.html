Two days after the shoe matter was settled... Rei and Set were in the woods outside Gilm.

It was a spot near a river where they had dismembered monsters several times before.

Rei's prediction upon seeing the snow had proven correct; it was now falling rapidly and piling up.

More than ten centimeters of snow had already accumulated around the river, and it continued to fall steadily.

The scenery looked bitterly cold, but thanks to the Dragon Robe, Rei didn't feel particularly chilled. And since Set was a griffin, this level of cold was no big deal for him either.

The real problem was the footing.

The snow made walking slightly awkward.

Even though adventurers frequently use this area, creating a somewhat compacted path, it still wasn't as easy to walk on as a proper road.

"...Maybe I should have ridden on Set."

At Rei's muttered words, Set gave a soft growl.

His growl seemed to say, "You should have done that from the start..."

Even as they walked, the snow continued to fall, accumulating on Lay's Dragon Robe and Set's body.

"Yeah, I think so too. ...Speaking of which, how was yesterday? You spent it with Johanna, right?"

"Grrr!"

At Rei's words, Set purred happily.

After spending the day with Milein, the next day was spent with Johanna.

To Rei, it felt like a tiring schedule, but for Set, who was being doted on, it seemed rather delightful.

Getting plenty of food was probably a big plus for Set too.

After spending such time, Set had come to this river with Rei today to absorb the Silver Lion's magic stone.

"I wonder what kind of power I'll gain..."

"Grrr?"

As Rei stroked Set's head, who tilted his little head, he recalled the battle with the Silver Lion.

The first ability that came to mind was undoubtedly its roar that froze the movements of those who heard it.

Beyond that, there was also the attack method where it transformed its fur into needle-like projectiles and fired them in all directions. It was also possible that it possessed attack methods that purely enhanced physical prowess.

Regardless, whatever abilities it acquired, it was an undeniable fact that they would become a significant combat asset for Set.

Beyond that, there was also the purpose of confirming just how much the terrain manipulation skill—or more accurately, the skill Death Scythe had acquired—had been enhanced.

"Alright, just confirm for me. There's no one around, right?"

"...Grr."

At Rei's words, Set briefly growled as he checked the surroundings.

What they were about to do was the familiar absorption of magic stones for Rei.

But even so, there was a reason he had to be more cautious than usual.

Because the magic stone Set was about to absorb belonged to a Silver Lion.

A Rank S monster's magic stone... its value was beyond question.

Numerous merchants had approached Rei seeking materials from the Silver Lion, but had they known he possessed its magic stone, their advances would have been far more aggressive.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, Ray had claimed the Silver Lion's heart and magic stone had been used in the ritual to restore Vihera's consciousness, and most merchants believed him.

Some merchants did doubt Rei's words, but a little investigation would reveal he'd spent nearly two months acting to save the unconscious Vihera. And when told that dealing with an entity like Ambris, which had appeared only once in ancient times, required the heart and magic stone of an S-rank monster, they had no choice but to accept it.

For that reason, if it became known that Rei currently possessed the Silver Lion's magic stone, it would undoubtedly cause a far greater uproar than anything before.

It would be like when they evacuated outside of Gilm after the Twilight Spear incident—they'd have to flee somewhere else again.

Rei hated the idea of that happening, which was precisely why he had Set thoroughly check the surroundings just to be safe.

"...Alright, then, Set. This is it."

Rei gently offered the Silver Lion Magic Stone, retrieved from the Misty Ring, toward Set.

Normally, he would have tossed it to Set for absorption, but he couldn't bring himself to treat such a precious item as the Silver Lion's magic stone that way.

Set seemed to understand this was different from usual. He gently picked up the Silver Lion Magic Stone resting on Rei's palm with his beak... and swallowed it whole.

[Set has acquired the skill 'King's Intimidation Lv. 3']

The announcement message echoed in his mind.

Even upon hearing it, Rei felt no particular surprise.

Because he had anticipated the skill level of King's Intimidation rising.

If anything about the Silver Lion's attacks was troublesome, it was undoubtedly that roar.

Of course, he couldn't rule out the possibility of learning a new skill called Roar.

But for Rei, he'd preferred the King's Intimidation level to rise, so this outcome was the best possible.

(Well, that attack where it shoots hair needles in all directions was pretty appealing too... but that would probably leave Set without any fur, wouldn't it?)

Since the Silver Lion had used that quill attack multiple times without losing any fur, Rei figured it was probably fine, but it wasn't a certainty.

Suddenly, an image flashed through Rei's mind—Set completely stripped of all feathers and body hair...

"GRRRR!"

"Ow!"

As if instinctively sensing what Rei was imagining, Set gently pecked Rei's hand with his beak.

Of course, Set wasn't genuinely angry, so it didn't draw blood from Rei's hand.

But it still hurt.

「Set……」

"Grrrrr"

Rei shot him a slightly reproachful look, but Set sulkily turned his face away.

"I get it. I'm sorry. Come on, forgive me."

When Rei admitted his fault and gently stroked his body, Set's mood cleared as if he hadn't been sulking just moments before.

"Well then... next is my turn."

After playing with Set for a few minutes—his mood fixed, rubbing his head against Rei demanding more strokes—Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

"Ideally, I'd want to test the King's Intimidation too... but trying it in an empty place is pointless, and if some other adventurer overheard it, that would cause all sorts of problems too."

"Grrr..."

Set let out a disappointed growl at Rei's words.

Truthfully, Set's Royal Intimidation required a roar, making it impossible not to attract attention.

It was winter now, but this forest was still a fairly popular spot for adventurers.

If he carelessly used the King's Intimidation, it could startle adventurers... and ultimately create a fatal opening for those currently battling monsters.

"We'll try it another time... Right, when we encounter a monster."

Saying this, Set let out a cry that sounded slightly disappointed yet resigned.

Running a hand over Set's body, Rei once more focused his consciousness while holding the Death Scythe.

Activating the skill didn't require that much concentration.

...If that were the case, it would be impossible to use carelessly during combat.

But this time, since it was the first use of the leveled-up skill, Rei felt a tension, ready to handle anything that might happen immediately.

"Alright... here goes. Terrain Manipulation!"

Rei shouted as he touched the stone tip of the Death Scythe to the ground.

Simultaneously, the ground a short distance from where Rei and Set stood rose up about a meter, like an elevator or something.

The square platform, roughly fifty centimeters on each side, rose up. If anyone charged toward it, they would undoubtedly collide with the raised mound of earth.

"About one meter, then... So, this side too? Terrain Manipulation."

Muttering with less intensity than before, Rei activated terrain manipulation again.

Then, right next to the ground that had risen about a meter, a hole about a meter deep opened up.

"...This is surprisingly useful."

While only about a meter deep, freely manipulating the ground wouldn't be particularly useful in one-on-one combat.

However, when facing the kind of foes Rei specialized in—small groups like bandits or large ones like armies—it would undoubtedly be extremely effective.

When dozens, hundreds, thousands, tens of thousands... such a group charges straight at you, what happens if the ground suddenly vanishes a meter beneath them, turning into a pitfall?

Lightly armored men falling in, barring exceptionally bad luck, probably wouldn't break their necks.

But what if those falling were men clad in full plate armor, or cavalry?

...It would undoubtedly result in what could be called fatal casualties.

Moreover, many assume that falling into a hole only a meter deep would allow them to climb back out.

But how many people, suddenly finding the ground vanish beneath them on the battlefield, could instantly decide to climb out?

And if that decision is delayed even by a few seconds, they'll be crushed by comrades closing in from behind.

What's more, even if they escape one pitfall, another similar one could be created the very next moment.

"Besides... it's not like they're only useful for combat."

For Rei... well, around Gilm, such jobs aren't particularly common, but there are tasks like flood control projects and expanding towns and villages.

In such situations, having the ability to freely raise or lower the ground even by just a meter would be incredibly useful—no need to even think about it.

"Maybe we shouldn't show this off too much? ...Nah, too late now."

"Grr."

For a moment, Rei wondered if revealing this might cause all sorts of trouble. But fundamentally, Rei wasn't one to hold back.

If Rei did hold back, he wouldn't be an adventurer with a nickname to begin with.

And carrying around Set, a Rank A monster... no, treated as a rare species and effectively Rank S equivalent? There was no self-restraint to speak of.

"Next time there's a request for flood control work or something like that, should we take it?"

"Grrr?"

Sensing something in Rei's words, Set tilted his head and growled softly.

Rei wondered what exactly Set found puzzling, but decided for now that they'd finished everything they needed to do.

"Shall we head back?"

"Grrr!"

Maybe they could go back to Gilm and have something warm to eat.

As Rei prepared to say this, having stowed the Death Scythe into the Misty Ring, Set shook his head for some reason.

"What's wrong? Aren't you coming back? Want to stay here a little longer?"

"Guruu!"

Rei couldn't understand why at all, but for some reason, Set growled that he wanted to stay here a little longer.

"...Still here?"

Thanks to the Dragon Robe, he didn't feel the cold, but even so, having grown up in a snowy region, Rei wasn't particularly fond of seeing snow.

Seeing the snow falling steadily now, his honest desire was to return to the Dusk Wheat inn and relax as soon as possible.

But unlike Rei, who disliked snow, Set—a griffin whose lower body was that of a feline lion, yet possessed a friendly, dog-like personality—never disliked running around and playing in the snow.

Near the stables of the Dusk Wheat inn at dusk... in the backyard, the sight of Set playing in the snow with Milein, Johanna, and various others wasn't all that unusual.

"Just for a little while,"

That's precisely why Rei couldn't resist the silent plea in Set's round eyes and let him roam freely.

Watching Set happily dash about, Rei suddenly had an idea and drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring once more.

He touched the stone tip to the ground...

"Terrain Manipulation"

He activated the Death Scythe's skill, raising the ground right beside him about a meter.

He then spread the cloth he'd taken from Misty Ring over it... and sat down.

"Hmm, it works fine as a regular chair. And considering the size, it could probably double as a makeshift table too. ...Or maybe not a table?"

Muttering, he activated Terrain Manipulation again, forcing the ground upward into a rectangular shape.

Since it was just forcibly raised ground, there was no place to put your feet.

It was clearly a bit awkward to use as a table.

"Well, it's not entirely useless as a makeshift table, I suppose."

He muttered, watching Set happily run around the woods, kicking up light dust with the shoes he'd had prepared at the shoemaker's.
