"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set let out a cry as he flew through the sky.

A figure resembling a harpy was visible quite a distance away, but upon hearing Set's cry, that shadow immediately fled the scene.

Just hearing that cry, it must have realized it could never defeat Set.

...Still, Rei paid no attention whatsoever to the harpy's behavior, stroking the back of Set's neck.

"You're in a good mood."

"Guruu!"

Set responded to Rei's question with a delighted cry.

Set's boundless energy was undoubtedly due to having eaten a hearty breakfast.

The day after they left the village where Shira lived. They were nearing their destination. ...Not the territory of Lerdact, but a nearby domain ruled by the Neutral Faction.

There, they would first gather intelligence on the Lerdact County and consider what retaliatory measures to take.

Even if he wasn't a particularly influential figure in the Noble Faction, he was still a noble.

"Then, it's highly likely they possess some sort of magic item, huh? ...Though going too far probably isn't a good idea."

Lerdact's emissaries had already claimed lives.

Even so, Daskar had forbidden killing Lerdact himself.

Though conversely, that meant anything short of killing him was permissible.

"Guru?"

Rei turned around, looking puzzled, as if asking, What's wrong?

Stroking Set's head, Rei said it was nothing... and soon spotted a town ahead.

Though, calling it a town was an exaggeration—it was minuscule compared to Gilm.

(No, Gilm wasn't really on the scale of a town. Comparing it to that is probably wrong. ...But it's definitely smaller than Abuelo or Saburusta.)

Rei thought this as he looked at the town ahead, but then again, both Abuero and Saburusta were towns that everyone heading to Gilm would inevitably pass through.

Given that, it was only natural they'd be more developed than ordinary towns.

But this place didn't have anything particularly special.

If anything, the vast grasslands meant dairy farming was fairly common, but from what Rei had heard, it wasn't the town itself but the surrounding villages that were the main centers for it.

In other words, the town before Rei's eyes was, for better or worse, best described as ordinary.

Not that there's anything wrong with that.

Even if it's ordinary, it's being run soundly without falling into decline, so that's certainly something to be proud of.

"Besides, there might be something you can't tell just by looking from afar... Let's go, Set. Look forward to some delicious local specialties."

"Gururu!"

Hearing Rei's words, Set must have recalled the river fish hot pot they ate in Zakunya. He let out a cheerful growl and flapped his wings, heading toward the ground.

Noticing Set's departure, the travelers heading toward the town—merchants and adventurers included—looked up and gasped in surprise.

But Rei paid them no mind. He landed Set a short distance from the town and walked straight toward the main gate with him.

Naturally, such a stunt drew considerable attention, and many of those heading into town turned their gaze toward Rei and Set... or more accurately, toward Set.

It was only natural, since they were seeing a griffin for the first time.

Under the gaze of many, Rei addressed the guard checking the town's entrances and exits.

"We'd like to enter the city. Could you handle the formalities for us?"

"Huh? Eh? What? Huh?"

Rei had spoken to the nearest guard, but the guard clearly hadn't expected to be addressed.

Faced with the griffin, Set, he hesitated, unsure how to react, his words stumbling.

Perhaps unable to bear watching his colleague like that, a seasoned guard in his forties stepped forward instead.

"Entry procedures, you say. Understood. Could I see your identification, please?"

"...Yeah."

Rei nodded at the guard's words and pulled out his guild card.

The brief silence that followed was due to two things: the guard didn't refuse the procedure despite Set being present, and the guard's attitude was surprisingly gentle.

Previously, when they had tried to enter towns or villages with Set, the guards, frightened by the griffin, had often refused to let Set enter the town at all... this had happened several times.

In that sense, the guards of this town were undoubtedly worthy of respect for both Rei and Set.

"Grrr? Grrr!"

Set must have understood Rei's feelings. He turned his round eyes toward the guard reaching out to take Rei's guild card and purred affectionately.

Seeing Set like this, the guard was slightly surprised.

A griffin was, after all, a high-rank monster straight out of stories.

That's precisely why he never expected it to display such an adorable gesture.

The guard who had first spoken to Rei had been terrified simply because Set was a griffin, but seeing him now, he was quite shocked by how adorable he looked.

The guard wasn't the only one shocked.

Even the other merchants and adventurers going through the city entry procedures were bewildered by how different Set looked from their image of a griffin, yet they couldn't help but be captivated by its adorable appearance.

"Guru?"

Set tilted his head slightly, asking those around him, "What's wrong?"

And those who saw Set suddenly recognized the warm, endearing, adorable... feelings that had sprung up inside them.

It was true that Set was a griffin, but he was also something of a mascot for Gilm.

This wasn't just for show; seeing Set's true nature, it was only natural that many found him endearing.

...What's more, Set wasn't doing this to provoke such reactions; he was simply being himself.

This was the result of Set's friendly personality being expressed in the most perfect way possible.

"Ah... um, is the paperwork all right?"

"...Huh! Eh, yeah. Yes, it's fine. My apologies, Lord Crimson Rei."

Whether they guessed who Rei was because he was with Set, or understood upon seeing his guild card,

In any case, after completing the entry procedures, Rei received the familiar's necklace from the guard.

Then, glancing briefly at the people still staring at Set, Rei entered the town with him.

Seeing Rei and Set like that, everyone who had been going through the entry procedures wore disappointed expressions.

Honestly, they probably wished they could have seen Set a little longer...

But Rei hadn't come here to show off Set.

Given that, he needed to properly attend to the work he had to do.

"First, the lord's mansion. ...Where is it?"

Though smaller than Gilm, it was still a town.

Not knowing where the lord's mansion was, Rei gathered information while buying food from the surrounding stalls, also looking for local specialties.

Naturally, the stall owners gasped in surprise at the sight of Set, but since Rei was generously buying large quantities, they soon stopped paying attention to Set.

...Also, the fact that Set remained quiet was a big reason the shopkeepers didn't panic.

And seeing the quiet Set, they naturally noticed his adorable charm too... which ultimately led to a repeat of the scene that had played out before the main gate.

As for Set, it quickly became known that Rei was an adventurer from Gilm, a man known by the nickname Crimson.

As a result, neither Rei nor Set were feared anymore.

It was also significant that people understood Rei to be a member of the Neutral Faction, just like Gale, the lord of this town.

Given that situation, finding where the lord's mansion was located wasn't difficult.

(Well, I didn't find any particularly noteworthy dishes, though.)

Grumbling, Rei ate a dish consisting of thinly grilled meat layered between long, hot dog-like buns, feeling slightly dissatisfied.

Not that the town's cuisine was low quality by any means; it was average.

But... still, having recently eaten that delicious river fish hot pot, it was only natural he felt a bit dissatisfied.

"Grr!"

"Ah, here. I'll give you this one."

Set, who was eating the same hot dog-like bread dish as Rei, made a gurgling sound, asking for more.

Rei handed over what he'd been eating to Set, who happily began eating it too.

"You've got it easy, don't you?"

"Gururu?"

Set tilted his head slightly, as if unsure what Rei was saying.

...Though in this case, the hardship referred to the food situation, not the current request.

Anyway, Rei and Set continued on, and soon the castle where the town's lord, Gale, resided came into view.

The manor house Rei had imagined was the one in Gilm, but this building was considerably smaller than what he had pictured.

But then, it was only natural that it would be smaller compared to the manor house in Gilm, which belonged to the central figure of the Neutral Faction, the lord entrusted with the sole frontier of the Mireana Kingdom, and who was also wealthy.

Even so, the gatekeepers at the manor house merely showed a hint of surprise at the sight of Rei and Set, without becoming flustered or raising a fuss.

That was only natural. After all, the two men currently serving as gatekeepers were participating in the war against the Bestir Empire.

They had witnessed scenes there that could truly be called cataclysmic.

Naturally, that alone would have been reason enough for them to fear Rei and Set. Fortunately, however, they had witnessed Rei interacting with the others after the war ended and understood that he was not someone even their own allies feared.

These two were also people Rei had spoken with once back then, though he didn't remember them.

"Um, this is definitely Viscount Gale's manor, right?"

"Yes, that is correct."

"I see. ...Then, please announce us. I have a letter of introduction from Marquis Larks."

"I'll inform them immediately. Please wait just a moment longer."

One of the gatekeepers, holding the letter of introduction handed to him by Rei, headed inside the lord's manor.

Watching him go, the other guard bowed to Rei.

"It's late... truly late, but I am grateful to you, Rei. Thanks to you, I was able to return safely alive even during the war with the Bestir Empire. Thank you."

Hearing the gatekeeper's words, Rei understood why both this man and the previous guard had treated him with such courtesy.

So he was someone who had experienced that war.

"Well, I did it for myself, not for thanks... but if you insist, I'll take your gratitude."

For Rei, it truly was something he'd done for himself. That fire whirlwind too—it had been done out of spite, revenge, payback, gratitude... for those reasons.

Still, if they insisted on expressing gratitude... Rei said this, and the gatekeeper nodded firmly.

"Whatever your reason, there's no doubt we were saved because of it. If it weren't for Rei... likely very few of us would have made it back alive to our homeland. So, regardless of the reason, please accept our thanks."

"...I see. Then I'll take your offer."

"Yes. But why this town? I mean, compared to Gilm, it's got nothing, you know?"

A town with nothing.

Even as he said this, the gatekeeper's demeanor showed no trace of servility.

Even in a town with nothing, his demeanor clearly conveyed his love for the place he called home.

"I've got some business here. ...Ah, don't worry. I'm not planning to cause trouble in this town. At least, I won't start anything myself. ...Right?"

"GRRR!"

Set naturally let out a growl at Rei's words... Seeing Set like that, the gatekeeper narrowed his eyes in a smile.

And so, for several minutes, Rei and the gatekeeper made small talk... until the other gatekeeper who had entered the lord's mansion earlier returned, and the peaceful time passed.
