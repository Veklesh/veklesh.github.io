A barrier field centered around the Dark Elf settlement. Beyond it lies another barrier known as the Barrier of Confusion, and the settlement is protected by the effects of both.

Within this protected barrier, Rei was walking around with Set.

"Alright, Set. It's just us this time, so you can unleash your full power without any worries."

"Guruu!"

"But just like yesterday, there's a chance Polo might barge in, so we need to stay alert about that."

"Gurrrrr!"

At Rei's words, Set purred happily, as if saying, "Leave it to me!"

Yes, currently Rei and Set were operating as one person and one beast.

Marina believed Rei and Set's keen senses might help locate whoever had tampered with the World Tree. Equally important, however, was patrolling the forest—specifically, dealing with any abnormally agitated monsters they encountered.

Especially now that the World Tree had begun weakening again, it was almost certain that the disturbances within the forest would be greater than yesterday's. Thus, Rei and Set, the strongest combatants in the group, were sent into the forest.

Others assigned to the forest included Vihera , who was there because combat seemed imminent, and Earth and Polo, who had originally been commissioned to investigate the forest's anomalies. Judging that this force alone might be insufficient, several skilled Dark Elves were also dispatched.

Among them were individuals like Audban and Jusla, but Rei and Set were operating separately from that group.

Vihera, wanting to enjoy the fighting to her heart's content, was also moving about on her own.

...To be precise, Yellow was accompanying Vihera because it was deemed too dangerous for her to go alone. However, in terms of pure combat strength, Yellow was practically useless.

Yellow's role was primarily as a backup communicator and someone to keep Vihera company.

Elena , who could access Yellow's memories, looked a little worried, but after Vihera promised not to show Yellow anything strange, Yellow ended up accompanying Vihera.

"Since no monsters showed up yesterday after all. Thinking about it that way, we absolutely have to find an Aerowing today."

"Gurururu!"

Set growled happily, pleased that Rei hadn't forgotten his promise to give him the Aerowings' magic stone.

His joy stemmed partly from the magic stone, but also from the prospect of Aero Wing meat.

The Aero Wing meat he'd eaten at the Dark Elf settlement had been incredibly delicious, but the quantity obtained from a single bird wasn't that large. For Set, who was already large and had a voracious appetite, it was far from satisfying.

That's precisely why Set had made it clear today he was absolutely determined to find an Aerowing!

"You're really motivated, Set."

Rei stroked Set's head as they made their way deeper into the forest.

All around them was the forest in its natural state.

There were no well-trodden paths, and it was a very difficult place to walk through normally, but neither Rei nor Set seemed to mind at all as they walked.

"Even if we're searching for monsters... or trying out that skill that failed last time, we can't exactly start messing with the trees growing here."

Since this was the forest where the Dark Elves lived, arbitrarily cutting down or destroying the trees could potentially antagonize them.

They had come all this way to help the Dark Elves; antagonizing them by doing something to anger them would defeat the purpose.

That's precisely why they were searching for things they could cut or destroy without issue—specifically, fallen trees and rocks—as the one person and one beast walked through the forest.

Of course, they hadn't forgotten their primary purpose for exploring this forest: searching for the abnormal monsters.

As an adventurer, Rei was well aware of that necessity.

...Though it was also true he wasn't overly concerned about that, since monsters capable of evading Set's senses weren't exactly common.

"GRRRR!"

Suddenly, Set let out a growl.

It wasn't a tense growl signaling alertness, so Rei didn't seem particularly concerned. He simply turned his gaze in the direction Set was looking... and noticed a tree lying on the ground.

The trunk bore claw-like marks, making it clear this was no natural fall.

"There's a monster around here... or at least there definitely was one."

The fallen tree trunk was thick enough that even a grown man couldn't wrap his arms around it.

A tree that thick seemed impossible for a low-rank monster to destroy.

(Well, not all low-rank monsters are incapable, I suppose.)

Some monsters possessed the ability to deliver astonishingly powerful blows.

It could have been destroyed by such a monster's strike, or perhaps it wasn't a monster at all, but a wild animal that snapped the tree.

"Well, either way, if it comes out, we'll just have to take it down. ...What do you say, Set? Want to try out your skill on that tree?"

"Grr."

At Rei's words, Set shook his head.

Seeing Set like that, Rei was surprised but also understood.

Originally, Set's strike combined the griffin's innate physical prowess with the magic item, the Bracelet of Might. Even in its base state, breaking a tree was easily within its capabilities.

Therefore, he likely felt that a tree like the one before them now wasn't suitable for testing the power of Power Crush, which had risen two levels.

Understanding this, Rei pulled out his Death Scythe.

It was a tree with a trunk so thick that even an adult would struggle to embrace it alone. If that was the case, it seemed perfect for testing the power of Flying Slash.

Trees grew all around them, but there was still enough space to swing the Death Scythe.

Within that space, Rei confirmed Set had stepped back slightly, moving away from where the Death Scythe would swing, before he swung it.

"Flying Slash!"

The slash released with that word headed straight for the fallen tree.

With a sharp "slicing" sound, the tree trunk—too thick for even an adult to lift—was cleaved clean in two.

"...Huh?"

A somewhat dazed voice escaped Rei's lips.

Even though he'd unleashed it himself, he hadn't expected that strike to pack such devastating power.

Of course. His previous Flying Slash had decent speed and range, but its power was equally mediocre.

At the very least, it never possessed the power to sever a tree trunk over a meter thick—one an adult couldn't lift.

(Because I leveled up? ...No, but I've leveled up from one to two, two to three, three to four like that before. There's never been such an extreme jump in power before?)

Completely shaken, Rei's gaze darted repeatedly between his own arm, the Death Scythe, and the severed tree trunk lying some distance away.

"Guruu!"

Set growled a "Congratulations!" to the flustered Rei.

Seeing Set rubbing his head against him in celebration, Rei finally began to recover from his confusion.

Reaching up to stroke Set's head, he pondered what that last strike had meant.

(Could that have been a fluke? Like a critical hit you see in games or something?)

Unable to believe the power of the Flying Slash he had just unleashed, Rei's gaze turned to the Death Scythe.

After pondering for a moment... he decided it would be quicker to actually try it again rather than keep thinking, and readied the Death Scythe once more.

"Flying Slash!"

The flying slash shot forth again, flying straight like before... eventually striking one half of the split tree trunk, cleaving it cleanly in two once more.

"GRRRR!"

Set let out a cry of delight at Rei's strike once more.

Hearing that sound, Rei began walking toward the fallen tree trunk severed by his own skill.

"Guru?"

Set seemed to ask, "What's wrong?", but said nothing more and followed Rei.

Arriving beneath the fallen tree, Rei stared intently at the freshly severed stump he had just created.

There was no mistaking it—the tree had been cut cleanly by a sharp blade.

(So... what does this mean? The extreme increase in Flying Slash's power... some reason... Level Five, maybe?)

Among the skills Death Scythe currently possessed, the highest level was, unsurprisingly, the Flying Slash he'd just experienced.

Its level was five—a level that often marked a significant milestone in games or similar contexts.

(Games? ...Right! Takumu!?)

Takumu Suzunose, who used magic called Numerology within the Zepairu clan. His true identity was that of someone born on Earth, just like Rei.

Unlike Rei, who came here as just a soul, Takumu had arrived in Elgin with his physical body intact, as evidenced by the student uniform he wore.

Therefore, since he was undoubtedly familiar with games of that sort, manipulating the spell formula of Beast Magic to become powerful at Level Five probably wasn't that difficult for him.

(If that's true, the next skills I'll likely reach Level 5 with are... Wind Hand and Corrosion at Level 3. As for Set, his Power Crush hit Level 4 with this incident, so he's almost at Level 5. Plus, he has quite a few other Level 3 skills. Guess I'll have to wait until other skills actually hit Level 5 before deciding.)

What's wrong? Set looked at him with round eyes. Rei shook his head, signaling it was nothing, and gently stroked his head.

"Whatever the reason, it's undeniable that Flying Slash has become more powerful than before. So for now, I guess I should just be happy about it. ...Well, I'm done checking my skills. Pain Burst is impossible to test anyway."

Pain Burst, which inflicts greater pain on the opponent, isn't a skill whose power you can understand just by seeing it like Flying Slash.

It's a skill whose true power can only be confirmed by actually using it on someone.

Therefore, naturally, I couldn't use it on Set. My only options were to use it on a monster that appeared or perhaps on some wild animal.

"For now, I need to find someone to test the power of Set's Power Crush."

"Guruu!"

Putting Pain Burst aside for now, Rei continued walking with Set, scanning their surroundings.

But, just like the day before, no monsters appeared.

(I hope Polo doesn't come to play again...)

As he pondered this, the sound of nearby bushes rustling suddenly made Rei and Set freeze.

Could it be a monster? They watched the bushes warily, yet also wondering if it might actually be Polo...

"Kon!"

A fox emerged from the bushes, letting out that cry... but upon noticing the griffin, Set, in its line of sight, it turned on its heel and fled.

"Grrr..."

Set let out a disappointed growl. Was it because he wanted to play with the fox, or because he saw it as prey?

In any case, after watching the fox disappear, Set suddenly shifted his gaze to the other side of the bushes... and spotted a large rock there.

"Guruu!"

The giant boulder, about four meters tall and five meters wide, was so enormous it seemed strange to find it in this forest.

(A rock in the forest? Well, I suppose it's not impossible... Still, it just feels... yeah, odd. Or am I just imagining things?)

Set gave Rei, who was tilting his head, a look full of expectation.

Rei, who understood what that look meant, thought for a moment and nodded.

"Alright. Well, a rock that big should be a suitable target for Power Crush. Just be careful, though. Like my Power Slash, it basically unleashes a powerful strike, so the strain on your body is considerable. ...Well, I guess I don't need to worry about you, Set."

For Set, a Griffin with a sturdy physique, even a blow of that caliber would likely cause little damage.

At Rei's words, Set growled in understanding, kicked off the ground, and sprinted toward the rock.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRGRRRR!"

The sound that erupted from Set's mouth could easily be described as a roar.

He leapt, immediately activating Power Crush, and slammed his front paw into the rock.

The blow unleashed by Set produced a result that could only be called overwhelmingly powerful.

As if crushing tofu, it easily shattered the rock... a rock easily twice Set's size.
