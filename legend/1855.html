Even in Anesis, the second-largest city of the Kingdom of Mireana, it naturally grows dark at night.

At the residence of Duke Kerebel, who ruled over Anesis, there was certainly no shortage of light, but such places were few and far between.

Even in Anesis, it was only natural that most of the city would be shrouded in darkness once night fell.

Through that darkness, the Black Wolf runs.

True to his name, the Black Wolf, the way he runs while blending into the darkness would convince anyone that he is a top-tier assassin.

Running along the streets, across rooftops, and even along walls when necessary, the Black Wolf heads toward his destination.

...And yet, the fact that he holds a meat bun given to him by Rei in his hand... it wouldn’t be wrong to say that this, too, was typical of the Black Wolf.

And yet, despite running across rooftops and walls, he showed no sign of being noticed by anyone inside the house.

Indeed, even if the people inside were sleeping, he didn’t seem to wake them; nor did he seem to notice them at all, whether they were working on something or even pretending to drink alcohol.

He even encountered a guard patrolling the streets along the way, but the guard was unable to spot Black Wolf.

Because he was an assassin who lived in the night and the darkness, the other residents—those living in the world of daylight—showed no sign of noticing the Black Wolf at all.

And so, the Black Wolf, who had been running through the darkness of the night, finally slowed his pace.

That said, he was undoubtedly still moving at a considerable speed; an ordinary person would likely be unable to spot the Black Wolf even if they saw him.

“……”

Silently scanning his surroundings, the Black Wolf eventually gazed longingly at the steamed bun in his hand before bringing it to his mouth.

The Black Wolf is currently on the rooftop of a building—one of the taller ones—in the slums.

It was the kind of place where, under normal circumstances, one wouldn’t even think of climbing up, even if it weren’t nighttime.

The building itself is old—so old, in fact, that it wouldn’t be surprising if it collapsed at any moment.

To remain so calm in such a situation—and not only that, but to be able to enjoy the night view, where one can see the moon even as snow falls—is something only Black Wolf could do.

If Rei were here, it wouldn’t be surprising if he complained, “Did you go out of your way to disappear just to watch the moon?!”

Amidst that nighttime scenery, despite the falling snow, he brings a steamed bun to his mouth without showing the slightest sign of feeling cold.

Since he’d escaped from Rei’s room and run through the winter night, the meat bun was naturally already cold.

But Kuroou slowly brings the bun to his mouth, eating it as if savoring every bite.

Naturally, a cold meat bun isn’t all that tasty.

In fact, some people would even go so far as to say they’re downright bad.

Even so, the Black Wolf continued to eat the bun in his hand as if it were a precious treasure.

No matter how carefully he ate it, it would inevitably run out eventually.

The Black Wolf gazed sadly at the meat bun shrinking in his hand, yet his hand never stopped eating as he continued to devour it… Eventually, when the last bite entered the Black Wolf’s mouth, he wore a sorrowful expression unlike any he had ever shown in front of Rei.

For a few minutes, Black Wolf licked his empty hand with a look of regret… as if reluctant to let it go, then leaped away from the spot.

The place where the Black Wolf had been standing was at a height where, for an ordinary human, jumping down would mean certain death.

Despite this, the black wolf leaped without showing even a hint of fear and landed on the ground without making a sound.

The moment he hit the ground, he bent his knees, completely absorbing the impact.

Although this maneuver requires immense skill, for the Black Wolf, it wasn’t particularly difficult—it was something he could do while humming a tune.

Having landed while performing a feat that could almost be called a waste of skill, the Black Wolf immediately began running again.

His destination was a special place even within the slums.

As Black Wolf raced through the darkness of the night, what eventually came into view was an ordinary single-family home—the kind you wouldn’t normally expect to find in the slums.

And yet, there were no guards in sight… likely because anyone in the neighborhood knows exactly who lives in this house.

No matter how much of a slum dweller one might be, they would absolutely never dare to break into this house.

They know that doing so would mean the end of their very lives.

The Black Wolf entered the house without the slightest hesitation… with the ease of someone entering their own home.

“Oh, welcome.”

Beyond the open door, a butler—who seemed to have been waiting as if he knew the Black Wolf was coming—bowed.

The man, who bowed with an elegance befitting a butler in a nobleman’s mansion rather than an ordinary house, suddenly noticed the scent wafting from Black Wolf. Though his expression remained unchanged, he was slightly startled.

For the scent wafting from the Black Wolf was unmistakably that of food… the aroma of a meal.

For the Black Wolf—who was highly wary and rarely ate even when invited by the master of the house himself—this was quite unusual.

Nevertheless, the butler said nothing further and simply led him inside.

The Duke of Kerebel’s residence… far from it; even compared to other noble mansions, this house was small.

If asked whether a butler’s guidance was necessary in such a house, it wouldn’t be strange to have doubts under normal circumstances.

But in this house’s case, it’s a different story.

It is common knowledge among those in the vicinity who lives here.

If that is the case, it follows that there are those who come here with the intent to take the life of the person living here.

Since someone with immense influence in the underworld of Anesis lives here, anyone who has come to Anesis from other towns or cities—let alone someone hot-headed—might just do something they absolutely shouldn’t do while in Anesis.

For that reason, there is a place here where this butler and several others… must act together.

A place where, if you act without knowing anything, there’s no guarantee that a life-threatening trap won’t be triggered.

(Then again, he is a man known as the Black Wolf. It wouldn’t be surprising if he could easily avoid any traps set in this house, even after they’ve been triggered.)

Keeping his eyes on the Black Wolf, the butler walks down the hallway toward his destination—the room where the master of the house resides.

Since the house isn’t particularly large to begin with, they arrive at the room in just a few dozen seconds and knock on the door.

“Excuse me, Master. Lord Black Wolf is here.”

“Let him in.”

Hearing the voice from inside the room, the butler opens the door.

That said, only Black Wolf entered the room; the butler simply stepped back and left.

Under normal circumstances, such behavior could easily be considered rude—both toward his master and toward Black Wolf, who had come as a guest.

However, this household had its own set of rules, and it was only natural to follow them.

Since the Black Wolf had been here many times before, he didn’t think anything of the exchange and simply entered the room.

The furniture in the hallway leading up to this point was all of the highest quality.

However, the furniture inside this room was clearly of a much higher caliber than any of those.

In fact, if even a single piece of this furniture were sold, it would be enough for an ordinary family to live comfortably for decades… or, if they lived frugally, perhaps even for a lifetime.

Above all, the fact that such high-end furniture was being used so casually spoke volumes about the status of the people living in this house.

In reality, someone who seems not to give it a second thought—someone who would find the very notion of the furniture’s value ridiculous—would have no trouble using the furniture in this room as if it were ordinary.

But the people living in this house use all this furniture fully aware of its worth.

...Though, to be honest, Black Wolf had absolutely no idea what that furniture was worth.

“Good to see you, Black Wolf.”

The man who spoke to him was in his mid-thirties.

For a man who owns a house like this and stands at the pinnacle of Anesis’s underworld, one might say he’s a bit young.

But there was no doubt that this man had used every means at his disposal to rise through the underworld of Anesis and reach the pinnacle.

Of course, Black Wolf had also cooperated in that fierce yet quiet power struggle.

Black Wolf was, after all, a freelance assassin—not this man’s subordinate.

Still, there was no doubt that he was the one who had looked after Black Wolf, and there was also no doubt that he was the person Black Wolf trusted most in Anesis.

“Hmm? You seem different from usual… I wonder what’s going on?”

Even to an outside observer, it would be impossible to tell the difference between Black Wolf now and his usual self.

The only reason he could tell was because he had spent so much time with Black Wolf.

Black Wolf had nothing to say to such a man; he simply curled up on the sofa a short distance from the desk where the man was organizing papers.

This sofa, too, was naturally an extremely expensive piece of furniture… but to Black Wolf, that probably didn’t matter.

“Really…”

Saying this, the man turned his gaze toward Kuroou, who was sleeping on the sofa.

There, sitting there, was not the man who ruled the underworld of Anesis... but a man wearing an expression that seemed to suggest he was worried about his family.

To the man… to Celestine, Black Wolf was both a pawn and a comrade, and he harbored feelings for him that were almost like those of a family member.

If any of Celestain’s subordinates had been present, they would have been surprised by the way he was looking at Black Wolf right now.

After all, he looked nothing like the Celestain they were used to.

(Still...)

Celestine muttered quietly as he turned his gaze back to the documents.

Black Wolf was, after all, somehow different today.

It was a feeling born of having known the Black Wolf for so long.

(At least, it doesn’t seem like anything bad has happened… Should I just leave him be?)

As an assassin, the Black Wolf was naturally sensitive to others’ ill will.

...For Celestine, seeing the Black Wolf sleeping so defenselessly right in front of her was a deeply heartwarming sight.

(In any case, it’s a fact that something has happened to Black Wolf. If I had to guess, it might be Crimson.)

The current assignment Black Wolf is on is to assassinate Crimson.

Of course, the man who placed that contract would never show his face in public.

In any case, if something were to happen while Black Wolf was on the job, the only possible culprit could be the target himself, Rei.

Normally, with the Black Wolf’s abilities, carrying out an assassination would be no trouble at all.

The fact that even Black Wolf is struggling is proof that Crimson’s nickname isn’t just for show.

“Even so… even if the client is no longer around, there’s no doubt that Black Wolf accepted this assignment. As long as that’s the case, there’s no option to abandon this mission.”

As he spoke, Celestan shook his head.

In reality, since Gaiska—the person who commissioned this job—couldn’t pay the remaining reward, there would be no problem in canceling the assignment right now.

However, if he were to do such a thing, some might come to believe that the Black Wolf, the assassin, had failed the mission.

Given Celestan’s standing, he didn’t need to worry about such nonsense, and Black Wolf wasn’t the type to care about others’ opinions either.

But still… even so, for Celestine, who knew Black Wolf personally, seeing his reputation tarnished was unwelcome, and something she would prefer to avoid if possible.

That is precisely why, even though he understands it’s a bit of a stretch, he has Black Wolf targeting Rei.

It’s not that Rei is a troublesome presence for Celestine…

To begin with, Celestane naturally knows that Rei will disappear from Anesis in just a few days.

If that were the case, there would be no need for Black Wolf to go out of his way to intervene here.

Even knowing that, Celestane had no intention of canceling the job for Black Wolf.

(Was his name Gaiska? There’s no doubt that man’s blood is worth that much. …I suppose an ancient bloodline holds more value than the individual’s own abilities.)

For just a moment, Celestane wondered how Gaiska was faring underground, but he quickly pushed the thought aside and turned his gaze toward the Black Wolf, who was sleeping peacefully.
