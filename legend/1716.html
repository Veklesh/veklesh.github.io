"Hmm... Well, I guess that's about it."

Saying this with a satisfied look, Rei strokes the head of Set, who is sitting next to him.

Set, whose head was being stroked by his beloved Rei, closed his eyes in bliss.

His cheerful mood is partly due to being stroked by Rei, but it’s likely also because he absorbed two magic stones, causing two of his skills to level up.

"Tornado" has reached Level 3, allowing him to create a tornado about 5 meters high.

Multi-Slash has reached Level 2, increasing the number of additional slashes from one to two per strike.

In other words, the Death Scythe’s original attack is followed by two additional slashes at the same time.

This is evident from the three deep gashes on the trunk of a nearby tree.

These gashes were carved by Multi-Slash, which I unleashed without putting much force into it just to confirm the skill’s effect.

……To be honest, I did consider unleashing Multi-Slash with full force, but the fact remains that this forest is a dungeon.

Given that, causing a big commotion here would undoubtedly attract monsters.

For Rei, who had already prepared camp and mentally shifted gears to call it a day, fighting goblins in the current situation was nothing but a hassle.

That said, a tornado about five meters tall would undoubtedly have been quite a sight.

In any case, having finished checking his skills, Rei stroked Set’s head with a satisfied expression and looked up at the sky—or rather, the ceiling.

There, for some reason, the moon and stars existed alongside the sun.

Gazing at a scene that was simply impossible to believe was inside a dungeon, Rei thought about the skills he had acquired—or, more accurately, leveled up.

Both Tornado and Multi-Slash are extremely useful skills.

Since the Tornado is used when Rei employs his secret weapon, the Fire Whirlwind, the greater the Tornado’s power, the greater the Fire Whirlwind’s power becomes.

That said, since Rei uses his Wind Hand to expand the scale of the Fire Whirlwind, the final power itself doesn’t change much from his previous level; it might be more accurate to say that the time it takes to generate the Fire Whirlwind is reduced.

And then there’s Multi-Slash.

This is extremely valuable to Rei in that it directly increases his attack power.

After all, a single attack yields the same result as three separate attacks.

(Well, having the additional slashes land right next to where I struck is a bit monotonous. If possible, it would have been easier to use if I could direct the slashes to exactly where I wanted them to go.)

That’s what Rei thought, but he immediately shook his head to dismiss the thought.

Right now, there are only two additional slashes.

However, if the Multi-Slash skill continues to level up, eventually a single slash could yield ten... no, considering how the skill dramatically powers up at Level 5, that number might increase even further.

If asked whether Rei could freely control all those additional slashes, he would likely shake his head.

That said, if it came to that, she would likely learn how to control the slashes.

“Well, I guess it’s about time we headed back. They’re probably getting ready for bed over there by now.”

“Grr…”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a slightly disappointed growl.

To be honest, Set probably wanted to play with Rei a little longer if possible.

Rei understood that, but since they were currently in a dungeon, he couldn’t afford to let his guard down.

He was confident he could handle any monster attack, but unless the danger served a purpose, Rei had no interest in taking unnecessary risks.

After convincing Set of this, he accepted it, though he looked a little sad.

(I really need to make time to play with Set properly next time.)

Lately, even when he did play with Set, the others were usually there too.

Of course, Set didn’t dislike that, but still, he couldn’t help but hope that, if possible, he could play alone with Rei.

For that reason, Set has been acting more clingy toward Rei lately.

……Seeing this, the others find Set even more endearing, and quite a few of them are jealous of Rei, but as the one responsible for Set, I suppose I just have to accept that.

With that in mind, Rei rode back to the camp on Set.

“Hm? Oh, you’re back.”

Elena, sensing that Rei and the others had arrived, emerged from the forest and smiled upon seeing Rei and Set.

Rei waved back in response, but Elena’s figure, illuminated by the campfire, seemed almost ethereal, thanks to her natural beauty.

That said, Elena wasn’t the only one possessing such ethereal beauty.

Though in a different way, Marina and Vihera, who possessed beauty equal to Elena’s, were similarly displaying an ethereal beauty to those around them.

Marina, in particular, stands out in terms of ethereal beauty.

Marina, who usually unconsciously exudes feminine allure to those around her, could be said to be displaying her femininity even more strongly now.

That said, even in the presence of such a Marina, Relieu showed no particular sign of being drawn to her.

He is simply devoted to his wife; it seems he does not recognize any woman other than his wife, Shumine.

That is likely why Marina and the others feel reassured that they won’t be subjected to any strange advances.

(Bistol was with us at sea, and Relieu is with us in this dungeon. In that sense, there’s no one around to make inappropriate advances toward Elena and the others, so it’s definitely easier to move about freely.)

Being captivated by her beauty and acting inappropriately... that’s perfectly normal behavior for an ordinary man.

However, as far as Bistol and Relieu are concerned, Rei concludes that there’s no need to worry about such behavior, for their own respective reasons.

Rei felt anew just how much easier life was when he had someone he could camp with without worry.

(Well, I’d rather Relieu hadn’t come along if possible… though that’s just me. Still, with Byune here, I can’t openly absorb magic stones anyway, so in that sense, nothing really changes, does it?)

Watching Byune pet Yellow, Rei sat down by the campfire.

Since he was wearing a Dragon Robe with a function similar to a portable air conditioner, it wasn’t cold even at night during this season, nor was it hot sitting by the campfire.

Still, he finds himself naturally gathering around the campfire with everyone like this.

“So, I hear we’re camping inside the dungeon tonight. Are we going to stay cooped up in here until we clear the dungeon?”

Relieu asks Rei while sipping her tea, though there’s no sign of displeasure in her tone.

At every meal, she gets to eat freshly prepared dishes made by a professional chef and freshly baked bread, and when it comes to sleeping, there’s a magic tent that feels just like a regular room inside… no, depending on the situation, it might even be more comfortable than a local inn.

On top of that, the water produced by the Dagger of Flowing Water tastes as delicious as heavenly nectar, and above all, as long as he’s traveling with Rei and the others, he can be with Set.

If there was any dissatisfaction with traveling with Rei and the others, it was the absence of his wife, Shumine.

Other than that, I was living such a comfortable life that I felt I could stay with Rei and the others indefinitely without any issues.

……Though, that one complaint is the one I find hardest to bear.

“That’s how it’ll be. I went back once yesterday to report on the situation inside the dungeon, but from now on, unless something extraordinary happens, I don’t intend to return to Golz. …Well, I am a bit curious about what that rock plant is, but I can’t exactly investigate it right away.”

The guild has agreed to look into it, but I have no idea when we’ll find out.

That said, judging by the impression Rei got from yesterday’s meeting, it looks like it’ll be quite a while.

“I see.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Relieu murmured, sounding disappointed as expected.

He must be thinking about how much he wants to see his wife, Shumine, again soon.

“If we clear the dungeon, we can go back to Gilm… and even in the worst-case scenario, we’ll be back in Gilm before winter, right? So, a month… no, maybe two months? No matter how long it takes, it won’t be three months…”

“Nooooooo! Th-three months… Are you telling me I won’t be able to see Shumine for three whole months!?”

At Marina’s words, Relieu lets out a cry of despair.

Hearing that cry, the others turned their gaze toward him… but once they realized it was Relieu making the noise, they immediately went back to what they were doing.

Though, to be honest, there wasn’t really anything specific they needed to do in the current situation.

Amidst all this... only Vihera was experimenting with various things a short distance away from the magic tent.

She kicks off the ground to move forward, but perhaps unsatisfied with the result, she tilts her head and kicks off again.

She repeats this action over and over, tilting her head each time.

It’s obvious that Vihera’s attempts aren’t going well. They aren’t… yet, there’s no trace of dissatisfaction, frustration, or anger on Vihera’s face.

If anything, she even has a smile on her face, enjoying her actions—even the ones that end in failure.

“The flow of magic just… doesn’t seem to fit right, you know?”

Saying that, she kicks the ground again... and boom. A sound that could easily be mistaken for an explosion echoes through the area.

Naturally, the sound reached Rei and the others standing near the Magic Tent, and everyone came over to see what it was.

“Vihera, what on earth was that sound?”

Upon arriving at the source of the noise and seeing that Vihera didn’t appear to be injured, Marina asked with a sigh of relief.

She had assumed that a monster had attacked, hiding in the darkness of the night.

However, there were no signs of a monster at the source of the sound, and given the situation, it was obvious without even thinking who had caused the noise.

“Oh, sorry. I was just trying out a new skill. I feel like I’m almost there.”

Marina looked at Vihera, who was saying this with a carefree expression, with a look of exasperation.

In the middle of the forest at night—a place where it wouldn’t be strange at all to be attacked by a monster—making such a loud noise… a sound almost like an explosion… there was no guarantee that a monster intrigued by it wouldn’t come closer.

That said, when it came to attracting monsters’ attention, the bonfire Rei and the others were keeping going was probably just as effective.

“Be careful. I know Vihera is strong, but this is a dungeon. There’s no guarantee some unknown monster we’ve never seen before won’t show up.”

“If that’s the case, I don’t really mind. I’m actually looking forward to fighting a strong opponent.”

“Listen…”

“I know. I was just joking. I think I’ll stop practicing my skills for now.”

Although Vihera said it was a joke, the fact that she’s looking forward to fighting an unknown monster isn’t a joke at all—she’s serious.

If you spend time with Vihera, it’s easy to understand that much.

At the same time, though, you’d also come to understand just how strong Vihera really is.

(Vihera says she wants to fight strong opponents… but by training like this and getting stronger… naturally, she’ll end up stronger than the opponents she used to be on par with, and the stronger she gets, won’t there be fewer opponents left for her to fight?)

That’s what Marina thought, but Vihera is the kind of person who simply can’t stop pursuing her own strength.

“Well, I guess we should head back. Starting tomorrow, we’ll have to explore this forest again and look for the stairs to the third floor.”

Vihera said this without seeming to care at all about Marina’s thoughts, and started walking toward the Magic Tent.

Following Vihera’s lead, the others also headed back to the magic tent.

“You’ve got your hands full, too. Your party members are all so unique.”

On the way back, Relieu said this to Rei.

In fact, there was no doubt that every member of the Crimson Wings had their quirks, to a greater or lesser extent.

……Then again, adventurers are a collection of all sorts of people.

Naturally, there are plenty of eccentrics among them.

"That might be true. But... I truly believe the Crimson Wings is the best party there is."

Saying that, Rei flashed a beaming, confident smile.
