"I did it!"

“Ah… I see.”

Hearing the woman who had been applying makeup to Rei exclaim happily, Rei replied briefly.

Though she called it makeup, it wasn’t anything too elaborate—just a light application of cosmetics to her face.

...Honestly, Rei couldn't understand why the simple act of applying makeup took over an hour. He couldn't understand it, but he kept his complaints to himself, figuring it must be part of a professional's work.

“Look, take a look. Isn’t it wonderful?”

Saying that, the woman held up a mirror for Rei to see.

Mirrors are terribly expensive items, but this is the mansion of Duke Kerebel, who leads the Noble Faction—one of the three major factions within the Kingdom of Mireana.

Naturally, mirrors that would normally be too expensive for anyone to afford are, of course, present here.

Rei looked at his own face reflected in that mirror, but...

“Hmm… Has anything changed?”

“It has!”

At the words that came out of Rei’s mouth, the woman shouted reflexively.

Normally, she wouldn’t dare shout like that at someone with a nickname.

The only reason she could do it was because her painstaking work had been mocked.

To be precise, Rei hadn’t actually mocked her; she’d merely muttered something to the effect that she didn’t understand.

In fact, looking at her own face in the mirror, she didn’t think it looked all that different from an hour ago.

Seeing Rei’s expression, the woman wore a gloomy look.

“Well, I can’t say I don’t understand why you’d feel that way. Actually, your skin is in pretty good condition, so you probably wouldn’t have had any problems even without makeup. …But, look. If you shine a little light on it like this, can’t you tell?”

Hearing that, Rei moved closer to the window and looked at her face in the mirror as the light hit it. It looked slightly—really, just slightly—different from how it had appeared in the mirror a moment ago.

Rei’s face had always been feminine, with features that were more dignified than fierce.

It was just a subtle difference in nuance, but that was how Rei perceived her own face.

But now, looking at her face in the mirror with the light shining on it, she seemed somehow more masculine than usual.

It wasn’t a drastic change, but there was no doubt that was how it appeared.

That said, even from Rei’s perspective, he had a feeling it might just be within the margin of error.

After all, the person who had applied the makeup was saying so...

(They say illness is all in the mind... or maybe that’s not quite right in this case?)

Thinking that, I decided to just convince myself that my face really was looking more masculine than usual, and turned my gaze toward Miranda, who had been assigned to look after me.

“So, what are we going to do now? I’m done with the makeup, at least.”

Rei didn’t notice—though it was only slightly, just a tiny bit—that there was a hint of satisfaction in her tone, but the others in the room had, of course, noticed it.

Still, the reason they didn’t say anything to Rei was partly out of kindness, but also because, as professionals, they knew that makeup could alter one’s mental state.

“Well, I think it’s still a little early to change into my party dress… so as for party etiquette… it doesn’t seem to be your cup of tea.”

She must have noticed that the word “etiquette” made Rei’s expression turn slightly displeased. Miranda considered what to do, but in the end, she gave up on teaching him etiquette.

One reason for this was likely that, even if she were to study it, it would only be a superficial, last-minute cram session. She figured it would be better for Rei if she didn’t force him into such a situation and instead let him behave like an adventurer.

Normally, Miranda wouldn’t need to worry about Rei that much.

Still, since she had been assigned to Rei and ended up spending a lot of time with him, Miranda had grown somewhat attached to him.

……It’s not entirely untrue that she also felt indebted to him for the large sum of money she received from the mock battle.

That said, the feelings Miranda harbors for Rei are strictly those of a friend, not romantic ones.

(Leodanis should know that, though.)

Miranda thought of Leodanis, who was jealous of the way she treated Rei, but the thought quickly subsided.

She decided that thinking about Rei was more important right now.

“Well then, shall I tell you who’s coming to the party today?”

“Uh… is it really necessary for me to know that? If possible, I’d rather not be told.”

“I understand why Rei might not like it, but at today’s party, in addition to the nobles from the Noble Faction… there will also be a few nobles from the Neutral Faction and the Royal Faction attending.”

“Is that so?”

Those words seemed to surprise Rei as well; he gave her a look that seemed to say, “Really?”

Since it was a party hosted by Liberte, the leader of the Noble Faction, he had naturally assumed that everyone attending would be a noble from the Noble Faction.

“Yes. That said, since the main purpose is to observe what the Noble Faction is up to, it doesn’t seem like they’ll be very active at the party.”

“So the Neutral Faction is coming too? In that case, setting aside the Royal Faction, I suppose I should make an appearance and greet the Neutral Faction nobles.”

“That’s true. Lately, the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction have been building friendly relations.”

Miranda said this while looking meaningfully at Rei.

Though she said nothing, her gaze clearly revealed that it was Rei who had fostered that friendly atmosphere.

Faced with such a look, Rei had no choice but to nod in agreement.

He didn’t want to use his own judgment to steer things in a strange direction, especially since the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction were finally getting along.

That said, even within the Noble Faction, there are those who believe it’s fine to get along with the Neutral Faction, and conversely, there are those who absolutely refuse to ally with them.

The same is true among the Neutral Factions, and as for the Royal Faction, they would never tolerate a faction hostile to them being on friendly terms with the Neutral Factions.

……That said, since today’s party is strictly for the Noble Faction, it’s highly unlikely that the Royal Faction would dare to make any overt moves.

“Got it. I don’t have anything to do until the party starts, so I’ll follow your instructions.”

As far as Rei was concerned, there was nothing he needed to do at the moment.

Now that he’s been made up and had his hair styled, it would be unthinkable to run around in that state, let alone engage in combat training.

So, deciding that this would be a good opportunity to learn about the nobility, he agreed to Miranda’s suggestion.

That said, since studying manners and such was a hassle for him, it wouldn’t be wrong to say he was simply compromising on that front.

“Hehe, I’d have liked to watch the two of you interact a little longer, but unfortunately, it looks like that’s not going to happen.”

The woman who had applied makeup to Rei murmured this as she looked toward the door.

There stood a man whom even Rei didn’t recognize, but if one understood what kind of room this was, it was obvious without even thinking why he had come here.

And since the woman doing Rei’s makeup is a professional, it goes without saying that she can’t just focus on Rei alone in a situation like this.

No, there are plenty of other people she needs to do makeup for, but the fact that she doesn’t cut corners even then is precisely because she’s a professional.

Rei and Miranda decided that since their makeup was finished, there was no need for them to stay any longer, so they simply left the room.

...As they did so, the man who had just entered the room clearly looked surprised to pass by Rei, but Rei and Miranda didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

(I’d like to pop into the kitchen, but they must be busy preparing food for the party.)

I wasn’t entirely without the urge to ask about the progress on the ramen, but today is one of the busiest days of the year.

Georgima and her subordinates must be busy cooking right about now.

(Elena and Ara must be busy too, of course.)

As a Princess General and the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, Elena had to handle party preparations and meet with guests who had arrived early to greet her.

As the commander of Elena’s guard, Ara must remain by her side.

Both of them are busy, so there’s no way they could spare the time to deal with Rei.

Well, to be precise, if Rei went to see them, they would undoubtedly spend time with him, but that would undoubtedly be a burden for Elena and Ara.

Given that, Rei doesn’t want to go out of his way to bother them either.

(Come to think of it, the reason I’m not busy like this is because I’m not a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s household—I’m strictly a guest. Well, even if they told me to entertain the nobles or decorate for the party, I couldn’t do it anyway.)

As I walked down the hallway thinking that, I passed by a few people who looked like nobles.

The moment they saw Rei, their eyes widened.

Although Rei was slightly puzzled by their reaction, he didn’t actually recognize their faces.

(Wait, haven’t I seen them somewhere…?)

He didn’t know their names for sure, but feeling as though he’d seen their faces somewhere before, Rei immediately remembered.

“Were they the nobles sitting in the noble section during the mock battle?”

But why were they so surprised to see him... or rather, why were they looking at him with such fear? Rei couldn’t understand.

...But that was only natural. After all, Rei had no direct connection to these nobles.

The nobles whose eyes met Rei’s hurriedly left the area.

“What was that?”

“I don’t know.”

At Rei’s words, Miranda tilted her head in puzzlement.

Rei had no idea, but those nobles were the very ones who had watched Set during the mock battle and were plotting to somehow secure him for themselves.

However, before any of them could take action, Count Caseres had already made his move, resulting in the Noble Faction being expelled.

It was precisely because they understood this that the nobles had hurriedly left Rei’s presence.

The expulsion of Count Caseres had come as a major shock to those who had been harboring such treacherous intentions.

If the Count of Cáceres had not acted, and they had taken action instead...

they would inevitably conclude that it would have been they, without a doubt, who would have been expelled from the Noble Faction.

If the Noble Faction were expelled, nothing but ruin awaited them.

Which would Duke Kerebel prioritize: the Noble Faction—which included them—or the guest, who, despite his nickname, was ultimately just an adventurer?

These nobles had believed the Duke would prioritize them unconditionally... but having witnessed Count Caseres—who held a higher status and greater power than they did—being so easily cast aside, they had absolutely no guarantee that Kerebel would not do the same to them.

No, in fact, since they ranked lower within the Noble Faction than Count Caseres, they concluded that they would naturally be cast aside as well.

...In reality, it was precisely because the negative influence Count Caseres had on the Noble Faction was so strong that they used the matter with Set as a convenient opportunity to cut him loose.

Unaware of these circumstances, Rei and Miranda could only watch the nobles hurrying away with expressions that defied description.

“Maybe Rei has something to hide?”

Even without knowing the reason, Miranda could surmise as much from the way the nobles looked at Rei.

After all, she had been serving the Duke of Kerebel for quite some time now.

For that very reason, it wasn’t all that difficult to read the emotions the nobles were harboring.

“Something shady, huh. …There are a few things that come to mind…”

The Black Wolf incident, and the Set incident. Beyond that, she understood that many people didn’t take kindly to the fact that Rei was on such close terms with Elena.

It wouldn’t be surprising if Rei were harboring a grudge against someone for reasons he didn’t even understand.

At Rei’s attitude, Miranda shot him a look filled with a slight… no, a rather strong sense of exasperation.

From Miranda’s perspective, she couldn’t imagine anyone going out of their way to do something that would make the nobility suspect them of shady dealings. …Though, technically speaking, Miranda is a member of the nobility herself.

“Well, let’s get going. Fortunately, it doesn’t seem like anything’s happening right now.”

Nodding at Miranda’s words, Rei resumed walking, having paused to speak.
