"Um, if we're going to keep watch or something, is there even a point in me coming along?"

As they headed toward their destination, Rei muttered that.

Since they needed to be as inconspicuous as possible this time, Set was nowhere to be seen by Rei’s side.

Instead, there were three guards and Grange.

That said, Grange was here to ensure they didn’t mistake the location of the base belonging to the guy who had given orders to Akabuna.

They had been told the general area beforehand, and many of the guards, who were familiar with the streets of Gilm, already knew the location.

But just in case… just in the very unlikely event… there was a possibility that the location Grange mentioned differed from what the others knew.

Given that, it was only natural to bring Grange here just to be on the safe side.

But Rei couldn’t help wondering, “Why am I here too?” That was the reason for his mutter.

“Even so, Rei, you should know where their base is, right? Even if we have a general idea of the location from Grange’s description, we can’t be absolutely certain that’s where the base is, so wouldn’t it be better if you came along to check it out?”

One of the guards replied to Rei’s mutter.

In fact, those words weren’t wrong, so Rei nodded in response to the guard’s remark.

“It’s not a mistake to say we don’t know the exact location, so that’s fine. …So, when do you think they’ll show their hand?”

“Hmm… It’s hard to say without knowing how often they visit that base. Plus, it’s tough for the lookouts this time of year.”

Hearing the guard’s words, everyone—including Grange himself—nodded in agreement.

It wasn’t snowing now, but that didn’t change the fact that it was winter.

The wind we feel as we walk is cold, and the sky is overcast today.

(When I think of stakeouts, I picture anpan and milk… but in a situation like this, meat buns and hot tea would probably be better. …Though, given how cold it is, even freshly steamed meat buns and freshly brewed tea would probably get cold in no time.)

As Rei was thinking this, he spotted a food cart selling meat buns at just the right moment.

Chinese buns, including meat buns, are simple to prepare—once the prep work is done, all that’s left is to steam them.

Even at a street stall, they were easy enough to prepare.

...However, what surprised Rei a little when he saw that stall was that they were using a steamer to make soups and the like as well.

Of course, the heat wouldn’t be enough to make soup the usual way, so there would likely be various issues… but even so, placing a pot on top of the steamer and using that heat to cook was surprising even to Rei.

I had seen a similar scene on a TV show back in Japan that featured food stalls...

(People think of similar things no matter where they are.)

Thinking that, I felt satisfied.

“Rei, what’s wrong?”

One of the guards asked Rei, who was staring intently at the food stall.

At those words, Rei speaks up, still looking at the food stalls.

“I was thinking that if we’re going to be staking out in this cold, we should probably buy some hot food like that to take with us.”

“...I totally get that.”

The guard muttered thoughtfully, likely because he’d been on stakeouts countless times before.

Staking out in the winter is particularly grueling because of the cold.

It would be ideal if you could find an empty house or rent a room in a building where people live, but if that’s not possible, you have to stake out the area outdoors.

(If I had some kind of vehicle, I could at least block the wind inside… a carriage? No, a carriage would probably be a bit too conspicuous.)

Unlike in Japan, carriages are quite expensive in this world.

They’re not quite like imported cars in Japan… but they’re certainly as rare and valuable as a high-end luxury car.

However, the difference from Japan is that carriages here are purely practical items.

...Of course, the rarity varies depending on the specific carriage.

In any case, if there’s a box carriage with a properly sealed body, it’s bound to stand out.

As a result, since we can’t pull off something like that, in the worst-case scenario, we might have to spend our time guarding our base out in the cold.

In times like that, having warm food and drinks would be a huge relief. …Though, given the current temperatures where it wouldn’t be surprising if it started snowing at any moment, I didn’t think that warmth would last very long.

“Right? What do you say? Should we buy some?”

The guard seemed slightly moved by Rei’s question, but ultimately shook his head in refusal.

Truth be told, the guard probably wanted to buy something too, but considering where they were headed, he likely understood that moving around while letting the scent of food waft through the air could lead to all sorts of trouble.

Perhaps Rei understood the guard’s reasoning, because he didn’t suggest stopping at the food stall again and simply moved on with the others, including Grange.

The fact that there was no real conversation as they walked... well, from the guard’s perspective, Grange wasn’t someone he could fully trust, and Grange, given his past history, certainly didn’t have a favorable impression of the guard either.

If Rei had stepped in to mediate between them, perhaps the two might have been able to find common ground, but Rei was too focused on surveying the surroundings to even notice the situation.

And so they continued walking in silence...

“Ah, I can see it now. It’s over there.”

Grange spoke up, and everyone’s gaze turned toward where he was looking.

“So it is there, after all.”

One of the guards muttered as he looked at the building.

The other guards nodded in agreement.

They’d been briefed beforehand, but it was indeed the place they’d expected.

Rei had been to this area a few times before, so when they pointed it out, he found himself nodding in agreement.

After staring at the building for nearly a minute, Rei finally spoke up.

“So, what do we do now? Are we going to look for a place to keep watch?”

The building serving as their base wasn’t particularly large, but it was still a single-family home.

It’s quite an old house, likely having been built quite some time ago, but the fact that they could acquire even a house like this as a disposable base indicates that those giving orders to the Red Cloth have abundant financial resources.

(If it were just Akabu’s crew, I might have wondered, “Why spend so much money on something like this?”… But considering the Kobold incident, it’s certain they’re not just a group formed solely to harass Gilm. If that’s the case, it wouldn’t be strange for them to have a certain level of financial resources.)

The Kobold incident goes far beyond mere harassment of Gilm.

In fact, if they were caught as the masterminds behind this, it wouldn’t be surprising if they were executed without question.

Given that they’re taking such risks, even if it appears to be harassment on the surface, it wouldn’t be surprising if there were something… something far more malicious lurking behind it.

That’s exactly why the guards, after hearing what Rei and Grange had to say, immediately sent people out like this.

“Right. First, we’ll go take a look around the area. …Rei, you and that guy wait in a spot where you can’t be seen from that building. If we find him, things could get messy.”

“Is that so? …Well, I guess I can’t leave Grange alone.”

Since Grange was originally hired by the people who were manipulating the Red Cloth, we can’t just leave him alone.

If Rei took his eyes off him, it wouldn’t be surprising if he made a run for it at any moment.

“Hahaha. I wouldn’t do something so reckless. I just learned the hard way that even if I run, they’ll catch up to me.”

Perhaps recalling the time he was easily discovered while hiding at the construction site, Grange let out a dry laugh as he said this.

For Grange, stealth was practically a specialty.

But since he’d been so easily exposed, it was only natural that he’d lost his confidence.

Although I couldn’t tell exactly what had happened from Grange’s demeanor, I could still understand roughly what had transpired.

Several guards cast sympathetic glances at Grange.

Outside of Gilm, he was undoubtedly a criminal, and that was precisely why they hadn’t been particularly fond of him… but seeing him like this now, the guards must have mixed feelings toward Grange.

“Ah… well, we can’t just stand here forever, so I guess it’s time to take action.”

In an atmosphere that felt like he was trying to gloss over something, the guard said this.

Hearing this, the other guards nodded, making no effort to hide their relief.

They must have felt it was awkward to stay there in the current situation.

The guards left as they were, and in the end, only Rei and Grange remained.

Now, what to do?

Rei cast a questioning glance at Grange, but Grange returned a look that seemed to say, “Do as you see fit.”

He likely felt that whatever he said here would probably just be misinterpreted.

Whether he sensed the mood or decided that staying here any longer might arouse suspicion, Rei eventually spoke up.

“Shall we move a little further away?”

“Yes, let’s do that. If they find me here, it’ll only cause trouble.”

At Grange’s words, Rei’s expression betrayed a hint of surprise.

From what she’d just said, it sounded as if she had no intention of running away.

……That said, now that Rei and the guards had already memorized his face, even if he managed to escape, the answer to whether he could live in peace was no.

At the very least, he certainly couldn’t do that here in this place called Gilm.

(Ah, but the earthen wall blocking the Kobolds is only about 150 cm high from the Gilm side, so it shouldn’t be too hard to climb over. …Well, once night falls, there’s a high chance monsters will be gathering on the other side of the wall too.)

In that case, what kind of monsters are there is entirely up to luck.

It’s certain there are plenty of Kobolds, but there’s no guarantee there aren’t monsters like Snow Cyclopses that prey on Kobolds.

Depending on the situation, there might even be monsters stronger than Snow Cyclops.

Of course, on the flip side, there might be monsters weaker than Snow Cyclops… no, in fact, there’s even a chance there might just be a few Kobolds.

(The people who created this situation definitely need a good punishment. And I’d also like to do something about the magic item situation.)

Rei, who still holds even a sliver of hope that the Kobold incident might be related to a magic item, thinks to himself.

Glancing at the building serving as the enemy’s base, Rei heads with Grange toward the shadow of a building a short distance away.

He acted on the assumption that they wouldn’t be visible from the building from there.

“Still, I wonder what on earth they were thinking, summoning a Kobold like that. …What do you think?”

Hidden out of sight of the enemy’s base, Rei asks Grange, who is standing beside him.

But even Grange, who was asked, hadn’t been briefed on the details by their employer.

“Even if you ask me, I don’t know the details. But… if they’d summoned a large number of stronger monsters—say, Rank C monsters—the chaos in Gilm would have spread even further. Instead, they deliberately chose Kobolds… so perhaps they didn’t want Gilm to descend into full-scale chaos either?”

“Well, that’s probably about right. …That said, I still wonder what they were thinking when they did it that way. …Of course, you haven’t asked them about that, have you?”

“No.”

Grange answered Rei’s question honestly.

Hearing that answer, Rei found himself wondering what on earth to do.

Of course, he knew that thinking about it now wouldn’t do much good.

As he was pondering this, one of the guards eventually approached Rei and the others.

Seeing the guard’s cheerful expression, Rei figured they wouldn’t have to worry about the stakeout for the time being… and indeed, the guard explained that there was a shop run by a former guard, and they had decided to use it.
