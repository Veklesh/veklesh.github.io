Several days after seeing Ilse off... Bistor seems to be quite busy over there, so I still haven't managed to meet him yet.

Amidst this situation, today too, beneath a summery blue sky dotted with cumulus clouds, Rei and the others were enjoying breakfast outdoors.

While they usually ate dinner in the garden, breakfast was either eaten indoors or, in the case of Rei, Vihera, and Byune, at the dining hall of Dusk Wheat inn.

So why were they having breakfast at Marina's house today of all days? The word "change of pace" seemed fitting.

Of course, the summery, fine weather certainly played a part.

Also, while Rei and the others were out working, Elena and Ara had nothing to do and were bored, which was a big factor.

So, Rei and the others were enjoying a slightly more luxurious breakfast than usual...

"Oh?"

Suddenly, Marina, who was drinking soup made with summer vegetables and dried sea fish, murmured.

Her voice held a hint of amusement, drawing the attention of everyone eating.

That said, the members present were Rei and the others from Crimson Wings, along with Elena and Ara, who had taken refuge at Marina's house.

Yellow and the horses pulling the carriage were also present.

In any case, since they were all close friends, hearing Marina's words tinged with amusement didn't particularly surprise anyone.

"So, what happened? Hope it's nothing too troublesome."

"A guest."

Marina replied to Rei's question, and upon hearing this, Elena and Ara flashed displeased expressions.

Though Elena and Ara lived at Marina's house, it wasn't unusual for nobles and merchants who knew this to come calling, wanting to meet them.

For that very reason, Elena and Ara generally ignored such visitors.

"Oh, more of those types..." the two thought. But Marina spoke up, as if to reassure them.

"It's not someone here to see Elena. This is one of Daskar's men."

"Daskar's? I see. So, has there been any progress regarding the Ajax matter?"

"Who knows? It'd be quicker to ask him directly when he arrives. Shall we finish our meal?"

"Kyuu!"

Hearing that the meal was over, Yellow, who had been eating a slice of oak ham cut as large as his own body, let out a disappointed squeak.

Incidentally, right next to the table where Rei and the others were eating, Set was consuming a massive oak-wrapped roast that seemed far too large for breakfast.

Though called a wrapped roast, it was more accurately a steamed roast.

It involved wrapping meat coated in spices in a giant leaf and then steaming it.

Naturally, steaming takes time to cook, so this wasn't something prepared this morning. It was something Rei had bought at a shop earlier and stored in the Misty Ring.

For humans, it would be a dish meant for four or five people to share, but for Set, it was easily enough for him to devour alone.

"Gurururuu"

Indeed, upon hearing Marina's words, Set growled as he devoured the remaining meat.

The others finished the remaining dishes... and soon, Marina stood up.

"Well, it seems they've arrived, so I'll be excusing myself now."

With that, Marina departed.

"What do you think?"

"What do you think? If it's about Ajax, she probably came to ask us for something..."

To Vihera, who murmured with a hint of expectation, Rei replied only that.

Considering a Daskar envoy had gone to the trouble of coming here, it was only natural to assume there was a request involved.

"Right. If that's the case... the country where the organization that ordered Ajax is based, Reburina, was it? Could it be they want us to go there?"

"Ah, that's highly likely. The problem is, if that's the case, who gets to go..."

Though Rei stated this, Vihera made no attempt to hide his attitude that he would naturally be the one to go.

Even during the patrols currently underway, encounters with fairly strong opponents weren't uncommon.

Still, the overwhelming majority of incidents were minor scuffles—the kind that might cause a bit of a commotion in town. The kind of tense, high-stakes fight Vihera craved almost never happened.

Having fought countless half-hearted battles, Vihera had become frustrated—frustrated with his lack of combat.

Even during the battle to avenge Ilse, the opponent Vihera fought, though a guard of Gestal, wasn't particularly skilled.

That's precisely why he now craves a proper fight against a formidable foe.

It's no wonder Vihera feels this way.

(Though, Reburina is a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom. And a fairly small one at that. If that's the case, I doubt there are many opponents here who could provide Vihera with the kind of combat he craves.)

Thinking this, Rei turned his gaze to the remaining members.

As usual, Byune showed no particular change in expression, while Elena merely wore a slightly downcast look.

Elena was here for a reason: to serve as a warning, a counterweight, a watchdog... call it what you will. Her purpose was to ensure the Noble Faction didn't cause any further trouble during the current expansion work on Gilm.

Given that, Elena leaving Gilm would be nothing short of putting the cart before the horse.

Elena understood that. She understood it, but... still, her honest desire as an individual was to be with Rei, the man she loved.

Unlike Marina and Vihera, who are part of Rei's party, Elena is neither a member of his party nor, more fundamentally, an adventurer at all.

Given that, it was only natural for her, as a woman, to feel envy and jealousy toward Marina and Vihera, who were always with Rei.

Of course, she wasn't thinking of trying to monopolize Rei.

But even so, it was only natural that she couldn't help wanting more time with Rei.

(Lady Elena)

Ara, Elena's confidante and best friend, naturally understood Elena's feelings.

Still, voicing it now was dangerous in many ways.

"Sorry to keep you waiting."

Marina's voice suddenly echoed through the morning air.

"Oh? Wasn't that a visitor?"

Wondering why Marina was alone, Vihera asked.

For Vihera, the prospect of a battle against a formidable foe was likely exciting, so his dissatisfaction at not being brought along must have been considerable.

But as if to calm Vihera, Marina smiled and spoke.

"Daskar wants us all to come to the lord's residence. ...Everyone here."

"...Huh?"

As expected, Elena was the first to mutter in confusion at Marina's words.

"Marina, let me confirm. When you say everyone present, does that include not only the members of Crimson Wings, but also me and Ara?"

"Kyuu!"

Yellow chirped from atop Set's back, where he'd moved without anyone noticing, as if to say, "Don't forget about me."

"Ah, right. Yellow's here too. ...Anyway, does that include us?"

"Yes. Everyone present here. I double-checked with the messenger, and they said they want Elena and the others to come too. They'll handle the work arrangements with the higher-ups, so it's fine if you're late."

"...Why?"

Elena had already reported directly to Daskar about the day Ilse avenged her.

Daskar led an extremely busy life, but he still needed to hear the details from Elena, who had been dispatched by the Noble Faction.

Admittedly, making time for that had made his already hectic schedule even busier.

Regardless of Daskar's busyness, Elena couldn't understand why he would summon her again after she'd already explained the situation.

"Hmm... Elena is definitely acting with us, so maybe it's related to that? Or perhaps because she lives with Marina, summoning only Marina and the others would be a bit awkward?"

Vihera's words made Elena pause, thinking, Oh? But if she was being summoned too, she had no choice but to go.

Besides, days spent just at Marina's house were often boring anyway. If she could go with them, that would be fine too. She nodded in agreement.

"Understood. I don't know why Lord Daskar called me too, but since I've been invited, I'll go as well."

When Elena nodded to those words, Ara naturally followed suit... and preparations for the carriage began immediately.

Normally, the carriage couldn't hold everyone present, but the one Elena owned had its interior space magically expanded.

This made it incredibly convenient for transporting large groups like this.

At first glance, the carriage didn't look like it cost a fortune, so it didn't attract attention.

...Though, of course, if Set was standing beside the carriage, it was obvious beyond a doubt who was riding inside.

Moreover, to anyone with discerning eyes, it was obvious that the carriage itself was an incredibly valuable item.

And currently, due to the expansion work, a large number of merchants had gathered in Gilm, meaning there were quite a few discerning eyes present.

As a result, the carriage drew more attention than expected due to Set and its quality as it made its way toward the lord's mansion.

"I'm sorry to have you come all this way,"

Daskar said this upon seeing Rei and the others enter his office.

But he immediately returned his attention to the documents before him.

"This last one should wrap things up, so please wait just a little longer."

"Oh? You summon us and then make us wait? ...Well, seeing that pile of paperwork, I suppose I can understand."

Marina briefly considered teasing Daskar, but seeing the mountain of papers on his desk, she couldn't say anything more.

Instead, she looked at Daskar with concern.

Daskar, however, seemed completely unfazed. He instructed the maid to bring tea and sweets before returning to his papers.

Then, after Rei and the others sat down on the sofa, a little while passed... The maid bringing tea and sweets and Daskar finishing reading the documents, signing them, and putting them into the wooden sorting box happened almost simultaneously.

As Daskar moved from his desk to the sofa, he immediately spoke up.

"My apologies for keeping you waiting after coming all this way."

"It's fine. ...But isn't your workload a bit excessive? Honestly, handling this much every day will ruin your health."

For Marina, Daskar was an old acquaintance.

Having known Daskar since he was a child, she couldn't accept him working himself to the point of exhaustion.

"Ah, yeah. Things should slow down a bit soon. Once that happens, I should be able to take it easier too, so just hang in there until then. ...Now then."

After replying to Marina, Daskar glanced again at Rei and the others sitting on the sofa.

Then, seeing Elena and Ara standing behind her as her guard, he opened his mouth.

"My apologies for summoning you, Elena."

"No, it's fine. I'm usually idle anyway."

In truth, Elena often found herself with nothing specific to do, frequently passing the time at Marina's house.

During the day, she had little to do besides sparring with Ara.

...Thanks to that, Ara's combat skills had improved quite a bit in this short time, so for her, these days were very fulfilling.

Though it was also true that Elena had hoped it would help distract Ara from the loneliness of being separated from her friend.

"That's the most important thing. ...However, the matter concerning you, Elena, will be last, so I ask you to wait a little longer."

At Daskar's words, Elena gave a small nod and reached for the tea on the table.

Perhaps because she had just finished breakfast, she showed no interest in the pastries.

However, Byune, naturally greedy for food, reached for the pastries.

Daskar watched Byune's actions for just a moment, a hint of amusement in his eyes. The next instant, his expression shifted back to that of the lord as he spoke.

"Now then, I imagine you've already guessed... I want Rei and the others—no, the Crimson Wings—to go to the Kingdom of Reburina. The reason should be obvious, right?"

He declared.
