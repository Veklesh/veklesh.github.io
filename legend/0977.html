Amidst the scattered corpses of Cyclopes, Mireine contemplated her next move.

Originally, their request was to defeat a single Cyclops.

But now that they had come to the forest, there were five Cyclopes, including a red one that seemed to be either a rare species or a higher-tier type.

They had decided retreat was their only option, judging they stood no chance against all of them, including that red Cyclops. Yet, when they reached the spot where the horses and carriage had been, two Cyclops were rampaging there.

A total of seven cyclopes, plus that red one that seemed rare or superior.

They knew there was no way they could handle this alone. But thanks to Dietz, who had gone to Gilm for reinforcements and returned, they managed to survive.

(Ecuril's injuries don't seem too bad either.)

Glancing at Rei lying a short distance away, Mireine murmured to herself.

Most of her external injuries had already been healed with potions, so all that remained was to wait for her to wake up.

It was fortunate for Ekril that the injuries themselves weren't particularly severe to begin with.

In reality, Ekril's injuries were limited to bruises and cuts from falling from the tree branch. He hadn't suffered any fractures, nor had he been struck by the lightning unleashed by the red cyclops' hammer.

Thanks to that, Mireine's potions had healed him almost completely.

"So, what are we doing now?"

The voice belonged to Vihera.

To Mireine, it was a face she had never seen before, and apparently someone familiar to her arch-enemy Johanna.

She was a beauty rarely seen, and despite being in the forest, she wore only the thin garments typical of dancers or courtesans, along with bracers on her arms and greaves on her legs.

No matter how you looked at her, she didn't seem like someone who could fight in the forest... no, she didn't look like someone who could fight monsters.

But having actually seen Vihera in that state fighting five Cyclopes—though Set had lent a hand—Mireine couldn't bring herself to say anything.

Even before that, just seeing her, he knew she was overwhelmingly stronger than himself.

"Y-Yeah. Bringing back seven Cyclopes is a bit much..."

Seven Cyclopes, each over four meters tall.

No matter how you looked at it, bringing them back was impossible.

After all, the one they'd originally targeted was meant to be loaded onto the carriage and taken to Gilm.

But that carriage had been destroyed by the cyclops' attack, making it nearly impossible to bring back the carcass as is.

Besides, since they hadn't killed the five Cyclops themselves, ownership of those belonged to Vihera.

"Since bringing back even one is impossible, we can only take the right ear for the slaying proof, the magic stone, and maybe the eye and horn that fetch a high price."

"Yes. It's a great pity, but..."

Surnin sighed as he responded to Mireine's words.

Though he sighed, there was no hint of regret in Surnin's expression.

He had braced for total annihilation when facing seven Cyclopes as enemies, so the mere fact everyone survived felt like sufficient reward.

The fact that Ekril's injuries were minor probably played a part too.

"Hmm, but isn't it a shame? Cyclops materials—like meat, skin, bones, tendons—sell for a pretty high price. Plus, some of their internal organs can be used as ingredients for potions and alchemy."

Johanna's words drew a displeased expression from Mireine.

However, that dissatisfied look was probably directed more at Set, the Cyclops Johanna was petting, than at Johanna's words.

She really wanted to pet Set too, but right now, she was the one leading this group that could be called a Cyclops extermination squad. She had various tasks to handle and no time to play with Set.

"Even so, what can't be done can't be done. Well, if the people who scattered into the forest earlier come back, we might be able to carry some by hand... Still, having the cart destroyed really hurts. Cyclops meat sells for a pretty high price, you know."

"GRRRRRR!"

At the mention of Cyclops meat, Set growled.

He wants to eat it! That much was clear from his greedy expression.

"We'll have to leave most of the Cyclops's carcass behind anyway. If you want to eat it, Set-chan, I suppose I could let you. ...Um, Vihera-san? No, -sama? Was that it? What about you?"

Mireine addressed Vihera, who had been watching their exchange alongside Byune. Johanna and Dietz, seeing this, flashed expressions that said, "Oh no."

They had glossed over explaining exactly who Vihera was, and now it had come back to bite them.

However, Vihera seemed completely unfazed by Mireine's attitude and spoke up.

"Well then, I suppose I'll just take the proof of extermination parts and the magic stones. You can do whatever you like with the materials. It's too much trouble."

The words that came out of Vihera's mouth were completely unexpected, even for Mireine.

"Um, we were saying we couldn't carry it all... Did you hear that?"

Vihera gave Mireine a look that said she didn't understand what she was talking about, even though Mireine was asking in polite language for some reason.

"You don't need a carriage or anything, right? Because... well..."

Vihera's gaze pointed toward the direction Mireine and the others had fled from.

Following that gaze, Mireille thought for a moment that the red cyclops might appear, parting the trees...

"What? It was already taken care of? We didn't need to rush over after all."

Emerging from beyond the thicket was Rei, clad as usual in his Dragon Robe.

Though one might normally question why he was here empty-handed, Rei carried an item box and didn't typically display his weapon, the great scythe... Just as that thought crossed her mind, Mireine turned her gaze toward Vihera.

She understood the meaning behind his earlier words about not needing a carriage.

"Oh, right. If Rei's here, then the item box..."

"Huh? What is it? ...Still, you sure went all out again."

Though puzzled by Mireine's words, Rei muttered while scanning the surroundings.

The corpses of the five Cyclopses Vihera had fought lay there, and a little further away were the corpses of two more Cyclopses.

A total of seven cyclops corpses lay scattered across this spot.

Seven dead Cyclops, each about four meters tall, made for a considerable amount of bulk.

"Heh heh. Right? Honestly, though, the Cyclops weren't as strong as I expected. Still, their sheer size made them tough, which was perfect for letting loose with attacks. Is it inappropriate to wish for slightly stronger opponents next time?"

As she spoke, Vihera shot a sidelong glance at Rei.

Her gesture, reminiscent of a courtesan enticing a man, combined with the sheer fabric of her clothing, possessed a seductive allure that made not only Rei, but even Dietz—who knew Vihera's true nature—and the usually mild-mannered Surnin's spines tingle.

"Hey, Rei. That woman looks like she might be an acquaintance of yours... What kind of person is she? An adventurer who can take on five Cyclops at once should be somewhat of a rumor around Gilm, right? If you've never heard of her, she must have just arrived in Gilm recently."

"Correct. She just arrived in Gilm with us yesterday."

Hearing Rei's words, Mireine's gaze shifted—not to Vihera, but to Set, who was being doted on by Johanna.

"Honestly, if you were coming back yesterday, you should have at least given us a heads-up. Then I wouldn't have taken that Cyclops job and could have waited for you."

"Don't be ridiculous. How was I supposed to contact you in the first place? It would be different if we had the Paired Orb."

「Paired Orb？」

Mireine tilted her head, clearly hearing the name for the first time.

"It's a type of magic item. A pair of crystals that let you talk to each other when used together."

Surnin, who seemed to recognize the name, explained it to Mireine.

"What kind of convenient magic item is that? I've never heard of it. Wait, if I had one, I could talk to Set anytime, right? Where do they sell them? I'll earn whatever it takes, even if it's light gold coins!"

She might be able to see Set anytime, anywhere.

The thought filled Mireine's body with a surge of strength from somewhere within.

Enough strength that she felt she could take on a few Cyclopes right now.

"Ah... Mireine. Unfortunately, the Paired Orbs are extremely rare and basically never appear on the market. If you absolutely want one, you'll have to search dungeons or ancient ruins. Or negotiate with someone who has one to sell it to you."

The moment she heard those words, Mireine's gaze shifted to Rei.

It was a gaze so intense that even Rei, a Rank S adventurer who had faced the Immovable Noiz, instinctively took a few steps back.

Of course, Rei didn't think he'd lose in a fight against Mireine, but he still found himself pushed back by an inexplicable, overwhelming pressure.

"Just so you know, even if you take it from me, the other half isn't here, so it won't do you any good."

Technically, he possessed both Paired Orbs, but while the one shared with Elena was one thing, he couldn't possibly reveal the one shared with Grimm.

At Rei's words, Mireine felt the power surging through her body visibly drain away as she opened her mouth.

"...I know that. Um, so what were we talking about? Oh, right. Who that Vihera person is. And that kid over there too. To be that skilled at that age, and to resort to such methods without hesitation... that's not normal."

"Means like that?"

Rei asked, not understanding what Mireine meant. Mireine, however, frowned as if the mere act of speaking the words caused her pain, yet she continued.

"That kid stuck a long needle between the Cyclops's claws. Well, threw it, to be precise."

"Whoa."

Rei understood why Mireine had looked so pained, and a similar expression appeared on her own face.

While it probably wasn't a fatal wound in terms of damage, just imagining the pain made him wince.

Though, it was Mireine and the others who attacked and defeated that Cyclops as a group.

"Um, Byune is a thief who was in a party with Vihera back when she was in Exil. You could even call him a skilled one."

"I knew he was skilled from the fight earlier... but Exil is a labyrinth city, right? If he was a thief there, he must be more skilled than I imagined. ...So, what kind of person is this Vihera guy anyway? If he's the sort who can handle five Cyclopes solo, I'd expect to have heard rumors about him even if he wasn't in Gilm."

"...He was an adventurer famous for his skill even in Exil."

Rei hesitated for a moment on how to answer, but ultimately replied thus.

He wasn't lying, but he wasn't telling the whole truth either.

"Hmm. ...Well, whatever."

Mireine seemed to understand vaguely that Rei was hiding something.

But perhaps sensing there was more to it, Mireine didn't press further.

"Rei, sorry to ask, but could you stow the Cyclops in the item box and carry it? For your group too."

At Vihera's request, Rei nodded without hesitation.

Most of the people here were family... well, maybe that was a slight exaggeration, but they were still close friends regardless.

Using the Misty Ring on such people was no big deal for Rei.

He promptly approached the five Cyclopes. Seeing this, Mireine, pleased that her task was temporarily complete, headed toward Set.

Of course, she wasn't completely careless in front of Set either, carefully checking her surroundings for any hostile presence.

Even so, she kept a watchful eye on Set, who purred contentedly as Johanna stroked him. She truly lived up to her reputation as a veteran adventurer.

Surnin stood guard beside Ekril, while Byune approached Vihera's side, purring happily as she was petted, her gaze fixed on Set. Dietz stood somewhat restlessly nearby, keeping a watchful eye on the surroundings.

"Looks like you fought with quite a bit of enthusiasm."

"Really? Honestly, I would've preferred a bit more of a challenge."

Standing before the Cyclops' corpse, Vihera sighed, as if disappointed.

"It certainly had stamina, no doubt about that. But it was just flailing its limbs around... I was hoping for an opponent with a bit more skill..."

While listening to this complaint, much like before, Rei touched the Cyclops's corpse... and began storing it away.
