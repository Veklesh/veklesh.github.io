While Rei and the maid were playing with Set and Yellow, Elena was receiving a visit from Teres.

“Good day, Lady Elena. I’m so glad to see you’re as beautiful as ever.”

“Hmph, I’m glad to hear you say that. Teres has become quite beautiful herself since I last saw her, hasn’t she?”

“Oh, I’m so glad to hear you say that, Lady Elena. …As for Ara… well, she seems to be just the same as ever.”

At those words from Teres, Ara’s expression, sitting beside Elena, twisted slightly.

“Is that so? Believe it or not, I’ve actually done quite a bit of work in Gilm.”

“Is that so? But… still… don’t you think?”

“……I don’t know what you’re getting at, but if you’re looking for a fight, I’ll take you up on it.”

“Oh, how scary.”

Saying that, Teres brought the tea in front of her to her lips… but the next moment, her movement froze as if time had stopped.

A few seconds later, with a movement that seemed to deserve the sound effect “creak,” she turned her gaze toward Ara.

There was no need to wonder who had brewed the tea in front of Teres.

“This tea is absolutely delicious.”

“Don’t you think so? I prepared special tea leaves just for Teres, and brewed it using a special method. I’m so glad you like it.”

"Hehe," she chuckled.

Ara sipped her tea, laughing in a way she would never normally do.

Of course, the tea Ara was drinking herself was brewed to perfection, unlike Teres’s.

It had no astringency, and a rich aroma filled her mouth.

As expected of the tea leaves used by Elena at the Kerebel Duke’s residence, it was quite a high-grade tea.

The way she fully savored the taste and aroma of such tea was the clearest indication that Ara was, after all, a member of the nobility.

“You two are still as close as ever.”

Elena watched the two of them with a smile.

Hearing this, the two immediately shot protesting glances at Elena.

However, the person on the receiving end of those glances didn’t seem particularly bothered and continued to enjoy the taste of her tea.

Realizing that anything more she said on the matter would only work against her, Teres changed the subject to divert the conversation.

“Speaking of which, Lady Elena, how was things in Gilm? I heard they’re currently expanding, so it’s gotten quite crowded.”

“Hmm? That’s right. From what I saw, there were quite a lot of people. …However, with so many people, it’s only natural that the number of those with ill intentions increases as well, so the people of Gilm seemed to be having quite a hard time.”

“I suppose so. I’ve heard that in our own territory, too, disturbances increase when people gather. But if we’re talking about large crowds, isn’t the Duchy of Kerebel a prime example of that? After all, Anesis is said to be the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana.”

What Teres said was the unvarnished truth.

Anesis, the seat of the Duke of Kerebel, is undoubtedly the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, surpassed only by the capital.

There are several other cities that rival Anesis in size, but as for one that might surpass it...

“Gilm might just end up surpassing Anesis someday.”

“……Huh? Are you serious?”

“Teres, that’s rude to Lady Elena.”

“Oh, my apologies.”

Though Teres apologized at Ara’s words, her opinion hadn’t changed one bit.

Although Gilm was currently undergoing expansion, she couldn’t possibly imagine its scale surpassing Anesis.

Since Teres had never seen Gilm with her own eyes, she had based this judgment solely on the information she had gathered, but she couldn’t possibly believe that judgment was wrong.

However, Elena—who could easily be called the goddess of beauty—didn’t seem to care at all about Teres’s attitude; she had judged that there was a high probability Gilm would eventually surpass Anesis.

“Lady Elena, I believe Gilm has the potential to grow. But, after all, it’s a town on the frontier… no, it’s becoming a city, isn’t it? In any case, it’s a remote location. If that’s the case, only a limited number of people will be able to visit, so I think it’s inevitably at a disadvantage compared to Anesis.”

“That may be true. But if my prediction is correct… Gilm will undoubtedly develop from here on out. It will become just like a labyrinth city.”

Labyrinth cities that developed around dungeons.

There are several such labyrinth cities even within the Kingdom of Mireana alone, and every single one of them has achieved a level of prosperity that far surpasses that of ordinary towns or cities.

Of course, this means having a dungeon within the city itself, and depending on the situation, there’s a possibility that monsters could emerge from it.

Given that, the prosperity of a labyrinth city goes hand in hand with danger… You could call it high risk, high reward.

In that regard, Gilm has the advantage of being on the frontier.

As Teres says, precisely because it is on the frontier, few people can reach it, and for those who shun danger, it is undoubtedly the worst possible place.

But even so, there are materials, monsters, and ingredients—all sorts of things—that exist only in the frontier.

It’s true that a certain level of skill—or luck—is essential, but the rewards are undoubtedly greater than what adventurers and merchants in ordinary towns or cities can earn.

“Oh, speaking of which, Lady Elena. If we’re talking about the only one… I heard that the Royal Faction is planning to build a massive arena. Have you heard about that?”

Teres brought up a topic she’d heard through the maids’ network.

She must have judged that if she continued talking about Gilm, Ara would have something to say about it.

“A grand arena? I think there were a few arenas, though none as grand as those in the Bestir Empire…”

When someone mentions an arena in this region, the Bestir Empire is naturally the first thing that comes to mind.

Built using the ruins of an ancient magical civilization, that arena was equipped with highly advanced technology that allowed combatants to survive even when they sustained injuries that would normally be fatal.

“Apparently, there’s a plan to build an arena that, if nothing else, matches the scale of the Bestir Empire’s—if not its functionality.”

“Colosseums have various uses, so I can’t say they’re necessarily a bad thing… but wouldn’t construction take a considerable amount of time?”

“That’s true. …By the way, I hear they’re planning to expand a section of the city to build that arena there.”

Hearing Teres’ words, I understood the intentions of those planning to build the arena.

“So, since Gilm—ruled by Lord Daskar of the Neutral Faction—is undertaking massive expansion projects, which have become quite the talk of the Kingdom of Mireana… is this a move to counter that?”

“That’s certainly one aspect of it. Of course, their primary rival is still the Bestir Empire, though.”

Upon hearing that the construction was being carried out out of rivalry with the Bestir Empire and the Neutral Faction, Elena… and Ara as well, wore expressions of slight exasperation.

“I don’t know what to say… No, it’s not really my place to comment.”

“I understand how you feel, Lady Elena, but apparently there’s quite a lot of activity going on around that city, even though it’s snowing? I hear they plan to continue construction even through the winter.”

“Since we’re not in Gilm, we don’t have to worry about winter monsters, so the only thing we need to watch out for is the snow… but even so, carrying out expansion work in the snow seems like it would be quite a challenge.”

Moving around in the snow is surprisingly exhausting.

On top of that, your body gets cold, increasing the risk of falling ill.

Furthermore, the snow itself can obstruct your vision or cause you to slip while walking.

...That is precisely why, in Gilm, construction during the winter is limited to areas where safety can be confirmed, and most projects are suspended.

“Just to clarify, is this being done on behalf of the Royal Faction? Or is it that noble?”

“The latter. To be precise, it’s the noble and his associates who are providing the funds…”

“They must be quite wealthy.”

Even as she said this, a look of worry crossed Elena’s face.

Building a massive arena that rivals the one in the capital of the Bestir Empire.

It sounds simple enough in theory, but she had just imagined how much funding would actually be required to build such an arena.

And even if the nobles were to provide the funds… the actual burden would fall on the estates they own.

In other words, tax revenue.

Elena didn’t believe that the nobles involved in the arena’s construction were not contributing money from their own fortunes.

However, there were surely quite a few nobles who, fearing a depletion of their own funds, would seek to raise the necessary amount by exploiting the residents.

And if the exploitation becomes too severe, the residents naturally won’t stay silent… and in the worst-case scenario, the possibility of a rebellion cannot be ruled out.

As a member of the Noble Faction, Elena wouldn’t be troubled if such a situation arose among the Royal Faction nobles… but that is strictly speaking only from the perspective of the Royal Faction versus the Noble Faction.

From the perspective of the Kingdom of Mireana, however, if a rebellion were to break out on the lands of the Royal Faction, it would undoubtedly be a loss for the nation.

Fortunately, since we are currently at peace with all nations, including the Bestir Empire, it would not become a major problem... but it is common knowledge that the Emperor of the Bestir Empire is highly territorial and covets the seas.

Depending on the situation, it’s by no means impossible that he might interpret this as a sign that the Kingdom of Mireana’s national power and military strength have declined, and decide to challenge us to war once again.

(If you think of him as Vihera’s father, his belligerent nature makes perfect sense.)

Knowing Vihera—who possesses a thirst for food, sleep, sex… and, following that, a thirst for battle—Elena actually finds the Bestir Empire’s belligerence quite understandable.

“Lady Elena? Is something wrong?”

“No, I was just thinking that since the arena is such a grand project, they’ll probably hold a grand unveiling once it’s finished.”

Hiding the fact that she had been thinking about Vihera, Elena replied.

Seeing Elena like this, Teres nodded back without seeming to notice anything in particular.

“That’s true. If you were to go to a place like that with a man, people would probably assume he’s your partner.”

“……I suppose so.”

At Teres’ words, Elena pictured a certain person in her mind, and given her personality, it was obvious who that person was.

And, of course, Teres, who knew all about Rei, wasn’t about to let such a golden opportunity slip by.

“Oh my, do you have someone in mind as a potential partner, Lady Elena? How enviable. A partner for someone of your stature must be truly remarkable…”

Hearing those words, Teres assumed Elena would try to brush off the topic of Rei.

But contrary to Teres’ expectations, Elena flashed a seductive smile.

Though she didn’t say it outright, that smile alone made it clear what Elena thought of the man she considered her partner.

For Elena, who was usually dignified and known by the nickname “Princess General,” was now showing her feminine side.

“Come to think of it, on my way to meet Elena today, I ran into someone who looked like an adventurer… I wonder who that was?”

Hearing Teres’ words, Elena immediately understood who he was referring to.

At the same time, she fully grasped the meaning of what had been said just moments ago.

Her expression, which had been that of a woman just seconds ago, changed completely, and Elena looked at Teres with an exasperated expression.

Teres, receiving that look, must have understood its meaning perfectly well. She looked back at Elena with a beaming smile.

“Lady Elena, I wonder if it was Crimson. It seems some people are feeling rather upset about that person, so I think you should be careful.”

“I suppose so.”

Though she nodded at Teres’ words, there was no trace of alarm in her expression.

She understood that no matter what any noble who disliked Rei might do, it would be completely meaningless… no, it was certain that getting involved with Rei would lead to the worst possible outcome.

(There might be some who use the nobles who’ve crossed paths with Rei as an excuse to plot something strange… but if such people exist, we’ll need to respond in kind.)

Rei’s presence was already being tacitly accepted by Duke and Duchess Kerebel.

It hadn’t been explicitly stated, but Elena recognized this.

For that very reason, there was no doubt that if anyone were to try anything against Rei, it would lead to the worst possible outcome for them.

“Whatever happens, it will be the result of that person’s own choice. It will likely be what they truly desire.”

Saying this, Elena took a sip of her tea.
