After checking the condition of the earthen walls, Rei and Set went around observing the adventurers fighting the Kobolds.

This was driven by the hope that many of the Kobolds they were fighting might be rare or higher-tier species.

Of course, since the adventurers were already fighting them, even if the Kobolds were rare or high-tier species, they couldn’t very well try to steal the fight away from them.

Or perhaps, given that the number of adventurers had dwindled, maybe… just maybe, there might be some rare or high-tier Kobolds wandering around aimlessly in the area…

“So, in the end, they were just regular Kobolds?”

“Grr…”

In response to Rei’s mutter, Set let out a disappointed growl.

Stroking Set as if to encourage him, Rei continued.

“Probably, they were lucky enough to step on one of their fellow Kobolds and manage to get over the earthen wall. Or… maybe they weren’t killed yesterday and were found hiding among the building materials.”

He didn’t know if the Kobolds had stayed quiet all night, but it wasn’t a possibility he could rule out.

However, considering that more Kobolds had scaled the earthen wall than Rei had anticipated, it wasn’t entirely out of the question.

“In that case, isn’t this actually a good thing for the guys who were in front of that earthen wall?”

One man voiced his dissatisfaction to Rei, pointing out that the number of Kobolds they had to defeat had decreased.

For that man, the current situation was by no means a bad thing… or so he might think.

Of course, compared to when the earthen wall wasn’t there, the number of Kobolds would undoubtedly be lower.

As I thought about this and was about to return to the Giganto Turtle disassembly site with Set... Set suddenly stopped in his tracks and looked ahead.

Within seconds of Set stopping, Rei also noticed the shouts coming from ahead… and above all, a menacing aura.

(What is this? It doesn’t look like a battle with Kobolds… Could it be a fight between adventurers over the Kobolds? No, but the aura is…)

For a moment, he thought it might be a fight over the dwindling number of Kobolds, but there were clearly far too many people for that.

No, there were far too many.

Rei didn’t know what was going on, but when he sensed the murderous aura drifting from ahead, he knew he couldn’t just ignore it.

Well, as an adventurer, it’s basically your own responsibility, but if I just left this alone, it could lead to a situation where people die—and that would leave a bad taste in my mouth, even before we get into the whole “personal responsibility” thing.

Above all, if trouble broke out here, there was a high chance it would negatively impact the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

“Let’s go, Set. Let’s get this hassle over with quickly… Ah, but if we head back to where we’re dismantling the Giganto Turtle right now, Vihera will probably be angry. If that’s the case, maybe we should kill a little more time first? Anyway, I guess we’ll just get this hassle over with quickly.”

“Grr!”

Growling in response to Rei’s words, the man and the beast ran across the ground toward the source of the sound… and toward the place emanating a menacing atmosphere.

And what met their eyes was… a larger crowd than they had anticipated.

It was a scene where a group of about ten faced off against a group of about thirty.

What surprised Rei most of all was that the group of ten were the very people who had been standing in front of the earthen wall earlier.

And the group of about thirty facing them—those people looked clearly hostile.

Next, Rei’s gaze fell on the group of about thirty.

That group consisted entirely of young men in their teens and twenties, and to top it off, every single one of them was holding an axe.

These were clearly unsuitable weapons for fighting Kobolds.

Kobolds are generally quick on their feet, which is precisely why fighting them with heavy weapons like axes or clubs is ill-suited.

It would be a different story if they were skilled fighters, but from what Rei could see, there was no doubt that all thirty or so men were weak.

(Is their strategy to rely on their numerical advantage, hoping that someone can land even a single blow on the Kobolds? No, but these are Kobolds, after all. Their numbers are already reduced by the earthen wall, so even if they managed to defeat them with that many men, it wouldn’t be worth the effort.)

Thinking this, and also wondering if they might have come just to check their coordination, he called out to the two groups facing off against each other.

“You guys are giving off a pretty tense vibe. What’s going on here?”

At that moment, as if to say “Don’t get in the way,” both groups turned their gaze toward him—and upon realizing that it was Rei who had spoken to them, they looked utterly stunned.

The group of about ten looked surprised that Rei—who should have left the area in front of the earthen wall before them—was still there… while the group of about thirty looked as if they feared he might have seen through their plans and beaten them to it.

“W-what the hell are you still doing here?!”

The first to snap out of it and shout that was the man who had voiced his dissatisfaction to Rei in front of the earthen wall.

In response to the man’s words, Rei spoke while petting Set beside him.

“Well, as for why… I was just checking to see how many Kobolds are around here, since we’re doing some expansion work on the site.”

To be precise, he had been looking around with a faint hope that there might be rare or high-ranking Kobolds here, but he had no intention of mentioning that specifically.

“So? I know I’m repeating my earlier question, but what kind of menacing atmosphere are you lot spreading around here?”

“W-well…”

The man speaking with Rei hesitated for a moment, then looked at the men holding axes and tried to say something...

“Sh-sh-shut up! It’s none of your business! Just get lost!”

Before the man talking to Rei could open his mouth, one of the men holding axes shouted.

The men with axes… though they weren’t currently wearing their distinctive red cloths, these former “Red Cloths” naturally knew who Rei was.

No, on the contrary, there were even some among them who had been captured by Rei back when they were rampaging as members of the Red Cloth. …Since they weren’t considered particularly important figures, and there were many similar-looking people, Rei hadn’t realized at all that there were people he had captured among the former Red Cloth members.

That is precisely why they are acting this way—judging that if Rei were to discover their true identities here, it would definitely lead to trouble.

Their anxiety turned to frustration, and they shouted as if they’d forgotten they were facing an opponent—Rei—whom they could never hope to defeat on their own.

……The fact that there were so many of them probably played a role in this situation as well.

However, the man’s attitude made Rei suspicious.

If he knew who they were, it would be understandable for him to want them to leave quickly, knowing they had no chance of winning if a fight broke out here.

I get that… but watching him like this, it seemed clear there was some other emotion at play besides that.

“Why do you want me to leave so badly?”

“That’s obvious, isn’t it?”

The man who had voiced his dissatisfaction to Rei in front of the earthen wall was the one who answered Rei’s question.

Ignoring the group of men with axes who were glaring at him and telling him to shut up, the man continued speaking.

“Rei doesn’t seem to realize it, but those guys… they’re the Red Cloth gang who were causing trouble in Gilm before the snow fell.”

“...What?”

Ray flinched.

At the man’s words, Rei froze.

It was the very person Rei had suspected—though it was only a hunch—of using a magic item or something to lure the Kobolds during the Kobold incident.

Hearing the man’s words, Rei looked at the men wielding axes—the men in red cloth—and suddenly realized.

(Wait. Why are these guys here?)

When he first saw the two groups here, he’d assumed the group with the axes had come to practice their battle coordination or something like that.

But the fact that they were wearing red cloth changed everything.

Red cloth, axes, a magic item that draws in Kobolds, and an earthen wall to prevent Kobold intrusion.

Combining all of these... Rei turns his gaze toward the men in red cloth, a smile spreading across his face at the conclusion he has reached.

However, that smile was no ordinary smile; it was one best described as ferocious.

The people of the Red Cloth who were met with that smile… no, not just the people of the Red Cloth, but even the group that had been arguing with them just moments ago—a chill ran down their spines.

It was as if the snow still piled up around them had been dumped onto their backs.

If they moved even slightly, they might be attacked by Rei.

Amidst that tension, Rei suddenly speaks up.

“Just to be clear… you guys aren’t going to say you came here to destroy the earthen wall I built, are you?”

Even as he asked, Rei anticipated that his guess was likely correct, given the information he had and the demeanor of the men in front of him—who appeared to rely primarily on axes.

In fact, the men in red cloth were unable to say a word in response to Rei’s words.

“What’s the matter? If it’s not true, say so. If it is, just tell me honestly. Otherwise, I’ll have a hard time deciding what measures to take.”

What exactly did he mean by “measures”?

To the men in red cloth, it was obvious without even having to think about it.

Hearing that, they realized they could no longer say they’d been ordered to destroy the earthen wall, but it was clear from Rei’s expression that even if they denied it now, he wouldn’t believe them.

“Grr!”

Perhaps finding the men in red cloth’s hesitation frustrating, Set, who had been watching the situation from beside Rei, let out a sharp cry.

“Eek!”

Although these men in red cloth had once caused a commotion among the residents of Gilm, in the end, they had only been committing petty misdeeds.

It wouldn’t be strange at all if one’s legs started trembling the moment a griffin like Set became displeased.

Some of the Red Cloth members had seen Set playing with children in town before, but the Set standing before them now bore absolutely no resemblance to that innocent figure.

Looking at the Red Cloth members, Rei smiled before speaking.

“What’s the matter? You’d better just tell the truth. Otherwise, Set’s mood will only get worse.”

“O-okay! I’ll tell you! I’ll tell you, so please stop!”

“Hey, you idiot! You know what’ll happen if you pull a stunt like that, don’t you?!”

One of the men, met not with his usual gentle gaze but with a sharp stare like that of a predator, shouted instinctively, and another man standing nearby shouted back in rebuke.

They knew full well that the person who had hired them was no ordinary individual.

After all, a normal person wouldn’t go so far as to hire people just to make the situation worse.

It was a given that anyone who did such a thing had circumstances they couldn’t possibly make public.

They understood that… but precisely because of that, they could predict what would happen if they betrayed the man who had hired them.

At the very least, it was absolutely impossible that it would end with just a verbal warning.

That’s exactly why I was annoyed by the actions of the man who shouted at Rei, but now that I’ve gone and said something so obviously suspicious, it was already too late.

“I see. Well then… let’s see. I’d very much like to hear what you were planning to do.”

“Tell me,” he said.

The one being stared at like that was the man who had just shouted at Rei.

Knowing that he had been the first to betray his comrades, he felt that whatever he said here, they would never believe him again... Even as he realized that several people around him were glaring at him, the man opened his mouth.

“Well, I was told to destroy that earthen wall you built.”

That single sentence wasn’t all that surprising to Rei, but to the group of men who had voiced their complaints to Rei in front of the earthen wall, it sounded unbelievable.

“Nonsense! Demolishing the earthen wall means letting the Kobolds back into Gilm!?”

The one who shouted this in response to the men’s words was the man who had voiced his dissatisfaction to Rei.

If anything, Rei was surprised by that man’s words.

After all, this man was supposed to have been the one who had complained about Rei building the earthen wall in the first place.

Considering what he’d said back then, Ray thought he might be pleased by the red-cloaked men’s words, but he couldn’t imagine him being dissatisfied with them.

However, he quickly decided that there was something more important to ask the men in red cloth right now... and turned his gaze toward them.
