Although Grange was pinned down by Rei and couldn’t move, he asked Rei to let go, as if he had truly given up.

But Rei kept his hand on the back of Grange’s neck and showed no sign of letting go.

“If you want me to let you go, you’ll have to answer my question first. You can manage that much, can’t you?”

“Even if you say that… it’s winter and there’s snow on the ground. If I stay pinned down like this, my body will get cold.”

“I suppose so. But if you give me the information I want, I won’t have to keep you in this position forever. …Now then. What were you thinking when you tried to get the Red Cloth gang to break down the earthen wall?”

It wasn’t exactly the thing Rei was most curious about… but to gauge whether Grange truly possessed the information he sought—and whether he was even willing to share it—this was the safest question to ask.

“Even if you ask me what I was thinking… Logically speaking, wasn’t it simply to get the Kobold inside Gilm?”

“Why would they do something like that?”

“Well… unfortunately, since they don’t trust me that much, I don’t know everything.”

At those words, Rei tightened his grip on Grange’s neck.

Naturally, Grange felt it too. Perhaps realizing his neck would be snapped if this continued, he hurriedly spoke up.

“It’s not like I don’t have any other information. For example… yes, if we destroyed the earthen wall, it would naturally follow that the Kobolds would pour out from there. I was told to use that to take care of the Red Cloth people.”

“...What?”

Perhaps because what came out of Grange’s mouth was unexpected, Rei released his grip on his neck.

“Why take out the Red Cloth guys? Aren’t they the ones we’re using as our minions? …Well, I suppose they have become a bit too infamous in a bad way.”

As far as Rei knew, the Red Cloth gang had been causing all sorts of trouble.

In fact, judging by how he’d just recognized one of the men from the group they’d been staring each other down as a Red Cloth man, that much was obvious.

In other words, it was clear that even if they took action, there was a high chance they’d be immediately identified as Red Cloth members, making them difficult to use as pawns.

But… even so, they still have some use.

If they were the kind of people who couldn’t be used in the open, then surely there was a way to use them behind the scenes… in places where it wouldn’t become public knowledge.

Nevertheless, if what Grange said is true, it means he was planning to dispose of all the Red Cloths—who were supposed to be his pawns—at once.

“Well, I don’t know the details either… but he did let slip something along the lines of ‘they’re no longer needed.’”

“...‘No longer needed’? Does that mean they’ve acquired other pawns?”

“I don’t know the details on that either. But it is a fact that they were trying to dispose of them.”

He used the word “dispose of,” but that essentially meant killing the men.

Even so, Grange made this statement without showing any sign of remorse.

(Why? Even if they’ve acquired new pawns, is there really a need to just casually discard them like that? There are plenty of ways to use them… No, wait. According to what I heard from the guards, the Red Cloth shouldn’t have been just thirty men like we saw earlier. So, was it to weed out the useless ones even within the Red Cloth? …If that’s the case, I suppose I can understand.)

I felt somewhat convinced, so I decided to set that aside for now.

In fact, judging by the Red Cloths’ reaction when they saw Rei, it wouldn’t have been strange at all for them to be classified as useless—that’s probably a major factor here.

“Well, I suppose I can accept that explanation for now. …Alright, next. How do you contact the person who hired you? Or, if you prefer, where do you go to get in touch?”

Rei was surprised to hear that they’d intended to eliminate Akabu, but in reality, this was the information he actually wanted to know more precisely.

If he knew their contact information, that would mean the person behind the Red Cloth was there, and there was a high probability he could uncover the mechanism by which they were summoning the Kobolds—though Rei hadn’t yet ruled out the magic item theory.

That was the information he absolutely needed to know.

……That said, since Rei had erected an earthen wall, most of the Kobolds were already unable to enter Gilm, so it wasn’t exactly a critical issue.

As for the ones heading toward the Giganto Turtle dismantling site, the guards are taking care of them, so there isn’t any major problem there.

In fact, the fact that the risk of Kobolds entering the town had been significantly reduced was a huge relief for Daskar, the Guild, and the residents of Gilm alike.

“Um, well… since this touches on my credibility, I’d rather not say if I can help it… Is that a problem?”

Grange asked Rei this, but instead of answering, Rei tightened his grip on Grange’s neck.

“Yaaah! O-okay! I’ll tell you, I’ll tell you, so please ease up a little!”

Feeling as if his neck might snap from the sheer force of the grip, Grange blurted out in panic.

That said, even if Grange had been a bit more stubborn, Rei probably wouldn’t have actually gone so far as to break his neck.

After all, Grange was finally—truly, finally—a lead on the Kobold case.

Given that, there was no way he would have done something so foolish as to kill that hard-won lead so easily.

...Though it was essential to make him believe that if he didn’t answer, he would be killed.

Fortunately, Grange was well aware of just how unconventional Rei was.

That’s precisely why he was willing to talk like this, without needing to resort to such serious threats.

“I see. I’m glad you’ve decided to cooperate and listen to what I have to say. …So, let me repeat: what is the method you use to contact the person who hired you, or the location where you go to make contact?”

“……The plan is to go directly to the hideout. However, that’s strictly just the place I’m supposed to go to make contact; I don’t think it’s the client’s actual base of operations. At least, when I was there, there was hardly any sign of anyone else.”

“You’re cautious. Well, considering you’re hiring someone, I suppose that’s only natural.”

If they were using subordinates they truly trusted—their own hand-picked men—that would be one thing. But setting up a separate location to meet with someone who’s just taking on a contract? That’s not unreasonable if you’re even a little cautious.

To begin with, even if they’re just Kobolds, they’re luring monsters into Gilm.

If we were discovered, we’d undoubtedly be captured and executed.

Given that, it was only natural that the option of not being cautious here simply didn’t exist.

“What do you want to do? The place I know is, after all, just a temporary base. Do you still want to hear about it?”

“Of course. Even if it’s just a temporary base, it’s still a base. If we investigate the people coming and going there, we should at least be able to find some clues about them.”

“...I understand.”

Perhaps realizing he could no longer evade the question, Grange nodded meekly, even as his neck remained gripped.

“I’ll tell you what I know, so would you mind letting go of my neck? If this keeps up, the cold will start to affect my ability to speak.”

Lying on the cold ground saps more energy than he had anticipated.

Grange must have said that precisely because he understood—no, because he was actually feeling it.

(Well, what should I do? …No, actually, since I’d be in trouble if this guy did something to me right now, maybe I should let him up.)

Having made that decision, I released my grip from the back of his neck.

Even as he let go, he remained ready to react the moment Grange moved—likely because he’d decided that if Grange tried to escape, he’d have to catch him immediately.

That said, Grange probably doesn’t think he can escape from Rei in this situation.

He’d been found even when I was in perfect condition, having concealed my presence and hidden myself.

And now, with Rei right in front of him—especially after having been lying on the winter ground just moments ago, his body chilled to the bone—

There was no way he could outrun Rei in such a condition.

...Though, of course, that implies he would have fled if he’d been confident he could get away.

“No complaints here. …So, where is this base you mentioned you’d contact?”

“It’s not too far from the slums. Do you know a general store called Mishun’s? From there…”

Saying that, Grange obediently explained the location of the base.

Of course, that’s just what Grange says; whether there’s actually a base at the location he described… or even if there is a base, whether it’s actually being used… I still don’t know.

“I see. …Well then, I’m heading there right away, so you’re coming with me.”

“Could you please spare me that? I’m currently employed. If I were to spill the beans on my employer, it would make things rather awkward.”

“That’s easier said than done. I have a general idea of the location you described, but once we actually get there, I think we need to confirm whether it’s still in use… or if it’s a trap. …Above all, I think you’re worrying about the future a bit too soon.”

That’s what Rei said, but his words weren’t an exaggeration in the slightest.

This was coming from someone who had been luring Kobolds into the city... and he had been hired knowing full well what that entailed.

Naturally, if he handed him over to the guards, it was certain to be a serious crime.

“Can’t you use your influence as a ‘Named One’ to sort that out? I think I’d be quite useful, despite my appearance.”

Grange said this with a touch of swagger, but though he wore a smile, there was a serious look in his eyes.

Of course. If he didn’t figure something out here, just as Rei had said, there would be no future for him.

Given that, he had to find a way to get Rei to put in a good word for him.

“Hmm, I see. …Well, I suppose I could give it some thought. Though, to be honest, there aren’t many places I could introduce you to.”

What came to Rei’s mind were Egg and his group—operating as Daskar’s subordinates in Gilm… and as the hidden forces behind the Neutral Faction.

Many of Egg’s subordinates have troubled pasts to begin with.

What’s essential for a covert operation—a so-called intelligence organization—is, above all, ability.

Precisely because the work is so dangerous and the risk of death is high, attrition rates are inevitably high.

That’s precisely why it’s not uncommon for people to be hired for such work even if they have some personality issues… or a shady past.

That said, since it also means that as long as one has the skills, it’s not a problem, it seemed to Rei that this might actually be a good fit for Grange, who was confident in his own abilities.

...Though, to be honest, he hadn’t given any thought to whether Granger himself would even accept such a job.

(If he doesn’t want that… I’ll just leave it to the guards for now. That’s about all I can offer.)

Watching Rei think this, Grange felt a chill run down his spine—not just from the cold.

But he must have realized that, given his current situation, there was nothing he could do about it.

Without letting any sign of anxiety show on his face, he smiled as he always did and spoke.

“Alright, then, let’s go with that for now.”

“Got it. …So, you’re coming with me, right?”

“Yes. …Given how things have turned out, I think that’s the best choice.”

If Grange were more than just a hired hand—if he possessed some sort of strong loyalty—he wouldn’t have betrayed me so easily… Unfortunately, Grange is truly nothing more than a hired hand.

Prioritizing his own safety over that of his employer was only natural given Grange’s character.

What’s more, standing before him was Crimson Rei, known by the nickname “Crimson,” and though not present here, Set of the Griffon was nearby as well.

Until just a short while ago, he had believed that even against opponents like Rei and Set, even if he couldn’t win a fight, he could at least hide and avoid being discovered… but that confidence has been shattered to pieces.

“I see. Well then, let’s get going right away. If we waste time here, they might start to get suspicious.”

That’s what Rei said.
