Lenora was about to get busy at the front desk, so Rei, escorted by another guild employee, headed to the guild warehouse. After confirming no one else was around, he retrieved the hammer Cyclops had used from the Misty Ring.

The hammer appeared silently and abruptly beside Rei. Naturally, in his current state, he couldn't hold it, and the handle crashed to the ground.

(...Huh? Wait, if that handle had come out pointing down, would that have been life-threatening?)

Watching the hammer's handle lie on the ground, making a loud clang, that thought suddenly crossed Rei's mind.

Though Rei had overcome what might actually have been a life-threatening situation, he didn't show it. Instead, he turned his gaze toward the guild employee before him—the person with the high ability to appraise magic items.

"So, this is the hammer the red cyclops was using—"

"...This one, you say? Hmm, well, looking at it like this, it certainly seems to be quite a fine piece."

A man in his fifties, with a white beard reaching down to his chest, murmured with admiration.

"May I touch it?"

"Ah... I wonder. Hold on a moment."

Responding to the guild clerk's words, Rei extended his own index finger and touched the hammer.

The guild clerk gave him a puzzled look, wondering what on earth he was doing, but no attempt to take over his will occurred from the handle when Rei touched it, unlike the first time he had touched it.

(Did that incident really erase whatever consciousness was inside this hammer? If so, that'd be great... but no, I shouldn't get my hopes up.)

Gently withdrawing his hand, Rei addressed the guild clerk.

"Actually, the red cyclops who used this hammer... eventually felt like its consciousness was being taken over by the hammer. Like, while unconscious, it had these tree root-like things spread throughout its body, being controlled by the hammer."

"What!?"

Hearing Rei's words, the guild official who had been watching the hammer's vicinity with interest leapt backward.

That movement was undeniably not that of someone unfamiliar with combat; it proved he was a person with considerable skill.

(A guild employee from the frontier... and one with deep knowledge of magic items? That wouldn't be so strange, would it?)

Slightly surprised by the guild employee's agility, Rei touched the head of the hammer as if to prove its safety.

"Rest assured. If it still possessed the ability to control its user, I'd be under the hammer's control right now, wouldn't I? And before that, I wouldn't have been able to store it in my item box either."

"...I see. If it controls whoever touches it, that makes sense."

Though Rei's words brought some relief, she still didn't carelessly approach the hammer.

This wasn't because he didn't trust Rei's words. Rather, it was because his extensive knowledge of magic items made him inherently cautious.

He'd heard from acquaintances several times about magic items possessing their own will.

This was his first time seeing one directly, but from what he'd heard, most such magic items were practically cursed, leading their owners to tragic ends.

Of course, not all met such an end.

Among the stories he'd heard, the best possible outcome was when the will residing within the magic item and its bearer formed a cooperative relationship.

But that was only a tiny minority. The will residing in most magic items brought tragedy to their owners.

That's precisely why the man couldn't help but be persistently wary about whether the hammer before him was truly safe and reliable.

"I don't think you need to worry that much. When this hammer tried to take me over, my own magic overrode its will... so there's no consciousness left inside it now."

"I'm sorry, but unless I see it with my own eyes, I can't be completely reassured."

Rei simply nodded back at the guild employee bowing apologetically, saying nothing more.

(If the professionals are this cautious, then probably... no, definitely, this magic item is dangerous.)

Thinking this, Rei watched as the guild employee put on the bracelet and necklace he'd taken from his pocket.

Judging from the conversation, these were likely magic items designed to prevent consciousness from being hijacked.

However, he could also imagine that they weren't designed to completely prevent mind control, but rather to increase the wearer's resistance to it...

Right in front of Rei, the man extended his index finger, touched the hammer, and immediately withdrew it.

After repeating this several times, he finally seemed to understand that Rei's words were true. Next, instead of just a finger, he touched it with his palm, then gripped the handle—his hand grew bolder and bolder as it touched the hammer.

"I see. It seems to be a magic item created through a rather complex process. From what I've heard, it possesses two abilities: the power to unleash lightning and the ability to grant the user powerful regenerative capabilities. ...It would be impossible to create either one without being an alchemist of considerable skill, yet it possesses both?"

He touched the handle, moved to the hammer head, and gently stroked both, as if to confirm them.

What kind of magical metal was used? What process created this hammer? Were there any traces of the alchemist's idiosyncrasies in crafting magic items?

Rei watched from a short distance as the guild official examined it meticulously, determined not to overlook even the slightest detail.

Suddenly, the male guild employee turned his gaze toward Rei.

"Excuse me, Rei. Could you turn this hammer over so we can examine the part that touches the ground?"

"This? ...Well, sure. I could probably do it without flipping it over."

Nodding, he reached for the handle of the hammer lying on the ground and lifted it up.

He didn't lift the hammer itself; he kept the head on the ground and lifted only the handle.

He then tilted the handle to the opposite side from before, exposing the part that had been buried in the dirt to the air.

The guild employee approached the hammer again the moment Rei stepped away and began examining it.

"I see. So the opposite side and this side weren't deliberately made symmetrical? It's unusual for someone with the skill to create a magic item of this caliber to be unable to achieve perfect symmetry. If that's the case, why disrupt the magic conductivity... Was that the reason the Cyclops could use two effects? Hmm? The distinctive traces left when this hammer was made into a magic item... No, but..."

He muttered upon discovering something, his murmurs developing his own theories.

Rei watched the guild employee's mutterings, but after about twenty minutes, he started to get bored.

While an alchemist or blacksmith might find it interesting, Rei was neither.

Given that, just standing there watching was nothing but boredom.

Still, he hadn't spoken to the guild clerk because he was curious about where this hammer had come from.

A weapon suitable for a Cyclops wasn't likely to just be lying around.

So it was only natural to assume someone had made it...

(But still, giving a magic item to a Cyclops? That's not something people normally do.)

As he pondered this, he suddenly noticed the guild clerk stop muttering and fall silent, deep in thought.

Rei, who had assumed the man had moved from muttering to deep thought, watched as the man began to tilt his head in puzzlement.

"What's wrong?"

Finally prompted by Rei's voice, the man scratched his head and spoke.

"Well, you see. It seems certain this hammer wasn't made all that long ago. And it was crafted with quite advanced techniques. But... I just can't figure out why someone would give such a precious magic item, made with such effort, to a Cyclops."

"...Was it really made recently? Like, could it be a magic item you just happened to find lying around somewhere, maybe in some ruins or something?"

"I don't think so."

Surprised by how decisively he stated it, more so than Rei had anticipated, the man continued his explanation to Rei.

"The details get technical, so I'll explain broadly. Some of the techniques used to create this magic item involve methods established only recently... within the last few years, actually. Not everything, but it's almost certainly been made within the last few years... possibly even within the last few months."

"...Who would go to such trouble?"

Giving a magic item to a Cyclops is easy to say, but incredibly difficult to actually do.

Cyclopes view humans as nothing but prey. Approach them carelessly... especially if you try to pull away a massive hammer like the one before Rei's eyes, you'd likely end up devoured.

Why go to such lengths to give a magic item to a Cyclops? And above all...

"The cyclops I defeated was a rare or higher-tier species, different from ordinary cyclopses. But that doesn't mean it could necessarily use magic items, right?"

"True. But even ordinary cyclopes can unleash magical energy as shockwaves from their single eye. If we're talking rare or higher-tier species, it wouldn't be strange to assume they're more skilled at handling magic, would it?"

"It's a possibility. But it's just that—a possibility. If we're going to give magic items anyway, couldn't we give them to monsters that aren't necessarily skilled at handling magic, like the Cyclops? Then we wouldn't have needed to make such a huge hammer."

"...That's something we can't really say for sure here. But it is indeed intriguing. I'll suggest to the Guildmaster that we look into it a bit. ...Well then, you may put this hammer away now."

Hearing the guild clerk's words, Rei's expression shifted slightly in surprise.

He had expected Ray to cling to it a bit longer, given how visibly fascinated he seemed by the hammer.

Noticing Rei's expression, the man opened his mouth, looking slightly embarrassed.

"Ahaha. To be honest, I'd love to examine this hammer more thoroughly. But if I did, I'd probably end up staring at it forever, so I'd rather stop here."

"...I see. So, from a professional perspective. Is this hammer dangerous?"

"Honestly, it's probably fine. I have to say 'probably' because of my own limitations. But if what Rei told me is true, the ability to control its owner that this hammer once had has likely already vanished. Being a magic item, it often involves magic power, for better or worse."

"I see. ...Huh? So maybe... in my case, if I try to use a cursed magic item—or rather, one with the ability to control its user—I can destroy that will?"

Rei asked as if suddenly realizing something, causing the guild clerk to widen his eyes in surprise.

Rei could tell the man hadn't considered that possibility at all.

But his surprised expression quickly turned into a frown.

"Well, yes. It's certainly a possibility, but personally, I wouldn't recommend it. Even though we lump them all together as magic items, some are relatively recent creations, while others are incredibly ancient relics, like remnants of the ancient magical civilization. You should be especially wary of relics from that ancient civilization – magic items that possess their own will and can take control of their users."

His words, spoken while looking Rei in the eye, came from genuine concern.

While his favorable impression stemmed partly from seeing the rare hammer-like item the Cyclops had used, what truly heightened the man's sense of danger was the thought of what might happen if Rei were to lose his consciousness to a magic item.

Given Rei's expertise in area-of-effect annihilation magic, it wouldn't be surprising if the entire Gilm were incinerated if things went wrong.

Suppressing that spine-chilling thought, the guild official warned Rei to be extremely careful not to touch cursed magic items carelessly.
