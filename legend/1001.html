Surprised by Elena suddenly appearing before them, Rei and Set nevertheless headed toward Adria, who needed securing first.

If Adria had been running normally, she wouldn't have fallen even if someone suddenly stuck a leg out in front of her.

After all, Adria was confident in her skills; she should have been able to dodge the outstretched leg in an instant.

The reason she couldn't was because she was running using a magic item.

Of course, that didn't mean she hadn't mastered the magic item.

Well, it might be similar in the sense that she hadn't fully mastered it yet.

Anyway, suddenly tripped by Elena, Adria tried to escape, refusing to be captured by Rei and Set...

"Grrr!"

Before she could even get up, Set pinned her down with his front paws, immobilizing her.

"Guh, you... let go!"

Adriana struggled desperately to rise, but with her midsection pinned down from directly above, she couldn't move an inch.

If it had been a human holding him down, he might have had a chance, but it was Set the griffin. No matter how much he struggled, it was futile.

"I stopped this one from escaping from Rei for now... but you didn't mind, did you?"

Elena asked, her white-based metal armor tinged with the colors of sunset. Rei nodded.

"Ah, yeah. That was helpful, but... when did you get to Gilm? When we contacted each other with the Paired Orbs a few days ago, you didn't mention anything about this, did you?"

"Ara says a little surprise is necessary between a man and a woman. I tried it out a bit. ...Did I surprise you?"

Her tone was arrogant, yet Elena's expression held multiple emotions: whether Rei was pleased, not angry, or genuinely surprised.

Though, the strongest emotion among them was undoubtedly her joy at seeing Rei.

And Rei, too, smiled at Elena's words and nodded in return.

"Yeah. Pretty surprised. But if you're here..."

After all, Elena had originally said she would come to Gilm when spring arrived, so there was no reason to be surprised she was here.

Rei knew that, yet the suddenness of Elena's appearance still startled him deeply.

In this regard, one could say Ara's plan had hit the mark perfectly.

And now, it finally made sense. The reason the town had been buzzing for a while now? It was because of Elena.

"So, why were you chasing this person? Since Rei was pursuing them, I assume there was some reason behind it."

Elena asked, running her fingers through hair as golden as sunlight itself. Rei finally recalled the current situation and spoke up.

"Lately, there've been all sorts of unusual incidents happening around Gilm. He's the prime suspect."

"Unusual incidents?"

Rei briefly explained the incidents involving the Cyclops, the Kobold, and the adventurer's commotion at the gates of Gilm to Elena, who tilted her head.

"...I see. Frankly, I'd rather not believe there are people capable of such things... but what do you know?"

Elena asked Adria, who had been pinned down by Set just moments ago and was struggling to break free.

Meanwhile, on Set's back, who was still holding Adrienne down, the small black dragon Yellow had landed unnoticed. As usual, they were conversing with squeaks and growls.

"Unfortunately, I don't know anything about it."

"Then why did this guy pick up a rotten smell from you?"

"The stench? Probably picked it up when we passed a scene where monsters killed each other while we were hunting them, I guess?"

"...Then why did he suddenly run away? If he had nothing to hide, he wouldn't need to flee, right?"

"A high-rank monster like this griffin suddenly appeared. Of course it ran away!"

"If you're a resident of Gilm, don't tell me you didn't know about this guy?"

"Unfortunately, I just arrived in Gilm and didn't know about Set's existence."

The moment Adria muttered this, Elena, who had been silently listening to their exchange, spoke up.

"Wait. Why do you know Set's name? Neither Rei nor I mentioned Set just now. ...Rei, did you mention Set's name before pursuing this person?"

Hearing Elena's words, Rei recalled the conversation in the alley and shook his head.

"No, I didn't."

"Is that so? Well then, why don't you tell me how you knew Set's name?"

"...Well, since I came to Gilm, it's only natural to remember the names of famous people, right? When he suddenly appeared in front of me, I was startled and instinctively ran away."

Adriana flashed a momentary look of unease at having mentioned Set's name, but quickly replied.

(I must say, I've made a rather foolish mistake. Between tripping me up and this woman being troublesome in every sense... Princess General, you're more than I bargained for.)

A sudden apparition, an embodiment of beauty itself.

No, anyone living in the Bestir Empire knew her essence wasn't merely beautiful.

The existence of the beautiful war goddess known by the epithet Princess General was, in fact, more famous in the Bestir Empire than in the Mireana Kingdom.

Such a figure had suddenly... truly suddenly, without any context whatsoever, abruptly appeared.

Adriana herself didn't think she was flustered by it, but in reality, she made a mistake she would never normally make.

She had claimed she fled upon encountering a griffin, yet she knew its name.

Even as Adria tried desperately to cover it up, Elena fixed her gaze firmly upon her.

Overwhelmed by that gaze, Adria nonetheless refused to look away.

Doing so would be like admitting she had something to hide.

"Hmm, I see. I suppose you have your reasons. But with someone this suspicious around, I can't just leave you be."

"...What are you going to do with me?"

"Nothing complicated. I'll just hand you over to the guards. If you truly have nothing to hide, then you should undergo their interrogation with confidence."

Under normal circumstances, those words might have been accepted.

If she truly had nothing to hide, that is.

But unfortunately, Adria had a reason she couldn't afford to be captured here.

(What should I do? Fighting is out of the question now that things have come to this.)

Adriana had reasonable confidence in her own abilities, but even so, she didn't think she could manage against two people and one creature like Rei, Elena, and Set.

In fact, she doubted she could defeat any one of them individually.

(So, the only option is to run...)

But even that was impossible with Set holding her back.

He had a magic item that reacted to the air and produced smoke for a few minutes, but even if he used it, being pinned down by Set meant it wouldn't change anything.

(So, I guess I just have to stay quiet for now. Luckily, Zuboz is focused on his research and won't come out to see the commotion.)

If Zuboz were here, he would undoubtedly react in some way upon seeing Rei.

If that happened, escape would be impossible.

Adria was confident she could escape on her own if it were anyone else besides this group, but with Zuboz along, she would inevitably become a liability.

It was only natural. Zuboz was originally an alchemist, not someone like Adria who actually engaged in combat.

Even if his skills as an alchemist were top-notch, whether they could be applied in actual combat was an entirely different matter.

Trapped by Set, Adria breathed a silent sigh of relief that Zuboz wasn't here, then spoke.

"Alright. I'll stay put until the guards arrive, so please bring them here as quickly as possible."

"Don't worry. Gilm's guards are quite diligent these days. With a commotion like this..."

Before Elena could finish, the guards appeared, perfectly timed.

Only Ranga, the captain of the guard, and a few others nearby knew about the commotion caused by the alchemist of the Bestir Empire.

Even so, under Ranga's orders, the guards were currently displaying an unusually high level of diligence.

Just as Elena had said, if there was a disturbance like this, they would rush over even more quickly than usual.

"What's the commotion?! ...Rei? Set? And you over there..."

Shouting like that, four guards forced their way through the crowd of onlookers.

Recognizing the familiar faces present, one guard turned a questioning gaze toward them.

"This woman is the target Daskar-sama specifically requested us to capture. More accurately, she's an associate of the target we were ordered to capture, and she likely knows the main target's whereabouts. But... would it be clearer if I said she's the person your captain, Ranga, is searching for?"

Mentioning Ranga's name forced the guards to become cautious.

They had seen it with their own eyes: the usually gentle Ranga had been wearing a grim expression at times over the past few days.

"Captain Ranga... Got it. So should we take this woman and hand her over to Captain Ranga?"

"Ah... Yeah. I'm not really good at interrogations or anything like that. Better to leave it to the professionals."

Rei, who had just said something that would make thieves cry out in protest if they heard it, immediately nodded in agreement at the guard's words.

With Rei's consent, the guard approached Adria, who was being held down by Set.

"Set, you heard him. Could you hand this woman over?"

"Grr."

Having heard Rei's explanation, Set had no intention of defying the guard's request.

After seeing the guard pin down Adria's limbs, he withdrew the front paws he'd been resting on her back.

"Now, now, you're not handling the lady very well. Isn't treating her a little more gently what makes a man a man?"

Adriana, having regained some breathing room once Set's front paws released her, remarked, but the guards paid her no heed.

They understood perfectly well just how dangerous Adria was, given that Set had been able to pin her down.

With all four limbs pinned down, Adria must have realized the guards weren't letting their guard down at all. She suppressed any visible sign of it, but she felt resentful.

(I'd heard Gilm's guards were highly trained and strict about discipline, but getting caught like this really drives the point home. Now then, with Crimson, the Griffin, and even the Princess General here, escaping is definitely out of the question. Best to lie low for a while, I suppose?)

He maintained his nonchalant demeanor, yet inwardly prepared himself mentally to escape at the first opportunity.

"Too bad, but Rei and Set don't make the mistake of underestimating their prisoners. If you just stay quiet, they won't treat you roughly. You'd do well to remember that."

"Yeah, yeah. It's not like I want to be treated roughly either, so I'll behave myself. I'm not going to throw away a chance to prove my innocence just because I feel like it."

Watching Adria deliver this with a hint of a smile, Rei recalled how fast she'd been running earlier.

"That woman... I don't know if she's using some kind of magic item or if she's enhanced her body with some skill, but her escape speed was unnaturally fast. You'd better be careful. Once she gets away, it'll be hard to catch up."

"...Understood. I'll take her to the guard station immediately. Rei, you've been a great help."

Rei shook his head slightly at the guard's words of thanks.

"Don't worry about it. I'm not exactly uninvolved either. Also, there might be some clues at that magic item shop down this street, so I'll head over there."

With that, Rei led Set and Elena back into the alleyway.

Watching them go, the guard immediately grabbed both of Adria's arms and forced her to her feet.

Though they'd tried to remove the magic items following Rei's advice, a mere guard couldn't tell which ones were magic items. For now, they firmly held both her arms from either side to prevent her from escaping.

"Come on, let's go. I've got a lot of questions for you."

"Yeah, yeah, I got it. Well then, let's just get this annoying business over with quickly."

Showing no sign of tension whatsoever, Adria was led away by the guards. As evening fell, she was paraded down the busy main street, drawing significant attention from passersby and onlookers.
