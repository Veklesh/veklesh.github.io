Goshu's guild was, naturally, the first building Rei had ever seen.

Yet it felt somehow familiar, likely because Rei was accustomed to guilds as places.

However, since it wasn't a familiar guild, there were many differences.

In the spaces for carriages and familiars, there were giant lizard-like monsters and camels. The guild walls were coated with a special paint to withstand the sand and intense sunlight.

It was also considerably smaller than the guild in Gilm.

...Though comparing a guild in the sole frontier region of the great nation, the Mireana Kingdom, to one in Solrain—a vassal state of Mireana, and not even the capital city of that vassal state, was fundamentally flawed.

"Set, same as always, then."

"Gururuu"

Set gave a short growl at Rei's words and headed straight for the waiting area.

Seeing Set like that, the lizard and camel froze solid, unable to utter a sound.

Set looked a little lonely, but he probably understood that if he let out a cry here, it would only frighten them more. He lay down on the spot.

The desert sun beat down with relentless intensity, making it impossible to nap in such heat, yet Set seemed utterly unconcerned.

Being a Rank A monster, a Griffin, this level of heat was likely within the margin of error for what Set found comfortable.

And naturally, a griffin sleeping so boldly drew the attention of those around them. But for now, no one dared approach it.

"Rei, let's go."

After parting ways with Set, Rei entered the guild building, summoned by Zarust.

Those who witnessed this were struck by the sight of Rei, who had a griffin as his familiar, doting on it like it was a dog or a cat.

"Huh, so they really do have a tavern attached after all."

That was the first thing Rei said upon entering the guild.

Every guild he'd seen so far had a tavern attached. That's why he wasn't particularly surprised to see one attached to Gosh's guild too.

"Hm? Oh. We use it for all sorts of things—after finishing requests, for meals, for meetings. The food's reasonably priced and tastes decent enough."

"So, once we're done introducing ourselves at the guild, should we head over there right away?"

"If we have time, that'd be fine, but you've got a meeting with Mr. Augusto later, right?"

At Zarust's words, Rei nodded in understanding.

Remembering the meals prepared by Augusto's wife, who prided herself on her cooking, Rei gave a wry smile... but seeing Rei and Zarust like that, the adventurers inside the guild and those eating and drinking at the tavern all wore expressions of astonishment.

And no wonder. Zarust was a fairly well-known adventurer in this town of Gosh.

And here he was, talking on equal terms with someone as small as Rei.

"H-hey. That kid... kid? No, wait, what the hell? He's talking to Zarust on equal terms?"

"Could he be connected to Mr. Augusto? I mean, Zarust did take that urgent request to escort Mr. Augusto's caravan, right?"

"That... could be possible?"

"But still, would that Zarust really let someone talk to him like that?"

"...Damn it. That Zaruust guy has really fallen. To a kid like that."

If they had seen Rei before he joined the guild... if they had seen him facing off against Set, not many would have felt this way.

It was unfortunate, but in a way, it was also fortunate that they didn't have to witness something beyond their imagination.

(This should make them recognize Rei as someone close to me. ...Though it seems a few still harbor strange thoughts about Rei.)

Zalust listened intently to the surrounding atmosphere, judging he'd achieved his primary objective.

It was understood that anyone who tried to mess with Rei would undoubtedly become an enemy of Zarust.

That said, there were indeed those who, as Zarust had anticipated, deliberately intended to mess with Rei... but Zarust had no intention of interfering with that.

If they knew everything and still chose to mess with Rei, that would be their own fault.

"Well then, Rei. I'm going to report the completion of the request... What about you?"

"Right. I want to recruit adventurers to dismantle the Sand Serpent."

"Ah, don't worry about that."

Zalust responded to Rei's words with a simple nod and continued.

"If it's dismantling the Sand Serpent, we'll take care of it. You saved our lives, Rei. Let us repay that debt."

Zalust's mention of saving his life caused the surrounding area to stir once more.

But... at Zarust's words, one man rose from his seat in the tavern with a smile, approaching them.

"Heh heh heh. Hey now, Zarust. You're exaggerating a bit, aren't you? There's no way that guy could take down a Sand Serpent!"

The smirking man had dismissed Zarust's words as lies from the start.

He stood over two meters tall, his body covered in a suit of muscle.

The most distinctive feature was his completely bald head. The shaved scalp bore tattoos that seemed to serve as a substitute for hair.

A scabbard holding a scimitar hung at his waist, giving him the look of a classic power fighter.

"Golcas, huh? I don't recall saying anything untrue. You should ask your master about that, don't you think?"

The word "master" clearly irked the man called Gorkas. Veins bulged beneath his tattoos as he opened his mouth.

"Oh, Zarust. Who do you think I'm some lapdog for? Keep spouting that nonsense, and I'll snap your arm in two."

Golcas glared as he spoke, a look that would make any ordinary person immediately apologize.

But Zarust, facing such a Gorkas, merely snorted derisively.

"Hmph, all bark and no bite. You think I don't know you're hired by the Elema Trading Company?"

"I'm an adventurer. What's wrong with being hired?"

"Nothing wrong with that. Though I do think you should start using your own head a bit more. You think I don't know about the Tira Tree business?"

"That's what I'm saying is ridiculous!"

Golcas reached for Zarust's collar, but his hand stopped moving before it could reach it.

...Yes, by Rei's hand.

"What!?"

Naturally, it was Gorkas who was shocked by this.

The wrist Rei had grabbed felt as if it were fixed to the space itself, unable to move even slightly.

And this was coming from Rei, who looked at least a head shorter than him... maybe even two heads shorter.

Golcas looked back and forth between his wrist and Rei's face, as if trying to understand what was happening.

"Hey, what the hell is Gorkas doing?"

"I dunno... You're not seriously telling me that kid Rei's got you by the wrist and you can't move?"

"Ha! That's ridiculous! He's the one who always boasts his strength rivals Zarust's! If he can't move because some kid grabbed his wrist, that's embarrassing, just embarrassing!"

The whispers around him made the veins on Gorkas's head bulge even more.

It signaled just how much force Gorkas was exerting... Yet Rei, bearing that force, remained utterly still. Though his face was hidden beneath his hood, it was plain to everyone watching.

After all, the reason he was respected in this guild was precisely because he possessed strength that could rival even high-ranking adventurers.

But if he lost to this small figure by force, he would be looked down upon from tomorrow onward.

He absolutely couldn't tolerate that. Golcas clenched his free hand into a fist and swung it toward Rei's hooded face... but at that very instant, a violent shock coursed through his entire body, followed by searing pain.

"Guhhhhhhh! Damn it! What the hell did you do?!"

He realized he'd collapsed onto the guild floor without noticing, and despite the pain, he groaned and pushed himself up.

"Don't you even understand what just happened? What are you, just showing off your strength?"

Those were the words that came out of Rei's mouth.

"What?! You little brat!"

Fury evident, he rose and swung his fist toward Rei once more...

"Stop right there. You really didn't understand what happened? You were thrown by Rei, you know."

At Zarust's words, Gorkas looked at Rei with an expression of disbelief.

The instant he made a move to strike, Rei grabbed Gorkas' wrist and slammed him forcefully to the floor.

It was no technique, just pure physical strength. Precisely because of that, he couldn't have failed to notice such a move.

To pull off something like that, you need the ability to completely overwhelm your opponent.

And Rei had just demonstrated that ability in front of everyone.

Already, the adventurers around them... and even the guild staff... their eyes on Rei were vastly different from before.

(Not quite what I planned... but in the end, it was the best choice.)

Watching Gorkas, who still didn't grasp the situation... or more accurately, refused to grasp it, Zarust addressed Rei.

"Rei, why not show your guild card at the guild reception desk? It's not mandatory, but doing so will prevent you from getting caught up in unnecessary trouble."

"...Alright."

Looking slightly displeased, Rei shot Golcas a glance and headed for the counter.

Even though it was nearing evening, the number of adventurers lined up there wasn't particularly large.

(Well, comparing it to Gilm is a mistake, but...)

The adventurers waiting at the counter silently moved aside when they saw Rei approaching.

Normally, they wouldn't need to do that, but after witnessing what just happened, they probably felt they couldn't say otherwise.

Though he felt the fearful stares from those around him, it was a gaze Rei had encountered countless times before.

He pays them no mind as he heads toward the guild counter.

In this case, the unfortunate one was probably the receptionist where Rei was headed.

Here was someone she'd never seen before, walking straight toward her. And this person had just effortlessly outmaneuvered Gorkas, notorious for his rough ways.

Even so, as a guild employee, she didn't show any emotion... In fact, she even smiled as she addressed Rei.

"Welcome to Goush Adventurers' Guild. I overheard your conversation with Mr. Zarust earlier. I understand you'd like to see a guild card?"

The smile she directed at Rei was charming, befitting the guild's flower.

A sensual smile befitting a woman in her twenties.

Paired with her sun-kissed skin, it was undoubtedly a smile that captivated everyone around her.

The only miscalculation for the receptionist was that Rei, the recipient of that smile, seemed completely unaffected.

But that was hardly surprising. Rei had met Marina countless times—a woman who embodied feminine allure itself, though technically a dark elf.

Compared to Marina, the receptionist before him was clearly inferior in terms of femininity.

"Here's my guild card."

Upon being told this, the receptionist took the card, feeling slightly dissatisfied that Rei showed no reaction to her proud smile.

She glanced at it... and froze.

After about thirty seconds of frozen stillness, the surrounding adventurers who had been watching the situation unfold turned their gaze toward the receptionist, wondering what had happened... but none had the courage to speak up in the current situation.

Eventually, the receptionist started moving again. Looking at Rei's face beneath the hood, she opened her mouth hesitantly.

"If it's not too much trouble... would you mind lowering your hood?"

Unable to hide her nervousness, the receptionist spoke. Rei silently lowered his hood.

What appeared beneath was a face that could best be described as feminine.

Those around who saw Rei's face reacted in various ways.

Many were surprised, but some, perhaps preferring younger types, found themselves captivated by Rei's face.

Still, the lack of any dismissive reactions was likely because they had witnessed the incident with Gorkas with their own eyes.

The receptionist, upon seeing Rei's face, drew a small breath.

As someone employed by the guild, she likely knew Rei's face. After a few seconds of silence, she slowly opened her mouth.

"The one with the alias... Rei of Crimson."
