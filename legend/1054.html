"...So, in the end, Rei was just playing with Set by the river, huh?"

Rei, who had been met with Marina's deadpan stare, nodded while quietly averting his gaze in the garden of the house Elena and the others were renting.

"I suppose that's how it turned out."

"Grrr..."

Seeing Rei like this, Set, who had been playing in the river with him, growled worriedly.

But when Marina's gaze fell on Set too, he quietly hid behind Rei.

It was like a child hiding from a scolding mother.

Marina felt her heart stir slightly at Set's reaction, but she decided scolding Rei came first. She turned her gaze back to him and spoke.

"It was indeed quite warm for spring today, and I understand wanting to play in the river. And if you were splashed with the blood and flesh of the monster that attacked, I understand wanting to wash it off. I wouldn't say anything if you just played in the river a little. But..."

Marina paused, a smile on her lips. To Rei, that smile seemed like a mask, one meant to conceal the very passion burning within Marina at that moment.

"Now, when exactly?"

Marina directed her gaze toward the sky... the sky dyed red by the setting sun, and told him so. Rei looked away again.

...Yes, spread out before them was the sunset sky.

No matter how you looked at it, it was unmistakably evening.

Which meant Rei and Set had been playing by the river for hours.

Of course, they hadn't been playing the entire time since entering the forest—there had been the battle with Aerowings, absorbing magic stones, and trying out the newly acquired Tornado spell.

Still, the undeniable fact remained that they had spent a long time playing in the river, and when Marina pointed that out, he couldn't argue.

"It's evening, isn't it?"

"Yes, evening. About the time we start getting hungry for dinner. ...So, Rei. Got any excuse ready?"

"My bad."

Rei bowed his head.

"......"

Earth, who had been watching the exchange from a distance, wore a slightly surprised expression at Rei's reaction.

Considering all the achievements Rei had made up until now, he hadn't expected him to apologize so readily.

Earth had visited many towns, villages, and cities, and most adventurers tended to be proud.

And a significant number of them couldn't easily admit their mistakes.

Of course, many were perfectly capable of acknowledging their mistakes and apologizing properly.

"Hmph, well, fine. It was hot today, and you couldn't stop Set from playing in the river, right? ...Anyway, about playing aside, was there anything unusual in the forest?"

Marina's words, as she decided to calm her anger for the moment, finally signaled to Rei that the lecture was over.

He immediately began wondering if anything unusual had happened in the forest. But thinking back on what he and Set had done today, it was basically confirming a new skill they hadn't been able to use before and fighting the Aerowings.

If anything unusual happened, it was probably just Set using Power Crush to turn the Aerowings into chunks of flesh, raining down a shower of blood and meat.

He couldn't exactly call that an incident, so after a moment's thought, Rei shook his head.

"Nah, nothing particularly unusual happened. Though you could call it unusual that there were fewer monsters than expected."

"Really? I encountered a few monsters myself. Orcs and such—nothing too strong, though."

Vihera , who had also gone out to explore the forest like Rei, reported, but her tone carried only a hint of dissatisfaction.

She was probably annoyed that, while she did get to fight monsters, they weren't powerful ones.

"I encountered a few goblins, but after I took down a couple, they must've realized they didn't stand a chance and just ran off."

"Pollurru!"

"I took them down with my lightning!" declared Pororu, wagging her nine tails. Everyone, including Marina, smiled.

"But goblins are entering the barrier of confusion too. They probably slipped in during the period before Rei infused it with magic... Troublesome."

Goblins might be synonymous with weak monsters, but despite their frailty, their reproductive capacity is incredibly strong.

It wasn't uncommon for one to become ten, ten to become a hundred... before you knew it.

And the goblins were in the space between the Barrier of Confusion and the Barrier of Protection.

It has a certain amount of space, but it's obviously not vast enough to extend endlessly.

So, what happens if goblins breed there?

Hearing Marina's words, Elena imagined the forest overflowing with goblins everywhere, her expression turning thoroughly disgusted.

(Of course, for goblins to multiply, females are absolutely necessary. In that sense, it's unlikely they'd ever reach such numbers... but still.)

The future for female monsters captured by goblins, or for women from races like humans, elves, beastmen, and dwarves, was so dire that calling it the worst possible outcome would be an understatement.

"Goblins, huh. Killing them isn't the problem, but their numbers are a pain. Plus, the moment they think they're outmatched, they just run off."

The one muttering this with obvious distaste and annoyance was Rei, who had just been lectured moments ago.

Vihera and Elena smiled slightly at his demeanor, acting as if the lecture had never happened, almost seeming to put on a brave front.

"Right. Sorry, but it'd be a big help if you could prioritize taking out goblins from tomorrow onward. And orcs too."

Marina's gaze fell on Rei.

Partly because he possessed the greatest ability suited for one-against-many combat among them, but also for other reasons...

"Needless to say, since we're in the forest, please refrain from using your signature fire magic, Rei."

"Got it. It's not like I can't use any magic besides fire anyway."

In reality, he was a mage specializing in fire attributes, but using the Death Scythe skill to disguise spells as other elements wasn't difficult.

In fact, now that his Flying Slash had reached level five and become extremely powerful, Rei wanted to master it properly if possible.

Greater power meant greater skill was required to wield it effectively.

Rei felt he had a decent grasp on Flying Slash up to level four, but level five Flying Slash promised to be quite a challenge to master.

But... precisely because of that, enemies like goblins, which are numerous, made excellent practice targets.

(Besides, there seem to be quite a few orcs still lurking around too. In terms of gathering meat, I'd rather prioritize taking down orcs over goblins.)

While the Misty Ring still held a massive stockpile of orc meat, both Rei and Set ate more than the average person.

For emergencies, they wanted as much orc meat as possible.

"...Anyway, goblins aside, what about the World Tree?"

At Vihera's words, Marina, Elena, and Ara (the female), who had been moving around the settlement today, all shook their heads in unison.

"Unfortunately, we found no clues. We did examine the barrier enchantment, though, and it showed no signs of being broken. ...Well, it's possible the barrier enchantment was repaired with such precision we didn't notice. ...Or perhaps the damage to the barrier enchantment from yesterday's incident has healed..."

Considering the dazzling light emitted by the World Tree, Marina's words were by no means exaggerated.

Even if the barrier had been slightly damaged, it wouldn't have been strange for the World Tree, possessing that much magical power, to repair it instantly.

That said, if the damage wasn't minor, it wouldn't be difficult for the Dark Elves to find it either.

"No leads in that direction, huh? If that's the case, it's probably best to assume the other clues have been destroyed too."

"Grr..."

Set, who had quietly moved to a spot a little further away, growled in agreement at Rei's words.

While Rei had been scolded by Marina for the river incident, Set had gotten off with only a mild reprimand.

Even Rei found that situation incredibly envious.

Furthermore, although Rei insisted it was Set who started playing in the river, when confronted with the valid point that managing familiars was the tamer's responsibility, he had no retort.

"So, what should we do now? If there were enemies we should defeat, that would be one thing, but having no direct foes to fight like this isn't very interesting."

Ara muttered, sounding a bit dissatisfied.

Being more inclined towards combat, Ara naturally felt frustrated at having no worthy opponent to fight.

"Calm down, Ara. If enemies were that easy to find, the dark elves in this settlement wouldn't have been sitting around doing nothing all this time."

"...True."

Marina agreed with Elena's words, though a wry smile played on her lips.

It was only natural, given she'd been told her own settlement's people hadn't been able to find them.

"So, what are we doing starting tomorrow? I've got a request to investigate the forest's disturbance, and I'd like to resolve it if possible."

Earth spoke while watching Polo and Yellow, who had somehow ended up sprawled across Set's back.

Earth hadn't expected things to get this dramatic in the first place.

He should have only needed to investigate what was wrong with the forest and report back... Despite his words, Earth had no intention of backing out now.

Part of it was his kind-hearted nature, unable to abandon the Dark Elves. But he also saw this as a trial to become a hero.

"Right. ...Vihera, could you have Byune come over tomorrow? Even if we don't notice something, a thief might pick up on it."

"...It'll be harder to track enemies without Byune... But, well, I suppose you're right. Dealing with the World Tree is the top priority. What do you say?"

"Mm."

In response to Vihera's words, Byune murmured a brief "No problem."

(Having Byune join is fine, but will communication be okay?)

Marina glanced at Marina and Byune, and suddenly that question surfaced in Rei's mind.

Currently, only Vihera could communicate fully with Byune.

Even Rei and Elena, who'd known her longer, could grasp some of what Byune meant, but if asked if it was complete understanding, the answer would be no.

And Marina, who had known Byune for even less time, could she really communicate with him? No matter how you looked at it, the answer was clearly no.

"Hey, Marina. If we're taking Byune, does that mean we're taking Vihera too?"

"Huh? Me?"

Vihera, who had planned to hunt monsters in the forest again tomorrow, was completely taken aback by Rei's words.

But she seemed to grasp what Rei meant almost immediately. With a bitter expression, she opened her mouth.

"If you insist, I suppose I could join you tomorrow?"

"...Hmm, I don't know. Honestly, I'd rather not reduce our forces outside. Like I said earlier, there's also the goblin problem."

"It's not like we have to handle the goblins ourselves, right? There are skilled fighters among the dark elves too."

Vihera said this, likely recalling the training ground where Rei had fought Optis. Marina, however, frowned slightly and fell into thought.

(In terms of sheer numbers, it's true that sending dark elves would be quicker than sending Vihera. That's a fact. But...)

She didn't know why. She didn't know, but Marina felt a kind of conviction that it was better not to send the dark elves outside the settlement right now.

A woman's intuition... or perhaps something tied to her lineage connected to the World Tree.

"...No. If possible, I'd rather not send the Dark Elves outside the settlement right now. It's... just a feeling, I suppose."

Under normal circumstances, acting based on intuition would often earn one disbelieving looks.

But perhaps it was a blessing in disguise—most of those remaining here possessed considerable skill, and they all knew firsthand that such intuition could save lives in a pinch.

Even Byune, the weakest fighter in the group, had experience soloing dungeons since childhood—and she was still quite small even now.

For that very reason, one could say she valued intuition more than anyone else here.

Earth, too, having weathered countless crises, never underestimated instinct—nor could he afford to.

"Then, tomorrow..."

"Wait."

Rei cut Elena off before she could speak.

He had sensed something... no, someone running toward the house.

Rei's gaze shifted toward the garden entrance, and the others followed suit.

And most of them sensed the approaching presence just as Rei had... until several dark elf children came running into the garden.
