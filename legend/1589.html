Silence fills the air around us.

All that could be heard was the sound of the wind and the cries of beasts and birds echoing from the forest.

Along with the silence, a palpable tension filled the air.

That said, the tension was felt only by those from the country of Reburina. Rei showed no sign of nervousness whatsoever.

When Rei asked Ryutas what to do with the people standing before them, Ryutas didn't seem particularly tense either.

"Well..."

Asked what to do with the people before him, Ryutas faltered.

Until yesterday... no, until just a few hours ago, if he were still in his previous position, as a member of Jaya, he would have thought it acceptable to kill them as agents of an enemy faction.

But now it was different.

Having concluded there was no future in following the organization known as Jaya, he was no longer part of it.

To be precise, he hadn't actually told his father, who led Jaya, that he was leaving, so Jaya might still consider him one of their own.

(Killing him here would undoubtedly be the quickest solution. But that would essentially benefit Jaya. So, wouldn't it be better to let these guys escape and have them clash with Jaya? ...But then, the problem would be Rei.)

The fact that Ryutas is acting together with Rei has already been clearly observed by the people right in front of us.

Meaning, if we let them escape now, word of that will undoubtedly reach higher-ups.

While Rajalas and his entourage might try to cover it up for the sake of their pride, the soldiers are here on duty. They'd have to report it to their superiors.

If even the soldiers tried to conceal the truth, they wouldn't be able to explain the incident where Rajalas and his men lost their right arms.

(So, whether to let them escape despite the danger... Well, whether it's Jaya or the Kingdom of Reburina, things will definitely end if this continues, so maybe I'm worrying too much.)

Ryutas' prediction wasn't entirely off the mark.

They'd meddled with the suzerain kingdom of Mireana, and that had been exposed. On top of that, the relationship between the Kingdom of Reburina and Jaya was already clear.

No matter how optimistically one might view it, it's simply impossible to imagine both the Kingdom of Reburina and Jaya continuing to thrive as they do now.

If that's the case, then perhaps there's no particular need to deal with the enemy before them... No, rather, as a favor owed to the Kingdom of Reburina, wouldn't it ultimately be more advantageous to let them return quietly?

Yes, that's what Ryutas thought.

Still, hundreds of soldiers were nearly wiped out... While some soldiers still had their lives, they were all injured to some degree, and some had suffered psychological wounds, like the one Ryutas had just knocked unconscious before his eyes.

He couldn't imagine them showing gratitude toward Ryutas and his group after inflicting such damage.

"Well, yeah. Maybe we should just leave them be?"

"Huh?"

At Ryutas' half-hearted remark, the same voice escaped from everyone present except Rei.

They hadn't expected to be let off so easily.

If those present had been people who knew the situation to some extent, Ryutas might have considered trying to extract information from them.

But the people actually present here knew nothing... They were likely sent here as sacrificial pawns.

(Well, if they're going to fight Titans anyway, I doubt they'd send anyone competent. No, there's no way that number could defeat nearly a thousand Titans. ...Was this unit sent with annihilation as the premise? If so, then maybe...)

Suddenly, it occurred to Ryutas: the annihilation of Rajalas's unit here could be used to publicly declare Jaya as the enemy.

And that they might be trying to create a common enemy for the diplomats from the Kingdom of Mireana who came regarding the Jaya matter?

With a common enemy, even if they had various grievances, they might be able to get along... Or even if they couldn't get along, if they showed a cooperative stance in the fight against Jaya, there was a possibility this whole incident could be resolved.

It wouldn't have been strange if someone had thought that way.

(I see. If that's the case, it makes sense why they rushed their forces here. Failure was inevitable... or rather, perhaps failure was what they actually wanted.)

Having vaguely sensed the underlying circumstances, Ryutas slowly opened his mouth.

"Go. I have no intention of harming you. Return quickly and explain this incident."

The first to question the words coming from Ryutas' mouth were not Rajaras' entourage... but his escort soldiers.

Why release us so easily? That was the question on their minds.

If he killed them all here, it would undoubtedly delay the news of this incident reaching the army... the country.

So why release them so easily here?

It wasn't strange for them to have such doubts.

(Something's off.)

He thought it, but couldn't pinpoint what exactly was wrong.

But even if it felt wrong, staying here meant certain death, or at the very least, suffering terrible consequences.

Given that, even if we didn't know the reason, the best course of action was undoubtedly to get out of here quickly.

"Understood. We'll take our leave now."

The soldier addressed Ryutas.

But naturally, when a soldier said something like that here, it provoked a backlash.

"Who the hell do you think you are, some lowly soldier, deciding things on your own?!"

"Do you even know what you're saying?!"

"You commoner think you get to decide what we do? You know what'll happen to you later, right?"

Such discontent from Rajaras's entourage was only to be expected.

Even as his entourage, they were undeniably of noble blood.

They voiced their discontent, wondering why they, of such lineage, should have to obey the orders of a mere soldier.

Their attitude, seemingly having forgotten their fear of Rei just seconds ago, left Ryutas looking utterly dumbfounded.

Rei was also stunned, but unlike Ryutas, he didn't wear it on his face.

Even Ryutas, accustomed to controlling his expressions, must have looked astonished. But for Rei, this wasn't the first time he'd seen such nobles, nor was it anything unusual.

Countless times before, he had seen nobles who seemed like the very embodiment of selfishness.

But being accustomed to it didn't mean he could be tolerant.

Watching Rajaras's entourage viciously berate the soldiers, Rei deliberately swung his Death Scythe.

A sharp strike that seemed to slice through the very air.

Rajaras's entourage surely heard that sound as well.

They frantically looked toward the source of the sound... toward Rei.

Realizing all eyes were on him, Rei flashed a small smile.

What meaning Rajaras's entourage read into that smile, even Rei couldn't say.

But those who saw it felt a chill run down their spines.

"We... we're leaving! We must report this situation immediately!"

It wasn't an expression of retreat or escape; they were leaving this place to report the current situation.

The others agreed with the attendant's words.

They were terrified by Rei, who wielded his blade so easily against a noble who should have been treated with respect.

...Though, to be fair, Rei hadn't shown mercy even to nobles of the sovereign nation of Mireana, not just those of a minor country like Reburina.

For such a man as Rei, the option to show restraint toward those before him simply did not exist.

Once one decided to return with a report... or more accurately, to flee, the rest happened quickly.

Ordering the soldiers to bring Rajalas, who had lost his right arm, and his entourage, he hurriedly left the scene.

He broke into a run toward the carriage waiting a short distance away.

Fortunately, the coachman had not fled despite witnessing the commotion. Rajalas, his entourage, and the guards immediately boarded several carriages and departed the scene.

Normally, he would have looked down on the guard soldiers as commoners, refusing to even breathe the same air as them... but at this moment, having the guards close by took precedence.

"...Well, if you don't want it, I'll take it."

Naturally, having fled the scene in such haste, the tents and such left behind remained untouched.

Even to Rei's eye, these tents, practically brand new, were reasonably high quality.

After all, Rajalas had been put in charge of the unit for this mission, and he'd gone out of his way to prepare these tents for appearances' sake. It was only natural they were reasonably high quality.

Then again, Rei had been adventuring in the remote region of Gilm and had traveled with important figures countless times.

He'd seen many rare items over the years and, without even realizing it, had developed a fairly keen eye for quality.

That's precisely why he thought the tent was reasonably good.

They pulled out the stakes driven into the ground and stored each one inside the Misty Ring.

Being brand new, he worked carefully to avoid soiling them.

Ryutas watched Rei's actions with an expression of utter bewilderment.

"Do you really have time for that?"

"There's no need to rush that much right now, is there? The giant situation isn't particularly worrisome. By now, they might have already finished taking care of all of them."

"...Are you sure it's nothing? They started rampaging, didn't they? Nearly a thousand giants."

"Yeah."

Though he didn't show it on his face, Ryutas was genuinely shocked by Rei's matter-of-fact admission.

Of course, he knew Rei and his crew possessed strength beyond belief.

Still, he never imagined they could handle nearly a thousand giants.

If it had been about buying time, Ryutas might have accepted that.

But what Rei was saying wasn't that. He had clearly stated they would defeat them.

Worse still, they weren't even fighting now—they had already defeated them.

(I had expected it. But... this power surpasses even my expectations.)

Of course, this operation wasn't accomplished solely by Rei's party... the Crimson Wings.

Beyond Rei and his group, another force had joined them: Elena, known by the epithet Princess General.

Even so... from Ryutas' perspective, there was no doubt that Rei's forces possessed strength truly worthy of being called peerless.

(Choosing to surrender to Rei and her group was not a mistake.)

An organization... one connected to the upper echelons of a nation, versus an individual.

By any normal logic, one would undoubtedly side with the former.

Yet Rytas deliberately chose to side with Rei, defying conventional wisdom.

It was a choice that could easily be called reckless at first glance, but now, from Ryutas' perspective, it was as clear as could be that it was the right one.

Feeling that gaze, Rei looked at Byune approaching him and addressed Ryutas.

"So, what do you think we should do now? You let them escape for a reason, right?"

"Of course."

Ryuutasu nodded immediately at Rei's words, a smile spreading across his face as he spoke.

"The soldiers came to this cave, probably to make us lose."

"...To make us lose? Well, none of them seemed particularly capable, including that noble who looked like the commander. If I had to pick, the soldiers themselves seemed reasonably strong."

"Right? Why do you think they'd send such mediocre troops to wipe out the people at Jaya, a crucial base connected to the nation? No matter how crazy the upper echelons of the Reburina Kingdom are, they wouldn't send such scum..."

"Normally, they wouldn't."

As if picking up where he left off, Rei stated.

Ryutas nodded, then prefaced his own speculation with a disclaimer before sharing his thoughts.

It was undeniably plausible to Rei as well, and he found himself nodding.

In truth, Rei had been wondering the same thing.

Why would they send someone of that caliber as a commander?

But if the goal was to make them lose from the start, even Rei could understand that.

"I see. ...Well then, let's head back to the cave for now. The giants should be taken care of by now."

Saying this, Rei headed back toward the cave with Ryutas, Byune, and Yellow.
