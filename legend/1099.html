The food that entered my mouth spread its flavor simultaneously—the tender texture of the thinly baked dough, the juicy meat, and the satisfying crunch of vegetables. A moment later, the sauce overflowing from within the dough asserted itself.

"Damn, this is good."

As Rei savored the taste of Panita, a famous dish from Gochu, he murmured. Across the table, Augusto smiled happily and spoke up.

"Right? My wife's cooking is exceptional. She's especially skilled at panita—honestly, I could eat it every day and never get tired of it."

"Oh, come on, Augusto. You're exaggerating."

"Not exaggerating at all. I always want to eat Kathy's home cooking. Even when I'm working, I'm always thinking about eating it."

"Oh, come on, Augusto. You think about that in the middle of work? You should be concentrating!"

Rei sighed at the sudden display unfolding before her.

(If only this weren't happening...)

This behavior she'd already been subjected to multiple times in such a short span.

No matter how many times he saw it, it made him sick.

But since this was Augusto's house, he couldn't very well reprimand him for it.

Rei could do nothing but wait patiently for it to end.

Fortunately, there was the delicious dish called panita right in front of him now.

Therefore, leaving Augusto and Kathy to their own world, ignoring him, and concentrating on the meal was undoubtedly the best course of action.

Besides the panita, various other dishes adorned the table.

Grilled meat, stewed dishes, stew. And, though it was a bit hard for Rei to believe, there was even steamed fish.

(...Why fish in the desert?)

If it had been dried fish, Rei would have understood.

After all, dried fish was originally conceived as a preserved food, so its presence in the desert wasn't strange.

Of course, transporting it all the way to the desert would make it incredibly expensive.

Even so, if it had been dried fish, he could have accepted it.

But what lay before Rei now was steamed fish.

It wasn't dried fish steamed, but a dish made by steaming raw fish.

At first, Rei's attention was focused on the panita, said to be a local specialty of Gosh. But once she saw the plate placed gently on the table, she couldn't overlook it.

(The only possible explanation...)

Rei glanced at the couple whispering sweet nothings right in front of her, then looked down at her own right wrist.

There, embedded, was the Misty Ring.

Since nothing could rot inside the Misty Ring, it should be possible to transport raw fish.

In fact, the fish Rei had bought earlier in the port town of Emotion was still perfectly fresh inside his Misty Ring.

(But Augusto was surprised when he saw my Misty Ring. If someone here had an item box, I wouldn't expect them to be that shocked. So then... was it the simplified version Elena had?)

Rei recalled the simplified version of an item box Elena had possessed before, but quickly dismissed the thought.

(The simplified version could store things, but whatever was inside should still be subject to the passage of time. If that's the case, then the simplified version is definitely out. So, did she freeze it with ice magic? Hmm, but there are so few mages around...)

As Rei pondered why fish were here—fish he'd never seen before—August, likely noticing Rei's puzzled expression, interrupted his romantic exchange with Kathy.

"What is it, Rei? You look puzzled. Could it be something you can't eat? All of Cathy's cooking is delicious, so I think anyone can eat it, even if they dislike certain foods."

Watching Cathy react with an embarrassed "Oh, no," Rei shook her head.

"Not at all. I've enjoyed every dish she's made."

What came out of Rei's mouth was an undeniable truth.

Watching Augusto and Cathy flirt made her feel queasy, but the description of Cathy's cooking given on the way to the mansion was absolutely true.

Even Rei, who had sampled various dishes, had to admit that.

"Then what's the matter?"

Augusto had been on the verge of getting a little... just a little angry, wondering if he didn't like Cathy's cooking. But from Rei's tone, he understood that wasn't the case. He asked calmly.

Rei directed his gaze toward the steamed fish dish.

"I'm a bit curious about this fish dish. Why is there fresh fish here in the desert, instead of dried fish or something?"

"...Ah, that's what you meant. Come to think of it, I didn't explain that part. It's only natural to be surprised seeing fish served in a town like Gosh."

Augusto nodded in understanding at Rei's words. He reached for the steamed fish, topped with a spicy sauce, using a serving fork. Skillfully, he separated the flesh from the bones and transferred it to a plate along with the sauce.

He placed the fish fillet on a piece of panita dough, rolled it up, and popped it into his mouth.

(Looks delicious.)

Having been told that panitas were typically eaten wrapped with meat and vegetables, Augusto's current eating method was slightly unexpected.

"This fish was caught in the oasis at Gosh."

"...Fish can be caught in an oasis?"

In Rei's mind, an oasis was just a place with spring water—he couldn't imagine any kind of living creature existing there.

That's precisely why hearing the steamed fish before him was caught in an oasis came as a surprise.

"Yes. Of course, it's not like we can catch huge quantities. The amount we can catch is fixed and strictly managed. Still, the fact that fish can be caught in an oasis is truly wonderful."

"...Can they even survive? Well, if they're being caught like this, they must be growing properly..."

"Yes. I don't know when fish first began growing in the oasis. They were already here when I became aware of my surroundings. But for those of us living in Goush, this fish is an extremely rare commodity."

"I suppose so."

When Rei was in Japan, he saw a TV program showing living fish to people living in the desert.

The desert dwellers couldn't comprehend what it was and even felt terror.

Given they were born in the desert, it was only natural... yet precisely because of that, it felt strangely odd even to Rei's eyes.

"August's guests are coming, after all. Preparing a feast is only natural, isn't it? ...I hope they enjoyed it?"

Rei smiled back and nodded at Kathy, who asked with a smile.

Rei had been surprised to see fish dishes served, but the taste of all the food that came out was undeniably delicious.

There was no way anyone could complain about this.

Kathy must have realized that Rei wasn't just paying lip service, but genuinely thought it was delicious. She smiled and turned her gaze toward Augusto...

(If this keeps up, it'll start again.)

The thought of the earlier scene repeating itself right before Rei's eyes was unbearable for Rei.

Trying to stop it somehow, he scanned the room... and the moment he saw the dish, he took what seemed the best course of action given the situation.

"As thanks for the delicious meal, would you try eating this?"

Saying that, he pulled out a small pot from his Misty Ring.

The aroma wafting from the pot was tantalizing.

While the blend of spices and seasonings was unfamiliar to Augusto and Cathy, there was no doubt it was a scent that whetted the appetite.

Cathy wondered where Rei had pulled this pot from, but her husband, Augusto, knew Rei carried an item box.

Therefore, he wasn't overly surprised. Seeing August's expression, Cathy, though startled herself, didn't let it show.

"It's meat from monsters that don't live in the desert. ...Do you know what winter is?"

"Yes, I've heard of it. The weather here is the same all year round, and it only rains once every few years."

Rei wondered how they could survive with so little rain, but concluded it must mean Gosh's oasis was exceptionally fertile and continued his explanation.

"There's a monster called a Gamelion that only appears in winter. Its meat is incredibly delicious and a winter specialty. This dish uses that meat."

"Gamelion, you say? I've heard of it before from another merchant. They say it's a huge, ferocious monster."

"That's about right."

The Gamelion's ears are razor-sharp, and its fangs can crush even flimsy armor. Its sheer size means even a single body slam can be deadly, and above all, being a rabbit-like monster gives it incredible leg strength.

Its movements are swift, and its kicks pack tremendous power.

A newly minted adventurer encountering a Gamelion would likely not survive without extraordinary luck.

That's the kind of opponent a Gamelion is.

Hearing Rei's description of the Gamelion, both Augusto and Kathy grimaced.

Why would anyone deliberately seek to defeat such a vicious monster?

Reading their confusion, Rei ladled Gamelion stew onto nearby plates.

...Normally, a maid would handle such tasks.

But August currently lacked the financial means to employ a maid full-time.

In that sense, hiring Günter as a gatekeeper was also difficult. However, Günter owed his life to August's father—not just once, but twice, three times—and felt deeply indebted.

That's why he accepted the gatekeeper position for low pay. More importantly, with the current situation where the Elema Trading Company and... Daldra are hostile, he couldn't leave only Cassie in this mansion.

Especially this time, even though it was outside the city, there had been an attack likely carried out by sand pirates hired by the Elema Trading Company.

In that sense, having Rei stay overnight at the mansion could be considered hiring the best possible guard.

Anyway, Rei and Kathy cautiously reached for their spoons over the Gamelion stew that Rei had portioned out.

Given how ferocious a monster the Gamelion was described to be, such an attitude was only natural.

They gently scooped up a piece of Gamelion meat with their spoons and took a bite along with the soup.

In that instant, an unprecedented shockwave raced through their mouths.

The soup was rich, made from multiple vegetables simmered until they dissolved.

Tender, yet when chewed, the juices spread throughout their mouths.

Yet the meat retained its natural chewiness, and with each bite, the flavors blended with the soup, making him want to savor it forever.

Besides the vegetables that had dissolved into the soup, the stew also contained substantial pieces of vegetables as ingredients, offering a palate cleanser beyond just the meat.

Augusto and Kathy, who had paused after their first spoonful, eventually came to their senses, swallowed what was in their mouths, and proceeded to take second, third spoonfuls... reaching for the stew again and again.

After several minutes, once they confirmed the stew on their plates was completely gone, Rei smiled happily and spoke up.

"Want seconds?"

He served them more stew as they silently nodded.

Seeing someone enjoy your favorite food so much they can't even speak is incredibly satisfying, and naturally, Rei felt the same way.

"Paniita is delicious, but Gamelion meat isn't bad either, right?"

"That's true. In terms of flavor, the panita certainly holds its own. Especially the panita Kathy made—it's among the best tastes in Gosh. But... this meat is new to me, and many of the seasonings used in this stew are unfamiliar to me."

"I see. This Gamelion meat probably has quite a lot of juices, but I'd love to know what seasonings they use to make it so refreshing in the mouth."

Augusto wore an expression as if he'd spotted a business opportunity in the new seasonings, while Kathy, purely wanting to feed her beloved Augusto something even tastier, turned her gaze toward Rei.

But... unfortunately, all Rei responded with a shake of his head.

"Sorry, but I'm not the one making this dish. I buy it ready-made. I don't know how it's made."

"...Then what about this Gamelion monster meat?"

"I have a bit more leeway with that one."

Though Rei said "somewhat," the Misty Ring contained far more than "somewhat" of Gamelion meat.

It was meat from Gamelions slaughtered in the dungeon last winter—so many it could be called a massacre.

"If it's okay, could I have a little bit?"

"Sure, that'll cover your lodging fees."

With that answer, Rei handed Cathy about a kilogram of Gamelion meat.
