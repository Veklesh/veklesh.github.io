Several days passed since Rei had argued with Melan... Since that day, peaceful times had passed without any major disturbances.

Of course, "peaceful" meant no major disturbances occurred. If you went to the site where a new wall was being built, Marutsu, interested in Rei's magic and magic items, would approach. Or, someone new to Gilm would fall head over heels for Marina or Vihera.

Such peaceful days flowed by, but simultaneously, the relentless midsummer heat continued day after day, steadily sapping the stamina of Gilm's residents.

That said, the members of Crimson Wings—like Rei, who wore a Dragon Robe with simple air-conditioning capabilities, or Marina, who could cool her surroundings with spirit magic—were able to stay cool at night.

Even now, in the garden of Marina's house, the Crimson Wings members were having dinner as usual with Elena and Ara.

Though Elena and Ara were initially surprised by the magic item kiln Rei had retrieved from the Misty Ring, they quickly grasped how to use the cooking appliance and had since progressed to preparing simple meals with it.

During the day, while Rei and the others were out working, Elena and Ara were usually free.

It was a different matter if someone came calling with a genuine reason, but while there were visitors seeking acquaintance, no one had arrived with any important business.

...Though, for the person who came to befriend Elena, that was probably a very important matter indeed.

For that reason, she'd recently started dabbling in cooking to pass the time.

Normally, noble daughters wouldn't cook at all.

It was different if it was a hobby like baking sweets, or if they were poor and couldn't afford cooks or maids.

But... fortunately, Elena and Ara were knights and soldiers.

It wasn't unusual for them to lead subordinates into battle, and they knew the basics of cooking for such occasions.

Thanks to that, we avoided disasters like ridiculously salty dishes or mix-ups like confusing salt and sugar, something Rei had seen in manga back in Japan.

Seasonings like salt, sugar, and pepper cost a fair amount.

If they'd wasted them by mixing them up like in a manga, Marina's wrath would have fallen upon Elena and Ara.

"So... what do you think? I feel like I've gotten pretty used to cooking in this oven now."

Elena asked timidly, watching the members of Crimson Wings eating the whole roasted bird on the table—especially Rei.

It was a whole roast using mountain fowl, but even Elena, who had a decent grasp of basic cooking, knew fundamentals were just that—fundamentals.

The skin was cracked, the meat torn, and the stuffing inside—nuts, vegetables, mushrooms—was faintly visible.

Still, since it was a dish Elena had made, Rei and the others ate it with relish.

In truth, the heat control wasn't too badly mishandled; the meat had a light char on the outside and wasn't raw inside.

While the appearance was that of a beginner's work, in terms of taste, it was a thoroughly delicious dish.

Particularly the sauce, likely made with tart fruit, gave the bird dish a refreshingly clean finish.

Of course, someone truly trained in fine cuisine could probably find plenty to criticize.

But while Rei loved good food, he didn't possess such a refined palate.

Marina and Vihera, given their positions, were accustomed to what could be called gourmet cuisine, but they weren't so uncouth as to mention it here.

"Yeah, I think it's quite tasty. Especially how the acidity of this sauce pairs well with the meat."

"I agree. Though next time, I'd be happy if you stuffed it with more mushrooms inside."

"Oh, really? I think this amount is just right... Well, I suppose it's a matter of personal preference."

"Mm."

Rei, Vihera, Marina, and Byune each shared their thoughts on the dish.

Relieved by the overall praise, Elena let out a sigh of relief.

Ara watched Elena with a smile.

Of course, it wasn't that Ara could cook well to begin with and taught Elena...

Still, the tender smile on her face stemmed from everyone happily eating Elena's cooking.

Seeing Elena, whom she deeply admired, happy was an immense joy for Ara.

"Kyuu! Kyukyuu!"

Elena's familiar, Yellow, was just as delighted to eat the food she had made.

...Beside Yellow, Set let out a disappointed purr.

Of course, there was food Elena had made in front of Set too, but the problem here was likely Set's size.

For a three-meter-long Set, a whole roasted bird was far too small a portion.

Well, if Set had been allowed to eat it all, it might have been a decent snack.

But Elena's cooking had been divided among everyone present.

So naturally, Set's portion was small.

Even so, Set savored that meager portion.

"I'm sorry, Set. I wish I could have made more... but honestly, with my skills, this was all I could manage."

"Grr? Grrrrrrr."

In response to Elena's apologetic words, Set purred reassuringly.

Truthfully, he couldn't eat much of Elena's homemade roast chicken, but plenty of other dishes were laid out on the table, including some prepared for him.

Thinking about it that way, it was a bit disappointing not to be able to eat just Elena's cooking, but it wasn't something worth making a big fuss about.

And so, Rei and the others enjoyed their meal.

Despite the pizza oven nearby on this midsummer night, Marina's garden was remarkably cool.

In many parts of Gilm, the midsummer heat lingered even after the sun had set.

If Rei had felt that temperature directly, he would have described it as a tropical night nearing thirty degrees.

But Marina's garden was currently at a very comfortable temperature.

Subjectively, it felt like just over twenty degrees.

Moreover, the fact that the heat from the pizza oven was completely blocked from the surroundings proved just how convenient the spirit magic Marina used truly was.

That's precisely why Rei and the others could stay comfortable even on such a tropical night.

...Naturally, the horses pulling the carriage Elena and Ara arrived in were also benefiting from this.

Even horses trained rigorously as military mounts couldn't withstand the summer heat.

In that sense, this garden environment, where they didn't suffer from summer fatigue, was probably the best possible place for the horses too.

For those few who could use magic items for cooling like this, Rei and the others were in an enviable situation.

"So, did you find out about the man Ilse mentioned?"

After the meal, while sipping cold fruit juice and tea, Rei asked. Marina swallowed a mango-like fruit and nodded in response.

The juice trickled down her throat, and the sight of her swallowing the fruit was incredibly sensual.

But she didn't seem to notice, opening her mouth to speak.

"Not completely, but to a certain extent. Apparently, it was pretty easy to track him down since he had a very noticeable snake tattoo on his left hand."

"...Well, I suppose so."

Tattoos themselves weren't that rare.

But a snake tattoo coiled around the left arm? Naturally, it stood out.

That's precisely why the man Egg ordered to investigate didn't take long to identify the target and look into it.

Even so, it took some time after identifying the target to actually investigate, and the results only came in today.

"Rank D adventurer, Ajas. A longsword user. As you can tell from his rank, he seems reasonably skilled. However, he hasn't caused any trouble since arriving in Gilm."

"Hearing only that, he sounds like an ordinary adventurer."

Elena murmured while sipping her tea, having listened to the description of the man named Ajas.

"True. His reputation among others seems fairly good too."

"Hearing only that, it's hard to believe he's the one responsible for Ilse's family's death, isn't it?"

"He repented his sins and reformed? ...From what Ilse said, that's impossible."

Everyone present nodded in agreement at Vihera's words.

Whether they understood the mood around them or just nodded along because everyone else was... Set and Yellow, the two beasts, nodded too.

"Right. I wouldn't say there's zero chance of reform, but once someone's tasted the easy life, do you really think they'd just... give it up? Honestly, if he truly regretted what he'd done, he'd have turned himself in to the Guard already."

Of course, since this wasn't the location where that person... Ajax had committed the crimes, turning himself in could mean being executed on the spot if things went badly.

But if they truly... if they genuinely regretted their actions from the bottom of their hearts, then doing something like that wouldn't be out of the question.

Rei stated this, but those listening tilted their heads in confusion.

"Really? Even if they did regret it, wouldn't it be natural to get cold feet if they faced the possibility of dying?"

Vihera murmured in puzzlement, and both Elena and Marina nodded in agreement.

The only one who didn't agree was Byune, but she wasn't even listening to the conversation, instead completely absorbed in savoring the fruit on the table.

"Is that how it is? ...Anyway. If this Ajash fellow hasn't reflected at all, what do you think his current well-behaved actions signify?"

Rei probably didn't believe Ajas had truly reformed either.

He didn't dwell on it further and moved the conversation along.

"Well-behaved, huh? Seems a bit odd for someone with a snake tattoo on their left hand."

"Well, even if I say 'well-behaved,' I don't mean he's looking after other adventurers or anything like that. He's just not causing any extra trouble in the current Gilm and focusing on his work. That's well-behaved enough, right?"

Considering the current situation at Gilm, where many adventurers had gathered, there was a lot to agree with in Marina's words.

Listening to Vihera and Marina's conversation, Rei wondered why he was trying so hard to avoid trouble.

And he quickly arrived at the answer.

"So, you don't want to cause problems and draw attention to yourself right now?"

"I suppose so."

Marina must have reached the same conclusion as Rei's prediction. She nodded while reaching for the fruit again.

"If that's the case, then it's highly likely she hasn't reformed, but is plotting something and trying to keep it from being discovered, right?"

"But... what could he possibly be scheming in Gilm now? I get what Vihera's saying, but Gilm is packed with adventurers... a huge concentration of fighting power! If he tried anything there, it'd just be a repeat of what happened in Lerdact."

Rei's words carried genuine conviction, coming from the one who had actually defeated Lerdact—though not killed him.

If they were plotting something, attempting anything against the current Gilm would undoubtedly be crushed without a fight.

"Hmm, I'm skeptical. Like Rei says, Gilm has gathered forces in every sense right now. Given that, I don't think there's anything we can do about whatever they try to pull. ...Why not have Rei make direct contact with Ajas once, just to unsettle them?"

"...Me?"

Rei was taken aback by Elena's unexpected suggestion.

He had assumed that if Ajas was plotting something, his own contact would raise their suspicions.

"I understand what you're worried about, Rei, but doesn't it depend on how you make contact? If it seems like a chance encounter, I don't think they'd be overly wary."

"But there's still a chance they might react to me making contact, right?"

Rei muttered his words, and Elena nodded.

"But... couldn't Ajas just run away?"

Vihera objected to Rei making contact.

But Marina, who had been listening to Elena and thinking about something, spoke up as if to counter that.

"No, I think it might be good for Rei to make contact. Rather than just swallowing the information passed down from Daskar whole, I think it makes sense for Rei to try interacting directly. ...What do you think?"

At those words, Rei thought for a moment... and eventually nodded.
