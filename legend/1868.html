After finishing their battle with the Black Wolf, Rei and Elena headed straight back to the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion.

The men serving as gatekeepers at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence saw that Elena, who had looked so happy when she left, now had a serious expression on her face, and they assumed that something must have gone wrong on her date.

Still, they didn’t say anything about it—probably because they didn’t want to risk saying something odd and getting scolded by their superiors.

Elena Kerebel is immensely popular within the Kerebel family.

For that very reason, if they carelessly made a mocking remark, there was a possibility they would be severely punished by Elena’s fans.

...In fact, having seen someone who had tried something like that before get nagged by their superior, gossiped about by the maids, and ignored, it was a precarious situation.

For that reason, the gatekeeper, keeping a low profile and remaining silent, simply continued with his work.

Meanwhile, unaware that he was causing the gatekeeper such distress, Rei was in his room at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

The reason he wasn’t with Elena was that he had gone to meet with those in charge of intelligence to gather information regarding the Black Wolf incident.

Even though Rei was on friendly terms with Elena, he had no way of knowing what could be considered confidential information within the Duke of Kerebel’s household, which was why he was waiting in his room until she returned.

Normally, he would have no trouble passing the time while waiting for someone, but right now, he just couldn’t bring himself to do so.

Naturally, Rei understood why he was feeling this way.

(There’s no doubt the Black Wolf incident is still weighing on him.)

It wasn’t someone he hated enough to want to kill.

He wanted to believe that Rei wasn’t the only one who felt they had become close, perhaps through feeding him and such.

Still, since he’d attacked with the clear intent to kill me, Rei had no choice but to fight back rather than let himself be killed without a struggle.

……But honestly, if that were all there was to it, it wouldn’t have lingered in his mind this much.

The problem was, after all, that the Black Wolf seemed to have come from Japan, just like him.

At the very least, he had uttered the words “Yokohama Chinatown”—words Rei had probably never spoken since arriving in Elgin.

And he said he wanted to eat the braised pork buns he used to eat with his mother again.

Given that he went that far, it seemed to Rei like an undeniable fact that the Black Wolf was indeed from Japan.

As Rei was thinking about the Black Wolf, he suddenly heard a knock at the door.

For a moment, he thought Elena might have returned, but the presence he sensed outside the door wasn’t hers.

That said, even if it wasn’t Elena’s, it was the presence of someone she knew.

“Come in, Miranda.”

“...How did you know it was me?”

Miranda, who was in charge of Rei, opened the door and said in a slightly surprised voice.

She probably never expected him to guess who she was before she even introduced herself.

“It was your aura, Miranda. …Did you bring me some tea?”

Seeing the tea, dried fruit, and bite-sized sandwiches on the tray Miranda was holding, Rei smiled happily.

“I envy that ability.”

“Is that so? Well, it’s a skill you naturally pick up if you become an adventurer and keep fighting life-or-death battles.”

“...I wouldn’t want to learn it that way.”

She thought it would be a handy ability to have, but if she were told she had to risk her life in battle just to get it, Miranda would immediately say she didn’t want it.

After all, it’s not a skill she wants that badly.

Rei understood that, so he didn’t seem particularly offended; he just muttered briefly, “I suppose so.”

It’s certainly a skill that a mere maid doesn’t need to learn, though it might be different for a soldier or a knight.

No, it was certainly true that it would be better to have it, but it was only natural that she wouldn’t want to risk her life to acquire it.

“So, since Miranda came here, does that mean she brought a message from Elena?”

“Yes. She said it might take a little while.”

“I see.”

What came out of Miranda’s mouth was, in a sense, something Rei had expected.

Setting aside the fact that they’d defeated the Black Wolf, information that likely belonged exclusively to the Duke of Kerebel’s household wasn’t something to be handed over so easily.

Perhaps those in charge of intelligence aren’t willing to share it willingly, or maybe it’s a procedural issue… or perhaps there’s another problem entirely.

Rei hadn’t expected to get the information right away, so he didn’t get particularly annoyed when he heard the message Miranda had brought.

After all, Miranda was the one who had brought him the message from Elena in the first place, so Rei recognized that taking it out on her would be unbecoming.

“So, she said, ‘Please eat these.’”

“Ah, that’s fine. …Well, it does seem like a bit of a small portion, though.”

What Miranda had brought was a decent amount for an average person, but for Rei, who had a huge appetite, it was nothing more than a snack.

Given that, eating the food Miranda brought wouldn’t be enough to fill him up.

(Well, since she brought it along with tea, she must have intended it as a tea snack, so that’s fine.)

As he thought this, he set the tea and food Miranda had brought down on the table in the room.

“Well then, please call me if you need anything.”

Saying that, Miranda bowed and left the room.

Left alone in the room like this, told to just drink tea and eat whatever he wanted, Rei thought to himself, “I might as well have eaten and drunk with Miranda instead of being alone.”

Even as he thought that, he realized that while Miranda was technically assigned to Rei, she probably had plenty of other work to do as well. He sat down on the sofa and took a sip of tea.

It wasn’t until he took a sip of tea that Rei realized how thirsty he’d been.

Under normal circumstances, this probably wouldn’t have happened, but after the incident with the Black Wolf—even if he wasn’t fully aware of it himself—he’d been thinking about a lot of things… that must be it.

“Could it be that Elena thought about that and prepared tea and such for me?”

Rei muttered to himself, feeling as though that might be the right answer, and took another sip of tea.

“Yeah, it’s good. …And the sandwiches are better than the ones you usually buy at a store, too.”

The sandwiches, likely made by Georgima, were filled with stewed meat and vegetables, and—as a bit of a rarity—dried fish soaked in water, all sandwiched between slices of bread.

The ingredients themselves weren’t particularly rare, but they still delighted Rei’s palate in a strangely delightful way.

Rei couldn’t quite figure out exactly what made them so special. He didn’t know… but there was no doubt about one thing: they were simply delicious.

It wasn’t just the sandwiches.

The dried fruit, too—whether she does something special while drying it or after it’s finished—tastes far superior to anything you’d buy in a regular store.

(Well, with dried fruit… since the flavor of the ingredients comes through directly, it’s probably just that the quality of the fruit itself is excellent.)

Thinking about that, Rei recalled the dried fruit she had made herself back in Japan.

(Huh? Do dried persimmons count as dried fruit, technically? I mean, they are dried, after all.)

There was a persimmon tree at Rei’s house, but it wasn’t a sweet persimmon tree—it was an astringent persimmon tree.

To eat them, you had to remove the astringency, and one way to do that was by making dried persimmons.

Another method involves soaking the persimmon stems in shochu, sealing them in a bag to keep them from touching the air, and leaving them for a certain period to remove the astringency.

Setting the latter aside, since the former—dried persimmons—are actually being dried, shouldn’t they be considered dried fruit?

Since Rei didn’t know the official definition of dried fruit, she decided that since they were being dried, they could probably be classified as such… and suddenly got a craving for the dried persimmons she’d made at home.

Because they’re dried, dried persimmons have a strong sweetness.

Rei actually quite liked eating them with bitter tea.

“Still, there aren’t any persimmons in this world.”

He muttered to himself, and while it wasn’t exactly a substitute for the dried persimmons he’d remembered, he enjoyed the taste of the dried fruit Miranda had brought.

As I savored them... it suddenly occurred to me that the reason I’d craved the dried persimmons I’d eaten back in Japan might also be related to the Black Wolf incident.

Perhaps meeting someone who knew Japan had made her miss her homeland.

(Well, it’s a bit late now… Hmm?)

As Rei was thinking about the Black Wolf and Japan, he noticed a presence approaching his room.

But it wasn’t Elena, nor was it Miranda… it was someone whose presence here he couldn’t fathom.

Soon, that person stood in front of Rei’s room and knocked roughly on the door.

“Hey, Rei. Are you there? You’re there, right? I heard you’re here.”

“Yeah, I’m here. I’m here, so don’t knock so hard. Come on in, Brewitt.”

At Rei’s words, the door swung open carelessly, and a muscular man appeared.

He was one of the nobles Rei had met since coming to Anesis, and—unusually for a noble—he got along well with Rei… a truly rare find.

That said, he was a maverick who, despite being the heir apparent to the Exoris Count family, walked the streets without a bodyguard.

That was probably why he and Rei got along so well.

“Oh, there you are.”

“I’m here, but how did you know I was in my room?”

“Well, I ran into that maid, Miranda. She said Rei seemed a little down, so she asked me to cheer him up.”

“……Miranda, huh?”

Miranda is just a maid.

At the very least, she shouldn’t have known the details of this whole incident.

And of course, she certainly wouldn’t know about Rei killing the Black Wolf.

Even so, she’s been put in charge of Rei, and within the Duke of Kerebel’s household, Miranda probably spends more time with Rei than anyone else besides Elena.

And just as she has been entrusted with looking after Rei—who is being treated as a guest by the Duke of Kerebel—Miranda is by no means incompetent.

No, in fact, it’s precisely because she is competent that she was entrusted with Rei’s care.

That is precisely why Miranda understood that Rei was feeling down, and in order to cheer him up, she mentioned Rei to Brewitt when they happened to meet.

That said, since Brewitt had come to the Duke of Kerebel’s residence specifically to see Rei, in that sense, the outcome would likely have been the same whether Miranda had intervened or not.

“So? What’s the deal? Why is Rei down? That’s not like him at all.”

“I don’t know what to say to that. It’s not like anything specific happened.”

Brewitt had been caught up in the incident when the Black Wolf used drugs to make Rei attack.

Given that, I thought maybe I should tell Brewitt about the Black Wolf—even if just for a moment—but for some reason, I didn’t feel like it.

If this had happened a few days later, Rei might have told Brewitt about the situation by then.

“Huh. Is that so? From what I can tell watching Rei, he doesn’t look like that at all.”

Rei was slightly taken aback by Brewitt’s words.

It was true that he had various thoughts regarding the Black Wolf, but he had no intention of showing it.

The fact that Miranda had seen through him was likely due to her exceptional skills as a maid, as well as the fact that, even though it had only been a short time since Rei had arrived at Anesis, they had spent a lot of time together.

However, Brewitt hadn’t known Rei for very long, and the time they’d spent together had been extremely brief.

Rei’s honest thought was that he never expected someone like Brewitt to see through his true feelings.

“Well, a lot has happened. Really, a lot.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Brewitt didn’t seem to want to ask anything further.

He must have decided that simply saying something had happened, without hiding it, was enough.

“What do you say? Doing stuff in a room like this isn’t exactly a change of pace, is it? Want to spar with me? I’ll show you that I’m not half bad myself.”

With that, Brewitt invited Rei to a sparring match.
