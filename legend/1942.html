Rei and Set, having left the guard post, were walking through the streets, snacking on whatever they could find at the food stalls.

They weren’t doing this with any particular purpose in mind; it was simply window shopping… or rather, a stroll for the sake of eating.

Rei had hoped that, if he was lucky, he might come across information about those involved in the Red Cloth or the Kobold incident, but unfortunately, no one attacked them, nor did anyone flee unnaturally upon seeing them.

...If anything, because Set was there, crowds began to gather, and the area became very crowded.

Earlier, when Rei and Set were heading to the station, people hadn’t had the time to spare to notice Rei’s demeanor and strike up a conversation with Set, but now that they had plenty of time to admire him, they happily fed him sandwiches, skewered meat, and jerky.

Some of the sandwiches were ones Rei had never even tasted before, and he felt a little envious of Set, though of course he didn’t let it show on his face.

In any case, they made their way around food stalls, cafeterias, and other shops selling ingredients, buying up delicious or unusual dishes and ingredients as they went.

「Rei！」

Suddenly called out to in a loud voice, Rei looked up from Set—who was still being petted by a few people—toward the person who had called him.

The person at the end of his gaze was a face Rei recognized.

He had a very stern expression, and to an uninformed observer, he would give the impression of a bandit boss… or perhaps the leader of an underground organization.

But in reality, he wasn’t part of the underworld or anything of the sort; he was one of the most skilled blacksmiths in Gilm.

“Pamidor, it’s been a while. Still, it’s pretty rare to see you in a place like this.”

“Is that so? It’s not like I’m living in the shop or anything. …By the way, I haven’t seen you around lately—were you out somewhere? I heard a few days ago that you’ve been butchering a Giganto Turtle.”

“Ah, so you knew about that. You know, you should’ve joined us. If you had, I could’ve shared a little bit of the Giganto Turtle meat with you.”

Rei’s words must have struck a chord with Pamidor, who was so family-oriented.

He shot him a slightly envious look.

……Though, to an outsider, the envious look on the intimidating Pamidor’s face probably just looked like a threat.

That said, perhaps because the locals know Pamidor fairly well, no major commotion ensued.

There were a few people who didn’t know Pamidol well, and they looked at Rei as if he were being blackmailed, but before they could make a scene, those in the know calmed them down, assuring them everything was fine.

“To be honest, I wanted to help you out too. But I had a last-minute job come in, and I just finished it a moment ago. I can’t exactly just drop everything and help you out, can I?”

“Ah… so you had work. As a blacksmith, I suppose you get jobs even in the winter.”

Adventurers generally don’t work in the winter.

That said, this year there were several jobs, like exterminating Kobolds and dismantling a Giganto Turtle, so many adventurers took on those jobs.

Even so, many still choose not to work during the winter and take a break—but that’s only because they’re adventurers.

Unlike such adventurers, a blacksmith—especially a highly skilled one like Pamidor—has no shortage of work.

If he weren’t so picky, he could even live a better life than he does now.

Pamidor was originally a very famous blacksmith even in the capital.

As for why he went out of his way to come to a remote frontier like Gilm, it was because he wanted to protect as many people as possible with his skills as a blacksmith.

In fact, because the weapons Pamidor forges attract many skilled adventurers, they boast top-tier performance even in Gilm, where there are many skilled blacksmiths.

Whether directly or indirectly, the number of lives saved by those weapons is surely beyond count.

Given who Pamidor is, it made perfect sense to Rei that he would be busy with work.

“Grrr…”

“Hahaha, it’s been a while, Set. Have you been doing well? …Well, looking at you, I can tell you’re doing just fine.”

Seeing Pamidor for the first time in a while, Set purrs happily.

Pamidor was one of Set’s favorite people, too.

Normally, people would often be scared just by looking at Pamidol’s face, but to Set, a griffin, Pamidol’s face isn’t scary at all.

That’s probably one of the reasons Set is so openly affectionate toward Pamidor.

“Well, there’s been a lot… really, a lot has happened, but there’s no doubt he’s doing well.”

Watching Pamidol stroke Set, Rei murmurs.

There was a weight to his tone that made it clear to anyone listening just how much had really happened.

Hearing Rei’s words, Pamidol decides it would be best not to press the matter any further.

Sensing the slightly heavy atmosphere, Rei spoke up, as if to break the ice.

“Speaking of which, there’ve been incidents lately of Kobolds invading Gilm. Is everything okay over there? Your family, I mean. Especially Kumito—he’s still little, isn’t he?”

“Even though she’s little, she’s already helping me out. It can’t be that dangerous, can it?”

“Helping Pamidor?” Rei wondered for a moment, but then he remembered that Pamidor had mentioned something about that before, and he had even seen it happen.

“But just because she’s helping Pamidor doesn’t mean she doesn’t play with other kids in town, does it?”

Even though Kumito had started helping Pamidor, it was still just the very basics.

It was really just a step or two beyond what she used to do.

Naturally, she isn’t with Pamidol 24/7; she still plays with the other kids.

……That said, when Rei first saw Kumito, he was being bullied by a group of kids who wanted to be adventurers.

“That’s true. But I’ve given Kumito a weapon for self-defense, so I think he can handle himself even if a Kobold attacks. Besides, I heard the Kobolds are being held back at the construction site, right?”

“Well, that’s not exactly a lie. …I built an earthen wall too, so the Kobolds can’t just waltz into Gilm.”

Even as he said that, Rei couldn’t help but wonder: Is it really okay to let Kumito carry a weapon for self-defense?

Of course, knowing Kumito’s personality, he was certain she wouldn’t do anything strange with that weapon. He knew that… but there was always a chance of the unexpected. More than that, what worried Rei was whether Kumito, carrying a weapon made by Pamidor, might become a target for shady characters.

Since Pamidor was known as a skilled blacksmith, it was only natural that the weapons he crafted were traded at high prices.

In that context, what would happen if a child were carrying a Pamidor weapon?

If it were just a matter of being hassled by a kid who wants to be an adventurer, like when Rei first saw Kumito, that would be the better-case scenario.

However, one cannot rule out the possibility that even worse types—those who want to turn Pamidor’s weapons into cash—might target Kumito.

Even though he carries a weapon for self-defense, Kumito is still just a child… and not even an ordinary child who’s training for combat with the goal of becoming an adventurer.

“If he has a weapon made by Pamidor, doesn’t that put Kumito in danger in a different way?”

“Hm? Oh, you don’t need to worry about that. From the looks of it, I’ve made sure it doesn’t look like a weapon I made.”

Seeing Pamidor’s faintly confident expression, Rei couldn’t help but wonder, Is he really sure this will be okay?

He wondered whether someone who would attack a child like Kumito would even care about such details.

Kumito is Pamidor’s child, and that child is carrying a weapon for self-defense.

Given that alone, it wouldn’t be strange for them to assume the weapon was made by Pamidor without even checking.

“Are you really sure this is okay?”

Rei asked again, but for some reason, Pamidor nodded with absolute confidence.

(Considering Pamidor’s personality, he’s probably not just a doting parent… If that’s the case, does he really have a plan? No, but… this is Kumito we’re talking about, right?)

Rei’s image of Kumito was, after all, largely shaped by his first impression—the one he had when they first met.

Precisely because of that, even if Pamidol told him that it wouldn’t be a problem if Kumito were carrying a weapon he’d made and someone were targeting him for it, Ray simply couldn’t bring himself to believe it.

But Pamidor, seeing Rei’s reaction, must have guessed what he was thinking.

As if to say there was no problem at all, he spoke up.

“I know what you’re thinking, Rei, but Kumito isn’t the same kid he used to be. He’s worked hard to get stronger, too.”

“……Is that how it is?”

“Yeah. Though, becoming like Rei is probably a bit out of the question.”

“Like me?”

Why “like me”? Rei wondered, but to Kumito, Rei was the one who had saved him when he was being bullied.

On top of that, having heard rumors of Rei’s heroic exploits, there was no way he wouldn’t look up to him. …Though, to be honest, many of those tales were pretty outlandish.

“That’s right. Still, as a parent, I wouldn’t want Kumito to become like Rei.”

“That’s a pretty harsh thing to say right in front of him.”

Even as he said that, Rei wasn’t entirely unaware of Pamidor’s feelings.

It had only been a few years since Rei came to Gilm… no, since he arrived in this world of Elgin. It hadn’t even been ten years.

And yet, considering all the turmoil Rei has been caught up in so far, it’s no wonder he wouldn’t want to put his beloved son in the same situation.

It’s true that Rei’s ability is extraordinary—that’s why he’s come through all those troubles unscathed—but the fact that he’s been blessed with such great companions has also played a huge role.

Set, Elena, Marina, Vihera, Ara, and Byune.

Byune aside, the combat prowess of the others is on par with—and in some cases exceeds—that of those with nicknames.

Furthermore, there are quite a few others who, while not currently traveling with them, have joined them for short periods.

It is precisely because of the cooperation of such people that Rei is still alive today.

“Anyway, speaking of which, lately even Kumito has been going to a dojo or something to train in between jobs. And, though it surprised me, he seems to have quite a knack for it.”

“Is that so? Considering he’s Pamidor’s son, it wouldn’t be strange if he had a natural aptitude.”

Partly due to the muscles used in blacksmithing, Pamidor has a physique that truly deserves to be called muscular and burly.

Also, I seem to recall hearing from Pamidor himself that he’s good at fighting.

If that’s the case, it wouldn’t be all that surprising for his son, Kumito, to have a natural aptitude for fighting.

Especially since he admires Rei and attends the dojo diligently, it wouldn’t be surprising if his talent were to blossom.

"Hearing Rei say that makes me feel a little happy, somehow."

For Pamidor, who has a bit of a doting parent side to him, hearing Kumito praised must be a source of joy.

After that, Rei talked about Kumito for a little while, but then... he suddenly changed the subject.

“Come to think of it, do you remember all the commotion the Red Cloth caused from fall through winter?”

“Huh? What’s with the sudden question? …Of course I remember. A few of them came to my shop and caused a ruckus, so I beat the crap out of them.”

“Ah… yeah. I guess that’s just like Pamidor.”

Thinking that, Rei felt a pang of pity for the Red Cloths who had committed such a reckless act as causing a disturbance at Pamidor’s shop.

They didn’t have to go and do something so reckless…

“Anyway, it seems those Red Cloth guys are at it again. Do you have any information on them?”

To be precise, it wasn’t the Red Cloths themselves but the people controlling them, but he figured there was no need to mention that to Pamidor, so he asked… but Pamidor shook his head with a troubled look on his face.
