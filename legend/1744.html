The day after encountering the bandits and illegal slave traders, Rei and his companions were, as planned, up in the sky.

As Marina had said, Ray had fully healed his wounds from the dark elf incident by the end of the previous day, so he decided to return to the settlement on his own.

Rei was thanked for using the potion, but if he’d been told that the potion was actually used to keep Marina’s angry gaze from turning toward him… I wonder what would have happened.

With such thoughts in mind, Rei looked up at the sky.

There were clouds floating here and there, but it wasn’t exactly overcast.

It was definitely the kind of weather you could call a clear autumn day.

(Come to think of it, we ended up clearing the dungeon in just a few days after all, but if I head back to Gilm now, will I make it in time for Gamelion’s peak season?)

Rei wasn’t even sure if the word “season” was the right one to use in this context.

But Rei’s honest thought was that, given the current situation, it was probably the most appropriate word to use.

Unless something truly unexpected happens, it wouldn’t be surprising if the Gamelion hunt has already begun.

Rei had originally thought he wouldn’t be able to participate in this year’s Gamelion hunt, but since they’d cleared the dungeon much faster than expected, he found himself hoping that even if he returned to Gilm now, he might still be able to join the hunt.

“Grr?”

Perhaps sensing Rei’s unease, Set turned around mid-flight and let out a cry that seemed to ask, “What’s wrong?”

“Nah, it’s nothing. I was just thinking that if we hurry, we might still make it in time for the Gamelion hunt.”

“Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

We can make it in time for the Gamelion hunt.

The moment Rei said that, Set flapped his wings violently and increased his speed.

What made Set do that... well, it was obvious without even having to think about it.

"Ah... did I go a little too far?"

Rei couldn’t help but think so, but since he’d already said it, he resigned himself to it.

Besides, there was still a very real chance they might actually make it in time for the Gamelion hunt.

“Still… it’s pretty boring while we’re flying like this. Though, whenever I say something like that, it usually ends up being a foreshadowing of the carriage getting attacked.”

Just like yesterday, when that illegal slave trader was attacked by bandits.

What’s more, those bandits were the very same gang that had attacked the person carrying the special potion Rei had been asked to deliver the day before, so in terms of foreshadowing, it was like he’d cashed in on a double whammy.

Worried that if something like that happened again, he might end up wasting time needlessly, Rei decided to stop thinking about such things for now and turned his gaze back to the sky.

Perhaps because autumn was turning into winter, the air felt crisp and clear.

Of course, that wasn’t just because it was cloudy today.

After traveling for several hours like that… in stark contrast to yesterday, with absolutely no obstacles in their way, it was nearly noon.

Of course, if they were traveling on the ground, it might be a different story, but since they were flying through the sky, it was only natural that they wouldn’t get tangled up in trouble so easily.

In any case, I spot a river flowing through the woods and ask Set to land there.

In winter, having lunch near a river can be a bit chilly, but although the temperature is fairly low today, the sun is out, so it doesn’t feel that cold.

That is precisely why Rei decided to eat near the river.

So, he lowered the Set Cage near the river, and Rei also stepped down onto the ground with Set.

“Growl?”

Seeing Rei start to prepare the meal, Set growled, as if to ask, “Aren’t you going to catch any fish?”

For Set, eating by the river and catching fish there must go hand in hand.

It might also have something to do with the fact that they caught a large amount of fish in the ocean not long ago.

Rei doesn’t dislike river fish either.

Many of them have a mild, delicate flavor that’s quite different from that of ocean fish.

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu! Kyuuu!”

Meanwhile, next to Set as he tries to catch a fish, Yellow has jumped out of the Set Cage and is chirping energetically.

From Yellow’s perspective, having faced crabs countless times in the sea and rivers, he must be determined to win this time.

“Yellow, calm down a bit. It’s fine to play, but first, let’s eat the lunch Rei prepared for us. Okay?”

Elena tells Yellow this in a soothing tone, and though Yellow looks a little disappointed, he seems to calm down.

It seems he’s been well-trained.

And… seeing Yellow like that, Set doesn’t press Rei any further to catch more fish.

It’s not just a matter of discipline; for Set, delicious food likely holds greater significance than playing.

“Ah, I guess there’s not much time left before I have to part ways with Rei and the others. To be honest, I was pretty envious of being able to eat freshly made food in the middle of a job.”

Watching the dishes Rei began preparing—the various dishes she pulled out one after another from the Misty Ring—Relieu murmured thoughtfully.

Although Relieu usually acted alone, by traveling with Rei and the others, he had already grown accustomed to the considerable luxury of being able to eat freshly prepared meals even outside the city.

...In reality, it hadn’t even been a month since Relieu had started traveling with Rei and the others... yet the fact that words of what a pity it is were coming from Relieu’s mouth like this showed just how impactful Rei’s cooking had been.

If it were just a matter of having a warm meal, they could easily make a simple soup over a campfire, and if they were lucky enough to defeat an animal or monster, they could skewer the meat and eat it.

But… what you can make that way is, after all, nothing more than amateur cooking.

Of course, there are those who have experience working in places like diners, but even for them, their skills fall far short of those of a professional chef who has trained for years to master the craft.

In fact, to enjoy a meal on par with what Rei prepares outside the city, simply bringing a cook along… isn’t enough.

You’d need to carry the cooking equipment itself, and while that might be feasible for nobles or wealthy merchants with money to spare, it’s simply impossible for an ordinary adventurer.

...A high-ranking adventurer with a nickname like Relieu would likely have the wealth and influence to pull something like that off... but then, if it came down to a fight, they’d have to protect the cook, which would be difficult for Relieu, who operates solo.

“I suppose that’s one way to look at it. Why don’t you head to a labyrinth city like Exil and explore the dungeons? If you do, maybe—and I mean just maybe—you might even find an item box.”

Rei suggested this as he ladled the hearty vegetable and bacon soup into their bowls.

That said, even Rei, who made the suggestion, didn’t really expect Relieu to accept it.

If they were to go to a labyrinth city, given how devoted a husband Relieu is, he couldn’t possibly go alone.

That meant he’d have to bring his wife, Shumine, along as well. But for ordinary people, traveling is physically demanding, and above all, there was the question of whether she could adapt to a labyrinth city.

In terms of the number of adventurers, there isn’t much difference between a labyrinth city like Exil and the frontier town of Gilm.

However, when it comes to the quality of the adventurers, there is a significant difference.

While I wouldn’t say all of them, the vast majority of adventurers who come to Gilm possess a certain level of skill. …That said, with the expansion work currently underway, there’s a severe labor shortage, so you can see quite a few people who aren’t adventurers—people who would normally never make it all the way to Gilm—hanging around.

In comparison, since a labyrinth city has a dungeon within its walls, entering it is undoubtedly very dangerous, but the city itself isn’t necessarily located in the frontier.

Naturally, the number of people gathering there increases, and among them are petty criminals who may lack strength but are cunning.

That said, in that sense, it means there are skilled criminals in Gilm as well… but since that’s the same no matter where you go, Relieu probably figures Gilm is better because the proportion of such people is still lower there.

“Considering Shumine’s safety, I’m a bit hesitant… I certainly can’t go alone. If I had a partner like Set, I could freely come and go between the Labyrinth City and Gilm, but I don’t have any talent as a Tamer.”

“Just being known as ‘Gale’ and being a high-rank adventurer is already incredibly rare among adventurers. Adding Tamer talent on top of that… honestly, what do you think?”

“Even if you say ‘what do you think,’ isn’t that kind of how your leader is?”

It consists of mashed steamed potatoes seasoned with salt and pepper, mixed with finely shredded dried meat, and boiled, finely chopped vegetables that resemble spinach—though they’re purple.

It doesn’t use mayonnaise or anything like that, but it’s a dish similar to potato salad.

Also, while I don’t know what secret ingredient is in it, it has a citrus-like tartness, making it a somewhat unusual dish.

Rei wasn’t the only one who thought so. Elena, while eating that potato salad—though strictly speaking, it wasn’t quite that—spoke up with a hint of admiration.

“This dish is delicious. Rei, do you remember where you bought this?”

“Hmm… I don’t really remember.”

Rei’s Misty Ring contains a massive amount of food.

Of course, the amount decreases as he eats it every day, so he has the nearby cafeteria prepare batches of food for him to buy.

In other words, because so much food comes in and out of his Misty Ring, he doesn’t really remember where he bought this particular dish.

Of course, if it’s a dish that leaves a strong impression, like udon, that’s a different story.

"Huh? So, is this dish no longer available?"

Vihera was a little shocked, probably because that potato salad was so delicious.

Next to Vihera, Byune also lowered the corners of her eyes slightly, wearing a look of shock.

Given that Byune is usually expressionless, the fact that she actually changed her expression—not just the atmosphere—suggests just how much of a shock Vihera’s words were.

As if to calm the two of them down, Rei shook his head to indicate it was no problem.

“I forgot where I bought it, but there’s still quite a bit left, so don’t worry.”

At Rei’s words that there was still a decent amount of potato salad left, Vihera and Byune… no, everyone else looked pleased as well.

The only one who didn’t share their joy was Relieu, who would be parting ways with Rei and the others once they returned to Gilm. She looked disappointed, knowing she couldn’t eat any more.

“Hmm, there are all sorts of subtle secret ingredients, but I don’t think it would be impossible to make something similar? Though, it would only be a rough approximation—it’d probably be several notches below this.”

The one who said this was Marina, who was the best cook among them.

Of course, the others could cook to some extent as well, but it was truly only to a limited degree, and they were nowhere near Marina’s level.

“Really!?”

“Yes. But like I just said, I think the taste would be several notches below this. …It wouldn’t be as good as a professional’s, after all.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Rei suddenly recalled a time back in Japan.

A friend’s older sister, who was famous for her cooking, had tried to recreate the delicious taste of a dish she’d eaten at a restaurant… but just as Marina had said, in the end, she could only produce a dish that was several levels inferior in taste.

Even though she was famous in the neighborhood for her cooking skills, she was told when Rei tasted her dish that she still couldn’t hold a candle to a professional.

...Though, as far as Rei was concerned, he thought the dish was more than delicious enough.

“Still, if you can make something similar, if you keep making small improvements… eventually, you’ll be able to get the same flavor, won’t you?”

“Oh, Vihera. Does that mean you want me to just keep cooking forever? Well, since I’ve resigned as Guild Master and returned to being an adventurer, it’s not impossible…”

Just because it wasn’t impossible didn’t mean she could be expected to do nothing but that, so Vihera gently averted her gaze from Marina and continued eating her potato salad.
