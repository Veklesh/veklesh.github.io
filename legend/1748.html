“Hey, Rei! Over here! Look, could you take this log away? Please! They keep cutting down trees one after another, and I’ve got nowhere left to put them.”

An adventurer shouts this to Rei, who has arrived at the Forest of Treants riding on Set.

Rei dismounted from Set, but tilted his head at the adventurer’s words.

“Really? From what I can see, I think there’s plenty of other space to put them.”

After all, since they were cutting down the trees growing in the Treant Forest, it wouldn’t be strange for the cleared area to be expanding as they went.

But… even though the trees were being cut down, there were many places where the stumps and roots were left untouched.

Normally, they would need to pull those out as well… but with winter approaching, the priority was to cut down as many trees as possible, so the work of digging up tree roots had been kept to a minimum.

As a result, it turned out to be faster to haul away the felled trees than to dig up the roots, which meant they were running out of space to store them.

“These tree roots have some use, don’t they?”

“If you ask whether they have a use, well, they do. But we won’t have time to dry them in time for winter firewood… and besides, there are all sorts of other hassles involved, so it inevitably gets put off.”

“...I see.”

Of course, that’s only true if you’re talking about letting them dry naturally.

For people like wizards and alchemists, turning tree roots from green wood into usable firewood isn’t exactly a difficult task.

But… many wizards and alchemists are currently extremely busy with the expansion project.

At first, there were other adventurers who envied the high rewards magicians and alchemists received, but seeing how exhausted the magicians and alchemists look when they come to the guild to collect their pay, they end up thinking, “No matter how good the pay is, I’m glad I’m not a magician…”

Especially now that winter is approaching, and since they’re being pushed to their absolute limits, wizards and alchemists simply don’t have the time or energy to dry green wood and turn it into firewood.

In other words, under the current circumstances, there’s absolutely no use in digging up stumps.

Figuring there was no point wasting effort on such a futile task, I’d been limiting myself to pulling out only the stumps that were absolutely in the way—the ones that hindered movement or made carrying wood impossible.

“...It’s in the way.”

Naturally, given that approach, while they could walk, the stumps scattered at their feet were undoubtedly a hindrance.

It was only natural that Rei, as he walked, would mutter something like that.

“Ah, sorry about that. But once you get used to it…”

It’ll get easier to walk.

The adventurer was about to say that, but Rei cut him off and spoke up.

“We don’t really have to leave these stumps here, do we? From what I heard earlier, you’d like to do something about them if possible, but you’re leaving them as is because logging takes priority right now.”

“Well, yeah, that’s true…”

Even as he said that, the male adventurer cast a hopeful glance at Rei.

Actually, it wasn’t just that male adventurer looking at Rei with expectation; the other adventurers standing guard around the woodcutter were doing the same.

Dealing with the stump is quite a difficult task.

Perhaps Rei could handle it with ease… Faced with those hopeful gazes, Rei nodded simply and spoke up.

“Alright, I’ll handle it. But I’ll only be working on the soil to make it possible to pull the stump out right away. Is that okay with you?”

“Um, hold on a second. Could you show me how you plan to do it first? If it looks okay, I’ll go tell the woodcutters.”

Even as he said this, he could easily predict that if he really could dig up the stump so easily, the woodcutters wouldn’t dare object.

In fact, the countless stumps were a huge nuisance.

It takes a tremendous amount of effort to dig them up, so they’d been left as they were… but if that effort could somehow be minimized, it was only natural to want to do something about the stumps.

With the expectant gazes of the surrounding adventurers fixed on him, Rei drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring.

Some of those watching Rei cast suspicious glances her way.

Of course, they understood just how powerful a weapon Rei’s Death Scythe was, and that it was a tool capable of easily taking a person’s life.

They understood that… but even so, they couldn’t fathom why he was pulling out the Death Scythe right here.

They wondered if he might use the Death Scythe to dig up the stumps, but while he might manage one or two, it seemed impossible that he could handle the entire expanse of stumps spread out before them.

However, some of the adventurers knew that Rei’s Death Scythe served as a magic catalyst—essentially, the same role as a wand for a mage—and those individuals, in fact, wore expressions of understanding.

Despite being the center of attention, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered. He lightly thrust the tip of the Death Scythe into the ground…

“Terrain Manipulation.”

He said nothing more than that brief command.

Yet the result of that single word was immense.

The stump in front of Rei rose up into the air.

No, that’s not exactly right. It would be more accurate to say that the ground where the stump had been rose up about a meter.

If Rei had been serious, he could have raised the ground much higher, but since he only needed to deal with the stump, there was no need to go that far.

The tree roots, which had extended deep into the ground, were forcibly displaced by Rei’s terrain manipulation. As the stump was lifted, most of them snapped off inside the soil.

……With the roots—the main reason the stump had been stuck in the ground—now gone, and the ground itself lifted by the terrain manipulation… yes, it had the softness of a freshly plowed field.

“Right.”

Uttering a short exclamation, Rei swings the Death Scythe.

It looked like a light—truly light—swing, but the Death Scythe weighs nearly 100 kg.

That much weight was swung by Rei’s sheer physical strength.

With the roots severed and the soil softened, there was no way the stump could withstand such a blow.

As a result, the stump was effortlessly blown away by the single swing of Rei’s Death Scythe.

(Well, using this method doesn’t completely remove the tree roots—they’re still in the ground… so in the worst case, it might grow back from there, but that won’t happen immediately; it’ll definitely take some time. At the very least, it shouldn’t be a problem while we’re working on the expansion.)

I used terrain manipulation again to flatten the raised ground.

“That’s how it’s done. I just blew the stump away with a single strike, but since the roots are severed and the soil is soft, you should be able to dig it up easily by hand. …What do you think?”

“What do you think?” The male adventurer, who had been staring at him, finally snapped back to reality at those words.

He had been completely captivated by the results, which exceeded his expectations.

“What do you think? No, I can’t say anything other than ‘amazing.’ …I get it. With this, we should be able to dig up all the stumps without taking too much time. I’ll go talk to the lumberjacks right away, so wait here.”

No sooner had he said that than the man left Rei behind and ran deep into the forest—toward where the lumberjacks were cutting down trees.

Watching him go, Rei decided that leaving the stump he’d just blown away where it was would be in the way, so he carried it over and placed it next to the felled tree.

Several people looked on in surprise as Rei held the sturdy stump—its roots still extending quite a distance—with just one hand.

Since the stump still had roots extending from it, it should have been quite heavy.

Even those who had seen him effortlessly blow it away with the Death Scythe probably never expected Rei to possess that much strength.

“U-um. Hey… was that… lifting the stump right out of the ground… really magic?”

“Hm? Oh, yeah. Earth magic.”

There was no way I could tell them the truth—that it was a Death Scythe skill I’d gained through Beast Magic—so I brushed it off as usual.

“Since you have a nickname like ‘Crimson’… oh, no, sorry.”

The man trailed off, but Rei understood what he was about to say.

The reason for that nickname was that she had used a firestorm to burn the Bestir Empire Army to the ground.

From that, he must have assumed that Rei couldn’t use any other kind of magic.

(Well, that’s actually correct.)

In fact, Rei specializes in fire magic and cannot use magic of any other element.

However, thanks to a skill on the Death Scythe, it was entirely possible to make it appear as though he could use magic of the earth or wind element.

Yes, just like right now.

“Hey! I went to ask! They said it’s okay! Actually, they said if you can do it, they’d really appreciate it!”

The adventurer from earlier runs back.

Nodding at those words, Rei told the adventurers around him to gather near him, asked Set to fly up and keep watch, and then thrust the tip of the Death Scythe into the ground.

“Terrain Manipulation”

The words that came out of Rei’s mouth were the same as before.

But… the event that occurred the moment those words were spoken was on a scale incomparable to what had happened earlier.

The ground in the entire surrounding area—everywhere there were stumps—began to rise.

Indeed, the sound of tree roots being torn apart could be heard by Rei and the adventurers, even through the soil.

“Whoa…”

Faced with that utterly overwhelming sight, everyone who had gathered around Rei let out a cry of astonishment.

To be honest, it would be fair to say they were repelled by the bizarre sight.

Rei, too, hadn’t expected the sound of tree roots being torn apart to be so unmistakably audible; even though he’d done it himself, he was slightly taken aback.

“Ah… Well, at least now we can pull the stumps out by hand… Right, for now, just pile them up where the felled trees are.”

At Rei’s words, the adventurers finally snapped out of their daze and sprang into action.

Though they were a bit hesitant, they reached for the stumps buried in the mounded earth and gently pulled.

At first, it didn’t budge, but with just a little more force, the stump was pulled out of the ground almost effortlessly the next moment.

Of course, since they’d removed it from the soil that had been supporting it, the full weight of the stump now rested on the adventurers’ hands.

Still, everyone present was an adventurer. Supporting a stump was no big deal.

The tree roots, which had burrowed deep into the ground, had snapped, and the soil wasn’t the kind that had been trampled hard; it was almost indistinguishable from the soft, freshly tilled soil of a field.

“Wow, it really came out easily.”

As one of the adventurers muttered this, the others began pulling out stumps one after another.

It must be amusing to them that a stump with such sturdy roots could be pulled out so easily.

Everyone pulled out stump after stump...

“What the—?!”

Suddenly, a loud shout rang out around them.

When Rei looked in the direction of the voice, he saw several lumberjacks standing there.

They must have been able to see this scene through the gaps between the trees.

But even so, most of it must have been hidden by the trees, making it impossible to see.

But now that they’d actually come out, there was a massive pile of dirt right in front of them—it would be impossible not to be surprised.

Then, one of the woodcutters spotted Rei and rushed over, his face pale with shock.

“Hey, Rei! Is this your doing!? Isn’t it!?”

He was practically accusing him outright, but since it was indeed the case, Rei didn’t deny it.

“Yeah, that’s right. It’s my doing. Those stumps were getting in the way. Or do you think I should have left them alone?”

“No, well… it’d be easier without the stumps, but…”

Perhaps finally calmed by Rei’s words, the woodcutter looked around again, looking somewhat flustered.

There, many adventurers were pulling stumps out of the ground and carrying them over to the felled trees.

“Ah… yeah. It’s helpful that you’re taking the stumps, but what to do with them is the problem, isn’t it? They’re in the way no matter what we do. …Rei, I’m sorry to ask, but could you take them somewhere and burn them?”

“Are you sure? I think they could be used for firewood.”

At Rei’s words, the woodcutter looked around… especially toward the forest where they had come from, where trees still stood.

“Yeah. There are this many trees. To be honest, the stumps are nothing but a nuisance. If Rei takes care of them like this, our work efficiency will definitely go up.”

“I’m glad to hear you say that. …But, come to think of it. Does that mean I can take all those stumps?”

“Huh? Well, I don’t mind, but… what are they good for?”

“Well, I have all sorts of uses for them. …Yes, all sorts.”

Saying that, Rei flashed a meaningful smile.
