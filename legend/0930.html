Though Rei used the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, time passed without the slightest reaction.

Of course, melted snow becomes water, and if Rei were to leave in that state, the water would freeze again under the winter night's conditions...

"Warm...? Is this... Rei-san?"

Whatever the reason, if he could do this much, it was nothing short of incredible.

He couldn't control it with precision yet, but he had reached a point where he could manipulate the temperature to some extent.

At the very least, it was fortunate that, as Rei had mentioned earlier, it hadn't resulted in instant incineration.

"This... is incredible. Rei, is this magic power?"

"Yeah. It's my magic power condensed, compressed... well, something like that, made visible."

"What kind of... what kind of effect does it have!?"

"Simply put, it's for physical enhancement and such."

There are other attack methods too, but those aren't things I can discuss here.

(...Hm?)

Rei, who had been talking with Samaloon, suddenly felt eyes on him.

Swiveling his head quickly in that direction, he found not an entity targeting Rei... but a small bird.

Its clearly displeased demeanor mirrored that of Erinde, who controlled the bird.

And no wonder.

It was late at night, the perfect time to relax with a glass of hot wine and get ready for bed... when suddenly, within the academy grounds, she sensed an enormous surge of magic.

Her relaxed mood vanished instantly. Could it be the enemy...? Had whoever tried to poison Rei with an arrow during the day set something else in motion? Calming her nerves, she headed for the Headmaster's office and sent her familiar bird.

...Incidentally, this bird created by Erinde naturally retains perfect vision even at night.

Using its excellent vision to check the location where the magic power was detected, it found not an enemy... but Rei herself, the one being targeted.

Samaloon was right beside Rei. Just as I wondered if Samaloon might be the enemy's pawn, I saw them conversing amicably.

Erinde wasn't the type to not get angry over something like this.

Erinde's anger was now reflected in the little bird's state.

"Cheep cheep cheep cheep cheep cheep"

Under normal circumstances, it might have been considered a pleasant chirp, but the little bird now looked visibly displeased.

This was no attitude to be brushed off lightly.

"Ah... um, I'm sorry, Headmaster. It was a bit of a mix-up, I guess. I never thought it would come to this..."

"Tch, tch-tch-tch-tch-tch! Tch-tch-tch!"

Neither Rei nor Samaloon, who was probably being scolded, had the slightest clue what the little bird was saying.

Still, they understood perfectly well that Erinde was furious, and they turned to look at each other, wondering what to do.

They exchanged silent glances for several seconds.

In the end, it was Samaloon who lost and looked away first.

Given that Samaloon already held a deep respect for Rei, he probably knew he'd lose if he tried to stand his ground.

Quietly... careful not to provoke the bird, he moved closer to the tree.

The snow melted under Rei's Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, posing no hindrance to their walk.

In fact, the surrounding temperature had risen to nearly twenty degrees Celsius.

From temperatures that felt like snowfall, it suddenly became spring... or even early summer, and the steam from the melted snow made it feel muggy.

Having moved close to the tree, Samaloon bowed deeply to the little bird.

"My apologies, Headmaster. I was just curious to see a little of Rei-san's skill..."

"Tch tch tch tch!"

Rei couldn't understand what the little bird was saying, but both Samaloon and Rei could clearly tell it was angry.

"Tee-tee-tee-tee-tee!"

The little bird chirped angrily once more...

Was it just his imagination? Wondering, Rei watched the exchange between the bird and Samaloon.

"Tch, tch-tch-tch!"

Pointing its beak somewhere... specifically toward the headmaster's office, the little bird chirped.

While it might normally be described as a cute chirp, perhaps due to Erinde's current bad mood, despite its tiny body, it carried a certain intensity.

Samaroon, met with a gaze so sharp it could easily be mistaken for that of a bird of prey, reluctantly turned his face toward Rei.

"Rei-san, I'm terribly sorry, but could you come with me? The headmaster is calling for you."
