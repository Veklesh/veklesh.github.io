Of all the men, the one who approached Rei and the others first was, naturally, the man who was standing closest to them.

It was the man who had been having a pointless conversation with Rei just moments ago.

Holding the longsword in his hand, he approached Rei and swung it down with all his might.

There was no technique or experience behind that strike; it was really just a simple swing.

That said, while one might expect it to have above-average speed, it was actually a very slow strike.

Naturally, Rei wasn’t about to take such a blow without a fight; he easily dodged it and countered by punching the man in the face.

It was a controlled strike, meant to spare his life so he could extract information.

Still, it had enough force to break his nose, and the man was knocked flying... but the moment Rei landed a counterattack on the next man’s strike and sent him flying, the sight that met his eyes made him widen his eyes in shock.

That was because the man he’d knocked down first was calmly getting back to his feet.

He had held back when he struck him, but not so much that the man could get back up so quickly... and yet, he didn’t recall holding back enough to allow the man to attack Rei again without any hesitation.

(What the hell?)

With that question in mind, Rei dodged the blow from the third man wielding a club, then clenched his elbow with all his might, shattering the joint.

“Guaaaaaah!”

Even with his elbow joint shattered, the man showed no sign of caring and swung his left, still-intact hand at Rei.

Rei dodged the blow and grabbed the club still in midair... but in the next instant, he moved the club in front of his body to use it as a shield.

The next moment, the spear pierced the torso of the man holding the club from behind and was thrust straight toward Rei.

This attack, too, lacked any sharpness whatsoever, but if a spear could feel sharp immediately after piercing a human body, it would be close to top-tier.

In any case, though he was surprised by the strike from a man who had mercilessly blinded his comrade—and not only that, but had even gone so far as to sacrifice his life—the spear could pierce a human body, but it was impossible for it to pierce the club Rei held, and so it was stopped.

But… despite being in that state, the man whose torso had been pierced even managed a smile as he swung his still-moving left fist down at Rei.

That strike, which pierced his torso—specifically the solar plexus—was undoubtedly a blow worthy of being called a fatal wound.

And yet, the man continued his attack as if he felt no pain at all.

On the contrary, Rei’s eyes caught him trying to clench his right arm—which should have been shattered and useless by now.

(I see. So, these guys don’t feel any pain at all. No, it’s not just that—they’re completely insane.)

Rei was surprised at first, but now that he understood the nature of his opponents, there were plenty of ways to deal with them.

He swung the club forcefully, the spearhead still embedded in it.

Naturally, the man with the spear lodged in his solar plexus was thrown off balance by the spear’s movement.

“Gah!”

Using only the movement of his wrist, Rei forcefully twisted the club, dislodging the spearhead that was stuck in it, and then brought the club down hard onto the fallen man’s left knee.

Just as it had been futile for him to attack Rei with his right arm earlier, these men were insensitive to pain… no, they didn’t feel it at all.

But that’s just because they don’t feel pain; they can’t move even if they want to.

If his elbow were to shatter and he immediately recovered to strike back, dealing with him would be a hassle.

However, these opponents showed no such signs; if physically destroyed, they would be unable to move.

Once he realized that, dealing with these men was no longer a difficult task for Rei.

Using his club, he dealt with them one by one—the man who attacked with a spear, the man he’d punched first and broken his nose, and so on.

He took them down one after another, and by the time he had defeated the fifth man, not a single one of them was left standing.

Although Rei had only taken down five men—roughly half of the attackers—the battle was over because Set and Brewitt had also been defeating the men.

That said, since the attackers hadn’t even glanced at Set or Brewitt, focusing their attacks solely on Rei, it had been easy for them to take them down.

“Still… they were really creepy, charging straight at Rei the whole time. Were they on some weird drug or something?”

“Probably what Brewitt said is correct. Or maybe, even if it wasn’t drugs, they were being controlled by some kind of magic item or spell.”

Some had lost consciousness; others were still conscious but couldn’t move properly because their joints had been shattered; and some were dead.

Looking at them, Rei muttered.

Unless there was actually a reason like that, this situation was just too eerie in every way.

Even with their joints shattered, they felt no pain whatsoever, their movements remained unhindered, and they continued to attack Rei without pause.

There was no doubt that this was a state that could only be described as abnormal.

“...This is a pain. At first, I thought I was the target, but I don’t like the fact that it turned out to be Rei. And given the state of things... I don’t think we can just leave them be, can we? We’ve already killed a few of them, too.”

At first, Blueitt had thought that if they simply knocked out the attackers—or, in some cases, broke a bone or two to sap their will to fight—they would flee on their own.

That’s what Brewitt had thought, but now that he’d killed several of them—and not just broken a bone or two, but shattered or snapped the joints of their limbs to immobilize them—he couldn’t just leave them like this.

At the very least, he’d need to bring in some guards and explain the situation.

“This has turned into a real hassle,” Brewitt muttered, scratching his head.

“Sorry to drag you into this mess. I was actually planning to be the one saving you.”

When he first spotted the men lying unconscious right in front of him, Rei realized that their target wasn’t him or Set, but Brewitt.

In fact, many of the festival attendees had seen the mock battle, and even those who hadn’t would likely know how it ended.

To pick a fight with such opponents—well, with only about ten of them… no, since they’d come here with the intent to kill, “challenge them to a fight” might be a more accurate way to put it—but in any case, Rei couldn’t imagine them doing something like that.

And to pick a fight while seeing Set right there with Rei? By any reasonable standard, that would be nothing short of suicide.

“Ah, well, don’t worry about it. Under normal circumstances, they wouldn’t think to target Rei. Anyway, before I go call the guards, let me ask you this: do you have any idea why these guys might be after you?”

“If you ask me if there’s a reason, there definitely is.”

Rei, for his part, never really thought he was well-liked by the nobles to begin with.

What’s more, among the nobles watching today’s mock battle, there was Gaiska, who was glaring at him with hatred in his eyes.

But… even so, Rei didn’t think they’d be able to do anything to him.

(In that case, the most suspicious thing is… this gaze, after all?)

A gaze that carries no trace of murderous intent, only a sense of unease.

Since he can still feel that gaze, it’s easy to surmise that the person behind it is likely the mastermind behind this whole scheme.

That said, the question of what purpose there could possibly be in making people like this attack him still remained.

(Did they just want to see how I fight? No, that would be strange in its own way. If they just wanted to watch a fight, a mock battle would have been enough.)

The people in the direction of Rei’s gaze and the opponent I fought in the mock battle.

It goes without saying which of the two possesses the more advanced combat skills.

Therefore, if the goal was purely to observe Rei’s combat skills, a mock battle would have yielded far more valuable data.

Despite that, the fact that she went out of her way to bring someone who isn’t particularly skilled means there must definitely be some reason for it.

(The difference from a mock battle… difference? Mock battle? Hey, wait a minute…)

The word “mock battle” made Rei think of a certain possibility.

To begin with, a mock battle is, as the name implies, a “mock” battle. In other words, it’s nothing more than a simulation of actual combat.

In other words, those battles are based on the premise that you do not kill your opponent.

Moreover, the mock battle Rei participated in was organized by Liberte. In such a setting, while an accident might be one thing, intentionally killing an opponent would be the ultimate disgrace to Liberte’s reputation.

Rei, for his part, didn’t particularly enjoy killing people either.

That’s precisely why the mock battle ended as just that—a mock battle… but in that sense, perhaps he failed to fully satisfy the person whose gaze he felt.

And, even if he doesn’t want to, can he properly kill his opponent when the opportunity arises?

To thoroughly test that, he manipulated these people and had them attack Rei and the others.

Thinking about it that way, Rei isn’t entirely unable to understand that behavior.

“Hey, Rei. What’s up? Did something occur to you?”

Brewitt calls out to Rei, who has been lost in thought while observing the corpses and the men who can no longer even move.

Snapped back to reality by the voice, Rei shook his head, saying it was nothing.

After all, what had come to mind was merely Rei’s speculation, and above all, he felt reluctant to drag Brewitt into this matter.

As was evident from the way he’d fought the attacking men without any particular difficulty, Brewitt possessed considerable strength.

However, that was merely a reasonable level of strength; it wasn’t the overwhelming power possessed by the likes of Rei, Elena, Vihera, and Marina.

To put it bluntly, one could say his strength still falls within the realm of what a human is capable of.

And the source of that gaze Rei had felt so many times was undoubtedly closer to Rei and the others than to Brewitt.

He had no concrete proof, but Rei was certain of it.

“I was starting to wonder if these guys were on some weird drug or something.”

“...Probably.”

Unlike ordinary nobles, Brewitt has firsthand experience of the lives of commoners.

For that very reason, he had a fair understanding of so-called dangerous drugs—which, in Rei’s view, were akin to narcotics.

He also knew that, depending on how they were used, such drugs could be beneficial to people.

However, seeing people who seemed to have lost their reason due to the effects of drugs was not a pleasant sight for Brewitt either.

Seeing Brewitt like this, Rei must have sensed something. He spoke to Brewitt again.

“Sorry to ask, but could you go get a guard? I already let the guys who were here escape… and more importantly, you probably shouldn’t be seeing this kind of thing, right?”

“...Hmph. Setting aside the second reason, I suppose I have to agree with the first. Even if they’ve lost their minds, it’s probably best not to leave these guys here like this.”

At Rei’s words, Brewitt replied, though with a hint of dissatisfaction.

In truth, since Brewitt had even fought on the battlefield himself, he didn’t particularly want to see a scene like this, though it wasn’t enough to make him sick.

“Then, I’ll leave it to you.”

Hearing Rei repeat his request, Brewitt looked somewhat unimpressed but turned and walked away.

After all, since they’d have to call for guards or knights anyway, he must have decided that someone had to go.

Above all, if left alone, even those still alive might end up dying.

Also, if I stayed here, I didn’t think I’d be able to handle it if something unexpected happened.

(If it were just this kind of crowd showing up, that wouldn’t have been a problem in itself. …But judging by Rei’s demeanor, there’s probably something going on that I wouldn’t be able to handle.)

With that thought in mind, Brewitt left the scene to summon the guards and knights.

“Guruu?”

“Is that okay?” Set asked, clearing his throat, and Rei nodded in response, indicating it was fine.

If Brewitt had stayed here, he thought, and gotten involved in the fight that was about to happen, he probably wouldn’t have been able to win. That was why he’d thought so.

After Brewitt had moved far enough away, Rei spoke up, listening to the groans of the men lying on the ground.

“Well then, how about you come out now? It’s no fun just watching forever, is it?”

As Rei said that... when he looked up, a man was standing right in front of him.
