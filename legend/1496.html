The intelligence unit's report reached Egg immediately, as expected, and from Egg it reached Daskar.

Outside, it was still dark, and it would be some time before the sun rose.

Amidst this, a light burned in the office of the lord's mansion.

"...The Kingdom of Reburina? Is that true?"

"Yes. Though apparently it's not the country of Reburlina itself, but an organization using it as their base."

"Even so, they're coming all the way to Gilm. It can't just be some ordinary organization."

Daskar muttered, looking bored. His body was wrapped in nightwear... a nightgown, to be precise.

In that state, he propped his elbows on the desk.

His bad mood was likely due to being woken from sleep.

Work had already piled up recently due to the expansion project, and it wasn't unusual for him to work late into the night.

In fact, today Daskar had been woken by Egg's report just as he'd finally fallen asleep.

Given that, it was hardly surprising he was in a foul mood.

Especially since the report brought was about an organization from the vassal state of the Kingdom of Mireana, the Kingdom of Reburina, causing trouble for Gilm.

"Yes. We're currently interrogating the captured individuals. One of the three was killed by Rei... or more accurately, by someone Rei knew seeking revenge. One of the remaining two has become stubborn after witnessing his comrade killed before his eyes. The other lost too much blood and remains unconscious."

Daskar frowned slightly at the word "enemy."

His face was already formidable, but being roused from sleep had heightened that almost vicious quality.

However, his frown wasn't because he disapproved of the act of revenge...

It was simply the painful realization that they had lost one of their sources of information.

But it was also true that without Rei and the others' cooperation, resolving this incident safely would have been difficult, so there was no reason to blame them.

After all, Ajax, Gestal, and the others had put considerable effort into covering up this incident to prevent it from being discovered.

In truth, the reason Ajax and Jestar's actions came to light was because Marina... or more precisely, because Rei had requested Marina to look into Ajax.

Had that request not been made, Ajax and the others would likely have left Gilm by now and been free men.

Thinking it through, Daskar drank the water sitting on the table.

Even at night, the temperature was far from cool.

The water in the glass was quite warm.

For Daskar, using a cooling magic item wouldn't have been difficult, but today he wasn't in the mood for it, so he hadn't used one.

Drinking the lukewarm water, Daskar opened his mouth, chasing away the lingering drowsiness in his head.

"So, about that Ajax fellow? Did you find out how he and his crew were planning to escape from Gilm?"

For Daskar, the identities of Ajax and his associates, and what they were plotting, were undoubtedly important. But more than that, he was intensely curious about how Ajax and his group had been planning to escape from Gilm.

Given the current situation, it seemed unlikely they could pull off such a simple feat. Therefore, how they intended to attempt it was what intrigued him.

"It seems few know about that matter. Jestar and the higher-ups are currently trying to get details out of them. Please wait a little longer for specifics."

"Understood. But please get that information as soon as possible. I don't know how Ajax and the others planned to leave Gilm, but if it's a method other organizations could use, it could get messy."

"Understood. We'll extract the information as quickly as possible."

"Do that. ...Ah, but don't push too hard during interrogation and get them to give false information... that kind of thing is troublesome."

"Understood. We don't plan to be overly aggressive in the initial interrogations. Though, that will depend on their cooperation."

Daskar nodded at Egg's words.

They used the term interrogation, but in reality, it was questioning... and torture.

If this were Japan, people would likely raise humanitarian concerns.

But not many people would say such things to Elgin.

"Now, regarding the escape from Gilm, we'll leave that to the intelligence unit... That leaves us with the problem of the Kingdom of Reburina. If this were within the Kingdom of Mireana, there wouldn't be any particular issues."

Daskar muttered, but the Lerdact incident was a prime example.

A noble from the Noble Faction who had meddled with the expansion work at Gilm.

Though he belonged to the Neutral Faction, distinct from Daskar's neutral stance, he was still a noble of the same Mireana Kingdom, making retaliation straightforward.

...Though, the ease of retaliation was solely due to the presence of Rei and Set; an ordinary noble couldn't pull off such a stunt so easily.

"Even as a vassal state, it's still a country. We can't just do as we please. ...Still, leaving things as they are isn't very interesting either."

This incident would undoubtedly be handled by the Kingdom of Mireana itself. If they did something like that, the benefits... the compensation money and such... would likely end up mostly in the hands of the Royal Faction and the Noble Faction.

The Neutral Faction has recently been friendly with the Noble Faction, but that doesn't mean they'll get a share of the Noble Faction's profits. The answer is no.

Daskar understands this too, so he doesn't think it unreasonable to voice his dissatisfaction.

After all, if Daskar belonged to the Royal Faction or the Noble Faction, he would undoubtedly do something similar.

Is it the smallness of the Neutral Faction that prevents them from doing so?

But even so, this incident was orchestrated in Gilm... the city ruled by Daskar.

Given that, remaining silent is not an option.

If he remained silent now, it would be interpreted as Daskar being weak-willed, inviting attacks from various factions—more organizations, other nations.

Especially since Gilm is the only city on the border of the Mireana Kingdom, and its location makes it an extremely wealthy place.

Daskar believed that while the Kingdom of Mireana must handle it as a nation, he also needed to address it separately as a Neutral Faction.

"But, Daskar-sama. The Kingdom of Reburina is quite far away, you know?"

"Ah, I understand."

The fact that Gilm is on the frontier of the Mireana Kingdom is a factor, but it would take over a month by carriage to reach the location of the Kingdom of Reburina.

Under those circumstances, sending just one person wasn't feasible.

Whether it was to destroy the organization behind this incident or whatever else, a certain level of military force would be necessary.

(The only saving grace is that we're not going as a nation. We're heading there solely to mete out punishment for this incident, so there's no need to send someone like a diplomatic envoy. ...Though, when it comes to who to send, I am torn. The top candidate is...)

The figure Daskar envisioned was, naturally, Rei.

While his deep involvement in this incident is one reason, the biggest factor is that Rei possesses the mobility of the griffin, Set.

With Set's speed, the journey to the Kingdom of Reburina shouldn't take too long.

Of course, when Rei travels long distances, he usually gets lost, gets attacked by bandits, attacks bandits himself, or stops at villages and towns along the way.

However, Set's speed is so overwhelming that even if Rei does any of those things, it won't be a problem at all.

It's so fast that even a dragon knight riding a wyvern couldn't match it.

(But I've been asking too much of Rei lately. The expansion project alone... I have no idea how much the payment will end up being. Should I ask him for more?)

Daskar was well aware of the burden he'd been placing on Rei lately.

Fortunately, Rei didn't seem particularly bothered by the fatigue, but that was only because he was Rei.

If an ordinary mage were told to work like Rei, they'd undoubtedly say it was impossible.

What Rei is doing is that significant.

Moreover, Rei's role in the expansion project is incredibly significant.

Sending Rei to the Kingdom of Reburina now would place a heavy burden on the expansion project.

Knowing this, Daskar hesitated.

"What about putting someone else on Set?"

"Probably not. From what I heard before, Set can only carry Rei. In fact, the women who are part of Rei's party can't ride Set, so they hold onto his legs to move around."

"Come to think of it, I did hear something like that before."

Since riding Set wasn't an option, borrowing just Set to send someone else to the Kingdom of Rebellina was impossible.

Daskar's plan would fail right from the start.

"Besides, even if someone could ride Set, I doubt Rei would lend him out. And above all, I can't imagine Set leaving Rei's side."

"...Probably not."

Egg understood the strong bond between Rei and Set. At the same time, he also understood that Set was extremely clingy.

Asking such a Set to leave Rei and go to another country... unless something extraordinary happened, there was no way he'd agree to that.

"So then... we'll have to ask Rei again?"

"But if we ask Rei, won't it affect the expansion project?"

"Yeah, I think so too. But... if we just sit here doing nothing, that arrogant bastard will come after Gilm again."

At Daskar's words, Egg wore an expression of utter weariness.

Even now, the intelligence unit was stretched to its limits. If someone else started pulling strange stunts on top of that, it would definitely exceed the unit's capacity.

"I thought the incident with Count Lerdact would serve as a good warning."

"It should be a warning. In fact, none of the members of the Noble Faction are secretly plotting anymore. ...Though whether that's the warning's effect or because the Princess General is here, I can't say."

Even as he said this, Daskar knew his true feelings: it was because of the Princess General... Elena.

If an aristocrat from the Noble Faction acted suspiciously in Elena's presence, it would be a direct insult to Duke Kerebel, who led that faction.

If that happened, expulsion from the Noble Faction would be a real possibility.

Given the substantial benefits they gained from their activities as the Noble Faction, such a stunt was simply unthinkable.

...That said, some individuals do act on emotion.

Since there's no guarantee such individuals won't snap, Elena's presence doesn't guarantee absolute safety.

"Anyway, I need to talk to Rei soon... Can you make time?"

Considering his recent workload, he couldn't readily agree that time could be easily made.

But as a Daskar, since I was asking him for a favor, I wanted to explain it to Rei myself.

"Ah, you really do seem incredibly busy with all sorts of things."

Egg also frequently communicated with Daskar, so he understood just how much work he usually handled.

"But if it's too difficult, why not try requesting it through the Guildmaster?"

"...Well, that would be the quickest way. But I've been asking a lot of Rei lately anyway, so I'd prefer to explain it to her directly if possible."

I know he's swamped with work, but if I'm going to ask Rei for a favor, I needed to properly fulfill my obligation.

If he kept using Rei like a convenient tool, Rei might eventually abandon Gilm... abandon Daskar, and move his base elsewhere.

No, in the worst-case scenario, assassination was also an option.

Rei possessed the skill to take on even a first-rate opponent single-handedly.

If he truly moved to assassinate Daskar, preventing it would be extremely difficult.

Moreover, if Rei became an enemy of Daskar, his allies would likely become enemies too.

Marina, who has long been associated with Daskar, might take a neutral stance—neither joining the attack nor stopping it...

(No, I should stop entertaining such reckless thoughts. If that became reality, there'd be no way to fix it.)

He muttered this to himself, resolving to find time to speak with Rei as soon as possible.
