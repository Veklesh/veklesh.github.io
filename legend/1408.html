"Rei, are you here?"

Rei, who was enjoying an early dinner in the dining hall of Dusk Wheat inn at dusk, turned his gaze toward the entrance.

It was still a bit early... around four o'clock, so there were hardly any customers in the dining hall.

If it were another hour or two later, many people would come to this dining hall for dinner.

...Although the town was unusually crowded due to the large-scale expansion, the number of people staying at the Dusk Wheat inn hadn't changed much from before.

That was only natural. This inn was known as one of the higher-end establishments in Gilm, and those living hand-to-mouth simply couldn't afford to stay here.

Furthermore, the dining hall is slightly more expensive than other establishments, and as a policy of the inn, they only serve enough alcohol for one to enjoy their meal. Consequently, most laborers head to taverns where they can drink and make noise.

Of course, not everyone fits that description, and a few do come to this dining hall.

In any case, for these reasons, despite what could be called a current expansion rush, the evening scene at Dusk Wheat inn remained largely unchanged from usual.

"Lenora? What's up?"

Marina, who was about to reach for a slice of pizza, called out to the person who appeared in the dining hall—a familiar face to Rei, the guild receptionist.

Pizza, which had become popular in Gilm, was naturally served in the cafeteria at Dusk Wheat inn too.

However, due to Gilm's location, seafood pizzas weren't on the menu.

If they provided the seafood from Rei's Misty Ring, they would make it for you.

"Ah, Marina-sama is here too. ...Actually, I have an urgent request for Rei-san."

"I figured."

People had come to this inn looking for Rei from the guild several times before.

And every single time, Rei had accepted the urgent request. For Rei, it had become a patterned routine.

That said, he certainly didn't dislike emergency requests, especially since the rewards were quite tasty.

"So, what's the request?"

"Yes. Apparently, a merchant caravan transporting materials for an expansion project was attacked, and all the materials were stolen. The surviving merchants and guards just arrived at Gilm and confirmed it."

"...Materials? Well, I'm surprised there are even bandits operating in the frontier to begin with."

The frontier meant, naturally, all sorts of monsters... monsters that wouldn't appear in ordinary places, were making their presence known.

To those monsters, bandits and merchants are just people.

They are targets to attack and, at the same time, food. That's what they are.

Given that, bandits couldn't easily operate in the frontier.

Especially considering it's near a town like Gilm, where skilled adventurers gather—it was utterly inconceivable that bandits could operate there.

That said, it's certainly true that the rewards would be substantial.

"So that bandit didn't specifically target the merchant caravan... right?"

"I don't think so,"

Lenora answered Marina's question.

It was probably just a way of confirming things.

After all, if they were targeting a specific caravan, there was no need to attack near Gilm.

Attacking near Aburo or Saburusta, farther from Gilm, would mean avoiding encounters with Gilm adventurers.

"...So then, it was a band of thieves operating around Gilm after all? I'm not really sure."

"Well, there's no point dwelling on that now anyway. So? You want us to take care of the bandits, right?"

"Yes. But this time, we'd like you to focus primarily on recovering the materials the bandits stole."

"I see. Well, that makes sense."

Generally, when bandits are defeated, the treasure they possess becomes the property of the party that defeated them.

Of course, if the bandits possess some sort of family heirloom, and after it's recovered, the family that owned it negotiates to buy it back, whether to accept or refuse that offer is left up to the adventurers.

But this request is about securing the materials.

Normally, one might say, "It's a shame it's materials that would normally be mine..." but in Rei's case, it's not really an issue.

Frankly, what would he even do with a huge pile of materials?

Staying at the Dusk Wheat inn, Rei doesn't particularly want to build a house.

He wasn't short on money, so he didn't plan to sell the materials for cash either.

The Misty Ring's storage capacity is practically infinite, so he might think, "Well, if I can get them, I might as well take them for now..."

"Well, I understand the situation. Delaying the Gilm expansion isn't exactly something I'm thrilled about either."

Rei had been busy with the expansion plans, but with so many people around, it was inevitable that some would try to get involved with him.

The nickname "Crimson" has spread far enough that no one bothers Set when he's around... but even though they usually act together, Rei and Set aren't together every single moment.

On top of that, when he brought along beauties like Marina and Vihera – women so stunning you might only see one in a lifetime – it was only natural that people new to Gilm, unaware of the situation, would hassle them more often.

Of course, Gilm residents would try to stop them if they saw it, but not all such individuals would back down.

In fact, many openly show irritation at being shielded.

To resolve this situation as quickly as possible, it was undoubtedly best to complete the expansion of Gilm as soon as possible.

...That said, the project to expand Gilm by fifty percent from its current size wasn't something that could be finished in a matter of days, weeks, or even months. It was almost certain to take several years.

In fact, some openly claimed it could take over ten years.

Given that, even if Rei pushed himself a bit here, it wouldn't make much difference... Still, being able to shorten the timeframe even slightly was fortunate for Rei.

"Then, could you please do it!?"

"Sure, that's fine. But... don't you have any leads or something? Honestly, finding the bandits will be tough without any clues."

It would be different if they were casually walking around empty areas or camping out there, but there's no way thieves operating around Gilm would be that careless.

Mountains, forests, woods... or caves.

They're definitely setting up their hideout in places like that.

"We did confirm the direction the bandits left from the merchant who reported it. Can't we do something with that?"

"...No, that's a bit much... Even tracking their scent would be tricky. Wait, could we follow them from the attack site?"

"Hm!"

Though Rei seemed slightly hesitant, Byune, who had been stuffing her mouth full of food without listening to Lenora's story until now, spoke up.

"Hey, swallow everything in your mouth first!"

And immediately, Vihera scolded her.

"So? What did Byune say?"

Only Vihera could fully communicate with Byune.

That's why Rei asked Vihera as usual.

"I think you can probably guess without me saying. ...Byune said he's going to search for those thieves."

"I see. So Byune's gotten good at that lately, huh?"

During the winter, he'd engaged in nothing but relentless combat training, and his fighting prowess had become quite formidable. ...Of course, he still fell far short of the other members of Crimson Wings.

But Byune's true profession was, after all, that of a thief.

Given that, skills like exploration, trap detection, and reconnaissance should naturally be prioritized.

(Come to think of it, the current Byune is actually closer to a warrior than a thief. ...Well, having combat strength is never a bad thing, so it's fine.)

After a moment's thought, he decided that with Set there, they could probably handle it even if Byune made a few mistakes.

"Alright, then. Let's start with Byune handling it. If it gets completely out of hand, we'll hand it over to Set."

The reason Rei had even a little leeway was largely because what had been stolen were supplies.

If it had been food stolen, that would be gone once eaten.

But supplies? They wouldn't just vanish overnight.

Of course, there was the possibility they'd be sold off somewhere after some time passed, so he didn't have complete peace of mind.

(Still, why would thieves target supplies? Even if they sell them... Hmm)

Pondering this, Rei suddenly had a question and asked Lenora.

"The stolen supplies... how much were we actually talking about?"

"Five wagonloads, they said."

"That's a fair amount, but not exceptionally large."

With that amount of materials, if someone wanted to sell them, they could probably do it without leaving a trace.

Or even if they did sell it, there's no need to dump everything at once.

You could split it up and sell it bit by bit.

"In that case, it'll take a fair amount of time to move it either way."

"That's true. But the merchant requested we retrieve the materials as quickly as possible."

"...Huh? Not from the guild or the knights, but a request from merchants?"

Marina, who had been listening to Rei and Lenora's conversation, asked with a hint of puzzlement.

Lenora nodded in response to Marina's question.

"Yes. It was before the materials were handed over..."

"Ah, I see. So that's the nature of the contract."

Marina nodded in understanding at Lenora's explanation alone.

Rei felt a slight question about Lenora's reaction, but decided there was no particular need for him to say anything. He promptly began preparing to leave... but not before quickly devouring the food on the table.

Marina and Vihera merely smiled faintly at Rei's attitude, thinking it was just his usual self.

Byune's help also helped to make the food on the table vanish in mere minutes.

Though he wished he could have savored it more, he settled the bill.

Marina was dressed in her usual party gown, so while Rei handled the payment, she changed into clothes stored in Vihera's room.

"The location of the attack is the place I heard about earlier, right?"

"Yes."

While Rei and Byune were clearing away the dishes, Marina had apparently asked Lenora about the location of the bandit attack and was now confirming it was the correct spot.

Seeing Lenora nod in agreement, the four of them headed straight out into town, taking Set from the stables with them.

It was already around four in the afternoon, the time when some workers were finishing up.

It was early summer, so the sun was still high... but even so, many people showed the fatigue of long hours on their faces.

Especially those who came to Gilm as manual laborers—even with breaks—had pushed their bodies all day, so their exhaustion must be considerable.

Of course, not everyone in town was tired.

Those shopping for dinner preparations were bustling about, haggling over prices.

"It looks lively, doesn't it?"

"Yes, it is."

While listening to Marina and Vihera exchange words, Rei surveyed the surroundings with Set.

"Grrrr..."

The aroma of cooking wafting through the air made Set's stomach growl.

Rei and the others had eaten at the Dusk Wheat inn, but unfortunately, Set hadn't eaten yet.

Considering that, it was only natural for Set to claim he was hungry.

"Hmm... Yeah, you're right. Should we get one of those sandwiches? Once we deal with the bandit business, I'll make sure you get to eat your fill, so hang in there a little longer."

"Gurrr..."

Set let out a sad growl at Rei's words.

Of course, if Ray bought a sandwich and gave it to Set, he'd immediately start purring happily.

Once he saw Set had calmed down a bit, Rei headed back toward the main gate.

It was only around four o'clock, so there was no need to worry about the main gate closing.

Also, due to the expansion of Gilm, many people were arriving now, significantly delaying the gate closing time.

...Even as Rei and the others prepared to leave Gilm, they could still see people going through the procedures to enter.

"Hm? Ah, Rei? I've heard about it. Dealing with bandits, huh? Be careful."

Whether it was the guild... or perhaps word had reached the lord's mansion regarding the expansion work, either way, the others besides Rei, who didn't need any procedures, quickly finished their paperwork and stepped outside Gilm.
