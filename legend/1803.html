"I'm Brewitt Exoris. I suppose you could call me the black sheep of the Exoris family."

Brewitt must have thought Rei didn’t know who he was. He addressed Rei with those words.

“What on earth is the son of a count doing in a place like this, acting like this?”

Normally, a nobleman’s son wouldn’t go out into the streets… and certainly wouldn’t get into a scuffle with people like that. Brewitt averted his gaze slightly as Rei looked at him as if to say just that.

There, a man and a woman—both in their early twenties, it seemed—were bowing their heads.

Seeing that, Rei vaguely understood.

Most likely, those two had been harassed by the thugs from earlier, and Brewitt had come to their rescue.

(That said, I’m not sure it’s appropriate for the son of a count’s family to go out of his way to help them like that. …No, wait—if he’s this skilled, maybe it’s not a problem even if he is the son of a count? Still, it could easily turn into a liability issue regarding his duties as a bodyguard…)

Although Rei had occasionally interacted with those holding high titles, such as dukes and marquises, to the average person, a member of a count’s family was still considered to be in a league of their own.

If word got out that such a person was getting into a fight with some thugs, it would undoubtedly cause a scandal.

However, Brewitt, who was in Rei’s line of sight, seemed completely unconcerned about such things; he simply waved lightly at the man and woman who had bowed their heads, and left it at that.

Seeing this scene, Leodanis couldn’t help but wear a… truly awkward expression.

Normally, maintaining order in Anesis is the job of the guards.

And yet, the one who resolved the trouble was the son of the Count of Exoris—and not just any son, but the heir apparent. It’s only natural that Leodanis would wear such an expression.

(What were the guards doing?)

Leodanis thought this with a touch of resentment, but it wasn’t as if the guards of Anesis were slacking off.

No, in fact, Anesis’s guards would likely rank among the best in the entire Kingdom of Mireana.

The fact that the guards didn’t arrive in time this time, leading to Brewitt causing a brawl, was purely a matter of luck.

A man and a woman—likely a couple—were being harassed by thugs, and by chance, there were no guards nearby; instead, Brewitt was there. That was the only issue.

Of course, since it was a matter of luck, that doesn’t mean everything is resolved.

“Ah… For now, why don’t we get out of here? If the guards show up, things could get pretty messy.”

Normally, as a knight, Leodanis should be actively stepping in to help the guards.

But if he did that now, it would waste the time of Rei and Brewitt, who were guiding him.

Given the current situation, he couldn’t very well do that, so in the end, he chose to leave the area.

Of course, that was only possible because Brewitt had rescued the couple who were being harassed by those thugs; if that hadn’t been resolved, he wouldn’t have been able to do such a thing.

“Hmm. I’ve been wanting to talk to Rei a bit myself. Since we’re leaving anyway, why don’t we stick together? It seems like it’d be more interesting that way.”

“……Um, Rei?”

The main character here is, after all, Rei.

That being the case, whether or not it’s okay to travel with Brewitt is entirely up to Rei to decide.

As for Leodanis, having met the future head of the Exoris family in a place like this, he really didn’t want to just leave Brewitt behind.

With that in mind, he looked at Rei, hoping she would agree…

“Ah, I don’t really mind. I’m actually a little curious about Brewitt myself.”

That was the voice Leodanis heard from Rei.

Perhaps taken aback by how readily Rei agreed, Leodanis’s face betrayed a hint of surprise.

He had heard rumors about Rei before, but after being completely outmatched and defeated by him in a mock battle, his frustration drove him to gather more detailed information on Rei.

What he came to understand was that Rei and the nobility generally didn’t get along.

There was even information that he had clashed repeatedly with nobles from the Noble Faction, so the fact that Rei nodded so readily at Brewitt’s words was completely unexpected.

“Are you really sure this is okay? If Rei starts causing trouble, it’ll be a problem for us.”

“I have no intention of causing trouble. …At least, not on my part.”

Leodanis watched Rei as he spoke, wondering what to do, but he judged that saying anything more in the current situation would only waste time here, so he reluctantly accepted his words.

After all, if they got into a pointless argument here, the guards might show up.

“Alright. Then… let’s get out of here.”

Nodding at Leodanis’s words, Rei and everyone else present moved away from the spot.

That said, Rei suspected that the fact Brewitt had been with them would be immediately discovered by the guards due to Set’s presence.

“I see. So the rumors that Rei is close to Lady Elena were true after all.”

Brewitt laughed happily as he washed down a chunk of stewed meat with ale.

Rei and the others were currently in a park.

Perhaps because of the snowfall, there was no one else in sight besides Rei and his group.

You’d think there’d be some children playing around, but there wasn’t a single one in sight.

Rei didn’t give it much thought, assuming they were probably playing somewhere else.

In this world, children generally play outside.

...At least, since there are no video game consoles like the ones on the Earth where Rei used to live, that’s the only way they can play.

There are some children who prefer reading books, but books are extremely expensive and not something ordinary families can afford, so those who do are likely an overwhelming minority.

“That’s right. That’s an undeniable fact.”

Seeing Rei respond to Brewitt’s words, Leodanis wore a subtle expression, while Miranda’s eyes sparkled with curiosity.

For Leodanis, Elena is not only a symbol of the Noble Faction but also a symbol of the Duke of Kerebel’s family.

Of course, he respected Philma, who led the Knights, from the bottom of his heart, but that didn’t mean he didn’t respect Elena as well.

And given Elena’s beauty, it would be impossible not to admire her.

Leodanis, naturally, is no exception; he harbors a strong admiration for Elena.

If told that Elena and Rei are close, he would naturally wear a complex expression.

As for Miranda, it’s not that she doesn’t admire Elena… but perhaps that’s just the difference between men and women.

To be honest, rather than admiration, she was more curious about Elena’s romantic life.

It was no rarity for Elena to be rumored to be a beauty without equal in the world, but contrary to such rumors, she had absolutely no close male friends.

Well, to be precise, there were a few men she was simply close to, but the feelings Elena harbored for them were not romantic; they were more like friendship or a sense of camaraderie.

Her beauty meant she was often approached by men, and this only served to further rob Elena of any romantic feelings.

So, when the maids learned a few years ago that Elena—who had come to be known by the nickname “Princess General”—had a man in her life, they all began speculating about who he might be.

However, despite the rumors, Rei had never actually come to Anesis until now… and this time, he finally did.

And they had even received what could be called tacit approval from Duke and Duchess Kerebel.

Thanks to the maids’ gossip, Miranda was also aware of the situation.

Still, if she had the chance to hear the story firsthand, it was only natural that she would want to hear it.

“Huh, I didn’t expect you to go that far. But… if that’s the case, there’ll be plenty of people who’ll turn hostile toward Rei, won’t there? I, for one, would gladly welcome the chance to marry Lady Elena.”

Even as he said this, there was no sign of obsession toward Elena in Bluitt’s demeanor.

He likely just wanted to see how Rei would react.

“If they come at us, we’ll just respond in kind.”

“...Just as the rumors say.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Brewitt flashed a rugged smile.

Brewitt, of course, had gathered all sorts of information about Rei as well.

Among them was the fact that he never held back, even when dealing with nobles, and he knew of nobles who had actually tried to harm Rei and ended up getting hurt.

“I don’t know exactly what those rumors are, but the fact that I can pretty much guess what they are is kind of a mixed bag.”

“Well, considering Rei’s personality…”

“……Anyway, putting my own rumors aside, what’s the deal with you? You’re the heir apparent to a noble house, yet you were out in the streets dressed like that and without a single escort?”

Normally, it would be unthinkable for the heir apparent of an earl’s family to walk around town without even a single bodyguard.

And yet, Brewitt was doing exactly that.

And he’d even gone so far as to prepare clothes that looked like something a commoner would wear—albeit high-quality ones.

Rei thought there was no way this could be just a coincidence.

“Don’t worry, I wasn’t plotting anything. I was just looking around the city out of pure curiosity. Even from my perspective, I can’t help but envy how prosperous Anesis is. My own domain is fairly prosperous as well, but it still falls several notches short compared to Anesis.”

Those words were likely spoken from the heart, not as flattery or anything of the sort.

Of course, since Anesis is the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, it would be quite difficult for any town or city in the Brewitt domain to surpass it in development.

“So, you’re really just here to look around?”

“Yeah. I was actually thinking about how I could improve my own territory by comparing it to this one. I wasn’t just wandering around aimlessly.”

“Huh, really? So, what do you think makes Anesis different from Brewitt’s territory?”

Rei asked this in response to Brewitt’s words, likely because Gilm—currently Rei’s base of operations—was undergoing expansion.

He wondered if applying some unique element found only in Anesis to Gilm might help Gilm prosper even more. That was why he asked.

……That said, although Rei was seen by those around him as Daskar’s right-hand man, in reality, their relationship wasn’t quite like that.

If Rei offered his opinion, Daskar would listen, but whether it would actually be implemented was another matter.

Urban planning is, after all, the domain of specialists in the field, and if Rei were to make a proposal based on the limited knowledge he’d picked up here and there, it would likely do more harm than good.

Regardless of whether it would be useful or not, Rei believed it was better to keep any knowledge in mind, and so he waited for Brewitt’s response.

Feeling Rei’s gaze upon him, Brewitt pauses to think for a moment… then speaks.

“Well, first off, I was surprised there wasn’t much litter lying around.”

“……Huh?”

Rei was surprised—he’d assumed Brewitt would talk about the city’s location or some kind of shop, so why bring up trash?

Surprised, though... he immediately recalled some knowledge he’d picked up in Japan.

(Was it called the Broken Windows Theory… or something like that?)

In places with broken windows, crime is more likely to occur.

To be precise, there are various more detailed theories, but that was the extent of what Rei remembered.

“When trash is left lying around, a lot of people think it’s okay for them to throw their own trash there too. As trash gets dumped one after another, the place naturally gets dirty, and people stop coming around—or, depending on the situation, the kind of people you’d rather not have around start gathering there. Once that happens, a slum forms in the blink of an eye, and shady characters start forming a group.”

Leodanis wore a smug expression as he listened to Brewitt’s words.

He probably didn’t mind having the policies his master was implementing praised.

Even from Rei’s perspective, it was surprising to see them implementing something so similar to the Broken Windows Theory.

Since there’s no way the Broken Windows Theory exists in this world, he must have arrived at this conclusion through empirical observation.

(No, wait—could it be that someone else who came here, whether through reincarnation or transfer, spread that theory?)

Since he knows of people who have transferred or been reincarnated into this world of Elgin, it wouldn’t be strange if some knowledge of that sort had remained.

With these thoughts in mind, Rei continued his conversation with Brewitt.
