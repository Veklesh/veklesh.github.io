Thanks to the reviving potion, Slesha regained consciousness, but at first she had no idea where she was... or what situation she was in.

Still, she recalled her last memory of meeting an adventurer—a man who called himself Runo—and realized this was the Gilm Guild. Frantically, she began explaining what had happened to her.

"...You said you were eaten by the forest. Did you mean that literally?"

Runo, who had assumed it was some kind of metaphor, murmured in surprise.

"Mr. Runo, that's not the issue. There haven't been any recent reports of a dangerous forest swarming with Treants. That makes it likely an entirely unknown entity."

The usually composed receptionist warned Runo with an unusually tense, stiff expression.

It wasn't impossible that Slesha had simply panicked for some reason and was now imagining things.

But even so, if what she just explained was true... it would spell trouble in more ways than one.

For adventurers, the forest is a place to gather materials, slay monsters, and perform dissections.

Beyond adventurers, hunters also hunt game and gather nuts, fruits, and wild vegetables.

If they didn't know it was a troublesome place, a man-eating forest, such people would naturally enter it. ...The result would undoubtedly be numerous casualties.

"This... I can't judge this alone. I need to report it to the Guildmaster, including whether Slesha's testimony is accurate."

"Ah, the Guildmaster. He's new now, but what's he like?"

Runo also knew that the Guildmaster had changed from Marina to Walker.

But naturally, guildmasters aren't the type to show their faces often.

Well, maybe it varies by person or place, but at least the Guildmasters of Gilm... neither Marina nor the new Guildmaster Walker ever seemed to enjoy being in the spotlight.

That's probably why the change in guildmaster didn't feel very real.

Runo had heard Walker's inaugural speech when he became Guildmaster, but hadn't seen him since.

That's why, while Runo knew what Walker looked like, she had no idea what his work style was like.

"She's excellent, as one would expect from someone entrusted by Lady Marina. Well, she did consolidate the branch offices scattered throughout the dungeon, so she couldn't be anything but capable."

With that, the receptionist told Runo and Slesha to wait there and left the room.

She then informed her superior that she had urgent business and headed for the office on the second floor.

"Excuse me, Guildmaster. I have something urgent to report."

"Come in."

Hearing the voice from inside the office, the receptionist opened the door.

The room hadn't changed much since Marina had been there.

Minor details had been adjusted to make Walker's work easier, but that was all.

The receptionist had been looking around the room until Walker, who was doing paperwork, finished a section. Once Walker signed the documents he was reading, she spoke again.

"Earlier, an adventurer was brought to the guild, and he made a report that caught my attention. If it's true, it could lead to quite a lot of trouble, so I came to report it."

"Information from an adventurer? Well then, let's hear it. What is it?"

Prompted, the receptionist began explaining the story Slesha had told her about the forest.

The entire forest attacked them as if it were a monster with its own will.

And that the forest was spreading, growing larger.

Hearing this, Walker, usually calm, slightly furrowed his brow.

He must have grasped the gravity of the situation.

"The forest... a group of Treants?"

As a tree monster, the first thing that came to mind was naturally a Treant.

Of course, there were many other tree monsters, but Walker suspected that if they were involved in this incident, it was highly likely to be the Treants.

"I don't know. He says so himself, so it's certainly possible... Still, the idea of Treants swarming in such numbers that they're called a forest feels a bit off."

"...Yeah, you're right."

"Besides, no such forest has ever been reported before. Even if it's slightly off the main roads, if such a forest existed, surely some adventurers would have found it by now."

"Yes. That part bothers me too. And among the adventurers heading to Gilm, was Slesha the only one who tried to claim some merit first, like this time? It might not be limited to that party. But if no other information has come in..."

Worst case, every adventurer who ventured into that forest—except for this one exception—might have been devoured.

Walker's words, wrapping up the conversation, made the receptionist gasp softly.

Every spring, many adventurers come to Gilm, but naturally, the numbers vary each year.

That's precisely why, even if the number arriving this year was on par with previous years... she couldn't help but think that perhaps even more adventurers had been devoured in the forest where Slesha and her group were attacked.

"What should we do?"

"...Not knowing the specific scale and danger is problematic. Even if we report it to Lord Daskar, it might very well be a small-scale incident."

If a vast forest were attacking Gilm, reporting to Daskar would certainly be necessary.

But if we reported to Daskar only to discover the forest wasn't actually that large, it could mean the guild made a misjudgment.

Of course, Daskar himself wouldn't care about such things, but it was clear not everyone felt that way.

"We need a bit more detailed information... Wait, come to think of it, there is someone quite suitable for this kind of situation. Could you go fetch Rei for us?"

The name that flashed through Walker's mind was Rei.

More precisely, Rei and Set.

They were extremely rare beings in this world, capable of flying.

(Stronger than dragon knights, yet rarely harming people. In fact, they're deeply loved by the residents of Gilm. ...The latter aside, the ability to fly makes them perfect for this job.)

Hearing this, the receptionist seemed to understand what Walker was thinking.

But at the same time, she felt a slight hesitation.

The receptionist also found Set adorable.

But more than that, among the guild receptionists, there was one who was completely infatuated with Rei.

Leaving that person alone while she went searching for Rei felt like it would cause all sorts of trouble later.

(Besides, it's a hassle.)

That was clearly the real reason, but regarding the receptionist in question... Kenny... it wasn't exactly a joke either.

In truth, this receptionist had almost no connection to Rei and didn't even know where he was now.

After all, Gilm was huge.

Searching for Rei within it would undoubtedly be difficult.

"If you want to call Rei, I think you should use Kenny."

"I see. Now that you mention it... but isn't Kenny supposed to be off today?"

"Yes. But if he knew he could see Rei, I think he'd come running."

That thought felt almost like certainty.

Considering Kenny's personality, he'd definitely go out of his way to see Rei.

But Walker shook his head at the receptionist's words.

"Right now, we need to call Rei as soon as possible. Going to fetch Kenny first and then... that would take too much time. Besides, Kenny might not even be at home."

"That's... true."

If it were winter, the chances of him being home would be higher.

But it was already spring, and the temperature outside was warm.

Considering Kenny's personality, he'd be more likely to be out playing on his day off than staying home.

If we have to find Kenny first, then go look for Rei, who knows how long that would take.

(But if I just yelled, "Come out because I'm looking for Rei!"... Kenny would probably just show up normally, I think.)

Knowing how deeply Kenny felt about Rei normally, it was only natural to think that.

Still, since it wasn't certain, I couldn't help but think I'd have to go myself.

"But... I don't know where Rei is, you know? Hopefully he's at his usual spot, the Dusk Wheat inn."

"That's true. If he's not at the Dusk Wheat inn, you might try going to Lady Marina's mansion in the Noble District. I've heard rumors that since forming the party, her mansion has become their hangout spot."

The receptionist was surprised by the words coming from Walker's mouth.

She had assumed he was just working in his office, never imagining he'd been gathering information so thoroughly.

Then again, Walker had deep connections with Rei and the others.

And given that they were high-ranking adventurers with nicknames, it was only natural he knew the details of their situation.

"Understood. Then I'll head to the Dusk Wheat inn first."

"Thank you."

With that, the conversation ended. The receptionist left the office and headed toward the treatment room.

"Oh, you're back. So, how did it go?"

"Looks like we're asking Rei about the forest matter. I'll go fetch Rei now."

"Well, that makes sense. Since we're searching the forest, Rei's... no, Set's ability to fly through the air should be quite useful."

"Yes. His movement speed is incredibly fast too."

After exchanging a few brief words, the receptionist left the treatment room... and then the guild.

And naturally, her first destination was the Dusk Wheat inn.

Fortunately, the morning rush had passed, so she was able to speak with the innkeeper right away.

"Excuse me, I'm from the guild. Is Rei here?"

Lana, who had been organizing documents at the counter, turned her gaze toward the voice.

Standing there was a woman wearing the guild uniform, just as she had described.

Lana recognized her face from previous visits to the guild to request supplies, confirming she was indeed a genuine guild employee.

"Well, well, this is unusual. Someone coming to ask for Rei?"

Rei was famous as a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname, but his personality often inspired fear.

As a result, people specifically seeking Rei out were... well, not exactly nonexistent, but certainly very few indeed.

Vaguely understanding why Lana was surprised, the receptionist asked again about Rei.

"I have some business with the guild... So, is Rei here?"

"Unfortunately, Rei is out."

"Ah... I figured. I didn't expect him to be in his room on such a sunny day. Do you know where he went?"

Hearing Rei wasn't there, the only place left for the receptionist to think of was Marina's mansion.

She'd rather ask about somewhere else, but...

"Hmm, maybe the food stalls?"

"Ah, I see."

She knew one of Rei's hobbies was eating his way around town.

Originally, Rei was also knowledgeable about cooking and had introduced dishes to Gilm that people had never eaten before.

Udon, pork buns, pizza.

Beyond that, I've even heard rumors that he's introduced different dishes in other towns.

Anyway, knowing Rei like that, hearing he's out eating his way through food stalls makes perfect sense. It's a perfectly plausible choice.

But... even so, considering how vast Gilm is, I still couldn't imagine actually finding Rei.

"Understood. I'll look into a few more possibilities."

In the end, the receptionist chose another possibility... heading to Marina's mansion, and if no one was there, she'd go back to searching the food stalls.

Considering how many stalls there were in Gilm, it was a logical choice.

So, leaving the Dusk Wheat inn, I hurried onward, glancing at the stalls along the way as I headed toward the Noble District.

(Rei should be with Set-chan. If that's the case, kids and others would be gathering around Set-chan, so I don't think it'd be impossible to find them.)

Thinking this, the receptionist pressed on, but ultimately failed to find them. She entered the Noble District and arrived before Marina's mansion.

Clinging to a sliver of hope, the receptionist called out before the mansion.

"Excuse me! Is Marina here?"
