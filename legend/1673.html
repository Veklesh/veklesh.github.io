Rei was momentarily surprised that the ship he had touched wouldn’t fit inside the Misty Ring, but his surprise quickly faded.

In principle, Rei’s Misty Ring can store objects of any size or weight, but even so, there are some things it absolutely cannot hold.

That is... living creatures.

Whether it’s a dog, a cat, a monster, or a fish, living beings cannot be stored inside the Misty Ring.

Yes, for example... even if it’s a human.

(Well, actually, judging by how dilapidated this ship is, there’s a possibility that it’s not a human but a rat or something like that inside the ship.)

However, ships that were even more dilapidated than the one Rei is currently touching could be stored inside the Misty Ring just fine.

Given that, the fact that only this ship can’t be stored leaves me with some doubts.

“Is there someone on this ship?”

At Rei’s muttered words, the pirates stiffened almost reflexively.

If these were top-tier pirates—the kind considered “the real deal”—they wouldn’t have been so shaken even if they’d been called out.

But the pirates here were, after all, operating in the sticks… the sort who wouldn’t be out of place being called “backwater pirates.”

Given that, it was only natural that they would be shaken by a situation like this.

“N-no, well…”

One of the pirates stammered, but judging by his demeanor, it was easy to see he was hiding something.

(It seems the rat isn’t involved after all.)

Having reached that conclusion, I turned my gaze back to the ship still floating out at sea.

“I don’t know what you’re hiding, but it’s certain there’s someone on board, isn’t it? Well then, I’ll just have to respond accordingly. Set, if anyone comes out of the ship, capture them. Judging by the way these guys are acting, there might be an important figure on board… like the guy leading these pirates, for instance.”

At Rei’s words, the pirates grew agitated once more… and seeing that, Rei could make a guess as to who was inside the ship.

(The leader of these pirates... or perhaps someone who holds some kind of influence, even if not the leader. In any case, there’s no doubt they’re well-respected.)

If they were dealing with someone hated by his subordinates, the pirates wouldn’t go to such lengths to protect him.

And the fact that he was being protected confirmed that he was someone respected by his subordinates.

“Well then, who could it be? I’m looking forward to finding out.”

Saying that, he boarded the ship.

He sensed the pirates making a commotion behind him, but Rei ignored it.

He had concluded that since all the pirates were bound, there was nothing they could do in the current situation.

“...I knew it, but this ship is really dilapidated. Well, it might be good enough for study purposes to develop land-based ships.”

The ship he had initially stored in the Misty Ring was even more dilapidated than the one Rei was on now.

Considering that, he had to force himself to accept it, even if it meant convincing himself by any means necessary.

Looking around the deck, he could see a door leading into the ship’s interior.

There was only one proper door, but there were several other openings on the deck.

He wasn’t sure if they weren’t connected inside or if it was simply a matter of convenience.

Rei didn’t know the answer to that, but he figured that once he got inside, things would become clear, so he headed toward the standard door.

Rei felt an absolute sense of security—even if someone inside the ship tried to escape through a hatch in the deck, they would simply be caught by Set.

As he stepped through the door into the ship, he could see numerous signs of repairs inside, just as he had on the outside.

“Is anyone there?”

He called out to the surroundings, but, as expected, there was no response from inside.

After confirming this, Rei scanned the ship for any signs of life.

A single presence down below.

“Down there, huh. …I don’t know what whoever’s hiding is thinking, but do they really think they can get away with hiding?”

Muttering to himself, wondering if some kind of trap had been set, Rei made his way down through the ship.

But even after walking down the passageway for a while, there’s no sign of any traps being triggered.

(Seriously, what were they thinking, pulling a stunt like this? Well, I guess it makes things easier for me, so that’s fine.)

As he walked through the ship, he eventually arrived at his destination—the place where he sensed a presence.

It was one of the rooms inside the ship.

However, it wasn’t the captain’s quarters or anything like that—just an ordinary, unremarkable room.

(So this is it.)

Rei stopped and gave the door a light knock.

A faint sound echoes around him, but there is no response from inside.

A few seconds later, he knocked again.

Still, there was no response from inside, and the sound of waves outside the ship drifted in from somewhere.

(Wait, isn’t it a bad sign that I can hear outside sounds in a place like this? …Well, as far as I can tell, there doesn’t seem to be any flooding or anything, so I guess it’s okay.)

If the ship were taking on water just from being anchored, it wouldn’t be surprising if it sank at any moment given the current situation.

With that thought in mind, Rei opens the door in front of him.

“Yaaaaaaah!”

The moment he opened the door, a blade lunged at him, accompanied by that scream.

While it could certainly be called sharp, for Rei—who had sensed a presence on the other side of the door and anticipated that someone was likely lying in wait—dodging that strike was no difficult feat.

Without any particular surprise or panic, he shifted his body sideways to dodge the attack, then grabbed the wrist of the man wielding the thrusting longsword—no, the weapon that should be called a saber—and used the opponent’s momentum to hurl him away.

“Gyah!”

The man, thrown aside, slammed his back against the hallway wall as expected, let out a scream, and collapsed to the floor.

However, seeing him immediately try to get back up, saber in hand, Rei’s expression shifted to one of slight admiration.

That blow had sent him flying with considerable force.

Of course, that also meant the man who had ambushed Rei had struck with that much force to begin with.

In any case, the man stood up despite the pain and pointed the tip of his saber at Rei.

“...You’re young.”

Standing face-to-face like this, Rei stared intently at the man’s face for the first time and muttered those words.

Of course, even though he’s young, he looks considerably older than Rei.

He looked as though he had just entered his twenties… no, perhaps he wasn’t even twenty yet.

At the very least, none of the pirates Rei had captured were younger than the man standing before him.

They ranged from their fifties on up to their late twenties, more or less.

“What are you talking about! I’m not about to be called ‘young’ by a kid like you!”

Pointing the tip of his saber at Rei, the man shouted, his face flushing red.

“Well, I suppose that’s true…” Though he understood the man’s anger, Rei wasn’t the least bit flustered.

Normally, in a situation where he had no weapons, while his opponent was already holding a saber and ready to swing it at any moment, being unarmed would put him at a massive disadvantage.

However, that only applies when the two sides are evenly matched.

When there’s an overwhelming difference in strength… yes, in a situation like the one between Rei and the man standing before him, it doesn’t matter what kind of weapon the opponent is holding.

“Well, I suppose so. …So, what are you going to do?”

“What are we going to do?” The man, upon hearing this, looked at Rei, seemingly unable to grasp what was being asked of him at first.

“What do you mean, ‘what are we going to do’?”

“I mean, are we going to keep this up? After our exchange just now, you should have a pretty good idea, shouldn’t you? Of the difference in strength between you and me.”

At Rei’s words, the man was at a loss for words.

In reality, the gap in strength between Rei and the man was clearly that of a child and an adult… no, even greater than that.

There wasn’t even a one-in-ten-thousand chance the man could beat Rei.

Not only could he not win, but even if he tried to escape from this situation, there was no chance he could get away.

Knowing that, are you still going to resist? That was what Rei asked.

“Guh… Damn it… But that doesn’t mean I can just let this pirate crew I just took over get wiped out!”

“Ah, I see. So that’s it.”

From the man’s words, Rei finally understood why such a young man was leading a pirate crew.

Still, the fact that his subordinates were defending him meant there was no doubt they looked up to him.

If he had simply inherited the crew based on blood ties alone, the other pirates would never have accepted him.

“That’s why… that’s why I can’t just sit here and lose or get captured, you know!”

As he shouted, he reached into his coat with his free hand, intending to slam it down on the ground...

“Too bad.”

With those words, his hand froze in place.

Before he knew it… truly, before he knew it, Rei had moved right up next to the man and grabbed his hand.

Just a moment ago, the tip of the saber had undoubtedly been pointed at him, but before he knew it, he was standing right next to the man.

What had happened… How could such a thing have occurred? The man couldn’t understand it at all.

And while he remained baffled, the grip on his hand gradually tightened, and before he knew it, what he had been holding fell to the floor.

It was a cube-shaped object small enough to be completely hidden in the palm of his hand.

(What is it? Since he tried to smash it on the floor in that situation, it must be a tool with some kind of effect… or perhaps a magic item.)

What came to Rei’s mind in this situation was the teleportation stones the people of the Bestir Empire had used before.

But, naturally, there was no magic circle of any kind on the floor.

If that’s the case, could he teleport just by smashing that cube-shaped object? He considered it for a moment, but he couldn’t imagine that a pirate from such a backwater could do something that even the most advanced alchemists of the Bestir Empire found impossible.

“What is this?”

Deciding that if he didn’t know, he should just ask someone who did, Rei turned to the man.

Since the man had originally tried to use what appeared to be a magic item, there was absolutely no way he didn’t know what kind of effect it had.

“Shut up! I’m not gonna tell an enemy something like that!”

“……Enemy?”

Seeing the puzzled look on Rei’s face, the man looked at him in bewilderment for a moment… then, realizing that Rei didn’t even see him as an enemy, his face twisted in frustration.

He had been feeling like a hero—or rather, a pirate—taking on a formidable foe… a foe so powerful he was practically invincible—yet Rei hadn’t even recognized him as an enemy.

In fact, given the man’s strength, there was no way Rei would see him as an enemy.

At best, he was probably just a target to be captured... or something like that.

“Whether I’m an enemy or not, what kind of magic item is this, anyway?”

“Don’t you think I’d just come clean after being treated like that?!”

“...You won’t tell me? Well, if that’s your intention, fine, but I want to know the effects of this magic item—or whatever it is. And since that’s the case, I’m not going to hold back.”

Saying that, Rei began to apply pressure to the man’s arm—little by little, very little by little.

At first, the man felt only a light squeeze, but gradually, Rei’s grip tightened, contorting the man’s face in pain.

“Guh! Gaaah! It hurts! Damn it, let go, let go!”

“Sorry, but I don’t plan on letting go until you cooperate.”

Rei could imagine that since he’d tried to use it in a situation like this, it must be a magic item with quite a bit of power.

The man’s misfortune was that Rei had a hobby of collecting magical items—specifically, ones that were actually useful in combat.

From Rei’s perspective, he absolutely had to find out what this magic item’s effect was.

Especially since the one who knew about it was a pirate, and Rei had no need to worry about the other guy’s safety.

The skin on the man’s arm, which was being gripped, had already torn, and the flesh was beginning to crush.

If left like this, the bones would undoubtedly be crushed as well.

The man looked at Rei while enduring the pain… and felt a chill run down his spine.

No, it would be more accurate to say he sensed a threat to his life.

“Wait, it’s a smoke screen! This magic item generates a smoke screen in response to any impact above a certain threshold!”

In the end, the man answered the question about the magic item.
