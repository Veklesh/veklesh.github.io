As the morning sun illuminated the streets of Gosh, Rei swung the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left near the stables where Set resided—part of Augusto's estate.

With only the Death Scythe, or only the Twilight Spear, he could move with a dance-like grace that seemed to captivate the eyes of those watching. But that was only possible when wielding just one of them.

With both the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear in hand now, his movements were far from what could be called a dance.

Far from it, his movements were the kind that would leave any observer dumbfounded.

He would strike with the Death Scythe, then attempt to follow up with a thrust from the Twilight Spear, only to immediately lose his balance.

The Death Scythe feels almost weightless, while the Twilight Spear is as heavy as any ordinary spear.

Using either one alone wouldn't cause him to lose his balance, but wielding both simultaneously like this was incredibly difficult, inevitably throwing him off.

"Whoa!"

Once again, the weight difference threw him off balance, and he barely managed to keep from falling.

"Grr?"

Set, who had been purring "Are you okay?", now looked worriedly at Rei.

Though he was lying down as usual, Set's face had been fixed on Rei, who was practicing the Two-Spear Style, with a look of concern since earlier.

The Rei Set knew was almost like a natural genius.

The kind of person who could do anything he set his mind to, almost instantly.

In truth, Rei had rarely struggled this much before.

Mastering the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and the Overlord’s Armour had been quite difficult, but the current Two-Spear Style was causing him trouble on an entirely different level.

But perhaps that was inevitable. Someone had actually demonstrated the Overlord’s Armour before his eyes, and the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour was merely an advanced version of that.

The Two-Spear Style is different. It's entirely Rei's original creation. While Rei himself calls it the Two-Spear Style, it's actually a combat method where he wields a spear and a large scythe in one hand each.

Howl's dual-sword style might serve as a reference, but wielding swords versus a spear and a scythe feels entirely different.

From the very way the weapons are wielded, Rei must master this entirely new technique himself.

Regaining his balance, Rei smiled reassuringly at Set, who was watching him anxiously.

"Don't worry. I don't know if anyone's ever done it before... but at the very least, I've never seen this fighting style before. I knew it would be tough."

There was no trace of disappointment on Rei's face as he said this.

Having actually tried this training, he understood it was incredibly difficult.

But at the same time, the fact that he was creating his own original combat method brought him a sense of fulfillment.

If it were literally a two-spear style using two spears, someone might have used it before.

In fact, back in Japan, Rei had seen a few examples of dual-spear users in anime, manga, novels, and games.

Compared to that, he had never seen or heard of anyone employing such a distorted fighting style—a spear in one hand and a large scythe in the other.

Especially since the large scythe felt almost weightless, Rei knew of no one who fought that way.

(Ah, but what about samurai who practiced dual-wielding with a heavy katana and a lighter wakizashi? Could that be a reference? ...Well, I've never actually seen a samurai, so it probably wouldn't be much help.)

The fleeting hope vanished almost as quickly as it had appeared.

(Besides, while a Japanese sword and wakizashi have a weight difference, it's nowhere near as extreme as the gap between the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.)

One of Rei's current struggles was the weight difference between the weapons.

Furthermore, since both are long weapons, their attack ranges naturally overlap, making collisions far from uncommon.

Overlapping means that when both weapons are swung, they might collide with each other.

"Well, I suppose that's just something you get used to."

Laying hold of Death Scythe and Twilight Spear once more, Rei began to move his body slowly.

First, he slowly moves the Death Scythe in his right hand.

Then, as if to confirm its trajectory, he moved the Twilight Spear in his left hand.

Both movements were extremely slow, reminiscent of set patterns within a fixed sequence.

Slowly swinging the weapons while confirming he could move both freely... Once that movement ended, what Rei did next was perform the same motion slightly faster.

Once that was done, he did it a little faster, then even faster... continuing like this. But the moment he reached a certain speed, a high-pitched metallic clang echoed throughout the surroundings.

"Whoa!"

Although the speed had gradually increased, it still wasn't particularly fast.

He forcibly suppressed the recoil from the clash between Death Scythe and Twilight Spear with sheer arm strength.

"...Come to think of it, maybe because I'm holding it, the Twilight Spear doesn't feel its true weight when it collides with the Death Scythe."

The Death Scythe's actual weight is around 100kg. Even the Twilight Spear should be significantly knocked back upon colliding with such a heavy weapon. Yet, in reality, it barely gets pushed back at all.

"This is actually a pretty important discovery,"

Rei murmured, shifting his gaze back to the Death Scythe... when suddenly, he sensed a presence approaching him.

He recognized that presence. And though Set should have sensed it too, he showed no sign of concern. So Rei, likewise, waited for the approaching figure without taking any particular defensive stance.

"Rei, lunch is ready. Let's eat."

The voice belonged to Kathy, the wife of Augusto, the master of this mansion.

The person who had cried and rejoiced so much upon Rei's return yesterday that it had surprised him.

At first, she had addressed him as "Rei," but now she simply called him by his first name.

Given who Cathy was, it was only natural she would object if Rei said he was heading back out into the desert.

Normally, Rei wouldn't need to worry about it that much, but considering how much Cathy cared about him, he decided it was necessary to kill time like this for a few days.

It wasn't purely out of concern for Cathy; he needed to refine his Two-Spear Style, and this seemed like a good opportunity to focus on that.

Plus, getting to eat Cathy's delicious cooking was a major plus.

"Alright, I get it. Set, see you later then."

"Grrr..."

Saying this to Set, who looked a little lonely, Ray took out a block of oak meat for lunch.

Rei asked if he wanted it cooked, but Set shook his head, clearly indicating he wanted it raw.

Though Set had grown quite accustomed to human food living with Rei, he was still a griffin. He probably still craved raw meat occasionally.

Leaving Set behind, who had started eating the block of orc meat as if to say, "What was that lonely look a few seconds ago?", Rei entered the mansion with Kathy.

"It was an item box, right? ...It's so convenient, I'm jealous. You can just store raw meat like that, right?"

"You can even store cooked food while it's still warm. It's incredibly handy."

"I bet. If you can store food like that, you can stock up when it's cheap. Trying that in Gosh? Most of the time, it'd go bad before you could eat it."

Even an item box—a magic item that anyone who saw it would desperately want to obtain—was valued by housewife Kathy solely for its ability to prevent food from spoiling.

(From a housewife's perspective, that might be natural. Especially in a desert like this.)

Rei, wearing his Dragon Robe, was largely unaffected by the harsh temperatures. But for Kathy, who didn't have such convenient magic items, securing food was likely extremely important.

(There were magic items like refrigerators, but being magic items, they were quite expensive. Maybe the not-so-wealthy Augusto household couldn't afford one? ...No, they spend quite a bit on meals, so you'd think they'd have one.)

Holding onto this slight question, she entered the mansion and headed straight for the dining hall.

Lunch was already laid out there, and the gatekeeper, Günter, was present.

"Hey, Rei. We're eating already."

"...Couldn't you have at least waited until Kathy arrived?"

He didn't particularly mind that they'd started eating, but he still thought they could have waited at least until Kathy returned...

"It's fine, Rei. Günter has to work hard as the gatekeeper, you know."

"That's how it is. Anyway, why don't you eat quickly too? Well, in your case, you can't go outside the city anyway."

Gunter said this with a spiteful grin at Kathy's words.

Since Cathy, who had prepared the meal, seemed fine with it, Rei sat down at the table too.

The meal consisted of hard-baked black bread, skewered meat, and a soup richly seasoned with spices.

Compared to the first meal he'd eaten when he first arrived at Gosh or the one he'd had yesterday after returning, there were fewer dishes. Still, each one had been carefully prepared by Kathy, enhancing the flavor.

It was far tastier than anything you'd get at a local shop.

"...So, what are you doing this afternoon, Rei? More training?"

"No, I was thinking of heading into town for a bit..."

Rei started to say, but before he could finish, Kathy spoke up.

"The city is dangerous, you know?"

"...Well, technically, I'm classified as a high-rank adventurer—a Rank B adventurer. I even have a nickname."

Normally, even if she said something like this, Rei wouldn't bother listening.

But this time, he understood that Kathy was genuinely worried about him, so he couldn't bring himself to push ahead with his plans.

"I know that, but you just got back yesterday, right? Why not take it easy a little longer? Rest your body first, okay?"

Kathy's concern was partly due to Rei's appearance.

He was visibly small, and in Cathy's eyes, he was still a child.

Of course, she'd heard from her beloved husband that Rei was strong.

Rei took pride in being an adventurer who had survived all this time, and indeed, in this world of Gosh... no, in the entire world of Elgin, there probably weren't many who had lived such a turbulent life as Rei had over the past few years.

But even someone like Rei finds his rhythm thrown off completely when faced with Cathy, who treats him like a child from the very start.

He must understand this. Günter watches Rei and Kathy's exchange with a somewhat amused smile.

"At least stay home today. ...Understood?"

Because it was a statement born of genuine goodwill, Rei couldn't bring himself to refuse.

(I kinda wanted to do that Sand Lizardman skinning thing... Huh? Oh, right. It doesn't have to be inside a building... Ah, but doing it on a cleared lot might make a mess with blood and stuff?)

Rei hesitated a little, but since he'd been told not to leave the grounds of this mansion, there wasn't much else he needed to do.

Besides skinning the Sand Lizardman, the only other thing was practicing the Two-Spear Style.

(But mastering the Two-Spear Style today isn't something I can just do right away. It requires long hours of training.)

What he was about to learn was a ridiculous act that no one in their right mind would even consider.

It was the kind of combat method that, under normal circumstances, one would likely abandon the moment it crossed their mind.

Precisely because of that, I needed to train and master it bit by bit.

"So, I want to dissect a Sand Lizardman at the spot where I was training earlier. Is that okay?"

"Sure, that spot is fine."

"Then I'll get started this afternoon. ...I don't expect to be able to dissect the whole thing, though."

There were over ten Sand Lizardman corpses currently inside Misty Ring.

Dismantling them all by myself wasn't impossible, but it was a pretty tedious task.

(Well, probably just two.)

For now, I'll dismantle two Sand Lizardmen to get two magic stones to absorb. Having decided on his afternoon plans, Rei stood up after finishing lunch.

"I'll serve Sand Lizardman meat for dinner tonight, so I'd be happy if you could make something delicious with it."

"Yes, leave it to me. I'll put my best effort into making a delicious dinner."

Leaving Kathy smiling behind, Rei ultimately left the mansion with Günter, who had stayed until the very end.
