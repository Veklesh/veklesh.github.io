"Yum!"

Relyu eats the dish served for lunch with obvious relish.

That said, the meal itself isn’t particularly expensive.

In fact, it’s the kind of food you’d normally find in a diner in Gilm.

...Relieu probably understands that, but I suppose it’s still a joy to be able to eat such warm, freshly made food in a place so far from the city.

“If Relue has a nickname, couldn’t she afford a magic pouch or something? With that, she could carry food around normally instead of relying on preserved foods like dried meat. …Well, as for lunch on the day we set out… even if everything goes perfectly, she probably won’t be able to eat until dinner at the earliest.”

It’s already starting to feel like autumn, but it’s still quite warm during the day.

The fact that it’s warm rather than hot is probably a sign that the season is advancing… but even so, I couldn’t rule out the possibility of the food spoiling.

“I don’t mind going out on assignments or whatever, but not being able to eat Shumine’s home-cooked meals really hurts.”

The way he muttered this with such feeling showed just how much Relieu loved his wife.

That said, the women—like Elena—who had heard similar stories countless times in the Set Cage wore expressions that suggested they were thoroughly fed up with it.

“Even when I’m working on a job, just knowing I’ll get to eat delicious food keeps me motivated.”

“That’s true, but if we’re eating food that smells delicious out in the woods, wouldn’t that just invite monsters or something to attack us?”

Naturally, smells that stimulate the appetite attract not only adventurers but also animals and monsters in the forest—and in some cases, even bandits.

Relyu said this not because she was worried she couldn’t defeat such opponents… but simply because being attacked while eating would be a huge hassle.

After all, Relue is a Rank A adventurer known as the “Gale.”

Even if some random monster attacked, someone like Relieu could easily defeat it.

Though, as a result, there’s a high chance that their hard-earned meal would end up spilled on the ground.

“True. That might be the case normally… but since we have Set with us, we don’t have to worry about that.”

“Grr!”

Set, who had been eating a chunk of lizardman meat, let out a cry of “Leave it to me!” upon hearing his name called.

To Relyu, who was watching Set enviously, Rei continued, “Well…”

“Normally, if they sense Set’s presence, they wouldn’t come near us. But there are some who can’t pick up on Set’s aura, so we can’t be completely at ease.”

Among monsters, including goblins, there are some that will challenge anyone to a fight without hesitation, even if that opponent is Set.

And if they’re overwhelmed by Set’s power, they’ll run away immediately.

As for thieves, many of them aren’t even the type to become adventurers.

While those operating around Sabrusta might be somewhat skilled, many thieves active in rural areas lack the ability to gauge an opponent’s strength.

Rei has experienced this many times before—thieves attacking without even realizing that Set the Griffin is present.

……Then again, Rei is known among thieves as the “Bandit Eater,” simply because hunting thieves is his hobby.

If the opponent is a bandit, Rei is actually the one who initiates the attack.

“So, how long exactly will it take to get to Golz?”

Vihera asks as she eats a soup packed with seafood made from fish Rei and the others had just caught at sea.

Although Rei and the others had paid for the ingredients and had the soup made for them in the dining hall, she still looked slightly dissatisfied—likely because it didn’t taste as good as the decadent seafood soup Bistol had made.

It was a perfectly delicious soup on its own, but having tasted a better one, it was only natural that her standards would be higher.

“Hmm, yeah. Considering Set’s speed, I think we’ll arrive sometime between tomorrow morning and noon.”

As Marina casually answers Vihera’s question, Relieu wears a slightly awkward expression.

She wanted to reach Golz as soon as possible and clear what appeared to be a dungeon.

There was no doubt she felt that way, but still… she never imagined they’d actually arrive in Golz tomorrow…

From what Relue had heard from Walker, the journey to Golz wasn’t something they could complete that quickly.

(Even if I consider the fact that they share the same alias, the moment Set is there, we don’t stand a chance. Plus, he’s got an item box too.)

Of course, it’s not that Relue has any particular feelings toward Rei and the others.

Still, as an adventurer who had made a name for himself from a young age—though he’s still quite young now—Relieu saw himself as their senior.

That’s precisely why, as an adventurer, she’d harbored a certain degree of rivalry toward the members of Rei’s “Crimson Wings”... but she’d come to understand firsthand just how outstanding their abilities were in every respect.

Set’s mobility, Rei’s item box, Marina’s spirit magic.

Even just off the top of my head, they have these distinct traits, and naturally, their combat prowess—essential for any adventurer—is extraordinary.

“Come to think of it, I know this is a bit late to ask, but are you working solo, Relieu?”

Ignoring the slight inner conflict stirring within Relieu, Rei asked the question, and Relieu nodded in response.

“Yeah, that’s right. I sometimes get invited to join a group on a temporary basis, but basically, I work solo.”

“……Well, I suppose that’s not surprising for someone with a nickname.”

Marina, who had been listening to Rei and Relieu’s conversation, muttered briefly.

Generally speaking, it’s standard for adventurers to work in parties.

Especially for high-rank adventurers, the quests they take on tend to be highly difficult, and it’s not uncommon to face high-rank monsters, particularly in extermination quests.

When fighting such high-rank monsters, there’s no doubt that the more combat power you have, the better, so going solo puts you at a significant disadvantage.

But… even in such a disadvantageous situation, adventurers with nicknames are the exception.

In Elgin, where quality often trumps quantity, adventurers with nicknames are prime examples of that quality.

That is precisely why it comes as no surprise to hear that Relieu is a solo adventurer.

Moreover, as a former guild master, Marina naturally knew of Relue, who bore the epithet “Gale.”

Since it had been quite some time since Marina had handed the guild master position over to Walker, the possibility that she might be in a party could not be ruled out.

Of course, even for an adventurer with a nickname, it’s undoubtedly safer to travel in a party.

Elk, who goes by the nickname “Axe of the Thunder God” yet travels with his family, is proof of that.

……That said, it’s also true that when you possess extraordinary abilities like those of a nickname-holder, there simply aren’t that many people who are capable of forming a party with you in the first place.

In Elk’s case, too, it’s undeniable that his party is based on family ties: his wife, Min, is a Rank A mage, and his son, Rodos, is the other party member.

“Well, I suppose so. In that sense, you guys from the Crimson Wings could be called a miraculous party formed by people who all possess a certain level of strength. …And you’re all beauties, too.”

That’s what Relieu said, but despite his tone, he didn’t seem to be envious of Rei.

As a devoted husband, completely devoted to his wife Shumine, even if he recognizes other women as beautiful, he probably doesn’t see them as potential partners.

(There are clearly some who are weaker than the others, but that’s not really my place to comment on.)

Watching Byune intently eating her meal, Relyu thought to himself.

Although Byune had grown considerably stronger compared to her days in Exil, her combat prowess was still inevitably inferior within the Crimson Wings party.

However, Relieu wouldn’t dare say anything about other parties.

He understood from past experience that everyone has their own circumstances, and meddling in them could lead to trouble later on.

After that, nothing much happened, and they finished their meal...

“Well then, shall we have a little mock battle?”

After a short break following lunch, Vihera said to Relue.

At Vihera’s words, Relue wore a slightly displeased expression.

Even though this was his first time meeting Rei and the others, he had naturally heard rumors about them.

And among those rumors was how much Vihera loved combat—especially fighting powerful enemies—so Relu vaguely understood why things were turning out this way.

Still, she didn’t refuse, likely because, as they were about to tackle the dungeon, it was necessary for them to understand each other’s capabilities.

Rei and Elena didn’t know Relu’s fighting style, and while Relu knew of their strength through rumors, she had never seen it with her own eyes.

The only one who knew both of their fighting styles was Marina, the former guild master, but it was certainly faster to actually show them the fight directly than for Marina to explain everything.

And so, an activity that was a bit too intense to simply call “after-dinner exercise” began.

“Just to be clear, no fatal injuries, please.”

“I suppose so. It’s a shame, but I guess we have no choice.”

Hearing Vihera’s words, spoken with genuine—truly heartfelt—regret, Relieu turned her gaze toward Rei, the party leader.

Is this really going to be okay?

His gaze was filled with that question, but Rei simply returned a smile.

(Come on, she’s not actually trying to kill me here just because I look suspicious… is she?)

That was just a joke on Relyu’s part, but seeing Vihera’s expression—brimming with determination… no, with a murderous intent—he couldn’t help but feel that it might not be entirely a joke after all.

Of course, Rei and the others have absolutely no such intention.

The reason Relu felt his life was in danger was solely due to Vihera’s fighting instinct—her desire to battle a formidable opponent.

“Well then… shall we begin?”

Vihera, who had been lightly tapping the ground with her greaves, kicked off the ground with a seductive smile, clearly relishing the prospect of a battle against a formidable foe.

Even for Relue, who goes by the nickname “Gale,” the speed was enough to make her eyes widen in surprise.

But even though he was surprised by her speed, Relieu wasn’t about to let Vihera’s attack land without a fight.

Relyu leaped backward to dodge Vihera’s fist as it came flying toward him… and the moment he landed, he kicked off the ground with a force that matched Vihera’s.

As Vihera, whose punch had been dodged, closed in before his very eyes, Relieu swung the longsword still sheathed in its scabbard.

The strike was so fast that an ordinary adventurer would likely be hit without even understanding what had happened.

But… Vihera clearly saw Relu’s attack coming, and with just a slight shift of her body, she dodged the blow.

On the contrary, she attempts to drive her clenched fist into Relieu’s torso as a counterattack.

The fact that she uses a fist rather than her secret technique, the Magic Palm—which channels magical energy directly into the body—suggests that Vihera is, at the very least, showing some consideration for Relieu.

Relyu is using his longsword without drawing it from its sheath for much the same reason.

Relyu uses the hilt of his longsword to deflect the fist aimed at his solar plexus.

The sound of the gauntlet and hilt colliding echoes through the area, producing a high-pitched metallic clang.

As the shockwave reverberated, Vihera and Relue both stepped back to create distance between them.

Seeing that her opponent could keep up with her attacks, Vihera flashes a pleased smile… while Relieu wears an expression that seems to say, “Please, spare me if you can.”

As far as Relu was concerned, Vihera was by no means an opponent he could afford to let his guard down against.

She had heard rumors of his skill before, but fighting him like this—even though neither of them was going all out—made that fact crystal clear.

Now that he understood that, his honest desire was to stop fighting any further.

“Hey, listen! You’ve got a pretty good idea of how strong I am now, right!? So, I don’t think we need to keep fighting any longer. What do you say?”

At Relieu’s shout, Marina glanced at the others, and after confirming they were nodding, she called off the mock battle.

...Vihera, who had been stopped in the middle of the fight, looked a little dissatisfied, though.
