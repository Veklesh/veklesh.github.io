"Well, the rest of the gang will be here around evening, so hang in there until then!"

The adventurer in the carriage waved vigorously at the men named Rei who remained behind as he drove off.

The number of adventurers still present had already dwindled considerably.

This was because, after handing over the inventory of the loot they’d acquired and the captured bandits to the adventurers in charge of transport, they’d scattered into the surrounding area to search for the next bandits.

“That carriage is something else. …Those bandits have it rough.”

An adventurer standing beside Rei muttered as he watched the carriage drive away.

In fact, Rei couldn’t help but agree with those words.

The treasure the adventurers had obtained—which included weapons and armor—was loaded in large quantities onto the wagon, and the captured bandits were walking as if being dragged along by it.

The pace itself wasn’t particularly fast, but as long as the carriage kept moving at a steady speed, if any of the bandits were to stumble or, worse yet, reach a point where they could no longer walk, they would be dragged along by the carriage, placing a heavy burden on the other bandits as well.

This continues all the way until they arrive in Gilm.

Naturally, they remain bound even while sleeping at night, and since there is no such thing as a bathroom break, they simply relieve themselves wherever they are.

Since they couldn’t be killed, they were given some preserved food—like dried meat and hardtack—and water, which was essential under the intense summer sun, but even that wasn’t enough.

At best, it’s just enough to keep them alive until they reach Gilm.

……Weakened bandits naturally fetch a lower price when sold as slaves, but the adventurers participating in the bandit hunt are well aware of this and are still relying on the adventurers mentioned earlier.

If they absolutely hated the idea of the bandits they captured growing weak, they could have simply arranged their own means of transporting the prisoners and bandits.

Having understood that, they entrusted the bandits to the adventurers prepared by the guild—or, more accurately, at Daskar’s request—so they have no grounds to complain.

“Well, considering what the bandits have done so far, I think it’s a natural outcome. In that sense, I think Tarania’s response was quite lenient.”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s words, Elena, who had been watching Set and Yellow playing a short distance away, approached Rei.

“Even so. For now, Tarania is still being treated as a suspect. The bandits’ words alone aren’t conclusive evidence. Until solid evidence comes to light, we can’t treat her the same as the bandits.”

“……But she looked completely guilty, didn’t she?”

“Even so, given her position as a direct subordinate of the magistrate, we have to show her some consideration. Though, there’s no doubt that the magistrate and the noble who sent her are in for a rough time from here on out.”

Was he saying that because the noble who ruled over Sublusta was, after all, a member of the Noble Faction?

Despite Duke Kerebel, the head of the Noble Faction, having explicitly ordered them not to interfere with Gilm’s expansion project, this incident occurred.

She had disregarded her father’s orders, causing trouble for Gilm… and for Rei… and inflicting great harm on innocent people just to satisfy her own selfish desires… For all these reasons, Elena harbored anger toward Talanian and her superior, the magistrate.

So much so that, if possible, she would like to head to Sublusta right now and capture the magistrate.

But she couldn’t go that far right now.

Understanding that, she chose the option of dealing with the bandits operating in this area first.

In fact, if asked which would have a more immediate impact—defeating the bandits here or capturing the magistrate—it would naturally be defeating the bandits who were actively operating.

(Come to think of it, what did the survivors of that bandit group I defeated earlier end up doing? The fact that so many bandits have been captured like this suggests my actions weren’t as effective as I’d thought. Well, considering we’ve managed to capture so many bandits thanks to that, I suppose the end result is fine.)

That was Rei’s thought, but in reality, thanks to the information spread by the bandits he let escape back then, the more cautious and capable bandits had already vanished from the area around Sublusta.

They had realized that attacking a caravan right now—while Gilm is undergoing expansion—would be a high-risk, high-reward endeavor.

Since they were a band of thieves capable of operating right on the edge of the frontier—in Sublusta—they likely judged that there was no point in going out of their way to operate in this high-risk area under the current circumstances.

In fact, among the vanished bandits were some with enough skill to take on even mediocre high-rank adventurers. For such bandits, while the pay might be lower than in the current area around Sublusta, there are plenty of places far safer than here.

……Of course, that also means that when those thieves do start operating, low-rank adventurers stuck out in the sticks won’t stand a chance against them.

“Well, then, let’s go find the next band of thieves.”

“Yeah. The fact that a bunch of bandits have been hunted down is probably already known to the others. Don’t expect it to go as smoothly as the first time; you’d better keep a close eye on your surroundings while you’re out there.”

“I know. Don’t let my appearance fool you—I’m actually pretty confident in my skills.”

“I’m sure you are. Otherwise, you wouldn’t have been called for this job. …Still, it never hurts to be careful, right?”

"You’re right. I’ll keep those words in mind. See ya."

Saying that, the adventurer who had been talking to Rei headed straight for his companions.

Before he knew it, the number of adventurers who had remained—already few to begin with—had dwindled even further.

“Rei, what should we do now? Should we split into three groups after all?”

The first to agree with Elena’s suggestion was Vihera.

As far as Vihera was concerned, she wanted to fight as many of them as possible.

Given that, splitting into three groups was exactly what she had hoped for.

“I agree with Elena. If we can reduce the number of bandits here even a little, that’ll help the expansion work on Gilm go more smoothly, right?”

Rei and the others knew that Vihera’s true feelings differed from those words—or rather, they weren’t exactly different—but none of them made a move to challenge her.

After all, there was no doubt that reducing the number of bandits here would make things easier for the merchants passing through the Sublusta area.

Since that was the goal, there was no way Rei wouldn’t agree with Vihera and Elena.

“Alright. Then, let’s do that. So, with that settled, let’s each go our separate ways.”

True to his word, Rei and the others immediately set off in different directions.

Rei went with Set, while Marina, Vihera, and Byune went together, and Elena, Ara, and Yero went together.

Once these three groups were gone, the adventurers who had remained until the very end also headed into the forest.

……Some of them headed in the direction Rei and the others had gone.

Were they trying to pick up the scraps left behind by the bandits Rei and Set had defeated? Or were they thinking that if Rei’s group had gone that way, there might be other bandits there?

What they were thinking about that was entirely up to the adventurers themselves.

“Damn it, damn it, damn it.”

In one of the carriages heading toward Gilm, Tarania was frantically trying to figure out how to resolve the situation.

Muttering to herself while biting the nail of her right thumb—a habit of hers—the adventurers serving as guards in the same carriage shot her exasperated glances.

At first, the adventurers had tried to strike up a conversation to help her relax a little… but once she told them, “Don’t talk to me so casually, you filthy-looking adventurers,” it was only natural that they would lose any desire to speak to her.

But if the adventurers talked among themselves, they’d end up on the receiving end of Tarania’s frustration with the current situation.

The group of adventurers in this carriage, along with the rest of the convoy, were all people who had once been captured by Tarania in Sublusta.

That said, even though they were “captured,” they weren’t thrown in jail or interrogated; they were simply brought along to the handover point for the bandits, so they didn’t harbor that much resentment.

Moreover, thanks to Elena’s actions, they had gone from heaven to hell—from being direct subordinates of the magistrate to criminals (though currently still treated as suspects)—so their pity outweighed any resentment or anger.

For that very reason, even seeing Tarania’s attitude, she had enough emotional resilience to forgive her.

“Why am I being put through this… That’s right, it’s all those bandits’ fault. Those bandits are to blame. Why can’t they understand the meaning of my glance?”

She muttered, her frustration evident, but expecting him to grasp her intentions from a mere glance—when they hadn’t even discussed anything beforehand—was simply unreasonable.

All Tarania could do now was try to figure out how to deal with the current situation... or, to be precise, engage in such behavior while essentially escaping from reality.

She was seriously trying to figure out a way to resolve the situation, but now that Elena knew her name and face, even if she managed to escape from this carriage, there would be no way out afterward.

Perhaps fed up with the atmosphere inside the carriage, one of the adventurers suddenly spoke up.

Even though he knew that speaking up in this situation might make Tarania take it out on him, he simply couldn’t stand the gloomy atmosphere any longer.

“Those bandits are idiots, aren’t they? They get captured like this for a moment’s pleasure, then get dragged along in a carriage and forced to walk… and in the end, they end up as slaves.”

Why on earth did he have to bring up a topic like that!

That was the heartfelt thought of the other adventurers riding in the wagon.

They were thinking, “If you had to say something, why not talk about how autumn is coming soon or something like that? Why…?”

“Hah, heh… Is that so? You’re trying to say that, in the end, you and I are no different from thieves, aren’t you? You filthy little adventurer. I can easily imagine what kind of people your parents were. If you were raised poorly, you’re also stupid. Since everything about you is bad, you’ll spend your whole life in a position where you’re being used by others.”

From Tarania’s perspective, it was nothing more than taking it out on him.

Just as he was agonizing over his hopeless current situation, she mocked the bandit as if to rub salt in the wound.

It wouldn’t have been strange for her to feel as though she, too—having been associated with that thief—had been insulted.

That’s why, filled with the helpless frustration of her current situation, Talanía lashed out at the adventurer who had made that flippant remark… but she clearly went too far.

If it had been just about himself, the adventurer probably wouldn’t have gotten that angry.

But when even his parents were mocked, there was no way he could put up with it.

“A filthy adventurer, huh? I never thought I’d hear that from a filthy criminal like you. Well, since you were in cahoots with that thief, you’ll likely be executed on the spot. Even if you’re lucky, you’ll be sold into slavery. …Ha! That’s a fitting end for a filthy criminal. Or should I say, a filthy slave?”

“What!?”

She never expected some adventurer to talk back to her like that… Tarania was stunned for a moment, but she quickly realized she’d been insulted, and her face flushed with anger.

You can insult others, but you’ll never be insulted yourself.

That was Tarania’s belief.

That wasn’t necessarily wrong.

After all, up until now, it had always been true.

But… that was only true up until now.

Yes, it was only possible because of his position as a direct subordinate of the magistrate of Sublusta.

Born and raised as the second son of a relatively large trading company in a certain town, he had worked without any real failures even after coming of age. However, since his older brother was just as capable as Tarania, it was only natural that the older brother would inherit the company.

Of course, if Tarania had wanted to, she could have worked as the eldest son’s subordinate, or perhaps been put in charge of a branch in some other town.

However, unable to forgive the fact that he had been outdone by his older brother, Tarania simply ran away.

After that, thanks to his cunning, resourcefulness, and competence—at least from a general perspective—he established connections with the magistrate… and came to Sublusta.

And now, someone like her was being mocked by this adventurer-type.

The moment that thought crossed her mind, she heard a snapping sound in her head, and in the next instant, Tarania’s consciousness sank into darkness.
