Although Rei was confident in his physical stamina, fighting monsters and performing a dance like this in front of an audience were, after all, two completely different matters.

Normally, this wouldn’t have been that physically demanding for Rei, but dancing while being watched by a large crowd was probably exhausting for him after all.

“Phew.”

Rei downed the fruit juice prepared not by Miranda, but by a maid working as a server at the party, and finally let out a sigh of relief.

Seeing Rei ask the maid for another glass of fruit water, Elena smiled and spoke.

“Hehe, looks like you’re more tired than I thought.”

“Yeah. A little more than I expected… no, actually, quite a bit. When I was dancing just with Elena, I didn’t feel that tired.”

“It’s probably because there were so many people watching.”

As Elena spoke while accepting another glass of fruit water from the maid, Rei glanced around and nodded in agreement.

While many people are watching Liberte and Arcadia dance, just as many are watching Rei and Elena.

For some reason, whenever those people made eye contact with Rei, they would quietly look away.

(No, it’s not like I just bite anyone and everyone, you know? …Oh, come to think of it…)

The word “bite” made Rei think of Leelin, and he scanned the room for her.

Judging by how she had been hanging around Elena, she might still be at the party.

That’s what he thought, but she was no longer there.

Elena, too, no longer showed any signs of the irritation she’d felt during her earlier exchange with Lirin.

That irritation must have been largely dispelled by dancing with Rei.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“Nothing, really.”

In response to Elena’s question, Rei brushed it off with those words.

He did so because if he said anything careless here, there was a chance he might be reminded of Lirin, whom he had temporarily forgotten.

“Is that so? …Still, Father and Mother seem to be enjoying themselves while they dance. Don’t you think so?”

“From where I’m standing, I don’t think it’s anything to make such a big deal about.”

At Rei’s words, Elena gave him a look that seemed to say, “Don’t you get it?”

However, while from Rei’s perspective they appeared to be dancing normally and gracefully, they still didn’t seem particularly happy.

Since he hadn’t known Liberte for very long, Rei had no choice but to accept that if Elena said so, then that must be the case.

“Hey, hey, Rei. How much have you been practicing your dancing?”

It was Brewitt who interrupted Rei and Elena’s conversation.

He probably never expected Rei to be able to dance that well.

He smiled as if he found it thoroughly amusing, raised the wine in his hand to his lips… and looked slightly disappointed.

“What’s wrong? I don’t think that wine tastes that bad.”

Seeing Brewitt’s expression, Elena asked immediately.

From Elena’s perspective, since this party was hosted by the Duke of Kerebel’s House, she felt there could be no room for error.

That’s why she asked about Brewitt’s reaction… but in response to Elena, Brewitt shook his head, saying it was nothing.

“Ah, there’s nothing wrong with the taste of the wine. It’s just that, while I can drink fancy wines like this, I don’t really like them that much. I prefer things like the ale served at taverns around town.”

As someone who often went out into the city alone, Brewitt preferred the kind of ale that ordinary people drank, rather than the wine favored by nobles and the wealthy. …Even though Brewitt was, in fact, a full-fledged noble himself.

That was precisely why he couldn’t drink the kind of wine he preferred in places like this, and why he was once again dissatisfied with the taste of the wine.

Even so, the fact that he could clearly tell the wine he was drinking was of high quality showed that he did, in fact, have a keen palate.

“I see. …If you’d like, I could have them bring you some ale or something?”

“No, it’s fine. Drinking ale in a place like this feels a bit out of place. …But anyway. Like I said earlier, how come Rei can dance so well? Look, even now, everyone else is staring at him.”

While telling Elena not to worry about it, Brewitt glanced around the room.

As if drawn by his gaze, Rei looked over, and when several nobles met her eyes, they hurriedly looked away, smiled and bowed slightly, and among them was a woman whose cheeks were faintly flushed.

Although he had only danced with Elena, that performance was enough to earn the recognition of some of the nobles attending the party.

It was only a select few nobles who acknowledged Rei; naturally, there were still many who viewed him with hostility.

Still, not a single noble would even consider picking a fight with Rei at this moment.

To be precise, there were indeed some who considered picking a fight with Rei, but they were stopped by the nobles around them.

With several nobles now viewing Rei favorably, any noble who tried to pick a fight with her would undoubtedly be met with scornful glances throughout high society.

For nobles, reputation isn’t everything… but it does carry significant weight.

Given that, ordinarily, no one would think to take actions that would damage their reputation.

“...I see. It seems she’s taken quite a liking to you. That woman was Viscount Mool’s sister, wasn’t she? She was famous for being lovely... but Rei is no slouch either.”

Looking at Rei and the woman blushing at him, Elena said this with a smile. ...She was smiling, but Elena’s eyes were certainly not smiling.

She was smiling, yet she wasn’t smiling.

Faced with that gaze, Rei hesitated, unsure of what to say, as if thinking, “Ah…”

He knew what Elena was worried about, but he wasn’t sure if he should bring it up himself here.

(I thought Elena was tolerant of polygamy to begin with... Is this something different from that?)

Or maybe it’s because the other person was someone like Marina or Vihera.

In any case, Rei glares at Brewitt, who is giving him an amused look, and opens his mouth.

“For now, just know there’s no need to worry…”

“…………I see.”

It was the same single phrase as a few seconds ago, but the time it took for those words to come out had definitely increased.

Elena, whatever she thought of Rei’s words, blushed even more than the woman she’d called the Viscount Mool’s sister, and said nothing more.

“Heh heh heh. Man, you two are really something.”

Glancing back and forth between Rei and Elena, Brewitt said this, looking as though he could barely contain his laughter.

It could be said that the teasing undertone in his words was typical of Brewitt.

“So, then. …What are we going to do now? Want to dance one more time?”

“That dance really wore me out. If possible, I don’t want to dance anymore today. …Like I told Elena, I never imagined dancing while being watched would be this exhausting.”

“I bet. I’ve been there too.”

“……You, Brewitt? You’re the last person I’d expect to look good dancing.”

“Listen, Rei, you seem to have completely forgotten, but I am, after all, the heir apparent to the Count’s House. I can handle something like that with ease. …Unlike a certain someone.”

It was obvious who “someone” referred to, and Brewitt said this while flashing a slightly spiteful smile at Rei.

Perhaps he didn’t find Brewitt’s attitude amusing. Rei smiled as he looked at Brewitt and spoke.

“Being able to dance and actually dancing with someone are two different things, aren’t they? …So, Brewitt, do you have someone who’d dance with you?”

“Guh, y-you…”

Brewitt understands that he is large.

While his massive frame was incredibly useful for street fights, he also understood that it was ill-suited for dancing at a party like this.

After all, some of the noblewomen would scream just at the sight of Brewitt’s size.

It wasn’t that no one wanted to dance with Brewitt… but the fact was, there weren’t that many.

“Hehe.”

Watching the exchange between Rei and Brewitt, a smile suddenly escaped Elena’s lips.

It was the kind of smile that suggested she’d tried to hold it back but couldn’t quite manage it.

Rei and Brewitt both turned their gaze toward her at the sudden chuckle, and Elena apologized with a laugh.

“S-sorry. Rei and Brewitt’s conversation was just so funny. I couldn’t help it…”

“Was it really that funny? I don’t think it was anything special.”

“No, you two are really in sync. It’s like you’ve been best friends forever.”

“Ugh.”

At the words that came out of Elena’s mouth, Rei and Brewitt both let out a disgusted sound in unison.

Their reaction was so perfectly in sync that it tickled Elena’s funny bone all over again.

Under normal circumstances, Elena wouldn’t have laughed this hard.

There was no doubt that the reason she was acting this way now was because they were at a party, and she was feeling exhilarated from dancing to her heart’s content with Rei.

“Ah… Anyway. Maybe we should take a look over there?”

Deciding that saying anything more on the matter would only make Elena’s situation worse, Brewitt noticed an old woman staring at them with a beaming smile and gestured for them to look that way.

That old woman was, of course, someone both Rei and Elena recognized.

“Remuria, you’re back?”

It wasn’t as if she had heard Rei’s mutter, but Lemuria approached them with a smile on her face.

She seemed to be in a very good mood, likely because she had succeeded in sending Set’s fresh feathers to her territory.

That’s what Rei thought, but…

“Ho ho ho. It seems you’ve been up to quite a bit of a stir, haven’t you?”

Remuria said this with a kind smile, and as she looked at the nobles watching the scene from the sidelines, a somewhat refreshed expression appeared on her face.

Setting aside those who knew that Rei had just left the party venue with Lemuria, there were many in this vast ballroom who were unaware of it.

For that very reason, there were certainly quite a few people wondering, “What kind of relationship do Rei and Lemuria have?”

Some were surprised when acquaintances told them about it, while many others found it understandable, given that Rei was a renowned adventurer.

Feeling slightly fed up with the increased attention from those around him, Rei spoke to Remuria.

“You’re here pretty early.”

A murmur ripples through the crowd.

Many of those who heard Rei’s words were taken aback by her tone.

They knew he was an adventurer, but they couldn’t believe he would speak that way to the head of a Duke’s House.

Although Remuria is known for her calm temperament now, she was famous for her fiery temper just over a decade ago.

In fact, it was not uncommon for him to mete out harsh punishment to those who had been disrespectful to him.

For that very reason, many wondered, “If she’s using language like Rei’s…”

Among those who disliked Rei, there were even some who wished he would suffer a terrible fate at Remuria’s hands… but the wishes of such people were never granted.

“Hmm. It is precisely because she can summon monsters with exceptional speed that I have hired her.”

Remuria’s satisfied expression made it clear she wasn’t the least bit offended by Rei’s choice of words.

It was only natural that many wondered what on earth had happened.

But Remuria, seemingly completely unconcerned by the stares around her, continued speaking.

“So, I’d like to pay the reward for the request. What do you say?”

“So, you’re asking when I’m free?”

“That’s right. I’d like to say ‘whenever you’re free…’ but unfortunately, I can’t stay here forever. So, if possible, I’d like you to come by tomorrow.”

“I understand. I can’t promise for sure, but I’ll do my best to make it happen.”

At Rei’s words, Lemuria flashed a satisfied smile.
