After hearing Rei’s explanation, Suu-chi looked pleased, but she couldn’t quite bring herself to trust him completely and remained somewhat skeptical.

"Can I really get such a big reward just for dismantling it?"

...In reality, the reward for dismantling a Giganto Turtle is actually less than what you’d get for a kobold extermination request.

However, compared to butchering kobolds in the slums, the reward is clearly higher.

And at the same time, if she sells the Giganto Turtle’s meat to a butcher or other adventurers, it wouldn’t be difficult to earn even more than the base reward.

That is, provided you can resist the temptation to try the Giganto Turtle’s meat, a delicacy in its own right.

“But I’m not an adventurer, you know? Can I really do something like that?”

“Normally, it’d be impossible. But since the Giganto Turtle butchering is a job I’m commissioning, they’ll go to some lengths to accommodate me. …Besides, as things stand now, I don’t know how much we’ll be able to butcher this winter. To be honest, I want to hire as many skilled butchers as possible. In fact, many people with butchering skills—even non-adventurers—are participating in the Giganto Turtle butchering.”

Even including those kinds of people, we’re still overwhelmingly short-handed.

Given that, people from the slums—even if their backgrounds were a bit shady—were more than welcome as long as they did their jobs properly. That said...

“But since you’re from the slums, I think people will look at you with a critical eye. And if you try anything like stealing meat, who knows what might happen. Are you okay with that?”

He emphasized this point.

Many within Gilm were put off simply by the fact that someone was from the slums.

The slums are full of criminals, and they often cause trouble for those living ordinary lives, so in a sense, that was inevitable.

“Eh, uh, yeah. I guess that’s unavoidable. I know we cause all sorts of trouble, too. …Hey, about that Giganto Turtle butchering job, right? Could we have more people doing that? I heard that the more hands on deck, the better, right? If so, I’d like to bring a few friends along.”

Hearing Suu-chi’s words, Rei wondered what he should do.

Having spoken with Suu-chi directly like this, he had a pretty good idea of her personality and was confident she wouldn’t do anything dishonorable.

However, even though they were Suu-chi’s acquaintances, since they were residents of the slums, he had no idea what kind of people they were.

Even if she’s close with Suu-chi, that doesn’t mean she’s close with everyone else.

It wouldn’t be strange at all for someone to think that way.

That said, the fact that they needed extra hands was undeniable.

Even for those without butchering skills, there are plenty of other tasks: carrying the butchered parts, wiping down knives stained with blood and fat, preparing meals... and various other chores.

On top of that, since the slums perform kobold dissections day and night, many people are skilled at it.

That was undoubtedly a welcome development for Rei as well.

“Right… If the slum dwellers Suu-chi brings along cause trouble—if they steal the butchered meat or ingredients, or steal money or tools from those employed by the guild—I’ll impose severe punishment on all of them, including Suu-chi, and I won’t hire them to butcher Giganto Turtles ever again....In fact, they might even lose access to the shops on Gilm’s main street, and strict restrictions would be placed on their use of Guild facilities. If you’re okay with that, feel free to bring in someone else.”

What Rei said was extremely harsh.

He was essentially saying that if even a single person brought in by Suu-chi were to commit theft, the entire group would face the punishment.

However, Rei understood that if he were to hire people from the slums, he wouldn’t feel at ease unless he took measures like that.

That said, it wasn’t him who felt uneasy—it was the people who would be working alongside the slum dwellers.

From the guild’s perspective, dismantling the Giganto Turtle was partly a reward for Rei, so there was no doubt they would listen to his opinions to some extent.

However, since it is a reward for Rei, there’s no doubt they’ll strictly supervise him to ensure he doesn’t try to steal any of it.

“Isn’t that… a little too strict?”

Even to Suu-chi, it must have seemed clearly excessive. She said this with a look of confusion.

But Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by Suu-chi’s confusion and spoke up.

“You’re right. Even I think it’s pretty strict. But since we’re hiring slum dwellers who aren’t adventurers or anything, that level of strictness is necessary, isn’t it? Besides… if Suu-chi only brings people she trusts—people who wouldn’t steal or anything—I don’t think there’ll be a problem, right?”

“W-well, now that you mention it… I suppose so… but what if some of them just get tempted to do something like that on a whim?”

“So just pick people who wouldn’t even think of doing that, right? Besides, it’s not like you have to bring anyone along. In fact, Suu-chi alone would be fine.”

“I’d love that, but it doesn’t work that way. It’s precisely because we’re in the slums that building relationships with people is so important.”

“I’m not saying you have to. It’s just that Suu-chi hasn’t really had any combat training, has she? Trying to take down a kobold in a situation like that seems downright reckless to me.”

In fact, it’s easy to imagine what would have happened to Suu-chi at the hands of those three kobolds if Rei hadn’t come to her rescue.

In the worst-case scenario, there’s a high chance she would have been devoured by them.

Considering that, the job of butchering a Giganto Turtle—which meant she wouldn’t have to fight—was a welcome opportunity for Su-chi.

“I see. I’ll get things moving once I get back home. So, how do we go about accepting that request? We can’t exactly just show up at the guild and ask them to let us take the job, can we?”

“That’s true. Hmm… How do people who aren’t adventurers get quests?”

He muttered the last part under his breath.

Regarding the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle, the Guild had determined—especially after the kobold raid—that adventurers alone would definitely not be enough, so they’d gathered a considerable number of people with dismantling skills, including former adventurers, butchers, and cooks.

Since he hadn’t heard the details, Rei didn’t know how those people were technically receiving the requests.

“For now, just gather in front of the gate when the bell rings at six in the morning tomorrow. I’ll talk to the guild staff. …Oh, and going back to what we were talking about earlier, it would be a big help if you could discreetly gather information on magic items or skills that control monsters, as well as the red cloth.”

When I asked her earlier, Suu-chi didn’t seem to have any specific information either.

But if she knew exactly what kind of information was needed, then maybe… just maybe… there was a chance she could actually get the information he wanted.

That was the reasoning behind Rei’s request.

(Well, I heard the red cloth was used as a warning in the slums, so there’s no guarantee there’s any new information. But as for the matter of controlling the kobolds, I might be able to get some kind of information on that.)

With that in mind, Rei took a cloth bag out of his Misty Ring and flicked a silver coin with his finger, handing it to Suu-chi.

“...What’s this?”

“A fee for information… no, more like war chest to get the information.”

By normal standards, that fee wasn’t particularly high.

In fact, it was the kind of amount an adventurer could easily earn.

But that was only true for adventurers; for a slum dweller like Soo-chi, earning that much money required a considerable amount of effort. …In fact, there were even people who died without ever seeing a silver coin in their entire lives.

When you think about it that way, even if it was just “operating funds,” Rei’s act of handing over a single silver coin was something Suu-chi found hard to believe.

“Is that really okay?”

“Yeah. Just know that I really want that information.”

At Rei’s words, Suu-chi took a deep breath and nodded.

“I understand. I don’t know how much information I’ll be able to gather, but I’ll do my best.”

“Do that. But what I want is real information. Or, even if it’s suspicious information, I want information that clearly shows why it’s suspicious. I don’t need any nonsense.”

With a determined look on her face, Suu-chi nodded silently.

Seeing Suu-chi like that, he decided there was no need to press the matter further for now and changed the subject.

“With tomorrow’s plans in mind, wouldn’t it be better to head back to the slums instead of staying here any longer? Since we don’t have the strength to defeat a kobold, staying here is suicide.”

“Ugh… well…”

She looked around, seeming a little reluctant to leave.

To the slum dwellers, hunting down a kobold must seem like a very lucrative job.

(Though, that’s assuming they have the strength to beat a kobold and the motivation to do so.)

Unlike Suu-chi, it’s by no means rare to find former adventurers in the slums who possess the strength to defeat kobolds.

Many adventurers come as far as Gilm, decide they can’t make it there, and head for the slums—but even those adventurers possess a certain level of skill.

That’s precisely why, in a situation like this one, it would normally be a golden opportunity for such adventurers to make some money… but the reality is that even if they have the skills, there’s nothing to be done if their spirit is broken.

(I do hear stories from time to time about adventurers who get their spirits crushed, become bitter, and end up joining underground organizations...)

As Rei pondered this, Suu-chi, who had been agonizing over what to do, finally nodded and spoke up.

“I understand. I’ll go back to the slums.”

Reluctantly… truly reluctantly, it seemed, but Suu-chi said just that.

For Suu-chi, getting this far had required her to steel herself in many ways.

A grim resolve so strong that she’d even considered it possible she might be devoured by the kobolds.

In fact, if Rei hadn’t saved her, she would almost certainly have been killed by those three kobolds a moment ago.

But precisely because she’d steeled herself like that, it was undeniably true that the whole situation felt somewhat unreal to her.

“Do that, then. I’d be in trouble if my only lead got killed by a kobold.”

“And I don’t want to die with silver coins in my pocket either.”

“Ah… yeah, you’re right.”

To Suu-chi, silver coins must be a fortune—far more than Rei had imagined.

Maybe I should have given her copper coins instead as war funds? Rei wondered, but there was no way he could ask for the silver coins back now.

(Lately... my sense of money has become a bit numb.)

In fact, Rei often offered to pay immediately if it was just gold coins or Platinum coins.

Above all, the magic items Rei coveted were extremely expensive—far beyond what a few silver coins could buy.

Also, the amount of treasure one can acquire through bandit hunting—which combines both hobby and profit—is quite substantial. …Though there are also some poor bandits who only have a few silver coins to their name.

Even when buying food, it wasn’t uncommon for him to purchase an entire pot of soup from a restaurant, just as one might order a bowl.

In terms of financial sense, Rei is clearly worlds apart from the average person these days.

That said, this isn’t limited to Rei alone; most adventurers tend to be that way.

They drink, sleep with women, and buy new gear with the rewards earned from life-or-death battles.

If you keep doing that, it’s only natural that your spending habits would become reckless.

Not everyone is like that, of course; there are some who save up funds to live on after they retire from adventuring.

“Well, I’ll be off then. …Thanks for everything.”

Saying that, she gave a slight bow, her cheeks flushing faintly—perhaps out of embarrassment—before running off.

Rei watched Suu-chi leave, then spoke to Set, who was keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

“Set, maybe we should head to the next place soon. We might have missed a magic item or two.”

“Growl?”

Is that so? Set let out a purr. Perhaps because wandering around this area with Rei was practically like playtime for him, he purred without any sign of reluctance.
