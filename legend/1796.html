The moment the words left Rei’s lips and echoed through the room, Gaiska froze upon hearing them.

The noble, distinguished bloodline of a marquis’s family.

For Gaiska, the very fact that he was descended from that bloodline was a source of pride and honor.

That was precisely why he couldn’t help but assert his superiority over anyone he considered his inferior.

And yet, what had Rei just said?

He said that all he could boast of was a worthless lineage.

That was an insult to the very existence of the Seisol Marquis family, and for Gaiska, it was a remark he could never accept… a remark he must not accept.

Until just a moment ago, Gaiska had completely ignored Rei’s presence, but now that something had been said that touched on his pride, he could not let it go.

If he were to back down without saying a word to Rei right here, it would be tantamount to dragging mud through the noble name of the House of Seysol.

“Hold on! Come on, Gaiska, calm down a bit. See? This is the Duke of Kerebel’s residence. If we cause a scene here, it’ll be a real mess.”

Gaiska was about to say something to Rei, but Ranigus stopped him before the words could actually leave his mouth.

He had intended to use the clash between Gaiska and Rei to turn Rei’s feelings toward him into something favorable, but this sudden, all-out confrontation must have been completely unexpected, even for Ranigus.

But with his bloodline—the very foundation of his standing—being disparaged, Gaiska couldn’t just stay silent and back down.

“Shut up! Even if you have a nickname, you’ve been mocked by nothing more than a lowly adventurer! I can’t just let that slide without doing anything!”

Gaiska’s tone was no longer the affected one he used in front of Elena.

The way he shouted while glaring hatefully at Rei was enough to make anyone tremble with fear—especially since he was a noble, and one descended from a marquis family, no less.

That was the scene, but unfortunately, the person currently standing before Gaiska was Rei.

He was a man famous for never hesitating to use violence, even against a noble.

Neither of them showed any sign of backing down, both fixing their gaze on the other.

Gaiska glared at Rei with hatred, while Rei looked at Gaiska as if he were merely observing a pebble lying on the ground.

……At that moment, Ranigus was trying to calm Gaesca down, but Rei, of course, was also feeling extremely annoyed with Gaesca.

Part of it was that his home in this world—specifically, Gilm, where he was actively cooperating on the current expansion project—had been mocked, and another major factor was that Ara, whom he considered an ally, had been insulted based on self-serving logic.

He wasn’t showing his irritation or anger on the surface, but he was fully prepared to unleash his power on Gaiska the moment the opportunity arose.

“Both of you, let’s leave it at that.”

As if she understood the feelings brewing within Rei, Elena spoke up.

Naturally, Elena wasn’t exactly pleased either, having seen her best friend and trusted subordinate, Ara, so blatantly belittled.

Still, she understood that causing a scene here would be problematic in many ways, which is why she stepped in to stop them.

“Elena! Why are you stopping me!? This man has insulted me… no, me! If I let this slide without doing anything, my honor… the honor of the Marquis of Seisol’s family will be ruined!”

If you’re a noble, you should stand by your own side.

So why won’t you take my side?

Facing Gaiska’s accusing gaze, Elena shifts her own perspective.

It was not the gaze of Elena Kerebel, the daughter of a duke, but the gaze of the Princess General.

Faced with that gaze, Gaiska instantly understood the gap in status between himself and Elena—not in terms of titles or combat prowess, but purely in terms of presence.

“Huh!?”

Elena hadn’t done anything to him; she was merely looking at him.

Yet despite that, a chill ran down Gaiska’s spine, and beads of sweat began to pour from his forehead.

And then, a clicking sound echoed through the room.

The sound was grating on Gaiska’s ears, yet it showed no sign of stopping.

Unable to move, Gaiska thought, “Stop that annoying sound…” but then suddenly realized where it was coming from.

That’s right—the source of the clicking sound was Gaiska’s mouth.

To be precise, it was his teeth, clacking unconsciously.

He was terrified of Elena.

The moment he realized this, the pressure he had been feeling vanished.

Elena had averted her gaze from Gaiska.

Gaiska realized this and instinctively took a few steps back.

Ranigus wondered what had come over Gaiska, who had been shouting just moments ago… but realizing it would likely be pointless to ask here, he turned his gaze back to Rei.

“I heard that Rei is from Gilm. If it’s not too much trouble, I’d like to hear a little about life in the frontier. Would that be all right?”

Normally, Ranigus would have intended to compare himself to Gaiska to make a good impression on Rei.

But before he could do so, Gaiska rendered himself useless through an act that could almost be called a case of reaping what he sowed.

Given that, he was convinced that trying to help Gaiska at this point would undoubtedly be of no benefit to him.

That is precisely why he so readily cut Gaiska loose and approached Rei to try to build a good relationship with him.

That said, his desire to ask about Gilm’s condition was by no means limited to that.

Information, no matter how trivial, can sometimes hold great significance.

This is especially true when it comes to the frontier town of Gilm.

That is precisely why Gilm has a noble district, complete with various noble mansions.

Since Rei and the others have come all the way from Gilm, it’s only natural that I’d want to get any information I can.

Another reason for building a friendly relationship with Rei is that it allows me to obtain necessary information quickly.

Of course, even before considering the information, the top priority is ensuring that Rei’s overwhelming strength doesn’t turn against me… or the Royal Faction.

(In the worst-case scenario, if he could direct his hostility toward anyone other than the faction I belong to...)

As the largest of the three major factions, the Royal Faction itself consists of several sub-factions.

It’s not uncommon for them to cooperate when facing the Noble Faction or the Neutral Faction, but to be hostile at other times.

I wouldn’t be happy to have the Royal Faction turn its hostility toward me, but if it absolutely must come to that, I’d at least prefer that it be directed at someone within the Royal Faction other than the faction I belong to.

It wouldn’t be strange for Ranigus to think that way.

“You mean Gilm? It seems your friend didn’t like Gilm, though?”

“Ah, I guess I gave you the wrong impression. Gaiska and I just happened to be together when we came to see Elena. Of course, we know each other, but that’s all it is. In fact, we belong to different factions. Actually, when it comes to factions, Gaiska is in the same Noble Faction as Elena.”

Since he mentioned the Noble Faction, it was only natural that his gaze turned to Elena.

From Ranigus’s perspective, a deterioration in the relationship between Rei and the Noble Faction was the best possible outcome. …That said, considering the nature of Rei and Elena’s relationship, he didn’t expect things to go so conveniently according to his plan.

“Well, I suppose there are people like that, given that they’re part of the Noble Faction.”

At those words from Rei, Gaiska—who had been listening silently until now… or, to be precise, had been frozen in place by the pressure from Elena—snapped back to his senses.

……Even in a situation like this, the fact that he snapped out of it at Rei’s single word could, in a sense, be called impressive.

“You… don’t you dare regret bringing shame upon the Marquis of Seisol’s family. I’ll make sure you regret it.”

“Wait, am I supposed to regret it, or not? Which is it? …Sigh, whatever. Want to do it right now? If so, I’ll be your opponent.”

At Gaiska’s words, Rei’s gaze hardens for just a moment.

It wasn’t quite murderous intent, and the pressure in his gaze wasn’t as intense as Elena’s had been moments ago.

But even so, knowing Rei’s reputation, Gaiska couldn’t help but worry that he might be in immediate danger.

In fact, if Gaiska accepted Rei’s proposal—though whether it could even be called a “duel” was debatable—Rei had no intention of taking his life, but he had almost no intention of holding back.

Gaiska himself wasn’t particularly physically fit, nor could he gauge his opponent’s strength.

He couldn’t… but even so, he didn’t think he could win against someone with a nickname.

If things continued like this, he would end up dueling Rei in earnest.

Realizing this, Gaiska took a step, then two, backward… yet instead of running off immediately, he turned to Elena and spoke.

“Well then, Elena. I’ll be taking my leave now. Unlike some adventurers out there, I’ve got a lot on my plate!”

It was clearly sour grapes, but the fact that he still had the energy left to utter a parting shot—perhaps that showed he had some guts after all.

In any case, after seeing Gaiska off with those final words… silence remained in the room.

All things considered, by disrupting the atmosphere here and leaving immediately, he had certainly succeeded in making Rei feel annoyed.

“Ah… well, I’m sorry about that. Gaiska, who came with me, caused quite a commotion. To be honest, I never expected him to go off the rails like that.”

“Hmm, I was under the impression you brought Gaiska here specifically for that purpose. Was I mistaken?”

“Hahaha. Come on, there’s no way I’d do something like that, right? It was just a coincidence, a coincidence.”

At Elena’s words, Ranigus smiled without showing the slightest sign of being flustered.

It was the kind of smile that, to anyone unaware of the truth, would make it perfectly natural to believe that Ranigus’s words were genuine.

In fact, even those who understood Ranigus’s true intentions had, for a moment, wondered if his words might actually be true—such was the quality of his acting.

“Well, we’ll see. I suppose I’ll have to judge that based on Ranigus’s words and actions from here on out.”

Hearing Elena’s words, Lanigus couldn’t help but wonder if he had made a slight misstep.

Normally, he wasn’t supposed to stand out this much.

He had planned to focus all attention on Gaiska and build a friendly relationship with Rei without drawing too much attention to himself… but that plan had gone completely awry.

Deep down, Ranigus couldn’t hide his irritation at Gaiska’s careless actions, but he could still vaguely understand why Rei didn’t harbor such strong ill will toward him at the moment.

Whether it was a silver lining or simply that Gaiska had done the bare minimum, the fact that the outcome wasn’t the absolute worst was at least some consolation.

“May I ask you what the town of Gilm was like from Elena’s perspective?”

“That’s a pretty blatant change of subject…”

She let out a soft chuckle.

She flashed a brief smile at Ranigus, then smiled back, deciding to go along with the change of subject.

Ranigus understood Elena’s intentions, but he also knew that saying anything here would put him at a disadvantage.

Given that, he needed to find a way to gloss over the matter of Gaiska for now.

Naturally, Elena and the others knew exactly why Ranigus had brought Gaiska along, but perhaps they decided it was better to keep that to themselves for now rather than bring it up here, so they said nothing more.

“Was it about Gilm? Well, to be honest, when it comes to Gilm, Rei, who’s lived here longer than I have, probably knows more than I do…”

“No, Elena… I want to know about Gilm’s situation from the perspective of the Noble Faction. Of course, I’ve been getting various reports, but the more sources of information, the better.”

Normally, there would be no need for Elena, a member of the Noble Faction, to go out of her way to share information with Ranigus, a member of the Royal Faction.

That’s true… but setting aside any information Elena possesses that might be considered valuable, I figure there’s no harm in sharing details that anyone who’s been to Gilm would know, so I open my mouth.

“That’s right. The first thing that struck me was how lively it was. Perhaps due to the expansion work underway, it’s probably even livelier than Anesis.”

That didn’t mean Anesis was inferior to Gilm; rather, it meant that while Anesis had entered a period of stability, Gilm was currently in a phase of development.

Hearing Elena’s words, Ranigus turned his gaze toward her with interest.
