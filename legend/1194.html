The night after defeating Ambros, Rei lay restlessly on his bed at the Dusk Wheat inn as dusk fell.

The absence of his beloved weapons, the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, was undoubtedly a major factor.

"Heh heh, seeing you like this, Rei, you really are acting your age after all."

Vihera, seated on the bed where Rei lay, remarked with a smile.

Rei shot Vihera a slightly displeased look.

Considering the matter with Ambris, he understood Marina's idea: to research whether any remnants remained and create a magic item that could locate what might be called Ambris's true form.

It was precisely because she understood that she entrusted the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

Of course, it couldn't be ruled out that some alchemists might try to abscond with the magic items, so guild staff kept a close watch on them in the warehouse.

Especially since the Death Scythe wasn't something a human could easily carry, making it difficult to steal.

Still, since we couldn't know what kind of magic items the alchemists possessed, keeping a strict watch was essential.

Moreover, while the Death Scythe's weight and size made it difficult to steal, the Twilight Spear was not significantly heavier than an ordinary spear.

Considering several individuals had cast envious glances at Azimov's abilities, the possibility couldn't be entirely dismissed.

"Well, if something like that were to happen, it'd be Set's turn, right? As long as there's a scent, he'd chase it wherever it leads."

"True... but it still sucks, doesn't it?"

Vihera smiled at Rei, who sighed.

Spending time like this with Rei was undoubtedly a blissful moment for Vihera.

It wasn't that Rei whispered words of love to him.

Even so, simply being with Rei like this allowed him to feel happiness.

...Of course, the sense of fulfillment gained from battling a strong opponent was a different matter entirely.

"Still, do you think we defeated Ambris so easily because it was a fake after all?"

"It wasn't fake. It was definitely one of the Ambris. There were just more of them out there."

"Well, that Ambris kinda seemed smaller than the one we found, you know? It was attached to the werewolf, so I couldn't tell for sure."

He sat up from his lying position.

The Ambris that Rei and Vihera first found... the one that burrowed underground... seemed much larger to Rei.

It was impossible to know if the Ambris Mireine and the others found was the same one Rei had spotted.

If there were more than just one or two Ambris, the possibility that there were many others couldn't be completely ruled out.

"Yeah, how about we change the mood? It's no fun just sitting here thinking about it pointlessly, right?"

"Change of pace... You mean go outside now?"

The only ways Rei could change his mood right now were eating delicious food, training, or playing with Set.

(Oh, reading was another option too... But this world hardly has any novels or such. Fairy tales and the like exist, but...)

Seeing Rei's troubled expression, Vihera shrugged her shoulders lightly.

Her ample curves swayed as she did so, but despite being in the midst of an act on the bed, Rei didn't notice Vihera's appearance.

Vihera felt her confidence in her own appeal waver slightly, but she was also aware that Rei wasn't uninterested in women.

Sometimes, Rei caught her gaze lingering on her body.

...Rei thought Vihera hadn't noticed, but women are sensitive to the gaze directed at them.

Vihera especially understood what kind of stares her outfit often drew, and above all, Rei was vastly different from the ordinary crowd.

"Hey, Rei, you had that magic item that let you talk to Elena, right? Was it a Paired Orb?"

"Ah, come to think of it, I haven't contacted it in a few days."

"Honestly, I envy you being able to talk to Rei even when you're apart."

Vihera murmured this, but Elena would probably say she envied Vihera for being able to act alongside Rei.

And in truth, Vihera understood that.

If someone offered her the Paired Orb, she'd gladly take it.

But if she were asked to give up her role of accompanying Rei in exchange for the Paired Orbs, she would refuse immediately.

(Well, I can do that because I left the royal family... In that sense, if Elena were to leave the duke's house, she could be in the same position as me. ...Though that's probably difficult now.)

Watching Rei retrieve the Paired Orb, Vihera let out a wry smile.

When she fled the Bestir Empire, she was reasonably well-known within the country, but she wasn't particularly famous abroad.

At the very least, the situation was vastly different from Elena's current status, where her nickname "Princess General" was known throughout the neighboring nations.

Had Vihera not fled the Bestir Empire, had she remained as a princess... and furthermore, had she distinguished herself on the battlefield to earn a nickname, it would likely have been difficult for her to leave.

That said, Vihera was aware that even if she had become a princess with a nickname, she would have fled anyway if she had met Rei.

For better or worse, unlike the highly responsible Elena, Vihera is free-spirited.

(I know my personality is troublesome... but thanks to that, I get to be with Rei like this, so I have no regrets.)

She was aware her personality was troublesome.

But she never belittled it. Rather, she believed that it was precisely because of this personality that she could be with Rei now, and she was glad she was this way.

As for Rei, the one making Vihera feel this way, he was channeling magic into the Paired Orb he'd taken from the Misty Ring, activating it.

"...Hm? Ah, Rei. I haven't heard from you in a few days. What's up?"

"Nah, just been busy. Remember I told you I was looking for Ambris?"

"Hmm. Even with Rei and Set looking, you still can't find him? That's troublesome."

"Well, I'm here too, you know."

With a flick, Vihera's face appeared before the Paired Orb.

Elena was startled for a moment by the sudden close-up of Vihera's face, but quickly let out a small sigh.

"It's nighttime, you know? Being in Rei's room at this hour is a bit problematic, isn't it?"

"Oh, really? We're traveling together, so it shouldn't matter, right? Oh, right. Since we're at it, why don't we form a party?"

Vihera said provocatively to Elena, but it was Rei who tilted his head at that.

"A party? Just the two of us? Well, I guess there are parties with just two people..."

When Rei thought of a two-person party, the first thing that came to mind was the Rank C party he'd once teamed up with for a harpy extermination request: the two members of the Shattered Warriors.

A party formed by a dwarf who loved his drink and a woman who truly lived up to the name "tough lady."

Being a Rank C party, they were skilled enough, and Rei had heard rumors that they'd energetically scattered a swarm of monsters during the Ambris incident.

They'd crossed paths several times and exchanged brief words, so they could be considered reasonably familiar with each other.

"Two of us is fine for now... but Byune will be back soon. Then we'll have Byune the thief, me who excels in close combat, and Rei who can handle long, mid, and short range equally well. See? The party balance isn't bad at all, right?"

Hearing Vihera's words, Elena on the other side of the Paired Orb wore an expression that clearly wasn't amused.

She understood perfectly well the difference between her own current position and Vihera's position, who could be by Rei's side.

But understanding it didn't mean she accepted it.

No, if there were someone present she wasn't particularly close to, it might be different. But currently, the only ones who could see the Paired Orbs were Rei, Vihera, and Elena.

"Kyuu?"

If you included Yellow, it would be more accurate to say three people and one creature.

"Kyuu! Kyuuuu?"

Where's Set? Not here? Watching Yellow peer into the Paired Orb like that, Rei and Vihera felt a sense of comfort.

"Heh heh, this is starting to feel rather silly. ...But, Elena. It's true that Rei and I are acting together, right? And the Guildmaster of the guild in Gilm where Rei and I live is Marina. ...You understand what I'm getting at without me finishing, don't you?"

'Reluctantly, yes.'

To Elena's slightly sullen reply, Vihera simply nodded, understanding there was nothing more to say.

Rei had been watching the two of them interact, but noticing Yellow staring intently at him, he shook his head and spoke up.

"Sorry, Yellow. This is an inn room, so Set's in the stables. Next time we use the Paired Orb outside, I'll make sure he shows his face properly. Just hang in there a little longer."

'Kyu...'

Yellow let out a disappointed cry at Rei's words and walked away.

...The fact that he was walking instead of flying clearly showed how disappointed Yellow was.

After finishing his exchange with Yellow, he noticed Elena and Vihera's conversation had also naturally concluded.

In fact, he tilted his head at the two of them, who were now looking at him with somewhat exasperated expressions.

"So, about Ambris..."

Sensing the odd looks directed at him, Rei brought up the topic of Ambris, trying to change the subject for the time being.

Though it wasn't really about dodging the topic; for Rei and Vihera, Ambris was a serious matter.

After all, he possessed a highly troublesome ability: forcing humanoid monsters to evolve into leader species.

To deal with it, they needed to defeat multiple Ambris.

But these Ambris were immune to physical attacks. To deal effective damage, one had to somehow break through their powerful magic defense and inflict damage through magic power.

(Magic-based attacks, huh? In that sense, Elena, who inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone at the Altar of Inheritance, does possess immense magical power. If she used Dragon Language Magic or something, she might be able to handle an Ambris...)

Daskar knows this, but even so, he couldn't possibly request that the Noble Faction send their symbolic princess general to Gilm just to defeat Anbrith.

Doing something like that would leave the Neutral Faction owing the Noble Faction a huge debt.

Relations between the Neutral Faction and the Noble Faction aren't bad at present. ...No, one could even say they're favorable.

Even so, creating debts between factions carries immense significance.

Things are fine now because relations are good, but these are the three major factions of the Mireana Kingdom. It's difficult for them to remain on good terms forever.

And when relations sour, the effort required to repay a large debt is obvious.

The Noble Faction would likely demand everything they could possibly extract... no, even more than that. And if that happened, the Neutral Faction wouldn't be able to avoid repaying the debt.

Considering all that, offering some form of compensation to bring Elena over would be the best option...

(As the lord of Gilm, it might be manageable, but as a central figure of the Neutral Faction, it would be difficult)

Rei wasn't particularly well-versed in such political matters.

Still, he could sense something was off.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

"Nothing. Just thinking about how to defeat Ambris."

He deflected with Vihera's words.

"Well, if it's just about defeating him, we've already done that, so I don't think you need to worry. Isn't the real problem how to find him?"

'An opponent whose scent can't be tracked and whose magic can't be sensed... Troublesome.'

On the other side of the Paired Orb, Elena murmured in agreement with Vihera's words.

In terms of defeating it, both Rei and Elena possessed powerful magical means of attack, so that part would be manageable. The real difficulty lay in finding it.

And even after finding and defeating it, there would still be more... There was no doubt it was an extremely troublesome foe.

Regardless, Rei, Vihera, and Elena enjoyed a leisurely conversation for the first time in several days.
