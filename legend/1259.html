When Rei was in Japan, he once saw a snow party on TV.

For Rei, who lived in a snowy region, it was puzzling why anyone would deliberately hold a barbecue party outside in the snow. Yet somehow... just somehow, he found himself wanting to try it.

The reason for this was probably the novel Rei had read at the library.

Takumu... or maybe someone else? I don't know if there were other Japanese people who came to this Elgin before Rei arrived, but it was definitely a novel written by someone with knowledge of Japan.

Reading it had made him vaguely homesick for Japan, and when asked when the party would be, he came up with the idea of a snow party.

Why his thoughts jumped to a snow party, even Rei himself couldn't say.

But even so, having thought of it and having a place where it could be done, there was no reason not to do it.

And so, a few days after Rei came up with the idea for the snow party... Snow had been falling until the day before, but today was what you'd call a clear winter day. Under a cloudless blue sky, preparations for the snow party steadily progressed.

"Hey! I brought some drinks. Is this okay?"

"You idiot. Didn't I tell you Rei doesn't drink alcohol? Get some tea or something ready!"

"Huh? Oh, I forgot. I'll go buy some right now!"

"Hey, about the veggies Rei-san gave us... is cutting them like this okay?"

"You idiot!? If you cut them that big, they'll be hard to eat!"

"Huh? Really? This size should be fine to eat normally, right?"

"You idiot! You're such an idiot! It might be fine for you, with your huge mouth, but Lady Vihera is coming to this party! And Guildmaster Marina too. People like them aren't going to eat like you do!"

"Ugh! W-well..."

While preparations proceeded like this, Rei laid out the ingredients he'd taken from the Misty Ring.

Snow had accumulated quite heavily, already piling up to a considerable height on the ground and becoming firmly packed.

The winter sun reflected off the snow, creating a dazzling glare.

(Honestly, if we're having a party here, wouldn't it be better to just do it inside the house... I mean, the mansion?)

Rei thought this as he roughly divided the ingredients taken from the Misty Ring.

Rei's gaze fell on the mansion where Johanna and the other former guerrilla fighters and their families lived.

It was a fairly large mansion even by Gilm standards, but Johanna and the others had purchased it and were now living together there.

Preparations for a snow barbecue party were currently underway in the mansion's courtyard.

"Rei-san, could I see what Silver Lion meat looks like?"

"Ah... sure. Here."

Lenora, who had been dividing the ingredients, asked Rei.

"Ah! Hey, Lenora! I said I'd handle Rei!"

Seeing her colleague like that, Kenny called out to Lenora enviously.

Kenny was dressed in layers so thick she looked like she was wearing a puffer jacket, just like when Rei had seen her before.

"Now, Kenny. Make sure you prep the ingredients properly. If you don't cut that part right, it'll get in the way when you eat, you know?"

"But Guildmaster!"

Marina scolded Kenny, but Kenny couldn't suppress his envy of Lenora.

Watching the commotion with amusement was Min.

While also prepping ingredients, he enjoyed watching this little love rivalry over Rei... though it lacked the intensity to truly be called a battle.

(Heh heh, things never turned out like this for Elk and me. Watching this play out is quite refreshing.)

Various others were also preparing for the barbecue party, and in the courtyard, Set could be seen playing in the snow with the children.

"Hey, Johanna! That's the spot I get to pet!"

"Oh dear, sorry about that. But since I petted him first, I claim ownership of this spot on little Set!"

"Hey, you two. Let me play with Set too!"

"Ah, me too! Me too! I wanna play with Set-chan!"

If you could even call Milein and Johanna's squabbling over Set "playing in the snow"...

"Honestly, those two... Now, now, stop it. It's not fair to the children if only you two get to play with Set."

Though displeased by Sulunin's words, Milein and Johanna couldn't bring themselves to take Set away from the children and reluctantly moved away.

As Set enthusiasts, they absolutely didn't want to leave Set here, but they also feared that if they acted meanly toward the children, Set might dislike them.

Besides, if they did anything else to anger Rei here, they might get kicked out and barred from joining the party.

"Milein and Johanna, you two, come help me over here."

The two turned to see Vihera standing before them.

Despite it being winter outside, she wore her usual thin clothing.

Her appearance naturally delighted the men and filled the women with envy and jealousy.

Milein and Johanna were likely no exception.

With slightly resentful expressions, they let themselves be led away by Vihera.

"Hmph!"

And what awaited them was vegetable peeling.

A knife lay on the surface where numerous vegetables were piled.

Use this to peel them! Byune indicated to Milein and Johanna with her attitude.

Rather than registering Byune's attitude, the two were more surprised to see Vihera peeling vegetables.

Though, while Mireine was surprised to see someone dressed like Vihera peeling vegetables, Johanna was surprised that Vihera... the former princess of the Bestir Empire... was peeling vegetables.

But even if someone pointed it out, Vihera probably wouldn't care much.

It had been quite some time since she fled the Bestir Empire.

Naturally, she'd cooked for herself during that time, so peeling vegetables wasn't anything unusual.

"...This turned into quite a large-scale preparation, didn't it?"

Rei muttered as she pulled out additional ingredients from the Misty Ring.

Rei had invited many of his acquaintances—or rather, people he knew—and almost all of them had wanted to participate.

The fact that it was winter and many people had free time probably played a part.

If it had been spring, summer, or fall, people would have had more obligations, and gathering this many might not have been possible.

"Well, yeah. Getting to eat Silver Lion meat was probably a big draw."

"Marina... Come to think of it, I know it's a bit late to ask, but is it okay for me to be here?"

"Oh? Didn't you invite me, Rei? Then I don't see anything strange about me being here, do you?"

The sun shone down on the earth, cloudless.

It was refreshing weather—one could certainly describe it that way—yet even under such skies, the smile Marina wore was exceptionally radiant.

Dressed in her usual party gown, she had an oddly mismatched air about her... yet it was utterly captivating.

Marina gave him a seductive smile and a sidelong glance. Rei smiled back and replied.

"Of course I welcome Marina joining us, but what's the status on the guildmaster succession? I heard the Walker's arrival is delayed."

The chaos caused by Rei and the others clearing the dungeon was, unsurprisingly, most severe in the immediate dungeon area.

Conquering a dungeon is its very purpose, but once conquered, those who previously profited from it inevitably lose their income.

For now, it wasn't a problem since monsters still spawned there, but in the not-too-distant future, the dungeon would undoubtedly become nothing more than a structure.

Once that happens, the entrance will likely be sealed to prevent surrounding monsters from entering, settling in, or breeding.

Knowing this, many people go to the guild's branch office to ask if there's anything that can be done.

The Walker treated such individuals with courtesy, and as a result, they hadn't yet come to Gilm.

"Well, yes. They're a bit late... but apparently they should be arriving soon?"

"...Is that so?"

Rei, who asked this, was surprised, especially since she'd been worried that her own dungeon conquest might be causing the delay.

"Yes. Since the Walker (Person) is the next Guild Master, they need to be able to handle things like that. ...I've already finished preparing everything on my end, so once the Walker (Person) arrives, we can start the handover process."

So there's time now, she added.

Nodding at Marina's explanation, Rei took out the meat.

It was not silver lion meat... but Gamelion meat.

The star of this barbecue party was, of course, silver lion meat. However, silver lion meat had one fatal flaw.

That flaw was that it was too delicious.

Once you tasted a single bite of silver lion meat, even ordinary dishes would seem unsatisfying for days afterward.

That's how astonishingly delicious this meat was.

That's precisely why, even though it was the main ingredient for this barbecue party, Rei had decided to save eating it for last.

Those tasting silver lion meat for the first time voiced their discontent, but anyone who had tasted it before, like Rei, understood their frustration all too well.

...Though, perhaps it was only natural that hearing it was such exceptional meat made everyone want to try it even more.

Still, since Rei, Vihera, Marina, and the others were saying it, he could only listen.

(Well, Gamelion meat is already delicious enough as it is.)

Even though many complain about Gamelion meat when they hear it's available because Silver Lion meat is scarce, in a normal year, Gamelion meat is undoubtedly a delicacy.

Especially since last year saw very little Gamelion meat hitting the market, this year many people thoroughly enjoyed their fill of it.

Perhaps due to last year's impact, or because a large amount of Gamelion meat flooded the market, the fact that people could eat their fill of it was likely one reason Gamelion meat wasn't as highly prized this year.

Time passed... and soon the barbecue party preparations were complete.

Participants gathered one after another, some already drinking ale.

In the center of the courtyard, several barbecue grills were set up, their coals already glowing.

Meat, vegetables... even seafood were being grilled, filling the air with an aroma that whetted the appetite.

"Hey, Rei. Looking forward to enjoying myself today."

Elk called out to Rei.

His beaming smile was likely due in large part to Rhodes regaining consciousness.

"Speaking of which, where's Rhodes?"

"Ah, I tried to bring him along. But he said he'd rather skip it today."

"...I see. I really wanted Rodos to come too."

Today's party wasn't just about showcasing the Silver Lion's meat; it was also to celebrate Vihera's awakening.

Though there was still about half a month left until year's end, it also served as a sort of year-end party for the year.

"Well, Rhodes probably has his own reasons. After explaining what those lost memories were like, he seemed pretty shaken up."

Elk's expression turned slightly sad for a moment, but he quickly changed it back to a smile.

"Leave him be for now. His body's still not fit for adventure yet."

He said this, then heard Min calling from a little ways off. He headed in that direction.

"Rei, here you go."

Marina handed a cup to Rei, who was seeing Elk off.

Inside was not alcohol... but herbal tea suitable for Rei to drink.

"Everyone should be ready by now. Would you mind declaring the party open?"

"Me?"

"Yes."

"I'm not really good at this sort of thing, though."

Even as he muttered this, being told he was the star who brought the silver lion meat meant Rei couldn't refuse.

Taking a cup in hand, he opened his mouth.

"Vihera's revival, dungeon conquest, Silver Lion meat... In every sense, cheers!"

"Cheers!"

With that cry, the snow barbecue party began.
