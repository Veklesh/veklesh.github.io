Rei and Ilse leave the tavern after an exchange with Melan that can only be described as unpleasant.

"I'm sorry, Rei. I dragged you into this unnecessary business."

Ilse bowed her head to Rei.

This whole incident wouldn't have happened if Ilse hadn't gotten involved; Melan wouldn't have interfered otherwise.

Understanding this, Ilse bowed her head.

"Don't worry about it. Given how things were going, we would have clashed with Melan sooner or later anyway."

There was no doubt Melan disliked revenge. But hadn't it been because Ilse was acting alongside Rei that Melan had become so adamant about not allowing revenge against her?

That was Rei's honest impression after leaving the tavern... after speaking with Melan.

Ilse didn't know if Melan himself realized it, but the gaze he directed at her was harsh.

Of course, part of the reason for that harsh gaze was undoubtedly Ray's involvement in helping Ilse seek revenge.

But at the same time... it was undoubtedly largely because Rei was close to Vihera – the very Vihera Melan had fallen for at first sight.

"Is that so? Hearing you say that makes me feel a little better."

At Rei's words, Ilse flashed a faint smile... before her expression hardened.

"Rei, we've wasted time here. It's about time we went."

"Yeah, you're right. Set must be waiting. ...If they'd just show their hand, it'd make things easier for us."

They exchanged words as they made their way through Gilm.

Set, positioned at a height of 100 meters, would normally be difficult to spot.

However, for Rei, whose senses were sharper than those of ordinary humans, even if inferior to Set's, spotting Set's form was not difficult.

This also meant that Set, whose senses were sharper than Rei's, could spot Rei even more easily.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

With that growl, Set flapped his wings and descended toward the ground.

This was something the usual Gilm would absolutely be incapable of; it was only possible because Gilm was under construction, allowing free access from the sky.

Those around Rei and Ilse who heard Set's cry were startled for a moment, wondering what had happened.

But upon recognizing the familiar sound, they quickly returned to their daily routines.

Those who had come to Gilm seeking work on the expansion project saw the reactions around them and concluded there was probably no problem... or perhaps forced themselves to believe that, and they too returned to their tasks.

Unconcerned with the reactions around him, Rei stroked Set, who had descended to the ground.

"Gururururu, guruguru"

Set purred happily as Rei stroked him.

Ilse watched the pair from a step back.

Several minutes passed, and when Rei finished petting him, she spoke to Set.

"So, Set. Do you know where Ajax ran off to?"

"Guru!"

"Leave it to me," Set growled, then led the way, guiding Rei and Ilse along the path.

A sudden thought crossed Rei's mind: What if, during this interaction with him, Ajax had slipped away from wherever he'd hidden...? But Rei was certain that even if Ajax did escape now, he'd be sure to be seen, and that Set would have no trouble tracking him down.

Guided by Set, they arrived at an inn...

"Here..."

Rei's muttered voice carried a heavy note of disappointment.

It was only natural. This inn was the location mentioned in the information about Ajax that Marina had given him—or more accurately, that Daskar had relayed from Egg.

Ideally, Rei had hoped they'd be heading toward the hideout Ajax had likely prepared. That would have been best.

(There's no way there's no hideout, right? If he'd actually reformed, that might have been a possibility... but judging by his attitude, that's unlikely.)

Outwardly, Ajax appeared to be a competent and personable adventurer. But judging by his demeanor when listening to Ilse's story, he absolutely did not seem reformed.

If that were the case, he wouldn't have abandoned the job midway and vanished in the first place.

(Could it be he couldn't bear the guilt? ...Nah.)

Seeing Ajax's demeanor while talking to Ilse, it was obvious he felt no guilt whatsoever.

Given that, there had to be a reason he was putting on that public persona... It was impossible he wasn't plotting something, Rei surmised, even bordering on certainty.

In other words, he absolutely needed a base of operations to carry out whatever he was planning.

(Surely he's not actually without such a place, making plans or carrying them out at inns or something like that? ...Nah.)

He was hiding his true nature, deceiving those around him. Naturally, he wouldn't let his guard down, no matter what he was doing.

Though, for Rei, it would be easier if they did it at the inn—he could gather evidence immediately.

"Well, we've made it to the inn... but if they haven't shown any movement yet, this is going to be a bit tricky."

"Yeah. ...Personally, I'd prefer to just storm the inn right now. But that's not really an option."

"Calm down. I know the Melan incident might be affecting you, but you're getting a bit too aggressive."

Rei couldn't help but think it was strange to hear himself say such a line.

After all, he... Rei was known for having a very combative personality.

To be precise, it's not that he's aggressive towards everyone, but rather that he's aggressive towards those who oppose him.

Once someone became an enemy, whether they were nobility or not, or held any other social status, he wouldn't hesitate to attack.

And the fact that he actually possessed the strength to carry it out was probably a big factor too.

So, being told to watch out because Rei is being aggressive... if anyone was listening, they'd definitely be surprised.

"Anyway, they definitely have other bases besides this one. Ideally, I'd like to destroy them. ...Though Ilse, who craves revenge, might find that too tedious."

"No."

Ilse immediately shook her head and denied Rei's words.

Being scolded by Rei for being aggressive had probably cooled her down a bit.

Even so, her composure didn't mean her desire for revenge against Ajax had vanished.

"I don't know what Ajax is planning, but crushing it right before their eyes would be more satisfying."

Ilse said this with a faint smile, her demeanor clearly different from when they first met.

(Could this also be a case of falling into darkness? Well, it was undoubtedly triggered by the incident with Melan. ...She sure knows how to make things complicated.)

If, by some mistake, Ajax had actually reformed... or if it was a case of mistaken identity... who knows what would happen.

Even though he understood it was highly unlikely, Rei couldn't help but think about it.

"Right. Whatever they're planning, they're over there... Huh? Who's that?"

Spotting someone walking toward them, Rei asked.

They were currently in the shadow of a building.

Admittedly, it wasn't a place where they were absolutely hidden, since there was enough space for Set to hide too.

"Rei, it's been a while,"

The man bowed his head as he spoke.

But Rei didn't recognize him and merely tilted her head.

(Who is he? I feel like I've seen his face somewhere before, maybe...)

Still, sensing no hostility from the man, she likely concluded he wasn't an enemy for the time being.

Rei opened his mouth to the man before him.

"Who are you?"

"Oh dear. I guess you really did forget me. I figured as much from your reaction just now. I'm Dahl, formerly of the Prairie Wolves."

"...Ah, right. You were there."

He recalled that when he first made contact with the Prairie Wolves, he thought he'd seen the man before him among their members.

"Glad you remembered. So, what brings you here, Rei? Well, I suppose there's only one reason you'd be here."

The man's gaze shifted from Rei and the others to the inn... the inn where Ajax was likely staying.

Seeing that, Rei understood what the man was implying.

Well, given he was Egg's subordinate, it was something he could have anticipated.

Originally, Rei had asked Daskar to look into Ajax through Marina.

Naturally, Daskar, having been asked, would bring it up with Egg, his professional contact. Considering that, the current situation wasn't hard to figure out.

"I figured as much. ...So, what brings you here? You didn't just come to say hello, did you?"

Rei wasn't optimistic enough to believe that he'd just happened to arrive at a spot overlooking Ajax's lodgings when Egg's subordinate showed up.

Seeing Rei's expression, Dahl nodded in return.

"Yes. Actually, I have something to discuss with you, Rei. ...And with you too, Ilse, if that's who you are. It concerns you as well."

At Dahl's words, Ilse's face showed surprise. Me too?

She had assumed any business would be with Rei.

But Dahl nodded in response to Ilse's words.

"Yes. I believe it will be of interest to both of you."

Hearing Darl say that, it was clear what the matter was about.

Still, Rei couldn't immediately nod in agreement to Dahl's words.

"I'm sure you understand, but I can't leave here right now. You know the reason why, don't you?"

"Yes, I understand. So, I'll leave a few people here in Rei's place. How does that sound? Of course, I'll let you know immediately if anything happens."

"...I see."

If someone would watch the inn for them, that was acceptable.

Having reached that decision, Rei turned his gaze to Ilse beside him.

Ilse hesitated under Rei's gaze.

The enemy she had spent years searching for was right there.

Therefore, she wanted to stay here and observe the inn herself, ready to act immediately if anything happened.

It was only natural for Ilse to think that.

But as if seeing right through Ilse's inner thoughts... no, actually seeing right through them, Dahl spoke up.

"Ilse, I think you should hear this story if possible. It will surely make your revenge easier."

"...Is that true?"

Ilse murmured, a hint of doubt in her voice.

It wasn't particularly surprising that the person before her knew her goal was revenge.

After all, she had repeatedly discussed revenge in public places before, so it was only natural.

Besides, since Rei trusted her, Ilse felt he wouldn't interfere unnecessarily even if he knew her purpose was revenge. ...At the very least, she doubted he'd do something like just tell her revenge was wrong, like Melan had done.

"Yes. Definitely. ...Or rather, we can't thank Rei-san and Ilse-san enough, can we?"

"Huh? Gratitude?"

For a moment, Rei wondered why those words came out... but then he understood. There was no doubt Ajax was up to something.

"Yes. Actually, he... well, he and his two companions did something rather troublesome for us. The trigger that uncovered it was investigating the person known as Ajax."

"Ah... I see."

Rei recalled how Marina had gathered information from Daskar, Egg, and others.

"It seems you understand. ...Originally, we planned to handle this matter ourselves..."

Dahl paused there, letting a smile—no, a wry smile—flicker across his face.

It was a smile that could only be called a bitter one.

Originally, they had planned to handle this matter solely with Egg and the other members of the intelligence unit.

But with Rei now involved, attempting to handle it alone could lead to a confrontation with her.

It was merely a possibility, not a certainty. Not a certainty... but if there was even the slightest chance of clashing with Rei, they couldn't afford to overlook that risk.

That was the reason Dahl had appeared here.
