"Huh... So this is Rossi. It's more prosperous than I expected."

Upon entering Rossi, the capital of the Republic of Reburina, Rei murmured in admiration as he surveyed his surroundings.

Indeed, Rossi was more prosperous than Rei had imagined.

Of course, compared to present-day Gilm, let alone Gilm before its expansion, the number of people was smaller.

Still, it wasn't exactly deserted, possessing enough vitality to be called a capital city.

"Rei, what should we do first? Find lodging? Or head to the guild?"

The one asking was Marina.

Naturally, Rei and his group stood out considerably, drawing various glances from those around them.

Starting with Marina, who just spoke to Rei, there were three beautiful women... and not just any beauties, but the kind you might see once in a lifetime.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be surprising if someone tried to hassle Rei for having such beauties in tow.

...Byune also gave off a somewhat doll-like impression, her expressionless face making it hard to read her emotions, but there was no doubt her features were so perfectly formed that one could only look forward to her future.

But the reason no one approached this group was undoubtedly thanks to Set standing beside Rei.

Set, with Yellow perched on his back, scanned the surroundings with his round eyes.

Anyone who had become somewhat familiar with Set could probably guess he was thinking about whether there was any tasty food around. Naturally, though, no one in this Rossy was familiar with Set.

In fact, when going through the entry procedures for Rossi, Rei and his group had to handle it from a distance because the others would have been scared otherwise.

Even regarding the familiar necklace, the guards were quite afraid of Set when handing it over, even though they weren't the ones putting it on him.

Seeing that guard, Set looked a little disappointed... but after being petted by Byune, his mood quickly improved.

"Hmm, I wonder. The guards did tell me about an inn with a large stable. Should we head there first and get a room? I know it's different from the current Gilm, where inns don't fill up immediately..."

In present-day Gilm, where many came seeking work, the existing inns weren't enough. Consequently, near the construction sites, several makeshift structures—barely worthy of being called lodgings—had been hastily erected.

Coming from a place like Gilm, it was only natural to feel compelled to secure lodging first.

"Hmm, what was the name of that inn the guard mentioned?"

"The Silver Fruit Inn. ...I hear it's where the big merchant caravans coming to Rossi stay."

Large caravans naturally often travel with multiple wagons.

That means they use many horses, requiring large stables to rest them.

Naturally, an inn that accommodates large caravans charges higher lodging fees than ordinary inns. But for Rei, a slightly higher rate was no problem at all.

Daskar had provided a decent sum as expenses, and there were also treasures obtained from bandit groups encountered along the way.

Even without counting Rei's original assets, they had more than enough money to spare.

"They said it's easy to find if we just head down the main street. Shall we go? Staying here any longer feels uncomfortable."

Marina nodded at her words, and Rei and the others began walking down the main street.

Naturally, the crowd that had gathered in front of them had no choice but to part.

For a moment, Rei recalled the phrase "Moses parting the Red Sea," but quickly dismissed it as an exaggeration and forgot it.

Though Rei walked on, watched by those around her, she still didn't know exactly where the Silver Fruit Inn was located.

The guards had only told them to follow the main street, so that's what they were doing...

"Grr?"

"Ah, wait, wait!"

Set looked at Rei with his round eyes. Should they head towards the smell wafting from that food stall? The stall was cooking meat and vegetables boldly on a griddle, then selling them sandwiched between bread.

The scent of the sauce added to season the stir-fried meat and vegetables... The aroma that instantly filled the air was almost violent in its intensity.

Rei agreed with Set's idea to stop at the stall, but since the residents of Rossi weren't used to Set, he couldn't just let him go.

"Alright, I'll go buy some. What about you guys?"

The first to nod in response to Rei's question was Byune.

When it came to eating, he possessed an appetite equal to—or perhaps even stronger than—Rei's, so it was only natural.

Elena and the others seemed interested in foreign cuisine too, and all three nodded.

Though not quite to the same extent as Rei or Byune, Elena and the others also tended to eat quite a lot.

The fact they don't gain weight is probably because they exercise to compensate. ...That exercise being something closer to duels than mock battles, but that's beside the point.

Anyway, since everyone was eating, Rei approached the stall.

Many people around them were watching Rei's group, but the stall owner Rei was heading towards seemed completely preoccupied with cooking and paid them no mind.

From Rei's perspective, the stall owner's attitude—focused solely on his work—seemed rather admirable.

"Excuse me?"

"Sure thing! How many servings?"

When Rei called out, the young vendor, who looked to be in his twenties, responded cheerfully.

"For now... ten servings, please."

"Huh? Ten servings? ...Oh, for the others too. But can you even carry ten servings?"

Perhaps because he was only looking at Rei, he didn't seem to notice the others, including Set, standing a little further away.

The shopkeeper, still puzzled by Rei's request, decided he'd call for help if needed and began taking out the bread.

...Only, the loaf was quite large—at least fifteen centimeters wide.

Rei's first thought upon seeing it was, French bread?

Of course, it was probably different in many ways, but seeing how the crust was baked so firmly, it definitely had an appearance that could easily give that impression.

However, the inside of the bread was almost entirely hollowed out, leaving mostly just the outer crust.

The shop owner added something white and square to the meat and vegetables he was stir-frying on the griddle, then continued cooking.

For a moment, Rei couldn't tell what the white thing was, but she quickly realized it was bread... the hollowed-out interior of the loaf, cut into dice-shaped pieces.

The white bread absorbed the sauce, meat juices, and moisture from the vegetables while being stir-fried on the griddle, ensuring it didn't become soggy.

The stir-fried ingredients were then placed into the hollowed-out sections of the bread.

"There, it's done. So, that was for ten people. I can carry it... Whoa!"

The stall owner couldn't finish his sentence because the moment he looked up, there stood Set... in his griffin form.

Rei had told Set to wait, but it seemed he couldn't resist the aroma wafting from the stall.

(Well, I can't say I don't understand that feeling.)

Okonomiyaki, yakisoba, grilled squid—all sold at summer festival stalls.

The smell of burnt sauce and soy sauce drifted through the air, creating a destructive force bordering on violent.

...Though, to be fair, most okonomiyaki are mostly cabbage and aren't actually that tasty.

Still, there's no denying the irresistible allure of that burnt sauce and soy aroma.

That's probably why Set... and Elena and the others came over to the food stalls like this.

"Oh, I seem to have startled you. My apologies."

Marina smiled and said this to the stall owner. The moment he saw her, his face flushed an astonishing shade of red.

It was clear to anyone watching that this wasn't because he was frying meat and vegetables on a hot plate in the middle of summer...

"Ah, ahaha. Um, well... my cooking is delicious, so please give it a try. It's Panyan, a specialty of the country of Reburina."

Hearing this, Rei looked at the dish handed to her.

Apparently, this dish was called Panyan.

He handed portions to the others as well, stowing the remaining items in his Misty Ring.

The stall owner looked surprised, but Rei paid no mind, settling the bill as he spoke up.

"So, I have a question. Could you tell me where the Silver Fruit Inn is?"

"Huh? Oh, the Silver Fruit Inn? Well, if you go straight down this street, there's a big inn on the right side. That's the one. It has a sign with a silver fruit painted on it, just like the name says, so you shouldn't have any trouble finding it."

"Got it. Thanks a lot."

"No... come back again sometime."

Was he saying that because Rei had bought so much, or because he wanted to see a beauty like Marina up close again?

Whatever the reason, Rei nodded and bit into the Panyan.

The first flavors to spread in his mouth were the savory richness of meat and the satisfying crunch of vegetables.

Overcooking vegetables ruins their texture.

But if they're stir-fried too briefly, they remain undercooked.

His precise understanding of that delicate balance in stir-frying showed he was also a skilled cook.

Beyond the meat and vegetables, the bread cut into bite-sized pieces absorbed the meat juices, sauce, and vegetable moisture, creating a harmonious blend in the mouth.

Moreover, the bread used as the dish itself was baked to a satisfyingly crispy texture on the outside. At the very least, Rei found it thoroughly delicious.

"Yeah, this is delicious. If the food's this good, I'm more than happy to come back. I'll definitely stop by again."

"Ah, yeah! I'll be waiting!"

Leaving the stall owner saying that behind them, Rei and the others walked down the main street, searching for a sign with a silver fruit painted on it.

After walking for about ten minutes...

"Hm? Isn't that it?"

Elena murmured, glancing ahead.

Rei and the others turned their gaze in the direction of her voice. There, just as Elena had said, was a sign. On it were the characters for "inn" and a silver fruit... an illustration shaped like an apple.

"No doubt about it. ...Well then, let's go."

"Guruu!"

"Kyuu?"

Set agreed with Rei's words, and Yellow, who had been sleeping on Set's back, lifted his head, wondering what was happening.

Seeing Yellow's reaction, several passersby who had been watching Rei and the others nearby couldn't help but relax their expressions.

(Well, Yellow is still a child, but he's definitely a dragon. ...Does being cute make it okay?)

Rei pondered this as he observed the surroundings, but seeing the atmosphere around them—as if "Cute is justice!"—he resigned himself to the fact that saying anything more would be pointless.

"Alright then, for now, Marina... will you come with me?"

"Oh, you requested me?"

Marina said with a smile, and Elena and Vihera flashed slightly envious looks.

But for Rei too, the people he could bring along in this situation were limited.

Elena was even more notorious than Rei, and Vihera's appearance made her likely to attract unwanted attention from men. As for Byune, bringing her along would serve little purpose.

For those reasons, Rei chose Marina.

That said, Marina was also a Dark Elf, a fairly rare race, so the chances of her being harassed were still quite high.

Especially since Ajax had possessed a slave collar, it was certain that the organization he belonged to, likely based in this country of Reburina, possessed similar magical items.

"Ah, well then, let's go."

Rei gave a short reply to Marina's words and walked straight into the Silver Fruit Inn.

The inn was quite spacious, likely due to catering to large caravans and other groups.

Amidst this, a slender woman in her thirties, busy with some task at the counter, spotted Rei—or more accurately, Marina—and gasped in surprise.

"W-welcome. ...Um, are you staying the night?"

If Marina were a guest staying at the Silver Fruit Inn, she wouldn't have asked such a question again.

But naturally, if someone as distinctive as Marina were staying, the woman should have remembered her.

Forgetting someone like that would make her completely unfit for a job like innkeeper.

"Ah. For now... well, about ten days, I suppose. Rooms for me, Elena, Marina... Vihera and Byune can share a room, so four rooms total. Also, I have familiars with me, so please take care of them too."

Rei said this instead of Marina.
