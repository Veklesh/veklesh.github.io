"This is..."

The sight before him left Rei speechless.

Seeing Rei's reaction, Relduct thought he must be speechless at the magnificence of the magic items he himself... and his ancestors had gathered. Biting back the pain still throbbing in his arms, he spoke.

"Well, what do you think of the many magic items passed down in my family?"

Until entering this room—the room where the magic items were stored—Lerdact had maintained a deferential attitude toward Rei. But seeing Rei's current state, he seemed to have regained some confidence.

Not quite like when they first met... but that confidence was evident in his demeanor.

His change in tone toward Rei likely stemmed from that newfound confidence.

But... that confidence shattered instantly at the next words to come from Rei's mouth.

"Useless."

"...What!?"

Though he'd been praised before, Lerdact never expected to be met with disappointment. He froze for a moment at Rei's reaction.

Still, he didn't snap back at Rei. The pain in his shoulders reminded him of the absolute power gap between them.

"Well, I was fairly confident... What exactly didn't meet your standards?"

His demeanor toward Rei reverted to its usual submissive tone.

Watching Lerdact's sudden change in demeanor with an exasperated look, Rei spoke up.

"First off. The magic items here are basically art pieces and that sort of thing, right?"

"Well, yes..."

Without hesitation, Lerdact nodded at Rei's words.

Seeing this, Rei finally understood that while Lerdact was gathering information about him and collecting magic items as a hobby, he didn't actually know what kind of items he was collecting.

The magic items Rei collected were, fundamentally, things that could actually be used in battle.

Things like the Death Scythe, the Twilight Spear, or the Thorn Spear—items usable in battle.

Magic tents, the Short Sword of Flowing Water, the Stone Oven—things that could be used normally in daily life.

He did possess a few other magic items, but all of them were frequently put to practical use.

Of course, the art-like items right in front of him—jewelry boxes made of gems, paintings, bronze statues, sculptures—those things that are both art and magic items are probably precious to those who understand their value.

But Rei has no particular interest in such artistic objects.

He might glance at the paintings decorating a Daskar mansion or a guildmaster's office, but he couldn't comprehend being captivated by such art or spending vast sums to collect it.

...Then again, perhaps others find it incomprehensible that Rei collects numerous magic items for actual use.

(In the end, values differ from person to person. ...Besides, it's not like these art pieces have no value.)

Rei doesn't know what specific effects the magic item before him possesses.

Still, he understood it must be valuable since Lerdact had kept it hidden away like this.

(Well, it's not impossible he's been duped into buying a fake.)

Even back in Japan, he'd seen TV reports about people getting scammed by fake brand goods.

If he wasn't being deceived like that, then the magic item Rei was staring at was probably extremely valuable.

"What effect does this jewel box have?"

"Well, it's a magic item that plays music when you open it."

"Ah... I see."

For a moment, Rei wondered if this had been made by someone from his hometown. But then he realized that if it was something like a music box, it wouldn't be that surprising for it to have been conceived here in Elgin... and he stored it away in Misty Ring.

"Ah!"

"Is something wrong?"

"...Nothing."

Normally, magic items like this weren't Rei's cup of tea. But he understood why Lerdact treasured this particular one, and he judged that taking it would be a powerful act of retaliation against Lerdact.

"Is this painting a magic item too?"

"Huh? Oh, yes. By channeling magic power through it, the background of the painting changes."

Slightly intrigued by Lerdact's explanation, Rei channeled magic into the displayed painting.

The painting showed a woman smiling in a spring meadow, but the next moment, it changed to a cloudless blue sky typical of summer, and the grass in the meadow was clearly longer than just seconds before.

Continuing to channel magic, the painting's content shifted through autumn, winter... or nighttime versions of spring, summer, autumn, and winter.

"...I see. That's kind of interesting."

Even for Rei, who had no interest in art, the fact that the painting's content could change so dramatically through the flow of magic was incredibly fascinating.

He naturally stored that painting inside the Misty Ring as well.

"Ah..."

Watching the magic items he and his clan had gathered being taken away by Rei one after another must have been a huge psychological blow to Lerdact.

A small sigh of lament escaped Relduct's lips.

As Rei continued storing the magic items disguised as artworks one after another, he suddenly noticed a sound like a cry.

(That's... Oh, so it's come to this already. Then I suppose there's no need for me to stay here any longer.)

Knowing what the sound signified, Rei addressed Lerdact, who likely hadn't heard it yet.

"I'm leaving soon, but the rebels should be arriving here shortly."

"Huh!? The rebels!? Why!? How!?"

"Why...? You really don't understand? It's because you're unfit to rule this domain."

Reldaxt reflexively gasped at Rei's words, likely because they struck too close to home. He tried to say something, but the words dissolved into silence.

And Lerdact's face was stained with despair.

It was only natural. He understood that if the rebel army attacked now, with his defensive forces nearly annihilated by Rei—or more accurately, most having fled—his own life would undoubtedly be forfeit.

"Rei! You said you'd spare my life just now! Yet you're handing me over to the rebels!"

It was likely out of fear for his life. Even his usual way of addressing Rei without honorifics had shifted to adding "Lord" to his name.

"You just shouldn't rule in a way that sparks rebellion. ...Well, never mind. Relax. I've made arrangements with the rebels. They shouldn't take your life."

"Huh!? From the start, you were in cahoots with the rebels...?"

...were you in cahoots with them from the start?

Lerdact started to say, but before he could finish, the tip of the Twilight Spear was thrust at him, and he fell silent.

"Correct... I'd like to say, but strictly speaking, that's not quite right. When I arrived in the Lerdact County, the rebels were already preparing to act."

Technically, they had gathered their weapons, but they hadn't yet reached the point of making such a large-scale move. Still, Rei stated it dramatically.

"And then, by chance, we made contact. I told them I'd be taking action like this today. It was mutually beneficial for both sides."

For Rei, it meant leaving the aftermath of her revenge against Lerdact to the rebels. For the rebels, it meant having Rei eliminate the military force Lerdact possessed.

It was a mutually beneficial arrangement.

"That is..."

Cutting off Lerdact before he could speak, Rei continued.

"For now, I guarantee you'll stay alive, so relax. ...Though I can't say what'll happen if you treat them arrogantly."

While Rei had made arrangements with the rebels to spare Lerdact's life, that didn't mean harm couldn't come to him depending on his attitude.

Then again, whether the rebels would actually harm the current Lerdact—his shoulders severed, his face smeared with every bodily fluid imaginable—was a bit questionable.

"...Understood."

Lerdact seemed to understand his current predicament. Instead of arguing with Rei, he simply nodded quietly.

"Just so you know, if you try to impose the same kind of oppressive rule again after we fix this situation, I might come back."

Rei issued this warning, though whether he would actually return was uncertain.

If he had business here, that would be another matter, but if asked whether he'd deliberately come back to check on things, the answer would be no.

Still, hearing Rei say he might return to check on things meant they had to consider that possibility, however infinitesimally low it was.

Of course, there was also the question of whether Lerdact, now missing both arms, could remain in his position as lord. Even considering that, it would be difficult for the Lerdact County to impose oppressive rule like the high taxes they had before.

Moreover, the recent rebel incident has brought significant shame upon Lerdact even among the Noble Faction, further diminishing his already low influence.

It was undeniable that from now on, he would no longer be able to act as he pleased.

"What you choose to do from here on out... I intend to observe that at my leisure."

With that declaration, Rei left Lerdact behind and exited the room where the magic items had been stored.

From behind came sounds of sobbing, wailing, lamentation... a cacophony of emotions like a roar. To Rei, however, it was nothing but a case of reaping what one sowed. He paid it no mind as he walked down the corridor.

Just hours ago, the lord's mansion had housed dozens, perhaps even hundreds of people. Now, that many souls were nowhere to be seen.

Many had fled at the sight of Rei, and seeing others flee, the rest followed suit, dwindling their numbers.

If there were those loyal to Lerdact, some might have stayed behind... but Rei couldn't see any such figures.

(Well, they brought it upon themselves. ...Still, I doubt those who fled like this will be hired by Lerdact again. If Lerdact survived... well, I suppose we can leave that to the rebel army's pride. My business here is done, so I'll be on my way...)

He should just return to Gilm.

"Ah."

The moment that thought crossed his mind, he suddenly remembered.

Come to think of it, ever since meeting the rebels, he'd completely forgotten—wasn't there a letter Jazz had entrusted him with?

Near where I encountered the rebel members was Hazlue. A letter for Dostrite, Jazz's brother who lives there.

Originally, my purpose was to obtain information necessary for retaliation against Lerdact, but that matter had already been resolved by encountering the rebels just before arriving at Hazlue.

That's precisely why I'd forgotten about the letter.

In any case, it was easy to imagine the letter contained a request for Rei's cooperation, but it undoubtedly also included updates on his current situation.

After all, Jazz had fled the Lerdact County.

Given that, it was unlikely he could casually exchange letters with his family.

"Damn it. ...Well, whatever. It's a bit late, but if I ask Set, it shouldn't take long."

Considering Set's speed, delivering the letter tonight shouldn't be too difficult.

...Though, it was an hour when most people would normally be asleep already.

Naturally, he couldn't possibly visit at such an hour, so the actual visit would have to wait until tomorrow.

"So, looks like I'll be camping out tonight."

Rei muttered this, but there was no hint of disappointment in his voice.

After all, when Rei said "sleeping rough," he meant using a magic tent.

Its comfort far surpassed that of any ordinary inn.

Besides, the Lerdact County wasn't exactly the frontier.

It was safe to assume vicious monsters wouldn't attack in the middle of the night.

Instead of monsters, bandits were more common, but with Set around, that worry was unnecessary.

"Gurrrrrrr!"

As I walked along thinking these thoughts, Set came running over, letting out a happy growl.

Stroking Set's face as he approached, Rei spoke.

"I'm thinking of heading to Hazrui. Is that okay?"

"Guruu!"

Set let out a happy cry at Rei's words... and within minutes, he took flight toward the night sky.

Some time passed... and eventually, the rebel army stormed the lord's mansion.
