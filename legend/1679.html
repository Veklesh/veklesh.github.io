“...It’s cheap, isn’t it?”

As he ate the fish—skewered, sprinkled with salt, and grilled—Bistol muttered.

His slight dissatisfaction was likely because the price Marina had told him they’d sold the pirates as slaves for was lower than he’d expected.

But Marina didn’t seem particularly bothered by Bistol’s reaction; she simply tilted her head slightly and asked, “Is that so?”

Illuminated by the campfire’s light, Marina possessed a seductive allure that would undoubtedly captivate any man… but for Bistol—a man in body but a woman… no, a maiden at heart—it had no effect.

“Of course. Since this is the countryside, I understand it’d be cheaper than in Gilm. But… this is way too cheap, no matter how you look at it!”

“But the slave trader knows that his client is Rei (a man)—a high-ranking adventurer with a reputation, right? Do you really think he’d try to take advantage of him like that?”

“Well…”

“Besides, there were guards present when I negotiated! Do you really think they’d get away with any shady dealings in a place like that?”

Faced with those words, Bistol had no rebuttal.

Deep down, he wished he’d gone with them, but since he’d tagged along with Rei and the others with the sole purpose of catching as many fish as possible, he couldn’t very well abandon that now.

“Galanka seems to be pretty far out in the countryside, so I guess that’s probably factored into the price. Besides, Rei doesn’t seem to mind, does he?”

Rei, who had been looked at, nodded as if to say, “Of course.”

“Marina negotiated that price. I don’t plan on complaining about it.”

“Are you really okay with that?”

From the merchant Bistol’s perspective, based on what he’d heard, he felt he’d been haggled down to a ridiculously low price… that was his impression.

But the fact that Rei—who under normal circumstances would never have allowed such a thing—accepted it so readily left Bistol feeling as if his momentum had been cut short.

“Yeah, that’s fine. Besides, even setting aside the fact that we sold the pirates as slaves, this whole pirate business has been nothing but a huge profit for us.”

At Rei’s words, Bisturu shot him a look that was slightly… no, quite full of disbelief.

From Bistol’s perspective, it was unthinkable not to make a profit where there was an opportunity to do so.

However, from Rei’s perspective, he had managed to seize all the treasure the pirates had been hiding—albeit only what they had confessed to—and had also acquired a large quantity of the disposable magic items the pirate captain possessed, which created smoke screens.

(And… getting our hands on a ship is a big deal.)

It was a rather old ship, showing signs of having been repaired countless times.

But for the purpose of studying ships in a place far from the sea—like Gilm—this old ship was more than sufficient, precisely because he felt it wouldn’t be a problem if it got damaged.

If the pirates had been using a new ship, it would have been too new to touch without caution.

In that sense, there was no doubt that being able to acquire an old ship was a tremendous blessing.

“Sigh… You really are too trusting.”

It was probably because she didn’t know exactly how much profit Rei had made. Bistol shook his head as if to say that anything else he said would be pointless, then went back to eating his salt-grilled fish.

“So, Rei. What are you going to do tomorrow?”

Perhaps deciding that her conversation with Bistol was over, Elena asked Rei that question.

But Rei, taken aback by the question, asked back in puzzlement.

“What do you mean, ‘what are we doing’? I plan to catch some fish—is there something else?”

“I see. I thought for sure you were going back to that village.”

“I’m planning to pop over there around evening. I intend to buy some fish and such.”

He had bought fish from the villagers who had returned from fishing today, but the amount wasn’t all that large.

From the village’s perspective, this was only natural, given that they had been worried about how to deal with the pirates.

Then Rei showed up and defeated the pirates that very day—not just that day, but within a few hours—and after seeing that, they figured it was safe and went out to fish.

In reality, they’d only been fishing for about two hours, if that, so the amount of fish they caught wasn’t all that much.

But tomorrow, they’ll fish all day long starting first thing in the morning, and Rei will buy the fish they catch.

...This was a beneficial arrangement for both the villagers of Galanka and for Rei, who wanted as much fish as possible while he could get it.

Of course, without the Misty Ring—where time doesn’t pass—no matter how large a catch they bought, it would have just gone bad.

“Hmm, I see. So, does that mean we don’t have to go over there tomorrow?”

“As long as you don’t have anything else to do, I think that’s fine. I plan to come right back after I buy the fish, too.”

They finished their meal while chatting… and once the meal was over, they had free time until bedtime.

Rei did some light combat training with Vihera, Bistol began preparing the fish they caught today to make dried fish, Byune went into the forest to gather fruit for dessert, Marina used spirit magic to keep watch on the surroundings, and Set and Yero played together.

Amidst all this, Elena, who had been talking to Ara using the Paired Orb, suddenly called out to Rei.

“Rei, could you come here for a moment!?”

At those words, Rei leaped backward to dodge a punch from Vihera, lowering the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear he had been holding in a fighting stance.

Seeing Rei like that, Vihera made no move to pursue him, and the mock battle—which, objectively speaking, looked like nothing but a real fight—came to an end.

“Hey, Elena. What’s with the sudden interruption? We haven’t even been fighting that long, have we?”

Vihera complained, but in reality, nearly ten minutes had already passed since the mock battle began.

Under normal circumstances, if you kept up such all-out physical exertion—even if it was called a mock battle—for nearly ten minutes, it wouldn’t be strange to be completely out of breath.

Especially if the opponents in that mock battle were people like Rei and Vihera, who possessed extraordinary strength, the fatigue should have been even greater.

But… Rei and Vihera showed absolutely no signs of being out of breath.

In fact, they still had so much physical energy left that it wouldn’t have been strange if someone had told them the sparring match had only started a few seconds ago.

Of course, that was precisely why Vihera, who had been forced to stop the sparring match midway, was openly expressing her dissatisfaction.

Appetite, the desire for sleep, sexual desire… For Vihera, who possesses something beyond these that could be called a “desire to fight,” sparring with Rei is an act that could be called the ultimate pleasure.

Ultimately, since it was just a mock battle and not a life-or-death fight, he couldn’t experience true… pleasure from the bottom of his heart.

Even so, fighting Rei brought Vihera far more overwhelming pleasure than fighting some random weakling with her life on the line.

That is precisely why she protested with a look of dissatisfaction toward Elena, who had suddenly interrupted it.

Elena, too, knew Vihera’s personality… or rather, in this case, perhaps it should be called a quirk. In any case, knowing this, she bowed her head apologetically.

“I’m sorry. But Lord Daskar has summoned you.”

“...I see.”

Although Vihera was dissatisfied, hearing that it was a summons from Daskar, she couldn’t throw a tantrum any longer.

“I suppose I have no choice,” she said regretfully, signaling with her posture that the mock battle would be temporarily suspended.

Only the moonlight and the glow of the campfire illuminated Vihera’s figure, and combined with her attire—which resembled that of a courtesan or dancer—it created a rather ethereal scene.

Rei was momentarily captivated by the scene, but he quickly remembered that Daskar was calling for him and turned toward Elena.

“So? Wasn’t Elena supposed to be talking to Ara?”

“That’s right. But the topic of pirates came up during their conversation. When Ara heard that, she suddenly asked us to wait a moment and went to fetch Lord Daskar… and Lord Daskar asked me to bring Rei here.”

“...Is it about the ship?”

He wondered if perhaps Daskar wanted him to sell the ship he’d acquired, but since Rei had already intended to do so anyway, he thought there was no need to rush things.

So, when I sat down in front of the Paired Orb, just as Elena had said, Daskar was there.

“Sorry about this, Rei. For calling you out on such short notice.”

“No, that’s fine. But what’s the matter? We’ve already secured the ship the pirates were using.”

“No, that’s not what this is about. …Of course, as for that pirate ship, I intend to purchase it outright since I’ll be using it as a reference for building land vessels. However, the reason I called you here today, Rei, is that I wanted to hear about the pirate you captured.”

“The pirate’s?”

Caught off guard by this completely unexpected question, Rei tilted his head.

Seeing Rei’s reaction, Daskar nodded frankly.

“That’s right. From what I heard from Lady Elena, although the captives were pirates, they apparently didn’t commit any heinous acts… like, for example, forcibly taking women away, did they?”

It wasn’t entirely impossible that they were hiding women somewhere else, but considering the pirates Rei had dealt with… and the personality of the man leading them, it didn’t seem like the sort of thing they would do.

(I wonder what it is. Maybe because he’s only just taken over as leader, there was definitely a certain… naivety? It was clear he wasn’t exactly averse to women, since he was so captivated by Vihera.)

On the way to Galanka by ship, Vihera arrived, clinging to Set.

The leader, upon seeing her, was mesmerized—even though a Death Scythe blade was pressed against his neck, and if Rei’s hand had wavered even slightly… if this man had made a single wrong move, it wouldn’t have been surprising if his head had been severed in an instant.

...Should one praise the man’s resolve for being so captivated by Vihera that he was willing to risk his own life, or should one admire Vihera’s beauty for capturing his gaze despite the life-threatening situation?

In any case, the possibility that the man had no interest in women was extremely low.

“Well, that works out perfectly. Since it’s a proposal from Rei, it goes without saying, but once the expansion work is finished… no, once things settle down a bit. We’ll build a facility for the research and manufacture of surface ships.”

That was old news.

After all, as Daskar had mentioned, it was Rei who had proposed the idea of the land ships.

“...Are you saying we’re going to take in those pirates to train them as sailors?”

“That’s right. Of course, ordinary sailors could serve as instructors too. But having a slave collar around their necks makes them ideal for the role. They won’t be able to pull stunts like lying or causing harm.”

“No, but… is that really okay? Even if they didn’t commit any atrocities, they’re still former pirates, aren’t they? And I imagine there are bound to be all sorts of differences between ships that float on the sea and land ships.”

“I’m aware of that. But land ships and ordinary ships are, at the end of the day, the same kind of vessel. I won’t say they’re exactly the same, but there must be many similarities.”

Rei was convinced by Daskar’s words.

There are no rivers or seas near Gilm.

Well, to be precise, there are some river-like streams flowing through the mountains, but it would be more accurate to say there are no major rivers suitable for navigation.

Among the adventurers gathering in Gilm, there are likely some who have knowledge of ships.

But as for Gilm, they lack that kind of knowledge.

Or, if they do, it is either very rudimentary or outdated.

In that sense, Rei found it reasonable to use the pirates—who, as slaves, could no longer defy orders—as instructors for ship handling.

(Well, since there are no seas or rivers, they probably can’t actually try to sail a ship. Or maybe they could travel to a place with a sea and do a practical training exercise there? …But that’s not really my place to decide.)

Ultimately, when it comes to the management of Gilm, Rei has no authority to make decisions.

All he can do is voice his opinion to Daskar; the final decision rests solely with Daskar.

“Understood. So, should I tell the slave trader to send that slave to Gilm?”

Autumn was approaching, and with the summer heat gone, work had become easier and more efficient, which pleased Daskar, and Rei was pleased as well.
