It was past four in the afternoon, and though the sun was visibly sinking, the sky had not yet turned to sunset. Set flew through it.

However, Set seemed to be flying with some difficulty, likely due to his current condition.

Rei was riding on his back as usual with no issues, and Vihera and Marina clinging to his front legs were also fine.

But this time, perhaps because Byune was clinging to his hind legs, his balance was slightly off while flying.

Of course, this was Set, who could easily fly while carrying a bear larger than himself.

Strength-wise, there's no issue whatsoever. But with Byune clinging to just one of his hind legs, he can't fly as he'd like.

...Though, "as he wishes" is strictly speaking from Set's perspective.

To an uninformed observer, it would likely appear he was flying without any particular difficulty.

While calling them one mind and body might be a slight exaggeration, Rei, who could understand Set's inner thoughts, grasped the discomfort he felt.

(We need to figure something out around here. ...Maybe have them make a cage for Set to carry things with his feet? But then, it'd probably make it easier for us to be spotted or attacked from the ground or something. ...Well, whether they could even reach Set's flying altitude is another matter entirely.)

At an altitude of about 100 meters like now, attacks from the ground wouldn't reach unless it was something extraordinary.

Maybe siege weapons like catapults could reach us, but Set could easily dodge stones, and with Rei and Marina around, intercepting them would be a breeze.

"Hey, Rei. Isn't it over there!?"

Rei looked down at the ground at Marina's words, hanging from Set's front legs.

There, slightly off the road, lay the wreckage of a carriage, abandoned.

Had they veered off the road while fleeing bandits, or had they deliberately left the road to slow their pursuers?

Rei couldn't tell which was the reason, but regardless, it was undoubtedly the location matching the information: the spot where one of the wagons carrying supplies had been destroyed.

Adventurers who had likely been guarding the supplies, along with the corpses of bandits, were scattered around the area.

"Set, please."

"Gluu!"

It had only been a few minutes since taking off from Gilm, but Set immediately began descending toward the ground.

Byune included, the three of them clung to Set's legs, so Set flapped his wings to maintain balance.

"Good thing we left a change of clothes in Vihera's room."

Marina murmured thoughtfully upon spotting several figures who appeared to be adventurers a short distance from where Set was about to land.

Marina usually wore a party dress, and if she had been wearing one now, her skirt would have been completely visible from the ground.

Of course, at this height, and considering the sun—the light source—was above Marina, there was basically no worry about anyone peeking up her skirt.

But that was only under normal circumstances.

There were beastmen whose senses—including sight—were sharper than ordinary humans, and elves and dark elves possessed superior vision by nature.

Even humans without such innate abilities often enhance their vision through magic or skills, given their access to magical power.

Flying through the air in a party dress under such circumstances was mentally taxing in more ways than one.

(I mean, Rei's one thing, but I've got no interest in showing my underwear to anyone else.)

While Marina pondered this, Set descended toward the ground.

Then, Marina, Vihera, and Byune timed their release as Set neared the ground.

The grassy field softly supported Marina and the others' weight, but unlike the other two, Byune lost her balance slightly upon landing.

This clearly showed the difference in skill between Byune and the other two.

Rei leapt off Set's back as if it were the most natural thing, landing on the ground without any loss of balance.

"Hmm..."

Having seen this, Byune murmured with a hint of frustration.

But Rei didn't notice, his gaze fixed on the wreckage of the carriage.

"It's spectacularly wrecked. ...They said the materials were stolen, but if they destroyed the carriage carrying the materials, how did they transport them?"

"...Did the bandits have their own transport arranged? If so, then perhaps they weren't targeting these merchants carrying the materials by chance after all?"

Vihera murmured in response to Rei's question.

But Marina shook her head.

"Gilm has massive amounts of materials delivered daily for the expansion. If they were targeting that, it doesn't necessarily mean they were after a specific group. ...More importantly, Rei. The dead adventurers and thieves—"

Rei understood what Marina was hoping for.

Still, burning the thieves' bodies gave him pause, however slight.

Still, it was this season.

If the bodies weren't disposed of quickly, they could turn into undead, attract monsters with the smell of blood, or worst of all, spread disease.

Especially since this spot wasn't far from the road; leaving bodies here would cause significant trouble.

"Guess there's no helping it," Rei sighed once, then used magic to burn the corpses.

From those who appeared to be adventurers, he took their guild cards and equipment as keepsakes.

He'd submit them to the guild later, to be returned if the adventurer's family or lover came looking.

...As for the guild cards, fine. But as for the personal belongings, if no such people came, Rei intended to keep them for himself.

Still, since it was meant as a keepsake substitute, he left the leather armor and such the adventurers had been wearing untouched.

Even Rei probably didn't want to go so far as to strip armor off a corpse.

A burnt smell hung in the air.

Rei's senses were sharper than most people's, and Set's were even sharper than Rei's.

Because of that, they couldn't help but feel uneasy.

Marina and Vihera seemed to sense Rei's mood and didn't force conversation.

Byune, paying no mind to Rei's state, was checking the direction the bandits had fled.

However, this was grassland.

They didn't know how much material had been stolen, but it was undoubtedly quite heavy.

Given that, traces would surely remain on the ground.

While ordinary people couldn't detect such traces, for a thief like Byune, it was easy enough.

...Recently, she'd been leaning more toward being a warrior than a thief, so perhaps Byune herself wanted to remind everyone she was still a thief.

After that moment of silent tribute—not exactly a prayer, but a mourning for the adventurer who died protecting the carriage—Rei and the others moved on to their next task.

"Byune, can you track them?"

"Mm!"

At Rei's words, Byune nodded with a confident expression.

He must be incredibly confident in his ability to pursue them.

"Then, count on you. Set, keep quiet from here on."

"Grr."

At Rei's words, Set growled, signaling he had it covered.

Being over three meters long, even the slightest movement from Set inevitably made noise.

And moving through mountains and forests, it was nearly impossible to avoid touching bushes and tree branches.

(Well, since they're bringing in supplies, it should be a fairly spacious place.)

Thinking this, Rei stroked Set as he followed Byune, who was leading the way.

"What do you think?"

As they walked, Marina suddenly asked Rei that question.

"About what?"

"This whole thing. Don't you think it's suspicious in all sorts of ways?"

"Yeah, I thought so too."

Interrupting Rei and Marina's conversation was Vihera, who, while keeping an eye on the surroundings, seemed somehow bored.

In terms of keeping watch, Set was essentially all they needed.

Still, Vihera was keeping watch simply because he had nothing better to do.

Vihera had confidence that whatever happened, they could handle it. The same went for Rei, Marina, and the others.

"It's true there are more merchants around for Gilm's expansion..."

"Ah, that matter."

Rei recalled the conversation he'd had with Lenora.

Truthfully, this whole situation was suspicious in more ways than one.

If the target was the materials being brought to Gilm, there was no need to specifically attack near Gilm itself.

They could just as easily attack near towns like Abuero or Saburusta, avoiding the risk of being ambushed by frontier monsters.

"Well, something's definitely up. ...Ideally, I'd have liked to talk to the merchant who escaped and his guards too."

"Even if they know something, wouldn't it be the merchant? The adventurer guards..."

Vihera cut herself off and glanced backward.

He gazed at the spot where corpses had been reduced to ash by Rei's flames.

"True. Since there were men dead over there, even if someone was plotting something, it doesn't mean everyone knew about it. ...Well, this is just speculation, of course."

"True. Maybe we should talk to Walker when we get back. If merchant carriages keep getting attacked here, it'll cause all sorts of problems."

"Hmph!"

While Rei and the others were talking, they had been moving forward guided by Byune. Suddenly, Byune gave a short warning.

Her gaze fell upon a grove.

It was the perfect spot for bandits to set up a hideout. ...If this weren't the frontier, that is.

"I wonder how they handle monsters. In a forest like this, monsters would come out in droves once night falls."

Yes, since this was the frontier, it was only natural that many monsters existed here.

And come nightfall, most monsters become active.

The surroundings are dark, with only the light from campfires. There might be moonlight, but if they've set up their hideout deep in the woods, they can't really count on that.

In such conditions, spending the entire night on guard, constantly alert to the possibility of a monster attack, would undoubtedly be exhausting both physically and mentally.

Even high-rank adventurers would likely prefer to avoid camping outside Gilm, not so much due to skill limitations but because of the sheer mental exhaustion.

Of course, when a quest requires camping, there's no room for such complaints.

At the very least, not many people would willingly choose to camp out.

(Well, if it means monsters attacking, Vihera, who enjoys combat, might not dislike it that much.)

That said, if asked directly, Vihera would probably make a difficult face.

She does enjoy fighting monsters, but only those of a certain strength or higher.

Even if a horde of goblins attacked, Vihera wouldn't be the least bit thrilled.

"There must be some reason for doing this. ...Well, this definitely rules out the possibility of him being just a common thief."

"Yeah. Well, once we take down the thieves, that part should become clear, right?"

Vihera murmured in agreement with Rei's words.

Indeed, if this was the work of a band of thieves with ulterior motives, capturing one of their members and interrogating them would undoubtedly be the quickest way to get answers.

"Right. ...Well then, let's go. To get our supplies back, we need to pinpoint their location precisely. ...Byune, can you handle it?"

"Mm!"

In response to Rei's question, Byune nodded confidently.

Rei had considered asking Set if it proved too much, but seeing her confident nod, he decided to leave it to her.

The sun hadn't yet turned to sunset, but it was already slanting low in the sky.

After all, they'd left Gilm around four in the afternoon, so that was only natural.

...It had only taken a few minutes to reach the ambush site after leaving Gilm.

In fact, it took considerably longer to leave Gilm from the Dusk Wheat inn.

And the journey from the ambush site to this forest had taken even longer than that.

(It's an extension of Gilm, and while transport using the Misty Ring is one thing, I figured there'd be almost no role for me in terms of combat... Looks like there'll be more commotion than I expected.)

With such thoughts running through his mind, Rei stepped deeper into the woods.
