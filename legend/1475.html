While Ajash and the others were reaching the conclusion to escape from Gilm, Rei frowned at the report he'd heard from Marina and Vihera.

The location was, as usual, Marina's garden.

Alongside the usual members of Crimson Wings, Ilse—the central figure in this matter—was also present.

Ilse, continuing from yesterday, was thoroughly enjoying the array of prepared dishes.

The dishes Rei had prepared were freshly bought from food stalls and cafeterias, stored in his Misty Ring.

Among them was a pizza made in a magic item kiln.

For Ilse, an adventurer who couldn't earn that much, every dish on the table was a feast for the eyes.

Yet, even as they ate these dishes, the words coming from Marina and Vihera's mouths were hardly fitting for enjoying a meal.

"So the number of missing persons is increasing after all?"

"Yes. It seems the guild has been getting a fair number of requests to search for missing persons."

Being the former guildmaster, it wasn't difficult for Marina to gather information on what kind of requests were being made.

She wasn't using illegal or shady methods; these were requests posted normally on the request board, facts easily ascertained by asking the receptionist.

Many of the receptionists admired Marina.

Moreover, while Marina was now an adventurer herself, she also held the position of advisor to the guild.

For someone like Marina, gathering information on publicly posted requests was no difficult task.

"While patrolling, I saw several people looking for someone they knew,"

The words spoken by Vihera, who was patrolling Gilm as a security guard assistant, corroborated Marina's statement.

What Rei mentioned during this meal wasn't just about Ajas, but also the story he'd heard earlier from the members of the Rank D party, Mercy Rain, at the construction site where they were doing the expansion work—about a missing comrade.

Rei didn't know why he'd decided to bring it up either.

After all, all sorts of people gathered at Gilm on a regular basis.

Among them, those who go missing for one reason or another are by no means few.

Especially now, with the expansion work, more people than usual were gathering.

Given that, it wasn't strange for someone to vanish from among them.

But... yes, but.

Even so, Rei couldn't shake the story he'd heard from the members of the Rain of Mercy.

There wasn't any clear reason.

No, the reason was probably that he'd been told about the personality of the missing Mercy Rain member, a woman named Ryusha.

"There are probably a lot of country bumpkins, right? If so, wouldn't it be possible that someone is targeting people like that?"

Elena murmured as she savored the soup, rich with vegetables and bacon.

Her words carried strong persuasive power.

Even though it's on the frontier... or rather, precisely because it's on the frontier, Gilm is quite developed.

After all, the current expansion project is transforming Gilm from a town into a city, clearly demonstrating its level of development.

...Then again, it was a town governed by Daskar, the central figure of the Neutral Faction, one of the three major factions within the Mireana Kingdom.

It was probably strange that it was still considered a town rather than a city in the first place.

"True. Gilm is undoubtedly a place full of temptations. ...But the ones disappearing are mostly young women, right? Of course, there are men among them too, so I can't say for certain..."

"Mostly women, huh? That makes it highly likely they got caught up in some kind of incident."

Munching on a sausage and summer vegetable pizza, Rei murmured in agreement.

Needless to say, women tend to get caught up in all sorts of trouble.

Since the missing people are adventurers, it'd be tough for some street thugs to mess with them.

But if physical force is impossible, then there are plenty of other ways to deal with it.

The simplest method would be to slip something into her food.

An even simpler method was to attack en masse, all at once.

From ambushes to sneak attacks, the methods are diverse.

"...Maybe we should ask the guards about it tomorrow."

Everyone present nodded at Vihera's words. She was assisting the guards.

Though Vihera seemed to be in a weak position as an assistant, in reality, very few guards could even come close to matching her in combat.

Because of this, Vihera held a strong presence among the guards.

Of course, strength alone doesn't earn unconditional trust or credibility from the guards or other adventurers.

However, the fact that he was active as a Crimson Wing, combined with his reputation for strength already known throughout Gilm, likely worked well in his favor in this situation.

Though it probably also helped that he routinely beat down anyone who tried to pick a fight based on Vihera's appearance and attire.

"Yeah, that'd be great. I'm pretty busy right now, so I'd rather not burden the guards too much if possible."

Those who heard Rei's words couldn't help but smile, to varying degrees.

The guards were busy because many adventurers, craftsmen, and even common folk came to Gilm seeking work.

With so many people gathered, disturbances were inevitable.

Among these, brawls were particularly common.

In fact, during their patrols of the city, Vihera and Byune had stopped... no, suppressed, such brawls more often than anything else.

Anyway, the matter of the missing person was settled with Vihera agreeing to consult the guards tomorrow.

Rei wondered if perhaps the members of the Rain of Mercy had already consulted the guards. But if that were the case, he thought, well, that wouldn't be so bad either.

An adventurer who just arrived in Gilm and Vihera, who had been active in Gilm for some time.

If both sides approached them, the guards would have no choice but to act.

(Besides, it gave him an oddly unpleasant feeling. It was as if something was stirring in the shadows... that sort of feeling.)

While pondering this strange feeling inside, Rei decided to wrap up the discussion about the missing person for now and spoke up.

"So, about Ajas..."

The moment the name Ajas was mentioned, Ilse's demeanor changed.

But since this was something Rei had anticipated, he said nothing in particular.

"I tried poking around again today, but they're still not going to show their hand that easily. Part of it's that I'm not exactly good at this sort of thing to begin with."

In Rei's mind, he was already certain Ajas was Ilse's enemy... or at least that he was definitely putting on a front.

But without any evidence to prove it, he couldn't just forcefully take action.

"Honestly, it'd be easier if Ajas was involved with that missing person we were talking about earlier,"

Vihera said with an exasperated sigh.

If that were the case, it would solve two problems at once, but he couldn't just assume that without any evidence.

"It would make things a lot easier, though. ...Anyway, Ajas must know I'm suspicious of him. If only he'd make some careless move and slip up, that would be ideal."

"If he made a careless move, we could make our own move. It's a bit of a shame he can't do that."

"...Perhaps I should confront Ajas directly?"

It was Ilse who said that.

But Marina immediately shook her head at Ilse's words.

"It's better to refrain. Ajas doesn't know why Rei targeted him. Well, of course, he might have guessed from his conversation with Rei based on his past actions, but even so, he shouldn't have any proof."

"But Marina, if Ajas finds out I'm here, I think he'll make some kind of move."

"True. But... the real problem is Ilse's own low skill level. What if you get attacked by Ajas? Of course, if one of us were nearby, we could handle it. But... I hate to say it, but we're all pretty busy right now."

Marina's words were vague, but their meaning was clear: they simply didn't have the time to focus solely on Ilse during this busy period.

Hearing Marina's words, Ilse quietly averted her gaze.

Ilse didn't believe her revenge was more important than expanding Gilm.

Still, deep down, Ilse couldn't help but want her revenge to take precedence over the Gilm expansion.

Sensing the awkward atmosphere, Rei suddenly voiced a thought.

"Couldn't Marina's spirit magic be used to warn us if Ilse is in danger? And while we're at it, to let us know where Ilse is..."

"Hmm... Well, it's a bit tricky, but it's not impossible. However, if we do that..."

Marina hesitated, closing her eyes as if deep in thought... then finally opened her mouth with a resigned smile.

"It'll be pushing it a bit, but if we're talking about possible or impossible, it is possible. However, if we do that, the extension work might get delayed a little."

"...I see."

Rei, who was constantly moving between various locations; Set, who was solely responsible for aerial surveillance; and Vihera and Byune, who were patrolling the town.

Just like those three and one creature, Marina was working at the site of the expansion project.

Marina, who wielded spirit magic with such freedom—to an extent that even fellow spirit mages found it unbelievable—was proving to be a tremendous asset at the construction site.

Pushing Marina too hard meant the strain would inevitably fall on someone else.

(The question is, just how much of a burden, right?)

If the fallout was within acceptable limits, it wouldn't be a problem.

But if it could lead to something irreversible... well, not quite irreversible, but potentially serious, then naturally that must be prioritized.

Marina watched Rei hesitate, then murmured, "Well, I suppose I have no choice," before speaking.

"Alright. If Rei cares this much about this child, I suppose I'll try a little harder too."

"Are you sure?"

Rei, who had been reluctant just moments ago, now looked at Marina with puzzlement as she suddenly said this.

Meeting Rei's gaze, Marina nodded with her usual, alluring smile.

"Yes, no problem. But I'd like a reward for my efforts, wouldn't I?"

"...Even so. What exactly do you want me to do?"

The thought of paying her as a part-timer flashed through Rei's mind for a moment, but they were currently in Marina's house.

Considering it was a house in the noble district, Marina certainly couldn't be short on money.

Moreover, Marina had been the Guildmaster until recently, so she had naturally received that remuneration.

The guild master was the top position in the guild... and this was the guild of a remote town, Gilm.

Naturally, that reward was a substantial sum. She possessed an amount equal to, or even greater than, what Rei had—enough for a family to live in luxury not just for a lifetime, but for ten lifetimes.

Of course, it wasn't stored as gold or silver coins, but rather converted into something like jewels.

The fact that Marina's house is protected by spirit magic also serves as a form of security.

...Marina herself wasn't particularly interested in money to that extent.

Being a Dark Elf, her material desires simply aren't that strong.

Anyway, the reward Marina wanted.

That was something Rei couldn't quite imagine.

Seeing Rei's confusion, Vihera spoke up with a slightly exasperated expression.

"...A single date with Rei. Something like that, right?"

"Huh? Is that okay? Well, I guess I wouldn't mind that either."

"Then, may I also request a d-d-date with Rei as a reward? Since I'm holding back the Noble Faction's rampage."

Elena hesitated slightly at the word "date," but Ara watched her with an expression that seemed to say, "Well said!"

"Well then, I suppose I'll request a date as well."

Vihera, who had been listening to Elena, said this. In the end, Rei agreed to go on dates with Elena and the other two in exchange for Ilse making direct contact with Ajash.
