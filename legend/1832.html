Naturally, the Black Wolf’s intrusion came as a shock to Liberte in the midst of the staff appreciation party.

That said, it seemed a shame to cut short the party the servants were enjoying as a year-end celebration, so in the end, the knights and guards who were free from their duties were the ones who ended up searching for the Black Wolf.

……Normally, a full-scale search for the Black Wolf would have been the standard procedure, but this wasn’t done partly because Rei had informed them that the Black Wolf was likely no longer in the mansion, and partly because they anticipated that the Black Wolf’s target was solely Rei, and that no one else would be harmed.

That said, regarding the latter reason, given that there had been an attacker who lost their mind due to drugs and assaulted Rei after the mock battle, they couldn’t be absolutely certain.

However, in Rei’s opinion, he believed that the Black Wolf likely used only those who were not ordinary, law-abiding citizens as pawns.

In fact, the people who attacked Rei after the mock battle were those who were already drug addicts.

In any case, while Black Wolf’s intrusion caused a bit of a commotion—which was purely bad luck for the knights and guards assigned to today’s security detail—it meant that everyone else attending the party was able to enjoy a perfectly trouble-free, fun time.

And so, the party—which appeared to be fun on the surface, yet behind the scenes saw those on duty today running around frantically—came to an end, and Rei headed toward the stables where Set was staying, carrying the various leftover dishes from the party.

Normally, the leftover food from the party would be eaten by the cooks, led by Georgima, or used for the servants’ meals the next day, but this time, Liberte had given Rei permission to take the leftovers to Set.

Technically, Set’s meal today had been more lavish than usual because of the party… but even so, it was still slightly inferior compared to the dishes actually served at the party.

For that reason, being able to feed Set the food served at the party was a joy for Rei, as it showed her that Liberte truly cared for him.

(Though, I seem to recall hearing that giving human food directly to dogs and such is a bad idea because it’s too salty… but, well, it’s too late to worry about that now.)

Set had always eaten the same food as Rei, and above all, he wasn’t an animal but a monster… and a rare breed at that, created through Beast Magic.

So I figured it probably wouldn’t be a problem to give him the same food I was eating…

“Huh!?”

A sound of destruction rang out, and Rei shot a sharp glance toward the source of the noise… toward Set’s stable.

And without a moment’s hesitation, he started running.

The sound of destruction clearly came from the direction where Set was.

Given that, it stands to reason that Set was the one who did it.

But since Set wouldn’t do something like that for no reason, there must have been some circumstance that forced him to do it.

And today, a black wolf has sneaked into the mansion.

Based on Rei’s intuition after facing him directly, she believed the Black Wolf wouldn’t lay a hand on anyone but her.

However, when it comes to Set… he couldn’t say for certain.

Set is recognized as Rei’s familiar, so it wouldn’t be strange for him to be seen as one and the same with Rei.

Even so, based on Rei’s instincts, he didn’t think the Black Wolf would lay a hand on Set, but since he could actually hear the sound of destruction, there was a slim chance—a one-in-a-million chance—that it might happen.

In the moonlight, Rei ran across the ground.

Fortunately, there was no snow on the ground, so the ground wasn’t muddy and difficult to run on.

As he ran, stamping down on the ground, Rei’s stable eventually came into view.

“……Huh?”

He could see the stable… but a few people were lying unconscious a short distance away from it, and no matter how he looked at them, they weren’t the Black Wolf.

Seeing this, Rei felt relieved that it wasn’t the Black Wolf who had attacked Set, yet at the same time, wondering, “Then who was it?” she approached the figures lying on the ground.

The sound of people running from afar was likely caused by the echo of the stable wall being destroyed.

Security was already tight tonight on the assumption that the Black Wolf had infiltrated the premises, so hearing that sound of destruction, it was only natural that guards and knights would gather.

“Well, for now… who exactly are you guys?”

Muttering to himself, Rei exposed the face of the man knocked unconscious by Set’s blow to the moonlight.

Set must have been careful not to kill them. The men appeared to have injuries like broken bones, but none of them were dead.

“All three of them are men, huh? …But I don’t recognize any of their faces.”

As far as he could tell from this initial glance, Rei had absolutely no recollection of the men’s faces.

Naturally, they weren’t Black Wolves either.

“Set, come out here for a moment!”

“Grrrrr…”

Obeying Rei’s voice, Set emerged from the stable.

Perhaps out of consideration, Set came out through the door instead of the wall he had destroyed.

Even so, the slightly apologetic look on Set’s face is likely because he did wreck the stable.

Rei strokes Set’s head, signaling with his demeanor that he shouldn’t worry about it, and asks,

“So? These guys attacked us… Is that definitely what happened?”

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growled in agreement.

Of course, since Rei knew Set wouldn’t do something like this without a reason, he had no intention of making an issue out of the incident.

That said, that was merely Rei’s own opinion; he had no idea how the Duke of Kerebel’s household would judge the matter.

“Hey, what was that noise just now!”

The voices that reached their ears belonged to the guards and knights who had finally arrived on the scene.

“You’re late. It seems these guys snuck into Set’s stables and launched an attack. So, Set fought back… and this is how it turned out.”

Perhaps because the guards and knights who had arrived were people he knew, Rei said this while glancing at the three men lying unconscious on the ground.

Since the guards and knights had some familiarity with Rei and Set, they didn’t think Set would do something like this for no reason, so they showed a certain degree of understanding for Rei’s explanation.

That said, just because they knew him didn’t mean they believed Rei’s words completely.

They carefully checked the faces of the men lying on the ground...

“Who are these guys?”

The knight muttered with a puzzled expression.

It wasn’t a joke; it was a genuine question from the bottom of his heart.

Seeing the knight’s reaction, the guard also grew suspicious and turned his gaze in that direction.

But in the end, they shared the same question as the knight.

“I don’t recognize you. …I just assumed it was one of our guys who’d somehow ended up in Set’s stables by mistake…”

It was a face the guard didn’t recognize either.

If only one of them had been unfamiliar, it wouldn’t have been so strange.

The Duke of Kerebel’s residence is a building so vast it could almost be called a castle.

Naturally, the number of people working there is large, so it wouldn’t be strange for a fair number of them not to be attending today’s party.

Even so, the fact that there were two of them—a knight and a guard—and that I had never seen the faces of either of them… no, of all three men—was clearly nothing short of abnormal. In other words…

“Does that mean these guys aren’t servants of the Duke of Kerebel’s household? And considering how I was attacked by Set in the stables… isn’t that a bit too suspicious?”

At Rei’s muttered words, the knight and the guard nodded, then looked at the three men lying unconscious on the ground with stern expressions.

“So, these guys are the intruders we heard about?”

“No. They’re probably a different group altogether. …Given that they came to Set’s stables, were they after materials like Set’s feathers, or was Set himself the target… or perhaps, was Set’s life the goal?”

Though Rei voiced his own conjecture while refuting the guard’s words, the smile on his face could easily be described as cold and calculating.

This time, since Set had intercepted them like this, there was no problem, but that didn’t mean he could simply let it slide.

For Rei to lay a hand on his partner—someone he could truly call his other half—was an act so heinous that even the Grim Reaper wielding his scythe would not have been out of place.

In that sense, the men knocked unconscious by Set’s attack were probably lucky.

The knight and the guard, judging from Rei’s demeanor that things were getting out of hand—and perhaps realizing that if the unconscious men woke up here, it would be trouble in more ways than one—spoke up in a panic.

“For now, there’s no doubt these men are the cause of this commotion. If we leave them unconscious here, we don’t know what they’ll do when they wake up, so let’s throw them in a cell. …Fortunately, reinforcements have arrived.”

As the knight spoke, the figures of guards and knights approaching Rei and the others could be clearly seen in the moonlight and the glow from the buildings.

…Though, of course, the fact that they could make out their faces so clearly was likely only possible because of Rei and Set’s keen night vision.

“You’re right.”

Though somewhat reluctantly, Rei must have decided that there was no point in forcing himself to say anything here. He agreed to the suggestion of taking the three unconscious men to the dungeon.

However, he didn’t just hand the men over in silence as if it were a matter of course…

“Once you figure out what these guys were thinking when they broke into Set’s stables, you’ll pass that information on to us too, right?”

“Ah… yeah. I’ll have to check with my superiors first, but I think it should be fine.”

“I see. Well, even if they say no, I have a feeling Kerebel Duke would tell me if I asked him.”

“That’s…”

At Rei’s words, the knight was at a loss for words.

After all, Rei’s familiar—treated as a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s household—had been attacked by someone unknown.

If Rei were to ask for that information, Liberte, in his position as head of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, would have no choice but to tell him.

Ultimately, if the information was going to reach Rei anyway, it would undoubtedly have been wiser to simply tell him themselves rather than go through the trouble of involving Liberte.

In the worst-case scenario, they might even get scolded by their superiors for withholding information and causing Liberte unnecessary trouble.

If Rei had asked the knights and soldiers to pass on information about an incident that had nothing to do with them, they would have refused.

But since it was Rei’s familiar, Set, who had been attacked this time, there was no problem at all with sharing that information.

The only drawback was that we didn’t know who the man was who had been knocked unconscious by Set… or rather, since it was highly likely he wasn’t from the Duke of Kerebel’s household, the fact that we didn’t know which faction he belonged to was a major issue.

“I understand. Once I find out who these guys are and where they’re from, I’ll let Rei know. But… promise me one thing. Even if I do find out who they are and where they’re from, don’t you dare go barging into their hideout or wherever on your own.”

“...Does that mean you want me to just take it?”

There was a look of dissatisfaction in Rei’s eyes as he said this.

That said, simply letting them get away with it would draw a line for those attacking Rei and Set.

They’d likely conclude that Rei wouldn’t fight back if they attacked Set, which would only increase the frequency of attacks.

Of course, it’s true that part of the reason Rei showed his dissatisfaction was because he didn’t find it fun to just take the hits, but that was the bigger factor.

Perhaps sensing Rei’s feelings, the knights and guards spoke up to calm him down.

“These people trespassed onto the Kerebel Duke’s estate without permission. Given that, we can’t just let this slide. And naturally, the same goes for whoever is pulling the strings behind them.”

At those words, Rei hesitated slightly, unsure of what to do… but eventually nodded.

That said, his agreement stemmed from the judgment that leaving this to Liberte—rather than taking action himself—would lead to the worst possible outcome for whoever had orchestrated this incident.

“Understood. I’ll leave it to you for now. But if I think Kerebel Duke’s judgment is too lenient, I’ll take matters into my own hands.”

He emphasized this point.

The knights and guards nodded in agreement, seeing no particular issue with Rei’s words.

In fact, considering what kind of retaliation Liberte would unleash on anyone who dared lay a hand on Rei’s familiar—her own guest—the knights and guards could state with absolute certainty that Rei would never allow such a thing to happen.
