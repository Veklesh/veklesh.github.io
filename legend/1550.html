"Hmm..."

As the man groaned in pain, Byune drew a new long needle.

Given that they were launching a surprise attack on an important base of the organization known as Jaya, it was only natural that they had brought more weapons this time.

She carried more long needles than usual to use as deterrents.

Furthermore, while she couldn't retrieve used long needles during combat, in a situation like this, there was the advantage of being able to retrieve them and reuse them.

For that reason, Byune tried to draw yet another new long needle, but...

"Stop. Stop right there."

Her hand was grabbed and stopped by Rei.

Why? Byune tilted her head slightly, her expression unchanged.

The other three had been stopped by Rei, but she shouldn't have been stopped.

With that thought... yet seeing only a gaze that revealed no emotion to Rei, he opened his mouth with a look of failure.

"You too, Byune. We need to extract information from him. Killing him now would defeat the purpose, wouldn't it? So bear with it for now."

Rei paused there, then reached for the long needle piercing the man's arm, its tip protruding.

"Gyaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!"

Even the slightest touch must have sent excruciating pain through the man.

Or perhaps it was just the sight of it causing him to feel pain, even if it didn't actually hurt.

Either way, he pinched the tip of the needle... and then yanked it out in one swift motion.

Since she'd grabbed the tip of the long needle and yanked it out in one swift motion, the pain the man felt this time was surely excruciating in the truest sense.

"Ah, sorry about that."

Rei said this, showing not the slightest hint of remorse.

"It... hurts... it hurts..."

As a researcher, he probably never goes outside.

And certainly not injuries like this... He probably only ever experienced minor injuries, like cutting his hand during a minor experiment mishap or bumping his foot or hand on a table in daily life.

That's precisely why the man, his arm pierced by the long needle, was experiencing pain so intense it could only be called unknown.

But... in the eyes of Rei, Elena, and the others watching the man clutching his pierced right arm with his left hand, there was not a shred of sympathy.

"More people have suffered because of your research. And they've felt far worse pain. There's no need to make such a fuss over this level of discomfort, is there?"

Facing Rei, who delivered this with a refreshing smile, the man felt an eerie sense of encountering something utterly unknowable.

"Heh heh, you're right. I'd love to make you fully understand the suffering those women endured because of that black crystal and the slave collars..."

Marina wore a smile just as radiant as Rei's.

Perhaps because she was suppressing her anger, she radiated a certain fierce sensuality.

The others wore expressions similar to Marina's, though each slightly different.

Only Byune remained as expressionless as ever.

...But since she'd just thrown the long needle with that same expression, the man couldn't possibly feel at ease.

"Wh-what... What the hell are you doing?!"

"Don't you get it? Well, I suppose you could have guessed from everything that's happened. Anyway, we're intruders in this underground facility. That means we're enemies of Jaya. Do you really think we'd treat you... a person of Jaya... with friendship?"

If the man had been kidnapped and forced into research, Rei wouldn't have harmed him. He would have at least used potions to heal the wounds inflicted by Byune's long needle.

But the man before Rei, while undoubtedly kidnapped at first, had since chosen to cooperate with Jaya of his own accord.

For Rei... and for the others too, if asked whether they could forgive the man, the answer was undoubtedly no.

"Wh-wh-wh-..."

"Well, judging by that look, it seems you still haven't grasped what kind of situation you're in."

Rei let a hint of exasperation show at the man's state.

With an air of annoyance, he pulled a potion from his Misty Ring.

...Though, the potion Rei pulled out wasn't one of the high-quality ones she hoarded; it was just a standard potion sold anywhere.

"Wh-!? An item box!?"

The man shouted, as if forgetting the pain in his right arm.

Watching him, Rei spoke again.

"Forget about me for now. I need to ask you a few things. First, about that black crystal you were researching—tell me where it is. Also, about the facility for producing giants and the place where the born giants are kept."

"...Well... the giant facility aside, couldn't you maybe spare me about the black crystal? That black crystal is something I've been researching for a long time."

"It's precisely because that black crystal exists that the slave collars are effective."

"Well..."

At Rei's words, the man tried to retort, but fell silent under the sharp stares from the women behind him.

He sensed that saying anything now would likely result in the excruciating pain in his right arm intensifying many times over.

That judgment was probably correct.

If he had stubbornly argued back, the worst possible outcome would likely have been his fate.

Facing the silent man, Rei spoke again, urging him on.

"For now, let's deal with the giant. So, where is the room for producing giants?"

"If this is the first basement level, then it's on the third basement level. By the way, the pregnant prostitutes are all gathered on the second basement level. But since that slave was impregnated while wearing that collar from Mejogo, it's already decided she'll give birth to a giant. There's no way around it."

Griiiit.

The sound of someone gritting their teeth echoed through the room.

Ignoring it deliberately, Rei confirmed the man had started speaking again. He opened the cap of the potion bottle in his hand and roughly splashed it onto the man's right arm.

It was a cheap potion, so it wouldn't provide a complete cure, but it could still seal a wound like the one caused by a long needle piercing through.

...The crucial point was that it only sealed the wound on the surface; it wouldn't heal the damage inside the arm where the needle had penetrated.

Proper healing of those deeper wounds would take some time.

"Hey!"

Vihera frowned at the use of the potion.

No, it wasn't just Vihera. Elena and Marina, and even Byune—though she didn't show it—seemed dissatisfied with Rei's actions.

If Rei had been in Elena and the others' position, he might have felt dissatisfied too.

But Rei hadn't treated the man's injuries out of any sense of mercy.

"If you hesitate, I'll just use the long needle again. In the same spot."

He stated it casually, without any particular force or tension in his voice, as if it were nothing.

But the man watching him closely understood precisely because of the casual tone that this wasn't a joke or a threat.

He was serious. If he didn't answer the other's questions, he would undoubtedly suffer the same excruciating pain as before... and in the exact same spot.

Facing such a Rei, the man had to speak his words clearly.

He couldn't lie, nor could he remain silent... he understood that.

Rei, judging that the man had fully grasped his meaning, spoke again.

"I understand about the Giant's Room. Next. Hmm... So, in the end, the only magic items used in Jaya are black crystals and slave collars? I'd assumed Jaya possessed a wide variety of magic items."

"We do make magic items, actually. A large number of researchers and alchemists were gathered to study the black crystal and the slave collars. Since we had gathered them anyway, some suggested it would be better to make magic items to sell on the black market."

Hearing they also made magic items, Rei felt relieved.

Originally, he'd come to this organization, Jaya, seeking revenge because Daskar had requested retribution for Gilm's meddling... but part of the reward agreement was that any magic items obtained could be Rei's.

That was precisely why Rei had gone out of his way to come to the small country of Reburina.

...Though, now that he'd arrived in that small country and learned what was happening there, his priorities had shifted slightly.

Still, his dislike for Jaya remained unchanged, and the core objective of obtaining the magic item was unchanged. Therefore, the man's words were a relief to Rei.

"So, where are those magic items?"

"They're split up across several warehouses, and apparently some are stored in warehouses outside this underground facility too. Unfortunately, though, I only know about a few of them."

Even though he was cooperating with Jaya, the man was still just someone who'd been forcibly brought here.

Given that, she judged she couldn't fully trust him yet, at least for now, regardless of what might happen in the future.

Even so, it was clearly better than having no information at all.

"Where are those warehouses?"

"One is in the room three doors down from this one, and another is in a room six doors away from that warehouse."

"I see. ...What kind of magic items are there?"

"Mostly consumables. I can't say for sure what kind of magic items you're looking for, since I don't know what you need."

At those words, Rei's expression turned slightly dissatisfied.

What Rei wanted, above all, were usable magic items.

Magic Swords, hidden weapons, and considering the Set Cage, a magic tent would be ideal... or so he thought.

But judging from what he'd just heard, it was probably best to assume such magic items weren't available.

(Are the magic items I want stored in other... warehouses only accessible to high-ranking officials? Or do they simply not have such items? It's a delicate matter.)

Realistically, it's hard to imagine a small country like Reburina having the kind of magic items Rei desires.

But with Mejogo, a veritable money tree, they could gather as much gold as they needed.

With that money, acquiring most magic items—barring the truly exorbitantly priced ones—wouldn't be difficult.

Considering that situation, it's not entirely impossible...

Though, while an organization-made magic item might make sense, I couldn't imagine them actually buying one with their own money and just storing it in a warehouse.

"I see. So, regarding the warehouse, we'll need to search it thoroughly. ...Though I don't hold out much hope. And what about the slave collars?"

I heard from the man guarding the underground facility that the slave collars are also stored in the warehouse.

But since he hadn't been told which specific warehouse, Ray wanted to get that information from the man in front of him if possible.

But... that optimistic hope of Rei's had already been dashed by the man's words.

It was only natural to expect that slave collars wouldn't just be lying around in some random warehouse.

Given that, it seemed unlikely that slave collars and the like could be obtained so easily.

(Should I just wipe out all the enemies in this underground facility? That would buy me some time... but no, considering the possibility of hidden storage rooms, that might be a bit too risky.)

In the end, I decided that if I encountered anyone further ahead, I could just ask them where the magic item warehouse was... And then, I moved on to the final question.

The final, and most crucial, question.

"So, where is the black crystal?"

Yes, the black crystal—the very cause of this entire incident.

Rei asked again where this very thing was—the very thing the man before him had begged him to spare him from.

"What... Didn't I answer your other questions!? Then, the black crystal is..."

Ah.

Rei realized why he had been so cooperative with them.

He must have thought that by giving us other information, he could somehow get us to spare him regarding the black crystal he was interested in.

"No. Without the black crystal, this whole mess wouldn't have happened in the first place. That being the case, we can't just leave it alone. ...Byune."

"Hmm?"

Though he'd only called her name, Byune understood what Rei expected of her and quietly handed him the long needle.

He must have remembered the pain of being pierced by the needle.

The man instinctively clutched his right arm... the spot where Byune's first long needle had pierced him, his face turning deathly pale.
