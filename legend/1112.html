The first to notice the sand ship approaching Gosh were, naturally, the guards stationed at the gate.

Next, the adventurers and merchants going through entry procedures into Gosh began to wonder if it was some kind of mirage... but seeing the sand ship unmistakably heading straight for Gosh, they each began taking action.

The adventurers and merchants hurriedly urged the guards to let them inside Gosh, while the guards themselves rushed through the procedures and reported the approaching sand ship to their superiors.

The commotion caused by the sand ship's approach was largely due to the sheer rarity of the vessel itself.

They are incredibly valuable, and there was also information that a sand pirate captured just days ago had been using one.

In other words, many feared another sand pirate might be attacking Gosh using a sand ship.

It's highly unlikely that many sand pirates possess sand ships, but given the recent example, it can't be ruled out that others might have them too.

(Damn it! If only we'd gotten information out of those sand pirates!)

One of the guards gritted his teeth, remembering the sand pirates who had all been dead by the next day after being handed over to them.

They managed to get the adventurers and merchants who had been in front of Gosh into the city, while the guards stationed in Gosh gathered at the main gate.

Word seemed to have reached the guild as well, and several adventurers had already gathered.

"Hey, listen. That sand ship..."

"Yeah, probably. Right?"

Amidst this, a few adventurers exchanged words as they watched the sand ship approaching.

In fact, some were even sheathing the longswords they held.

The guard, who had been seeking information about the sand ship, naturally turned his gaze toward the adventurers and spoke up.

"You lot, do you know anything about that sand ship over there?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. You know, that time we caught those sand pirates? They were on a sand ship, right?"

The guard, having just been thinking about the same thing himself, immediately understood what the adventurer was referring to.

He also recalled that the two standing before him had been among the adventurers who had captured the sand pirates back then.

"I see. You two were there... So, what do you know about that sand ship?"

"That sand ship... it's what Rei took from the sand pirates who attacked us. Meaning, Rei is the one on that sand ship."

Hearing the adventurer's words, the guard recalled the figure of the adventurer with the griffin.

Though he had only just arrived in Gosh, information had already spread that he was a Rank B adventurer with a nickname.

It was only natural. An adventurer of that caliber was an extremely powerful asset in terms of combat strength.

For those responsible for Gosh's security, obtaining that information was only natural.

"So that sand ship was stolen from the sand pirates?"

The guard, who hadn't seen the sand ship Daldora possessed, muttered in a daze, yet didn't release his grip on his spear.

Even if Rei had seized the sand ship from the sand pirates, it couldn't be ruled out that the ship had somehow been taken from Rei afterward and returned to the pirates.

But that worry vanished instantly.

Because they saw a griffin standing on the sand ship's deck.

"Ah, Set."

At the murmur from one of the adventurers, the guards finally relaxed their vigilance.

The other guards and adventurers also relaxed their vigilance, and many began returning to the city streets.

...Though some remained out of curiosity, not knowing Rei's face and wondering what kind of person was aboard that sand ship.

Under the gaze of such onlookers, the sand ship came to a stop about a hundred meters from Gosh's main gate.

Soon, a long gangway extended from the deck to the ground, and four figures emerged from it.

Four!? The guards and adventurers who knew Rei gasped in surprise. As the faces of the four became clearly visible, one adventurer shouted.

"It's those guys from the Claws of the Sandstorm!"

Some of those remaining near the gate reacted, likely recognizing the party name.

Howl looked quite startled to realize they were attracting attention, though considering they'd arrived aboard a sand ship, it wasn't entirely surprising.

Then, seeing the adventurers and guards at the main gate relax when Rei appeared behind them, Howl breathed a sigh of relief himself, glad they'd avoided unnecessary trouble.

...Though, truth be told, he was already deeply entangled in trouble at this point.

"Huh? What's up?"

Rei asked as he landed in the desert, and Howl turned his gaze toward Gosh.

That glance alone seemed to tell him everything. Sighing, Rei touched the sand ship and stored it away in the Misty Ring.

Those unaware of the Misty Ring's existence gasped in astonishment at the sight, but Rei, accustomed to such reactions, simply continued toward Gosh alongside the four members of the Sand Claw.

Howl, wanting to avoid being the frontline for this situation, walked behind Rei, while Set, as usual, walked beside him.

"So it was Rei after all. What's up with coming in a sand ship?"

Rei addressed them, speaking for those gathered before the main gate.

"Nah, I just helped these guys out when they were getting attacked. It was perfect for getting around, so I came by sand ship like this."

Rei successfully recalled who the familiar face was—the man who had guarded Augusto's caravan, right? He replied.

"I figured as much. ...I mean, come on."

"Huh? Oh, ah. Yeah. Well, if that's the case, then there's no problem. No problem at all, yeah."

The sand ship itself was one thing, but then it suddenly vanished, and seeing the griffin casually surveying the area beside Rei... There were simply too many astonishing things happening.

Had the guards on duty at the main gate when Rei arrived or left been the same ones, they might not have been so shocked. Unfortunately, the guards currently manning the gate had only been assigned here this morning.

Of course, they had heard during the shift handover about the adventurer with the griffin and the capture of the sand pirates. Still, there was a huge difference between hearing about it and seeing it directly.

"So, I'd like to enter Gosh now. Is that okay?"

The guard looked so flustered when Rei asked that it was almost pitiful, but after a moment, he seemed to calm down. He managed to regain his composure and proceed with the formalities.

And so, Rei entered Gosh alongside Set... and the other adventurers who remained nearby.

"Rei, we need to head to the guild. We have to report on the request too."

"Right."

Rei replied briefly to Howl's words.

Rei didn't know what kind of request the Sand Claw had been on this time, but even so, they had abandoned most of their belongings to flee from the Sand Lizardman.

Whether it was an extermination or a gathering job, he could guess the request had probably failed.

But unlike Rei, Howl didn't seem particularly downcast.

The mission itself had failed, but the fact remained that they had narrowly escaped death.

He was certain that if Rei hadn't come, they would have been killed by the Sand Lizardmen without a doubt.

Thinking that way, even though the job failed, surviving was pure luck.

"I'm grateful to Rei. Without you, all of us would have died. ...Thank you."

As Howl bowed his head to say this, the other three bowed as well.

The mission had failed, but knowing Rei had saved their lives, they couldn't help but feel grateful.

"Don't worry about it. I did it because it was beneficial for me too."

In truth, the Sand Lizardman affair hadn't been entirely bad.

The Sand Lizardman's magic stone, materials, meat, and the trident.

In terms of spear-throwing, since I started using the Twilight Spear, I've mainly been using that.

Still, disposable spears are always useful to have.

Also, besides the Sand Lizardman, the idea of using the Sand Ship as a hotel substitute came to mind... Beyond that, the biggest gain was probably the idea I had about the Two-Spear Style.

The dual spear style might drastically change Rei's combat style up to now, but even so, Rei had no intention of abandoning its potential.

Mastering the Two-Spear Style would undoubtedly make him stronger than ever before.

The Rank S adventurer Immovable Noiz, whom he fought in the Bestir Empire... Though it felt like Noiz had held back considerably during their previous encounter, there was no doubt that if Rei's power increased through the Two-Spear Style, that gap would narrow significantly.

Considering such gains, saving the four of the Sand Claw felt like hardly any effort at all.

If anything, the benefits were overwhelmingly greater.

"Still, we were saved by Rei. At least let us say thank you."

"That's right. And as fellow spear users, Rei's fighting style was... helpful... or maybe not... but seeing how Rei fought, realizing there are different ways to fight like that... it was useful."

Kaldina cut herself off mid-sentence, her words trailing off.

Rei's fighting style was instructive, but if asked to do the same thing, she couldn't possibly manage it.

In fact, she understood that Rei's fighting style relied entirely on his physical abilities, and if she tried to copy it, she'd die instantly.

"Don't worry about it for now. It's true we gained plenty from this too."

"...I'm sorry. When the time comes to repay this debt, I will."

With that, Haul bowed his head once more and departed.

Watching his retreating figure, and noticing several people still staring at him, Rei too tried to blend into the streets of Gosh...

"Rei-san! Haa haa, it really was you! I was terribly worried when you didn't come back yesterday!"

Augusto appeared, panting heavily.

Beside Augusto stood Zarust and a monkey beastman Rei had never seen before, but of the three, only Augusto was panting.

"Augusto... Sorry for worrying you. I had some business to attend to."

"Circumstances... The adventurer you were with earlier?"

"Yeah. I saved them from being attacked by Sand Lizardmen, but they apparently abandoned most of their luggage while fleeing. I couldn't just leave them behind in that situation, could I?"

To be precise, he'd lost track of Gosh's location and gotten lost himself, but he kept that to himself.

"...I see. Well then. Yes, as someone who lives in Gosh, I'm grateful you helped the young adventurer. Thank you."

"No, it's not like Augusto is their guardian or anything, right? So there's no need to thank me..."

Rei said this, but Augusto shook his head vehemently.

"Gosh... no, the Kingdom of Solrain, as you can see, is by no means a wealthy country. Much of its land is covered by desert, and while the Kingdom of Mireana is another matter, compared to the surrounding nations, its national strength is clearly inferior."

Hearing Augusto's words, those around them wore expressions of frustration.

Even if they didn't fully grasp the dynamics between nations, the realization that their own country was inferior must be humiliating for its inhabitants.

Some nations deliberately adopted a tough stance toward their neighbors to mask this humiliation, claiming that what other countries possessed was originally theirs, or that the origins of things found elsewhere lay with them. Fortunately, the Kingdom of Solrain was not such a nation.

But precisely because of that, understanding their nation's position only deepened their frustration.

"That's precisely why we need as many promising talents as possible. In that sense, I suppose I'm disqualified."

Rei couldn't fathom what made him say he was unqualified, but he surmised it must be about the scale of the shop.

Given the overwhelming stranglehold Erema Trading Company held over the market, that must be what he meant.

"Allow me to express my gratitude as well."

Zalust, who had been beside Augusto, also addressed Rei and bowed his head.

"You know Howl and the others? Well, as fellow adventurers from Gosh, you probably know them."

"Yeah. I helped them out a few times back when they were just starting out as adventurers."

Rei nodded in understanding at those words.

Even if it was back when Howl and the others had just become adventurers, it made perfect sense that he'd care about them if he'd looked after them like that.

Many adventurers didn't value that sort of connection, but Zarust was different, it seemed.

"...Well then, I suppose we should head back home soon. Kathy's worried, after all. ...She'll definitely be angry, you know?"

Augusto said this with a mischievous smile, quite different from his earlier expression.
