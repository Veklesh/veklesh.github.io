Rei had been ready to deal with the person who had leaped from the roof onto the balcony at any moment, but…

Seeing the figure appear before him, Rei couldn’t help but let out a startled gasp.

“Huh?”

It was only natural that such a slightly dazed sound escaped his lips.

That’s because the person who appeared before Rei looked completely different from the black wolf he had encountered before.

First of all, the hair reflecting the moonlight was silver.

While his build seemed similar to the one he’d encountered before, his hair—which had previously reached his back—now only came down to his shoulders.

Rei couldn’t help but wonder if the person had simply cut their hair, but even so, he felt something was off about the person standing before him.

At the very least, the figure Rei knew as the Black Wolf was not like this.

But if you were to ask whether the person before him was a different person altogether… well, that wasn’t quite right either.

The kind of gaze directed at him was undoubtedly the same as the one he had received from the person he believed to be Black Wolf before.

Moreover, although the hair color had changed from black to silver and the length was different, in terms of build and facial features, there was no doubt that this person was the same as the one he had encountered before.

Given that, Rei simply couldn’t bring himself to conclude that the person standing before him was anyone other than the one from that time.

“Are you Black Wolf…?”

Rei asked this just to confirm, but Black Wolf showed no particular reaction to his words, merely staring back at him intently.

Yes, that same observant gaze as always.

Faced with such a gaze, Rei hesitated for a moment, unsure of what to do.

Normally, once he’d lured the Black Wolf out here, he would have defeated it immediately… that had been his original plan.

If the Black Wolf that appeared before Rei were the same one he’d faced before, he wouldn’t have hesitated like this.

But the opponent currently standing before Rei, while appearing to be the same one he had encountered before, was subtly different.

The moment he realized this, the thought that it would not be wise to act here crossed Rei’s mind.

Since he didn’t know what kind of power this Black Wolf—or whoever it was—possessed, he realized that if he tried to fight here, he might end up dragging the people holding the New Year’s party into this mess as well.

Above all, Rei still didn’t understand how this creature had managed to evade not only him but even Set’s sight and sense of smell.

Rather than letting it escape in the middle of a fight and risk it taking valuable intelligence back with it… Rei concluded it would be better to let it go today.

(Well, if you put it that way, I guess I shouldn’t have gotten involved in the first place.)

“……”

In stark contrast to Rei’s thoughts, the figure—presumably the Black Wolf—stared intently at him.

Just like before, a scrutinizing gaze was fixed on Rei.

That gaze alone was unmistakably that of the Black Wolf Rei knew, but even that, he could no longer be certain of.

Maybe… just maybe, there’s a possibility that “Black Wolf” isn’t actually a single person, but rather a sort of group name for several individuals.

(No, even if it is a group, would there really be someone with a face this similar?)

Rei had that thought for a moment, but upon reflection, he remembered that it was the people of this world who had created his current body.

It was Zepair, the greatest wizard, who created Rei’s body.

For that reason, Rei had always believed it would be impossible to create a body with abilities equal to his own. But upon further reflection, he realized that we are now living in a time far in the future compared to when Zepair was alive.

It wouldn’t be surprising if technology had advanced enough in the meantime to make something like this possible.

(But… Zepair is a once-in-a-lifetime genius. A body with abilities similar to this one—which was created by gathering geniuses of equal caliber under Zepair’s guidance… It feels a bit far-fetched, though. …Yeah, there’s no point in just standing here in silence.)

With that thought, Rei opened his mouth.

“Are you the one I met before?”

At Rei’s question, the figure who appeared to be the Black Wolf said nothing in particular, merely staring intently at Rei.

If even a hint of doubt had flashed across his face, it might have provided some clue.

But the Black Wolf, staring back at him like this, showed absolutely no change in expression.

Judging by his demeanor, Rei couldn’t read what the other was thinking.

(Troublesome. …Come to think of it, considering his silver hair, wouldn’t “Silver Wolf” be a better description than “Black Wolf”?)

Rei couldn’t help but think that, but he remembered hearing from Elena that the Black Wolf’s face was unknown to others.

“Just to be sure, are you definitely the assassin known as the Black Wolf?”

The moment he heard the word “Black Wolf,” the man reacted—if only for an instant.

Given that he hadn’t reacted at all until now, this reaction gave Rei a glimmer of hope.

“If you’re the assassin known as the Black Wolf, why aren’t you attacking me? You were hired to kill me, weren’t you?”

“……”

Though he asked with near certainty, the only response was silence.

Although he reacted somewhat to the name “Black Wolf,” he showed no reaction whatsoever beyond that.

Finding this annoying, the man who appeared to be Black Wolf simply continued to stare intently at him.

And so, how much time had passed?

Rei had nothing more to say, and Black Wolf simply kept staring at him.

For Rei, the sight of two men staring at each other was not one he particularly approved of, yet neither of them looked away.

From Rei’s perspective, he probably wanted to know the secret behind how he’d vanished without being detected—not just by himself, but by Set as well—while the Black Wolf simply observed Rei’s figure without changing his expression.

As they stood there silently staring at each other, Rei found himself unable to look away.

This was because he was certain that if he looked away from the Black Wolf at that moment, the Black Wolf would undoubtedly vanish again.

That was precisely why he had been staring at Rei, as if to say, “Vanish right in front of me so I can see how you do it”… but whether the Black Wolf understood Rei’s thoughts or not, he showed no sign of vanishing.

Gradually, the air around them became thick with mounting tension.

Little by little—truly, bit by bit—that tense atmosphere intensified… and when it reached its peak, a battle would likely begin, even if neither of them desired it.

Such an atmosphere—one that could easily be described as strange—spread throughout the area…

Suddenly, the door behind Rei—the door leading into the building—swing open.

In that instant, a sound like the tearing of the wind echoed around them.

At the same time, the person who had come out onto the balcony noticed Rei and hurriedly bowed.

“Huh? Ah, Lord Rei? I’m sorry, I never expected to find you in a place like this.”

A man in his forties who had stepped out onto the balcony from the door bowed his head in panic upon seeing Rei.

The man had likely come out onto the balcony to sober up from the alcohol he’d drunk at the party by letting the cold wind clear his head.

He probably apologized to Rei, thinking he was the first one there, but Rei shook his head, indicating it was no problem.

“Don’t worry about it. I was just about to head back myself. …And I’ve got my ‘haul’.”

“A find?”

The man didn’t understand what Rei was talking about, but he must have realized that Rei wasn’t upset. Looking relieved, he stepped aside to let Rei go inside.

But just as Rei was about to pass by the man, he suddenly noticed that Rei was holding his right hand with his left.

“Rei? What’s wrong with your right hand?”

“Hm? Oh. I messed up a bit.”

Perhaps because Rei said it so casually, the man didn’t think it was anything serious and simply nodded in understanding.

Perhaps out of a desire not to disturb Rei’s schedule, the man did not press the matter further.

Grateful for that, Rei entered the mansion.

The numbness in his right arm—which had blocked the attack from the man who appeared to be the Black Wolf—had almost completely subsided, though it was not yet fully recovered.

The Black Wolf’s strike was launched as if to blend in with the moonlight and the lights inside the building.

Rei blocked it with his right hand and launched a left-handed counterattack.

As a result, Rei buried his left fist into the Black Wolf’s side with that counterpunch, so it seemed he had won this opening skirmish.

(That said, a nickname like “Black Wolf”… is it even a nickname? Well, for now, let’s just call it a nickname. He certainly lived up to that nickname. To have my hand go numb from a single blow…)

Rei regularly engages in sparring matches with Elena and Vihera, and since his hand sometimes goes numb after blocking a strike during those sessions, it’s not exactly that unusual for his hand to go numb.

Still, that’s strictly because those were fights against people like Elena and Vihera; he hasn’t taken many blows from other opponents that left his hand numb.

In that sense, the Black Wolf is undoubtedly a top-tier fighter… no, he’s likely beyond even that.

Based on the information from Elena, Rei had expected as much, but naturally, the experience of fighting him directly was far more important than information heard secondhand.

At the very least, it was helpful to know for certain that he possessed the strength to hold his own against me to some extent, as I began to formulate a strategy.

(Still, I still don’t know how he vanished this time either. I really wanted to figure that out today, if possible.)

To be honest, while the Black Wolf’s high level of skill was certainly important information for Rei, it was even more crucial to understand how the Black Wolf managed to move without being detected by Rei or Set.

But the fact that he couldn’t figure that out until the very end was a major setback for Rei.

To be honest, I really wanted to figure that out during this incident.

(Is it impossible for me to figure this out on my own? In that case... should I consult Elena?)

He reasoned that since Elena, as the daughter of a duke, possessed deep knowledge on various subjects and, as a Princess General, was well-versed in combat, it wouldn’t be surprising if she had insights he himself lacked.

As I returned to the party venue with these thoughts in mind, I found the place even livelier than when Rei had left earlier.

Still, there was no one acting out of line—lying on the floor or screaming—and that was likely because the Duke of Kerebel and his family were attending this party.

No matter how much of a year-end party it was, they couldn’t afford to put on a disgraceful show in front of the master they served. So that’s how it was.

Watching everyone behave with proper restraint while still clearly enjoying themselves, Rei made his way toward the table where the Duke of Kerebel and his family were seated.

When Rei left earlier, a man who appeared to be a butler was dining with the Duke of Kerebel and his family, but perhaps that meal had ended, for now only the Duke of Kerebel and his family were eating at the table.

As Rei approaches the table, he naturally draws the attention of those around him who were enjoying the party.

That said, the nature of Rei and Elena’s relationship was already common knowledge among the staff at the Kerebel Duke’s residence, and there was no doubt that Rei was here as a guest.

Given that, no one there took issue with it.

That said, being the center of everyone’s attention wasn’t exactly a pleasant experience for Rei.

However, for better or worse, Rei was used to being the center of attention, and despite the stares, he showed no sign of being bothered as he approached the table of the Duke of Kerebel and his family.

Naturally, Elena, Liberte, Arcadia, and the others noticed Rei, but since he wasn’t approaching as a suspicious figure, they smiled at him warmly, seeming to welcome him.

Even Liberte, who usually wore a stern expression, was smiling—likely because today was a party and everyone was relaxed.

In particular, tomorrow’s New Year’s party, though attended by many nobles, is akin to a war fought without weapons.

After all, within the “Noble Faction,” everyone is maneuvering to secure as much personal gain as possible.

In that sense, perhaps today’s party is the one where one can truly enjoy oneself.

Though I felt a little guilty about interrupting the party while everyone was having such a good time...

“Elena, can I have a moment? I’d like to talk to you about something.”

And so, I called out to her.
