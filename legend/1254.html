After finishing his conversation with Min, Rei headed to a shoe store.

Since his Sleipnir boots were being stored, Rei was currently borrowing a pair of larger shoes from Azimov's house, and they were quite big.

Given that, they were inevitably difficult to walk in, and above all, there was the question of how long it would take to reinforce Sleipnir's shoes.

If it were only a day or two, Rei wouldn't have minded continuing to use Azimov's shoes. But if it stretched to a week, half a month, a month... well, that could lead to some inconveniences.

Additionally, she decided it would be wise to have an alternative pair of shoes besides Sleipnir's... which is why she came to this shoe store.

"Welcome. ...Oh? You're Rei? That's unusual."

A man in his twenties looked at Rei with a surprised expression.

...Of course. Rei mostly shopped for food in Gilm, followed by weapons, tools, magic items... that sort of thing.

At best, Rei had visited the shoe shop in Gilm only a handful of times.

And even then, it was more of a quick peek than a proper look inside.

That's precisely why the shopkeeper exclaimed in surprise upon seeing Rei now.

Normally, showing such an attitude to a customer is not desirable for a merchant.

But Rei understood perfectly well why he was being looked at like that, so he didn't seem particularly bothered as he opened his mouth.

"Yeah, actually, I'm looking for some shoes. As you can see, the ones I'm wearing now are a bit... no, quite a bit too big."

Rei stated, extending his right foot toward the clerk.

Indeed, the shoes Rei wore were quite large and clearly didn't fit his foot size at all.

"What happened? Didn't Rei have those amazing shoes?"

In this case, "amazing" referred not to the abilities of the Sleipnir shoes, but purely to their high level of craftsmanship as shoes.

In fact, the Sleipnir shoes Rei had been wearing were crafted during the era when the Zepairu clan still lived. Even now, as a merchant who deals in shoes, he could confidently say that shoes of such unparalleled craftsmanship were extremely rare.

"Ah, I left those boots with someone, so I borrowed these instead..."

"They're big,"

Rei nodded at the shoemaker's words.

Seeing Rei like that, the merchant thought it was a shame.

If he was going to handle such exceptional shoes, the merchant wished he'd entrusted them to his own shop... or more precisely, to his father, the shoemaker.

He knew those shoes were magic items, but still... even as a merchant, he couldn't help but feel that way. Or rather, precisely because he was a merchant, he cared deeply about the quality of shoes.

"Hmm, I see. Well then, could you wait just a moment? I'll go get some shoes ready for you right away."

The merchant had much he wanted to say, but he understood it wasn't the place to voice it. He headed toward the back of the shop.

Watching him go, Rei looked around the shop with admiration.

The image of a shoe shop Rei knew was largely shaped by his experiences back in Japan.

But unlike mass-produced shoes made by machines, here in Elgin, craftsmen made each pair by hand.

That's precisely why a single pair of shoes cost considerably more than they had back in Japan.

...Though even in modern Japan, if you went to a high-end shoe shop, you could get custom-made shoes handcrafted by artisans. Unfortunately, living in the countryside, Rei never had the chance to visit such a place.

"Hmm... Adventurers sometimes have light steel plates inserted into the instep or use monster leather, but I guess you're looking for something more ordinary?"

The merchant asked, "What will it be?" and Rei hesitated slightly.

Logically speaking, there was no need to buy boots designed like adventurer armor.

After all, the reason Rei was borrowing shoes from Azimov was only until the reinforcement of Sleipnir's boots was finished.

But the biggest problem was when that reinforcement would be finished.

Even for a skilled alchemist like Azimov, this was his first time using Silver Lion materials.

If that's the case, it could take until spring... or even longer.

Given that it was a high-performance magic item, that was simply unavoidable.

Indeed, the Twilight Spear, crafted over such an extended period, possessed performance that fully satisfied Rei and had become one of her primary weapons alongside the Death Scythe.

That's precisely why he understood that creating a good magic item takes time.

In other words, there was a very real possibility that even when spring arrived and adventurers were actively moving about, the enhancement of Sleipnir's Boots might still not be complete.

(Well, the shoemaker isn't going to run away or anything. If the Sleipnir Boots still aren't reinforced by spring, I could always buy them then.)

Thinking this, he concluded that since he couldn't predict what might happen ahead, having extra shoes could never be too many, and opened his mouth.

"Right. Then I'll take five pairs of regular town shoes... no, ten pairs. And I want some sturdy ones like adventurers wear. For those, I'd like ones reinforced with metal on the instep and toe. Ten pairs of those too."

"...That many?"

Rei's words must have been quite unexpected. The merchant's eyes widened as he asked again.

Clothes were the same; since they were basically handmade by artisans, each item was expensive.

Of course, quite a few people could make them themselves, but the difference in quality between an amateur's work and a craftsman's was obvious.

That's precisely why the merchant was so shocked when Rei suddenly demanded twenty pairs at once.

But, being a merchant, he quickly regained his composure.

After all, it was widely known throughout Gilm that Rei was wealthy.

...Then again, if he weren't wealthy, he couldn't afford to buy large quantities of food daily, make the high-class inn "Dusk Wheat inn" his regular lodging, or most importantly, keep Set the griffin as his familiar, which required enormous amounts of feed.

(Then again, he does have that nickname, so it's only natural, I suppose.)

Satisfied with his own reasoning, the merchant smiled and spoke.

"Alright then, I'll go get my father. Could you wait here a moment? I need to check the shape of your feet and such."

Rei nodded at his words. Not wanting to lose such a valuable customer, the merchant urged Rei to take a seat before heading to the back of the shop.

That's where the merchant's father—the man who made the shoes sold in this shop—was likely working.

Watching the merchant leave, Rei glanced around idly to pass the time.

True to being a shoe shop, numerous pairs of shoes were displayed.

But then he wondered for a moment: If they basically make custom-made shoes, what's the point of making and displaying products like this?

(Well, I guess it's fine in the sense that you can tell it's a shoe shop from the outside...)

Why not just put up a sign like the guild, the tavern, or the inn? Just as Rei was thinking this, he heard footsteps approaching from the back of the shop—the merchant from earlier and another person.

And the figure that appeared was someone so stubborn-looking, it was hard to believe he was the merchant's father.

More stubborn than intimidating.

That description seemed fitting.

If asked if he looked like a craftsman, his appearance was strangely convincing.

"You're Rei? ...Show me your foot."

Without beating around the bush, his direct words felt refreshingly straightforward to Rei.

Rei nodded, removed his right shoe, and held it out toward the man.

Looking at that foot... or more accurately, seeing how the shoe she wore didn't fit her foot at all, the craftsman frowned, his stubborn expression deepening.

Having spent his life making shoes, he clearly found it distasteful to see someone wearing footwear that didn't fit properly.

"..."

But in the end, he said nothing. He reached out and touched Rei's foot.

Then, taking a wooden stick, he measured its size.

Within ten seconds of finishing the measurement, the craftsman spoke.

"Ten pairs each of regular street shoes and those with steel plates in the instep and toe, right?"

"Yeah."

"What about the appearance of the shoes? Do you want them all different, or the same?"

"...How does that change things?"

"If you want each pair to look different, it'll take a bit longer. If they're all the same, it'll be quicker."

"Then make them the same."

Rei didn't particularly care what the shoes looked like.

Of course, if they were something that stood out in a bad way, that would be another story. But judging by the many pairs of shoes around him, he didn't think they'd make shoes that stood out in a bad way.

(Ah, I see. To show off this level of skill, having samples is definitely better.)

Rei, belatedly understanding why a custom-made shop would have so many shoes on display, was impressed. Meanwhile, the measurement for her right foot must have been finished.

Prompted by the craftsman's gaze, "Next," he offered his left foot.

In the blink of an eye, the measurement was complete. The craftsman swiftly calculated in his head how many days it would take to finish the shoes.

"Four days. Come back around then to pick them up."

"...That's... quite fast."

That's fast.

He swallowed those words.

He had assumed that custom-made items usually took much longer.

But at the same time, I suppose that's possible if the craftsman is truly skilled.

"And... hey, Seritos. Give this guy at least one pair of shoes that fit his feet. As a shoemaker, I can't stand by and let someone with feet like these wear cheap, ill-fitting shoes."

"Yeah, got it, Dad."

"Alright, I'll leave the rest to you. I'll get started on the preparations right away."

With just that, the craftsman retreated to the back of the shop.

True to his word, he was about to start making shoes for Rei.

Watching the craftsman leave... Rei suddenly voiced a phrase from their exchange that had caught his attention.

"Just these feet?"

"Huh? Oh, you mean Dad's words? Dad might look like this, but he's actually the finest shoemaker in Gilm. The reason, according to him, is that by looking at someone's feet, you can understand everything about them... or so he says. Well, I don't really get it myself."

But thanks to that ability, he can make shoes that fit a person's feet perfectly.

Hearing the merchant say that, Rei couldn't help but think, "He really is a master craftsman."

"Alright... hold on a sec. I'm not quite as good as my dad, but I am his son after all. I can make a decent assessment."

Cutting off his conversation with Rei, the merchant turned his gaze to Rei's feet.

After a dozen seconds passed, he brought over a pair of shoes displayed nearby.

Made from what appeared to be some kind of monster hide, the shoes were undoubtedly top-tier quality.

"Um, well... is this okay?"

"Yeah, it's fine. You brought us a pretty big deal this time. Think of it as a thank you. These boots, made from lizardman leather, should be fine for just walking around town, even if they're not ideal for adventuring."

Rei put on the lizardman leather shoes handed to him as the man spoke.

Probably because the shoes he'd been wearing until just now didn't fit his feet at all. These felt like they fit his feet perfectly, as if they couldn't fit any better.

"Wow... these are amazing."

"Nah, it's not that amazing. It's just that the shoes Rei was wearing before were terrible in every way."

"...Is that all? To me, it feels pretty amazing."

Though Rei said that, in terms of fitting his feet perfectly, the Sleipnir boots he'd left with Azimov were the same.

After all, since the shoes he'd been wearing until now were Azimov's and didn't fit his feet, it made sense that these newly prepared shoes would feel perfectly comfortable.

"Well, anyway, Dad's a man who keeps his promises, so he'll definitely make it on the agreed date and time."

The slightly smug smile on his face showed just how much he trusted his father's work.

Rei smiled at the father-son exchange and tested the feel of his new shoes.

They had felt perfectly snug the moment he put them on, and now, moving around a bit, they proved to have a fit that could only be described as just right.

Satisfied with the shoes, Rei paid the slightly higher price... and left the shoe store.

The snow that had been falling earlier had grown heavier, and it was certain to accumulate by tomorrow... With that thought in mind, he began walking through the snow toward the Dusk Wheat inn.
