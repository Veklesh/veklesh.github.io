After Rei and the others left, Precious's figure was once again inside the building.

But... the atmosphere now drifting within the room had changed drastically from before Rei and the others had arrived.

First, the man who had been with Precious as Slein's body double was visibly exhausted.

He hadn't done anything specific to Rei.

They had only spoken briefly... yes, truly only briefly. Yet, this was the result.

And the guards who had been stationed around Rei as this building's protectors looked as if they could barely stand.

"That's... the one with the alias..."

Who murmured that?

If it were just some random opponent, merely facing them wouldn't leave them this exhausted.

But when the opponent was Rei, the one with the nickname, it was a different story.

Given that he routinely fought high-rank monsters, the pressure and killing intent emanating from Rei demanded immense mental fortitude to endure, even for underworld figures accustomed to rough business.

"...Well? That's the man you underestimated."

Slein declared to Precious, seated in his chair.

Facing Slein, who seemed to be saying this was the price for underestimating him, Precious could only offer a wry smile.

"Honestly, it was more than I expected... but even so, what I must do remains unchanged. Could you arrange things with Mr. Tris?"

"Hold on."

Slein immediately cut in at Precious's words.

Then, seeing Precious looking at him curiously, wondering what was wrong, he realized the person before him was serious... that even after Rei appeared and warned him, he was still intent on antagonizing him.

"Are you serious? You're seriously going to make Rei your enemy and still proceed? Fine if you haven't aroused suspicion yet. But judging by the look over there just now, you're clearly under suspicion, aren't you?"

"I suppose so. After all, it was Tris who told Rei I was suspicious. ...But rest assured. Of course, I have no intention of making Rei my enemy either."

Even from Precious's perspective, Rei was a kind of abnormal person... well, that might be overstating it, but she understood he was someone who lived by a logic completely different from her own.

He had no desire to provoke such a person unnecessarily.

Besides, as things stood now, there was no particular need to make a move against Rei.

"No!"

But Slain shouted, cutting off Precious's thoughts.

What was there was fear.

Until yesterday... no, until just a few hours ago, even if he'd heard Precious was going to lay a hand on Tris, he wouldn't have opposed it this strongly.

But now it's bad news.

Because Rei had become aware of their existence.

If they foolishly antagonized Rei in this situation, he would instantly identify them as enemies to be eliminated.

And there's no way Rei would show mercy to an enemy.

If it were just a minor skirmish provoked by getting tangled up with Rei, you might get hurt, but you wouldn't have to worry about your life.

But... this time is different.

Because of what happened earlier, he's aware of our existence.

If we antagonize Rei in this situation... especially if they learn about the attack on the alchemist, they will definitely come for us.

The one called Crimson, possessing power strong enough to fight even the first squad on equal terms, would come to kill them.

In such a situation, there was absolutely no way they could handle it.

That's precisely why I shouted, cutting off Precious's words.

"We antagonized Rei. That's fine. They hadn't recognized us yet. But... not now. There's no way we can make a move in our current situation."

"...Why? We're not attacking Rei-san, are we?"

"It's not about that anymore. ...Dammit, don't you get it? The only thing we can do right now is retreat from this place immediately. Somehow avoid being spotted by Rei... No, judging from earlier, even if we were spotted now, he probably wouldn't attack."

Slein considered leaving Gilm immediately.

Originally, he planned to have Precious introduce him... and them... to their employer, then leave Gilm with the reward for this job... but he couldn't afford to get involved with someone who'd antagonized Rei and was still trying to go after Tris.

If he continued acting alongside Precious, he'd be dragged into ruin himself.

He absolutely refused to let that happen.

It was a shame to give up on Precious finding me a new job, but I absolutely couldn't accept falling into despair together like this.

Especially since, as things stood, my own subordinates might get dragged into it too.

"Srain? What are you saying? That sounds like..."

"Ah. I'm leaving Gilm. Ideally, I'd leave Gilm right now, today, even..."

"Brother, that's a bad idea for many reasons. We have commitments to other organizations."

The man who called Slain "Brother" was the one who stood before Rei as his shadow.

At the man's words, Slein scratched his head, trying to hide his irritation.

"I know. Besides, there are others who want to leave Gilm with me. And it'll take time for the ones we've sent to other places to return."

Slein had intended to leave Gilm using Precious's connections, but since there were people who, for whatever reason, wanted to leave with him, he couldn't just abandon them.

Honestly, Slain couldn't help but wonder what on earth possessed them to want to leave with him... but he couldn't abandon someone who looked up to him.

(Especially now that we've clearly become enemies with Rei. ...Though I never intended to openly oppose him in the first place.)

They knew they were being targeted by Rei because of the Azimov incident.

But even so, they had managed to mask their scent using a magic item they'd lucked into before, so he hadn't expected their presence to be discovered.

Despite that, Precious was troubled by Tris's actions and was trying to deal with that... Thinking that far, Slein shook his head.

(No, there's no way he wouldn't have considered that. After all, if he had business with us, he wouldn't need to come here himself like this. So, he really did intend to involve me more openly from the start, huh?)

He could understand that sentiment.

Precious had brought quite a few people with him, but this was still Gilm.

With Tris establishing a branch here, this place had effectively become part of Tris's sphere of influence.

Given that, it's difficult for him to gather forces within the Spier Trading Company's territory.

Of course, I could hire members from other trading houses, adventurers, or mercenaries if I wanted to.

But doing so openly would draw Tris's attention.

That's precisely why they hired people like us from the underworld.

Underworld types are less likely to be discovered than hiring adventurers.

That much was fine. It was fine... but coming openly to the slums while under Rei's suspicion was something Slain couldn't tolerate.

In other words, he was thinking, "Let's just drag him into this mess gradually."

That must have been his reasoning.

"Sorry, but I'm not going along with your suicide mission. This relationship ends here."

"...Are you sure? Cutting ties with me here means the letter of introduction we agreed on..."

"Yeah, I know."

For Precious, the letter of introduction to Slain was essentially her trump card.

It was precisely because this trump card existed that Slain had cooperated in this matter and resorted to the means of Azimov's attack.

The plan was to make it look like Azoth Trading Company and Rei were clashing, while making it appear that Spier Trading Company was involved.

The result would be Tris taking responsibility... and hopefully, she would take his place.

But the means to achieve that were slipping from her grasp.

Of course, Precious had eyes on more than just Slein's forces.

While they had established a certain presence in the slums of Gilm, they were by no means the only ones.

Several other groups possessed comparable influence and combat strength.

Yet, Precious chose to form a bond with Slain because he was the most sincere... or rather, because he valued honor above all else.

Within the slums, it was far from uncommon for people to deceive or even kill those who came seeking favors.

In that sense, Slain, who faithfully carried out the terms of his contracts, was a rare and valuable asset.

In terms of combat strength, Precious brought many people with her when she came to Gilm.

People from the orphanage inherited from her father, and others acquired through various connections.

But those people were important assets to Precious, not disposable pieces.

That was precisely why she had come to the slums seeking disposable combat power.

For Precious, Slain voluntarily leaving her side was something she had to prevent at all costs.

Deciding that a letter of introduction alone wouldn't suffice, Precious took a fist-sized leather pouch from her pocket and placed it on the table.

The sound that echoed around them was that of something striking something else.

It wasn't the sound of gold coins, but something different... yet somehow lighter.

Everyone in the room, including Slein, turned their gaze toward the table.

Peeking out from the opening of the leather pouch were gems of various colors: red, blue, green, yellow, purple...

About twenty of them, perhaps.

By any normal standard, these weren't the sort of things one would bring to the slums.

"What do you say? Would you consider staying on my payroll a little longer, with these gems as payment?"

Gifnan, one of Precious's guards—an orphanage-raised man who felt a deep sense of gratitude toward her—watched the scene unfold. Stunned, he tried to say something... but was silenced by the other guards.

The value of the gems on the table was considerable.

Why offer them to such people... That's what must have crossed Gifnan's mind.

"Well, I don't know exactly how much that gem is worth. But since I'm offering it like this, it must be of decent quality, right?"

"Yes. Of course, there are no fakes among them. They are genuine, high-quality gems. If you took them to a shop, you could probably exchange them for platinum coins... no, even light gold coins. What do you say? We'll pay you with these as your reward."

Under normal circumstances, this would undoubtedly be a reward worth accepting.

Even with some risk involved, the value of the jewel before him was worth far more than that.

...Right. That's true under normal circumstances, and if the danger were only a little.

"Sorry, I'll pass."

For Slein, it might have been different if his true identity remained unknown. But the fact that they'd penetrated this far into the building meant it was no longer just a matter of some danger.

He had shown them a decoy, but he didn't know how far that would hold up.

If things went badly... no, he had to assume they'd definitely seen through it.

In such a situation, being told to take this jewel and oppose Rei was, by Slain's standards, utterly impossible to accept.

Rather, it seemed wiser to take Precious and her guards' lives here, escape Gilm with this jewel, and head for another city.

Still, even living in the slums, Slain would never consider such a thing.

Because betraying the pride within himself like that would undoubtedly lead to regret later.

"What!? ...Are you serious!?"

Precious clearly hadn't expected him to refuse under these terms.

She just muttered, looking stunned.

"Yeah. Just know how bad the situation is right now. Sorry, but we're pulling out. Of course, since we're backing out, I won't ask for that letter of introduction. We'll leave Gilm immediately and start over somewhere else... somewhere where someone like Rei doesn't exist."

Slein's words made Precious's guards narrow their eyes.

They couldn't believe their master had been pushed this far, and yet they were still backing out.

"Let me make this clear,"

Before any of the guards could speak, Slein cut them off.

"This is the slums. ...This is our turf. If you're going to make a move, make sure you know what you're getting into."

With that warning, Gifnan and the rest of Precious's guards couldn't bring themselves to act.
