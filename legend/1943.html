After finishing his conversation with Pamidol, Rei was walking around town again with Set.

Meanwhile, Pamidol had mentioned he had a specific place to go, so they had already parted ways.

“Hmm… Hey, Set. How do you think we should lure out the guy pulling the strings behind all this?”

“Grr…”

Rei asked, but Set shook his head while growling.

Even when Rei asked, Set probably didn’t have any specific ideas.

That said, even so, Rei wouldn’t dream of blaming Set.

After all, Rei hadn’t come up with anything himself; he’d only asked Set because he had a hunch.

“Ah, yeah. Don’t worry about it. I’m not blaming you or anything. It’s just… we can’t stay like this forever, can we?”

Rei muttered as he stroked Set.

Set, being stroked, must have realized that Rei wasn’t angry. He purred happily.

While petting Set like that, Rei thought about what he should do about this whole situation.

(It would be nice if they’d just come clean, but there’s absolutely no sign of that. Whenever they try to do something, they use the red cloth. …It’s a real problem that I don’t even know how many red cloths are left. It seems Grange didn’t know either.)

To begin with, Grange was hired solely to oversee the demolition of the earthen wall and, at the same time, to have the Kobolds dispose of the Red Cloths afterward.

Given that, there’s no way Grange himself could know anything about the Red Cloths… let alone the party behind them.

He had been keeping watch on their known base, but the guards on watch ended up getting attacked by the Red Cloth.

Given how things turned out, Rei’s current impression was that there were almost no leads left.

(It seems they captured most of the Red Cloth members who launched the surprise attack, but given how things have gone so far, it’s highly unlikely they have any leads. In that case… is the safest bet really just to walk around town like this and show that I’m here? If that’s the case, then walking around town like this does have some meaning after all.)

Thinking that, he walked along the ground while looking around...

“Ah.”

Suddenly, he spotted a familiar face and stopped in his tracks.

It was a similar sequence to when Pamidor had called out to me earlier, but the difference this time was that Rei was the one who spotted him.

And since they’d just met, Rei hadn’t forgotten the other person’s face.

“Huh!?”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s gasp, the person suddenly looked in Rei’s direction and caught their breath.

The reason they didn’t try to run away was likely because they judged that Rei wouldn’t attack them without reason.

……In reality, Rei was only chasing after him because Grange—a more important figure—had fled; as for that man… one of the Red Cloths, the one who had been arguing with the man Rei had encountered earlier—the one who had complained about the earthen wall—Rei didn’t care about him, so he let him go.

In any case, Rei and Set approached the man together.

Even when he saw Rei approaching, the man showed no sign of running away; he simply waited.

Though, perhaps he simply figures that even if he runs, he’ll just get caught.

“You’re still in Gilm?”

“...No, of course I am. Leaving Gilm in this situation would be suicide. Especially alone.”

“I suppose so. But even so, isn’t being out here on the main street itself an act of suicide? You’d be in danger if you were found, too.”

In this case, “danger” doesn’t mean being spotted by the guards…

It means being in danger if the people who hired Grange find him.

This man was traveling with Grange, and he knew that Grange intended to have the Kobolds kill these men… Just based on that assumption alone, it wouldn’t be strange for the mastermind behind this plot to want to kill him, and he surely had the power to do so.

And yet, here he was, strolling leisurely down the main street. It was only natural that Rei was baffled.

“Even so, I can’t exactly go to the slums.”

It wasn’t as if this man had caused trouble in the slums as a Red Cloth, but it was a fact that another Red Cloth had done so.

Given that, if the man went to the slums and was recognized as a Red Cloth, he could very well end up with life-threatening injuries.

It was precisely because he understood this that he wasn’t making a move to go to the slums.

“It’s his own fault, isn’t it? …Still, what should we do? Why don’t you just go to the same place as Grange?”

“...With him? What on earth happened to him?”

Since they parted ways there, the man naturally had no idea where Grange was now.

That’s why he asked, but the slight fear in his expression… was it because he thought I might say Grange was in the afterlife?

Actually, considering the intensity with which Rei had chased after Grange yesterday, it wouldn’t be surprising if the man had thought that.

Still, from Rei’s words, he understood that Granger wasn’t dead and hadn’t been captured by the guards, so he asked.

“I handed him over to Gilm’s Intelligence Unit. He’s probably undergoing various kinds of training right now. …Well, maybe ‘studying’ would be a better way to put it.”

Just because it’s an Intelligence Unit operating behind the scenes for Gilm doesn’t mean they don’t have to do classroom-style training.

No, in fact, precisely because they are that kind of unit, there are many things they need to learn.

Even as an underground operation… no, precisely because they’re an underground operation, they have to be thorough in that regard.

(Come to think of it, even Egg’s subordinates—those guys who look like nothing more than thugs—are doing that kind of classroom study, aren’t they?)

As he thought that, the man talking to Rei wore an expression of utter distaste… and Rei began to wonder.

The fact that he reacted so strongly to the word “studying” suggested that the man in front of him actually knew what studying entailed.

If he had never studied at all, it seemed unlikely he would react with such intense aversion just at the mention of “studying.”

Or perhaps he’d heard from someone just how grueling, tedious, and torturous “studying” really is.

“What’s the matter? Do you hate studying that much?”

“……Yeah.”

“But is there anyone else you can rely on right now? If there is, you’re free to turn to them… but someone like that wouldn’t be strolling down the main street in a situation like this, would they? Well, maybe you did something like that because you weren’t thinking at all.”

“Guh, w-well…”

The man was at a loss for words.

In fact, until Rei pointed it out, he probably hadn’t realized just how dangerous his actions were.

Even so, perhaps because he hated studying, the man thought for a moment… and eventually spoke up.

“Then, instead of staying here, I’ll hide at that place I heard about earlier. That way, I won’t have to rely on you, and I won’t have to study either, right?”

“Well, as long as they don’t find you over there…”

"Fine by me."

Just as Rei was about to say that, he suddenly stopped.

Something had caught his attention, and as he recalled their conversation… he suddenly realized.

“Did you say ‘the place I heard about a little while ago’? Who exactly told you that?”

“Huh? No, who… ah… I can’t remember who it was.”

It was probably because Rei’s question caught him off guard. The man tried to remember who he’d heard it from, and eventually spoke up.

“It was the guy who used to bring us food and stuff. Since we couldn’t go outside and were bored, we ended up talking quite a bit with the people who brought us food. I think I just happened to hear it during one of those conversations…”

“For someone who said they were going there, that’s a pretty vague description.”

“Even if you say that, I only remembered it because of what Rei said, so what can I do?”

The man spoke with a hint of dissatisfaction, but for Rei, that was the least of his concerns.

The place the man who’d been delivering supplies to this guy had mentioned… that meant there was a high probability it was one of the mastermind’s bases behind this whole Kobold incident.

In fact, it was highly likely that this wasn’t just a disposable outpost that Grange knew about, but a true, permanent base.

(Could it be that the guy who brought the supplies intentionally leaked that information...? No, but the other side surely didn’t expect this man to be found by me and start talking like this. If that’s the case, it’s a very real possibility. So then...)

I quickly gathered my thoughts.

After all, there’s a high probability that this man is the key to breaking the deadlock I’ve been facing in this situation.

“Where is it?”

“Huh? Wait, hey. What’s with the sudden question?”

“Just tell me. If you do… well, I’ll tell the guards to hide you.”

“...Really?”

Rei asked suspiciously, taken aback by Rei’s sudden change in attitude.

It was impossible not to be suspicious. After all, Rei—who until now had been ready to completely abandon him… well, maybe not quite abandon him, but certainly had no intention of going out of his way to help him—had suddenly offered to hide him.

That said, all Rei had said was that he’d ask the guards to hide him; he had no intention of hiding the man himself.

“It’s true. That just shows how important the information you have is. …Though you don’t seem to realize it yourself.”

There were absolutely no clues leading to the person behind this incident, and Rei and the guards had completely hit a dead end in their investigation.

Given that this clue had suddenly appeared out of nowhere, letting it slip away was simply not an option.

Moreover, judging by the man’s words, there might be other pieces of information that he himself doesn’t realize are important, but which are actually crucial.

If that’s the case, Rei had no choice but to pursue such a valuable source of information.

“For now, let’s head to the security guard’s station. There, I want you to spill everything you know.”

“H-hey? W-wait a second. You’re not going to interrogate me or anything like that, are you!?”

Told to spill everything, the man naturally wonders if being “taken in” by the guards might actually mean he’s being arrested.

……Actually, since what Rei has in mind is keeping him safe by locking him up in a cell or somewhere, that might not be entirely wrong.

“That’s not my intention, but to protect you, it’s best to keep you somewhere secure. In that sense, a prison cell is actually a pretty good option. …At the very least, since there are guards there, you don’t have to worry about their people getting to the cell that easily.”

“That’s true, but… won’t I be able to walk around freely outside?”

“Of course not.”

Hearing the man’s words, Rei looked at him as if to say, “What are you talking about?”

“If you’re wandering around freely like you are now, it’s definitely a golden opportunity for anyone targeting you. Or do you think you can handle an attacker who blends into the crowd? If you had the ability to sense a killer’s intent, you might be able to manage it…”

“That’s impossible,” Rei implied.

The man had his own thoughts about Rei’s attitude, but since he couldn’t actually sense things like killing intent or auras, he couldn’t say anything.

If he had that kind of ability, he’d be somewhat safe even in a crowd… but since he couldn’t, going out into the city wasn’t an option for him right now.

For a split second, he considered refusing to ask Rei for protection just to save face, but judging by Rei’s words and actions, he knew that if he did that, he would undoubtedly die.

“Ah.”

“Then, there’s no problem with me going to the guard post, right?”

“Please do. Just so you know, though, don’t get mad if there’s nothing at the place I mentioned earlier, okay? After all, it was just something that came up in casual conversation.”

It would be unbearable to be told that the information was useless just as I was about to be protected by the guards, and then be unceremoniously thrown out.

With that in mind, Rei nodded in agreement to the man’s words.

“That shouldn’t be a problem. The guards have a strong sense of responsibility, after all. Even if you don’t know anything, I think they’ll be fine with just protecting you for the time being.”

Hearing Rei’s words, the man finally agreed to go to the guards’ station.
