"What do you think of those stairs over there?"

Rei and the others stuffed all the long-lasting food they could find—like the dried meat and fruit in the warehouse—into their Misty Ring pouches, then continued down the underground facility's passageway.

As Rei and the others advanced through the underground facility's passageway, a descending staircase soon came into view ahead.

Of course, appearing suddenly in this situation offered no guarantee it was genuine.

Still... it was only natural to assume it led down without being a trap or anything.

"Since there's no other staircase or anything like that leading down, I think it's probably safe. Ah. Of course, if you ask whether there might be other staircases, I suppose there could be something like a hidden staircase, not a hidden passageway."

As he spoke, Rei recalled the information he'd extracted from the man.

On the second basement level, there was a prostitute pregnant with a giant. And on the third basement level, there was a facility for giving birth to giants.

If that was true, he wanted to get to that facility sooner rather than later.

(Sentimentality... huh)

With the power he possessed, he couldn't save the prostitute carrying the giant.

Even knowing that, a strong sense of dissatisfaction welled up inside Rei.

They were forcibly taken from their villages and towns, had their very wills altered by slave collars, forced into prostitution, became pregnant with giants, and would die after giving birth.

Rei understood he was no hero of justice.

His past actions proved that.

He'd severed the limbs of hostile nobles, attacked bandits instead of being attacked, and seized their hoarded treasures... It was obvious to anyone that he couldn't possibly be called a champion of justice.

Even so... the intense rage welling up inside Rei was unstoppable.

"If there is a hidden staircase, we'd need to search every other room too... but that would definitely take time, right?"

Marina's words were absolutely correct.

A hidden staircase wouldn't be in an obvious place, and given that, it was only natural that finding it would take time.

Moreover, Byune, the expert in such matters, was a thief specialized in combat.

Whether she could actually find a hidden staircase... well, that was far from certain.

Given that they understood they were launching a surprise attack on this underground facility, it was only natural they didn't want to waste too much time here.

"Hmm."

Perhaps because Byune properly understood her own capabilities, she didn't object to Marina's words.

Though still young, Byune's ability to make such precise judgments is proof of her high level of discernment.

It wasn't for nothing that she'd been tackling dungeons alone since she was even smaller... no, since she was a child.

Only Vihera, who had known her for a long time, noticed the fleeting look of frustration that flashed across Byune's face.

"Then, I suppose we have no choice but to proceed this way. ...Let's go."

Everyone nodded at Rei's words and descended the stairs.

The stairs were quite long, likely to prevent the floor of the underground facility from collapsing.

They descended halfway down the straight staircase without incident... until suddenly, the steps vanished, giving way to a mere slope.

Had they just started descending, they could have easily returned to the first basement level.

But Rei and the others had already descended halfway down the stairs.

Even if they were wary of possible traps, the sudden disappearance of the steps and the transition to a slope was completely unexpected.

The second basement level was still nearly ten meters below.

To prevent the underground facility from collapsing, each basement level had to be built exceptionally thick. Naturally, this meant the staircases leading down to the lower levels also became longer.

Jaya intended to exploit this, sending Rei and the others tumbling down to the second basement level to inflict as much damage as possible. Ideally, he wanted this trap to kill them.

But... that plan was nothing short of naive.

(He triggered the trap as if timing it perfectly for when we reached the middle of the stairs. So, could the one who activated this trap actually see us? Surveillance cameras... no, something like that, a magic item or something similar, wouldn't be too far-fetched.)

The moment the path turned into a slope, Rei had half-instinctively leaped off the stairs.

...Under normal circumstances, jumping from a ten-meter height would result in at least some injury. A failed landing could even be fatal.

But that was assuming normal circumstances. Classifying Rei as normal was, in many ways, a mistake.

He'd been descending the stairs suspecting a trap all along, so there was no hesitation in his actions when the moment came.

Activating the Sleipnir Boots mid-air, he used the air itself as footholds, stepping down to the basement level.

Because he had anticipated the trap, Rei showed no sign of confusion whatsoever. In fact, he even had the composure to survey his surroundings.

Elena activated Sleipnir Boots just like Rei, transforming the air into footholds as she descended toward the second basement level.

Marina, aided by the wind spirit, glided down to the second basement level at a speed that could only be called a glide.

Vihera grabbed Byune with one hand, adjusting her posture mid-air as she descended.

(Vihera was simply falling, but judging by her demeanor, it seemed fine.)

Vihera already possessed exceptionally high physical abilities, but now, having absorbed Ambris, she possessed even greater physical prowess.

Moreover, as Vihera, whose weapon was combat itself, moving her body was something she excelled at.

Falling from this height posed absolutely no problem for him.

For Yellow, who possessed wings, this height was utterly insignificant.

As a result, everyone successfully lands on the second basement floor without any apparent injuries.

"I never expected the stairs to be booby-trapped like this. That was a bit surprising,"

Having arrived first on the second basement floor during the free fall, Vihera muttered while lowering Byune.

Hearing that, Rei also looked back at the stairs. There, the gap that had vanished was already restored, and it had once again become something worthy of being called stairs.

"Yeah... Well, at least no one got hurt in the end. Judging by the state of these stairs, it was a pretty elaborate trap... Must be frustrating, huh?"

Though Rei said it would be frustrating, a smile played on his lips.

Was he laughing at the shallow wisdom of the Jaya people who thought they could kill them with something this simple? Or was he imagining his opponents, frustrated that despite all the effort put into such an elaborate trap, they hadn't even managed to scratch Rei and his companions, let alone kill them?

Either way, a smile played on Rei's lips.

"...Rei, that smile..."

Marina, who had been watching Rei, spoke up, sounding somewhat hesitant.

"Hm? Is something wrong?"

"No, if it's fine with you, Rei, then I don't mind. You're you, after all."

In the end, Marina couldn't bring herself to say anything more and looked around again.

She'd heard upstairs that the second basement level housed pregnant prostitutes, but even to Marina's eyes, it didn't seem much different from the first basement level.

Well, there were corridors and doors, just like the first basement level, but the number of doors was extremely low, and they were all large double doors. That was a significant difference.

As she looked at those doors, it was Vihera who spoke first.

"What do we do? Should we try opening that door first? It probably has a trap or something."

"...Well, they are that big, after all."

Elena responded to Vihera's words, and Rei and the others agreed with their opinion.

(About two meters wide... no, maybe three? The height is about the same too. ...Why such a huge door... giants?)

If this was the place where pregnant prostitutes were gathered to give birth to giants, then perhaps due to some kind of trouble, a giant could be born inside that room.

Could that be the reason? That's what he thought.

Of course, even Rei didn't know if a newborn giant would possess a body worthy of being called a giant from the start.

But if they were going to such lengths to make them give birth to giants, it wouldn't be surprising if such an irregularity occurred.

"What should we do? Should we peek inside? There's no guarantee we'll find anything useful, though."

"Yeah. ...I'm not really keen on it, though."

Marina replied to Rei's suggestion.

The reluctance was likely shared by the others as well.

Still, they decided it would be best to see what lay beyond that door and moved up to the massive entrance.

"Byune, what do you think?"

"Hmm."

Vihera asked if it was locked, but Byune murmured softly as usual.

"Looks like it's not locked."

"...Not locked? Why? Every door on the first basement level was locked. Jaya should know we're already in this underground facility."

"Otherwise, she wouldn't have gone so far as to set the trap on the stairs."

Elena murmurs in agreement with Rei's words, but precisely because she understands that, it's only natural to wonder why this door isn't locked.

"Maybe he doesn't mind us seeing it? Anyway, if it's unlocked, shouldn't we just open the door?"

"...Yeah. Byune, move aside."

"Mm."

At Marina's prompting, Rei moved Byune aside from the doorway and reached out to grasp the door.

Given the door's dimensions of three meters by three meters, its weight was considerable.

Normally, it would take several people working together to open it... but for Rei, it was only a weight he could handle without issue.

(Could it be that they didn't lock it or post a guard because they thought this door would be fine?)

The door was heavy enough to make him wonder that.

Given that a pregnant woman was inside, it seemed only natural to assume this door would never be opened.

The sound of metal scraping echoed around them as the door slowly began to open.

The scent wafting through the opening was one Rei had never smelled before.

It wasn't an unpleasant smell, but rather something relaxing.

Breathing in that scent, he finally pushed the door open completely.

What met Rei's eyes as he stepped inside was a crowd of pregnant women—dozens, perhaps even over a hundred.

And yet, the scene inside the room was almost worthy of being called silence.

Of course, it wasn't truly silent.

The pregnant women on the beds all seemed unconscious, emitting faint moans, and some were even murmuring in their sleep.

"What... what is going on here?"

Vihera, who had followed Rei into the room, murmured as she scanned the scene.

They were asleep... or perhaps just unconscious, but regardless, the pregnant women lying on the beds looked abnormal to anyone who saw them.

"They're not just sleeping because they're tired... surely not. I can't imagine all this many people sleeping at the same time."

"Really? I see what you mean, Vihera, but everyone here is a prostitute. Meaning they lived in Mejogo, right? If that's the case, it's possible they're just sleeping normally at this hour."

Marina was right; it was past noon.

So it was clearly the time when prostitutes would normally be sleeping, and thinking that way, it wasn't strange for all of them to be asleep.

"But all of them? And despite us coming in, none of them show any sign of waking up. That door opening was pretty loud, after all."

At those words, everyone fell silent.

Indeed, the sound Rei made opening the door had been quite shrill.

It was the kind of noise that would definitely wake them under normal circumstances.

And yet, inside the room Rei and the others were looking at, no one showed any sign of waking up.

It wasn't strange to wonder what the hell was going on.

"So, if we think about it normally, maybe they're being put to sleep?"

"For what purpose?"

The words slipped out of Rei's mouth, but Vihera asked while looking at the many pregnant women in the room.

But even Rei, who had spoken, hadn't said it with any particular conviction.

It was simply a question born from a basic suspicion.

In the end, he couldn't answer Vihera's question. He could only stare blankly at the scene before him.
