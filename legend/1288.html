A winter night garden—a place you wouldn't normally think of for a party.

Yet, thanks to Marina's spirit magic and the heat from the kiln prepared by Rei, the garden was warm enough to feel almost like spring, if not quite summer.

And yet, the snow remained completely untouched, even around the kiln. It was either a blatant waste of spirit magic... or a testament to Marina's truly exceptional skill with it.

At first glance, it looked like a wasteful display of spirit magic and magic items.

Yet, the other residents of the Noble Quarter didn't give them strange looks. This was partly because the mansion wasn't in a conspicuous location within the Noble Quarter, but more importantly, because the residents knew this mansion belonged to Marina.

Considering Marina's beauty and her status as Guildmaster, it wouldn't be surprising if some sought to curry favor with her.

But on this particular occasion, no such individuals made an appearance.

...Was it because Set was present at the party? Or because Marina had done something with her spirit magic? Only she herself knew the answer.

"Since Rei's the party leader, I really think the party name should have something like 'Flame' in it, don't you?"

"I never said I accepted being party leader, though."

With the pizza ingredients already cleared away and only a few pizzas left on the table, Rei set down the tea he'd been drinking and muttered discontentedly at Vihera's words.

"Give it up. This party was formed around Rei in the first place."

"Heh heh, that's true. From my perspective, I don't see anyone else who could lead this group besides Rei, do you?"

At Marina's words, Elena, reflected in the Paired Orb placed on the table, nodded in agreement.

Elena had initially been busy with her own family gathering, but now that it had finally settled down, she was able to join Rei and the others' New Year's Eve party—albeit through the Paired Orb.

Still, being separated by the Paired Orb meant she couldn't interact with Rei directly, which was a disappointment for Elena too.

"Even so. If I were to be the party leader, problems would definitely pile up, right?"

"...Well, it is Rei, after all."

While Marina murmured Rei's words with deep feeling, Vihera wore a delighted smile.

For Vihera, who relished combat, trouble brewing and someone becoming a target was far from a negative.

In fact, it was practically a plus for Vihera.

Of course, the opponents who showed up in such situations weren't always strong; many were just loudmouths who looked tough but were weak.

"Setting aside the party leader issue for now..."

Rei, judging that things weren't going his way, decided to deflect the conversation for now.

...Though everyone listening understood exactly what Rei meant and didn't argue.

"In our party, the most noticeable member is definitely Set, right? So I think we should have a party name that makes Set stand out."

"No objections there."

Party names often reflect the party's characteristics.

As far as Rei knew, Elk's party, Axe of the Thunder God, was a prime example.

It was Elk's weapon, his alias, and his party name.

Hear the name Axe of the Thunder God, and anyone would think of Elk and his party.

Of course, that's not always the case. There are patterns like Milein's party name, Scorching Wind.

Though that name was also chosen to signify their fierce attacks, much like Milein's swordsmanship, Sulunin's magic, and Ekril's archery.

"Set's name, huh... Milein or Johanna might come up with something good."

"Stop it."

Vihera immediately put a stop to Rei's words.

It was a warning born from knowing just how much Milein and Johanna doted on... no, adored Set.

The thought flashed through Vihera's mind: if those two were to name the party, they'd probably come up with something like 'Team We Love Set-chan', 'Set-chan is the Best', or 'Set-chan is Above All Else'.

After a moment's thought, Rei understood this too... and ultimately, he could only nod in agreement with Vihera's words.

"Hmm, but a party name... I'm stuck."

Putting aside thoughts of Milein and Johanna for now, they turned their attention back to their own party name.

But Rei wasn't exactly confident in his naming skills to begin with.

Honestly, the only names that came to mind were the kind Milein or Johanna would come up with.

And when it came to a name that clearly reflected their characteristics or goals... well, almost nothing came to mind.

"Being able to agonize over something like that... I envy you."

"Oh? What do you mean?"

Marina asked back at the words coming from the Paired Orb.

"I don't mean to mock you. It's just... those moments when you're thinking up names like that are probably the most fun, right?"

"Well, I can't deny that."

You sometimes hear people say that preparing for a festival is more fun than the festival itself...

If someone said they were doing the same thing now, Marina wouldn't deny it either.

Besides, the fact that Elena's face reflected in the Paired Orb truly seemed to look at them with envy probably played a big part too.

"What are you talking about? Elena will be joining this party eventually, so we need her to help us come up with names too."

"...Huh?"

That's precisely why, when Rei said that, the Elena reflected in the Paired Orb wore a look of utter surprise.

She froze for a few seconds... then snapped back to reality and hurriedly opened her mouth.

"But... Rei, you know I could never be an adventurer, right?"

Those words were undoubtedly true.

Elena was a symbolic figure within the Noble Faction, one of the three major factions of the Mireana Kingdom.

There was absolutely no way she could function as an adventurer.

That was precisely why she envied Rei and the others talking about their party... or rather, she was almost grateful that she wasn't physically present, that their exchange was happening through the Paired Orbs.

She was certain that if she had been physically present at the party held at Marina's mansion, she would have acted out of character.

That's precisely why the words that came out of Rei's mouth took her completely by surprise... The expression on Elena's face right now wasn't that of a duke's daughter or a princess general... It was the face of a woman, filled with astonishment, standing before the man she loved.

The only one who could make Elena look like that was Rei.

Not even her father, Duke Kerebel, could ever see Elena look like that.

(I wonder if he realizes it... or not...)

Suppressing a smile, Marina pondered as she watched Elena reflected in the Paired Orb.

But she would never voice it aloud.

Because if she did, those very words would come right back at her.

This applied not only to Marina but to Vihera as well. The only one present who did not love Rei—though she did harbor affection for him—was Byune. She let the conversation pass her by, focusing instead on savoring the dried fruit on the table.

Amidst those harboring various emotions, Rei nodded to Elena.

"Yeah. Elena as she is now probably can't. But... it won't last forever, right? Thanks to the succession ritual, Elena has become closer to me. So even if it's impossible right now, she might eventually be freed from her current position."

Then she can join our party.

Hearing Rei's words, Elena fell silent for a moment, unsure what she'd just heard... but burst out laughing the next instant.

'Pfft, kuku... hahahaha. Right, that's true. Yeah. I hope that happens.'

Elena couldn't suppress the urge to laugh.

This smile, too, was something those who knew Elena normally would never see.

Any man from the Noble Faction... no, any man who knew Elena would envy this sight.

But Rei, completely unaware of his own good fortune, opened his mouth with a displeased expression at Elena, who was laughing across the Paired Orbs, her golden hair—like the very shape of sunlight itself—tossed wildly.

"What's with the laugh? There's no need for that, right? I'm just being straightforward with my invitation."

'Heh heh, ah, ah. Sorry. But... well, Rei's words are appreciated, and I'm happy. That's for sure.'

Elena, reflected in the Paired Orb, laughed happily... truly happily.

"Anyway, about the party name... Hmm, nothing comes to mind right away."

Rei decided that if he kept talking with Elena like this, they'd get nowhere, so he forcefully steered the conversation back to the party name... but even then, nothing good came up.

A few suggestions did come up, but none of them felt quite right.

Moreover, even while discussing the party name, the conversation didn't stay on that topic alone.

It jumped around, hopping from one thing to another.

Then again, they hadn't gathered just to decide on a party name, so perhaps it was fitting for a New Year's Eve party.

'Kyuu! Kyu-kyuu!'

"Gurururuu"

Once again, Yellow had come to Elena's side, chatting with Set across the Paired Orb with an adorableness that was hard to believe came from a black dragon.

"But I've been wondering for a while now—Yellow and Set are completely different species, right? So why can they understand each other?"

Vihera murmured curiously as she sipped her slightly cooled tea.

The gaze was directed at... the one being asked was Rei, but even Rei couldn't answer that question.

From their very first meeting, Set had already been able to converse with Yellow.

That was simply how it was, in Rei's mind.

"I wonder. Well, I have a few theories... but they're just assumptions piled on assumptions, pure speculation really."

Muttering, Rei turned his gaze toward Yellow.

Yellow was created through dragon language magic... or more precisely, through the Inheritance Ceremony, a ritual performed by Elena, who had inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone.

And Set was a being brought into existence by magic only the Zepairu clan could wield, known as Beast Magic.

There seemed to be some vague commonality between the two, but even that couldn't be said for certain.

"...Oh dear, it'll be midnight soon. Rei, you're going to eat udon, right?"

Rei, who had been pondering the similarities between Beast Magic and Dragon Language Magic, snapped back to reality at Marina's voice.

"Ah, right. ...Sorry about that, Elena."

"Hm? Ah, no need to worry. Lately, more shops around here have started serving udon. I've made arrangements with them, so it's all set. ...I would have preferred the same udon as Rei and the others, though."

"Udon from the Kerebel Duchy? That's interesting in its own way. It probably varies quite a bit by region."

Udon spread from Gilm, and fried udon already exists, along with various types of broth.

(If it catches on in the port town, it'll probably become seafood udon instead of seafood ramen, I suppose.)

That's what Rei thought, but perhaps because he was Japanese, he preferred to have soba noodles rather than udon for New Year's Eve.

...Not that he was particularly attached to rice.

"Anyway, if Elena's got the udon ready, that's perfect timing. Then, shall we all eat udon together? It's about time the date changes."

At Rei's words, Elena nodded on the other side of the Paired Orb and rang the bell.

Almost immediately, there was a knock at the door, and the maid appeared.

"Miss, did you call?"

"Yes. The udon should be ready in the kitchen. Please bring it here."

The maid nodded at Elena's words and left.

Within minutes, the udon was brought in.

"Well then, I'll serve this too."

Seeing Elena holding the bowl of udon, Rei began serving udon from the Misty Ring.

The Paired Orb could only transmit images; conveying scent to the other party was impossible.

But... even so, to Elena, the authentic udon looked many times more delicious than the udon in her own hands.

Though she looked a little envious of that, once everyone had received their udon, the year ended... and the new year began.

"Happy New Year,"

With that greeting, they began eating their udon.
