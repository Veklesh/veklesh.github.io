The man called an intruder by Rei tilted his head, looking as if he didn't understand at first.

Despite being woken up by being hit, he probably wasn't fully awake yet.

He stared intently at Rei again... and then, finally grasping his situation, opened and closed his mouth repeatedly.

"I... I, intruder!? Why!? Why the hell is an intruder even here!?"

"Well, what do you expect? I just walked right in, didn't I?"

Rei said this, but in reality, he'd fought off the gatekeepers guarding the entrance to get here, so it was hardly a normal entry.

"N-normal...?"

"That's enough."

The man tried to say something else, but Rei cut him off.

He hadn't done anything.

He'd merely said "That's enough"... and the researcher found himself unable to say anything more.

Had the man been an adventurer, seasoned by countless violent encounters, he would have recognized the killing intent emanating from Rei.

Unfortunately, though the man belonged to the dark organization known as Jaya, he was, at his core, nothing more than a researcher.

That's precisely why he had no idea what Rei had done to him, and yet he couldn't speak any further.

"We're intruders. Meaning, we want information about this place. You understand that, right?"

The intensity with which Rei asked this question made it feel as if a giant stood before him... no, even compared to the giants the man knew, Rei's presence was overwhelmingly greater.

Purely in terms of size, Rei was vastly smaller than any giant.

Regardless, the man seemed to grasp that defying the figure before him would be the worst possible move. He said nothing more, merely nodding repeatedly.

Seeing this, Rei nodded with satisfaction before speaking.

"Now then, the first thing I want to ask is, there are giants in this underground facility, right?"

It was a fundamental question.

If he had denied it, Ray would have judged him as uncooperative.

But the man, who appeared to be a researcher, nodded in response to Rei's words.

"Ah, yes. Of course I know. Though, to be honest, we still don't fully understand exactly how they're made."

"...I've heard it's the effect of those slave collars?"

"That's certainly part of it. But the actual mechanism of how the giants are born remains unknown."

"Huh? You developed it as a magic item, right? Or was it just a magic item created by accident?"

Given that the man Rei and the others had brought along as a guide was ultimately the one tasked with the physical labor, it wasn't surprising he wasn't knowledgeable about magic items.

But the man standing before Rei now, judging by the room's appearance, clearly looked like a researcher or an alchemist.

(Could I have been mistaken?)

Just because he looked the part didn't mean he was actually a researcher or alchemist.

There was a slight possibility he was someone completely unrelated.

"Just to be sure, you are a researcher or alchemist affiliated with Jaya... correct?"

"Yes, of course."

The man immediately affirmed Rei's words.

For a split second—just a split second—Rei wondered if maybe he'd been left in charge of cleaning this room, gotten tired, and fallen asleep. But that thought was instantly negated by the man's words.

"...Then let me ask again. Why don't you understand why the giant is born from the effect of that slave collar?"

"Because neither that slave collar nor the black crystal at its core were things we made. They were things we were given."

The man answered simply, showing no sign of hesitation or difficulty.

Rei and the others realized his words contained information they didn't know and voiced their confusion.

"Black crystal?"

"Yeah. That thing you call the slave collar. But strictly speaking, it isn't a slave collar. It has similar effects, though."

The words that came out of the man's mouth were completely unexpected.

Yet, while unexpected, they were also words that made perfect sense.

To begin with, the slave collars used in Mejogo were strange in many ways.

A normal slave collar could force the wearer to act against their will, but it couldn't alter their will itself.

But the slave collars used in Mejogo actually altered the wearer's will itself.

That meant even a woman who was normally fastidious about relationships between men and women would willingly work as a prostitute... and so on.

And according to Rei's prediction—something she was half-convinced of herself—that very slave collar was supposed to be the condition necessary for giving birth to a giant.

"Is giving birth to a giant one of its effects?"

"Yes. Though to be precise, merely wearing that slave collar doesn't make one capable of bearing a giant. It's only when someone wearing the collar becomes pregnant within this Mejogo, where the black crystal exerts its magical influence, that they can actually give birth to a giant."

The man's tone held a hint of regret as he spoke.

"So, what exactly is this black crystal? Is it literally just black crystal?"

"No, we don't know its exact name. We call it black crystal because of its appearance, but it's not actually crystal."

He was forcing women into prostitution and even making them bear giants, yet he showed no sign of guilt whatsoever.

This caused everyone except Rei's mood to sour rapidly, though the man failed to notice.

Realizing things were heading in a bad direction, Rei cut to the chase.

"...I should have asked this question first. That black crystal and the slave collar. You didn't make those yourselves, did you?"

"That's right. I'm just researching the black crystal."

"Then who made that black crystal? Or where did you find it?"

If the black crystal was some kind of special magical ore excavated from somewhere, there wouldn't be much to worry about.

But if someone actually made the black crystal... that would undoubtedly be dangerous in many ways.

Because once it was made, it meant there was a possibility they could make the same thing again.

Rei asked with that thought in mind, but the man answered by shaking his head.

"I don't know. Originally, I was researching magical minerals in other countries... famous ones like fire crystals and such. I was brought here specifically to study the black crystal."

"...Brought here? Are you saying you were forced to conduct research here?"

Hearing the man's words, Rei wondered if the man before him had also been brought here like the prostitutes and forced into research. But once again, the man shook his head.

"Of course, when I was brought here, it was against my will. But staying here now and doing research? That's purely because I want to. That black crystal... no matter how much I study it, I learn almost nothing."

The man paused there, then shook his head dramatically the next instant.

"That black crystal! It's not exactly a magic item, so why does it create such a powerful field? It's driving me crazy, I just can't stop wondering! Honestly, it doesn't look like it has any special crafting or anything, does it? Yet..."

He continued, pouring out question after question about the black crystal.

From his demeanor, it was impossible to believe he was conducting research in this underground facility against his will, as he himself had claimed.

It was obvious to anyone watching that he was here of his own volition.

(Being kidnapped, then developing sympathy and becoming close with the kidnapper while working together... What was that called? Stockholm syndrome? Surely it couldn't be that?)

That's what Rei thought based on his knowledge from Japan, but the person before him didn't seem to be in that kind of situation.

Then again, Rei hadn't actually seen anyone with Stockholm syndrome firsthand. She'd only encountered it through stories in anime, manga, novels, movies, and dramas that used it as a theme.

That's precisely why, even if the man before him truly had Stockholm syndrome, he couldn't be sure if it was real or not.

Even if he did have Stockholm Syndrome, the fact remained that this man had been cooperating with Jaya. And now, he was happily explaining the Black Crystal to Rei.

At that point, Rei understood that this man had always been this kind of person, someone who believed sacrificing however many people was acceptable for the sake of research.

He might not be conscious of sacrificing many people, but even if he wasn't directly sacrificing them, the fact that he was cooperating with Jaya on research meant that was inevitably the result.

"I see. I understand now. So you're... our enemy, then?"

"No way! That couldn't be true!? I'm just a researcher. Who you are or where you're from doesn't concern me. Well, I do appreciate you waking me up, though. My research is getting pretty busy right now. If my hypothesis is correct..."

"Yes, that's enough."

The man, cut off mid-sentence, shot Rei a displeased look, clearly wanting to say more.

But Rei, receiving that look, silently gestured for the man to look at Elena and the others standing nearby.

What the man saw there were Elena and the others, their faces clearly displeased.

Precisely because of their refined features, the man could clearly discern the frustration mixed within the glances directed at him.

Faced with stares that could only be described as absolute zero, even the man couldn't continue explaining his research with any further smugness.

"Um, why am I getting those looks? I don't think I've done anything particularly wrong..."

The man genuinely couldn't understand why he was being looked at like that.

He probably understood that things would turn out badly if this continued.

He looked at Rei with a confused expression, yet one that also seemed to plead for help.

Receiving that look, Rei's first action was to calm Elena and the others down.

Though, this wasn't out of concern for the man himself. The real reason was that if Elena and the others acted on their emotions and harmed him, they wouldn't get the information they wanted.

...It wasn't entirely wrong to say she was concerned for his safety, since she had stopped them precisely to avoid him getting injured or rendered unable to speak, which would have hindered obtaining the information.

"Elena, Marina, Vihera, stop right there."

That's precisely why Rei told those three to calm their anger.

However, Rei's mistake at that moment was telling the three, who were radiating intense anger, to calm down... and saying nothing about the person standing behind them. As a result...

"Whoa! Ow! That hurts!"

As if seizing the moment while Rei was telling the three to calm down, a long needle flew through the air.

While Rei had been trying to calm Elena and the others, he was naturally also on guard to prevent the man who was the source of the information from escaping.

But... no, precisely because of that, he had completely forgotten about the last person present: Byune.

The man screamed upon seeing the long needle piercing his arm.

Was he truly feeling pain, or was he merely screaming in pain from the sight before him despite feeling none? Even Rei couldn't tell.

The only thing clear was that, even if she didn't show it on her face, Byune was just as furious as the other three.

Though still young enough to be called a girl, Byune was a woman in her own right.

Precisely because of that, she naturally felt a strong revulsion toward what was happening in this Mejogo.

The reason Rei hadn't noticed it before was simply because Byune never showed her emotions on her face.

That was precisely why Rei hadn't grasped just how furious Byune was.

...Or perhaps he still saw Byune as a child and hadn't thought to consider it.

In any case, Rei only stopped Elena, Marina, and Vihera.

Realizing she hadn't been stopped, Byune seized the opportunity and hurled her signature weapon—a long needle—at the man.

Still, the fact she didn't bring out the White Cloud, forged from Silver Lion material, suggested she hadn't completely lost herself to rage.

The man was lucky to have merely been wounded, but he certainly had no intention of thanking her for that while screaming in agony.
