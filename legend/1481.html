Silence. The tavern fell silent.

At Ilse's words, the patrons in the tavern watched the situation unfold in silence.

Rei, present at the scene, watched the conversation unfold without interjecting, just like the other patrons.

"Why... why can't you understand? Revenge is meaningless. You know that, don't you?"

"Well, yes, it might be pointless. But who decides that? It certainly isn't you, Melan. Ultimately, that decision is mine alone."

"That's exactly why! Why would you say something like that?! Human life isn't something you can just take away like that!"

"...That's strange. Since you're an adventurer too, Melan, I doubt you've never killed anyone, right? Especially in places like Gilm aside, there are plenty of bandits elsewhere."

"That's different. Bandits are a breed that causes trouble for others. I think killing them is unavoidable."

"I think my enemy fits that description too, causing trouble for others. After all, my family was killed."

Ilse couldn't comprehend the thoughts of the person before her.

He says killing your enemy is wrong, yet claims it's acceptable to kill thieves because they harm others.

Melan's words felt nothing but contradictory.

But Melan probably couldn't grasp Ilse's confusion.

He stared at Ilse, his expression still displeased... Then, as if suddenly noticing something, Melan's gaze shifted toward Rei.

"You... Did you perhaps persuade Ilse? Otherwise, she wouldn't suddenly start saying things like this."

"No, I didn't."

Before Rei could deny Melan's accusation, Ilse stated it as if it were obvious.

"Rei is helping me. But that's only because I asked him to. At the very least, it's not like I'm being deceived by Rei or anything like that."

"That... might just be what Ilse believes, right? Rei uses powerful magic. It's possible he's using magic on Ilse without her even knowing."

"Please stop insulting Rei. I asked him personally to help me."

"But! Because of Rei, Ilse was able to stop pursuing meaningless acts of revenge..."

...was able to even consider doing.

Melan tried to say that, but couldn't finish the sentence.

Ilse slammed her fist on the table, cutting Melan off.

The force and intensity were far greater than when Melan had struck the table earlier, producing a sound that seemed to reverberate throughout the entire tavern.

It was a sound unimaginable coming from Ilse, whose physical abilities were several levels below Melan's.

"Shut up!"

It was a shout—no, a roar—that was a world apart from Ilse's usual tone.

Melan, struck by the impression that Ilse's green hair had instantly flared out, gasped and stopped speaking.

"To you, revenge might seem like a trivial act. But do you understand the agony of having an innocent family torn from you by another's malice? If you wish, I could slaughter your family, friends, and lover right now. If even then you still claim revenge is meaningless, that it only leads to emptiness, then I'll accept your words."

Ilse's expression as she spoke was unmistakably serious.

If Melan continued to deny her revenge... to reject her actions, Ilse would use any means necessary to kill everyone close to Melan.

Even if Ilse's power wasn't enough, there were countless ways for one person to kill another.

She wouldn't even need to challenge them head-on.

"...What?! What are you saying, Ilse?! Why would it come to that?!"

Melan, whose thoughts had been frozen by the extreme, abnormal nature of Ilse's words, snapped back to reality and cried out at her statement.

It was only natural. She'd been told to kill those close to her.

"Why are you so shocked? Revenge is meaningless, isn't it? Then I think it's fine for you to show me that with your own life."

Ilse's tone toward Melan now held no trace of the warmth or familiarity of friendship.

Cold... like she was looking at nothing more than a stone by the roadside.

But Melan, completely oblivious to Ilse's gaze, opened his mouth.

"That's absurd!"

"But even if someone did something that absurd to you, Melan wouldn't seek revenge, right? Then it's fine."

"Don't be ridiculous! The fundamental premise is different! Don't lump someone who's already dead in with someone who's still alive!"

"Oh? But you won't put yourself in the same position? You just intend to condemn my actions based on your own flimsy sense of justice?"

"Shallow!?"

Melan was speechless, clearly not expecting such a bold accusation.

Ilse fired another verbal arrow at the stunned Melan.

"I can't see it any other way. Your words, Melan, are shallow and utterly lacking in conviction. And while saying you're following your heart might sound noble, don't try to force what you believe is right onto others. Your shallow words don't reach me."

"Ilse... Are you seriously saying that? Are you saying what I'm saying is wrong?"

"No? I suppose justice is justice in your world. It's just unfortunate that it doesn't reach me. And forcing your own convenience on others without considering theirs... I see why you're a solo adventurer, not part of a party."

Ilse declared this without hesitation, staring at Melan. Rei... and most of those listening nearby silently agreed.

A few individuals present could see Meran's point, but they remained a distinct minority.

"Why... why don't you get it?! I'm telling you, revenge is meaningless!"

"...Unfortunately, I don't know what else to say to you anymore. Let's end this conversation here. Besides, we have our own things to do and don't have the time to waste on you."

With that, she stood up, deciding further conversation with Melan was a waste of time, and turned her gaze to Rei.

"Rei-san, I apologize for wasting your time on this. We should be going now."

"Is that all?"

"Yes. It's clear that no matter what I say to him, it's pointless... He's listening to me, but he's not really hearing me."

At first glance, he seemed to be listening to her, and indeed, Melan was probably hearing Ilse's words.

But within Melan, the conclusion was predetermined from the start. No matter what Ilse said, he wouldn't accept it.

Understanding this, Ilse concluded it was pointless to engage with Melan any further and ended the conversation.

Though...

"Wait, Ilse! This isn't over yet! We'll talk properly until you give up this notion of revenge!"

Melan was completely unconvinced by Ilse's words.

"Unfortunately, my words won't reach you. Since that's the case, there's no need for us to talk any further."

Ilse told Melan only that, as he continued to plead.

To an outsider, it might have looked like a lover trying to stop a breakup, but Rei showed no sign of caring as he stood up.

"Wait!"

Melan shouted at Rei.

Melan understood, sadly, that her words alone couldn't stop Ilse.

But... precisely because of that, Melan could not let Rei—the very person who had driven Ilse to such a foolish act of revenge—go unpunished.

At Melan's words, Rei paused for just an instant... and in the next moment, swiftly drew the blade of the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and thrust it toward Melan.

The time it took to turn around, draw the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring, and press its blade against the back of Melan's neck was less than a second.

And yet, despite swinging such a long weapon as the Death Scythe in a tavern filled with tables and chairs, neither people nor property suffered any damage.

Had it been evening, when the place was crowded, casualties might have occurred... but it was still daytime, and few patrons remained in the tavern.

That was precisely why it was possible.

"Guh!"

Suddenly confronted by the blade, Melan could only groan.

He understood that if he tried to say anything now... or if he tried to move, the blade of the Death Scythe pressed against him would undoubtedly demonstrate its sharpness.

If Rei applied even a little force to the Death Scythe, Melan's head would undoubtedly fly through the air in an instant.

"Stop chirping. Didn't Ilse tell you? Your words are shallow. No matter how desperately you try to convey something, they don't reach the heart. Especially coming from someone like you, who tries to force your self-righteous justice onto others."

The gaze from beneath Rei's Dragon Robe hood held no trace of friendliness.

When they had fought before—over Vihera, or more accurately, after accepting Melan's challenge when he fell for her at first sight—there had still been warmth in the gaze directed at Melan... but now, that warmth had completely vanished.

No matter what Rei said, Melan couldn't retort back now.

He understood that any careless retort could literally cost him his life.

Glancing at Melan's state, Rei simply retracted the Death Scythe into the Misty Ring.

Melan noticed the blade pressed against the back of his neck had vanished, yet he still couldn't bring himself to say anything more.

She was overwhelmed by the aura emanating from the smaller figure before her.

Even without the Death Scythe, Rei's symbol, she couldn't possibly imagine defeating him in battle.

"Hmph. So that's all you've got."

Even after the Death Scythe vanished, she remained silent, unable to utter any new words with the same forceful tone she had used before.

Seeing Melan like this, Rei muttered with a mix of exasperation and disappointment before leaving the scene.

Ilse had watched the entire scene from the tavern entrance, but she had nothing in particular to say.

In Ilse's mind, Melan, who had already dismissed the revenge for his family as meaningless and contemptible, was no longer worth engaging with.

"When you're in danger, you stop talking. ...Well, that's normal, I suppose. But in your case, if family, friends, or lovers were killed, you'd probably force some justification and rush into revenge. You'd conveniently forget your own words from earlier about revenge being meaningless."

Melan tried to say something in response to Rei's words.

But... something inside Melan wouldn't let him deny Rei's words.

Melan, who had opened his mouth to speak, ultimately fell silent, unable to utter another word.

Whether she herself thought there was some truth to Rei's words, or for some other reason... either way, Melan found herself unable to speak.

Rei gave Melan one last glance before heading toward Ilse.

"We've wasted our time."

"...I suppose so. No, considering I learned what kind of person he is, it wasn't entirely wasted."

Ilse had no intention of listening to anything Melan might say from now on.

Ilse declared that just knowing that much made talking to Melan worthwhile this time.

It seemed like she was forcing herself to say it, partly to convince herself, but Rei knew there was no point in saying more about Melan.

"But are you sure you'll be okay? You're staying at the same inn as Melan, right?"

"Yes, it's fine. Even if we're staying at the same inn, I just won't talk to her."

Exchanging words, the two left the tavern.

However, those left behind in the tavern, having overheard their exchange and witnessed Rei wielding the Death Scythe, found themselves in an awkwardly tense atmosphere.

"Am I wrong? No, that can't be. I should be walking the right path. But then, why couldn't I say anything to Rei? That... that..."

In that tavern, Melan could only mutter quietly to himself.
