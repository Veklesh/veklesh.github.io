Let's go check on the forest again today, just like yesterday.

That's what Rei had been thinking, but Marina, who had just joined him, said something unexpected, revealing her surprise.

"Um, are you serious?"

"Yes. Please take me with you. As a Dark Elf, I'm a little concerned about the state of that forest."

"...I see. So that's why you're dressed like that today."

Rei gazed at her thoughtfully, finally understanding why Marina, who usually wore a party dress, was unusually dressed in pants today.

Traveling with Rei... or more accurately, traveling by riding on Set was impossible.

For some reason, Set couldn't fly with anyone else besides Rei aboard.

Well, not exactly impossible, but doing so would make it nearly impossible to fly at all.

Despite that, it could manage feats like flying while carrying Rei and gripping a bear carcass with its front paws.

Taking advantage of this trait, Vihera had clung to Set's forepaws during the previous incident with Ambris, moving at high speed alongside Rei and the others.

Learning this, Marina likely thought to use the same method for travel.

However, Marina's usual attire... attempting such a stunt in a party dress would inevitably reveal everything underneath to anyone watching from below.

For Marina, the only person she'd be comfortable showing her undergarments to was the one she was looking at. Therefore, clinging to Set's legs in a party dress was out of the question.

As a result, her choice was to change her outfit from the party dress to something more practical for movement, including pants.

Of course, that outfit was also a type of magic item, crafted from fabric imbued with magic, just like the party dress.

To Rei, who was used to seeing her in her usual glamorous party dress, Marina in pants—a more practical outfit—had a strange charm.

Her upper body wasn't a dress either, so she wore clothing closer to men's wear, but it couldn't completely hide her enormous twin peaks, which rose prominently.

Perhaps it was precisely because it was men's clothing that Marina radiated a different kind of charm than usual.

Marina must have noticed this from Rei's expression. She smiled and spoke.

"Well? Don't you think this outfit suits me too?"

"Yeah. If we're talking about whether it suits me or not, it definitely suits me."

When Rei gave his honest opinion, Marina smiled happily.

Passersby walking nearby were captivated by Marina's smile... but she herself seemed completely oblivious.

"Marina aside, is Vihera okay?"

"Yes. There weren't any monsters when we went yesterday, right? Then it's better for her to wait quietly at Gilm."

Rei nodded in agreement at Vihera's words.

If there were many monsters in the forest, it would be a different story, but from what he saw yesterday, there were no monsters in sight.

There had been talk of a Treant being there, but since even Set hadn't been able to spot one, the chances of encountering one today were slim.

It wasn't unreasonable to think it would be better to stay with Byune than go to such a place.

"Besides, there's no guarantee Marina and I could both grab onto Set's front legs."

"Nah, I don't think that'd be a problem,"

Rei replied, though he didn't voice the thought that if the two of them together weighed more than the bear, that would be a different story...

Rei knew better than to say such a thing, and besides, the mere thought of it sent a chill down his spine, even if only for an instant.

Just as they were about to end the conversation...

"Rei-san, Rei-san! Wait a moment!"

A voice called out.

Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the voice and saw Lenora running toward them, her ponytail swinging wildly.

「Lenora？」

"Haa, haa, haa... Good, you made it. Actually, it's an additional request from the Guild Master. If you go check on the forest today, please cut down a tree... one of a certain thickness. Of course, there's supposed to be additional compensation for this matter."

"...A tree? Why on earth?"

"Well, I don't really know the details either..."

After answering that much, Lenora caught her breath and spoke again.

"I had the day off yesterday, so I don't know the details, but I suspect it has something to do with the forest's abnormality. ...What do you think? The Guildmaster said he'd prefer... no, he wants you to take it if possible."

"Would you do me this favor?" asked Lenora. Without any hesitation, Rei nodded.

"Yeah, got it. One tree, right? If that's all it takes, no problem. I'll take it."

"...Only Rei could say that."

Without the Misty Ring, cutting down a single tree... especially one of considerable thickness, and transporting it all the way to Gilm would be a tremendous challenge.

It was precisely because it was Rei that he could accept it so easily.

(Besides, if that tree in the forest really was a Treant... I doubt it, but even if it could deceive Set's senses, it would reveal itself if I were in a situation where I might be cut down.)

Rei trusted Set's senses.

At the very least, while a higher-ranked monster might slip past him, a monster like a Treant simply couldn't evade Set's perception.

"Alright, I got the tree thing. I'll make sure to bring it back, so don't worry. ...Well then, Set, Marina. Let's go."

"Huh? Marina is coming too!?"

Lenora's expression turned surprised at Rei's words, which also included Marina.

But Marina simply nodded back at Lenora, indicating it was no problem.

"Yes, this matter leaves many questions unanswered. ...Well, I'm rather skilled in that area."

Given that Marina was a Dark Elf, Lenora couldn't really argue with that.

Besides, the guild itself still knew almost nothing about this incident.

If Marina, who knew the forest well, volunteered to investigate, it was more than welcome.

That was undoubtedly part of her reasoning.

At the same time, it was also true that Lenora was worried about Marina's safety, whom she respected.

"I see. ...Be careful."

In the end, that was all Lenora could say to Marina.

Marina seemed to understand Lenora's feelings. She gently stroked Lenora's head, saying there was no need to worry.

After about ten seconds, Marina released her hand from Lenora and turned to speak to Rei.

"Well then, shall we go?"

"Yeah. As for cutting down the trees... well, with the Death Scythe, it should be no problem, and there's nothing special we need to prepare anyway."

If any lumberjacks heard that, they'd undoubtedly turn bright red with anger.

After all, the hardest part of a woodcutter's job isn't cutting down trees, but hauling the felled logs away.

To think that the Misty Ring could store them just by touching them, transporting them without any weight or effort... it wasn't hard to imagine how a lumberjack would feel about that.

"Ah, right. Wait a moment, please."

Lenora called out to Rei, Marina, and Set, who were heading toward the main gate, looking flustered.

Catching the gaze of the two people and one beast... and also Vihera and Byune standing near her, Lenora voiced what she'd forgotten to mention.

"The forest you're heading to has been provisionally named Treant Forest."

"Treant Forest?"

Rei murmured in puzzlement.

It was precisely because they hadn't actually entered the forest and found Treant that he voiced his question, but Rei simply nodded back at him.

"Yes. We figured we needed some sort of name for now, so we went with something easy to remember. The forest where Slesha was attacked is now called Treant's Forest. Of course, Rei wasn't attacked in the forest yesterday... but it's just for now."

"Got it. I'm not exactly hung up on the name either, so that's fine. Treant Forest, huh?"

After confirming this, Rei headed toward the main gate with Marina and Set.

After seeing them off, Vihera returned to the inn with Byune, while Lenora headed back to the guild.

Set flapped his wings, and Rei climbed onto his back. Marina clung to his front paws, secured by a safety rope.

Hanging onto Set's front paws while moving required considerable grip strength and stamina, but Marina seemed unconcerned, gazing down at the ground as they flew through the sky.

"Marina, we're landing."

"Yes, understood."

Marina gave a short reply to Rei's words, heard from atop Set's back as she gazed at the green carpet spreading out below.

And so, Set began his descent toward the ground.

"...From what I can see, the forest probably extends quite far."

As Rei dismounted and muttered while surveying the surroundings, Set let out a low growl, and Marina stared intently toward the forest.

There wasn't really anything to use as a reference point, but even so, from Rei's perspective, the forest did appear to be expanding.

That would become clear if they headed toward the spot where Slesha and the others had camped.

"Marina, Set and I are going into the forest. What about you?"

"...Right. I'll come too. I can't really tell just by looking from outside like this."

"You don't know?"

"Yes. It's definitely not a normal forest. But I don't think we'll truly understand what happened to make it this way until we go deep inside."

If the dark elf Marina said so, then it must be true, Rei concluded.

Even to Rei's eyes, the forest before them—Treant Forest—felt nothing but off.

The complete absence of animal or insect sounds was enough to call it abnormal for a forest.

"Set, what about you... ah... what are you going to do?"

"Grrr? Grrr!"

Given Set's size, wouldn't it be difficult for him to move around once they entered the forest?

That was the thought behind Rei's question, but Set growled in his throat, saying he was fine!

After all, he'd gone into the forest yesterday too, so logically speaking, it should be fine.

"Alright then, let's go."

And so, Rei entered the forest with Marina and Set.

"...I guess I still can't hear animal sounds."

"Yeah. It's blatantly unnatural. ...This tree too..."

Marina nodded at Rei's words and gently stroked the trunk of a nearby tree.

"When I touch it like this, it feels like a normal tree trunk, but there's definitely something off about it."

"First off, Slesha said she was attacked by a Treant, but this tree looks perfectly normal from any angle. There's no way it's a Treant... right?"

"Grr."

Set growled in agreement at Rei's words.

Indeed, even as Rei touched it, there was no sign of it attacking... In fact, it didn't even seem to move at all.

(What's going on? Slesha and the others were definitely attacked by a Treant, right? So, are these multiplying trees not Treants? But... if they're not Treants, and they're not monsters, would they multiply like this?)

Rei muttered while touching a different tree trunk than the one Marina was touching, but that one was also, of course, just an ordinary tree.

"What do you think?"

Rei asked Marina, who could be called a forest expert, but even she frowned and shook her head.

"I don't know. But at the very least, it's definitely not just ordinary Treants or anything like that."

Hearing Marina's words, Rei looked again at the tree he was touching... and then at the surrounding trees.

Even though the forest had only recently spread out, the trees growing there weren't thin saplings... they were massive trees that would take several adults holding hands to encircle.

Even to Rei, raised in the mountains, it was clear these were magnificent trees that couldn't possibly have grown this large in just a few years.

(Well, this is Elgin, and magic exists here... Besides, these trees are probably connected to monsters or something, so Japanese common sense doesn't apply here anyway.)

Thinking such things, he proceeded toward the campsite he'd seen the previous day.

As expected, it took nearly twice the distance Rei had walked yesterday to finally reach the campsite.

"Looking at it like this, the forest really is vast, isn't it?"

Muttering to himself, Rei drew his Death Scythe.

Marina stood slightly apart, nocking an arrow onto her bow, ready for any emergency.

Set also positioned himself to react immediately if anything happened... After confirming this, Rei channeled magic into the Death Scythe and swung it wide.

"Haah!"

A sharp flash streaked across the tree trunk... The next instant, the trunk was severed in the opposite direction from where Rei and the others stood... No, the felled tree began to collapse.
