"Ah, there you are."

“Grrrrrr…”

As Rei muttered from atop Set’s back, Set growled in response, as if to say, “Exactly.”

Currently, the forest lies beneath Set. However, if asked which forest it was... Rei would simply point to the town visible in the distance and answer, “The forest near Saburusta.”

Several days had passed since they trained with Rocks… After delivering supplies and additional guards to Sura and the others, traveling for several days while undergoing rigorous combat training along the way, and training to the point where they felt they needed to rest their bodies for the time being, Rei and the others set out from there to return to Gilm.

And, naturally, heading toward Gilm meant traveling along the main road, which meant they would have to pass near Sublusta.

Having heard from Rocks that this area was dangerous, they decided to combine business with pleasure by searching for bandits in the vicinity… and, unexpectedly, succeeded in spotting a group that appeared to be bandits with surprising ease.

Of course, from above, it was impossible to tell whether the group in the forest was actually a band of bandits.

It would be a different story if I’d witnessed them attacking merchants, but for now, they’re just a group gathered in the woods.

(Based on circumstantial evidence, they’re definitely guilty. …Well, even if I dealt with those suspected bandits now, another group would likely have gathered by the time Sura and the others arrive in the Saburusta area.)

This area is a prime hunting ground for bandits. Of course, there’s a caveat: this applies only to those who have the strength to take on adventurers or the sharp instincts to flee immediately if they sense danger.

Especially now, with the expansion work on Gilm, there are more people around than usual.

Among them, there are a fair number of merchants who haven’t hired bodyguards—whether out of confidence in their own abilities or sheer carelessness… Such targets were prime prey for the bandits.

Thanks to the thieves’ own intelligence network, many thieves have gathered here knowing it’s a prime spot to make a profit, so the number of thieves around Sublusta is currently higher than usual.

The bandits Rei and the others found were likely just a group that had recently arrived in Sublusta.

As if to prove it, the bandits aren’t paying attention to their surroundings, and their attempts at camouflage to avoid detection are extremely crude.

Of course, under normal circumstances, there would be no need to take such measures against the sky… but in the case of Sublusta, it’s a different story.

Since they’re near the frontier, there are a fair number of flying monsters.

While not as common as with Gilm or Abuero, the possibility of being attacked from the sky isn’t entirely nonexistent.

As Set’s example clearly shows, flying monsters move much faster than those that travel on the ground.

...That’s right. Even the area around Sublusta could serve as a feeding ground for the flying monsters found near Gilm.

Moreover, in Gilm—the Mireana Kingdom’s only frontier town—there is frequent communication with other villages, towns, cities… and even the royal capital.

At such times, summoned or tamed monsters may travel through the skies, and in some cases, dragon knights may arrive to serve as messengers.

The fact that they aren’t paying attention to the sky in a place like this makes it clear that these bandits have only just arrived in the area.

“Well, let’s hunt them down for now. …Sorry, but I’ve spotted a group that looks like bandits. It’s not confirmed yet, but based on my hunch, I’m almost certain. So, I’m going to lower the Set Cage to the ground.”

After saying this to the Set Cage, Rei asks Set to place it a short distance away from the forest.

Ideally, since the Set Cage is designed to blend into the surrounding scenery, it would be best to approach while still holding it... but unfortunately, doing so would require lowering the Set Cage into the forest as things stand.

If that happened, there was a risk the Set Cage would be destroyed by the trees growing in the forest.

To prevent that, they needed to land the Set Cage somewhere else—a place where it would be safe to set it down—and then return to their original spot.

Inside the Set Cage, Vihera had mentioned something about wanting to fight herself, but Rei brushed that aside for the time being and lowered the Set Cage onto a grassy field a short distance from the highway.

The sound of the Set Cage hitting the ground startled the merchants and their guards in several carriages traveling along the road; they looked around warily, suspecting an attack, but Rei and Seto paid them no mind and returned to the forest.

However, if Set were to step down directly in front of those suspected of being bandits, they wouldn’t have actually seen him in the act of banditry, so there was a chance he could play innocent.

If that were the case, it would be pointless for them to play innocent; they needed to let them carry out their bandit activities properly before dealing with those men.

For that reason, Rei asked Set to drop him off at a spot away from the bandits. Since anyone could recognize Rei at a glance, he had Set go around to the other side of the bandits.

(Though there’s no guarantee they’ll just quietly run away. No, actually, they’ll probably scatter in all directions. To minimize the chance of getting caught, even slightly. …Actually, that kind of chaotic escape is more troublesome than a neat, orderly retreat.)

For now, to pretend to be a novice who’s just become an adventurer… Rei doesn’t reach for his signature Death Scythe from the Misty Ring, nor the Twilight Spear—which anyone could tell is a masterwork at a glance… Instead, he picks up an ordinary spear, the kind sold anywhere.

For a moment, he wondered if a spear like the ones he used to throw—ones that couldn’t really be used as proper spears—would do. But since Sublusta was a place close to the frontier, it was home to adventurers with considerable skill.

At the very least, they were the kind of adventurers who would leave those from the backwoods near the border completely outclassed.

Given that, it would seem unnatural to pick up a spear with a chipped tip.

Having decided that, I took a standard spear and made my way into the forest...

(There they are.)

Soon, Rei sensed multiple presences coming from the direction he was heading.

Despite sensing those auras, Rei didn’t stop but kept moving forward… and after walking slowly for about five minutes, five men suddenly appeared before him.

“Whoa, that’s enough.”

“Who are you guys?”

“Well, I wonder who it is. But whoever we are… I don’t think it matters to you anymore.”

“What do you mean by that?”

He asked, feigning a look of fear.

He knew his acting wasn’t that good, so he spoke with a sense of anxiety, wondering if they might see through him. Fortunately, the five who appeared didn’t seem to notice Rei’s act at all.

Holding daggers and long swords, they were flashing sadistic grins at Rei.

(The fact that they don’t have any spears or the like is probably because they’re operating on the assumption that they’ll be in the forest. …Well, that’s a natural consideration for bandits, I suppose.)

As he thought this, he kept a watchful eye on his surroundings… and, sensing that no one other than the five in front of him was moving at all, he began to wonder.

(Did they come with just the five of them? Or did they simply think five would be enough... Either way, it’s a bit of a letdown.)

Seeing Rei holding a spear, the man with the dagger lets out a mocking laugh.

“A spear in the woods? What are you thinking? And judging by that spear, you’re just a rookie who’s only just become an adventurer, aren’t you?”

His words and tone were almost accusatory.

Yet at the same time, his expression also conveyed a hope that this was indeed the case.

Given that they were thieves, it was only natural, but these men weren’t the type to bother hiding their true feelings.

(Well, even among thieves, those who lead large bands are often quite formidable.)

Thinking that, Rei pointed the tip of his spear at them, trying to coax a decisive answer out of them.

“You guys… being out here in the middle of the woods like this… you’re bandits, aren’t you! The guild mentioned that there’s been a rise in bandits around here lately.”

What came out of Rei’s mouth was pure nonsense.

Since he hadn’t stopped by Sublusta on his way here, that was only natural.

But since he knew there had been reports of increased bandit activity around Sublusta and Abuero due to the expansion of Gilm, it wasn’t entirely made-up.

(How should I respond? Should I brush it off, or should I play it off as a newbie who just became an adventurer...)

Rei waited for them to speak, but perhaps mistaking his gaze for fear, the men smiled and spoke up.

“Correct. We’re bandits. But too bad for you. Even knowing that, you—guh—”

The bandit who had tried to scare Rei couldn’t finish his sentence.

Because a spear had pierced right through his torso.

Naturally, the spear came from Rei.

Thrown so as not to catch on the surrounding trees, the spear pierced the torso of one bandit, then pierced the side of another bandit standing behind him, and finally embedded its tip completely into the tree trunk behind him.

In that instant, the bandits had absolutely no idea what was happening.

All they could make out was the high-pitched sound of the spearhead striking the tree trunk and the sound of their two comrades collapsing to the ground.

The three remaining men stared at Rei with unnatural movements, as if they were broken dolls.

What had happened? Who had done it?

They had absolutely no idea.

Well, to be precise, they knew who had done it—they had seen it with their own eyes. But the feeling that they didn’t want to understand it, that they couldn’t understand it, was stronger.

That the figure standing before them was not some weak prey... but an opponent so powerful they were no match for it.

The high-pitched sound of the spear piercing the tree trunk seemed to alert the other bandits that something was amiss; they were each preparing themselves to handle whatever might happen.

Feeling the presence of those bandits, Rei pulled a different weapon from the Misty Ring instead of the spear she had thrown.

...Yes, the weapon that symbolized Crimson: the Death Scythe.

Normally, in a place like a forest, where trees grow in such great numbers, a weapon like the Death Scythe is far from suitable.

If it were a spear, designed primarily for thrusting, that might be one thing, but the Death Scythe is a scythe, and its primary function is to swing.

Nevertheless, the reason he drew the Death Scythe here was to demonstrate to his opponent, in the most unmistakable way possible, exactly who he is.

A person carrying a scythe and an item box both larger than themselves.

There are very few people like that.

No, not just few—in fact, looking across the entire Kingdom of Mireana… or even all of Elgin, there is likely no one else but Rei.

A figure feared by bandits and known as the “Bandit Eater” for his habit of ambushing them and seizing their loot… Rei of Crimson.

Did he realize that the person standing before him was that Crimson Rei, or did Rei act before he could even realize it?

In the next instant, the blade of the Death Scythe, wielded with magical power, sliced through the bandits’ torsos from top to bottom, along with the trees growing around them.

Unable to utter a word, the five corpses collapsed to the ground, filling the air with a pungent, rusty iron stench.

Given that it was summer, the heat seemed to make that smell of rust spread even further.

Leaving that scent of rust behind, Rei, still holding the Death Scythe, advanced straight into the depths.

His speed, as if he were leaving the very air behind him, must have been completely unexpected even to the bandits waiting further in.

“What?!”

Amidst the chaos, the first bandit to spot Rei tried to say something, but before he could finish, Rei swung his Death Scythe.

“Gah!”

The man, who had been about to shout, ended up uttering only a single groan before his torso was split in two and he died.

However, since this happened in the midst of a battle, the remaining bandits immediately prepared for combat.

Closing the distance on them, Rei switched the Death Scythe to his left hand, drew the Twilight Spear with his right, and hurled it.

Flying with a force that seemed to slice through the air itself, the Twilight Spear pierced the torsos of several bandits, taking their lives in an instant.

And the spear, having taken several lives, vanished immediately… only to return to Rei’s hand the very next instant.

What began then was a display of Rei’s true prowess—the “Bandit Eater,” feared for devouring the very bandits who prey on human lives.

There was no way a band of bandits—especially ones who had only just arrived in the area—could possibly stand a chance against an adventurer with such a notorious reputation. Within minutes, all but one of the bandits present—left behind for intelligence gathering—had breathed their last.
