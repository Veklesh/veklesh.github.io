"Grrrrrr!"

Spotting Golz ahead, Set growled to alert Rei, who was behind him.

Hearing Set’s growl, Rei also looks down at the ground… and after a moment, finally realizes it’s Golz.

It was already night, and the sky held only the moon and stars, hidden behind clouds.

Since Golz was the most prosperous town in the area, there were still some lights visible even at night.

Set spotted those lights... and a moment later, Rei saw them too.

“Ah… so it ended up getting dark after all. If we hadn’t been looking for those bandits, I think we could’ve made it back a little sooner.”

“Grr…”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s words, Set let out a comforting purr.

If they had searched for the bandits and found them, things might have worked out.

But, unfortunately... Rei and the others were unable to find the bandits.

That was only to be expected. If they’d known the bandits were somewhere, Rei and Set’s abilities would have found them in a flash.

But they had no idea where the bandit was—the distance from Somis to Golz was one that would take even Set several hours to cover.

Moreover, since they didn’t have time to search thoroughly, it was only natural that finding the bandits in such a short time would be difficult.

That’s why it’s gotten this late, and Rei’s prediction is that, in the worst-case scenario, it might even be touch-and-go whether they’ll make it into Golz…

That said, since Rei’s Misty Ring contained a Magic Tent, sleeping out in the open wouldn’t have been a problem at all.

However, he wanted to hand over the special potion he’d gone out of his way to bring from Somis today, if possible.

“Alright, count on me.”

“Grrrrr!”

Set flapped his wings and descended toward the approaching Golz.

As it was already night, there was no one walking along the road.

Even without streetlights, Set’s keen eyesight allowed him to clearly see the road, illuminated by the moonlight streaming through the gaps in the clouds.

Since he naturally possessed night vision, Set—and Rei as well—landed on the road near Golz without any trouble.

But, naturally, doing something like that meant they’d be spotted by the guards on watch… Even to Rei’s eyes, it was clear they were on high alert.

If they tried to approach like this, things would get messy.

Realizing this, Rei waved his arms widely so the guards could see him and called out.

“It’s me, Rei! I was out for a bit, but I’m back! If possible, let me into the city! Even if that’s not possible, I’d like to deliver the potions I brought at the request of the magic item shop!”

They must have heard Rei’s voice. The guards standing on the wall seemed to calm down, if only slightly.

Relieved by this, Rei approached the main gate of Golz.

Then, a small door for the guards next to the main gate opened... but there was no sign of a guard coming out.

Rei was puzzled by this, but soon a voice called out from through the crack in the door, sounding hesitant.

“Is that really Rei?”

“Who else would it be? Besides, look—Set’s here too.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl.

……Under normal circumstances, a griffin growling at night would clearly be nothing but a source of terror.

But to the residents of Golz, Set is a benefactor—albeit a monster—who helped them conquer the nearby dungeon.

Also, since Set had been moving about Golz for some time now, while they didn’t exactly adore him like Gilm did… they generally didn’t scream in terror at the sight of him.

Peering through the gaps in the large shields—enough to hide a human body—that had been set up behind the door, the guards confirmed the figures of Rei and Set, and this time, they truly breathed a sigh of relief.

“It’s Rei and Set.”

“Yeah. Ah, thank goodness. To be honest, I was worried they might be bandits or monsters.”

“Me too, me too.”

As the guards exchanged words like this, one of them stepped out from behind the door.

“Normally, you wouldn’t be allowed into the city at this hour… but today is a special case. Come on in.”

“That’s a relief. …But is that really okay? I was actually thinking of spending the night out in the open.”

Hearing Rei’s words, the guard didn’t know what to say and wore an awkward expression.

“Listen, you’re the one who cleared that dungeon near Golz… the one with monsters so powerful you’d never guess it was just built. You’re my savior! How could I possibly let someone like you sleep rough outside the city?”

If he tried something like that, who knows what the townspeople would do to him… Rei could tell the guard wasn’t joking; he seemed to be speaking in all seriousness.

Rei didn’t particularly want to sleep rough either.

If he could sleep in a bed at an inn, that would be ideal, so he entered through the guards’ door.

...By the way, Set couldn’t fit through at his normal size, but by using his size-changing skill to shrink down, he managed to squeeze through, so they didn’t have to open the main gate.

Naturally, everyone was surprised when Set suddenly shrank, but as long as he emphasized that he was a rare species, no one pressed the matter.

Or rather, it’s not that they couldn’t question it—it’s more accurate to say that once told he was a rare species, they simply accepted it.

After all, considering that they’re called “rare species” precisely because they can do things ordinary monsters can’t, that’s certainly not wrong.

That said, if they’d been more powerful beings, they might not have been satisfied with the explanation that Set was a rare species just because he used the Size Reduction skill.

In any case, Rei and Set completed the procedures to enter Golz without any particular problems.

“So, what are you going to do now, Rei?”

In response to the guard’s question, Rei strokes Set—who is wearing the familiar’s necklace—and states what needs to be done.

“First off, the magic item shop. Since I went to the trouble of bringing it, I’m going to deliver the potion.”

“I see. Got it. Do you need directions?”

“No, I remember where the shop is, so it’s fine. …Besides, I don’t think there’s any idiot in Golz who’d come bothering me.”

The fact that Rei had cleared the dungeon was, naturally, common knowledge in Golz.

What’s more, he had been butchering monsters like the Galangiga and the Kelpie he’d defeated in that dungeon right next to the main gate.

Especially the boss monster, the Galangiga—even without its head, its sheer size was something the residents of Golz… no, even the adventurers of Golz had never seen before.

Only a suicide candidate would even consider picking a fight with someone powerful enough to defeat such a monster.

That said, if it were just Rei, he might be underestimated due to his Dragon Robe and physique, but right now, Rei is with Set.

As long as he’s with the griffin, there’s no one who wouldn’t recognize Rei as Rei.

Perhaps the guards understood this, for they made no further attempt to detain them.

After parting ways with the guards, Rei heads toward the magic item shop with Set.

“Growl…”

Set’s throat growls at the appetizing aroma wafting from the food stall.

Rei, too, was just as hungry as Set.

“The food at Somis wasn’t exactly bad, but it wasn’t that great either. …Well, I guess it can’t be helped since he had to buy it in such a short time.”

“Gurrr…”

At Rei’s words, Set growled regretfully.

If they’d had more time, they probably could have eaten all sorts of delicious dishes.

But in this particular case, there simply wasn’t enough time.

For that reason alone, it was truly a shame they couldn’t enjoy Somis’s delicious food.

(I might go back if I get the chance… but honestly, I don’t really have any reason to go to Somis anymore.)

Somis is a fairly large town, but really, that’s all it is.

There are plenty of other towns of similar size, so there’s really no need to go all the way to Somis.

As for the special potion that was the reason for my trip to Somis this time, the fact that it can’t be made here is simply because the alchemists in Golz lack the skill—it wouldn’t be surprising if there were plenty of people in other towns who possess the skill to manage it.

Especially in Gilm, where Rei is based—precisely because it’s on the frontier, many alchemists gather there in search of unknown ingredients.

Given that, there surely must be an alchemist there capable of making the potion that’s become an issue this time.

“Well, never mind that for now. More importantly, we need to hurry to the magic item shop.”

Saying that, he bought some bread from a nearby stall and shared it with Set.

The bread contained nuts that resembled walnuts, and while it wasn’t exactly fresh out of the oven, the rich flavor and texture of the nuts still complemented the bread’s deliciousness perfectly.

That’s exactly why it was only natural for Rei to wish he could have eaten that bread fresh out of the oven.

“Oh, there it is.”

After tucking a few pieces of the bread into his stomach, Set finished eating about twice as much as Rei… and just as their hunger had subsided a bit, the shop they were looking for came into view.

As expected, all the other shops were already closed at this hour.

But… fortunately, Rei could tell that the magic item shop they were heading to was still open.

That was because the lights were still on inside the shop.

Seeing that, Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

He was glad they’d made it back, but he’d been worried about what they would do if the shop had already closed.

In that case, they would have had to come to this magic item shop early tomorrow morning—before leaving Golz.

Of course, that much effort wouldn’t be a big deal, but if you asked whether it was a hassle or not, it would undoubtedly be a hassle.

Given that, it’s only natural that he’d want to hand over the potion now.

Especially since the sister of the man who wants that potion is still suffering from an illness.

To be honest, if I were in a hurry for some reason right now, I wouldn’t have time to worry about a stranger.

But when someone who already knows the situation tells me they’re suffering even though I could help… if I were to ignore that, I’d undoubtedly be left with a bad taste in my mouth.

Given that, Rei simply couldn’t choose to ignore it.

That said, if the person in distress had come at him with a haughty attitude, Rei’s personality would have led him to ignore it.

“Anyway, I went to the trouble of bringing this. There’s no need to waste time here.”

Muttering briefly, Rei told Set to eat the remaining bread and walked straight into the magic item shop.

The moment the sound of the door opening reached his ears, he could see a man inside the shop turn around sharply.

And there was no need to guess who it was.

It wasn’t the magic item shop clerk… but the male adventurer who had been waiting for a special potion to cure his sister’s illness.

“O… oh… ooooooooooooooooh!”

First, he saw Rei and muttered in disbelief; next, he checked to see if the person in front of him was the real deal; and finally, convinced that it was indeed him, the man let out a loud cry.

The clerk shot the man a look of exasperation… and then, for some reason, Vihera who had been inside the shop waved at Rei with a beaming smile.

“Vihera? What are you doing here?”

Ignoring the adventurer who was still shouting, Rei asked Vihera.

It had been quite some time since Rei and Vihera had come to this shop together.

Given that, he probably hadn’t expected Vihera to still be here.

But when asked that, Vihera smiled and spoke up.

“Oh, well, today’s our date, isn’t it? So, of course we’d be going back to the inn together.”

That meant Vihera had been at this shop the whole time, even after Rei had left for Somis.

“Well, I only came back to this shop a little while ago myself.”

At Vihera’s words, Rei let out an “Ah…” and hesitated slightly, unsure of what to say.

Should he thank her for waiting for him, or should he warn her that she was causing trouble for the shop?

Unsure of what to do... in the end, Rei forced a smile as if to cover something up and spoke.

“I’m back.”

“Welcome back.”
