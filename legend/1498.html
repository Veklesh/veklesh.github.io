"Nuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuu

"Ah... yeah. I totally forgot about you."

As usual, Rei arrived at the wall construction site. Spotting him, Marz approached at a speed defying his age and lamented.

The old wizard, who was utterly devoted to Rei, had sulked like a child yesterday because Rei had barely shown up at the construction site.

Yet he didn't complain to Rei, only lamenting—proof of just how much he respected her.

"Sorry about that. I had some business yesterday. I was completely tied up with it and forgot to stop by here."

"N-nuu... When you apologize so sincerely like that, I can't very well stay angry... I suppose... I suppose it can't be helped. Just this once, though."

Marz stopped being angry after Rei apologized so readily.

(Who even benefits from a man's... no, an old man's tsundere act?)

That's what Rei thought, but since Marutsu had said he'd come back later yesterday and never actually returned, he didn't press the issue.

"So, what's this about? Just so you know, yesterday was busy, and today is busy too... Huh?"

While talking with Marz, Ray suddenly noticed a figure running straight toward him. Given it was the day after yesterday, he instantly went on guard.

But recognizing them as familiar faces, he relaxed immediately.

"Rain of Mercy... was it?"

Yes, the ones running toward Rei were the members of the Rank D party, Rain of Mercy.

The difference from before was that there were only five of them.

(I heard one went missing... Ah, I see. Given the timing...)

Rei recalled last night's events... more precisely, the three carriages at the scene where Ajax and the others had been.

The women they'd taken away had been forced into those carriages, collars around their necks. The reason one more woman was here now was obvious without needing to think about it.

"Haa, haa, haa. Rei, um, thank you so much!"

The blue-haired woman who had been running ahead arrived before Rei and bowed deeply.

Marz flashed a momentary look of annoyance at this group of mercy-rainers, but quickly hid it.

For Marz, having his time talking with Rei interrupted was far from amusing.

However, seeing the group bowing to Rei, he decided it was best not to interrupt them now.

...Though, to be honest, that consideration wasn't really for the Mercy Rain members. It was purely because he didn't want to interrupt the conversation here and risk upsetting Rei.

"Ah... seems you understand the situation."

"Yes. I heard it from Ryusha."

The blue-haired woman said this while turning her gaze toward a woman unfamiliar to Rei... toward Ryusha.

"So, you're the one from yesterday, right? Thank you?"

Rei was slightly bothered by her drawn-out speech and the fact that her last sentence ended in a question mark for some reason, but he decided there was no need to point it out for now and simply nodded in response.

"Ah. ...So you've been told about yesterday?"

"Yes. And I was on the outside of the carriage—"

Through the window, or perhaps a gap in the carriage body—either way, she could see outside through a crack. That seemed to be the case.

(The Intelligence Corps... No, the Intelligence Corps is fundamentally an organization that doesn't operate openly. If that's the case, then who explained the situation to this kid? The Security Corps?)

Reaching that conclusion, he simultaneously voiced a sudden thought.

"You mentioned being forced to wear a slave collar. Were you conscious the whole time it was on?"

"Well... basically, yes. But I was ordered not to do anything unnecessary, so I couldn't move."

"I see."

Even among slave collars, their capabilities vary greatly.

The truly expensive ones can even freely manipulate the wearer's emotions.

...Of course, such items are incredibly expensive, and certainly not something you could prepare dozens of like this time.

Anyway, the one Ryusha wore wasn't that powerful, which is probably why she could still see and hear what was going on outside.

"Well, I'm just glad you're safe. Your people were getting worried about you, almost starting a fight."

When Rei's gaze fell on the remaining four, they awkwardly looked away.

"Seriously. You guys need to get along, okay?"

At Rei's words, Ryusha admonished the other four.

But the way she did it was like scolding children, and it somehow made the onlookers feel warm and fuzzy inside.

Seeing Ryusha like this, Rei felt relieved that she hadn't been subjected to anything strange while she was captured.

While there were many suspicious points about the reason, she had still been taken away to be made into a prostitute.

It wouldn't have been surprising if she'd been assaulted by the organization's members or Ajax and the others.

"I know, I know. Seriously, don't make me do embarrassing things in front of Rei-san."

One of the men from the Rain of Mercy blushed at Ryusha's words, his embarrassment evident.

Honestly, having been saved by Rei, he probably wanted to save face a little.

"Well, at least you're safe now. So, what are you guys going to do? Considering what happened to your comrades, are you done working for Gilm?"

"We did consider that at first. But it feels like admitting defeat, doesn't it? So, we've decided to keep working for Gilm a little longer. ...Luckily, we're not short on jobs right now."

"I see. Well then, hang in there."

For now, all Rei could do was offer those words of encouragement.

Honestly, no matter what Rei said, it wouldn't matter if they weren't motivated themselves. So that was probably the right approach.

And this time, that choice was definitely not wrong.

At Rei's words, the members of Mercy Rain nodded happily.

To the members of Mercy Rain, Rei was a high-ranking adventurer with a notorious reputation.

Normally, they wouldn't dare casually approach someone like him.

Given that he saved their comrades, it's only natural they feel strong affection toward Rei.

"Yes. Well then, we'll get started preparing for the job right away."

With that, the members of Mercy Rain began to leave... but before they did, only Ryusha paused, bowing her head once more to Rei.

"Rei, thank you so very much this time. Because of you, I can be here with my comrades again."

She bowed to Rei using proper, formal speech, not her usual drawn-out way of speaking.

When she raised her bowed head, the familiar Ryusha was there.

"Well then—we'll be taking our leave now."

Ryusha announced this and followed after her companions.

Rei watched the five of them depart, but suddenly noticed a gaze fixed on him.

It was Marz, who had been listening to the conversation between Rei and the members of Rain of Mercy.

Noticing the somewhat surprised look directed at him, Rei opened his mouth with a puzzled expression.

"What's up?"

"Ah... no. I was just thinking that Rei is involved in quite a few things besides this expansion project. Considering what we just discussed, I wondered if you'd gotten caught up in that party's troubles. Come to think of it, Rei did speak to those people before. Is it related to that?"

"No, that's not it. It's just... well, other things happened. Unfortunately, I can't talk about it."

Rei decided it best not to mention anything about Ilse's revenge and deflected the conversation.

Many already knew about Ilse's revenge.

But still, since it was part of what could be called Ilse's private life, it felt a bit off for Rei to be the one to say it...

Seeing Rei's attitude, Marz seemed to sense something and didn't press further.

"More importantly, Rei. Show me the Death Scythe."

"Again? Well, fine, I guess."

Marz changed the subject with that request. Though looking annoyed, Rei pulled the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring and placed it on the ground.

Seeing this, Marz began examining it closely, trying to analyze the Death Scythe as much as possible.

Watching Marz, Rei felt relieved that the topic had changed.

Experience... that's probably the right word for it.

Still, the way he demanded Rei produce the Death Scythe she was interested in while simultaneously diverting the conversation made Rei think he was a tricky old man.

"Hmm... Not only does it function as a magic activation device, but it possesses this weight on its own. Furthermore, it has the effect of preventing Rei from sensing it. Granted, imbuing a magic activation device with the properties of a magic item isn't an entirely uncommon concept..."

"You've said that before. Seeing the Death Scythe over and over and saying the same thing... don't you ever get bored?"

"Not at all, I never tire of it. It might seem like I'm repeating myself, but in truth, I discover something new every time I look at it. And above all, observing the Death Scythe sparks countless new ideas within me."

"...You, weren't you originally interested in me because of the magic itself, not the magic items? Why did you end up getting more interested in the magic items than the magic itself?"

That's what Rei said, but what actually caught Marz's interest was terrain manipulation... not Rei, but the skill possessed by the Death Scythe.

Externally, it was classified as earth-based magic.

(Did Marz sense that? ...Well, I hear there are Magic Swords that spew flames or are wrapped in lightning too. Thinking about it that way, it wouldn't be strange for people to misunderstand the Death Scythe as having earth manipulation abilities, right?)

If that were the case, it would be a bit troublesome... but still not fatal.

Of course, if he learned about abilities other than terrain manipulation, or worse, witnessed them directly, that would be a different story... Fortunately, Marz didn't seem to have noticed anything about that.

(Though, in Marz's case, even if he did know, he probably wouldn't tell anyone.)

As if he hadn't heard Rei's words... or perhaps deliberately ignoring them, he glanced at Marz staring intently at the Death Scythe, then surveyed the surroundings.

By now, the others probably understood that Marz was special in more ways than one.

Even seeing him desperately examining the Death Scythe placed on the ground, no one said anything in particular.

In fact, since work was about to start, they were focused on preparing for that instead.

"The wall's coming along nicely, I see."

"Hm? Ah, yes. Though it's still only in the early stages. Who knows when it'll finally be finished. It's fine now since it's still summer, but come winter..."

Marz, who had been examining the Death Scythe, must have heard Rei's muttered words this time. He replied.

"Winter, huh. ...Some people probably wish winter would come sooner at this time of year, but once winter arrives, most will probably wish summer would come sooner."

Looking up at the summer sky filled with cumulus clouds, Rei murmured.

It was still the height of summer.

Despite it being morning, quite a few people already had sweat on their foreheads. ...As usual, Rei was comfortably wearing his Dragon Robe with its built-in air conditioning function.

"Indeed. At my age, the summer heat is becoming quite unbearable. Especially this year—it feels hotter than usual."

"Is that so?"

Was it because of his age? Or was it because Marz was experiencing summer in Gilm for the first time?

Even Rei couldn't tell.

Wearing a Dragon Robe, it was probably only natural.

No matter how high or low the temperature... for Rei, it never made it unbearable.

Well, taking off the Dragon Robe would be a different story, but basically, Rei only took it off when staying at an inn.

And at the Dusk Wheat inn, the temperature was kept constant by magic items.

At Marina's house too, that aspect was taken care of by Marina's personal ability, spirit magic.

Thinking about it that way, it was only natural that Rei couldn't truly understand what Marz meant.

Thinking along these lines, Rei let his thoughts drift to the work he would be doing today.
