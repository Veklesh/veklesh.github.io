"...Why did things turn out like this?"

Rei sighed and turned his gaze to the man standing before him.

There stood a man in his twenties, his face flushed crimson with rage.

He held a long sword in his hand, glaring at Rei with a look mixed with fury.

The fact that his gaze contained anger but no murderous intent was perhaps the only saving grace.

Laying his gaze to the side, Rei caught sight of Vihera, wearing a troubled expression, and Byune, still utterly expressionless.

It wasn't hard to figure out how Rei ended up in this situation.

"Got it! If I win, you better never bother Vihera again!"

The man pointed the tip of his longsword at Rei and shouted.

It all started when Rei finished talking with Walker, exchanged words with Lenora and Kenny, and stepped out of the guild.

As if timing it perfectly, Vihera and Byune, having finished their patrol of Gilm, returned just then.

...However, Vihera and Byune weren't the only ones patrolling.

Due to a shortage of guards, adventurers had been asked to patrol the town, and this was being done by several groups.

The man in Vihera's group, whether he was overly presumptuous or simply had zero tolerance for women... in any case, he was completely smitten by Vihera, a beauty rarely seen.

It wasn't that Vihera had done anything to give him hope.

He simply fell head over heels the moment he laid eyes on Vihera.

His half-baked confidence in his own abilities only reinforced his belief that he was the perfect match for Vihera.

He launched a fierce offensive, but naturally, Vihera had no intention of entertaining men... Faced with Vihera, who beamed at Rei's face, he'd essentially lost control.

"Ah... you really intend to fight me?"

The man standing before Rei now wasn't the only one traveling with Vihera.

There were others who had been adventuring in Gilm for a long time, and naturally, they knew Rei's capabilities.

That's precisely why they tried to stop the man. But the man, now half-deprived of his ability to think due to his infatuation with Vihera and his jealousy towards Rei, paid no heed to such warnings.

「Vihera」

Rei turned his gaze to Vihera as a last resort, but all he got in return was a shake of the head.

Vihera had repeatedly refused the man's advances each time he pursued her.

But somehow, the man twisted this into believing Vihera was being deceived by Rei.

And he decided he couldn't allow Rei to approach Vihera... leading to the current situation.

"What's the matter?! Scared?! Fight me, Melan Canning, like a man!"

The man shouted... no, Rei was slightly surprised by the name Melan.

The man before him now had called himself Melan Canning...

Yes, he had stated his surname.

Having a surname in this world was extremely rare.

It wasn't something a mere adventurer could claim.

(A noble? Or had he accomplished some great feat?)

He considered it for a moment, but decided it wasn't his place to speculate.

After all, this was a kind of duel, and his victory was already assured.

"Ah, this is getting tiresome. ...Alright. I get your point. Then let's just get this over with."

With that, Rei took his stance.

However, what he readied was neither Death Scythe, Twilight Spear, nor Thorn Spear... but his fist.

"Hey, what's that supposed to be?"

While he himself was holding a longsword, Rei wasn't holding anything resembling a weapon.

He merely clenched his fist lightly and held it before his face.

To Melan, it could only be seen as contempt.

Those around them couldn't help but look up to the sky at Melan's words, as if to say, "Oh, come on."

They were probably thinking... well, that Rei not fighting seriously was fortunate, but Melan seemed completely unaware of that.

"Meaning I don't need a weapon to take you on. ...Besides, I'm actually pretty good at fighting barehanded, despite how I look."

Those words were certainly not wrong.

Moreover, using a weapon carried the risk of accidentally killing his opponent, but fighting barehanded meant he hardly had to worry about that.

...He said "almost" because with Rei's physical abilities, he could easily kill someone with his bare hands.

"Huh!? That arrogance! You'll regret it after you lose!"

As he spoke, Melan closed the distance to Rei in one swift motion and swung his longsword down.

...Even so, aiming for the middle of the blade rather than the tip was proof that, despite challenging him to a duel, she had no intention whatsoever of killing Rei.

(Huh. He seemed like he was all fired up, but he's actually thinking things through, huh?)

Watching the flat of the longsword swing toward him, Rei took a step back.

The longsword whizzed past right before Rei's eyes.

Out of the corner of his eye, he saw the man stop the sword just before it hit the ground, then stepped forward.

Seeing Melan's movement freeze for an instant after his all-out strike missed, Rei thrust his fist forward.

The sound that echoed around them in that instant was utterly unlike anything a human could possibly produce from striking something.

Melan wore leather armor, but the impact was so immense it rendered it utterly useless. Just as he felt a sensation of floating, he lost consciousness.

In this case, Melan was probably lucky to have lost consciousness.

He avoided the experience of being blown several meters through the air, scraping along the ground, and then tumbling several more meters.

"Hey, look at that leather armor. Could that possibly be a fist mark?"

One of the onlookers murmured.

Those who heard the words turned their gaze to Melan's armor. There, just as the man had said, what appeared to be fist marks were clearly imprinted on Melan's leather armor.

Normally, punching leather armor wouldn't leave such a clean, distinct mark.

It was proof of just how much power Rei had unleashed in the single blow.

"You're being so forceful again."

While those around them stared in shock at the fist marks left on Melan's leather armor, only Vihera watched Rei approach her with an exasperated look.

Vihera understood exactly what Rei had done. ...Or rather, perhaps it was precisely because she was Vihera that she understood.

Among Vihera's skills was one called Immersion Palm. It allowed her to deliver a magical shock directly into an opponent's body the moment she touched them.

...Though, now that Vihera had absorbed Ambris, her Immersion Palm had transformed into a vicious skill that sent magic into the target upon contact alone, causing an explosion from within.

What Rei did was similar to the Immersion Palm, yet different.

While it shared the goal of delivering an impact within the opponent's body, his method involved punching the leather armor to forcefully deliver that impact.

Even with Rei holding back considerably, the force was enough to send Melan flying spectacularly.

"Is that so? Well, I did hold back to avoid killing him, so he shouldn't be in any life-threatening condition. I don't think any bones are broken either."

"...Unusually restrained for Rei."

"No, I don't always go all out to beat the crap out of people, you know?"

Rei declared this, but Vihera looked at him with an expression that clearly didn't believe him.

"Considering everything you've done up until now, I can't quite bring myself to believe you."

Hearing that, Rei couldn't help but recall his own past actions.

"Well, if I think of it as you fighting for me, I suppose it makes me happy as a woman."

Vihera smiled genuinely, looking truly pleased, just as she'd said.

Nearby, Rei thought he saw Byune giving Vihera a somewhat exasperated look.

Her expression remained unchanged as usual, but perhaps it was her aura that felt different.

In any case, perhaps noticing Byune's gaze, Vihera changed the subject.

"So, Rei's back, which means that business over there is settled, right?"

"Yeah, that's done. But I hear we're finally going to break down the wall?"

"Seems so. I haven't been involved much in that part, though."

Given that Vihera's role was basically assisting the guards, it made sense she hadn't been involved much.

"I see. Is Marina heading to Treant Forest again today?"

"Yes. I hear the spirit magic is helping them a lot? Word is the woodcutters' morale is sky-high."

"...Well, it is a male-dominated group."

There are exceptions, but woodcutters are mostly men.

At least, among the woodcutters Rei knew in Gilm, there were only men.

In that environment, if a bewitchingly beautiful woman like Marina was working alongside them, naturally many would want to show off their best side.

But Marina also has spirit magic.

Working as a woodcutter during the hot summer season is incredibly hard labor.

While cutting wood does mean they sometimes work in the shade, it's only a slight relief.

But... what if there was someone skilled in spirit magic present?

Marina's specialty, water and wind spirits, are particularly adept at this kind of effect.

In other words, the woodcutters could work in a remarkably cool environment, despite the summer heat.

Being able to work comfortably in the summer heat meant Marina naturally became popular among the woodcutters.

"But it's about time we headed back... What do you say? Return to the Dusk Wheat inn?"

"Ah... yeah. I did have something to ask Marina, but that can wait until tonight."

"Oh? So Marina's the only one with business here?"

"No, it's not like that exactly..."

The magic item I snatched from the Lerdact mansion—I don't need it myself, so just display it at Marina's place or something.

There was no way Rei could say something like that in a crowded place like this, so Rei glossed over it.

Seeing Rei's expression, Vihera seemed to understand there was some reason behind it.

Vihera didn't press Rei further, just gave a small nod.

"Well then, I'll go report this job to the guild. Can you wait here for a bit?"

"I don't mind waiting... but what about him?"

Rei asked, looking at Melan, who had fallen for Vihera at first sight, misunderstood the situation, and started bothering Rei, only to be knocked unconscious with a single blow.

"Right. ...Well then, could someone just carry him into the shade or something? We can't exactly leave him lying out in the summer sun."

At Vihera's words, several of those who had been traveling with them nodded and dragged Melan into the shade.

(So she really does keep tabs on her allies.)

Though not to the same extent as Melan, many were charmed by Vihera.

"Well, I'll be playing with Set then."

"Yes, I'll be right back."

With that, Vihera led Byune and the others into the guild.

After seeing them off, Rei headed toward where Set lay sprawled beside the guild.

Set had naturally witnessed the earlier duel—though calling it that was a stretch given how one-sided it was—and clearly recognized the vast difference in skill.

Lying down, he made no move to interfere with Rei and Melan's duel.

...Or perhaps he was concentrating on the sandwich someone had left behind.

"Grrrr..."

Seeing Rei, Set growled, leaving the sandwich he'd been eating while lying down untouched.

Seeing Set like that, Rei let out a small smile.

"Seriously, couldn't you at least pretend to be a little worried about me?"

"Grrr?"

Did I need to worry? Rei gently stroked Set's head as he purred.

In winter, Set's body and fur were warm, but now it was summer.

That's probably why people don't gather around to pet Set like they usually do.

Though, of course, there were those who didn't care about that, hugging Set regardless of the season.

Seeing the sandwich Set was eating made Rei a little hungry, so he took a sandwich out of his Misty Ring and started eating it too.

"Growl..."

Seeing Rei eat, Set must have thought it looked delicious. He purred, wanting some too.

Rei, who was somehow always lenient with Set, shared his sandwich with him while waiting for Vihera and Byune to appear.
