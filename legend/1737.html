"Grrrrrrr!"

"Hm? Oh, that town. Set, good job finding it."

At Rei’s words, Set purrs happily.

It had taken even Set several hours to reach the town where the alchemist capable of creating special potions resided.

Considering Set’s flying speed, Rei could all too well understand just how far away that town was from Golz.

“Still, I’m really glad Set answered my call right away.”

“Growl? Grrrr!”

“Is that so?” Set tilted his head in a puzzled manner.

In fact, he had appeared within minutes of Rei leaving Golz and calling out Set’s name at the top of his lungs.

And since Rei had asked him casually, and Set didn’t have anything else to do, they set off right away.

……That said, Yellow, who had been playing with Set, looked a little displeased, but when Vihera stroked him, he closed his eyes in pleasure and quickly forgot his anger.

“So… um, could you land somewhere where there aren’t any people?”

Rei noticed a few people walking along the road pointing at the sky—or, more precisely, at Set flying through the sky.

It was easy to predict that if Set were to land right next to their destination, the town of Somis, it would undoubtedly cause a commotion.

To prevent that, he decided to have Set land in a deserted spot and then walk toward Somis.

As if understanding Rei’s thoughts, Set let out a low growl and landed a short distance from Somis.

Naturally, upon seeing the griffin land near Somis, the city’s guards grew tense and approached cautiously—though with a hint of fear.

But then, the guards noticed that someone was with the griffin... and as they realized who it was, they let out a cry of surprise.

“Excuse me, but are you by any chance Rei of Crimson?”

Rei, who had been walking alongside Set, nodded at the guard’s call.

“That’s right. I was asked by the magic item shop in Golz to come to Somis. I have business with an alchemist here named Wock.”

Saying this, Rei takes her guild card out of her Misty Ring and shows it to the guard.

Of course, Set, standing right beside Rei, was the clearest indication of who Rei really was.

As far as anyone knows, Rei is the only person who has a griffin as a familiar.

Still, just to be on the safe side, the guard checked his credentials and let out a sigh of relief.

“I’ve confirmed you’re Rei himself, and I understand why you’ve come to Somis. Wock’s workshop is a bit hard to find—would you like a guide?”

“That would be a big help. With a guard around, I won’t get hassled by any weirdos.”

In reality, with Set around, there’s hardly anyone who would normally think of bothering him.

However, there are many people in the world who believe the world should revolve around them, and there’s no guarantee such people aren’t in this town.

Given that, having a guard accompany them just in case something happened would undoubtedly be a huge help in preventing any trouble down the line.

After completing the entry procedures and receiving their familiar necklaces, Rei and Set entered Somis accompanied by the guards.

(Inside… well, it’s not much different from Golz. No, actually, it’s slightly more prosperous and lively than Golz, isn’t it?)

Perhaps due to the preconception that alchemists capable of creating special potions—ones that alchemists in Golz couldn’t make—gathered here, Somis seemed, to Rei’s eyes, to be slightly more prosperous than Golz.

That said, the fact that both cities pale in comparison to Gilm… well, that’s partly because it’s simply the truth, but it also has to do with the fact that Rei considers Gilm to be the place he belongs.

Even as he thought this, Rei and his companions pressed on, drawing the attention of the townspeople.

The fact that the townspeople didn’t seem to make a fuss even though Set was there was thanks to the familiar’s necklace… and, above all, the presence of the guards was a major factor.

If anything happens, the guards will probably handle it...

In reality, ordinary guards wouldn’t stand a chance against Set, but the townspeople believe that’s the case and feel reassured.

Of course, unless something truly extraordinary happens, Set isn’t likely to cause a disturbance, so there’s no need to worry about the residents’ concerns.

The only thing that’s a bit of a shame is…

(Even though I’ve finally come to a new town, I can’t eat any of the local specialties.)

That’s right—it’s simply not a situation where I can afford to buy food on the go.

Since this was a town I’d never visited before, there might be dishes and ingredients I’d never tasted.

It would be a lie to say I didn’t have those expectations while flying through the sky with Set.

But… given the current situation, there was absolutely no way I could even suggest grabbing a quick bite to eat.

Above all, it was clear that the owners of the food stalls and restaurants were too scared to serve us properly.

“Well, I guess it can’t be helped.”

“What’s wrong?”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s mutter, a guard asked.

But Rei shook his head at the guard, saying it was nothing.

“No, nothing. I was just thinking I’d like to make a few detours if possible. But looking at the situation, it doesn’t seem like I’ll be able to do that.”

“Ah… I guess so.”

The guard looked around and nodded in agreement.

The residents, having suddenly encountered a griffin, were casting threatening glances at Set—and, depending on the situation, even at Rei, who was leading him.

Since the guard had been traveling with Rei and Set, even if only for a short time, he understood to some extent that Set was following Rei’s orders.

However, there was no way he could allow just one person and one creature to freely wander off like that.

That said, since they had entered through proper channels, the guards had no authority to interfere with Rei and the others’ actions.

Even so, the reason Rei and his group did not act in such a manner was because they felt that suddenly starting to move around with Set would startle the innocent residents.

Of course, if there were some reason—such as an actual emergency—Rei had no intention of hesitating to take action.

In other words, unless it was an emergency, he had no intention of doing such a thing.

Rei shook his head at the guard, who was looking at him with a somewhat apologetic expression, signaling him not to worry.

“I don’t plan on forcing anything, and once I’ve done what I need to do, I’ll leave town right away, so don’t worry about it.”

“Is that so? I’m sorry. But if there’s anything you need, just let me know. If it’s something I can do, I’ll do my best to help.”

“Is that so? Well, I’ll pay you later, so it’d be great if you could pick up some of the town’s local specialties for me.”

“……Huh?”

The words that came out of Rei’s mouth must have been completely unexpected.

The guard shot him a look that seemed to say, “What on earth is this guy talking about?”

But looking at Rei’s expression, it was immediately clear that this wasn’t a joke or anything of the sort—he was speaking from the bottom of his heart.

He had assumed they would ask for something like a weapon or intelligence… but he had completely caught the guard off guard.

Mind you, Rei didn’t do it with any ulterior motive; he simply spoke his mind.

"Alright, I’ll leave it to you."

Hearing Rei repeat those words, the guard instinctively looks up at the sky.

The sky, still bright enough, confirmed that what the guard had heard was neither a dream nor anything of the sort.

Since they were walking, there was a risk of bumping into passersby or objects, but perhaps because he was a guard accustomed to walking the streets, that didn’t happen.

……Though the fact that the townspeople were keeping their distance from Rei and Set certainly helped.

“Got it. While you’re at Wock’s workshop, I’ll pick up something. Something local, right?”

“Yeah. Do that. Luckily, it’s autumn now, so there must be all sorts of delicious ingredients. This town must have some local specialties of its own, right?”

It was a somewhat provocative remark, but it lit a fire in the heart of the guard, who was a native of this town and naturally loved his hometown.

Even after being told this, it wasn’t pleasant for the guard to be thought of as someone who didn’t have any delicious food in Somis.

“Fine. Then I’ll prepare a dish that’s guaranteed to surprise you. …Ah, that’s Wock’s workshop over there.”

Since they had been walking while talking, Wock’s workshop—their destination—soon came into view.

It wasn’t a particularly conspicuous workshop; it didn’t look like the kind of place where someone capable of making special potions would live.

(Wait, considering the size of Somis, isn’t it actually normal for it not to be that big?)

Rei didn’t know many alchemists’ workshops.

Even considering those, none of them were particularly large workshops.

Thinking this, he continued on… and eventually arrived.

“Sorry, but that griffin over there can’t come in. Please wait outside. …You’ll be okay, right?”

“Yeah, no problem. Hey, Set.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a “I’m fine!”-style growl, then simply lay down a short distance away.

It was a spot that wouldn’t obstruct the path in front of the workshop, and seeing him there, it didn’t seem like Set would try to get in the way of passersby.

Relieved by this, the guard entered the workshop with Rei.

“Wock, are you there?”

“Hm? Oh, Mr. Welzas. What’s up?”

Fortunately, Wock was inside the workshop, organizing some paperwork.

While this was technically part of his job, the fact that he didn’t seem particularly busy was a relief to the guard… and to Welzas as well.

“This person seems to want to talk to you. …It’s Rei of Crimson. You’ve at least heard the name, right?”

“Huh? Crimson… you mean… the one who supposedly burned the entire Bestir Empire Army to the ground?”

“Just how exaggerated has this story gotten… I only burned the vanguard unit, and I didn’t burn everyone in it.”

Rumors tend to grow as they spread, but Rei naturally wasn’t happy about that.

He corrects the man in front of him—a man in his early thirties—as if to say, “Well, sort of…”

“Whoa… I know rumors get exaggerated, but is it still true that you burned them? Come to think of it, I heard Crimson has a griffin as a familiar… is that griffin not here?”

Wock fixes Rei with a look of intense curiosity.

But that’s only natural. For an alchemist, a Rank A monster like a griffin is nothing less than a dream come true—a treasure trove of materials.

Lately, with the expansion of Gilm, more people have been gathering here, and the fact that quite a few alchemists are among them is undoubtedly because they hope that by staying in Gilm, they might be able to obtain materials from a griffin—specifically, a rare species—a monster equivalent to Rank S.

In fact, some of the alchemists in Gilm have managed to obtain Set materials—things like shed fur and feathers.

Given that, it wouldn’t be surprising if others started thinking, “If they can do it, so can I!”

“Set is sleeping outside. It’s not impossible to get inside… but it would’ve been better that way, I suppose.”

To carry out the magic items made in this workshop, the door naturally has to be larger than the items themselves.

In the worst-case scenario, one could resort to breaking down a wall... but such methods couldn’t be used frequently.

As a countermeasure, the workshop door had been made sufficiently wide.

Wide enough, in fact, that Set could slip through if he wanted to.

But judging by the townspeople’s reactions, there’s no way he could pull something like that off.

Furthermore, even if he could get inside, it’s an undeniable fact that the space is cramped.

Given that, there’s no doubt that having Set wait outside is the best course of action.

……After all, you never know if someone might come along with some foolish idea and try to hassle Rei.

“Just so you know, I didn’t come here to talk about Set’s materials or anything like that.”

“Come to think of it, why would a famous person like you come to see me?”

At those words, Rei handed him a letter he’d taken out of his Misty Ring.

……Perhaps because he’s an alchemist, Wock’s gaze naturally turns to Rei’s Misty Ring, showing his interest in the item box.

Still, once he was handed the letter, he read it…

“Huh? I thought I’d already taken that potion a long time ago…”

he said.
