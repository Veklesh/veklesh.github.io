The exchange of information between Rei and the guards resumes immediately.

“So, were you able to get any information from the Red Cloths? I mean, from their perspective, wouldn’t they have information about where we’ve been all this time?”

If the Red Cloths were being used by the ones responsible for the Kobold incident, it wouldn’t be surprising if they had some kind of clue.

Of course, it wasn’t certain that the Red Cloth held any crucial information, but there was a possibility they possessed vital details without even realizing it. Even if the information seemed trivial, combining it with insights from many others might yield a significant clue.

With that in mind, Rei asked, but the only response he got was a shake of the head.

“The people behind this incident must have thought that through carefully. I sent someone to the place where Red Cloth used to live, but there wasn’t a single clue to be found.”

The fact that he moved so quickly before Rei even spoke was partly due to his profession as a guard, but it was likely also largely because he was furious that his comrades had been attacked.

“I see. …So, does that mean we’re back to square one? It’s a hassle, or maybe just a pain in the neck. I wonder what we should do. We still haven’t found a single clue.”

“Don’t worry. We’re still conducting our own on-the-ground investigation to gather information. That’s the kind of work we’re better at.”

“Ah… I suppose so.”

In Rei’s view, the guards were essentially the police… no, in a more direct sense, given their use of force, they were more like an armed police force.

Even so, since they are the ones maintaining order in Gilm, they enjoy the deep trust of the residents.

Naturally, not all security guards are good people; just as you’d expect, there are some among them who engage in criminal activities behind the scenes.

But even so, overall, the security guards are viewed favorably by the residents.

That’s precisely why, even if I go around asking people for information directly, many are willing to cooperate.

“That’s your area of expertise, so I’ll leave it to you. Given how many people live in Gilm, there’s bound to be a clue lying around somewhere.”

“Yeah, that’s for sure. To begin with, if you consider the ones Rei encountered, the Red Cloth members who attacked the group we were watching today, and the number of Red Cloth members who were active back when they were moving around in force, just preparing food and supplies for that many people would be pretty conspicuous.”

“Besides, the Red Cloth crew is mostly made up of selfish young punks. I don’t really think guys like that would just sit quietly…”

Another guard, different from the one Rei had been talking to, said this.

Rei understood that the Red Cloth was indeed a group of hot-blooded, easily provoked individuals, so he found himself nodding in agreement with that opinion.

“I get what you’re saying. I get it, but… in the end, we still haven’t found any leads, have we?”

“...That’s true. Well, the Red Cloths got their name because they used to tie red cloths somewhere on their bodies. If you think about it that way, if they take off the red cloth and pretend to be ordinary people, you probably wouldn’t be able to tell them apart from anyone else.”

“But someone who happens to recognize a Red Cloth would still spot them, right? In fact, yesterday, an adventurer happened to identify the opponent as a Red Cloth, and that’s how I figured out who they were too.”

“That could happen, I suppose. …But the odds of not getting caught are higher than the odds of getting caught, right?”

“Considering Gilm’s population, there’s no doubt the odds of not getting caught are higher.”

As another guard said this, the others nodded in agreement.

In fact, if someone were to say that yesterday’s incident was just a matter of luck, Rei would have to admit they were right.

(In the end, even though there was a major development, it didn’t quite lead to a clue connecting us to the person behind this incident…)

When Lenora rushed over, Rei had definitely thought she might have some kind of lead… but it turned out to be a bit different from what he’d expected.

“More importantly, the real problem is… why the people pulling the strings noticed the guards.”

With an indescribable expression, one of the guards muttered.

Rei understood exactly what that implied.

In other words, it meant there might be someone among the guards who was in cahoots with those moving the red cloth.

Naturally, the guards didn’t want to suspect their own comrades, but given the situation, it was highly likely that information from someone monitoring the base had been leaked to the enemy.

“How many of them knew about the lookouts?”

“If we’re talking about those who actually knew, it’s quite a few people. I wrote it in the report.”

“...If that’s the case, it’s going to be really hard to figure out who leaked the information.”

As Rei said this, several of the guards in the room wore looks of distaste.

Even though they knew it was a strong possibility, they probably didn’t like the idea of suspecting their own comrades.

But the fact remained that it was the most likely scenario, and given the current situation, they couldn’t afford not to suspect them.

(Besides, if we don’t get a handle on this now, if there really is a traitor, our information will keep leaking to the other side indefinitely.)

As a guard, I’d prefer to avoid suspecting my comrades.

But it’s also an undeniable fact that if they don’t get a handle on this, there really won’t be anything they can do about it.

That’s exactly why, no matter how much the guards in front of him might hate it, Rei is in a bind unless they stay vigilant.

Perhaps the guards understand this, because eventually, the guard who had been speaking with Rei nods, though with a bitter expression.

“I understand. We’ll take immediate action on our end, so don’t worry about that.”

The other guards standing nearby seemed to have their own thoughts about the guard who said that, but given the current situation, they couldn’t very well voice their dissatisfaction.

“I’m counting on you. …Is there anything else you need to tell me?”

At Rei’s words, the guard said, “Well then…” and they went through various detailed arrangements.

That said, while most of it was related to this incident, it wasn’t particularly important.

For example, regarding the group believed to be the Red Cloth, and what to do with the captives from here on out.

According to the guard, the likely outcome was that they would be classified as criminal slaves and sold off.

Since they had attacked the guards hired by Daskar to maintain order in Gilm, that outcome was only natural.

That said, even as criminal slaves, their crimes were considerably lighter than those of common thieves, so their time in slavery wouldn’t be all that long.

In fact, they were supposed to be freed from their status as criminal slaves by the end of this year at the earliest… or by next year at the very latest.

...That is, unless he showed absolutely no motivation for his work despite becoming a criminal slave.

After wrapping up their conversation, Rei left the guardhouse.

“Well, let me know if anything else comes up. …I never would’ve thought Lenora would come riding up on a horse to call for me.”

“Lenora?”

Many of the guards probably knew who Lenora was.

At Rei’s words, several of them wore expressions that seemed to say, “You’ve got to be kidding.”

Mind you, since Lenora doesn’t exactly look like the type who could handle a horse in her everyday life, it’s perhaps understandable that they’d think that.

……The reason the word “demure” doesn’t come to mind is probably because her comedic banter with Kenny is so famous.

“Ah. To be honest, I had no idea Lenora was such a skilled rider. Though I suppose it makes sense, given she’s a guild employee.”

It’s not uncommon for guild staff to transport adventurers long distances by carriage, depending on the situation.

Usually, such work falls to former adventurers or lower-ranking staff, but if things get too busy and no one else is available, it’s possible the receptionist might have to step in.

If it turned out they couldn’t actually drive a carriage or ride a horse, and that led to unnecessary injuries or deaths, that would be absolutely unacceptable for a guild employee.

“Guild staff are a pretty impressive bunch, after all.”

One of the guards muttered under his breath.

In reality, being hired as a guild official requires exceptional ability; it’s a workplace that truly deserves to be described as a “narrow gate.”

If you were to ask which is harder—becoming a guard or a Guild official—unless you were completely out of touch with reality, you’d probably answer “Guild official.”

……That said, while becoming a guard is easy, the training is extremely rigorous due to the need to maintain order in Gilm, and whether one can actually stick with it is another matter entirely.

“Well, I’m going to be heading out now. …I know you’ve got your own share of challenges, but do your best.”

“Leave it to me.”

Exchanging those few words, Rei left the guardhouse.

In front of the guardhouse, a guard was carefully scanning the surroundings just as he had been earlier, but upon seeing Rei emerge, he gave a slight nod.

Rei didn’t know exactly what that nod meant.

However, he figured it must mean something to the guard, so he didn’t voice his curiosity and simply nodded back.

“Growl!”

Noticing Rei emerge from the guardhouse, Set approached him, purring happily.

……At that moment, Rei vaguely understood why a scent that whetted the appetite was wafting from Set’s mouth.

Rei guessed that while he had been talking inside the guardhouse, someone must have come by to pet Set.

That said, a guard was standing a short distance away from Set, scanning the surroundings with a tense expression, so it seemed they hadn’t been able to pet Set for very long.

“I was going to say, ‘Did I keep you waiting?’ but it looks like Set had plenty of fun on his own.”

Under normal circumstances, making someone wait outside in this cold would be fair game for criticism.

But that’s only under normal circumstances; in Set’s case, it’s not a problem at all.

After all, Set can sleep soundly without showing the slightest sign of feeling cold, even if he’s buried in snow in the dead of winter.

That said, there’s no doubt that sleeping in a place like a stable would be more comfortable for Set, though.

“Growl? Growl!”

Set purrs happily in response to Rei’s words.

Assuming he must have eaten something quite delicious, Rei strokes Set as he rubs his head against her.

“Ahem.”

As Rei played with Set, a guard standing in front of the guardhouse cleared his throat softly.

Deciding that staying there with Set would be a nuisance, Rei leaves the area with him.

“So, it turns out this whole incident was about a guard who was watching over the base getting attacked.”

“Grr…”

As Rei spoke regretfully while walking, Set purred encouragingly.

Rei stroked Set, who was walking beside him, as he pondered what to do.

(Some of the guards were arguing that since the enemy already knew we were guarding the base, we should just go ahead and force our way into the building to search it… but I wonder if that’s really the best idea.)

Since they went so far as to attack the guards on watch, it’s only natural that any important items inside the base have already been taken, so I doubt we’d find anything if we searched it now.

On the contrary, depending on the situation, it’s impossible to rule out the possibility that some kind of trap has been set.

Some of the guards even voiced such concerns.

But since there might still be something unexpected left behind, and since there were no other places to search, the others had no choice but to agree.

(If we hire thieves… would we be able to detect any traps before they go off? Still, I wonder if we can even hire thieves… no, maybe I should ask Egg to make the arrangements.)

Naturally, among Egg’s subordinates are thieves who are also adventurers.

If that’s the case, it wouldn’t be strange for the guards to have dispatched such people this time.

“Grr? Grrrr!”

Suddenly, Rei felt his Dragon Robe being tugged.

When he looked over, there was, of course, Set.

Having tugged at Rei’s Dragon Robe with his beak, Set’s gaze was fixed on a food stall a short distance away.

A scent that whets the appetite wafts from there.

Even though Set had received food from someone in front of the guardhouse, he insisted, “I’m still hungry!”

Petting Set as he spoke, Rei headed toward the food stall.
