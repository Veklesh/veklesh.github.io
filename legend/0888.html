Rei returned to the guild after leaving Gamelion at the training grounds.

What greeted Rei was the usual sight: a sparsely populated guild hall and a crowded tavern.

(Wait, is the tavern even busier than usual?)

Perhaps influenced by the first snowfall, the tavern was bustling with noise.

Not just adventurers, but many merchants and townspeople had also shown up.

Many had likely come seeking drink to ward off the cold.

Since it was already dinnertime, many had come seeking not just drink but also food.

(Where are Mireine and the others...?)

As they head toward the training grounds, they look around for the members of the Scorching Winds they parted ways with earlier, but they aren't at the guild.

Rei concluded they had likely completed their quest procedures, received their rewards, and left. He headed toward the counter.

Few people were either seeking quests or reporting completions at the guild; most receptionists were busy organizing paperwork or chatting.

Amidst this, anyone heading towards the counter naturally stood out... and the two familiar receptionists, Lenora and Rei, immediately noticed them.

"Ah, Rei. Good work today. Is it about the Gamelion?"

"Good work today, Rei-kun. Looks like it's started snowing. Wasn't it cold outside?"

Smiling as they asked, Rei also smiled back and spoke.

"Yeah, I was surprised when it suddenly started snowing, but I'm glad it wasn't during the Gamelion hunt."

"...I heard from Mireine-san. You really did go Gamelion hunting, didn't you? After defeating so many in the dungeon, isn't that a bit excessive? Considering Gamelion resource conservation, I'd prefer you not hunt too much right now, regardless of the dungeon situation."

Lenora had relayed the message awkwardly, but Rei shook his head at her words.

"No, it's not like I hunted Gamelions myself. I was just helping out. I didn't actually fight any Gamelions. All I did was scout for them from the sky using Set and carry the carcasses."

"...If Set-chan scouted from the sky, it seems like you could find Gamelions right away."

"Well, it is Set."

"Look, Rei. Like I said before, please be careful not to hunt too many Gamelions. If you hunt them excessively out of momentary greed, it'll cause problems next year and beyond."

"Ah, I'll be careful. But I had my reasons too."

That reason being the offer of top-quality cheese as a reward, or to help Johanna and the others adjust to life in Gilm, but he didn't voice that much.

...Especially since mentioning cheese as the reason would guarantee a lecture.

"More importantly, Rei, Rei, you've made good progress butchering the Gamelion you defeated in the dungeon. What are you going to do with it? The materials, the meat?"

Perhaps dissatisfied that only Lenora was talking to Rei, Kenny cut in with that question.

If it were any other time, Lenora could have told him to mind his own business since it was evening and she was busy.

But this time of year, there were few adventurers active, and Kenny had already finished all his tasks.

Given the situation, Lenora couldn't really scold Kenny.

Rei, too, seeing this as a chance to avoid Lenora's nagging, turned to Kenny.

"Huh, you're already done?"

"Not completely, though. How many days do you think have passed since then? Well, it seems true that more people went out to hunt Gamelions than expected, so it took longer than anticipated."

"Then, we'll take the magic stones and meat here, but we'd like to purchase the other materials."

"The purchase of materials, magic stones, and meat is supposed to happen after everything's finished. For now, shall we just take the meat we have on hand?"

Doing the transaction in multiple parts might make the paperwork a hassle.

That thought flashed through his mind for an instant, but in the end, he succumbed to the temptation of Gamelion meat and nodded to Kenny.

"Yeah, let's do that. So, can we get it right now?"

"Um... just wait a moment."

"Hey, wait, Kenny!?"

Ignoring Lenora's attempt to stop him, Kenny headed straight for his boss at the back of the counter.

Lenora watched him go with a look of displeasure, but in the end, she said nothing more. She sighed and returned to her seat to look over the documents.

(Unusual)

Rei murmured to himself, observing Lenora's reaction.

Usually, she'd use her strength to drag Kenny back, he thought.

Of course, in terms of pure physical strength, Lenora, being human, was no match for Kenny, a beastman.

In that sense, it wasn't that Kenny usually held back with Lenora... it was simply the result of their everyday power dynamic.

"Speaking of which, Rei. Do you have any plans set for this winter?"

Perhaps noticing Rei's gaze on her, Lenora looked up from her papers and asked.

Rei shook his head at the question.

"No, I don't really have any plans. I figure I'll just take it easy. This past year has been busy in all sorts of ways."

Spring brought the war with the Bestir Empire, summer saw battles against the Remleas in Emotion and dungeon runs in the Labyrinth City, and autumn involved the Bestir Empire's martial arts tournament and civil unrest.

Those were just the major events; counting the minor incidents would add even more.

It was simply too much to experience consecutively in a single year.

In terms of density, it was a series of events so significant that an ordinary adventurer might not experience them all in several years, or even a lifetime.

Given all that, it was only natural to want to relax during winter.

Knowing just how many troubles Rei had been caught up in, Lenora let out a small sigh before speaking.

"It's true, Rei has been caught up in all sorts of trouble, so taking a slow rest might be good. But to avoid resting too much and getting sluggish, then finding you can't move when spring comes... I think it's better to move your body occasionally."

"Yeah, that would be a problem. Thanks for the advice. I'll make sure to get some proper exercise now and then."

Even as he answered, the fact remained that he had to hunt monsters to feed Set, meaning he inevitably needed to venture outside Gilm.

Of course, he had enough money to feed Set properly, but he still felt it was better not to waste it unnecessarily.

Besides, staying cooped up in the stables all winter, only able to walk around town and not really get a good workout, would undoubtedly cause Set stress.

Considering all that, venturing outside Gilm occasionally for hunting was an absolutely necessary activity.

"Ah, speaking of which, Baron Sisne came by saying he had a specific request if Rei was here... What should we do?"

"Baron Sisne? ...Ah, Baslero."

Lenora's words reminded him of the child he'd trained in combat last winter.

Though it was combat training, it wasn't anything as serious as what Rei did with the guerrilla squad. It was more like teaching the absolute basics.

Considering the trainees were ten-year-olds, that was only natural.

If he'd done the kind of training he did with the guerrilla squad, the kid would probably have ended up with combat phobia.

"Yes, yes. It was a designated request last year. This time it's also technically a designated request... What do you say?"

Lenora's words made Rei hesitate slightly, but he eventually nodded.

"Alright, then. Let's do it. Snow's coming, everyone will be taking proper breaks, and it's true we won't have anything to do until spring."

"Alright, I'll contact them then... but what about the date?"

"Leave it to them. I'm basically free every day anyway."

"I see. Well then..."

As Lenora wrote something on the paperwork, Kenny returned.

"Hey, Rei. I got the approval... Huh? What's up, Lenora?"

"Here, Rei-san's request for assignment."

"Oh, right, there was that. Are you taking it, Rei?"

"It's started snowing, and I've got nothing better to do every day."

"Hmm... Well, Baron Sisne doesn't have a bad reputation, so it should probably be fine. Alright, let's head to the warehouse. Honestly, having no work and being bored isn't all bad, is it?"

She draped a shawl over her uniform, stepped out from behind the counter, and immediately tugged Rei's hand.

Lenora watched Kenny's antics with a somewhat exasperated look.

Truthfully, they were definitely idle right now, and Kenny slipping away from the counter wouldn't have caused any trouble in this situation.

Of course, that was only with her boss's permission.

"Kenny, make sure you come back before it gets too late."

"Yeah, yeah, I know. That's why sisters-in-law are such a pain."

"...Who's the nagging sister-in-law?"

"Oh no! The nagging sister-in-law got mad. Let's go, Rei-kun. It's a romantic getaway!"

"Kenny!"

Kenny deliberately announced it loud enough for Lenora to hear, then grabbed Rei's hand and headed straight for the guild exit.

"Hee-hee, I'm jealous!"

"No, no. That's not envy, that's jealousy."

"Kenny-chan, take me on a romantic getaway toooo!"

"Dammit, Kenny, why'd you go with a guy like that..."

"Well, I mean, he's got good looks, he's a Rank B adventurer at that age, and he's got a nickname. ...It's impossible for him not to be popular."

"Shut up! Don't suddenly get serious like that!"

Leaving the taunting voices echoing from the tavern behind them, Rei was led by the hand out of the guild by Kenny.

The first thing the two saw upon leaving the guild was...

"Set-chan, how about this? Or maybe this one? Oh, and these skewers too..."

...was the sight of Mireine offering food she'd likely bought from a nearby stall to Set.

And this was happening in the snow.

The absence of Surnin, who was also Mireine's guardian, was likely because he had already completed his escort duties and was now free to act on his own.

Rei briefly considered calling out to Set, but seeing how happily Set was being petted by Mireine, he simply walked away.

Of course, there was no way Set hadn't noticed Rei's presence. When those round eyes turned toward Rei, she gave a slight wave, intending to signal she'd be back shortly.

The fact that Set understood Rei's signal like that showed just how deep the bond between them was.

If Rei or Johanna heard that, they'd surely writhe in jealousy.

"Hey, is that okay, Rei?"

"I'll be back soon anyway. Let's just hurry to the warehouse."

"Well, if it's okay with you, Rei. Then let's go on a little date, shall we?"

Kenny said teasingly, pulling Rei's hand toward the warehouse.

True to Kenny's word, they arrived at the warehouse almost instantly.

Since they'd been moving outside in the snow, Rei brushed snow off his hood and Kenny brushed it off his uniform before entering the warehouse.

Kenny, in particular, was wearing a shawl, but his uniform had a large opening at the chest.

Inside the guild, magic items kept them warm, but outside in the snow, it was naturally cold.

Even in such conditions, wearing a uniform with such high exposure suggested Kenny had some sort of personal policy.

In fact, it was true that Kenny's uniform was popular among adventurers visiting the guild.

In that sense, Kenny insisted she was doing her best as the receptionist, who was also the guild's face.

"Well then, shall we go? You'll be surprised, I promise?"

Listening to Kenny's mischievous smile as she spoke, Rei scanned the warehouse interior... and was indeed surprised, just as she'd said.

The floor of the warehouse, once completely covered with Gamelion corpses, was now mostly cleared. Most of the bodies had been disassembled, stripped of materials, and of course, their magic stones extracted.

Given that each Gamelion carcass was about three meters long, they had nearly filled the warehouse floor when brought in, but now even that was gone.

Of course, not every Gamelion had been processed, so a fair number of corpses still lay scattered about.

And in place of the Gamelion corpses, meat, materials, and magic stones were now laid out.

"This is certainly surprising. ...It's quite a sight to behold like this."

Near the Gamelion carcasses, several adventurers were still busy dissecting them and stripping off materials.

Their technique was precise, clearly more skilled than Rei's.

"Well then, Rei. Shall we choose the Gamelion meat to take?"

Nodding at Kenny's words, Rei reached out toward the Gamelion's meat.
