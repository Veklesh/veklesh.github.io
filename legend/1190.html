Upon hearing Set's cry, Rei ran toward the sound alongside Vihera.

Though Vihera started running a little later than Rei, she quickly caught up, partly because Rei had slowed his pace.

"What do you think that cry from Set was just now? A monster, maybe?"

Running alongside him, Vihera asked. Rei nodded in response.

"Judging by that cry, it's probably a monster for sure. But if it was just a regular monster appearing, Set would've taken care of it himself already."

Vihera, who knew Set's strength, nodded in agreement.

He masterfully wields a wide variety of skills while possessing the high physical prowess of a griffin. On top of that, he has magic items that enhance his physical abilities and can even nullify an enemy's ranged attacks, at least once.

And being a griffin, he could naturally fly through the air.

In this particular incident, no swarm of flying monsters had been confirmed.

That made sense, since these were subhuman monsters forming a pack under a leader species.

(Wait, could harpies technically be considered demi-humans too?)

As Vihera ran, pondering such things, Set's figure eventually came into view ahead.

"Set, what's wrong!?"

"Grrrrr!"

At Rei's call, Set directed his gaze toward a tree a short distance away.

Following Rei's gaze, Rei and Vihera spotted a werewolf shrouded in black mist, understanding why Set had let out such a growl.

Seeing the werewolf cloaked in black mist, a smile touched Rei's lips.

Yes, in terms of being shrouded in black mist, it was the same as the goblin that had evolved into a leader species when he first saw Ambris.

But... the amount of black mist enveloping it was incomparably greater than that of the goblin. Seeing this werewolf now, it was immediately clear that the black mist the goblin had once worn was merely a remnant of Ambris.

And above all else, the moment he saw that black mist, Rei instantly recognized it as Ambris.

There was no clear reason for it.

Still, Rei understood instinctively, not rationally, that the being present with the werewolf was Ambris.

"I never thought we'd find it twice in a row. This was completely unexpected. ...What do you think? Do you believe this is coincidence?"

"It's coincidence, right? Besides, having Set gives us an advantage in search range compared to other adventurers. ...More importantly, what do we do?"

As Vihera asked while watching the werewolf and Ambris, Rei opened his mouth with a smile.

"It's obvious. We have to take this thing down; this whole mess won't settle otherwise. Since we've run into it here, we're obviously going to finish it off right here."

In this case, it was fortunate that Ambris lacked self-awareness.

The sight of the werewolf evolving into a leader species was not something you'd normally want humans to see.

Even if it wasn't an on-the-spot evolution like with the goblin.

Just witnessing this scene alone could yield a wealth of information.

Normally, this should have been something Ambris avoided.

A major reason Ambris is considered troublesome is the mystery surrounding its biology.

The unknown can be a source of fear for humans.

Any revelation that unravels even a fraction of that mystery can only be detrimental to the Umbrus.

Of course, that judgment assumes the existence of self-awareness.

For an Ambris without self-awareness, the only thing to do is to make the humanoid monsters their leader species.

This time, that worked to Rei and the others' advantage.

Precisely because it lacks self-awareness, it doesn't flee the scene even when faced with hostile entities like Rei and Vihera.

(Wait, maybe it can't move when it's trying to make someone a leader species? Anyway, I can't tell for sure since it's attached to the werewolf, but it seems smaller than before?)

As he drew the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring, Rei fixed his gaze on the werewolf and Ambris.

Even with Rei and Vihera nearby, and even as Rei assumed a combat stance with Death Scythe and Twilight Spear drawn, Ambris remained motionless.

To Rei's eyes, it seemed smaller than the Ambris he had seen before.

But perhaps it was because it was attached to the werewolf, or maybe evolving into a leader species caused it to shrink—neither explanation was particularly surprising.

"...Vihera, I think it'll probably be okay..."

Rei had already heard how he planned to defeat Ambris beforehand.

Given that, Vhela had no intention of arguing with Rei's words.

She and Set moved away from Rei.

Moreover, the fact that Vihera obeyed Rei so readily was likely largely because Ambris lacked self-awareness and didn't understand the act of combat itself.

All Ambris could do was make humanoid monsters into leader species.

Nothing else.

Even so, because physical attacks were ineffective and it possessed powerful magical defense due to being a being born from magical aberration, no one had ever been able to harm Ambris until now.

But... now, Rei was here.

Rei's immense magical power compressed and condensed.

Soon, the fire-attribute magic power within Rei reached a density visible to the eye... and a visible red magic power began to entwine itself around Rei's entire body.

The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. It was Rei’s strongest skill at present, and for Rei, who possessed immense magical power, it was the skill that allowed him to utilize that power to its fullest extent.

The werewolf shrouded in black mist, and Rei, enveloped in red magical power condensed to the point of visibility.

At first glance, the one and the beast seemed remarkably similar.

But their very essence was entirely different.

Even facing Rei wielding the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the werewolf, as expected... and Ambris showed no sign of moving at all.

It merely surrendered itself to the black mist of Ambris's true form near the tree.

"Well, better than running away, I suppose."

Rei shifted his gaze to the Twilight Spear clutched in his left hand. The spear, possessing a deep Crimson hue that matched the red magic enveloping Rei's body, showed no particular signs of distress.

A normal magic item would likely break, shatter, or melt under the immense magical power Rei unleashed... but the Twilight Spear showed no change whatsoever, even when exposed to Rei's magic power while the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour was active.

(This spear really is something special.)

Rei murmured inwardly as he gripped the handle of the Twilight Spear, crafted from the diverse materials he had gathered.

In his current state, using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, he should undoubtedly be able to defeat Ambris.

And certainly, the werewolf being turned into a leader species by Ambris would be no match for him.

Holding both weapons, Rei confirmed Vihera and Set had retreated to safety before fixing his gaze on Ambris once more and charging forward in one swift motion.

His physical abilities, enhanced by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, instantly closed the distance between him and Rei.

Despite the attacking presence closing in right before him, Ambris showed no reaction whatsoever, remaining wrapped around the werewolf.

"Vanish."

The Death Scythe swung with those brief words.

The grim reaper's strike from his right hand easily severed the werewolf's torso, while the Twilight Spear launched from his left hand simultaneously crushed its skull.

Brain matter, blood, bones, flesh, and eyeballs scattered across the surrounding ground, and as expected, the werewolf embarked on its journey to death.

The headless, torso-severed corpse of the werewolf collapsed to the ground... leaving only Ambris standing there.

But this caused Rei a slight moment of confusion.

The strike just now, unleashed using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, had undoubtedly struck both the werewolf and Ambris.

Meaning, for Ambris—whose pride in his magical defense was, admittedly, lacking any ego to boast about—it had been breached, and damage had been inflicted upon the core of the black mist.

Despite this situation, Ambris remained suspended in the air.

As if the werewolf's body were still there.

(What the hell?)

Rei was certain his strike couldn't possibly have been ineffective.

Yet, Ambris showed no sign of moving.

He was puzzled by this... but in the next instant, Ambris's form gradually began to fade, eventually disappearing.

The time from when it began fading until it vanished was less than five seconds.

...Though, in terms of time since defeating the werewolf, it had taken nearly a minute.

"Um... Huh? What? Did I defeat it? Did it escape? Which is it?"

Rei muttered in confusion at Ambris, who had vanished so easily from his single strike.

He was certain he'd finished it off. Yet at the same time, he wondered: Could Ambris really be defeated so easily?

"I think we defeated it. ...There's no black mist anywhere."

"Right?"

While agreeing with Vihera's words, Rei still couldn't shake the feeling something was off.

(Killed? No, Ambris wasn't exactly a living creature. So instead of 'killed,' wouldn't 'destroyed' or 'vanished' be more accurate? Whatever the reason, it's gone now... but is this really the end of it?)

This incident claimed many lives, and even more were injured.

And right now, many adventurers were still hunting down the monster hordes or searching for the Ambris that had caused it all.

Given the scale of the chaos, was it really acceptable for it to be resolved so easily with just one strike from Rei...?

That doubt lingered within Rei.

Seeing Rei's state, Vihera spoke up to calm him.

"I didn't expect it to end so easily either, but defeating Ambris is a fact. Shouldn't we report it to the guild?"

"...I see. Well, yeah. It's true that Ambris vanished, so we should report it."

While many aspects still didn't sit right with him, having defeated Ambris, reporting this incident was necessary.

If this meant the end of the current turmoil, it would be a huge relief for the residents of Gilm.

But... even so, a lingering doubt remained within Rei.

He just couldn't shake the feeling that this wasn't the end of it.

It felt like there might be another wave of trouble coming...

(But since Ambris is gone, maybe it's just my imagination?)

He wondered, though he had no concrete evidence to back it up.

It was only the intuition deep within Rei that insisted this matter was not yet resolved.

Given that, it might actually be all in my head.

(Maybe I should mention when I report this that it might not be over yet. ...Still, with Ambris gone, this should be the end of it.)

Still wondering, he released the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and stored the werewolf corpse lying on the ground—no, the corpse of the werewolf that was about to become a werewolf leader—into the Misty Ring.

Not as material, but because I thought it might reveal something about Ambris.

(From what I saw before defeating it, it seemed no different from a regular werewolf.)

Even so, it was a corpse with its torso severed vertically and its head crushed.

Realistically, examining it in detail in this state would be difficult.

Then again, if Ambris is truly dead, there's no rush to examine it.

"Anyway, let's head back for now. They might have been attacked too. ...Since we defeated Ambris, the leader species shouldn't appear anymore... right?"

The fact she didn't say it definitely won't appear suggests Vihera shares some of the unease Rei is feeling.

"Ah, Vihera! Rei! And Set too!"

Seeing Rei and the others return, Mina called out happily.

Beside Mina were Warain and Giacomo, mounted on horses.

All three wore expressions of relief and joy at seeing Rei and the others return safely.

...Though the horse Warain was riding had stopped the moment it saw Set.

"How was it? Did you find anything?"

"Yes. There was a werewolf lurking."

Vihera replied, perhaps deciding it best not to mention Ambris yet.

If they knew Rei and the others well, they would have known that a single werewolf wouldn't cause Set to make such a sound.

But the three of them—Walein and the others—had only just met Rei and the others today.

Unaware of the circumstances, they accepted Vihera's explanation.

...Though Giacomo, as always, was drooling over Vihera's alluring appearance.

"Well then, let's head to Gilm. Hopefully no more unnecessary monsters show up."

Everyone nodded at Rei's words and headed for Gilm. ...Though dealing with the horses, terrified of Set, was quite a hassle.
