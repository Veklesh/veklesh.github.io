The day after the absorption of the magic stones through Beast Magic was complete… Unlike the previous day, clouds covered the sky, but it still hadn’t started raining, and, as expected, the butchering of monsters continued near Golz, just as it had the day before.

That said, since the butchering of the two Kelpies and the Argos Bear had been finished yesterday, the only one left was the Galangiga.

...However, butchering that Galangiga was a real pain. While about eighty percent of the scales had been stripped off by yesterday, the task of pulling out the spines embedded within them was only about thirty percent complete.

“We’re lucky it didn’t have a head, aren’t we? If it had a head, butchering it would’ve been even harder.”

One of the adventurers working on the Garangiga muttered with a weary expression, and no one disagreed.

To begin with, the Galangiga the adventurers are butchering is of a size that’s incomparable to a normal Galangiga.

And since the head has such a complex structure, it goes without saying that dealing with it is overwhelmingly more difficult than dealing with other parts.

Just imagine what it would be like if the head—which is already such a hassle to butcher—belonged to a monster as massive as the one the adventurers are currently butchering… There’s no doubt that the amount of work involved would be significantly different.

“You’re right. If we’d messed up, we might not have finished today and could’ve ended up having to do this again tomorrow.”

Another man working alongside the one who had muttered those words replied without seeming particularly bothered.

In fact, this job—where simply butchering monsters provided a decent income—was a very lucrative one for the man… and for the others as well.

Especially since the dungeon had been cleared, the guild had advised everyone to stay away from that cliff for a while. Dismantling monsters—which meant easy money (in this case, in terms of avoiding mortal danger)—was a welcome job for many.

Even after receiving the guild’s notice, there were still some heading toward the cliff where the dungeon was… or rather, had been, but at least the group currently dismembering monsters here had no intention of doing anything so foolish.

That said, this job isn’t all smooth sailing either.

“Hey, you there. It’s fine to chat, but don’t neglect your work.”

A warning came from the guild official supervising the work.

That’s right—since so many adventurers are working together, someone needs to coordinate them.

Especially since this Garangiga is classified as a Rank A-equivalent monster; it wouldn’t be surprising if some were tempted to make off with the materials.

Fortunately, there hasn’t been anyone like that so far, but if such a person were to actually appear, it would be a major embarrassment for the Guild. After all, they had just cleared a dungeon near Golz—one that was believed to have only recently been discovered—and it contained such a powerful boss monster.

That is precisely why so many guild staff members have been dispatched here to keep watch.

“If we finish today, Rei-san says he’ll treat us to some orc meat. So, please do your best.”

At the guild staff member’s words, the adventurers, who had been feeling a bit demotivated, happily got back to work.

Orc meat isn’t exactly an expensive delicacy, but it’s surprisingly delicious for the price.

Given that they can eat it without spending their own money, there’s no way the adventurers wouldn’t be fired up.

……If this were in Gilm, where many adventurers have plenty of disposable income, they probably wouldn’t have gotten this excited.

Slightly exasperated by such mercenary adventurers… yet finding it somewhat endearing at the same time, the guild clerk focused on his job of supervising them.

“Hey, you there. Please be gentle when pulling out the thorns. If you pull them out along with the surrounding meat, it will affect the quality of the ingredients.”

The other guild staff members, too, kept an eye on the adventurers nearby as they worked diligently on the butchering.

“Hmm!”

With a voice full of determination—unusual for a woman like Byune—she muttered and swung the White Cloud, crafted from Silver Lion materials.

However, the strike—one that would be difficult for the average adventurer to block—was effortlessly dodged by Relieu.

Seeing this, Byune’s expression remained neutral, but her demeanor betrayed a hint of irritation.

It had been about ten minutes since the combat training with Relieu began, yet Relieu still hadn’t drawn his longsword from its sheath, continuing to evade Byune’s attacks solely through his footwork.

“You’re quite good.”

“Well, she does have a nickname. That power… I hate to say it, but someone like Byune wouldn’t stand a chance against her.”

Vihera murmurs in admiration, and Elena replies.

A wasteland located a short distance from Golz.

There, Byune and Relieu were currently engaged in a mock battle.

Elena and Vihera were among the four watching them.

Marina wasn’t there because she was on a date with Rei in Golz, and the two pets, Set and Yellow, had wandered off on their own.

The reason they’re split into two groups—or three, if you count Set and Yellow—today is that since Elena and Rei went on a date yesterday, it was decided that Marina and Rei would go on a date today.

And tomorrow, Vihera and Rei are scheduled to go on a date, and they’re set to leave Golz the day after tomorrow.

Relieu was dissatisfied with Rei and the others’ decision—or, more accurately, with Marina and Vihera’s desire to go on dates with Rei—but he understood that using the Set Cage was the fastest way to return to Gilm.

Furthermore, Marina had asked him, “Is it really okay to pick out a souvenir for your wife, Shumine, in just one day? Isn’t taking more time to choose a sign of love?” Since Relieu knew no compromise when it came to his wife, he found himself convinced by her words.

……Aside from the souvenir issue, Marina’s remark that they could also choose to view it as having time left to spend with Set undoubtedly carried significant weight for Relieu.

For that reason, Relieu agreed to postpone his departure until the day after tomorrow.

Although he’s staying here under the pretext of carefully selecting a souvenir, in reality, he’s engaged in a mock battle with Byune like this.

That said, there’s no denying that Relieu has a caring nature, despite everything.

It was true that she felt that if a child like Byune was going to be traveling with Rei and the others, then perhaps her sparring sessions with him would be of some help...

“Look, you’re focusing too much on your attacks. Don’t neglect your defense. Especially since Byune is small, if you take a hit carelessly, you’ll get blown away!”

With those words, he dodged Byune’s strike… Relieu grabbed Byune’s wrist and forcefully threw him.

Although Byune was sent flying a good distance, being a thief, her agility was superior to that of the average adventurer.

Twisting her body mid-air, she landed on both feet.

Of course, that was only possible because Relieu had intentionally thrown Byune in a way that gave her that much leeway.

If Relieu had truly intended to throw Byune, he wouldn’t have gone to the trouble of flinging him so far; he would have slammed him directly into the ground.

If that had happened, Byune would undoubtedly have been rendered unable to fight.

Perhaps realizing this, Byune narrowed his eyes slightly, showing a look of frustration, and turned back to Relieu.

To such a Byune, Relieu beckons him to come at him.

It was a clear provocation, but Byune deliberately took the bait, kicking off the ground and sprinting toward Relieu.

Relieu waited for Byune, showing no sign of tension.

While Byune and Relieu were engaged in combat training... Rei and Marina were on the main street of Golz.

That said, the main street was by no means as lively as the one in Gilm.

Golz is a relatively large town for this area, but it is still, after all, the countryside.

Compared to Gilm, a frontier town where large numbers of merchants and adventurers gather, it’s only natural that it seems less lively.

Even in a place like this, if you’re on a date, you can still have a decent time—perhaps because they’re still young, both in appearance and spirit.

“Rei, what do you think? Does this look good on me?”

Saying that, Marina showed him a hair ornament.

It wasn’t exactly a hairpin with intricate carvings, but the green hairpin still complemented Marina’s silver hair beautifully.

“Yeah, it looks good on you.”

“Really? Hehe, then maybe I’ll buy this.”

As she said that, Marina reached out to pay the shopkeeper, but... Rei grabbed her shoulder and stopped her.

“Since we’re here, I’ll cover this. It’s a memento… well, not exactly.”

“Oh, really? Hehe, a present from Rei, then.”

“My, my… you two are quite the close couple. Have you just started dating?”

The female clerk, having accepted the payment from Rei, smiled as she watched Marina happily put on the hair ornament.

Setting aside Rei, who was wearing the Dragon Robe, Marina—a dark elf whose sensual allure radiated intensely—possessed a beauty that was unforgettable at first glance.

Naturally, rumors spread like wildfire that Marina was in Golz.

Naturally, rumors also spread that such a person was among the members who had cleared the dungeon... and there was no way a woman working as a shop clerk wouldn’t know about it.

Yet she didn’t say anything—perhaps because she could tell Marina was enjoying this date and didn’t want to interrupt it.

Marina could sense this subtle consideration, so she gave the clerk a grateful look.

The clerk returned a look that said, “I understand,” and before Rei even noticed, the silent exchange between the two women was over, and they left the shop.

“So, where should we go next? I’d like to have something sweet.”

“Something sweet, huh? …I do have quite a stockpile of fruit we gathered in the dungeon forest, but that’s not what you mean, is it?”

“Hmm… Well, that’s fine then. But let’s go somewhere where there aren’t many people.”

She said this after a moment’s thought, likely because many passersby were staring at Marina in her party dress.

Her neckline was cut low, revealing the deep cleft between her breasts, and her back was exposed as well.

It was simply unthinkable to see a dark elf like Marina in a party dress in a place like this, so she was likely bothered by the various stares directed her way.

Normally, since she chose to dress this way, Marina wouldn’t care at all about other people’s stares—especially men’s—but now it was different.

Since she was on a date with Rei, she didn’t want to be the object of stares from just anyone.

“Even so… I’m not exactly an expert on Golz either… Shall we just start walking? We might find a good spot along the way.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Marina nodded immediately.

Probably because Marina wasn’t exactly familiar with Golz either.

……Though, in Marina’s case, she could easily find a deserted spot if she used her wind spirit magic.

The reason she didn’t do that was probably simply because she wanted to walk through the town with Rei.

The two of them walked through the town and eventually arrived at a vacant lot.

The weather was far from what you’d call a clear autumn day—the sky was overcast with clouds—but even so, children were running around and playing in the open lot.

Adults might be another story, but children wouldn’t look at Marina with such rude stares.

It would likely take nearly another decade for them to develop such feelings.

However, while the boys didn’t seem to harbor such feelings, to the girls, Marina, dressed in a beautiful party dress, must have seemed incredibly charming.

Several girls who had been playing in the vacant lot approached Marina.

“Hey, big sister! What’s that pretty dress?! Why are you wearing such a pretty dress? I want to wear one too!”

Speaking on behalf of the five girls who had gathered, one of them asked.

It was clear that the other girls, though they didn’t say so, were also deeply interested in the party dress Marina was wearing.

Marina smiled at the girls... not her usual seductive smile, but a gentle one, as if looking at someone endearing, and spoke.

“Hehe, that’s right. You might be able to wear clothes like this in the future, too, you know? Though, to do that, you’ll need to find a wonderful boyfriend.”

Saying this, Marina wraps her arm around Rei’s, as if to show them off.

Seeing this, the girls let out a cheer.

Even at a young age, a woman is still a woman, after all.

Rei and Marina enjoyed a brief holiday together with the children.
