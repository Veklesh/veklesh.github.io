Going to Gilm garnered more support than Rei had anticipated.

Of course, not everyone present felt the same way.

Some wished to return to their own homelands, and among the Resistance, there were those who hoped to remain in their homeland, the Kingdom of Reburina.

Still, overall, about eighty percent... maybe even ninety percent of the personnel agreed to go to Gilm.

This was a pleasant surprise for Rei, but a large number also meant leading them would draw attention.

"Got it? Until we return, try to avoid any resistance activities as much as possible, okay?"

"Got it, Sura. Don't worry. It's not like we can't do anything without you. We'll stay hidden until you come back. Besides... if what Rei says is true..."

The man paused there, shifting his gaze to Rei, who was talking with three women—all of them stunning beauties.

Lowering his voice, as if to ensure Rei couldn't hear, he spoke again.

"An envoy from the Kingdom of Mireana is on its way, right? And I hear that in connection with that, the Kingdom of Reburina and Jaya have become enemies. I don't think either of them has the spare capacity to mess with us."

"I wish that were the case. But we absolutely cannot afford to be complacent."

Whether it was the Kingdom of Reburina or Jaya, the Resistance organization was undoubtedly a major nuisance.

Moreover, the atrocities they had committed... forcibly abducting women from other nations, using slave collars to force them into prostitution, using black crystals to turn the children they conceived into giants, and ultimately having those giants devour their mothers alive from within. ...No, calling it monstrous would be more accurate.

Sura couldn't fathom that the Resistance, having been informed of the details of these atrocities by Rei, would be left alone by the Kingdom of Reburina or Jaya.

"Don't worry. We'll manage without you. Trust us a little."

"I... I know that."

Even as she said this, Sura couldn't help worrying about her comrades.

Maybe she should stay here instead of heading to Gilm with Rei?

She couldn't help but think that way too.

But some within the Resistance had decided to emigrate to Gilm.

Among those with families or lovers, none could decide to move immediately. Conversely, for those with no one to leave behind, that was reason enough to resolve to move.

To properly report the events that had unfolded in the Kingdom of Reburina, Sura, as the leader of the Resistance, needed to go herself.

Of course, if it were merely a matter of speaking with Daskar, the Paired Orb could have been used.

However, in a case like this, Sura's personal presence held significant meaning.

...If circumstances had prevented the Resistance from leaving the Kingdom of Reburina, that would have been another matter. But through this series of disturbances, the Resistance had already achieved its primary objective, and there was nothing urgent requiring immediate action now.

If anything, the risk was that involvement in this incident might invite retaliation... but that should be manageable if we lay low.

"Well, leave this to us. Sura, as the Resistance's representative, I'm counting on you."

"Yes. ...Be careful."

Sura turned her gaze toward the man she'd been speaking with... and also toward the other Resistance members nearby, then turned on her heel and left.

There was still some time before departure, but she felt staying with her comrades any longer would only expose her weaknesses.

After seeing Sura off, Rei finished talking with Elena and the others. He then began stowing the luggage handed to him by the women who had returned to Mejogo into the Misty Ring one after another.

Of course, with nearly a thousand people, there was no time to handle each individual's belongings. Instead, they were gathered into suitable containers and stored that way.

Besides, the women's belongings from Mejogo weren't that extensive.

They'd likely just bring the clothes they wore as prostitutes, packed as underwear and changes.

"Rei, I'm done over here. So, how long do you think it'll take you?"

"It won't take too long. But... I'll probably need to buy some clothes along the way."

Some had taken clothes from the Jaya soldiers at Mejogo, others had taken them from the bodies of Jaya soldiers killed in the recent battle—specifically those whose uniforms weren't badly damaged. Still others had gotten clothes supplied by the Resistance.

There were those who had managed to get clothes, but with nearly a thousand people, there weren't enough for everyone.

That meant clothes would inevitably need to be procured somewhere.

There were some clothes inside the Misty Ring, but nowhere near enough to distribute to everyone.

(The cloth inside the Misty Ring might be usable for something. Needles... Could we manage with Byune's long needle? If we had thread, could we make something resembling clothes?)

Rei thought it might be quicker to make clothes from cloth than to buy them all at once, but that assumption relied on there being someone who could actually make clothes. And making enough for everyone who didn't have any was also going to be tough.

"We'll depart before nightfall, so rest until then."

At Rei's words, those heading to Gilm each responded and rested their bodies.

Glancing at them, he noticed Sura approaching him and suddenly realized.

"Huh? Hey, Sura. What about Sharia?"

Rei mentioned the name of the beastman she knew, who had shown considerable skill on the battlefield.

But at Rei's words, Sura directed her gaze toward a group gathered a short distance away.

In the center of that gathering of various female beastmen, Sharia lay sprawled out, seemingly unaffected by the summer sunset.

Rei understood she was likely recovering from the battle's exhaustion.

"Over there, huh... So, are Sharia and the others coming to Gilm too? Personally, I'd really like them to come."

Even to Rei's eyes, the beastkin unit led by Sharia had performed remarkably well in the recent battle.

For Rei, they were people he very much wanted to have come with him.

The journey from here to Gilm would take about a month by carriage.

With nearly a thousand people, using carriages was out of the question, so they would have to travel on foot.

That meant two months, three months... possibly even longer.

Arriving in Gilm in autumn or early winter was a distinct possibility.

Under such circumstances, Rei couldn't possibly stay with them. He would likely leave the group early on, return to Gilm, and assist with the expansion work.

Even if he later joined the group again riding Set, it would be nearly impossible for him to stay with them the entire journey.

That's precisely why a skilled escort was necessary.

(Worst case, I'll have to hire adventurers somewhere... but I'd rather not bring in too many outsiders, you know?)

This group was composed of women—nine-tenths... no, more than nine-tenths of them.

And since most of them were forcibly brought here to be made into prostitutes, many are quite attractive.

Of course, whether one finds them beautiful depends entirely on personal taste.

That's precisely why, while it's not an absolute certainty, having this many women inevitably attracts those with strange intentions.

To avoid unnecessary trouble, it's best to rely on a party composed entirely of female guards... but all-female parties that are also sufficiently skilled aren't that common.

In that sense, Sharia's beastwomen were undeniably a highly attractive combat force.

"Don't worry. I hear they're all going to Gilm."

"I see."

Sura's words brought relief to Rei.

Of course, he didn't think Sharia and her group alone could protect all this many people, but he could still feel reassured knowing they were a highly capable force.

"So, can I leave the food and drinks to Rei, anyway?"

"Yeah, that's no problem. And worst case, we can hunt animals or monsters for food."

Inside Rei's Misty Ring was a large quantity of food.

Enough to easily feed this many people for half a year or more.

And besides, traveling with Rei is Set, the griffin.

Hunting animals or monsters would undoubtedly be incredibly easy.

"I see. That's good to know. Still, how did you manage to get enough food and drink for this many people? ...Is that also thanks to the power of the item box?"

"That's right. That's not wrong."

Truthfully, without the Misty Ring, managing food for this many people would be nearly impossible.

Even if we could gather the food, it's summer now.

It would inevitably spoil over time.

But inside the Misty Ring, time stands still. That's precisely why we can pull out exactly what we need, when we need it.

"I envy you,"

Sura murmured wistfully.

As the leader of the resistance, she had surely thought countless times before how useful an item box would be...

It couldn't store living beings, but it could hold weapons, armor, food... and all sorts of other things.

As someone leading an organization... especially one like the Resistance that must operate in secret, it was no wonder she envied it.

"Well, it's definitely rare. But even if it's not an item box, they sell inferior versions, right?"

"...They do, but the price is way out of my league. Nobles or well-paid adventurers might manage, I suppose."

Sura shot Rei a look that said, Rich bastards...

That said, Rei hadn't acquired his Misty Ring with money.

It had been prepared for Rei when she arrived in this world.

Knowing that would probably make her envy Rei even more.

In fact, it wouldn't be surprising if she gave him an even more resentful look.

"Anyway, I'll say it again—hurry up with the preparations as much as possible. By the way, we'll basically be sleeping rough, so be prepared for that. ...I have my own tent, so I'll use that."

"You girls... especially those who could be called victims of Jaya... you sleep outside. I'll sleep in my tent."

That's what Rei said, but unlike with the item box, Sura didn't seem dissatisfied.

Sura understood too. She understood that Rei and the others were doing everything they could for them.

In reality, just preparing food for nearly a thousand people for several months would normally be an astronomical expense.

...Though, in Rei's case, he planned to bill the client for the expenses later.

It wasn't just food. Rei had even said he'd provide water—something essential for human survival—free of charge.

Under normal circumstances, such a feat would be impossible without a dedicated unit or something similar.

Even with the Resistance's cooperation, it's not a job for just a few adventurers.

That said, it's only possible because it's summer now.

If it were winter, especially with snow falling, the very choice to travel to Gilm would be impossible.

(In that sense, it was fortunate that Rei and the others came to the Kingdom of Reburina during this season, wasn't it?)

Sura nodded at Rei's words, giving instructions to each resistance member joining her journey while silently thanking the timing.

"Hurry up, everyone! Get ready to depart as quickly as possible! Don't forget your water bottles! And of course, make sure to refill your water!"

Traveling outdoors in summer meant hydration was essential.

Of course, Rei had said he would prepare water, but that didn't mean they could completely rely on him.

With this many people moving, they had to do what they could properly.

"Rei, there's a river a little way past Rossi. Would it be alright if we camp there tonight?"

"Yeah, that's fine by me. With Set around, goblins and such won't come near us unless they're complete idiots."

"...That's a relief."

Night watch was essential, of course, but knowing only goblin-level monsters would approach was a relief for those on guard duty.

Sura efficiently gave orders to her allies.

In the end, they didn't fully depart Mejogo until about thirty minutes later.
