Rei and the others walked along a path trodden down by people and carriages, not a main road.

More precisely, the carriage Elena had prepared moved forward, with Set walking right beside it.

It was already late autumn, and perhaps due to the temperature, there were almost no monsters attacking them.

Most monsters wouldn't attack with Set present, but some, like goblins, simply couldn't grasp the strength of their opponents.

Those goblins, perhaps due to the cold, didn't attack the carriage either.

(Come to think of it, how do goblins survive the winter? Well, I do hear stories about goblins appearing occasionally even in winter, so they must be active, though not as much as in summer.)

Rei pondered this while gazing out the carriage window.

Inside the carriage, Elena, Marina, and Vihera were each sipping tea, nibbling on light snacks, and chatting cheerfully.

It was what you might call a tea party.

Rei had joined them at first, but feeling a bit tired, he had moved to this spot a little apart to gaze out the window.

(Ah, Yellow's playing with Set.)

Watching Yellow run around the carriage with Set outside the window, a warm feeling welled up somewhere inside him.

He wondered if it was a nuisance for the horses pulling the carriage, but seeing no issues with the carriage itself, he felt relieved.

"Hey, Rei. How long are you gonna stay over there? Come join us already. Get in on the conversation."

"Huh? Oh, sorry."

Though Rei had been spacing out, Vihera's call drew him closer to the table where Elena and the others were seated.

Sitting on the sofa drinking tea, Elena and the others were all beauties so stunning the word "peerless" seemed fitting. Even Rei, who often spent time with them, found himself captivated.

Knowing how many people had approached Elena and the others before, Rei silently agreed to himself.

Three women this beautiful.

It was only natural that many men would approach them, perhaps harboring such hopes.

Of course, men who were just average wouldn't even think of approaching Elena and the others. ...Couldn't imagine it.

But it was a different story for men who possessed self-confidence.

And in this place, Elgin, there were many men—nobles, adventurers, merchants—who radiated more confidence than any man Rei knew. That's precisely why so many felt compelled to try their luck with Elena and the others.

(Even so, Elena and her companions are each extraordinary, so those who know that don't try to hit on them.)

Elena, the only daughter of Duke Kerebel, who leads one of the three major factions in the Kingdom of Mireana, the Noble Faction, and is known by the nickname Princess General.

A dark elf serving as Guildmaster in Gilm, the sole frontier region of the Mireana Kingdom. And Marina, who is not widely known but shares a deep connection with the World Tree.

Vihera, a former member of the Imperial Family of the Bestir Empire who fled her country seeking battle.

All three are beauties, but not ordinary ones.

In fact, it could be said each had her own complicated past.

Among them, Vihera was the one most easily pursued by men.

This was largely because her status as imperial royalty of the Bestir Empire was virtually unknown in the Kingdom of Mireana, and also because her attire was provocative, resembling that of a courtesan or dancer.

"Then why not change your clothes? It's clearly Vihera's attire that's attracting men, right?"

Marina muttered, sounding slightly exasperated.

But Elena interjected, addressing Marina.

"If we're talking about clothing, I think Marina's outfit is pretty provocative too, not just Vihera's."

Her party dress, with its plunging neckline and low back, wasn't quite as revealing as Vihera's sheer gown, but it was certainly provocative enough.

Normally, such dresses might be acceptable at parties hosted by nobles or wealthy merchants, but wearing them in everyday life would make one stand out for all the wrong reasons... Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, Marina wore her party dress with impeccable grace.

While not as overtly sexually provocative as Vihera's attire, the very nature of the party dress stirred desire in many.

Moreover, Marina undoubtedly surpassed Vihera in terms of feminine allure.

Some might even find Marina's party dress more alluring than Vihera's sheer garments.

"In that regard, I show no weakness to other men,"

Elena declared to Vihera and Marina with a beaming smile.

But... Marina and Vihera exchanged glances at Elena.

Seeing their clearly suggestive expressions, Elena frowns.

"What is it? If you have something to say, I'm listening."

"...Not exactly a counterargument... but?"

"Yes?"

Their words were as if they had something stuck between their teeth.

Hearing this, Elena opened her mouth again, sounding slightly annoyed.

"What is it? If you have something to say, why don't you just say it clearly?"

At Elena's words, Marina and Vihera exchanged glances.

It was less a conversation through their eyes and more an exchange where each tried to push the responsibility of explaining to Elena onto the other.

The battle of glances lasted several seconds... until finally, Vihera prevailed over Marina, leaving Marina to explain to Elena.

Prompted by Vihera's gaze, Marina reluctantly opened her mouth.

"Alright, Elena. Unlike me or Vihera, Elena doesn't wear clothes that flaunt her body."

"Right?"

At Marina's words, Elena flashed a confident smile.

But the looks Marina and Vihera gave Elena held a hint of pity.

"But you see. While men are often captivated by bodies flaunted boldly like Vihera's, they can also become aroused simply by their own delusions... I mean, imagination."

"Imagination?"

Uncertain what Marina meant, Elena tilted her head slightly.

Marina nodded again at Elena.

"Yes. Take Elena, for example. It might be awkward for us to say, but you have quite large breasts, don't you?"

Marina, who said this, and Vihera too, both possessed twin peaks that far exceeded the average.

"Well, even if you say they're big... Marina and Vihera are probably about the same size as me, right?"

"True. That's precisely why, when we're together, Elena stands out. ...It's precisely because they're hidden that men want to see them. That's one aspect of male psychology."

"Because it's hidden..."

As Elena murmured Marina's words back to her, Vihera, who had been silently observing their exchange until now, suddenly spoke up.

"Meaning, for some men, Elena might be the object of desire more than me or Marina."

"Wh-what!?"

It was clearly unexpected. Elena let out a short gasp of surprise, then froze completely.

She'd been approached by men countless times, and she was well aware that many men looked at her with lustful eyes.

But that it was precisely because it was hidden that she drew even more male attention... Realizing this, Elena turned her gaze back to Marina and Vihera standing before her.

She wanted them to say it was a joke.

Contrary to that wish...

"Especially in Elena's case, she always carries herself with such dignity, and on top of that, she's covered in armor, right? That's precisely why, for men... you know?"

Whether Vihera held back the last part out of concern for Elena or because it would have been more effective as a tease, Elena couldn't tell.

At Vihera's words, Elena's cheeks flushed faintly.

"Hey, Rei. What do you think?"

"...Now you're bringing me into this?"

Rei, who had been acting like he wasn't involved in this women's conversation, frowned as if he'd swallowed a bitter pill.

He didn't want to get dragged into their conversation right now.

"Of course. It affects your preferences too."

But Vihera nodded as if it were obvious, and Marina nodded in agreement too.

Elena, her cheeks flushed, waited for Rei's response.

"Ah... well. I think all three of you are great women in your own ways. If you just be yourselves, isn't that enough?"

The words that came out of Rei's mouth, facing the gazes of the three, could be described as ambiguous.

Yet, even with such words, the three of them smiled happily upon hearing Rei speak.

"Well, I'd really like to press you further, but I'll let you off for now."

"...Thanks."

Reluctantly, words of gratitude slipped from Rei's mouth in response to Vihera's statement.

Judging that continuing this way would lead the conversation in a direction he didn't particularly enjoy, Rei changed the subject.

"We ended up leaving the dungeon core with the Walkers, but when exactly will it be coming back here?"

"Hmm, let me see. After the initial investigations, it'll be transported to Gilm. There, it'll need further examination... Spring... no, you should expect it to take about half a year."

Marina answered after a moment's thought, but Rei's expression darkened slightly upon hearing it.

"Half a year? That seems like it'll take way too long."

"It can't be helped. Let's face it, a dungeon core isn't something you can just casually examine."

"Well, conquering a dungeon is undoubtedly a major event in itself. And Rei's name will probably spread even further than before, right?"

Vihera chimed in after Marina's words, and even Elena nodded in agreement.

"Come to think of it, there was a rumor last year about Rei conquering a dungeon too..."

"Ah... well, that wasn't exactly a lie. It was just a bit special to call it a dungeon—it was a brand-new one, so the difficulty wasn't that high. Gamelion was a tasty opponent in many ways, though."

"Thanks to that, Gamelion meat was more expensive than usual last year."

"...We did sell some to the guild, right?"

Marina stated, sounding slightly dissatisfied.

With her salary as Guildmaster and her own accumulated wealth, she could have easily bought Gamelion meat without issue.

But with Gamelion meat already scarce, if she used her wealth to buy up all the available supply, the portion allocated to other residents would naturally decrease.

Considering that, the amount of Gamelion meat Marina was able to eat last year was ultimately not very much.

For Marina, who counted Gamelion meat among her favorite foods, it was a deeply regrettable situation.

"Well, it's fine. I'll make sure to eat my fill this year. In Gilm, it shouldn't be too surprising if Gamelion meat is circulating reasonably well by now. ...If the handover had progressed a bit further, I could have gone Gamelion hunting myself."

She still seemed a little disappointed, but somehow she radiated a cheerful atmosphere.

To such a Marina, Elena suddenly spoke up as if she'd just realized something.

"Come to think of it, the taste of the Silver Lion meat made other dishes seem a bit lackluster. I wonder how Gamelion meat compares?"

At Elena's words, Marina paused for just an instant.

It was something Marina herself probably didn't want to think about.

The dish made with silver lion meat was, quite literally, so delicious it seemed otherworldly.

But that overwhelmingly delicious dish left its flavor lingering in the mouth, making ordinary dishes taste unremarkable for quite some time afterward.

Unless it was something truly exceptional, like the dishes served at the Oak Stew Inn, nothing else tasted good.

"Don't say that. ...But the food we had at camp yesterday tasted decent enough, so maybe the effect is wearing off?"

"...True. But ingredients that are too delicious are troublesome, aren't they? Seems like a lot of people would get hooked after trying them just once."

As they chatted like that, the carriage moved forward...

"Lady Elena, we can see Gilm!"

Hearing the voice of Ara from the driver's seat, everyone finally exhaled... as if returning to reality.

...Though Elena doesn't actually live in Gilm, the sight of a walled city still brought a sense of relief.
