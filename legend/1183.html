"Set-chan, Set-chan, Set-chaaaan!"

Rei and the others had descended to the ground, but the moment they saw them, a woman dashed toward them.

...Well, to be precise, not toward Rei, but toward Set.

In any case, seeing Mireine running toward Set, Rei smiled, thinking she was just the same as ever, and spoke up.

"You're searching for Ambris too?"

"Yes. Since I'm a mage, they thought maybe I could help find Ambris."

Rank C Party Sorceress of the Scorching Wind, Surunin, gave a slight bow to Rei and Vihera before stating this.

"But we never actually find him, do we? And I have pretty good eyesight myself."

The archer, Ekril, muttered discontentedly.

True to her profession as an archer, Ekril had excellent eyesight.

It wasn't quite as sharp as Set's, but it was still more than good enough.

Even so, Ekril couldn't spot Ambrith.

She couldn't help but wonder if she was even needed here.

It was only natural for her to feel that way.

"Ecuril, calm yourself. The enemy is a black mist-like entity. And even that was only true three hundred years ago. There's no guarantee it will appear as a black mist this time."

Surnin's words, meant to encourage Ekril, were met with a nod of agreement from Rei as well.

"That's right. Even Set, whose senses are sharper than a human's, is having trouble finding it. Considering that, it's not like we'll find it right away."

In fact, if they could find it faster than Set, that alone would prove Ecuril's abilities were exceptionally high.

"So, you two don't have any leads on Ambris either, then?"

Surnin bowed apologetically to Vihera, who had glanced at Mireine playing with Set before speaking.

That apology stemmed partly from his inability to provide information on Ambrith, but more than anything, it was likely directed at the sight of their party leader.

Normally, such information exchange would be spearheaded by the party leader.

But right now, Mireine's entire focus was on doting on Set.

Of course, if the situation were truly urgent, Mireine wouldn't be acting like this. She was also aware that Surnin was better suited for information exchange.

But... even so, the reason Mireine was doting on Set like this was because Set was there.

Rei recalled seeing or hearing in Japan about the story where someone answered the question "Why climb a mountain?" with "Because it's there."

To put it in those terms, this was because Set was there that she cherished him.

Naturally, she only does such things because Set is there.

If they had encountered an ordinary party instead of Rei and the others here, Mireine would have properly exchanged information.

It wasn't an emergency; there was some leeway... and precisely because it was Rei and the others they encountered, Mireine took this attitude.

Watching Mireine like this, Rei didn't blame her.

Mireine's fondness for Set began back when Rei first arrived at Gilm and they defeated that orc settlement not too long ago.

Considering they'd known each other for several years since then, it was only natural for Rei to grow accustomed to Mireine's behavior.

Moreover, it was also true that thanks to Mireine, Set had been accepted into Gilm quickly.

Even if Mireine hadn't doted on Set, it wouldn't change the fact that Set would have been accepted by Gilm eventually.

But even so, it's an undeniable fact that it took longer for him to be accepted.

Moreover, as someone who makes a hobby of doting on Set, Mireine often gives him food.

...In this case, it might be more accurate to say she feeds him, but what Mireine gives Set is often proper meals sold around town, or even more expensive dried meat than that.

Calling that "bait" is simply impossible.

Of course, Rei's Misty Rings contain large quantities of monster meat, along with various other dishes that Rei found delicious.

Therefore, Set never goes hungry, and from his adventuring activities so far, he possesses a great deal of money. ...Though it must be said, a significant portion of that wealth comes from raiding thieves.

So, there's no real worry about Set's food expenses... Still, I can't help but wonder if Mireine giving him food might be saving a little bit?

"...It's good to see Mireine looking well again today. Anyway, setting Ambris aside, have you encountered any monster swarms?"

"Yes. ...You asking that means you have?"

Rei nodded at Surnin's probing question.

Of course, Surnin understood Rei's capabilities well.

He didn't think encountering a few monster groups would pose any real threat to Rei.

In fact, the goblin and kobold group Rei had found had been annihilated by a merciless aerial barrage.

"Just as I imagined. Well, since it was just a one-sided attack from the air, there might actually be survivors... But even if there are, they wouldn't be much of a threat unless they're in a group, right?"

The reason goblins and kobolds are troublesome this time is solely because they're in a group.

If they were moving in small groups of just a few... and especially if they had Rei's attack etched into their minds as terror, they were no longer enemies at all.

They'd probably just retreat deep into forests and woods to live quietly, never daring to attack humans again.

Somehow, that's what Rei found himself thinking.

"I see. But a pack is still troublesome, isn't it? ...If Ambris can't move, he's likely where the pack is densest."

The exhaustion visible on Surnin's sighing face wasn't physical fatigue.

Surnin was a mage who wasn't particularly adept at physical exertion, yet he still worked as an adventurer. A bit of walking around wouldn't tire him.

He himself has been concerned lately about his declining stamina due to age.

"Right. If he's staying put somewhere, we could just find the direction the pack is coming from and he'd be there. ...But if he's moving around..."

The currently confirmed groups are goblins, kobolds, orcs, lizardmen, and werewolves.

There might be others, but those are the only ones confirmed so far.

Furthermore, the areas where the hordes have appeared are so widely scattered that it's unlikely they all originated from a single location.

It's more natural to assume that the location where the leader species formed and created the hordes was different from the start, rather than them becoming leader species and then each heading to their preferred location...

"Yes. It's extremely difficult that even those who can sense magic can't locate them, forcing us to rely solely on direct visual confirmation."

"Exactly. Because of that, we've been staring into the distance constantly, and our eyes are getting tired..."

Ecuril's voice trailed off in agreement with Surnin's words.

"Hey, Set is working hard, so we have to work hard too, right? Ekiru, you need to try harder too."

"Mireine... I think that's asking a bit much."

Mireine said to Ekril while stroking Set, and Rei muttered in slight exasperation.

Ecuril and Set... A human and a griffin have fundamentally different abilities.

Trying to combine the two was fundamentally flawed.

Watching Mireine like that, Rei suddenly had an idea.

"Right. Well then, if Mireine can find Ambris, I'll give her the right to spend a whole day relaxing with Set. How about it?"

For Rei, it was just a spur-of-the-moment idea to motivate Mireine, who liked Set.

But the words that came out of Rei's mouth sparked Mireine's motivation far more than he had anticipated.

"Really!? You mean it? You won't call it a lie later, right? If you try anything like that, I won't forgive you!"

What flashed in Mireine's eyes wasn't mere motivation...

It was more like a fierce, almost demonic will.

Her gaze pierced through Rei with such intensity that one might have thought flames were burning within her eyes.

Even Rei, who had overcome countless crises before, took a step back. That alone should give you an idea of just how intense that gaze was.

Rei glanced around, wondering if he'd messed up. Seeing Surunin, Ecuril, and Vihera all silently nod in unison, he realized exactly what he'd done.

For Mireine, spending a day with Set was a reward rarer than gold coins, silver coins... even platinum coins.

A reward like this would push Mireine's motivation to its absolute peak.

"Grrr?"

Seeing Mireine's excited expression, Set purred, "What's up?"

To that Set, Mireine opened her mouth with a beaming smile.

"Wait for me, Set-chan. I'll find Ambris soon, so we can spend time together. ...Surunin, Ecuril, let's go!"

Giving Set one last pat, Mireine moved away from the spot.

Surnin and Rei followed after her.

As they did, Surnin bowed his head to Rei and Vihera before following after Mireine.

"Considering Rei's determination, she'll probably find Ambris without a hitch."

After all, Mireine could spot and identify Set flying at an altitude of 100 meters.

It made one wonder if she might actually be able to spot Ambrith.

"Yeah. Judging by her attitude, she might actually find him for real."

Rei couldn't help but nod in agreement at Vihera's heartfelt words.

Honestly, considering Mireine's personality, it wouldn't be surprising at all if that happened.

"...Well, we should probably go too. I don't mind letting Mireine and Set spend the day together, but that doesn't mean we can just stop searching ourselves."

Even if he left Set with Mireine, Rei trusted she wouldn't mistreat him. Still, he had absolutely no intention of letting Mireine beat him to finding Ambris.

Simply having Set was an advantage in itself.

If Mireine found Ambris first, it would mean Rei had lost in a situation where he held an overwhelming advantage.

It wouldn't cause any particular inconvenience, but it still wouldn't feel good.

Seeing Rei like this, Vihera nodded with a faint smile.

Vihera, who loved combat, naturally hated losing too. Of course, she had no intention of letting Mireine beat her.

"Right. Well then, let's go. We absolutely must find Ambris before they do."

"GRRRRRRRR!"

As if agreeing with their words, Set's growl echoed around them.

Set didn't dislike Mireine... no, considering how she doted on him and gave him food, he could even say he liked her. But like Rei, he simply hated losing.

(Though with Set, I get the feeling Mireine would admit defeat the moment he showed up.)

That wasn't a prediction, but a near certainty.

But it wasn't just Rei who sensed it; Vihera likely felt the same.

When Rei looked over, Vihera smiled as if understanding exactly what he wanted to say and spoke.

"The last resort would be Set, I suppose. ...Though, Set doesn't seem to dislike spending a day with Mireine all that much."

"Guruu? Gururururu!"

What's wrong? Let's hurry up? Set growled like that.

He turned his back to Rei, urging him to get on quickly.

"Alright, let's go. I don't know where Ambris is, but we have to find him somehow. Otherwise, the pack issue won't be resolved."

At those words, Set growled... and then Rei mounted his back.

After a few paces of running, Set flapped his wings and soared into the sky. Soon, he flapped his wings again, making a wide curve through the air, and began descending toward the ground.

Timing his jump perfectly, Vihera leapt and grabbed onto Set's front legs.

And so, the two humans and one beast began their journey—faster than the scorching winds, faster than Mireine herself—to find Ambris.
