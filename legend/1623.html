"You're awfully early."

"Well, we prepared all this for this very purpose, so it would be a problem if it were slower than walking."

Inside the carriage, Vihera murmured as she watched the surroundings through the window. Gimekara smiled and replied.

Gimekara, who could be called an agent of the Zolge Trading Company, was currently traveling with Rei and the others.

This was based on the idea that if anything happened, acting in a way that would benefit Rei and the others even slightly through the power of the Zolge Trading Company would lead to more favorable treatment in Gilm.

Originally, Gimekara had no intention of traveling with Rei and the others.

However, his conversation with Daskar through the Paired Orb last night had likely instilled a profound sense of crisis in him.

Therefore, he acted to benefit Rei and her group as much as possible, keeping in mind the possibility of seeking their help if anything happened in Gilm.

Gimekara's choice was by no means wrong.

After all, Rei and the others wielded influence in Gilm that set them apart from ordinary adventurers.

"Still... I didn't expect it would take this long to get going,"

Elena muttered, sounding slightly dissatisfied.

Until now, they had been moving while leading the women who had escaped from Mejogo and the resistance.

Their pace hadn't been particularly swift, but Elena had never voiced complaints about it... However, when it came to moving by carriage, her side as a military officer, known by the nickname Princess General, inevitably surfaced.

And as the Princess General, she found the clumsiness of everyone boarding their respective carriages before departure rather amusing.

"Well, fine, if they were soldiers or something, but most of them are just ordinary folks... people who've never had any training. Don't be unreasonable."

"But, Rei..."

Even after hearing Rei's words, Elena couldn't hide her dissatisfaction. But she must have decided that saying anything more here and now would be pointless.

She pushed back her golden hair and fell silent.

"Ah, ahahaha. Well, I suppose being nicknamed the Princess General does come with high expectations, huh?"

Gimekara, who had been watching the exchange, spoke up to lighten the mood.

Surprisingly to Rei, the first to react to Gimekara's words was Vihera.

"Well, it was your first day today, so it can't be helped. It'll still take quite a while to reach Gilm, so if we keep going like this, you'll get used to it."

"Besides, if we keep going like this, the numbers will thin out a bit... and then it might be a little easier to move around."

Just as Marina had said, when they departed from Dorgana today, over a dozen people left the group.

Those individuals, for various reasons, chose to remain and live in Dorgana from this point forward.

Given that Dolgana is a relatively large city within the Kingdom of Reburina, many likely found it a more livable place.

Some had even used their wiles to ingratiate themselves with the wealthier folk, but that wasn't something the group remaining here needed to concern themselves with.

(Staying in the current state of the Kingdom of Reburina feels like suicide, though. ...Well, they made their own choice, so I guess I have no right to say anything.)

Rei pondered this as he gazed out the carriage window.

Where Rei's gaze fell, he could see Set running a short distance ahead of the carriage.

The horses pulling the carriage provided by the Zolge Trading Company weren't military horses subjected to rigorous training...

If they were the horses pulling Elena's carriage, they wouldn't have bolted in terror upon seeing Set for the first time. But horses trained to that level of discipline were, naturally, extremely expensive.

The horses provided by the Zolge Trading Company were ordinary horses, not that kind. It was only natural they would be frightened if they were near Set.

That was precisely why Set was running a little way behind Rei and the others.

Even so, the reason Rei could see Set was likely because Rei's carriage was leading the procession.

"The guards Gime Kara arranged seem to be getting along well with the Resistance folks, huh?"

From what Rei had observed before departure, there was a slight tension between the guards and the Resistance members, but it wasn't hostile.

If anything, it was more accurate to say they were unsure how to gauge their distance from each other...

Then, for some reason, Ryutas had meddled, and things had gradually worked themselves out.

"That's right. We went through a lot to select the people, so it's a relief that it paid off. ...Though that doesn't mean their skills are particularly outstanding."

"Don't worry about that. If something happens, having numbers means we can usually handle most opponents. And if it comes down to it, we can step in."

"That's true, but then there'd be problems after you guys leave... We really do need to gather some skilled guards, don't we?"

We'd been told beforehand that Rei and the others wouldn't be staying with this group forever, and above all, Daskar made it perfectly clear during our conversation yesterday.

Given that, it was undeniable that Gimekara needed capable individuals who could handle any situation that arose.

If word got out that the guards were all incompetent, this group would be nothing but prime prey for bandits.

(As for that... the villages we'll be passing through ahead probably won't have anyone that skilled, so we'll have to look in the cities. Ideally, we'd find them within the borders of the Kingdom of Reburina.)

Within the Kingdom of Reburina, we might be able to hire strong adventurers using the influence of the Zolge Commerce Guild.

But once we leave the Kingdom of Reburina, that becomes impossible.

Of course, if you ask whether we lose all influence entirely, the answer is no.

"For that... we'll probably have to find someone reasonably skilled somewhere. ...Though finding such a person will likely be difficult in the first place."

"That's... true. Skilled individuals without personality issues are naturally in very high demand."

Gimekara said this with regret, but it was only natural.

A skilled adventurer with no personality issues is someone anyone would rely on.

It's no wonder they're in high demand for jobs everywhere.

"But that doesn't mean we absolutely can't hire such adventurers. After all, this party is heading to Gilm."

"Is there such a place? ...If we set out now, we could reach Gilm before winter. Besides, this year, even if we haven't saved up enough funds to get through the winter, it shouldn't be too much of a problem."

"True. They say the expansion work can continue through winter unless the snow gets really bad. So, for this year, next year, and maybe the year after, we probably won't need to hunt monsters in winter to save up for the winter, even if we haven't saved up yet."

Working on the expansion in sub-zero temperatures and snow was a job Rei wanted to avoid if at all possible, but it was still more reliable and safer than going out to hunt monsters in winter.

If adventurers from around here went to Gilm now, even those without much financial leeway should be able to earn considerably more than usual this year, assuming such work was available.

(In my case, with Set around, I don't struggle to find monsters even in winter, and moving is easy... but ordinary adventurers have to walk through the snow.)

Rei pondered winter life since arriving in Elgin, though in reality, Set often ventured out of Gilm on his own during winter to hunt, partly to combat his lack of exercise.

Normally, the monsters that appear around the Gilm in the frontier are often high-rank.

In fact, winter brought monsters that only appeared during that season.

...But even against such monsters, Set could easily defeat them alone.

For ordinary adventurers, Set's combat prowess and mobility must be incredibly enviable.

Additionally, Set would occasionally bring back the meat of slain monsters as souvenirs, making Rei the envy of others in that regard too.

Under normal circumstances, no one would send Set out alone, let alone allow him to enter town unchecked upon his return.

But that was only under normal circumstances. Set possessed a level of fame that made him anything but ordinary.

Set is practically Gilm's mascot character; anyone unaware of that would be considered a complete outsider.

"Rei-san, a group that looks like a merchant caravan is approaching from the opposite direction. What should we do?"

At the coachman's voice, Rei opened the door leading to the driver's seat and looked in the direction of travel.

The midsummer sun beat down, and the scent of grass drifting in from the surrounding meadows stung Rei's nose.

Though the intense direct sunlight beat down, the driver's seat had a solid roof... and above all, Rei was wearing the Dragon Robe, so he didn't seem particularly bothered by it.

Amidst this scene, several carriages approached in Rei's direction.

The reason those carriages seemed somewhat hesitant... was likely because Rei's group was exceptionally large.

A typical merchant caravan would be small, with a few to a dozen people... or large, perhaps a hundred or so.

But Rei's group numbered close to a thousand.

With that many people moving, they naturally weren't traveling in a single file. They were utilizing the road to its fullest, and where that wasn't enough, they were moving off the road.

Naturally, when encountering a group coming from the opposite direction like this, various precautions were necessary.

"For now, signal them to leave a gap. I'll give a quick greeting over there..."

"No, wait, Rei. I'll handle this."

Gimekara cut off Rei, who was about to say he'd go greet them.

Since the other party was a merchant caravan and this was the territory of the Reburina Kingdom, it was better for him to make the appearance. Rei hesitated slightly at Gimekara's explanation but ultimately left it to him.

He reasoned that in the Kingdom of Reburina, the name of the Zolge Commerce Guild would carry sufficient weight. More than anything, he wanted to give Gimekara a chance to earn some credit.

Normally, he wouldn't need to be so considerate toward Gimekara, but since Gimekara had arranged for a large quantity of supplies, including the carriage, Rei decided it wouldn't hurt to be a little flexible beyond just introducing him to Daskar.

Whether or not Gimekara understood Rei's feelings, he called over one of the guards riding horses instead of in the carriage and asked to ride that horse as they approached the caravan.

"Are you sure this is okay?"

Vihera murmured, watching Gimekara's retreating figure.

But considering Gimekara's affable personality and the name value of the Zolge Trading Company, Rei anticipated there would likely be no issues.

"Probably won't be a problem. For them too, if they're operating in the Kingdom of Reburina, they'll have to treat the name Zolgee Trading Company with proper respect."

Vihera shot Rei a skeptical look, her eyes asking, Really?

But where Rei was looking, Gimekara, mounted on a guard horse, was seen conversing amicably with a stout figure who appeared to be the caravan's representative.

Seeing that scene, even someone other than Rei could understand that there probably wouldn't be any trouble.

After talking for several minutes, Gimekara eventually returned toward Rei and the others' carriage.

There, as always, was his gentle smile.

It was a smile that made it impossible to believe the discussion with the caravan had ended in a hostile atmosphere.

"It's fine. The greetings went smoothly. They seem to understand that making any rash moves in the current situation could be dangerous, so they'll wait for us to pass through."

"I see. That's a relief."

"Not at all."

Gimekara smiled at Rei's words and returned to the carriage from his horse.

Just like before, the way he leapt from horse to carriage driver's seat wasn't that of a mere merchant, but carried the bearing of someone possessing some measure of power.

(Well, he's representing the Zolgee Trading Company in contacting us. He's definitely not just a merchant.)

As Rei watched Gimekara again, he thought to himself... and the carriage continued on toward Gilm.
