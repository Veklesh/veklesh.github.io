Time rewinds a little.

Erinde was working in the headmaster's office as usual when she suddenly sensed a familiar magical presence.

If that magic had simply entered the academy building housing the principal's office... that wouldn't have been a problem.

But the fact that it suddenly appeared right beside her, as she sat organizing documents at her desk, was a different matter entirely.

Even so, Erinde showed no sign of panic. Perhaps it was because she knew the owner of that magic well.

She continued reading the contents of the document, confirmed there was nothing amiss, and then signed it.

Then, reaching for the teacup sitting on the desk, she drank the tea, which had grown quite cold, before speaking.

"So, what exactly are you doing here?"

Her gaze fell upon someone standing there—an elf like herself, carrying a bow on their back... a magnificent bow that looked far beyond what one could ever hope to buy off the street, along with a quiver that was clearly a magic item just by looking at it.

Though she had the typical slender build of an elf, Erinde knew this figure's body was thoroughly trained.

And then there was the face—elegant, a beauty one could describe as quintessentially elven.

While elves tended to be naturally well-featured, this one possessed a beauty that stood out even among them.

Yet, one could still clearly recognize him as a man, likely due to his personality.

The figure had been observing Erinde with an interested gaze from beside her. Finally, as if relieved to be acknowledged, he smiled and spoke.

"You're terrible. You noticed me the moment I arrived, didn't you? Yet you ignored me and prioritized your work."

"I wouldn't mind spending time with you, but my job is being the headmaster of this academy. Given that, you must understand I have to prioritize work even when friends come visiting, right?"

"Work, huh. Paperwork? I absolutely refuse to do that. If anyone tried to make me, I'd refuse outright. And if they tried to force me, I'd protest using this if I had to."

With that, the elf man touched the bow he carried on his back with affection.

Crafted from metal interwoven with white and blue lines, the bow was both the man's nickname and an immensely powerful magic item.

In other words...

"The Heavenly Bow would surely be displeased to be used for such trivial matters."

Yes, that bow was the weapon known as the Heavenly Bow, and the elf wielding it was Saldart, a Rank S adventurer who shared the Heavenly Bow's alias.

"Is that so? Since it was Erinde who originally requested the escort, I think you should be praised for doing your job properly."

"But you only intervened in Rei's fight, didn't you? You could have captured the attacker yourself, couldn't you?"

"What kind of nonsense are you spouting!?"

Sardot spread his arms wide, as if to say he was deeply offended.

His gestures were exaggerated, each one over-the-top, making it seem like those watching were witnessing a play.

(Come to think of it, according to intelligence from the royalist faction, there's a subordinate of the Third Prince—who emerged victorious in the civil war and is now the next emperor of the Bestir Empire—said to have a similar personality... I both want to see him meeting Sardat and don't want to see it.)

As if completely unaware of Erinde's inner thoughts, Sardat shook his head sadly, still holding his arms wide, and spoke.

"I'm an elf, you know? While I excel at bows and spirit magic, close-quarters combat isn't my forte. That sort of thing is best left to those who are good at it. ...Ah, but only men. Girls have such beautiful skin—I'd rather they avoid getting scarred, if possible."

"...You really haven't changed a bit, have you? Honestly, if this is what a Rank S adventurer of the Mireana Kingdom is like, I can't say the future of this country looks very bright."

"That's hurtful. Little girls, young girls, girls, women... They're the world's treasures!"

Sardat declared it as if stating a universal truth, and Erinde sighed.

"Yeah, yeah. That's all well and good, but do you have any idea who attacked Rei?"

"Ah. I've crossed paths with them several times before. That was Stigma."

"...Stigma?"

Erinde tilted her head, apparently unfamiliar with the name.

Seeing this, Sardat tilted his head as well.

It was a strange sight, two elves tilting their heads at each other in silence. But it was Saldat who broke the silence first.

"Huh? You really don't know? I thought it was pretty famous in the Underworld."

"Look... I'm not an adventurer or an inhabitant of the Otherworld. I'm just the ordinary headmistress of a military academy. Why would you think someone like me would know about famous figures from the Otherworld?"

"...You're ordinary, huh. That changes my definition of 'ordinary'... Well, whatever. Digging deeper into that would just stir up trouble. So, about the Stigma, right? Simply put, it's the Holy Light Church's ultimate weapon."

"The Holy Light Church? The Holy Light Church... I'm pretty sure they caused a commotion in Exil last year..."

Though Exil was treated as an autonomous city due to its status as a labyrinth city, it remained part of the Mireana Kingdom.

Given that, news of last year's Holy Light Church incident had naturally reached them.

"Exactly. That Holy Light Church. You know how Mireana cracked down on them after that incident... well, maybe not exactly cracked down, but they definitely started keeping a close eye on them, right? Stigma is the Holy Light Church's ultimate combat force."

"Do you know roughly what level of ability they possess?"

"Right. On average, they're about equivalent to Rank A adventurers. Of course, some are weaker, and some are stronger. ...Ah, but naturally, I haven't seen anyone stronger than me so far, so I don't think they're Rank S equivalent."

Erinde exhaled in relief at those words.

But her beautiful expression quickly turned bitter.

It was only natural. An average A-rank adventurer was a formidable force.

She didn't think her friend would lose, but still, there was only one Saldat.

If multiple disturbances erupted simultaneously, it was certain Sardat alone couldn't handle them all.

(The only saving grace was that Saldat was an adventurer known as the Sky Bow. His ability to hit targets with arrows from afar could be a trump card. And then...)

Next, the image of Rei flashed through Erinde's mind.

Naturally, she had some information about his strength. This was Graciall, ruled by a major figure within the royalist faction. She had heard about Rei's actions during the civil war in the Bestir Empire, and even rumors that he had defeated some of the empire's proud Rank S adventurers, albeit in limited encounters.

And above all, the immense magical power she sensed when Rei used the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Seeing that alone made it clear just how much power he possessed.

(Though... according to the gathered intelligence, Rei specializes in wide-area annihilation magic. Meaning, battles in open spaces. Of course, we know his personal strength is also considerable... but fundamentally, we hired him as an instructor to temporarily evacuate Rei. So, the very act of having Rei fight means we've already failed.)

Sardat watched Erinde sigh with an amused expression.

It was precisely at that moment. A knock sounded on the academy director's office door.

The moment he sensed the approaching footsteps, a smile spread further across Saldat's lips.

Seeing Saldat like this, Erinde must have realized who had arrived. With a look of half-hearted resignation, she gestured for them to enter.

And of course, it was Rei who entered.

"......"

"Hmm?"

Erinde had expected Saldat to react to Rei entering the Headmaster's office, but she found it suspicious that he showed no reaction at all.

What's wrong? With that thought, Erinde turned her gaze toward Saldat. Or rather, she thought she did...

She didn't know how or when it happened, but when she realized it, Saldart's gaze was already fixed on Rei, who had just entered the room.

"Well, well, you must be Rei? I heard rumors you were a man, but it seems that was a lie."

Sardat said this, flashing his teeth—though Erinde couldn't see it—and it wasn't just Erinde who was surprised.

Rei, who had naturally been watching him approach, was equally taken aback. ...No, in terms of pure surprise, Rei was probably even more shocked.

Having lowered the hood of his Dragon Robe to meet Erinde, Rei's face was now clearly visible.

As a result, Rei's face—which could easily be called feminine—was clearly visible, prompting Saldat to start flirting with Rei like this.

"No, I was focused on the Stigma earlier, so I didn't realize you were this beautiful. Still, a girl like you getting attacked by the Stigma... what did you do?"

"...Wait. Wait a moment. Please, just wait a moment."

Uncharacteristically flustered, Rei thrust her hands forward, tilting her head as she tried to create distance from Saldat.

She felt like a strange word had slipped into their conversation just now.

"Ah, of course, if you say wait, I'll wait as long as you need. Still, that robe of yours looks like a pretty powerful magic item, but it doesn't quite bring out your full charm. What do you say? After this, I'd like to give you a dress that suits you."

Fasari, Sardat said, running a hand through his vivid green hair as he invited Rei to go shopping.

If it were any ordinary woman here instead of Rei, she'd probably fall head over heels at that look.

It was the kind of look—no, the kind of presentation—that came from knowing her own beauty inside out, knowing exactly how to make it shine most effectively.

But unfortunately... regardless of her appearance, Rei was unmistakably male.

"Ah... Sardat. Sorry to interrupt your flirting, but Rei-dono is a fine man."

"...Huh?"

Rei shot a glare at Sardat, who froze mid-motion, hair still tousled, as if you could almost hear a sharp snap.

He knew he had what you'd call feminine features and was smaller than the average man in Elgin.

But seeing his protruding belly beneath the Dragon Robe, it should be obvious he has some muscle there.

(Wait, isn't it common for women to have muscles too?)

The images of several female warriors flashed through Rei's mind—not those like Vihera who maintained feminine beauty, but women with muscles that rivaled men's.

I've hardly ever spoken to such individuals, but if I had to name one, it would be Fron, a member of the Rank C party, the Shattered Warriors.

(Thinking about it that way, it's no wonder she mistook me for a woman... or is it? Not that I'm happy about it.)

Rei recognized himself as a proper man, and precisely because of that, being hit on by men—no matter how handsome they were—wasn't exactly pleasant.

"Um, well... are you really a guy?"

"Yeah. What else would you take me for?"

"Well... I guess..."

Sardat almost blurted out "woman," but Rei's sullen glare and Erinde's exasperated look made him clam up.

He sensed that saying anything careless now would likely lead to a rough time in more ways than one.

In fact, that judgment was probably correct.

If he'd dared call Rei a woman here, Rei would have taken a firm stance, no matter who the other person was... even if it were the Heavenly Bow, the sole Rank S adventurer of the Mireana Kingdom.

(Rank S adventurer, Heavenly Bow... She's not actually that, is she? Wait, earlier she mentioned something about being attacked by those guys called Stigma...? Considering that, she really is Heavenly Bow, no doubt... But doesn't she seem way too different from Noiz?)

Noticing Saldato's troubled expression, Rei shook his head.

Where Noiz had made him sweat just facing him, Sardat had an air of such casualness that he couldn't possibly be considered Noiz's equal.

However, considering that Erinde, the host of this gathering, hadn't questioned the story at all, it was undoubtedly true that the person before them was indeed the rank S adventurer, Heavenly Bow.

Feeling a sense of unease at this fact, Rei turned his gaze toward Saldat.
