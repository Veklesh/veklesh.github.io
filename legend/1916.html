"Hmm, I don't mind personally, but I can't say for sure until I check with the higher-ups."

Suu-chi had suggested that not only the people working here, but their families as well, might be able to stay overnight in the warehouse.

Rei asked a guild staff member about this, but that was the response he received.

“The problem is, once spring comes and we can no longer dismantle the Giganto Turtles, will they actually be willing to leave… that’s the real question, I suppose.”

“Ah… I suppose that’s a possibility.”

Since it’s a guild warehouse, the roof and walls are sturdy.

At the very least, they wouldn’t have to worry about drafts like in the slums or snow piling up inside the house.

(Come to think of it, there was a time in middle school when snow piled up in the hallway.)

The thought of snow piling up inside a house brings back memories of Rei’s own middle school days.

The school building was decades old and made of wood rather than concrete, which is why, after all those years, gaps had formed around the windows.

As a result, when winter came, snow would blow in through those gaps, and a thin layer would accumulate in the hallways.

Walking down the hallway was one thing, but living in a place like that was something Rei would absolutely prefer to avoid.

This wasn’t just Rei’s sentiment; it was likely the same for the residents of the slums as well.

That’s likely why the guild staff are worried that people might continue to occupy the warehouse even after the Giganto Turtle has been dismantled.

“If it were just the people who come here, since they have families in the slums, some would probably go back there if they lost their jobs. But if family and friends were to live here together… well, we’d prefer not to resort to forceful measures if we can help it.”

Since it’s a guild warehouse, the guild naturally wants to use it for various purposes.

If the slum dwellers refuse to leave, the Guild might have to contact the guards… or, depending on the situation, hire adventurers to forcibly remove them.

While that would be a natural course of action for the Guild, it would reflect very poorly on them.

That’s precisely why, as a guild official, my honest feeling was that I’d prefer to avoid such measures if possible.

“But it doesn’t necessarily have to come to that, does it? Considering Su-chi’s personality, I think she’ll leave once the deadline arrives.”

“The Guild can’t act based on mere wishful thinking like that. …That said, it’s also true that we’re short-handed for the demolition. I’ll try to make that case to the higher-ups.”

“...Is that okay?”

Given what had been said earlier, Rei had assumed the guild official was firmly opposed to his—or, more accurately, Su-chi’s—opinion, so he was surprised by the tone of acceptance in the official’s response.

Perhaps finding Rei’s surprised expression—visible even under his hood—amusing, the Guild official smiled cheerfully and spoke.

“Yes, of course. Regarding the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle, I’ve been instructed to accommodate Rei’s requests as much as possible. It’s likely that the higher-ups won’t be able to act immediately, but I believe they’ll ultimately agree to it.”

From the Guild’s perspective, they had entrusted Rei with numerous difficult requests in the past.

The kind that would be virtually impossible for an ordinary adventurer.

That is precisely why the Guild Master himself has instructed them to accommodate his requests as much as possible in this matter.

Given that, it was almost certain they would accept it in the end, unless it was an utterly unreasonable demand.

“I see. In that case, could you go get permission from the higher-ups as soon as possible?”

“Understood. I’ll head to the Guild right away and make the request.”

Bowing to Rei’s words, the guild clerk said something to another clerk who was giving instructions to the group dismantling equipment a short distance away. The clerk who was addressed nodded and ran off.

……Even though this place isn’t that far from Gilm, since it’s still outside Gilm, there was a high chance monsters would appear…

(Well, I’m sure it’ll be fine.)

The guild clerk’s movements were those of someone with considerable strength.

Given that, unless a very high-rank monster appeared… at the very least, something like the kobolds—which have been appearing most frequently lately—seemed like an opponent they could handle however they wanted.

“Sorry to keep you waiting. I’ve instructed them to report the general situation to headquarters, so now we just have to wait for their response. …Though, completely emptying several of the warehouses seems like it’ll be quite a hassle. Especially at this time of year.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes. Well… the adventurers tend to really push themselves in the fall, as if it’s their final push of the season.”

“Ah… well, I suppose so. Especially those worried about whether they’ll have enough funds to get through the winter—they’ll be desperate not to have to work in the cold while it’s snowing.”

In other words, they work that hard, and as a result, the warehouses end up packed with materials and magic stones.

Some of those items are sold to merchants who come to Gilm to stock up on goods, but since the merchants leave Gilm before the snow falls, any materials the guild buys after that can’t be sold off and naturally end up in the warehouse.

The most common items are, as expected, materials from Gamelion—a true autumn specialty.

Since the animals themselves are fairly large, their pelts are correspondingly large as well.

……The silver lining is that most of the meat has already been sold by now.

In any case, we need to clear out one of the Guild’s warehouses—specifically, the one with the least amount of materials stored in it.

“Haha, you’re right. Though, due to the expansion construction this year, things are quite different from before.”

“I suppose so. I never imagined kobolds would invade the city… and not just once, but day after day, night after night. Maybe we should just build a wall, even if it’s a makeshift one?”

“I hear the higher-ups at Gilm are considering that. I’ve also heard the Guild is pushing for it because of the adventurer issue. …Though, even if it’s just a makeshift wall, making sure they can’t get into Gilm would require a pretty massive construction project.”

“Shouldn’t we have done that construction work before the snow fell?”

“...You’re right. Looking back now, I think so too. That said, I never imagined at the start that kobolds would swarm in such numbers and keep invading like this. Honestly, I have no idea what’s causing it.”

The fact that this is the first time the town of Gilm has undergone such a large-scale expansion project is likely one of the reasons.

We’d been planning various ways to increase Gilm’s capacity for quite some time, but the existence of Treant Forest forced us to accelerate those plans all at once.

But above all, the primary motivation was likely to provide job placement opportunities for the adventurers who had come to Gilm specifically for the expansion project.

Initially, no one had anticipated that the kobolds would advance this far, so it was necessary to prioritize the expansion project over building makeshift walls.

These various circumstances became intricately intertwined, leading to the current situation.

“Even so, I hear they did manage to rush up a makeshift wall, at least? Though, since it was truly just a makeshift wall, it seems it was pretty much useless against the massive horde of kobolds that came.”

Another guild staff member, who was giving instructions to the workers dismantling structures a short distance away, chimed in.

After hearing that explanation, Rei thought for a moment… and then spoke up.

“Just to be clear, Gilm and its members aren’t actually accepting this situation where kobolds keep pouring out endlessly, are they?”

“That’s right. To be honest, I don’t think the higher-ups ever imagined things would turn out like this.”

“In that case, if we can prevent the kobolds from entering Gilm… or significantly reduce their numbers, can I assume that will free up more manpower for our demolition work?”

“Huh? Ah, well, that would definitely increase the manpower. …However, there’s also the possibility that the kobolds will be drawn even more by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood and come here.”

“What about the residents of the slums? Isn’t kobold meat highly valued as food? I heard they also sell materials and magic stones.”

Rei asked this based on what he’d heard from Suu-chi, but the two guild staff members exchanged glances, looking troubled.

Eventually, the person in charge of the disposal, wearing a troubled expression, spoke up.

“To be honest, I don’t really know about that. It’s certain that the slum dwellers have obtained a considerable number of kobold corpses, but…”

“In that case, is it okay for us to just go ahead and build a wall here to block off the connection to the outside?”

“……Huh?”

For a moment, the guild official seemed completely baffled by what Rei was saying.

Still, the fact that he was able to snap out of it and start thinking clearly so quickly was proof of this guild official’s competence.

“I’ll need to run this by the higher-ups, but in principle, I don’t think there’s a problem. As for the slum dwellers… given that they’ve been acquiring large quantities of kobold corpses up until now, I doubt they’ll have any trouble finding food… though I can’t say for certain.”

“From the slum dwellers’ perspective, kobolds are a resource they can obtain without having to take dangerous risks themselves. If they suddenly can’t get them anymore, I can’t rule out the possibility that they’ll become dissatisfied.”

Rei nodded in agreement at the other guild official’s words.

To the slum dwellers, kobold corpses are a cash cow.

If he did anything to prevent them from getting their hands on them, there was no doubt Rei would earn their hatred.

Rei understood that, but he believed that increasing the manpower for dismantling the Giganto Turtle took priority over being resented by the slum dwellers.

“But how exactly are we going to build the wall?”

“Ah, I can handle that with my earth magic. Though… I’m not that skilled with earth magic, so the wall itself won’t be very high.”

To be precise, it’s not earth magic, but the terrain manipulation skill from Death Scythe.

At Level 4, he can raise or lower the ground by 150 cm within a 70-meter radius.

...Normally, a height of 150 cm is something a kobold could easily jump over.

But if I raise the ground by 150 cm to create a wall and lower the area outside Gilm by 150 cm, the resulting wall will be three meters high.

While three meters might not be enough to completely block the kobolds… Rei’s prediction was that it would at least shut out the majority of them.

“I see, earth magic, huh? I think it’s better to try it than not, but if it’s an earthen wall, wouldn’t the kobolds just dig through it?”

“Well, there’s not much we can do about that. As for a countermeasure… let’s see. If we have Marina use water and wind elemental magic to freeze the earthen wall, wouldn’t that make it a bit harder for them to dig through?”

What specific effect would freezing the earthen wall have?

Rei didn’t know for sure, but he could easily predict that it would have greater durability than simply building a standard earth wall.

“Lady Marina, you say… Ah, yes. That’s right. Since you’re in a party with Lady Marina, Rei, you should be able to pull something like that off, shouldn’t you?”

Saying this, the guild clerk looked at Rei with admiration.

To the guild staff, Marina was seen primarily as the great former guildmaster.

That’s precisely why Rei, who was so casually asking that very Marina for help, was someone worthy of respect.

“So, could you run this by Gilm’s higher-ups through Gilm? If we get permission, I’d like to get that wall built today, if possible.”

“Today? …You’re as quick to act as ever. All right. I’ll have someone run to the guild again…”

“Hold on.”

I’ll have someone run over there.

Before the guild clerk could finish his sentence, Rei cut him off.

Confused as to why he’d been stopped, the guild clerk shot him a questioning look. Rei called out to Set, who had been standing a short distance away, keeping a watchful eye on the surroundings.

“Guruu?”

“What’s up?” Rei stroked Set’s head as he purred, then addressed the guild clerk.

“I’m free right now anyway. …I can’t find any magic items or anything like that.”

Rei muttered this with a hint of disappointment—likely because he hadn’t been able to find a magic item that would attract the kobolds.

And so, Rei set off for Gilm together with Set.
