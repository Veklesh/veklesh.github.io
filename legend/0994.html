Rei watched the green carpet below pass by in an instant, then turned her gaze to the sky.

Just a moment ago, the spring-like sun had been shining, but now clouds had gathered, obscuring it.

Fortunately, the clouds weren't the kind that brought rain.

(It'd be a pain if it started raining now, just as I'm about to look for an alchemist.)

Praying silently that the rain wouldn't start, Rei kept his eyes on the sky, but then noticed Set move his face.

"What is it?"

"Grrr..."

Set's gaze shifted toward the forest, but the trees blocked Rei's view, so he couldn't see what Set was looking at.

Still, he figured if Set was looking, there must be something there. He tapped Set lightly on the neck.

"Get down."

"Grrr..."

Responding instantly to Rei's words, Set flapped his wings and headed toward the ground.

As they neared the ground, Rei understood what had caught Set's attention.

The smell wafting up from the ground was rust.

It was so thick it suggested more than just one or two creatures.

Mixed in was a stench of decay, and that alone made it clear what state the ground was in.

He understood the situation, but he couldn't grasp why it had come to this.

"Did monsters fight each other? But that alone wouldn't explain this intense stench of decay and blood."

Monsters attacking other monsters is almost always for food.

If they're eating them, the monster corpses should naturally end up inside their stomachs. I couldn't imagine such an intense stench of decay and blood lingering like this.

If the corpses had been left long enough to rot, the thick, lingering smell of blood should have vanished.

This meant that on the ground now, corpses that had been there long enough to emit a stench were mixed in with corpses giving off the scent of fresh blood.

That prediction of Rei's would be proven correct in the most unforeseen way the instant Set landed on the ground.

Countless monster corpses lay scattered carelessly across the ground.

The only way to describe it as countless was because almost none of the monster corpses retained their original form.

Nearly every corpse was reduced to fragments of flesh—a state that could only be accurately described as minced meat.

Some monsters that seemed to have been feeding on the corpses were no longer within Rei's visible range.

Set... had probably noticed the griffin, a high-rank monster, descending and fled.

Monsters with low intelligence, like goblins, might have continued eating the flesh without noticing Set. But the monsters present before Rei and Set descended were likely proper monsters.

"What the hell is this? Did a bunch of monsters fight each other to death or something? The moment we stepped down, this stench hit us..."

"Gyaaah!"

Rei muttered, pinching his nose at the sight before him.

For Rei, whose senses were sharper than average to begin with, the stench of decay before him was quite overwhelming.

For Set, whose sense of smell was even sharper than Rei's, the sight before him inflicted damage incomparable to Rei's.

Previously, when they had ventured into an undead-infested floor of a dungeon, he had been mentally prepared and managed to endure it. This time, however, it was a complete ambush.

Normally, he could have completely detected the stench while flying...

"Set, it's too much for you to stay here right now. Fly up into the sky and wait for me. Once we're a bit further away, the smell was almost gone."

Rei told Set, who had his face pressed against the ground and was covering it further with his front paws. Set immediately flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

Looking up at Set, Rei then surveyed the surroundings once more.

The exact number of monster corpses scattered about was unclear, as most had been reduced to flesh fragments, many further rotting. Still, it was a considerable number, and he realized they weren't just one type, but several, perhaps even dozens of different kinds.

Horns, fangs, wing bones, tail bones—clearly diverse monster parts lay scattered and rotting.

Amidst this, Rei's gaze settled on a magic circle located near the center of the area where several corpses lay scattered.

The magic circle itself emitted a dull glow. At its center stood what appeared to be a pedestal, upon which rested an incense burner.

"An incense burner...? I see. So this wasn't eliminating the stench... but suppressing it."

Deep within the forest, this location naturally harbored a considerable number of monsters and beasts.

If the smell of blood or decay were wafting through the air, it would naturally attract many monsters.

To Rei, it seemed the one who created this scene here must have disliked that and set up that incense burner.

"But then why were there monsters when Set came..."

Considering how he had ridden Set down here himself, the answer to that question came immediately.

"So the incense burner's effect is merely to mask odors to a certain extent, not some kind of barrier?"

His gaze fell upon the incense burner placed atop the pedestal at the center of the magic circle.

Since it was an incense burner, its effect being related to smell wasn't particularly unnatural.

"Still, the real question is why the hell are they slaughtering so many monsters..."

Rei muttered while scanning the surroundings, but suddenly, among the corpses scattered on the ground, the rotting head of a Cyclops and the still mostly intact head of a Kobold came into view.

"Cyclops, kobold... Hey, this can't be..."

Rei understood that an alchemist had been doing something here, given the magic circle and incense burner. But seeing the familiar monster heads of a cyclops and a kobold, he vaguely grasped what had happened.

"Yeah, that makes sense. No matter how powerful a magic item is, monsters can't just master it that easily. So, they were testing whether monsters could handle magic items here. The two I saw succeeded, and these guys... failed?"

The exact number of corpses scattered on the ground was unclear.

Still, from the vast amount of rotting flesh and bone fragments, he could tell it wasn't just a few dozen.

"And it's not just kobolds and cyclopes. There are several other types of monsters too... Did they succeed in equipping those monsters with magic items, or did they fail? I guess I'll have to ask the alchemist directly for the details."

He muttered, deciding he had no further business here and started to leave... but Rei's feet stopped.

A few seconds passed. He contemplated something, then finally took a step forward. Not away from here, but toward the magic circle.

"Since it's left here, this incense burner might not be that important. But it's better if they have one less card up their sleeve. Besides, if this burner's gone, they naturally can't experiment here anymore. If the guy helping the alchemist is skilled, he might manage something..."

Still, if it could hinder their actions, it wouldn't hurt to try. He stepped into the magic circle...

"Ow!"

For an instant, he felt a pain like being zapped by static electricity, but it vanished just as quickly.

"...What? That felt oddly weak for something meant to protect an incense burner."

Tilting his head in confusion, Rei touched the incense burner resting on the pedestal. The next instant, it vanished into the Misty Ring.

Simultaneously, he noticed something was definitely different about the surroundings now, though he couldn't quite put his finger on what it was.

"Must be the incense burner's effect. Well, if it bothers them even a little, that's fine. I suppose I should report this to Lord Daskar, though. ...Having them turn into undead would be a real nuisance."

The stench of decay was gradually fading, but Rei, seemingly oblivious—perhaps his nose was numb from the rot—continued burning the corpses with fire magic.

Fortunately, this spot was well-concealed by the branches overhead, and it was spacious enough that there was no risk of the fire spreading.

He hadn't managed to incinerate every corpse, but after burning about eighty percent of them, he left the site.

He could have called for Set here, but thinking Set would dislike it, he headed to a slightly more distant location.

「Set！」

At the call, Set purred and descended to Rei's side.

In the darkness, the movements of a man conducting some sort of experiment suddenly froze.

The figure watching from a short distance away reached for the scabbard at his waist and spoke.

"What's wrong?"

The voice was neutral, and to an outsider, the figure appeared to be nothing more than a slender man.

Though covered in leather armor, a closer look revealed a body tracing curved lines.

From a distance, one might mistake it for a man, but up close, some would recognize it as female.

"Just now... someone moved the incense burner from the magic circle."

"Huh. So someone came over there, huh? Who do you think it was? A passing adventurer?"

At the woman's teasing words, the man stirred blue liquid into some powder he held and voiced his disagreement.

"Probably not. That spot is deep within the forest. Someone who would deliberately pass through such a place..."

No one. As the man tried to say that, the woman smiled faintly.

"True, that might be the case in a normal town. But unfortunately, this is Gilm. It's the sole frontier of the Mireana Kingdom, and precisely because of that, it attracts many skilled adventurers. There are plenty who could easily push through a forest like that. The Magic Forest is another matter entirely."

"The Demon Forest, huh? Honestly, I am interested. But you stopped me, didn't you, Adria?"

The woman called Adria sighed at the man's discontented mutter.

"Of course. That place is a veritable hell on earth. With my skills, I wouldn't last a day inside. It's like the Demon Mountain in the Bestir Empire, right?"

"Is that so?"

The man stopped working and turned his gaze toward her, as if surprised by her unexpected words. Adrienne sighed in exasperation.

"Listen. The Demon Forest is famous, right? Even I knew about it back in the Bestir Empire."

"Isn't that because you're an adventurer, Adria?"

"Just because you're an alchemist and skilled doesn't mean you should be so ignorant of the world."

Though her words carried a hint of mockery, the expression on Adria's face was more amused than anything else.

Of course. If the person before her truly knew the world, they would never have pulled such a stunt.

The fact that she was enjoying herself right now was precisely because she was naive.

"Well, gathering monsters was a pain, but seeing that spectacle was worth it. If monsters can't withstand the magic item's corruption, they explode from the inside. ...Still, why would anyone design a magic item to corrupt its owner? Doesn't it require some seriously rare materials as a catalyst?"

"Yes. Honestly, when I left Bestia, I didn't have many materials left. But without that, ordinary monsters couldn't use magic items at all. They literally have to be taught into their bodies."

"Hmm. ...I'm fine as long as I get to see an interesting spectacle. Anyway, back to the topic—did you ever figure out who tampered with that censer?"

At Adria's hopeful tone, the man shook his head.

"Not exactly. That incense burner was an improvised magic item. Honestly, I never imagined so many other monsters would gather there."

"Well, if monster corpses are piled up, it's only natural other monsters would come for them. More importantly, the fact the incense burner was tampered with probably means it's now known that pile of monster corpses is man-made, right?"

"That's true. Though it doesn't really change anything. It'll definitely be harder to conduct experiments there now, but if it's just about doing experiments, there are plenty of other places."

"It feels weird for me to say this as your partner, but you've really let loose, haven't you?"

"Is that so? But if I remained as I was before, I could never avenge Crimson... Prince Kabajido. Given that, I'll commit any act, no matter how heinous. The punishment for killing my benefactor... I will make them pay, without fail."

Infusing magic into the powdered Wind-Thunder Ore, the man—Zuboz, the former alchemist of the Bestir Empire—muttered, his words thick with hatred for Rei.
