The enormous cicada, measuring over twenty meters in length, had its torso cleaved clean in two. Its upper half was blown away by Set's strike, while the lower half collapsed to the ground.

Seeing this, Rei finally released the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. Set, too, killed the cicada by landing on the ground with the recoil from unleashing Power Crush, then rushed over to Rei.

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Set, purring happily, rubbed his face against Rei. Rei stroked his head with the hand not holding the Death Scythe.

"Thanks to you, I managed to pull through."

Upon hearing this praise, Set growled even louder, clearly delighted by Rei's words.

Several minutes passed like this. Suddenly, they noticed the orb of light slowly descending toward Rei and the others.

Its flickering light was slow, almost as if it hesitated to approach Rei.

Rei tilted his head at the orb's behavior, wondering why it wasn't approaching.

But... that was only natural.

The orb was, after all, the will of the World Tree, and the World Tree was, needless to say, a tree.

And trees, naturally, feared fire.

True to its name as the World Tree, the very essence of the orb possessed strong fire resistance, even against bonfires burning nearby or the flames unleashed by magicians.

Even for such a light sphere, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour that Rei had been wearing until just moments ago was different.

An immense amount of magical power, transformed into flame magic by Rei's innate talent, had been condensed to the point of becoming visible.

It was as if it were the very embodiment of flame.

Even the World Tree, renowned for its fire resistance, had its limits.

Rei’s unleashed Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour far exceeded that limit.

That was precisely why they hesitated to approach Rei, even if they wanted to.

If he could communicate with the light sphere, Rei might have understood the situation, but unfortunately, he possessed no such ability.

All he could think was, "Hmm, the light sphere isn't coming near me."

"Hey, what's up? Is something wrong?"

As Rei asked this, the light sphere slowly approached... then suddenly, as if its previous behavior had been a lie, it began to flicker violently.

Simultaneously, sensing movement behind him, Rei leapt backward almost reflexively.

Along with Rei, Set also leapt sideways on all fours.

Moving away from the spot, Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the movement and was stunned to see the upper half of a giant cicada there, his eyes widening.

"What!?"

Its upper body had been severed, and then, after taking Set's Power Crush, it had been blown away from the point of severance.

Despite this, the giant cicada somehow moved using only its upper body and lunged toward Rei.

Even so, it seemed that attack had truly been its last ounce of strength. The giant cicada's upper body collapsed to the ground and didn't twitch again.

"...Is it really dead this time?"

Having been attacked by just its upper body once, Rei remained on guard, Death Scythe in hand.

After several seconds, tens of seconds, dozens of seconds passed without the giant cicada moving, he finally relaxed his guard.

"Grrr?"

Is it okay? Set growled from his throat. Rei nodded reassuringly and gently stroked him.

Beside him, the light orb that had been hesitating whether to approach Rei moments ago had quietly moved closer, now at the same distance it had been before the battle with the giant cicada began.

Rei glanced at the orb but said nothing more, picking up a stone that lay nearby.

To confirm the giant cicada's condition, he lightly... yet at a speed that would undoubtedly cause pain if it connected, hurls the stone.

The stone struck its mark, sending an unpleasant sound echoing through the air, like stone sinking into flesh.

Even struck at such a speed, the giant cicada's upper body didn't move even slightly.

Well, it did move slightly from the impact of the stone, but it was clear that this movement was not of the giant cicada's own volition.

"Looks like it really is dead. ...I'll go check. Set, cover me just in case."

"Grrrr..."

Rei told Set, stepping forward while keeping the Death Scythe ready. A light sphere moved before his eyes, flickering.

As if asking, "What about me?", Rei considered for a moment before speaking.

"You stay near Set. If that giant cicada is a monster nurtured by your magic or sap, it'd be the worst possible matchup for you."

After hearing Rei's explanation, the orb flickered several times before obediently heading toward Set.

It understood it meant he was being told to stay near Set out of concern for his own safety.

Glancing at the orb near Set, Rei approached the giant cicada once more.

He closed the distance to the giant cicada—or more precisely, its upper body—while remaining ready to respond immediately if anything happened. Seeing it still didn't move, he then jabbed it several times with the stone tip of the Death Scythe.

Seeing still no reaction whatsoever, he thrust a little harder next... and eventually understood it was truly dead.

"I never thought it'd still be alive even after splitting its body in two. I saw on TV or something before that insects don't feel pain... Is that why? No, but I've fought insect-type monsters before, and some clearly felt pain."

Tilting his head, he examined the giant cicada's corpse once more.

Naturally, Rei's first interest was the giant cicada's magic stone.

But at twenty meters long... even split clean through the torso, there was no way he could quickly extract the magic stone from a ten-meter-long body.

Well, technically, if he entered the body through the cut, he could probably retrieve the magic stone. But that would mean getting completely drenched in the giant cicada's bodily fluids, and Rei wanted to avoid that situation if possible.

On the other hand, slicing through the upper body with Death Scythe... even considering that, it would undoubtedly take time.

In the end, Rei put the magic stone aside for now and shifted his focus to the legs.

Some had been severed by a single strike of the Death Scythe wielding the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, but the giant cicada originally had ten legs in total, five on each side.

Though severed, nearly half remained attached to the lower body, leaving several still attached to the upper torso.

"These legs... They made a metallic clang when they blocked the Death Scythe's blade, didn't they?"

Recalling the battle at the World Tree, Rei fixed his gaze intently on the tips of the giant cicada's legs.

The tips of the legs were sharp blades... or rather, needle-like points, and yet there was a hole-like opening at their center.

What flashed through Rei's mind upon seeing it was how it had clung to the World Tree's trunk, sucking sap and magical power from it as well.

In other words, the giant cicada's legs served as both weapons and mouthparts.

"But then, why does it have a proper mouth on its face?"

He touched the tip of the leg, but what he felt in return was a hard, almost metallic texture.

Having deflected a strike from Rei's Death Scythe, it undoubtedly possessed considerable hardness.

That meant it was also an excellent material for crafting weapons.

(Though, there's a hole right in the middle... If it were just a sharp needle, it could be used as a weapon as-is.)

As I pondered this while touching one of the giant cicada's legs, I suddenly sensed someone approaching.

Turning my gaze in that direction, I saw three Dark Elves standing there.

Seeing them, I recalled that while fighting the giant cicada overhead, several had been heading toward the clearing... the one created by the World Tree's light spheres.

(Oh, right... they were there...)

Given how intense and chaotic the battle had been, Rei had completely forgotten about that incident. He cautiously addressed the three dark elves approaching him.

"What is it?"

The somewhat light tone of their voice seemed to ease his tension. He hurriedly opened his mouth.

"What's wrong isn't the point! What is this cleared land?! Ugh, seriously. I don't know what method they used, but destroying the forest like this..."

The female dark elf, likely the leader of the trio, approached as she spoke.

"And that huge thing—isn't that the monster that was flying around the forest sky just now? It seemed to be fighting Mr. Rei's griffin... But something like that suddenly fell to the ground! Of course we'd come check on it!"

The other two dark elves nodded in agreement with their leader's words.

"If you go around destroying the forest like that... if things go badly, we could end up in a fight with the whole settlement!"

Rei was slightly surprised to detect a hint of concern for him in those words.

(Guess I am appreciated after all.)

Though Rei seemed taken aback, he had healed the World Tree at the Dark Elf settlement and safely found and protected the four missing children.

To the dark elves, Rei was practically a benefactor.

He absolutely didn't want to do something so ungrateful as fight such a benefactor, and above all, he didn't think he could win.

Seeing the dark elf woman hesitating over how to explain things to the others in the settlement, Rei spoke reassuringly.

"It's all right. The forest hasn't been destroyed."

"Huh? What are you talking about? Look, it's completely cleared out right now. You can't possibly expect us to believe that..."

The dark elf woman said this with a hint of exasperation, and the other two dark elf men nodded in agreement.

But... while the three of them were like that, Rei's gaze was fixed on the trees beyond the cleared land.

Following Rei's gaze, the dark elves looked back at Rei, unsure what he intended to do.

(Honestly, even if someone said a forest... just a part of it, had been moved, it'd be hard to believe. ...How did they really do it? The roots and all should have been firmly anchored in the soil.)

It was an utterly absurd occurrence, but it was typical of Rei not to realize that the very existence of Rei and Set could itself be called unreasonable.

"If you absolutely can't believe what I'm saying... well then, why not step outside the forest? You'll see that the forest itself has grown larger to compensate for the cleared land here."

Suddenly, Rei wondered what had become of the barrier of confusion, if the shape of the forest had warped. The barrier enveloping the settlement itself was one thing, but... Then again, in this land where magic and magical power existed, it was the World Tree that had performed this feat.

He concluded worrying about that was pointless.

"The forest... expanded by the size of this cleared land?"

Even for a Dark Elf who lived alongside the World Tree, the words coming from Rei's mouth were unexpected and utterly unbelievable.

Yet, he found himself wanting to believe it. The fact that Rei had, at least temporarily, restored the World Tree to full health, and above all, the enormous cicada corpse spread out before them now, held powerful persuasive force.

Someone with this much power wouldn't tell a lie that could be easily exposed with a little investigation.

"Right now, shouldn't we be more concerned about the settlement than the forest? When you headed for this cleared land, the battle with the monsters was still ongoing, wasn't it? Is it really okay not to go back?"

"Huh!? Y-Yeah, you're right. I was so overwhelmed by the sight... But this clearing is safe now, right? I can assume that, right?"

"I can't guarantee absolute safety since I didn't create it, but I can only say it's probably fine."

After saying that, Rei touched the giant cicada's upper and lower halves, storing them in Misty Ring. He called out to Set and mounted his back.

"Wait, what about us...?"

The dark elf woman implied she wanted them to take her along too. From atop Set's back, Rei shook his head at her.

"Sorry, but that's impossible. Set can only carry me... or maybe a child at best. You're clearly heavier than a child, right?"

Though not as large as Marina's, the dark elf woman's chest was still quite substantial.

She was also taller than Rei, and there was no way she weighed as little as a child.

"So, I'm sorry, but you'll have to make your own way back. But... I think we can probably handle the monsters, so don't worry."

"Huh?"

Just like when he'd mentioned the cleared land, the dark elf woman looked utterly confused. Ignoring her reaction, Rei gave Set a light tap on the neck.

As usual, sensing Rei's intent, Set took a few running steps and leapt into the air.

The ground quickly grew distant beneath him... and in mere minutes, he arrived at the settlement.

"Set, count on you."

"Gururuu"

He gave a low growl... and then, circling near the settlement, Set let out a loud, piercing cry.

Not to intimidate as a king, but purely to show the other monsters that Set... that a griffin was here.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

Set's cry reached every monster near the settlement. Realizing a griffin was close by, they fled in a panic.
