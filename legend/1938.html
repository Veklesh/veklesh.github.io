After gathering information on the Viscount Cobbs’ household at the guard post, Rei was unable to obtain any further significant details, so he headed next to the earthen wall.

Since he couldn’t very well have a guard escort him, there was a possibility that, following yesterday’s incident at Grange, the Red Cloth had sent new forces to destroy the earthen wall.

In fact, Rei had no idea exactly how many Red Cloths remained.

For all Rei knew, the ones she saw yesterday might have been the only survivors, but there was still a possibility that many more Red Cloth members remained.

If that were the case, it wouldn’t be surprising if they were making their next move after Grange’s group failed.

(Though the chances are slim.)

Given that these were the very people controlling the Red Cloths, it wouldn’t be strange if they’d received word that Grange had clashed with Rei.

Given that, if asked whether they would take actions likely to cause further casualties among the Red Cloth, Ray could honestly only say it was a toss-up.

That said, if they’re aware of that, it wouldn’t be unreasonable to assume that Rei also knows about the base Grange is using… the building they’re renting from Viscount Cobbs.

“Gruu?”

“What’s wrong?” Set asked, clearing his throat.

Rei stroked Set’s head as they made their way through the town… and eventually entered the construction site for the expansion.

And what he sensed was that there wasn’t much of a fighting atmosphere.

At the very least, compared to when he’d visited this construction site yesterday, the signs of battle had clearly diminished.

That meant the number of Kobolds climbing over the earthen walls was smaller than yesterday.

(Or could it be—as I suspected yesterday—that the Kobolds hiding in the gaps between the building materials were defeated by other adventurers yesterday, resulting in fewer battles here now…?)

That was a hopeful assumption—one he’d like to believe—but it wasn’t entirely out of the question.

With that thought in mind, Rei pressed on… and soon, he caught sight of an adventurer fighting a Kobold.

That said, these weren’t the strong Kobolds he’d struggled with before; they were, after all, just ordinary Kobolds.

From every angle, the adventurer clearly had the upper hand.

“Hey, Set. Which side do you think that adventurer is on?”

“Huh?”

“Which one?” Set tilted his head, seemingly unsure what Rei meant.

Finding that adorable gesture soothing, Rei continued speaking as they walked along the path.

“Is he an adventurer hired by the guild to hunt down Kobolds, or someone who’s still fixated on hunting Kobolds here?”

In both cases, what they’re doing is the same: fighting Kobolds here.

But if it’s the former, that means they’re fighting here because the guild commissioned them to do so; if it’s the latter, it means they’re still choosing to fight here for some reason.

The former is fighting to prevent the Kobolds that have scaled the earthen walls from entering the town if no one else is left to fight them, while the latter is serving as an escort for the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle—a job that involves fighting even more Kobolds, yet offers the benefit of having comrades to help in a pinch, and even the perk of receiving Giganto Turtle meat once the job is done.

Logically speaking, guarding the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle is clearly more profitable and safer, so if someone is choosing to fight Kobolds here of their own free will, you could say they’re quite the eccentric.

……That said, when it comes to being eccentric, Rei is one of the most eccentric people in Gilm.

In fact, Rei was considered to be so eccentric that there were likely few others even in Gilm—no, perhaps even in the entire Kingdom of Mireana—who were as eccentric as he was.

“Anyway, the former is one thing, but the latter… well, it’s not really my place to say anything.”

Muttering to himself, he watched the adventurer fighting the Kobold.

The adventurer fighting there dodged the Kobold’s attacks and, as he passed by, severed its neck with the longsword he held.

The head flew through the air as if it were a ball or something.

Just seeing that sharp strike, Rei surmised that this adventurer would fall into the former category of the classification he’d mentioned earlier.

It was that sharp of a strike.

The adventurer who had severed the Kobold’s neck with a single blow seemed to notice Rei’s gaze and looked back at him.

But perhaps because there was still some distance between them, he didn’t call out to Rei; instead, he gave a slight nod in greeting and walked away.

The other party likely recognized Rei, but perhaps decided there was no particular need to speak with him at the moment.

That said, Rei knew the adventurer was quite skilled, but that didn’t mean he wanted to talk to him.

(Judging by that level of strength, he’s probably someone hired by Gilm. …Though, as is typical of Gilm, just because someone is skilled doesn’t necessarily mean that’s the case.)

Wondering if he might be a true solo adventurer—someone with that level of skill but who struggles with social interaction—Rei continued on through the construction site.

In the end, that was the only group he’d actually seen fighting; he’d sensed signs of battle elsewhere, but hadn’t gone out of his way to check them out.

They kept walking, and eventually, Rei and Set arrived in front of an earthen wall.

“……Hm?”

He looked at the earthen wall—or, more precisely, at the broken longsword lying around it—and felt a pang of doubt.

It was clearly odd to find such a weapon here.

Moreover, the fact that it was a broken longsword meant that someone had fought here… or that someone had come here to dump the broken longsword.

(Well, it’s not like it’s completely unthinkable… but would anyone go out of their way to do something like that? It doesn’t seem like they tried to break the earthen wall and failed either.)

I haven’t checked thoroughly, but at a glance, there doesn’t seem to be any sign that the earthen wall has been chipped away.

I did consider the possibility that the longsword had broken while someone was trying to chip away at the earthen wall, but there’s no sign of that so far.

To begin with, even though this earthen wall has been reinforced by Marina and Meru’s spirit magic, it isn’t so sturdy that a longsword couldn’t leave a single scratch on it.

However, weapons can break easily depending on how they’re handled.

Moreover, for an adventurer who doesn’t properly maintain their equipment, there was a very real possibility they wouldn’t even notice that the longsword’s blade was worn down.

“Guruu?”

“What’s wrong?” Set asked, clearing his throat. Rei shook his head, saying it was nothing, and picked up the broken longsword.

To the untrained eye, at least, the longsword didn’t seem particularly worn down.

(So, did the weapon break during a fight with a Kobold, and they just left it here? Well, that’s a problem in itself.)

Even if the kobolds had somehow managed to climb over the earthen wall, that didn’t mean every single one of them was carrying a weapon.

No, considering the act of scaling the earthen wall, those without weapons would have a decisive advantage.

So, if such a Kobold scaled the earthen wall with bare hands and landed here, what would happen if there were a longsword lying around—even if the blade were broken in the middle?

Naturally, the Kobold would use it as a weapon.

“Hm? But there was a Kobold fighting that solo adventurer earlier, wasn’t there?”

Recalling the earlier battle, Rei looked at the weapon he was holding once more.

Was that Kobold skilled in unarmed combat, or did it simply climb over the earthen wall from a different spot—not where Rei was—and fail to notice the longsword lying on the ground?

(It’s not like he doesn’t want to use a broken weapon because it doesn’t match his aesthetic sensibilities, is it?)

With that vague thought in mind, and not wanting the broken longsword to be picked up by another Kobold later, he stowed it away in his Misty Ring.

For a split second, I wondered if it might come in handy later, but upon further reflection, I realized it probably wouldn’t be of any use at all.

If I were to use it at all, given that it’s made of metal, I’d probably use it as a throwing weapon—or maybe even melt it down to make something else.

As Rei observed the earthen wall with these thoughts in mind, he sensed a change in Set’s demeanor, and in the next instant, he understood why Set was acting that way.

“Huh. So that’s how you climb over the earthen wall. Though, in a way, it’s exactly what I expected.”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s mutter, the Kobold that had just leaped over the earthen wall was startled for a moment, then let out a growl at Rei and Set.

Rei aside, even though it was facing Set—a monster clearly of a higher rank than itself—the Kobold showed no sign of fear or of trying to flee.

Anyone could tell at a glance that this was clearly no ordinary Kobold.

That said… even if this Kobold was different from the norm, that didn’t mean its strength had increased.

Before Rei could do anything, Set moved and effortlessly snapped the Kobold’s neck.

It was, quite literally, a single blow.

“Grrr…”

Set growled at Rei, as if begging for praise.

“Ah… yeah, I guess so. Good job.”

Though a little hesitant, Rei petted Set and praised him.

Set accepted this with a happy look, his eyes narrowing.

“Anyway, I’m glad I got to see how the Kobold managed to climb over the earthen wall.”

Saying that, Rei picks up the Kobold’s corpse by the ankles and flings it over the earthen wall.

Whether the corpse hit its mark or not, a scream that sounded like it came from the Kobold could be heard, but Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

Since they had known all along that there were Kobolds on the other side of the earthen wall, it was only natural that they would hear a scream.

“It’s good that there’s no problem with the earthen wall. It doesn’t look like any more of those Red Cloth guys showed up.”

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growled in agreement.

“Well, I figured there probably wouldn’t be anything, anyway.”

Rei’s words were almost a bit of bravado, but he wasn’t saying that without any basis.

After all, even though Grange had led a group of former Red Cloth members to destroy the earthen wall yesterday, he didn’t bother returning to base to report—he simply vanished without a trace.

To be precise, Rei had handed him over to the Daskar Intelligence Unit, but there was no way they could have known that.

Consequently, the fact that Grange hadn’t returned led them to assume that Grange and his entire group had either been killed or captured, and that their base had been discovered… It wouldn’t have been wrong to think that.

That’s precisely why they’re likely busy dealing with that right now, so it wouldn’t be strange if destroying the earthen wall Rei built were put on the back burner for the time being.

Rei had anticipated this very scenario, which is why he assumed there would be nothing there.

“Grr?”

“So, what do we do now?” Rei paused to think for a moment as Set let out a low growl.

There wasn’t much for Rei to do at the moment.

He’s confirmed there’s nothing wrong with the earthen wall, and the guards are keeping watch over the temporary base used by those handling the Kobold incident.

The option of helping dismantle the Giganto Turtle remained, but if Rei tried to get involved clumsily, there was a high chance he’d just end up getting in the way of those currently working on it.

“In that case… patrols?”

That said, it wouldn’t be a full-scale patrol like the guards do; more accurately, it would be a stroll where he checks out the food stalls and restaurants while eating.

He wasn’t entirely without the thought of bringing some kind of snack or treat to the people dismantling the Giganto Turtle while he was at it.

“Grrrrrrrrrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a delighted growl.

It seems that getting to eat delicious food is a real treat for Set, too.

“What do you want to eat? If I’m going to bring something, it’s gotta be soup, right? …Come to think of it, the pot I used the other day is washed and in the Misty Ring, so I guess I’ll just have them put the soup in that.”

Rei usually bought soup in the pot it came in, but naturally, once the soup was finished, he wouldn’t use that pot anymore.

Well, depending on the situation, he might use it for something else, but there’s no doubt they just keep piling up.

That’s precisely why he often opted to have the soup poured directly into the pot.

In search of a hearty, warming soup packed with meat, Rei and Set walked away from the earthen wall.
