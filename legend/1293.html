The Snow Salamander lay collapsed on the ground, its mouth pierced by the Twilight Spear.

Though it had been slashed across the neck by the white cloud forged from Silver Lion material, sustaining a near-fatal wound at that point, it still possessed enough vitality to attempt an attack on Byune. Yet, pierced through the mouth, throat, and body by the Twilight Spear, it could not survive.

"Marina, you seemed to know this one's name?"

Hearing the sounds of the recent battle, Vihera asked while keeping watch for any new monsters approaching.

It was not Marina who answered Vihera's words, but Rei.

"Snow Salamander. A Rank B monster."

At the start of the fight, Rei hadn't been able to recall what kind of monster stood before him, but now that time had passed, remembering wasn't difficult.

"A Rank B monster? Isn't it a bit strange for such a monster to be in a place like this?"

Vihera muttered, staring at the corpse of the Snow Salamander, which could be called enormous.

Rei... and Marina agreed with that sentiment.

"Yeah. Even though we're in the forest, this is still a shallow area. For a Snow Salamander to appear here... I wouldn't say it's impossible, but it's a bit questionable."

"If it weren't winter, I'd say it's impossible."

Monsters active from spring to fall rarely show themselves so easily, especially high-rank ones.

But now was winter.

Monsters that only appeared in winter were undoubtedly special creatures in their own right.

"Hmm."

While listening to Rei and the others talk, Byune looked a little downcast.

She was clearly still bothered by her failure to take down the Snow Salamander in one hit.

Though Byune rarely showed emotion, failing to make a significant impact in the party's first battle seemed to have been a major blow.

The one who keenly noticed Byune's state was, naturally, Vihera, who had known her the longest.

Comfortingly... and to calm her down, Byune gently stroked Buune's head.

"Come on, don't worry about it too much. You did well enough in this fight, right? You managed to dodge the Byune's final blow cleanly."

"Hmm..."

Even with Vihera's comfort, Byune couldn't hide her dejected look, clearly still troubled by various thoughts.

"I hate to say it, but we formed this party knowing full well about Byune's lack of strength, so I don't think you need to worry about it that much."

"Grrrr..."

Set growled in agreement at Rei's words, though he hadn't done much in this battle himself.

Of course, the situation had largely progressed before Set could intervene.

But there was no doubt Set could have intervened whenever he wanted.

That he chose not to act, whether due to a lack of opportunity or simply believing it wasn't necessary, remained unclear.

"Hmm."

That was Set's consolation, but unexpectedly... well, perhaps it was predictable to those aware of the surprisingly good rapport between Byune and Set. In any case, Byune's mood improved somewhat.

At the very least, she no longer looked visibly depressed.

Seeing Byune like this, Vihera, Marina, and Rei breathed a sigh of relief. While keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings, they immediately began discussing the battle they'd just fought.

...Despite the Snow Salamander's considerable weight, it posed no problem at all for Rei and the others, who possessed the Misty Ring.

Rei, who had stored the Snow Salamander's corpse—now devoid of the Twilight Spear—into the Misty Ring, spoke first.

"I think that fight went pretty well. What do you think?"

"Yeah, I think we coordinated well together. Not perfect, of course."

Vihera nodded at Rei's words.

Considering it was their first battle, their coordination wasn't bad at all.

But compared to those who'd been partying together for years... compared to those scorching winds who were close to Rei, their coordination was naturally clumsy.

To be honest, Marina was the only one here who had ever clearly fought as part of a party.

Rei had been operating solo due to matters involving Set and his own secrets, and Vihera had always been a solo fighter.

Well, Vihera had been in a party with Byune, but there was an unspoken agreement that Vihera handled most of the fighting.

Of course, that doesn't mean Byune wasn't fully participating in combat at the time...

Still, because of that, Rei is more accustomed to party combat than Ray.

"I thought the spirit magic Marina used was pretty good, don't you think?"

At Vihera's words, Rei... and Byune nodded.

Especially Byune—it was Marina's spirit magic that allowed him to slice through the Snow Salamander's neck.

And above all, it was also thanks to Marina that she avoided the Snow Salamander's bite.

Both of those feats were possible because the wind spirit magic had boosted Byune's physical abilities.

"The bow attack was pretty good too, in terms of keeping the Snow Salamander at bay. ...But, Byune. It was good you landed a hit on the Snow Salamander, but maybe you should have used the long needle when Marina used her bow."

"Is that so?"

Vihera tilted her head at Rei's words.

"Wasn't Byune's primary role originally to guard Marina?"

That was an undeniable fact.

Within this party, Rei and Vihera, who served as the frontline, possessed extremely powerful combat strength.

Assigning such individuals solely to Marina's protection was clearly wasteful.

In that sense, Byune, who wasn't particularly strong in combat within the group, was the perfect choice for the role of escort.

Of course, when we say her combat strength isn't high, that's strictly within the context of this party.

Considering her as a typical adventurer, she possessed sufficient combat strength; she was by no means inadequate as a guard.

The disagreement between Rei and Vihera centers on whether to prioritize escort duties or deterrence.

Rei argued that using the Long Needle would allow Byune's strength to be fully utilized for deterrence, while Vihera insisted that prioritizing her role as an escort was better.

Neither is necessarily wrong.

(Still, it's rather amusing that aggressive Vihera insists on prioritizing protection, while Rei advocates for deterrence.)

Listening to their exchange, Marina speaks up.

"Why specialize in just one? I think we should adapt—play defense when needed, pressure when needed. This is our first time fighting together as a party anyway. We'll figure out the balance over time."

To some, her words might sound like a noncommittal answer, but the fact they were fighting outside Gilm now was largely for that very purpose—to adjust.

Rei and Vihera had been operating together for a considerable time, developing a near-perfect combat rhythm – not quite unspoken understanding, but close enough.

Even with Byune joining them, they were somewhat accustomed to it... but adding Marina, who hadn't been heavily involved in combat, meant various adjustments would be necessary.

Underestimating someone as just one person could lead to unexpected casualties in battle.

For a temporary party, it might not be a problem, but if forming a fixed party, they needed to properly adjust those aspects beforehand.

"So, how about next time, Byune takes on support duties, and in the fight after that, focuses solely on escorting... something like that?"

Everyone nodded at Rei's suggestion. ...Set didn't seem to fully grasp it either, but he nodded along, swept up by the flow of the situation.

(Honestly, Rei's pretty good at taking charge, you know. ...I think he's got plenty of potential as a party leader.)

Marina glanced at Rei and thought that for a moment before speaking again.

"Still, I never expected Snow Salamanders to appear here. Maybe there's no food around?"

"Maybe there aren't many other monsters around. ...Weren't there any reports hinting at something like that?"

Marina shook her head at Rei's question.

"Adventurers naturally become scarce in winter, so that kind of information rarely comes in. You know that well, Rei?"

Given that, Rei could only nod in agreement.

Adventurers venturing outside Gilm during this season to earn money were mostly not the most skilled.

...Though, admittedly, there are exceptions like that Set-obsessed adventurer who spent all his money on offerings to Set and now takes quests out of desperation.

"So, does that mean venturing deeper into this area might bring us face-to-face with monsters even more dangerous than the ones we've encountered so far?"

Though Vihera's words sounded concerned at first, her eyes held the anticipation of a battle with a formidable foe.

For Vihera, powerful monsters weren't something to avoid, but rather something she should seek out herself.

Rei and Byune understood this, and though she hadn't known her as long as they had, Marina also grasped Vihera's nature.

That's precisely why she judged that saying anything here would likely be futile.

Besides, they had come here specifically to test their party coordination, so avoiding a fight was fundamentally not an option.

Of course, things would be different if something like the Silver Lion appeared.

But such a powerful monster wasn't likely to appear just like that.

"Though I can't say whether we'll get to fight an opponent Vihera would enjoy,"

Marina stated it as if asking "What should we do?" was unnecessary, and Vihera nodded in agreement.

"Let's go."

"Yeah, yeah, let's go then. ...But attacking us even though Set's here is a bit much. A Rank B monster should obviously be smarter than a goblin. ...Is it because it's a Rank B monster?"

Rank D or C monsters recognize Rank A monsters as beings they absolutely cannot defeat. Especially since Set is treated as a rare species, equivalent to Rank S, it's only natural they sense the power difference.

But a Rank B... Maybe, just maybe, there was a slight chance... a tiny possibility... that it thought it could actually defeat Set and attacked?

That was Rei's speculation, but before he could voice it, Vihera moved on.

"Come on, let's go. I need to get used to this body, and next time, I want a slightly stronger enemy."

"Ah... yeah, you're right. Let's go then."

The strike he'd just landed on the Snow Salamander must have been reasonably satisfying.

Led by Vihera as she advanced deeper, Rei and the others followed.

Of course, thanks to Marina's spirit magic, they weren't bogged down by the snow and could walk normally.

"Spirit magic really is incredibly convenient, isn't it?"

"Really? Maybe I'm just used to it, but I don't think so."

"You're such a spoiled brat."

"...I'd rather not hear that from Rei. Logically speaking, you're the one who definitely has all sorts of convenient magic items. Especially... you know?"

Marina's gaze fell on Rei's right hand. ...More precisely, her right wrist.

Most residents of Gilm knew exactly what that bracelet was.

If you're an adventurer... no, even if you're not an adventurer, it's something everyone would want.

"Just having an item box makes things way too convenient, don't you think?"

"I won't deny that."

In fact, even while on quests, Rei could eat warm meals and sleep in rooms made by magic tents that were far more comfortable than any inn.

Recently, he'd even acquired a magic item kiln, allowing him to cook his own meals.

The richness of that life was hardly adventurer-like. ...When it came to camping, it might even surpass that of nobles.

(Come to think of it, if we're active as a party, we'll use the magic tent for camping too. If that's the case, we'll need to get new beds or something...)

He needed to buy a bed somewhere.
