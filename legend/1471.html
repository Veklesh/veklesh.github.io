While Ajax was discussing their situation with Levjeff and Haston... Rei was also meeting with Ilse to report on today's events.

Of course, such a report couldn't be given just anywhere, so Rei and the others were, as usual, in Marina's garden.

At first, he was surprised to see only beautiful women present, briefly thinking, "So this is the legendary harem party..." Fortunately, he managed to keep that thought to himself and took his seat at the table.

Though, even if someone called it a harem party, most of the people present probably wouldn't have denied it.

The only exception was Ara. While she did harbor feelings for Rei, they were those of friendship and, at the same time, respect for someone stronger than herself.

Unlike the others, it wasn't romantic affection between a man and a woman.

That said, while not quite as stunning as Elena, Ara still possessed features that could easily be called beautiful.

And being the third daughter of the Scarley Count's household, she naturally received many advances.

She had held onto the faint hope that being seen as part of Rei's party might reduce such suitors...

...As for Byune, she didn't deny being called a harem member, since being with Rei meant she could eat delicious food whenever she wanted and even make money.

In truth, her actions stemmed from understanding that Rei would never harbor sexual desire for someone like her, a child.

Additionally, she used it like insect repellent against those who approached her, much like Ara did.

"...Rei-san, that... that person you're playing with, Set... Unless my eyesight has failed me, he looks like a dragon..."

Even though the nights had been hot and sleepless, the moment she entered this garden, she was astonished by the unbelievably comfortable temperature. Despite it being a summer night, while she could hear the sounds of insects, there were no mosquitoes or other bugs to be found... She'd been amazed ever since entering Marina's garden, but seeing Yellow must have pushed her astonishment to its peak.

Of course, she was also astonished by Elena, a beauty unforgettable at first glance, on par with Marina and Vihera.

No, in this case, it would be more accurate to say I wasn't surprised by Elena's beauty, but rather by the fact that the Princess General was here.

Ilse, ever the sharp-eared one gathering intel on Ajas, naturally knew what the famous Elena looked like.

But... even so, the shock of seeing a dragon child right before her eyes was probably greater than seeing the Princess General standing there.

A high-rank monster like the griffin Set was certainly rare enough, but for better or worse, having met Rei and Set many times before, she was somewhat accustomed to them.

"Ah, Yellow. Yellow is Elena's familiar."

"Hehe, seems I managed to surprise you after all."

At Rei's words, Elena flashed a pleased smile and turned her gaze to Ilse.

Seeing someone surprised by her familiar—though she treated it more like a pet—must have made a favorable impression on Elena.

Furthermore, the fact that someone like Ilse was striving to avenge her family also contributed to Elena's softer attitude.

Elena didn't condemn the act of revenge itself.

Well, not all revenge, but the revenge Ilse was attempting now seemed sufficiently justified even to Elena's eyes.

If this were a case where Ilse's father committed a crime, was captured by a guard, and consequently executed, and Ilse then sought revenge against the guard who captured him, she would never have approved of such vengeance.

That wouldn't be revenge; it would be nothing but a grudge.

"Anyway, let's finish eating before we talk. No one can think straight on an empty stomach, right?"

Everyone nodded in agreement at Marina's words.

They all knew hunger saps concentration.

...Though it was also true that fasting existed, so it wasn't necessarily the norm.

Regardless, no one objected to Marina's suggestion, and they all began preparing.

Eating in this garden had become a daily occurrence lately.

Given that, preparations proceeded without any particular confusion.

Only Ilse, who was here for the first time, simply watched as the meal was quickly prepared in the garden.

Of course, Ilse had camped before.

While Ilse primarily handled gathering requests, she couldn't always return home the same day.

Sometimes, gathering medicinal herbs growing far away, specific tree nuts, young shoots, flowers... those required overnight stays, and that happened fairly often.

That's why she was accustomed to eating outdoors, but what was being prepared right before her eyes bore no resemblance to the meals she made while camping.

"Ara, bring the food from the kitchen."

"Understood, Elena-sama."

"I wonder what kind of meal it is today. I'm looking forward to it."

"Mm!"

Even as this exchange took place, tables and chairs were brought out. The dishes from Rei's Misty Ring, along with the food Ara had brought, were laid out on the table.

"Now, let's eat."

With Marina, the mistress of the house, speaking, dinner time began immediately.

It was already pitch dark outside, so it was quite late for dinner... but precisely because of that, everyone present eagerly began eating the delicious-looking food.

Usually, they would bring out Rei's magic item, the kiln, and use it to bake various things.

But today, because there was an important matter to discuss, the oven wasn't prepared.

"So... how is it? I tried making it the way Marina taught me the other day."

Elena timidly asked for opinions on her cooking, her usual dignified demeanor seemingly a lie.

Today, Elena and Ara had made a stew packed with oak meat and summer vegetables.

The dish itself wasn't particularly unusual, but for Elena, it was something she was preparing for the one she loved, so she probably had a lot on her mind.

"Yeah, it's delicious."

Stew is inherently a dish that's hard to mess up.

Of course, in terms of effort, there are various ways to refine it, and the final taste of stew made by an ordinary person versus a professional chef can be vastly different.

Naturally, Elena's stew was several steps below what a professional chef would make.

Still, because Elena made it, everyone, including Rei, found it tasted better than what you'd get at a regular diner.

"Yeah, it's plenty tasty."

"...I see."

This wasn't the first time she'd served Rei a meal.

But still, hearing Rei say it was delicious felt fresh to Elena... and brought her boundless joy.

"Oh my, how enviable. Next time, you'll have to treat me to your home cooking too."

Seeing Elena's delighted expression, she must have felt envious. Or perhaps she just felt like teasing her.

In any case, Marina murmured this with a smile.

(The Princess General's Home Cooking)

Amidst that group, only Ilse was half-frozen in place, staring at the scene before her.

"What's wrong? Aren't you going to eat? It's delicious!"

"Huh? Oh, yes."

Ilse snapped back to reality at Rei's words and brought the stew to her mouth.

It wasn't a flavor that hit you directly, but rather something gentle... a taste that seemed to seep into your very body.

It was different from the food served in the cafeteria. It made her wonder when she had last eaten something like this.

"Looks like you enjoyed it."

Seeing Ilse like this, Elena said happily.

Even without saying it, Ilse's expression made it perfectly clear what the stew tasted like.

Anyway, as they ate various dishes Rei had taken out from Misty Ring besides the stew, time passed.

Ilse, who had been tense at first, gradually relaxed and began to enjoy the meal.

Once the meal had settled, Rei finally got down to business.

"Now then, regarding our contact with Ajash today as the woodcutter's escort..."

Ilse stiffened involuntarily at the name Ajas coming from Rei's mouth.

It was only natural. In Ilse's mind, he was already half-certain Ajash was her enemy.

But the only objective evidence was the snake tattoo.

If someone claimed it was just a similar tattoo, it would be difficult to argue against that.

That was precisely why Ilse was paying close attention, not wanting to miss a single word coming from Rei's mouth.

Rei understood the gaze Ilse was directing at him, and without any unnecessary pretense, he opened his mouth.

"From the vibe I got talking to him... he's shady. At the very least, he's definitely got something to hide."

Hearing Rei's words, Ilse wore a complex expression—one that seemed both relieved and disappointed.

"What was he like when you talked?"

Marina asked, watching Ilse. Rei recalled his conversation with Ajas.

"Well, at first, he seemed like the serious adventurer the guild described. He was the first to check the surroundings and terrain to see what the situation was like."

Based solely on that, he could undoubtedly be called an excellent adventurer.

Compared to those who'd immediately charged into Treant Forest to start cutting down trees, Rei recognized him as undoubtedly superior.

Of course, being competent as an adventurer doesn't necessarily mean they're a good person.

No, actually, the more capable they are as adventurers, the more troublesome it could be if they have character flaws.

"But when I hinted during our conversation that I knew your true identity... your hidden side, he definitely reacted."

"So... it doesn't necessarily mean it's settled as revenge..."

Marina nodded at Elena's words.

"Right. But if he's hiding something shady, I think we should dig a little deeper. ...Gilm is in a rather delicate phase right now."

Marina's gaze was fixed on Elena.

Yes, precisely because it's a delicate time, she was sent here to ensure the Noble Faction doesn't do anything foolish.

Understanding this, her words carried strong persuasive power.

"Hmm. To the best of my knowledge, there shouldn't be anyone named Ajas among the Noble Faction associates... Of course, since I don't know every single person affiliated with the Noble Faction, I can't say for certain."

Perhaps judging that Marina's conversation might lead to suspicion that she herself could be a member of the Noble Faction, Elena preemptively declared.

But as she herself said, no matter how much Elena was known as the Princess General and treated as a symbol of the Noble Faction, she couldn't possibly know every single member of the Noble Faction.

She might remember the heads of noble houses affiliated with the Noble Faction, but she couldn't possibly know their relatives... let alone those secretly hired for tasks too sensitive to be made public.

"He could be with the Royal Faction, or even the Neutral Faction—not everyone there is above suspicion either, right? That makes it difficult to determine where this Ajas belongs... If he's competent, he'd leave no trace."

Everyone nodded at Vihera's words.

Indeed, if you think that deeply, it becomes impossible to pinpoint who might be suspicious.

"Lady Elena, could it be a faction outside the three major ones?"

"I can't dismiss Ara's point. No, actually, that would make things even more troublesome, wouldn't it?"

If it's one of the three major factions, you can at least predict their methods to some extent.

But as Ara said, if Ajax is working for another faction, there's the trouble of not knowing what tricks they might pull.

Moreover, they might casually break what you'd call an unspoken agreement, and that aspect alone would undoubtedly be troublesome.

"Anyway, my job guarding the woodcutter in Treant Forest was only for today. But starting tomorrow, I'll need to go collect the felled timber, so I'll be showing my face over there. I'll try making contact with Ajas then."

"...Be careful, okay? If Ajas is a strange one, you never know what he might do. ...Well, I guess I shouldn't be telling you that."

Whether it's Ajas or anyone else, if they try to harm Rei, they'll only get their comeuppance.

Knowing Rei's capabilities all too well, Marina couldn't help but let a wry smile slip at her own words.
