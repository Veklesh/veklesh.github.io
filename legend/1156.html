Falling from Set's back to the ground, Rei drew the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring in midair.

Using them as a two-spear style was still difficult... or rather, nearly impossible, but given the current situation, wielding those two weapons was the better choice.

In the forest... surrounded by trees, the Death Scythe was somewhat difficult to use.

In such terrain, the Twilight Spear, capable of thrusting attacks, proved more convenient.

Of course, he could thrust using the pommel of the Death Scythe, but the massive blade itself was still cumbersome.

Considering that, using the Twilight Spear for normal attacks and the Death Scythe for skill usage was the best choice.

As usual, Rei used Sleipnir's Boots to slow his descent once he'd fallen a certain distance, kicking the air several times before landing on the ground.

The goblins didn't notice Rei's sudden appearance from above at first.

...That's how engrossed they were in their current task... likely focused solely on hurling stones and wood at the man hanging from the tree by vines.

The man hung there, stripped of armor and clothing, completely naked.

They could only tell he was a man because he was naked; they couldn't have identified him by his face.

His face was horribly swollen and bleeding, making it impossible to tell what his original features had been.

"Gagyo?"

The first to notice Rei's presence was a goblin a short distance away, fiddling with a stone in its hand.

Judging by its larger size compared to the other goblins, it was almost certainly the goblin leader commanding this group.

Rei made that judgment and swung his Death Scythe wide with his right hand.

"Flying Slash!"

With that word, a slash flew from the blade of the Death Scythe.

Flying straight ahead, the slash severed the goblin leader's neck the instant he tried to move.

The head flew through the air like a thrown ball, eventually crashing to the ground with a dull thud that echoed around them.

Only now did the goblins finally notice Rei's presence and emerge, but Rei had already acted before they could react.

"Haaaaaah!"

The blade of the Death Scythe, swung with fury, severed several goblins from their torsos at once.

Without pausing after that strike, he thrust forward the Twilight Spear held in his left hand.

During his two-spear style training, he hadn't been able to wield these two weapons with such fluidity.

Of course, he still couldn't manage it now either, but he could manage some movement... The tip of the Twilight Spear crushed a goblin's skull.

"Gyaa!"

Only then did the surrounding goblins finally notice Rei's presence.

Several goblins charged at Rei, bellowing loudly.

They didn't even bother to gauge his strength... they simply charged.

It was precisely because they were goblins, but even so, their actions could only be described as reckless.

Perhaps, had the goblin leader still been alive, they might have responded differently.

Unfortunately for the goblins, their leader was already dead, struck down by Rei's flying slash.

Without a leader to rally them, the goblins naturally devolved into a rabble, each acting on their own whims.

As a result, the goblins had lost all interest in the man hanging from the tree. All that mattered now was how to defeat Rei standing before them.

...Even though several goblins, including their leader, were already dead, the fact that they still hadn't fled was a little surprising to Rei.

(Well, having them focus their attention on us isn't exactly a bad thing for us either.)

Swinging his right hand's Death Scythe to keep the goblins at bay, he glanced skyward for just an instant.

Though dense foliage blocked most of the sky, Rei instinctively knew Set was descending toward the ground.

(If we're going to safely rescue that man hanging over there, I need to keep these guys focused on me right now. At the same time, I can't let them escape, so I can't take them all out either.)

Death Scythe in his right hand, Twilight Spear in his left.

A slight smile touched Rei's lips at the unexpected debut of his dual spear style in combat.

Ideally, he would have preferred to showcase his dual-spear style in a more proper setting, facing a formidable opponent.

Still, considering this was merely practice for the dual-spear style rather than an actual debut, it wasn't such a bad situation.

He didn't know what would have happened if the clever goblin leader had been left alive, but since he'd already taken care of that, it wasn't an issue.

"Come on, what's the matter? Come at me. Come on, come on!"

He swung the Twilight Spear and the Death Scythe.

He kept taunting them with such antics.

"Gyaa-gyo!"

Irritated by Rei's actions, one goblin snatched up what appeared to be a club fashioned from a broken branch and charged at him.

And it wasn't just that one. The other goblins nearby also charged at Rei in the same way.

"You're just in time!"

Several goblins were severed at the torso, limbs, or neck by the swinging Death Scythe, while the Twilight Spear pierced through several goblins at once.

"You too!"

With the Twilight Spear still piercing a goblin's torso, Rei forcefully swung his left arm.

The momentum sent the impaled goblin flying, crashing into the goblin that had been about to attack Rei.

Being struck by a goblin its own size was no easy blow to endure.

Crushed by its comrade's body, it collapsed to the ground.

Rei didn't miss this opportunity. Still in the motion of slicing through several goblins, he swung his Death Scythe.

"Flying Slash!"

The slash severed the goblin lying on the ground.

"Gigi?"

Only now did they seem to grasp Rei's strength. Several goblins looked around frantically, searching for an escape route...

"GRRRRRRRR!"

In an instant, the goblin trying to find an escape route was struck by a falling blow released with a roar. Its skull shattered instantly, and it lost its life.

"Wahhhhh! Dammit!"

Hester, who had been thrown before Set unleashed his strike, was caught on a tree branch. Screaming, she managed to land on the ground.

Once on the ground, Hester frantically scanned her surroundings...

"Huh!? Pumpkin?"

She noticed the figure of a man hanging from a tree branch.

His face was unrecognizable from blood and swelling, and his body bore marks of being beaten, stabbed, and burned.

Yet Hester recognized him as Pump, likely because she remembered the tattoo on his arm.

In the village where he was born and raised, tattoos were carved onto the arms as a sign of adulthood.

Most of that tattoo was now unrecognizable due to burns, but she could still make out what might be called its faint traces.

"What!? Damn it, I'll help you right now!"

Hester shot a gaze filled with hatred at the goblin, but she didn't simply give in to her emotions. She knew what needed to be done now.

She sliced through the vine binding Pump, who was dangling from a tree branch, with her knife, catching him just as he was about to fall to the ground.

"Ugh... Huh?"

Hester thought Pump might no longer be able to speak, but seeing his face only twisted her own expression further in rage.

...Of course. Pump's tongue had been severed, and there were burn marks as if to say it was meant to stop the bleeding.

Without a tongue, it was only natural a human couldn't speak.

No, more accurately, he was barely conscious, so speaking was impossible anyway.

"I'll kill you."

The words that escaped Hester's lips, despite their quietness, were saturated with murderous intent.

It was only natural, given what had been done to her party member.

Even so, she couldn't just leave Pump alone and give in to her murderous intent.

All Hester could do now was watch the goblins being trampled by Rei and Set, her eyes dripping with murderous intent.

Unaware of Hester's burning hatred for the goblins, Rei slaughtered them one after another, treating it as perfect practice for his two-spear style.

Combat was a life-or-death, full-body exertion, and naturally, sustaining it for long periods was difficult.

When either physical or mental stamina wanes, it inevitably becomes a matter of life and death.

That's why, even against something like goblins, facing a hundred of them would exhaust an adventurer's stamina and lead to defeat.

But... that was only true for ordinary adventurers.

If the entity is extraordinary... like Zepail, a Rank B adventurer who nonetheless held a nickname, whose very body was crafted by those called demon lords, and his followers... and on top of that, if Rei has a partner like the griffin, Set... then there's no way Rei could be ordinary.

The Death Scythe he swung sliced through several goblins at once, while he hurled the Twilight Spear at the goblins trying to flee the scene.

Pierced from behind... or with their skulls crushed, the goblins' corpses lay exposed on the ground. The next instant, the Twilight Spear returned to Rei's hand.

Right now, Rei was truly a being worthy of being called the Grim Reaper to the goblins.

...Though it would be more accurate to say that was the result.

Using the Two-Spear Style in actual combat for the first time, she made numerous mistakes.

He misjudged the range of the Death Scythe swung with one hand, striking a goblin with the handle instead of slicing its torso with the blade... Or, when he should have pierced a goblin's head with the Twilight Spear, the handles of the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear collided, and the Crimson blade ended up slashing the goblin's throat.

Even so, the fact that goblin corpses were being produced with such precision only served to highlight just how abnormally strong Rei truly was.

At this point, the goblins, terrified by Rei's power, try to flee... but there's no way they could escape from Set at their goblin-level speed.

If these were slightly smarter monsters, they might have realized they couldn't escape alone and chosen to scatter in different directions... Unfortunately, goblins aren't capable of such thinking.

The goblin leader commanding these troops was the first to have his life taken by Rei's hand.

They judged the danger and scattered individually, sometimes coincidentally forming a group of a certain size... but even those were swiftly cut down by the ranged attacks wielded by Set and Rei.

And so, before they knew it, Hester's party of four, facing a hundred goblins they couldn't handle, found themselves with Rei and Set alone, one person and one creature, succeeding in nearly wiping them all out.

The reason it was "nearly" was because, when several goblins fled simultaneously, the trees in the forest happened to form a shield-like barrier, allowing a few to escape.

"Well, with the goblin leader gone, a few stragglers can't do much anyway."

"I get that... but I can't accept it."

Rei, who had been guarding Pump, retorted discontentedly at Rei's mutter.

From Hester's perspective, she didn't want to spare a single goblin that had put her party members through such an ordeal.

But she knew full well that her own strength wasn't enough to achieve that, so she could only voice her frustration.

"...Thanks to Rei, we were able to save Pump. I appreciate it."

She concluded with those final words.

"Ah, if you're satisfied with that, then that's fine. More importantly... we should deal with Pump first."

Whether relieved or physically exhausted... either way, as Rei spoke while looking at Pump, who had lost consciousness and fainted without anyone noticing, Hester turned her gaze toward Set.

The meaning behind that gaze was asking if he could carry him like she had...

What should we do? Set asked, looking at Rei.

Rei, too, hesitated, unsure what to do.

Hester would be fine, but Pump was unconscious now. If he woke up and went berserk, it would definitely be dangerous.

But leaving him like this wasn't an option either.

Pump was in a state where he could die at any moment.

"Please."

Hester bowed her head imploringly to the hesitant Rei... In the end, settling for a temporary recovery, Hester used the potion she carried to restore Pump somewhat. To prevent him from regaining consciousness and lashing out during transport, they bound him with rope from the Misty Ring. They left the magic stones on the goblins, finding it too much trouble to retrieve them... After that, they collected the goblin leader's corpse and flew away from the scene.
