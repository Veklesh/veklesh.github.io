The one dispatched by the Knights was Leodanis.

Since he had experience sparring with Rei, that was likely one of the reasons he was chosen.

Even Leodanis let out a cry of surprise when he saw Set up close.

“Whoa! So this is… a griffin?”

“Huh? Didn’t you take part in the Bestir Empire’s war? I was fighting alongside Set just like everyone else back then.”

Rei stroked Set’s head as he asked this, and upon hearing it, Leodanis gently looked away and spoke.

“I wasn’t officially assigned to the Order as a knight until after that war ended.”

“Ah… I see.”

All things considered, several years have already passed since that war ended.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange if someone had become a knight during those years.

“As long as Set doesn’t try to harm anyone, he won’t do anything in particular. …But Leo Danis is coming with me to make sure the people of Anesis don’t cause a commotion when they see Set, right? So, are you really okay being so afraid of him?”

Rei says this, half in a provocative tone.

In response to Rei’s words, Leodanis, as expected, wiped the look of fear and surprise from his face and spoke.

“I-I’m not that scared of him. Looking at him like this, isn’t he kind of cute?”

It was clear that Leodanis was putting on a brave face, but he seemed oblivious to his own demeanor as he reached out to Set.

“What’s going to happen?” Miranda watched the scene unfold.

That said, Miranda was the only one feeling tense; Rei didn’t seem particularly concerned about Set’s reaction.

It would be a different story if Leodanis were to try to harm Set, but all Leodanis was trying to do was pet him.

No, in fact, for Set, even if it was tentatively at the moment, having someone else stroke him was a welcome development.

Since he doesn’t sense any hostility, he even turns his head toward Leodanis to make it easier for him to pet him.

“……Oh?”

It’s not as if he noticed Set’s consideration, but the hand that gently rested on Set’s head conveyed a softer sensation than Leodanis had expected.

Leodanis was slightly surprised by this, but he enjoyed the sensation of stroking him and stroked Set’s head over and over again.

As for Rei, he would have been happy to watch Leo Danis get used to Set for a little longer, but since he also wanted to look around Anesis, he couldn’t really say that.

Since it was winter, the sun set early.

Since it was already afternoon, he wanted to leave the mansion as soon as possible.

“Leodanis seems to have gotten used to Set, so shall we head out right away? …Is that okay?”

“Huh? Oh, yeah. No problem. I’ll make sure to stay close so I don’t startle Set.”

Saying this, Leodanis, still rubbing the hand that had been petting Set just moments ago, looked at Set with a somewhat timid expression.

Set turned his round eyes toward Leodanis, as if to ask, “What’s wrong?”

Of course, Set knows that griffins are terrifying monsters.

Yet, when met with such a round, innocent gaze, he couldn’t help but wonder: Is Set really someone I should be wary of?

“Growl?”

“It looks like he’s wondering if something’s wrong with you, Set.”

Rei interpreted Set’s words.

Rei doesn’t fully understand everything Set is trying to say, but he gets the gist of it.

“Huh? Oh, no. It’s nothing. It’s just… well, from what I’d heard, griffins were supposed to be pretty ferocious. So the fact that this one is so friendly is pretty unexpected.”

“Ah, I see.”

Rei looks convinced by Leodanis’s words.

It was a question most people who had interacted with Set would ask.

In fact, some people even asked Rei, “If Set is this adorable, isn’t it strange that he’s rated as a Rank A monster?”

“Set is basically docile and friendly. In fact, he’s so intelligent you’d hardly believe he’s a monster. But that’s strictly because he’s Set as an individual. It’s the same with humans, isn’t it? There are good people and there are bad people. In that sense, Set falls into the ‘good’ category.”

Rei could sort of understand why that was the case.

Set isn’t a pure griffin; he’s strictly a griffin created by Rei using beast magic.

It’s precisely because of his origins that Set likely inherited something you might call Rei’s humanity, leading him to develop such a kind nature.

(Of course, this is just a guess, and it’s not something I can just blurt out to anyone. Besides, for someone who’s supposed to have inherited my humanity, he seems a bit too friendly, if you ask me.)

Considering my own personality, I do wonder if I’m really that similar to Set.

But there were aspects where I did resemble Rei and Set.

Like the way they both enjoy their meals, or the fact that they show no mercy once they’ve judged someone to be an enemy…

When I think about it that way, I suppose we aren’t entirely different after all.

“For now, just think of Set as special. I’ll warn you, though: if you ever encounter a wild griffin, don’t ever go near it. Wild griffins don’t watch you like Set does, let alone act affectionate.”

For anyone who knew how adorable Set was, it wouldn’t be strange to think they could admire a wild griffin just as much as they did Set.

But if you tried to do something like that with a wild griffin, you’d surely lose your life.

……The only consolation, perhaps, is that griffins are Rank A monsters.

After all, for someone living an ordinary life, it’s highly unlikely they’d ever encounter one.

“Ah, yeah. I get it. …I don’t think it’s okay to do something like this to any griffin other than Set, either.”

Perhaps because Rei’s expression was as serious as could be, Leodanis’s expression grew slightly more serious as he nodded in response.

“If you understand, that’s fine. Well then… shall we head into town?”

Given that Anesis is large enough to be considered a city, the phrase “heading into town” might be a bit odd.

But deciding that it suited the situation better, Rei ultimately didn’t correct himself.

Leodanis and Miranda, seemingly finding nothing amiss with Rei’s words, left Kerebel Duke’s mansion without saying a word.

“Come on in, come on in! Dried meat is cheap right now! Once the snow really starts falling, it’ll be a hassle to go outside. So you should stock up on some long-lasting dried meat while you still can!”

“Speaking of winter, check this out! I’ve got plenty of warm clothes to help you get through the winter. They’re secondhand, not brand new, so you can get them at a reasonable price!”

“When it snows in winter, just clearing the snow in front of your house is a big job. That’s where I come in—I’ll shovel the snow for you! It’s a contract for the whole winter, so if you sign up now, you won’t have to worry about shoveling this winter!”

"Here’s something for a cold body! How about some of our special soup packed with meat? It’ll warm you right up!"

It had been a few dozen minutes since they left the Kerebel Duke’s mansion.

Rei and the others had arrived at a street bustling with people.

As befits a city of this size, there were quite a lot of people there.

That said, compared to Gilm just a short while ago, it still feels a bit sparse.

(But that’s only natural, since people had been flocking to Gilm from all over.)

In Gilm, in particular, the population had grown so much at one point that there weren’t enough inns to go around, leading to the creation of makeshift lodgings—though they weren’t exactly “inns” in the traditional sense, but rather just places to sleep without meals.

Even so, there were people who had to sleep on the streets because there weren’t enough buildings, so it’s clear just how dense the population of Gilm was back then.

“So, what did you say you wanted to see, Rei? If it’s weapons or something, I… well, actually, the whole knight’s order has a shop we go to.”

Leodanis asked this, but the fact that there was no one around Rei and the others could be described as somewhat strange.

No doubt, Set’s presence was influencing the situation.

Even so, Leodanis knew that Set was by no means the sort of creature that would attack people.

Of course, it had only been about an hour since they met Set, but his docility, charm, and friendliness were enough for Leodanis to make that judgment.

...In this regard, one could say that whoever chose Leodanis to serve as Rei’s guide and escort had a keen eye.

If the knight dispatched by the Order had been older than Leodanis, he likely wouldn’t have been able to adapt so quickly, having witnessed firsthand just how much Rei and Set had distinguished themselves in the war against the Bestir Empire.

Naturally, Leodanis was aware of the events in the Bestir Empire.

But that is merely knowledge... nothing more than secondhand information.

There is a world of difference between someone who witnessed the battle firsthand and someone who merely heard about it secondhand.

Even if they possess the same information, the difference between the two is immense.

It wouldn’t be strange to describe it as a matter of “real-life experience.”

It is precisely because Rei understands this that he is grateful to Philma and Duke Kerebel for sending Leodanis.

“A weapons shop, huh? It’s not that I’m not interested, but I’m not exactly in need of weapons.”

“...Well, seeing that scythe and spear, I can’t say I don’t understand what you mean, Rei.”

For Leodanis and his acquaintances, the armory that tried to show Rei around could be called their regular haunt.

Of course, that doesn’t mean it’s a regular haunt for the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights; it’s strictly just Leodanis’s regular spot.

As a result, the weapons in that shop weren’t exactly top-notch.

They were the kind of weapons that were cheap but of decent quality.

Of course, many of the weapons Leodanis and his companions normally used were provided by the Order.

However, the items provided by the Order were, after all, only of decent quality. If they wanted to use other weapons—specifically, weapons that fit their hands perfectly—they had to procure them themselves.

That’s why the weapon shop—their regular haunt—existed.

That said, the shop was also frequently used to procure weapons to serve as backups for their primary weapons.

After hearing this explanation from Leodanis, Rei became slightly interested in the shop.

He was intrigued by the idea of weapons used as supplements rather than primary weapons.

That said, it was merely a passing interest; he had no intention of buying any weapons.

Speaking of auxiliary weapons, Rei had Nebula’s Eye.

This magic item, which generates arrowheads using magic, is a supplementary weapon that allows him to attack opponents by flicking or throwing them with his fingers. Since the arrowheads disappear after a short time, it was extremely convenient as a disposable weapon.

The downside is that, as a result of modifying the original magic item—which generated arrows through magic—into its current form, using Nebula’s Eye requires a massive amount of magic power.

In fact, I had previously tried to have Byune use Nebula’s Eye, thinking she might be able to handle it better than Rei, but she was unable to generate the arrowheads due to a lack of magical power.

“Speaking of which, there’s a pastry shop that’s popular among the maids. Would you like to go there?”

Miranda must have sensed that Rei wasn’t particularly interested in the weapon shop, so she suggested this alternative destination.

“Baked goods? What kind of things are those?”

“Oh, yes. They use something like bread dough, but they brush it with honey and bake it. The crispy texture is irresistible. Plus, since it contains nuts and dried fruits, the flavor changes with every bite.”

She must be recalling the taste. Seeing her murmur so happily, Rei found himself wanting to try those baked goods too.

(Huh? But since it’s made with bread dough, shouldn’t I technically call it a sweet bread instead of a pastry? Also, I feel like I’ve eaten something similar before… Ah, the bakery in Exil.)

She remembered eating a bread just like the one Miranda had just described at the bakery in the labyrinth city of Exil, where she had gone with Elena.

It might not be exactly the same, but that’s precisely why he wanted to enjoy a different flavor.

This was true not only for Rei but for Set as well, and as they cleared their throats to head to the shop Miranda had mentioned... Rei completely forgot about the weapon shop and set off to buy some baked goods.
