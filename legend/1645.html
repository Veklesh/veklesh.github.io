It’s been a few days since I returned to Gilm.

The work that had been causing problems has mostly been taken care of, and the issues in the Treant Forest, in particular, have been completely resolved.

...That said, since the woodcutters work in the Treant Forest almost every day, it’s only natural that felled trees will start piling up again over time.

In any case, we’re catching up on the construction site, and there are fewer troubles in town.

Rei and the others had been spending their days in Gilm feeling reassured by this, but today they were summoned by Daskar and had come to the lord’s manor.

“What do you think happened? Judging by the situation in Gilm, it doesn’t seem like there’s any particular problem. …Well, I suppose there are various minor issues, but…”

Hearing Rei’s words, everyone present nodded in agreement.

In fact, while the major issues have largely been resolved, there are still quite a few minor ones remaining.

That was only to be expected, given that they were in the middle of an expansion project.

“I don’t think we were called here because of a specific problem, though. Elena and Ara were called too.”

Marina’s gaze turned toward Elena, who was drinking tea.

Naturally, that tea was brewed by Ara, and it was far more delicious than anything a regular maid would make.

A cup of tea brewed by Ara was also placed in front of Rei and the others, and as he sipped it, Rei spoke up.

“In that case, does that mean it’s not a problem, but something good, or something like that?”

Even as he said this, Rei couldn’t fully believe his own words.

Based on his past experiences, he simply couldn’t imagine anything good coming out of a situation like this.

On the contrary, the fact that he could recall countless bad things... probably showed just how often Rei got caught up in trouble.

But this time, Rei’s prediction would prove wrong.

Just then, the sound of a knock on the door echoed through the room.

With a sense of foreboding, Rei tentatively asked if they could come in.

In response to Rei’s words, the person who opened the door and stepped inside was someone familiar not only to Rei… but to the others as well.

“Amaros!?”

Yes, it was the Set Cage... the man who had created the Mirage Cage.

“It’s been a while, everyone.”

Seeing Rei and the others, he greets them with a smile... but it was by no means a face that put Rei and the others at ease.

It wasn’t as if he had any visible bruises from being beaten.

It’s just that he had some pretty dark circles under his eyes.

Seeing how clearly sleep-deprived he looked, there was no way he could possibly be smiling and greeting them like that.

“Um… are you okay?”

Seeing Amalos’s state, Rei’s voice betrayed his concern.

In response to Rei’s words, Amalos flashes a weary—or, depending on the situation, one might even say haunted—smile.

“Yeah. I’ve been going through a busy stretch lately. The renovation is moving forward, and I’ve been working on the lumber for the expansion, plus a bunch of other things…”

“……Ah, yeah. Sorry about that.”

There was no doubt that the alchemists’ sudden busyness was due to Rei and his group.

Knowing this, Rei offered an apology almost reflexively, but in reality, there was no need for him to apologize.

After all, Rei and the others had simply done their jobs properly.

Precisely because he understood that, Amalos didn’t look at Rei with a reproachful gaze.

Part of the reason was likely that, being such a dedicated alchemist to begin with, he was actually doing the improvements to the Set Cage of his own volition.

“No, don’t worry about it. I knew the Mirage Cage was hastily made, so I expected it to have some flaws. …What’s wrong?”

“Huh? Oh, no. It’s nothing. Right?”

“Yeah, right. It’s nothing.”

The moment the name “Mirage Cage” was spoken, Rei and the others briefly looked dazed, as if they didn’t understand what Amalos was saying, but they hurriedly replied.

Since they’d been calling it the “Set Cage,” the name “Mirage Cage” had already been completely forgotten among Rei and the others… but they couldn’t very well say that to Amalos, who had made the Mirage Cage for them, so they just laughed it off.

Seeing Rei and the others’ reaction, Amalos looked slightly puzzled, but quickly got down to business.

“So, regarding the modifications to the Mirage Cage, I expect they’ll be finished in a few days. This time, I’ve focused particularly on improving the livability inside the cage.”

“That’s true. Since we spend quite a bit of time aboard the Mirage Cage, it would be a huge help if you could make it as comfortable as possible. Actually, if it’s possible, I’d love it if you could make the interior a little more spacious.”

At Marina’s words, Amalos shook his head apologetically.

“If we had the time, we could probably work on that too, but we don’t have the time this time around, so it’s simply impossible.”

“...Well, I suppose so. We haven’t decided exactly when we’ll be joining them again. For all we know, Daskar might even make an unreasonable demand for us to go back as early as tomorrow, right?”

“Well… I can’t really say anything about that for now.”

Given his position as Daskar’s subordinate, no matter how much Amalos agreed with Marina’s words, he couldn’t possibly voice that opinion.

Daskar isn’t the type to worry about such things, but even so, to go and do something like that on his own initiative would be nothing short of suicide.

“If we have time, we could expand the interior… We discussed this before, but would it be possible, for example, to set up a magic tent inside?”

Perhaps trying to change the subject away from Daskar, Elena spoke up.

Stowing a Magic Tent inside the basket was one of the ideas that came up during a previous discussion among Rei and the others.

A Magic Tent with a spacious interior could, in theory, carry more people than the Set Cage itself, and transport them comfortably.

As a means of transport, it would be the best possible option.

In fact, it would even allow them to transport those among the group being carried by Sura and the others who were feeling unwell by placing them in a Set Cage.

“A magic tent, you say? I understand what Elena is thinking, but since magic tents are rare magical items to begin with, it’s not so easy to obtain one…”

While not quite as rare as an Item Box, it is true, as Amalos mentioned, that a Magic Tent is a very rare magical item.

Getting one right away isn’t impossible… though it’s certainly difficult given Amalos’s authority.

“I think it would be possible for Rei and the others to acquire a Magic Tent on their own and incorporate it.”

“Even so…”

There’s no doubt that having a Magic Tent would be incredibly convenient.

However, given that Magic Tents themselves are so rare, it’s simply not a matter of just paying money to buy one.

If anything, connections are more useful than money in this case.

In fact, the Magic Tent Rei has wasn’t bought with his own money, but was given to him by Daskar as payment for a job.

(Surely, if I asked Lord Daskar for another Magic Tent right now... Ah, no. The expansion project we worked on, the retaliation against Jaya, leading nearly a thousand people who escaped from Mejogo... Thinking about it that way, maybe I could somehow get another Magic Tent?)

The contract stipulates that the reward will be paid in a lump sum at a later date.

But in Rei’s case, even if the reward were paid in gold coins, platinum coins… let alone Light Coins, he wouldn’t be the least bit happy.

After all, he already has enough funds to live a life of leisure for hundreds of years.

Besides, if he ever runs short on money, he can simply hunt down bandits—he’ll never be short on cash.

After all, there’s no shortage of bandits looking to make a quick buck.

“If I ask Lord Daskar to pay me for this series of jobs via the Magic Tent… do you think he’d agree to that?”

“Huh? Hmm, I’m not sure. I think there’s a possibility, and since Rei has contributed so much to Gilm, I’m sure it’ll be fine. The question is whether the Magic Tent has the capacity. The Magic Tent Rei is using is the one Daskar used before, right?”

“Yeah. That’s what I’ve been told. Wait, before that, wasn’t it Marina who gave the Magic Tent to me? Haven’t you been told about the circumstances surrounding that?”

“Hehe, that’s right. …Anyway, from Daskar’s perspective as the leader of the Neutral Faction… well, for example, if a war breaks out, we’ll definitely need something like a Magic Tent.”

“……Wouldn’t a regular tent or something like that work?”

If the only purpose is to gather people, wouldn’t a regular tent be sufficient without going to the trouble of using a Magic Tent?

At Rei’s words, Marina shook her head.

“That might be fine for an ordinary noble, but for Daskar, who leads one of the three major factions, appearances and reputation are absolutely essential.”

“Is that why we need a magic tent?”

“Precisely because magic tents are so rare, simply owning one is enough to attract attention. In that sense, it’s better to have one than not. Especially for someone in Daskar’s position.”

If Daskar were merely one of the nobles comprising the Neutral Faction rather than its central figure, not having a Magic Tent wouldn’t have been a problem at all.

But as long as he holds the position of leader of one of the three major factions, maintaining appearances is crucial—no matter how ridiculous he might personally find it.

“Hmm. …So, I’ll ask him just in case, but if that doesn’t work out, I’ll give up on it, right?”

“Or, even if it’s not right now, there’s always the option of having Daskar buy a new one. To begin with, the Magic Tent used for the Mirage Cage doesn’t have to be anything too fancy, does it? Even one that’s several grades lower than the one Rei has would do.”

Since the reason we need a magic tent is to transport a larger group at once… naturally, comfort isn’t a priority.

Well, of course, it would be better if it were comfortable, but if I had to choose between that and capacity, I’d naturally choose the latter.

“Ah, of course, it’s out of the question if there are so many people crammed in that they can’t even relax normally.”

“Right. In that case, we might be able to work something out. I’ll talk to Daskar myself. But I don’t think we can expect anything right away. It’ll probably be next spring at the earliest.”

Even if we don’t prioritize comfort that much, when it comes to creating a magic item using spatial magic, it’s not as simple as just saying, “Please make it,” and having it done.

Even from Daskar’s perspective, it’s the same.

……Though, if it were the king himself, he could probably pull off such a reckless request.

“Hmm. In that case…”

The one who interrupted the conversation was Elena, who had been listening to Rei and Marina’s exchange.

“We’ve discussed this before, but what about storing my carriage inside the Mirage Cage?”

“……Your carriage, Lady Elena?”

Elena nodded at Amalos, who asked the question with a puzzled look.

“I don’t know if you’re aware, but my carriage is also enchanted with spatial magic, so the interior is about the same size as a magic tent. If that’s the case, I think it could be used just like the magic tent you’ve been describing, provided we can fit it inside the Mirage Cage.”

“Well… hmm, that’s probably a bit difficult. No, not that it’s impossible—I mean, it’s a matter of time.”

“A matter of time?”

“Yes. First, to put the carriage inside the Mirage Cage, we’d need to disassemble the Mirage Cage. Of course, we wouldn’t be taking it apart completely, but even so, it would require various tasks, starting with magical preparations. That alone would likely take a few days.”

Rei couldn’t help but wonder if it would really take that long.

On top of that, when he heard the detailed explanation—that even after that, there would be various time-consuming tasks, starting with magical adjustments—he realized he couldn’t just force a solution.

Amaros was already visibly sleep-deprived, so if they made him work even harder now, he would undoubtedly collapse.

In the end, it was decided that the plan to install a magic tent or a carriage—at least for now—wouldn’t be pursued.

Even though he was actually quite enthusiastic about it.
