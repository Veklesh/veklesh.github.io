"...I expected it, but so only Byune will be our main combat force?"

With a sigh, Rei spoke, and Byune—who had gouged out the Cyclops's single eye—stretched his chest proudly.

"Hmph!"

Though still very much a child, Byune's chest was far from the majestic mountain ranges of Vihera, let alone as ample as those of Mireine or Johanna.

"Oh, I can strip materials off decently enough, you know?"

"But the Cyclops' skin has a lot of meat on it."

Rei declared, her gaze fixed on the Cyclops skin Vihera had stripped off.

True to his words, a considerable amount of flesh clung to the skin.

Taking it to the guild or a shop like this would surely result in it being heavily haggled down, deemed poorly processed.

(Then again, Vihera was the one who took down this Cyclops. I'm only getting the magic stones anyway. If Vihera plans to start adventuring with Gilm from now on, maybe practicing skinning here wouldn't be a bad idea... Though practicing on a Cyclops is a bit of a luxury.)

Originally, when Vihera was active in the Labyrinth City of Exil, she traveled with Byune.

Since Byune was better at stripping materials, they divided tasks: Byune handled stripping while Vihera kept watch.

...Though normally, it's the thieves who handle the lookout duties.

"Well, well, Mireine and Johanna said they'd help us once they're done, so it should be fine, right? Just do what you can for now."

"...Mm."

Vihera's words drew Byune's gaze.

Others wouldn't have noticed, but Vihera, who had known Byune for a long time, recognized the hint of exasperation in that look.

Faced with Byune's gaze, Vihera felt a rare flicker of impatience. She grabbed her knife again and ran the blade along the Cyclops's arm, aiming to slice off a piece of skin.

In this case, it wasn't just that Vihera's skin-stripping skills weren't particularly high; the fact that the target was a Cyclops was also a factor.

The Cyclops, proud of its strength, naturally had well-developed muscles, making the skin difficult to peel away.

Because of this, when cutting the skin, he couldn't cleanly sever the muscle, and chunks of flesh came along with the skin.

"Well, I guess I can't really talk,"

Even the skin Rei cut off had some flesh attached, though not as much as Vihera's.

Shifting his gaze to the other Cyclops... the one Mireine and Johanna were dismembering, they were working at a speed that could only be described as strength in numbers.

"They're fast over there."

"...Well, we only have Byune as our main force over here, so it can't be helped."

"I have been practicing skinning and such, though."

"Should I try it too?"

While Byune deftly extracted the cyclops's single eye, severed its horns, and began skinning it, Rei and Vihera each handled only one arm—left or right.

Depending on the monster, the hardest part of butchering is probably skinning it.

If you mess up, like Vihera did, you end up with flesh stuck to the skin, forcing you to go through the trouble of removing it multiple times.

And if you don't manage to separate the flesh cleanly from the skin at that point...

"Ah!"

...you end up tearing the skin, just like Vihera did.

"Hmph!"

Seeing this, Byune let out a scolding voice.

Rei wasn't as clumsy at skinning as Vihera, but he certainly wasn't skilled either.

While they were dissecting the Cyclops like that, due to the difference in numbers and their respective abilities, Mireine and Johanna's dissection finished.

"Rei, we're done over here. We'll help you guys."

Mireine announced, and Johanna and the others nodded, gathering around the cyclops Rei and the others were skinning.

"Sorry about that."

"It's fine. You saved my life yesterday, so this is the least I can do. ...Besides, if we finish skinning faster, that means more time to play with Set-chan."

Considering Rei and Johanna's usual behavior, there was no doubt the latter reason was far more genuine than the former.

"Anyway, leave this dissection to us. Rei, could you stow the other one in the item box?"

The expression she'd shown seconds ago vanished instantly. Where Mireine looked, the Cyclops' materials lay spread out before her, meticulously dissected to the utmost precision.

The single eye was carefully placed in its dedicated container. Sellable parts like horns, bones, tendons, meat, skin, and organs were all neatly grouped together. The unusable parts had been tossed into a hole they'd dug.

The fact that she'd accomplished all this while Rei and the others were still struggling with the cyclops's skinning clearly showed the difference in their speed... and the meticulousness they applied to the task.

"This is incredible. Truly worthy of a Rank C party,"

Rei, a Rank B adventurer, murmured in admiration.

Deciding it could wait until after the other Cyclops were dealt with to incinerate what was in the pit, he touched the materials and stored them inside his Misty Ring.

Given the size of the cyclops, standing about four meters tall, there were particularly large quantities of parts like meat and bones.

Still, storing it in the Misty Ring only required a touch, so he finished stowing everything away in under a few minutes.

"Done. ...Whoa, that was fast."

Rei had only taken his eyes off the Cyclops for a few minutes, yet when he looked back, the skinning process that Rei and Vihera had been struggling with just moments ago had advanced significantly in that short time.

"Well, of course. The first Cyclops was our first time skinning one, so we fumbled a bit. But this is the second one in a row. Plus, Rei, Vihera, and Byune had already made some progress."

The former guerrilla fighter stated this while carefully extracting tendons from the leg with his knife.

Since they'd started skinning without bleeding it out first, blood stained his cheeks, making it a rather unsettling sight to anyone watching.

(Would it actually be faster if I left the skinning to Rei and the others and focused solely on bleeding them out?)

Several pieces of flesh lay scattered on the ground, and the intense, iron-rust stench of blood hung heavy in the air.

Rei had seen chickens, rabbits, and even deer and bears hunted by hunters butchered since he was little back in Japan, so he was naturally desensitized. Plus, Zepair had adjusted his senses to match Elgin standards, so he didn't think much of it.

But if any Japanese person unaware of such things were here, they would undoubtedly scream at the sight.

To an outsider, it was indeed a gruesome sight, but for adventurers, stripping monsters was an everyday occurrence.

Even the child, Byune, showed no sign of fear at this sight... no, rather, she was gleefully thrusting her knife into the Cyclops' body—though it might not be obvious to an outsider—filled with the expectation that the valuable materials would fetch a high price.

Vihera, apparently already deemed non-combatant, carried the meat and bones cut by Mireine and Johanna, gathering them in one place.

Some questioned whether it was right to make the former princess of the Bestir Empire do such work, but Vihera herself worked without seeming particularly bothered.

While it wasn't unusual in Exil for Byune to transport materials from dismantled monsters, the former guerrilla fighters couldn't possibly know that.

"I'll get the next cyclops out and bleed it. A few of you come help me over here."

"Ah, I'll help then."

Ekril, who had been removing the Cyclops's innards, said this, and several others headed toward Rei.

They judged that if they skinned it without bleeding it out first, the body would become even more soaked in blood than it already was.

Although this was primarily about harvesting materials, many still wore some form of armor since they were venturing outside of Gilm.

If blood got on that armor, it would take time to wash off.

Given that, it was only natural to want to avoid adding to that hassle if possible.

"Got it. So... um, yeah. Let's use that tree over there."

There are several methods for bleeding out, but the simplest is still turning the carcass upside down and letting the blood drain naturally.

However, the creature being skinned this time was a Cyclops nearly four meters tall. It wasn't as simple as slaughtering a bird and bleeding it out.

Since we'd be hanging it from a tree taller than the Cyclops itself, the thickness of the branches mattered.

(Ideally, slashing its carotid artery right after killing it would make bleeding out easiest. Or, since there's a river nearby, sinking it in the river and letting the current do it might work too. ...Well, a Cyclops isn't just an ordinary animal, it's a monster, so my amateur knowledge might not necessarily be useful.)

Thinking like this, Rei activated Sleipnir's Boots, kicked off the ground, and headed for the tree branch.

Those seeing Sleipnir's Boots for the first time gasped in surprise, but Rei paid them no mind. Reaching a branch about five meters high, he judged it could safely bear the Cyclops' weight and pulled a rope from the Misty Ring.

He hooked it onto the branch and descended back to the ground.

"Give me a hand."

He retrieved one Cyclops corpse from the Misty Ring and called out to Ekril and the others. They wrapped the rope multiple times around the Cyclops' legs and tied it securely so it wouldn't come loose.

The task took only a few minutes. They all pulled on the rope attached to the branch, suspending the Cyclops upside down.

Finally, he slit the Cyclops' carotid artery with his knife. In the next instant, blood spurted violently from the wound.

(Just as I thought. It's been dead for quite some time, its heart should have stopped, yet for blood to spurt out like this with such force is impossible for a wild animal. It's obvious now, but monsters seem to have remarkably unique bodies.)

While pondering the difference between monsters and wild animals, Rei scanned his surroundings, searching for a thick, tall branch to use for bleeding out the next Cyclops...

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set's voice suddenly echoed through the area.

Everyone present understood this wasn't his usual affectionate whine.

After all, Set wasn't playing right now; he was keeping watch.

"Watch the perimeter!"

Rei's voice rang out, and immediately everyone shifted into alert mode.

His experience as an adventurer clearly came into play here.

Rei reached for his usual Death Scythe from his Misty Ring, but upon seeing the situation around him... seeing faces like Mireine and Johanna present, he changed the weapon he drew to the Thorn Spear.

He would have used the Death Scythe if fighting alone or with Set, but there were many allies nearby, and this location was far from spacious.

Given that, the Death Scythe, primarily designed for sweeping attacks, was too inconvenient to use here.

Considering what weapon was most appropriate for the current situation, the spear immediately came to mind.

...Choosing a long-handled weapon over a longsword or dagger was likely related to Rei's preferences and what suited him best.

"Who's that, at a time like this? Surunin, guard the materials over here."

Mireine drew a longsword left nearby, which would get in the way of skinning, and gave instructions to Surnin.

Surnin picked up a staff that had been placed nearby, gave a small nod, and headed toward the pile where the Cyclops materials he had just stripped were gathered.

Being a mage who was fundamentally incapable of close combat, staying in one spot and casting spells from there suited Surnin perfectly.

Additionally, several members of Eclir and the former guerrilla squad, armed with bows, moved to Surnin's side.

Mireine and Johanna positioned themselves in front to protect these rear guards, and naturally, Rei and Vihera were among them.

After a few seconds, the sound of someone forcibly parting the thicket reached them. Everyone present immediately aimed their weapons in the direction of the noise...

"Haa, haa, haa. Dammit! What the hell is going on here?! This was just a kobold extermination request, right?!"

"Who cares?! Come on, run already! They're chasing us from behind!"

With those shouts, two men suddenly burst out from the bushes.

Their leather armor bore countless large and small scars, and while their limbs and faces showed no deep wounds, they had several small cuts here and there.

"H-help us! We're being attacked by monsters!"

Shouting as they burst from the bushes, the men hurriedly approached Rei and the others while screaming.

As if to prove their words, several kobolds leapt out from behind them... but Rei's gaze was drawn to the weapon held by one of them.

It was clearly different from the weapons the other kobolds carried—a longsword that looked brand new.

If that were all, it could have been dismissed as a weapon stolen from travelers or adventurers.

But... when Rei saw that longsword, the image that flashed through his mind was the magic item hammer wielded by the Cyclops.
