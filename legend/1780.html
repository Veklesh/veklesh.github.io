The return of Elena and the arrival of Rei, known by the epithet "Crimson."

Naturally, news of this quickly reached the nobles in Anesis.

In Anesis, the stronghold of Duke Kerebel, not only do the nobles aligned with the Noble Faction have mansions... but many nobles aligned with the Royal Faction or the Neutral Faction have also prepared mansions for themselves.

This is similar to the various noble residences in Gilm; they are there to gather even the slightest bit of information.

Although there is still some time before the new year, for various reasons—such as discussions with other nobles or to establish connections with nobles outside their existing circles—some nobles have already arrived in Anesis despite the time remaining until the new year.

Naturally, many of these nobles are gathering information about the Duke of Kerebel’s family, and there are certainly no shortage of those eyeing Elena as a potential bride for themselves or their sons.

...That said, there are many nobles in the Royal Faction who are also considering Elena as a marriage partner.

As a “Princess General,” Elena has become a symbolic figure even among the nobility.

She herself is both highly capable and beautiful.

If a noble from the Royal Faction were to marry such a figure, the influence of the Noble Faction would undoubtedly wane.

Of course, the Noble Faction did not reach its current size solely because of Elena, so it is highly unlikely that it would collapse simply because she is gone, but there is no doubt that its influence would diminish.

It should be noted that there are a fair number of members of the Neutral Faction in Anesis as well, but among them, there are none—at least not openly—who wish to take Elena as their wife.

That said, the reason they can get away with this is likely because the Neutral Faction is, after all, a much smaller faction compared to the Royal Faction or the Noble Faction.

It is precisely because they are a small faction that they are able to unite under the strong charisma of their leader, Daskar.

That said, although smaller than the Royal Faction or the Noble Faction, the Neutral Faction is still one of the so-called “three major factions.”

Its scale is overwhelmingly larger than that of the various miscellaneous groups of nobles.

That said, it goes without saying that there are certainly some who harbor grievances against Daskar.

In any case, since various nobles have gathered in Anesis, upon learning of Elena’s return and Rei’s arrival, they each react in their own way and begin to take action.

“Don’t be ridiculous! Even if he has a nickname, how dare a mere adventurer act as if he’s on the same level as Elena! Give me a break!”

The man shouted, hurling the wine glass he’d been holding with all his might at the man who had brought the report.

The glass cup shattered as it struck the man’s face, as one would expect, and wine—along with the man’s blood, drawn by the shards—spilled onto the floor.

Even so, the man didn’t flinch, showing no sign of complaint toward the master he served.

Perhaps intimidated by the man’s gaze, the nobleman who had thrown the wine glass sat back down in his chair, though his face still betrayed his displeasure.

“Ugh, Father told me to get closer to Elena, yet… Elena is just Elena. If she’s part of the Noble Faction, she’d surely prioritize associating with other nobles over getting involved with a stray dog like an adventurer.”

Although the man in front of him was bleeding from the face, the man voicing his complaints didn’t seem to care.

On the contrary, acting as if the man weren’t even there, he poured bright red wine into a new glass and raised it to his lips.

Perhaps the rich flavor of the wine spreading through his mouth helped ease some of the irritation he’d been feeling.

The man paused to consider what he should do next… and immediately broke into a smile.

However, it was a sadistic smile.

“Well then, no matter how much of a nickname like ‘Deep Crimson’ he has, in the end, he’s just a mere adventurer. So, it’d be best to make him regret trying to come between me and Elena. I hear he’s got a griffin as a familiar, after all. Or maybe I should just make him hand over his item box?”

Muttering to himself, the man imagines the moment he acquires something that even other nobles—no, even royalty—could never obtain.

But… while it might be fine for him to imagine it and revel in the pleasure, actually attempting it would lead to the worst possible outcome.

It was precisely because he understood this that the man, with drops of blood and wine dripping from his face, spoke up.

“Please stop, Lord Gaiska. I’ve heard that Crimson is not one to hesitate to use force, even against nobles. If you do anything here that tarnishes the name of the Marquis of Seisol…”

“Shut up.”

Perhaps annoyed that the man had interrupted his vision of the future—or, more accurately, his delusion—Gaiska glared at the man before him.

“What are you talking about? I’m a member of the Seisole Marquis family. Do you really think some lowly adventurer could do anything to me?”

There was no way a mere adventurer could lay a finger on him. No, it simply wouldn’t be allowed.

At Gaiska’s words, the man shook his head.

……As he did so, drops of blood and wine splattered around him, but the man had something more urgent to attend to than worrying about that.

The task of stopping Gaiska, who was trying to pick a fight with Rei.

Having lived his entire life being bowed to and revered by everyone, Gaiska likely never imagined that an adventurer—even one with a nickname—would dare to harm him.

From Gaiska’s perspective, that was undoubtedly true. Undoubtedly true… but that was strictly limited to the narrow world Gaiska inhabited.

The world is full of things Gaiska could never imagine.

And there was no doubt that Rei, with her nickname “Crimson,” was also a presence far beyond Gaiska’s imagination.

“Do you remember Lord Renosis, who was supposed to become the next head of the Imahen Marquis family?”

“The Marquess of Imahen’s…?”

Gaiska wondered why that name had suddenly come up.

The House of Imahen, like the House of Seisol, is a noble family belonging to the Noble Faction.

Since they were both marquis families, they harbored a strong rivalry toward one another.

It was such a fierce rivalry that it could easily turn into outright hostility if things went slightly wrong, which was precisely why Gaiska was familiar with the House of Imahen.

That said, compared to Renosis, the heir apparent, Gaiska was the fourth son, even though he was the son of a marquis.

Between the eldest son and the fourth son, even though they share the same title of marquis, their status differs greatly.

If he were the second son, he would be treated as a backup in case something happened to the eldest son and would be regarded as fairly important, but as the fourth son... he becomes nothing more than a backup’s backup’s backup to the eldest son.

Given that position, he had spoken with Renosis only a handful of times.

Recalling the face of the man who had struck him as so confident, Gaiska wondered, Why is that name coming up here?

And naturally, that question was directed at the man standing before him who had just mentioned Renosis’s name.

“Why is Renosis’s name coming up here?”

“Do you remember the war with the Bestir Empire that took place a few years ago?”

“Of course I do. That war made it clear that our country is superior to the Bestir Empire.”

For Gaiska, the fact that his country had defeated a nation it had fought countless times before was something to celebrate.

After all, it proved that those barbarians were indeed inferior to them.

That was the war that made that clear.

...And yet, the fact that he knows almost nothing about Rei—the very person who was the greatest factor in that victory—is likely because, deep down, Gaiska views adventurers as nothing more than lowly scum.

“The fact that Lord Renosis has been stripped of his position as the next head of the Marquess of Imahen’s family is due to the influence of Crimson Rei.”

“……What? Tell me more.”

“Yes. On the battlefield, Lord Renosis had a confrontation with Rei, and as a result, he sustained an injury in which his arm was severed by Rei’s hand.”

At those words, Gaiska was momentarily at a loss for what he had just heard.

By Gaiska’s standards, it was unthinkable for a noble—let alone the heir apparent to a marquisate—to be injured by a mere adventurer.

And not just a scratch, but a severe injury like having an arm severed.

“Is that true?”

“Yes. Because of that, Rei has come to be seen as someone who will show no hesitation or mercy, even against a noble.”

“...How unpleasant.”

For Gaiska, who took pride in his lineage as a descendant of the marquis family, Rei—who showed no respect for the nobility—was nothing but an unpleasant presence.

After all, even the heir apparent to the marquisate would go so far as to sever an arm without a second thought.

It was easy to imagine that he would raise his hand against Gaiska, the fourth son of the marquis’s family, without the slightest hesitation.

That, too, was infuriating, and Gaiska glared at the food on the table as if it were Rei’s face, his expression filled with loathing.

“Huh… So Lady Elena has a man in her life, does she? Is he handsome? He’s that adventurer with the nickname ‘Crimson,’ right?”

The residence of the Count of Myls, located a short distance from the Marquis of Seisol’s estate where Gaiska resided.

Teres Myls, the second daughter of the Earl of Myls and the one in charge of the estate, sipped her tea as she asked the person who had brought her the news.

“According to what Mary told me, he’s a small boy.”

“A boy? But since the one who came with Elena is an adventurer with a nickname, isn’t that description a bit off?”

“You’re right. Calling him a ‘boy’ might be a bit of an exaggeration… but I’m sure he’s small.”

Information travels fast through the maids’ network.

Even if it’s a maid serving in a different household, there are times when they get information before the head of the household does.

Teres had noticed this and instructed the maids working at the Anesis mansion to actively interact with maids from other households.

As a result, the ability to obtain information relatively quickly was likely thanks to Telles.

...That said, since the source of that information is word of mouth among maids, it’s bound to be mixed with rumors and wishful thinking.

“Well then, shall we go see Lady Elena tomorrow? I’d also like to see this ‘Crimson Rei’ person with my own eyes.”

“You’re quite the curious one, aren’t you, young lady? …No, I suppose there are plenty of people who would take an interest if they knew about this.”

For the aristocrats of the Noble Faction… no, for aristocrats outside the Noble Faction as well, Elena—who bears the nickname “Princess General”—is a figure of great significance.

Since such a figure has returned with a man in tow, there are few who wouldn’t be intrigued.

Whether that interest is favorable or malicious is another matter entirely.

The maid said this with a smile at Telles’ words, but then tilted her head in thought.

“But, young lady. Now that Lady Elena has returned, naturally the number of people wishing to see her will increase. In that case, I don’t think even you’ll be able to meet her so easily…”

“As for that… well, I think if I meet with Ara, we’ll figure something out. Besides, I never said I’d meet Lady Elena all by myself. I’ll invite a few friends along.”

With a look of pure, unadulterated joy—a joy that seemed to come from the very depths of her heart—Teres smiled.

“What? That Crimson Rei? …I see. Come to think of it, the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction have been getting closer lately.”

Upon hearing the news of Elena’s return and that Rei had accompanied her, Lanigus Musey—a noble of the Royal Faction and the second son of the Musey family—nodded in understanding.

Hearing Lanigus’s words, the knight who had brought the news tilted his head.

“Is that so? The last time I saw them, the nobles from the Noble Faction were picking on the nobles from the Neutral Faction. …Though, they did run off immediately when Margrave Larks showed up.”

“Well, the Noble Faction may not be as large as the Royal Faction, but it’s still significant. There are plenty of foolish nobles who possess nothing but a sense of entitlement. But the higher-ups… at the very least, Duke Kerebel must be seriously trying to form an alliance with the Neutral Faction. The proof is that he sent his beloved daughter to Gilm, which is currently undergoing expansion.”

Naturally, the Royal Faction has received intelligence that some nobles from the Noble Faction were displeased with the expansion of Gilm and were attempting to obstruct it.

As the largest faction in the Kingdom of Mireana, and with the King—the pinnacle of power—as their leader, the Royal Faction naturally possesses exceptional intelligence capabilities.

That is precisely why they had obtained the very information Lanigus had just mentioned.

“The higher-ups, you say? I hope this latest incident doesn’t turn into a real headache.”

“I wonder. The Royal Faction is undoubtedly the largest. Precisely because of that, there are many who act on their own accord, and there are certainly no shortage of those trying to curry favor with His Majesty.”

At Lanigus’s words, the knight’s face twisted into a look of distaste.

“Whenever Lord Ranigus says things like that, it usually ends up causing a commotion.”

“Don’t blame me for that. …Anyway, since Crimson Rei has come here now, there’s no doubt she’ll be attending the New Year’s party. If that happens, there’s a good chance… no, it’s certain that some kind of commotion will break out.”

“So please, don’t say things like that.”

The knight sighed, imagining the trouble that was about to unfold.
