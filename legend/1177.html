Rei and Vihera, along with Set and the beast, took flight from the forest and landed not far away.

Originally, this action was necessary to escape monsters and carnivores drawn to the blood spilled by werewolves and cyclopses. Once they gained sufficient distance from the woods, there was no need to move further away.

More importantly, absorbing magic stones and learning skills was now their top priority.

Landing on a grassy plain with a good view, Rei and the others confirmed there were no monsters nearby and immediately began extracting magic stones from the Misty Ring.

Two were from the werewolves, and two were from the werewolf leader. A total of four magic stones.

This was their first encounter with werewolves, so they hadn't absorbed either type of magic stone yet.

(Well, wolf faces with human bodies. Thinking of them that way, it's not impossible to consider them a higher species of kobold...)

Unlike goblin variants like Goblin Leader, Goblin Mage, Goblin General, or Goblin King, which are higher-tier forms of the same species...

Still, Rei understood from his life as an adventurer that judging everything solely by a monster's appearance was dangerous.

"Well then, let's get this magic stone absorption over with."

"Gurrrr!"

Set growled happily at Rei's words, and though she didn't say it, Vihera also turned her gaze toward Rei with keen interest.

The pinnacle of beast magic: acquiring skills by absorbing magic stones.

Though he couldn't hear the announcement message echoing in his mind, likely involving Takumu, the sight of acquiring a new skill still piqued Vihera's curiosity.

Under Vihera's gaze, Rei first extracted the werewolf's magic stone.

As if to say this was merely an appetizer compared to the main course—the magic stone of the Werewolf Leader.

...Actually, that was pretty much how Rei felt about it, so it wasn't wrong.

"Alright, first... let's see. How about starting with Set? Set!"

With those words, the werewolf magic stone is tossed into the air.

Set caught it perfectly with his beak and swallowed it whole.

[Set has acquired the skill 'Enhanced Sense of Smell Lv. 3']

An announcement message flashed through his mind.

"...Considering it's a werewolf, maybe it's not so strange?"

Having fully expected him to learn an offensive skill, Rei felt a slight letdown.

But then again, since werewolves have the heads of wolves, it made perfect sense that their sense of smell would be sharp.

"Rei, what skill did you learn?"

"Enhanced Sense of Smell. ...It's actually a pretty useful skill, despite everything. Just not the skill I was expecting."

Set's Enhanced Sense of Smell had saved Rei countless times before.

Thinking about it that way, it certainly wasn't a useless skill.

Still, it was disappointing for Rei that it wasn't the skill he'd expected.

"Grrr..."

Set too seemed disappointed that it wasn't the skill he'd expected, letting out a low growl.

Gently stroking him to cheer up the dejected Set, Rei spoke.

"Come on, don't be down. Luck is a major factor in skill acquisition to begin with."

To be precise, he wasn't entirely sure if luck truly played a role, but from Rei's perspective, having absorbed countless magic stones, it certainly seemed like luck was a major factor.

At Rei's words, Set gave a low growl, his attitude seeming to say, "Is that so?"

Nodding back at Set's reaction, Rei took the Death Scythe in hand, knowing it was his turn next, and tossed the magic stone into the air.

The massive blade of the Death Scythe flashed, and in the next instant, the magic stone was cleaved clean in two...

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Penetrate Lv.3']

The announcement flashed through his mind.

"...Penetrate?"

That skill involved wrapping wind around the thrust of the Death Scythe's pommel to increase its piercing power.

The skill itself wasn't problematic.

No, considering its practicality, it could be considered a satisfying skill.

But it's also true that the skills of a werewolf and Penetrate don't quite mesh.

As I explained that point along with Penetrate's effect, Vihera nodded as if she understood.

"The werewolf Rei fought might not have used it, but the one I fought? Some of them used claw thrusts, you know? Maybe that's where it comes from?"

"...Yeah, now that you mention it, I think I remember some like that."

Vihera's words brought back memories of the fight. Come to think of it, that did happen...

When he was attacked with a thrust, he'd landed a strike before it connected, countering and finishing the werewolf.

Thinking about it that way, it wasn't surprising that it hadn't left much of an impression.

"Let's try it out. ...Stay back a bit, just in case."

After giving that warning and confirming Set and Vihera had moved away, he readied his Death Scythe and activated his skill.

"Penetrate!"

The thrust from the Death Scythe's pommel, released with the skill activation, was wrapped in wind as it flew.

Just as Rei had predicted, the wind enveloping the Death Scythe was noticeably stronger than before it absorbed the magic stone.

That must be the effect of leveling up.

"Huh. Seeing it like this, you can actually see the effect with your own eyes."

Vihera's voice betrayed her admiration as she observed the Death Scythe's form.

Alongside her admiration, there was also a hint of envy.

The ability to easily enhance skills by absorbing magic stones was something Vihera herself envied.

There was a restriction: she couldn't absorb magic stones given to her by others, and she had to fight monsters possessing magic stones, albeit to a limited extent. However, as demonstrated with the Werewolf Leader, landing the final blow alone fulfilled the condition.

Thinking about it that way, it wasn't such a harsh restriction after all.

(Beast Magic... huh. Only once per lifetime. If anything, that might be the strictest restriction of all.)

Someday, I want to try it myself. With that thought in Vihera's mind, Rei began preparing for the next step.

Yes, the main course of this operation: absorbing the magic stone from the Werewolf Leader.

Holding the magic stone, clearly larger than the one from the werewolf, Rei called out to Set as usual.

「Set！」

The thrown magic stone of the Werewolf Leader easily entered Set's mouth, just as before.

[Set has acquired the skill 'Poison Claws Lv.5']

An announcement message flashed through his mind.

What surprised Rei most upon hearing this was undoubtedly that Poison Claw had reached Level 5.

"Alright!"

Knowing from experience that skills undergo a dramatic change in performance at Level 5, Rei couldn't help but exclaim.

He did wonder about Poison Claw, but then again, it made sense for a Werewolf Leader to use a poison-based attack.

"Looks like you've acquired a pretty powerful skill, huh?"

"Yeah. Poison Claws. Well, the skill itself wasn't that powerful, but it's Set's first skill to break Level 5. And once it hits Level 5..."

Its power increases dramatically.

Having been briefed on Flying Slash, Vihera understood why Rei was so pleased.

But at the same time, she wondered: Does the Poison Claw skill even suit Set?

Rei, observing Vihera's expression, recalled that Seto had acquired several skills that seemed mismatched before.

Skills like Optical Camouflage or King's Intimidation probably didn't suit Set's personality either.

(If you think of it as being a Griffin rather than Set's personality, it's not that strange, though.)

"Grrr?"

Perhaps sensing unease in Rei and Vihera's exchange, Set growled softly.

His growl, which seemed to ask, "What's wrong?", certainly didn't look like he was about to use a skill like Royal Intimidation.

"Nothing. Never mind. More importantly, could you use your poison claws?"

"Grrr..."

At Rei's words, Set moved slightly away.

That he looked somewhat lonely wasn't just in Rei and Vihera's heads.

(Once this matter is settled, I'll need to properly show Set some affection.)

Watching Set, Rei thought this to himself. Once he moved to a spot where Rei and Vihera wouldn't be harmed, he let out a high-pitched cry, as if switching his mood.

"Gurrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

With that sound, his Poison Claws skill activated. The claws protruding from Set's front paws turned a poisonous purple.

The fact they didn't liquefy and drip onto the ground was perhaps the only saving grace.

"...Grr?"

After activating the poison claws, he must have realized there was no target to attack. Set looked around in confusion... then eventually turned his round eyes toward Rei.

What should I do? With that question in his gaze, Set also wore an expression that said, "Oh no."

It was fine that he was happy about his Poison Claw reaching Level 5, but it was also undeniably true that he'd gotten a little carried away because of it.

"Ah—right... See that rock over there? Could you try attacking that rock for a moment?"

Rei pointed to a rock about two meters tall, located just a minute's run away if Set sprinted.

Set looked slightly hesitant, but he must have decided he needed to see the Poison Claw's effect for himself. He ran across the ground... then, using that momentum, leaped up and swung his purple-tinged claw down at the rock in one swift motion.

Whether due to Set's sheer strength or not, the rock shattered instantly.

"Hey, Rei... I was just wondering, since it's poison, doesn't that mean it only works on living things?"

"...Yeah, you're right."

Rei had told Set to attack the rock, but upon reflection, it was only natural that he couldn't clearly understand whether attacking an inanimate object with poison would have any effect or not.

If this were some kind of monster, he could confirm the poison's effect by observing its suffering.

"Grrr..."

Rei gently stroked Set's head as he returned with a disappointed look, partly as an apology.

Simply that gesture was enough to instantly lift Set's spirits. Purring contentedly, he rubbed his head against Rei, demanding more and more.

"Hey, don't get too frisky. Besides, the last one was mine."

Realizing Rei had turned serious, Set gently moved away from him.

Vihera, too, set aside the matter of the poison claws for now, her eyes filled with expectation as she watched Rei... or more accurately, Death Scythe, to see what kind of skill it could acquire.

Under the gaze of one person and one beast, Rei flung the Werewolf Leader's magic stone into the air... and in the next instant, swung Death Scythe.

The magic stone was sliced clean in one flash...

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Multi-Strike Lv.1']

The announcement message played.

"Multi-Slash? ...It's a new skill, and judging by the name, it's probably a combo-type skill?"

"What? That Multi-Slash is a new skill?"

Hearing Vihera's sharp response to Rei's mutter, Rei nodded back.

"Yeah. I finally learned a new skill after so long. ...I can guess the basic effect from the name, but let's try it out anyway. Step back a bit."

At Rei's words, Vihera and Set each moved back.

Since it was a completely new skill, they had no idea what kind of effect it would have.

Given that, it wasn't surprising that Rei was being somewhat cautious.

Once he confirmed the surroundings were safe, Rei swung his Death Scythe.

"Multi-Slash!"

The strike delivered with those words... to an outside observer, it didn't seem like anything particularly special had happened.

"...Huh? What's going on?"

Even when he had learned skills before, it had never happened that a skill failed to take effect at all.

Even if it did, it was because some condition hadn't been met—it never meant the learned skill was meaningless.

If that's the case... Rei looked around. There were no monsters like goblins that could be targets, nor any monsters like the werewolf they'd been fighting a moment ago. The rocks had all been shattered by Set's strike.

He briefly considered returning to the forest to attack a tree, but then glanced at the ground and had an idea.

If it was just about using the skill, couldn't he try it on the ground?

"...Let's try it. Multi-Slash!"

The Death Scythe's blade swung down toward the ground.

The blade of the Death Scythe sliced through the ground for about a meter... and right next to the blade of the Death Scythe, an identical scar remained.
