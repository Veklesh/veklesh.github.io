That night, Daskar listened to the report while dining with Walker at the lord's manor.

"Hmm, a corrupting forest... and Trent is likely involved, you say?"

"Yes. I had Rei conduct a preliminary investigation, and it appears to cover a considerable area. Furthermore, whether it's spreading, proliferating, or moving, it seems to be heading this way toward Gilm."

Walker, who had been enjoying sautéed spring vegetables, swallowed what was in his mouth and replied to Daskar.

While it was true the forest was approaching the Gilm area, the lack of urgency suggested the distance wasn't actually that close.

Even within the forest, if it was only approaching Gilm by a few minutes' walk, it wasn't an emergency situation.

Of course, that didn't mean it could be ignored.

But Walker, even considering that, wasn't overly concerned.

Because Gilm had an adventurer named Rei, skilled in fire magic.

If push came to shove, they could simply burn the entire forest.

That thought gave Walker a sense of ease.

And even Daskar, who knew Rei better than Walker, while somewhat concerned about the suspicious forest, wasn't overly worried thanks to Rei's presence.

...That is, until now.

"Daskar-sama, may I have a moment?"

The butler who appeared in the room announced this.

The fact that he came directly to interrupt Walker's meal meant something was definitely wrong.

If it were merely a matter of signing some papers, Daskar wouldn't dismiss it with a laugh.

"What is it?"

"Yes, Lord Zumous has an urgent report."

"From Zumos? ...Send him in."

"Yes."

The butler nodded at Daskar's words and departed.

Zumos is one of the alchemists belonging to Daskar's... more precisely, to the knightly order under Daskar's command.

Walker had received a wooden branch from Rei... a branch he'd picked up in the forest he'd gone to investigate. Daskar wondered if it might be useful for something.

On a whim, Daskar had Zumos examine it.

Trent wood was inherently a material well-suited for crafting wands.

And if the entire forest were made of Trent, it would undoubtedly be a massive source of income.

Indeed, by not destroying the entire forest at once, but instead cutting down a certain amount and then allowing it to regrow... that would undoubtedly become an infinite source of income.

If they had known beforehand that the forest was composed of trents, the danger could have been fully controlled.

As a human, Daskar certainly had his thoughts about the adventurer party devoured by that forest.

But Daskar was the lord of Gilm.

As such, he had to do whatever it took to develop Gilm.

"Did something happen?"

"I had them examine the branch Walker brought back, so they must have discovered something. Otherwise, they wouldn't have rushed to report it to me like this."

"I suppose so. But when we examined it here, all we could tell was that it contained a bit more magic than usual... Was there something else?"

"Well, I wonder. He's about to tell us now, isn't he? ...Ah, here he comes."

With Daskar's words, the butler who had left earlier entered the room accompanied by a man.

"Daskar-sama, where did you obtain this branch!?"

The man who asked this the moment he entered was in his thirties.

His build was neither fat nor thin, just average. Yet, for some reason, his aura made him seem oddly frail.

That said, seeing only his eyes gleaming brightly in that frail-looking state would make most people think twice about getting involved carelessly.

But among the alchemists employed by the Knights, he was exceptionally skilled.

Precisely because of that, in a situation like this, one couldn't help but hope he'd found something.

"It's a forest tree that appeared a short distance from Gilm. However, it's a place where Trents... or monsters similar to them... breed in large numbers."

"So you mean the Forest of Trolls?"

"I don't know. According to survivors of the first attack on that forest, they were assaulted by trents. But Rei, who went to investigate, said the trees in the forest weren't actually trents."

Zumos frowned at the mention of Rei from Daskar's mouth.

Living in Gilm... no, working as Daskar's subordinate, Zumos naturally knew Rei.

Of course, he only knew of Set and had never actually spoken to him... but since he had obtained materials like Set's feathers and fur for alchemy, he could even say he was grateful to Rei.

Moreover, judging by his track record, there was no doubt about his abilities.

Perhaps that was precisely why.

He couldn't imagine Rei and Set would overlook the existence of Trent.

"So what exactly does that mean?"

"I don't know. But... anyway. You came rushing here like this for a reason, right? Tell me what it is."

"Ah, yes. ...This tree branch, at first glance, seems to have only slightly more magic power than usual."

Walker nodded at Zumous' words.

The Dark Elves possessed advanced knowledge of plants and magic... and Marina, who stood out even among them, had stated it definitively. There was no room for doubt.

Daskar, having known Marina for a long time, also had no objections.

Facing the two of them, Zumous continued his explanation.

"However, upon examining this branch, we discovered that when coated with a specific liquid and infused with a certain amount of magic... it gains considerable density and magical defensive properties."

"...What?"

Daskar, who had been about to take a bite of bread, froze at Zmos's explanation.

That's how shocking the explanation coming from Zumos's mouth was.

Walker, too, stared intently, as if trying to verify the truth of Zumos' words.

The lord of Gilm and the guildmaster.

For those two, if this explanation was true, it meant they might be able to obtain infinite armor.

Being made of wood, it would be difficult to use as a weapon.

But what about as shields or armor?

With that hope in mind, Daskar spoke up.

"So, that forest... It's inconvenient without a name. For now, let's call it Trent Forest. Is it possible to make armor from the trees growing in that Trent Forest?"

Walker felt that based on Rei's description, it hardly deserved to be called a forest, but given the fact that they needed to name it for now, he didn't intend to make unnecessary comments.

Zmous, meeting the hopeful gazes of Walker and Daskar, shook his head apologetically.

"Unfortunately, they don't possess the strength required for armor."

"...I see."

Daskar let out an audible sigh, but Walker, who had been listening along, suddenly realized something.

"Wait. It doesn't have enough strength to be used as armor. So... is there some other use for it?"

At those words, Daskar looked at him with hopeful eyes, and Zmos nodded in agreement, as if his thoughts had been read.

"Yes. Well... what comes to mind for me is building material."

"Building material?"

Daskar looked puzzled for a moment, clearly not expecting that suggestion here. But the next instant, he drew in a sharp breath.

As a lord who understood Gilm's current situation, he grasped the full meaning of those words.

While Gilm still had some breathing room, if the population continued growing at this rate, it would undoubtedly reach saturation in the near future.

Once that happened, various problems would inevitably arise.

That was precisely why Daskar had been considering expanding Gilm if possible. But doing so required vast quantities of materials, manpower, funds... and numerous other resources.

But... no matter how many trees were cut down in Trent Forest, it would naturally spread further.

And while they might not be strong enough for armor, if they possess sufficient strength for building materials...

"Hmm..."

Daskar grunted.

...While he was seriously agonizing over what to do next, to an outsider, it just looked like he was glaring at Zmos, who had brought the report about the trees.

Still, Zumos showed no sign of fear, likely because he'd worked under Daskar long enough to be somewhat accustomed to this sort of thing.

If anything, Walker seemed more startled by Daskar's current demeanor than Zumous did.

Then again, Walker and Daskar hadn't known each other very long.

Considering that, Walker's surprise was only natural.

Daskar remained silent for several minutes, deep in thought, before finally turning his gaze toward Walker.

"Walker, what does the guild intend to do about Trent Forest?"

"...It's a difficult question. Since adventurers were attacked there, I thought burning it down would be best... but if it can be used for the staff, then it could be a resource."

"True. Especially after hearing what you just said, we can't act recklessly. If it can genuinely be used for Gilm's expansion project, then at the very least, burning it all down with Rei's magic is out of the question."

"I suppose so."

Walker offered no objection to Daskar's words.

The trees he'd considered as potential staff materials—if burned, they'd obviously be useless for that purpose.

"Daskar-sama, I have a request."

Zmous, who had been listening to their conversation, interjected.

"What is it?"

"Yes. That forest... Trent Forest, was it? If possible, I'd like to bring back not a branch, but a whole tree from there. What I mentioned earlier about it being usable as building material was based solely on what we found out about this branch."

"So you mean, if we examine the tree itself instead of the branches, we might learn something more?"

Zumos answered the question.

"We won't know until we investigate it thoroughly. It might have different properties than the branch. But if you're using it as building material, it's best to thoroughly examine the real thing... though 'real' might not be quite the right word."

Hearing Zumos say that, both Walker and Daskar had no choice but to agree.

They hadn't yet decided to use the trees from Trent Forest as building materials, but Daskar was actively considering it.

Given that, it was definitely wise to thoroughly investigate the properties of the trees in Trent Forest.

"Besides, since we only had branches this time, we could only examine a small amount."

For Zumous, that was likely the most significant reason, even more so than the building materials themselves.

In a way, it was quite alchemist-like, you might say.

But Zumos's opinion wasn't wrong at all.

It's not uncommon for monster materials to have different properties depending on the body part.

...Though, to be clear, what Rei handed over this time wasn't a Trent branch, but just an ordinary tree branch.

"Walker"

"Yes, understood. Since Rei is supposed to check on the forest daily, I'll ask him to bring back a tree branch when he goes to look tomorrow."

This is something Walker couldn't easily request of an ordinary adventurer.

Even though it's just wood, what Daskar wants brought is a tree of a certain thickness... specifically, wood suitable for construction materials.

A tree of that size would be quite a hassle to cut down, and transporting it all the way to Gilm would be difficult considering its weight.

You'd have to either build something like a cart with several wagons connected or cut the tree down to a manageable size on the spot.

But since Rei carries an item box, he doesn't have to worry about bringing back the wood or anything else.

In every sense, cutting down a single tree was a job perfectly suited for Rei.

(Well, though that probably means the reward will be even higher.)

Even so, since this request came from Daskar, there was no need to worry about Walker's pocketbook suffering over the reward.

"Daskar-sama, cutting down a tree is fine, but in that case, it would be an additional request on top of just checking the situation. Naturally, that would incur a fee..."

"Ah, I'll cover that. How much exactly?"

"Hmm... Well, for a Rank B adventurer with a nickname, it would be a few gold coins, I suppose."

Daskar was surprised it was cheaper than he'd expected, but the proximity of Trent Forest was a major factor.

If it was cheap, he had no complaints... Daskar nodded at Walker's words.
