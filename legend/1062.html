"Ugh... Ah...?"

Laying on the bed, Rei sat up, uttering sounds that weren't quite words.

He looked around, then froze.

Several minutes passed like this. As Rei stared blankly into the distance, a glimmer of awareness gradually returned to his eyes.

"This place... Ah, right..."

Looking around again, Rei remembered he was in the house he'd borrowed in the Dark Elf settlement. Finally, last night's events came back to him.

He remembered heading into the forest to rescue the four children who had gone in there.

That they had rescued the four and returned to the settlement.

After that, when they met up with Elena and the others, who had gone in a different direction from Rei, it was already close to midnight.

Ultimately, overcome by sleepiness, they had returned to this house and fallen asleep immediately.

"You slept well. ...Or maybe too well?"

The sun visible through the window was already directly overhead.

Was it nearly noon already? Thinking that, Misty Ring pulled out her watch. Rei's expression twisted in surprise.

Not only was it nearly noon, but it was already past noon.

"I slept in again, didn't I? ...It's surprisingly quiet though."

He listened carefully, but the voices of the dark elves making noise were barely audible.

Rei could easily imagine they were still asleep, likely from yesterday's events.

"It was late yesterday, so it's only natural."

Muttering to himself, Rei finally felt ready to get up. He finished getting dressed and left the room.

Stepping out of the room, he found himself in the living room.

And just as expected, Earth was asleep on the living room sofa.

Near Earth, just like yesterday, Polo was sleeping with his own nine tails as a pillow.

(Same as yesterday. ...No way.)

He remembered Ragdo running over yesterday from a similar situation to this one.

I hope nothing strange happens two days in a row... I was thinking that when my stomach started demanding something to eat.

"I'll think about today's events after breakfast... No, lunch."

As Rei muttered, remembering it was already past noon, Polo, who had been curled up asleep, lifted his head as if reacting to those words.

"Poro-ro-ro-ro? Poru?"

Rice? Rice? Rei, met with that hopeful gaze, smiled and gently stroked Polo.

The soft, silky fur of the nine-tailed fox made him want to keep stroking it forever... but that wasn't possible. Pulling his hand away from Polo, Rei's hand moved towards Earth next.

"Hey, wake up, Earth. It's almost time to eat."

"Mmm... uu... just a little longer..."

"I'd love to let you sleep, but it's already past noon. If you keep this up, you'll sleep all day. Besides, you're supposed to solve the forest's problem, right?"

"Aah..."

For several minutes after that, Rei tried to wake Earth, but Earth just whined and showed no sign of getting up.

He'd spent the day moving around the forest, exerting himself physically, and then had to go back into the forest that night and fight a bunch of monsters. Rei understood he was exhausted and wanted to let him sleep.

Still, leaving Earth behind for today's likely forest exploration felt wrong for an adventurer. He tried waking him again several times, but...

"Enough. I've tried waking you plenty of times. Don't regret this later."

In the end, he couldn't bring himself to fully wake Rei. When Rei's patience finally snapped, he left Earth behind and headed out of the house.

...Meanwhile, Polo hesitated over what to do. Perhaps feeling reluctant to leave his partner behind, he decided to stay put.

And the place Rei headed straight for after leaving home wasn't the house Elena and the others were renting... but the stable of the house Rei himself was renting.

"Grrrrr."

Sensing Rei's arrival, Set rose with a pleased purr.

Despite having been lying down, he sprang up instantly—typical of Set, who adored Rei.

"Looks like you were awake. I'm hungry too. How about some food?"

"Gurrr!"

At Rei's words, Set purred happily.

While hungry was certainly true, it seemed he was even happier that Rei had come.

Laying him out of the stable, Rei was about to head straight to the house Elena was renting with Set when he suddenly stopped.

"Grrr?"

Set tilted his head, asking "What's wrong?" Rei said nothing, instead pulling something out from his Misty Ring.

Seeing what Rei had taken out, Set tilted his head again.

He couldn't understand why she would take it out here at all.

What was now in Rei's hand was the corpse of a monster called a Del Torro, shaped like an owl.

Rei opened his mouth to speak to Set.

"We'll probably head into the forest again today. But considering last night's incident, it's unlikely things will go smoothly... In fact, since we're moving to resolve the forest's disturbance, it's certain something needs to change. So..."

He paused there, drew a dissection knife, and thrust it into DelTroy's torso.

What he extracted was the magic stone, the most crucial material for both Rei and Set.

It was only one magic stone, but precisely because of that, it was undoubtedly a precious thing to Rei and Set.

"Grr?"

What are you going to do? Set asked, growling. After washing his hands, the dagger, and the magic stone with the flowing water dagger, Rei thought.

Meanwhile, the corpse of Del Troy, now stripped of its magic stone, had been returned to the Misty Ring for proper material extraction later.

"This magic stone... Set should absorb it."

"GRRRRRR!"

Ignoring Set, who purred happily at Rei's words, Rei closed his eyes to sense the surrounding atmosphere.

(No presence of anyone monitoring us nearby. No trace of the kind of gaze I felt before, like at the World Tree or inside the house... Well, considering yesterday's incident, it makes sense that few are still awake and alert.)

Still, it was impossible for the entire settlement to be asleep. Perhaps there was a Dark Elf watching over them.

That thought crossed his mind, but it seemed to be an unfounded worry.

He asked Set to scan the area for any presence as well. Even Set, who possessed greater perception than Rei, confirmed there was no one watching them. Only then did Rei turn his gaze back to the magic stone he held.

(Considering Del Troy's stealth abilities, it was probably...)

Having unexpectedly struggled against Del Troy in battle, Rei could somehow predict the skills he could learn from that magic stone.

Of course, this wasn't based on any concrete evidence.

It was a prediction mixed with a hunch that it would probably be so and a hopeful wish that it would be.

Still, he judged it would likely be fine. Holding the magic stone, Rei turned his gaze to Set and spoke.

"Set, let's go."

The magic stone he tossed was caught as usual by Set's beak and swallowed.

[Set has acquired the skill 'Optical Camouflage Lv. 3']

The announcement echoed in his mind simultaneously.

Hearing the voice, Rei showed no particular surprise... rather, he exhaled in relief.

"So the optical camouflage really did get enhanced."

Considering Del Troy's ability to blend into darkness, it made sense that Optical Camouflage would be enhanced. Rei's prediction had proven correct.

Yet the smile playing on Rei's lips was likely because, among Set's skills, Optical Camouflage was one of the most powerful.

It was a skill that simply made him invisible to others... but when that invisible figure was Set, it was a different story entirely.

Set, capable of delivering devastating blows through sheer physical prowess alone, becomes invisible.

This meant enemies would be caught completely unaware as the hostile Set closed in at close range, forced to endure the griffin's uniquely devastating strike without any chance to prepare defenses.

For anyone familiar with Set's combat prowess, this skill is nothing short of a nightmare.

Set possessed skills like Fire Breath and King's Intimidation that could attack a wide area, though not quite as extensively as Rei's. If he used optical camouflage skillfully, he could potentially stall... or even trample an entire first-rate unit, if he chose to.

"Getting optical camouflage was huge. ...At Level 2, I could stay invisible for about twenty seconds. How long is it now?"

"Gururu!"

With that growl, the optical camouflage activated, and Set's form vanished.

He reappeared only after about forty seconds had passed.

"Forty seconds at Level Three. Since Level Two was twenty seconds, does it double with each level? Eighty seconds at Level Four, one hundred sixty seconds at Level Five... and so on?"

"Grr?"

Is that so? Set tilted his head at Rei's words.

It was his own skill, but it was still the optical camouflage he had only just mastered. Above all, he couldn't possibly nod in agreement when told about things he hadn't even learned yet.

Realizing he was getting too carried away, Rei gave a wry smile, reached out to Set, and gently stroked his head.

"Yeah, you're right. There's no way you'd know the details of a skill you haven't even mastered yet. ...We're done absorbing the magic stones, so maybe we should head out for a meal soon."

"Growl!"

Rei's words must have reminded him of his hunger. Set growled, urging them to hurry, to go now!

Dragged along by Set, Rei started to head out from the stable area...

"There you are! Rei, were you planning to leave me behind!? That's a bit harsh, don't you think!"

They encountered Earth, who had rushed out of the house looking flustered.

"...Ah, ah."

Rei gasped for a moment at Earth's appearance.

The timing was so perfect, he wondered if Earth had seen Set using optical camouflage.

No, if he'd just been seen using optical camouflage, it wouldn't be a big deal.

After all, anyone even slightly familiar with Rei and Set knew that Set was a rare breed of Griffin capable of wielding various skills.

As for the optical camouflage, he'd used it during last year's civil war in the Bestir Empire. And he'd done so in front of a large number of soldiers.

Given that, it was only a matter of time before information about it spread, and indeed, it has spread.

(Though, the fact that Set can become invisible probably makes it even more terrifying. But before that, considering Earth's personality, he'd probably say something if he saw it. The fact he hasn't said anything probably means he didn't see it.)

Judging by Earth's attitude, the two humans and two animals headed straight for the house Elena and the others were renting.

As they drew nearer to the house, the pleasant aroma that drifted toward them made not only Rei's stomach rumble, but the others' too.

"Poro-ro-ro-ro!"

"Gururururu!"

The two animals whined urgently, as if driven by instinct, urging them to hurry, hurry.

...Though, Polo was perched on Set's head.

Polo usually sat on his back, but now he wanted to see as far as possible... Elena's house, no doubt.

Rei and Earth each smiled slightly at the sight of their partners, exchanged glances, and then quickened their pace.

When they arrived at the house Elena and the others were renting, Marina was there to greet them.

"Oh, good morning. Arriving just in time for a meal—you must have been quite hungry."

"Marina... Did you stay here last night too?"

"No. I came over after I woke up. ...Come on in. Let's have breakfast."

Marina led them, naturally, to the courtyard.

She had anticipated Rei and the others coming, and since Rei's arrival meant Set would undoubtedly come too, breakfast in the garden was planned accordingly.

"Good morning, Rei. Did Earth sleep well last night?"

The first to greet them was Elena, who had just placed a basket of freshly baked bread on the table.

Some might be surprised to learn that the Duchess's daughter was preparing the meal.

Moreover, Elena wasn't just any duchess's daughter. She was also known by the nickname Princess General.

Even if someone witnessed this scene and told others, it would be utterly impossible for anyone unfamiliar with Elena's true nature to believe it.

Next, Ara , Vihera , and Byune brought in dishes... and soon breakfast began for everyone.

"This bread is delicious. Freshly baked and soft."

As Rei murmured this while taking a bite, Marina smiled and nodded.

"Isn't it? It was made by the artisan baker known as the master of bread in this settlement."

"...It's impressive they managed to bake fresh bread so soon after yesterday."

Baking bread requires starting from kneading the dough, inevitably taking considerable time.

Considering most of the dark elves in this settlement had stayed up until the middle of the night last night, the artisan who baked the bread probably had almost no sleep.

Marina nodded slightly, looking a bit troubled, at Vihera's words.

"That's right. But he said he sleeps soundly after baking bread, so he's probably in dreamland by now."

At those words, everyone nodded in understanding... and right at that moment, the pulsing sensation they'd felt before struck the entire dark elf settlement with a thud.
