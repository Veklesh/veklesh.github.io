While Gaiska was wallowing in despair... naturally, the battle between Rei and the Black Wolf was still raging.

As time passed, the battle grew fiercer—it was a contest of such high caliber that anyone who witnessed it would have been willing to pay to see it.

It was a battle that was incomparable to the mock battle Rei had engaged in just a short while ago.

However, the only person watching the battle was Elena.

Watching her beloved man fight intently, Elena had already completely sobered up.

(That ability—what appears to be teleportation. That’s going to be a real pain.)

The strikes from Rei’s Death Scythe and Twilight Spear possessed enough power to take the Black Wolf’s life in a single blow if they connected.

Even if they didn’t go that far, they were certain to inflict wounds severe enough to hinder the Black Wolf’s combat effectiveness.

Despite this, the Black Wolf would instantly teleport to evade Rei’s strikes the moment they were launched.

……That was precisely because the Black Wolf had judged that there was absolutely no way he could withstand the blows Rei unleashed—and that very judgment proved to be exceptionally accurate.

Normally, it would be no easy feat to calmly and continuously dodge a series of strikes that could easily take one’s life.

But for Black Wolf, a master assassin, continuing to dodge those attacks was no difficult feat.

“Ugh, you’re getting a bit too persistent!”

Rei showed his frustration toward Black Wolf, who had dodged Death Scythe’s strike and leaped backward to put distance between himself and Rei.

He had used several skills, but Black Wolf had dodged them all.

He shouldn’t know the specific effects of Rei’s skills… yet he managed to dodge every single one.

While the attacks themselves weren’t particularly powerful, in terms of evasion ability alone, the Black Wolf undoubtedly ranked among the top opponents Rei had ever faced.

That said, even if they aren’t that powerful, the Black Wolf’s claws possess a sharpness akin to daggers.

If the Black Wolf were merely carrying out its job—assassination—that level of attack power would be perfectly sufficient.

But the Black Wolf right now is practically like having daggers for fingers on both hands, and sharp fangs protrude from his mouth... fangs truly worthy of the name "Black Wolf."

“……”

Perhaps irritated by Rei’s words, the Black Wolf took a step back to create some distance… and in the next instant, vanished.

Just as Rei thought his figure had vanished without a trace, the Black Wolf was already behind him, bringing his claws down.

But Rei had been attacked like this countless times before, with the wolf vanishing right before his eyes.

No matter how difficult such an attack would normally be to dodge, having experienced it this many times, it was only natural that he had grown accustomed to it.

...Under normal circumstances, it would have been a blow that could easily have been fatal, but Rei dodged it while spinning his body to swing the Death Scythe.

That strike possessed enough power and sharpness to easily take a human life.

Naturally, since such a blow would be fatal even to the Black Wolf, he quickly leaped backward to put distance between himself and Rei.

It was a sequence of actions he had performed countless times before, but precisely because of that, Rei found himself wondering.

(Why does he always appear right behind me?)

That was the question.

Of course, it’s true that teleporting behind an opponent is effective for catching them off guard.

But that’s only effective for the very first time, or perhaps a few times after that.

Despite that, the Black Wolf invariably appeared behind Rei.

Rei couldn’t help but feel that the Black Wolf, who possessed enough skill to go toe-to-toe with him like this, surely must understand that. In other words…

(Is there a restriction that he can only teleport behind his opponent? No, but I’ve seen him vanish right in front of me several times before, and it didn’t seem like there was any such restriction.)

Although it wasn’t during combat, whenever Rei encountered the Black Wolf, it would invariably vanish before he knew it.

Since he hadn’t vanished in a way that specifically involved reappearing behind Rei back then, the Black Wolf’s current behavior was nothing but a mystery to him.

(To begin with, if it teleported above my head instead of behind me, that would throw me off too. The fact that it doesn’t do that… no, the fact that it can’t do that—does that mean there’s some significance to it after all?)

He gripped the Death Scythe tightly and, instead of waiting for the Black Wolf to make a move, took the initiative himself.

“Magic Shield!”

A shield of light materialized at his command.

A skill that creates a shield of light—one that, though usable only once, would reliably block any attack.

To the Black Wolf, that shield of light—which Rei had conjured out of nowhere—was a highly suspicious presence that demanded caution.

His gaze toward Rei grew even more intense, and the dagger-like claws extending from the Black Wolf’s fingers clacked together.

Though the sound was faint, strangely enough, it reached Rei’s ears clearly.

However, completely unfazed by the Black Wolf’s wariness—which was only natural, given that they were fighting—Rei approached the Black Wolf while keeping his Magic Shield active.

(Judging by that reaction, the Black Wolf is definitely wary of the Magic Shield. If so, the Black Wolf’s next move will likely be…)

While thinking about his next move, Rei approached the black wolf and swung his Death Scythe.

Whatever the Black Wolf was thinking, it showed no sign of reacting even as Rei charged toward it.

And so, the Death Scythe is swung… and in the next instant, as expected, the Black Wolf vanishes.

The moment he saw that, Rei tried to swing the Death Scythe behind him...

(No, wait!?)

Rei swung the Death Scythe expecting the Black Wolf to teleport behind him as it had before, but the Black Wolf’s presence emanated from Rei’s right side.

However, since Rei had been about to swing the Death Scythe counterclockwise—the opposite direction of a clock’s second hand—once he assumed that stance, the Black Wolf was now directly behind him.

For a split second, Rei hesitated, unsure of what to do, but that hesitation lasted only an instant… no, not even an instant.

Rei swung the Death Scythe as is.

Since the Black Wolf wasn’t where he expected it to be, the blow sliced through the air as if cutting through nothing… and at that exact moment, the Magic Shield Rei had been using took the Black Wolf’s attack and scattered into particles of light.

But… naturally, Rei’s actions didn’t end there.

Using the momentum from attacking where he had expected the Black Wolf to be, he twisted his body around his feet and swung the Death Scythe toward the wolf’s current position.

This was a move made possible by the Magic Shield’s ability to completely block an enemy’s attack—albeit just once.

A faint sensation of something being sliced through, and the sensation of something heavy colliding and being blown away, transmitted through his hand.

As he swung the Death Scythe, he glanced at the spot where the Black Wolf had been, and there, he saw the Black Wolf flying straight through the air.

Seeing the Black Wolf like that, Rei wondered, “Did I deal a fatal blow?” but immediately turned his gaze back to the Black Wolf flying away with a grim expression.

If the blade of the Death Scythe had struck it squarely, it wouldn’t have simply been sent flying like this; its torso would have been split clean in two.

The fact that it was still intact, despite the smell of blood... meant that the Black Wolf had somehow managed to withstand the blow from the Death Scythe.

(Did it instinctively step back?)

Watching the black wolf twist its body in midair and land on the ground after being sent flying, Rei speculates.

If the Black Wolf had been behind him from the start, just as he’d intended, he wouldn’t have had the time to react that way.

However, this time, the Black Wolf had appeared not behind Rei as before... but to his right.

Because of that, even though it was a fraction of a second, Rei had wasted time.

As a result, even against a strike from the Death Scythe, the Black Wolf managed to jump backward, weakening the attack’s power and gaining just enough leeway to parry it.

(You’d think he could just teleport to dodge… no, maybe it’s not that simple. In fact, even in previous battles, there were times when he could have easily avoided attacks by teleporting, but he chose to dodge using his physical abilities instead. It’s likely that, even though it’s a skill, there are definitely some limitations, just as I thought a moment ago.)

Holding the Death Scythe, Rei observed the Black Wolf once more.

That strike hadn’t been enough to inflict a fatal wound on the Black Wolf.

But it had undoubtedly inflicted a wound, and while it wasn’t fatal, it was certainly a wound that would affect the battle.

“What are you going to do? With that wound, you won’t be able to fight the way you have been, will you? If you surrender, I might spare your life.”

Upon hearing this, the Black Wolf removed his hand from his abdomen, which he had been holding since landing.

Was he trying to prevent unnecessary blood loss?

That’s what Rei thought… but when he saw where the hand had been, he gasped in surprise.

Because the wound that should have been there when he removed his hand was clearly much smaller. ...No, it would be more accurate to say it was actively shrinking.

Several small, bubble-like substances were emerging from the wound—whether due to the effect of those bubbles or some other factor.

In any case, there was no doubt that the fresh wound was healing rapidly—at a speed visible to the naked eye.

“You’ve got to be kidding me,”

Rei muttered, but what was shrinking before his very eyes at a visible speed was no joke—it was an undeniable fact.

At this rate, wouldn’t the only way to win be to sever the neck or the torso in a single blow?

No, depending on the situation, even if I severed its neck or torso, wouldn’t it just reattach itself instantly?

It might be called astonishing regenerative ability—or perhaps regenerative power—but looking at the Black Wolf now, that’s exactly what comes to mind.

(So, to defeat it, I need to kill it with a single blow, huh? And I’ll also have to make sure it doesn’t regenerate the moment I kill it.)

For Rei, the Black Wolf is by no means an enemy she hates.

Well, he does have some reservations about the fact that it was drugged into attacking him, but perhaps because of his experience feeding it, he doesn’t hate it enough to want to kill it.

That said, naturally, if it attacks him, he has no intention of just standing there and taking it.

That is precisely why Rei is fighting the Black Wolf right now.

But even so, she couldn’t help but wonder: Is it really okay to kill the Black Wolf?

(It would be best if he just surrendered… huh?)

Even though she knew it was a hopeless situation, Rei found herself thinking such things... when suddenly, she noticed that the Black Wolf was acting strangely.

“Ugh… gah…”

Those were the first words to escape the Black Wolf’s mouth.

Although Rei had spent a fair amount of time with the Black Wolf, this was the first time he’d ever heard him speak.

But now was not the time to be thinking about such things.

The sound that came from the Black Wolf’s mouth was unmistakably a groan, as if he were enduring something.

“Black Wolf?”

Not knowing what on earth was going on, Rei called out to him, as if just to check on him.

But... the moment Rei spoke, Black Wolf’s gaze fixed directly on him.

Though the Black Wolf usually showed almost no emotion, a single emotion was clearly evident on his face now.

It was... hunger.

(Why?)

Until just a moment ago, there had been no particular sign of hunger.

I knew he had a hearty appetite, especially after the steamed bun incident, but even so, it would be unthinkable for him to feel hungry in the midst of a life-or-death battle like this.

Given that, there was only one possible explanation for why this had suddenly happened.

“I see. Setting aside the teleportation, so there was a trick behind that intense healing ability after all.”

Judging by the current situation, it was clear that the sudden recovery from earlier wasn’t something he could use repeatedly.

(Then there’s no particular problem. …All that’s left is to keep slashing until I push the Black Wolf past its limits… keep slashing… can I do it?)

He possesses extremely high physical ability, and in terms of evasion, he’s undoubtedly one of the best opponents Rei has ever faced.

Could he really land multiple hits on such an opponent like he did just now?

He had that doubt for a split second, but since he had to do it anyway, he couldn’t back down now.
