The sun exists in the sky, asserting itself intensely.

Just yesterday it was snowing, but now the weather seems to suggest that was all an illusion.

Yet the temperature is by no means warm, and even with the sun pouring down, the snow shows little sign of melting.

Instead, the sunlight reflects off the snow, blindingly glaring at those walking on the ground.

It's the kind of weather where only children would willingly go outside.

Yet in such weather... a large crowd had gathered near the lord's mansion in Gilm.

Food stalls were everywhere, selling various hot dishes to ward off the cold.

Though the lord's mansion was a place civilians rarely approached under normal circumstances, today was different.

Because today was a festival.

A festival that seemed to sweep away the uniquely wintry, somewhat melancholy atmosphere.

Of course, being held right near the lord's mansion, security was tight.

Knights and guards patrolled the area, keeping a strict watch for anyone attempting to enter the lord's residence, anyone trying to cause trouble, anyone getting rowdy from the festive atmosphere, or anyone drinking themselves into a drunken stupor.

Those with guilty consciences kept quiet, while those without enjoyed the festival purely.

"Gamelion skewers! Special super-low price today! Buy five, get one free!"

"Udon! How about Gamelion stew udon?"

"Gamelion stew! Our hearty Gamelion meat is specially prepared today—you'll regret it if you miss it!"

"Gamelion meat soup! Packed with Gamelion meat, it'll warm you right up on a cold day like this!"

"How about a Gamelion meat sandwich? Freshly baked bread stuffed with Gamelion meat—a seasonal sandwich only available this time of year!"

Stalls selling Gamelion dishes like these are plentiful.

Because the dungeon delayed the spread of Gamelion meat more than usual this year, many are likely hoping to make a killing here today where crowds gather.

Of course, it's not just Gamelion dishes; there are also many stalls selling other kinds of food.

Amidst this, the fact that very few stalls are selling anything other than food, even though it's a festival, is largely because this festival was decided on just a few days ago.

Also, selling goods in the snow wasn't exactly boosting sales—especially compared to stalls serving hot food.

Still, a few vendors had spread mats on the snow, warming themselves by bonfires, selling accessories, clothing, and small goods. These were the ones who had gotten wind of the festival early.

Amidst this, straight ahead along the street lined with stalls and booths lay the training grounds of the Larks Marquisate's knight order. This was also where the festival's main event would take place.

"The reason for this duel began when Lord Kip, heir to the Count of Eriell, set his sights on a maid from the Baron of Sisne household. The Count of Eriell, aligned with the royal faction, wields significant power. It seemed the Baron of Sisne household would have no choice but to meekly comply with demands from such a superior opponent in every respect. But... but! Baron Muet, head of the Sisne household, resolutely refused!"

At the entrance to the square where the duel would take place, a man recounted the events leading up to it.

Listening were those attending the festival. Many were unaware why such a duel had come to pass, and most listened with keen interest.

"Naturally, Lord Keep was displeased that the lower-ranked Mueet defied him. So he challenged him to a duel. Of course, in this case, the duel wouldn't be fought by the lords themselves, but by surrogates. At this point, Lord Keep must have been confident of victory. And why wouldn't he be? The Count of Eriell family is quite wealthy. With money to burn, they could hire high-ranking adventurers or mercenaries."

Those who had heard the man's words now wore dissatisfied expressions, voicing their discontent with Keep to those nearby.

"But! ...But, however! Lord Keep never could have imagined this.That the person near Lady Muet and the maid was a formidable adventurer! That person was someone who had previously accepted a request to train the heir of the Baron Sysne family and had shown up after a long absence to see the results. Such an adventurer couldn't possibly remain silent when someone connected to him was in danger. That man was none other than Rank B adventurer Rei. The man known by the epithet Crimson!"

The moment Rei's name was uttered, the audience's excitement reached its peak.

Noticing this, the narrator flashed a satisfied smile.

"Well then, Lord Keep, you've made such an opponent your enemy. Have you prepared an adventurer who can fight Rei!? Come on, place your bets. The long shot is Rei losing, Lord Keep's representative winning. The odds are five hundred to one!"

The odds the man announced seemed too unfavorable to warrant a bet.

But... even so, some of those listening believed in the possibility of a dramatic reversal and joined the wager.

The others placed their bets based on how long they thought the opponent could hold out against Rei.

Even though the name and background of the representative from the Eriell family, who was still set to fight Rei, remained unknown, the fact that bets were being placed spoke volumes about Rei's widespread recognition in Gilm.

"Hey, Johanna. Aren't you going to place a bet?"

"Hmm, even if it's a bet, isn't it already a foregone conclusion that Rei-san will win? If that's the case, then it all comes down to Rei-san's mood, right? Like, maybe he'll start within seconds and show off how strong his opponent is to some extent."

Lent, who had luckily managed to catch Johanna during her escort break, approached her with a skewer of Gamelion meat in hand. Johanna's response, however, sounded somewhat unenthusiastic.

It wasn't that she wasn't enjoying the festival; she was probably disappointed she hadn't been able to meet Set.

Johanna, who had once served as a guard for the Baron Sysne household as part of the former guerrilla squad, was now taking a break specifically to look for Set.

Even so, when she went to the Dusk Wheat inn, Set had already left for a duel with Rei. On her way back, Rent invited her, and that's how she ended up at the festival.

"Well, I know how the match will turn out... The biggest problem is tonight, after the duel ends. Since he made the foolish mistake of betting half his fortune, the only final option is for the recipient to disappear."

"Hey, hey, don't say such scary things."

"But seriously, thugs have been sent to the Baron Sisne household day after day, right? If they're going to do it, they should send stronger opponents. Do they really think those guys can do anything to us?"

Johanna muttered discontentedly, but Keep and Furtos also had their reasons—if they sent someone connected to them, their actions might be exposed.

Of course, it was obvious who was pulling the strings behind the scenes. After all, the peaceful daily life of the Baroness Sisne household had been shattered the moment the duel was decided, and now attacks were happening one after another.

But without proof, they couldn't bring charges.

Taking advantage of this, Keep and Furtos repeatedly sent in numerous individuals—small-time thugs who, even if captured, wouldn't lead back to them.

They certainly didn't expect to kill Muet and the others this way; they saw it as merely a stroke of luck if they succeeded.

Their real goal was intimidation. They wanted to wear them down mentally through constant attacks, forcing them to withdraw from the duel.

Since they had no real chance of defeating Rei, this was the best course of action Keep and Fultos could take.

...However, the men of the Baron de Sisne household—Muet, Ashié, Baslero—knew Rei's strength. And they believed in him from the bottom of their hearts.

Furthermore, the deadline until the duel wasn't that far off, so they could endure it.

"They really did something foolish. If they'd at least fought fairly, Rei might have shown some restraint."

"Nah, with half his fortune on the line, he couldn't afford to be picky about methods."

Having heard the details from Rei, Rent could only say that.

After all, if he were in the keeper's position, attacking Rei himself would be impossible. The only option was to deal with Muet.

The best option, of course, would be to apologize and have the duel declared null and void.

But given his position as the next head of the Eriell family, Keep's own pride, and above all, the fact that the matter had been blown up into a festival-like spectacle, apologizing to Muet and having the duel nullified was impossible.

"What a clown..."

Gazing up at the sky where the sun asserted itself fiercely, Rent murmured pityingly toward Keep.

Yet Rent himself was also something of a clown, oblivious to the scorching wind members—or more accurately, Mireine—approaching him and Johanna.

While Rent was trembling in fear at the blizzard Johanna and Mireine were stirring up under the sun, Rei was eating lunch in the space prepared as a waiting room.

As a participant in the duel, he couldn't carelessly wander around the festival grounds. With no other choice, he had one of the knights assigned as his escort buy him food from various stalls at random.

The knight briefly questioned why he was being used like a servant despite being dispatched as a guard, but when asked if Rei needed protection, having participated in the Spring War, he could only answer no.

Besides, the knight was also interested in the festival food stalls. He kept buying dishes indiscriminately, bringing them back to the waiting room where Rei, Set, Muet, Baslero, and Ashié were gathered, then going back to buy more.

"Um, Rei-san. Is it really okay to eat until you're stuffed right before a duel?"

Baslero asked Rei anxiously as he devoured a soup packed with Gamelion and vegetables, then bit into a skewer.

For Baslero, this was a critical moment where Asie, whom he cherished like a sister, might be lost.

He believed in Rei's strength, but still couldn't help worrying about the worst-case scenario.

"Grrr."

Set growled at Baslerro, as if to say, "Don't worry."

...Though his beak still bore traces of the Gamelion stew sauce he'd been eating just moments before.

"We're about to fight, so we need to fuel up, right? Eating so much you can't move is a problem, but this much is totally fine."

Chewing on the Gamelion meat, savoring the flavor and texture filling his mouth, Rei handed the unopened skewered meat to Baslero.

"See, training is necessary to get stronger, but eating properly is too. We still don't know who your opponent is for today's duel, but you don't need to worry about losing. The fight itself will probably end surprisingly easily."

"Really? Well, I know you're strong, Rei, but I don't know what kind of opponent they've prepared."

"Well, if a truly formidable opponent shows up, I might need to use my secret weapon... but we'll see if it even comes to that."

The secret weapon, of course, referred to the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. Naturally unaware of this, Baslero's eyes lit up at the phrase "secret weapon."

"D-Does that mean... a finishing move!?"

"A finishing move... huh. I don't know. It doesn't really feel like a finishing move to me."

For Rei, a finishing move was an attack meant to slaughter the opponent in one blow.

It carried a strong impression of literally killing with a single strike.

Of course, when attacking while wearing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, it could defeat an ordinary opponent in one hit.

But fundamentally, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour is more of a support skill... it involves compressing magical power to the point where it becomes visible on one's body.

Regardless of how others perceived it, in Rei's mind, the Fire Whirlwind felt more like a finishing move than the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Especially if metal fragments were embedded within the Fire Whirlwind, its destructive power would skyrocket.

(Though, precisely because its attack power is so high, it's tricky to use in places like this.)

The Fire Whirlwind possessed an effective range capable of annihilating an entire first-line unit or a single defensive position outright.

Using it in a place like this could easily lead to the annihilation of Gilm itself.

"Is that so..."

At Rei's words, Baslero hung his head in disappointment.

No matter how mature he seemed, he was still a child at heart—the word "special move" made his eyes sparkle.

Rei gently placed a hand on Basler's head and spoke.

"I can't show you a finishing move, but watching me fight is also training."

"Yes!"

Though the master-disciple dynamic felt awkward for the master, Muet and Asie, watching them, wore faintly amused expressions.

Their expressions, showing not a hint of concern about losing the duel, revealed their complete trust in Rei.

Amidst this, a knock sounded at the door.

Muet answered, and the knight escort entered the room.

"Rei, it's time for the duel."

At those words, Rei nodded. He stuffed the last remaining skewer of Gamelion into his mouth, then led everyone out of the room.

...To head to the dueling grounds.
