As announced by the guild staff, as noon approaches, a sizable group of people begins to show up at the Giganto Turtle dismantling site.

The fact that some of them look dissatisfied is likely largely due to the hassle of having to make their way all the way here from the construction site.

To begin with, Gilm was of a size that made it questionable whether it could even be called a town.

The expansion project is taking place deep within Gilm.

Naturally, getting from there to here takes time and is tiring.

……Yet they don’t voice their complaints directly, likely because they’re still in awe of seeing the Giganto Turtle being dismantled right before their eyes, and above all, because they know it was Rei who commissioned the guild to dismantle this Giganto Turtle.

If anyone were to voice their dissatisfaction in such a situation, let alone act arrogantly... Rei would undoubtedly step in.

Or perhaps, if Rei weren’t here, some might have voiced their complaints directly, but for now, Rei, Set, Vihera, and Byune are all present. …Though, to be fair, Byune isn’t here to watch the Giganto Turtle being butchered; she’s engaged in a battle—or rather, a rampage—against the kobolds.

“Oh, thank you for coming. Well then, let’s get right to it. Please split into the dismantling and the extermination teams and get to work.”

Even if they wanted to complain to the person who said that, as long as they were wearing the guild uniform, they couldn’t get away with it.

To begin with, most of the people here chose the Kobold extermination—which pays a little more—over the Giganto Turtle disassembly, which offers a meager reward.

I’m broke, and I plan to keep taking jobs from the guild, so there’s no way I could afford to pick a fight with a guild official.

(Huh? But come to think of it... if we went to dissect the Giganto Turtles, we’d get a share of the meat, so wouldn’t selling that meat to a butcher or a wealthy person yield a bigger reward? Plus, unless we’re escorting the dissection, we wouldn’t even have to fight.)

Rei had only just realized this, but in reality, the ones who had joined the kobold hunt were those who had judged that killing a large number of kobolds would yield a higher reward. …Though there were some who hadn’t even considered the idea of selling the meat.

“With this many people, the butchering should go by pretty quickly, right?”

“I don’t know. Considering how big the Giganto Turtle is, I don’t think it’ll make that much of a difference.”

The phrase “a drop in the bucket” is probably meant for moments like this.

Thinking this, Rei replied to Vihera… and pondered the kobold incident.

When he first heard about the kobolds, he’d thought that, at worst, if he stationed Set at the construction site, the kobolds would be too afraid of him to attack.

But the kobolds, likely drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood, didn’t flee or cower even with Set right there.

That fact bothered Rei.

If they were ordinary monsters, it wouldn’t be strange for them to flee the moment they noticed Set’s presence.

Or they might be so terrified by Set’s presence that they’d freeze in place.

Despite that, the kobold that came here showed no sign of either.

(There’s no doubt this isn’t an ordinary kobold. But… for one that’s supposed to be special, it doesn’t seem particularly strong either.)

Where Rei’s gaze falls, he sees the kobold being effortlessly killed by Byune, who wields a White Cloud.

That one-sided battle made it impossible to believe this was a monster strong enough to not be intimidated by Set’s presence.

“Hey, Vihera, you’ve fought kobolds too, right?”

“Huh? What’s with the sudden question? …Yeah, I’ve fought them. Though, they were just ordinary kobolds.”

“But why do you think they’re not even trying to run away, even with Set here?”

“……Huh?”

At Rei’s words, Vihera glanced once more at Set, who was standing a short distance away.

To Vihera, Set was simply a party member, so even seeing him standing there like that, she didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

But that was only because she was Vihera; ordinarily, one couldn’t help but notice Set’s presence.

And that would be even more true if he were a monster.

“Come to think of it… ah, but there are monsters other than kobolds here too, aren’t there? Maybe Set isn’t really trying his hardest, so they haven’t noticed him. Or maybe the scent of a Giganto Turtle’s blood is just that alluring?”

“Hmm… well, I can’t say that’s impossible…”

Given the scent of blood from such a massive, high-rank monster, it wouldn’t be strange for other monsters who caught the scent to go wild with excitement.

But even if he was told that, Rei could understand it, but he couldn’t quite accept it.

Logically, he knew it was a strong possibility, but there was still a subtle sense of unease about it.

“Then why don’t you take Set with you and check out the construction site next time?”

“I’ve thought about that, too. But defeating kobolds is a great way for those who don’t have enough money to take quests outside the city, or who find it a hassle to move around in the snow-covered outdoors, to make some cash, right? I wasn’t sure if I should take that away from them.”

“...I get that, but what are we going to do if things stay like this? It’s fine for now. It’s winter, so the expansion work isn’t happening. But once a little more time passes and spring comes, the expansion work will really get underway, right? If the kobold attacks keep happening just like they are now, the work will definitely get delayed.”

Vihera’s words were an undeniable fact.

Adventurers who can protect themselves are one thing, but it’s an undeniable fact that the majority of those working on the expansion aren’t adventurers at all—they’re migrant workers.

It’s unreasonable to expect such people to work in a place where they don’t know when a kobold attack might strike.

No, even before that, once they heard about the kobold incident, they would likely refuse to work, prioritizing their own lives.

Even for an adventurer capable of defeating kobolds, if they were to be ambushed by one in the middle of construction work… if caught off guard, it would be difficult to handle.

“I see. It’s fine for now, but things can’t stay this way forever. In that case…”

Rei paused there and called out to a guild employee standing a short distance away—the one who seemed to be in charge of the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

“Hey, can I have a sec? I’d like to talk to you for a moment!”

He must have heard her. The guild official gave instructions to a few subordinates nearby and hurried over to Rei.

“Yes, what is it?”

“Sorry to bother you while you’re busy. I’d like to ask you a few questions about the kobold attack.”

“Huh? Oh, sure, that’s fine.”

He must have assumed Rei was going to ask about the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle. The guild official looked slightly surprised, but quickly hid his surprise and waited for Rei to ask his question.

“Right now, the kobolds are attacking Gilm every day and every night, but when you think about what’ll happen come spring, isn’t that going to be a problem?”

“It is a problem.”

The guild clerk answered without a moment’s hesitation.

That very attitude showed that the current situation was by no means good.

Yet he didn’t seem particularly desperate, likely because there was still some time left before spring.

It’s a hopeful assumption that this matter will be resolved by spring.

...That said, most of the adventurers in Gilm are truly skilled.

Currently, due to the expansion project, there are many who are hard to call “skilled,” but it wouldn’t be unreasonable to think that if the adventurers of Gilm put their minds to it this time, they could resolve the issue without any trouble.

“I think it’d be better to start thinking about this now, just in case, so we don’t panic when the time comes. …If someone holds a grudge against us… Wait a minute.”

Having said that much, Rei suddenly realized something.

For now, this situation is being welcomed by the cash-strapped adventurers.

But objectively speaking, if this is happening for some artificial reason, it’s undoubtedly an act of sabotage against Gilm… no, you could even call it an act of hostility.

Even if they’re just kobolds, summoning a massive number of monsters into Gilm’s territory—that’s only natural.

“What’s wrong?”

The guild clerk asked curiously, puzzled by Rei’s sudden interruption mid-sentence.

To the guild clerk, Rei asks, as if just to be sure.

“Just to be sure, is there any method, ritual, magic item, or spell that can specifically summon only kobolds into the Gilm?”

“Huh? Um…”

Caught off guard by Rei’s sudden question, the guild official paused to think.

A Guild official from Gilm… and as the person in charge of this particular case, this man holds a fairly high position within the Gilm Guild.

In a sense, you could call him the boss of Lenora and Kenny.

Precisely because of that, he knows a great deal of information that even Lenora and Kenny are unaware of… but he immediately shook his head.

“No, unfortunately, I’ve never heard anything like that. To be clear, since this incident began, the guild has checked into that area on Daskar’s orders, so it’s a fact.”

Since Gilm is located on the frontier, the knowledge stored within its guild is far greater than that of an ordinary guild.

At Daskar’s instruction, they had investigated whether anything of that nature existed. Since nearly all the guild staff had looked into it, she could confidently say there was nothing to Rei’s inquiry.

“That said…” the guild official continued.

“It wouldn’t be strange for there to be technologies, magic, or magical items out there that we don’t know about, nor would it be strange for there to be an alchemist who created such a magical item. Especially in a dungeon, there’s a possibility that magical items we’re not aware of could turn up.”

“...I suppose so. In that case, if this incident was caused by human intervention, we wouldn’t know exactly how it happened, would we?”

“Unfortunately, that’s the case. Besides… the number of people who hold a grudge against Gilm isn’t just a matter of ten or twenty people.”

Rei couldn’t help but agree with that explanation.

Because Gilm is located on the frontier, it’s easy to obtain rare materials, ores, magic stones… all sorts of things.

There are those who envy that, and the city is ruled by Daskar, who leads the Neutral Faction... and now, they’re in the middle of expansion work, trying to expand their scale even further.

Considering all that, the number of people who harbor a grudge against Gilm or who want the expansion project to fail isn’t just ten or twenty, as the guild official said—it wouldn’t be surprising if there were hundreds or even two hundred of them.

“If it’s not man-made, then there might be some high-tier or rare species around, but there aren’t any of those, right?”

“Yes. So far, there have been no reports of that sort. We even had a few adventurers with excellent detection skills look into it, but…”

The guild clerk shook his head.

Given that, Rei had no choice but to consider the possibility of a man-made method rather than a high-tier or rare species.

(Or maybe there’s a higher-tier or rare species with an ability like Set’s optical camouflage. …Personally, I’d prefer that option.)

If there were monsters with abilities like optical camouflage, defeating them and obtaining their magic stones would raise the level of Set’s optical camouflage.

From his past battles, Rei knew that optical camouflage was incredibly effective in combat, and there was no doubt it was a highly versatile skill even outside of battle.

“For now, as for interference from the Noble Faction… I suppose we can safely assume that’s not the case.”

With Elena personally present in Gilm, pulling a stunt like this would be tantamount to suicide.

Of course, that’s only true if Elena finds out; if she doesn’t discover what’s going on, there’s nothing she can do about it.

“What else could it be…?”

Thinking this, Rei suddenly recalled the group that had been causing trouble in Gilm before he went to Anesis.

“Come to think of it, what happened to those guys in red cloth who were causing trouble before I went to Anesis? I haven’t heard anything about them since I got back.”

That’s what he asked.
