The night Stigma was encountered, Rei's figure was near the stable.

Not a single cloud marred the night sky; a beautiful moon displayed its graceful form, showering the earth with gentle moonlight.

Under that moonlight, despite the late hour, Rei was playing in the snow with Set.

For Rei, who was being targeted by Stigma, such behavior was tantamount to suicide. However, they were within the grounds of the officer academy, and above all, he knew that Sardat was guarding him.

"Well, I have no idea where Sardat is, though."

"Gurururu?"

What's wrong? Set tilted his head slightly.

Having been left alone all day—well, not exactly, but that was the result—Set was now indulging in being spoiled by Rei to his heart's content.

He carried Rei on his back through the snow, rolled around together in the snow leaving their tracks, caught snowballs Rei threw with his beak... enjoying snow play to the fullest.

If it had been daytime, someone might have screamed at the sight of Set.

But it was night, and a winter night at that, so almost no one was out.

The rare exceptions were Rei and Set, and likely the Sardat guarding Rei in this snow.

"Any sign of anyone else besides us?"

"Grrr..."

At Rei's words, Set strained every sense—his physical senses, his magical perception, even his sixth sense—to scan the surroundings. Unable to detect any sign of the Sardat, he gave a apologetic growl.

"Don't worry about it," Rei said, stroking Set's head.

Pleased by the gesture, Set rubbed his face against Rei again, his attitude clearly saying, "Play with me, play with me."

"Ah, yeah. I left you alone today, so let's play for a while. ...Well, time is limited, though."

With classes tomorrow, Rei couldn't afford to stay up too late.

Set seemed to understand that too. Looking a little disappointed, he still ran through the snow alongside Rei.

"Too soft. I think I've told you before, but you lack decisiveness when attacking. If you attack while holding back, it's just for show. There's no momentum behind the power."

He parried the long sword swing aimed at him with his mock battle spear, then thrust the spearhead straight toward the opponent's face.

Four students had already fallen, and the fifth was now considered dead from that strike.

Showing no sign of fatigue or lack of sleep from playing with Set until late the previous night, Rei's mock battle training remained relentless today.

Just how intense it was could be understood by anyone seeing the pile of bodies left behind by the third-year S-class students.

"Alright, you lot are the last. We'll take a break now, so think carefully about what went wrong in this mock battle. Anyone who repeats the same mistakes will see it reflected in their grades."

All forty students responded with a unified "Understood" to Rei's words.

...Though, while those who fought Rei earlier were manageable, those who faced him later were barely able to move, their voices sounding like zombies.

Glancing at those students, Rei approached Grink, who had been watching nearby.

More accurately, it was more about distancing himself from the students.

It was true that he felt they'd be too tense to rest with him nearby... but more importantly, he didn't want anyone else to hear what he was about to say.

"Good work. As always, a strict yet beneficial lesson for the students. It's educational even for us watching."

"What are you talking about? I'm just a temporary instructor, after all. Compared to the guys who are properly teaching at this academy, my lessons are bound to be less efficient."

"No, that's not true. We certainly consider efficiency when planning our lessons. But it's also true that relying solely on that leads to lessons that always feel similar. In that regard, Rei's lessons are different from what we've been doing. Just watching is educational."

"Educational? ...I'm just doing nothing but mock battles. Anyone could do that just as well, couldn't they?"

Even if praised for something anyone could do... he said, though clearly pleased by the compliment.

Perhaps wanting to avoid showing his embarrassed expression, Rei pulled his hood back over his head.

Glink watched Rei, the corners of his lips curving slightly in a faint smile.

To the students who'd been pushed to their physical limits in the mock battle, this scene undoubtedly made them want to say, "What the hell are you talking about? What are you doing?"

Perhaps tired of being watched with that amused look, Rei cleared his throat softly and changed the subject.

"What happened with yesterday's Eland incident? You handed the attackers over to the guards, right?"

"Yes. But apparently, the ones captured were just pawns... none of them had any important information. Eland insists they were definitely my uncle's men, but without any evidence, it's nothing more than a baseless accusation."

"So for now, we're stuck with nothing but a bitter pill to swallow, huh?"

"Yes. But the guards have lost face over this. They'll likely scramble to find any leads they can. ...It'll be tough, though."

"They deliberately caused a disturbance at Duke Quent's stronghold. We should assume they've made arrangements to handle any fallout immediately."

At Rei's words, Glink gave a slight grimace and nodded in return.

The idea that his student was being targeted by someone was certainly not amusing.

And he'd witnessed the attack firsthand. ...Though it was after Rei had already defeated all the assailants.

"So, what about my situation?"

He already understood that the force that had stormed in during yesterday's battle with the attackers was the Stigma, the elite unit of the Holy Light Church.

He could also reasonably assume the poisoned arrows using Heaven's Tears were likely the work of the Holy Light Church.

But regarding the Heavenly Tears, it was still just a prediction, not a certainty.

Perhaps... just maybe, it was possible the attackers were completely separate from the Holy Light Church.

(It's awkward to say this myself, but I've made enemies more than enough times to remember. I really need to be concerned about the possibility of other attackers.)

Glink, asked with such thoughts, silently shook his head.

"I hear you tried questioning the captured men about Heaven's Tears, but none of them seemed to know anything about it."

"So it was useless after all. ...At this point, it's hard to believe they could keep it hidden, so they probably truly knew nothing about it."

"Yes, I'm certain of that. Still, I never imagined the Holy Light Church would show up here. And Stigmata, too? With such people appearing, honestly, I..."

Glink shook his head apologetically, but Rei had no intention of blaming him.

After all, he'd heard from Sardat that Stigmata possessed combat strength equivalent to Rank A adventurers.

Given that this came from a Rank S adventurer, the pride of the Mireana Kingdom, he had no choice but to believe it. ...Considering Saldat's personality, Rei did feel a little uneasy about it.

In any case, that meant the enemy was at least three Rank A adventurers. It was clear that Glinck, currently just an instructor at the Officer Academy, would struggle to handle them.

(Three... At least there are only three of them. That's some consolation. Three means me, Sardat... and Set. If things go well, we might even be able to borrow Coan from Marka. Then, we should be able to match their strength. No, we might even have the upper hand in terms of combat power.)

If asked whether he could handle Stigma alone, Rei would have to shake his head.

He could probably handle them one at a time, but three together? There's no way he could manage that.

But if he had help from others, it would be a different story.

(Marka... no, should I meet with Duke Quent and arrange to borrow Coan in case of emergency? Duke Quent has Iskeld as his guard...)

The face of the Knight Commander of Quent's domain flashed through Rei's mind.

It wasn't about his title as Knight Commander; from meeting him directly, Rei had a good grasp of Iskeld's capabilities.

He wasn't quite at Rank S level, and Ray believed he could defeat him. But he possessed strength equivalent to a Rank A adventurer.

"The problem is how they'll respond..."

A small mutter.

Rei had intended it as a private thought, but Grink seemed to take it as addressed to him. He nodded back at Rei.

"I suppose so. But as long as we're inside the academy, they probably won't be able to pull off anything too reckless. Even if they don't reach Rank A adventurer level, there are former Rank B adventurers with experience, and if push comes to shove, there's the academy headmaster. Plus, Samaloon is quite powerful as a pure mage."

"Seems like it. Plus, there are plenty of adventurers inside the academy now. Given that, unless they have a serious death wish, they probably won't try anything like an attack. ...Though dragging adventurers into this is a bit questionable."

As adventurers, they have an obligation to respond if the location they're contracted to protect is attacked.

Strictly speaking, they aren't obligated to do so, but if they were to flee while an attack was happening, they'd undoubtedly face a lot of awkwardness afterward.

What's more, this academy isn't just for commoners; children of nobles and merchants attend here too.

If he were to abandon such people to their fate, he might not only be expelled from Graciall, but could even risk being unable to remain in the Kingdom of Mireana itself.

Of course, if no students were killed or injured in the attack, it wouldn't be a problem.

But when asked whether cadets, still merely students, could survive against assassins with more experience than themselves, few could confidently nod in the affirmative—unless they were exceptionally optimistic.

"Right. Maybe we should introduce ourselves to other adventurers first. It'd be a disaster if they didn't recognize me when push comes to shove."

"That's right. There happens to be a mock battle for Class 3B this afternoon, with adventurers serving as opponents. Why not show your face there? I'll accompany you, of course."

"Alright, I'll count on you. ...Right then."

With that matter settled, Rei's gaze shifted from Glink to the students.

There, students were discussing what went wrong in the earlier mock battle and how they could land a hit on Rei.

(It's partly getting used to it, I suppose, but they've gotten better at recovering stamina quickly. ...No, to be precise, maybe this is their true strength, and before they were just nervous about facing me? ...That seems like a reasonable explanation.)

Despite his inner thoughts, seeing the students he taught gradually growing stronger filled Rei with a strange feeling.

If we're talking purely about students, he had taught Basleru as well.

But with Baslerro, it was only the absolute basics.

It wasn't truly combat training.

...Though, speaking of proper training, he had been doing that against the former guerrilla squad members during the Bestir Empire's civil war.

"Well then, it's about time..."

Prompted by Glink's gaze, Rei nodded in response.

"Right. Break's over."

With that, Rei left Glink behind and returned to his original spot.

"Now then, has everyone thoroughly reviewed their own strengths and weaknesses? You have, right? You're not going to tell me you haven't?"

Rei pressed the point almost annoyingly, and the third-year S-class students nodded back, feeling a sense of foreboding despite themselves.

They probably understood vaguely what was about to happen, but they hoped they were wrong.

With that thought, they turned their gaze to Rei...

"Alright, since you've finished reflecting, we'll restart the mock battle. Just so you know, anyone who makes the same mistake... Hmm, this time instead of a group, you'll have a mock battle one-on-one with either me or Set."

"Whaaaah!"

Hearing Rei's words, the students voiced their discontent.

Set wasn't in the gym today, but many believed he'd come immediately if Rei called him.

Their discontent likely stemmed from knowing that if Rei truly intended to follow through on what he'd just said, he could make it happen immediately.

Hearing this, Rei flashed a grin that could be described as smug and spoke.

"I see, I see. Nothing makes me happier than seeing you all so pleased. Well then, I'll go all out too. Instead of just me or Set, how about you have a mock battle against both me and Set? Don't worry, this is an officer academy, so we've got people who can use healing magic ready and waiting. And if anything happens, I'll use potions on you myself."

At Rei's words, the students let out cries of despair... and then, steeled with desperate resolve, they charged into the mock battle.
