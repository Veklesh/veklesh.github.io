"Um... Huh? Hey, is this some kind of joke or something?"

After climbing the stairs inside the dungeon and ascending to the next floor—though in this case, it might be more accurate to call it the second floor—what Rei and his companions saw was a forest stretching out as far as the eye could see.

There was no doubt that this was a dungeon carved into the side of a cliff, yet the forest stretching out before them was so vast it seemed impossible for the dungeon to contain it all.

It was only natural that Relieu, who had explored dungeons several times before but had never seen a place like this inside one, let out a cry of surprise.

That said, Relieu was the only one voicing his surprise.

Rei and Elena had seen forests in the dungeon housing the Altar of Succession, and Vihera and Byune had experienced the Exil dungeon.

Marina had also seen a similar sight in a dungeon she had challenged before becoming a guild master.

In that sense, it’s only natural that Relieu—even as a Rank A adventurer with a nickname—would be surprised by this sight, given that she hadn’t ventured into many dungeons before.

She had heard similar stories even before finding the stairs on the first floor… but as the saying goes, seeing is believing.

Paying no mind to Relieu’s reaction, Rei turned her gaze toward the forest.

“Well, for now… is there any chance this forest is an illusion or something like that…?”

“No, I don’t think so. I think the space itself is warped.”

Marina was able to answer Rei’s question immediately because she had used a wind spirit to scout the area to some extent.

“So, this forest is real, then. …I’m not sure whether to call it a hassle or feel relieved that we’re out of that cramped cave.”

“Grrrr!”

As Rei muttered while looking around, Set let out a happy growl.

The cave on the first floor had been spacious enough for Set to move around freely.

However, the ceiling wasn’t particularly high, so Set couldn’t actually fly around.

In that sense, it was only natural that Set would be delighted with this forest where he could fly freely.

“So, was the story about this dungeon being brand-new a mistake?”

“I don’t know. Dungeons are absurd things. Even a brand-new dungeon could easily pull something like this off, I’d say.”

Rei replied to Relieu’s muttered words, spoken with obvious surprise yet without letting his guard down.

That said, for Rei, who wanted to return to Gilm by winter, it would be best if they could clear the dungeon as quickly as possible.

In that sense, there’s no doubt that the scenario where the dungeon’s core was located right after ascending to the second floor would have been the easiest… but Rei’s optimistic hopes vanished the moment he laid eyes on the forest spreading out before him.

“So, what do we do now? Weren’t we just supposed to take a quick look around the dungeon today? I think heading back to Golz would be fine.”

“Hmm, I see what Elena’s getting at, but if possible, I’d like to explore this forest a little more. After all, there was only one fork on the first floor, so there wasn’t really anything to ‘check out’ there.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Elena—who had suggested they return—merely nodded slightly in agreement.

Although she’d said it as if it were just a formality, Elena probably felt that turning back now would be a bit of a letdown.

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu!”

As if to catch Elena’s attention, Yellow, who had been watching the surroundings from a nearby tree branch, chirped happily.

Those who heard the sound looked up and saw Yellow holding a green fruit in his hand.

Holding the fruit, Yellow descended to the ground, wobbling slightly under its weight.

Elena caught Yellow and gently stroked him.

“I don’t think there’s any problem with Yellow, but just in case something happens, you’d better be careful, okay?”

“Kyu!”

Was he nodding in response to the warning, or was he chirping proudly because he was handing over the fruit he’d picked himself?

Elena couldn’t tell, but there was no doubt that Yellow had brought the fruit as a gift for her.

“This fruit is… Emna. I thought it was a fruit harvested further south in the spring… but no, it’s probably a mistake to worry about such things in a dungeon.”

Emna is a fruit with a refreshing tartness and a subtle sweetness; it’s not exactly a rare commodity.

However, its juiciness makes it one of the fruits people enjoy eating.

Holding the fruit in her hand, Elena looked up at the branch where Yellow had been just moments ago... and, unusually, her eyes widened in surprise.

That’s because, on that tree, there were various fruits, starting with the Emna.

...Yes, various kinds. Completely different types of fruit, aside from Emna, were growing on a single tree.

It was clearly nothing short of abnormal—such a tree stood right before her eyes.

Not only spring fruits, but even those that ripen in summer and autumn were there, ready to eat.

“What’s going on with this tree…?”

“Oh, did you notice it too, Elena? It’s a pretty interesting tree, isn’t it?”

Marina had naturally noticed the tree as well. She spoke to me with a smile.

Being a dark elf, Marina naturally possessed deep knowledge of nature.

“No, is it really okay to just call this ‘interesting’? Clearly, this tree is abnormal. Why are fruits from various seasons growing on a single tree… and why are they all ripe and ready to eat?”

“I guess the only explanation is that we’re in a dungeon. Actually, I found a similar tree in a dungeon I explored before. Though, apparently, it can only survive within that dungeon.”

“Is that so? If we could take this tree outside, I bet we could sell it for a pretty high price.”

Relieu, who had overheard Elena and Marina’s conversation, looked up at the tree with a disappointed expression.

Indeed, if there were a tree that bore fruit from every season all year round, there was no doubt it would fetch a hefty price.

It was certain that major merchants, nobles, and royalty—people like that—would be scrambling to buy it.

But if it only grows in the dungeon, there’s no way to sell it even if I brought it back.

“I think Shumine would be happy if we had a tree like this. …Is there any way we could make it work?”

“Of course it’s impossible. Well, maybe if we could make the environment at Relieu’s house exactly the same as this dungeon, we might be able to manage it… but that would mean building an artificial dungeon, wouldn’t it?”

“No, shouldn’t we just say that’s impossible?”

At Elena’s words, everyone, including Rei, nodded.

After all, the basic premise was that creating a dungeon artificially was, first and foremost, impossible.

If it were possible, it would surely be targeted by various organizations, including the government.

Even before that, the core of the dungeon hasn’t been fully understood yet; it’s still treated as an unknown substance.

“Right? So, if Relieu really wants to use this tree… then she’d have to move here or something, wouldn’t she?”

“That’s impossible,”

Relieu declared immediately.

If it were just Relieu, living here wouldn’t be difficult.

After all, every monster that has appeared so far is an opponent that Relieu could easily handle on her own.

……The only exception is the Yellowbird we fought yesterday. Even for a Relieu who can use the Wind Slash skill, it’s undoubtedly a troublesome… no, a bothersome opponent.

But that’s only if it were just Relieu.

Since Relieu’s wife, Shumine, is nothing more than an ordinary person, living in a dungeon like this is practically impossible.

Especially since goblins and orcs—a woman’s natural enemies—appear in this dungeon.

As a devoted husband, the idea of living here with Shumine is simply out of the question for Relieu.

“I suppose so. In other words, you’ll have to give up on this tree.”

At Marina’s words, Relieu looks slightly disappointed, but… leaving him behind, Rei climbs the tree and begins picking the fruits one after another.

Fruits, including dried fruits, cannot be stored for long periods unless they are processed for preservation.

But for Rei, who possesses an item box, they can be preserved fresh and juicy for years, even decades.

For Rei, this tree bearing fruits from various seasons was the perfect target.

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu!”

Not to be outdone by Rei, Yellow also takes flight from beneath Elena and begins picking fruit once more.

“Hey, Rei. It’s fine that you’re picking fruit, but are you planning to rest here for a while?”

“Yeah. I’ll stay here a bit to check the surroundings. Then, if everything looks okay, I’ll explore this second floor a little more.”

While picking the red-and-blue-speckled fruit, Rei replied to Relieu.

Normally, when you’re on a floor of a dungeon you’ve never been to before, you need to be on high alert.

In such a place, picking fruit from a nearby tree like this is practically suicide.

If there were someone who didn’t know Rei and his group well but was reasonably knowledgeable about dungeons, they would surely scold them for underestimating the dungeon.

But Rei, who has a partner named Set, isn’t worried about that at all.

He understands that Set, whose senses are sharper than his own, would undoubtedly detect any monsters approaching.

Especially in this dungeon, while it’s not certain, there’s a high probability that Rei and his group were the first to enter.

In other words, it wouldn’t be strange to assume that any approaching presence is basically an enemy…

“Grrrrrrrrrrrr!”

Just as Set let out a sudden growl, twenty wind arrows materialized around him and shot skyward.

“Shaaahhhhhhhhhhhhh!”

“Shh!”

“Shaaah!”

In an instant, such cries echoed around them… and two familiar monsters fell to the ground.

As if that were the trigger, nearly ten Yellowbirds flew away from the scene.

“Hmm. The monsters in this dungeon run away if they think they can’t win. Well, I guess that’s not so strange. …Besides, it doesn’t look like they were actually trying to attack us just now.”

Vihera muttered as she watched the Yellow Bird—which had taken a full-body hit from a wind arrow yet was still alive… though now unable to fly and thrashing about.

“A Yellowbird, huh. …I would’ve preferred a different monster than yesterday’s, though.”

As he popped a bite-sized fruit with green spots into his mouth, Rei let out a sigh.

They had already defeated the Yellowbird yesterday and obtained its magic stone.

(Ah, come to think of it, is that why Set used Wind Arrow?)

Suddenly, he remembered that even though Set had learned Level 4 Wind Arrow, he hadn’t tried out the upgraded skill because there were other people around.

He had encountered monsters, including goblins, on the first floor as well, but at that time, he hadn’t had the chance to use Wind Arrow.

In that sense, this Yellow Bird was probably the perfect target for Set.

(At Level 3, there were fifteen Wind Arrows. Now, at Level 4, there are twenty. Is it Level × 5 arrows? ...Well, since the next level is Level 5, the skill’s performance will probably jump up significantly.)

To Set, who was looking at him with round eyes, begging for praise, Rei tossed a red, strawberry-like fruit that was lying nearby.

Rei tried one himself; the fruit was larger than a regular strawberry and possessed a sweetness that rivaled even the hybrid varieties he’d eaten back in Japan.

Though, strictly speaking, it probably isn’t a strawberry since it’s growing on a tree.

Catching the fruit in his beak, Set purrs happily as the sweet-and-sour flavor spreads through his mouth.

Then, he turned his round eyes toward Rei, as if to say, “Give me more!”

……There was no trace of the ferocity he’d shown just moments ago when he’d shot down two Yellowbirds without a second thought.

Seeing Set like this, Rei plucks a nearby fruit and tosses it to him again.

As he clamped the yellow fruit between his beak, a different—yet still thoroughly delicious—sweet-and-sour flavor delighted Set.

(For now, I’ll just pick as many fruits as I can from around here and store them in the Misty Ring… I guess that means I’ll head back to Golz for the day?)

Watching Byune finish off the still-living Yellowbird, Rei found himself thinking just that.
