Day two of Rei's instructor life. Just like yesterday, Rei stood inside the gymnasium alongside Grink.

Before them stood forty students, Rei included.

They were the students of Class S, Year 3.

They had been deeply shocked by the mock battle with Rei the previous day, which had decisively shown them the gap between themselves and her. Still, being young, they had managed to recover somewhat mentally after a night's rest.

(Well, saying they're young doesn't exactly carry much weight coming from me.)

Holding the mock battle spear he'd used yesterday, Rei glanced over the third-year S-class students.

"Now then, we'll be starting the mock battle right away today..."

The moment they heard Rei's words, the students murmured restlessly.

It was only natural. In yesterday's mock battle, they had been shown the difference in strength in the most brutal way possible.

Facing someone like Rei, whose strength could be described as monstrous, and being told to fight again... no one could laugh it off.

But... it wasn't Rei who spoke to the flustered students. It was Glinck.

"Everyone, please calm down. I understand being intimidated by the idea of fighting Rei so soon after yesterday."

The moment Glinck uttered the word "intimidated," several students frowned in displeasure.

They were likely the more competitive ones among the students.

Yet they remained silent, perhaps because they knew voicing such thoughts would make another mock battle with Rei unavoidable.

Facing those students, Glinck continued.

"But think about it. Most of you here will choose one path in the future: knight or adventurer. And whether knight or adventurer, your opponents won't always be weaker than you."

Glink's words were true.

It wasn't unthinkable that knights dispatched to battle might face famed adventurers or opponents clearly superior to themselves.

And for adventurers specifically, this was even more pronounced.

After all, it was far from rare for monsters different from the intended target to appear when accepting an adventurer's request. More often than not, such monsters tended to be powerful ones.

Confirming the students were listening intently, Glinck continued.

"When faced with such an opponent, what would you do? Encountering an absolute powerhouse can freeze your very movements. It can overwhelm you so completely you don't even have time to flee. ...But—"

Glink paused, shifting his gaze toward Rei.

"As you all experienced yesterday, Rei here is an overwhelmingly powerful opponent. The fact that you can fight her and survive is fortunate, don't you think? What would happen if you encountered someone like Rei in a real battle? ...You understood that perfectly well in yesterday's mock battle, didn't you?"

At Glink's words, the students who had been quiet suddenly opened their eyes wide, as if realizing something.

"Yeah, you're right. Surviving a fight against a monster like that? That alone makes getting into this academy worth it."

"Safe? ...Well, in the sense that it doesn't threaten your life, yes. But in my case, I usually end up with quite a few bruises."

"Isn't it a bit harsh to unleash that power without mercy, even on women?"

"Are you stupid? Do you think monsters or whatever they are care if their enemy is male or female?"

"Actually, orcs and such tend to target women first."

"There are others who'd make that kind of judgment too."

"...Ah, come to think of it... that person is a bit..."

"But that person's strength is undeniably real. If I had to fight that person, I'd definitely choose to fight Rei instead."

"No, normally no one would make the call to fight an S-rank adventurer."

The students exchanged words like that.

Glink watched them silently for several minutes, but eventually, judging the discussion settled, he spoke again.

"Well then, it seems we've settled this. I'll ask one last time. Those wishing to spar with Rei, draw your weapons. If you choose to run away instead, I won't force you. What will it be?"

Hearing that question, Rei recognized the spite lurking beneath Glinck's polite words.

These were the third-year S-class cadets.

In other words, they were the elite among the third-year cadets at the academy.

Asking such students to run away in front of everyone just because they're scared of their opponent? That would be difficult.

Their pride as the elite meant such an act was far from easy.

If they did something like that, they might avoid fighting today, but they would undoubtedly be looked down upon by the other students.

Furthermore, if word of this spread to other classes, the S Class itself could become a laughingstock. No one here could stomach that.

Well, if that were the only consequence, some might still have fled. But here was Grink... the academy instructor.

Doing something like that in front of him would undoubtedly damage their grades.

To be in the S Class, recognized as the pinnacle of ability, only to be labeled as someone who runs away when facing a strong opponent... it was unthinkable.

To avoid that fate, there was no choice but to confront Rei here.

Glancing at the students, Rei let a small smile play beneath his hood.

But because he wore a hood, the students couldn't see Rei's expression clearly. To them, he gave the impression of a carnivorous beast licking its lips before its prey.

...Though he himself was smiling, impressed that no one was running away in this situation.

"H-hey. Could it be... we're actually going to get beaten to a pulp?"

"No way... no way. Yeah, probably not. Probably... probably."

"You're totally freaking out, you know."

Listening to the students' whispered exchange, Rei wondered inwardly.

Why was he so feared?

He thought about it for a moment, but still couldn't find an answer. Deciding it was something he could put off for later, he pulled his hood down.

"Alright then, it's time for mock combat again today. ...But doing it one-on-one takes too long, and facing everyone at once would be too chaotic. So, we'll do it in groups of five. Eight groups will make up one class. We'll keep repeating mock combat with me until the end of class."

Under normal circumstances, that would have been a relief.

Even an opponent you couldn't handle alone, five of you together could usually manage.

But when your sparring partner is Rei, it's a different story.

"Hey, what are we supposed to do with just five people?"

"Five guys? That's not nearly enough. We couldn't even beat him when all of us fought together!"

That's right. Even when the entire class of forty threw themselves into battle, they were easily brushed aside.

There was no way five of us could hope to win against such an opponent.

"Divide into groups of five now. ...You have one hundred seconds. After that, we'll pair up any stragglers ourselves."

At Rei's words, the students scrambled to find partners for their teams.

Even within the S-Class bracket, not all students possessed the same level of strength.

It was a fact that within S-Class, they were clearly separated by skill level, and because of those relationships, not everyone paired up smoothly...

"You, you over there, the one over there, the one standing there, and you."

In the end, Rei arbitrarily decided on a group of five.

While weapon compatibility should have been a factor, he showed no sign of considering it at all.

But Glink showed no sign of stopping him, just watching silently.

Of course. Just because you're a knight doesn't mean you can always be fully equipped with weapons, ready to provide rear support with your bow at any moment.

And that goes even more for adventurers.

Given that reality, fighting while covering each other's backs, even in somewhat awkward situations, was crucial.

"Alright, starting from that side, Team One, Team Two, Team Three... and so on. Team One first. The mock battle lasts five minutes. If you can land a hit on me within that time, you pass. The results are..."

Rei paused, shifting his gaze toward Glink.

Catching that look, Glink understood what Rei meant and gave a small nod in return.

"Since I have Grink's permission, anyone who lands even one hit on me will receive the highest score for this mock battle."

The moment Rei spoke, the room erupted in murmurs.

It was only natural. This was a military academy, and most of them would become knights or adventurers in the future.

While intelligence is valued, what's ultimately judged most harshly is strength in actual combat.

In other words, mock battles were one of the most crucial classes for the students attending this academy.

The prospect of earning the highest score in those mock battles meant the students couldn't help but be fired up.

The fact that their opponent was Rei also made them reconsider—this was a chance to fight someone as strong as Rei without risking death.

(I know I just said it myself, but they sure are a bunch of mercenaries, aren't they?)

The cadets who just moments ago seemed utterly unwilling to fight him were suddenly brimming with motivation the instant the carrot of high scores was dangled before them.

The fact that they only needed to land a single blow on Rei, rather than defeat him outright, likely reduced their psychological resistance.

At their sudden change, Rei gave a wry smile and took up his spear.

"Well then, let's begin. Team One... come on!"

As soon as Rei gave the order, the students classified as Team One sprang into action.

Each armed with mock combat weapons: two wielding longswords advanced toward Rei, one spearman followed behind them, ready to exploit any opening, and bringing up the rear... likely the main threat in terms of landing that strike on Rei, were two archers, bows drawn.

This first squad was fortunate to have two archers capable of ranged attacks in their group.

Whether the arrows actually hit Rei was another matter entirely.

Indeed, the arrows fired as a warning were easily knocked down by the spear that Rei swung.

Even with rounded arrowheads, the arrows' speed was nearly identical to that of ordinary arrows.

No, fighting inside a gymnasium meant they were closer to the archers, so in terms of speed, they might even be faster.

"No way!"

Even though he'd witnessed similar scenes countless times during yesterday's battle, seeing it happen again like this still made him gasp in surprise.

That fluster easily showed in the accuracy of their arrows. Seeing their comrade's movements falter, the other archer's actions also slowed.

With their supporting fire weakened, Rei could easily handle the attacks from the men wielding longswords. Dodging their strikes, he delivered a sweeping thrust with his spear. One of the longsword-wielding men took the blow squarely to the torso and collapsed to the ground.

He wasn't unconscious.

He had merely been struck on the armor by the spear shaft, but the impact had penetrated the armor.

It wasn't a fracture, but it was definitely a blow that would leave a bruise.

The other man wielding the longsword also had his blade entangled by the spear, sending his weapon flying effortlessly into the air.

The man glanced upward for an instant, and his torso was kicked, forcefully controlled, sending him flying.

Seeing the two longsword-wielding frontline fighters knocked out, the archers must have snapped back to their senses in panic. They fired multiple arrows in rapid succession.

But every single arrow was easily knocked down by the spear.

Seizing the opening, the spear-wielding man thrust forward at full speed. Yet Rei's agile footwork allowed him to dodge effortlessly. Worse still, Rei seized the thrusting spear's shaft with one hand and lifted it high in a brutal display of strength.

Rei understood this was a mock battle, so instead of slamming the spear-wielding student to the floor as he might have done normally, he slowed his descent.

Of course, that student was already considered incapacitated at that point.

With the three front-line fighters taken down like that, the archer had no chance of doing anything. He took a direct hit and was eliminated.

Having fought a battle that could be called one-sided, Rei turned his gaze toward the archer... specifically the one whose movements had faltered first, and spoke.

"Don't let the support from the rear unsettle you so easily. For those fighting on the front lines, the accuracy of your support literally means life or death. Strengthen your resolve. And you too, don't let yourself be dragged down just because your comrades falter.Both of you with the longswords are stepping in too timidly. You with the spear, thrusting is fine, but pulling back is a step too slow. That's exactly why I grabbed your hilt. Also, the moment you feel your body being lifted, let go immediately."

He pointed out flaws starting with the most obvious ones... Leaving the students' expressions downcast after hearing this, Rei spoke again.

"Next."

At that brief command, the next five students stepped forward toward Rei, wondering if they too would be torn apart.
