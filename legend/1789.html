Georgima immediately began experimenting with tonkotsu ramen, but once that happened, there was nothing left for Rei to do.

All Rei could do was pass on theoretical knowledge he had no practical experience with, so once he’d shared that knowledge, he could simply leave the rest to the professional chefs.

For Rei, who didn’t even consider cooking a hobby to begin with, the option of using that knowledge to cook himself was completely out of the question.

He should just leave what he could do to the professionals.

Having made that decision, and having fulfilled his promise to teach the unknown dish for the time being, he was now heading toward the stable prepared for Set, guided by a maid.

“Oh?”

As she walked down the hallway, just as she was about to pass a group coming from the opposite direction, one of them suddenly muttered under her breath.

Rei turned her gaze toward the sound and saw a woman standing at the center of the group.

Judging by her attire, she was likely a noble, and she was watching Rei with her hand pressed to her mouth.

But her gaze quickly shifted to the maid guiding Rei.

“Miranda, who is that?”

“Lady Teres, this is Lady Rei. She is a guest of Lady Elena.”

“……I see. I thought so, but you’re the one…”

The maid, called Miranda, introduced Rei to the woman named Teres with a deep bow.

Naturally, the woman named Teres was a stranger to Rei.

Given that she had only just arrived in the Duchy of Kerebel yesterday, it would be a problem in itself if she were someone she recognized.

Still, given that she appeared to be a noble, it wasn’t hard to surmise that she was someone with business with Elena… or perhaps with the Duke of Kerebel, Liberte, or his wife, Arcadia… or someone who had been summoned by them.

Given that, Rei couldn’t very well ignore the noblewoman who was looking at him.

“I’m Rei, an adventurer from the Gilm Guild.”

“……Oh.”

Perhaps she hadn’t expected such a blunt introduction, for Teres seemed slightly surprised.

But behind that surprise lay a sense of interest… indeed, a positive sentiment.

If anything, it was the people around Teres who were casting disapproving glances at Rei’s rude attitude.

From the perspective of Teres’s attendants, Rei—who was clearly not a noble and had openly declared himself an adventurer—was behaving disrespectfully toward their mistress, and they likely found it distasteful.

Yet they refrained from voicing their dissatisfaction with Rei because they could sense that Teres harbored favorable feelings toward him.

If the person they served were displeased, that would be one thing, but if they were to act in a way that seemed to criticize someone their master held in high regard, they risked being reprimanded later.

Teres isn’t that strict a person, but even so, few would want to go out of their way to lower their own standing with her.

“I see. So you really are Rei. I’d heard the rumors, but I didn’t expect to meet you this soon. I’m Teres Myls. Nice to meet you.”

“Rumors…? I only arrived in Anesis yesterday.”

“Oh, you mustn’t underestimate how fast rumors spread among women. Besides, since you’re the man Lady Elena brought here, I’d say anyone with even a passing interest in gossip probably already knows all about Rei.”

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to agree.

Rumors don’t exactly travel a thousand miles… but still, that sort of rumor does tend to spread pretty quickly.

“Ah… I see. So, why is Teres here?”

“Since Lady Elena is back, it’s only natural to come and greet her, isn’t it? I think I got here pretty early, but I expect quite a few more people will be arriving later.”

Given Elena’s reputation as the symbol of the Noble Faction, that was only natural.

Normally, Rei should have been able to figure out the situation, but perhaps spending too much time with Elena had dulled his sense of it.

He nodded in agreement at Teres’ words.

“Still, I thought there was a chance I might see her, but I never imagined I’d actually get to meet that Crimson One. …Hey, if you’d like, why don’t you go see Lady Elena too? I’d like to ask her about all sorts of things.”

From Teres’ perspective, she probably wanted to establish a connection with Rei while she had the chance.

She asked Rei that, but... Rei shook his head.

“Sorry, but I was actually planning to go see Set in a bit. Oh, but I’d have time after that’s done… What do you say?”

Rei had come to Anesis with Elena, but that didn’t mean he had anything specific he had to do.

Teaching Georgima how to make ramen was something he had to do, but he’d already finished that.

Given that, Rei and Set were basically free.

...Though, knowing that, plenty of people would likely try to approach Rei.

As the man Elena brought along—though this isn’t about Teres—Rei was attracting attention from all sorts of people.

People harbored all sorts of emotions—curiosity, jealousy, goodwill, and ill will—so in such cases, it was up to Rei to decide who he would meet with.

“Oh, really? That’s too bad. But… well. I’d like to hear about Rei and Lady Elena too, so could you come see me once you’re done with your business? Actually, Rei’s familiar, the griffin… Set, was it? I’d have liked to meet that griffin as well.”

Unusual, or perhaps quite bold.

That was the thought that crossed Rei’s mind as he faced Teres.

It would be one thing if she’d already known Set’s personality, but she’s saying she wants to meet him without knowing anything about him.

(Ah, but if she was able to find out so quickly that I’d come to Anesis, wouldn’t it make sense for her to know about Set’s personality? It seems word has been spreading quite a bit.)

A Rank S-class monster like the griffin is friendly and has become something of a mascot character in the frontier town of Gilm.

With information like that, it wouldn’t be strange at all if word had spread.

It’s a rumor with that much impact.

Above all, Rei and Set have been to various places besides Gilm.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange at all for word of Set’s friendly nature to have spread from various places.

Above all, for anyone who has witnessed Set’s charm, it’s only natural to want to tell others about it.

Rei isn’t exactly stopping people from talking about Set either.

“Ah… well, if you’re not afraid of Set or the griffin, I’ll be happy to show you around later.”

At the very least, there was no doubt that the person standing before him, Teres, was friendly toward him.

Given that, Rei had no intention of treating such a person coldly.

Of course, he couldn’t rule out the possibility that this friendliness was merely a facade and that he might actually be plotting something.

However, he decided there was no point in being suspicious when nothing had actually happened yet, so he told her it would be fine to meet Set.

“Hehe, thanks. Well then, I’m going to go say hello to Lady Elena. Like I said before, Rei, come by if you have time.”

Saying that, Teres walked away.

Teres’s accompanying maids were casting somewhat reproachful glances at Rei… but the person on the receiving end of those glances didn’t seem particularly bothered and called out to the maid who was guiding him.

“Alright, I’ll leave the rest to you.”

The maid nodded at Rei’s words, and he began walking down the hallway once more.

Along the way, he passed several maids and what appeared to be butlers, as well as those who likely served Duke Kerebel, but none of them spoke to Rei the way Teres had.

That said, there were some who shot him hostile glances, making it clear that not everyone in the mansion welcomed Rei’s presence.

And then, upon leaving the mansion...

“Snow, huh?”

Rei murmurs, watching the snow falling steadily.

Snow itself wasn’t particularly rare.

Even back in Gilm, it had already snowed several times this year.

Still, Rei found herself somehow captivated by the falling snow.

“Lord Rei?”

“Hm? Oh, sorry. I’m coming.”

The voice of the maid, who had asked in surprise when she saw Rei stop, snapped her back to reality. Rei gave a brief reply and continued on her way.

As he walked on, the silhouette of a rather massive stable eventually came into view.

It was the kind of stable where even Set could run around at a decent speed without any problems...

(Did they move all the horses and such from this stable to another location? …I’ll have to thank them later.)

Moving the horses themselves probably wasn’t that much of a hassle.

But knowing they went to all that trouble just for Set’s sake, there was no way he could not express his gratitude.

“I’m going inside. What about you?”

The maid paused for a moment after hearing Rei’s words before speaking.

“Um, if it’s okay, could I take a look too? I’ve heard Set is quite adorable.”

Hearing the maid’s words, Rei wondered for a moment, “Who told her that?” but concluded it was probably either Elena or Ara (the female one).

“Sure, why not. I’m sure Set wouldn’t mind having a few more people who dote on him.”

“Thank you. I just love cute things. It’s been a shame I haven’t been able to see Yellow-chan lately.”

The maid, sounding a little disappointed, opened the stable door.

That said, the door wasn’t locked, so anyone could open it if they wanted to.

In fact, it would have been easy even for Set or Yellow to open it from the inside.

……For someone who didn’t know Set or Yellow, it would have been a scene that could easily have prompted an outburst of anger that the stable wasn’t fulfilling its purpose, but Rei and the maid didn’t seem particularly bothered as they stepped inside.

“Grrrrrrr!”

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu!”

The two horses inside the stable—Set and Yellow—had obviously noticed Rei and the maid approaching.

The moment they saw them enter, they bolted.

“Huh!?”

With Set—over three meters tall—charging toward her, the maid naturally gasped in terror for a split second.

But Rei stepped forward to shield the maid and skillfully caught Set, who was charging straight at him.

“Ugh, seriously… You were playing with Yellow yesterday, weren’t you? If so, you shouldn’t have been that lonely, right?”

“Grrrr, grr, grrrr…”

Set let out a growl, as if to argue with Rei’s words.

Rei hugged Set and stroked his head.

At the sight of Rei petting Set, the maid was initially unsure how to react, but eventually she must have realized that Set had no intention of attacking her. She caught Yellow, who had flown toward her, and petted him.

Just as the maid had told Rei, she doted on Yellow, and Yellow must have remembered that.

“Hehe, looks like Yellow’s doing well too.”

“Kyu!”

At the maid’s words, Yellow let out a happy chirp.

Seeing Yellow like that, the maid smiled happily and continued to pet him.

The two cats and the two people stayed like that for a few minutes.

Eventually, they looked at each other without a word.

“Set-chan… Rei really seems to have taken a liking to you, doesn’t he?”

“He’s my partner, after all. It seems Yellow’s quite taken with you, too.”

“Yellow-chan is loved by everyone in the Duke of Kerebel’s household.”

As she said this, the maid stroked Yellow, her face radiating the utmost happiness.

Watching the maid, Rei found himself thinking of Milein for some reason.

(Well, I guess there are plenty of people besides Milein who love cute things. It’s not like only Milein and her crew are the ones with an intense love for cute things. It wouldn’t be surprising if there were other versions of Milein out there, I suppose.)

Thinking that, Rei spoke to Set.

“Sorry for leaving you alone. But since Yellow was with you, you weren’t that lonely, were you?”

At Rei’s words, Set lets out a slight growl of discontent.

It was certainly true that he hadn’t felt lonely thanks to Yellow, but Set’s honest feeling was that he still wanted Rei to be there.

Perhaps sensing Set’s feelings, Rei ruffled his head and pulled some jerky out of his Misty Ring.

The jerky made from Gamelion meat is a preserved food, but it’s a high-quality product—no, a delicacy—that offers a rich flavor.

“Growl!?”

At the sight of that dried meat, Set’s mood improved in an instant… and not only that, but Yellow’s keen sense of smell picked up on the delicious aroma and drew her over to where Rei and Set were standing, slightly dampening the maid’s spirits.
