As he walked down that path, the smell of rust drifted toward him.

Rei knew exactly what it was, and he knew it all too well.

It was only natural, having been exposed to it countless times before... so many times, in fact, that it felt as if the source of that smell had become ingrained in his very being.

Thanks in part to the man with the spear running off earlier, and Zarust following after him, they could move straight ahead to that spot without needing to dodge people.

The fact it was daytime and relatively quiet probably helped too.

Rei continued running and soon arrived at the gathering place.

"Huh!? Who's there!"

One of the people gathered there noticed Rei approaching and shouted instinctively.

He held a dagger, but his trembling hands and lack of proper stance made it clear he was untrained.

Still, seeing the hooded figure in robes before them, they tried to protect their superior from this suspicious intruder... until they finally noticed Set standing behind Rei.

And once he saw Set, it was only natural he'd realize the robed man with him was Rei.

"Re... Rei!?"

"Yeah. ...Where's Augusto?"

"...Over here."

The man who said this told the gathered crowd to make way.

Rei stepped into the cleared space and saw Augusto lying there, blood pouring from his abdomen.

It was clear he'd been stabbed through with that spear earlier, and a pool of blood had formed on the ground.

(The blood on that spearhead... It really was Augusto's after all.)

Thinking this, he turned his gaze toward the figure tending to Augusto's wound.

There stood the ape-like beastman he had seen before.

"How is he?"

"Ah, Rei-san..."

After muttering a single word, the monkey beastman gently shook his head.

"His intestines..."

If the man who attacked Augusto had wielded a longsword, perhaps it might have been different.

But the weapon the man used was a spear, and it pierced Augusto's abdomen.

With luck, it might have slipped between his organs... but unfortunately, such luck was not to be expected this time.

"If this continues..."

The ape beastman muttered sadly, and those gathered around him also wore expressions of sorrow.

"Ha... ha... I'm... I'm sorry, Rei. I'm showing you such a pitiful sight..."

"...The one who tried to kill you has been captured. You can hear the details later."

"Heh heh, un, unfortunately, I don't have time for that..."

"Shut up."

Rei cut off Augusto's words.

Several people nearby shot him hostile glances, annoyed that he'd interrupted their final farewell, but Rei paid them no mind and spoke up.

"Where's the potion?"

At those words, one of the men nearby shook his head.

"Gosh's alchemy skills aren't that high. There's no potion sold that could heal injuries this severe. If anyone has one..."

"The Elema Trading Company, huh?"

The man nodded at the suggestion.

"And there's no way Daldora would give up a potion to heal Augusto's wounds. No, wait, maybe..."

The man stopped mid-sentence, likely wanting to say that the man who attacked Augusto was an agent of the Elema Trading Company.

"Wait, calm down."

Rei stopped the man as he was about to utter the decisive words, then turned his gaze back to Augusto... and slowly drew a single potion from his Misty Ring.

The bottle holding the potion clearly indicated it was of such high quality that it couldn't be purchased in Gosh.

The bottle itself, meant merely to hold the potion, was adorned with intricate decorations and carvings.

The potion itself, which almost seemed like a work of art, was something Rei had received from Azimov.

Of course, it was a potion that came with a hefty price tag, but as a magic item useful in combat, it had become part of Rei's collection. He had kept it in Misty Ring for emergencies...

(Well, I hardly ever get injured anyway.)

Rei's combat prowess was exceptionally high, and he prioritized evasion over enduring enemy attacks.

Even when attacked, he could block with his Death Scythe or rely on his Dragon Robe armor.

Of course, it wasn't enough to dodge every single attack, as evidenced by the damage Rei had taken before.

That potion was meant for such a moment, and now seemed precisely the time to use it. He opened the potion's cap...

(Huh? What if it's internal injuries? Pour it directly on the organs? No, in this case...)

Rei hesitated for a moment, unsure how to use it. He concluded the potion would be most effective if administered orally, and brought it to the mouth of Augusto, who lay in a pool of blood.

Augusto seemed to realize what Rei was about to do. He tried to say it was pointless and to stop... but ignoring Augusto, Rei forcefully shoved the potion bottle into his mouth and poured the liquid inside.

As expected, the potion entered Augusto's mouth. Choking, he swallowed the abnormally foul-tasting liquid—one that seemed capable of destroying his very sense of taste.

"Guh-huh, guh-huh!"

Augusto started to cough, but Rei forcibly covered his mouth with her hand, preventing him from spitting out the potion.

(Did it get stuck somewhere weird and make him choke, or is it really the taste?)

The potion was disgusting... so disgusting it would impair his taste buds for days. That was an unalterable truth, just like how goblin meat was disgusting.

...Though, regarding goblin meat, Rei was trying to do something about it.

(I really need to get him to make a potion using World Tree Dew.)

If it were just me, I wouldn't need potions that much, but considering emergencies, it's probably better to have one or two powerful potions on hand.

For potions that heal moderate injuries, even in Gilm they're somewhat expensive but not impossible to obtain.

But a critical injury like this one, where internal organs were pierced, wasn't just a "certain degree of wound." It wasn't something that could be easily healed with a potion sold on the street.

"Hey, hey! Look at that! Check out Augusto's wound!"

One of the people watching Rei's exchange suddenly looked at Augusto and shouted.

What they saw was the spear wound in his abdomen automatically healing before their eyes.

While the healing itself was good, the sheer speed of the regeneration was, to an outsider, slightly unsettling.

Still, the reason no one found it unpleasant was likely because they understood the wound belonged to Augusto, and the reason for the recovery was the potion... a potion that was clearly high quality.

"Guhhhhhhhhhhhhh!"

Augusto, the one recovering, was groaning as if enduring something.

Those around him all silently fixed their gaze on Augusto... and then, after several minutes passed, the groaning that had risen from his mouth suddenly ceased.

『...』

As those around him held their breath and watched... Augusto suddenly raised his upper body from where he had been lying down.

Not with a feeble gesture, but with a strength that seemed to defy any injury.

"Huh?"

That must be why. That's why everyone uttered that sound in unison.

Just minutes ago, he'd been on the brink of life and death... no, he'd been thought to be certain to die. Yet here he was, rising so easily.

"Um... what?"

And above all, the most confused person was probably Augusto himself.

He sat up, looked around, and touched his own stomach where the spear had just pierced him.

There was no trace of a wound there anymore.

"I... think I was dying..."

He couldn't believe what had happened to him. He looked down again at the ground where he was sitting.

There lay a pool of blood that should have flowed from his own body.

The existence of that pool meant his near-death experience was definitely not a dream.

"It's a potion."

Rei called out to the confused Augusto.

In his hand was the bottle that had contained the potion.

Seeing it, he must have remembered being forced to drink the potion while still near death, because he reflexively covered his tongue... no, his mouth.

His current sense of taste... Even the word "disgusting" for the potion probably brought back memories of its lukewarm flavor.

"Th-th-thank you..."

Even understanding his taste buds were gone, he managed to force out words of thanks to Rei. Perhaps it was because he knew the potion used on him was incredibly valuable.

Even if it were Daldora of the Elema Trading Company, it would be beyond reach for Augusto, whose shop was nowhere near Elema's scale.

In truth, the potion Rei had received from Azimov—or more accurately, as payment for the materials from Set—possessed a value that would be impossible to purchase with mere gold or silver coins.

"Don't worry about it. If the landlord were to die, it would cause all sorts of trouble. Besides, I wouldn't be able to eat delicious food anymore."

It felt like the emphasis was more on the latter point, but even so, Augusto bowed his head to Rei once more.

The others around them watched Augusto, but suddenly one of them murmured.

"Still, why would anyone... target only you, Augusto-san, like this..."

"Yeah, who could possibly hold a grudge against Augusto?"

"There's only one person who would, right?"

Augusto's reputation as an honest merchant meant those who resented his character were severely limited.

And the foremost person harboring resentment toward Augusto was...

「Daldora」

Who had spoken that name?

Rei didn't know either, but the moment Daldora's name was uttered, the gazes of those around them grew harsh.

"Exactly. Who else could have put Augusto through this? It's obvious, isn't it?"

"Exactly. There's no doubt Daldora is the one who hates Augusto the most right now."

"Damn it! Just because Elema Trading Company is big, they think they can do whatever they want!"

"Unforgivable... absolutely unforgivable!"

Those around Augusto each spoke bitterly, their words filled with resentment.

Daldora had sabotaged them countless times before.

But even so, the recent sabotage goes beyond mere obstruction—it's come to threaten Augusto's very life.

First, they lent sand ships to sand pirates to target Augusto's caravan. When that failed, they came directly to kill Augusto himself.

Because they admired Augusto so deeply, those around him could not forgive Daldora.

If this continued, they might run wild and even storm the Elema Trading Company.

Filled with that fear, Augusto hurriedly spoke up.

"Wait! This incident... it might not be the work of the Elema Trading Company... or Daldora!"

"But still, Mr. Augusto..."

One of the men nearby tried to retort, clearly displeased with Augusto's words.

But Augusto spoke up, as if to calm the man.

"Calm down. ...First of all, it's not even clear if Daldora really did this. We don't have any evidence or anything."

"...Wouldn't this man serve as evidence?"

With that voice, a man's body was thrown to the ground.

"Zalust! This guy!"

One of the men nearby shouted at Zarust, who had just appeared.

Of course. The man lying unconscious was the one who had just attacked Augusto.

"Well, it wasn't me who caught him, it was Rei."

Zalust shrugged slightly and said this while looking at Rei.

The surrounding gazes turned to Rei, but he only shrugged his shoulders slightly in response.

"Anyway, to find out why that man attacked me, I can't hand him over to the guards..."

It was Augusto who said this.

Considering that the sand pirates who attacked them had all been killed by the guards on the very day they were handed over, he couldn't afford to be careless.

But that didn't mean he could extract information from a man who was unconscious.

What should I do... As Augusto pondered this, he noticed someone approaching, making a commotion.

Could this be another attack targeting him, just like the man before him? He thought that for a moment, but it turned out to be different.

"Hurry! Daldora has been attacked! Send reinforcements immediately!"

"Damn it! Who would do such a thing!?"

"Who cares!? Just hurry!"

Shouting like that, several men ran past Augusto and his companions.

"...Daldora was attacked?"

A stunned voice escaped from Augusto's lips.
