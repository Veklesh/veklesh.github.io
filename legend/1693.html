"Rei, look. Isn't this the one?"

Saying that, Kenny showed Rei the book she was holding, wiggling her ears happily.

The illustration inside resembled the black sea bream monster Rei had defeated at the beach, and he was certain it was the same monster.

"Ah, I think this is probably it. ...I feel bad asking you to help me on your day off."

“It’s fine. I didn’t have anything else to do today anyway.”

In fact, Kenny, who had the day off from the guild, was grateful for her good fortune.

Since she had nothing to do, she had gone out for a bit of shopping and happened to run into Rei on her way to the library.

After finishing her meeting with Daskar yesterday, Rei had gone to the guild to gather information on the monsters they’d caught this time. For most of them, she’d managed to find out which body parts served as proof of defeat, whether the materials could be sold, and whether they were suitable for eating.

Perhaps because they were in the frontier, even a guild like Gilm—which had no sea nearby—had proper information on sea monsters in its archives.

But there was just one exception… there was absolutely no information whatsoever on the Black Sea Bream monster.

Was it a new species, or was it simply too rare for any information to exist?

Rei didn’t know the reason either, but in any case, if that was the case, he figured he should head to the library where there would be more information… so the day after he returned to Gilm, after delivering the wood he’d cut down in Treant Forest, he headed straight for the library.

On the way there, he ran into Kenny, who was out shopping… and that’s how they ended up coming to the library together.

“Come to think of it, since it’s in the book, it wasn’t a new species of monster after all.”

Kenny said this, but there was no particular hint of disappointment in her tone.

That was only natural. Monsters are creatures born in this world through various factors—such as animals being transformed by magic, or magic itself taking physical form…

For that very reason, finding a new species of monster doesn’t really mean anything.

...That said, since it’s a new species, if you consider the possibility of it yielding unknown materials, it might not bring honor, but it could certainly bring profit.

In any case, considering that the black sea bream monster was already listed in the book, it was certainly a rare monster, but it was definitely not a new species.

“Well, I don’t really care about that sort of thing anyway.”

If you find a new species of monster, you can even name it yourself.

That said, in this world where literacy rates aren’t that high, many people find it a hassle to name a new monster even if they do find one.

(Come to think of it, Japan… no, Earth. I remember seeing on TV that many insects in places like the Amazon on Earth are supposedly new species or something.)

Thinking about that, Rei immediately scanned the page depicting the black sea bream monster…

“Guh…”

Soon, a strange sound escaped his mouth.

Even Kenny, who was close to Rei, was curious about the expression he’d never seen before and asked.

“What’s wrong?”

“Well, this…”

Saying that, Rei pointed to a sentence written in the book.

It described a black sea bream monster—officially known as a Baeros—stating that its internal organs were extremely valuable as alchemical materials.

In fact, depending on its condition, it can be worth several platinum coins, so Kenny’s eyes widened in surprise.

That said, it’s not all that rare for monster materials to be extremely valuable.

Such as materials with rare effects or those from high-rank monsters.

...In that sense, Set’s shed fur and feathers are naturally traded at high prices, quietly lining Rei’s pockets.

In any case, while Baeros’s internal organs are indeed a rare and valuable material… the Baeros stored in Rei’s Misty Ring has almost no internal organs left.

That’s right—while dragging the defeated Baeros back to shore, most of its innards scattered into the sea… and became food for the creatures lurking beneath the waves.

(If it’s such an expensive material, I guess the creatures in the sea were naturally overjoyed.)

Just because something is expensive doesn’t mean it tastes good.

But by telling himself that, Rei was trying to somehow mask the bitter feelings inside him.

“The innards… are they gone?”

Judging by the text Rei showed him and his demeanor, Kenny must have guessed the general situation.

In response to Kenny’s words, Rei nodded silently.

“Um… l-look. But it says the meat is delicious, doesn’t it? So, don’t you think that alone would have been enough for you, Rei? Right?”

“Well… I guess I can’t deny that.”

“See? So, even if the organs are out of the question, you were able to get the body itself safely, so you have to think that’s good enough.”

Hearing that, Rei managed to convince himself.

True, it was what a pity to have thrown away such expensive ingredients into the sea, but as Kenny said, they had managed to secure most of the body, which was said to be delicious.

So, it was better than getting nothing at all.

Of course, they’d successfully obtained the magic stone, which was their primary objective, and on top of that, Set had upgraded his Ice Arrow to Level 3, so she really had no reason to complain at that point.

“Besides, look—even though we couldn’t get the internal organs, I heard the scales and fangs are still pretty useful. So, isn’t that better than getting nothing at all?”

“True. …I’d like to gut it for now, but… I wonder what to do.”

“Oh, then why don’t you help me gut it? I’m off today.”

Kenny made the suggestion with the look of someone who’d just had a brilliant idea, but Rei couldn’t just nod along to it.

“I’m free right now, but in a few hours I need to go to Treant Forest to haul back some felled trees.”

For Rei, who possessed the Misty Ring, it was an easy… very easy task, but that was only because he was Rei.

For an ordinary person, hauling felled trees would be quite a heavy lift.

That’s exactly why he couldn’t just casually skip out on the job Rei had entrusted to him.

However, upon hearing Rei’s words, Kenny smiled and nodded, saying it wasn’t a problem.

"The butchering itself doesn’t seem to involve any internal organs, so it shouldn’t take that long, right? Besides, if Rei puts his mind to it, he’ll get the job done without taking too much time, won’t he?"

What Kenny said was an undeniable fact.

In fact, with Set’s speed, they’d reach Treant Forest in just a few minutes, and the felled trees could be stored in the Misty Ring the moment they were touched.

Given that, the part that would actually take the most time was transporting the felled trees from Gilm to the storage area.

Since the barrier isn’t active right now, Set could even fly through the air and land directly in the middle of town.

But if he did something like that, Rei would undoubtedly be reprimanded later.

If they were truly in a hurry, that might be one thing, but Rei had absolutely no intention of doing anything like that in a situation like this.

“Hmm… but still,”

Rei looked a little troubled at Kenny’s words.

Seeing Rei like that, Kenny smiled and spoke to him.

“I’d like to try butchering a monster with you, Rei. …Is that okay?”

Kenny, tilting her head as she asked, was undeniably charming.

In fact, several men in the library were shooting jealous glances at Rei.

As if pressured by those glances, Rei eventually nods.

“Alright. Then, I’ll have you help me.”

“Yay. Thanks.”

Kenny smiled happily.

Up until now, I’d eaten meals and gone shopping with Rei, but we hadn’t gotten as close as I’d hoped.

In that case, I’ll try taking things a step further.

That was his reasoning, and so he put it into action.

……The fact that he went from deciding to acting on it in an instant is just typical of Kenny.

In any case, it seems to have paid off this time.

“So, where are we going to dismantle it?”

“Hmm… the Full Belly Inn warehouse… ah, no, that won’t work.”

Right now, there are more people in Gilm than usual.

With that many people around, aside from the inn’s issues, the biggest priority was, of course, food.

And Rei has heard that Full Belly Inn—which offers cheap, delicious, and generous portions—has naturally become incredibly popular and is bustling every single day.

With the Full Belly Inn getting busier, naturally they need massive amounts of ingredients. The butchering warehouse run by Hasta—who handles the ingredients for the Full Belly Inn managed by Dishot—has hired extra staff and is working around the clock.

If he were to take a monster like Baelos to a place like that and try to butcher it, he’d just be in the way.

Given that, there’s only one place Rei can think of for the dissection.

“I’m thinking of going to the river that flows through the forest not too far from Gilm. Is that okay?”

“Huh? Well… I suppose so. …Considering you’ll be there with me, I don’t think that’ll be a problem.”

“In that case, Set… no, Set needs to keep watch over the skies, so that won’t work.”

Now that the barrier is down, the skies are being guarded by Set.

That said, it’s been quite some time since Set actually started guarding the skies, and during that time, they’ve only been attacked by monsters a few times.

Even while Rei was at the sea, there were no attacks from flying monsters.

Of course, with this many adventurers gathered here, there’s no doubt we’d manage to handle most situations.

“Alright, then I’ll have the guild send a carriage.”

“Is that okay?”

“Yes. …Actually, to be honest, we have quite a few extra carriages right now.”

Rei, puzzled by the mention of spare carriages, tilted his head in confusion.

Perhaps finding Rei’s reaction amusing, Kenny spoke while stifling a laugh.

“Quite a few people have broken down the carriages they used to drive here to Gilm and are trying to sell them. Well, even if they have a carriage, there’s a fee to store it at an inn, and repairs would cost a fair bit too.”

“But wouldn’t they need those to do business?”

“That might be true for merchants, but in this case, there are people whose sole purpose is simply to get to Gilm.”

“……Is that how it works?”

I wasn’t entirely convinced, but since Kenny had no reason to lie to me, it was easy to assume that was probably true.

“Right. Well, I’ll go get everything ready right away… Let’s meet at the main gate. Is there anything you need to bring, Rei?”

“No, in my case, I usually have everything in this.”

Saying that, he showed the Misty Ring on his right hand.

Most of the things he needed for daily life were stored inside the Misty Ring.

That’s why there weren’t any personal belongings in his room at the inn either.

In fact, even the items needed to handle requests... and, of course, knives for butchering are inside as well.

(Ah, but Baelos had scales. …I heard those scales could be sold as a decent material, so the tools needed to peel them off…)

Even as he thought about it, Rei didn’t know how to actually scale a monster.

Well, to be precise, he knows that with ordinary fish, you can scrape off the scales by rubbing them with the back of a kitchen knife, but he couldn’t imagine that such a method would work for a monster’s scales.

“Monster scales… especially in this case, the scales of a fish-type monster—how do you go about removing them?”

“Huh? Hmm, well…”

Hearing Rei’s words, Kenny also looks troubled.

If this were a guild near the ocean, there would likely be plenty of information on the subject, and it wouldn’t be strange for a receptionist working at the guild to have some knowledge about it.

But around here, when people say “fish,” they mean river fish.

Since most fish coming in from the sea are already processed for eating and salted, Kenny had no idea how to skin a fish-type monster.

……If this had been a more experienced receptionist or guild staff member, they might have known.

There’s no doubt Kenny is a capable receptionist, but there’s still a gap in experience that can’t be ignored.

“I’m not quite sure, so I’ll ask one of the senior members at the guild. That might make me a little late… is that okay?”

“Yeah, it’d be a problem if it takes until evening, but a little delay is fine.”

“Hehe, I wouldn’t dream of making you wait that long.”

Saying that, Kenny beamed with a huge smile at the prospect of her date with Rei—though in reality, it was just butchering a monster.
