For better or worse, Set was extremely conspicuous.

A man of modest rank and humble bearing.

Naturally, the adventurers' eyes turned to Set, whose presence far outshone Rugluno's.

"All right, everyone. First, you'll be escorting the woodcutters. The key thing to remember is not to get too close to them. If you get too close, you'll get in the way while they're cutting down trees... and worse, you could get crushed by a felled tree."

Crushed by a tree.

While an extraordinary being like Rei might be an exception, for ordinary people, being crushed by a tree like those growing in Treant Forest would mean serious injury at best. At worst, it could mean death.

For the adventurers, it was a matter of life and death, so it was only natural they couldn't afford to keep staring at the novelty of Set.

Ruguruno smiled at the adventurers' reactions as he continued his explanation.

What kind of monsters appear in the Treant Forest?

The common behaviors of those monsters.

Whether monsters coordinate with each other.

In this way, he detailed the precautions for combat within Treant Forest.

Rei listened to the explanation while keeping an eye on Ajas.

If he only watched Ajas, the other might catch on to something.

That must have been their reasoning.

Being here didn't necessarily mean they were new to adventuring.

Even with such people... or rather, precisely because of that, if the other party acts submissive, they assume they're beneath them.

At the very least, compared to those who judge Ruguruno as inferior, his combat skills are clearly superior.

Those wielding axes immediately set to work felling trees, likely hoping to earn a little money.

"Come to think of it, it's been quite a while since I've gone directly into the forest itself. The forest's power seems weaker than before... Is this also due to the Giganto Turtle being gone? ...I just hope it doesn't have a negative effect on the trees."

Walking while observing the forest's state, he soon spotted his target.

They weren't cutting down trees like some of the adventurers who had rushed ahead, nor were they guarding a lumberjack.

All they were doing was checking the surroundings.

"Hey, what are you doing?"

"Huh? ...Ah, Rei."

"Just thought I'd go check on the others. ...So, what are you doing, Ajas? Not exactly cutting down trees like the others... but is guarding the woodcutter okay?"

"Huh, you're being pretty cautious."

Even after hearing Rei's words, Ajas answered without changing his expression.

Specifically, that if one were to kill a merchant family traveling as peddlers, one would need to be cautious enough to avoid being seen by anyone.

Of course, Rei understood that this was his own preconceived notion.

"I see. If only everyone were as careful as you."

"...Hearing you say that makes me happy, too."

Rei... the high-ranking adventurer with the nickname... must have been pleased to be praised.

Ajas smiled and turned his gaze toward Rei.

"It's good for adventurers to be cautious, but I'd rather they not be too cautious about thieves or the like."

"Generally speaking, yes. But they do exist when they exist. In fact, there was a bandit incident just a little while ago."

To be precise, they weren't thieves, but agents of the Lerdact... but he didn't mention that part. Rei continued.

Like you.
