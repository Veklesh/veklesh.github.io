Vihera stared at the kobold leader's corpse lying on the ground and muttered, sounding slightly exasperated.

"For someone who made such a big show of it, that was surprisingly anticlimactic. ...Well, I suppose it's only natural, considering he fought Rei."

"No matter what you say, he was still the Kobold Leader. Besides, what was truly abnormal was that the Kobold Leader barely thought at all. He was savage... well, that might sound good, but he basically sent his subordinates charging without any thought whatsoever."

"You might be right. A normal kobold would at least gauge their opponent's strength. It's hard to believe they attacked when someone like Set was present."

"Grr?"

He must have heard his name. Set, who had been lightly nudging the kobold leader's corpse with his front paws, tilted his head slightly, as if to say, "What?" and turned his gaze toward Vihera.

"Nothing. Just... that kobold leader seems a bit dangerous. You'd be wise not to mess with him too much."

At that, Set gently moved away from the kobold leader.

He didn't seem particularly disappointed, probably because he hadn't been that attached to the kobold leader to begin with.

It was more that... well, it was just a bit of idle fun, a way to pass the time. That was Set's true feeling.

"So, Rei. What do we do with this?"

"What do you mean, what about it? ...Well, unlike with the goblins, there's no real rush here. I suppose we could recover the kobold's magic stone if we want."

"No, not that. The kobold leader. Are we reporting this to the guild?"

"...Probably should. Besides, even if I don't report it, that trio that ran off earlier will. They seemed to recognize me clearly, so my name will definitely come up at the guild."

If they didn't know Rei, that might be one thing, but any adventurer in Gilm who didn't know Rei would be called a fake without question.

There was no way that trio would explain this incident to the guild without mentioning Rei's name.

"Oh? Sounds like more trouble. If it were a group of Cyclops like last time, I'd actually be motivated."

The look on Vihera's face as she said this was likely recalling a previous battle against a formidable foe. It was a look that could only be described as lewd.

Normally, few would desire to fight powerful monsters like cyclopses.

But for Vihera, who possessed what could only be called a combat craving, such opponents were precisely what she craved.

Watching Vihera, Rei stroked Set and spoke.

"Anyway, let's at least extract this kobold leader's magic stone. Since we absorbed the goblin leader's magic stone, this one should be fine too."

"...Are you sure?"

Vihera murmured, just as he had when warning Set.

From Vihera's perspective, the Kobold Leader was by no means a strong opponent.

But it was still different from an ordinary kobold leader, and Vihera felt it would be best to avoid absorbing the magic stone, essentially the crystallized essence of such a monster's life.

"Relax. No matter what happens to a magic stone from a mere Kobold Leader, it can't do anything against Beast Magic. ...Besides, nothing unusual happened to Set after he absorbed the Goblin Leader's magic stone, right?"

"Guruu!"

At Rei's words, Set growled happily.

"...Are you really sure it's okay?"

Even so, concern colored Vihera's voice.

While Vihera was fundamentally optimistic and hedonistic, considering the current situation, she couldn't help but worry about Rei and Set.

Understanding Vihera's feelings, Rei still held a firm conviction that a kobold's magic stone alone couldn't possibly be the issue.

There was no particular reason. But something inside him held that conviction.

"Trust me."

"...You idiot. When you say that, I can't say anything back."

Vihera muttered sulkily, gently stroking Rei's arm.

Seeing Vihera stroke his arm affectionately, he nodded again, conveying his assurance that it would be fine, and approached the corpse of the kobold leader.

(For now, I should at least extract the magic stones from the other kobolds too.)

Unlike goblins, kobold fur could fetch a decent price.

Not for a high price, of course, but still better than goblins, whose materials were completely useless.

(If research on goblin meat goes well, the abundant goblin meat might eventually fetch a decent price... but who knows when that'll happen?)

Goblin meat, synonymous with foul-tasting flesh.

Rei was conducting research to make that meat edible... or more accurately, he was collaborating with one of the merchants on the research. Even so, he still hadn't found a breakthrough to make goblin meat palatable.

(Maybe I should try kobold meat instead? Kobold meat is perfectly edible, after all. ...But then again, kobolds are far fewer in number than goblins, so the quantity of meat is inferior.)

He pulled out his butcher's knife, extracting the magic stone from the goblin leader's heart as he pondered this.

(Right. I'll bring the meat along for now. If I leave it here anyway, it'll just become food for other animals or monsters. And worst case, it could turn into undead. Thinking about it that way...)

Understanding the abnormality of the kobold leader, Rei, having extracted the magic stone, pondered what to do with it... eventually pulling out the Death Scythe.

"Set, I gave the Goblin Leader's magic stone to you, so is it okay if I absorb this one with the Death Scythe?"

"Guruu? ...Gurururuu!"

Set hesitated slightly at Rei's words, but quickly nodded in agreement.

Giving Set a small pat on the head in gratitude, Rei took the magic stone and turned back toward Vihera.

"Vihera, I've seen Set absorb magic stones, but I haven't seen the Death Scythe absorb one, have I?"

"Huh? Yeah. ...Set swallowed it, but what about the Death Scythe? Could it be... since the Death Scythe was also born from Rei's magic power, it has its own will and absorbs magic stones that way?"

At Vihera's words, an image flashed through Rei's mind: a mouth forming on the Death Scythe, devouring the magic stone.

But the notion was so unrealistic that only a wry smile escaped Rei's lips.

It would be interesting if the magic item Death Scythe had such a function.

(If that were the case, could it extend just its mouth to tear out an enemy's magic stone? That would be pretty entertaining in its own way...)

A magic item with its own will sounded intriguing just from the description.

But if someone actually succeeded in creating such a thing, it would undoubtedly lead to all sorts of troublesome situations.

(Ah, but what about holy swords only wielded by the chosen ones... Could that be how it works?)

Of course, legends of that sort existed in this world of Elgin too.

No, in fact, quite a few stories remain.

Rei knew there were even stories about picture books, like those used by nobles to learn to read, though they weren't available to commoners.

He'd only been allowed to glance at one briefly in a bookstore.

Given that books in this world are quite expensive, naturally, you couldn't just browse them.

Attempting such a thing would get you kicked out of the shop immediately.

That's precisely why Rei didn't actually read the picture book thoroughly; he merely glanced through a few pages casually.

Even so, seeing the scene where the holy sword was drawn, there was no way he couldn't understand.

「Rei？」

Lost in thought over the phrase "magic item with a will," Rei snapped back to reality at Vihera's voice.

"No, nothing. It's not that Death Scythe has a will of its own. It just does this when absorbing magic stones."

He flung the magic stone in his hand into the air, and in the next instant, the Death Scythe flashed.

The magic stone was cleanly sliced apart... but the usual announcement message didn't play in Rei's mind.

"...No good. Or rather, if you think about it normally, to get a skill that boosts your sense of smell, you'd probably need a kobold magic stone instead of a goblin's, right?"

"Guru?"

At Rei's words, Set tilted his head slightly, as if to say, "Oh?"

For Set, learning the Enhanced Sense of Smell skill from a goblin magic stone... or more accurately, having it enhanced that way, probably wasn't that strange.

(Well, it was a higher-tier species... Thinking about it that way, maybe it's not that wrong? Though, considering that, it seems like it couldn't detect us in the air.)

As he pondered this, Vihera spoke up, sounding slightly surprised.

"Wait, does absorbing a magic stone not guarantee you'll acquire a skill?"

"Yeah. I don't know exactly what factors are involved, but sometimes you learn skills and sometimes you don't. Even the skills you can learn might be related to the monster's characteristics that possessed the magic stone, or they might not be."

That's especially true for the magic stones from this goblin leader and kobold leader, he added.

Vihera's expression softened into understanding at Rei's explanation.

"I see. So even that demon Zepair couldn't make skill acquisition that easy."

The expression on Vihera's face as she murmured this was one of relief.

For a moment, Vihera wondered why she wore such an expression, but she understood immediately.

It was the fear of being left behind by Rei.

Because she cared for Rei, she couldn't bear the thought of him leaving her behind.

"Yeah, that's how it is. ...Well then, I guess we should get going. If we stay in this forest too long, we might get caught up in something else."

"I feel like we're already deeply involved enough as it is..."

Rei couldn't help but agree with what Vihera said, as if suppressing the unease within his own chest.

While he had taken on the Goblin Leader case himself, this Kobold Leader situation was clearly something he'd been dragged into.

Rei understood he was prone to trouble, so staying here would likely mean getting tangled up in something else.

Given Rei's past experiences, it was only natural for him to think that way.

"Well then, let's go. I need to explain this situation to the guild too."

"Yeah, yeah. And something else will probably happen, right? ...But honestly, I'd prefer it if a slightly stronger monster showed up next time."

Listening to Vihera's words, spoken with a hint of a wry smile, Rei stored the Kobold Leader and all the Kobold corpses into the Misty Ring.

"Oh, you're taking all of them after all?"

"Yeah. Storing them in the item box won't make them rot. If you think of them as emergency rations in case something happens, it's not a bad idea at all."

"I wouldn't say it's bad either. Still, that item box sure is convenient, isn't it?"

Vihera murmured thoughtfully.

Vihera, who basically dressed in a way that could easily be mistaken for a dancer or a prostitute, naturally had no pockets or storage spaces like that. She only carried things occasionally in a bag she might have.

For someone like Vihera, the Misty Ring—which probably just looked like another piece of jewelry to her—must have been incredibly enviable.

"There's a simplified version, or rather, a mass-produced item box, right? Aren't you going to buy one? It's pretty expensive, but I don't think it'd be too hard for someone like Vihera to earn enough to afford it."

What crossed Rei's mind was the simplified item box Elena possessed.

However, it was quite expensive, and its downside was that it could only hold a relatively small amount compared to the price.

Still, compared to a bag, it allowed carrying an overwhelmingly larger amount of luggage, so many people desired it.

"Yeah. I'd love to have one if possible... but it's an extremely rare item to begin with, so it's not just a money issue. Plus, it's pretty hard to get even in Gilm."

"Should we ask the one who made the Twilight Spear? It's a magic item, so maybe they could do something?"

"...I'm not sure. Creating an item box system requires a significant amount of magical power, and the magical attributes are also crucial. It's not simply a matter of being able to use alchemy either."

"But if we don't try, nothing will happen, right? If it doesn't work, we can just think of another way."

"Well... I suppose you're right."

"Then, after reporting to the guild about the kobold leader, shall we go take a look?"

"...If that's what Rei says, let's go. Though the chances of it not working are probably higher."

With that, Vihera left the forest with Rei and Set, heading toward Gilm.
