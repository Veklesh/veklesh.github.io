The sight, sparkling as it reflected the sunlight, truly showcased the overwhelming grandeur of nature.

The sea, shimmering blue.

That is the scene currently spreading out before Rei’s eyes.

"Wow... It’s not like this is my first time at the ocean, but a view like this is still amazing."

As far as the ocean goes, Rei had visited a port town called Emoshion a few years ago.

That said, since he’d gone there to defeat a bounty monster named Remrace, he hadn’t exactly been able to indulge in the luxury of enjoying a leisurely swim.

“Hey, Rei-chan! If you can see the ocean, hurry up, okay? I want to see the ocean soon, too!”

Bistol’s voice came from inside the Set Cage.

The others, including Elena, didn’t say anything, but it was clear they agreed with Bistol.

“Got it, just wait a sec. I’ll look for a place to land. …A rocky shore, huh?”

Rei looked out toward the sea and saw—not a white sandy beach, but a place covered in countless rocks that looked hard just by looking at them.

That said, Rei’s goal wasn’t to go swimming, but to catch as many fish and other seafood as possible.

In that sense, a rocky shore was actually better suited than a sandy beach.

Of course, there are fish on sandy beaches too, but rocky shores are definitely better for catching fish, shellfish, octopus, squid... and all sorts of other seafood.

(Besides, there are some things you can only do in the sea with rocks.)

When Rei was in Japan, he used to go to the beach with his family when he was little, and with friends once he got older.

The coast near where Rei and his family lived had both sandy beaches and rocky shores.

Among those, Rei’s family often went to play at the rocky coast.

On rocky shores, various shellfish like whelks live there. You crush them with a stone, tie them to a fishing line, and drop them into the shallow water.

Then, small crabs and fish would come swimming over.

It wasn’t crayfish fishing—it was crab fishing.

It was plenty of fun as a seaside activity.

...Also, even though they were just crabs, Rei remembers that because they were small, their shells weren’t that hard, and they were incredibly delicious when deep-fried and sprinkled with salt.

(Besides, there are octopuses in the shallows too. …Since there probably aren’t any fishing rights or anything like that, there’s a chance we could catch abalone, turban shells, oysters, and the like. …Well, though it’s not certain that the abalone in this world are exactly the same species as those on Earth.)

Thinking about all that, Rei asks Set to go down to the ground.

However, they didn’t head straight for the water’s edge.

Instead, he heads toward a spot a short distance away where steam is rising.

(If we were using a village or town as a base, we wouldn’t have to worry about water after coming out of the sea… but if we’re using a place like this with no people around, we’ll need to secure a water supply. Especially since we’re by the sea this time.)

If you swim in the ocean, your body will naturally be submerged in seawater—saltwater.

When that happens, salt will naturally remain all over your body, including your hair.

To prevent that, I really needed to wash myself after getting out of the sea.

(In that sense, I was lucky to find a hot spring.)

Even if there were no hot spring, Rei’s Misty Ring holds a large amount of water, and in the worst case, there’s also the Dagger of Flowing Water.

……That said, washing my body with water from the Dagger of Flowing Water—often described as “heavenly nectar”—would look like nothing short of extravagance to an outsider.

However, that’s strictly speaking from anyone other than Rei’s perspective.

To Rei, the Dagger of Flowing Water is simply a source of water that produces as much as he wants when he channels his magic into it; to others, it’s nothing more than water he can drink whenever and however much he likes, even if its taste is worthy of being called “heavenly nectar.”

That is precisely why, no matter how much of that water she uses, she doesn’t feel it’s all that extravagant.

There was another option—using Marina’s spirit magic instead of the Dagger of Flowing Water—but… considering the amount of magic power required, there’s no doubt that it’s quicker for Rei to do it himself.

This time, since we found a hot spring, we didn’t have to wash our bodies or hair with the water produced by the Dagger of Flowing Water.

(The question is whether that hot spring is one we can actually bathe in. I’ve heard there are some hot springs you can’t bathe in.)

As far as Rei knew, there were hot springs with water so scalding you couldn’t enter them, or ones mixed with substances that made them unsuitable for bathing.

Hoping it wasn’t one of those, I descended toward the ground… and right before my eyes, I spotted several monkeys soaking in a hot spring.

The moment those monkeys saw Set flying over the hot spring, they immediately fled.

Seeing this, Rei thought, “If wild animals can bathe in it, then we should be able to as well,” and a look of relief spread across his face.

They lowered the Set Cage onto the grassland beside the hot spring, and Set and Rei also stepped down onto the grass.

“Oh, Rei. Isn’t the sea over there?”

Vihera, having stepped down from the Set Cage, asked Rei while looking at the sea in the distance.

Marina, using her spirit-user abilities to figure out why Rei had come down here, smiled at Vihera and spoke.

“We’re making this our base.”

“Why?”

“If you want to know, why don’t you take a look over there? I think you’ll understand once you do.”

Hearing that, Vihera seems to have become curious, so she invites Byune and the two of them head over together.

“So, Rei-chan. You said we’re making this our base—what are we going to do?”

“We’ve got the Magic Tent, so you don’t need to worry about that. There’s even a place for Bistol to sleep, so don’t worry.”

Even as she said that, Rei couldn’t completely shake the doubt: Is it really okay for her to sleep in the same place as Bistol?

Of course, if Rei were to voice that honestly, Bistol would likely get angry, insisting that he has his own preferences.

To Bistol, Rei wasn’t a romantic interest… rather, it would be more accurate to describe her as someone he doted on.

Whether being adored by Bistol was a source of happiness was another matter entirely, though.

“Oh? Really? So, what are we doing from here on out today? Are we going to go catch some seafood right away? Or are we going to set up our base?”

“No, I mean, about the base…”

“I’m not just talking about the Magic Tent. I’m talking about the area around it.”

“Around the Magic Tent? Since Set’s here, monsters aren’t exactly going to attack us, are they? Though bandits might show up.”

“If bandits show up, Rei would probably be thrilled.”

Marina said this with an amused smile.

In fact, Rei is feared by bandits and known as the “Bandit Eater.”

Even during the bandit hunt around Sabrusta, it was Rei who ended up killing the most bandits.

Ray alone—though Set was there too—had hunted that many bandits, and if you added the numbers from Marina, Vihera, and Byune, as well as Elena, Ara, and Yellow—who weren’t members of the Crimson Wings—the total number of bandits they’d hunted combined put him in a clear first place, with an overwhelming lead over everyone else.

Naturally, the quantity and quality of the treasure the bandits had hoarded were also the best.

……Though, in Rei’s case, since he had Set fly and searched from above alongside him, it might have been inevitable, in a sense, that he’d find the most bandits.

Even regarding locations that were well-camouflaged and would have been impossible to find in the past, if you keep searching for five days straight, you eventually start to notice places that seem somewhat out of place.

In that sense, the bandits who fled upon learning of Rei’s presence were probably wise.

“I don’t think there are any bandits around here, though.”

Bandits, of course, need someone to attack.

In a place like this, where there’s no one around—where there aren’t even any villages, towns, or cities within sight, even from the sky—Rei simply couldn’t imagine there being any bandits.

“But even if there aren’t any bandit hideouts, wouldn’t this be a pretty good place to hide treasure? Since there’s no one around, that means no one would ever find the treasure we hide here.”

Hearing Vihera’s words, Rei realized she had a point.

(It does have the advantage of not being found by people, but the chance of goblins or something finding it isn’t exactly zero, though.)

Even as he agreed, he couldn’t help but think that, but he decided not to voice it for the time being.

“Anyway, I don’t think there are any bandits around here, so what do we need to prepare?”

“That’s true. For example, building a hearth. We don’t need to go to the trouble of building a fancy hearth just to camp out for one night, but we’re going to use this place as a base for a few days, right? In that case, why not make a few preparations—not just a hearth—to make our stay a little more comfortable?”

“Like this?”

Saying that, Rei pulled a kiln out of the Misty Ring.

Not a hearth, but a kiln.

And it was the very oven she always used to bake pizza.

“Um… Rei. This is…”

“As you can see, it’s a kiln. It’s not a hearth, but you can cook quite a bit with this.”

“I mean, I guess so… I guess so… Aaaaaah, why is this… I don’t even know how to put it into words!?”

Unable to put her thoughts into words, Bistol clutched her head.

Her exposed arm muscles bulged as she did so, but in this situation, it was far from impressive.

Everyone except Rei watched Bistol like that for a moment, but eventually, as if his thoughts had settled, Bistol opened his mouth while looking at the kiln.

“All right, Rei-chan. From what I can tell, this kiln is a magic item, right?”

“Yeah, I guess so. Well, if it weren’t, camping would be a real hassle.”

With a standard pizza oven, you’d have to burn firewood for an hour—or even longer in some cases—to preheat it before you could actually use it.

But you can’t exactly do that every time you’re camping.

This magical item oven was designed to solve that problem.

It wasn’t just a regular portable oven—it was a magic item, which is why we didn’t have to go through the trouble of preheating it.

If a professional chef were to say it was unorthodox compared to a regular wood-fired oven, it wouldn’t be surprising.

In fact, even if someone were to say the flavor is inferior compared to a regular oven, Rei wouldn’t be able to argue against it.

That said, Rei doesn’t have a palate refined enough to detect such subtle differences in flavor.

……In reality, the body created for Rei by Zepair possesses extremely keen senses.

In other words, his sense of taste is overwhelmingly sharper than most people’s… but perhaps that’s just due to Rei’s lack of experience.

To be precise, it would be more accurate to say that the sensations from when he was still living in Japan remain.

That’s not to say Rei didn’t eat delicious food while in Japan.

On the contrary, in terms of ingredients, she was very fortunate—eating fresh vegetables harvested from her parents’ fields on the very day they were picked, enjoying river fish like sweetfish, and savoring the bounty of the mountains, such as wild vegetables and mushrooms…

Even so, the fact that her palate isn’t particularly refined—and is, if anything, rather unrefined—is likely due more to her personality.

“Huh… so there are magic items like this, too. But I think this would be a little hard for ordinary people to use.”

Rei understood what Bistol was getting at.

In terms of size, this kiln isn’t much different from an ordinary one.

No, if anything, it’s actually slightly larger than a standard one.

It’s not that it couldn’t be made any smaller as a magic item… but rather, since they’re camping, other adventurers might use it alongside Rei and her group.

That’s why it was made a little larger.

Given that, carrying this kiln around would… well, require something like Rei’s Misty Ring.

Or, if it were a large enough group to have a dedicated wagon to carry this kiln, they might be able to make good use of it.

“You’d need to channel magic into it, or use magic stones or something. I won’t deny that it’s generally a hassle to use. Still, you don’t need firewood, and you can cook whenever you want—there are plenty of advantages.”

Bistol’s gaze toward Rei, who muttered this, was one of exasperation… and envy at the thought of being able to eat freshly cooked meals anywhere.

After all, even for a merchant like Bistol, meals while traveling often consist of preserved foods, so that’s only natural.

And Bistoll, who owned a “Magic Bag”—a budget version of an item box—found himself wanting something just like this kiln… just a little bit.
