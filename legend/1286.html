Rei received the kiln, and after some time passed... eventually, as the year drew to a close, Rei, Vihera, Byune, and Set found themselves at Marina's house in the Noble District.

The year-end party that had been proposed earlier was finally happening.

While some had heard about the party and wanted to attend, this gathering was strictly a bonding event for the party forming in spring, so only Rei and his group were invited.

Those who had hoped to spend New Year's Eve with Rei, like Kenny, or with Set, like Milein and Johanna, were extremely disappointed.

Some witnessed Kenny lamenting why she wasn't an adventurer but just the guild receptionist, receiving various consolations from Lenora.

...Though, even if Kenny had intended to join this party, she might have declined upon learning it was an outdoor gathering.

"Wow, that's amazing... Spirit magic sure is convenient."

Seeing how Marina's spirit magic made the accumulated snow move on its own, creating a space large enough for the party, Vihera let out a gasp of admiration.

Hearing Vihera's words, Byune nodded in agreement.

"Hehe, I'm glad to hear you say that. ...Now, Rei. Please."

Seemingly not entirely displeased by the praise, Marina urged Rei on with a pleased expression.

"Alright, then, here it comes."

With those words, a kiln suddenly materialized in the courtyard the very next moment.

"Huh, so this is Rei's dedicated... well, maybe not exactly dedicated, but it's a kiln? From what I can see, it doesn't look much different from the kilns you'd find in a regular bakery. It is quite a bit smaller, though."

"Hmm."

Compared to the ovens found in bakeries, its size was considerably smaller.

...It was only natural that the size differed between a kiln meant to cook meals for a few people and one meant to bake bread for dozens at once.

In fact, for cooking meals for four people and one beast, you could even say the oven was too big.

Then again, with Rei, Byune, and Set—all surprisingly big eaters—making up most of the party, this size might be just right.

"So it's a furnace that generates heat using magic... I think it's quite something, don't you? And Rei, you got the bread dough too, right?"

"Not bread dough, pizza dough. Should I bring out the other ingredients too?"

"No, save the udon for last. We eat it when the year changes, right?"

"Right. ...Well, technically it's soba, not udon. But hey, I hear some regions eat New Year's udon too, so it's not exactly wrong."

From Golden Bread House, even though they were still experimenting, I got nearly a hundred pieces of pizza dough rolled out into rounds—a version they were reasonably satisfied with.

Of course, we weren't going to eat it all today; it was for making pizza in this oven going forward.

Also, since we had the chance, instead of New Year's soba, we bought udon from Manpukutei to eat udon instead.

Many people eat New Year's Eve soba for lunch or dinner, but when Rei was in Japan, she ate it around midnight because it was New Year's Eve soba.

(If New Year's udon catches on, eating it around midnight might become the norm... Well, I'd prefer that. If anyone came to Elgin after me, they might say, "The time to eat New Year's udon is different!"?)

Thinking like that, I took the pizza dough out of the Misty Ring, then pulled out the toppings: sauce, sausage, vegetables, mushrooms, and cheese.

The tomato sauce was in short supply, but we could still buy a reasonable amount from shops and cafeterias.

Near the oven stood a table brought from Marina's mansion.

The sight of a table in the snow felt somehow strange... yet faintly fantastical.

As Rei felt an indescribable mood at the sight, Vihera spoke up upon seeing the toppings on the table.

"Huh... so these are the ingredients for this dish called pizza?"

"Yeah. I doubt you'd find this dish anywhere else, not just in Gilm... but probably not even in the Kingdom of Mireana. Of course, it's possible some place I don't know about has something similar."

Pizza is a dish that, if you can bake bread normally, anyone could conceive of with a slight shift in thinking... well, maybe not anyone, but it wouldn't be strange if someone came up with it.

That's precisely why Rei understood it was entirely possible similar dishes existed, even if he hadn't heard of them.

Still, he figured even if there were similar dishes, they probably wouldn't be called 'pizza'.

"Right. There are breads baked with toppings inside or on top, so it wouldn't be surprising if similar dishes existed somewhere."

Marina, with her long experience as an adventurer, nodded at Rei's words.

"So, yeah... well, this pizza is more of a trial run, I suppose. Anyway, let's get started making it."

On the table set up on the snow, Rei placed pizza dough in front of each of them.

"Grr."

"I know. I've got plenty for Set too, so don't worry. Here."

"For me too!" Set purred, and Ray placed his pizza dough on the table as well.

Seeing this, Set purred happily too.

"Now then, first... no, before that, I should get the oven going. Even if it's not snowing, we'll need warmth."

Leaving the dough alone, Rei looked up at the sky.

Despite it being a late afternoon in the year-end season, the weather today was exceptionally fine.

It was hard to believe that just a few days ago... no, just last night, there had been heavy snowfall.

However, even though the weather was clear and sunny, it was still winter, and the temperature was low.

Rei figured it would be better to warm things up so everyone could enjoy making pizza.

Marina's spirit magic had created a strange state where the snow didn't melt yet it was somewhat warm... but still, Rei thought heating with a pizza oven would feel more like winter.

He touched the oven and channeled his magic into it.

Starting the oven required a fair amount of magic, but since Rei possessed magic power that could rightly be called beyond the ordinary, he felt no fatigue whatsoever.

Soon, the oven activated by Rei's magic began to radiate a gentle warmth around it.

The warmth felt even with Marina's spirit magic at work had a comforting quality to it.

(I've heard somewhere that feeling the heat from a wood stove is more reassuring than an electric heater or air conditioner... Is this similar?)

With a slight question lingering, Rei returned to the table she was using as a work surface.

However, Vihera, Marina, Byune... and the three humans and one beast were all staring at the newly activated kiln with keen interest.

Since there was nothing inside the kiln yet, it wasn't giving off any intense heat.

Still, perhaps seeing a magic item kiln for the first time, they stared intently at the interior as it glowed red-hot.

"Is it really that interesting? Aside from the fact it runs on magic, the kiln itself doesn't seem that different from a regular one."

"Well, the fact that it runs on magic is what's amazing. I've lived quite a while, but a kiln powered by magic... and one you can carry around? That's a first for me."

Marina murmured thoughtfully.

Why? Rei felt a twinge of doubt at her words.

Magic items powered by magic weren't uncommon.

Of course, using magic stones to power them is the norm.

"Is that all? ...Well, never mind the kiln for now. First, let's make some pizza. Spread tomato sauce on the pizza dough, then add whatever toppings you like."

After saying that much, Rei thought maybe he should have taken out the seafood from inside the Misty Ring? But for today, since it was their first time making pizza, he opted for a regular pizza.

Rei had eaten seafood pizza several times before. ...Of course, the kind sold at the supermarket.

Even so, it was delicious enough, so he resolved to try it if the pizza they made today turned out tasty.

With that somewhat tentative resolve, Rei joined the others in spreading tomato sauce on the pizza crust and adding toppings.

"Set, which one are you having?"

"Gurrrr!"

As Rei asked Set while placing vegetables, sausage, and ham on his own dough, Set happily turned his beak toward several ingredients on the table.

Whether it was to be expected or just as predicted, Set's choices centered around meat: ham, sausage, grilled meat, stewed meat...

Rei knew Set loved meat, but that didn't mean meat alone was balanced.

"You have to pick some vegetables too, okay? How about this?"

"Gurrr."

When Rei selected several vegetables and mushrooms, Set happily made a gurgling sound.

While Set loved meat, he didn't dislike vegetables.

He thought he could eat as many of the vegetables Rei picked out as he wanted.

(If Milein and Johanna were here... No, better not think about that.)

Rei remembered the faces of the two who loved playing with and fussing over Set. He knew they would have been overjoyed if they were here, but he stopped himself from thinking about it further.

Today's party was, above all, a New Year's gathering meant to deepen the bonds among the members who would be forming a party starting in spring.

Inviting other members to it felt a bit off. That's what Rei thought.

"Rei, I'm done... but is this okay?"

At Vihera's words, Rei turned her gaze that way... and couldn't help but smile.

The pizza Vihera had called out to Rei was a well-balanced creation topped with sausage and vegetables. Marina's pizza had slightly more vegetables, but it could still be called a normal pizza.

But... calling the pizza Byune made a pizza was difficult.

Vegetables, sausage, ham, and other toppings were piled on the pizza crust as if to say, "Here, take this!"

In fact, the pizza crust itself was completely hidden, and the toppings alone were several centimeters thick.

"Huh!"

Rei was momentarily speechless, but Byune, the creator of this pizza, stood proudly with her chest puffed out, uttering her usual single word.

"Ah... Byune. Sorry, but this has way too many toppings. It's excessive. Cut it down to about Vihera or Marina's level."

"Huh!?"

Being told this by Rei was probably unexpected for Byune too.

Her confidence had been so strong that the shock seemed all the greater.

"See, Byune? I told you so. You know, too much of anything isn't good. This looks a bit embarrassing, don't you think?"

Vihera declared with an exasperated look, and Byune, though clearly displeased, began picking off the excess toppings from the pizza.

The dish, which had been more like something else than pizza, gradually transformed into something worthy of being called pizza.

"I get that you want to try all the dishes Rei came up with, but let's start with a basic pizza, okay? Huh?"

Rei, asked for agreement by Vihera, nodded in agreement.

"It's not like this is the only pizza crust. There are plenty more."

"...Hmm."

Even so, Byune replied with only that, sounding disappointed by Rei's words.

Once the pizza dough was topped with a decent amount of toppings—still more than Vihera or Marina had—cheese was used for the finishing touch.

They roughly scattered the pre-shredded cheese over the pizza dough.

Normally, the act of placing the cheese would showcase professional skill... but since this was Rei and the others' first time making pizza, they were rather haphazard about it.

...As expected, Byune piled quite a bit more cheese onto her own pizza.

Still, compared to the mountain of toppings they'd piled on earlier, it wasn't exactly eye-popping. They shoved the pizza dough into the heated oven.

Incidentally, the giant spatula-like tool used to place the pizza dough inside was something Pamidor had made for them a few days prior.

And so, they placed the pizza dough into the sufficiently heated oven... and within a few minutes, the cheese melted, and the pizza was baked to perfection.
