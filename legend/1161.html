After finishing his conversation with Vihera, Rei walked through the forest with her... well, technically, with her and one other being, since Set was following behind them.

"Hey, Rei. I'm glad you told me about your secrets, including the beast magic, but Elena knows about this too, right?"

"Yeah. After the whole mess when I first met Elena was over."

"...Hmm. So you told Elena the first time you met her. I met Rei quite a while ago, and only just found out about it now."

Vihera said, looking a little sulky.

Rei opened his mouth to Vihera, looking slightly apologetic.

"I really intended to tell you sooner. It just got delayed because of the Twilight Spear incident and stuff. Besides, Vihera went back to Exil with Byune, didn't she?"

"That's true, but don't you think it's only natural I'd feel dissatisfied? You were keeping secrets from someone you care about."

Hearing "like" stated so clearly made Rei blush a little.

He understood Vihera's feelings, but even so, being told so plainly like this was bound to make him blush.

As they walked through the forest in an awkward silence that made Rei uncomfortable, Vihera finally spoke up.

"Hey, Rei. Does Marina know about this?"

"No."

Rei shook his head at Vihera's question.

Seeing this, Vihera's expression shifted to one of slight surprise.

Rei had met Marina before she had.

Therefore, it wouldn't have been strange if she had been told about Rei's secret before him.

But unexpectedly, the word that came out of Rei's mouth was "no."

"Really? Well, I mean, I guess it's fine that I found out about this before you? I mean, I'm happy, of course."

Vihera smiled, looking slightly troubled yet pleased, and Rei frowned ever so slightly.

"I actually meant to tell Marina this time too, but she said she was busy with research, right? So, I'll tell Marina another time."

"Honestly, I think it would have been better to tell me at the same time as Marina. She'll probably be disappointed, you know?"

To be honest, Vihera was happy to learn it before Marina did.

But even so, it was true she felt a certain guilt about being taught before Marina, who recognized her as a romantic rival.

That's precisely why Vihera wished they could have been told together.

"Well, it's just that the goblin leader was a bit smarter, right? Then the research will be done quickly. In that case, today or tomorrow..."

As they walked out of the forest, chatting like that, Set, who had been walking near Rei, suddenly stopped moving.

"Grrrr..."

He growled, his gaze fixed on the bushes.

From beyond the thick, summer-green foliage came a sound like a scream.

Rei drew the Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring.

The fact that it wasn't his familiar Death Scythe was likely because they were in the forest.

Since Vihera's combat style was hand-to-hand fighting, she didn't need to draw a weapon.

While he could create claws from his gauntlets and blades from his greaves as weapons, these were formed by channeling magic and required no special preparation.

As for Set, his powerful limbs, beak, and tail were weapons in themselves.

Just as the three finished preparing for battle, three adventurers burst out from the thicket.

Leading the way was a male feline beastman. Two humans followed closely behind him.

The cat beastman froze in shock at the sight of Rei, Set, and Vihera—two humans and one beast—appearing suddenly before him, their appearance far too distinctive.

But as soon as he recognized who stood before him, he shouted about the enemies pursuing them from behind.

"Kobolds! Kobolds are chasing us!"

He shouted as he ran past Rei. The two men running behind the cat beastman looked so desperate they couldn't even speak.

"Goblins? Well, we were attacked by goblins in this forest before, so it's possible..."

Watching the three men disappear into the forest trees, Rei readied his Twilight Spear... but his motivation wasn't particularly high.

Naturally. Kobolds were only slightly stronger monsters than goblins.

Unlike goblins, they can coordinate well with their allies, making them somewhat troublesome, but to Rei, the difference wasn't that great.

"They're coming."

Vihera, fists clenched, was naturally unenthusiastic too.

She'd gladly fight if it were a powerful enemy, but even to Vihera, kobolds were monsters not much different from goblins.

「Set」

Hearing Vihera's words, Rei called out to Set.

Fighting kobolds was pointless, so there was no need to force a battle.

If he understood Set's presence, a smart kobold like this one, unlike goblins, should flee without a fight.

Urging him with that thought, Set took a small breath before letting out a high-pitched cry.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

I am here,

A roar that seemed to declare it.

If the enemy were an ordinary monster, knowing a griffin stood in their path, they would flee first.

...Yes. If it were ordinary.

"Waaaaaah!"

Despite Set roaring to assert his presence, the kobolds chasing from beyond the thicket showed no sign of turning back. They charged straight ahead... and soon burst through the foliage, revealing themselves.

"What?"

Faced with this unexpected sight, Rei let out a small gasp of surprise.

It was only natural. The difference in stature between a kobold and a griffin was simply too great.

No matter how you looked at it, the kobolds had no chance.

And yet, the kobolds burst out of the bushes without a care in the world.

「Vihera！」

"Well, what can you do?"

At Rei's words, Vihera let out a small sigh as she prepared for battle.

Beside Vihera, Rei thrust his Twilight Spear at the kobold that had leapt from the bushes.

The instant after thrusting, the Twilight Spear was pulled back into Rei's hand. The kobold's head was crushed, leaving only its torso, which collapsed to the ground at the same speed it had burst forth.

Vihera, too, dodged the long sword swing aimed at her the instant she judged an enemy was there as she burst from the bushes, and swung her fist.

His fist sank a full fist-width into the goblin's solar plexus, shattering ribs.

But the number of kobolds bursting from the bushes showed no sign of decreasing, appearing one after another.

"GRRRRRRRRGRRRR!"

Along with Set's battle cry, several wind arrows were created.

The Wind Arrow skill.

While the individual power wasn't exceptionally high, multiple wind arrows still knocked down the kobolds that kept charging out.

"Gyaaah!"

Such screams reached the ears of multiple Reis.

But... Rei's expectation that they would retreat immediately after being hurt that badly was wrong.

Even as their comrades were being beaten, more kobolds kept emerging from the bushes.

(What? Kobolds aren't fundamentally that stupid. At least, I can't imagine them charging headlong into a fight against a stronger enemy without any thought for the consequences, like goblins do...)

Rei unleashed strike after strike from the Twilight Spear at the kobolds bursting from the bushes.

A kobold bursts out, takes a strike from the Twilight Spear, its head shattered, and falls dead to the ground.

It was clearly nothing more than routine work.

As this exchange repeated itself, the question within Rei grew even larger.

Their movements, uncharacteristic of goblins, were clearly off somehow.

It wasn't just Rei who sensed this; Vihera likely felt it too.

Dodge the attacking kobold's strikes, deliver a kick to its torso, snap its neck with your greaves, and speak.

"Hey, Rei. Something's off, you know?"

"Yeah, I've been wondering too. But precisely because it's strange, we can't just leave these things alone, right? Those three earlier might have fled like that because their opponents weren't ordinary kobolds either."

He kept defeating the kobolds emerging from the bushes, defeating them, defeating them, defeating them.

Rei, Vihera, and Set—the two humans and one beast present—were not the sort to be outmatched by mere kobolds.

That's precisely why the kobolds kept emerging from the bushes only to be killed, their bodies piling up on the ground.

If Rei and the others were slightly less skilled, or if the kobolds had better coordination, the ones beyond the bushes might have sensed something was wrong.

But every single kobold, as if in a frenzy, showed absolutely no coordination with their comrades.

Even without using a howl-like signal to communicate with each other, it wouldn't be difficult to hear Set's cries from beyond the bushes, and above all, there was no way they couldn't sense his presence.

Despite this, the kobolds acted as if none of that mattered, each moving independently. They repeatedly emerged from the bushes one after another, only to be killed outright by Rei and the others.

With such reckless behavior, the kobolds' numbers inevitably dwindled... and eventually, after the last one fell to the ground, no more kobolds appeared from behind the bushes.

The number of kobold corpses scattered on the ground was about twenty.

Compared to the goblins led by the goblin leader, the number was small, but for a kobold pack, it was average or perhaps slightly above average.

"...Is it over? Or are there still more?"

Rei felt a momentary sense of relief, but then noticed a lingering presence beyond the bushes.

Vihera and Set also took up positions ready to intercept.

And then, pushing through the bushes to reveal itself, was, unsurprisingly, a kobold.

However, these weren't the ordinary kobolds Rei and the others had been defeating. These were a size larger than the others.

"A kobold leader. ...Just a coincidence?"

Goblin Leader and Kobold Leader.

Both were higher-ranking species, but neither was particularly powerful.

But what lingered as a question in Rei's mind above all else was this: the Goblin Leader, normally the superior species to the dim-witted goblins, was intelligent, and the Kobold Leader, normally reasonably smart and adept at coordinating with its comrades, was giving orders as if it hadn't thought things through at all.

"Grrrrrrr"

The Kobold Leader emitted a growl distinct from Set's, more dog-like.

Its gaze fixed menacingly on its surroundings.

Despite Rei, Vihera, and Set being present, the Kobold Leader showed no sign of fear whatsoever.

He pointed the tip of the spear in his hand toward Rei and the others, ready to start fighting at any moment.

"...Even if you're a higher species, you're awfully cocky for a Kobold Leader. Rei, may I fight him?"

Apparently struck by the Kobold Leader's demeanor, Vihera stepped forward.

Seeing this, the Kobold Leader shifted the spear's tip toward Vihera, baring its fangs and letting out a threatening growl.

But... before Vihera could even take a stance facing the Kobold Leader, Rei spoke up.

"Sorry, Vihera. This is my first time fighting a Kobold Leader, so I'll take it this time. I want the magic stone."

"If you need the magic stone, I could just let you have it after I take him down?"

Though Vihera's words implied she didn't care about the Kobold Leader's magic stone, Rei shook his head while keeping the tip of Twilight Spear pointed at the Kobold Leader to keep it at bay.

"No, sorry, but to absorb a magic stone, I need to fight the monster that possesses it to some extent. It's a necessary step to synchronize our magical wavelengths."

At Rei's words, Vihera showed a hint of surprise... then, lowering her fist as if accepting the explanation, she spoke.

"I see. I was wondering why you kept mentioning needing magic stones but never placed a request with the guild to purchase them... So that's why."

The explanation seemed familiar to her. ...In Vihera's mind at that moment, the scene of fighting alongside Rei in Exil's dungeon earlier flashed back.

(Even just throwing stones... could it be that their magical wavelengths match?)

Though she harbored some doubts, she decided to abandon the fight against the kobold leader, reasoning that if Rei insisted that much, it must be true.

And so, Rei stepped forward in Vihera's place...

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Seizing the opening as Rei stepped forward, the Kobold Leader lunged.

"Too naive."

But without doing anything else, it simply collapsed to the ground, its head crushed cleanly by the Twilight Spear held by Rei.
