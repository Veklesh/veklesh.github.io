"Set-chaaan!"

Those nearby braced themselves at the sudden sound and shock... but among them, the first to spring into action was, unsurprisingly, Mirei.

He set the Set Cage down on the ground... By setting it down, Seto's figure became visible from the ground as well, allowing him to spot Seto in the sky first.

From the cry he'd heard earlier, Milein had sensed it was Set approaching.

That was likely why he was so quick to take the next action.

Sulunin and Ekril, Milein's two companions, showed no particular surprise at seeing Milein like this.

Unlike the others, they fully understood how keen Milein's instincts were regarding Set.

In contrast to Milein, the other guards... and even one of the envoys who had dismounted from the carriage... could only stare at her in stunned silence.

They had been clamoring about it being Set, but surely none of them had truly believed it actually was him.

No, that information... The envoy who had heard about Rei and the others snapped back to his senses quickly.

"He is not the enemy! That is Set, the familiar of Crimson Rei, whom you all know! I repeat, he is not the enemy! I will not permit any attack on Set!"

At the man's shout, the adventurers other than the Burning Wind finally lowered their guard.

Still, just in case... just in the absolute worst case scenario, they remained ready to respond immediately if anything happened.

It was an order from one of the targets they were protecting, but they couldn't truly relax until they confirmed with their own eyes whether the griffin actually flying was Set.

Of course, it was unlikely that high-rank monsters like griffins were just lying around.

Watching the adventurers' reactions, the envoy sighed in relief.

(Good. This means I won't get scolded by Serena or Chrom.)

Knowing how much his wife and son back in Gilm both adored Set, he couldn't bear the thought of them finding out if his orders led to Set being attacked here...

That was obvious, even without thinking about it.

Though usually gentle, his wife was terrifying when angry—something he knew well from their courtship, marriage, and life together.

The man felt deep relief that it hadn't come to that.

From the man's line of sight, two women emerged from within the Set Cage.

Both were beauties so stunning that the phrase "peerless" seemed fitting... Naturally, the envoy knew who they were.

One was Marina, the former guildmaster of Gilm.

The other was Elena, known by the epithet Princess General.

Both were figures Gilm recognized as undeniably important.

As he hesitated over which one to address... a little distance away from the hesitant man, a scene that could be called a moving reunion in a way was unfolding.

"Set-chan, Set-chan, Set-chan! Are you okay? Are you hungry? Tired?"

Milein asked, stroking the body of Set who had descended to the ground.

"Don't say such rude things. That makes it sound like I haven't given Set any food at all."

"What's with you always being the one with Set-chan? ...I think it's okay for me to be with Set-chan sometimes too."

Hugging Set's neck, Milein voiced her dissatisfaction toward Rei.

But for Rei, the target of this complaint, it wasn't unreasonable to feel aggrieved.

"Well, even so. Set is my familiar to begin with, so it's only natural he'd be with me, right?"

"Guh... T-that's unfair, using logic like that!"

"No, why is stating the obvious cowardly?"

"Grrr..."

Milein tried to say something else to Rei, but the moment Set growled, she immediately became engrossed in doting on him instead of Rei.

Watching Milein like that, Rei suddenly recalled the group of women he led.

Even now that they had carriages for travel, back when most traveled on foot, many women—and men of the Resistance too—had endured the hardships by drawing strength from Set's adorable nature.

Indeed, when Rei and the others mentioned leaving to join this envoy, many had looked more saddened by the thought of losing Set than by Rei's departure. It was clear just how beloved Set was.

But... what if Milein were to join them?

Rei could almost predict the outcome.

After all, Johanna, who was just as fond of Set as Milein, was on absolutely terrible terms with Milein.

In other words, if Milein were brought along to join the other group... what would happen? If things went badly, it could even turn into a bloody battle.

The thought sent a chill down Rei's spine.

(The only saving grace is that Milein is escorting the envoy, and we're heading to Gilm. Even if we act together, it shouldn't last that long. It better not... I hope.)

While Rei was trying to figure out how to prevent a battle over Set, the man who had been talking with Marina and Elena approached him.

Well, perhaps "men" was the more accurate term.

The other members of the envoy, who had been riding in carriages for safety until now, had also gotten out, judging it safe.

"Rei, can I have a word?"

The one who called out was the first person to get out of the carriage earlier.

He was in his forties, with a rather stern expression.

"Um..."

"Ah, my apologies for the late introduction. I'm Ronitas. I'm the one in charge of this delegation."

"I see. You're the one. I'm Rei. As you probably know, I arrived in the Kingdom of Reburina ahead of time on Daskar's request."

"I was informed of that matter beforehand by Lord Daskar. ...However, according to what I heard from Lady Elena and Lady Marina, it seems things have become rather troublesome?"

Without seeming to mind the difference in speech or age, Ronitas smiled as he addressed Rei.

But that was only natural. Ronitas was merely a subordinate of Daskar, while Rei was a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname... one who had a high-rank monster, a Griffin, as his familiar, and furthermore, had formed a party with skilled individuals like Elena, Marina, and Vihera.

...The reason Byune's name wasn't among those Ronitas recalled just now was partly because Byune wasn't particularly prominent, and partly because, compared to the others, her skills inevitably fell short.

In any case, Ronitas held the firm belief that if Daskar ever had to choose between Rei and himself, Daskar would undoubtedly choose Rei.

For better or worse, Gilm was a town where skilled adventurers gathered, and Ronitas was well accustomed to that sensibility.

"Yeah. It's... well, it's pretty incredible."

Leading nearly a thousand people, especially those not particularly seasoned travelers, all the way to Gilm? That's hardly what you'd call easy.

"I hear that. I'll be discussing various matters with Lord Daskar about this, so come along, Rei. It'd be better if you were there in case something happens. And besides..."

Ronitas paused there, shifting his gaze to Milein, who was admiring Set.

Rei followed that gaze and instantly understood what he meant.

(As long as Set is around, Milein is useless, huh? Or rather, should I say that because Set is here, there's no need to be wary of our surroundings?)

In any case, given the current situation, moving on like this was out of the question. Rei and the others moved the carriage slightly off the main road.

So they could respond immediately if anything happened. ...And to avoid drawing the attention of those traveling the road.

The Set Cage could be stored in the Misty Ring, but the carriage couldn't be done that way.

Well, it could be done if they tried, but given their position as an envoy, it wouldn't be appropriate.

"Here? Or does this spot itch?"

Milein walked toward where the carriage had been moved, stroking—or more accurately, scratching—Set as she went.

Seeing Milein like this, many of the guards besides Sulunin and Ekril wore expressions of surprise.

To those unfamiliar with Milein, the stark contrast between the formidable, dignified female adventurer they knew—one considered among the finest young adventurers—and the Milein before them now was striking.

Given Milein's striking features, some had hoped this escort mission might offer an opportunity to get closer to her... but seeing how affectionately she treated Set now, many of those thoughts vanished.

While observing the men's interactions, Rei found himself impressed by the horses pulling the carriage.

He'd heard that some had departed from Daskar ahead of him and Rei, and that their carriage could reach considerable speeds. Yet, despite Set's proximity, the horses pulling that carriage showed no signs of agitation or fear.

Among the horses Daskar had prepared to pull carriages, many still feared Set.

Compared to them, this horse had clearly undergone training as rigorous as the one pulling Elena's carriage.

(Well, no matter how high-performance or special the carriage is, it's meaningless if the horses pulling it are useless nags. ...Actually, considering they came from Gilm, it wouldn't be strange if they used tamed monsters instead of horses.)

Rei watched the horses showing no sign of fear toward Set, but was called over by Ronitas of the envoy.

Gathered near the carriage were nearly ten people, led by Ronitas.

Given their presence here, it was clear they had all come as part of the envoy.

"Rei, I've explained the general situation to him briefly."

"...That was fast."

Rei had assumed he would be the one to explain, never expecting they would have already done so beforehand.

But if asked who could explain the situation more concisely, Marina or Rei, he would probably think Marina could do it more accurately, even if it was Rei.

"Better to get it over with quickly, right? So, we've decided to contact Daskar from now on. Elena."

"Mm."

Elena, meeting Marina's gaze, gave a small nod and took out the Paired Orb, activating it.

Within seconds, the female Ara appeared on the Paired Orb.

'Elena-sama!'

"Ara, I'm glad to see you're doing well."

After exchanging a few words with Ara, who joyfully called out Elena's name, Ara's image vanished from the Paired Orb.

Since the matter had already been discussed yesterday, she must have gone to fetch Daskar.

Normally, daytime would be a busy time for Daskar too, but the matter involving Rei and the others promised significant profit for Gilm as well.

Within a few minutes, Daskar appeared in the Paired Orb.

Seeing Daskar like that, Ronitas and the others were naturally surprised.

They had heard about the Paired Orbs, but this was their first time seeing them directly. And seeing Daskar projected within them was undoubtedly a shock.

Had it been Rei, his experiences in Japan might have made this less startling, but magic items possessing such capabilities were extremely rare in this Elgin.

"Ronitas, huh? Looks like you managed to safely reunite with Rei and the others."

"Yes. And, um... I heard about it from Marina-sama, but is it true?"

Ronitas, unable to simply call Marina by her first name after she had served as Guildmaster of the Gilm Guild for so many years, addressed her with the honorific "-sama."

...Though that likely had a lot to do with Marina being the sort who could stand up to Daskar herself.

"Yes, it's true. As you know, there have been various issues concerning Jaya. Have you heard about the circumstances?"

"Most of it. But I never imagined the retaliation would lead to such actions..."

Ronitas hedged his words, but the other members of the envoy present... no, even the guards who'd merely heard the story had to agree with Ronitas's assessment.

Who in their right mind would think that retaliating against a criminal organization would involve leading thousands of people on a journey from Mejogo to Gilm?

Then again, that's precisely why they're a Rank B party, he thought... as everyone present exchanged information.
