There was nothing particularly forceful about Elena’s voice.

But... even so, her voice resonated around her in a strangely compelling way.

The party hall was quite noisy, with many people chatting with other nobles or asking maids to bring them food or drinks.

Yet, even amidst all that noise, Elena’s voice undoubtedly carried.

“Wh… What is it?”

Although Lirin heard the voice right next to her, she was able to snap out of it and speak immediately—likely because she was jealous of Elena.

A woman’s pride—she didn’t want to look foolish in front of the very person she envied.

Driven by that pride, Leelin, though pushed back a few steps by the force of Elena’s words, managed not to make herself look any more foolish than she already had.

That said, if asked whether she could actually go head-to-head with Elena, the answer would be no.

Lirin’s voice trembled slightly, and though she would never admit it, fear lingered in the gaze she directed at Elena.

Realizing she had been speaking with such intensity, Elena exhaled, as if releasing the tension.

With Elena, the oppressive atmosphere that had been hanging in the air dissipated.

“Lirin, I know you resent me. But I will never tolerate you slandering Rei just because of that.”

“……What the hell……”

In response to Elena’s words, that was all Lirin could manage to say.

Lirin probably had plenty of other things she wanted to say.

But standing before Elena, she simply couldn’t bring herself to say them.

Elena glanced at Lirin and suddenly noticed that music had begun to play in the party hall.

It was music for dancing.

「Rei」

Elena muttered just that one word and turned her gaze toward Rei, but Rei, understanding what she meant with that look, turned his gaze toward Lirin.

“Is that okay?”

“I don’t mind. If people see you dancing with me, Rei, they’ll stop saying such stupid things. I just can’t stand seeing you made fun of. Or… don’t you want to dance with me?”

Elena looked at him with eyes that wavered slightly.

Elena had seen people like this countless times before—people who gossiped out of jealousy or made snide remarks.

So, if Lirin’s jealousy had been directed solely at her, she probably wouldn’t have gotten this angry.

But when Leelin realized her words had no effect on Elena, she shifted her target to Rei.

If it had been directed at her, she might have tolerated it, but seeing such behavior aimed at the man she loved—there was no way Elena, known as the “Princess General,” could put up with it.

That’s precisely why she wanted to show everyone that she was dancing with him—to prove that Rei was a worthy guest at this party.

……That said, although Rei had trained intensively with Elena and had become a competent dancer, the reality was that he could only dance with her.

If they’d spent more time training, things might have been different, but as it stood, Rei felt like he was entirely Elena’s personal dance partner.

In response to Elena’s gaze, Rei nodded back as if it were only natural.

“No, of course I’d love to dance with Elena.”

Saying that, Rei reached out his hand to Elena.

Lirin was already out of his line of sight.

“……Hmm.”

Elena, too, pushed Lirin completely out of her field of vision and took Rei’s hand.

And so, as if in time with the music filling the hall, the two of them head toward the center of the dance floor.

“Huh? Hey… wait a sec…”

“No way! Elena is dancing? And with… who?”

“Look, it’s him. Crimson, the guest of the Duke of Kerebel.”

“No, but… can an adventurer like him even dance?”

The nobles who had been chatting and laughing on the sidelines, seeing Elena and Rei arrive at the center of the dance floor, began whispering rumors about them.

Many believed that Rei dancing with Elena was a gross mismatch of social standing, and that he likely couldn’t even dance properly… but once Elena and Rei actually began to dance, those doubts naturally vanished.

“Beautiful… They’re dancing with such lightness, it’s as if they’re both dancing in the heavens.”

A woman—likely the daughter of some noble—murmured as she watched the two dance.

In fact, while Rei and Elena were undoubtedly dancing to the music, there was more to it than that.

Their rhythm and tempo were different from those of the other dancers.

And yet, they didn’t stand out in a bad way; they blended in perfectly.

On the contrary, before anyone realized it, they had drawn the others in, and Rei and Elena had become the center of the dance.

“Wow, that’s Rei for you. I never expected to see such an amazing dance.”

Watching Rei and Elena dance passionately, the man raised his glass of wine and murmured with a smile.

Ranigus Musey. He is the second son of the Musey family, a baronial House aligned with the Royal Faction.

Naturally, he was acquainted with both Elena and Rei. …Though, since he had gone to where Rei, Elena, and Ara were with Gaiska, his impression of Rei was by no means a good one.

Ranigus, acting as a noble of the Royal Faction, had been gathering information while conversing with nobles of the Noble Faction... but just as he took a break from that intelligence-gathering and was drinking some wine to wet his whistle, the dancing began.

Considering it a fine accompaniment to his drink, Ranigus enjoyed the wine while watching the two of them dance... and he took pleasure in seeing how those dancing around them were drawn into their performance.

“Well, what an interesting place to meet,”

A voice called out from behind Ranigus.

Savoring the taste of the wine, he turned his gaze toward the source of the familiar voice and saw a single, beautifully dressed woman standing there.

Teres Myls, the second daughter of the Count of Myls.

Naturally, Ranigus knew her well.

“Hello, Teres. Take a look.”

Saying this, Ranigus pointed to Rei and Elena, who were dancing with the wine glass he was holding, and Teres naturally turned her gaze in that direction.

“It seems Leelin got into a fight with Lady Elena. And apparently, she insulted Rei.”

“Ah… I see. So that’s why they’re dancing like that. Well, I think I get the gist of it now.”

He muttered this with a hint of exasperation, likely because Ranigus knew exactly what kind of person Leelin was.

A person who fancies herself the flower of high society.

No, normally, Lirin is undoubtedly a beauty, and rather than just pretending to be the flower of high society, it wouldn’t be strange for her to actually be treated as such.

But… when pitted against Elena, whose beauty is so radiant she could easily be called a goddess of beauty, the odds are stacked against her.

Standing next to Elena, even someone who prides herself on her beauty would inevitably be reduced to a supporting role.

In Lirin’s case, having been blessed with more than just average beauty and having been feted by everyone since childhood, she simply cannot bear to play second fiddle to Elena.

That is precisely why she harbors such intense jealousy toward Elena.

That said… Lirin challenging Elena is like a lizard challenging a dragon.

She is simply no match for Elena.

This was evident not only in terms of beauty, but also in terms of her charm as a person.

“To insult Rei in front of Lady Elena… It wouldn’t be strange at all if people called you out of your depth.”

Teres looked on in exasperation, yet a smile played on her lips.

Of course, that’s something only Teres—who had actually met Rei and Elena in person—could say.

If she hadn’t known anything, it’s not impossible that she might have made remarks insulting Rei in front of Elena.

That said, anyone who had gathered even a little information would have realized that Rei and Elena had a good relationship.

What was careless of Lirin was that she hadn’t bothered to gather that sort of information.

No, to be precise, Leelin did know that Elena had returned home with an adventurer named Rei—though perhaps that phrasing isn’t quite appropriate.

Still, the reason she said things that insulted Rei was probably because she had no idea Elena harbored such deep feelings for him.

(What a fool.)

Teres thought to herself, inwardly mocking Lirin, though there was hardly any contempt in her thoughts.

Lirin was undoubtedly the kind of person who could have become the belle of high society, but there was Elena.

Considering that misfortune, the emotion Teres felt toward Leelin was more one of pity.

She averted her gaze from Rei and Elena, who were performing a dance that captivated everyone around them, and turned her eyes toward Leelin.

Where Teres’ gaze falls, she can see a scene in which several women have gathered around Lirin.

This means that Leelin’s pretensions to being the belle of society are by no means self-serving; she is genuinely liked by others.

……That said, this applies strictly to the younger generation—those in their teens and twenties. There are, of course, separate groups consisting of older women… specifically, the wives of nobles.

In any case, as she watched Leelin and her entourage... Teres found herself hoping that the upcoming party would be at least a little more bearable.

Just as she was impressed by Rei and Elena’s dancing, so too were Elena’s parents, Liberte and Arcadia.

It wasn’t the first time they’d seen their daughter dance at a party.

But even so, seeing her dance with such obvious joy was a first for Liberte as well.

“I never thought Elena would dance with such joy,”

“I suppose so. But isn’t that because her partner is Rei?”

Arcadia replied to Liberte’s unbidden murmur.

Though somewhat reluctantly, Liberte had to admit that Arcadia was right.

Although he accepted her relationship with Rei, as a father, he still had mixed feelings about his adorable daughter bringing her boyfriend home.

“Still…”

He must have realized that if the conversation continued like this, it would put him at a disadvantage. After glancing around once more, Liberte spoke up.

“Elena and Rei’s dance was wonderful. I’ll admit that. But… do you think there’s anyone brave enough to dance after those two have finished?”

“That would be a bit difficult, wouldn’t it?”

Arcadia said this, covering her mouth with the fan she held.

A dance that magnificent—one that even people like Liberte and Arcadia would consider superb—had just been performed.

There surely weren’t many daredevils who would even consider trying to dance right after that.

“Worst-case scenario, you and I will dance…?”

Normally, Liberte isn’t particularly skilled at dancing.

As a noble, she possesses above-average skills, but she couldn’t possibly dance in a way that would captivate an audience like Elena and Rei were doing right now.

But precisely because of that, it was necessary for Elena and Arcadia—Elena’s parents—to dance so as not to dampen the mood.

In that sense, there was no doubt that Liberte and Arcadia’s dance would be just right.

“That’s right. I’ve been wanting to dance with you again for quite some time now.”

Arcadia’s gaze, filled with a lustrous glow, turned toward Liberte.

Perhaps due to his recent late-night efforts, that lustre was even more intense than the gaze Liberte was accustomed to.

Faced with that gaze, Liberte was momentarily at a loss for words... and, as if to cover it up, raised the wine glass in her hand to her lips.

While she was doing that, the dance eventually reached its climax… In the center of the dance floor, Rei and Elena continued dancing, each firmly supporting the other while also leading their partner.

Finally, just as the dance reached its peak, Rei took Elena’s hand, drew her into his embrace, and held her close… and then the music ended.

Silence fell over the party hall for a moment, but it was suddenly broken by the sound of applause.

Many of the nobles turn their gaze to see who is clapping… and upon realizing that it is Liberte and Arcadia, the others eventually join in the applause.

Watching this, Liberte gently extends his hand to Arcadia, who has been clapping beside him.

“Would you dance with me?”

“Yes. Of course.”

Arcadia smiled broadly and gently placed her hand over her husband’s outstretched one.
