Rei and Rent, along with Set and the dog, left the Dusk Wheat inn and headed toward the main gate.

Rei had wanted to stop by the guild to check on the progress of Gamelion's dismantling, but since Rent had already told him they were meeting at the main gate, he couldn't really insist on going there.

No, if it had been truly important business, he would have headed to the guild.

But in this case, it was merely something he'd prefer to know if possible; there was no real need to force himself to go all the way to the guild.

That's precisely why Rei agreed with Rent and was heading toward the main gate...

"Huh? What's up? Aren't we going to the main gate?"

Rent looked surprised and exasperated as he nodded back at Rei, who asked while holding a skewer.

"Well, yeah... but I never thought just walking around town would lead to this. I mean, I'd heard about it, but actually seeing it..."

Rent watched Set, who walked along happily eating dried meat.

Incidentally, Rent's appearance was quite different from when they met in the dining hall of the Dusk Wheat inn.

Back then, he'd only had his longsword, but now he was naturally clad in armor.

After leaving the Dusk Wheat inn, he had properly equipped himself at the inn where he was staying.

Standard leather armor made from monster hides and cold-weather gear to ward off the chill.

The slightly unusual part was the metal shield he carried on his back.

As Rent's gaze met mine, he seemed to notice my stare and spoke up, looking puzzled.

"What's up? It's not like shields are that unusual, right?"

"No, it is unusual. Among the adventurers I've met so far, not many use shields."

Then again, Rei hadn't been active as an adventurer for all that long.

...Though nearly two years had passed, so it couldn't exactly be called short either.

Even so, in those less than two years, Rei had been caught up in many incidents, and among them, adventurers wielding shields weren't particularly common.

Of course, compared to those adventurers clad in full plate armor, it was more common, but the weight issue was a significant factor.

That weight meant he couldn't carry back as much loot from monsters—magic stones, proof-of-kill parts, plus meat, mined ore, gathered plants, and all sorts of other stuff.

That thought must have shown in Rei's eyes. Rent, still carrying his shield on his back, shrugged his shoulders deftly.

"I get what you're saying, Rei, but for peace of mind in combat, this much is necessary. Besides, our enemy this time is a Gamelion, right? They've got high attack power."

"I won't deny that..."

Beyond the sharp fangs protruding from its mouth, ears that functioned like blades, and a tail that whipped like a whip, the Gamelion's body itself—roughly three meters long—could be considered a weapon.

Furthermore, its body was composed of dense muscle, and its temperament was ferocious.

Facing such a Gamelion, it's certainly not unreasonable for someone to need shield-like armor.

"That's the reason. I was always the more defensive front-line fighter in our party anyway."

"...Speaking of which, you mentioned joining us to hunt the Gamelion. From what you've said, it doesn't sound like you're planning to solo it, right?"

"Huh? Oh, of course. Actually, soloing at Rank C is incredibly rare. Unless you're a skilled fighter like you."

He said this enviously to Rei, who soloed despite being Rank B, let alone C, then got back to the topic.

"Well, my party's already prepped for winter. So we can each spend our time however we like."

"So, you're going after Gamelions in this cold? And for a girl, no less?"

"Ugh, wh-what's wrong with that!? I'm paying Rei properly for it, after all!"

Rento stroked his stubble, perhaps to deflect the topic.

Rei wanted to tease Rent a little more, but seeing the main gate ahead, he cut the conversation short.

Perhaps because the busiest part of the morning had passed, there weren't many people around.

It was probably because fewer people were coming in and out of Gilm this time of year, but that made the few gathered near the main gate stand out all the more.

"You're meeting inside the gate, not outside?"

"Nah, the paperwork should be done by now. Besides, it's better to wait by the gate than outside to avoid the cold wind. That's why... I mean, in your case, you should be using that adventurer's wisdom..."

...don't you learn or use that sort of adventurer's wisdom?"

Rent started to say, but stopped mid-sentence.

It might be different if he was part of a party, but Rei basically operated solo.

In other words, aside from meeting for quests, he probably had little experience meeting up with other adventurers.

...Actually, the truth was that thanks to the Dragon Robe, he didn't need to worry about the cold, but there was no way Rent could understand that.

"Huh? Hey, Rent. I told you I was coming, right? So why the horse-drawn carriage is one thing, but why the cart?"

Seeing the carriage and cart near the gathered group, Rei asked in confusion.

Indeed, if they managed to defeat Gamelion, transporting it would be impossible by normal means.

Item boxes, needless to say, and even the degraded copies circulating on the market cost a pretty penny, making them far from something adventurers could easily obtain.

Plus, loading Gamelion into the carriage body would make it reek of blood.

Given that, the simplest solution was indeed to use a cart...

But since someone suggested inviting him along, wouldn't that mean there was no need to prepare a cart?

At Rei's question, Rent scratched his head awkwardly.

"Well, actually, inviting Rei was my own decision."

"...I see. So it was mostly about trying to score points with Johanna."

"..."

Rent quietly averted his gaze at Rei's words.

Rei felt it was pointless to press further, but he felt compelled to say one more thing.

"Sure, it was effective in surprising her. But... that doesn't necessarily mean it'll lead to a good outcome, you know?"

"Huh? What do you mean?"

"I told you in the cafeteria... Well, you'll see."

Not understanding what Rei meant, Rent asked. But seeing is believing, Rei shifted his gaze forward... toward those within the former guerrilla squad who aspired to be adventurers—not all of them, but some.

At the end of his gaze, those who had noticed Rei's presence wore expressions of surprise... and not only that, figures were already running towards him.

"Set-chan, long time no see!"

The woman who approached, her face beaming with joy... Johanna rushed to Set's side and immediately began petting him.

That behavior was familiar to Rei from someone else.

(Avoiding meeting Mireine... that's probably impossible.)

The face of someone who acted similarly flashed through Rei's mind. He hoped it wouldn't lead to some strange conflict... though he knew it was probably wishful thinking.

"Cut it out. More importantly, have you two already left the lord's mansion?"

Hearing that voice, Johanna finally seemed to notice Rei. She hurriedly bowed her head toward him.

...Though, the fact her hand kept stroking Set while doing so probably revealed Johanna's true feelings.

"Captain Rei... I mean, Rei-san. Why are you here?"

"I kind of hoped you'd notice me when you saw Set here."

Sighing, Rei watched as the other former members of the guerrilla squad gathered around him.

"Rei, you look as energetic as ever."

"You idiot... It's Rei-san, okay? With that kind of power, illness just blows right over him."

"I'm not sure about that, but somehow, when I think of Rei doing it, I just accept it."

As the three of them exchanged words, Rei spoke up to the four of them, including Johanna.

"Long time no see... though that might be a bit of an exaggeration. It hasn't been that long since we parted ways. So, how are things now?"

"Ah, yes. Through Lord Daskar's connections, we've arranged to rent a large house. We plan to live together there with several people. Now that the move is done, those of us free are thinking of going to hunt down Gamelion."

Hearing that, Rei recalled the time he'd stored his belongings in the imperial capital of the Bestir Empire... and the time he'd been summoned by Daskar to explain the dungeon matter and retrieved the belongings he'd left stored in the Misty Ring.

"Johanna's belongings were quite substantial, I recall... Did you manage to sort through all of that?"

"...Well, somehow, someone helped me out."

The adventurers' glances flickered toward Rent, who was trying to find an opening to speak to Johanna.

Understanding immediately what had happened, Rei decided, out of samurai honor, to say no more.

(Carrying Johanna's luggage... That's nothing but hard labor. Classic case of weakness from being smitten.)

Glancing at Rent, Rei turned his attention back to the people before him.

"Well, never mind that. Since you're new to Gilm, I figured I'd lend a hand. Plus, Rent asked me to, so here I am."

"...Huh? Rei-san is helping us? Wait... Could it be... snow?"

"Snow could fall any time now, couldn't it? If you're going to say that, how about spears falling? Huh?"

"More like fire falling from the sky, especially with Rei-san involved..."

Seeing them fussing over not believing what Rei said, Rei smiled beneath his hood and opened his mouth.

"What if I actually made it rain fire or spears? I'm good at both, so I'll make it rain as much as you want."

Sensing the seriousness in Rei's words, the three hurriedly shook their heads.

"No, no way. We wouldn't want that. Right?"

"Absolutely not. Yeah, definitely not."

"Exactly, absolutely not at all."

Johanna, who had been admiring Set, tilted her head at the sight of the three men scrambling to speak.

"Huh? What's going on?"

Hearing Johanna's voice, the three men shook their heads as if to say, "Thank heavens!"

"Rei-san is going to help us with our work, you know. You're happy about that too, Johanna?"

"Huh? Of course I'm happy... but is that okay?"

As Johanna asked while stroking Set's body, Rei nodded in agreement.

"You guys still aren't really used to the area around Gilm, are you? Rent being here means things will mostly work out, but... Yeah, think of this as a parting gift from me, who led you as the mobile squad. Luckily, catching a Gamelion shouldn't be too hard right now... I hope."

"Oh, I heard about that. Rei-san, I heard you cleared that dungeon recently. That's why we can get Gamelions now, right?"

Rei nodded at the question from one of the men.

"Well, it was just a shallow dungeon that opened recently. Anyway, if you're going, let's get moving. Just so you know, my part ends at finding the Gamelion. Taking it down is your job."

Though Rei stated he wouldn't fight, Johanna and the other three didn't seem particularly displeased upon hearing this. They simply nodded in response.

Lent secretly wished he would help, but if Johanna, his target, accepted it, he couldn't very well complain.

(Besides, if Rei fought, he'd steal all the glory. I'd rather Johanna see me shine instead.)

Rent was also an adventurer of Gilm.

He understood deep down just how much power the small figure before him actually possessed, despite her appearance.

It was easy to imagine Rei dominating the fight against Gamelion.

After all, Rei had already crushed nearly fifty Gamelions during the dungeon run, and above all, as a former member of the mobile unit, he'd proven himself beyond measure during the Bestir Empire's civil war.

"Looks like we borrowed the cart, but what should we do? I could carry the monster corpses, including Gamelion, in my item box if you want."

Given that, no one suggested taking the cart, which would only be a hindrance during travel. In the end, the cart was promptly returned to where it had been borrowed.

"Well then, let's go. If we leave now, we should be back by evening. Oh, and just so you know, my help only goes as far as getting back to Gilm. I won't be assisting with dissecting Gamelion or anything like that. Keep that in mind. You're not exactly skilled at that sort of thing anyway."

"That's true. Then, I'll leave that to you. I'll count on you, Rei, to bring back the Gamelion."

And so, Rei, Set, Rent, Johanna, and her three companions—six people and one beast—began their mission to defeat the Gamelion.
