Walking beside Rei and Set, Zarust wore a look of slight dissatisfaction.

Despite having made plans the day before, Rei hadn't shown up at the guild no matter how long he waited.

It was only natural, since he ended up having to go pick him up himself, making the trip all the way to Auguste's mansion.

Rei spoke to Zarust.

"Sorry about that. I traveled all the way here from Gilm too. It's only natural I'm tired, right?"

Since they had traveled aboard Set, hardly any time had passed since leaving Gilm.

At the very least, the time it took Rei to travel was astonishingly short compared to the time it normally took to travel from Gilm to the Kingdom of Solrain.

Still, this was only his second trip to another country after the Bestir Empire... and while he'd traveled before, this was his first time moving purely alone with just one companion.

Even if he intended to act as he normally would, it wouldn't have been strange for some tension to linger within Rei.

He had met with Auguste and Zarust, but they camped that night. The next day, he ended up staying at Auguste's house and slept soundly.

"I get that you're tired. But honestly, playing with Set after waking up? I'm not sure about that."

"Well, now you mention it... I was actually trying to train with Set."

Running around to his heart's content with Set was nothing but play to Rei, but to ordinary humans, it was clearly equivalent to training.

Zalust seemed to understand this. He let out a small sigh and didn't press the matter further.

"Alright, fine. More importantly... how was yesterday?"

Rei tilted his head at the abrupt change of subject, but quickly understood what Zarust was referring to.

"You knew that couple was like that, didn't you?"

Just from those words, Zarust must have sensed what Rei was feeling. He wiped the displeased look from his face and gave a small smile.

"So, you went through that baptism too?"

"...Baptism, huh. Well, I guess I can't say I don't understand."

The sight of Auguste and Kathy whispering sweet nothings to each other right in front of Rei tested Rei's patience in more ways than one.

(That's what they mean by wanting to spit out sugar, I guess.)

Recalling last night, a sigh escaped Rei's lips.

If the food had been bad, he might have moved to camping with the Magic Tent, but unfortunately—or perhaps fortunately—Cathy's cooking was incredibly delicious.

"It's a path everyone who gets involved with Auguste has to walk. Just accept it. At least the food was good, right?"

"Yeah. No complaints there. Never thought I'd be eating fish in the middle of the desert."

Finding fish in an oasis was quite unexpected for Rei too.

Seeing Rei still visibly surprised even after a night had passed, Zarust looked slightly taken aback before his expression softened into understanding.

Fish could be caught at the oasis, but naturally, in this desert, fish was an expensive ingredient.

The surprise at serving such a luxury item... the desire to win Rei over to their side... and above all, probably the strongest reason: Auguste himself simply enjoyed the meal.

"So you ate fish from the oasis. ...I'm jealous."

Recalling the taste of fish he'd eaten before, Zarust swallowed hard.

Zalust was also a well-known adventurer in Gosh, renowned for his skill, but even he wasn't wealthy enough to eat fish whenever he pleased. ...Though if he cut back a bit on his hobby of frequenting brothels, that might be a different story.

But Rei, who could eat fish—river fish, admittedly—quite normally in Gilm, sensed Zarust's envious gaze yet couldn't truly grasp the depth of his longing.

(There's still quite a bit of seafood I bought with Emotion in the Misty Ring. Might be interesting to let him try some next time.)

Fish aside, what reaction would the inhabitants of Gosh show when seeing shellfish, squid, octopus, and other seafood?

And how they'd react when they actually tasted it, despite being repulsed by its appearance.

Even though he thought it a bit mean, Rei smiled at the thought as he walked through the town.

As he walked along thinking like this, he eventually arrived at the guild he had visited yesterday.

...However, Zarust didn't head for the guild's entrance, but rather the building behind it.

Without waiting for Rei to say anything, Set moved to the space for familiars and carriages and lay down.

"Huh, so this is how it's set up."

At Rei's muttered words, Zarust opened his mouth in puzzlement.

"Does the Kingdom of Mireana not have a dismantling site?"

"No, there is. It just doesn't feel like it's directly run by the guild. There were warehouses and such, though."

Rei recalled the time they'd transported a large number of Gamelions and reported this, though even that warehouse could easily handle monster dismantling if they wanted to use it.

Besides, many Gilm members have their own dedicated butchering buildings, and some even rent them out to make a living.

"So, while there are facilities like that, it's generally common to skin monsters outside the city."

"...I envy that. Around here, normal monsters aside, monsters like Sand Serpents are tough to skin outside unless the conditions are really favorable."

"Conditions?"

"Yeah. Being a desert, the sun's intense, and above all, the sand gets in the way. Of course, depending on the material, you might not need to worry about that. But when you want to process valuable materials, you often end up renting a facility like this one."

"This facility belongs to the Guild, but are there other similar facilities?"

"Quite a few, actually. But for something as large as a Sand Serpent... Outside the Guild, places like the Elema Trading Company or other major trading houses probably have them..."

Anything beyond that is difficult, Zarust implied.

As they talked, they moved forward, and eventually, Zarust stopped walking.

"Here it is."

"...It's huge."

What Zarust pointed to was a rather large building.

It seemed almost larger than the guild itself.

"Well, the desert has plenty of monsters even bigger than Sand Serpents. This is a facility the guild owns for butchering those kinds of monsters."

"...Was it really okay to borrow it for a Sand Serpent?"

No matter how big a Sand Serpent is, it's only about five meters long at most.

Of course, it's vastly larger than ordinary monsters, but even so, when asked if it matched the size of the facility now before Rei, he couldn't help but tilt his head.

"I know. There are places that would be just right normally. There are... but the Elema Trading Company, you see."

Just those words made it clear to Rei what Zarust meant.

Given Erema Trading Company's strong influence in Gosh, he probably didn't want to get involved with Zarust, who was close to Auguste.

(And maybe they also hold a grudge against me for stealing the Sand Ship.)

That was Rei's thought, but it was completely overthinking things.

For Daldora, who now knew Rei's true identity as the Crimson-named adventurer, avoiding hostility with Rei was the absolute priority.

Rather than provoking Rei here and earning his resentment, it was best—indeed, the only viable option—to wait for Rei to leave Gosh, hoping his claim of stopping by by chance was true.

In reality, Rei had come here because Marina suggested it, not by chance, and he had absolutely no intention of heading to another town.

His primary goal was to use Gosh as a base to hunt monsters found only in the desert and obtain magic stones.

"Come on in. Everyone else is already gathered. All we need now is for Rei to arrive... or more accurately, for the Sand Serpent to arrive."

With that, Zarust led Rei inside the facility.

"...Huh?"

Seeing Rei enter the facility and look around without seeming to sense anything unusual, Zarust voiced his confusion.

"Hey, Rei. Doesn't this strike you as odd?"

"...Huh? What is?"

He couldn't grasp what was being said, not even in the slightest.

Zalust tilted his head in puzzlement at Rei's tone.

"Inside this warehouse, it's a lot cooler than outside, right?"

"Huh? ...Oh, I see."

Rei pulled down the hood covering his face and wore a look of understanding.

Compared to the outside temperature, which was over thirty degrees and approaching forty, the inside of this warehouse was only around twenty degrees.

For the inhabitants of Gosh, it was comfortable... no, it was even a bit chilly.

Zalust couldn't understand why Rei showed no sign of noticing the temperature change at all upon entering such a place.

"This robe is a magic item. It has the ability to maintain a constant temperature."

Rei explained it in such terms, knowing Zarust wouldn't grasp the concept of it functioning like an air conditioner.

But that explanation seemed sufficient for Zarust. He shot Rei an envious look... truly, an envious look from the bottom of his heart.

Rei, receiving that gaze, showed no particular concern and directed his own gaze deeper into the facility.

"There's no point standing around like this. Shall we head further in?"

"...Yeah, okay."

Though slightly displeased, Zarust led Rei deeper into the building.

There, most of the adventurers who had been guarding Auguste's caravan were gathered.

"A few are missing, but they had jobs lined up even today, right after yesterday. Sorry, they didn't have the time."

"Well, being busy as an adventurer is a good thing, right?"

While responding to Zarust with those words, a question arose within Rei: Could it be that the ones currently absent were those connected to the Elema Trading Company... specifically, the ones who had been leaking information?

The possibility that someone among the merchants or guards had betrayed Auguste was extremely high.

That's precisely why, if someone was missing here, he couldn't help but think that way.

"Look, we don't have time for this... Get that Sand Serpent out already. It's huge. Dismantling it will take time. ...Well, since the head's missing, it should save a bit of time."

Following Zarust's words, Rei retrieved the Sand Serpent's carcass from the Misty Ring.

A murmur rose from the adventurers who saw it.

They knew with their own eyes that Rei possessed an item box.

They'd witnessed him stow it away after actually defeating the Sand Serpent, so it was only natural.

But... even so, seeing the Sand Serpent's corpse appear out of nowhere still left them utterly stunned.

Amidst this, the first to regain his composure was, unsurprisingly, Zarust.

"Alright, let's get started! Luckily, there's no tricky head to deal with, so it shouldn't take too long! First, we skin it! Don't forget this is material for our benefactor!"

His voice snapped everyone back to reality, and they each began their assigned tasks.

As Zarust said, the first task was to skin the Sand Serpent.

But with a Sand Serpent this size, skinning it inevitably took time.

If we could just hack away recklessly, it would be easy. But Sand Serpent hide is popular both as armor and as material for clothing.

Its resistance to blades and its striking beauty make it a first-rate material for leather armor and cloaks crafted in Gosh.

You insert the blade of a butcher's knife between the skin and flesh, carefully avoiding cuts to either, and peel the skin away.

This task is too difficult for those who have just become adventurers, so only those with a certain level of skill actually perform the butchering.

New adventurers hold the skin in place to prevent it from moving awkwardly and interfering with the skinner's work.

With more hands, the work naturally speeds up, and within an hour, the Sand Serpent's hide is completely stripped.

Since skinning the Sand Serpent takes the longest part of the process, once that's done, the rest goes quickly.

The meat was cut into suitable blocks for Set and stored in the Misty Ring one after another. Next, the internal organs were processed, and usable parts were stored in the Misty Ring alongside the skin.

Among the entrails, of course, were magic stones. Finally, Rei could obtain what he wanted most.

And finally, the Sand Serpent was reduced to nothing but bones... Bones that could also be used as alchemy materials, Rei thought as he stored them away in the Misty Ring.
