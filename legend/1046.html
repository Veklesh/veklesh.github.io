Rei and Set walk through the forest.

There was no one else around; just one man and one dog.

At first, they walked cautiously, alert to their surroundings, but now they weren't quite so wary.

Because, although they had entered the forest and started moving according to the team assignments Marina had chosen, there was absolutely no sign of monsters attacking Rei and Set.

(Probably the other teams aren't getting attacked by monsters either, just like us.)

Looking around, Rei thought about that.

Even straining his ears, all he could hear were birds chirping and the wind rustling through the trees.

He couldn't possibly believe monsters were in the forest.

"Could it be... since the World Tree recovered, there are no monsters left in here anymore?"

Thanks to the magic power infused by Rei, the World Tree had recovered significantly.

If that were the case, the barrier surrounding the area should naturally have become stronger too, making it impossible for anyone to enter the Lost Barrier.

"No, even if the Confusion Barrier has been strengthened, it wouldn't affect someone already inside the barrier, right?"

If the space between the Barrier Barrier and the Confusion Barrier was still affected by the Confusion Barrier, then Rei and the others should be lost too.

In other words, even if the World Tree recovered, the monsters and human-like beings currently trapped between the barriers would be helpless.

(Though the barrier should be stronger now that the World Tree has regained its power, so it's probably safer than before.)

As Rei muttered while scanning his surroundings, Set turned his round eyes toward him, asking, "What's wrong?"

"Nothing. Just... I was thinking that even if we search for Aerowings for Set's sake, we won't find any if things stay like this."

"Grrr..."

Set growled disappointedly at Rei's words.

He looked around, but being on the ground in the middle of a forest meant his view was inevitably blocked by the surrounding trees.

"Hey, calm down. If it comes down to it, we can just fly and search. They said there's a barrier up high, but right above the trees should be fine."

"Guru?"

Set looked at Rei questioningly. "Really?" Rei nodded, then changed the subject.

"Since we're finally acting on our own, why not try out some new skills?"

"GRRRRRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a delighted growl.

Learning the skill had caused the level of the same skill to rise consecutively.

Given that, it was only natural he'd want to test it out.

And it wasn't just Set. Rei, who had leveled up Flying Slash with Death Scythe, felt the same way.

As he looked around, wondering who he could test it on... suddenly, nearby bushes began shaking violently.

Both Rei and Set sensed it simultaneously... Could it be a monster!? With that tension, they both turned their gaze toward the violently shaking bushes.

Rei gripped the Death Scythe in his hand, and Set also assumed a stance ready to attack at any moment.

They waited silently for something to emerge from the bushes...

"Porururu!"

With that cry, what emerged from the bushes was a nine-tailed Carbuncle.

That scene, somehow familiar, made Rei sigh.

"What the hell are you doing out here alone? You were supposed to be with Earth and Marina!"

"Pororo? Poruu!"

"...I've seen this scene before, haven't I?"

Watching Pororo run around Set, Rei realized the source of that earlier sense of déjà vu.

Yes, Polo emerging from the bushes was the exact same scene as when Rei first met Polo and Earth.

"Poruru, Porororo!"

Somehow, his cheerful demeanor drew Set in, and he purred in response.

"Gurururuu, gururu"

Glancing sideways at the two of them growling affectionately together, Rei gave up on testing his skill.

He felt it probably wouldn't be a problem if Polo saw it, but at the same time, he worried that perhaps detailed information about his skill might be passed on to Earth.

Plus, if he suddenly tried out the skill, it might raise questions about why he was doing it.

(I don't know if Polo would even notice, but better safe than sorry.)

Watching Polo leap onto Set's back and let out a triumphant "Porurururu!" cry, Rei let out a small sigh.

It wasn't that he doubted Earth.

They'd only spent a little time together, but he understood Earth wasn't a bad guy.

It was a bit annoying how he went on and on about past heroes during meals, but if that was his personal hobby, so be it.

But... still, he'd only just met Earth, and it was difficult to trust him completely like he trusted Elena , Vihera , or Marina.

Then again, even though he'd known Marina for quite a while, he didn't trust her as much as Elena or Vihera.

This was purely a matter of time spent together.

After all, it was undeniable that during the journey to this forest and the interactions upon arriving at the settlement, Rei had developed feelings for Marina.

...Whether that affection is romantic or simply for someone trustworthy is beside the point.

"Polo, porururururu!"

Polo was running around on Set's back, moving to the top of his head the next moment, or sliding down his neck like a slide. He was treating Set's body like some kind of plaything.

(He's being quite hyper... Could it be he's upset about losing to Yellow in that race earlier?)

As Rei and Set finished absorbing the magic stones and headed toward the house Elena and the others were renting, a sudden race began, with everyone aiming for Set's back.

While Polo had the advantage in speed, the gap between Polo running on the ground and Yellow flying through the air proved larger than expected. Ultimately, Yellow reached the top of Set's back first and claimed victory.

Perhaps stung by that loss, Polo was now acting as if venting his frustration to his heart's content, running all over Set's body. Set growled slightly, looking a bit troubled.

"Grrr..."

Set shot a pleading glance at Rei for help, but Rei merely watched Set's flustered state with amusement.

Of course, if Set truly disliked it, he would probably shake Polo off or something.

The fact he didn't mean he must like Polo after all, despite everything.

"Well, he's the monster friend Set managed to make. Give him a little more time."

"Grrr..."

"What can you do," Set growled.

Truth be told, monster friends are surprisingly rare for Set.

The only one he could clearly call a friend at present was probably Yellow.

To begin with, tamers are few in number, and it's rare for monsters to be with people at all.

Considering that Rei and Set's bond wasn't actually taming but magic beast arts, and Elena and Yellow's relationship was that of familiar, then Poro was arguably the first officially tamed monster friend.

(I did meet a tamer back when I went to the port town before, but that was the last I saw of them.)

The image of that tamer who tamed an Ice Bird flashed briefly through his mind, but it vanished just as quickly.

Then, when I turned my gaze back to Set, he was no longer looking troubled. In fact, he had started playing with Polo on his own initiative.

"Ah... well, I can't check the skills anyway, so it can't be helped."

Watching the two of them, Rei muttered and sighed, resigned.

"Set, what we need to do right now is find the anomaly in the forest and, if possible, resolve it. Playing with Polo is fine, but keep an eye on that too, okay?"

"Grr? Grrrrr!"

"It's fine!" Set growled reassuringly, then led the way forward as if guiding Rei.

Naturally, Polo was perched on Set's back. Having played with Set enough to satisfy himself for now, he was constantly alert, scanning the surroundings from his vantage point.

"Well, looks like it's just me and the two of you now. ...But Polo, is it really okay to leave Earth behind? You're his familiar, so I think you should keep a close eye on him too."

"Polo? Porururu!"

Polo tilted his head at Rei's words, then let out a small cry.

Whether that cry meant he understood what Rei was saying, or was simply a sign of confusion... even Rei couldn't tell.

Still, Ray figured he must have grasped something, since Polo had at least made a sound. He continued following Set, scanning the area for any signs of trouble.

"There really isn't a monster in sight anywhere."

Hearing Rei mutter this while scanning the area, Set suddenly looked around as if he'd just realized something.

One of their original objectives this time had been to defeat Aerowings.

But playing with Polo had made him completely forget about it.

"Guruu! Gururu, gurururu!"

Set looked around, slightly flustered, but unfortunately, there was no sign of Aero Wing anywhere.

"Grrr..."

Seeing Set whimper sadly, Rei reached out to stroke his head... only to notice Polo perched on top of Set's head.

"Polo? Porururu..."

Leaving Polo chirping, Rei reached out and stroked Set's neck.

"Hey, Set. Aero Wing flies in the sky, so why don't we fly up here and look for it?"

"Guru?"

Set looked back at him, asking if it was okay. Rei nodded in agreement.

After all, he figured they wouldn't find anything if they kept walking on the ground while searching for monsters that flew through the air.

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Set let out a happy growl and turned his back toward Rei.

Rei immediately understood what he wanted and climbed onto Set's back.

Set took a few running steps, then flapped his wings and shot upward into the sky.

Of course, Set understood Marina's warning and didn't ascend too high.

He flew at a height just above the surrounding trees.

He scanned the area, searching for the distinctive shape of the four-winged Aerowings, but unfortunately, a quick glance around yielded nothing.

"...Not here. Could it be the World Tree's healing is affecting them?"

"Poro-ro-ro-ro! Poru-ru-ru-ru!"

Near Rei, who was muttering to himself, Pororo kept letting out loud cries of excitement.

For Polo, who usually ran on the ground, flying through the air was clearly something special.

Especially since he'd just lost to Yellow flying through the air earlier—that was undoubtedly related to his current excitement.

"Ah, look. Calm down. If you fall from this height... Wait, they said it's a rare species of Carbuncle. Maybe falling from this height wouldn't be a problem?"

A rare species of Rank B monster... meaning it possessed power equivalent to a Rank A monster. Falling from ten or twenty meters couldn't possibly be fatal.

Still, the worried look on its face as it restrained its movements to avoid falling—was it because even high-rank monsters looked small?

Satisfied with the soft feel of the spot he was gently holding, he suddenly spotted something moving below.

For a moment, Rei thought, An Aerowing!? But Aerowings were flying monsters; they didn't exist on the ground.

Of course, that doesn't mean they never land, so it wasn't entirely impossible.

But even so, to Rei's eyes, it was almost a given that a monster moving on the ground couldn't be an Aerowing.

In fact, upon closer inspection, what was there wasn't a monster at all, but a human.

Moreover, it was someone familiar to Rei.

「Set」

"Guruu"

Understanding what Rei meant with that brief call, Set descended straight toward the ground.

"Whoa! ...Polo! Where the hell did you suddenly go? I was looking for you! Apologize to Marina later. She saw you disappear and told me to go find you."

Earth scolded Polo, though he was surprised to see Set come down.
