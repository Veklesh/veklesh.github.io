After finishing their discussion with Unchu, Rei and the others passed through the tavern they had just been in to head outside.

Having spent a considerable amount of time discussing matters, it was already past mid-afternoon.

Naturally, the patrons who had been eating at the tavern had vanished, leaving the place in a temporary lull until the busiest evening hours.

As Rei and the others stepped outside the tavern, someone immediately approached them.

Rei had sensed the presence, but knowing exactly whose it was, he accepted it without any particular guardedness.

"Gurrrrrrr!"

The owner of that presence... Set, upon seeing Rei emerge from the tavern, dashed straight toward him.

Since Set had been lying down not far from the tavern to begin with, it didn't take him long to reach Rei's location.

...Though, the sudden rise of the three-meter-long Set did startle those who had been keeping their distance.

"Ahaha. Hey, stop rubbing your face against me like that."

Rei didn't mind being doted on by his important partner, Set.

He smiled happily as he stroked Set, who was rubbing his face and body against him.

There was no trace on Rei's face of the thoughts he'd been having just moments before about how to attack the underground facility in Mejougo.

Though Rei was genuinely happy, playing with Set from the bottom of his heart, Elena addressed him.

"So, Rei. What are your plans for today? Are you heading out to scout... Mejou... no, over there?"

"Ah... Yeah. What should we do?"

Rei pondered while dealing with Set, who was rubbing his face against hers.

Ideally, before launching a surprise attack using the Setokago, he wanted to fly over Mejougo once to scout out the location of the underground facility's entrance beforehand.

However, given that Jarya had prepared, or could craft, quite a few magic items, it couldn't be ruled out that they were monitoring the skies somehow, even without a barrier.

Especially since Rei had seen Unchu's expression turn quite stern when Rei showed him the magic item entrusted to him by Sharia.

That said, the information they obtained wasn't vastly different from what Sharia had already told them.

Fundamentally, anyone forced to wear that slave collar had their will sealed.

Well, it probably wasn't quite that extreme in reality, but forcing someone who didn't want to be a prostitute to bend their will and make them want to be one themselves? To Rei, that could only be described as bending their will.

In other words, once that slave collar was fastened, they wouldn't even think of running away.

Except for exceptions like Sharia, where some irregularity caused the slave collar's effect to fail.

"If there's nothing specific we need to do, why not go? Even if we're scouting, if we fly higher than usual, I don't think we'll be detectable from the ground."

"Vihera is right. And if you use the Set Cage, it should be hard to spot you from the ground looking up."

"Ah... I see."

Marina's words made Rei recall the Set Cage's capabilities.

Since it had the ability to change color to match the surrounding scenery, it would likely be harder to spot than a regular Set flying overhead, even when viewed from below.

"It'll take time for Unchu to make contact anyway, so I think it's not a bad idea to get moving just in case."

When Elena said it too, Rei couldn't really say no.

Well, Rei could have refused if he really wanted to, but he did want to thoroughly check on Mejougo's situation.

Knowing exactly what kind of place Mejougo was versus not knowing would make a huge difference if something unexpected happened once they took action.

Rei had walked around and checked the terrain when he went to Mejougo yesterday, but there was no way he could have confirmed all the geography after just a few hours of walking.

"Alright. Then I'll take you up on that. Luckily, Set is right here with us, so there's no need to go back to the inn."

"Guru?"

Realizing Rei had called his name, Set turned his gaze toward Rei and purred, as if to say, What is it?

"I'd like you to fly a little closer, okay?"

"Guruu!"

Set responded with a clear "Of course!"

Hearing Set's growl, several people who had been watching from a distance hurriedly left.

(Wonder how many of those had Jaya's influence on them. Or maybe not Jaya, but possibly subordinates of some bigwig in the Kingdom of Reburina?)

The news that Rei and the others had entered Rossi had surely reached the upper echelons of the Reburina Kingdom as well.

Moreover, many within the Reburina Kingdom's upper echelons had connections to Jaya.

To such people, the existence of high-ranking adventurers with nicknames could only be seen as nothing but a nuisance.

But just because they're a nuisance... And even though they understood they had no chance of winning no matter how hard they struggled, they couldn't afford not to monitor Rei and the others' movements.

No, precisely because victory was impossible, they needed to watch Rei and his group's movements even more closely.

If they made the reckless mistake of antagonizing Rei and his group, it would spell nothing short of ruin.

...If they were ordinary adventurers, even in a small country, the power of nobility could handle it.

Indeed, adventurers operating in the Kingdom of Reburina had always been dealt with that way until now.

But Rei was a different story.

He was known as a high-ranking adventurer with a notorious nickname, and rumors spread far and wide that he showed no mercy whatsoever to anyone who crossed him, even if they were nobility.

Normally, most nobles would dismiss such talk as mere rumor. But in Rei's case, there was the undeniable fact that he had actually laid hands on nobles countless times before.

Moreover, as the information spread, it became increasingly embellished... Some rumors even claimed, 'Rei harbors a grudge against nobles and takes pleasure in tormenting and torturing them.'

Such an entity could not be left unchecked.

Furthermore... making matters even more troublesome for the nobles, the symbol of the Noble Faction, Princess General Elena Kerebel, was acting in concert with Rei, making it impossible for them to act rashly.

Despite all their talk of nobility and grandeur, the Kingdom of Reburina was, at its core, nothing more than a vassal state of the Kingdom of Mireana.

And among the countless vassal states, it was a minor power whose national strength and military might ranked near the bottom.

If nobles from such a minor state were to harm Princess General Elena, the symbol of the Noble Faction—one of the three major factions within the Kingdom of Mireana—well... it was certain their heads would roll, in the most literal sense.

Furthermore, while this information is still known only to a select few, Marina, one of Rei's party members, is also a fairly well-known figure within the Adventurers' Guild.

And while no one in the Kingdom of Reburina knows this yet, there is Vihera, the former princess of the Bestir Empire.

Regarding this, it was probably better that she didn't know.

(Well, if they make a move, it'll be helpful for us... But will Unchu be okay? Well, the fact they let us through so openly probably means they've prepared some countermeasures.)

With these thoughts running through his mind, Rei parted ways with Elena and the others and headed for the main gate of Rossi.

If they were to scout Mejougo from the air, they first needed to exit Rossi.

Leaving Rossi wasn't particularly difficult.

If it had just been Rei alone, he might have gotten into trouble.

But seeing Set with Rei, there probably wasn't anyone foolish enough to pick a fight.

If this had been a place where stronger adventurers gathered, he couldn't rule out the possibility of someone overestimating their own strength and picking a fight.

Once they smoothly exited Rossi, Rei briefly rode Set away from the spot.

Using the Set Cage to conceal their surroundings was one thing, but doing so here would have been utterly unnatural.

(I should have brought someone along. Well, since you can't see down from inside the Set cage, they'd probably just be bored anyway.)

Slightly regretting coming alone with only Set, Rei asked Set to veer off the road leading to Mejougo and land in a nearby grove.

If this were the frontier of Gilm, they'd have to be wary of monsters, but fortunately, while rural, this wasn't the frontier.

Thanks to that, there was no need for particular monster vigilance, allowing him to pull the Set Cage out of the Misty Ring.

Of course, outside the frontier, monsters were replaced by bandits... This road connecting Mejougo and Rossi lay under the control of Jayah, the largest underground organization in the entire country of Reburina.

To engage in banditry in such a place would require either utter recklessness, complete ignorance of the world, or confidence in one's ability to fight Jaya.

"...Yeah, that's what I thought."

Hearing the scream, Rei sighed.

Of course, that scream wasn't necessarily from someone being attacked by bandits.

Monsters, though few in number, were present in these lands. Even if it wasn't monsters, it was entirely possible someone was being attacked by wild beasts.

But... Rei understood that scream belonged to someone being attacked by bandits.

Because after the scream, a vulgar laugh followed.

"Guru?"

Beside the cage, Set growled, as if asking, "What are you going to do?"

If Rei were an ordinary adventurer in the Kingdom of Reburina, fleeing would be the only option here.

Adventurers in this country of Reburina were generally low-level, and not many could handle multiple bandits alone.

And since he was here on reconnaissance for Mejougo, causing unnecessary trouble would be tantamount to suicide.

But... this was Rei.

For Rei, a Rank B adventurer with a nickname, one or two bandits... or even ten or twenty? It made little difference.

"Let's go."

Stowing the newly deployed Set Cage back into the Misty Ring, Rei uttered only that brief command.

If it were someone he absolutely had to abandon, Rei would leave them behind here.

But since the situation wasn't that dire right now, it was only natural he'd want to help someone he could save.

...Of course, there was also the expectation that someone causing trouble in Jarya's backyard might have valuable information. Above all, hunting thieves was practically a hobby for Rei.

It was a hobby that served a practical purpose: reducing the number of people attacked by thieves while also lining Rei's pockets.

A man with such a hobby as Rei was not about to let his prey escape here.

"Guruu!"

At Rei's command, Set let out a short cry and dashed through the woods.

Running through wooded areas like forests or groves is quite challenging for the unaccustomed.

While running, one must plan their route, balance carefully to avoid tripping over fallen leaves, branches, stones, or nuts, and steer clear of exposed tree roots.

There were many other things to watch out for, but Rei and Set continued running as if none of that mattered at all.

After running for perhaps a minute... they soon spotted a young man running toward them.

Behind him, five men armed with long swords and daggers gave chase.

At a glance, Rei understood the relationship between them.

No, anyone could understand just by seeing this scene, not just Rei.

Pretending to be attacked by bandits to infiltrate the enemy's ranks... wasn't an impossible tactic, but it certainly wouldn't happen in a forest like this.

"Over here! Run this way! I'm on your side!"

The man, who had been staring at Rei with despair, found a glimmer of hope in the voice that came from Rei's mouth.

If Set had been here, the man wouldn't have trusted Rei's words so easily, but Set wasn't present.

He had split off from Rei mid-run and should be circling around behind the bandits by now.

"Huh!? Watch out! You run too!"

The man thought he'd been saved for a moment by Rei's voice, but upon realizing Rei was a small figure, and alone, he shouted.

But Rei didn't seem particularly bothered by the man's voice... She simply ran past him and lunged at the bandit who had been chasing the man.
