The scene before him was unbelievable to Marquis.

Even when he and all his like-minded companions from Gilm had teamed up against it, they hadn’t been able to so much as scratch the monster known as Gamelion. Yet this small figure who had suddenly appeared—Rei, nicknamed “Crimson”—had severed Gamelion’s neck with the same casual ease as if he were mowing down some weeds.

For a moment, Marquis wondered if the scene before him was a dream or something.

But the feel of the spear in his hand… and the intense, metallic stench of blood pouring from the severed head as it rolled across the ground, unable to be stopped even after being decapitated—all of this told Marquis with overwhelming certainty that the scene before him was no dream.

“A-are you okay? You’re not dead, are you?”

Relieved that Gamelion had been killed, Marquis called out to his comrades behind him.

“Y-yeah. I’m okay, somehow.”

“I’m fine too. But Orbinié is still unconscious.”

“She got blown away by Gamelion’s charge. …I hope she didn’t break any bones. The potions we have aren’t that effective for that kind of thing.”

“True. More importantly… that’s Crimson, right? Unless I’m seeing things…”

"Yeah, it is."

The man was about to say that, but before he could finish, a violent, dull thud echoed through the area.

“Huh!?”

Hearing that sound from behind them, Marquis and his companions reflexively turned their gaze toward the source.

……If the Gamelion they had been fighting just moments ago were still alive, they wouldn’t have had a moment’s respite.

But fortunately… truly fortunately, the Gamelion that Marquis and his companions had been fighting had been killed effortlessly by Rei.

That was precisely why they were able to look behind them at the source of the sound.

And when they did, they saw the Gamelion being blown through the air for over ten meters, bouncing off the ground repeatedly… and then rolling around, scraping the ground as it went.

A Gamelion they themselves couldn’t defeat even when fighting with all their might.

The shock of witnessing two of these creatures being effortlessly defeated in quick succession was profound.

Faced with a being clearly in a different league from themselves, whether out of fear or admiration, Marquis swallowed the saliva that had gathered in his mouth.

While Marquis and the others looked like that, Rei, who had defeated the first Gamelion, didn’t seem particularly bothered.

For Rei, being treated this way was by no means unusual.

“Set, looks like you took care of that one too.”

“Grrrrr!”

Set, noticing Rei’s voice, let out a joyful growl.

Was there a hint of pride in that growl because he’d managed to hold back?

Before the battle with the Gamelion began, Rei had told him that if he unleashed a devastating blow like he usually did, there wouldn’t be much left to eat… but the fact that he’d managed to take down the Gamelion without doing that must have made Set all the happier.

Having just taken down the Gamelion himself… Glancing at Set as he went to retrieve the Gamelion that had been blown far away, Rei addressed Marquis and the others once more.

“So, then… just to be sure, I’ll ask one more time: that Gamelion belongs to me, right?”

“You’re not going to say I stole your prey, are you?” Rei asked again. Speaking on behalf of the group, Marquis nodded in response.

“Ah, yeah. Of course. You saved us, Rei. We’re not about to be so selfish as to demand you hand over the kill.”

Rei isn’t particularly surprised that Marquis knows his name.

After all, given that he practices the Two-Spear Style with the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, it wouldn’t be strange for him to know Rei’s identity, especially with the griffin Set right there.

If they didn’t recognize Rei even with all that… well, that would be a problem far beyond whether he was a fake or not.

That said, a large number of adventurers—all sorts of people—are currently gathering in Gilm.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange if there were some among them who knew absolutely nothing about the common sense of adventurers.

At the very least, the Marquis and his men standing before Rei seem to understand those basics, so it’s a relief that this didn’t turn into an unnecessary commotion for Rei.

“I see. In that case…”

“Growl? Grrrrrrrrr!”

As if to interrupt Rei’s words, Set’s growl rang out.

But it wasn’t a growl meant to intimidate enemies or warn of danger; it was a growl filled with joy.

“What is it?” Rei turned his gaze toward Set and saw several adventurers approaching them from the distance… When he spotted one of them, he understood why Set had let out such a joyful growl.

One of those adventurers was a figure Rei recognized as well.

(Brasso mentioned that Fron was hunting Gamelions.)

He recalled the words of the dwarf he’d met at the guild.

One of those adventurers—Fron, who seemed to be leading the others—stroked Set, who was chirping happily at the sight of him.

A few seconds later, Fron led the other adventurers over to Rei.

Meanwhile, Set is walking right beside Fron, carrying a Gamelion in his mouth, and the adventurers led by Fron are looking at him with envy.

Since the Gamelion hunt is already nearing its end, the number of Gamelions remaining in this area is dwindling.

Amidst this, Rei had taken down two Gamelions at once—one he had killed himself and one that Set had killed.

As adventurers who have come here to hunt Gamelions, they couldn’t help but envy him.

Of course, that doesn’t mean they could actually ask Rei to give them any of it.

“Hey, Rei. Two Gamelions? I’m jealous. I wish I could have some of that luck.”

At those words from Fron, the adventurers—including Marquis—stopped in their tracks.

Fron was older than Marquis and the others, but she still had a face that could easily be described as beautiful.

In fact, she was the kind of beauty who, if she were so inclined, would likely have no trouble finding a man.

It would be unreasonable to expect anyone not to be surprised when such a beauty suddenly refers to herself as “I.”

That said, Rei knew that Fron had a tomboyish personality that didn’t quite match her looks, so he wasn’t particularly surprised.

“I heard from Brasso, but so you really were hunting Gamelions, huh?”

“Did you run into Brasso?”

Fron’s face twisted slightly at the mention of Brasso’s name, but the expression vanished just as quickly.

“Yeah, at the guild. That’s where I heard about you.”

“...That bastard.”

Fron muttered a single word with a look of resentment, but the expression vanished just as quickly.

He probably realized that now was not the time to be dwelling on his partner.

“So… what about you?”

As Fron asked, glancing toward Marquis and the others, Rei directed his gaze at the headless corpse of the Gamelion, which was still emitting a strong, rusty iron stench.

“I saved him while he was fighting that Gamelion.”

“I see. …If you were going to do that, you might as well have come over to help us.”

He cast a look of pity—and envy—toward Marquis and the others… and then realized that the unconscious woman was seriously injured.

“Halkis, give that unconscious woman a potion.”

“……Huh? Are you serious?”

The man with the bow, called Harukis, asked in return, as if he hadn’t expected to be told to do such a thing.

But Fron didn’t find Harukis’s attitude amusing; he shot him a sharp look.

“What’s the matter? Can’t you do as I say?”

“Huh!? O-okay. I get it. I’ll use the potion right away!”

Glared at by Fron, the man hurriedly approached the fallen female adventurer, pulled out a potion, and forced her to drink it.

Normally, it’s applied directly to the wound… but the unconscious female adventurer had been struck by Gamelion’s body slam.

Since there were no obvious external injuries, even if she had a fracture or something similar, it was necessary to have her drink it directly to treat it.

...Of course, administering it orally is more effective and provides continuous healing effects for a while.

However... in exchange, the woman’s sense of taste would be destroyed for a while.

Still, since it saves her life, I suppose it’s better than dying.

“Ah, thank you!”

Marquis bowed his head toward Fron.

Seeing Marquis like that, Fron let out a slight smile.

To sincerely thank someone who saved a comrade.

To an outsider, it might seem obvious, but there are those who truly can’t do it—especially among rough-and-tumble types like adventurers.

But Marquis was truly grateful, from the bottom of his heart.

Realizing this, Fron smiled… and the other adventurers following him nodded in agreement.

“So, what are you guys going to do now, Fron? Are you still going to look for Gamelion?”

“Of course. We haven’t taken down a single one today. …Rei took down two, huh? I’m jealous.”

Fron cast an envious glance at Rei from the bottom of his heart.

Fron’s companions, too, were looking at Rei with the same envious gaze as Fron.

“Well, I’ve got Set, after all.”

“Growl!”

As Rei says this while petting Set’s head, Set lets out a happy meow.

“I know, it’s really enviable. …Well, there’s no point in staying here like this anyway, so I guess we should go look for Gamelion soon. Our numbers are already dwindling, so if we don’t hurry, the others might snatch it away from us.”

With that, Fron tried to walk away, but...

“Wait a second.”

Rei called out to Fron’s back.

“What do you want? Like I just said, we need to find the Gamelion quickly, so please keep it brief if possible.”

“Right. Then I’ll keep it short. …Could you take these guys with you?”

“……What?”

“Huh?”

Everyone present voiced the same question.

However, the meaning behind that question differed between Fron’s group and Marquis’s group.

From Fron’s group’s perspective, they were resentful about why they had to take such a burden along.

Of course, from their earlier exchange, even Fron could tell that Marquise and his group were good people.

But even so, they were frustrated, wondering why they had to look after Marquis and his group.

Adventurers are responsible for themselves.

Marquis and his group came to hunt Gamelion because they believed they could defeat it on their own, so that’s no reason for Fron to go out of his way to take on the burden of looking after them.

To begin with, just using that potion earlier was already a generous gesture on his part.

On top of that, asking him to make further concessions is, for Fron... And the others also look displeased at Rei’s words.

“Um… Huh?”

As for Marquis and the others, they haven’t grasped the flow of the conversation at all.

They don’t understand why Rei would go to such lengths for them.

That said, Rei’s proposal isn’t bad at all; in fact, for Marquis, the terms couldn’t be better.

“Just let me ask. Why would I go out of my way to do something like that?”

Fron asked, looking at Rei.

Rei and Fron know each other.

But even so, Fron has no intention of accepting a request that would clearly put him at a disadvantage.

After all, even though this was a temporary party formed to hunt Gamelions, he was in the position of party leader.

As long as he was leading his comrades, he had absolutely no intention of meddling excessively in a way that wouldn’t benefit the party.

“To put it simply, it’s just because I like these guys…”

“In that case, you don’t need to go out of your way to ask me—Rei could just go with them, couldn’t he?”

“That might be true. But my situation is different from that of a typical adventurer.”

“...I see.”

Fron had no choice but to accept that explanation.

It’s certainly not common for an adventurer to rise to Rank B and earn a nickname within just a few years of registering.

Precisely because they understood that, neither Fron nor anyone else could argue against it. Still…

“Still, as I’ve said many times, I don’t have to do what you ask. There’s no benefit for me.”

“……Benefits, huh? Benefits. Well, then. I won’t help you defeat Gamelion, but I’ll tell you where he is—just this once. How about that?”

At those words, Fron shot Rei a sharp look.
