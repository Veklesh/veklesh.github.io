A blade possessing enough power to shatter everything passed right beside Vihera.

Close by... Normally, one might call it close by, but considering Vihera's usual evasive maneuvers against enemy attacks, it was clearly an exaggerated movement.

But she wasn't doing it without reason...

"Troublesome, huh!"

Seeing that the claymore he dodged hadn't struck the ground, yet somehow multiple gashes had appeared in the earth, Vihera spat out the word with obvious annoyance.

...Yet a faint smile played at the corners of her mouth. Despite her words about it being troublesome, it was clear she was enjoying this very trouble right now.

The Magic Sword named Wind Scales appeared to be nothing more than an ordinary claymore to an outside observer.

Generally speaking, a claymore is a type of greatsword, with a length from hilt to tip approaching two meters. It is a weapon used less to slice enemies and more to crush or smash them with its immense weight.

But the characteristics of the Magic Sword Dias wielded didn't end there.

Already massive, its blade was enveloped in wind... a wind that could only be described as sharp.

As a result, even without direct contact, the wind enveloping the blade itself slices and tears at the surrounding area.

The Claymore, already a greatsword, had its attack range further expanded by the invisible wind it carried. For Vihera, this made it an exceedingly difficult opponent to fight.

Moreover, Dias was a highly skilled fighter himself, never relying solely on the power of Wind Scales.

"WAAAAAAAH!"

With a battle cry, he forced the Wind Scales into a sweeping horizontal strike.

It was a blow relying purely on brute force, yet its sheer power and sharpness made it seem impossible for any ordinary human to evade.

But then again, Vihera was far from ordinary either.

He dodged the sweeping blow of the Wind Scale by leaping backward.

The lightness of his leap, combined with the clothes he wore, made it look almost like a dancer performing a dance.

Vihera's movements embodied the saying that martial arts and dance are one and the same.

The wind scales sliced through the air at a speed that shattered the atmosphere, passing through the spot where Vihera's body had been just an instant before.

It was likely thanks to the timing of his backward leap being just right. It was precisely because of that quick anticipation that Vihera was able to evade the wind-wrapped claymore's attacks one after another, even with a certain degree of ease.

Well, it probably just looked like he was dodging with ease from the outside.

That was largely because Vihera wore a smile on his lips.

It was precisely because Vihera enjoyed combat that he fought with a smile like this, but in reality, he wasn't that relaxed.

In terms of pure physical ability, Vihera was clearly superior.

That much was evident from the way he kept evading Dias' attacks.

But what overturns that advantage is Dias's Wind Scales.

For Vihera, whose combat style relies on evading enemy attacks with extreme precision and then landing counterattacks, the inability to execute that extreme evasion is a major disadvantage.

Simply put, they were poorly matched in combat.

Even so, Vihera's high physical prowess and sharp combat instincts had allowed him to successfully weather the Wind Scales' attacks unscathed.

Taking advantage of the distance between them, Dias pointed the tip of his Wind Scale sword at Vihera, who remained on guard, ready to counter any attack without letting his guard down.

"What's the matter? Just running away? If you're the Vihera I've heard about, I'm told you wouldn't hesitate to attack just to enjoy the fight."

Dias declared with a smile on his bearded mouth.

It was a clear provocation, but Vihera deliberately took the bait.

"Is that so? Then perhaps I should fight a little more seriously. If we're going to fight anyway, an opponent who puts up a decent fight is preferable, don't you think?"

"...Oh? Are you saying you were holding back against me?"

"It's not quite holding back. If we're going to fight, wouldn't you rather fight someone strong? I was just confirming that."

Hearing Vihera's words—that she had deliberately let him fight freely to gauge his strength—Dias opened his mouth in disbelief, his Wind Scales ready.

"You live up to your reputation as a battle maniac. Are you really the princess of the Bestir Empire?"

"Formerly, yes. But with a personality like mine, living quietly in a castle just wasn't my thing."

"Ha! That's rich. You wouldn't have gone hungry in the castle. You abandoned that just to seek battle?"

"Exactly. I don't expect anyone to understand, but this is how I live. Besides... it's precisely because I've lived this way that I was able to meet Rei. Had I lived as a princess, I never would have met someone like him."

Dias let out a small sigh at Vihera's words, which had shifted from discussing battle mere seconds ago to sounding like lovey-dovey talk.

He really didn't want her doing anything that might break his concentration in the middle of a fight.

...Still, even as they exchanged such words, they were both making movements to probe for openings in each other. If either showed a decisive weakness, the next instant would likely see the kind of fierce combat that had just unfolded.

Precisely because he understood this, Dias recognized the Vihera before him as a woman he couldn't figure out.

"Is that so? That's enviable. But... if you've already grasped a woman's happiness like that, you wouldn't need to come all the way to a place like this to fight, would you?"

"Oh, that's out of the question. Like I said before, since I get the chance to fight such a strong opponent, I'm going to make sure I enjoy it properly."

"...Enjoying battle, huh? That feeling is beyond me."

For Dias, fighting was merely a means to earn his livelihood.

For that very reason, he had never found the act of fighting itself interesting... at least not since he'd started living in these slums.

Still, he never stopped his daily training because he understood that strength was necessary for his survival.

He couldn't fathom Vihera's desire to enjoy fighting.

His honest feeling was that if he could live comfortably, fighting wasn't necessary at all.

"Is that so? Then... I'll teach you that enjoyment, even if just a little!"

Shouting sharply, Vihera kicked off the ground, closing the distance between himself and Dias.

With his high explosive power and flexible muscles, he approached Dias as if to dazzle him, repeatedly subtly slowing or speeding up his pace.

Through this fluid alternation of pace and intricate footwork, Vihera's movements appeared to waver momentarily to Dias's eyes.

Since she was moving her body, it wasn't strange for her to appear shaky.

Nevertheless, Dias's eyes unmistakably perceived a waver in Vihera's movement.

To those without sufficient skill, Vihera would likely appear to be moving normally.

But those possessing a certain level of skill could clearly grasp the meaning behind Vihera's current movements.

His movements were so disorienting that reactions were slightly delayed.

That instant when you react simply because you see it.

And in a battle between the strong, that single instant could decisively sway the outcome.

"Dammit!"

Vihera's unexpected movement disrupted Dias's strike, wielding the Wind Scales.

Dias was fortunate that the Wind Scales, a Magic Sword, possessed a larger and wider attack range than its blade alone.

Thanks to that, even when Vihera's bewildering footwork disrupted the distance, the attack never completely missed.

"Heh heh."

Catching the wind-blade strike with his left gauntlet, Vihera smiled as he gently extended his right hand toward Dias' torso.

"Huh!?"

The moment that seemingly ordinary hand—one that should be described as delicate as a whitebait fish—was about to touch his torso, a chill ran down Dias' spine, and he forcibly leapt backward.

The leap, accompanied by the fluttering of wind scales, caused him to lose his balance midair... Vihera, of course, didn't miss this opening. She planted her feet on the ground to close the distance, channeling magic into the blade on her right foot's shank as she delivered a kick.

Dias had no time to admire Vihera's voluptuous thigh—a sight one might even call artistic—before the shrill clash of the blade against the wind blade and the ensuing impact sent him flying.

Even so, the fact he managed to block the blade with wind scales and avoid a fatal wound spoke to Dias's extraordinary combat prowess.

His body, over two meters tall and covered in steel-like muscles, was sent flying nearly five meters.

Yet, planting his right hand on the ground, he immediately regained his footing and readied the Wind Scales against Vihera, who was surely heading his way.

But... the Wind Scale, being a claymore, naturally carried significant weight and was difficult to wield.

Before he knew it, Vihera was already right beside Dias when he raised the Wind Scale.

"Damn!"

This distance, almost close enough to touch, was Vihera's range—the perfect distance for a fighter who relied entirely on hand-to-hand combat.

Seeing the hand reaching out toward his body, Dias, almost reflexively, slammed the hilt of the Wind Scale into that hand.

Vihera's gauntlet and the Wind Scale's handle collided, sending a sharp metallic clang echoing through the air.

But Dias's strike with the hilt was nothing more than a sudden, instinctive blow. At best, it could only deflect Vihera's hand from its intended target.

Vihera's hand, originally aimed for the abdomen, was deflected by the strike of the handle and touched Dias's side...

"Gah!"

Though not a fatal blow, the strike to his side carried enough force to make Dias cry out in pain.

The Demon-Infused Palm was meant to send the shock directly into the opponent's body via magic, but it struck the Wind Scale hilt just before impact. This caused it to hit a different spot than intended, significantly reducing its power.

Even so, the experience of having a direct shock hammered into his body through his leather armor was likely a first for Dias.

The shockwave spreading from the point of impact on his side into his body was enough to make him hold his breath for several seconds.

And in a battle between combatants of such high caliber as Vihera and Dias, those few seconds held immense significance.

Vihera stepped forward to pursue Dias, who had staggered back several paces from the Demon-Infused Palm's force.

Despite having the shock transmitted directly through his leather armor into his body, Dias, refusing to relinquish the Wind Scales, forcibly swung the Magic Sword at Vihera.

Given it was a strike delivered while gasping for breath, it couldn't be called particularly sharp...

"Huh!? Impressive!"

Vihera, who hadn't expected Dais to counterattack immediately after taking the Immersion Palm—even if it was a glancing blow—exclaimed in admiration at Dais's movement. Yet, a smile touched his lips as he crouched swiftly.

The wind-scale blade sliced through the space where Vihera's body had been just an instant before.

It was hardly a strike that sliced through the air; it was an attack that could even be called dull.

Even so, the blade wrapped in wind severed a lock of Vihera's hair as she crouched to the ground, evading the attack.

But... that was all Dias could manage.

His final, sweeping horizontal strike was also dodged. The internal damage from the shock of the earlier Demon-Infused Palm still tormented Dias's body, unrecovered.

If he had lost consciousness from the first strike, perhaps he wouldn't have prolonged his suffering...

Crouching on the ground, Vihera kept her right hand touching the earth as she spun swiftly, unleashing a sweeping kick that severed the leg before her.

"Guh!"

Falling backward onto the ground, Dias finally released the Wind Scale he held.

The greatsword, a Claymore, rolled across the ground with a loud clang, but no one reached for it now.

And then...

"That was a fun moment, after so long. ...Now, sleep."

With words that seemed almost gentle, Vihera's right hand touched Dias's abdomen... and in the next instant, the strike of the Demon-Infused Palm sent Dias's consciousness plunging into darkness.
