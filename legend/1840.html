Liberte smiled warmly at the greeting from his daughter and her fiancé.

“Hmm, don’t worry about it. Even though you’re late, the party hasn’t started yet. …Hmm. Still, Elena and Rei look great together.”

As Liberte said this with a look of satisfaction, Arcadia, who had been standing next to him and watching Rei and Elena—especially Elena—also broke into a satisfied smile.

“Elena doesn’t come out to events like this very often, but I really think she should make an appearance at parties like this more often, don’t you think?”

At first glance, Arcadia, asking her husband this, looked like nothing more than an ordinary noblewoman.

Of course, having given birth to Elena, her beauty was undeniably striking.

However, that attitude of Arcadia’s is merely her public face… or rather, to be precise, it’s not exactly her hidden side either; the attitude she displayed when she first met Rei is likely just another facet of her personality.

In any case, how many people actually know that Arcadia has such a personality?

Suddenly, looking at Arcadia now—seeing her delighting in her daughter’s dressed-up appearance just like any ordinary mother—Rei finds himself thinking about that.

Perhaps sensing Rei’s gaze, for an instant—just a fleeting moment—Arcadia directed the same look at him that she had shown him before.

Rei felt a chill run down his spine at that gaze, but since she quickly returned to her usual Arcadia self, he managed to avoid getting particularly flustered. …Though, to be fair, Liberte was casting a pitying glance at Rei, who had been forced to put up with his wife’s playful antics.

“Duke Kerebel, is this the person we’ve been talking about…?”

“Aye. You’ve surely heard the name at least. Rei of Crimson.”

The man who had been speaking with Liberte nodded in understanding as he looked at Rei.

Though not quite as imposing as Liberte, the man still had a certain presence about him.

“So, it’s you. …I am Zascal Seysol, head of the Seysol Marquis family.”

“...The Seysol Marquis family?”

Rei stared intently at the man’s face as he said this with a smile.

He looked to be in his forties, not much different in age from Liberte.

That said, Liberte is actually older than he looks.

In any case, Rei focused his attention not on Liberte, but on the man standing before him.

Zascal Seysol, head of the Seysol Marquis family.

In other words, he was the father of Gaiska, the man believed to have hired the Black Wolf.

Zascal surely knows who Rei is, but at least from Rei’s perspective, he doesn’t seem to know anything.

“Hmm, the head of the Seysol Marquis family. Ah, it seems my son and you get along quite well. Despite his appearance, he has a rather impulsive personality. Has he been causing you any trouble?”

“Well…”

“You mean the Black Wolf?”

Rei was about to say that, but Zascal spoke up again, as if to stop him.

“That said, it seems he’s been a bit too rowdy. Unfortunately, Gaiska is currently under house arrest.”

Rei didn’t know if Zascal was aware that Gaiska had hired the Black Wolf.

But it was safe to assume that Zascal knew Gaiska had been plotting something unsavory against Rei and had actually carried it out.

At the very least, that was how Zascal appeared to Rei.

(If he’s acting this way knowing full well that Gaiska has hired the Black Wolf, that’s either incredibly brazen… or it just goes to show why he holds such strong influence within the Noble Faction.)

Though he felt this deeply, he couldn’t very well voice it while the other man was pretending not to notice.

To begin with, the idea that Gaiska hired the Black Wolf is nothing more than circumstantial evidence.

The Black Wolf hadn’t said he’d been hired by Gaiska, nor had Gaiska himself admitted to hiring him.

Under these circumstances, if Rei were to claim right here that Gaiska had hired Black Wolf, it could easily be seen as him just looking for a fight.

Moreover, seeing Rei with his arm around Elena, making a scene here could turn even Elena—known as the “Princess General”—and the Duke and Duchess of Kerebel against him.

As the head of the Seisol Marquis family, he certainly couldn’t afford to do something like that.

“Mischief, you say? …As long as that mischief doesn’t take a strange turn, I’d be grateful.”

The words carried a hint of provocation, but to Zascal—who had dealt with countless seasoned nobles—Rei’s attitude was hardly worth taking seriously.

Zaskar smiled and nodded.

“You’re right. It seems I’ve given my son a bit too much freedom. Considering the trouble he’s caused you, I suppose I’ll need to be a bit stricter with his upbringing from now on. I don’t think Rei will ever meet Gaiska again, but I’ll make sure to remember this incident clearly.”

“Huh,”

Rei was slightly surprised.

Although Zascal’s words weren’t explicit, they amounted to an admission that Gaiska was at fault.

He had expected that, as Gaiska’s father, Zaskal would defend his son and become a staunch enemy.

Despite that, the fact that he so readily admitted Gaiska’s fault left Ray feeling, quite frankly, more surprised than anything else.

However, Elena, who had been listening to the conversation beside Rei, nodded as if she found it quite reasonable.

As the head of the Seisol Marquis family, Zascal wields undeniable influence even within the Noble Faction.

While he may not match Liberte, who leads the Noble Faction itself, Zascal’s influence ranks among the top five within the faction.

A man like Zascal could not possibly be incompetent.

……He may have failed in raising his son, Gaiska, but as the head of the Marquis family, he is undoubtedly capable.

Precisely because of that, he fully understands what it would mean to antagonize Rei in the current situation.

That is precisely why he has indirectly requested a truce.

「Rei」

As she spoke, Elena gently tightened her grip on Rei’s right arm, which she was holding.

Elena’s action snapped Rei out of his surprise, and he spoke up.

“If it doesn’t create extra work for me, that’s fine. …But, I don’t know whose people they are, but I’m currently being targeted by some strange individuals, aren’t I?”

“...I’ll ask Gaiska.”

The moment Rei said he was being targeted right now, Zascal’s face flashed a slight look of surprise.

He knew that Gaiska was raising funds. However, according to Deotres, he had reported that the money to hire an assassin hadn’t been fully secured yet.

Despite that, the fact that Rei is already being attacked by someone is clearly odd.

(Did Deotres overlook something? Or was there someone else targeting Rei besides Gaiska?)

He quickly ran through these possibilities in his mind and concluded that the latter was the more likely scenario.

In fact, according to the intelligence gathered by Zascal, just yesterday someone had committed the foolish act of attacking Set.

Why on earth would three people think they could take on a high-rank monster like a griffin?

Count Caseres had been squeezing taxes out of his subjects through oppressive measures, but that didn’t mean he was incompetent.

……Though, if he were truly competent, he wouldn’t have imposed such harsh taxes—as if oppressing his subjects—and created a situation where a rebellion could break out at any moment.

(Or perhaps he simply misjudged the situation, assuming that because they were familiars, they would never harm a human.)

With that, Zascal dismissed his thoughts about Count Caseres and focused entirely on the conversation with the person standing before him.

Judging by the relationship between Rei and Elena, it was safe to assume that the relationship between the Duke of Kerebel’s family and Rei was also good.

In other words, he wanted to avoid, as much as possible, the Marquis of Seisol’s family viewing Rei with hostility because of Gaiska.

“Still, seeing them like this, Elena and Rei really do make a fine pair.”

“Hearing you say that makes all the effort I put into my makeup worthwhile.”

“That Elena… well. Of course, it’s not a bad thing for Her Ladyship to feel that way, but still, seeing Elena—who’s never shown any interest in men before—here with Rei like this feels refreshing.”

There was no intention to disparage Elena in the way he said it.

On the contrary, it seemed he was genuinely delighted that Elena had taken an interest in a man.

...As if he had never once instructed Gaiska to get closer to Elena.

With a smile on his face, Zaskal’s gaze then turned to Rei.

He had been talking with Rei just moments ago, but upon seeing him again...

“I see. To have captured the heart of Crimson Rei. It seems Elena wasn’t uninterested in men; she simply had very high standards when it came to choosing a partner. And it was Rei who met her standards.”

Zaskar, who had been staring intently at Rei, broke into a smile the next moment.

“Well, Duke Kerebel, your future looks bright indeed.”

“Hearing you say that is a great relief to me. After all, the power of the Marquis of Seisol’s family is indispensable as I lead the Noble Faction from here on out.”

“I’m glad to hear you say that.”

Liberte and Zascal exchanged pleasantries with cheerful smiles, but even to Rei, it was clear that behind those words, various kinds of political maneuvering were taking place.

That said, Rei wasn’t particularly interested in any of that.

To begin with, Rei wasn’t actually a member of the Noble Faction, nor was he a noble himself.

Given that, it didn’t matter to him what kind of political maneuvering was taking place at this party, where so many members of the Noble Faction had gathered.

Of course, it would have been a different story if those maneuvers had resulted in harm coming to him or his companions.

“Master, it is nearly time. The guests have all arrived.”

The butler approached Liberte, who had been talking with Zascal, and informed her of this.

“I feel like I’ve seen this butler somewhere before…” Rei wondered for a moment, but he quickly remembered.

It was the man who had sat at the same table as Liberte during yesterday’s appreciation party for the servants.

“Hmm. Well then, let’s get started. Glasses for everyone.”

After giving the butler that instruction, Liberte took his glass from a nearby maid and stepped up onto the stage, which was raised slightly above the rest of the party hall.

Rei could guess that this was likely the stage used for special events.

Once on the stage, Liberte glanced around.

She hadn’t done anything particularly dramatic, but just by doing that, the eyes of everyone in the ballroom mysteriously turned toward Liberte.

Almost everyone in this party hall was a noble.

They were a proud lot, to say the least… yet, no one seemed dissatisfied with Liberte’s demeanor.

Well, perhaps there were some who did harbor dissatisfaction with Liberte, but none of them made a show of it.

(This kind of presence must be one of Liberte’s strengths, too.)

In a sense, this power Liberte possessed was more effective than the direct military might that Rei possessed.

At least, certainly in this situation.

“I am very pleased that everyone has gathered here again this year. It seems some of you are new faces, perhaps due to a generational change… but even so, I want you to conduct yourselves properly as members of the Noble Faction. Whatever you do, do not act in a manner unbecoming of the Noble Faction.”

What exactly was Liberte referring to? The nobles present here would surely understand.

Rei, who is being treated as a guest by the Duke of Kerebel’s household. Count Caseres, who went so far as to infiltrate the Duke of Kerebel’s household in an attempt to make his familiar, Set, his own.

Everyone present here naturally knows that Count Caseres has been expelled from the Noble Faction.

There was no doubt that Liberte was referring to that very matter.

“I hope that this year, too, everyone will stay healthy, develop their own territories, and conduct themselves in a manner befitting the Noble Faction.”

Though Rei, standing near the stage, hadn’t noticed, Liberte’s words were clearly heard by everyone in the ballroom.

This was the effect of a magical item embedded in the stage where Liberte stood.

That said, since it was a rather large-scale magical item—not exactly portable—it was questionable whether Rei, who desired magical items usable in actual combat, would actually want it.

“Cheers!”

With that, Liberte raised the wine glass she was holding high.
