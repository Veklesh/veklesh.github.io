The moment she saw that person, Talaniya froze.

Part of it was that she was captivated by Elena’s beauty, but that wasn’t the only reason.

After all, if we’re talking about beauty on Elena’s level, there were already Marina and Vihera (both women) standing beside Rei.

The problem was, of course, that it was Elena who was standing there.

……Yes, Elena Kerebel, known by the epithet “Princess General,” who could be called the very symbol of the Noble Faction.

Elena’s father, Duke Kerebel, is, needless to say, the leader of the Noble Faction.

And the faction to which the nobles who have sent a magistrate to Sublusta belong is also the Noble Faction.

In other words, it wouldn’t be wrong to say that the person currently standing before Tarania is the daughter of their own boss.

From Elena’s perspective, Tarania was merely a subordinate of a subordinate hired by her father’s subordinate.

She was by no means someone she could afford to defy.

Moreover, although the information hadn’t reached Tarania yet, Duke Kerebel had even issued an order forbidding anyone from interfering with the expansion of Gilm.

But whether he knew of these circumstances or not, Rei remained completely oblivious to Talanian’s demeanor… no, upon seeing Talanian stiffen at the sight of Elena, he opened his mouth, seeing this as an opportunity.

“This is Tarania. Apparently, she’s a direct subordinate of the Sublusta magistrate. So, the bandits we captured were actually just hunters or something who happened to be in the forest, and we’re illegally capturing people like that to sell them as slaves… or so they claim.”

“...I see.”

Upon hearing Rei’s words, Elena glanced at the bandits Rei and his group—or, more accurately, the other adventurers—had captured, then at the bandits they themselves had captured, whom Ara was dragging along, and finally turned her gaze toward Tarania.

“So, are you saying the bandits I captured are hunters too? And that we’re capturing them to illegally turn them into slaves?”

At Elena’s words, Tarania was at a loss for words.

No matter what she says, in this situation, it will only dig her own grave.

She understands that.

……That said, once the captured bandits have been questioned, they will be punished, and barring some special circumstances, most of them will be sold off as slaves.

In fact, the reason adventurers capture bandits is not only for the bounty on their heads and the treasure they’ve hoarded, but also for the money they can make by selling them into slavery.

In that sense, not everything Tarania says is completely wrong.

But there’s no way I could possibly admit that here.

“Well… um…”

Though Elena urged her to continue with a look, Talanian merely hemmed and hawed.

Seeing Tarania like this, Ara—who had finally arrived beside Elena with the bandits in tow—shot her a piercing glare.

That gaze was filled with displeasure.

Even from a distance, Ara could naturally hear the conversation between Elena, Rei, and Tarania.

To treat Elena—whom she deeply respected—as an illegal slave trader was something Ara could absolutely not forgive.

Furthermore, even if not to the same extent as Elena, the fact that she harbored such suspicions toward Rei and the others was something she could never forgive.

“You mentioned you’re a direct subordinate of the magistrate of Sublusta, but does that magistrate know about this matter… or, for that matter, does his superior know as well?”

Tarania, met with a cold gaze—one that seemed to view her not as a person, but as an object—wondered if there was any way she could somehow gloss over the situation.

(Why is Elena in a place like this!)

That was Talanian’s sole—and greatest—miscalculation.

She had received information that Elena was staying in Gilm, but the intelligence she’d been given was that only the adventurers were involved in this particular incident.

Of course, Tarania had only learned of this situation a short while ago.

For that reason alone, the fact that Elena was acting alongside Rei and the others must have been completely unexpected.

……If she had been a little more informed, she would have known that Elena was acting alongside Rei and the others, but unfortunately, Talanian’s ears weren’t that sharp.

“Kyu? Kyu-kyu?”

“Grr…”

She wanted to say something, but couldn’t.

A short distance away from Talanian, Set and Yellow were cheerfully chatting.

Those around Set watched the two of them interact with a warm, contented feeling… but the atmosphere, the world, the vibe was completely different from where Tarania and the others were.

“It seems you haven’t answered Ara’s question? Does your superior know about this matter?”

“...It was my own decision.”

In response to Elena’s repeated question, a voice that sounded as if it had been forced out escaped from Tarania’s lips.

It wasn’t that she hadn’t considered shifting the blame onto her superior for a moment.

But doing so would mean incurring the wrath of her superior—someone so closely connected to her.

She judged that, while it might not matter now, it would undoubtedly be a fatal blow to her in the future—and in some cases, even a threat to her life.

Considering what lay ahead, she decided it would be better for her to take the fall.

...In fact, much of the profit from this operation—specifically, if they had seized the treasure the bandits had hoarded from the adventurers, half of it was already designated as Talanian’s share—so in the sense that she had taken the lead, her words weren’t entirely wrong.

“So, are you saying that Tarania deliberately tried to obstruct our actions of her own accord?”

“Yes.”

Trying to gloss over it any further would only make my position worse.

So, I decided to quietly answer Elena’s question and try to make a good impression. That was the attitude I adopted because of that line of thinking.

“Then why did you try to do such a thing?”

“Well… I acted on the intention of seizing the treasures the bandits had been hoarding.”

“...I see. I had assumed you were in cahoots with the bandits.”

“That’s not true!”

Tarania immediately denied Elena’s words.

Even if she admitted to trying to steal the bandits’ treasure for herself, she couldn’t possibly admit to having ties to them.

In the former case, even if she were punished, it would likely amount to nothing more than being reassigned to a menial position. But if the latter were to become public knowledge, she could very well be beheaded.

And not just being fired—but having her head literally cut off… in other words, execution.

If she were executed for having ties to the bandits, she would lose all honor.

Worse still, depending on the circumstances, the blame could even extend to his family.

That is precisely why it was absolutely imperative that no one find out about his connection to the bandits.

“Huh? …This one says so, but is it true?”

Upon hearing Tarania’s words, Elena turned her gaze toward the bandit being held captive by the adventurers and asked.

Tarania frantically signaled to the bandits.

Don’t say anything about me. That was the message she was sending.

But in the first place, Tarania’s connection to the bandits was nothing more than a matter of money.

Tarania would lobby the guild to prevent the suppression of bandits by citing various reasons, and if she found a suitable target, she would inform the bandits.

In return, whenever the bandits provided information on anyone who was a nuisance to Tarania, she would prioritize targeting them. She would then hand over a portion of the spoils to Tarania.

There was no trust between them; it was a cold, transactional relationship where each used the other. That’s why, even when Talanian gave desperate glances, few understood what she meant.

And those who didn’t understand concluded that Tarania had abandoned them, and voiced their grievances.

“Don’t mess with us! You’ve taken money from us all this time, and now you’re the first to cut us loose!?”

“That’s right! If you’re going to abandon us, you’re coming down with us! Hey, you guys! Tarania teamed up with us to make us attack people who were in her way!”

“She even killed a guy once just because he was in the way of the woman she was after!”

“I was asked to kill someone who was an enemy of Tarania!”

Several other bandits began shouting out the various tasks Tarania had asked them to perform up until now.

Since they had no trust or loyalty to begin with—their relationship was based solely on money—and they had concluded that Tarania had discarded them, it was only natural that they couldn’t understand the meaning of her nods, no matter how many times she gave them.

“Y-y-you bastards!”

Tarania, who had been dragged into this, shouted back, trying to cover up the truth in the face of the bandits’ accusations.

“Just because you committed a crime doesn’t mean you should drag me into this! I have nothing to do with it! Don’t spout such nonsense!”

Tarania shouted with her face flushed red, but her very words and actions proved that the bandits’ claims were true.

It wasn’t Rei and his group… nor Elena and her group… but the guards Tarania had brought with her who were troubled by her reaction.

While some of the guards could be called Tarania’s personal troops, most of them were ordinary guards.

In other words, if what the bandits were saying was true, they were in a position where they had to arrest Tarania.

However, knowing the extent of Tarania’s power in Sublusta, they couldn’t afford to act rashly here.

Even if we manage to capture Talanian here, what happens if she is found innocent due to some mistake?

Anyone who knows Talanian’s temperament would undoubtedly worry about their own safety… and the safety of their family.

In fact, a considerable number of people have suffered terrible fates for defying Tarania.

If you include those whose cases haven’t come to light, there’s no need to even think about how many that would be. But…

“Well, whether what the bandits are saying is true or not, we’ll find out right away if we interrogate them in Gilm. Naturally, you… Tarania, wasn’t it? Since your name came up from the bandits, I’m afraid I’ll have to take you into custody for the time being.”

“What!? W-wait, please, Lady Elena! I work at Sublusta. Even if you are Lady Elena, you can’t just…”

"You can’t just arrest me like that."

Tarania was about to shout that, but the moment she met Elena’s gaze, she froze.

It wasn’t that she was captivated by Elena’s stunning beauty…

It was simply that, despite possessing such beauty, there was not a shred of compassion in the way she looked at her.

If there had been even a trace of anger or irritation in that gaze, Talanian might have been able to accept it.

But what now filled Elena’s eyes was a gaze as if she were looking at a mere pebble by the roadside.

The moment she met Elena’s gaze, Talaniya was completely overwhelmed and found herself unable to say another word.

Elena glanced at the silent Tarania, then turned her gaze to the guards behind her and spoke.

“You lot should return to Sublusta and report this matter to your superiors or the magistrate. Then, it will become clear how we should proceed. However… make sure to tell them that I will report their actions to my father without any lies or evasion.”

All the guards could do in response to Elena’s words was nod in agreement.

They understood that if they showed the slightest carelessness here, they would undoubtedly be judged as being on Talanian’s side.

At the same time, even those among the guards who could be called Tarania’s private soldiers did not take any action, accepting that there was nothing they could do in the current situation.

That said, even if they had had some means to do so, it would likely have been unthinkable to take action against an opponent like the Princess General.

Although they were Taranian private soldiers… or perhaps I should say precisely because they were private soldiers, they were not bound by trust, duty, or even loyalty; their relationship was purely one of money.

In that sense, there’s likely little difference between the private soldiers and the bandits when it comes to their connection to Tarania.

Of course, there’s no doubt that the private soldiers were treated better than the bandits, but that’s not something worth addressing here… or even later.

“Well then, let’s consider this matter closed. Does anyone have any objections?”

“I do!”

“I’m not asking you. Be quiet.”

Tarania tried to protest to Elena, but she was immediately cut off.

With that, no one else spoke up… and the commotion was successfully quelled.

That said, there was no doubt that Talanian’s superior, the magistrate, and the noble who had dispatched him would face repercussions later on.
