Duke Quent. A leading figure in the Royalist faction—the largest faction within the Kingdom of Mireana, comprising more nobles than either the Aristocratic or Centrist factions.

Surprised that such a figure was extending his hand to shake hers, Rei nevertheless grasped the offered hand.

He had heard stories about the man before him being overly particular about etiquette, and had preconceived notions that he would be difficult to deal with.

But the man shaking his hand now didn't strike him as nearly as particular about etiquette as he'd been made out to be.

"Hmm, so this is the hand of the adventurer known as Crimson. ...A curious hand indeed. Though I may not look it, I'm no stranger to powerful opponents. Yet, even to my eyes, you don't seem particularly formidable."

Ronald gently released Rei's hand, looked at Rei's face after his hand, and murmured thoughtfully.

He understood the numerous actions Rei had taken—actions that could even be called great feats—and that was precisely why he wouldn't dare underestimate him. But if he knew nothing, he would never have thought Rei was a high-ranking adventurer like Rank B.

That's how strange the boy before Ronald seemed.

That said, Ronald felt no desire to discriminate against him simply because he seemed strange.

After all, if we're talking about strange beings, his beloved daughter was certainly strange enough herself.

At around ten years old, she possessed a brilliant mind, mastered numerous spells, and even handled her father's duties as a noble in his stead.

Having thought that far, Ronald realized now was not the time for such musings. He turned his gaze back to the person before him and spoke.

"Thanks to you, those committing major corruption within the royalist faction are now being punished one after another. Of course, we can't deal with every noble involved in wrongdoing, but still, a significant number of nobles will lose their titles over this affair. In the worst cases, some may even be stripped of their titles entirely."

"...To that extent?"

Ronald's words prompted Rei to speak up in surprise.

He had heard about the crackdown on discipline, but he hadn't imagined it would go as far as stripping titles.

Stripped of their titles... meaning they would be demoted from nobility to commoners.

For those who had lived arrogantly as nobles, indulging in extravagance, being reduced to commoners would make survival difficult.

Once humans become accustomed to luxury, it's impossible to give it up... well, perhaps not impossible, but it's certainly difficult.

If they had enemies among the commoners, they might be killed. Or, unable to lower their standard of living, they'd pile up debts, forcing their wives and daughters into prostitution while they and their sons became slaves... This wasn't an uncommon outcome.

In fact, this is often the typical outcome for those stripped of their titles.

Of course, not all nobles meet this fate.

Some start anew by turning to commerce and succeed as merchants. Others become adventurers, achieve great feats, and regain their noble status. There are even those who found their own kingdoms and became kings.

Still, it's true that only a few achieve such success.

"That's right. Some imposed outrageous taxes on their subjects, and others even enforced the droit du seigneur with deadly seriousness within their own domains. ...Fools."

Just recalling it seemed unbearable; Ronald's brow furrowed in displeasure.

Just seeing that, combined with Ronald's words, gave Rei—who disliked nobles—some understanding of what the royalist nobles had been doing.

"If this makes the Kingdom of Mireana even slightly more livable, then I'll be glad to help."

Rei's brow furrowed briefly at Rei referring to himself as "I" rather than "me."

But since he'd already heard from Erinde that Rei struggled with proper etiquette towards nobles, he didn't voice any dissatisfaction.

He knew full well about Rei's attitude, and that was precisely why he had come to see him.

Besides, if even Daskar, the noble Rei was closest to, tolerated this attitude, it would seem petty and uninteresting for him to reprimand Rei here.

More than that, in this incident, they had invited Rei and then allowed the attack to happen. They deserved blame, yet they were being forgiven. From Ronald's position, he could only feel gratitude.

...Though if Ronald knew that the noble closest to Rei was actually Elena Kerebel, he'd be so shocked he wouldn't be able to care about anything else.

That the woman nicknamed the Princess General, a symbolic figure of the noble faction, was romantically involved with Rei was something most people would find utterly unbelievable.

"That's right. You've been inconvenienced by the royalist faction's affairs. But once this disciplinary purge concludes, the royalists should be able to clear their names."

Rei could only nod at Ronald's words, which carried the resolve that it must be so—no, that he would make it so with his own strength.

Though half of it was hope.

Rei was urged to sit on the sofa, taking a seat opposite Ronald and Marka.

This naturally placed him facing Coan and Iskeld, who were present as Ronald and Marka's guards. However, even when their gazes met Rei's, neither showed any sign of speaking.

Coan merely gave a slight nod.

They were likely aware that, in this situation, they were nothing more than bodyguards.

"So then. What do you intend to do now?"

Rei tilted his head at Ronald, who took a sip of the tea on the table and asked what he intended to do next.

"What do you mean, 'what do I plan'? I suppose I'll continue teaching at the academy for a while?"

"Is that really what you want? I hate to say it, but you were attacked here in Gracielle—my own backyard, if you will—and specifically within the academy grounds! We went out of our way to bring you here precisely to prevent retaliatory attacks from royalist nobles..."

He must have felt pathetic saying it himself. Ronald paused, letting out a sigh.

For Ronald, holding the very high rank of Duke, this incident was clearly a failure.

Fortunately, Rei had no such intentions. But had Rei been someone hostile to Ronald, rumors would have spread throughout noble society that the Quent House couldn't even protect guests they'd invited themselves.

Of course, such a rumor alone would never destroy the House of Quent.

Nevertheless, the Quent House would not emerge unscathed; it would suffer damage that was by no means insignificant.

"And in the end, it was not my men who defeated the assassin who attacked, but you yourself. If that is the case, I cannot stop you from leaving, claiming you cannot trust the security arrangements at this Gracielle. The fault lies with us, after all."

At Ronald's words, Marka cast a glance toward Rei, her expression tinged with faint unease.

It was her father who had instructed her, but she was the one who had brought Rei from Gilm to this Gracielle—though strictly speaking, since Rei had gone ahead, "brought" wasn't quite accurate.

It wasn't that Marka was at fault, but it was only natural she felt as if she were being blamed.

To reassure Marka, Rei flashed a small smile and spoke.

"Don't worry. I'm actually quite fond of life here myself. I have no intention of leaving on my own. At least not until the spring deadline for the request."

Rei's words must have been quite unexpected. Not only Ronald, but even Coan and Iskeld wore expressions of surprise.

Amidst this, Erinde and Sardat wore expressions that seemed to say they knew exactly how Rei would respond, as if it were only natural.

"...Erinde, what are this man's qualifications as an instructor?"

"Not the absolute best... but he's doing a solid job. He hasn't been teaching for that long, but I hear the third and fourth-year S-Class students have improved significantly. His feedback after mock battles seems spot-on too."

The words that slipped from Erinde's mouth could be described as casual toward Ronald.

Ronald was normally strict about etiquette, but Erinde was a friend from his youth... no, from his childhood, and he'd practically forced her to serve as the academy's headmistress.

Because she was such a long-time friend, he accepted her casual words without issue, as long as it wasn't in a public setting.

Had someone merely acquainted with Ronald spoken to him in such a familiar tone, he likely would have been displeased.

"Hmm, I see. ...Well then, Rei. Have you any intention of formally becoming an instructor at the academy? Having a skilled individual like you serve under me would be immensely helpful. Both for the royalist faction and for me personally."

"No, I'm afraid I cannot accept that offer. As I've told Lord Daskar, while I might take on requests as an adventurer like this time, I have absolutely no intention of becoming an officer."

"...Not even if I ask you personally?"

This was Ronald, a heavyweight within the Royalist faction. His power within the Kingdom of Mireana ranked among the highest.

To be asked that question while being stared at intently by such a man... Most people would have been unable to say anything and simply nodded.

But the one being stared at was Rei.

Without seeming to feel anything in particular, he shook his head.

"Serving nobility just isn't my thing. I've never been good with all that stuffy formality, and I'm pretty impatient myself. If I were to take someone like me, prone to causing trouble, as a subordinate, it'd probably just lead to more unnecessary chaos."

"If you know that, then you should just avoid causing unnecessary trouble. ...Well, fine. I won't force you to join us either. Trying something like that would only bring trouble later on."

Rei, a heavyweight within the royalist faction, knew full well just how much power Rei possessed.

Especially that Stigma that even a Sardat couldn't stop. He faced two of those Stigmas and ultimately won—though it was only because Set was there—so there was no doubt about it.

Forcing someone like that into your ranks by any means would guarantee immense damage if they ever betrayed you.

(Besides...)

Ronald turned his gaze to his beloved daughter sitting beside him.

Though Marka possessed a clarity of mind beyond her years, she also had many age-appropriate traits.

For example, her food preferences... she disliked bitter vegetables.

For example, her daily play... when it snows, she wants to dash outside and play.

And... her fixation on her favorite person.

This fixation doesn't just mean she wants them by her side. It also means she wants her favorite person to be living happily.

The person sitting before Ronald is the one this girl with such preferences has taken a liking to lately.

Ronald understood this well enough from their conversations during meals. ...It was also true that this accounted for a significant portion of why he wanted Rei to serve him.

If Rei's loyalty were completely directed towards him, there might still be some room to maneuver.

But based on the information Ronald had gathered so far, and the impression gained from this conversation, he couldn't possibly imagine Ray ever developing such loyalty.

(Then again, having someone like this on Marka's side might be detrimental to his upbringing. Seen that way, not promoting this man wasn't entirely a bad thing.)

For now, he treated him with the deference due a superior.

But Ronald could see immediately that this was not born of genuine respect.

His character, which didn't regard nobles as nobles, was, as far as Ronald knew, something that could even be called monstrous.

Having such a person far from his beloved daughter was certainly no bad thing.

If Rei were to learn of Ronald's thoughts, he would likely see him as a parent unwilling to tolerate his child associating with bad company.

And that impression wouldn't have been wrong.

"Then, for now, I'll continue teaching here until spring as per our previous agreement. Is that acceptable to you?"

"Yes. That is my intention."

"I see. Well then, I look forward to it. ...Ah, and as an apology for the trouble caused by this matter, I'd like to add a little something extra to your compensation."

"Thank you."

And so, Rei would once again serve as an instructor at the officer academy.
