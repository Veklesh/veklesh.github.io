Rei was moving through Gosh in search of information about Daldora, but he realized he had no idea where Daldora actually was.

(For starters, I don't even know where the Elema Trading Company is. And naturally, I have no clue where Daldora's house is either. Wait, but if it's a house...)

Daldora's house—meaning the home of one of the most powerful figures in this entire Gosh.

Naturally, it must be larger than Augusto's mansion.

Thinking this, Rei was about to head for the residential district where Gosh's wealthier inhabitants lived, but then a stroke of luck came his way.

"Hurry! They say to gather at Daldora's shop and fortify our defenses!"

"But who would do such a thing? There's no one in Gosh who'd target Daldora, right?"

Two different adventurers, not the ones he'd met where Augusto was, exchanged words as they ran past Rei.

The Elema Trading Company held significant influence even within Gosh.

Naturally, they employed many people, so it was only logical that such individuals would be contacted if something happened.

(This is all thanks to my good deeds.)

Thinking that anyone who knew Rei would immediately shake their head at this, Rei hurriedly followed the two adventurers heading toward the center of Gosh.

"But didn't Daldora have Narsus with her? How did she get injured then?"

"Seems so. ...Could it be that Narsus isn't that strong after all?"

"Hey, don't call him by his first name. What if he hears you? Mr. Narsus is strict, you know."

"But still..."

That exchange reached Rei's ears as he advanced a short distance away.

(Is Narsus actually disliked? Judging from their conversation, he's probably the most skilled guard or even the leader among Daldora's escorts.)

Rei tilted his head in confusion internally as he followed the two adventurers. Soon, they arrived in front of a large shop.

(So this is the Elema Trading Company's store.)

Rei had explored the streets of Gosh to some extent, but mainly focusing on food-related places.

He had some interest in weapon shops, but considering he was an acquaintance of Augusto, he doubted he could make a proper purchase if he casually stopped by.

...Though, to be fair, the same applied to food-related shops. Staying at Augusto's mansion meant that thanks to Kathy's connections, they wouldn't be denied food.

Near the Elema Trading Company, large groups of people were still gathered, huddled together in clusters, talking.

Amidst them, I spotted a familiar woman... Cathy's friend, the shopkeeper who had generously sold food to Rei as well. I made my way over to her.

"Hey, what happened?"

"Huh!? ...What? It's you? Don't startle me like that."

The shopkeeper gasped at being suddenly addressed, but exhaled in relief upon realizing it was Rei.

But she must have immediately realized the danger of Rei being here... someone thought to be one of Augusto's associates. She pulled Rei into a nearby cafeteria.

He would have preferred to pull him into his own shop, but that person's shop was quite far from here.

The shopkeeper was here because he'd come to the nearby stores to do some shopping.

On the way to that shop, he had stumbled upon the scene of Daldora being attacked.

"Ah, something to eat. And water."

Since he had entered the shop, he ordered a meal for the shopkeeper in front of him as payment for the information.

The shopkeeper looked a little embarrassed, but the cafeteria staff heard Rei's order and immediately headed to the kitchen to relay it.

Fortunately, since Rei was currently wearing the hood of the Dragon Robe, it seemed no one had recognized him as the Rei who was hostile towards Daldora.

"Is that alright?"

"In this scorching heat? You must be thirsty and a bit hungry, right? It's in lieu of payment for the information, so don't worry about it. I was asked by... well, to be precise, by Augusto to gather information."

"Ah, I see. ...But even I don't know the details that well, you know?"

"Huh? You were watching from the start, weren't you?"

Rei, who had been watching from the start, wondered why he didn't know the details. Cathy's friend, the shop owner, waved her hand dismissively.

"Well, someone used to rough stuff might have understood, but I've never been involved in anything like that."

"...Ah, I see. Yeah, got it."

Just as Kathy's friend finished speaking, a shop employee brought over the food.

Simple dishes—grilled meat skewers and water—were placed on the table.

After paying with copper coins and the server carrying the food had left, Rei spoke again.

"So, could you just tell me what you know for now?"

"Well, if that's all right with you..."

Nodding at Rei's words, she immediately began thinking about where to start.

He had always enjoyed talking to people, and gossip was the perfect form of entertainment.

That's precisely why he was so delighted now, hesitating over where to begin.

Taking a sip of water, she began to speak as if it had loosened her tongue.

"First, Daldora was about to go out somewhere and was getting into her carriage. Then suddenly, three men attacked Daldora and her guards!"

"...Three?"

"Yes."

Considering only one man attacked Augusto, this meant the number had tripled.

(If that's the case... was the attack on Augusto a decoy, or perhaps just collateral damage, with Daldora being the real target?)

Thinking that way, it wasn't something Rei couldn't accept either.

After all, Daldora held overwhelming influence in the city of Gosh.

"But those three were strange too. ...Somehow, even when attacked, they didn't flinch at all. They didn't even seem bothered when arrows pierced their bodies."

"More trouble."

Rei had faced enemies seemingly immune to pain several times before.

In terms of strengthening weak forces without investing time and effort, it was an effective measure. But that was only when considering them as disposable assets.

In the world of Elgin, human life is cheap and easily discarded.

Even so, if asked whether they could be discarded as combat assets, most would likely answer no.

Of course, there were those who valued weapons and armor more than human life, and such individuals certainly felt no qualms about discarding soldiers.

"But Daldora's guards were also skilled fighters recruited with money. Especially Lord Narsus, the guard—he was quite the sight."

Daldora addressed him without honorifics, yet for some reason, she used "-sama" with Narsus. This revealed the feelings the woman harbored for him.

"He really was impressive. The moment he realized we were under attack, he immediately gave orders to everyone..."

"Ah, yeah. I get that. But tell me about the others too."

Even as he said this, Rei somehow understood what kind of person Narsus was.

The strongest-looking man among those she had met with Daldora before.

The face of a man who was probably a Rank B adventurer flashed through Rei's mind.

"Anyway, the men who attacked on Lord Narsus's orders were defeated."

"...Defeated?"

Rei, who had assumed Daldora had been wounded by those three, showed a slight look of surprise at those words.

But the woman nodded again at Rei's words.

"Yes, we defeated him. ...But the problem started after that. Anyway, because of the enemy attack, Daldora tried to retreat back inside the shop for a moment..."

It was a deliberately dramatic way of putting it, but that was likely a technique to grab the attention of her audience, Rei.

It was a skill she'd naturally acquired, being so fond of gossip.

Rei understood this, but figured it was necessary to keep the conversation flowing smoothly. She opened her mouth, urging the woman to continue.

"But?"

"Right then, arrows flew out of nowhere. If Lord Narsus hadn't shielded Daldora in that instant, she probably would've died."

"...Do you know roughly how many arrows it was?"

"No way. I'm just a regular person, not some adventurer or anything. All I know is it was a lot."

Hearing that, Rei couldn't very well press her.

After all, the woman before him was just an ordinary person, as she herself had said, unaccustomed to violent situations.

In fact, the very fact that she hadn't fled during the attack but had stayed to witness the entire incident could be considered a stroke of luck for Rei.

Normally, when something like the attack on Daldora happened, most ordinary people would flee to avoid getting caught up in it.

Some might stay behind as rubberneckers, but those people would die on the spot if their luck ran out.

Of course, part of the reason the woman was unharmed was likely that the attack ended quickly.

The situation unfolded too quickly for anyone to witness the attack, come to their senses, and flee.

"So, I heard Daldora got injured. Was it thanks to that Narsus guy that he survived?"

"Yes. An arrow that was about to pierce his head ended up lodging in his right shoulder. That's exactly what they mean when they say he had a narrow escape."

The woman murmured thoughtfully, but her words carried a hint of what a pity it was.

Being Kathy's friend, she probably had her own thoughts about Daldora.

Understanding that, Rei continued.

"So, what happened to the guy who shot the arrow?"

"What happened? He just vanished without a trace."

"...There were a lot of arrows, weren't there?"

"Yeah. Even to an amateur like me, it seemed like a lot."

"Then you didn't see the face of even one of the perpetrators?"

At those words, the woman smiled as if she'd been waiting for this and spoke up.

"That's exactly it. It's incredibly strange, but not just me—no one else around saw anyone holding a bow either. ...Lord Narsus apparently asked the people nearby too, but he couldn't get any information either."

"That's odd."

Bows are generally large.

Smaller ones exist, but arrows fired from such bows lack power.

There's no way it could have taken down skilled guards, let alone a Rank B adventurer.

(A crossbow or something like that... No, but I've hardly ever seen a crossbow before. I don't know how to make one either.)

That's what I thought based on my knowledge from Japan, but since Rei came to Elgin, I hadn't actually seen any crossbows or similar weapons.

(Then... a magic item?)

It's true that a bow needs to be a certain size, but if it's a magic item, that changes everything.

For example, though not a bow, Nebra's Eye was created by modifying a magic item that originally possessed the ability to generate arrows using magic power.

It stands to reason that there are other magic items related to bows and arrows that Rei simply doesn't know about.

Rei understood he didn't know every magic item in Elgin.

(Besides, it's weird that no one saw the face of the person who attacked using a bow. Was it a magic item or something too?)

But Gosh wasn't exactly a city that advanced.

Sure, it ranked among the top five cities in the Solrain Kingdom, but it remained a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom... and one with relatively low national power compared to its neighbors.

From what Rei observed, thanks to Ryubrand's competent governance, the residents of Gosh weren't suffering. But that didn't mean they were wealthy enough to amass large quantities of magic items.

When it comes to individuals who could afford many magic items, Daldora, who possessed the Sand Ship, immediately comes to mind.

But now, that very Daldora was being targeted for assassination.

At first, Rei had considered it might be an alibi-making scheme, but when told that without the intervention of his guard, Narsus, his head would have been pierced by an arrow, he had no choice but to abandon that thought.

As that thought crossed Rei's mind, Rei's face twisted slightly in displeasure.

Things were already complicated enough as it was; getting dragged into even more trouble was something he'd prefer to avoid if possible.

...Though, knowing his own personality, he was certain he'd probably charge right into any trouble that arose.
