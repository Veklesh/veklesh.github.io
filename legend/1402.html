At the spot where the Giganto Turtle collapsed to the ground, those who had participated in the battle, including Rei, shared their joy with one another.

It was only natural. Even among adventurers, few could challenge such a colossal monster in battle.

And especially...

"Injuries, yes, but no deaths... Truly a remarkable feat."

While there were certainly those with broken limbs, from the perspective of fatalities, there were none.

Considering they had fought a monster like the Giganto Turtle, it was a result that could almost be called a miracle.

Still, even if it was a miracle, it was a miracle with a reason.

The Giganto Turtle ignored all others, targeting only Rei.

And Rei was flying around in the air on Set, not acting together with the other adventurers.

Thanks to that, the other adventurers remained unnoticed by the Giganto Turtle as the battle continued.

To the Giganto Turtle, whose body was like a small mountain, everyone except Rei and Set was insignificant... likely no more than flies buzzing around it.

That's precisely why, even under relentless attack, they paid it no mind... until finally, their legs were swept out from under them.

"Well, I suppose facing a monster this size and only having casualties is quite an achievement."

Marina, having reached Rei's side, said this with a smile.

The fact that none of the adventurers died owed much to Marina's spirit magic.

She had saved adventurers from being crushed under the Giganto Turtle's feet countless times.

Without Marina, there would undoubtedly have been casualties.

"You played a part in that too. ...Still, what are we going to do about this monster?"

Her gaze fell upon the Giganto Turtle's corpse.

A short distance away lay one of the severed heads Rei had cut off, along with the tip of its tail.

Given the sheer size of this Giganto Turtle, butchering it for materials would be a massive undertaking.

And now, it was already spring.

With the weather only getting warmer from here on out, it was certain to rot.

Of course, if Rei used Misty Ring, that problem would be solved.

"We'll just have to hire adventurers to do the butchering, right? Like Rei used to do. ...And of course, everyone here will help out too."

At Marina's words, the adventurers who had heard her responded as if it were only natural—waving their hands, nodding, or letting out a roar.

Having participated in the defeat of such a massive monster, many were likely thinking of selling the Giganto Turtle's materials or using them to craft their own equipment.

Of course, Rei didn't intend to deny that.

If asked whether he could have defeated the Giganto Turtle alone, he'd have to admit he wasn't sure.

(No, I think I could have. I think so... but I probably wouldn't have come out unscathed.)

The sheer size difference was the fundamental issue.

Even wearing the Dragon Robe, if attacked by a monster with the mass of a Giganto Turtle, it's entirely possible to sustain injuries from the sheer impact, even if not directly wounded.

"At this size... dismantling it could take months,"

Vihera announced this as she moved over to Rei and the others.

Set was naturally by Rei's side too, so all the members of Crimson Wings were gathered here now.

...Only Byune was absent, making a full gathering impossible.

"Anyway... right. I'll speak with those who joined the battle later and find out how they plan to divide the spoils."

"Ah, that would be a great help."

As someone who had been active as a guildmaster, Marina was skilled at that sort of coordination.

Rei was naturally bad at that sort of coordination, and while Vihera could handle it if pushed, she wasn't exactly eager to do so. As for Byune, communication itself would likely be impossible before any negotiation could even begin.

Considering that, entrusting this kind of negotiation to Marina was definitely the best choice.

"I know you understand this..."

"Yes, I'll make sure we secure the magic stones."

Before Rei could finish, Marina cut him off.

Knowing about beast magic, it was unthinkable that Rei would overlook the magic stones from such a massive monster.

Her words came from that understanding, and Rei breathed a sigh of relief at Marina's assurance.

Then, as if it were an afterthought, he voiced another request.

"Secure this monster's meat too."

"I don't think it's necessary to go out of your way for that."

Given its size, easily mistaken for a small mountain, it was certain there was more than enough meat to feed everyone present.

Considering that, there's no need to fuss over portions.

"Well, if it's just for us to eat, then sure... But obviously, we can sell it, right?"

"Well... I suppose so."

The Giganto Turtle is a monster I've never seen before.

It was truly an embodiment of Treant Forest itself, and as such, it was undoubtedly a monster rarely seen elsewhere.

Precisely because of that, its exact rank was unclear, but it was undoubtedly at least Rank A.

That said, for Rei, who had combat experience against the Silver Lion, he could state with certainty that while it was Rank A, it was not S-rank.

In any case, it was certain it wasn't a Rank B or Rank C monster, let alone a Rank D or Rank E one.

And with few exceptions, the meat of high-rank monsters generally tastes excellent.

In other words, this Giganto Turtle was also an exceptionally high-quality ingredient.

With this much of it available, it would undoubtedly fetch a considerable sum if sold.

"If possible, negotiate so they accept gold coins instead of the raw materials. Of course, if they insist only the slayer gets to eat the meat, I'll let them have an armful or so."

Normally, you'd eat what you could and sell the rest.

But with the Misty Ring, Rei could preserve the meat indefinitely.

In fact, it wouldn't be difficult for Rei and the others alone to consume all this meat.

...Especially since Rei and Set are basically huge eaters, and Byune eats more than you'd expect for her size.

"Right. I understand. I think it should be fine."

Rei was slightly surprised by Marina's casual response.

Shifting her gaze to the fallen Giganto Turtle, she was reminded once more of its sheer size... and just how formidable an opponent it had been. It made her wonder why Marina could say it so easily.

"That's awfully casual. Are you really sure it'll be okay?"

"Yes. ...Besides, you were the one who did the most to defeat this monster... or rather, you defeated the Giganto Turtle almost single-handedly, right? So I think everyone will accept putting your wishes first. Of course, some might not fully accept it inwardly."

Honestly, if Rei and Set weren't here, if asked whether we could have defeated the Giganto Turtle... most wouldn't be able to nod with confidence.

Some possessed attack methods that might have worked, like Marina's spirit magic or Vihera's Demon-Infused Palm.

But... even so, sheer size alone can be a massive advantage.

It was Rei who overcame that disadvantage... and when such a request came from Rei, there was basically no one who would say no.

If anyone foolishly tried to complain to Rei about it, they'd be making an enemy of Rei.

Considering those who've witnessed his power up close, few would willingly choose to antagonize Rei.

Of course, if Rei were to act tyrannically regarding payment... like offering only a few copper coins instead of materials after fighting a Giganto Turtle, that would be a different story.

But Rei insisted on paying in gold coins, so there was no need to worry about that.

"Well, I suppose it's time for a little rest."

Rei murmured, gazing up at the sky.

The excitement of battle kept any drowsiness at bay, but it was now a time truly worthy of being called midnight.

...No, perhaps it would be more accurate to call it dawn rather than midnight.

Truthfully, Rei felt like staying up and making noise.

But once morning came, he'd have to report this incident to the guild, and the guild would also need to report it to Lord Daskar.

"Right. ...Oh? But it seems we have one more job to do before that?"

Marina nodded at Rei's words, but the next moment, she turned her gaze toward Gilm and murmured.

Following Marina's gaze, Rei saw over ten carriages.

Among them were the carriages returned to Gilm because the battle with the Giganto Turtle was about to begin.

Of course, it was still night, so only those with night vision abilities, like Rei, could make them out.

"What are they here for?"

"Well... I asked you to call for reinforcements, right? Probably that. But when the reinforcements arrived, the Giganto Turtle was already defeated. Must have been a letdown for them."

A monster appeared that could threaten Gilm itself, so I asked for reinforcements.

That's the reinforcements I gathered by making such unreasonable demands.

At this hour, most adventurers would naturally be asleep.

Some might have been drinking at the tavern, or even heading to the brothel.

Even among those, they probably gathered the ones who weren't too drunk and sent them as reinforcements... but when they actually rushed over in carriages, the battle was already over.

It's no wonder some of those rushed here as reinforcements feel dissatisfied.

"Well, what can I say? There's no way we'd deliberately prolong the fight when we could have finished it off earlier."

Rei looked again at the corpse of the Giganto Turtle and murmured.

Rei hadn't suffered any injuries, and not a single adventurer had died.

Looking only at the result, one might think it was a complete victory for Rei and his group... and that wouldn't be unreasonable.

But the truth was, it wasn't quite that kind of complete victory. That was the honest feeling of everyone who participated in the battle, including Rei.

Especially for ordinary adventurers lacking Rei's skill, even with Marina's spirit magic, it had been a battle akin to walking a tightrope.

So, if they were told to drag out the fight until reinforcements arrived, the adventurers' reaction was as obvious as day.

(Please, let no one come here suggesting such foolishness. Especially knights.)

Whether it's due to their position on the frontier or the fact that Daskar stands at the pinnacle... fundamentally, few knights affiliated with Gilm would ever suggest such foolishness.

...Few, but not none. That part was a headache for Rei too.

In any case, they should determine how to respond after confirming who exactly this person arriving as reinforcements is.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei waited with Marina, Vihera, and Set for the approaching carriage convoy.

Naturally, the sight of Rei and the others drew the attention of the other adventurers.

So, naturally, adventurers began gathering around Rei and the others... and then the carriages stopped.

When they left Gilm, they were probably going full speed... or at least as fast as they could manage.

But upon seeing the Giganto Turtle lying prone on the ground, they must have judged there was no urgency. The carriage's speed upon approaching wasn't particularly high.

Because of that, it was able to stop abruptly when it came to a halt.

The carriage door opened, and eventually a knight appeared.

The fact that he could recognize the figure as a knight was partly due to the knight wearing metal armor, unusual for an adventurer... but more significantly, it was because Rei knew the person.

Had it been someone Rei didn't know, it might have been questionable whether he would have recognized the figure as a knight.

Adventurers, who generally handle various requests like exploration and gathering in addition to combat, prefer equipment that allows for easy movement.

Leather armor made from monster hides, for instance, rather than metal armor.

Of course, that's just a strong tendency, not an absolute rule.

"Rei, I'm glad to see you're safe. ...Looks like we didn't need to come after all."

The knight addressed Rei with a friendly smile.
