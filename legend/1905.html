"Hmm, so you didn't find the magic item after all."

That night, after the Giganto Turtle had been dismantled, as they ate in Marina’s backyard and talked about the day’s events, Elena said just that.

Marina, Vihera, and Ara all cast slightly exasperated glances at Rei... but Rei nodded without seeming particularly bothered.

“Yeah. At any rate, there’s no doubt that there weren’t any magic items within the area I searched today. I thought Set’s five senses would pick up some kind of clue… too bad.”

Saying that, Rei watches Set, who has Yellow on his back, running around the garden.

It was a half-hearted attempt, based on the hope that a magic item might be there.

That’s precisely why he doesn’t seem particularly bothered that they didn’t find one this time.

“Hmm, but honestly, it does make me curious. What kind of method could possibly be used to control a kobold like that…? If it’s not a magic item, then it must be something else… like, perhaps some kind of ritual?”

“So you’re the ritual type, Ara? Well then, I’m… let’s see. I’ll go with a skill.”

“Lady Elena, isn’t ‘skill’ a bit of a stretch…?”

“Is that so? Skills come in all shapes and sizes. Since they’re special abilities that people who can’t use magic acquire by harnessing magical power, there’s a real possibility it could actually be a skill.”

At Marina’s words, Ara wore an expression of disbelief.

Ara couldn’t use magic and didn’t have enough magical power to create a skill.

That’s probably why she doesn’t quite understand that aspect of things.

...Though, in a sense, Ara’s brute strength might be something akin to a skill.

After all, that strength, which belies her appearance, is more than enough to swing a Power Axe larger than a standard one.

“Hmm… In that case, I’d say I’m voting for a rare species or a higher-tier species.”

“No, in Vihera’s case, she just wants to fight strong monsters. To begin with, no matter how high-tier or rare a species is, I don’t think we can expect it to be based on a kobold.”

Given that Vihera loves combat, that choice was less a prediction and more a strong desire for things to be that way.

That said, if we’re going to talk about that, Rei is also collecting kobolds with magic items precisely because she wants them herself, so she certainly can’t talk.

“So, in the end, we won’t know what’s causing the kobolds to keep attacking until we actually investigate, right? …Marina, can’t you figure anything out with your spirit magic?”

“Listen, Elena. Just because I have spirit magic doesn’t mean I can do anything, you know?”

Marina said this with a hint of exasperation, but to Rei, who had been listening nearby, it felt a little disappointing.

He had been planning to ask Marina if she could use her elemental magic to see if she could find any clues, if possible.

But perhaps noticing Rei’s expression, Marina now turned a look of exasperation toward him as well.

“What’s up, Rei? Were you also planning to have me use spirit magic to investigate the kobold situation?”

“Uh… no, that’s not it. Come to think of it, I realized I’d forgotten to give you guys the souvenirs from Anesis.”

Pressed by Marina, he recalled what had happened earlier that day and realized he hadn’t given them the souvenirs yet.

Patting himself on the back for remembering the souvenirs earlier that day, Rei took a gem out of his Misty Ring.

It was a white gem.

It was the centerpiece of the souvenirs from Anesis.

...There were plenty of other souvenirs, but the fact that he pulled out the gem—which should have been the last thing he took out—first was probably because Rei was in a hurry, after all.

“Oh, a gemstone? A white gemstone is quite rare, isn’t it? …Rei, what is this gemstone?”

At first, Marina seemed intrigued by the gem, but when she picked one up, she must have realized it wasn’t an ordinary gem. She shot Rei a slightly stern look and asked.

“Oh, you figured it out? …Yes, that’s not an ordinary gem. It’s a kind of… well, you could call it a magic item. That white gem is imbued with healing magic. It’s a one-time use item, but it allows you to cast a healing spell instantly.”

Gems, magic.

At those keywords, Byune, who had been silently eating her meal, stopped moving.

If it had been just one of those things, Byune probably wouldn’t have given it a second thought.

But when those two things come together, it’s a different story.

Because that… is connected to the fate of Byune’s parents.

Her parents, who were sacrificed by a certain madman.

The reason that madman needed a sacrifice was because of an object that could be called gem magic.

“...Hmm.”

A low... yet resolute murmur.

Upon hearing that murmur, Rei—perhaps having anticipated Byune’s reaction—spoke without a trace of surprise.

And, having understood what Byune was trying to say, he spoke.

“That’s right. The fact that healing magic is sealed within this gemstone makes it exactly what you think it is, Byune. …However!”

"Exactly what you think." Hearing Rei say this, Byune instinctively tried to hurl her long needle at him, but... the force of Rei’s words froze her movement.

“Hmm.”

Rei felt relieved at that murmur, spoken as if to urge him to continue.

If it had been an attack from Byune, it wouldn’t have been difficult to dodge, even from a seated position.

But the fact that Byune had stopped moving at Rei’s words before things escalated to such a violent confrontation meant that Byune hadn’t completely lost her cool yet.

The fact that she was taking such a cautious approach regarding the matter involving gem magic was proof that Byune had matured compared to before.

……Though, even for the usually expressionless Byune, where had that impassive demeanor gone now? She was glaring at Rei with a stern gaze.

As if to calm such a Byune, Rei spoke up.

“Listen. First, as a basic premise, the technology to seal magic within this gem… is, as you suspect, based on the technology developed by Puri.”

At the mention of “Pri,” Byune’s gaze grows even harsher.

That’s only natural, given that the name of the one directly responsible for her parents’ deaths had been mentioned.

Still, the fact that he didn’t reflexively attack like he did earlier suggests that Byune has calmed down somewhat.

“This technology originated from Pri… no, it was technology possessed by the Marschel family that was acquired and developed by the Noble Faction. But of course, there’s no way they would tolerate practices that require human sacrifices, so you can rest assured on that front.”

At Rei’s mention of “nobles of the Noble Faction,” Byune glanced at Elena, but immediately looked back at Rei, urging him to continue.

The shift from his earlier stern expression to a serious one—was it because he had briefly looked at Elena in between?

...Or perhaps it was because Vihera had stroked his back to calm Byune down.

(Given this situation, maybe I shouldn’t mention Lemuria’s name. …Judging by Byune’s demeanor, if I’m not careful, he might actually go and try to meet Lemuria in person.)

Byune is a thief, but given her personality, she’s not good at gathering information.

Well, to be precise, while she’s good at gathering information by eavesdropping on others’ conversations, she’s not good at taking the initiative to seek out specific information herself.

Given that he usually only says “Hmm,” that might be to be expected.

Given that, Rei concluded that if he didn’t mention Lemuria’s name here, it would be difficult for them to arrive at that name on their own.

That said, if Vihera were here—since she can generally understand what Byune is trying to say—things might be different.

“So, does that gem really not require a sacrifice?”

It wasn’t exactly on Byune’s behalf, but Vihera asked Rei.

“I didn’t actually see the gem being made myself, but that’s what the person who gave it to me told me.”

“Wait a minute, if that’s the case…”

“If that person was lying, then it’s all for nothing.”

Vihera said.

In fact, Vihera isn’t wrong, and her negative impression of the aristocrats in the Noble Faction likely plays a role as well.

While she might be more lenient with people she knows, like Elena or Ara, Vihera hasn’t forgotten that there are nobles who tried to obstruct the expansion of Gilm.

Given that, it was only natural that Vihera’s impression of the Noble Faction would be poor.

“Don’t worry. The person who made this gem isn’t the type to lie. I’ll vouch for that.”

Hearing these words from Elena, who knew Lemuria, Vihera… and even Byune, though somewhat reluctantly, were convinced.

“That’s why, when sealing magic into this gem, we avoid using sacrifices. To put it simply, this gem is a one-time-use item.”

The gem magic Puri used in battle allowed her to cast spells repeatedly from the same gem.

In reality, there was likely a limit to the number of uses, but at least from the sensation Rei felt while fighting, it seemed as though he could cast magic a dozen or so times.

In that sense, the gem Rei placed on the table was clearly inferior in terms of performance.

……That said, this is strictly limited to the number of uses; the fact that it doesn’t require a sacrifice is of great significance.

If they were to market it on a large scale, the benefits would be particularly significant.

If it were made using sacrifices, marketing it that way would be out of the question, but if gem magic could be created without sacrifices, there was no problem with selling it. …Though knowing where that technology originally came from, and what it actually entailed, might make one feel a bit queasy.

“…………Hmm”

After a few seconds of silence, a small sound like that finally escaped from Byune’s lips.

She probably wasn’t entirely convinced by Rei’s explanation.

But she must have realized that, even so, she couldn’t complain.

In fact, if this gem were put up for sale, there’s no doubt it would be a valuable item for adventurers… no, for soldiers, knights, merchants, and all sorts of other people as well.

Since it involves using a gem, it would be expensive and not something one could easily afford.

“Anyway… I’ve explained a lot, but this is a magic item that lets you cast healing spells immediately in an emergency. Please take it.”

Saying that, Rei placed a white gem in front of each of the three—Marina, Vihera, and Byune.

He had considered that giving this gem to Byune might seem like a form of harassment in a way, but currently, Byune had the lowest combat ability among the members of the Crimson Wings led by Rei.

Furthermore, given her profession as a thief, Byune would need to scout ahead to check on the enemy’s movements, so having a means of recovery was essential for her.

Of course, she already had potions and the like, but given the gem’s size, it wouldn’t hurt to have it on hand.

After all, it’s clear that in a pinch, it would be easier to use than a potion.

“……Hmm.”

Perhaps realizing this, Byune picks up the gem Rei placed before her, her face clouded with a complex expression.

Precisely because Byune is usually expressionless, that complex look revealed even more of her inner thoughts.

Once Byune took the gem, Marina and Vihera also reached for theirs.

“Speaking of which, healing… Is it really okay for me to have this?”

The one who turned to Rei and asked this was Marina, who was in charge of healing using water spirit magic within the Crimson Wings.

In response, Rei nodded, as if to say it was only natural.

“Marina’s spirit magic can heal too, but it takes some time, right? This provides instant healing. Though, of course, it wouldn’t work for a fatal wound.”

“I suppose so. The magical power I sense from this gem isn’t that strong.”

Even so, in terms of being able to activate it immediately, gem magic is an incredibly convenient tool.

“If this were actually put up for sale, it would probably sell quite well, wouldn’t it?”

“It would definitely sell.”

Marina replied immediately to Vihera’s words.

“Personally, it would be a huge help if I could seal my own magic into a gem that doesn’t already have magic sealed inside it.”

The biggest hurdle in using magic is, of course, the incantation.

But if it were possible to seal magic inside something, that would mean being able to use magic instantly without having to chant a spell.

“That sounds interesting… but is it possible?”

“Well, I’m not sure. I do hope it’ll be possible in the future, though.”

That was Rei’s reply to Vihera’s words.
