The smell of something burning hung in the air.

There was no need to wonder where that smell was coming from.

It came from what could be called the former Zoboz, located a short distance away from Rei.

"...Is it over? That was quite a struggle, no matter how you look at it."

Rei murmured thoughtfully as he released the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, his eyes already fixed on the sunset, now about eighty percent submerged.

Dusk was drawing to a close, and night would soon fall.

For the slums, this was the most dangerous time of all.

Though, they probably wouldn't dare try anything against Rei and his group now.

The slums seemed vast yet felt confined.

Especially with so many desperate to survive, information spreads at an astonishing speed.

Naturally, for such people, Rei... and those with him, were figures who would undoubtedly inflict heavy damage on anyone who dared to attack them.

Those who would attack such people without knowing their own limits... well, it's precisely why the slums are the slums that you can't say there are none.

Still, such people are probably few and far between.

"So, Rei. What are we doing now? We can't just leave these three like this, can we?"

Vihera asked Rei as he retracted the claws on his gauntlets and the blades on his greaves.

His gaze fell upon three figures: Dias, whose right arm had been torn off along with his leather armor; Adria, still immobilized with Elena's foot planted firmly on her back; and Paul, whose entire body was now covered in thorn spears, leaving only his face untouched by the invasive thorns.

All but Adria were injured, though none were fatally wounded.

(Well, that Dias over there... he'll probably die if left like this. Ah well, necessary expenses, I suppose.)

The mastermind behind the recent incidents in Gilm involving monsters wielding magic items was dead, but as his accomplices, their deaths would cause various problems for the Daskar. Misty Ring retrieved a potion.

That said, the potion he took wasn't one of the more expensive ones Rei carried.

He chose it based on the judgment that as long as Dias stayed alive until he could hand him over to the guards, they'd figure out the rest over there.

「Vihera」

"What? ...Is that okay?"

Upon receiving the potion, Vihera seemed to understand what Rei was urging her to do. She asked if it was truly okay to heal him.

"Yeah. Having more people around makes things easier for the guards when we explain the situation. ...Besides, I don't mind skilled fighters."

"Hmm. Well, if that's what you say, Rei, I don't mind."

Even as she said this, a smile touched Vihera's lips. She too didn't dislike skilled individuals. ...No, perhaps Vihera felt it even more strongly, given her preference for combat.

Watching Vihera administer the potion to Dias, Rei shifted his gaze slightly away... to where a wind scale lay on the ground.

It was true he didn't dislike skilled opponents. But it was also true that the wind scales were one of the major reasons he'd helped Dias.

(They said I could keep the magic item, but they'll definitely investigate it. ...I suppose I should secure it for now.)

Considering this incident was caused by an alchemist, the magic items used in the battle would undoubtedly be temporarily confiscated for investigation by the guard or the knights.

Rei understood it was unavoidable for an item that could take over a person's body. Yet, having already prevented it from possessing his own body when he acquired the hammer, he felt strongly that he himself wouldn't be a problem, even with that kind of magic item.

Azimov likely shared this interest in the Wind Scale. Deciding there were no enemies left to fight, he dashed toward the Wind Scale lying on the ground before Rei could move.

Behind him, Byune followed, presumably as a guard of sorts.

Rei hesitated for a moment about stopping Azimov, but called out to him anyway.

"Azimov, many magic items made by Zubozu have the ability to take control of their users. That claymore didn't seem to show any signs of taking control, but be careful not to touch it carelessly."

In the end, he didn't stop him, only offering a warning.

Normally, it would be better to stop him.

But he believed that since Ajimov was a skilled alchemist, he was likely acting with that understanding.

At the same time, he couldn't deny the thought that seeing the magic item Zubozu had created might provide some reference for the spear he was currently having Azimov work on.

"I know. I'm an alchemist, after all. You don't need to worry about that."

"...I hope so. Anyway, the question is what we do next."

Leaving Azimov observing the Wind Scale, Rei contemplated what they should do next.

"Normally, causing this much commotion would bring guards rushing over immediately... right?"

If this weren't the slums, that might have been possible, but given that it was the slums, they couldn't expect the guards to show up.

"So... does that mean we have to take them ourselves?"

At Vihera's words, Elena scanned her surroundings.

Dias's bleeding right arm had stopped for now, and moving it posed no problem.

Paul was still bound by the thorns. Removing the thorn spear would require treatment for the puncture wounds, but he could also move without hindrance.

And the last one... Elena's gaze fell to Adria, lying at her feet.

Though unable to see Elena's face, he must have sensed her gaze. Despite being unable to move, Adria, who was trying desperately to escape this situation, opened his mouth.

"I don't plan to do anything more either, so I'd like you to move your foot soon... Is that okay?"

"No."

Elena immediately rejected Adria's request.

Surprised for a moment, as if she hadn't expected such a quick refusal, Adria quickly spoke again.

"Why? I know my own strength better than anyone. If I were to oppose you two, I'd have absolutely no chance of winning in this state. I probably couldn't even escape. So, I don't see why you need to keep me pinned down like this?"

"Is that so? It's true I can't escape as things stand now. But if I could move freely, wouldn't I be able to escape then? ...Right. I suppose I should investigate while I have the chance. Byune."

"Mm."

Byune, who had been checking for enemies around Azimov, muttered briefly when Elena called her and turned her gaze.

"Sorry, but could you check if this one has any magic items?"

"Mm."

Nodding at Elena's words, he headed straight for Adria.

Currently, Byune was the most suitable person to examine what Adria possessed.

Being a thief, Byune had a keen eye for such things, which was a major factor.

Beyond that, Elena had asked Byune because she couldn't very well have men like Rei or Azimov conduct a physical search of Adria.

Anyway, as Byune groped Adria's body, pulling out magic items hidden in various places along with ordinary tools, Elena shifted her gaze to a small hut a short distance away.

It was the hut Yellow had spotted from above.

The slum district was like a maze, so they ended up having to hire a guide after all.

"Rei, aren't you going to check that hut?"

At Elena's words, Rei also turned his gaze toward the hut.

If asked whether they needed to check that hut, it was definitely worth investigating.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei called out to Azimov, who was concentrating on the Wind Scales.

"Azimov, can I have a word?"

"What is it? Sorry, but I need to focus on this right now. If you have business, after I finish examining this..."

Azimov immediately replied, then turned his gaze back to the Wind Scale and resumed his examination.

Watching this, Rei thought for a moment before letting out a conspicuously loud sigh.

"I see. That's too bad. I was planning to go check out that alchemist's hideout, whatever it's called. But if that's how you feel, Ajimov, I'll go alone..."

"Wait!"

"Whoa!"

Go investigate.

That's what Rei was about to say, but suddenly he realized that Azimov, who should have been examining the wind scales just moments ago, had somehow... truly, somehow, appeared beside Rei without a sound.

Rei stared at Azimov in utter shock. He had appeared right next to him without giving off the slightest hint of his presence.

Unable to believe Ajimov was capable of such stealth, Rei was momentarily speechless. But Ajimov's words snapped her back to reality.

"Hey, Rei. You're going to that hut, right? I'm coming too, so let's get moving."

"...Ah, yeah."

Rei vaguely understood what Azimov meant, his eyes gleaming with curiosity.

(So he's the type who surpasses his own limits when something interests him... Maybe it's a good thing he's an alchemist.)

If Azimov had been interested in thievery and gotten hooked on it... a large-scale band of thieves would probably have been born.

That's what Rei thought, but even so, he couldn't picture Azimov actually being a thief.

"Rei, come on, you're going, right!"

"Got it. Got it, so stop rushing me."

Though Azimov looked every bit an adult on the outside, seeing him now brought to mind a child unable to resist sweets.

Anyway, Rei was also curious about what kind of magic items might be hidden in Zubo's lair, so he followed Azimov.

Seeing Rei like that, a faint... truly faint curve appeared at the corner of Adria's mouth. But perhaps because she was lying face-down, no one noticed.

After moving from the battle site to the hut's entrance, Rei realized he had a strong curiosity about the hut himself.

The instant he reached for the door to open it, Rei saw the hut bulge outward from within. Almost reflexively, he grabbed Ajimov's clothes nearby and leapt backward from the spot.

"Guh!"

The sound of Azimov being suddenly yanked by his clothes reached Rei's ears, but it was instantly drowned out by a much louder boom.

Rei landed on the ground, and the hut exploded.

Yes. It wasn't fire; explosion was the correct term.

"Dammit!"

Cursing under his breath, he focused entirely on defense, using his Dragon Robe to block the countless fragments of the hut hurtling toward him.

Fortunately, Azimov was behind Rei, so shielding her wasn't difficult.

The fragments struck the Dragon Robe one after another, but the Dragon Robe was a magic item crafted by sandwiching the scales of a dragon that had lived for hundreds of years between layers of dragon hide.

Possessing extremely high physical and magical defense, the fragments couldn't breach its protection.

However, the impact of the fragments striking the Dragon Robe was another matter.

Even though the Dragon Robe mitigated it, the impact itself passed through to Rei's body one after another.

Had he drawn the Death Scythe from Misty Ring, he could have easily used its length to spin it like a shield in his hand or employed a Magic Shield for defense.

But right now, since they were searching inside the hut, the Death Scythe remained stored inside the Misty Ring.

Calling it carelessness... would be harsh.

Regardless, Rei, who had taken the successive blows of the destroyed hut's fragments at close range, endured the pain inflicted by the impacts and focused his awareness on Azimov, who should be behind him.

Being an alchemist dedicated solely to research, Azimov wasn't adept at moving his body on the spur of the moment.

He could use a certain amount of magic, but that required incantations.

In other words, in the current situation, there was nothing Azimov could do... Just as Rei thought this, the impact he felt on his body suddenly lessened.

Rei couldn't comprehend what had happened, but if he had turned around, he would have noticed the bracelet on Azimov's wrist emitting a blue light.

Azimov couldn't suddenly cast magic or dodge attacks.

However, he could channel magical power into the magic item he wore and activate it.

The blue circular barrier that appeared within a few meters, including Rei, had the effect of slightly reducing the momentum of the flying debris from the hut.
