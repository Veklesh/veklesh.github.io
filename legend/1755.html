A few days had passed since Rei went Gamelion hunting… That day, a Daskar was seen near the main gate of Gilm.

Naturally, Daskar wasn’t the only one there.

Some were assigned to guard Daskar, while others served as his aides.

It was only natural, but acting that way was bound to draw attention.

Since Daskar was the lord of Gilm, many people knew who he was.

Among those who had come for the expansion of Gilm, there were some who did not recognize Daskar’s face, but once told by those around them, they immediately understood who he was—and were surprised at the same time.

Perhaps someone important was coming.

In fact, given the scale of this expansion project, it wouldn’t be surprising if someone important were to arrive.

……In that sense, Princess General Elena is undoubtedly an important figure as well.

“Excuse me. Is Lord Daskar actually out here in the town?”

A merchant who was about to enter Gilm happened to spot a guard he knew and called out to him.

The guard looked around at the merchant’s words.

Perhaps because quite a crowd had gathered, no one seemed to be paying attention to him talking to the merchant.

Realizing this, and seeing that the guard was still hesitating over whether he should speak up, the merchant seized the moment to address him.

“I’ll treat you to some Gamelion skewers at Sudras Inn tonight.”

“……Hmm, ahem. Fine. If you insist that much, I’ll tell you.”

Sudras Inn is a hidden gem known among connoisseurs for its meat dishes—especially those that pair perfectly with alcohol.

So, being treated to grilled Gamelion skewers—which are in season right now (though, to be precise, the season is already winding down)—was more than enough reward for the guard.

That said, other guards who overheard the conversation were giving that guard a look of disbelief.

But they didn’t say anything to reprimand him.

Of course, if he were about to reveal some kind of top-secret information, his fellow guards would have stopped him.

However, today’s incident wasn’t something that needed to be kept secret at all costs.

On the contrary, it was the sort of thing that would be more effective if shared with others, and right now, people hired by Daskar—or, more precisely, his subordinates—were spreading information throughout the city about those who were on their way.

Given that, there is absolutely no problem with the guard mentioning that information here.

In fact, once inside Gilm, that sort of information is readily available without having to go so far as to treat someone to Gamelion skewers.

In that sense, the merchant clearly stands to lose out, but the guards, who naturally know this, say nothing.

After all, they get to eat Gamelion skewers at a restaurant with great food.

“Do you know the Kingdom of Reburina?”

“Um… It’s a vassal state of the Kingdom of Mireana, right? And a pretty small one at that.”

The merchant answered as if recalling something, but the fact that he knew the name of the small country of Reburina was, in a sense, unusual.

The guard, who had asked, “Do you actually know it?”, looked slightly surprised at the straightforward answer.

“I’m surprised. You really know your stuff.”

“Yeah, well, apparently my grandpa was from a village near the Kingdom of Reburina.”

“I see. Well, to put it simply, refugees from the Kingdom of Reburina are coming to Gilm. We’re taking them in.”

“……Refugees? Is that why Lord Daskar went out of his way to come to the outskirts of town?”

Even if they aren’t exactly refugees, many people come to Gilm in search of work.

That’s especially noticeable now that we’re in the middle of expansion work.

So why would someone with such a high title as a Margrave go out of his way to leave Gilm just to welcome refugees?

It was only natural for the merchant to have such doubts.

But the guard merely smiled at the merchant’s words.

Of course, if only a few refugees were arriving, there would be no need for Daskar, the lord of Gilm, to go out of his way to greet them like this.

However… the situation was entirely different when the group consisted of nearly a thousand people, all traveling by carriage, and—more importantly—a group that had become proficient with bows—albeit only bows—during their journey from the Kingdom of Reburina to Gilm.

That said, it was true that only a few actually mastered the bow; most of them demonstrated their high combat effectiveness by firing arrows in large numbers.

And above all… the one leading them was Rei, a figure Daskar could rely on immensely.

Given that, there was no doubt that the arriving refugees would be a tremendous asset to Daskar as well.

While there are many exceptions, such as those with aliases like Rei and high-ranking adventurers, the truth that numbers equal strength remains unchanged.

Since such a force is coming to Gilm of its own volition, there was simply no option for Daskar not to welcome them.

Of course, not everyone who arrived would necessarily join the guard or the military; in fact, if that were to happen, it would be a problem for Daskar.

Still, the best-case scenario was that a certain percentage—those who were sufficiently trained and worthy of being called immediate combatants—would become part of their fighting force.

“They’re here!”

Suddenly, a man known for his keen eyesight among the guards shouted.

At first, the others didn’t see it, but after a moment, a few of those with keen eyesight… and then, a little later, the rest of them too, began to notice the massive number of carriages gradually coming into view.

Naturally, a single road couldn’t possibly accommodate over a hundred carriages, and many were spilling out onto the sides of the road.

Daskar and his men had heard about this, but seeing over a hundred carriages in person still took them by surprise.

Of course, this wasn’t the first time they’d seen such a large number of carriages.

After all, during the Bestir Empire’s wars, it wasn’t uncommon to see a thousand… no, when you add them all up, over ten thousand carriages on the move.

But even so, seeing it all with his own eyes like this brought various thoughts to mind.

「Rei」

“Yes?”

Daskar called out to Rei, who was waiting nearby.

This whole incident began with Rei’s actions.

Of course, the reason for that... since it was Daskar who asked him to take revenge on the organization that had been harassing Gilm, it might be natural in a way.

“Just to confirm, you’re sure it was that group of carriages, right?”

“Yes. …Actually, if anyone other than Sura and her group had made it all the way to Gilm with that many carriages, things would probably have gotten pretty messy.”

Over a hundred carriages appearing out of nowhere.

If such a thing existed, it would undoubtedly spell trouble no matter how you looked at it.

Of course, Daskar probably understood Rei’s concerns, but it was only natural that he still felt compelled to voice them.

“Still…”

Daskar, who had been watching the approaching herd of carriages, muttered suddenly.

“What is it?” Rei asked, turning his gaze toward him, only to find Daskar wearing an unexpectedly stern expression.

Rei, who had assumed Daskar would say whatever he had to say in a lighthearted manner, was slightly taken aback by his appearance.

“What’s wrong? Is there something about that group of carriages… something that concerns you regarding Sura and the others?”

“No, it’s the Zolge Trading Company. Even though they arranged for carriages wherever they went, preparing that many must have required a considerable sum of money. In other words, despite being a trading company from the small nation of Reburina, the Zolge Trading Company was making that much profit.”

A bitter expression crossed Daskar’s face as he muttered this.

And no wonder. The fact that the Zolge Trading Company made money in the Kingdom of Reburina is related to the work Jaya was doing… forcibly bringing women there, putting slave collars on them, brainwashing them, and making them work as prostitutes.

Daskar didn’t know whether they were involved in using women as surrogates to bear giants. He didn’t know, but…

“It seems the Zolge Trading Company was serious about moving its base to Gilm in earnest.”

“Huh? Didn’t we talk about that before? I even spoke with Gimekara from the Zolge Trading Company via the Paired Orb.”

“Ah, yes. But they’re merchants. I’d considered the possibility that moving their base to Gilm might have been a lie. …No, while it’s certain they’ll establish some kind of base, I thought it might just be a front. But seeing this, I suppose they really were serious after all.”

From Daskar’s perspective, having more guilds establish their headquarters in Gilm is a welcome development.

To be honest, given that expansion work is currently underway in Gilm, he wants to secure as many residents as possible for the expanded area.

Having a merchant guild with a certain level of influence move in there isn’t a bad thing for Daskar, either.

He understands that… but even so, when it comes to a merchant guild with a questionable reputation like the Zolge Trading Company, he isn’t sure if he should be happy about it.

Especially since Gimekara, who was dispatched by the Zolge Trading Company, told me during our conversation at the Paired Orb that they do business with illegal organizations.

“That’s entirely up to your skill, Lord Daskar.”

“Don’t make it sound so easy.”

Though he replied to Rei’s words in this way, Daskar didn’t seem displeased.

After all, it’s only natural, since the decision of how to handle the Zolge Trading Company truly rests on Daskar’s judgment.

Even as they spoke, the convoy of carriages drew closer… and eventually, the lead carriage came to a stop near Daskar and Rei.

The other carriages stopped in succession, and then, as if they’d been waiting for the right moment, the door of the lead carriage opened.

Emerging from it were the key figures who had led this convoy all the way here:

The four of them: Sura, Sharia, Gimekara, and Rox.

Even Sharia, who usually disliked formal occasions, must have realized that this was necessary.

Although they looked quite uncomfortable, the four of them stepped down from the carriages, moved before Daskar, and knelt on one knee.

“Lord Daskar. We are grateful that you have accepted us. From now on, we intend to make Gilm our home and contribute to its development.”

Speaking on behalf of the four, Sura declared this.

(So, in the end, Sura has become the de facto leader of this group.)

Watching the four of them from near Daskar, Rei accepted this as a natural outcome.

First of all, Rocks was originally an adventurer from Gilm, and he had been acting as an escort as part of his job—though since he eventually started dating Sura, it could no longer be considered purely work.

Sharia is treated as Sura’s subordinate—though in reality, they are friends.

Gimekara’s main focus was on moving the Zolge Trading Company’s headquarters to Gilm; he wasn’t the one leading this group.

As a result, Sura was the only one left.

Given that she had originally led the Resistance, there was no issue with her being a figure everyone could easily accept.

“You’ve come a long way to reach Gilm. The wounds you’ve suffered must be deep. But I hope that life in Gilm will help heal them, even if only a little.”

Daskar’s words, spoken in this manner, exuded the authority befitting his position as the leader of a faction.

Although he does not usually adopt such an attitude intentionally, Daskar is, after all, the leader of the Neutral Faction.

Therefore, naturally, when he chooses to, he can adopt an attitude befitting his position.

“Thank you. We cannot thank you enough, Lord Daskar and all your subordinates.”

“Don’t worry about it. You will now become citizens of Gilm. That means you’ll be just like family to me. If that’s the case, it’s only natural that I’d do everything in my power for my family.”

Daskar’s words were somewhat exaggerated, but they also contained his true feelings.

Sura must have realized this as well, for though she kept her head bowed, her body trembled.

No, it wasn’t just Sura. The others, too, were trembling in a similar way, even if they had their own thoughts on the matter.

Especially for Rocks, who had just started dating Sura, Daskar’s assurance was surely the most welcome thing of all.

“Come on, stand up. Let’s wrap up the formalities here. It’s time to go inside. Sarajiros!”

At Daskar’s call, a man who appeared to be a civil official, standing a short distance away, approached.

“This man, Sarajiros, will be looking after you for a while. If there’s anything you don’t understand, ask him.”

“My name is Sarajiros. I’ll be looking after you all for a while, so please treat me kindly.”

The man, who appeared to be in his forties, bowed deeply. His movements were incredibly refined, and when he looked up, all of the Sura members found their eyes drawn to him.
