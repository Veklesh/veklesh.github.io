After finishing the dissection of the snow salamander, Rei and the others headed straight for the Full Belly Inn.

The battle in the forest this time couldn't be called a complete success.

Still, it was undoubtedly beneficial in terms of confirming their teamwork, and viewed that way, it wasn't a failure.

Of course, the only monster of any real strength was the single Rank B Snow Salamander; the rest were merely goblins.

From Set's perspective, in terms of food, it wasn't a particularly satisfying result.

Regardless, seeing themselves as having ended up with a positive net gain, Rei and the others decided to use the meat for a celebratory feast at the Full Belly Inn.

"Gurrrrrr!"

The one purring happily was, naturally, Set.

Byune was perched on his back, and the people around them who saw Rei and the others looked surprised or curiously at the one person and one beast.

But among Rei and the others, the gaze Husta directed at Set overflowed with apologetic feelings.

...It was only natural. It was winter now.

To eat with Set, they'd have to leave the doors of the restaurant wide open... but considering the outside temperature, that was simply impossible.

In other words, though Set was delighted to join Rei and the others for the celebration, it simply wasn't possible.

"Huh? What's wrong?"

Perhaps noticing Hasta's expression—one that seemed disappointed, sad, apologetic...—Rei asked.

"No, I'm happy you're coming to Manpukutei, but Set coming inside the shop..."

"Ah, that. No need to worry about it."

"Huh?"

Though not quite a grim resolve, Hasta seemed hesitant to speak. Rei simply stated,

"As long as we can borrow a table and chairs, we'll eat outside."

"Huh? Huh? ...But it's cold out there?"

If it were just a quick meal, it might be manageable, but a celebration would mean spending an hour or more outside.

When Husta asked if that was really okay health-wise, Rei smiled and said it was fine.

Thanks to his Dragon Robe, Rei didn't particularly mind the cold, even in the dead of winter.

Eating outside could be troublesome if it snowed, but fortunately, it wasn't snowing now.

Furthermore, regarding the others, it had been confirmed that Marina, who could use spirit magic, could freely manipulate the surrounding temperature to some extent, so that wasn't a problem either.

"Well, it'll work out, so don't worry. Just bring the food we ordered outside the shop, if you could."

"Well, that's fine, but..."

Are you sure that's okay?

Husta's gaze turned toward Marina and Vihera.

The reason that gaze wasn't directed at Byune was simply because she was still riding on Set's back.

"Yes, it's fine. With Marina around, we can usually manage most things."

"...I think that's a bit of an exaggeration."

Marina spoke modestly, but it was true that things had become considerably easier since she started traveling with Marina.

Above all, not having to walk on snowy paths anymore was a huge relief, and she could now adjust the temperature.

Thinking about it that way, it was undeniably true that the constraints on their actions had decreased.

Hasta watched Marina and Vihera's exchange with an envious look.

Of course, part of it was envy toward Rei for being able to travel with such a beauty, but more than that, he envied the idea of having a fixed party.

Hasta became an adventurer to procure meat for his home-run restaurant, Manpukutei. Naturally, forming a fixed party was difficult.

He often joined what were called "random parties" – temporary groups formed on the fly. But there was a huge difference between a random party and a fixed one.

A random party, formed with strangers... or even people you barely know, is vastly stronger than fighting alone in terms of combat power. However, precisely because they are strangers, miscommunication and lack of coordination are not uncommon.

What's more, some players join parties with the intent to harm others, so party members can't focus solely on the enemies.

Of course, such individuals are only a small minority, but there's no guarantee that the people you team up with aren't among them.

That said, it's not uncommon for members who meet in a random party to hit it off and form a permanent party.

In any case, Husta needs to regularly supply meat to the Full Belly Inn, making it difficult to form a fixed party due to reward considerations.

At the same time, he can't leave Gilm... or more accurately, he can't leave the Full Belly Inn, so he can't take on escort-type requests either.

Hasta himself is polite, so many adventurers take a liking to him.

But that very trait became a stumbling block, preventing him from forming a fixed party.

...His father, Dishot, worries about his son, but Husta himself is happy to supply meat to the Full Belly Inn... to be useful to his family, so there was nothing to be done.

Hasta understood this, but he still couldn't help but yearn for a fixed party.

"Oh, speaking of which, I heard those meat buns that are popular lately were also a dish created by Rei-san. Is that true?"

Perhaps thinking that talking about parties would only bring him down, Hasta changed the subject.

Dishot's shop did serve meat buns, and he'd heard rumors that Rei had come up with the recipe. He asked if it was true...

"Hmm, I'm not sure. I only taught them the basic method and stuff like that. I didn't actually make them into proper meat buns, so saying I came up with it might be a bit off."

"Is that so? My dad seems to be trying all sorts of things too, but he can't quite get anything he's satisfied with. If you wouldn't mind, it would really help if you could taste them. Of course, since it's just tasting the meat buns, there's no charge. What do you say?"

Husta's slightly eager tone suggested he wasn't entirely satisfied with the meat buns Dishot made.

Considering that the basic method for making meat buns is known to anyone who wants to learn it, what's needed beyond that is each shop's unique touch.

That's probably what Hasta honestly wants advice on.

"Well, that's something. ...Alright, if you're giving them away for free, I'll try one."

"Really?! Then I'll go tell my dad about it. We'll need to set up chairs and tables too."

With that, Husta ran toward the approaching Manpukutei.

"She's full of energy,"

Marina murmured, seemingly unable to help herself.

From Marina's perspective, Husta must have seemed quite likable.

Working hard for one's family is something that seems possible, but isn't actually that easy.

Especially when it comes to wild parties or solo activities, the hardships are likely even greater.

That's precisely why Hasta's determination to keep going like this seemed admirable.

"Yeah. No doubt he's a filial guy in many ways. ...Well, we're here."

Rei replied to Marina and opened the door to the Manpukutei.

Inside, a buzzing commotion greeted them—a true cacophony worthy of the name.

Manpukutei, offering delicious food, generous portions, and low prices – the perfect trifecta – was an ideal eatery for adventurers.

Even though they were saving up for the winter, it was undoubtedly better to save money whenever possible.

Still, this was a restaurant, not a tavern.

They served some alcohol, but you couldn't have a full-blown party like you could at a tavern.

Rei and the others entered the Mankyutei.

Set waited quietly outside the shop, probably knowing that Rei and the others would be celebrating their victory outside.

Once inside, Rei and the others naturally drew the gaze of the customers already seated.

But upon recognizing them as Rei, Vihera, Marina, and other well-known figures in Gilm, they quickly averted their gaze and returned to their own meals.

(If this were a tavern or guild hall, it might feel a bit more fitting... but doing it like this in a dining hall feels a little off.)

Rei thought this as he watched the adventurers return to their meals.

"Rei, they said we can take this table and chairs over here!"

Hasta, who must have noticed Rei and the others entering the shop, waved and called out to them.

Fortunately, or perhaps not, there seemed to be an empty table left. Told they could take it outside, Rei immediately nodded and headed over.

Ignoring the puzzled stares from nearby customers who had tilted their heads upon hearing Husta's words, "Take the table?" he began stowing the table and chairs one after another into the Misty Ring.

Carrying them as is to move them outside the shop wouldn't have been a problem, but he chose this method because he thought it might be inappropriate to make such a dusty mess while many people were eating.

While it was fairly well-known that Rei possessed an item box, not many had actually seen it used directly before their eyes.

That's precisely why the adventurers dining near the table Rei stowed away stared wide-eyed at his sudden action... but none of them said anything more.

"Ah, well then, just bring over some celebratory food. Oh, and... I'd be happy if you could make something with this."

As if swapping places with the table and chairs, pure white meat appeared... Rei handed the Snow Salamander meat to Husta, then left the shop alongside Marina and Vihera.

"Hey, did we really need to come inside the shop?"

"I dunno? But does it really matter either way?"

Vihera and Marina exchanged words before heading outside.

Then, as Rei pulled out a table and chairs onto the snow, Marina used her spirit magic to arrange the surroundings.

In no time, the preparations for the... celebration feast are complete. As if waiting for this moment, Hasta arrives carrying several plates of food.

The meat-heavy spread was undoubtedly thanks to the ample supply stored in the hangar.

But there were vegetables too.

Vegetables were naturally expensive this time of year, but a merchant who felt indebted to the Manpukutei sold them at a price close to cost.

Even so, they still cost a fair amount. Still, since they included root vegetables like potatoes that could be stored for a winter, they didn't use an excessive amount of vegetables.

It was a dish only possible because Rei and the others had the financial means.

"As for drinks..."

"Wine is fine for me."

"Then I'll have the same as Marina."

Marina and Vihera answered, and Hasta's gaze turned to Rei.

Hasta had heard before that Rei didn't care for alcohol.

Furthermore, Byune was still a child and too young to drink alcohol.

...While Elgin didn't have a strict age limit for drinking alcohol, Byune was still too young.

That's precisely why he asked...

"Do you have fruit water?"

"No, not this time of year..."

If it were spring, summer, or even autumn, they might have had some kind of fruit water available.

But it was winter.

A high-end establishment might be different, but at Manpukutei, there was no way they could provide fruit water.

"I see. Well, I have my own bottle I'd like to drink... Is that okay? If not, water is fine."

"No, if you have your own, that's fine too. ...It's not exactly ideal for the shop, but drinking water at a celebration feels a bit lonely. We do have tea, though."

With that, Rei and Byune decided to drink the fruit water from the Misty Ring.

Hasta brought over two cups filled with wine and two empty cups. Rei poured the fruit water into the cups.

With everything ready, it was only natural that Rei, as the party leader, would propose the toast.

"This extermination request... well, maybe it wasn't a request. But to celebrate the successful completion of this extermination to test our coordination... Cheers!"

'Cheers'

"Mm."

Each of them uttered a sound and raised their cups.

And the first thing everyone dug into... well, predictably, it was the skewered meat—pure white yet faintly charred.

The color of the meat made its origin obvious without a second thought.

The moment it touched their tongues, the fibers dissolved.

Probably to preserve the meat's color. It was seasoned with salt and something faintly spicy, without any sauce.

(Sansho pepper?)

The spice that always accompanied the eel he ate back in Japan.

Of course, Rei's tongue couldn't tell if it was truly sansho, but it still felt similar... and she turned her gaze toward Husta.
