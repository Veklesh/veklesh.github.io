"Wow, Rei has quite a collection of magical items. In fact, many of them are the sort of things even our family—the House of Exoris—could never hope to acquire."

In the park, with snow fluttering down, Rei and the others were still talking with Brewitt.

Brewitt, in particular, showed interest in the magic items Rei possessed.

Still, the reason Rei showed him a few of them was that, despite his interest, Brewitt didn’t seem to want them.

Starting with the Misty Ring, some of the magic items Rei possesses are extremely valuable.

The number of times he’d been attacked specifically for them was so high that even using all the fingers and toes on both hands and feet wouldn’t be enough to count them.

However, while Brewitt was amazed by the effects of the magic items, he didn’t seem to want them.

Well, to be precise, it does want them, but it doesn’t try to take them from Rei.

Because of that attitude, Rei felt like he’d found a kindred spirit and was able to interact with him easily.

“Some were gifts from my master, and many others I’ve collected from various places or had alchemists craft for me.”

“Hmm, alchemists, huh. I do provide support to alchemists in my own territory as well… but it’s no use. Skilled alchemists just don’t gather here. Well, I can understand why, though. It’s not like my territory has any materials that are particularly attractive to alchemists.”

In a frontier region like Gilm, where rare materials—and even unknown ones—can be obtained, it wouldn’t be strange for alchemists to flock here in search of them.

That said, among the alchemists who have gathered here over the past few years, many have come specifically seeking materials from Set, the familiar of Rei, who is based in Gilm.

As a rare variant of the Rank A monster, the griffin—and considered equivalent to a Rank S monster—Set’s materials are something alchemists truly crave.

That said, they aren’t exactly killing or injuring Set to obtain the materials; instead, they mainly collect things like shed fur and feathers.

In any case, while the number of alchemists in Gilm is increasing for this reason, the Exoris County governed by the Brewitt family lacks materials that would excite alchemists.

Of course, there are common materials, but they still pale in comparison to a place like Gilm, which could be called a treasure trove of materials.

Nevertheless, the Exoris family, eager to secure alchemists, provides them with a certain amount of financial support and implements policies that favor them... yet, despite this, the number of alchemists remains low.

From Brewitt’s perspective, it’s only natural that he would envy Gilm.

That said, Gilm has its own set of problems.

Above all, while its location on the frontier is advantageous for securing rare materials, it also gives rise to various problems precisely because it is on the frontier.

Take, for instance, the Treant Forest that suddenly appeared near Gilm, or the Giganto Turtle that emerged from it—it’s not hard to imagine what would have happened if Gilm were an ordinary town.

“If we’re talking about increasing the number of alchemists, it might be a good idea to take a cue from the Bestir Empire. In that country, alchemists are given preferential treatment on a national scale.”

“Ah, I’ve heard about that. Actually, we’re already using it as a reference. Alchemists get reduced taxes, lower rent for housing and laboratories, and partial subsidies for material costs, and so on.”

“...Do you go that far?”

Leodanis looked surprised at Brewitt’s words.

As for Leodanis, having always dreamed of becoming a knight, he was interested in Magic Swords and the like, but he hadn’t really cared much about other magical items.

Although he had originally joined the knightly order because of his swordsmanship, Leodanis wasn’t exactly born into nobility.

That’s precisely why he was so surprised by Brewitt’s explanation—that they would go to such lengths just to secure an alchemist.

Since Miranda worked as a maid at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, she had heard a thing or two about estate management, so she could vaguely understand what Brewitt was getting at.

“That’s right. The magic items created by alchemists become a major source of power and a driving force for enriching the estate. …Even if it doesn’t go that far, merchants will come seeking magic items, so trade will naturally flourish. …Though, of course, it’s not that simple.”

A sigh escaped Brewitt’s lips.

He must be reflecting on just how difficult it is to gather alchemists.

Watching Brewitt like that, Rei also recalled the faces of alchemists he knew… and nodded in understanding.

(Well, alchemists do tend to have pretty intense personalities, after all.)

The alchemist who created the Twilight Spear, and the one who made the Set Cage.

He’s met several other alchemists over the years, but most of them aren’t the type to readily listen to others.

Even when told they would be given preferential treatment in various ways, some seemed unconcerned about such matters, prioritizing their own path above all else, while others showed interest only in things that struck a chord with them.

Not all alchemists have that kind of personality, though—as Brewitt’s words suggest, the fact that we’ve managed to attract some of them at all proves that.

“Well, all I can say is, you’ll just have to do your best with that. Monster materials aside, I wouldn’t be surprised if there were rare materials among ores and the like.”

“I’ve been looking into it, but so far, nothing’s turned up. …Anyway, let’s leave the gloomy talk at that. Aren’t there any more interesting magic items?”

Bluitt asked this not only because he had a strong interest in magic items himself, but also because he thought that knowledge of such items might come in handy when he eventually inherited the fiefdom.

That must have been why.

Rei had half-expected Brewitt to think that way, but since it wasn’t something he’d mind others seeing, he showed him Nebra’s Eye, which he wore at his waist, without hesitation and explained its capabilities.

“So as long as you have magic power, you can keep creating weapons? That’s enviable… but it also requires a massive amount of magic power to use them. It’s a bit hard to judge whether it’s practical or not.”

“I won’t deny that.”

Rei uses Nebula’s Eye to some extent, but only to a limited degree.

Ideally, it would be best for Byune—who normally throws long needles—to use Nebula’s Eye.

However, Byune lacks the magical power required to use Nebula’s Eye.

As a result, even if Byune possessed Nebula’s Eye, it would be a wasted asset.

To demonstrate exactly what kind of weapon it is, Rei activates Nebula’s Eye and creates an arrowhead.

To someone unaware of the circumstances, it would be impossible to tell that using Nebula’s Eye requires a significant amount of magical power.

That’s how effortlessly Rei activated Nebula’s Eye.

If she hadn’t been briefed on the situation beforehand, it wouldn’t be strange for her to question the incident involving Byune, wondering, “If it can be activated this easily, why…?” That’s how effortlessly Rei activated Nebula’s Eye.

He then handed the arrowhead he had created to Brewitt.

Brewitt, upon receiving it, gazed at the arrowhead with interest.

“So this is it… but this isn’t exactly a powerful weapon, is it?”

“True. It’s really just a weapon meant to catch the enemy off guard, or something like that. That said, this kind of weapon can be quite powerful depending on how you use it.”

Saying that, he watched as the arrowhead vanished in Brewitt’s hand after a moment, then activated Nebra’s Eye once more to create another arrowhead.

Then, aiming at a piece of dead wood lying a short distance away... he hurled the arrowhead.

The arrow flew through the cold winter air, striking the dead tree lying on the ground and shattering it.

Since the deadwood was about as thick as Leodanis’s arm, the visual impact was striking.

Of course, since it was just dead wood, it had become brittle, unlike living wood.

No, depending on the circumstances, it might even have been rotted by snow or rain.

But even taking that into account, the sight before him was still shocking.

“Well, it turned out like this because it was a dead tree this time, but even against a living tree, it has enough power to sink into the trunk. That said, as I’ve mentioned many times, the range is short, so basically it’s used for keeping an opponent at bay or attacking someone who’s a little farther away—that sort of thing.”

“Huh… Can I try it? I’d like to give it a go.”

At Brewitt’s words, Rei conjured a new arrowhead and handed it to him.

He must have understood from the earlier incident that it would vanish immediately. Without hesitation, Brewitt threw the arrowhead at the ground a short distance away.

The arrow released from Brewitt’s hand sank into the ground, though not very deeply.

If Rei had thrown it at the ground, it would probably have sunk much deeper—deep enough that digging it out would have been a bit of a hassle.

Knowing this, Brewitt looks at his hand with a hint of disappointment.

“It’s a little hard to get the hang of,”

“Yeah, I couldn’t use it that effectively at first either. This kind of thing takes practice, right?”

Relying on his physical abilities, Rei had been able to use it to some extent from the very beginning.

But the fact that he can now unleash its full power, as he is doing now, is the result of consistent training.

“I see. That’s a magic item with quite a unique quirk. The fact that the arrowheads it creates vanish immediately… you could even say it’s perfect for assassination.”

At Brewitt’s remark about it being suited for assassination, both Leodanis and Miranda looked surprised, but Rei nodded without trying to hide his agreement.

“That’s a fact I can’t deny.”

Since the arrowheads used in the attack vanish immediately, there’s no evidence left to indicate what kind of weapon was used in the assassination.

Well, examining the wound might reveal the shape of the weapon used, but the absence of the actual weapon itself makes subsequent investigations difficult.

Simply possessing such a weapon would make it only natural to be viewed with suspicion in an emergency.

Of course, there are plenty of other weapons that could be used for assassination, so it’s not as if only Rei’s Nebula Eye is suspicious.

“But if this is the only weapon of its kind I possess, wouldn’t using it actually make it obvious that I’m the culprit?”

“Now that you mention it, you’re right. I’m praying that magic item isn’t mass-produced. …Though even if it were, it looks pretty hard to use.”

“This was custom-made for me by an alchemist in the Bestir Empire. I don’t think it would be easy to mass-produce.”

To be precise, it’s a modified version of a magic item that creates arrows using magic.

(If I’d known Marina was going to resign as Guild Master and join us, I wouldn’t have had it modified.)

Rei felt a slight twinge of regret, but it would have been unreasonable to expect him to foresee that someone who had served as Guild Master would resign and return to life as an adventurer.

Besides, while Marina does use a bow, her main method of attack is still spirit magic.

Of course, that doesn’t mean she’s a poor archer. In terms of pure archery skill alone, she would likely rank among the top even among the skilled adventurers of Gilm.

This means that, for an archer, Marina has mastered the highest level of skill.

……Being a Dark Elf, she likely had a natural aptitude for the bow to begin with, but there is no doubt that her skills were honed to perfection during her long life as an adventurer.

Objectively speaking, it wouldn’t be strange to say that Marina was beloved by the bow as a weapon, but she was loved even more by the spirits.

And that love was far deeper and broader than her bond with the bow.

As a result, while Marina still uses a bow, she has come to rely more heavily on spirit magic.

“The Bestir Empire, huh. …I do want to visit it at least once, but… it’ll probably be difficult.”

“For the heir apparent of the Exoris family to travel to a neighboring country with which we’ve been at odds for so long—even if there’s no war currently underway—I’m afraid it might be best to reconsider.”

Hearing Brewitt’s words, Leodanis immediately responded.

Leodanis wasn’t exactly Brewitt’s subordinate, but as a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights and a patriot, he had no choice but to say so.
