This time, it was Jaya who brought Rei and the others all the way to the Kingdom of Reburina.

Since Ryutas was the son of the man leading that Jaya, he had many questions he wanted to ask...

"I'm afraid I can't tell you that."

"Sorry, but I can't say."

"I can't tell you."

Whenever he asked about the Jaya, he usually got that kind of response.

But Rei understood it wasn't because Ryutas was being spiteful, or trying to extract some kind of reward from him.

Because when they tried to extract information from someone in the underground facility at Mejogo, that person died without a fight.

Given that possibility, there was no way Rei and the others could force Ryutasu to reveal information.

(Though, to be fair, Ryutas could just be using that to his advantage—not answering what he doesn't want to... That's the tricky part.)

Uncertain what to ask Ryutas next, Rei addressed Elena and the others watching the situation unfold behind him.

"Is there anything you want to ask? I can't guarantee he'll answer, but asking doesn't cost anything."

"It's not that I don't want to answer, you know. It's just that there are things I can't say."

Perhaps sensing even a hint of barbs in Rei's words.

Ryuuta declared, as if defending his own words.

"For starters, questions about the Jaya organization are pretty much off-limits. If that's the case... next up is this cave. You said there were over a thousand giants in there, right?"

When Rei asked for confirmation, Ryutas didn't say he couldn't answer; he gave a firm nod instead.

With that confirmed, Rei got to the point.

"Then let me ask. How do you feed that many giants? I saw giants being born in that underground facility. When they burst out of their mothers' bellies, they devoured the corpses too."

As he explained, he must have recalled the scene.

Not just Rei, but the others too... even Byune, usually expressionless, frowned in disgust.

"Over a thousand giants with appetites like that. How do you keep them all fed? You're not feeding them cave bats or something, are you?"

"Ah, that's simple. Giants basically just sleep. At least, until we give them an order."

"...Sleeping?"

Hearing that, Rei remembered the giants curled up around the black crystal.

But back then, even though they hadn't done anything in particular, they had attacked him.

It wasn't like anyone had ordered them to.

(If anyone did give an order... it would be more accurate to say the black crystal gave the command.)

By some means unknown, the black crystal had sensed Rei and the others' presence and judged them to be a threat.

And to prevent such an act, it moved the giants.

Thinking about it that way, Rei could understand why the giants had attacked them.

"Come to think of it, when we found the Black Crystal, the giants were sleeping too... To me, it looked like it was supplying some kind of energy to the giants. But couldn't that only happen because the giants were near the Black Crystal? Without it, I doubt the giants' bellies would fill up even if they were sleeping, right?"

"...I can't go into detail about that."

As usual, Ryutas gave that reply, and Rei sighed again.

But forcing him further would mean losing a vital source of information.

Thinking that, he couldn't possibly press him further.

"I see. ...Destroying that black crystal was a mistake, wasn't it?"

To be precise, it wasn't that Rei had intended to destroy it. Rather, when the black crystal attempted to corrupt Rei's mind, a defensive instinct kicked in, activating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which resulted in its annihilation.

"Huh?"

Rei's muttered remark drew Ryutas's immediate reaction first.

No, not just Ryutas—the others remaining as guards around him also wore expressions that clearly showed they had no idea what Rei had just said.

"Um... Rei. I might have misheard, but I heard you say you destroyed the Black Crystal?"

"Ah, yes."

"...To be precise, 'destroyed' isn't quite right. 'Annihilated' is more accurate."

Elena clarified, as if supplementing Rei's words.

Vanish? Hearing that word, Ryutas shot Rei an incredulous look before turning it to Elena.

Ryuutasu found himself once again captivated by Elena's beauty and that of the others present, yet he quickly snapped out of what might be called the spell of that beauty. The subject at hand was simply too important for him to overlook.

The Black Crystal was the one and only entity at the core of the actions of the current Jaya... no, the nation of Reburina, which was effectively Jaya's superior organization.

At the same time, it was also a super-dangerous substance of the highest order. Countless individuals had attempted to decipher its capabilities, only to have their minds shattered and be reduced to invalids.

It was only natural. Black Crystal possessed—or rather, possessed—the outrageous ability to create giants, control them... and even alter a person's consciousness using a slave collar.

Naturally, countless individuals within the Kingdom of Reburina and Jaya sought to unravel the secret of that power.

However, after creating several broken souls, the conclusion was reached that it was better to use the Black Crystal than to understand it, leading to the current situation.

So when he heard that Black Crystal had been destroyed, that it had vanished, even Ryutas—who usually maintained an air of detached nonchalance—was bound to be shocked.

Even the men gathered as Ryutas's guards were startled by the news, a testament to the magnitude of the shock.

"Unbelievable... No, wait. He is an adventurer with a nickname, so perhaps it's only natural he could accomplish something like that? Even so... I still can't believe it."

Faced once more with the reality of what a renowned adventurer like Rei truly was, Ryutas could only stand in astonishment.

As the son of the leader of Jaya, Ryutas had met many adventurers before.

Even so, they were ultimately adventurers from a small country like Reburina.

Even those reputed as skilled often possessed abilities that were several levels below the elite fighters in Jaya.

But the person... no, the people standing before him were different.

They weren't merely skilled adventurers from the Kingdom of Reburina; they were adventurers possessing some of the finest skills even within the great nation of the Kingdom of Mireana. Ryutas could feel that truth anew.

That said, Rei and the others, who were perceived this way, weren't particularly concerned about Ryutas's state. They were discussing what to do next.

"What do we do? Asking him anything carelessly here could get Ryutas killed... and wouldn't that lead to all sorts of trouble later?"

There was no doubt that Ryutas was a member of the organization Jaya, which Rei despised.

No, even before being a member, he was the son of the man who led Jaya.

Yet, for some reason, Rei didn't feel it was acceptable to kill Ryutas like the others did.

That said, if asked whether he could tolerate the organization known as Jaya, he would answer no immediately.

"Right. We've been talking here for quite a while now. It wouldn't be strange to think they're preparing to intercept us by now. So, how about we just take him as a hostage?"

"...Hostage, huh. That's fine, but if we try something like that, those guards will put up a fight, you know?"

Vihera glanced at the guards surrounding Ryutas with a hint of interest.

The person who fought Rei was a formidable opponent.

Even if they were only slightly less skilled, she wanted to fight them if she could.

That was the thought behind her words.

A red tongue lasciviously licked her lips.

Seeing Vihera like this, the guards near Ryutas... and even the guards who had been hiding to protect him since the earlier incident... felt a chill run down their spines.

It was as if they were herbivores thrown before a carnivore.

But that feeling, somewhere between fear and awe, dissipated the instant Elena spoke.

"Vihera, that's enough."

"Oh, my apologies. I just couldn't help myself."

She probably didn't intend to intimidate her opponent, but the fierce aura of battle emanating from Vihera was still enough to make the guards shrink back.

(She's a troublemaker in a different way than Rei.)

Keeping a wary eye on Vihera, Elena thought this to herself.

Normally, Vihera was reasonably... well, reasonably sensible. But when it came to fighting, that sense flew right out the window.

"So, should we just leave Ryutas here for now? Or should we take him along, even if he can't share information, just in case something happens?"

"That's an option, but having more people would put us at a disadvantage in many ways, wouldn't it? Besides, the guards wouldn't allow us to take just Ryutas alone."

Marina's gaze swept over the guards surrounding Ryutas, reaching the ears of the other guards hidden nearby.

Hearing this, the guards who had felt fear—no, awe—towards Vihera mere seconds ago each regained their composure and nodded in response.

They had judged that, as guards, it was their duty to protect Ryutas.

One could argue their capabilities didn't match the task, but still, performing the job entrusted to them diligently earned the respect of those watching.

(Well, the fact they're cooperating with an organization like Jaya is a bit questionable, though.)

Thinking that, and considering what lay ahead... eventually, Rei shook his head.

"If we bring the others along too, it'll probably just cause all sorts of trouble. Maybe we should skip it."

Ryuutasu aside, some of the guards were dissatisfied with his methods.

That much was clear from the fact someone had just tried to attack Rei earlier.

Glancing at the man who still hadn't regained consciousness, Rei stated.

The guards on Ryutas's side did look slightly displeased about being suspected, but none of them actually voiced their dissatisfaction.

"So then... is it okay to ask about what's inside this cave?"

"Yeah, that's fine. It's not like this place is particularly big, though. Simply put, you enter the cave and walk for a while until you reach a fork. Take the left path, and you'll arrive at the place where the Jaya people are. Take the right path, and you'll arrive at the place where the giants are."

"...It's surprisingly simple. I'd assumed a cave would be quite intricate inside."

The caves Rei knew were often literally like mazes...

That said, even that knowledge wasn't based on firsthand experience, but rather knowledge gained from anime, manga, and novels back in Japan.

Since arriving in this world, he'd seen several caves, including those used as bandit hideouts, and most weren't nearly that complex.

"Well, places big enough to hold a large number of giants aren't exactly common. This was the largest space we could find. Oh, and obviously, the cave slopes downward, so you should be careful."

Hearing that, Rei could only agree.

Indeed, with over a thousand giants, it was undeniably impossible to gather them just anywhere.

Trying to do something like that would inevitably expose the giants' existence.

It was certain the Resistance knew about the giants now, but that knowledge was limited to the Resistance and a few close associates.

But if they just gathered giants carelessly around here, word would undoubtedly spread to all sorts of people.

Neither Jaya nor the Kingdom of Reburina could ever permit such a thing.

"So, how many are there inside?"

"About twenty. Ah, that's excluding the guards, of course."

Twenty.

Rei couldn't tell if that number was large or not.

But considering how few people were in the underground facility at Mejogo, it might be considered a lot.

(Though, they probably evacuated most of the researchers because of our raid.)

With that thought, Rei continued asking about various other things inside the cave.
