Early the next morning, after Rei and Set had met with Johanna and the others.

Normally, this would have been a time when Rei was still asleep, but he was walking toward the guild.

It was still before six in the morning, but true to its reputation as the high-class inn “Dusk Wheat inn,” a hearty breakfast was already prepared, and eating it had completely banished Rei’s drowsiness.

That said, most of the guests staying at the Dusk Wheat inn were merchants and adventurers.

Among them, there were quite a few who left Gilm first thing in the morning.

…Though since it’s winter now, there are hardly any who leave Gilm that way.

If there were someone who had accepted a quest to hunt kobolds, they might leave early in the morning, but the kind of adventurers staying at the Dusk Wheat inn were the sort worthy of being called high-ranking adventurers.

For that very reason, they wouldn’t participate unless they wanted to keep their bodies in shape or needed to relieve some pent-up stress.

In fact, Vihera and Byune, who have plenty of free time this winter, are still wrapped up in their futons, fast asleep.

Even so, the fact that breakfast had been thoroughly prepared made Rei very happy.

Also, since breakfast had just been prepared, that meant there was freshly baked bread available, so in that sense, Rei felt like he’d truly experienced the saying “the early bird catches the worm.”

Above all...

“Gurrr!”

Set growled at Rei.

Rei understood exactly what that meant.

“We’ll have our bento later. You just ate, didn’t you, Set?”

Saying this, he ruffled Set’s head.

That’s right—when Lana, the proprietress of Dusk Wheat inn, learned at dusk that Rei would be leaving early this morning, she had packed him a sandwich filled with plenty of fillings to take as his lunch.

And not just for Rei, but for Set as well.

...It wasn’t quite enough to truly fill Set’s stomach, but that couldn’t be helped.

In any case, since it was still before six in the morning, Rei and Set made their way through the pitch-black darkness.

Although Rei and Set had good night vision, Rei held the lamp Lana had prepared for them.

This wasn’t because she felt sorry for Rei and Set having to walk in the dark… but rather because, without a lamp or something similar, people might bump into them without realizing they were there.

And so, the man and the dog walked along the nearly deserted main street toward the guild, but as they neared the guild, the number of people gradually increased.

Most of the people gathering were dressed in a way that made them instantly recognizable as adventurers.

“Wow, even though it’s still dark, there are so many… Doesn’t that kind of move you a little?”

“Grr?”

At Rei’s words, Set tilted his head, as if to say, “Really?”

To Set, the sight of so many adventurers out this early in the morning didn’t seem particularly surprising.

As they exchanged these remarks on their way to the guild, Rei noticed that several people were staring at him and Set.

Rei wasn’t particularly bothered by the attention, since he and Set were used to being the center of attention by now.

However, the stares directed at them now seemed different from the usual mix of awe, admiration, jealousy, fear, and respect they were accustomed to.

“What’s going on?” he wondered briefly… but upon seeing an adventurer whispering to a companion about the dissection of the Giganto Turtle, it all made sense.

(So, to these guys right now, I’m not someone to be wary of, but rather an employer who gives them work, or a generous person who lets them investigate unknown monsters. …Well, the pay is low, though.)

Since the Kobold hunt takes priority for the Gilm, even if the Gilm makes that decision, Rei has no choice but to accept it, and he doesn’t harbor any resentment.

……That said, if Rei were actually the one doing the butchering and the pay turned out to be lower than for a kobold hunt, he might feel dissatisfied.

That said, since the reward for this particular job is clearly stated, there’s no way the pay would turn out to be low after she’s already accepted it.

“Oh, Rei-san. Good morning!”

When she turned her gaze toward the sudden voice, she saw a familiar face.

“Hasta, is it? It’s been a while since we’ve met like this, but have you been doing well? …Well, judging by the look on your face, you seem to be doing just fine.”

“Hahaha. Yeah, I guess so. I’ve been doing my best in my own way. The shop’s been pretty popular, too.”

“Well, Full Belly Inn is a place that’s delicious, fast, and cheap, after all.”

Full Belly Inn, the diner run by Husta’s parents, is a familiar place to Rei as well.

Many of the dishes featured in Misty Ring are from the Full Belly Inn.

It’s also famous in Gilm as the first shop to start selling udon.

Hasta became an adventurer to provide the meat needed as ingredients for his parents, who run the Full Belly Inn.

He met Rei during the incident involving Gamelion, a staple of the autumn season.

Given who he is, it wasn’t hard to imagine why he had approached me.

“Are you here to butcher a Giganto Turtle too, Hasta?”

You can get meat from kobold hunting quests, too.

But since kobolds are low-rank monsters, their meat is by no means delicious.

You could say it’s far tastier than goblin meat, but the fact that I’m comparing it to goblin meat probably gives you a pretty good idea of how it tastes.

Still, unlike goblin meat, you can make it reasonably edible without too much effort.

"Yeah. By the way... it's not just me. Quite a few of the shop's regulars have said they're joining in."

“Are they after the meat too? Well, I can pretty much guess what they’re thinking.”

Dishot, the father of Hasta—the chef at Full Belly Inn—is a skilled cook.

It wouldn’t be surprising if some people thought that if they brought Giganto Turtle meat to a chef like Dishot, they’d be treated to a delicious meal.

(Well, Dishot probably hasn’t cooked Giganto Turtle meat before, and since the cuts you get will vary… he’ll probably have to experiment a bit.)

Thinking it through, Rei suddenly realized.

This might not be such a bad idea for him, either.

The adventurers bring the Giganto Turtle meat to the Full Belly Inn, and Dishot experiments until he turns it into a delicious dish.

And once he’s gotten good at making delicious dishes, Rei could bring in the meat from a butchered Giganto Turtle and have him cook it—that way, he could enjoy delicious turtle meat dishes without lifting a finger.

(Though, since I have a massive amount of meat, I feel like it might be better for me to provide it and let them experiment with it.)

With these thoughts in mind, Rei heads toward the guild with Hasta.

And the closer they get to the guild, the more adventurers there are.

“There are quite a few of them. I suppose some are here for the Kobold hunt too.”

“That’s true. But compared to just recently, there are definitely more people. Word about your Giganto Turtle incident has been spreading quite a bit since yesterday. I suppose that’s why.”

“As far as I’m concerned, the more people there are, the faster we can butcher the Giganto Turtles, so that’s a big help.”

“Haha. As for me, the fewer people there are, the longer the dismemberment work takes, which means I get more Giganto Turtle meat, so I actually prefer it that way.”

Perhaps hearing Husta’s voice, several adventurers nearby nodded in agreement.

Rei tried to say something to Hasta, but… just then, they arrived right in front of the guild.

“Well, I’ve got a quick meeting with the guild, so I’ll leave you here. I’m sure it’ll be fine, but do your best with the job.”

As for this Giganto Turtle butchering job, the more people there are, the better. Even those with limited butchering skills can handle tasks like transporting the cut-up parts, and there are plenty of other jobs available, so they’re welcome.

However, that’s only the case for the Giganto Turtle dismantling—for regular requests, the number of recruits is fixed.

Basically, it’s first come, first served, so to secure a lucrative job—one with high pay, light work, or some kind of bonus—you need to be at the guild early in the morning when the job notices are posted on the bulletin board.

That said, while new requests are often posted on the bulletin board early in the morning, it’s not uncommon for them to be posted closer to noon or even in the afternoon.

“Hahaha. Since it’s a quest, there seem to be a lot of people here, but the competition doesn’t seem that fierce, so I feel at ease. Well then, I’ll take this one.”

Saying that, Hasta bowed and stepped inside the guild.

“Well, I’ve got a meeting or something, so Set, please wait here as usual. …It’s busy right now, so I don’t think many people will have time to talk to you.”

“Grr…”

As Rei spoke while petting him, Set purred and headed to his usual spot, where he lay down.

Normally, several adventurers and passersby would come to admire Set.

But as Rei said, it was still dark, and most adventurers were heading to the guild to accept jobs.

...Though there might be some who’d stop by to pet Set if they had time after accepting a quest.

Glancing at Set, Rei entered the guild…

“Whoa.”

The exclamation slipped out before he knew it.

Despite the early hour, the tavern was still filled with people drinking or passed out drunk, while others were having a quick drink to get pumped up before work or eating breakfast.

However, in stark contrast to the tavern, the guild was filled with even more adventurers.

From spring through fall, this would be a common sight, nothing out of the ordinary… but unfortunately, since Rei rarely comes to the guild at this time of day, the surprise was all the more intense.

Still, as the owner of a Giganto Turtle, he couldn’t just stand there watching forever.

Rei made his way through what could rightly be called a crowd.

Those who noticed Rei quietly made way for him, while those who didn’t simply let him slip past… and eventually, he arrived at the counter.

That said, with this many adventurers here looking to take on jobs, it’s only natural that a long line has formed in front of the counter.

If it were a little later, he might be able to pull a stunt like trying to flirt with the receptionist while accepting a quest, but doing something like that in this situation would only earn him the resentment of the other adventurers waiting in line behind him.

Knowing this, the adventurers didn’t make any strange advances; once their requests were accepted, they left immediately.

Rei was watching this scene from his spot in line, but one of the guild staff members noticed him.

“Hey, Rei!? Why are you just standing in line like that!? You’re not an adventurer today—you’re a client, so you don’t need to stand there!”

Saying this, the guild clerk stepped out from behind the counter and pulled Rei back inside.

The adventurer standing behind Rei noticed that a spot had opened up in front of him now that Rei was gone, and, thinking he was in luck today, took a step forward.

Unaware of what was happening behind him, Rei moves behind the counter.

……As Rei moved, Lenora gave him an apologetic look, realizing she hadn’t noticed him due to her busyness, while Kenny gave him a disappointed look, wishing she could have been the one to assist him. However, they were too busy accepting requests from adventurers to keep their eyes on him.

“I’m sorry it took me so long to notice you.”

A guild staff member said this to Rei, who had been ushered inside the counter, and bowed his head.

Rei shook his head at the staff member, telling him not to worry.

“I’ve hardly ever come at this time of day, so to be honest, it was definitely a bit unexpected. Don’t worry about it. So, what’s the plan from here?”

“Ah, yes. In a little while, we’ll all head outside Gilm and start the disassembly right away. And, um… actually, considering the size of the Giganto Turtle, to finish the disassembly as quickly as possible, I’d like to temporarily hire people with disassembly skills—even if they aren’t adventurers—just for this time. What do you think?”

I really hope he’ll agree.

Facing the guild staff member who looked at him with that hope in their eyes, Rei nodded in agreement without any hesitation.
