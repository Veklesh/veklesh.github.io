May I have the Sand Serpent? When asked this, Zarust hesitated slightly before nodding in agreement.

After all, it wasn't them who had actually taken down the Sand Serpent, but the person standing before them.

They had encountered it first, but honestly, if the fight had continued as it was, some of the new recruits among the guards might have lost their lives.

Considering that, it was undeniably true they had been saved. He didn't want to make the shameless move of claiming ownership here.

He remembered he hadn't gotten approval from Augusto, Zarust's charge and the leader of this caravan. Nodding, he hurriedly turned his gaze toward Augusto, who immediately nodded back at Zarust's look.

He understood it would be dangerous to upset someone capable of defeating a Sand Serpent with a single blow.

(Still, judging by that small stature, probably a child? The way they speak doesn't suggest they're female, though...)

Unable to see Rei's face beneath the hood of the Dragon Robe, Zarust harbored this question internally.

That his expression remained neutral was likely due to years of experience as an adventurer.

"I see. That's helpful. I've never seen this monster before. I wanted to capture one. ...If possible, another one would be ideal... but there isn't one."

"Ah, ah. Sand Serpents are desert-dwelling monsters, but they usually stay active within sand deserts. This area mixes sand and rocky deserts, so it's rare for them to appear here."

Zalust pressed on, trying to glean as much information as possible from the person before him.

"Still, I don't mind letting you have the Sand Serpent, but how do you plan to transport it? It's quite large."

"Huh? Oh, don't worry about that. ...Still, being a serpent-type monster, it probably had a lot of materials in its head."

"That's right. Sand Serpent fangs are especially scarce and relatively expensive. It seems the single silver scale on the head, the part used for the slaying proof, has also vanished."

"...I see."

Rei felt a little down upon learning that even the proof-of-kill part had been blown away.

It wasn't like he was struggling for money, so it wasn't a devastating loss.

Still, it was frustrating to have materials and proof parts he should have obtained vanish because of his own mistake.

(Those Sand Serpent fangs would have made pretty good materials...)

Glancing at the Sand Serpent's corpse lying on the ground, headless, he pulled himself together and started walking toward it.

Though the Sand Serpent was about five meters long, roughly half its body was still buried in the sand.

Pulling it out would require considerable effort... While those watching thought this, Rei, seemingly unconcerned by their gazes, touched the Sand Serpent's torso.

Running his fingers over the scales, something suddenly caught Rei's attention. He turned his gaze toward Zarust and spoke.

"Hey, I've got a question. Can this monster's blood be sold as a material too?"

"Huh? Eh? Oh. I think they'd buy it as an alchemy ingredient..."

"I see. Well, that's a bit of a failure then."

The blood flowing from where the head had been shattered was now trickling out with significantly less force.

This meant there was almost no blood left inside the Sand Serpent's body.

"Draining it was easy enough, though. ...Well, it's done now, so can't be helped. Still, I knew it, but this spear is a bit too powerful."

Rei muttered, his gaze fixed on the Crimson spear he held.

This was the first time he'd actually used it on a living creature, and the blow had been far more powerful than he'd anticipated.

Zalust must have heard Rei's mutter. While he didn't show it outwardly, he inwardly agreed wholeheartedly that it was far too powerful.

Of course. A Sand Serpent was normally a high-ranking C-rank monster, an opponent that a party of adventurers could barely match.

Then, with a strange twang, most of its head vanished in the next instant. To say this wasn't powerful was simply impossible to accept.

And whatever method had been used, the spear that had pierced the Sand Serpent's head and stuck it into the desert vanished the next instant, returning to Rei's hand.

(Probably... definitely... likely not unlimited, but Rei can unleash attacks like that repeatedly. ...What kind of monster is this?)

The ability to unleash attacks capable of obliterating a Sand Serpent's head in a single strike, repeatedly and consecutively. Just contemplating that sent a chill run down Zarust's spine.

Even though he was currently in the desert, surrounded by the usual intense heat.

Just contemplating what would happen if that spear attack were directed at him made Zarust conclude that antagonizing Rei was absolutely something to avoid.

While Zarust... and the others, starting with August, similarly believed they absolutely could not oppose Rei, Rei touched the Sand Serpent... and the next instant, its massive form vanished as if it had never been there.

"Huh? Hey, where's the Sand Serpent's corpse?"

"Huh? Did I see wrong? That Sand Serpent corpse was definitely there just a moment ago, right?"

"...An illusion?"

Amidst the commotion of the escorting adventurers and merchants, each unable to comprehend what had just unfolded before their eyes, a few had noticed what Rei had done.

The leaders, like August, who commanded the caravan, and Zarust, who oversaw the guards, were among them.

But even if they understood the act performed before their eyes, they found it hard to voice it aloud.

It was only natural, since while his name was known, few had actually seen him with their own eyes.

Griffin, Spear of Twilight, Item Box. ...Though August and Zarust had been astonished by Rei countless times in this short span, Rei was already thoroughly accustomed to such reactions and showed no particular concern as he approached Set.

"Wait!"

Augusto called out to Rei's retreating back.

It was an instinctive action born of his merchant's intuition—if he let Rei slip away here, he might never encounter such a person again.

Hearing the voice from behind, Rei paused and considered.

This time, he wanted the magic stone from the unknown monster, the Sand Serpent, and he also had the goal of trying out the Twilight Spear. Besides, leaving him behind would have left a bad taste in his mouth, so he helped him. But fundamentally, Rei was heading to Gosh because he was being hounded by the merchants.

Recalling the recent events, Rei turned around.

"What is it? I'm trying to get to Gosh."

"...Well, how should I put this? Rei isn't exactly familiar with Gosh or the desert, is she?"

"Well, I won't deny that."

Rei's knowledge of the desert was limited to conquering its layers in the labyrinth city of Exil. ...He also had knowledge gained while in Japan, but that wasn't particularly useful in the current situation.

August smiled at not being contradicted and continued.

"In that case, how about this? Actually, we're also heading to Gosh. How about we travel together? I think I can provide useful information about this area."

"In exchange, you want me as your escort, huh?"

Augusto nodded without denying Rei's words.

"Yes. What do you say?"

This attitude was clearly different from the merchants who had swarmed around him in Gilm.

If it had been those merchants in Gilm who had persistently tried to get him to sell the Twilight Spear, he probably wouldn't have managed to dodge the conversation, use Rei's power, or get them to admit they wanted to use him.

That might be the correct approach for a merchant, but from Rei's perspective, they were extremely troublesome.

Of course, he knew not all merchants in Gilm were like that.

Especially the merchant from the Azoth Trading Company, who had clashed with Rei before, now strove for honest dealings. While his past bad reputation hadn't completely vanished... well, it hadn't, but he had nonetheless earned a degree of renewed respect from the residents of Gilm.

Rei was also on reasonably good terms with several other merchants, and those merchants, too, now made a point of treating Rei with sincerity.

It was only natural. With his nickname, Rei was now so well-known in Gilm that there was hardly anyone who didn't know him, and his familiar, Set, was practically an idol in Gilm.

If anyone were to treat Rei dishonestly and word got out through his mouth, it wouldn't necessarily mean they couldn't open a shop in Gilm anymore... but it would definitely drive away customers.

Rei himself doesn't seem to realize just how much influence he wields.

In any case, for the merchants living in Gilm, Rei was both a top customer who made large purchases and someone who was absolutely not to be underestimated.

But... the merchants swarming around Rei weren't particularly capable.

Well, some might have known the situation, but they probably wanted to procure the Twilight Spear even if it meant taking a risk.

For Rei, weary from dealing with such merchants, Augusto's words—easily accepting him as his escort—felt strangely comforting.

That must be why. Normally, he could have simply continued on to his destination, Gosh, with Set...

"Alright, I'll take it."

...he found himself saying.

August was the one surprised by those words.

He'd asked the question thinking it was probably a no, but figured it couldn't hurt to ask. He hadn't expected such a straightforward agreement.

"Huh? Are you sure?"

It was only natural he asked that question, looking genuinely taken aback.

"What's the big deal? You're the one who invited me, right? Besides, I've had some experience with other deserts, but this one is new to me. It'd be helpful if you could teach me about that sort of thing."

"...Huh? Oh, sure. Of course. I'll tell you everything I know. To be able to hire an amazing adventurer like Rei, with just that much information... Honestly, I've been struggling with a lot of things, so this is a huge help for me too."

"Struggling?"

Rei glanced at the adventurers, starting with Zarust, questioning August's words.

To Rei's eyes, most of them, starting with Zarust, looked like capable adventurers.

He could see a few who probably hadn't been adventurers for very long, but they were definitely in the minority.

Rei had defeated the Sand Serpent, but he couldn't imagine Zaruust and his group losing to it.

(Well, a few newbies might lose their lives, though.)

Augusto, seeming to understand what Rei was thinking, continued with a smile.

"On the road to Gosh, you may encounter monsters unique to the desert. It's not uncommon for some to be more powerful than the Sand Serpent you defeated... Normally, you wouldn't need to worry about that..."

Rei vaguely understood the meaning behind the difficult words.

He suspected there was some reason behind it.

Normally, one would want to ask about such a reason, but Rei said no more.

For some reason, Rei often encountered people with secrets, and because of that, he was used to it.

"Well, never mind that. Since we're traveling together, what about our formation? How long until we reach Gosh?"

"Huh? ...Um, well... I've left the formation up to Zarust, so you'll have to discuss that with him. As for Gosh... Let's see, we'll probably arrive sometime tomorrow during the day."

When traveling through the desert, one normally avoids walking during the day.

But traveling by carriage was a different matter.

Especially since the camels owned by Augusto were a breed specifically bred for desert travel, and the carriage itself was equipped with various modifications, including magic items, to handle desert travel.

Furthermore, Rei recognized the robes Augusto and his men wore.

They were the same ones used when conquering the desert layers in Exil.

It was precisely because of these meticulous preparations that they could travel normally even through the desert in daylight.

"I see, tomorrow during the day. ...Understood. Then, shall we head out soon? What should I do?"

As he asked Zarust, a near-certainty mingled with a foreboding sense within Rei: once they reached Gosh, they'd likely be dragged into trouble again.
