Rei and his Rank B party, Crimson Wings, arrived in the slums.

The news spread quickly among the slum dwellers.

While they generally only share information with allies, word inevitably spread... and the slums themselves were left in utter shock.

Amidst this... naturally, the news reached Precious and her business partner, Slain.

"...This is troublesome, Precious. You've brought in a troublesome individual."

The room was so well-appointed it was hard to believe it was in the slums.

The sofas and tables within were all high-end items; if asked whether they were fitting for the slums, anyone would shake their head.

"What are you talking about?"

Precious stroked his catfish beard, asking, unsure whether he understood what Slain was saying or not.

Stroking his catfish beard, Precious asked.

Slein snorted in annoyance at Precious's audacious demeanor.

"You knew you were being targeted and came here anyway, didn't you? This job is already over. If you drag us into it now... you understand what that means, right?"

Slein's words were quiet.

But precisely because it was quiet, the pressure it carried was intense.

At the very least, Gifnan, present as Precious's bodyguard, could barely stand his ground.

Moreover, Slain, Precious, and Gifnan weren't the only ones in this room.

There was the man who brought news of Rei and the others arriving in the slums... and others waiting as Slein's guards.

Precious and Gifnan. And while there were other guards besides Gifnan, this place was undoubtedly under Slein's control.

Facing Slein, who was clearly stronger than himself, if asked whether he could protect Precious... well, that was a bit—no, quite difficult.

"Calm down. First of all, I want to have a long relationship with Mr. Slein, you know? Do you really think someone like me would try to set him up?"

If it came to a fight, there was no chance of winning. Death was certain.

Despite this dire situation, Precious showed no sign of flinching as she continued speaking.

"What do you think? There's nothing in it for me to do such a thing. If I had to give a reason... perhaps selling Mr. Slein to Mr. Rei and the others?"

In that instant, the concentration of killing intent in the room thickened further.

It was only natural, given she'd suggested selling their leader.

Yet Precious, the target of that killing intent, remained utterly unfazed, showing no particular concern.

Though Precious was inexperienced in violent situations, it was impossible for her not to sense this directed hostility.

Gifnan and the other guards prepared to move immediately if anything happened to Precious... Seeing this, Slein's companions likewise readied themselves to act at a moment's notice.

Yet, the two facing each other, Slein and Precious, showed no reaction whatsoever to the guards and subordinates making their moves.

Seeing Precious like this, the killing intent emanating from Slein gradually faded.

"Then, let me ask you. Why did you come here... specifically now, to this place? Considering your current situation, didn't you realize that appearing openly in the slums would draw Rei to you?"

"But to begin with, I believe it was you who summoned me, Slein-san."

"Ah, yes. But I should have also told you this: Come inconspicuously. So why did you bring five guards? And you didn't even bother to disguise yourself, marching boldly through the slums. Word of your arrival has spread far and wide, hasn't it?"

Yes, that was Slein's biggest miscalculation.

Considering Precious's position, he wouldn't have told her not to bring guards.

Still, he never imagined she'd be this blatantly conspicuous.

"Well, you see... As you know, I'm currently being targeted. I absolutely must be cautious."

"Then what about disguises? Walking around openly like that, anyone who knows will recognize you as Precious. Don't you think you should at least have worn a hood or something to hide your face?"

"My position prevents me from doing such things. I'm sorry."

Precious said this, bowing his head apologetically, but his eyes betrayed no remorse whatsoever.

Slein understood this incident was clearly no accident—it had been deliberate.

Honestly, Slain wasn't amused... In fact, he was extremely displeased with the current situation. Still, he understood it would be unwise to confront Precious here and now.

"Hmph. Fine. Consider this one debt owed. I'll make sure you pay me back later."

"Yes. Of course."

Slein felt a slight sense of doubt at Precious's straightforward admission of owing him a favor.

He had dealt with numerous merchants before.

From that experience, he understood that merchants deeply disliked the concept of a "favor."

If it were a clear transaction—like paying money as proof of apology, or transferring goods like food—they'd often agree readily.

Naturally, there were limits to that as well.

But a debt without concrete form? That was something merchants must be extremely wary of.

Precisely because it lacks form, they don't know what might be demanded of them.

...Though, that level of caution is likely reserved for underworld figures like Slein; ordinary transactions probably wouldn't warrant such suspicion.

Pressing her further here wouldn't unsettle Precious.

Having reached that conclusion, Slein let out a small sigh before returning to the original topic... bringing up the reason he'd summoned Precious in the first place.

"Well, fine. So what about that promise? Since I crossed that dangerous bridge to fulfill your request, you'd better keep that promise properly."

"No problem. I've laid the groundwork. If you go to them, they'll hire you without issue. As for the contract..."

He paused, then pulled a single sheet of paper from a leather pouch he'd taken from his coat.

The paper was clearly larger than the pouch itself, and it seemed unlikely it would normally fit inside.

"You've got some pretty impressive magic items. Is it expanded internally with spatial magic? Come to think of it, I heard there's a downgraded version of that item box Rei has... Is this it?"

"Yes. It cost a pretty penny, but it really comes in handy in situations like this. Though, to be fair, this leather pouch is actually one of the less effective magic items of its kind."

"Hmm. Might I ask, specifically, how much it can hold?"

Precious shook her head at Slain, who was looking at her with keen interest.

"I'm afraid not. This is one of my trump cards. I can't just reveal its capacity to anyone. Not even to you, Slain."

Slein snorted softly at Precious's words.

Slein wasn't particularly desperate to know either.

He'd only asked because he was slightly curious.

That's precisely why he didn't press further and moved on to the main topic.

"So, Mr. Slein. How many operatives do you currently have available?"

"If you mean 'usable,' then there are about thirty. ...But you don't need that kind of person this time, right?"

"Yes. They seem to have done quite a bit of research on us. If possible..."

"Hold on."

Slein cut off Precious's words.

"Yes? What is it?"

"...Looks like we're misunderstanding each other. I thought you meant we should prepare forces to attack Rei and the others?"

In fact, Rei was already in the slums.

If the goal was purely to gather numbers without considering combat strength, then as Slein mentioned, assembling around thirty people wouldn't be difficult.

In fact, by using connections, he could probably gather even more people.

But Precious's words made him suspect she might be targeting Rei.

Of course, Precious had told him various details about the situation.

She knows her people are investigating Tris and Rei.

But... Precious's words now sound like she wants us to target Tris, not Rei.

And Precious nodded back as if it were only natural.

"Yes. This time, the target is not Rei, but Mr. Tris. ...I've taken various measures to obstruct them, but they're proving quite formidable."

"...Is it okay to leave Rei alone?"

"Yes. They're probably looking for me, but under our agreement with Tris, they can't take direct action until they find evidence. In that sense, Tris is a more troublesome opponent than Rei."

Those words seemed to imply that Rei was no match for him...

"Aren't you underestimating Rei? You know what he's capable of, right?"

"Yes, of course. But fortunately... or should I say, they're in a position where they can't resort to their ultimate means: force. So, is there really that much to fear?"

"...Let me say this again. Don't underestimate Rei. Rei is the type who attacks without mercy when someone becomes his enemy, even if they're nobility."

Slein's gaze was serious... so serious it seemed to say that if she still didn't understand, cutting ties might be unavoidable. But Precious nodded reassuringly and spoke up.

"Trust me on this. At the very least, the chances of encountering them in this slum district are slim. And even if we did run into them, they wouldn't resort to force."

Slein silently fixed his gaze on Precious's words.

He disliked her underestimating Rei. He disliked it... but it was an undeniable fact that leaving Gilm and finding a worthy opponent required Precious's help.

Leaving Gilm itself wouldn't be a problem at all.

But while many active adventurers in Gilm would recognize his worth, he hadn't taken a single job in years.

He had already discarded his guild card, leaving him with nothing to prove he was an adventurer.

Well, if I requested a reissue from the guild, it would cost some money, but I could technically become an adventurer again. I could, but... if the guild did even a little digging, they'd quickly discover that Slain hasn't taken any jobs for years.

Then it would become known immediately that even though I'm a Gilm adventurer, I'm not an active one.

If it came out I'd been active in the slums of Gilm, I couldn't imagine anyone hiring me.

If he searched, he might find a place willing to hire him, but in such cases, he'd likely be treated as little more than disposable labor.

In fact, there was a very real possibility that the conditions would be worse than his current situation.

To avoid that, he needed proper backing... and in that sense, Precious, who had approached them, was the perfect partner.

The Spier Trading Company wasn't particularly famous in Gilm.

At the very least, it couldn't compare to the Azoth Trading Company, which had tried to pit Rei against us this time.

But... even so, there's no end to looking up.

Logically speaking, a merchant from the Spier Trading Company was someone with ample wealth and influence.

In any case, to put an end to this life lurking in the slums, Precious's existence was absolutely essential. We couldn't let this opportunity slip away.

"Alright. For now, I'll trust you. But if Rei shows up, I'll either retreat or surrender at that point... At the very least, fighting isn't an option."

"I suppose that's unavoidable. I have no plans to fight Rei, so that's fine. In exchange, however, regarding your countermeasures for Tris..."

"I understand. Reges can handle it, so I'll leave it to him."

"Reges? Wasn't he the one who attacked Rei last time?"

"That's right. Among the people I can currently mobilize, he's the best fit for this job."

"But..."

The reason for the hesitation was hearing that Set had an exceptionally keen sense when gathering information on Rei.

If that was the case, and since Rei was involved in this matter, wouldn't using that person be unwise?

That's what Precious couldn't help but think.

Precious had gathered information on Rei, but he hadn't fully understood it yet.

...Which was probably why she couldn't comprehend what happened in the next instant.

That voice came from outside the window of the room where Precious and the others were.
