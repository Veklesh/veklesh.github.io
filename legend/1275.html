"Huh? A portable kiln? Ask Pamidor for that. I'm busy right now modifying your male Rei Pnil's boots."

Rei's words drew a sullen reply from Azimov.

Judging by his expression, it was clear the modification of Sleipnir Boots was far from going smoothly.

Snow had been falling for some time now, and it was nearly year-end.

The modifications to Sleipnir Boots, which had started off reasonably well, had apparently hit a wall.

It was at this moment that Rei suddenly dropped by and asked him to build a portable kiln.

It was hardly unreasonable for Azimov to suggest asking Pamidor.

But Rei shook his head.

"If I wanted a regular kiln, I'd naturally ask Pamidor or borrow one. But what I want is a portable kiln, and not just any kiln—a magic item kiln."

Hearing the words "magic item kiln," Azimov's interest finally seemed piqued.

Azimov's irritated gaze shifted slightly, becoming more intrigued.

True to his nature as an alchemist, the phrase "magic item kiln" had clearly piqued his interest.

"Interesting. Tell me more about it."

"Yeah. Though it's not that complicated. It's a kiln you use for cooking or heating when camping."

"...Then wouldn't a regular campfire suffice? Your item box is packed with firewood, isn't it?"

"True. For that alone, a campfire would suffice. But... there's a dish I'd like to try making. To make it, I need a kiln, not a campfire."

"Cooking? ...Well, I guess I can see why you'd need a kiln for that... Rei cooking?"

Azimov shot Rei a suspicious look.

Since ovens are normally used for baking bread, he wouldn't normally question using one for cooking.

But hearing that Rei was cooking? That was another matter entirely.

Rei looked slightly annoyed at Azimov's reaction.

Of course, if you asked if he could cook like a chef or a housewife, the answer would be no.

Still, when it came to simple cooking... well, he was more confident in his cooking than Azimov was.

"What's the big deal? Is it bad that I cook?"

"...Well, I guess Rei did popularize udon. It wouldn't be weird if you could cook, right?"

Azimov tilted his head, but soon seemed to regain his composure and spoke again.

"So, why the oven? Baking bread or something?"

"Close, but no. I remembered a pretty interesting way to cook something, so I thought I'd try making it."

"That's all it takes to warrant a kiln? Like I said before, I'm swamped with stuff related to Sleipnir Boots, you know?"

Azimov's words seemed to imply he wasn't amused, but honestly, there was no doubt he was at least a little intrigued by Rei's words.

The spark of curiosity in his eyes hadn't completely faded.

"Like I said, what I'm baking isn't bread. It's the dish I remembered. It's probably the first time this dish has been made in the Kingdom of Mireana. Well, maybe something similar exists somewhere, but I've never seen it."

"Huh. So it's that rare of a dish? ...And is it tasty?"

"Well... I've never made it before, and since it'll be trial and error, it might taste awful at first. But once I get it right, I think it'll be delicious."

Of course, Rei had never made pizza before.

Back when Rei was in Japan, there were pizza delivery places in the city center, but they were a bit pricey, so he hardly ever ate them.

He did eat pizza bread from bakeries and pizza-like items from supermarkets to some extent, though.

(Why is pizza so expensive? I remember reading somewhere that pizza in places like America is pretty cheap, but Japanese pizza is absurdly expensive.)

As Rei pondered this, Azimov gave him an intriguing look.

"Is it really that delicious?"

"Yeah... Well, I've never actually eaten it, so I can only say 'probably'."

"You still know some weird stuff, don't you?"

"It's info I got from my master's books. Can't really say about that part. ...So, what do you say? Will you make me a magic item kiln?"

"Hmm... Yeah. Might be just the thing for a change of pace. ...Huh? A kiln?"

Stuck on the Sleipnir Boots case, Azimov thought a change of pace might be good. But then an idea struck him.

"Hey, if we do build a kiln, could we use Silver Lion bones in it? From my experience, I think using Silver Lion bones as one of the brick materials would make bricks that can withstand pretty high temperatures."

Rei understood what Azimov was thinking.

He probably wanted to break the current deadlock with the Sleipnir Boots by using Silver Lion material to create some other magic item.

Of course, the techniques used to build the kiln wouldn't necessarily be applicable to Sleipnir Boots.

But in terms of inspiration, it was an important development.

Additionally, progress on the Sleipnir Boots was welcome news for Rei, as was the prospect of getting a kiln built.

Especially the kiln—it could be used for all sorts of cooking, not just pizza.

Until now, when eating during campouts, they could only serve pre-made dishes from the Misty Ring or grill skewered meat over the campfire...

Of course, that wasn't a problem for meals.

But with an oven, even bread baked some time ago could be enjoyed fresh again. And if dough were placed inside the Misty Ring, it would literally mean eating bread straight from the oven.

...Though, naturally, Rei has never baked bread before, so she'll need to practice quite a bit.

Also, the reason she requested the oven this time was partly related to Rei officially forming a party starting this spring.

While she planned to handle most cooking from the Misty Ring, with the increased number of people and the possibility of unforeseen circumstances, she decided it was better to be overly cautious.

"Silver Lion materials... bones, no less. Hmm..."

Even with such compelling reasons, Rei hesitated because Silver Lion bones seemed like an incredibly versatile material.

As Pamidor and Azimov had mentioned, they could be ground into bone powder and mixed into various materials, or shaped into weapons themselves... there were many potential uses.

Especially bone powder, which could be applied to so many things. Honestly, Rei preferred not to use it if he could help it.

"Don't agonize over it so much. I'm not saying you have to use the whole Silver Lion bone. Of course, you'll need to test it a few times first to figure out the optimal amount."

"...Alright."

After much deliberation, Rei ultimately decided to follow Azimov's advice.

He genuinely wanted to see a magic item furnace crafted from Rank S monster materials, and he trusted Azimov's skill, especially after the Twilight Spear incident.

He was certain that even if Azimov went a bit overboard, the result would undoubtedly be a high-performance kiln.

(The biggest problem is whether I'll even be able to use such an amazing kiln properly.)

Even a first-rate item is meaningless if the user is second-rate or third-rate.

It was true that I needed at least the skill level to match that kiln.

Still, the fact that this skill was limited to judging the firing process itself, and not encompassing all aspects of cooking, was probably a relief to Rei.

"Well, don't worry. Fortunately, thanks to the Silver Lion being so large, we have plenty of bones. They won't run out anytime soon."

"...But don't go wasting it, okay?"

While telling Azimov this, Rei recalled what Grimm had told him: the Silver Lion he'd defeated wasn't even one of the larger specimens among Silver Lions.

(If only it had been a bigger one... No, even then, it's doubtful I'd have had a chance.)

He shook his head immediately, stopping himself from dwelling on something pointless now.

In stark contrast to Rei's demeanor, Azimov, having received Rei's permission, wore a broad smile.

Given his usually gruff demeanor, this was a very rare sight for Rei.

Seeing Azimov promptly start preparing to build the kiln, Rei decided staying any longer would only be in the way and spoke up.

"Well, I'm heading back. Let me know when the kiln's ready."

"Alright, leave it to me. ...Well, since it's a kiln, we'll need heat. For that, should we use magic stones... no, maybe fire ore? Or perhaps..."

His mind was already focused on building the kiln. Without turning around, Azimov waved lightly at Rei's words from behind him.

Seeing this quintessentially Azimov-like behavior, Rei flashed a small smile and left the house.

"Gururu!"

The moment he stepped outside, Set approached Rei, letting out a hopeful bark.

He had been told that Rei had come to Azimov's house today to have a kiln for magic items made.

And he had explained to Set that building the kiln was necessary to make a new dish called pizza.

That was probably why Set was growling, eager to eat pizza soon! But... Rei gently stroked Set's head.

"Calm down. Just because I asked him to make it today doesn't mean it'll be ready right away."

"Grrr..."

Set looked down disappointedly at Rei's words.

He didn't know what kind of dish pizza was, but he could imagine it must be delicious since Rei said so.

Basically, everything Rei made... well, more accurately, everything she taught others to cook, was incredibly delicious.

He said "basically" because the meat bun-like thing he'd eaten a little while ago hadn't been bad, but it hadn't been good either.

Not that he sensed Set's thoughts, but Rei suddenly murmured.

"Yeah, maybe we should swing by Golden Bread House before heading back to the inn. I'm kinda curious how the steamed buns have been improved."

At Rei's words, Set's pace slowed just a little.

He probably didn't want to eat them, precisely because he remembered the taste of that imitation meat bun.

Noticing Set's hesitation, Rei gently stroked his arm encouragingly.

"Last time, they tasted like that because I made them from my vague memories. But Rodrigo has probably refined them properly by now, so don't worry. And Sandrine probably wants to see you too, right?"

"Grrrr..."

Whether encouraged by Rei's words or simply wanting Sandrine to play with him, Set started walking beside Rei.

They reached the main street and passed by the Golden Bread Shop. There was no line outside, but the seating inside wasn't exactly packed either.

No, considering it was afternoon, the fact that there were still customers at this hour meant it was doing quite well.

"Welcome. I'm afraid we're already preparing for the evening service... Oh! Mr. Rei. And little Set too?"

The moment the door opened, Sandrine, working as a waitress, quickly tried to offer that refusal, but upon recognizing it was Rei and Set, her apologetic expression instantly turned to a smile.

Seeing this, the customers still in the shop shot Rei a look filled with jealousy.

Sandrine wasn't exactly the type of beauty who drew men's attention, but she had an incredibly bright personality. Perhaps it showed on her face, because she was very popular as the Golden Bread Shop's poster girl.

However, Rei, receiving the customers' jealous stares, could only manage a wry smile.

It was obvious Sandrine's joy wasn't specifically because she'd seen Rei.

Of course, Set growled from outside the dining hall at Sandrine.

"I came to check on the meat buns... How are they?"

"Oh, about that? You're so reliable. Dad, Rei's here!"

Asking why Rei had come, Sandrine called out toward the kitchen.

And emerging from the kitchen was Rodrigo, looking just as timid as before.

At first glance, he certainly didn't look like a skilled chef.

"Ah, Ms. Rei. Welcome. ...Please wait just a moment. The prep work will be finished shortly. ...Until then, yes. Please wait a little while."

Rodrigo, wearing a delighted smile at seeing Rei, returned to the kitchen... only to bring out a bowl of udon noodles.
