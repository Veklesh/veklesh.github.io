The day after Vihera and Byune arrived in Gilm, Rei was walking through the city with the two of them and Set.

Having only arrived yesterday, the two naturally had no idea where the various shops were located, so Rei was acting as their guide.

Vihera, eager to take on a guild quest to fight the powerful monsters unique to the frontier, felt strongly about it. Still, she recognized she needed guidance through Gilm, so Rei was showing her around with Set.

It made perfect sense. If Vihera and Byune had wandered the streets alone, it was certain some man trying to hit on Vihera would cause trouble.

Therefore, it was better for Rei and Set—whom most in the Gilm knew—to accompany them. Half for the snacks, Rei and Set were tagging along with Vihera and the others.

During the tour, just like yesterday, many people tried to engage with Set. Still, things had calmed down somewhat. There were fewer people than yesterday.

Rei had assumed Mireine and Johanna would storm into the Dusk Wheat inn yesterday... or more accurately, into the stables. But there was no sign of them coming at all.

Finding this odd, Rei gathered some information... or rather, while buying food at a stall like he was doing now, he listened to people talk and managed to get some information from several shopkeepers.

"I never imagined Johanna and the others would take on a joint request with Scorching Wind."

Hearing this completely unexpected news, he muttered in disbelief.

But then, upon reflection, maybe it wasn't so strange after all? The thought crossed Rei's mind.

It was true that Mireine and Johanna were like cats and dogs over Set, but perhaps the other members of Scorching Wind and the former guerrilla fighters besides Johanna got along quite well?

Thinking that way, it wasn't strange at all for those two groups to act together.

(Besides, it's a mission to hunt a Rank C monster, a Cyclops. The Scorching Wind should be fine as a Rank C party, but considering the possibility of something irregular happening, having the former guerrilla members along probably isn't such a bad idea.)

Based on the shopkeeper's account of seeing the Blazing Wind and former guerrilla squad members—about ten people total—depart yesterday, it seemed they could handle the Cyclops. So, rather than worrying about the extermination request itself, Rei was more concerned about the tension when Mireine and Johanna returned and met Set for the first time in two months.

Based on past experience, there was a high chance things would get completely out of hand.

Especially since Mireine was present when Marka arrived, but Johanna wasn't even there—she was completely caught off guard when Rei and Set disappeared.

"Grr?"

Feeling sorry for him, Rei stroked Set's head. The stroked one looked up at Rei with round eyes, asking, "What's wrong?"

For Set, both Mireine and Johanna were people who doted on him, and he certainly didn't dislike them.

In fact, since they always gave him delicious food, he was quite attached to them, though not quite as much as to Rei.

"Is something wrong?"

After talking with the shopkeeper, Vihera shot Rei a puzzled look, clearly wondering why he'd suddenly started petting Set.

"No, I was just doting on Set... Ah, speaking of which, do you know Vihera? That former guerrilla fighter. I heard her family runs a big trading company."

Rei asked curiously, but the response was a shake of the head.

"How many trading houses do you think are over there? Maybe she'd know if it was a really famous one..."

"I see. Well, I think they'd know about Vihera over there."

"That... makes sense."

The former guerrilla fighters hailed from the Bestir Empire and had fought on the side of the Third Prince during last year's civil war.

Naturally, they would know about Vihera, who served as a crucial combat force within the Third Prince's faction. Moreover, they must have seen Vihera come to meet Rei countless times during the civil war.

Given that, it was certain he would be stunned to see Vihera in Gilm.

"Hm?"

While eating the lizardman skewers Vihera had bought for her, Byune looked over at the two of them with a puzzled expression.

Rei and Vihera simply shook their heads as if to say it was nothing and continued walking through the town.

Though even if Rei was acting as a guide, he didn't fully know his way around Gilm himself.

His usual range in Gilm was mostly limited to the area around the guild itself, the food stalls, and shops dealing in weapons and tools.

...The fact that the lord's mansion falls within that range probably speaks to how unusual Rei is.

"That weapons shop is where I sometimes stop by to buy spears. They sell weapons that are beyond use—with chipped tips or handles about to break—for practically nothing, just a few copper coins. It's a lifesaver for me."

"Selling weapons that are already unusable? That's a bit questionable, isn't it?"

Rei frowned slightly at Rei's words, but Rei shook his head in denial at Vihera's remark.

"Obviously, you can't use broken weapons for real combat. But they're good for training, like seeing if a weapon suits you. Of course, you get what you pay for, so they break easily."

Hearing they weren't meant for actual combat, Vihera finally looked satisfied.

Still, he wondered if using weapons that seemed likely to break was really wise, even for training.

"Ah, and then there are adventurers who've had a string of failed quests and are desperately short on cash, right? You sometimes see those adventurers looking for decent weapons."

"...Seriously? Your life's on the line here?"

Vihera muttered with a look of incomprehension, but Byune gently tugged at her thin robe and shook her head.

When it came to being broke, Byune had experienced it for a long time in Exil.

That was probably why she understood why adventurers would try to acquire weapons with limited funds.

「Byune……」

"Well, try to understand what Byune's saying. Besides, when they say 'useless weapons,' they mean weapons that can't be used for their original purpose. Some are actually in decent condition. You could use a broken spear as a short spear, or a cleaver with a chipped blade as a melee weapon."

Normally, melting down these weapons would yield some money.

But the shopkeeper offered them as a form of relief for adventurers who had little money, accepting a small loss in the process.

Some might argue that if he truly cared about adventurers, he should just provide cheap but functional weapons. But the shop itself wasn't that big to begin with. He couldn't afford to cut profits that deeply.

In that sense, a customer like Rei, who bought even broken spears at a slight markup, could be considered a valuable customer for the shop.

...Though, of course, Rei buying so many spears meant they became harder to come by for other spear-wielding adventurers.

"Well, the ones who use them are usually newbies who grew up here and just became adventurers, or those who came to Gilm with stronger adventurers. Though apparently, there are some reckless fools who come straight here despite just becoming adventurers."

For adventurers of the Mireana Kingdom, the frontier town of Gilm was the heartland of adventuring.

It's no small number who dream of visiting it at least once, of making a name for themselves there, much like the royal capital or the labyrinth city.

This feeling was especially strong among those who had just become adventurers.

But while most would choose the steady path of building strength and rank if they felt underprepared, occasionally reckless souls would head straight for Gilm.

Naturally, most of these individuals meet their end along the way, falling victim to monsters, bandits, or some other incident. Yet, by chance or sheer luck, some do manage to reach Gilm.

Naturally, upon reaching Gilm, it's as if they've used up all their luck, and due to their lack of skill, they can't take on many jobs.

They can take on simple jobs like those offered within Gilm, but the pay is barely enough to scrape by.

It is these individuals who come seeking weapons to the very shop Rei and the others are now observing.

"See, people like that,"

Rei's gaze fell on a man who appeared a few years older than himself.

He probably arrived at Gilm this spring. He wore what looked like the remains of leather armor and carried no weapon in his hands.

Watching the man enter the weapons shop, Rei spoke again.

"Well, even so, if someone's got real drive or talent, they could rise up from a situation like that. ...Alright, let's move on."

He announced this and began walking down the street.

Occasionally buying snacks as they moved along, he pointed out the library's location, showed her shops selling books, recommended cheap and tasty cafeterias, pointed out stalls with delicious skewers, told her about shops and stalls with great sandwiches, and recommended places with delicious soup.

"Wait. Hold on a second. There's been an unusually high number of food-related places lately. And since we've been stopping at every single one of those shops and stalls, I'm already stuffed!"

"Oh? ...How about it?"

"Growl."

Set growled, as if to say, I can still eat more!

Rei knew his own stomach still had plenty of room, so he shot Vihera a look that said they were fine.

But the look Vihera gave Rei in return was one of utter bewilderment.

"To be fine after eating that much... What kind of constitution do you have? ...For us, that's the real surprise, isn't it? Right?"

Vihera turned to ask Byune, who was walking beside her.

She assumed Byune, being smaller than herself, couldn't possibly eat any more...

"Hm?"

For some reason, Byune, who was even smaller than Vihera, was happily munching on the wild bird skewers she'd gotten as a bonus from the yakitori stall owner earlier, showing no sign of discomfort whatsoever.

「……Byune？」

"Hm?"

The voice that answered the question was the same as a few seconds ago.

But there was no trace of the expression Vihera had expected to see—the look of having eaten too much, of being full, of not being able to eat any more.

"Was Byune always such a big eater?"

Vihera wondered, but Rei spoke to her as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

"Kids grow by eating well, sleeping well, and playing well. So isn't it fine to eat as much as you can?"

"You're not wrong, but there's a limit to everything. Byune, are you really okay? I won't be responsible if you eat too much and get a stomachache later, you know?"

"Mm!"

Since Byune was basically expressionless, maybe it just wasn't showing on her face.

That was Vihera's concern as she asked, but the only response was the same small voice as always.

Vihera had known Byune for quite some time. Even to her eyes, which could catch the slightest change in expression beneath the apparent blankness, Byune didn't seem to be forcing herself.

She had no choice but to conclude he genuinely still had room in his stomach.

"I thought you only ate what was appropriate for your build before... What's going on? You're not sick, are you?"

Vihera murmured worriedly, but Byune, the subject of her concern, didn't seem ill at all.

Having confirmed this, Vihera seemed slightly unconvinced. Still, deciding it wasn't illness, she sighed and stroked Byune's head.

"It's fine to eat, but don't get fat from eating too much, okay? You're a girl, so you always have to stay pretty. And above all, it wouldn't be funny if a thief got slow."

"...Hmm."

Byune reluctantly nodded at Vihera's words.

Seemingly satisfied for now, Vihera turned her gaze to Rei walking beside her and spoke.

"So, where are we going next?"

"Since we're already here, maybe we should drop by the guild again, like yesterday?"

"Why? You don't plan on taking any jobs, do you?"

"Depends on the job. But if anything, it's just to show our faces. ...Vihera stands out, after all."

Given her attire, which made her look like nothing more than a prostitute or dancer, the remark was only natural.

In fact, those still secretly watching Vihera were nodding vigorously inside.

"Hmm. Well, if that's what Rei says, Alright then, let's go."

Saying that, she didn't let Rei guide her. Instead, she took the lead as if she were guiding Rei.

Having been there yesterday, she must already remember the guild's location.

Indeed, without showing the slightest sign of hesitation, Vihera arrived at the guild.

The moment she parted ways with Set and stepped inside...

"Five cyclopes! That's why I said to call for reinforcements quickly!"

Such a voice echoed through the air.
