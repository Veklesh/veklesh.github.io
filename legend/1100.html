"...Hm..."

When Rei woke up in the morning, he looked around, his sleepy expression still lingering, yet his face showed confusion.

The room was completely unfamiliar, and the bedding he had been sleeping on was also unfamiliar.

After scanning his surroundings for a few seconds and concluding it was indeed an unfamiliar place, he froze completely, not moving a muscle.

Several minutes passed like this. Finally, it seemed Rei's consciousness had fully awakened, and he looked around again, just as before.

Then, he finally remembered where he was.

"Right, I came to Gosh and ended up staying at Auguste's mansion."

Looking around again, it seemed this wasn't a room normally used. It felt a bit dusty somehow.

Only Auguste and Kathy lived in this mansion.

The gatekeeper, Günter, commuted in, and there were no maids.

Given that, even when cleaning, they probably focus on the areas used daily and inevitably put guest rooms like this one off until later.

(Still, it seems he cleans various things whenever he has free time, which is why I could sleep here normally.)

Muttering to myself, I got out of bed and began getting ready.

His face and body felt sticky, likely because the sun had already risen and the temperature had climbed above thirty degrees.

Though Rei usually wore the Dragon Robe with its simple air conditioning capabilities, he didn't keep it on while sleeping.

Inside the Dusk Wheat inn or the Magic Tent, she wouldn't have to worry about feeling cold or hot from the outside temperature... but this mansion, while fairly spacious, was still just a regular mansion.

Rei drew the Water-Flowing Dagger from Misty Ring, dampened the cloth provided in the room, and wiped away the sweat.

If Auguste, who knew its true nature, saw her wiping her body with water from the Water-Flowing Dagger—water that could be called heavenly nectar—he would likely scream in horror.

...Though, for Rei, the Water-Flowing Dagger was simply a magic item that produced water. Using it to wipe his face and body like this, or to wash his hands, was perfectly ordinary.

After all, Rei could use the Water-Flowing Dagger anytime. To him, it was simply a magic item that let him produce water whenever he pleased.

If Rei could wield water like a whip or a longsword with the Flowing Water Dagger, that would be a different story...

Anyway, after wiping away the sweat from sleeping at night with the Flowing Water Dagger and finishing her other preparations, she donned the Dragon Robe.

"Ah, cool."

The moment he put on the Dragon Robe, its simple air-conditioning effect kicked in.

Air conditioners back in Japan would sometimes cause joint pain if you sat directly in the cold air, but the Dragon Robe, perhaps because it uses magical power, doesn't do that.

...Wearing the Dragon Robe, a cold breeze generated by magic circulates within it, constantly blowing directly against my body... If it worked like a regular air conditioner, I might have suffered from joint pain.

(Could it be that Takumu or someone like him thought of that aspect?)

I recall the face of someone from the Zepairu clan who, like me, had come from Earth.

Having come from Japan, it wouldn't be strange for them to understand the drawbacks of air conditioning.

It makes sense they'd think to use magic-powered cold air to counteract that.

(Though I still don't get why magic-powered cold air doesn't make your body ache...)

Feeling a slight doubt, he still forced himself to accept that using magic... well, since it was the power within his own body, it probably wasn't that strange after all. He left the room and walked down the hallway.

His destination was the living room where he'd eaten dinner last night.

Though this mansion was sparsely populated, he understood perfectly well that someone would likely be there.

"Oh, good morning. August has already left, but would you like breakfast?"

Kathy had likely already eaten. She asked Rei while sewing something.

"Ah, yeah. That would be great."

"Alright. Just wait a moment. I'll just warm it up, so it won't take long."

Kathy stood up from the sofa, placed her sewing on the table, and headed straight for the kitchen.

(Seeing her like this, she seems normal.)

Yesterday, when she was with Auguste, she'd been sick of how they'd start flirting the moment they got the chance. But now, seeing that she could interact normally without him around, Rei breathed a silent sigh of relief.

If Cathy had been alone and he'd been forced to listen to her endless lovey-dovey talk about Auguste... the mental damage would undoubtedly have been worse than being attacked by some random monster.

"It's nothing fancy, but will this do?"

Cathy returned from the kitchen carrying four fist-sized loaves of bread and a soup made with vegetables and Gamelion meat.

As mentioned earlier, it had likely been reheated. Steam rose from the soup.

It had a simple aroma, different from last night's heavily spiced dish.

Even so, having vegetables in it was likely a luxury for Gosh, a desert town.

Gamelion meat spoils quickly in Gosh. Considering that, using it all up for breakfast was probably best, and as if to emphasize this, the soup was packed with meat.

(Could it be that she can't afford to hire a maid because she splurges on meals? Or maybe she just doesn't want anyone else in her life with Auguste? But then, why would she let me stay?)

Though he harbored various questions, Rei finished every last bit of the breakfast spread.

Just like yesterday's meal, Auguste's pride in it was well-founded—Cathy's cooking skills were truly exceptional.

He would have liked more, but since he was staying as a guest of August's kindness, he couldn't just eat as much as he pleased.

"Set's meals..."

"Auguste seemed to be handling it."

"How much did he make him?"

"I'm not sure. But Auguste is thoughtful, so I doubt he'd do something half-hearted like that. Just the other day, he..."

Hearing that far, Rei judged that continuing would likely lead to a lovey-dovey exchange about Auguste. She thanked him for breakfast and left the mansion immediately.

Her destination, naturally, was the stable where Set was.

He'd stopped by after dinner yesterday too, so he didn't get lost.

"Grrrrrrr!"

Sensing Rei's approach, a cheerful whinny rose from the stable.

Yet he didn't come out. He understood that destroying the building was something he simply couldn't do.

If Rei were in danger, it would be a different story, but in the current situation, that was highly unlikely.

Set, peeking out from between the trees, purred "This way, this way!" as Rei entered the stable.

To someone seeing Set for the first time, it might sound like a threatening growl, but to his partner Rei, it was unmistakably the sound of someone eagerly awaiting his return.

"Seems he was doing fine. No one snuck in at night or anything."

"Grrr."

For some reason, Set growled proudly at Rei's words.

Smiling at Set's behavior, Ray removed the wooden stick preventing Set from going outside and headed out of the stable together.

Beside the stable was a fairly large yard. ...Well, strictly speaking, it wasn't exactly overgrown with grass, so calling it a plaza might be more accurate.

While some grass grew near the main gate and the mansion, this stable was an unused space.

Given that, Augustus, who certainly wasn't rolling in money, probably hadn't bothered with any maintenance.

"Well, that's why we can roam freely, though."

Set whinnied in agreement to Rei's mutter.

It was obvious what would happen if Set were to run full speed across a neatly manicured lawn.

But in a place like this open square, there was no need to worry about that.

"...Right then. Set, is anyone watching us from around here? If they are, I'd guess it's people from the Elema Trading Company."

"Grrrr."

Set shook his head at Rei's question.

"Huh. Based on yesterday, I figured they'd at least be gathering intel on us. ...Well, if that's the case, fine. Then, let's do a little training. We're skinning the Sand Serpent today, so we won't be moving our bodies much anyway."

"Guru?"

Set tilted his head at Rei's words.

Zarust had offered to handle the Sand Serpent skinning.

So, wouldn't it be fine even without Rei and Set? That was the question behind his action.

"I trust Zarust, but I don't trust everyone completely. That much should be clear from the sand pirate incident, right?"

There was no doubt someone had leaked the information the sand pirates needed to attack Auguste and the others.

In the end, they hadn't shown any movement at the campsite, perhaps wary of Set, but even so, it was certain that someone within the escort... or among the merchants in the caravan led by Auguste, had ties to the Elema Trading Company.

"Grrr..."

At Rei's words, Set growled softly, sounding slightly disappointed.

Having shared the campsite, most of the members of the caravan led by Auguste had become friendly with Set.

That's precisely why, upon hearing there was a traitor among them, it was only natural for Set to feel sad.

"Well, there's always the possibility they betrayed us for some unavoidable reason, so don't get too down about it."

"Guru?"

"Really?" Set tilted his head slightly. Rei nodded and spoke.

"Like needing expensive medicine to treat a family member or lover's illness. ...Come to think of it..."

The World Tree's Dew he received as payment for Marina's request.

He'd used some to create the Twilight Spear, but not all of it.

He'd heard that with advanced techniques, alchemy could create incredibly potent potions... the kind that could restore someone to full health in an instant, even if they were on the brink of death.

(When I get back to Gilm, maybe I'll ask Azimov. My health should be back to normal by then.)

A potion made using World Tree Dew as an ingredient. I had originally planned to have him make that too, but Azimov literally poured his entire being into creating the Twilight Spear.

Azimov, renowned as a skilled alchemist, and Pamidor, equally famous as a master blacksmith.

The Twilight Spear was a magic item created by these two, pushing their skills to the absolute limit, a masterpiece they declared could not be surpassed. It was only natural that Ajimov, especially through the magic-infused crafting that defined the very essence of a magic item, would be exhausted to the point of being ready to die at any moment.

He had been bedridden for some time to recover, and that hadn't changed even when Rei set out from Gilm to Gosh.

That was Azimov. Rei thought that if he spent some time in Gosh and then returned to Gilm, his health should be restored. And a new request to create a potion using World Tree Dew should be a decent job...

World Tree Dew is an extremely rare ingredient, but Ray felt Azimov could handle it—perhaps because he'd created the Twilight Spear, that supreme magic item.

(Besides, he was delighted with the materials for Set too.)

Stroking Set, Rei recalled the time he handed over Set's material... his feathers... to Azimov.

"Grrrrr!"

"Let's play before training!" Set's purring brought Rei back to himself, and he immediately started running around the stable yard with Set.

Tag... or perhaps it was better described as simple chase.

Even such a simple game made Set incredibly happy just to be playing with Rei, and he ran around visibly in high spirits.

How long had they been at it? More than an hour, but not quite two. About that long.

Just as he thought it might be time to use the Death Scythe for some light training... he suddenly sensed an approaching presence near their location.

"Who's there? If you're coming to a place like this, you must have business with us."

"Grrrrr."

Set growled discontentedly, as if agreeing with Rei's words.

Based on past experience, he judged that this meant... no, it definitely meant the time he spent with Rei was about to end.

The figure that appeared before the gaze of the one human and one beast was... Zarust.

"I heard from Kathy you were here. Looks like she was right."

"Zalust? What's up?"

Relieved to learn he wasn't from the Elema Trading Company, Rei asked with a sigh of relief. Zarust opened his mouth, sighing heavily.

"I was wondering what you were up to, not showing up at the guild... Didn't I tell you yesterday? To get ready for the Sand Serpent skinning."

That was his message.
