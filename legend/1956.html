For the time being, having gathered clues—including the bodies found in the mansion, the documents and various tools discovered in the hidden compartments, and, most importantly, the underground chamber and the pink tentacles that emerged from it—Rei and his companions decided to leave the mansion for the time being.

That said, some of the guards felt that even with that much evidence gathered, it was far from certain whether they could convince their superiors to allow them to conduct a full-scale search of this mansion and the surrounding properties as security personnel.

Still, the reason Rei insisted they should end the search was, after all, because of that pink tentacle.

Even Rei had struggled to deal with that tentacle—what on earth would happen if, for some reason, it were to escape from that underground space?

No matter how you look at it, that would be nothing short of dangerous.

(The Kobold situation is dangerous in its own way, but fortunately, thanks to the earthen walls, the number entering Gilm is extremely low. Given that, there’s no doubt that those pink tentacles are clearly the greater danger right now.)

Rei thought this, and the guards who had actually gone into the underground space with her agreed with her words.

Ultimately, whether the tentacles emerging from the spatial rift are dangerous or not depends largely on whether one has actually seen them—the impression they leave varies greatly depending on that.

Even if the guards knew Rei’s capabilities and he himself had said they were dangerous… even if they understood that, as long as they hadn’t seen them directly, it was perhaps an unavoidable human tendency to underestimate the threat by several degrees.

“Anyway, we need to gather the guards scattered throughout this mansion. …Besides, there’s still a possibility that there are people guarding this place.”

Rei let escape had vanished from that underground space.

It was easy to surmise that she had likely escaped to another mansion via one of the staircases in the underground area, but since the situation had already escalated before he could pursue her, he was unable to give chase.

However, since there was no guarantee that the girl was the last person remaining in the mansion, there was still a very real possibility that other guards searching the premises might be attacked.

“You’re right. Then let’s get out of this mansion immediately. We’ll need to get information from that man we captured at the entrance, too.”

“……Come to think of it, he was there.”

Normally, he wasn’t the kind of person you’d forget so quickly.

But to be honest, the sight of those pink tentacles had left such a strong impression on Rei that he’d completely forgotten about him.

“Hey, don’t forget about him.”

"My bad. So, how are we going to round up the guys scattered throughout the mansion?"

“I’ll use this.”

Saying that, the guard pulled out a flute.

Seeing the whistle, Rei remembered having seen them use one to communicate before, and a look of understanding crossed his face.

“So, you can communicate to some extent using that whistle?”

The term “Morse code” came to mind, but unfortunately, while Rei knew the term, he didn’t actually know how to use Morse code.

However, he figured that the guard was likely using a similar method to communicate, so he understood the intention.

“That’s right. It’s a bit noisy in here, so I’ll go blow it out in the hallway.”

Rei watched the guard leave the room after saying that... and as soon as he stepped into the hallway, he heard the high-pitched sound of a whistle: "Beep!"

He blew the whistle several times in a regular pattern, presumably to finish giving his instructions. The guard opened the door and poked his head out.

“I’m done giving orders, so let’s go.”

Following his words, Rei and the others left the room.

As they were leaving the room... Rei suddenly asked one of the guards—the one who had gone down to the underground space with him—about something that had been on his mind.

“Why didn’t you use that whistle when we were under attack just now?”

If the whistle allowed for some degree of communication, wouldn’t they have been able to call for backup from their comrades while they were under attack?

The guard addressed by Rei shook his head.

“That girl was incredibly strong. Even with the three of us, we didn’t have the luxury of time to blow the whistle.”

“That’s true. If we’d carelessly tried anything strange back there, we probably… no, we definitely would’ve lost our lives, not just gotten injured.”

Another guard who had been with Rei nodded in agreement with his colleague’s words.

"Is that just how it is?" Rei thought to himself as he walked down the hallway.

They must have gathered because of the whistle. On their way to the entrance, several guards joined Rei and the others.

Since they’d been summoned by the whistle like that, some of the guards asked if they’d found any clues, and the guards answered them.

Conversely, there were also moments when guards who had been traveling with Rei and his group asked if they had found any clues.

Naturally, security guards are experts in their field.

It was only natural that they had found clues of their own, in addition to the ones Rei and his group had discovered.

...That said, it was an undeniable fact that the clues Rei and the others had found were the most important ones.

As they went about their business, they eventually arrived at the mansion’s entrance.

As expected, there they found the middle-aged man who had attacked them when they first arrived at the mansion, bound and gagged, with a guard watching over him.

“I heard the sound of a flute, so it seems everything went off without a hitch.”

At the words of the guard waiting at the entrance, the guards who had been searching the mansion nodded in response.

“Yeah. We managed to gather quite a few leads. In fact, enough to probably get the higher-ups involved.”

Hearing the guard’s words, Rei felt a slight pang of doubt.

That was because, according to what he’d heard earlier, it was said that getting the higher-ups involved in this case would be difficult.

(No, I guess that kind of judgment ultimately depends on the person. It wouldn’t be strange if some thought it was feasible while others thought it wasn’t.)

Though he hadn’t heard it directly from the guard, Rei came to that conclusion and turned his gaze toward the middle-aged man, who was gagged and unable to speak.

“So, regarding this mansion… and the surrounding mansions as well, we’ll be launching an investigation. It might not be very pleasant for you, since you were the one guarding this place.”

“Mmmph!”

The middle-aged man tried to say something in response to Rei’s words, but naturally, he couldn’t do such a thing in his current situation.

For a moment, Rei wondered if he should try removing the gag. But considering the possibility that the middle-aged man might bite off his own tongue before he could speak—choosing suicide to prevent him from revealing any information—he realized such a move wouldn’t be so simple.

“Well, all that’s left is to make it back to the station safely. We might get attacked along the way by someone trying to take this back… or to retrieve the clues and whatever else we got from that mansion, so we’ll need to be careful.”

Even as he said this, Rei wasn’t the least bit worried about being ambushed.

That was because, while things might be different inside the mansion, once they were outside, Set would be accompanying him.

Rei’s senses were sharper than those of an ordinary person, but Set’s were even sharper than Rei’s.

In other words, logically speaking, even if the mastermind behind this incident were to send assassins, the fact is that as long as Rei and Set are there, there’s nothing they can do.

The guards must have realized that as well.

They nodded in relief at Rei’s words, helped the bound middle-aged man to his feet, and left the mansion.

“Grrrrr!”

Just then, as if he’d been waiting right by the entrance, Set charged toward Rei, growling happily.

……Normally, having a massive creature like Set charge at you would be nothing short of terrifying.

But to Rei, Set was a partner he could trust with all his heart—and in a sense, a being he could even describe as another version of himself.

For that reason, even though the guards around them were startled by Set’s sudden charge, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered and simply accepted him.

“Hey… you don’t need to be so clingy, do you? It’s not like we were apart for that long. You’re such a mama’s boy.”

Rei says this while stroking Set, who’s rubbing his face against him… but seeing the smile on Rei’s face, those words don’t seem very convincing.

“More importantly, while we were inside the mansion, did anyone try to sneak out?”

The mansion Rei and the others had entered was undoubtedly connected to the surrounding mansions via underground passages.

But it was hard to imagine anyone willingly using a place where tentacles like those might appear.

If that’s the case, the only other conceivable way to escape this mansion would be to slip out through a back entrance somewhere.

(Well, there’s always the possibility that they found a secret passage that neither I nor the guards could find and escaped to another mansion through it.)

As he pondered this, Rei’s hand continued to stroke Set.

“Growl…”

Set purred contentedly, clearly enjoying Rei’s stroking hand.

But Rei, who had been petting Set, eventually stopped.

“Aren’t you going to pet me anymore?” Set gave Rei a round, pleading look, but Rei couldn’t afford to waste any more time here.

“Sorry, Set. You’ll have to wait on the petting for now. Once this matter is settled… no, I’ll pet you to your heart’s content at dinner.”

“Growl!”

At Rei’s words, Set purrs happily.

Pulling his hand away from Set, Rei turned his gaze toward the guards… and then toward the middle-aged man who had been captured.

“Well, let’s go. First, we need to head to the station to gather information, report to our superiors, and examine the evidence we’ve obtained.”

And so, having concluded their search—which had yielded various findings… but also raised new mysteries—Rei and the others headed for the guardhouse.

“Hmm. …So, Rei was fighting a strong opponent, then.”

At dinnertime, in the courtyard of Marina’s house as usual, Vihera listened to Rei recount what had happened that day and remarked enviously.

This was likely because she’d been told that while she’d been fighting Kobolds—or, when she wasn’t in the mood to fight, just killing time—Rei had been blessed with the opportunity to battle a powerful foe.

To be honest, though, from Rei’s perspective, aside from the pink tentacles, none of the opponents were really strong enough to be called “powerful enemies.”

Since there was no doubt they were formidable opponents for the guards, he wasn’t sure what to say to Vihera.

“Even so. They might be stronger than Kobolds, but they weren’t strong enough to make Vihera want to fight them, were they?”

Hearing this reply as he stroked Set, Vihera wore an expression that suggested she didn’t know how to respond.

From Vihera’s perspective, even after hearing that, she still had the desire to fight them.

Still, perhaps realizing that whatever she said now was too late, she eventually let out a small sigh before speaking.

“So, what are the guards going to do now? Can they take action based on the evidence and testimony Rei found?”

“I don’t know. We don’t know that yet. Even the higher-ups in the guard will need to consider various options, right?”

“I suppose so.”

Marina interjected into the conversation between Rei and Vihera.

As a former guild master, she likely has some connections with the guards and is trying to predict how the current guard force will act.

Next to Marina, Elena nodded in agreement.

That said, there’s no doubt that this situation leaves no room for taking things slowly.

Just like with the Kobold incident, what Rei and the guards were worried about was that if the pink tentacles Rei encountered in the underground space emerged from there, they would undoubtedly pose a major threat.

“But the mansion we raided today is being sealed off by the guards, right? …Though, it seems they haven’t been able to seal off the other mansions connected to that underground space.”

“Yeah. Apparently, there are various complications with that. …If you climb the stairs in that underground space, you could probably get into the other mansions as well. …I think that would be less of a hassle in many ways, but apparently, there are some difficulties involved.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Elena and the others who had been listening couldn’t help but frown.
