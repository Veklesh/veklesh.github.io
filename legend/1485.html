Rei and Ilse were brought to the tavern in Dahl, where Marina, Vihera, and Byune were also present.

However, Elena and Ara, who were staying at Marina's house, were not present.

In the current situation, if Elena were to venture outside, it was certain that various people would approach her.

The most likely candidates were nobles from the Noble Faction.

To avoid dealing with such people, Elena had holed up at Marina's house.

"Rei-san. There's been movement."

Rei and the others, who had been eating light snacks and fruit water while talking, turned their gaze toward Dahl who had entered the room.

"Movement? Did Ajax leave the inn?"

The first to react to Rei's muttered words was... unsurprisingly, Ilse.

She reacted before Marina, Vihera, and the others, likely because it concerned the enemy of her family.

...Though Marina and Vihera weren't particularly fixated on Ajax either.

Vihera, especially, knew Ajax himself wasn't that strong, so she judged him not worth her time.

Marina, on the other hand, likely had more to say about Ajax, given that he had used his status as an adventurer to commit evil deeds.

Byune remained indifferent, savoring the food on the table.

"No, Ajax himself hasn't moved yet. But his associates... According to our intel, there are two: Levjef and Hastun. It seems Levjef has left the inn."

"...Since they're Ajax's associates, they must understand his current situation, right?"

"Yes. That's almost certainly the case. ...We have our people infiltrated inside the inn, so if Ajax tries to alert anyone about his situation or makes a move, we should get word immediately."

"That's quick."

Dahl's words surprised not only Rei but the others as well. ...Only Byune seemed completely unaware of the conversation, continuing to eat.

For a moment, Dahl shot Byune a look that seemed to ask where on earth he could possibly fit all that food, but he quickly continued.

"It seems Levjef headed for the slums. ...Unfortunately, we lost the tail partway, so it's not certain. But judging by the direction he went, it's almost definitely him."

"Huh. Shaking you guys off? Looks like Rebejev's got some skill after all."

"Yes. Unfortunately, it seems so. Though, if he's been working as an adventurer like Ajax, he was probably hiding his true abilities."

"He's skilled, huh? ...You seem pleased."

"Oh? Really?"

Vihera tilted her head slightly when Rei's gaze fell on her.

She probably thought she was hiding it, but for Rei and the others, who'd known Vihera for quite some time now, her act had no effect.

She seemed to understand that too. She let out a small sigh before speaking.

"If you were hiding your true strength, that means we can expect quite a bit, right? Even if you leave that Ajax fellow to Ilse, the fights against the other two should be quite enjoyable."

"...I wonder. Hope it doesn't end up disappointing."

Vihera murmured with hope, but Rei said that as a precaution.

He understood that, given Vihera's skill level, opponents merely somewhat capable would undoubtedly leave her disappointed.

Not that that was necessarily a bad thing, but it meant there was a high chance he'd end up sparring with a disgruntled Vihera.

Given that Vihera was a combat maniac, the mock battle would naturally become intense.

Getting dragged into that would undoubtedly be exhausting, both physically and mentally.

(Ah, maybe I'll leave it to Elena. It might be interesting to have her fight someone like Ara.)

Since she's got free time, it should be fine to dump Vihera on her, Rei thought.

In reality, many would likely volunteer for a mock battle with Vihera.

Especially the men—if they could see Vihera's alluring physique up close, they'd likely volunteer, prepared to take some damage.

Of course, if they ended up unable to show much of their strength, they'd likely earn a cold stare from Vihera.

...Though some might actually enjoy that cold stare, possessing peculiar tastes. Unfortunately, Rei couldn't fathom such preferences.

"Anyway. I'm glad things are finally moving, but... what do we do now? Can we start making our own moves?"

Dahl shook his head at Rei's question.

"No, things have moved, but we still don't have any definitive proof. It would be helpful if you could only act once they make such a move."

"But what if Ajax tries to leave Gilm without doing anything? They must know they're under suspicion. Given that, there's a chance they could flee with only the bare minimum..."

Ilse's concern wasn't what Ajax was planning, but the possibility of letting him escape.

Finally... finally, she'd found her enemy.

And since he was aware of her presence, letting him escape now meant it would be extremely uncertain whether she could find him again.

At the very least, she couldn't imagine finding him again so easily.

Above all, right now, skilled allies like Rei were lending her their strength, but that was only because they were in Gilm.

If Ajax escaped from Gilm, asking if Rei and the others would pursue him... well, that would be quite difficult.

If his location were known, that would be one thing, but they'd have to search for Ajax throughout the Kingdom of Mireana... or even the surrounding nations.

And there's no guarantee Ajax would stay put in one place. There's even the possibility he'd arrive after Rei and the others had moved on.

Exchanging information would require messages or letters.

While summoners and tamers could potentially deliver messages quickly, that would require a significant fee.

At the very least, it wasn't an amount Ilse, a low-rank adventurer, could casually afford.

Considering all that, letting Ajax escape here would make finding him again extremely difficult. Given Ilse's abilities, it simply wasn't realistic.

In other words, Ilse absolutely could not let Ajax escape here.

It was practically impossible to confront Ajax under better conditions than the current situation.

"Rest assured. Ajax needs to transport the kidnapped people. That will inevitably draw attention, and abandoning them would be difficult for them."

For Ajax and his group, leaving the kidnapped women behind in Gilm was something they absolutely wanted to avoid.

Dahl and his group probably spent a considerable amount on that operation, though the exact sum was unknown.

Given that, it's certain they'll try to retrieve those women by any means possible.

"But in a situation where danger is imminent to oneself..."

"Of course, that possibility exists. That's why I've made arrangements. Even if they tried to leave Gilm now, getting out through the main gate would be difficult at the very least. I've made sure the guards have the information too."

Hearing those words, Ilse finally felt relieved.

That said, this only applied to exiting through the main gate. If you asked whether Ajax, who was already forcibly abducting people, would actually try to walk out of Gilm openly and brazenly, the answer was no.

Still, Ilse was undoubtedly relieved by those words.

At the very least, knowing they couldn't leave through the main gate was one reassuring factor.

"Anyway, please rest assured. I can't promise absolutely... but I won't do anything to let Ajax and the others escape from Gilm. If anything happens over there again, I'll let you know immediately."

"...I beg you."

Ultimately, with nothing else she could do herself, Ilse could only bow her head to Dahl like that.

Dahl understood that, too. He nodded firmly, as if to calm Ilse.

Watching him now, Ilze felt certain he wouldn't let Ajax escape.

Rei watched this side of Dahl with a slightly suspicious look.

To be clear, it wasn't that he suspected Dahl had betrayed the intelligence unit to side with Ajax... it was simply that his demeanor felt suspicious.

"Yes, leave it to me. Whatever we do, it ultimately depends on Ajax and his group making a move. We've got them well covered."

Ilse smiled reassuringly as she said this, and for the moment, Rei and the others said nothing more about the matter.

"Elena must be lonely, huh?"

"Ah... Yeah. But walking around openly with Elena is tricky for all sorts of reasons."

Marina said, seemingly trying to change the subject. Rei agreed and replied.

True to his words, Rei's expression showed he felt somewhat bad about Elena.

For Elena now, dinner with the Crimson Wings crew was the most enjoyable time of her day.

Though strictly speaking, it would be more accurate to say it was her meal with Rei, not with the Crimson Wings.

"I'm sorry, for my sake..."

The one wearing an apologetic expression was, naturally, Ilse, who had brought up this whole incident.

But Marina, who had started the conversation, shook her head at Ilse.

"There's no need to worry that much. Ilse, you spoke with Elena, didn't you? Even if she feels sorry about her situation, she doesn't blame others for it. ...Though, if it were blatant, that would be a different story."

Marina's gaze flicked toward Vihera for a moment, likely because Vihera had monopolized Rei's time before. ...Though it was monopolization through mock battles, not anything suggestive.

"What?"

Whether she understood that or not.

In any case, Vihera asked while chewing on a piece of dried fish as Marina spoke.

This dried fish wasn't the river fish commonly eaten around Gilm... it was dried sea fish.

Naturally, in Gilm, far from the sea, sea fish commanded a hefty price. But for Dahl... no, for the Intelligence Corps, this incident presented a golden opportunity to secure Rei's cooperation.

As it was, the intelligence unit was already busy dealing with various factions secretly maneuvering due to the current Gilm expansion project, and others arriving in Gilm to gather information.

In such circumstances, the prospect of a major disturbance erupting was certain. Having not only a powerhouse like Rei, but also top-tier forces like Marina and Vihera—who could be called super-elite, surpassing even the best—agreeing to cooperate meant that spending the equivalent of dried sea fish was no problem.

The intelligence unit was already quite well-funded by nature.

This was because Daskar understood the value of information, which is why the intelligence unit was allocated a substantial budget.

...Though, that's probably only possible because it's a city like Gilm, located on the frontier.

Ordinary towns simply don't have that kind of financial leeway.

"Oh, this is delicious."

"It was brought from Emoshion. They say the fish there is quite tasty."

Dahl replied to Vihera's words.

"True. But in Emoshion, while seafood is plentiful, meat products seem to be in relatively short supply, right? Of course, there are mountains near Emoshion too, but it'd be tough to satisfy everyone living there with just that."

"Ah... Come to think of it, meat was a bit pricey there, wasn't it?"

Rei muttered, recalling his previous trip to Emoshion, though his overall impression was still that it was somewhat overpriced.

At the very least, considering the cost of eating seafood in Gilm, it was financially more advantageous there. But...

"That meat was mostly just regular animal meat, not the top-grade stuff you get in Gilm."

Generally, monster meat becomes tastier as the monsters become higher-ranked and possess stronger magical power.

And in the frontier town of Gilm, there's no shortage of such monsters.

This isn't about which is superior; it's simply a matter of the land.

With these thoughts in mind, Rei enjoyed his meal until Ajax and the others began their movements.
