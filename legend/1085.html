"Oh, you two made it. I've been waiting."

It was the alchemist Azimov who called out to Rei and Pamidor.

He was about to head off to his research on how to cook goblin meat deliciously today, but Pamidol had come to meet Rei.

The spear was finally complete, and he'd come to collect it.

It had been quite some time since Rei had asked him to make the spear.

He had given him the Magic Sword possessed by Noiz, the hammer wielded by a rare cyclops species, and after returning from the dark elf settlement, the branches, sap, and leaves of the World Tree Marina had given him as payment. He had also handed over various other small items. Finally, the spear was complete.

Azimov must have poured his energy into finishing it too, his eyes were ringed with dark circles.

...Yet he was still brimming with motivation, likely because the spear he'd spent so much time and effort on was finally complete.

Under normal circumstances, the materials Rei had provided would have been sufficient for a top-tier item alone.

Materials valuable enough to live comfortably for years... no, even decades if sold.

World Tree materials, in particular, were extremely rare, and beyond branches, there were also leaves and sap.

These are items that are simply impossible to obtain under normal circumstances.

Even merchants trading with Dark Elf settlements would find them difficult to acquire.

Given the opportunity to use such a variety of rare materials, even Azimov, with his stubborn personality, couldn't help but be enthusiastic.

Even so, the various rare materials Rei brought weren't something Azimov could master easily, and it had taken him quite a while to get this far.

Pamidor, who had been working alongside Rei on the new spear and worried about Azimov's near-constant state of sleeplessness, had been dropping by daily to check on him and sometimes bringing food... yet even so, he now looked like he'd turned into an undead creature.

"Hey, hey. Are you really okay? You look even worse than when I came yesterday! Shouldn't you get some more sleep?"

"I'm fine. Once I give the spear to Rei, I'll hibernate for about three days."

"No, you're human. You can't hibernate. Besides, it's summer now."

Pamidor said this with a slightly exasperated tone, but his face still bore the air of a great bandit chieftain. To an outsider, it could only look like he was intimidating a sickly man.

(Should I say they're a perfect match, or a mismatched pair... Well, they're definitely a good team, no doubt about it.)

Watching their exchange, Rei seemed strangely convinced. Perhaps sensing Rei's gaze, Azimov opened his mouth, looking displeased.

"Come on, get inside. I'll show you my masterpiece."

Rei and Pamidor followed Azimov as he entered the house.

But even from behind, Azimov's gait swayed dangerously, looking extremely precarious to watch.

"Hey, are you really okay?"

"Well, what can I say... I've warned him about it many times before, but when he focuses on one thing, he just can't help..."

Pamidor murmured worriedly, speaking only to his friend and business partner. ...Though to an outsider, he probably just looked like a master thief plotting something.

"But hey, like I said earlier, once this job's done, you'll probably sleep like a log for a while. So right now, just focus on accepting my magic spear."

Though worried about Azimov, Pamidor's tone held strong confidence.

He'd left the final touches to Azimov, but the preliminary stage... when he forged the spear, he could declare it the finest weapon he'd ever crafted. That confidence was only natural.

Watching that confident smile, Rei and Pamidor arrived at Azimov's laboratory.

Naturally, Azimov entered the room first.

He turned to Rei and Pamidor, smiling, and declared in a voice brimming with confidence.

"This is my greatest masterpiece, the magic spear... the Spear of Twilight!"

Pointing ahead, Azimov revealed a spear... no, a magic spear.

Its form wasn't particularly complex. While the shaft had a bit more carving than a spear you'd find in a regular shop, it was only a little more.

The unusual part was that something was also carved into the tip.

Normally, such carving on the tip would weaken its strength, but the magic spear before Rei, named the Spear of Twilight, was different.

Its appearance was not vastly different from a spear sold in a regular weapons shop, yet its very presence was distinct.

It possessed a magnetic charm, a magnetism that seemed to draw the eyes of those who saw it.

It possessed that peculiar power that only a truly exceptional item wields over those who behold it.

But... despite being captivated by the spear, Rei spoke.

"Something... is missing?"

"Yes, exactly!"

Azimov shouted in agreement at Rei's muttered words.

"...Ah. Just looking at this magic spear, I can tell it's a masterpiece. Yet, I also understand that something is missing."

Pamidor, seemingly sharing Rei's thoughts, murmured while gazing at the Twilight Spear... then glared at Azimov with a vicious expression.

"Hey, Azimov. What's going on? You didn't make a magic item this powerful with half-assed craftsmanship, did you?"

If so, I absolutely won't forgive you. As Pamidor clenched his fists, Azimov smiled and spoke with complete confidence.

"Calm down, Pamidor. There's no way I'd cut corners with such top-tier materials."

Under normal circumstances, he might have been intimidated by Pamidor's attitude, but Azimov seemed utterly unfazed, perhaps holding absolute confidence in the Twilight Spear.

Seeing Azimov like this, Pamidor must have decided there was something to it. He urged him on with his gaze.

"As you two noticed, this Spear of Twilight isn't finished yet. ...Oh, I'm still in the middle of explaining."

Rei tried to interject at the words "not yet complete," but Azimov cut him off and continued his explanation.

Perhaps because he was explaining the magic item he had created, his gaze turned toward Rei with more eloquence than usual.

"The final touch for this Twilight Spear... that is for Rei to channel magic power into it."

"...Magic power? Like when activating a magic item?"

"Yes. By imbuing it with Rei's magic in that way, the Twilight Spear will be truly complete."

With a beaming smile, Rei delivered those words. Feeling Pamidor's gaze, Rei quietly moved forward to stand before the Twilight Spear... and reached out.

"By the way, the reason the spear glows a pale green right now is because we used leaves and sap from the World Tree."

"...Sounds like bread."

Somehow, Azimov's words made him recall the sight of vegetable juice being squeezed into bread dough or noodle dough.

Perhaps finding Rei's remark unexpectedly amusing, not only Azimov but even Pamidor struggled to suppress their laughter.

Once the urge to laugh subsided, Rei once again channeled magic into the Twilight Spear he was touching... and it pulsed, thudding, just like when he first entered the Dark Elf settlement.

The difference from before was that neither Azimov nor Pamidor could sense that pulsation.

Only Rei, holding the Twilight Spear, could sense its pulse.

But... the next anomaly was something even Azimov and Pamidor could clearly see.

Because the Twilight Spear Rei held began to turn red.

Twilight... True to the word signifying the lingering glow of sunset, the spear was dyed red, vermilion, crimson by Rei's magic.

Yes, as if befitting Rei's epithet, "Crimson."

When the Twilight Spear's color had fully changed, what remained was a spear with red as its base color. Perhaps influenced by the World Tree material used in crafting the Twilight Spear, a pattern resembling green vines entwined around the spear's handle emerged. At the spearhead, a pattern of green vines also appeared, as if covering the original decoration.

"This is..."

The sense of incompleteness he had felt when he first saw the Twilight Spear. That feeling was now completely gone.

Rei understood that this state was the moment the magic spear truly worthy of being called the Twilight Spear was complete.

"It's complete."

As if confirming Rei's thoughts, Azimov murmured.

A satisfied smile spread across his face.

A smile that conveyed his absolute conviction that his work had been completed in the most perfect form possible.

In fact, Azimov's belief was not mistaken.

The magic spear in Rei's hand was undoubtedly a first-rate magic item.

"...What are this magic spear's abilities?"

Rei asked only the essentials, and Azimov opened his mouth with a satisfied smile.

"First and foremost, anyone wounded by this spear will heal more slowly. It doesn't make healing impossible, just difficult. Recovery is still possible with high-level healing magic or high-quality potions. That's the effect of the hammer you brought."

"But didn't that hammer grant its wielder regenerative abilities? Isn't that completely opposite to preventing an opponent's wounds from healing?"

"Everything has two sides. A good effect can become a bad one when reversed. Well, that's how it is."

Though he didn't grasp the exact mechanics, Rei understood the advantage of slowing an opponent's healing.

"If it comes down to a war of attrition, that's a pretty advantageous effect. ...So, specifically, how much harder does it make healing?"

"It's not fixed. It depends on various factors: how much magic power you infuse into the Twilight Spear, how resistant the opponent is to magic, both of your physical conditions, the location of the battle, and so on."

"...The effect not being consistent makes it a bit tricky to use. I guess I should just think of it as getting lucky if the effect is strong. ...Anything else?"

There must be other abilities, right? As if certain of this, Rei's question prompted Azimov to continue his explanation.

"Rei, you primarily use the spear as a throwing weapon, right?"

"Yeah."

"Now, throwing spears are usually disposable... but not this Twilight Spear. After you throw it and hit an enemy, it returns to you."

"That... is quite a convenient function."

Rei's specialty: long-range spear throwing.

As Azimov mentioned, spears are fundamentally disposable.

But if this explanation is true, then after throwing this spear, it would immediately return to his hand.

At Rei's remark about its convenience, Azimov smiled... but then opened his mouth to caution.

"Just one thing I want you to be careful about. As you've probably felt yourself, Rei, this Twilight Spear is powerful. ...You could even say it's too powerful. Especially when thrown, it still has the effect of hindering an enemy's recovery from injuries."

"So, depending on the situation, sometimes the disposable spears we've used before might be better than the Twilight Spear?"

"That's right. As for that balance, you'll have to get used to it yourself by using it."

"...Well then, I'd like to try using it a bit first... How about the backyard of this house?"

"It's not wide enough for spear throws. I'll come with you, so if you want to test it, you'd be better off using the guild's training grounds."

Hearing this, Rei and his companions headed straight for the guild.

...Along with Azimov, who had dark circles under his eyes.

When Rei arrived at the guild's training grounds, he naturally drew attention from those around him.

(If Set were here, he'd probably be getting even more attention.)

A fleeting image of his partner, who had split off to play with the children, crossed Rei's mind.

Though, to be fair, the one currently drawing all the stares was Pamidor.

"Hey, there's this guy with a seriously villainous look about him."

"...Whoa, you're right. But since nobody's doing anything, maybe he's not actually a villain?"

"What? You guys don't know? That guy's Pamidor, one of the best blacksmiths in Gilm!"

"A blacksmith!? ...Wow. Doesn't suit him at all."

Amidst such conversations, Rei, Azimov, and Pamidor moved to a secluded spot to prepare.

Their target was a dummy used for archery practice.

Starting with that dummy, they positioned themselves about ten meters away. The Misty Ring drew the Twilight Spear.

Rustle. The others, seeing the Twilight Spear, began to stir.

It was only natural. Even those without magic-sensing abilities could tell just by looking that it possessed a quality—or perhaps prestige—that marked it as a top-tier magic item.

Still, it was fairly well known that Rei collected magic items usable in actual combat, much like collecting magic stones. Above all, it made sense that a skilled adventurer like Rei would possess such a magic item.

Under such gazes... Rei hurls the Twilight Spear, infused with magic power.

With a sound that sliced through the air, it flew straight toward its target... obliterating the doll's head before vanishing itself, only to reappear in Rei's hand the very next instant.
