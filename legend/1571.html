He had every reason to be confident.

That was what Rei thought when he saw the man charging straight at him.

It wasn't that Rei had completely let down his guard against the man.

After all, why would someone who intended to oppose him act so casually?

Still, the wariness Rei felt toward the man wasn't that strong.

Rei's prediction was slightly off... but that didn't make Rei feel panic or despair. Instead, it even gave him a sense of anticipation.

His running speed was fast, but not so fast that it was impossible to follow with the eye.

But seeing how he ran with almost no loss of balance made it clear the man's skill wasn't just talk.

"Shah!"

A short sword thrust forward with a sharp exhale.

He aimed straight for the torso, not the face or neck, likely because it was the largest target and easiest to hit.

It wasn't meant to kill with one strike. It was an attack designed to immobilize the target before finishing them off.

Yet even witnessing this, Elena and the others showed no particular reaction.

It wasn't that they were frozen by the unexpected turn of events... they remained still precisely because there was no need to move.

As if to prove this, Rei took a step back while swinging the leg encased in Sleipnir Boots.

The next moment, the surrounding air filled with the sound of flesh crushing and bones snapping.

A moment later, the sound of the man's dagger hitting the ground.

『……』

Caught off guard by the unexpected turn of events, those who had agreed with the man's words couldn't utter a single syllable, and silence filled the air.

All that could be heard was the rustling of leaves stirred by the wind blowing through the forest, and the cries of birds, beasts, or monsters.

"No doubt he's skilled, but ultimately, just skilled. Not quite beyond imagination..."

Judging from Rei's impression after facing him, his skill was only marginally above Byune's.

(Thinking about it that way, Byune's skill level has been improving too, somehow.)

Though not as intensely as in winter, Byune still undergoes combat training.

Furthermore, by acting as a member of the Crimson Wings—a group of highly skilled fighters—she can constantly observe the combat of those possessing exceptional strength up close.

Being able to observe mock battles between individuals like Vihera and Rei up close is an incredibly valuable learning experience.

And now, Elena has joined them as well.

...Marina also possesses high combat prowess, but since she primarily specializes in spirit magic and bow attacks, she doesn't offer Byune quite the same level of benefit.

These thoughts flashed through Rei's mind in an instant. The man whose wrist bones had been shattered froze from the shock and pain, but he immediately began moving again.

His method of deliberately ignoring the pain proved extremely useful in a situation like this.

His actions were driven by the determination to defeat Rei at any cost.

But... even if he could ignore the pain, it inevitably affected his physical movements.

His speed toward Rei was definitely slower than before, and the impact of the earlier blow was evident in his movements.

The man surely knew this too, but having gone that far with Ryutas, he absolutely could not give up just because he'd taken a hit from Rei.

Closing the distance to Rei, he swung his unarmed left hand wide to the side.

In that instant, several long needles shot out from it.

(An assassin?)

Thinking that, Rei instinctively used his Dragon Robe to defend against the needles.

Crafted from dragon scales and hide, the Dragon Robe offered formidable defense against physical attacks.

Every single needle fired was easily blocked by the Dragon Robe.

Had Rei been standing anywhere else, he wouldn't have even attempted to block the needles; he would have dodged them.

But behind where Rei stood now were Elena and the others.

Of course, with Elena and the others' abilities, it was obvious they wouldn't be affected by such an attack. Still, considering the possibility of the worst-case scenario, Rei couldn't bring himself to dodge the long needles.

As expected, the man's strike had been calculated, anticipating that Rei would take such action.

And now that Rei had made a move to protect his back, it was the perfect opportunity.

"Nuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuu

The man had served as Ryutas's bodyguard for many years.

No, to be precise, it was more accurate to call him a guard for the Ryutasu clan.

Precisely because of that, even though he was a guard... no, precisely because he was a guard, he fully understood how an assassin would act.

An excellent guard could also become an excellent assassin.

At least in this man's clan, they underwent training as assassins to gain an assassin's perspective.

For a man like that, killing someone even with one hand immobilized would be easy.

...However, that was only true if the opponent wasn't Rei.

No, even Elena and the others wouldn't have found it particularly difficult to deal with.

In any case, the man's scheme was easily crushed by Rei's fist.

"Guh!"

Rei deflected the hand reaching for his throat with his left hand, then thrust his right fist forward.

A perfect counter strike landed, and the man was blown away, his jaw shattered by the unexpectedly powerful punch.

His body slammed into the cave wall, and the impact knocked him unconscious.

"...Well, I suppose that's the end of that for now? Since you lot showed yourselves in agreement with that guy, does that mean you intend to fight me?"

He drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring and readied it.

That last time was bare-handed, but if they come at me next, I'll use a weapon—the Death Scythe—to deal with them.

Seeing Rei's decisive action, the men did nothing further and quietly retreated.

Even the most skilled among them couldn't defeat Rei with his bare hands.

Facing such a monster, even with weapons and enhanced strength, they knew they stood absolutely no chance.

Beyond that, seeing the man defeated... and defeated so easily, almost as an afterthought, made many of them conclude that the person before them was in a different league altogether.

"I see. Honesty is the best policy. ...Well then, Ryutas. Now that the others have been subdued, I suppose it's time to hear what you have to say."

"...No, I knew you were strong, but not that strong..."

Ryuutasu, addressed by Rei, could only mutter in stunned silence.

Having served as his bodyguard for years, he naturally knew the extent of the man's abilities.

He had also gathered ample information about Rei's own abilities.

Even so, he probably never imagined the gap in their abilities would be this vast.

There was a huge difference between knowing something intellectually and actually seeing that power confirmed right before your eyes.

"True. But it was Jaya who came looking for trouble with the guild we belong to. ...Well, even without this incident, we probably would have gotten Jaya's information sooner or later."

Mejogo was a city that was essentially one giant entertainment district.

Naturally, rumors about it have already spread far and wide. It wouldn't be surprising if someone in Gilm knew about Mejogo, even if Rei didn't.

And then there were the giants.

If there are large numbers of giants, all with the same face and standing about three meters tall, that is something that cannot be hidden forever.

In fact, even in this country of Reburina, when they attacked the Resistance, they couldn't completely annihilate their forces, and the matter of the giants is known.

And completely sealing off information is extremely difficult.

At the very least, it was impossible for just the organization Jaya and the small nation of Reburina to completely seal off information about the giants.

"That may be true. But I really didn't want to get into a fight with Gilm... with the Kingdom of Mireana."

"From what I've heard, many can't accept being a vassal state. If that's the case, it was inevitable we'd fight eventually, right? ...Though winning solely with this country's forces would be difficult."

Indeed, if the giants Rei and his group fought were facing ordinary soldiers of the Kingdom of Mireana, they could likely trample them.

But there were skilled fighters among the soldiers, and combat specialists like knights.

And above all, high-rank adventurers could undoubtedly handle any number of giants.

Having fought giants directly, Rei could say that with certainty.

"Yeah. Honestly, I don't really believe it's possible either. But the higher-ups genuinely think it is."

"...The higher-ups, huh. I heard them call you 'youngster' earlier too. I think it's about time you revealed your status."

At Rei's words, Ryutasu flashed a look of momentary confusion... the wind blowing through the forest ruffled his hair.

Several seconds passed before Ryutas finally grasped what Rei meant and spoke.

"Ah, I see. You didn't know who I was yet? ...Well, since you have a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname like Rei, I just assumed you already knew my background."

Ryutas laughed brightly, "Ahahaha."

Of course, Rei didn't think Ryutas was just a merchant either.

But at the same time, he didn't know exactly what kind of person he was either.

"So? Who the hell are you, anyway?"

At Rei's words, Elena and the others, who had been silently watching the exchange from behind, also turned their gaze toward Ryutas.

Up until now, they'd assumed it would be quicker to leave things to Rei since they were acquainted, rather than risk meddling themselves. But depending on the status of this man calling himself Ryutas, they judged it might be necessary to intervene.

"Right. Maybe explaining it this way to Rei and the others would be quickest? One of the sons of the man who leads Jaya."

The words that came from Ryutas' mouth were somewhat expected, yet still astonishing.

Being called "Young Master" and having this many guards meant he couldn't just be some common merchant, and it was unlikely he was merely one of Jaya's executives.

But hearing Ryutas state it so boldly from his own mouth was still unexpected.

"You're pretty bold saying that to us, who are enemies of Jaya. Didn't you think you'd be killed?"

"Hmm, I figured if we surrendered quietly, we'd probably be fine. Based on how things went when we last made contact."

The way he said it without any particular hesitation made it seem like Ryutas genuinely believed that.

At least, that's how it seemed to Rei.

Of course, whether that was truly his belief was something Rei couldn't know, unable to read Ryutas's mind.

"Well, whatever. So why would someone like that surrender so easily?"

"Because he can't beat you guys, of course. And... what Dad's doing just looks like he's charging headlong down a path to self-destruction."

From Ryutasu's use of "Father," it was clear he meant the person leading Jaya.

(So there must be power struggles within Jaya too. ...Though whether those struggles ever surface publicly is another matter.)

Jaya is a shadowy organization cooperating with the Kingdom of Reburina.

No, Rei even considered the possibility that it might actually be a direct branch of the Reburina Kingdom.

Given that, even if Ryutas were to oppose Jaya, it's doubtful that would cause an internal split.

"So? Your father is trying to destroy himself, yet you, his son, won't take any action?"

"Of course, I've said that many times. But unfortunately, he shows no sign of accepting it whatsoever."

Ryuutas sighed as he said this, and to Rei, he truly seemed disappointed.

"I see. ...Well, never mind that. Tell me about this facility instead. The giants shipped from Mejogo's underground facility come here, right? How many are there now?"

"Right. I don't know the exact number, but it's over a thousand."

"...I figured there'd be that many."

Given the number of prostitutes in Mejogo, he'd easily imagined that many.

No, one could even say a thousand was too few.

(Even so, it's certain that over a thousand women forced into prostitution have died, right?)

Suppressing the anger welling up inside him, Rei pondered what to ask next.
