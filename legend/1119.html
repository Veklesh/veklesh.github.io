"Seeking personnel to dismantle Sand Lizardmen"

After finishing an early lunch with Ryu Brand at the lord's mansion, Rei placed a request at the guild to recruit personnel for dismantling Sand Lizardmen, as per his original plan.

Responding to Rei was a single receptionist.

...She was the receptionist who had looked after Rei when he first came to the guild, and circumstances had led her to become his assigned contact.

Though she'd been somewhat forced into the role by those around her, she'd come to realize Rei wasn't the truly vicious person he was rumored to be. Now, she was able to interact with him somewhat... well, just a little bit more casually.

It seemed she'd decided to set aside the incident where Rei had caused a bit of a ruckus when he first arrived at the guild.

Also, becoming Rei's assigned attendant meant she received a special allowance, which was a big plus. But what pleased the receptionist most was probably that the number of men trying to hit on her out of fear of Rei had decreased.

Receptionists at the guild are generally chosen for their good looks.

Naturally, this means they get hit on a lot by adventurers. However, the receptionist assigned to Rei was someone who was, to put it mildly, not very skilled at handling men.

This had caused several incidents before, so in that sense, she was almost grateful to Rei for reducing the advances from other adventurers.

"Yes, how many do you need?"

"Ten or more."

"...Ah, now that you mention it, when you helped the Sand Claw members, you defeated about that many Sand Lizardmen, didn't you? The guild is also grateful to have had such a promising new recruit assisted."

Hearing about ten or more Sand Lizardmen, she must have recalled the story of the Sand Claw led by Howl. The receptionist bowed deeply.

"No, I was happy to defeat an unknown monster myself, and it's really no big deal."

"Not at all! As I just said, Sand Claw is a promising newcomer in Gosh!"

Is that so? Rei tilted his head.

He'd heard that before, but hadn't the promising newcomer been taken by Zarust to serve as Augusto's escort?

He thought about it, then immediately shook his head.

(It's not like those guys are the only promising newcomers. Besides, he took whoever volunteered to come as an escort. Some probably didn't want to be hired by Augusto and get noticed by Daldora or the Elema Trading Company.)

In fact, considering that, it was surprising anyone even wanted to escort with Zarust that much... While pondering this, Rei filled in the necessary details on the request form the receptionist had handed him.

Job details, reward, and... just as he thought that, Rei asked the receptionist again.

"I heard that when dismantling a Gosh, you need to rent a special warehouse somewhere. How does that work?"

"Well... You could arrange your own, Rei, or we could lend you guild storage for an additional fee. However..."

The receptionist trailed off.

It was already fairly well-known that Rei was on good terms with Augusto.

Given that, those who didn't want to draw the ire of Daldora or the Elema Trading Company were likely to find various excuses to refuse renting out their warehouses.

Of course, there were also those aligned with Augusto, so it wasn't entirely impossible to secure one...

"Well, I hate to say it like this, but whether anyone would rent to someone as close to Augusto as Rei is... It might be better to rent from the guild itself, even if it costs a bit more."

Rei knew about the guild's warehouse, having used it before when dismantling the Sand Serpent.

According to Zarust, who had rented that facility, it was the best in terms of having all the necessary functions.

(Well, I suppose there's also the matter of the guild's reputation...)

Adventurers handle the monster dissection, and the guild buys the materials and magic stones stripped from them.

Given that dynamic, Rei understood that from the guild's perspective, lacking adequate facilities would be a blow to their reputation.

"Alright then, let's do it."

The warehouse he'd used before had worked perfectly fine, and even if the cost was a bit higher, it wasn't a problem for Rei since he wasn't exactly strapped for cash.

Especially since selling the materials obtained from dissecting the Sand Lizardman he'd requested this time would cover the cost immediately.

"Yes, understood. Um... this should cover it, right? Anything else?"

Hearing the receptionist's words, Zarust's face flashed through Rei's mind.

During the Sand Serpent dismantling, Zarust had kept watch, so no one had tried to pocket any materials. This time, however, he would be hiring adventurers he didn't know.

If he didn't want the materials taken without permission, Rei could just watch over them himself. But he also wanted to use that time more effectively.

No, wait. Since he was posting this as a request for that very reason, there was no point in him being there anyway.

"If possible, I'd like the guild to hire a trusted adventurer as a supervisor. Or a guild employee would be fine too."

"Understood. Normally we wouldn't go this far, but with over ten Sand Lizardmen, I can see why you're concerned, Rei."

"Huh?"

Rei let out a surprised gasp at the receptionist's words.

Based on Rei's actual combat experience, Sand Lizardmen weren't particularly strong opponents.

He'd thought they were the kind of enemy that could be handled with a certain level of strength.

(Well, Lizardmen fight in coordination, so she must be talking about that... No, wait. Come to think of it, most adventurers in Gosh aren't that strong to begin with. ...Ah, I see. That explains it.)

As a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom, and one where much of its territory was desert, its national power was significantly lower compared to neighboring countries.

Its population is naturally small, so it stands to reason that the number of powerful adventurers would be low too.

"Rei-san?"

The receptionist shot Rei a puzzled look when he suddenly let out a strange sound.

But Rei shook his head as if to say it was nothing before speaking.

"I don't know if it'll work, but I want it clearly stated that I'll take a firm stance against anyone who tries to embezzle materials."

"A firm stance...?"

Knowing Rei's capabilities, the receptionist's expression twisted.

Still, rejecting this request here risked an adventurer who later stole materials becoming useless, so she carefully added the clause to the request form.

The adventurer known by the alias Crimson was the client for this dismantling request, and if materials were embezzled, appropriate action would be taken.

...Several adventurers who overheard Rei and the receptionist's conversation grimaced at the implication.

Several days had passed since Rei arrived in Gosh. In just those few days, information about Rei's immense power had spread far and wide.

The adventurers who had guarded Augusto alongside Zarust, those who witnessed adventurers who had provoked Rei at the guild being swiftly beaten down, and the report that he single-handedly slaughtered a group of Sand Lizardmen from the Claws of Sand, led by Howl.

Considering the stories told by such individuals, it was clear just how formidable Rei truly was.

Moreover, though the guild members were unaware, the skilled adventurers guarding Daldora also understood Rei's strength.

Even so, some had only been in Gosh for a few days and didn't know much about Rei.

For those individuals, there was a risk of incurring Rei's wrath... That was the receptionist's concern.

(If anyone wants to take this job, they should observe the client carefully. We can't refuse the job itself, but we can warn trustworthy people to be cautious.)

If a Rank E adventurer were to accept a Rank B request, the guild could refuse the acceptance under its authority.

However, in this case, since it involved dismantling a Sand Lizardman, the rank required to accept the request was by no means high.

To reject it using the guild's authority, the person would have to be seriously problematic... someone who's caused numerous issues before.

To prevent embezzlement by someone who wasn't that problematic, it was still necessary to rely on someone trustworthy.

"Alright, then."

"Yes. So, what if there are multiple applicants?"

"Hmm, let me see. I'll leave that to the guild. They can weed out any suspicious characters there."

"Thank you. Then, please come to the guild tomorrow. If no one accepts the request, we'll try again the day after tomorrow."

"I'll count on you."

Leaving the receptionist bowing deeply behind, Rei departed the guild.

After Rei was gone, the adventurers in the guild focused their attention on his request.

The reason was simple: the reward was slightly higher than usual for such a request.

Adventurers had been waiting for it to be posted on the request board.

What was even luckier for the adventurers was that it was daytime.

Many adventurers were currently in the middle of jobs, so those present at the guild were either those who had planned to take the day off, those who couldn't make it to the guild in the morning for some reason,

Others were planning to eat at the tavern.

For that very reason, the competition for the request Rei brought wasn't particularly fierce.

In other words, it was a golden opportunity to earn a lot of money with minimal effort.

The adventurers waited, their eyes sharp as predators eyeing their prey.

Rei had just presented the adventurers left at the guild with the spark of a fight, but upon returning to Augusto's mansion, two figures stood before the house.

One was Günter, the gatekeeper, but the other was Augusto's wife, Kathy.

Kathy, who should normally be inside the house, was somehow outside.

The moment she spotted Rei and Set, she started running.

"You're late! I thought you'd be back by noon. Don't make me worry like that. ...Did something happen?"

Though she was yelling, Rei could tell right away that she was worried about him.

That's precisely why Rei bowed his head obediently.

"Sorry, I got invited to the lord's mansion."

"What?! ...Are you okay? They didn't do anything to you?"

"Yeah, I'm fine. We just had lunch together."

"...Lunch? With Count Salmas? ...Really?"

Kathy murmured, looking utterly incredulous.

She knew that Ryu Brand was on good terms with Daldora, who was at odds with her husband, Augusto.

Given that, it wasn't unreasonable for her to wonder if something had happened to Rei under the guise of a meal.

But to Cathy, who voiced such worries, Rei simply nodded reassuringly.

"See, Cathy, I told you you worry too much."

"But Günter..."

Even after Rei assured her it was fine, Kathy still seemed unconvinced and tried to argue back at Günter.

Watching the exchange from beside Rei, Set growled.

"Grr?"

It's okay, he seemed to say. Seeing Set's expression, Kathy flashed a momentary look of surprise... She didn't understand why his action had that effect, but it did calm her down.

"Heh heh, I suppose so. If Set-chan says it's okay, then it must be okay. ...Welcome back, Rei, Set-chan. So, you didn't end up going to the guild today after all?"

"No, I went. I properly submitted the request. If I go to the guild tomorrow morning, I think there'll be adventurers waiting to dissect the Sand Lizardman."

"I see. That's good news. ...But does that mean you'll be going to the guild again tomorrow?"

"Looks like it."

"I see..."

At Rei's words, Kathy's expression turned slightly sad.

She probably felt disappointed, having enjoyed taking care of Rei, to hear he'd be heading back to the guild tomorrow.

Feeling a bit uncomfortable seeing Cathy like this, Rei broke the silence.

"If there's any lunch left, I'd like to eat it."

"...Huh? Didn't you just have lunch with Count Salmas?"

"It's been a while since I ate, and I'm feeling a bit peckish."

"...Oh. Well then, I'll get it ready right away. Please come inside."

Saying that, Rei followed Cathy as she entered the house.

Set, too, headed straight for the stables without Rei needing to say anything.

And so, Rei managed to successfully gloss over the conversation.
