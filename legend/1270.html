"A dagger?"

"Yes. With this Silver Lion's claw, it could be shaped into a dagger with a little work. And for the hilt... well, if we grind the Silver Lion's bones into powder and refine the metal, it'll be considerably sturdier than usual."

At Pamidor's words, Rei turned his gaze to the silver lion's claws.

Though he had divided the claws, Rei still held over ten of them in his hands.

Normally, even if someone suggested making a dagger from a claw, the blade length would be insufficient when considered as a dagger.

But in this case, they were claws from a silver lion boasting a massive frame. They possessed more than enough size to make a dagger.

"Well, Azimov is busy with the Sleipnir Boots matter, so we can only get minimal help. That's precisely why we're making daggers that utilize the material's inherent form... What do you think?"

"Yeah, I see. What should I do..."

Laying his gaze on the silver lion's claw, Rei contemplated what to do.

Rei rarely uses daggers in his fighting style.

With the Misty Ring, he could pull a weapon from his hand at any moment, even in sudden danger.

Moreover, since he also possessed a knife made of mithril, he had no trouble with disassembly. ...Though, to be honest, for regular disassembly, he just used a cheap knife bought somewhere.

Furthermore, Rei possessed the Nebula Eye, a magic item that could create arrowheads simply by channeling magic power, making it perfectly usable as a close-range weapon.

Considering all that, a dagger made from the Silver Lion's claw isn't strictly necessary.

Moreover, since Azimov is busy, the dagger Pamidor mentioned making is not a magic item, but a dagger in the purest sense.

Considering all that...

"Understood. Then, I'll leave it to you."

Rei declared.

A dagger crafted by the skilled Pamidor using the Silver Lion's Claw would undoubtedly be a masterpiece.

Having one wouldn't hurt, and since Rei already carried the Misty Ring, it wouldn't take up extra space.

Considering all that, he decided it was definitely better to have it made.

"I see!"

Pamidor shouted, his face beaming with delight at Rei's words.

Given Pamidor's menacing appearance, to an uninformed observer, that smile looked like that of a bandit—no, a great bandit chieftain—who had found his target and was flashing a ferocious grin.

Having seen his friend Azimov craft magic items using Silver Lion materials, Pamidor was undoubtedly envious.

Though not an alchemist himself, that didn't mean Pamidor couldn't craft weapons or armor using monster materials.

...Of course, they wouldn't be magic items like those made by an alchemist like Azimov, but he was confident he could craft weapons of the highest quality... or even better.

"But. I can't just ask for several right away. Make one first. If it looks good, I'll order a few more."

Though not meant for a master craftsman—especially one currently possessing the finest skills within the Gilm—Pamidor nodded without seeming particularly displeased by Rei's words.

That said, seeing Pamidor so delighted at the prospect of crafting daggers from Silver Lion materials, most observers wouldn't question if they were told his imposing presence radiated murderous intent.

He was overjoyed to be able to use materials from an S-rank monster.

(Ah, come to think of it, I'm forming a party with Byune too. Maybe I could give this dagger to her.)

Rei, Marina, Vihera, and Byune were set to officially form a party once the handover to Walker was complete in the spring.

However, within that party, Byune alone inevitably lagged behind the other three in skill.

Of course, this doesn't mean Byune is simply bad at her job.

While she didn't possess any particularly outstanding abilities, considering the roles required of a thief—like disarming traps and reconnaissance skills—Byune wasn't top-tier, but she still easily met the required standard.

The problem lay with the other three... or rather, the three and one beast.

The others were truly top-tier individuals, making Byune inevitably seem inferior.

Whether she realizes it or not, she's trying hard: getting combat training from Vihera to avoid holding them back, reading books in the library to deepen her knowledge, buying locks sold for thief training and picking them...

But combat prowess doesn't improve overnight.

Well, at this point, she's reached a skill level where she can hold her own against higher-ranked thieves, but she's still nowhere near Rei and the others.

The quickest way to boost the strength of someone with low combat power was to upgrade the weapon they used to a higher-performance one.

Of course, if one repeatedly did that and came to rely solely on weapon performance, one's own combat skills wouldn't improve.

(I recall reading somewhere that even a Japanese sword breaks or chips easily when wielded by someone lacking skill. Of course, Byune wouldn't use a Japanese sword, but in terms of combat technique, the principle is the same.)

That concern wasn't entirely unfounded, but considering Byune's personality, it seemed unlikely she'd neglect improving her combat skills by relying solely on weapon performance.

"When will the dagger be ready?"

"No, it's just that we're forming a party once spring comes, and one member is Byune. Since Byune uses a dagger, I thought I'd have her use the one made from this Silver Lion's claw."

"Party!? Rei!?"

"Yeah. ...You don't have to be so surprised."

Rei muttered this, seeing Pamidor's genuinely shocked expression, but upon reflection, it was only natural.

After all, this was Rei—someone who'd always gone solo—saying he was forming a party.

Of course, it wasn't that Rei didn't want to form a party.

The reason Rei hadn't formed one until now was purely due to his own circumstances.

The cost of feeding Set was one thing, but the secret behind Set and Death Scythe absorbing magic stones—created through beast magic—was something he had to keep hidden as much as possible.

However, the members of this new party—Marina and Vihera—knew about Rei's secret.

Only Byune hasn't been told anything yet, but with Marina and Vihera in the know, it should be possible to somehow cover it up.

(Well, Byune tends to respond with just 'Hmm' when talking to people, so telling her the secret probably wouldn't be a problem... but her position is different.)

Right now, Rei and the others... well, more accurately, Byune is acting together with Vihera, but that's strictly temporary.

Eventually, she'll return to the Labyrinth City of Exil and live there.

Given that, she certainly couldn't carelessly reveal Rei's secret.

There are spells to seal someone's lips, but Rei didn't want to use such a spell on Byune if he could help it.

"No, I mean, I'm surprised. But still... Rei, huh. No, it's deeply moving."

Looking at Rei, Pamidor declared with genuine emotion, just as the words suggested.

To Pamidor, Rei was a special existence in many ways.

Still only in his mid-teens, he commanded a griffin as his familiar, wielded several powerful magic items unlike anything seen before, and possessed strength that could hold his own against, no, even surpass, a Rank A adventurer.

His power far exceeded his appearance, which was precisely why it made sense to Pamidor that Rei, who had always worked solo, would now form a party.

He would never say it aloud, but Pamidor thought of Rei as a son... well, that was a bit too close in age, so more like a younger brother.

That's precisely why the words "form a party" coming from Rei's mouth were accepted with genuine joy.

Adventurers typically operate in parties.

While some, like Rei, worked solo, the majority still operated in parties.

Especially out here in the frontier, going solo is just too difficult in many ways.

"I never imagined you'd be so happy about it... No, really."

Rei tilted his head at Pamidor's unexpectedly surprised and delighted expression.

He hadn't imagined she'd be this pleased...

"Well, from my perspective, Rei was pretty reckless in many ways. Thinking about it that way, if you form a party safely, I can watch over you with peace of mind. Besides, Rei has helped us out in many ways too."

Those words were not an excuse or anything like that.

After all, today had brought a major benefit: the ability to craft daggers using the Silver Lion's claws and bones.

"Anyway, forget about the party for now. I'm counting on you for the daggers."

"Yeah, leave it to me!"

Pamidor replied, unable to hide his delight at being able to freely use the Silver Lion's materials, flashing a grin that could only be described as ferocious.

His eyes were already fixed on the forge deep within the room.

Deciding he'd only be in Pamidor's way if he stayed, Rei handed over a slightly larger amount of claws and bones before leaving the shop.

Kumito had his fair share of opportunities to play with Set.
