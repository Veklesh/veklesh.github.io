The day after Rei and the others returned, the group set out once more.

Their movements this time were a stark contrast to the previous day, drawing wry smiles from many who knew the circumstances.

"Isn't this a bit too blatant?"

Sura, who led the resistance, voiced her concern.

Of course, Sura was glad to have the combat strength of Rei and the others.

But even so, having it displayed so blatantly in their attitude wasn't exactly amusing for someone leading the resistance.

"What can we do? It's a fact we're weaker than Rei and the others."

Sharia, riding in the same carriage as Sura, stated it matter-of-factly.

As a beastkin... or rather, due to Sharia's personality, she had a side that respected stronger opponents. In that sense, it was undeniable that Rei and the others were definitely above Sharia.

"I understand that part, you know. ...But understanding something and accepting it are two different things."

"Then why not try your best not to lose to Rei and the others?"

For Sharia, it was undeniable that she deeply admired becoming as strong as Rei and the others.

But it wasn't just admiration; she naturally also wanted to achieve that same level of strength herself.

Even Sura, who hadn't known Sharia for very long, seemed to understand those feelings.

Above all, considering yesterday's incident... and thinking about the future when Rei and the others might be gone, she inevitably reached the conclusion that they absolutely had to become stronger themselves.

(I knew we weren't strong. I knew it... but still, maybe winning that battle at Mejogo just naturally made me a bit conceited.)

Thinking about yesterday, Sura felt this anew. Sharia spoke up, trying to encourage her.

"Come on, don't be down. It's true we're weak now, but then we just need to get stronger. Or is it okay to stay weak?"

"Well..."

To begin with, the resistance currently led by Sura wasn't particularly strong to begin with.

The main force had been ambushed by a giant, losing nearly all their strength.

Many survivors were also broken by that battle... That's precisely why a young woman like Sura ended up leading the resistance.

But the current situation isn't so kind that we can just accept our weakness and wallow in it.

"That's not good."

"Right? Then we should focus on getting stronger, even just a little. After all, we're heading to Gilm... to the only frontier left in the Mireana Kingdom, aren't we? Rei and the others won't stay with us forever until we get there. You understand that, don't you?"

"That's true."

They'd heard about this situation many times before from Rei and the others.

In Gilm, where expansion work is currently underway, Rei and the others are a major force. Normally, they should have returned there already.

The reason Rei and the others are still traveling with Sura and the others must be because they can't fully entrust the defensive capabilities to the others just yet.

That's precisely why, from here on out, they'd be hiring adventurer escorts arranged by the Zolgee Trading Company at each destination.

Being tasked with guarding this group, it was impossible not to feel resentful.

"Then you know what you have to do, right? Fortunately, we'll be traveling by carriage, so even if you're a bit tired from resting, it shouldn't interfere with your guard duties. Besides... with Rei and the others present, there's no need to worry that much about the escort."

Sura nodded in response to Sharia's words.

"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAH!"

Sura screamed as she was blown away, rolling across the ground to slow her momentum.

"What's wrong? Is that all it takes to lead the Resistance? At this rate, the Resistance will all be monster fodder before we even reach Gilm. And it won't just be you guards getting eaten—the people you're supposed to be protecting will be too."

"Guh!"

Despite the pain, Sura groaned and used her spear as a cane to stand up, responding to Rei's words.

Normally, Sura was in a position to lead the resistance and had no need to be on the front lines.

But that was only under normal circumstances. The current Resistance had no luxury of leaving capable fighters idle.

It was as if they were saying, "Use anyone you can, even your own parents," and Sura herself was participating in combat training.

...For Sura, there was no doubt she wanted to boost the morale of the other Resistance members, even just a little, by being the first to participate in combat training as their leader.

"Not yet... not yet!"

Sura shouted, taking up her spear once more and charging toward Rei.

Around them, Rei's companions—Elena and the other women—along with the Resistance, the adventurer mercenaries hired for their reliable personalities... and even volunteers from among the former prostitutes who were normally just protected—all participated in the training.

Until now, the time between finishing lunch and departure had been a break period.

That's when they'd enjoy a nap after lunch or chat with friends from other squads.

But today, most weren't chatting. Their attention was riveted on the mock battle that had suddenly begun.

"Too naive. How do you plan to handle having your weapon taken so easily?"

Elena, wielding her longsword Mirage, swept the weapon into the air by wrapping it around the shaft of the thrusting spear.

"Your attack speed itself is too slow. Sharpness and such... that's something to work on after you can increase your strike speed a bit more... and after you can attack consecutively."

Vihera casually deflected the swung club with her gauntlet.

"See? A water strike is coming from behind you! Oh? You don't need to watch the ground beneath your feet? A wind strike is coming from above—how about that one?"

Marina dances, unleashing elemental magic attacks one after another against multiple opponents. Letting your guard down will undoubtedly lead to a painful lesson.

"Hmph."

Byune effortlessly dodged a spear strike, stepped inside her opponent's range, and in an instant thrust the blade of White Cloud against their throat.

Like this, everyone present was striving to improve their strength, engaging in mock battles whenever possible.

This gloomy atmosphere was undoubtedly influenced by yesterday's incident... The mere absence of Rei and the others had cast a shadow over the entire group.

Even though they were present, the feeling that they weren't relied upon as heavily as Rei and the others as escorts made the Resistance members and the adventurer escorts keenly aware of their own inadequacy. It was more than enough to spur them into action.

(They're putting up quite a fight.)

Dodging Sura's spear strike, Twilight Spear lightly swept at her feet.

Had Rei truly unleashed that strike with full force, Sura's leg would undoubtedly have been broken. But training didn't call for such severity, and Sura merely fell to the ground, escaping without serious injury.

"Guh... Haa, haa, haa..."

"Leave Sura be for now. Pushing yourself to the limit here is pointless. Just because you can rest in the carriage doesn't mean you should be unable to move quickly if something happens. That'd be bad."

Though dissatisfied with the words, Sura seemed to acknowledge the logic in them and walked away from Rei.

Rei watched Sura's retreating figure, then shifted his gaze to Sharia, who stepped forward to replace her.

"So? Next up is you, huh?"

"That's how it goes. ...You will fight me, right?"

Sharia fixed a gaze on Rei that demanded a real fight, not a mock battle or combat training.

To Sharia, Rei's presence was like a wall standing before her.

Of course, she didn't harbor hostility.

For some reason, her slave collar had come off, and when she was hiding in Mejogo, unsure what to do in that situation, Ray had given her food.

She felt gratitude, not resentment.

But... even so, Rei's presence was undeniably a wall for Sharia.

And Sharia is not a woman who would simply stand by and watch the wall before her.

From her beastman instincts, she wanted to fight Rei. And she wanted to win, deep down in her heart.

Though he probably didn't sense Sharia's feelings, Rei nodded after she sheathed the Twilight Spear in the Misty Ring.

"Understood. Then it's Sharia's turn next."

"I'll leave it to you."

Under normal circumstances, she would be furious that he chose to fight her bare-handed. But knowing Rei's strength, Sharia didn't think that.

After all, Rei had wielded the Twilight Spear because Sura used a spear as her weapon; he'd judged it would be more beneficial to fight with the same weapon.

Given that, it was only natural for Rei to fight bare-handed against Sharia, who was also unarmed.

Although Sharia had used a weapon during their battle at Mejogo, she was originally skilled in hand-to-hand combat.

Given that, challenging Rei unarmed was indeed the best course now.

"Come."

Rei muttered, still unarmed.

Sharia nodded in response, kicking off the ground to close the distance between herself and Rei.

Being a beastman, his speed was clearly faster than that of an ordinary human.

A speed that any adventurer hired as a bodyguard by Gime Kara would absolutely be unable to match.

Sharia's fist was aimed straight for his face, but Rei merely shifted his right side back slightly to evade the blow.

Sharia's fist passed just a few centimeters from his face, yet even witnessing this, Rei showed no sign of being particularly unsettled.

Instead, he launched a hook-like strike that grazed Sharia's jaw... but Sharia, knowing Rei's strength, never imagined her blow would decide the fight so easily. She managed to block Rei's strike with her left arm.

Had it connected to the jaw, it would have cleanly knocked him unconscious.

Having blocked it, Sharia instantly thought to exploit the opening and launch a counter-counter...

"Huh?"

Before she could land that strike, the blow she thought she'd blocked surged forward with even greater force, violently sending her body flying.

Even so, thanks to the beastkin's exceptional physical abilities, he regained his balance mid-air. He managed to land without rolling on the ground... but still couldn't completely stop his momentum, sliding several meters along the surface.

"Don't let your guard down just because you blocked one strike. Sometimes that one strike is more powerful than you expect. Honestly, my recommendation is still to dodge enemy attacks rather than block them. I put power into that last strike, but I pushed you back instead of punching you, so it didn't hurt that much, right?"

Rei wasn't lying. While Sharia's arm ached somewhat, it wasn't the kind of pain you'd feel from being properly punched and knocked back.

Normally, an impact like the one that sent her flying earlier would have undoubtedly caused significant damage.

But because Rei delivered a pushing strike instead of a punching one, it ended up being only this bad.

"Uh, yeah. I'm fine."

Saying that, Sharia checked the condition of her arm.

There was a slight dull ache, but it was a mild pain she was certain would fade within a few hours... or even a few minutes.

Besides, Sharia was currently pumped up from the fight, and minor pain didn't bother her at all.

"I'm going!"

She shouted briefly and began running toward Rei again.

She was faster than before, but the type of attack remained unchanged.

Rather than resorting to unnecessary tricks, the attack was launched solely to land on her opponent even a fraction of a second faster. As expected, it was dodged by Rei, who countered with the same strike as before.

Sura and the others watching thought the outcome would be exactly the same as before... but Sharia forcibly shifted her body to evade the blow Rei aimed for the tip of her jaw.

"No way!"

Who had uttered that short cry...? But there was no doubt that everyone watching was shocked by what they had just witnessed.

However, that gasp of astonishment was instantly replaced by an even louder one.

Sharia tried to pull back the fist she'd initially swung at Rei, but before it fully returned to her hand, Rei grabbed it with his left hand and threw her with brute force.

To anyone familiar with judo, it would have been a crude one-armed shoulder throw, a brutal toss.

Incidentally, Ray had grabbed Sharia's arm with his left hand, not his right, because his right hand was occupied delivering a blow aimed at her jaw.

Just as Sharia's back slammed into the ground, Rei lifted her body slightly upward, minimizing the impact.

"Guh!"

Even so, he couldn't completely absorb the impact, and Sharia's training ended with Rei's one-shoulder throw.
