Rei and the others returned to the forest and headed straight for the cave.

As they walked through the forest, Ryutas asked Rei.

"So, what are you going to do now, Rei? I imagine Jaya will be busy fighting the country from now on."

"Well, even if you say that... Fundamentally, what I need to do is take revenge against Jaya."

"Hmm."

Hearing Rei's words, Byune, with Yellow perched on her head, murmured softly.

Above Byune's head, Yellow let out a small chirp, whether understanding the conversation or not.

Seeing the two people and one creature like that, Ryutas wore an exasperated expression and opened his mouth.

"Look, normally, taking revenge against an entire organization... that sort of thing isn't something an individual can just pull off easily, right?"

"Well, even if you say that... those who can do it, can do it."

In fact, it was precisely because he could do it that Daskar had requested revenge against Rei and the others.

But if asked whether Ryutas could accept that, the answer was no.

As if understanding Ryutas's feelings, Rei averted his gaze from Ryutas and looked at the surrounding trees before speaking.

"At least in terms of retaliation against Jaya, I think we dealt them significant damage."

"Yeah. They destroyed the giant's nest in Mejogo however they pleased, and the black crystal that was extremely important to Jaya was destroyed too. On top of that, they even came to the cave where the giants were being held in reserve. If someone told me the damage was small after all that, I'd have to rethink the organization I belonged to."

"Giant's nest?" Rei tilted his head for a moment, but quickly understood from the context that it referred to the underground facility in Mejogo.

(But if we're talking about a nest, wouldn't the cave be more fitting than the underground facility in Mejogo? After all, most of the giants were inside that cave... Wait.)

Thinking that far, something suddenly occurred to Rei.

"Ryutas. Were all the giants in that cave? Couldn't there have been other standby locations somewhere else?"

"...I don't know. At least as far as I know, the giants were all in that cave. But then again, I wasn't exactly trusted completely by the higher-ups either."

Though Ryutas was a candidate to succeed Jaya, there were several others in the same position as him.

Given that, he wasn't naive enough to think he alone was trusted by the higher-ups.

There were undoubtedly others who enjoyed greater trust from above than he did. And if such a person existed, then perhaps... just maybe, there was a possibility that giants had actually been transported to other locations as well.

"I see. Well, even if that were the case... it'd probably just mean that place would suffer major damage."

"...Whoa."

Hearing Rei's muttered words, Ryutas forgot to even form an expression and let out a gasp.

But in truth, Rei's words were undeniably accurate.

According to what Ryutas had heard from Rei, the giant inside the cave had gone berserk—no, driven by instinct—perhaps due to the destruction of the black crystal.

If that was the case, it was absolutely impossible that such rampage was confined only to the cave they were about to enter.

If anyone had been controlling the giant, unless that person was exceptionally strong, they would undoubtedly have been devoured by the giant now ruled by its instincts.

While extremely unlikely, there might be someone strong enough to defeat the giant, or someone who somehow managed to survive by sheer luck.

But there was no way a small country like Reburina had anyone that strong.

"For now, I'm praying this doesn't turn into a huge commotion. ...Ah, I see it."

Shaking his head as if to dismiss the sight of the rampaging giants, Ryutas spotted a cave ahead and murmured happily.

Around the cave stood Ryutas's guards.

Upon seeing Ryutas return, they visibly relaxed.

"Young Master, you're safe. Any injuries..."

"None. Honestly, I barely even needed to fight at all."

Had he actually fought the guard soldiers, Ryutas might have been injured.

But in truth, no real combat had occurred.

...To be precise, there had been minor skirmishes with nobles and their entourages, but to Rei, such clashes could hardly be called combat.

The same was true for Ryutas himself, and he casually informed them there was no need to worry about the guards.

Indeed, the guards breathed a sigh of relief at Ryutas' words.

"So, how about over here? Anything unusual?"

"No, nothing particularly noteworthy since the young master left... Ah, a few soldiers came by while we were out, probably got separated from their group, but that's all."

The male guard conversing with Ryutas said this while shifting his gaze slightly away.

There, several soldiers lay unconscious on the ground.

It was obvious to anyone watching that they weren't dead, just unconscious.

"Getting separated in this forest? Honestly, what kind of soldiers are you?"

As the term "forest" implied, this place was quite deep... far too large to be called mere woods.

However, unlike the frontier regions like Gilm, there were no high-rank monsters here, and moving around normally shouldn't have been particularly difficult.

Despite these conditions, getting separated from the unit he was supposed to be with suggested a lack of soldierly aptitude, even before considering issues of inadequate training.

It wasn't strange at all for Ryutas to harbor such doubts.

Then again, with little actual combat experience... and above all, since this was a troop deployment premised on defeat, it was highly likely the quality of the soldiers hadn't been considered at all.

"Well, I can't really say anything about that, even if you ask us. More importantly, what are we going to do now? I think it's safe to assume Jaya already knows about the young master. If that's the case, the safest option would be to flee to another country."

Even though he knew his words would likely fall on deaf ears, the escort made the suggestion. As expected, Ryutas shook his head, rejecting the idea.

"Even if we did something like that, we'd just live in constant fear of assassins being sent after us. Rather than that, it's quicker to crush Jaya here and now."

"...If you decide to leave this country, Young Master, we intend to accompany you."

"I appreciate you offering to guard us. I do... but even so, playing defense isn't very interesting. Besides, I'd rather strike first than live with the constant fear of not knowing when they'll come."

Hearing this, the guards sighed resignedly.

Yet the absence of displeasure on their faces was proof of how much they respected Ryutas.

"Well then..."

"Oh? You've already finished? Just as I expected."

Interrupting the guards' conversation, a voice called out. It was Vihera, emerging from within the cave.

'Huh!?'

Her sudden appearance... especially from behind them, caused the guards to collectively gasp.

It was only natural. This time, it was Vihera... and Elena who appeared from behind, so it was fortunate. But they hadn't sensed their presence at all.

If it had been an assassin instead, they would likely be dead by now.

(Well, facing Vihera and Elena, I suppose it's hopeless anyway.)

He felt a touch of pity for the guards' shock.

Yellow, who had been perched on Byune's head, flew up and surveyed the surroundings... landing on Elena's right shoulder as she looked down at the unconscious soldier and the numerous dead bodies scattered across the ground.

Stroking Yellow, who was happily rubbing his face against Elena, Elena spoke.

"I came here just in case... but it seems like a wasted trip after all. Maybe I should have gone to Marina instead."

"Yeah. The attacking soldiers weren't exactly elite either. ...Well, maybe by Reburina's standards they could be considered elite... or maybe not."

"Besides, I doubt they'd assemble such elite troops for a unit meant to be wiped out anyway."

Ryutas declared, looking slightly exasperated at Rei's words.

At Ryutas's attitude, Elena and Vihera both showed a momentary flicker of surprise.

But it was only for a moment.

They quickly decided this wasn't the place to question Ryutas and spoke up.

"Assuming total annihilation?"

"Yes. According to Ryutas, that's the condition under which the Kingdom of Mireana and the Kingdom of Reburina will cooperate to fight Jaya."

"...Ah, I see."

Realizing what Reburina was scheming from Rei's words, Elena muttered in exasperation.

"The goal itself isn't bad. Not bad... but that should have been contingent on us not knowing the upper echelons of the country were involved in this incident. Now that we know, I think it's pointless to try any little tricks at this stage, don't you?"

Ryutas, whose gaze was met, found himself momentarily captivated by Elena's beauty as their eyes locked in direct confrontation.

But he quickly recalled their current situation and spoke.

"I don't know if that was the only way you could think of to handle the current situation, or if there's another move you should make."

"Of course, this is merely Ryutas' speculation. We can't exactly ask the attacking soldiers or their commanders if they're operating under the assumption that their entire unit will be wiped out."

"I suppose not. ...Though it wouldn't have been surprising if someone among them knew the truth. If we'd asked someone else, things might have gotten quite interesting. I almost wish I could have seen it."

Vihera, who had been listening, said this with a smile.

If Vihera had actually been there, she might have really asked.

"More importantly... um, the fact that you two are here means... could it be that the giant..."

Not that he was trying to change the subject... but Ryutas voiced what had been on his mind.

He was genuinely puzzled.

A thousand rampaging giants, and the fact that Elena and Vihera, who had stayed behind to deal with them, appeared here... it was strange... no, it was downright abnormal.

A thousand giants should have posed that level of threat.

But the fact that the two left to handle them appeared here meant the giants had been dealt with.

Unless the giants suddenly stopped rampaging and regained their composure... the outcome was inevitable.

(Did they defeat them? All of those giants?)

Ryutas had seen the place where the giants gathered.

Nearly a thousand giants, hugging their knees, motionless... in a state of slumber.

Even that sight alone, the sheer form of the giants and above all their numbers, had inspired awe. Yet to challenge them in battle while they were moving about and annihilate them...

Knowing the true nature of the giants, it was painfully clear just how incredible that feat was.

For an instant... yes, just an instant, Ryutas thought the two beautiful women before him were unbelievable monsters.

Of course, he knew their power far exceeded his imagination. After all, Rei had single-handedly dealt with hundreds of soldiers.

But the combat strength of soldiers and giants was vastly different.

Despite that, the fact she had defeated them without suffering any apparent injuries was nothing short of astonishing to Ryutas.

"Well, the giant business is settled for now. Next... what to do next, huh. Letting the Kingdom of Reburina's scheme play out as planned doesn't sound very interesting."

Rei, naturally, didn't believe Reburina's schemes would truly succeed.

But at the same time... he couldn't completely rule out the possibility that some mistake might make it work.

Given that, this matter needed to be dealt with as quickly as possible.

(For starters, I suppose I should inform Lord Daskar about this incident.)

Since they were here in the first place at Daskar's request, it was certain they needed to inform him of the current situation.

Moreover, this matter wasn't merely an act of retaliation; it had escalated into a political situation.

Therefore, it was absolutely certain that Rei and the others should avoid acting on their own whims as much as possible.

"For now... let's go pick up Marina."

He murmured to himself.
