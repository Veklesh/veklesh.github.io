"...Mr. Two-Bird, how about it?"

At Walker's words, the middle-aged man called Two Bird lifted his face.

Before him lay what, at first glance, appeared to be nothing more than Treant's corpse.

But this was what Rei had brought from Trent's forest—a Treant imitation, one might say, devoid of magic stones.

Looking toward Walker, Two-Bird—a man possessing the highest skill in discerning monster materials within the Gilm Guild—shook his head.

Possessing the highest skill in Gilm... the power of discernment held great significance.

The Gilms of the frontier, precisely because they were on the frontier, received various monsters that wouldn't be seen in ordinary places.

That very fact demanded a corresponding level of expertise in appraisal.

Even for Two-Bird, the dead Treant corpse before him was nothing short of a mystery.

"No good. Just looking at the appearance alone, it's clearly a Treant no matter how you look at it..."

Tubird's muttered words weren't directed solely at the Treant whose body Rei had cut open searching for magic stones.

By Two Bird's hand, several other Treants had already been cut open.

Though, in the case of the tree monster Treant, it was debatable whether "opening its body" was even the right way to describe it.

"At first, I thought he was bringing a Treant with its magic stone removed and claiming it had none. Well, since it was Rei who brought this, I wasn't too worried about that part."

Two-Bird's suspicion likely stemmed from the timing of the season.

Among adventurers newly arrived in Gilm, it wasn't uncommon for fools to try to make a quick name for themselves.

He knew it was Rei who brought this, but considering the circumstances, Two-Bird couldn't help but be suspicious.

After all, it was a monster that didn't possess magic stones.

"I suppose so. I can't imagine Rei doing something like that... Ah, but considering he does have a kind-hearted side, the possibility of him being deceived by someone unscrupulous... No, that's unlikely."

If it were just Rei, there might be a chance he could be deceived.

But when Rei went to Treant Forest, Marina was there too.

Marina, the guildmaster before Walker, was someone Walker couldn't imagine falling for such a lie.

Walker may be working as guildmaster now, but he didn't believe he could surpass Marina purely based on guildmaster abilities.

In fact, Walker honestly felt he hadn't even come close to matching her level yet.

"But... if that's the case, it makes things a bit troublesome. Mr. Two-Bird, what are your honest thoughts on this Treant?"

"Well... let me see. Among the monsters I've encountered, there's never been one without a magic stone."

After all, monsters without magic stones simply don't exist, he added.

Walker nodded in agreement at Two-Bird's words.

Walker was the one entrusted with the guild's branch office at the location where the dungeon had been.

While not as knowledgeable as Two-Bird, he knew a great deal about monsters.

Even to Walker, this incident was utterly unbelievable.

If it hadn't been Rei who brought the corpse of the Treant without a magic stone... and if it hadn't been proven right before his eyes that it lacked one, Walker himself would never have been able to believe it.

This matter was that significant.

"However, to expand Gilm, we absolutely need timber from Treant Forest."

To be precise, if push came to shove, we could have it transported from another town.

But that would naturally require significantly more funds.

And if we're talking about expanding Gilm by fifty percent, then no amount of timber would be enough.

Above all, the wood from Treant Forest possesses power beyond ordinary timber due to its magical properties.

(Or rather, it's precisely because the wood from Treant Forest possesses such properties that the idea of using it for the Gilm expansion arose... If it were ordinary wood, this wouldn't have been the case.)

Walker found himself troubled by the situation. Even considering the troublesome, eerie nature of Treant Forest, he couldn't help but see its usefulness.

"Anyway, Mr. Two-Bird, I'd like you to investigate this Treant. ...If we can somehow manage to control the extent of the Treant Forest's corruption, it could be nothing short of a blessing for Gilm."

"...I don't think it'll go that smoothly, though."

Having spent years observing monsters, especially those of the frontier, Two-Bird knew exactly how dangerous these creatures could be.

That's precisely why, when Walker suggested they could control monsters to their will, he couldn't help but tilt his head in doubt.

"Guildmaster, may I speak frankly?"

"Hmm? What is it? Of course, go ahead. It might lead to a better outcome."

Walker was someone who listened to others' opinions.

For guild staff, this was fortunate.

Some guildmasters ran their guilds in a manner bordering on dictatorship.

They'd immediately dismiss anyone who couldn't agree with their views.

Compared to such guildmasters, Walker is a much easier person to deal with.

Of course, the former guildmaster, Marina, would never have entrusted the position to someone like that.

"Honestly, this Treant Forest? It's become quite well-known, hasn't it? Then wouldn't it be better to destroy it now?"

"...I understand your concern, Mr. Two-Bird. However, there's no doubt that the Treant Forest is extremely important to the current Gilm. Naturally, we are prepared to respond immediately should anything happen, so please rest assured."

"Even so," Walker countered. "Monsters don't always act as we predict. We should expect them to constantly defy our expectations."

Though Two-Bird warned of the monsters' danger, Walker also had his reasons for not backing down here.

If we can't use the timber from Treant Forest now, it means Gilm's development will be delayed.

Simultaneously, it would prevent Gilm's adventurers from securing numerous jobs.

As Guildmaster, he wanted to proceed with the expansion work—which would greatly benefit Gilm's adventurers—as soon as possible.

(Besides, who knows how long the Crimson Wings, including Rei, will stay in Gilm?)

It's true that Rei and the others currently use Gilm as their base, and it's hard to imagine them moving it so easily.

But... just because it's hard to imagine doesn't mean it's impossible. That's why I wanted to get the major task of expanding Gilm finished while the Crimson Wings, an exceptional party in many ways, were still here.

If this were a normal request, it probably wouldn't warrant such concern... but expanding the city, especially by fifty percent, was no simple matter.

It was only natural for Walker to think they should do it while they still had some combat strength, and as quickly as possible.

Especially since Rei possessed incredibly valuable abilities, like the item box and the ability to fly with Set.

Just how effective those abilities were was something anyone connected to Rei knew all too well.

Given that, Walker's urgency was understandable.

(Of course, it's an undeniable fact that Treant Forest is a dangerous place in many ways. Given that, it's impossible to rule out the possibility that we might unnecessarily expose adventurers to danger if we're careless.)

Danger was an inherent part of the adventurer's profession.

But that didn't mean they needed to deliberately charge headlong into dangers they could avoid.

(Perhaps I should speak with Daskar about this once.)

Fortunately, Walker met with Daskar daily to discuss Gilm's expansion work.

Given that, consulting him about this matter wouldn't be difficult.

Having decided what needed to be done, Walker instructed Two-Bird once more to investigate Treant, determined to unravel even a sliver of the mystery surrounding this unknown entity.

"GYOGAAAAAH!"

A scream echoed through the forest.

And it wasn't just one.

Corpses were scattered everywhere... Goblin corpses lay strewn about.

No, not just goblins. There were also monsters like orcs and lizardmen.

But... among those monsters, hardly any were still alive.

In fact, it was rare to find a corpse that could be clearly identified as a goblin, an orc, or a lizardman.

Many monsters had roots sprawling all over their bodies while they were still alive, pulsing beneath their skin like veins. Others had their lower halves pierced by plants growing from the ground, their flesh and internal organs completely dissolved and absorbed by digestive fluids, leaving only bones and skin.

If Rei and the others saw this scene, they would be utterly stunned.

After all, when they arrived in this forest just a few hours ago, there were no monsters other than plant-like Treants.

But now it was different.

It would be accurate to say the ground was literally covered in countless monster corpses.

"Gyaaaaaaaah!"

Sharp thorns wrapped around its entire body, tearing into its skin and flesh.

The goblin screamed uncontrollably, overwhelmed by the excruciating pain radiating from every part of its body.

If this were an adventurer, they might be able to use a blade to cut through the thorns, but unfortunately, for the goblin, the scene before its eyes was all that mattered.

Besides, since the goblin's weapon was a club, once the thorns had tangled around its entire body, there was nothing it could do.

And so, bleeding profusely from every part of its body, the thorns swung wildly.

It felt like a child swinging a toy...

Of course, the goblin being swung around was flesh and blood, so it was bound to get hurt.

...No, perhaps it was fortunate for the goblin that it crashed into a nearby tree trunk, snapping its neck and ending its life.

And when the last surviving goblin finally died, only corpses remained scattered throughout the forest.

Thud.

The ground stirred, and something emerged from it.

No, it was likely a tree root. But the thickness of that root was truly as thick as an adult's torso.

The roots wrapped around the corpses of orcs and lizardmen, sinking back into the earth.

If you ignored its appearance as a tree root, the scene closely resembled a snake constricting its prey with its body.

After dozens, perhaps hundreds, of snake-like roots dragged everything—including the goblin corpses—beneath the earth, what followed next were not roots, but vines. A quantity of vines so vast it could only be called a swarm.

They sucked up every last bit of blood, flesh, bone, and organ left on the ground.

If Rei had seen it, she would have thought it looked like a vacuum cleaner...

Not only that, the vines even absorbed the thick, pungent smell of blood that lingered in the air.

Nothing that could become nourishment for the forest was to be spared—not a single drop of blood, not a single scrap of flesh, not even the scent of blood lingering in the air.

The sight of countless vines devouring their prey resembled a swarm of snakes swallowing their victim whole.

And so, a few minutes later... not a single trace of the corpses remained at the scene.

Considering even the scent of blood lingering in the air had vanished completely, no one would ever suspect a massacre worthy of a monster had occurred here.

Indeed, the fact that even Set failed to notice it proved just how perfect it was.

And so, only silence remained within the forest.

Under the moonlight alone... something small, no bigger than a grain of sand, flowed out from the surrounding trees.

Those tiny things glowed faintly as they drifted on the wind, heading out of the forest.

Yes, to gather new prey... beings that would become the forest's nourishment.

Creatures consumed by single-minded focus, thinking only of the forest, would surely return here, just like the monsters slain moments before.

The forest waits, filled with silence, solely for the prey to come.
