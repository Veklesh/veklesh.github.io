As if sensing that the atmosphere at the table had softened slightly, the dining room door swung open.

The maid brought in the main meat dish.

The moment the door opened, an aroma that whetted the appetite wafted through the room, and everyone who caught a whiff couldn’t help but smile.

Even without tasting it, they knew the dish was undoubtedly delicious. That was likely why.

But… when Rei saw the dish brought in and placed right before his eyes… no, everyone else, including Elena, wore expressions of mild disappointment.

And no wonder. The dish set before them was nothing more than meat that had simply been grilled.

If someone knew nothing about Georgima, it wouldn’t be strange for them to yell, “Don’t mess with me!” at such a dish.

The reason that didn’t happen was that everyone present—including Duke Kerebel, and indeed Rei himself—trusted Georgima’s culinary skills.

After all, the meal—which included soup, salad, bread, and various other appetizers—had been absolutely delicious.

It was simply inconceivable that Georgima, who had created such a meal, would serve nothing more than meat that had simply been grilled.

Above all, Georgima is so obsessed with cooking.

It was certain that she would never do anything so frivolous in a setting like this.

Besides, if she could make Rei genuinely think it was delicious, she’d be taught an unknown recipe, so there was no way she’d cut corners in a situation like that.

“...I’ll try it first.”

The first to speak was Duke Kerebel.

Normally, he’s the type of person who would first have someone else try the dish before tasting it himself.

Yet the fact that Kerebel Duke said he would eat it first must mean he trusts Georgima that much.

……Even in a situation like this, doing something like that here would be considered reckless.

In any case, he brought the meat the maid had served to his mouth……

“Huh!?”

The moment the meat touched his lips, Kerebel Duke froze.

“……”

Rei, Elena, and Arcadia all stared intently at Kerebel Duke’s reaction.

If it were bad, or even just not that good, Duke Kerebel would surely have said something right away.

But since he hadn’t done so, it was proof that this meat—which, at first glance, looked like nothing more than a simple roast—was something far more than that.

A few seconds passed after Kerebel put the meat in his mouth. Eventually, he seemed to savor the flavor of the meat... and swallowed.

“Phew!”

A sound that seemed impossible to come from someone who had just swallowed meat escaped Duke Kerebel’s mouth.

It was exactly the kind of sound a sweaty man might make when drinking a cold beer or something similar.

Perhaps because they never expected such a sound to come from Kerebel’s mouth, his wife Arcadia, his daughter Elena, and even the maid all looked at him with expressions of disbelief.

“You?”

“...Just try eating it for now. Then you’ll understand the meaning behind my actions. Words alone cannot do justice to this flavor. It’s a taste you can only truly grasp once you experience it for yourself.”

Saying that, Duke Kerebel brought the wine to his lips, as if to wash away the aftertaste of the dish in his mouth.

Prompted by his words, Elena brought the piece of meat the maid had cut to her mouth… and in the next instant, just like Duke Kerebel, she was left speechless.

Seeing this, it was only natural that Arcadia and Rei, who hadn’t eaten the meat yet, would also show interest in it.

At first glance, it looked like nothing more than simply grilled meat.

But since it was hard to believe that simply eating such a dish could cause Duke Kerebel and Elena to react this way, it obviously couldn’t be just grilled meat.

Strangely enough… or perhaps it was inevitable, but Rei and Arcadia, unable to resist the curiosity welling up inside them, brought the meat on their plates to their mouths.

“Huh!?”

In that instant, Rei and Arcadia were both struck by a shock.

First, a refreshing flavor spread through their mouths.

Next, the taste of the meat instantly filled their mouths, but with every bite, the flavor shifted—as if the meat had been prepared using some special method.

Just when you thought it was a spicy flavor, the sweetness of the fat, an appetite-stimulating tartness, and the rich umami of the meat itself would emerge.

Just when you thought that, the next moment a rich, intense flavor—perhaps from shellfish or something similar—spread through their mouths, allowing them to savor the taste of a seafood broth that had been simmered for hours.

There is no doubt that what is in my mouth is simply a slice of meat that looks like it has been nothing more than grilled.

And yet, despite being just meat, a variety of flavors spread through my mouth… and I even felt a sense of satisfaction as if I’d eaten a full course meal, all from just a single slice of meat.

“...This is... amazing...”

The words slipped out of Rei’s mouth naturally, and no one disagreed.

If the goal were simply to pack complex flavors into a single ingredient, it wouldn’t be that difficult.

But if you tried to do that, all the flavors would blend together, and the dish would no longer be anything you could call delicious.

Moreover, everyone has their own way of eating... the spot where they chew the meat or savor its flavor differs from person to person.

Despite that, the meat before him gives the eater a sense of satisfaction—deliciousness—without the flavors ever blending together.

Rei has absolutely no idea what kind of cooking technique produces a flavor like this.

Moreover, the meat wasn’t prepared individually from the start; it was portioned out onto each plate by the maids.

It’s possible Georgima had instructed the maids beforehand on which piece of meat to give to whom… but even so, if asked whether they could mimic it with such precision, Rei couldn’t help but tilt his head in puzzlement.

(How on earth did they season it like this in the first place?)

That, in itself, was something Rei simply couldn’t grasp.

Even if he were asked to do the same thing himself, he couldn’t possibly imagine being able to replicate it.

Since coming to this world, Rei had eaten all sorts of dishes, so even if he didn’t know the exact cooking method, he could at least imagine how it might be done.

Whether that guess is right or wrong is beside the point—he can imagine it, but... even looking at the meat on the plate in front of him, he couldn’t begin to imagine how to make a dish like this.

To be honest, it really just looks like plain grilled meat.

(No, actually, I should probably assume that Kerebel Duke employs him precisely because he possesses this level of culinary skill.)

If it were a luxurious dish made with rare ingredients, I suppose I could manage to make something—though it would still require skills worthy of being called top-tier.

But to create a dish that leaves the diner completely unable to discern what kind of cooking technique produced this flavor—that, Rei surmised, was the mark of a chef whose skill could truly be called top-tier.

“This is… I’ve eaten Georgima’s cooking many times before… but this is the first time I’ve been so surprised. Really.”

Duke Kerebel put another piece of meat into his mouth and murmured as he savored the ever-changing, seven-hued flavors.

The mix of frustration and satisfaction in his tone—was it because he had never been able to create a dish of this caliber with the ingredients he had prepared up to that point?

And yet, simply by meeting a person named Rei… in her quest for the unknown cuisine within him, she had created a dish of this caliber… yes, Georgima had created an entirely new dish.

At the very least, Duke Kerebel, having tasted a wide variety of dishes throughout his life, had never before eaten anything like this.

While he was delighted by this, he also felt a twinge of regret.

(Was I just a little lucky, I wonder?)

As he ate the meat dish—which he couldn’t possibly believe had been prepared in a conventional manner—Rei found himself thinking that.

He was probably glad that the atmosphere surrounding Duke Kerebel before they ate the meat had vanished, even if only temporarily.

Considering the gravity of the situation, this was ultimately only temporary, and there was no doubt that eventually they would have to discuss Elena’s future—though that phrasing might not be entirely accurate in this case.

Still, the fact that everyone’s attention had temporarily shifted to the food gave Rei a brief moment to catch his breath.

“You know, Georgima… how on earth did you manage to make a dish like this? I’m no slouch in the kitchen myself, but I can’t even begin to imagine how someone could create something like this.”

Arcadia asked her husband, but it’s not very common for the children of nobles to take up cooking as a hobby.

Well, that is, unless we’re talking about the truly impoverished—those so-called nobles who live lives barely distinguishable from the common people.

However, Arcadia was the Duchess of Kerebel. She was the wife of a duke, a title second only to royalty within the Kingdom of Mireana.

Normally, someone in her position would be discouraged from cooking.

...But the fact that she persists in doing it anyway is what makes Arcadia so remarkable.

What’s more, Arcadia must have had a natural talent for it, because she makes food that’s far more delicious than anything a run-of-the-mill cook could produce.

In fact, considering that Georgima, who tasted Arcadia’s cooking, lamented the loss of her skills, it’s not hard to imagine just how skilled she is.

“Even so. Just because Arcadia doesn’t understand it doesn’t mean I do.”

Unlike his wife, Arcadia, Duke Kerebel isn’t particularly knowledgeable about cooking.

That said, this is strictly in comparison to Arcadia.

Generally speaking, Duke Kerebel has a sufficiently deep understanding of cooking and possesses knowledge that goes beyond the general education expected of a noble.

“Father, Mother, why don’t you ask Georgima about it later? She’ll be more than happy to tell you exactly how she prepared it.”

Generally speaking, people with high-level skills tend to want to keep those skills to themselves.

But Georgima is not that type; in fact, she has no qualms about sharing her knowledge and skills.

If anything, she even hopes that her skills will serve as a foundation for further development.

Of course, there aren’t many who possess skills surpassing Georgima’s, so in the end, she is always at the forefront of culinary innovation.

“That’s all well and good… but even if I listen, I’m not sure I’ll be able to understand it. I really wonder what exactly needs to be done to achieve this kind of cooking.”

Muttering to himself thoughtfully, Duke Kerebel once again brought a piece of meat to his mouth.

And once again, a multi-course feast of diverse flavors unfolds in his mouth.

Being able to enjoy the flavors of vegetables and seafood while eating meat is an experience that would normally be impossible.

Moreover, since what you’re eating is a single piece of meat, even after finishing the course, you can still eat as much as you want.

And yet, since you can still fully satisfy your craving for meat, there’s no other way to describe it than as a truly mysterious dish.

What’s more, the flavor varies slightly depending on the cut of meat.

For example, one piece might taste like fish, while another tastes like crab or shrimp.

In other words, it’s as if you can enjoy a different course meal with every bite… Just as everyone was savoring such a dish, the cafeteria door opened.

“Hey, how was today’s meal? Did you find it delicious from the bottom of your heart?”

As soon as he entered the dining hall, it was, of course, Georgima who called out to Rei.

He opened the door before Kerebel could reply, and even went so far as to ignore Kerebel, the head of the household, to speak to Rei.

By any standard, this would be considered rude, but since they had thoroughly enjoyed Georgima’s cooking, they couldn’t complain.

Georgima had served food so exceptional that even such behavior was forgiven.

……That said, everyone except Rei—including Duke Kerebel—knew that Georgima always behaved this way.

The only exception was Rei, who—leaving aside her own interactions with Georgima—had no idea she behaved this way even toward Duke Kerebel.

But even so, he quickly realized from the atmosphere around him that this was nothing out of the ordinary. He turned his gaze back to Georgima and spoke.

“Ah, honestly, it’s this delicious… no, that’s not all. This is the first time I’ve ever had a dish that’s left me so shocked and amazed.”

“Ha ha. I knew it! It was worth all the effort I put into making such an elaborate dish. To make this, aside from culinary skill, you need rare ingredients, magic items, the cooperation of an alchemist, and all sorts of other things, you know? If you weren’t surprised by this, I would’ve lost my confidence. So? What kind of dish are you going to teach me?”

With eyes sparkling with anticipation, Georgima asked, and Rei, sighing in resignation, uttered the name of the dish he’d been thinking about before this meal. …Ramen.
