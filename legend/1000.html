After confirming that the magic item Seymour had purchased was indeed crafted by the alchemist causing trouble around Gilm this time, just as Rei had predicted, Rei and the others parted ways with Seymour and made their way to a cafeteria a short distance away.

It was evening, so the cafeteria was quite busy, but it wasn't completely full yet, so they managed to find seats.

...Set was eating the food Rei had ordered outside.

"You guys are getting quite a bit of attention, huh?"

As Azimov said this, washing down the dried meat on the table with ale, Rei looked at Vihera, and Vihera looked at Rei.

Each seemed to want to say it was the other's fault they were attracting attention.

In truth, that wasn't entirely wrong.

Rei was famous in Gilm in many ways, and Vihera naturally drew attention with her beauty and attire.

Rei without Set wouldn't normally stand out much, but now, with her hood down, her face—best described as feminine—was exposed, making her impossible to ignore.

Though not quite as much as those two, Byune also possessed a well-proportioned appearance, certainly beautiful enough to be called a maiden.

If anything, the least conspicuous among them was the alchemist, Azimov.

His features weren't exactly handsome, and he gave off a slightly rough vibe, but even so, he had fewer standout elements compared to the other three.

Anyway, this conversation wasn't going anywhere. Ignoring the talk about attention for now, Rei turned to Azimov and spoke up.

"Enough about attention. Tell me the details. Are you absolutely certain that magic item Seymour used was indeed crafted by that alchemist?"

"That's right. You could say it's almost certain."

"Couldn't someone be faking it?"

As Vihera reached for the bean dish, which was nothing more than beans roasted and sprinkled with salt, Azimov snorted before answering.

"Hmph. Some fools might be fooled by such tricks, but you think you can pull that on me? I may look like this, but I'm a skilled alchemist."

Though Azimov called himself skilled, Rei knew his actual abilities were quite high. She said nothing in particular, simply watching the exchange between him and Vihera.

"Well, since Rei doesn't seem to have any objections, I suppose I'll take your word for it for now. So, you're saying that's definitely the work of the same person?"

In the crowded cafeteria, Vihera asked, deliberately avoiding the crucial word, and Azimov nodded.

"Like I said before, it's almost certainly him. You can't erase the kind of quirks that show up when you make magic items."

"If Asimov says so, we can trust it. So, the biggest problem is still who brought it to that shop to sell..."

"Getting info out of that shopkeeper? I doubt it's possible. He looked stubborn as hell. And considering his business connections, I don't think he'd tell us either."

"Yeah, probably. ...So the only real possibility left is catching whoever comes to sell it directly..."

"But they might not come back to that shop to sell again, right?"

"Hmm."

Vihera's words met with Byune's agreement as she ate her skewered meat.

Rei couldn't help but agree with that assessment.

Even if the alchemist involved in this case was selling magic items for some reason, there was no need to sell them repeatedly at the same shop.

"But it's also true we have no other leads. So, we have no choice but to head to the place with even the slightest clue, right?"

"That's true... No, you're right. Rei has a point. So, should we go now?"

"Yeah. ...Azimov, sorry, but hang with us a little longer."

"Huh!?"

For Azimov, the words coming from Rei's mouth were completely unexpected.

He slammed his beer glass down on the table and snapped, making it clear he wasn't joking.

"I still have a lot of work to do. There's your spear, for one thing, and other experiments too."

"...I'm sorry, but since an alchemist is involved in this matter, we absolutely need an alchemist's cooperation. And the most skilled alchemist I know is Azimov."

Azimov's eyes widened for a moment at the blunt, decisive statement.

Though Azimov hadn't known Rei for all that long, he couldn't believe Rei would lie to keep him here.

Which meant what Rei had just said was undoubtedly his true feelings.

That much was true. ...Though it was also true that Ajimov was pretty much the only alchemist Rei knew in Gilm.

Of course, Rei had no doubt that Azimov was a skilled alchemist.

After all, Pamidor had introduced him with confidence... admittedly, he had some personality flaws, but he was still a skilled alchemist. There was no way he wasn't skilled.

"Well, if you insist that much, I suppose I could go along with it."

"Sorry about that."

"Hmph, I just don't want to back down after you've gone that far. Besides, you've given me rare materials like hammers, Set's hair, and feathers."

Even though he felt like saying something about being treated tsundere by the man... Rei kept it to himself and stood up.

...Incidentally, the food laid out on the table had been almost entirely devoured by Rei and Byune during their conversation, leaving hardly anything behind.

In fact, even the side vegetables had vanished completely from the plates.

The sauce used in the dishes had been completely wiped up with bread. If the cafeteria cook saw these plates, they'd be impressed by the sheer amount eaten.

Even though they'd eaten lightly before heading to Azimov's house, their appetite showed no sign of it.

"I barely ate anything, though. And I only had one ale,"

Azimov complained, but Rei smiled and replied.

"Since we're going on stakeout, isn't that actually a good thing? If the alcohol smell blew the stakeout, that wouldn't be funny at all."

"Ugh, fine. Whatever."

Apparently conceding Rei had a point, Azimov downed the last bit of ale left in his glass and stood up from the table.

Rei and the others were hiding in a spot where they could see the magic item shop they'd visited a little while ago.

Fortunately, the magic item shop was located in a back alley, so finding a hiding spot wasn't difficult.

That said, they were positioned a bit further away because, given Set's size, hiding without being spotted by the shop staff was impossible.

"Still..."

Perhaps bored by the lack of movement in the shop ahead, Rei murmured suddenly.

Vihera seemed equally bored, her gaze drifting toward Rei without much thought.

...It was undeniable that he couldn't help but be conscious of the situation. A stunning beauty like Vihera, clad only in her usual sheer garment, was standing right next to Rei... literally just a few centimeters away.

Rei opened his mouth, perhaps hoping to break the awkward atmosphere between them.

Though, to be fair, Rei was the only one who actually felt that awkwardness.

Byune was still young and, due to his upbringing, had almost no interest in relationships with the opposite sex. Azimov was an absolute eccentric who preferred magic items over women. As for Vihera, she was actually delighted that Rei was paying her attention.

"What? What's wrong?"

"No, it's just... didn't the whole town seem unusually noisy on our way here? I know it always gets noisy in the evening, but it seemed even noisier than usual..."

Rei muttered, half-embarrassed, but it was also true.

Being Gilm, a town teeming with adventurers, the evening bustle was amplified by the high spirits of adventurers finishing their quests, combined with the revelry in the taverns. Yet, the noise he'd felt walking through the town from the dining hall to here had felt more intense than usual.

"Some idiot must've caused trouble again, huh? Maybe something happened with that magic item thing Rei mentioned?"

"...It didn't feel like that kind of commotion to me."

To Rei, it seemed like a noise filled with a certain joy, but the moment he tried to voice that thought, he stopped.

A moment later, Vihera, then Byune, Azimov, and the others froze... and soon, the door to the magic item shop opened.

The figure who emerged walked down the street without showing any particular signs of being wary of their surroundings.

"How long has it been since we got here?"

"Not that long, I guess?"

Rei answered Rei's muttered question.

Rei nodded at the words, yet remained unconvinced.

He hadn't taken out the clock inside the Misty Ring to check the time precisely, but even so, over thirty minutes had passed.

That meant the person in front of Rei had been inside the shop the entire time.

(Thirty minutes. It's not that long, really. And they looked like an adventurer, so if they were negotiating to buy or sell a magic item they'd acquired, that amount of time wouldn't be unusual. It wouldn't be unusual... but...)

Even so, the person Rei saw had something about them that touched a nerve within him.

He couldn't quite put his finger on what it was, but following that instinct within him, Rei leapt out from his hiding spot.

The person had just exited the shop and was walking toward where Rei and the others were, so they ended up facing Rei head-on.

"Oh? What's this? You need something from me?"

The person addressed Rei standing before them asked this. Seeing Rei, they showed a slight look of surprise, but the next moment, an amused smile appeared on their lips as they asked Rei.

"I'd like to ask you something."

"Hmm... From your voice, you sound like a guy? Are you trying to hit on me? Sorry, but I'm not really into short guys. My bad."

Rei, who seemed to have been mistaken for a pick-up artist, was taken aback by the surprise that the person he thought was a man was actually a woman, but he opened his mouth anyway.

"No, that's not what I meant. Actually, I have something I'd like to ask you."

"Oh, lost? But I'm not too familiar with this area either."

"...Didn't you just come out of that shop over there?"

"Huh? Oh, I had some business there. As you can probably tell, I'm an adventurer. I stop by this area sometimes to sell magic items. But I only ever come to that shop, so I really don't know the surrounding area."

The woman shrugged her shoulders slightly, and for some reason, the suspicion within Rei only grew stronger.

Despite her words holding no obvious inconsistencies and her manner seeming perfectly normal, he couldn't shake it.

(What is it? What's bothering me? Something...)

Though Rei inwardly tilted his head in confusion, the woman seemed to decide their business was done. She gave him a light wave and started to leave...

"GRRRRRR!"

That growl echoed throughout the area.

There was no mistaking that voice. It belonged to the entity that could be called Rei's other half.

Set, who appeared while letting out that growl, stood in front of the woman, blocking her path.

"...Hey, I need you to do something about this. Did I do something?"

"Grrrr"

Ignoring the woman's words, Rei's gaze was fixed on Set.

The moment Set turned his face toward the woman, the unease within Rei immediately took shape.

It was something Rei herself hadn't recognized.

Yet, it was something that existed within every living creature.

Namely...

(Body odor!?)

To be precise, it wasn't the woman's body odor itself, but the stench of decay and rust that had permeated her body.

Even Set had screamed at the sheer stench, so it wasn't something that would simply vanish.

No, a human nose probably couldn't have detected it, but Rei, possessing senses sharper than a human's, picked up the lingering stench on the woman's body somewhere, even if she didn't consciously understand it, and it registered as a sense of unease within her.

And above all, it couldn't fool Set's nose. His senses were even sharper than Rei's, and on top of that, he possessed the skill of heightened olfaction.

"I see. So you're one of the people involved in this matter. Then, let's talk..."

...let me hear your side of the story." Just as he was about to say that, the woman broke into a run without a word.

Not toward Rei or Set, but in the opposite direction.

Naturally, Rei and Set weren't about to let her get away. They gave chase, but...

"She's fast!?"

They were gradually closing the gap, but still, the woman running—Adriana—moved at a speed that made it impossible for Set and Rei to catch up immediately.

While she possessed high physical ability, it wasn't a speed that Rei and Set couldn't normally match.

The reason she could maintain this speed was obviously not her own strength, but because of the magic item Zubozo had created.

Even so, thanks to Rei and Set's speed, the gap was gradually closing. If they kept running like this, they should eventually catch up.

...If they were on back streets, that is.

But the path Adria was running on led straight to the main street.

Once she blended into the crowd, catching up would become difficult.

Just as that sense of urgency began to grow within Rei, Adria emerged onto the main street...

"Kyaaah!"

In that instant, she screamed and fell to the ground.

What happened!? Just as that thought crossed Rei's mind, the voice reached her ears.

"Hmm. It seems you're caught up in quite a commotion again, even though it's been a while since we last met face-to-face, Rei."

The speaker was a woman with golden hair that seemed to embody sunlight itself, possessing flawless features that captivated all who saw her and an imposing, majestic aura.

Rei knew without needing to think who this opponent, worthy of being called a goddess of war, was.

Watching her touch her golden vertical curls, Rei voiced the name of the person before him.

「Elena……」
