News that Rei and his group had cleared the dungeon spread like wildfire throughout Golz that very day.

Many people hadn’t realized the hole in the cliff was a dungeon, assuming it was just a monster’s nest or something similar. However, some of those people learned not only that the hole in the cliff face was indeed a dungeon—not a nest—but also that the dungeon had been conquered.

Furthermore, Guild Master Vanillas, based on information he received from Rei—namely, that since the dungeon’s core had been destroyed, it was unclear what would happen to the expanded space within the dungeon—issued a proclamation through the guild warning people not to approach the dungeon.

Ideally, he would have issued such an order, but unfortunately, the Guild Master did not have that authority.

That is precisely why she urged everyone to stay as far away as possible.

That said, hearing such a warning only made some people suspect there might be something there, prompting them to approach the dungeon.

Vanillas couldn’t keep an eye on everyone, so she simply considered it their own responsibility and their own fault, and did nothing further.

All things considered, Vanillas is one of the most influential figures in Golz.

Even if someone tried to blame him for this, if he simply stated that he had made it clear beforehand, no one could argue with him.

In any case, while things were getting heated within the Golz... as for Rei and the others, they returned to their inn without doing anything in particular, had a meal, and went to sleep.

And so, the next day...

“Alright then, Rei. First, please bring out the Kelpie!”

At the guild clerk’s words, Rei pulled the Kelpie out of his Misty Ring.

Being a horse-like monster, the Kelpie is quite large.

At the very least, most adventurers in Golz would be seeing one for the first time.

A murmur rises.

The adventurers who had come to dissect it after accepting the guild’s request let out loud cries of surprise at the sight of the Kelpie.

“Alright, alright, everyone, please calm down. The Kelpie is already dead, so it won’t suddenly come back to life and attack you—please rest assured. Besides, if you’re this surprised by this Kelpie, you’ll be scared out of your wits when you see the next one.”

Guided by the guild staff member who said this, we move to a spot a short distance away from the kelpie.

(It’s nice that we can just butcher like this outside the city. If this were Gilm, there’d be a high chance monsters would attack us mid-butchering. Ah, but even in Golz, there’s a chance beasts like boars, bears, or wolves might attack instead of monsters.)

Especially now that it’s autumn; whether it’s bears preparing for hibernation or other animals, they all need as much nutrition as they can get.

At a time like this, even if we’re just outside the city, but still out in the open, butchering a monster would make us prime prey.

That said, as long as Set is around, that probably wouldn’t happen unless they were absolutely starving.

In any case, it was undeniably much easier than in Gilm, where monsters are everywhere.

“I’m taking it out.”

After giving that warning... the next moment, Rei pulled the Garangiga’s corpse out of the Misty Ring.

The adventurers began to murmur again.

Of course, since it had lost its head to Set’s single blow, there was no doubt that the corpse, though massive, lacked a certain sense of grandeur.

Even so, the sheer size of the Galangiga was something completely unexpected for the adventurers of Golz.

It was hardly a monster one would expect to see in these parts, so that was only natural.

With its head gone, blood began to flow from the wound.

Normally, the carcass of a monster slain the day before would have been completely drained of blood, but since it had been stored in the Misty Ring, the Garangiga remained exactly as it was when it was killed.

A strong smell of blood hung in the air amidst the clear autumn weather.

“Alright, please split up as planned and begin the butchering. Just to be clear, please do not under any circumstances steal materials, magic stones, or parts required for the subjugation certificate. Guild staff are watching, so if you try anything like that…”

The guild staff member explaining this paused there.

Perhaps because he didn’t specify exactly what would happen, those listening felt their fear intensify.

Of course, those participating in this dismemberment were well aware of who Rei and his group were.

Given that, there was no way they would be so reckless as to steal monster materials and make enemies of high-ranking adventurers with nicknames—let alone Set of the Griffins.

...There were a few who seemed to have something to hide, but there was no doubt they wouldn’t—and couldn’t—do anything out of line in front of the guild staff.

“Well then, Rei. Please leave the monster dissection to us. With a monster this size… I think we’ll be done by noon at the earliest. What do you say? Of course, you’re welcome to wait here if you’d like.”

From the guild staff’s perspective, having Rei there served as a deterrent, so they likely figured it would make their job easier.

However, upon hearing the guild clerk’s words, Rei decided without hesitation to leave.

“I want to look around Golz myself, so I’ll leave this to you. With a guild staff member here, I’m sure no one will steal the materials or anything.”

“Understood. Well then, please enjoy your time in Golz.”

The guild staff member had hoped he would stay, but since he said he wanted to look around the town, they couldn’t very well refuse.

Besides, Golz was a beloved hometown for the guild employee as well.

Since he was going to explore such a place, the staff member would surely be delighted rather than reluctant.

“Alright, I’m going to buy some souvenirs, so let’s go!”

As expected, Relieu was the first to leave.

He headed toward Golz, not even bothering to think about how much money he had, determined to buy a souvenir for his beloved wife.

As a Rank A adventurer with a nickname, Relieu has enough money to buy just about anything sold in town... but he seems to be focused solely on what kind of souvenir to buy, and hasn’t given that aspect much thought.

“Ah… well, do your best.”

Rei called out to Relieu’s retreating back in a slightly half-hearted tone, then wondered what he himself should do…

It might be nice to explore Golz and look for something interesting, rare ingredients, or—though the chances are slim—a hidden gem of a magic item.

Also, since arriving in Golz, he’d been so focused on the dungeon that he hadn’t had a chance to explore the town. If there were any local specialties, he’d like to try them.

“I’ve got some business to take care of with Vihera and Byune, so why don’t you go look around with Rei?”

“Marina… is that okay?”

Hearing Marina’s words, Elena asked, “Are you sure that’s okay?”

After all, what Elena was essentially saying was, “Go explore Golz with Rei all by yourselves… go on a date.”

Of course, for Elena, the suggestion coming from Marina’s mouth was the happiest thing imaginable.

Being able to go on a date alone with the man she likes… the man she loves—for Elena, who is a “Princess General” on the outside but a maiden at heart, this is incredibly delightful.

“Yes. Vihera and I can be with Rei anytime, but given your position, Elena, you’ll have to leave Gilm someday, won’t you?”

“That’s…”

What Marina said was an undeniable fact.

Elena is currently acting as if she belongs to the same party as Rei, and her family has implicitly encouraged her to do so.

However, nominally, her role is to monitor the situation to ensure the Noble Faction doesn’t interfere unnecessarily with the expansion of Gilm.

That said, currently, Ara, who remained at Gilm, is handling that task entirely on her own.

In any case, precisely because of that, Elena would need to act as the Noble Faction’s Princess General if anything were to happen, and there was a possibility she might have to leave Gilm.

That’s precisely why Marina is subtly telling her to enjoy their dates while she can still be with Rei.

Realizing this, Elena nods, her expression a mix of joy and regret—a complex blend of emotions.

“Hmm. Well, since this is a rare opportunity, I’ll take you up on that. …Rei, do you mind?”

“Hm? Oh. I don’t mind, but what about Set?”

“Grr? Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set lets out a growl while looking up at the sky.

Hearing Set’s growl, some of the adventurers from Golz were startled, but Rei and the others paid no mind and continued their conversation.

“I see. Go ahead and fly to your heart’s content for the first time in a while. Just make sure you’re back by evening, okay?”

"Grr!"

Rei stroked Set as he spoke, after Set had told him he’d be flying alone for a change, hunting and eating monsters and animals as he pleased.

Set rubbed his face against Rei, then pulled away. He took a few steps to build up speed, flapped his wings, and flew off.

“Well, now that Set’s gone, I suppose we should get moving too. Leave Yellow to us.”

Saying that, Marina grabbed Yellow and pushed Elena toward Rei.

Elena shot Marina, who had pushed her, a look that was part protest and part gratitude, then spoke to Rei.

“Well, shall we go too?”

“Yeah. I hope we find something interesting.”

“Heh, I don’t think we’ll find something Rei would like that easily.”

Exchanging cheerful smiles, Rei and Elena headed back into Golz.

Watching them go, Vihera called out to Marina and Byune.

“So, what should we do? I don’t think it’d be very fun if we just wandered around inside Golz and happened to run into Rei and the others…”

“True. Well then… shall we head over to the guild for a bit?”

“Eh… all the way to the guild? It’s our day off, after all.”

“That’s exactly why. I found a pretty interesting dish at the guild tavern—are you interested?”

“Hmm!”

As expected, it was Byune who jumped at Marina’s suggestion first.

For Byune, the mere mention of an interesting dish was enough to capture her attention.

Seeing Byune like that, Vihera muttered, “Oh well.”

From Vihera’s perspective, since there wasn’t anything in particular she needed or wanted to do in Golz, she probably figured there was no harm in going to the guild tavern.

If it turned out there were actually powerful fighters in Golz, Vihera would have either insisted on going there or gone off on her own.

In any case, with everyone’s plans for the day settled, they went their separate ways.

“...An adventurer with a nickname, huh. Just watching them like this, they’ve got a certain vibe, don’t they?”

A male adventurer in his mid-twenties, who had been assigned to dismantle the kelpie, muttered as he watched Marina and the others walk away.

That said, even as he muttered this, the man’s gaze was fixed on Vihera’s retreating figure… captivated by the alluring curves visible beneath her skimpy attire, reminiscent of a courtesan or dancer.

“Hmph! Someday, I’ll become just as famous as that guy Rei!”

Standing beside him was a boy—still in his mid-teens, and judging by his appearance, not much older than Rei himself—who muttered with obvious defiance.

From this boy’s perspective, he must be envious that Rei—who is likely not much older than himself—holds the position of a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname.

The man, who had been captivated by Vihera’s back, wore a faintly amused expression at the boy’s words.

He recalled that when he himself had first become an adventurer, he had always been focused on climbing higher and higher.

Of course, even now, he hasn’t given up on becoming a higher-ranked adventurer.

But even so, once you reach your mid-twenties, you start to get a pretty good idea of just how much talent you actually have.

At the very least, the man knew that no matter how hard he tried, he’d be lucky to reach Rank C; he’d never even entertained the idea of becoming a Rank B or A adventurer, let alone one with a nickname.

“I see. Well, do your best. Striving relentlessly to climb higher is a privilege of the young. Why don’t you hone your skills in Golz, and once you’ve gained a certain level of strength, try heading to Gilm? If you do, you might see things there that you couldn’t see in Golz.”

“Of course! I’ll prove myself in Gilm, too!”

Giving the shouting boy a light tap on the head, the man set to work butchering the kelpie.
