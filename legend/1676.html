"Rei, really... I just went out to do some shopping—how on earth did that turn into a pirate hunt?"

After transporting all the pirates to the village by boat, and finally helping the man whom Rei had held at knifepoint off the ship, Rei boarded Set, who had returned from the village, and stowed the now-empty ship in the Misty Ring.

Then he returned to the village with the boat.

The first words spoken to Rei upon his return to the village came from Marina.

“Well, even if you say that… I can’t really explain it any other way than to say it just happened.”

In truth, Rei hadn’t acted this way with any specific plan in mind.

It really was just a matter of circumstances.

“You know… No, I mean, people always say that whenever Rei gets involved, trouble follows. I guess that’s just how it is.”

“...Wait. I don’t remember hearing that. Is that really what people say?”

Rei responded to Marina’s unexpected remark.

Marina’s remark, delivered as if to say “you can’t walk down the street without bumping into a stick,” was naturally not something Rei could accept.

But Marina, paying no heed to Rei’s reaction, moved the conversation forward.

“For now, the pirates have been gathered in the village square… but what should we do?”

“Hmm… well. For now, we need to tie them up so they can’t move. Wait, couldn’t you handle that with spirit magic?”

“Hmm, well… technically, I could do it, but it wouldn’t last very long.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Elena, who had also come to greet Rei, tilted her head in puzzlement.

“Huh? I thought Marina’s house was set up to keep intruders out with spirit magic? That magic has been working just fine for quite some time now, hasn’t it?”

“That’s true. But that’s only because that spirit has been bound to me for a long time—it’s a spirit I’m particularly compatible with. Since this is my first time here, it’s a bit difficult to set it up the same way as my house. …Well, even so, I can manage for a night or two, so there’s no need to worry.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Rei felt reassured that things would be fine for a short time, at least.

“In that case, I’ll leave it to you for now. I need to head to a nearby town right away and bring back some guards and slave traders. …Is that okay?”

Rei asked this of the old woman who had been watching them—the village chief.

Standing near the village chief were several guards, presumably as a precaution.

Suddenly addressed by Rei, the village chief nodded back without showing any particular sign of being flustered.

...Unlike the guards around her, who were completely captivated by the three rare beauties—Elena, Marina, and Vihera—perhaps it was simply a matter of experience.

“Hmm, that’s fine by me. As the head of this village, I’m just grateful that the village has been spared any harm.”

“Hearing you say that is a relief to us as well. Oh, and regarding the fishing gear and the fish…”

“I’ll make sure to prepare those as well. We were really saved by Rei this time. I’ll keep the price as low as possible.”

“Heh heh heh,” the village chief chuckled. Hearing his words, Rei nodded happily in agreement.

“Got it. So, where is the relatively large town near here?”

“Hmm… Well then. There’s a town called Jubis about three days’ journey by carriage. That’s the biggest town around here.”

“Huh… It’s closer than I expected. I thought it would take about ten days one way.”

“If it were that far out in the countryside, all sorts of problems would likely arise. …Anyway, can I leave all that to you?”

“Yes. I’ll head straight to that town, Jubis, right away. …Wait, before that—and I know this is a bit late in the game—what’s the name of this village, anyway?”

Realizing only now that he didn’t know the village’s name, Rei asked the village chief.

The village chief, who had apparently assumed Rei knew the area, widened his eyes slightly.

“This village doesn’t have much contact with the outside world, you see. There are even people here who don’t know the village’s name. But… well. For now, if you just say ‘Galanka Village,’ people should understand.”

“Got it. …So, which direction is Jubis in?”

“Once you leave the village, head east, and you’ll eventually come to a main road. If you take that road and head left, you should arrive in Jubis.”

After exchanging a few brief words with the village chief, Rei simply told Elena and the others to take care of things from here, then left the village with Set.

Seeing him in such a hurry, Elena and the others looked slightly dumbfounded, but soon they each began making preparations.

The first thing to do was to prepare a place to hold the pirates.

As Rei had mentioned earlier, Marina took charge of this, building a simple earthen cell just outside the village.

Naturally, the villagers’ feelings toward the pirates were far from friendly.

For that very reason, Marina believed it would be best to avoid contact between the pirates and the villagers as much as possible.

Rei had originally intended to have the villagers look after the pirates themselves, but he worried that the villagers might accidentally harm the pirates if they tried to handle them, so he decided to go along with Marina’s suggestion on that point.

“I wonder how long it’ll take them to get back.”

“Hmm? Considering Set’s speed, wouldn’t they be back by the end of the day? They took Set Cage with them, after all.”

Elena replied to Marina’s mutter, but Marina knew that Rei… and Set… were both a bit directionally challenged, or frequently got caught up in trouble.

It wouldn’t be surprising if, before they even reached the town of Jubis, they ran into other bandits or came across a carriage under attack by monsters.

No, in fact, she even found herself thinking that if it were only that, it would be a simple matter.

Elena, perhaps sensing a hint of anxiety in Marina’s demeanor, gently averted her gaze.

“Grr?”

“Hm? Oh, that road over there. If you head left along that, there should be a town called Jubis.”

While Elena and Marina were feeling anxious... surprisingly, Rei and Set were proceeding exactly as the village chief had instructed, without getting caught up in any particular trouble.

That said, while Rei gives the strong impression of frequently getting into trouble, in reality, it’s not actually that often.

If he were getting into trouble almost every day, year-round, he’d have no time to rest at all.

...Though, to be honest, it’s actually quite rare for someone to get caught up in trouble as often as Rei does.

Riding on Set and heading left along the highway, a town eventually comes into view.

It’s naturally smaller than Gilm, and quite a bit smaller even compared to towns like Abuero and Sabrusta.

As you’d expect from a rural area, the town isn’t particularly large.

Still, compared to the village of Garanka where Rei had been just a moment ago, it’s incomparably larger.

“There are… quite a few people here.”

There are quite a few people walking along the road toward Jubis.

In fact, compared to Galanka, the volume of foot traffic here is overwhelmingly greater.

(Galanka felt like a village that had been forgotten, after all.)

Of course, that’s just how Rei felt; it doesn’t mean that’s actually the case.

After all, they pay their taxes properly every year.

“Set, get down.”

“Grrrr!”

Seeing the town approaching, Rei asked Set to do so.

Set immediately growled and began to descend, flapping his wings.

Naturally, the people walking on the ground noticed Set’s behavior, and a few of them began to make a commotion.

But since this was nothing out of the ordinary, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

Still, out of consideration for the guards at the gate, they had landed a short distance away from the town.

“Hey. Are you sure that town over there is Jubis?”

He asked this of a man who appeared to be a merchant, standing near where Set had landed.

The merchant’s face froze in surprise… yet he still nodded in response to Rei’s words.

Was it a merchant’s pride, or simply a matter of grit?

Whatever the reason, Rei thanked the merchant for the information and, satisfied that this was indeed Jubis, headed toward the town.

Naturally, Rei, riding Set as he approached, was visible to the guards manning the gate.

They could see him, but... there was no way the guards in such a rural town could do anything about a griffin like Set.

Several guards had already gone to report to their superiors.

Rei understood the guards’ movements, but he had nothing to hide… in fact, he had come to report that he’d captured a pirate, so he didn’t seem particularly concerned as he called out to the guard standing in front of the main gate.

“I’m Rei. I’m a Rank B adventurer. I captured a pirate in Galanka, so I’d like to hand him over and have him verified.”

“...Rei? The Rei who rides a griffin... the Crimson Ray!?”

Fortunately, the guard seemed to know who Rei was. He lowered the spear he’d been holding at the ready, just in case he was attacked.

(It seems I’m somewhat well-known in Jubis after all. …I’m glad this didn’t turn into a big hassle.)

Up until now, Rei had been underestimated and picked on countless times because of his appearance.

Such incidents had stopped recently, but that was strictly limited to places like Gilm and other relatively prosperous areas.

Even Rei herself didn’t know if rumors about her had reached a backwater like Jubis.

Fortunately, however, it seemed the rumors had reached him.

Feeling relieved, Rei climbed down from Set’s back and spoke again.

“Looks like you know who I am. …Ah, here’s my guild card. So, what about the pirates? I’d like you to come as soon as possible.”

“W-wait a second! I can’t decide this on my own! I’ll go get my superiors right away!”

The guard shouted this, but Rei had also noticed that someone had left the scene earlier to call for backup when they spotted Rei and his group.

(Are they still wary of us?)

He couldn’t help but think so, but when he stopped to consider it, it made perfect sense: a famous adventurer had suddenly appeared in this backwater town, and on top of that, they were asking to be taken away because they’d captured a pirate.

It would be crazy not to find that suspicious.

In the end, Rei and Set simply waited near the main gate for the so-called superior officer to arrive.

In the meantime, the guards had nothing much to do, so they tried to process those attempting to enter the city… but since Rei and Set were behaving themselves, and many people realized they posed no threat, most of them were watching Rei with curiosity, wondering what was about to happen, and showed no intention of going through the entry procedures.

“Hey, hey, why are you all so nosy?”

“What can you do? A commotion this big doesn’t happen very often in this backwater.”

The guards each voiced their complaints.

In fact, the people around them were proof that those words were true.

If they were to force people to enter the town under these circumstances, they’d likely just voice their complaints.

...Though if Rei and Set were the kind of people who posed a threat to others, things might have been a little different.

A little time passed… As Rei and Set ate the dried meat Rei had taken out of the Misty Ring, several guards eventually emerged from the town.

Among them, the figure who stepped forward at the front was clearly a high-ranking guard—at the very least, he held a higher position than the guard who had spoken with Rei.

“Are you Rei of Crimson?”

“Yeah. And you’re the big shot among the guards, right?”

“That’s correct. So, I hear you captured a pirate. Where exactly is that pirate?”

“Do you know the village of Galanka? It’s a village quite a distance from here.”

“...Garanka? Ah, I know it. I’ve never been there, but I’ve at least heard the name.”

Given that he was the captain of the guards, it was only to be expected that he remembered the name Galanka.

In fact, when Rei mentioned the name “Garanka,” many of those listening nearby looked completely confused, as if they had no idea what he was talking about.

“There was a pirate hideout on a deserted island near that Galanka. So, the villagers asked me to help, and I caught those guys. But… if you know where Galanka is, that’d make things easier, but it’s pretty far away, right? That’s why I came all the way out here.”

At Rei’s words, the captain of the guards tilted his head in puzzlement.
