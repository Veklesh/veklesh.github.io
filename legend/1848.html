The New Year's party is coming to an end.

It’s already past midnight, and under normal circumstances, many people would be asleep by now.

Still, perhaps because it’s the once-a-year party, or because everyone is excited and in high spirits, not many people are fighting off sleep.

“So, I’ll take on that flood control project, then.”

“Hmm, I’ll leave it to you. I’ll pay you well, so please rest assured.”

“Thank you. That’s fortunate. We have many earth mages in our group, so we’re quite skilled at that sort of work.”

Just as I was listening to that conversation...

“Damn it! Is there really no hope for Lady Elena anymore!? After all this… after I’ve finally gotten this strong…”

“Just give it up. No matter how strong you get, you’ll never be able to earn a nickname like Crimson, will you?”

“That’s…”

“Anyway, look. See those girls over there? They’ve been staring at you nonstop since a little while ago.”

“What!? Is that true!?”

The noble who had been after Elena seems to have found a new love interest.

In any case, many of the nobles are spending their time as they please.

Those who managed to strike good deals at this party are in high spirits, sipping wine and savoring the dishes prepared by Georgima and her apprentices.

Those whose negotiations fell through were eating and drinking to suppress their frustration.

Amidst all this, a few nobles had gathered in a corner of the party hall, each engaged in their own conversation.

“Still, today’s party was full of surprises. I never would have thought that Lady Elena would perform such a magnificent dance.”

“Indeed. When I saw that dance, I was completely captivated. I got so excited, even though I’m not a young girl anymore.”

“If you ask me, so was I. That dance was truly magnificent. …And then there’s Elena’s partner, the adventurer. He goes by the alias ‘Crimson’ and is said to be quite powerful.”

As one of the nobles said this, several others who had watched Rei’s mock battle earlier immediately chimed in.

“I saw the mock battle Rei fought, and that strength was the real deal. …Plus, he has a griffin as a familiar.”

“That’s right. To be honest, when I first heard about it, I wondered why on earth Duke Kerebel would approve of some adventurer-type as a match for Lady Elena… but seeing that he has a griffin as a familiar, I can’t help but be convinced.”

“...N-no, that’s not it. That man’s strength is…”

Suddenly, one of the nobles who had been listening silently up until then muttered that.

The tone of his voice betrayed unmistakable fear.

Precisely because this noble was usually so dignified, the other nobles around him were puzzled by his reaction.

“Baron Chilmno, what’s wrong? That’s not like you at all.”

At the words of his friend—a friend from before he inherited his title—the man known as Baron Chilmuno took a sip of wine, as if to calm himself.

Perhaps the wine was quite strong, for his mouth grew hot… and as if to expel that heat, Baron Chilmuno exhaled.

“You see, I once went to the battlefield with Rei. You know, that war with the Bestir Empire a few years back.”

“Ah, come to think of it, he did mention he was going to participate.”

Even among the Noble Faction, not everyone is financially well-off.

Especially for noble houses with lower titles, like the barons, it’s not uncommon for them to participate in wars to earn merit, hoping for rewards or a higher title as a result.

Baron Chilmno was no exception; he took part in the war against the Bestir Empire.

Having trained rigorously, he was confident in his own strength, and the soldiers under his command had been rigorously drilled, possessing the skill to be rightly called elite troops.

But… what Baron Chilmno witnessed on the battlefield was Rei inflicting massive damage on the Bestir Empire’s vanguard with a gigantic fiery tornado.

And immediately after that, he saw Rei using a large scythe to kill enemy soldiers one after another.

Because he was wielding a scythe, Baron Chilmno couldn’t help but see his actions as if he were mowing down human lives just as one might mow weeds with a scythe.

Indeed, the sight of him wielding that great scythe and dancing as he reaped the lives of his enemies would not have been out of place if he had been called the Grim Reaper.

Having described the battle, Baron Chilmno—perhaps somewhat calmer now that he had spoken—continued his story slowly.

“To be honest, I think Duke Kerebel’s decision to forge friendly relations with the Neutral Faction was the wisest choice possible. If it had come to fighting Margrave Daskar, who keeps Rei as his right-hand man…”

A shudder ran through him.

Perhaps imagining what it would be like to face Rei as an enemy, Baron Chilmno’s body trembled.

Seeing this, those listening to Baron Chilmuno realized that their own understanding of the situation had been naive.

In particular, the nobles who had watched the mock battle and thought they understood Rei’s strength highly praised Duke Kerebel’s decision to build friendly relations with the Neutral Faction, just as Baron Chilmuno had said.

After all, they wanted to avoid at all costs the prospect of opposing the Neutral Faction and encountering Rei on the battlefield.

“Is it really enough to make a man of Baron Chilmno’s caliber say such a thing? …From what I’ve seen of him, I certainly wouldn’t think so.”

One of the nobles listening to Baron Chilmuno remarked as he watched Rei chatting and laughing with several other nobles.

The fact that his gaze had taken on a slightly probing quality was likely because he had come to understand that Rei possessed that much power.

“That’s right. I’ll say this to you all as well. For heaven’s sake, for the love of God, you’d better not do anything to antagonize Rei. If you antagonize Rei, the only thing waiting for you is ruin. After all, Rei isn’t the type to go easy on someone just because they’re a noble.”

Baron Chilmno knew the fact that, during the war that earned Rei his nickname, he had swung his scythe without the slightest mercy, even against the nobles of the Kingdom of Mireana.

Normally, one wouldn’t dare attack a noble so casually.

But Rei, who did so without a second thought, was truly a natural enemy to the nobility.

Even if they tried to silence him with power, he saw no value in that power whatsoever.

Or perhaps they did see some value in it, but it was a value they could simply ignore.

Even if they hired bodyguards, there was no way they could find opponents capable of easily defeating Rei—let alone Set, the griffin.

And as long as Set is there, even if they hired someone Rei truly couldn’t defeat, it would be difficult to pursue them if they escaped into the sky.

On top of that, if they let him escape, there’s no telling when or where he’ll strike next.

Anyone strong enough to defeat Rei would naturally command a hefty price, and unless one were exceptionally wealthy, it would be difficult to keep them on the payroll.

Hearing Baron Chirumno’s words, those listening unconsciously swallowed the saliva in their mouths.

“Ha ha ha. You’re a smart one, aren’t you?”

“No, no, that’s not true. If anything, I think you’re the one who gets it.”

A somewhat unexpected scene unfolded before Rei’s eyes.

That was because Brewitt and Ranigus were laughing together.

Although they were both nobles, Brewitt wasn’t actually staying in Anesis, and they belonged to different factions—Ranigus was part of the Royal Faction, while Brewitt was part of the Noble Faction.

And yet, these two must have gotten along remarkably well. They had become friends in the blink of an eye.

A short distance away from Ranigus and Brewitt, Teres was complimenting Elena on her dress and her earlier dance.

Further away still, Ara was watching Teres talking to Elena with a look of envy, but she had to attend to a nobleman who had approached her and couldn’t leave the spot right away.

The public perception of Ara is that she is Elena’s confidante. …And in truth, that isn’t exactly wrong.

To the nobles of the Noble Faction, Ara was quite an attractive prospect as a marriage partner.

That is precisely why so many young men are trying to woo Ara.

...That said, she herself has absolutely no interest in men; if she had to choose between talking to these men and going to be with Elena, she would far prefer the latter.

If the men hitting on Ara had tried anything forceful, she could have used her legendary strength to beat them senseless.

But whether the men knew this, or simply thought it would be unseemly to resort to violence, they just tried to woo Ara normally.

“What do you say, Ara? There’s a vast snowfield near my House this time of year. It’s a pure white expanse with no footprints. I’d really love to see it with you.”

“I’m sorry, but as the captain of Lady Elena’s guard, I can’t just leave Anesis so easily.”

In reality, she had been in Gilm with Elena the whole time, but she turned down his invitation while making sure not to reveal that fact.

“Well then, how about you come explore Anesis with me? I just got here, so it’d be a big help if you could show me around.”

“I’m too busy with work right now; I don’t have the time. I’m sorry.”

Aside from that exchange...

“Ara, your eyes hold such a strong will—they’re truly beautiful. I could look at them forever, if I could.”

“I want to celebrate having met a gem like you.”

“That dignified aura of yours… I could watch you forever.”

And so, compliments like these keep coming one after another.

(Ugh... this is miserable.)

Watching Ara like that, Rei brings a dish she’d taken from a nearby table to her mouth.

Although the food had been prepared several hours ago, as expected of dishes made by Georgima and her apprentices, it was simply delicious.

Since these were dishes served at a party, they must have focused on dishes that didn’t lose their flavor even when cold.

Among the dishes, there are some that are incredibly delicious while hot, but lose their flavor drastically once they cool down... you could call it the "shelf life of deliciousness."

These are dishes suitable for a restaurant, but not really meant to be eaten at a party like this.

Georgima, of course, understands this, which is why she prepared dishes like the one Rei is currently eating as the main course.

And yet, the fact that you can still enjoy a variety of dishes even after they’ve cooled down really shows Georgima’s true colors.

“Hey, Rei. What do you think?”

Rei, who had been enjoying the meal, snapped back to reality when Brewitt suddenly spoke to him.

But since he hadn’t been listening at all, there was no way he could answer when asked what he thought.

“Sorry, I was eating and didn’t hear you. What were you talking about?”

So, he apologized honestly and asked what Blueitt had wanted to say.

Brewitt looked slightly exasperated at Rei’s reaction, but he didn’t voice any complaints and simply explained the situation.

“I was telling you there’s a good hunting ground near the Ranigus House. Apparently, no monsters show up there, so you can hunt safely. Are you interested?”

“Interested… well, I’m afraid I’m not really that interested.”

The prey you catch through hunting is, basically, wild animals.

Especially since Ranigus says there are no monsters there right now, that’s only natural.

But… setting aside the enjoyment of the hunt itself, if you consider the taste of the meat purely, wild animals simply can’t compare to monster meat.

Some people say that wild, gamey flavor is what makes it good, and Rei doesn’t deny that… but still, if he’s going to eat it anyway, he honestly wanted to eat meat that tasted good.

Of course, there are monsters that taste bad, starting with goblins.

However, the meat of high-rank monsters like the ones Rei fights is definitely tastier than that of wild animals, thanks to its magical properties.

In some cases, there’s even meat that wouldn’t be out of place on the tables of nobles or royalty.

“Is that so? Hunting is actually pretty fun, you know. …Ah, no. I guess you’re used to that kind of hunting, Rei.”

“Yeah. I don’t know what kind of hunting nobles do, but living with Set, I end up hunting monsters a lot. For the meat.”

In reality, it was also because he wanted unknown magic stones, but he couldn’t very well teach her about beast magic, so he just brushed it off with a vague excuse.

And so, having managed to shake off the men who kept hitting on them, Rei and the others spent the time until Liberte announced the end of the party in the company of Ara, among friends they could relax with.
