Upon arriving at the lord’s manor, Rei and his companions were immediately ushered inside.

The gatekeepers were naturally on friendly terms with Rei and his group, and since they had been instructed by their superiors to let them through immediately, there was no need to keep them waiting.

As usual, merchants were gathered near the lord’s mansion, but upon seeing the group enter so quickly, they could only watch with looks of envy.

(There are fewer merchants than before. Is this thanks to Lord Daskar’s tireless efforts? Or perhaps they decided they couldn’t meet Lord Daskar and went off in search of other profitable opportunities.)

Thinking about the smaller number of merchants, I entered the lord’s mansion…

“Lady Elena!?”

Instantly, that voice echoed around her.

There was no need to wonder who had called out—it was obvious.

Standing there was a woman with short hair—a familiar sight not only to Elena, who had been called by name, but to everyone else as well.

“Ara, sorry to keep you waiting. I’m back now.”

Elena replied with a smile just as happy as Ara’s.

Seeing Elena like that, Ara nodded, looking truly… genuinely happy from the bottom of her heart.

“I’m so glad you’re safe…”

“It’s a bit awkward when you get that emotional. We’ve been talking through the Paired Orbs almost every day, haven’t we?”

"Of course I know that, but even so, I’m still just so happy to be able to see you in person like this."

It must have been sheer joy, or perhaps sheer emotion. Even in the usually strong-willed Ara’s eyes, a faint glimmer of tears could be seen.

“Um, well… Lady Ara?”

Seeing Ara like this, the maid who had come to guide Rei and the others to Daskar’s quarters was at a loss.

She’d come to escort them to the office, only to find this sudden turn of events—it was only natural she’d be taken aback.

“Rei, I’m going to talk with Ara for a bit. Can I leave the explanation to Daskar to you?”

“Sure, that’s fine. Ara must have been worried about a lot of things, so you can leave this to us. Just make sure to come see us later.”

“Mm-hmm.”

Elena’s position is that of a representative sent by the Noble Faction.

Given that, Rei’s words reflected his judgment that Elena needed to hear the full story regarding this incident.

It was precisely because Elena understood this that she nodded immediately.

“Alright, then. Could you show us to Lord Daskar?”

The maid nodded at Rei’s words and began walking, leaving Elena and Ara behind.

That said, since their destination was the office—a place Rei had visited countless times—they didn’t really need a guide.

Still, the reason they’ve arranged for a guide is likely because there are those who might try to take advantage of this opportunity to do something suspicious.

It wasn’t that he suspected Rei and the others of such behavior, but considering the risk of “just in case,” he couldn’t simply let them go freely.

……In fact, the fact that even Ara and Elena each had a maid assigned to them showed just how thorough they were.

“I wonder if Daskar is getting pretty tired by now. I suppose I really need to help him recover from that fatigue.”

“Please don’t do that.”

Rei blurted that out instinctively to Marina, who was speaking with a mischievous tone.

In reality, there aren’t many people capable of pulling a stunt like that on Daskar, and Marina is probably the prime example.

Whether Daskar actually wanted that or not was another matter.

“Oh, but isn’t it necessary to help Daskar relax after he’s tired from work?”

“I won’t deny that, but don’t you think it would be pointless if it ended up making Lord Daskar even more tired?”

“Is that so?”

“Um, well… if possible, I’d appreciate it if you could keep it to a minimum…”

A maid who had been listening to Rei and Marina’s conversation spoke up, as if to defend Daskar.

As a maid who served Daskar, she naturally knew just how busy he was at the moment.

She wanted to avoid causing Lord Daskar any further mental strain, if at all possible.

That was her intention, but...

“Oh, but a change of pace is necessary, isn’t it? Daskar is so busy right now that he has no mental bandwidth. So, we need to help him with that, even if just a little, don’t we?”

“Well…”

It wasn’t just Daskar; his subordinates had been assigned various tasks as well, so it was undoubtedly true that they were busy.

Even so, is it really okay to tease him like Marina suggests...? The fact that I’m even thinking this is probably because I’m a maid.

She wondered if it would really be a welcome change of pace for Daskar, her respected master, to be teased by Marina.

With that doubt in mind… yet, as long as they kept walking, they would naturally arrive at their destination.

Should I just go ahead and tell Daskar that Marina and the others have arrived?

I hesitated for a moment, but as a maid, I simply couldn’t just send away someone who had come to see Daskar—especially someone he had specifically told me to bring to him.

Out of pride as a maid, there was no way I could do such a thing.

“Excuse me, Daskar-sama. I’ve brought your guests.”

“Oh, come in.”

Hearing the voice from beyond the study door, the maid opened it.

Even to Rei’s eyes, the door looked as opulent as ever, and beyond it stood Daskar, seemingly buried under a mountain of papers on his desk.

Still, compared to before—when Rei and the others had been commissioned to take revenge on Jaya—the number of documents had decreased considerably.

“Bring me a cold drink and something to nibble on.”

At Daskar’s command, the maid bowed, closed the door, and left.

Watching her leave, Daskar set the papers he was holding on the table and approached Rei and the others.

“I’m glad you’re back. To be honest, I didn’t think things would get this big.”

He must have been truly relieved that Rei and the others had returned. Daskar smiled with genuine joy and gestured for them to sit on the sofa.

Since they’d been communicating through the Paired Orbs, he already knew they were safe and sound.

Still, seeing him in person was vastly different from seeing him through the Paired Orb.

Coincidentally, it was the same feeling Ara had when she saw Elena.

“Yeah. Honestly, I didn’t think it would turn into such a big deal either. Though, it seems like it turned out pretty well for Gilm.”

Securing nearly a thousand people was by no means a bad outcome for Gilm.

On top of that, nearly a thousand women—all of them so strikingly beautiful they could be called beauties or young beauties—were certainly something for Gilm, as lord of the domain, to rejoice over.

Furthermore, the people who had been active in the resistance, as well as the carriages and horses used to transport them, were certainly not a burden for Gilm, who was in the midst of expansion construction.

And the presence of the Zolge Trading Company, which was effectively being held by the throat by Daskar, wasn’t a bad thing either.

Of course, it’s not all good news.

Given that nearly a thousand women were brainwashed and forced into prostitution, many of them harbor a deep aversion to men.

Furthermore, some may decide to return to their hometowns or settle in the villages and towns they pass through on their way to Gilm.

As for the Resistance members, if Daskar governs fairly, there’s no problem; but if he becomes an arrogant noble, based on past experience, they’re highly likely to resume their activities as the Resistance.

Furthermore, the Zolge Trading Company has even explicitly stated that, while they may be cautious at first, once they have established a firm foothold, they will do business with criminal organizations if it proves profitable.

Ultimately, the group led by Rei could be a huge asset to Gilm if handled properly, but if mishandled, they could even become a liability.

(Well, that’s not really my concern. Once they’re brought to Gilm, it’s all up to Daskar’s skills.)

Even as he thought this, Rei wasn’t worried about Daskar’s abilities.

After all, he’s managed to run Gilm smoothly up to this point, despite everything.

In reality, there had been various minor issues, but they had been properly resolved before they could escalate.

Therefore, Rei judged that there was no need to worry that much.

Naturally, Daskar understood that perfectly well too...

“I suppose so.”

In response to Rei’s words, he replied with a complex expression—one that showed both joy and a hint of difficulty.

“That’s where Daskar gets to show off his skills, isn’t it? Besides, didn’t you say before that you’d make this city of Gilm the greatest in the Kingdom of Mireana?”

“Marina! That was when we were kids, wasn’t it!?”

Hearing those words from Marina’s mouth, Daskar cried out in alarm.

Childhood… that was right around the time he proposed to Marina.

Bringing up that time was something Daskar absolutely wanted to avoid.

The sight of the usually stoic Daskar looking flustered wasn’t exactly rare for Rei…

After all, while he’s often teased when he’s with Rei, he’s teased even more when he’s with Marina.

Despite this, when the maid returned to the office with tea and food, Daskar managed to save face.

“Well then, Lord Daskar. I’ll take my leave now.”

After the maid bowed and left, Daskar finally spoke to Marina.

“Really, Marina, you never change.”

“Is that so? Well, I am a dark elf, after all. But you like that, don’t you?”

As Marina said this with a mischievous smile, Daskar brought a sandwich from the plate to his mouth, as if trying to hide something.

Following her lead, Rei and Byune also took a bite of their sandwiches.

Since they were meant to accompany tea, they weren’t substantial sandwiches—just bite-sized ones—but they were filled with a variety of ingredients, including ham, cheese, vegetables, fish, and meat.

The taste was more than satisfying for Rei and Byune as well.

As they ate the sandwiches... eventually, the conversation turned to their travels.

“Using a sand ship as lodging, huh? …There must have been a thousand of you, right? Was that really okay?”

“We managed somehow, since quite a few people squeezed into the cargo hold—the part of the ship normally used to store food and other supplies. Though, it’s true we were packed in pretty tightly.”

“I see. …But now that we’ve lost that sand ship, things are going to get tough. Even though it’s warm during the day, it’s gotten quite cool in the mornings and evenings.”

“That’s true. But it’s just getting cooler—it doesn’t feel like it’s getting cold or freezing yet, so I still think it’s bearable. Besides, the Zolge Trading Company should have stocked up on camping bedding and such.”

Even though we’re heading into fall, it’s still summer, so as long as you sleep with even a single blanket, you won’t be freezing cold… or anything like that.

Besides, even if it gets a little chilly, sleeping in a carriage should protect us from the rain and wind.

“As the lord of Gilm, having taken various precautions, I’d be happy if as many people as possible arrived here safely.”

“That’s true. Since I went to the trouble of saving them, I want them to survive, too. Anyway, once things here settle down a bit, I’ll pop over there again.”

“...Having Set around really does seem convenient.”

Daskar remarked thoughtfully.

Set’s speed of travel far surpasses even that of a carriage.

Even though he parted ways with Sura and the others after entering the Kingdom of Mireana, the fact that he was able to reach Gilm in less than two days speaks to his speed.

For someone in Daskar’s position, possessing such mobility could only be the stuff of envy.

That said, he certainly couldn’t possibly tell his subordinates to go out and make a griffin their familiar.

Saying something like that would be tantamount to telling them to commit suicide, and above all, it’s not as if one can easily come across a high-rank monster like a griffin in the first place.

“That’s right. I’m truly grateful to have Set here. …Anyway, let’s get right to business.”

“Hm? Oh, right. Rei, I naturally want you to go to Treant Forest to haul timber. To be honest, it’s great that the lumberjacks are working hard, but we’re starting to run short-handed when it comes to transporting the wood. Plus, it seems monsters have started appearing there recently.”

“I suppose so. Once the attacks on the Treant Forest itself stop, the monster population will naturally increase. …Got it. I’ll handle things over here. If I leave it to Set, he’ll take care of the monsters as he sees fit.”

With that, they went on to discuss the tasks for the rest of the group.
