The feast celebrating the resolution of all problems—the World Tree's full recovery, the defeat of the giant cicada responsible for its affliction, and the elimination of the monster horde that had attacked the settlement—lasted until midnight.

Naturally, this meant going to bed late, and consequently, waking up late the next morning.

On such a morning... Rei had come with Set to the area near the stable of the house they were renting.

It was still early morning, a time when most of the settlement was still asleep.

If one were to do something requiring discretion, there was no more suitable time than this.

"Ever since I came to this village, I feel like I've been absorbing magic stones strangely... Well, I guess it's only natural with so many monsters around."

In Rei's hand were two magic stones obtained from the Forest Green Wolf, a green wolf, and a magic stone about the size of two fists.

The latter magic stone was one that Optis had taken from a giant cicada during last night's feast when he was in high spirits.

Although the giant cicada hadn't been completely stripped, Rei was fortunate to have been able to extract at least the magic stones for now.

He put the magic stone from the giant cicada, which could be considered the main prize this time, aside for the moment, and turned his attention to the magic stone from the Forest Green Wolf.

"A Forest Green Wolf? It's a Rank D monster, so it's not exactly a weakling... but this time, most of the others were goblins and kobolds."

The Forest Green Wolf wasn't listed in the monster dictionary that Rei had, so he had asked Ragdo about its abilities during last night's banquet.

The Forest Green Wolf, a Rank D monster, is a wolf-like monster that, as its name suggests, is adept at blending into the green of the forest.

Its fur is a mixture of light and dark shades of green, and it excels at group ambushes, camouflaging itself in groups among the trees and bushes of the forest before attacking its prey all at once.

Even so, the Forest Green Wolf's physical abilities weren't that high, which is why it was classified as Rank D.

Of course, as Rei had said, Rank D monsters were not generally considered weak, but that was only in general terms, and for Rei, it was a different story.

In fact, Rei obtained the Forest Green Wolf's magic stone when he charged into a group of monsters and used Death Scythe to slash and kill every enemy in sight.

"Considering this monster's abilities, if all goes well... Set!"

Hoping that he would learn the skill he expected, Rei threw the Forest Green Wolf's magic stone, and Set caught it in his beak... and swallowed it the next moment.

[Set has acquired the skill "Optical Camouflage Lv. 4".]

Rei smiled at the voice echoing in his mind.

Given its ability to blend into its surroundings, he had predicted that if it were to level up, it would probably be with optical camouflage.

His prediction had been spot on, so he couldn't help but be happy.

Moreover, Optical Camouflage was one of the most powerful skills Set possessed.

It wasn't flashy like Fire Breath; it was simply a skill that made his own form and the forms of those touching Set disappear. But with Set, a griffin, vanishing and moving, it was unbearable for anyone hostile.

"Now, the only question is how much the skill's duration has increased..."

At level one, it was ten seconds; at level two, twenty seconds; at level three, forty seconds. Doubling, doubling, doubling each time. If Rei's prediction was correct, reaching level four should mean Set could remain invisible for eighty seconds.

To confirm this, Rei called out to Set.

"Set, could you try it out right away? Disappear to the limit at level four."

"GRRRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set growled and activated his optical camouflage.

Before their eyes, Set began to blend into the surrounding scenery.

Then, after Set vanished, Rei began counting the time in his mind.

Ten seconds, twenty seconds, thirty seconds, forty seconds passed... and just as it neared eighty seconds, Set reappeared.

Though Rei hadn't counted exactly eighty seconds, since he hadn't timed it precisely anyway, merely sensing it was around eighty seconds was sufficient.

"Alright. That was as expected. ...Well, considering the example of Flying Slash, its performance spikes dramatically once the skill reaches level five, so it might not necessarily take 160 seconds once it hits level five."

"Grrr?"

How about it? How about it? Set tilted his head and growled, prompting Rei to smile and ruffle his fur.

"Set's abilities really are incredible."

Perhaps pleased by Rei's praise, Set purred happily like a cat.

Rei stroked Set for a moment, then picked up another Forest Green Wolf magic stone.

"Now then, I hope I can learn some new skills with Death Scythe...!"

With those words, he threw the magic stone into the air and swung Death Scythe in a flash.

The sharp blow cut the magic stone in two... but even after several seconds had passed, no announcement message about acquiring a skill played in his mind.

"No luck?"

Rei looked a little disappointed.

Since Set had acquired a skill, perhaps the Death Scythe could too.

That was the thought he had held onto.

But he'd experienced absorbing magic stones without gaining skills countless times before.

That's precisely why he recovered quickly.

...Though the fact that the remaining magic stone belonged to a giant cicada probably had something to do with it too.

"Now then, what should we do with this magic stone... What do you think?"

"Guru?"

At Rei's words, Set tilted his head slightly.

For Set, it was clear he meant to let Rei decide as he saw fit.

"Honestly, Set is such a good person. Wait, he's not a person, so should I say a good griffin? Anyway, I'm really torn about what to do with this."

It was a magic stone from a giant cicada, a monster that was special in many ways. Rei was certain that he would be able to acquire a skill with it.

If that was the case, since Set had acquired skills with the Forest Green Wolf's magic stone, wouldn't it be okay to let Death Scythe acquire skills too?

While thinking that way, it was also true that he wanted to make Set stronger.

Although he usually acted together with Set, it wasn't as if they never acted separately, as in this case.

There had been times before when only Set had been transferred to a different location, and times when they had acted separately during the guild's rank-up exams.

Thinking about it that way, Rei felt that it might be better to have Set learn a new skill or level up his existing skills so that he wouldn't get injured in an emergency.

"Hmm... What should I do?"

"Grrr..."

Set let out a growl in agreement with Rei's words.

Rei pondered for a few seconds, but then suddenly noticed a glowing orb floating in the air.

Rei didn't need to think to know what it was.

"Is it really okay for the World Tree to move around so freely like this? Well, I guess it's fine since the main trunk is still firmly rooted..."

Rei's gaze shifted to the World Tree, visible from anywhere in the settlement.

Seeing its lush, green leaves, it was hard to believe it had been on the verge of withering just yesterday.

But the fact remained it had nearly withered... no, nearly died. Honestly, Rei thought it should probably rest a little longer.

(Well, from what Marina said, it seems my magic is the reason it can become a light sphere, so I can't really say that too strongly.)

Telling it not to move after granting it freedom of movement would be cruel in more ways than one.

The light sphere drifted toward Rei and flickered as if greeting him.

"You're free, aren't you? Or should I say, you're enjoying your freedom?"

At Rei's slightly exasperated words, the light sphere protested by flashing intensely.

Flying around Rei like that in this state, it was incredibly annoying from her perspective.

"Alright, alright. I'm sorry, just stop it."

After offering a half-hearted apology, the orb seemed satisfied and drifted away from Rei.

Watching the orb, Rei held the giant cicada magic stone in his hand and pondered for a moment.

Should he absorb the magic stone in front of this orb?

But after a moment's thought, he decided it probably wouldn't matter.

After all, the light sphere could only express itself through flickering.

That meant even if it knew about beast magic, it couldn't tell anyone.

(Well, Marina's clan seems to have deep ties to the World Tree... But even if they found out my secret, including Optis, I doubt they'd do anything about it. Still, I should probably make sure.)

Looking at the light sphere, Rei opened his mouth as if to convince himself.

"Got it? From now on, Set and I are going to do something important... really important. It's fine if you watch this, but don't you dare tell anyone about it, okay?"

The orb nodded in response to Rei's words, flickering repeatedly.

Deciding that was its sign of agreement, Rei stopped worrying about the orb's presence and turned his gaze back to the giant cicada's magic stone.

Should he have Set absorb the magic stone, roughly the size of two of Rei's fists? Or should he have Death Scythe absorb it?

(Even if I have Set absorb it, it's quite large compared to the magic stones from previous monsters. ...Can he swallow it?)

For Set to absorb a magic stone, he needed to get it inside his body. That meant he had to swallow it.

Most of the magic stones obtained so far were small, easily swallowed by Set, who was two meters long.

But one the size of two fists... wouldn't that be a bit hard to swallow? That's what I thought...

"Guru?"

Watching Set tilt his head slightly, I somehow felt sure it would probably be fine.

"...Yeah, I guess I'll let Set absorb it after all."

Rei, who had been watching Set, made the decision so decisively it made one wonder what the hesitation from a moment ago had been about.

"Set, you absorb this magic stone."

"Gurururuu?"

Is that okay? Set tilted his head slightly, looking at Rei, who nodded in response.

"Yes. This magic stone will undoubtedly become part of your power, Set. ...So, please absorb it."

"Grrrrr..."

Rei understood why Death Scythe was wanted to be absorbed.

Having been born through demon beast magic himself, Set naturally desired to absorb many magic stones.

But even so, he wondered if it might be okay to let Death Scythe absorb it this time.

The main reason was, of course, the battle against the giant cicada this time, just like Rei.

Fighting separately from Rei had given Set plenty to think about.

He had sulked when it came to the Aerowings' magic stones, but back then, he hadn't expected to actually fight separately from Rei.

Of course, Set and Rei had fought apart several times before.

But even so, this battle gave Set plenty to ponder.

Though Rei caught Set's gaze, when Set urged Rei with his eyes to hurry up and absorb it, Set himself was also interested in the giant cicada's magicite.

"Gururuu"

Expressing his gratitude by rubbing his face against Rei's, Set's beak seized the giant cicada's magic stone and swallowed it whole in the next instant.

Watching this, Ray wondered what skill he would acquire... or worse, if he wouldn't acquire any skill at all... Just as he was thinking this...

[Set has acquired the skill 'King's Intimidation Lv.2']

That message flashed through both Rei and Set's minds.

"King's Intimidation? ...King?"

They had previously obtained Royal Intimidation when absorbing the Orc King's magic stone.

Since he was an Orc King, acquiring Royal Intimidation made sense... but this time, he wasn't exactly a king.

(Could it be that I just don't know, and it's actually the king of cicada-type monsters or something?)

Rei tilted his head, then shifted his gaze to the light orb. It was flickering wildly as it darted around Set, perhaps startled by having swallowed the magic stone.

Set followed the orb with his eyes too, but didn't seem inclined to do anything about it.

(Is it because it absorbed the World Tree's sap and magic power? That seems somewhat understandable... No, the magic power is mine. If so, it's a monster that grew by absorbing my magic power and the World Tree's sap, so it possesses the King's Authority? Then... could it be...?)

Suddenly, a thought flashed through Rei's mind: If he used his own magic, he might be able to create another giant cicada-like monster and absorb its magic stone...
