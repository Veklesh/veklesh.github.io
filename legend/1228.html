The identity of the voice echoing through the room was obvious to everyone present, requiring no thought whatsoever.

Among the members who had ventured into the dungeon this time, Johanna was the only one who felt such an obsession toward Set.

Of course, that was excluding Rei.

In any case, the first thing Johanna saw upon entering the room was, unsurprisingly, the enormous silver lion... and Rei and the others gathered around it. But above all, it was Set, purring happily beside Rei.

That alone wouldn't have been a problem, but seeing Set bleeding profusely from all over his body, Johanna couldn't possibly stay silent.

She ordered the men to lay Vihera, whom she was carrying on her back, onto the cloth spread on the floor, then hurried over to Set.

"Hey, Set. Are you okay!?"

"Grrrr..."

In response to Johanna's words, Set growled to indicate he was fine.

Even hearing that sound, Johanna couldn't relax. She began examining Set's body directly.

Ara dodged Johanna as she lunged forward, but Johanna didn't even notice her evasive movement.

She thoroughly examined Set's body... and eventually, upon confirming there were truly no injuries serious enough to be called critical, she breathed a sigh of relief.

"Phew... That's good. Looks like there are hardly any injuries left. Are you okay, Set? Did it hurt?"

"GRRRR!"

In response to Johanna's concerned question, Set growled energetically, signaling he was fine!

Silver Lions have high defense but their attack power isn't that great—compared to other S-rank monsters, of course—which is precisely why the wounds weren't that deep.

However, while most of those listening nearby thought, "He still has energy left," Johanna gently stroked Set's head.

"So, you're tired, huh?"

"Huh? Tired? I thought he sounded fine."

One of Johanna's companions muttered, tilting their head.

The others seemed to feel the same way.

Johanna's companions... even Elk and Min wore expressions of surprise.

For Min, who was usually calm and collected, it was rare for him to show such an obvious expression.

In that sense, most of those present could be considered fortunate... but almost none of them understood that.

"Well, he fought a monster higher-ranked than himself, like a Silver Lion. Set must be tired."

At Rei's words, several people turned surprised expressions toward Set.

Those were the ones who hadn't witnessed the battle with the Silver Lion.

Conversely, those who had fought alongside him against the Silver Lion looked convinced that Set was indeed exhausted.

After healing Rei, Set, Elk, and Ara's wounds with potions, everyone's attention finally turned to the Silver Lion.

"...Rei, isn't it about time? The heart's effect weakens the longer you wait, right?"

Urged by Marina, and similarly met with intense stares from Elk and Min, Rei stood up from leaning against the Silver Lion, understanding his body still ached.

The intensity of Elk and Min's gazes stemmed from the plan to restore Vihera's consciousness first, followed by Rodos'.

In other words, to restore Rhodes' consciousness, they needed to extract the heart from the Silver Lion as quickly as possible and get Grimm to act.

"Grim, can you hear me? Grim?"

Using the Paired Orbs, Rei called out to Grimm.

Most of the wounds on his face had healed, but the damage inflicted by the Silver Lion's attack through the Dragon Robe wasn't visible, making potion recovery difficult.

Well, drinking potions could heal him from within, but the downside was that it would numb his sense of taste for a while.

For Rei, who looked forward to meals, that was something he'd prefer to avoid if possible.

Of course, if there were still battles ahead... or if they had to escape the dungeon under their own power, he would have forced himself to drink the potion.

But Rei had reached the lowest level using Grimm's power.

And returning to the surface... more precisely, to the dungeon's entrance area... was also planned to be handled by Grimm.

They hadn't explicitly made a promise, but based on Rei's past experiences, he could reasonably expect Grimm to deliver.

Even if that failed, the other combatants besides Rei were more than sufficiently powerful.

(Once Vihera regained consciousness, she'd undoubtedly want to fight.)

While pondering this, they waited for the Paired Orb's reaction...

"Hm? Oh, Rei? You're contacting me like this, so you won, I see?"

"Yeah, it was a tough fight, but we managed."

'A tough fight, huh? Hmm. The Silver Lion is a fairly powerful monster, but Zepair...'

Lord Zepair could have defeated it easily without struggling this much.

Rei hurriedly cut off Grimm's words before they escaped his lips.

The only ones here who knew the details of his situation were Elena and Marina.

Well, Vihera knew too, but she was unconscious right now.

He had absolutely no intention of revealing his secret to the others.

"More importantly, I defeated the Silver Lion as promised. Come over here and help restore Vihera and Rodos' consciousness."

'I understand.'

"Huh!?"

The voice that reached his ears didn't come from the Paired Orbs, but from right beside Rei.

He spun around instantly to find the familiar figure of Grimm standing there.

Just seconds ago, they'd been conversing through the Paired Orbs, yet now he was suddenly standing beside him.

Rei understood perfectly well that he must have used some sort of teleportation magic, but even so, it was impossible not to be startled.

The sudden appearance of Grimm startled Rei and the others as well.

Especially for those meeting Grimm for the first time today, this was their second encounter.

Because of that, despite the overwhelming pressure Grim radiated—a pressure he couldn't fully conceal—they nearly sank to the floor but managed to stay standing.

Paying no heed to the reactions around him, Grimm's skull eyes fell naturally upon the figure of the Silver Lion lying on the floor.

"Well, well. A rather large silver lion, isn't it?"

"Quite? ...Is this it?"

Given the silver lion's length of about six meters, even Rei probably hadn't expected the phrase "quite large" to be used.

He simply stared at Grimm in stunned silence.

"Hmm. Slightly above average... that's about the size of it."

"I see. ...Huh?"

That was a strange thing to ask.

That thought flashed through Rei's mind, and she tilted her head slightly.

Then, after a moment's thought... he understood why.

"Hold on a second. Average... Are there really that many Silver Lions?"

"Hmm. Not exactly forming packs, but there are quite a few of them."

Grim answered Rei's question without hesitation... and those who heard it—especially those who had actually participated in the battle—twitched their cheeks.

They couldn't believe that the Silver Lions they'd barely managed to defeat with this force were still numerous elsewhere.

Seeing Rei and the others react like this, Grimm chuckled amusedly.

"Ho ho ho. The world is far vaster than you imagine. Realizing that alone is a good thing, isn't it? ...Well then, shall we get down to the main topic?"

Grim paused his words and turned his gaze back to the Silver Lion.

Then, with a seemingly casual... truly casual wave of his hand, the Silver Lion's massive body floated into the air, flipped onto its back, and was placed back down on the floor.

But that wasn't the only surprise. With another light wave of Grimm's hand, a gash appeared in the Silver Lion's abdomen.

...For Rei and the others, who had experienced firsthand just how tough the Silver Lion's fur was during the fight, the sight was unbelievable.

"No way..."

Elk muttered involuntarily as he watched the belly split open without any apparent action.

Having fought the Silver Lion directly, Elk knew just how powerful it was—and above all, that blades couldn't cut through it.

That was precisely why weapons needed to take on a Silver Lion weren't sharp blades like longswords or spears, but rather weapons like Elk's Axe of the Thunder God or Ara's Power Axe. Other effective weapons were clubs or hammers.

But... the scene unfolding before Elk now was one where the Silver Lion was being dismembered without even using hands.

Of course, one could expect the effectiveness of the Silver Lion's fur to be diminished compared to when it was alive, but even so, seeing its corpse being disassembled so effortlessly was hard to believe.

"Hmm, isn't there something like a barrel? Silver lion blood is a fairly rare material, you know?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. There's a barrel."

Jolted back to reality by Grimm's words, Rei pulled a barrel out from within the Misty Ring.

Not just one, but two, three, four—one after another.

Naturally, the amount of blood flowing through a silver lion's body, which was about six meters long, was considerable.

To preserve every drop, this many barrels were necessary.

That said, during the fight... specifically when finally defeating the Silver Lion, he had pierced its eyeball with the Twilight Spear and then impaled its upper jaw or skull with the Death Scythe, so blood had been flowing from those wounds.

Even after pulling the weapons out, blood naturally continued to flow from those wounds.

Because of that, we couldn't obtain every last drop, but we still succeeded in extracting most of it.

It was likely due to Grimm's necromantic magic. Blood rose from the slashed wound in his abdomen, forming a sphere of blood that materialized before Rei and the others.

Many who witnessed it were stunned, but some wore expressions of envy.

When butchering a monster, the first step is bleeding it out.

Typically, you make cuts into thick blood vessels like the neck or legs, then hang the carcass from a tree to drain it.

But even that method takes a fair amount of time.

Compared to that, Grimm's method involved extracting blood directly from the target. Honestly, just being able to use this magic would undoubtedly make him extremely valuable within the party. In fact, he could probably even make a living solely from this skill.

Using such a convenient spell so casually is certainly something to marvel at.

At the same time, it was fitting for a Lich Lord.

That thought crossed the minds of those watching the scene unfold.

Blood spheres were divided into barrels one after another, vanishing from the air.

After disposing of the blood, Grimm turned his attention to the internal organs.

Again, with just a light wave of his hand, the silver lion's entrails spilled out from its slit belly, one after another, floating into the air.

『Rei』

"I understand."

Without needing to be told, Rei understood what was being urged of him and began pulling preservation containers one after another from the Misty Ring.

For ordinary... lower-ranked monsters, usable parts of the organs were limited, and unusable parts had to be discarded or otherwise disposed of.

But with the S-rank Silver Lion's organs? There was essentially nothing to discard.

And among the organs, the most crucial one... the heart.

The reason they had fought the Silver Lion this time was to obtain this heart, and now that it appeared before them, everyone present was transfixed.

The Silver Lion was already dead, and its heart had been removed from its body.

Despite that, the Silver Lion's heart still pulsed in the air.

(Hey, wait... Could this mean... the Silver Lion wasn't actually dead?)

Seeing the heart still pulsing... still alive, a chill ran down Rei's spine.

It was fortunate he had summoned Grimm promptly, but if he had delayed, perhaps the Silver Lion would have revived.

Of course, with its brain destroyed by the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, it should be fine... but even so, an S-rank monster was an existence beyond Rei's expectations.

Even so, seeing the heart still beating after he'd judged it dead was nothing short of a shock to Rei.

And Rei wasn't the only one.

All eyes were fixed on the pulsing heart, leaving them all dumbstruck.

"Hmm... Here."

With Grimm's words, the magic stone embedded in the heart was extracted and flew toward Rei.

A Rank S monster's magic stone... an item worth a small fortune in itself. Rei caught it gently.

Though for Rei, magic stones weren't something to be exchanged for money, but rather for growing Set and Death Scythe.

(The Silver Lion's magic stone. And...)

Storing the received magic stone in his Misty Ring, Rei turned his gaze toward another room connected to this one—the room likely housing the dungeon's core.

(This dungeon too... ends today, I suppose.)

Thinking this, Rei watched Grim, who was holding the Silver Lion's heart suspended in midair, to see what he would do next.
