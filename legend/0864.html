"So this is the entrance to this dungeon."

"Grr."

At Rei's words, Set, who had accompanied him to the dungeon's vicinity, let out a regretful growl.

Though Set had decided to wait outside while Rei explored the dungeon, seeing the entrance up close like this still made him regret not being able to go inside with Rei.

While petting Set, who was rubbing his head against him, saying he felt lonely, Rei turned his gaze toward the dungeon entrance.

Giant rocks stood on either side, with another rock placed atop them like a roof. It was more accurately a gap between rocks than an entrance to a dungeon.

The gap was incredibly narrow; even Rei, unarmed, could barely squeeze through.

Even using Set's size-changing skill, it was clear before trying that getting inside was impossible.

No, rather than just Set, even a tall adventurer would likely struggle to fit through.

"This is definitely narrow..."

"Right? Well, thanks to that, the monsters that come out are weak and small, and they seem to be coming out little by little."

"Then maybe we don't need to ambush them here? If we leave them alone, they'll just get killed by other monsters anyway."

This was the frontier, a place where all manner of monsters roamed freely.

Especially at night, high-rank monsters appearing isn't uncommon, so goblins shouldn't be a problem.

To Rei's question, Ranga shook his head.

"This place isn't that far from the highway. Sure, fewer people use it this time of year, but it's not like no one does. Monsters that come out here might head that way, and in a dungeon, anything could happen. Considering that, we can't just leave them be."

"The dungeon's growth, huh?"

"Exactly. When you factor that in, vigilance is absolutely necessary."

With those words, Ranga turned an expectant gaze toward Rei.

Knowing full well how much power Rei possessed, he likely hoped, if possible, that Rei would conquer this dungeon.

Rei nodded in response to that gaze.

"I can't guarantee anything, but I'll do my best. Luckily, I don't have to worry about food or anything."

Inside the Mistiling was a large amount of food.

...Much of that food, incidentally, was bought by Rei at Gilm's stall over the past few days.

"I don't know how long this will take, so if you get hungry, could you just hunt down some monsters and eat them?"

"Guruu!"

"It's okay!" Rei gently stroked Set's head as he purred, then turned his gaze to Ranga.

"Well then, I'll head inside for now."

"Be careful."

After exchanging a few brief words, Rei squeezed through the narrow rock crevice—just wide enough for one person to pass through...

"Guh?"

In that instant, he came face to face with a figure ascending the stairs leading underground.

Technically, since it had come up the stairs, its head was one or two heads lower than Rei's.

Even for the one facing Rei, this must have been a completely unexpected turn of events. It tilted its head slightly, as if asking, What just happened?

But its face was ugly, and even tilting its head like that couldn't make it feel as endearing as Set.

...Of course. The one staring back at Rei was one of the most notorious monsters: a goblin.

"...Whoa!"

Rei hadn't expected to suddenly come face-to-face with a goblin either. After a moment's silence, she let out a startled cry and swung her fist reflexively.

The raw sound of flesh being struck and bones breaking echoed around them. The goblin right next to Rei, its face caved in, tumbled down the stairs.

Behind the goblin Rei just struck, several more seemed to be waiting. They tumbled down the stairs leading underground alongside the goblin that had been knocked off the top.

"GABUBUBUBU!"

"Gyaaaah!"

"Jyaaaanyaaaaaaa!"

As Ray watched the goblins vanish from sight, emitting such cries, a voice suddenly came from behind him.

"Rei, what's wrong?"

Langa peered out from between the rocks, asking after hearing the goblin's screams.

Rei shook his head at Ranga, signaling it was nothing.

"I just stepped in here, and the goblin was coming out. So, I punched it reflexively, and it fell down."

"...My condolences."

Ranga said with a wry smile. Rei shook the fist he'd used to punch the goblin earlier and sighed.

"Well, I heard this kind of dungeon was like this, so it can't be helped. Alright, I'm going in for real this time."

"Be careful."

Hearing the same words again from behind him, Rei descended the stairs.

The stairs seemed carved from stone itself, not made by human hands but formed when the dungeon's core created the dungeon.

(Thinking about it that way, dungeons are pretty amazing. No wonder you can get terrain manipulation skills here. ...Though this dungeon's core is headed for Set.)

He descended the stairs in less than a minute. Leaving the goblin that had tumbled down the stairs and died behind, he pressed onward. What appeared before Rei was a stone-carved passageway.

The stones themselves emitted a faint glow, so visibility wasn't an issue.

Though, in Rei's case, he could see reasonably well even in dark places.

But the moment he saw the passageway, Rei frowned.

The passage was barely wide enough for two humans to walk side by side.

There was clearly no room to swing a Deathscythe. At best, he could use the pommel as a spear.

But then, why not just use a spear designed for thrusting from the start?

"Only spears and daggers would be effective? ...A spear will do."

He could probably wield a longsword, but Rei wasn't particularly skilled with them to begin with. Long weapons like the Deathscythe or a spear suited his nature better.

What he pulled from Mistyring's list was a spear he'd taken from a bandit he'd killed on his way back from the Imperial Capital to Gilm.

It wasn't a high-quality spear, but it was perfectly adequate for killing goblins.

Taking the spear in hand, he stepped into the stone corridor... and suddenly, a question flashed through Rei's mind.

"I'm pretty sure they said an ogre appeared... in this passageway?"

As a resident of Elgin, he was small, and the passage was just wide enough for two people to walk side by side. The height was about three meters, fairly high, but even so, it would be impossible for an ogre to walk through here.

"Better ask around,"

He muttered, turning back the way he came with the spear in hand and climbing the stairs.

Peering through a crack in the rock, he saw Ranga giving orders to his subordinates.

"Grr."

Set, seeing Rei, purred happily, and that sound alerted Ranga to Rei's presence as well.

"Rei? Is something wrong?"

"According to the intel we gathered in Gilm, there's supposed to be an ogre inside the dungeon... But in that passageway, an ogre couldn't possibly move freely, right? So, I was wondering if you knew anything about that."

"Ah, the ogres. There are some areas inside the dungeon that are somewhat spacious, like large rooms. The people who fought the ogres probably battled them there. Well, the ogres are too big to come out of those areas, though."

"I see. That's helpful."

After exchanging only those words, Rei gave Set a light wave before reentering the dungeon and descending the stairs.

He passed by several goblin corpses as if they weren't even there, returning to the stone-paved passageway.

The narrowness he'd noticed minutes earlier remained unchanged. He advanced with a slightly displeased expression...

"Gigi-gigi?"

"Gyaaah!"

Once again, a voice he recognized somewhere echoed.

Rei frowned in annoyance at the sound.

But since the path from the entrance to here was a straight line, turning back now would be pointless.

He gripped his spear and pressed forward.

Then, in the dim light of the dungeon, a familiar figure gradually came into view.

The owners of the voices he'd heard just moments ago were, unsurprisingly, goblins. Two of them.

In their hands were a rusty dagger and a club made from nothing more than a broken branch.

(Come to think of it, what did that goblin have earlier? Well, it's just a goblin's weapon anyway, so there's no point collecting it.)

While pondering, he readied his spear, waiting for the goblin that spotted Rei to cry out and charge.

"Gaga gaga!"

"Gyojojo gyo!"

The goblin's build was small, even smaller than Rei's.

Meaning he could fight freely in this narrow passageway without hindrance.

"...Well, that doesn't mean a bunch of goblins like these will make any difference."

As he muttered, two flashes of light erupted.

Thanks to Rei's physical prowess, the two thrusts fired in an instant pierced the goblin's forehead, simultaneously blowing its head apart.

The two goblins stumbled forward several steps at their running speed before collapsing, scattering blood, brain matter, bones, and eyeballs across the stone pavement in their headless state.

Leaping backward to avoid it, Rei, unwilling to smell the foul stench lingering around any longer, used Sleipnir's boots to walk through the air, jumping over the goblin corpses.

"If only some unknown monster would show up. Well, since this is a goblin-filled dungeon, I can't expect much in terms of magic stones, but still... there's always a chance."

As he walked deeper into the dungeon, he eventually reached a T-junction.

He hesitated for a few seconds, unsure which way to go.

But while he hesitated, footsteps approached from the right. ...And then from the left as well.

And so they appeared: three goblins from the right. Two goblins from the left.

A total of five goblins showed themselves...

"Out of the way!"

The moment they noticed him and went on the offensive, Rei's figure was already before the goblins.

And once more, a flash of movement.

Seconds later, only the goblins' corpses remained.

"Enough with the goblins. I hope some monsters I haven't encountered yet show up."

Once more, he strode over the goblin corpses in his Sleipnir boots.

Normally, he would strip them for proof of slaying parts or magic stones, but with goblins, both the proof parts and magic stones were extremely cheap.

For Rei, they weren't worth the trouble of obtaining.

Therefore, he simply ignores them and moves on.

Leaving them be wasn't a problem anyway; slimes, the dungeon's cleaners, would handle them.

For now, he turned right at the T-junction and continued straight for about five minutes.

What appeared before him was a door to the left and a passageway straight ahead and to the right.

"A door, huh. I'm definitely curious about what's inside, but what's more intriguing is..."

His gaze was drawn to the path stretching straight ahead.

Thinking it might be what he suspected, Rei ignored the door and the right path, heading straight ahead.

Continuing on, he came to a place with two paths: one that turned left twice, and another that went straight and then right.

And on the stone pavement lay the corpses of five goblins.

Clearly, these were the goblins Rei had just defeated.

"So, I've come full circle around this room. It's quite spacious."

Just to be sure, he used Sleipnir's Boots again to leap over the goblin corpses and followed the path he thought he had taken earlier.

Sure enough, ahead lay a left door and a right path.

"Now then, what to do. Doors might have traps, you know. So, first, this one?"

Rei's gaze fell on the passageway leading right.

Keeping his spear ready at all times, he ignored the door and advanced down the right passageway.

He walked straight ahead for about ten minutes.

Eventually, a space large enough to be called a great hall came into view.

And there stood an ogre, a creature of such immense size it dwarfed any goblin.

But upon seeing it, Rei couldn't help but frown.

Rei saw an ogre that stood only about three meters tall before him.

Of course, it was still sufficiently massive, and to Rei, it was nearly twice his own height.

He had to look up to see its face, but even so, compared to the ogre he'd encountered in the dungeon with the Inheritance Altar, it simply lacked presence.

"Could this be an ogre child? Or maybe this dungeon is brand new, so only weak ogres are here?"

Hearing Rei's mutter, the ogre—likely not understanding its meaning—shouted while gripping its club.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

But even that roar felt like nothing more than a lack of impact to Rei.
