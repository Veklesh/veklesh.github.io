"Hey, Rei. Long time no see."

Rei and his group arrived at Gilm... or rather, since they were escorting merchants, perhaps it should be called a merchant group. In any case, it was only natural that Gilm's guards greeted the group.

But what surprised Rei most among them was recognizing a familiar face.

His intimidating face and large build would make any timid person flee immediately if they encountered him at night.

But, should one say his appearance and personality didn't match? The voice that came from his mouth was gentle, you could even say.

(A tough-looking exterior with a gentle personality... It's not weird that I picture a forest bear, right? Well, bears in this world get monsterized and are far from gentle. Wait, come to think of it, Japanese bears are the same.)

Perhaps influenced by the fact that the first monster Rei encountered in this world was a Rank C Water Bear, he glanced at Ranga and spoke while thinking such thoughts.

"So, you're back. Does that mean things are settled with the Eliel family?"

After all, the reason Rei left Gilm and ended up teaching at the officer academy in Glacial was... a duel that resulted from a dispute with Kip, the heir to the Eliel family.

From there, events unfolded in a chain reaction, ultimately escalating to a purge of discipline by the royalist faction.

Amidst this, Ranga had headed for the Earl of Eriell's domain to seize the assets pledged by the house for the duel.

Though Ranga had set out in winter, it seemed he had returned properly by this time in spring.

But that was only natural. As could be seen from the two merchants Rei had escorted this time, many came to Gilm in spring to procure goods.

Due to its unique location on the frontier, many goods available within the Kingdom of Mireana could only be obtained here. ...No, even looking across the entire world, this region possessed numerous materials and items unique to itself.

That's precisely why merchants come here not only from within the Kingdom of Mireana, but also from other countries.

Even from the Bestir Empire, which has long been at odds with the Kingdom of Mireana.

During this season when such people arrive in large numbers, the guards' work naturally reaches its peak busyness.

Late autumn... Just like the rush right before the snow falls, this was the busiest time of year for the guards.

Precisely because it was such a time, it was unthinkable that Ranga, the captain of the guard, would not be present.

It was a matter of face, but more importantly, there were many decisions only Ranga could make.

...Yes, for instance, dealing with the situation where the Second Princess of the Bestir Empire, though she had fled, was attempting to enter Gilm alongside Rei.

"Yes. That situation over there is under control. ...Rei, she..."

Ranga shifted his gaze toward Vihera and his words trailed off.

The civil war that erupted in the Bestir Empire last autumn had deeply involved the neutral faction and the aristocratic faction within the Mireana Kingdom.

Daskar had obtained the characteristics of key figures in the Bestir Empire through that affair, and naturally, information on Vihera was included.

She was a striking beauty with a face that drew attention, dressed in revealing clothing that seemed to lure men like a courtesan or dancer, with gauntlets on her hands and greaves on her legs.

With such a distinctive figure, even without knowing her exact features, Ranga, who had heard the story from Daskar, could not possibly mistake her.

Furthermore, there was information that this person held deep affection for Rei, and the fact that she was with Rei also corroborated the rumor.

Realizing that Rei had also guessed Vihera's true identity, Ranga gave a small nod in return.

"...I'd really like you to meet Daskar once, if possible. What do you think?"

"That's what he said. What do you think?"

At those words, which felt more like a plea than a request, Rei turned his gaze toward Vihera.

The two merchants and Magita couldn't understand what Ranga was saying, while Set happily gobbled down dried meat he'd gotten from another guard, his throat making satisfied noises.

After glancing around at the scene, Vihera finally turned her gaze to Rei and nodded in return.

"Well, what can you do? We'd have to meet the lord of Gilm eventually, no matter what we do. Alright. But could we do this after I report the request to the guild?"

"Of course, that's fine. ...I'll contact Lord Daskar, so please come see me once you've finished your report at the guild and have some free time."

"Yes, thank you."

After exchanging a few brief words, they swiftly completed the procedures to enter the city and passed through the gate.

The guard who had been watching over Set wore a disappointed look, but seeing several others lined up behind Rei and the others, he seemed unable to insist and soon gave up.

And so they entered Gilm...

"Whoa, this isn't like Subursta... Aburo was pretty similar to Subursta though."

Magita murmured thoughtfully as she scanned the surroundings of Gilm.

Compared to Saburusta and Abuero, the number of people moving through the streets was greater, and above all, the energy was different.

Precisely because it was on the frontier, its liveliness was noticeably more bustling than other towns.

Adventurers were particularly numerous.

No matter where you looked, you were bound to see an adventurer—a sight unique to Gilm.

Similar scenes could be seen in labyrinth cities like Exil, but for Magita, the existence of labyrinth cities she had never visited was completely absent from her mind.

"Come on, let's go. First stop, the guild."

Rei called out to Magita, who was looking around curiously.

At those words, Magita wore a displeased expression.

"I don't really need to go to the guild. I wasn't the one who took the request."

"If I leave you alone, you'll cause trouble. I can't stand it if you do that while you're with me. Besides..."

He paused dramatically, drawing Magita's attention before continuing.

"The guild has plenty of guys stronger than you."

"I'm going."

Immediate decision... no, Magita declared it like a reflex. Rei gave a wry smile and turned his gaze toward Vihera and Byune.

There, the two were receiving the completion sign from the merchant.

"Master, let's hurry to the guild."

Seeing Magita's demeanor, which seemed nothing like his earlier reluctance, Rei wondered if he had been a bit hasty.

It was true that the guild housed many adventurers more skilled than Magita.

But it was daytime.

Most adventurers were out on quests. Those at the guild now were likely either those who had finished their quests, like Vihera and the others, or those like Rei who disliked crowds and were hunting for quests during quieter hours.

Or perhaps those who'd decided to take the day off and were drinking at the tavern.

Either way, it was highly likely that the adventurer Magita was hoping for simply wasn't here.

(Did I go a bit too far?)

Thinking he might have gone too far, Rei turned his gaze toward the two merchants approaching him.

"Rei, having you here this time was a huge help. If possible, I'd like to meet you again for a proper request next time..."

"Yeah, if the chance comes up, I'll take the job."

"...You really saved us this time."

The gruff merchant reached out his hand with his usual blunt manner.

When Ray shook his hand back, the two merchants seemed satisfied and drove off with their carriage.

The horse, which had never fully gotten used to Set until the very end, now seemed finally at ease, pulling the carriage cheerfully.

Watching the carriage disappear into the bustling crowd, only four people remained here: Rei, Vihera, Byune, Magita, and one horse, Set.

(Well, "four people and one horse" might be a more accurate way to put it than "just four people and one horse.")

Purely speaking, with combatants like Rei, Set, and Vihera present, this group possessed formidable fighting strength.

However, the arrival of spring meant more new adventurers would be coming to Gilm...

"Hey, that chick's a total knockout, huh? And look at that body under those clothes. Wonder how much she'd go for?"

"Whoa, whoa, you just got to Gilm and you're already... worried... about... women?"

Two adventurers passing by exchanged words upon seeing Vihera.

One of them, in particular, was instantly captivated by Vihera's beauty the moment he saw her. His eyes were stolen, his heart stolen, even his thoughts stolen.

Seeing his partner staring intently at Vihera with burning eyes, the adventurer who had spotted her first gave a wry smile, resigned to it.

Vihera was that captivating a woman, and she wore a thin, revealing dress that could only be seen as meant to lure men—the sort of attire typically worn by dancers or prostitutes.

The men's misfortune was that their consciousness was completely fixed on Vihera.

This wasn't just true for the man who fell in love with Vihera at first sight, but also for the man who had spotted Vihera first.

It showed just how magnetic Vihera was to the male gaze. Had the men been a little more observant, they might have noticed not Rei beside Vihera... but rather, Set nearby, munching away on a sandwich he'd gotten from a passerby.

Then they might have realized that Set, accompanied by a griffin, meant the adventurer known as Crimson was nearby.

"Hey, wait... Aren't those guys trying to mess with Rei?"

"Huh? No way, who'd be stupid enough to... Oh, right. They mentioned they just arrived in Gilm recently."

"Maybe you should stop them? Knowing Rei, if someone messes with her weirdly, she'll hit back hard, you know?"

"Yeah. Hold on a sec. I'll go talk to them."

Three other adventurers, returning after finishing a request, saw the pair approaching the group that included Rei and started talking. One of them then hurried off in a panic.

If that adventurer had been someone he disliked, he might have left them be. But when they'd spoken at the guild, he'd seemed somewhat cocky yet approachable, so he couldn't just ignore it.

"Hey, you two. Cut it out with that woman."

The two adventurers, suddenly addressed like that, shot an annoyed glance toward the voice.

But when they recognized the voice belonged to a senior adventurer they'd spoken with several times at the guild, their expressions vanished instantly.

"What's up, all of a sudden?"

The senior adventurer sighed at their response, which seemed to say they didn't understand what he was talking about.

"The one you were trying to mess with is dangerous in more ways than one. If you want to keep adventuring in Gilm for a long time, never antagonize her."

"...Is that woman really that big a deal?"

"She's certainly the most beautiful woman I've ever seen, but she's not that big of a deal..."

"No."

The veteran adventurer immediately shot down the misunderstanding of the two.

"The one I'm telling you not to mess with isn't the woman. Well, sure, just looking at her, she's attractive. Honestly, I'd want to spend not just one night, but days with her. But I'm not talking about the woman. I'm talking about the guy in the robe."

"...The man?"

"That little guy?"

"That's the one. Looks-wise... well, it's hard to tell with that hood up, but the face underneath doesn't exactly scream 'man'. Still, it's only the looks that are pretty. Inside, he's downright nasty. If you want to live in Gilm, don't go messing with him."

"He doesn't look that strong, though."

One of the adventurer duo muttered, clearly unconvinced by that statement. Hearing this, the senior adventurer sighed in exasperation and shifted his gaze away from Rei and Vihera... toward the griffin, who was eating a sandwich while being petted by a girl who looked about ten years old.

"Look at that. Look closely. Look so you won't forget."

After being told repeatedly, the two adventurers finally noticed its existence... the griffin, Set.

"What the...!? A g-griffon!?"

"No way, what's it doing here... Wait, wait a minute. A griffin? If you say griffin, then surely..."

Just as the adventurer, finally recalling something, tried to retrieve the memory from his knowledge, the senior adventurer cut him off with a quick answer.

"That's right. That guy's a Rank B adventurer, already has a nickname, and was active in last spring's war. Crimson. His real name is Rei."

「Crimson……」

"That kid is Crimson...?"

The two adventurers shot the senior adventurer a look that said, "You've got to be kidding," but the only response they got was a serious stare.

And so, unable even to speak to Vihera, the two adventurers watched Rei and his group depart, left staring blankly after them.
