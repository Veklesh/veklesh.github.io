"I told you, that's not right. You scale the fish with the back of the knife. Got it? Like this, like this!"

As he says this, Bistol begins to scale the fish with his knife.

From Rei’s perspective, it looked as though he was merely tracing the fish with the back of the knife, but there was no doubt that the scales were coming off cleanly.

But when he looked down at the fish on his own cutting board... there it was, stripped of not just its scales and skin, but even its flesh.

“...Why is that?”

“That’s why it’s important to control your pressure. Also, you have to run the back of the knife along the fish’s flesh. Like this, like this!”

Saying that, Bistol flipped the fish on the cutting board over and began scaling the flesh on the other side.

Although she was doing the same thing as Rei, the results were worlds apart.

That’s how great the difference was between Rei and Bistol’s fish-filleting techniques.

(Why is that? When it comes to butchering monsters or animals, I’m not that bad at it. So why can’t I fillet a fish?)

Puzzled, Rei shifted his gaze to the side.

Not at Bistoll... but at the others.

Elena is struggling a bit, but she’s still better at scaling fish than Rei.

Vihera and Marina were quite skilled, though not quite as good as Bistol.

And Byune... she was scaling the fish so beautifully that I thought she might be on par with Bistul, or perhaps even better than him.

Her skill was so impressive that Bistol could confidently declare he had nothing to teach Byune about filleting fish.

And... it becomes clear that, in the end, the only ones worse at filleting fish than Rei are Set and Yellow.

Of course, with a fish as big as a tuna, that’s one thing, but for the kind of fish Rei and the others are filleting now, Set usually just eats them as is without even scaling them.

“Hmm, it’s not like Rei-chan is clumsy or anything, so I think it’s just a matter of practice. I wasn’t good at filleting fish from the start either.”

Rei nodded in agreement at Bistol’s words.

In fact, Rei had never filleted a fish before, not even back when she was in Japan.

She had, however, slaughtered the chickens her father kept.

In that sense, she looks convinced by Bistrol’s words that with practice, she’ll be able to manage it.

(Well, just how long it’ll actually take to get used to it… that’s a bit of a mystery. At the very least, I don’t think I’ll get the hang of it just by being out at sea.)

If he practiced filleting fish the entire time he was at sea, he would undoubtedly improve.

But Rei came to this sea strictly to catch fish.

Given that, he simply doesn’t have the luxury of time to keep practicing filleting fish.

“Well, since we’re here, why don’t we use this fish for tonight’s meal?”

“We don’t have to go to the trouble of cooking it ourselves—we’ve got plenty of food prepared by the chef.”

“Oh, no way. At times like this, we really have to cook for ourselves. Of course, the cook’s food is delicious, but cooking for ourselves is fun too, isn’t it? Right?”

At Rei’s words, Bistol looked meaningfully at Elena and the others.

Facing Bistol’s gaze, Elena, Marina, and Vihera all nodded slightly… but firmly.

It was only natural for Elena and the others, as women, to want to try the food made by the man they loved.

And at the same time, it was only natural for them to want Rei to eat the food they had prepared themselves.

Only Byune looked away, giving off a slightly disappointed vibe.

Unlike Elena and the others, Byune didn’t harbor any romantic feelings for Rei.

Well, she does feel affection for him as a fellow party member, but perhaps it’s more accurate to say it’s not the kind of affection one feels for a romantic interest.

Perhaps that’s why she doesn’t particularly want to eat Rei’s home-cooked meal, nor does she want to feed it to him.

Rather, it’s only natural that she would prefer to eat the food prepared by Rei, the chef she has with her.

However, it’s easy to tell how Vihera, whom Byune is close to, feels about Rei.

So, even though she looks disappointed, she ends up saying nothing.

“Well, with that settled, shall we each get to cooking?”

“No, what do you mean, ‘so’? …It seems there’s no point in saying anything more, so I won’t say anything.”

Saying that, Rei stopped defying Bistol any further.

In fact, if you were to ask whether Rei didn’t want to eat the home-cooked meals Elena and the others had prepared, it’s likely that he was actually quite eager to try them.

Since he’d eaten the home-cooked meals Marina and the others made when they ate in Marina’s backyard, this wasn’t exactly his first time trying their cooking.

That said, since Rei himself uses an oven to bake pizza, this isn’t exactly his first time cooking either.

Still, the reason they all want to eat home-cooked meals is probably because this isn’t their usual spot—Marina’s backyard—but a different place… and because they’re here purely for a vacation, not on an adventurer’s quest.

“First, grab a frying pan and pour this oil into it.”

Saying that, Bistol handed Rei the oil contained within a nut about the size of a fist.

“...You’re really well-prepared. You haven’t even taken out the frying pan yet.”

Saying that, Rei takes a frying pan from the Misty Ring and brings it over to the campfire.

After lightly heating the frying pan and adding the oil, letting it coat the surface...

“Okay, time to add the fish Rei cut up. Oh, and make sure the skin is facing down.”

Following the instructions, Rei placed the fish—now cut into rather awkward-looking pieces—into the frying pan.

As the oil heated up, a savory aroma wafted from the skin into the air.

It was the kind of smell and sound that made your stomach growl just from the scent alone.

(Is this oil a little different from regular oil?)

Along with the savory aroma of the frying fish, a faintly sweet and sour scent wafts through the air.

Oil used in ordinary cooking doesn’t usually give off that kind of scent.

Of course, that’s just based on Rei’s experience; in reality, it wouldn’t be strange if such oil existed.

In fact, there is such oil right in front of Rei right now.

“Right, right. Give the pan a gentle shake so the skin doesn’t stick. And… next, add this.”

Saying that, she handed Rei a vegetable… or rather, a plant that should more accurately be called a wild herb.

“Where did you find something like this?”

“Oh, there are actually quite a few edible wild plants growing around here. Especially since we’re in the mountains, there are plenty of them. Besides, I got that oil from this mountain too.”

As Bistol said this with one eye closed, Rei found himself wondering if she might actually be able to make it as an adventurer.

In fact, Rei knew that Bistol was a burly, muscular man with exceptional physical abilities.

And since she could so easily gather food like this in a mountain range she’d never been to before, it was clear even to Rei that she would undoubtedly be able to make it as an adventurer.

“Hey, Bistol. Are you planning on becoming an adventurer?”

“No way. Instead, keep your eyes on the frying pan. I’m adding more oil.”

After flatly rejecting Rei’s suggestion, Bistol poured more oil into the frying pan Rei was holding.

Rei wondered if she’d put in a little too much oil, but Bistrel didn’t look like she’d made a mistake; she just stared intently at the frying pan with a serious expression.

“Next, pour the juice from this fruit all over it.”

There was no way Rei could defy Bistol, who was better at cooking than either of them, so he took the fruit she handed him and squeezed its juice over the fish and wild herbs in the pan.

“Finally, lightly sprinkle some salt on top… and it’s done.”

“...That was surprisingly simple.”

“I suppose so. But all the ingredients used in this dish come from these mountains and the sea. When you think about it that way, don’t you think it has a certain charm?”

“Now that you mention it… I guess so?”

Rei thought of the phrase “locally sourced,” but in this case, it didn’t quite fit.

In any case, the finished dish was placed on a plate Rei had taken out of the Misty Ring.

“The fish will fall apart if you handle it roughly, so be careful with that. Also, since we went to the trouble of making this, let’s plate it nicely.”

“Don’t expect me to be good at that sort of thing.”

Saying that, Rei placed the fish on the plate and began arranging the wild herbs around it.

“Okay, and finally, please add this too. It’ll make the aroma even better.”

What Bistol was handed was a blue-purple flower.

When he placed the flower on top of the fish, the heat from the fish warmed it, and a refreshing green scent began to waft through the air.

“Did this flower grow around here too?”

“Yes. It’s called a Darness flower, and it’s perfectly safe to eat.”

“...I really can’t figure out where you get this kind of knowledge.”

“Really? Isn’t it only natural to want to learn about flowers?”

As she said this, Bistol’s expression suggested she hadn’t said anything strange at all, and that she was speaking nothing but the truth.

Seeing this, Rei tried to say something to Bistol, but ultimately clamped his mouth shut.

He probably thought that no matter what he said, it would be like trying to push against a closed door.

Besides, in reality, there are quite a few people who take an interest in flowers. …Whether that extends to edible flowers, however, is really a matter of personal preference.

“Ah… well, fine. So, does that mean this dish is finished? If so, I’d like to eat it.”

“No way.”

Instantly—at a speed that could only be described as reflexive—Bistrel rejected Rei’s suggestion.

Then, she looked at Rei with an expression of utter exasperation.

“Listen, the dish Rei made is for those three over there to eat. And the dish those three made is for Rei to eat. …Okay?”

At Bistrol’s words, Elena, Marina, and Vihera—who had been lumped together as “those three”—each nodded.

“No, there’s no way the food you guys made isn’t better than what I made, right? If you’re okay with that, then I’m fine with it too…”

Because he just couldn’t get the hang of filleting the fish into three pieces, the fish meat in Rei’s dish had fallen apart.

It wouldn’t be a problem at all if he were eating it himself… but when it came to serving it to others, he hesitated a little.

Especially since Elena and the others had filleted the fish much more neatly than Rei had.

“Hey, why don’t you learn a little about a woman’s heart?”

If Elena and the others had said that, Rei might have accepted it.

But since the one telling him to study the female heart is a burly, muscular giant, at first glance, there’s absolutely no persuasiveness to it.

That said, once you understand what kind of person Bistoll is, his persuasiveness would likely transform into something incredibly powerful.

“Anyway, I get what Bistoll is saying. Is that okay with you guys? It’ll be a pretty small portion for three people, though.”

“Yeah, I’m fine with that. What about Marina and Vihera?”

“Of course, I don’t mind either.”

“I’m fine with it too.”

With that, the three of them shared Rei’s meal.

After that, Elena and the others made the same dish, and Rei ate it.

This meant that Rei was the only one who ate three portions, but no one seemed to mind.

Tonight’s meal wasn’t limited to this; the fact that Rei had also brought out a regular dish from the Misty Ring played a big part.

Set, perhaps happy to have eaten a whole pot of stew packed with plenty of orc meat, lay sprawled on the ground, purring contentedly.

Meanwhile... After finishing their meal, Rei and the others decided to retrieve the magic stones from Guztos at a spot a short distance away.

Since they were inside the Misty Ring, there was no risk of them spoiling, but Rei wanted to finish absorbing the magic stones as soon as possible.

Since he couldn’t explain the details to Bistol, he simply told him he’d somehow felt the urge to extract the monster’s magic stone as soon as possible, and set to work.

That said, they weren’t going to completely dismantle it; they were merely extracting the magic stone.

Since that alone didn’t take much time, the only ones working on it were Rei and Set, who was there more out of formality than anything else.

……To be honest, though, since the magic stones would be absorbed immediately, Rei was actually relieved that people like Bistol and Byune hadn’t come along.

“Well then, Set. It’s been a while since I’ve absorbed a magic stone… I wonder how it’ll go?”
