As Rei and the others were returning to the cave... inside that cave, Elena and Vihera were locked in battle against the giant.

However, the battle was already nearing its end.

The number of giants remaining had already fallen below fifty.

But... that meant the corpses of nearly nine hundred giants slain so far were scattered all around, becoming obstacles in the fight.

"Ugh, really... Maybe we should have fought a bit cleaner,"

After leaping onto a giant's head and striking it down with her Magic Palm, Vihera landed and muttered with a hint of annoyance.

Several chunks of giant flesh lay scattered on the ground where she had intended to land.

If she landed there, she might slip on the flesh and fall, halting her movement.

Of course, Vihera understood this possibility and wouldn't simply land there. She twisted her body lightly in midair, landing on a patch of ground free of the giant's flesh.

Twisting his body as he landed, he naturally lost his balance.

However, when it came to moving his body, Vihera possessed top-tier skill even among Rei and the others.

The instant he touched down, he shifted his body again, regaining his balance on the spot.

"This makes me envy Elena,"

Watching Elena slice through the giant with her whip-like Mirage, Vihera murmured enviously.

Elena had intended to land on the ground covered in flesh fragments like Vihera, but in the next instant, she landed on top of the fragments... more precisely, on top of them in a spatial sense. The very next moment, she kicked off that space and soared through the air once more.

She had used the Sleipnir Boots to kick off mid-air.

For Vihera, skilled in close combat, the Sleipnir Boots that allowed Elena and... and Rei to kick off mid-air were an incredibly enviable possession.

Enough that he wished he could have a pair himself.

...Though, in Vihera's case, she equipped greaves with blades extending from the heels.

Thinking about it that way, it was definitely true that equipping the Sleipnir Boots wasn't something she could do so easily.

(If I ever get them, I'll need to adjust them so they don't interfere with my greaves.)

Sleipnir Boots are indeed a fairly rare magic item.

But even so, they aren't as extremely rare as something like an item box, where only a handful exist in the world...

"Elena, how's it going over there?"

Using a Mirage shaped like a longsword, Elena dodged the giant's attacks while slashing at its neck. She gave a light wave in response to Vihera's words.

Without saying a word, she conveyed she was fine through her actions.

In that situation, they continued defeating the giants without paying each other any particular attention.

...Under normal circumstances, no matter how strong they were, defeating a thousand giants together would be nearly impossible physically.

No, even monsters like goblins—two people couldn't last long enough to kill a thousand of them.

But Elena and Vihera both existed outside the bounds of what was considered normal.

Sweat glistened slightly on their foreheads, and their breathing was somewhat labored, but they still had plenty of stamina left.

(In that case, it was definitely for the best that Marina and Byune weren't here.)

Byune was originally a child, and while she might have bursts of speed, she inevitably lacked endurance.

Compared to Byune, Marina is an adult... and a seasoned adventurer at that, so her stamina is likely superior to that of the average adventurer.

But that's only compared to ordinary adventurers. She was vastly inferior to Elena, who had inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, or Vihera, who had absorbed Ambris.

Even as a seasoned adventurer, Marina is still just a Dark Elf in the flesh—though whether that's the right way to put it is debatable.

(They said she was a World Tree Priestess... but that doesn't seem related to Marina's abilities. Ah, but maybe her exceptional skill with spirit magic is connected to being a World Tree Priestess?)

She dodged the giant's strike as it reached out to grab her torso, then sliced through its neck with magic claws extending from her gauntlets.

A fountain of blood erupted violently around them.

But by the time the blood splattered, Vihera had already passed the giant by.

The giant, its throat slashed, prioritized saving its own life over its appetite. It desperately tried to stop the blood gushing from its neck, pressing its hands against the wound.

To an observer, it looked as if it were strangling itself.

...Yet, paying no heed to the giant displaying such a somewhat comical sight, Vihera turned his attention to the remaining few enemies.

The joyful expression Vihera had worn when the fight began was gone.

Giants had never been the kind of enemy that truly stirred Vihera's blood and made his heart race.

Still, their sheer numbers made them reasonably satisfying as punching bags, but fighting such opponents became monotonous and easy to tire of.

They attacked purely by instinct, offering no clever feints or tricks, just simple, direct assaults.

They were far from satisfying opponents for Vihera.

(If only there were higher-tier species or rare types... No, wait. These giants aren't monsters to begin with, so maybe such things don't exist here at all?)

Seeing the dwindling number of giants, Vihera thought this once more.

She had already confirmed through investigation that the giants possessed no magic stones.

It certainly wasn't pleasant work, but she couldn't deny she'd harbored a faint hope...

In the end, however, contrary to that hope, there were no magic stones or the like.

Which meant these giants weren't monsters.

That said, if asked whether they were human, no one could nod in agreement.

Logically, they should be considered human. Yet, the way they acted on their appetite instinct—devouring their own mothers and attacking everyone around them except their own kind—made it utterly impossible to accept them as human.

Then again, the fact they weren't running wild with lust was probably a relief to all the women, including Vihera.

A newborn... especially one as colossal as this creature... it's hard to imagine it could even judge beauty or ugliness.

But honestly, Vihera couldn't bear to imagine the scale of destruction if it had been sexual desire, not hunger, that caused it to rampage.

"This is the end!"

Hearing that voice, Vihera looked around.

Just as Elena had said, the giant that had been standing there was nowhere to be seen.

Of course, that didn't mean every last giant had been completely eliminated.

After all, there had been nearly a thousand to begin with, so some giants were likely still alive.

Still, it was almost certain that the flames of life within those giants would be extinguished before long.

In any case, Elena's Mirage strike had severed the giant's neck, meaning not a single giant remained standing in this place.

"Phew... Well, the giants are taken care of for now... What do you think we should do next?"

"Right."

Brushing the giant's blood from the blade of her sword, Elena asked Vihera.

At her words, Vihera paused to think... then shifted her gaze to Ryutas's guard, standing slightly away from where the door had been.

"Hey, just checking, but the giants didn't head out toward the passageway, did they?"

Vihera, a woman of unparalleled beauty. On top of that, her alluring, man-pleasing figure was wrapped in light clothing like a courtesan or dancer.

Any ordinary man would be over the moon just to be spoken to by such a woman, Vihera.

Or perhaps be so dazzled by her beauty that he'd lose his senses, unable even to speak.

It would have been one or the other... but this time was different.

The escort couldn't respond immediately, but it wasn't because he was mesmerized by Vihera's beauty... He was desperately trying to suppress the trembling in his body, trembling from fear he didn't even realize he felt.

As Ryutas's bodyguard—that is, the bodyguard of the future heir to lead Jaya—the man knew full well the immense power a giant possessed.

In terms of combat skill, he was undeniably a very crude being.

But his physical capabilities were beyond comparison to those of ordinary humans.

To the extent that he knew he could never win if he faced even a few of them head-on.

And yet, those two alone—though Rei did lend a hand—had defeated nearly a thousand of them.

To the guard, the two women before him might be beautiful, but more than that, they possessed power he couldn't comprehend.

To be addressed by such individuals... It was only natural his movements froze.

"Oh? What's wrong? Did you not hear me?"

"N-no! I heard you! The giants didn't come out, so please rest assured!"

Jolted back to reality by Vihera's repeated call, the man shouted his reply.

He thought that if he didn't answer now, he might end up like the giant.

...Though neither Vihera nor Elena had any such intention.

Anyway, hearing that the giant hadn't come out, Vihera looked at Elena with relief.

"What should we do? Should we go check on Rei and the others?"

"Going there is fine, but there's probably nothing we can do even if we go."

At Elena's muttered words, Vihera could only nod in agreement.

Facing nearly a thousand giants, Elena and Vihera had exhausted their stamina, but in terms of damage, they'd slipped through unscathed.

If even they were like that, it was hard to imagine Rei managing anything against ordinary soldiers—who seemed weaker than the giants—especially since there were only a few hundred of them, fewer than the giants.

(Ah, but it was a forest outside... That might have made it a bit difficult for Rei to fight.)

Rei's dual-spear style, wielding a large scythe in her right hand and a spear in her left.

Both weapons had long shafts, making them less agile.

Given that, fighting in a place like a forest would put her at a disadvantage against opponents wielding long swords or similar weapons.

...That said, the difference in strength between Rei and the soldiers was overwhelming. Even if the soldiers had a slight advantage due to weapon handling, it was hard to imagine Rei being defeated.

In Vihera's mind, images flashed: Rei slicing soldiers clean in two along with the trees in the forest using Death Scythe, and Twilight Spear piercing through tree trunks without losing any speed, impaling several, perhaps even over a dozen soldiers.

"Right. I don't think we need to worry about Rei and the others. Then, shall we head over to Marina? Maybe she's having some trouble."

With no other good ideas in sight, they decided to head towards Marina.

"Marina, um, could you please summon the water spirit?"

Marina nodded in response to the soldier's request and called upon the water spirit.

The next moment, a water sphere slightly larger than a fist floated into the air.

"Is this okay?"

"Thank you!"

The soldier gave Marina a brief bow of thanks and departed, taking the water sphere with him.

Watching his retreating figure, Marina looked around her surroundings once more.

Several giants had been rampaging here.

Even so, their overwhelmingly superior physical abilities compared to humans meant the damage was far from minor.

...The relatively low death toll was likely because the giants weren't solely intent on killing; they were consuming the soldiers they had slain.

Adult males, after all, possessed large bodies.

And since they were guarding this cave, a crucial location within Jaya, many were reasonably well-trained.

Yes, that meant they had more muscle than the average man around... more places for the giants to eat.

Thanks to that, it was possible to buy a little... really just a little bit of time, allowing some to survive to some extent.

(Though what that ultimately led to... well, that's a bit questionable.)

Even if they survived here, no happy future awaited the people of Jaya.

After all, Marina herself harbors no goodwill whatsoever toward the people of Jaya.

As a woman herself, she could never forgive the exploitation of so many women as sacrifices—it's no exaggeration to call them offerings—for the sake of producing Titans.

Still, the biggest reason she was helping the people of Jaya here and now was because Rei had asked her to.

And she hoped that those involved in this matter could receive proper judgment... that was her thought.
