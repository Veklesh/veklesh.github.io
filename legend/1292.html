Though the forest was covered in deep snow, it had been somewhat packed down by those who had gone ahead of Rei and the others.

Rei and the others walked straight ahead through it.

But suddenly, Rei spoke up.

"Hey, if we keep going like this, won't we run into other adventurers?"

Under normal circumstances, this wouldn't be much of an issue.

But today, Rei and the others had come to this forest specifically to test whether they could coordinate as a party... or to identify any issues.

They didn't want to show such behavior to others if possible.

There was the possibility their weaknesses would be exposed, and besides, this party was attracting attention for various reasons.

They probably wouldn't be a threat, but who knows? Maybe some crazed person might attack them.

This party possessed enough compelling elements to make such a thought entirely plausible.

Though, if you asked whether anyone would actually do such a thing after seeing Set, many would probably just tilt their heads in confusion.

Still, the fact remained that minimizing potential danger was always preferable, so Rei had a point.

Moreover, under normal circumstances, the snow would make walking difficult, so it wouldn't be strange to plan to stick to packed-down areas. But in this case, Marina was... an exceptional user of spirit magic.

With conditions this favorable, there was no need to deliberately follow a path already trodden by others.

"Right. I think Rei's opinion is correct. He is the party leader, after all. Besides, if we have to fight monsters, I'd rather face ones that are full of energy, not ones worn out or injured from fighting other adventurers."

"...Well, we haven't officially formed a party yet, though."

While accepting Vihera's reasoning, Rei added this as an afterthought.

"Well, we haven't formally formed a party yet, but it's already decided we'll form one with this group, right? Then I don't see any problem."

Marina chimed in after Vihera... When Rei looked over at the other person, he saw Byune nodding in agreement.

"Grr."

Amidst this, only Set let out a whimper, as if comforting Rei.

Lightly stroking Set, who was comforting him, Rei turned his gaze back to Marina and nodded.

Without needing words, Marina seemed to understand what Rei wanted to say. She murmured a small incantation... and then, the snow that had accumulated like a branch branching off from the path they had been walking on began to disappear.

"Convenient, isn't it?"

Vihera murmured thoughtfully, and both Rei and Byune seemed to agree, nodding.

In practical terms, Marina's elemental magic proved remarkably versatile.

Like when they'd held a party at Marina's mansion, she could change the surrounding temperature to spring-like without melting the snow, or clear untrodden fresh snow to make a path like this.

Considering she also used spirit magic to clean her mansion, it was easy to grasp just how powerful it was.

At the very least, compared to Rei's fire magic, which was mostly used for attack, it was incomparable in terms of practicality.

...Then again, since Rei had all the tools necessary for daily life consolidated in the Misty Ring, he could manage just fine even without elemental magic.

But that's only possible because Rei possesses the Misty Ring, a massive stockpile of magic items, and an unimaginable fortune. Ordinary people couldn't pull off something like that.

The elemental magic primarily used by Dark Elves and Elves clearly surpasses human magic in terms of flexibility.

(If more people could use spirit magic, it'd be so much more convenient... but there just aren't many humans who can wield it. Even regular mages are rare.)

Rei thought about this while surveying her surroundings.

Many adventurers gather in Gilm, so as a result, there are a fair number of mages there.

But if asked how many spirit mages were among them... even Rei couldn't give a clear answer.

Rei's lack of familiarity with other adventurers' information certainly played a part in that.

"Gluu!"

As they walked through the forest, Set, who was beside Rei, suddenly let out a sharp cry.

Set's gaze was fixed on the spot ahead of where Rei and the others were walking.

No one present failed to understand the meaning of that cry.

Each readied the weapon they held and formed ranks.

First, Marina, who had been using spirit magic at the front, moved to the rear. Behind her, Vihera and Rei moved forward. Then, Byune took the rear guard position to protect Marina.

This was the party's basic formation.

The combat-proficient Rei and Vihera take the front, Marina provides support, and Byune, while guarding Marina, exploits enemy openings to launch her long needles.

Additionally, Set, who can fly, would primarily act as a hit-and-run specialist.

Given that he was an exceptional combat asset, a griffin, it was judged better to let him operate freely rather than force him into a rigid role.

...Though this hit-and-run role was only possible because Set possessed the intelligence to understand commands.

"I never expected monsters to come out from over there,"

Rei muttered, holding only one Twilight Spear in his hand—likely because of the surrounding trees.

He usually fought using a dual-spear style, but the Death Scythe, with its wide arc, was extremely difficult to use in such a confined space.

Furthermore, since he was fighting alongside Vihera, the Death Scythe's wide attack range made it an unsuitable weapon for this situation.

Normally, monsters sensing Set's presence wouldn't reveal themselves so easily.

That's precisely why Rei and the others had to move themselves to find monsters... or so he'd thought. But this time, they were appearing on their own.

(Could it be goblins...? No, judging by Set's demeanor, that's unlikely.)

The only foes that would attack without hesitation even with Set present were, in Rei's mind, strongly associated with goblins.

And this wasn't just a baseless assumption; he'd seen goblins attack Set countless times with his own eyes.

And when they saw Set's power, they'd flee in an instant... that kind of scene.

But from what Rei could see, Set was acting differently.

If it were goblins, there's no way Set would show even the slightest caution.

"Shhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

Accompanied by that threatening cry, a creature appeared that, at first glance, looked like nothing more than a lizard.

For a moment, Rei thought it might be a dragon because it was a lizard, but it was just an ordinary lizard without any feathers.

...That is, if you could even call it a lizard, given its size: nearly a meter tall, and nearly two meters long including its tail.

And its color wasn't the typical lizard green, but a pure white like fresh snow.

Its shape looked like a lizard, but everything else about it was clearly not a lizard.

Rei tried to recall the monster's name, searching his memory of reading the Monster Compendium, but someone spoke before he could.

"Snow Salamander!? Why is it here!?"

The voice of shock belonged to Marina.

As if responding to that cry, the Snow Salamander opened its mouth.

"Shhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

Along with the cry, a blizzard erupted from its mouth, studded with numerous short fangs.

"Watch out, Blizzard Breath!"

Hearing Marina's voice, Rei and Vihera split to the left and right.

They could have chosen to fly backward, but they didn't know the breath's range.

However, the Snow Salamander, still spewing its Blizzard Breath, swiveled its neck as if to pursue Rei.

"Dammit!"

Thanks to the Dragon Robe, he felt almost none of the cold.

But the Dragon Robe only protected him from the cold.

It couldn't counter the momentum from the Blizzard Breath hitting him, and Rei was blown through the air.

For a moment, he considered using the Sleipnir Boots, but immediately remembered that his current footwear wasn't the Sleipnir Boots, but ordinary boots worn by adventurers.

Even so, while being blown through the air, he somehow managed to regain his balance and land on the snow.

"Shh!?"

Almost the instant Rei touched down, a scream erupted from the Snow Salamander's mouth instead of a Blizzard Breath.

As Rei regained his footing and turned his gaze toward the Snow Salamander, he saw Vihera rapidly closing the distance between them. The Snow Salamander, several arrows now embedded in its body, was screaming in agony.

"Haah!"

The swinging fist struck the Snow Salamander's body squarely.

The fist sank into its white body... and the sheer force of the blow sent the Snow Salamander flying several meters sideways.

"Shhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

A different scream from the one that had come from its mouth earlier.

Rei understood exactly what that scream meant.

Vihera's single blow had inflicted greater damage than the arrow attack.

Vihera was slightly surprised that his strike had proven so potent.

He had swung his fist, but this time he hadn't used his signature technique, the Immersion Palm, nor had he created magical claws on his gauntlets for the strike.

It was a purely physical punch.

Yet, despite that, Vihera's strike was undeniably more powerful than any he had delivered before.

Vihera understood exactly why that was the case.

It was due to the ritual performed using the Silver Lion's heart.

He hadn't pushed himself that hard during combat training with Byune, but considering this strike, it was clearly the right call.

"Sha... SAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!"

Even so, the Snow Salamander wasn't defeated outright. It turned toward Rei, who was nearby, and unleashed another Blizzard Breath.

"Not if I can help it!"

The moment Rei saw the Blizzard Breath aimed at him, he channeled magic and swung the Twilight Spear in front of himself.

The Twilight Spear, infused with magic power, completely blocked the Blizzard Breath.

"Shaa!?"

Even for the Snow Salamander, this seemed completely unexpected. It halted its Blizzard Breath with a startled expression and let out a cry of surprise...

"Hmph."

Seizing that opening, Byune closed the distance in an instant and swung the dagger made from the Silver Lion's material... White Cloud.

True to its name, the pure white blade cleanly sliced through the Snow Salamander's neck, drawing several crimson streaks in the untouched fresh snow on the ground.

Normally, a Snow Salamander is a Rank B monster.

Its strength was on par with an Orc King or Goblin King, possessing enough power to wipe out a Rank C party without hesitation.

In other words, it was far beyond anything Byune could handle alone.

In fact, if Byune had encountered the Snow Salamander alone, victory would have been virtually impossible.

Byune understood this. She would undoubtedly have retreated.

But... the current Byune possessed the White Cloud, crafted from the materials of an S-rank monster.

While her own combat prowess was by no means high, the weapon's power alone made it top-tier.

It was powerful enough that even a Rank A monster... or in some cases, it could even wound a Rank S monster.

...Though, being able to inflict damage and being able to land an attack are similar concepts, yet entirely different in meaning.

The reason Byune managed to slice through the Snow Salamander's neck this time was likely due to the shock of Marina's arrow and Vihera's strike slowing the Snow Salamander's movements, allowing it to block the Blizzard Breath.

Furthermore, Marina's spirit magic boosted Byune's movement speed beyond normal. Using that boost, she closed the distance at full speed and flashed through the white clouds.

Even after all that, she finally landed the attack. Rank B monsters truly live up to their reputation.

"Hmph!"

Though Byune had severed the Snow Salamander's neck, she didn't let her guard down, leaping backward instantly.

With Marina's spirit magic assisting her, the movement was incredibly smooth.

But... even then, it was a close call.

The Snow Salamander's fangs, its throat now slashed open, swept past the spot where Byune's face had been just an instant before.

Had Byune's leap been even a fraction slower... or without the spirit magic's support, the Snow Salamander would have torn her face apart.

Despite suffering the fatal wound of having its neck slashed, its ferocity did not subside.

And just as it opened its mouth again to pursue Byune... the Twilight Spear, infused with magic, pierced through that gaping maw.
