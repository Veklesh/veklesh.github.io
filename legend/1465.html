"Huh? Rei is going to make direct contact?"

The day after Elena suggested to Rei that he try contacting Ajas, and Rei nodded in agreement.

While transporting construction materials needed for building a new wall via the Misty Ring, Rei encountered Ilse and promptly explained the proposal Elena had made yesterday.

"That's right. I did find the person you mentioned, but according to my investigation, he hasn't caused any trouble in Gilm. He's practically a model adventurer."

"What!?"

Ilze couldn't believe the words coming out of Rei's mouth.

Ilse was speechless for a moment, but then her face began to flush.

Watching from the side, Rei instantly understood that this flush wasn't born of shame or embarrassment, but rather anger and indignation.

"Calm down."

Rei called out to Ilse, who looked ready to bolt from the spot at any moment.

Ilse calmed down a little at Rei's words, but she still couldn't forgive Ajas, her fists clenched tightly.

If Ilse had a little more strength, or if her nails were sharper, the skin on her palm might have been torn, blood dripping onto the ground.

"...I know. But..."

She managed to reply only that much to Rei's words.

The person who killed her family was accepted by everyone as a well-behaved adventurer.

That was something Ilse could never, ever accept.

Her face, beautiful enough to be called lovely, twisted into a complex expression of humiliation, anger, and sorrow.

Still, even so, she didn't look ugly to Rei's eyes.

(Guess being beautiful has its perks.)

Thinking this, Rei decided to speak to Ilse.

"About that 'upstanding character' thing... I've got a question. That Ajash guy is really your enemy, right? Could it be... maybe you're mistaking him for someone with a similar tattoo?"

"No way!"

Ilse's response came out loud enough for everyone nearby to hear.

Her green hair flew wildly as she fixed Rei with a gaze bordering on hostility.

Was she protecting her enemy... the one who killed her family?

Ilse's words, of course, reached the ears of those nearby.

Especially once they realized one of the speakers was Rei, it was no surprise that curious onlookers turned their eyes toward them, wondering what had happened.

Rei must have realized this too. He pulled Ilse behind a building—out of sight of passersby—knowing it would be bad to draw attention from others here.

"Damn it. Is Rei going to make another beauty his woman? That sucks."

"Come on, don't say that. It's only natural women are drawn to strong men. If you're jealous, just get stronger yourself."

"There's no way I could become a monster like that!"

"Hey, did you just insult Set-chan?"

"Yes, he did. I heard it too. You just called Set-chan a monster, didn't you?"

"I didn't say that! How the hell did you mishear that?!"

The man who called Rei a monster was being confronted by those around him who always admired Set, but Rei, hearing the exchange, did nothing in particular and went with Ilse to hide behind a nearby building.

Once they confirmed they were out of sight, Rei spoke again.

"About that Ajash guy..."

"Yes?"

Ilse, having regained some composure after moving locations, suppressed her earlier excitement and responded to Rei's words.

Hearing the name Ajas didn't seem to surprise her at all.

After all, she hadn't been doing absolutely nothing these past few days.

Using her well-groomed appearance to her advantage, she'd been gathering various pieces of information from adventurers at the tavern.

Through that, she had already pinpointed the name of her enemy: a man called Ajas.

She had been hassled by drunks on several occasions, but she had managed to get through it all with her natural quick wit.

The fact he could hold his own, at least somewhat, against the man from the Intelligence Division led by Egg was surely thanks to Ilse's exceptional abilities.

...Though, to be fair, only one person from the Intelligence Division had been sent to investigate Ajas.

Watching Ilse, who seemed somewhat calmer now, Rei cautiously opened his mouth.

"You identified Ajas as Ajas... as your enemy, because of that snake tattoo, right? If that's the case, then... just maybe, just maybe... could it be someone else who happened to have the same tattoo?"

"No."

Ilse declared immediately, convinced beyond doubt that she was absolutely right.

If it had been just the face, a case of mistaken identity might have been possible.

Years had passed since the day her family was killed.

No matter how much hatred filled her being, memories still faded with time.

But... that left hand.

Ilse could never have mistaken the snake tattoo on his left hand, as if it were climbing a tree.

Rei understood that too. Hearing Ilse's assertion, he nodded without any particular surprise before speaking.

"If that's the case, then the question becomes what Ajas is thinking by lying low like this... no, that's not quite the right expression. Anyway, the issue is whether he's trying to keep a low profile..."

"Probably. I suspect he's plotting something."

"Yeah. We came to that conclusion here yesterday too. If Ilse's story is correct, he certainly doesn't strike me as the type to take on a normal job and work diligently."

"That's undoubtedly the case. At the very least, I can't for the life of me believe that man... Ajax has repented his past deeds and reformed."

Ilse murmured, as if suppressing the passion surging through her.

(To be precise, it's not that I can't believe it, but that I refuse to believe it.)

From Rei's perspective, Ilse was stubbornly fixated on revenge.

Of course, as a child... before she truly stood on her own, she witnessed her family being killed. It was only natural for Ilse to harbor a strong desire for revenge.

Rei understood that.

But... precisely because she'd lived with that vengeance since childhood, it had already become a part of Ilse herself.

From Rei's perspective, it was uncharacteristic of him to feel this way, but he found himself worrying about Ilse.

(Come to think of it, why am I so worried about Ilse?)

Suddenly, Rei questioned his own feelings.

Normally, Rei wouldn't delve so deeply into someone else's circumstances.

Even hearing about revenge might make him feel something, but that was all.

At the very least, he certainly wouldn't have thought to cooperate this seriously, like he was doing now.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

"Hm? Oh, nothing. Just wondering how to make contact with Ajash. If I just suddenly reached out, they'd probably be surprised."

"...Well, yeah."

Rei is famous in Gilm in many ways.

If someone like that suddenly reached out, it'd be impossible for them to just take it at face value.

"That's exactly why how I make contact is the problem, you see."

Ilse frowned at Rei's words.

But a good solution wasn't going to come to mind immediately.

As Rei and Ilze racked their brains together... suddenly, he realized something.

If Ajas had come to Gilm to accept a request, he would naturally have done so at the guild.

Furthermore, since he was operating as a well-behaved adventurer in Gilm, his standing with the guild was naturally high.

Then...

"Should we ask Lenora and Kenny?"

"Who are they? They sound like women's names."

"The receptionists at the guild. My assigned contact and her friend. They've helped me out a lot."

"...Huh."

For some reason, Ilse's voice dropped a notch.

The slight hint of a woman's enemy in Rei's eyes wasn't just Rei's imagination.

"Is something wrong?"

"No, nothing. I was just thinking you have quite a few beautiful acquaintances."

Ilse had been researching Ajas over the past few days, and naturally, she'd also looked into Rei, who had asked for her help with his revenge.

After all, Rei was famous in Gilm.

Compared to Ajas, he was much easier to research.

And researching Rei naturally led to researching the Crimson Wings he led, gathering various information about his party members as well.

Given that, it was only natural to hear stories about him being surrounded by stunning beauties like Marina and Vihera.

Especially since one of them was a former guildmaster, making it quite a hot topic.

For Ilse, hearing that Rei, who had such beauties at his side, was also close to the guild receptionist, it was only natural that a look of contempt mixed into her gaze.

After all, receptionists are the face of the guild, so naturally, those with attractive features are chosen.

Of course, mere beauty isn't enough; possessing the capability to handle the job properly is an absolute prerequisite.

These receptionists, who could be considered a kind of elite, naturally enjoy high popularity among adventurers.

Especially in Gilm, perhaps due to its frontier location, there were many beauties like Lenora and Kenny.

Moreover, anyone gathering even a little information about Rei would easily understand the feelings Kenny harbors toward him.

Knowing those circumstances, it wasn't strange at all that Ilse shot Rei a look of contempt.

"...Well, I won't deny that."

Rei couldn't deny that he had many beautiful acquaintances.

In fact, it was an undeniable truth. After all, he was loved by three women—Elena, Marina, and Vihera—each a peerless beauty in her own right, if not of the same type. If he denied it and those three heard, he'd likely suffer terribly in more ways than one later.

Seeing Rei's attitude, Ilse seemed to decide it was best to say nothing more. She spoke again.

"So, if we talk to the guild, can we make something work?"

"I can't guarantee it, but probably."

Rei made a bold promise, but considering how much he had contributed to the guild up to this point, it wasn't an exaggeration at all.

Especially regarding the current Gilm expansion project, Rei... and the members of the Crimson Wings he leads, were playing an incredibly significant role.

Rei used the Misty Ring to transport materials, haul timber felled in the Treant Forest, carry slain monsters... and so on.

Marina, using her spirit magic, achieves work efficiency dozens of times greater than ordinary workers... or even more.

Vihera, with her high combat skills, assists the guards by patrolling Gilm, capturing numerous suspicious individuals and troublemakers.

Byune isn't quite that much of a combat asset, but being a thief, she's still useful in various ways... though she can only communicate with a limited number of people.

Set has been entrusted with the crucial task of aerial patrols and also serves as a mascot, soothing the hearts of those within Gilm.

Considering all this, it's clear just how much the Crimson Wings are contributing.

Given how much work Rei and the others put in, the guild should be willing to cut them some slack.

In reality, they'd tolerate not just a little, but pretty much anything.

"...Understood."

After hearing Rei's explanation, Ilse eventually replied with those words, though she still harbored some dissatisfaction.

Ilse was absolutely certain that Ajas was her enemy.

But considering Ajas's current disguise, making a careless move now could lead to the worst possible outcome for Ilse—Ajas escaping and Ilse becoming a wanted criminal.

Ilse understood that, too.

That was precisely why she reluctantly nodded to Rei's words.

Confirming Ilse's agreement, Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

If she had refused, things could have gotten very messy.

Worst case, Ilse might have defied Rei and charged straight at Ajas...

"Alright, since we've settled that, I guess we should head out soon."

"Huh? Wait, what about your work, Rei?"

"This side is pretty flexible. Besides, no one should be that desperate for help right now."

Hearing Rei's words, Ilse felt the sheer power of a high-ranking adventurer.
