"...Well, I guess that's just how it goes."

Facing the five thieves sprawled on the ground, Rei muttered without much emotion.

Rei's innate combat prowess was already beyond human.

Even without the core elements like Set, Death Scythe, or the Twilight Spear, defeating opponents of this caliber with his bare hands was only natural.

"Th-thank you. You're strong. I'm surprised, and I'm saved."

From the direction Rei had come running, the man they had passed earlier appeared.

A young man, likely in his late teens or early twenties. ...Though being called young by Rei, who looked even younger, probably felt a bit awkward.

Rei shook his head at the man, as if to say not to worry about it.

"An opponent like that is no problem. More importantly, what were you doing getting ambushed by bandits in a place like this? If you'd been walking the main road, you wouldn't have been attacked by bandits so easily, right? This is near Rossi."

More precisely, it was the road between Rossi and Mejougo, a town that, regardless of its official status, was effectively under Jayah's control.

Frankly, Rei couldn't believe anyone would dare commit banditry right under Jaya's nose.

"Even if you ask why... I wasn't doing anything particularly special. I was just walking along the road when these people attacked me."

Though slightly puzzled by the man's somewhat refined manner of speaking, Rei decided not to press further.

(Well, he's probably some noble's bastard son or something like that. ...Not like I need to get involved in that anyway, so whatever.)

Leaving the man behind, Rei approached the unconscious bandits once more.

"Hey, wait! You're going to get hurt!"

"Relax. They're completely knocked out. Even if they woke up and attacked, I could handle it. ...You saw me take them down, so you know there's no need to worry that much, right?"

"Well... maybe you're right, but..."

Hearing Rei's words, the man stammered.

After all, he had seen with his own eyes how Rei defeated the thieves who attacked him... how he literally killed them instantly.

And without any weapons, just his bare hands.

But... still, considering Rei's appearance, it was probably understandable that the man felt uneasy.

"More importantly, were those all the bandits? Were there any others, or anything like that?"

"Well, to be honest, I'm not sure about that. I don't think there were any others, though."

As long as it was under Jarya's control, the road the man had been using naturally boasted some of the highest safety standards in the Kingdom of Reburina.

It was an ironic outcome, to say the least.

"I see. ...In that case, I don't expect we'll make much profit."

A bandit gang of five was quite small in Rei's eyes.

It would be more accurate to call them highway robbers than a proper bandit group.

Honestly, for Rei, who hunted bandits as a hobby, it seemed the expected haul would be considerably smaller than anticipated.

"Um... profits, you say?"

"Yeah. These guys are thieves. So, since they steal from others, don't you think it's okay for them to get robbed themselves?"

"Eh... uh...?"

The man looked genuinely perplexed, having understood that Rei was trying to take property from the bandits, whether joking or not.

Moreover, if his eyes weren't deceiving him, the man seemed quite skilled at both defeating thieves and taking their money.

The man watched in astonishment as Rei took the weapons from the men and stored them in the Misty Ring.

But he managed to suppress his shock and addressed Rei, who was now tying up the men.

"Um, thank you for helping us this time. My name is Ryutas. ...If it's not too much trouble, might I ask your name?"

"Hm? Oh, Rei. I'm an adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana, normally. Right now, I'm staying in Rossi for a little business."

A hint of surprise flashed across Ryutas' face at Rei's words.

He had thought Rei was strong, but he certainly hadn't expected him to be an adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana.

Even if he wasn't an adventurer from the Kingdom of Reburina, he probably assumed Ray was from one of the surrounding kingdoms. It was only natural to be surprised when it turned out to be the Kingdom of Mireana.

After answering Ryutas, Rei kicked the five unconscious men to wake them.

He hadn't intended to break any bones, but the kicks would undoubtedly leave bruises turning blue and black... kicks powerful enough to easily predict such an outcome.

"Gah!"

"Nuh!"

"Ow!"

"Gyah!"

"NYAAAAA!"

Each of them woke up screaming in pain.

Then, realizing they were bound, they looked in shock at Rei, who had done it.

"Hey! You think you can just do this to us?!"

The bearded man, likely the leader of the five, declared threateningly to Rei.

Rei was small in stature, so they probably thought threatening him would make him do their bidding.

But... naturally, Rei had no reason to obey such an opponent's orders.

"Adventurers hunting thieves isn't such a strange thing, is it? Since you guys are active as thieves yourselves, you must have acted with that kind of resolve, right?"

"W-well..."

The way the word "hunt" rolled off Rei's tongue, as if it were nothing special, finally made the man realize the person before him was no ordinary individual.

The leader's posture faltered slightly.

Then again, he had been knocked unconscious by Rei's attack earlier, so perhaps his realization was delayed.

"Now that we've established our relationship, here's the first question. Oh, and naturally, if you don't answer, you'll suffer. Be prepared."

He declared this while drawing a dagger from the Misty Ring.

...Actually, that dagger had belonged to the bandits... but in Rei's mind, it was already firmly established as something he'd taken from them, and therefore rightfully his own.

Rei gave the dagger in his hand a light flick.

But even without putting any force into it, the blade cut through the air with a sharp, piercing sound that echoed around them.

Hearing the wind-cutting sound of Rei's blade, the first to turn pale was the dagger's original owner.

Because it was the dagger he had used until now, he knew.

To produce that kind of wind-cutting sound required more than just a little skill... At the very least, it demanded a level of mastery far beyond his own.

Fixing his gaze on one such man, Rei flashed a small smile... then thrust the tip of the dagger toward the man who appeared to be the leader.

"Now then, I'm going to ask you some questions. Well, you don't have to answer if you don't want to... but if you don't, you might lose a finger, or an ear, or a nose, or an eye... or even a hand or a foot, depending on the situation. So be careful."

Rei delivered this warning in a completely relaxed, effortless manner.

That very composure sent a chill down the spines of the thieves listening.

Because they understood that the words coming from Rei's mouth weren't threats, but came straight from the heart.

Observing the thieves' reactions, Rei suddenly stopped moving as if he'd noticed something, then spoke again.

"Ah, I'd hate for you to be unable to spill the beans, so I'll spare your tongues."

What exactly were they supposed to feel relieved about?

That was the unanimous opinion of everyone present—the thieves, and even Ryutas who had been listening in—except for Rei himself.

But of course, Rei showed none of that. He stepped forward, dagger in hand.

One step... truly just one step forward, but that alone was enough to break the bandits' will.

"W-What? We'll give you any information you want! Please, spare us!"

The man who had been the leader of the bandits, who had been intimidating Rei just moments ago and even uttered words of contempt, immediately declared this and bowed his head, begging for mercy.

Seeing this man, Rei wore a look of surprise.

Rei had planned to make an example of this leader by hurting him, forcing the dagger's original owner to spill the information.

But in reality, before Rei could do anything, the leader surrendered completely without a fight.

Then again, considering Ray had intended to hurt him, the leader's immediate surrender could be seen as seizing the moment.

Anyway, watching the leader-type man who had just said he'd spill the information easily, Rei opened his mouth.

"Right. So, first off... is this all of you? All five of you?"

"Yes, these five are all of us."

The man answered without a moment's hesitation.

It was clear to anyone watching that he understood what would happen if he hesitated even for a few seconds.

"Operating in a place like this means you're not affiliated with Jayah, right?"

"No, we're not. Until recently, we were active in Silalune."

"Shirarune?"

Hearing the unfamiliar word, Rei turned his gaze to Ryutas. Ryutas seemed to understand what Rei wanted to know and opened his mouth.

"It's the name of a town in the provinces. Though, it's considerably smaller in scale compared to Rossi."

"...I see. I get the gist."

With Ryutas' explanation, Rei understood why the men had come here.

Probably, the five had been operating as bandits near that town of Shirarune, things had gone well, and they'd gained an odd confidence... Or perhaps they'd been sent as adventurers to subdue them and had fled.

(Probably the former.)

Recalling their confident expressions during their first encounter, Rei made that assumption.

"Where's your hideout?"

"There's a cave a little ways from here, so we use that as our hideout."

After hearing a few more details... suddenly, Rei sensed several figures approaching.

Glancing skyward without alerting those around him, he spotted Set flying above a spot a short distance away.

Judging that someone was likely approaching below him... he turned his gaze to Ryutas.

He couldn't imagine the thieves lying here, and Rei and Elena had been left behind with Rossi.

Therefore, the most likely possibility was Ryutas.

Though, whether Ryutas noticed the presence or not, he showed no particular change in demeanor.

(Looks like Ryutas isn't quite the spoiled brat he appears to be.)

Thinking this, Rei decided it didn't matter now and asked the bandits again for the exact location of their hideout.

After hearing it... and with no further need for the bandits, he addressed Ryutas again.

"So, what should we do with these guys? Normally, we'd just kill them or sell them to slave traders or something."

"Huh? You're asking my opinion?"

I didn't help capture these bandits at all.

That was Ryutas's argument, but for Rei, hauling these five all the way to Rossi just to sell them to slave traders seemed like a hassle.

He might as well dispose of them right here. But since he was going to kill them anyway, he figured turning them over to this Ryutas fellow as property would at least earn him a small favor.

"Yeah, if you leave it to me, killing them is the only option... but you're different, right?"

Ryuutas tilted his head in puzzlement, meeting that meaningful gaze.

"Well, if you say you have no use for these guys anyway, I'll dispose of them here... What do you say?"

"In that case, I'll take care of it myself."

The matter was settled quickly after that. Rei left Ryutas and the bound bandits behind and promptly departed.

The bandits finally relaxed, relieved that the source of their fear was gone.

For about ten minutes, Ryutas and the bandits remained silent together in that spot... until suddenly, nearby bushes rustled.

The bandits startled at the man, but Ryutas showed no particular concern and spoke up.

"You're late."

"My apologies, young master. But with that Crimson one as our opponent... if we'd been careless, he might have noticed us."

Over ten men emerged, speaking thus, but Ryutas smiled and shook his head.

"Not 'might have noticed'. They definitely would have noticed. Otherwise, they wouldn't have left me here and slipped away so quickly."

"...I see. I've heard that Crimson is investigating us. Young Master, it was sheer luck that they didn't realize you were one of Jaya's men. That's how we should put it."

"Heh, yes. ...Then I'll leave the disposal of these men to you. Honestly, causing unnecessary trouble in Jaya's territory."

The gaze Ryutas cast as he muttered this wasn't the friendly one directed at Rei... it was one that regarded the bandits as if they were nothing more than stones by the roadside.
