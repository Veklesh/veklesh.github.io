The night Rei and the others arrived in Dolgana, the town was in an uproar.

That said, it wasn't a disturbance in the negative sense; it was more like a festive commotion.

Dorgana was a town in terms of scale, but still just a town in the small country of Reburina.

Naturally, it was unusual for nearly a thousand people to arrive in a single day, so it was only natural that this alone caused quite a stir.

Furthermore, the Zolge Trading Company made substantial commercial gains by purchasing various goods from Dorgana's shops. Then, come nightfall, a massive ship suddenly appeared just outside the city, and many of the women who had arrived during the day—along with a few men—boarded it. It was impossible for this not to attract attention.

To begin with, not many residents of Dorgana had ever seen a ship before, and for many, the sand ship was the first vessel they'd ever laid eyes on.

As a result, many people made the trip out to the edge of town just to see the sand ship. Seeing this, Gimekara even blurted out to Rei, "Shouldn't we have charged admission?"

Of course, by nightfall, the onlookers had all returned to Dorgana. Rei and the others had finished their meal and now had free time.

...Now, Rei had thought that since they were traveling, most people would likely eat in Dolgana. In reality, however, nearly everyone had returned to the sand ship to eat.

While financial considerations played a part, the primary reason was likely the water produced by the flowing blade dagger, available only at night.

It was just water, but in terms of taste, it was an exquisite water that even royalty and nobility couldn't drink.

Given that opportunity, it was only natural that many prioritized drinking it over dining in town.

After all...

"Rei-san! This water sells like crazy!"

Even Gime Kara, usually so mild-mannered, said it with a gleam in his eyes.

With the Running Water Dagger and Rei's magic, it was possible to create water worthy of being called heavenly nectar.

With no material costs whatsoever, it was undeniably the most profitable product imaginable.

"Sorry, but I have no intention of turning this into a business. It's enough for us to drink among ourselves."

To be precise, it was obvious that selling this water would lead to all sorts of trouble.

It wouldn't be surprising if nobles seeking delicious water... or even royalty... wanted to surround Rei.

If that happened, Rei would naturally refuse, leading to conflict.

And in the worst case, he might not be able to stay in the Kingdom of Mireana any longer.

While Rei was prepared to abandon the kingdom if push came to shove, he still had no intention of forcing things in that direction himself.

After all, the water produced didn't flow in large quantities; it only came out little by little.

Given that, if he were to seriously sell the water, he'd have to keep producing it nonstop all day long.

"I see. ...But, no, the reason so few people have fallen behind in this group is definitely this water. You only distribute it at night, right?"

"That's right."

"If they use this water to relieve their daytime fatigue, it's no wonder they just fall asleep. Then, when they wake up the next day, they want this water again and push themselves to walk all day... and when night comes, they can drink this water again. Then they sleep to recover their fatigue... and so on."

That's true, but it's not like Rei planned it that way. It just happened naturally.

To be honest, when Rei first started giving them the water from the flowing dagger at night, it was meant as nothing more than a simple "good work today."

"Well, water aside... Elena, bring me the Paired Orb."

"Got it."

Outside the magic tent, near the campfire, Elena activated the Paired Orb.

Gimekara seemed to be seeing the Paired Orb for the first time too, watching Elena's actions with keen interest.

The Paired Orbs activated, and soon the figure of Ara appeared within them.

'Elena-sama, how are things on your end?'

"Not bad. ...No, thanks to the Zolge Trading Company pulling some strings, our travel speed should increase significantly starting tomorrow. So... Rei."

Rei, meeting Elena's gaze, nodded and addressed Ara.

"As Elena mentioned, since Gime Kara fulfilled their promise, we must fulfill ours. Are you still at the lord's manor, Ara?"

'Yes. We don't know when we might need to contact Lord Daskar.'

"Perfect timing. Then, could you put me through to Lord Daskar?"

'Understood. Please wait a moment.'

At Rei's words, Ara nodded immediately and disappeared from before the Paired Orbs.

For Daskar, this was a busy time.

Under normal circumstances, Daskar wouldn't just show up immediately because someone needed him.

The only reason it was possible was because Daskar held great expectations for this action involving Rei.

Gimekara shot Rei a surprised look at how casually he'd sent Daskar away without any prior arrangement.

"That's impressive, Rei."

"It just means Lord Daskar has high hopes for our actions. For him, welcoming this many people must have been an unexpected stroke of luck."

'It was unexpected, but I did anticipate something might happen.'

Interrupting Rei and Gimechara's conversation, Daskar appeared on the other side of the Paired Orb.

"My apologies for disturbing you during your busy schedule, Lord Daskar."

'No need to apologize. So... this concerns the Zolgea Trading Company, correct?'

"Yes. We've gathered everything we offered as conditions for introducing you to Lord Daskar, so we've decided to pay the corresponding price."

"I see. The carriage, the horses to pull it, various supplies, and the guards... Did you genuinely gather all that?"

"I personally verified everything. The carriages, the horses, the guards. The other supplies too—all seem in order."

"Understood. ...And? Where are the Zolgee Trading Company people?"

Prompted by Daskar, Rei turned his gaze toward Gimechara.

At that signal, Gimekara stood before the Paired Orb, looking tense.

"Pleased to meet you, Daskar-sama. I am Gime Kara of the Zolgea Trading Company."

Gimekara bowed before the Paired Orb.

What hung there was an intense sense of tension.

Gimekara, who had encountered countless people in his daily life, would not normally be this nervous about meeting someone... especially not through a Paired Orb rather than face-to-face.

But the figure reflected in the Paired Orb before him now was Daskar—the central figure of the Neutral Faction, one of the three major factions of the Kingdom of Mireana.

The outcome of negotiations with this man could mean the very collapse of the Zolge Trading Company.

Moreover, the vast sums of money and effort expended to arrange this meeting would be wasted.

Considering that, there was no such thing as being too focused for a negotiation where even the slightest mistake was unforgivable.

"I hear you. Jaya and her companions, is that right?"

Daskar stated in a slightly provocative tone.

His question was meant to observe how Gime Kara, present as a negotiating partner, would react to the words coming from his own mouth.

If he had flown into a rage here, Daskar would have considered him unworthy of negotiation.

But... whether Gime Kara understood this or sensed it instinctively, he spoke calmly.

"It may have appeared that way to an outsider, but the Zolge Trading Company has merely fulfilled its duty as a trading company. As long as the other party desires our goods and possesses sufficient funds, we have no choice but to comply."

"Oh? Then let me ask you this: if you were to come to Gilm, and a criminal organization approached you with a deal, would you agree to it?"

One wrong word here, and the negotiations would end immediately.

Thinking this, feeling a chill down his spine, he managed to speak.

"In the Kingdom of Reburina, such actions might be possible. But in Gilm—a place utterly unknown to us, an unknown world—I don't believe we could make such a reckless move."

If one read deeply into it, this statement meant they wouldn't get involved with the underworld until they moved to Gilm and built their strength, while simultaneously declaring that once they gained that strength, they would engage in deals with criminal organizations.

Daskar didn't respond immediately to Gime Kara's words, returning his gaze intently.

Though not physically present before him, the intensity of that gaze was so powerful it felt as if it carried a tangible, physical pressure.

Gimekara had survived countless hellish situations before, yet the pressure emanating from someone he hadn't even met face-to-face made him involuntarily want to step back.

Of course, the hellish situations Gimekara had experienced weren't literally life-or-death struggles.

But in terms of whether dozens or hundreds of people working for the Zolgee Trading Company would survive if the negotiations failed, they were far more brutal than any direct combat.

In that sense, it could be said he had survived even more brutal trials than those risking their own lives.

Even someone like Gime Kara cannot negotiate with Daskar as he normally would.

Daskar stared intently, uttering no particular words, yet still forced Gimekara to endure an unreasonable experience—as if the surrounding temperature were dropping, or rising...

Several minutes passed in silence. ...Suddenly, the pressure emanating from the Paired Orb on the other side vanished.

"I see. I understand your reasoning. ...But just because I accept you into Gilm here, don't think I'll go easy on you when it comes to investigations or disciplinary actions if something happens, got it?"

The confident words from Daskar's mouth signified just how much he trusted his subordinates.

Indeed, the capabilities of Gilm's guards and knights were exceptionally high.

Being the sole frontier of the Mireana Kingdom, it held various benefits.

Materials found only in the frontier, monster materials, meat, and magic stones unique to the frontier.

Moreover, the high-rank adventurers drawn precisely because it is a frontier could be considered a significant human asset.

Given such a location, it was only natural that criminal organizations would gather to profit, and various underground groups, large and small, existed, including those in the slums.

The guards and knight orders tasked with policing these groups, and sometimes fighting them... naturally boasted extremely high levels of training.

Even though the Zolge Trading Company held considerable influence in a small country like Reburina, if asked whether they could come to Gilm and operate as they had before, the answer would be no.

Rather, if they tried such a thing, they would undoubtedly be devoured by the existing organizations.

Even if Gimekara claimed they'd take such action after growing stronger, could they really navigate things smoothly against organizations that had survived in Gilm all this time? It wouldn't be that simple.

"Besides, if you lot were connected to criminal organizations in Gilm, that would be spitting in my face—I'm the one who allowed you into Gilm. If that happened... you know what would follow, right?"

Seeing Daskar say this with a smile made it easy to imagine what would happen if they really tried something like that.

If you disgraced the lord like that, it wouldn't be surprising if they sent skilled adventurers... the likes of Rei, no less.

"If you're still willing, do as you please. ...Of course, if you dislike that, you always have the option of not joining Gilm."

Gimekara was told this, but he had already prepared various supplies, including the carriage and horses.

Refusing to go to Gilm at this point would inflict significant losses on the Zolge Trading Company.

Above all, considering the damage the Zolgei Trading Company would suffer if he remained in the Kingdom of Reburina, refusing here was simply not an option.

Then, perhaps somewhere other than Gilm... he thought, but places as appealing as Gilm were few and far between.

Moreover, the fact that construction was underway was an irresistible draw for the Zolgei Trading Company.

In the end... Gimekara nodded firmly, having fully understood Daskar's words.
