Violent... a tremor worthy of being called a violent shock struck the forest of Treant.

But the shaking itself didn't last very long.

It probably didn't even shake for a full minute.

As the shaking subsided, making it barely possible to stand, the first to speak wasn't Rei... nor Marina or Vihera... nor even Slesha or Runo. It was one of the other adventurers.

"Hey, look! The monsters are collapsing!"

The one who shouted was an adventurer who had been attacking the monsters that had frozen in place just before the earthquake struck.

At his words, everyone nearby turned to look at the monsters.

There were various monsters, including Treants, but now every single one of them was disintegrating.

It was as if their entire bodies had turned to dust, being carried away by the wind and vanishing.

"What's happening? The trees growing here aren't affected at all... Why is this happening?"

One of the researchers, eyes gleaming, fixed his gaze on the collapsing monsters.

The others around him, seeing the monsters crumbling before their eyes, must have felt something eerie.

They instinctively took a step back, then another.

G...g...g... A deep, rumbling bass note echoed... no, resounded from deep within Treant Forest.

That sound, which came with the monster's collapse, was certain to hold some meaning for anyone who heard it.

"Everyone, prepare to respond to anything that happens!"

The moment she heard that deep rumble, Marina shouted to all the adventurers present.

It was likely because her command during the battle against the monster horde had been so precise. The adventurers, who had been in a daze, snapped back to reality at Marina's voice and immediately readied their weapons and assumed battle stances.

"You all must escape from here immediately. Take the carriage if you must, or run if you have to."

"No, but..."

Marina's words were directed at the researchers next, but those addressed couldn't simply retreat in silence.

After all, before them stood something they had never seen or heard of before—the very existence of Treant Forest... or rather, its presence.

Their intellectual curiosity as researchers was far too strong. Leaving this behind and simply returning to Gilm was utterly impossible.

"If you stay here, you might die! I won't have the luxury to protect you like I did earlier."

"...We don't mind."

One of the researchers replied to Marina's words, though with a slight hesitation.

In the eyes of the researcher who spoke, a strong will burned... a light colored by intellectual curiosity.

That they, themselves, would unlock the secrets of Treant Forest. Marina was rendered speechless by his declaration.

And as Marina faltered, the other researchers nodded in agreement with the first researcher's words.

"Me too."

"Me too."

"Me too."

"Me too."

"Me too."

Each declared this, fixing Marina with a gaze that said they would not budge from this spot.

Faced with these researchers, Marina tried to speak again... but the next instant, a sound louder than anything before echoed through the forest.

It was like an explosion.

An explosion that could be seen, heard, and understood even from outside Treant Forest, where the adventurers currently stood.

It had occurred within Treant Forest.

Only the three present—Rei, Vihera, and Set—could deduce that the explosion had occurred at the very heart of Treant Forest... the place they had been just moments before, now vanished by the earthquake.

"...What do you think?"

Vihera asked briefly, but that alone was enough for Rei to understand what Vihera was trying to say.

"Well. I don't have a good feeling about this. No matter how you look at it, it only gives me a bad feeling."

"I figured."

"Grr."

A short distance away from the two people and one beast engaged in this conversation, Marina spoke up, deciding she had no more time to waste on the researchers.

"Fine. Those who think it's okay to die here can stay. But we don't have the resources to protect you anymore, so protect yourselves. ...And you lot, head to the Guild immediately. Tell them something's wrong... and if possible, bring reinforcements."

Marina addressed the carriage driver—a guild employee.

The guild employees nodded at Marina's words and managed to get the horses moving.

The horses, unsettled by the earlier rumbling and explosions, looked around restlessly.

They seemed almost like small animals that understood something was amiss.

For that very reason, the driver's attempt to leave this place must have been a welcome relief for the horses as well.

Pulling the carriage, the horses immediately departed from the spot.

No, it would be more accurate to say they fled.

Watching this out of the corner of his eye, Rei turned his gaze to the sky... to the forest of Treant illuminated by moonlight.

"Well... I wonder what will come out."

"Well, considering Rei angered Treant Forest, I doubt anything friendly will appear here."

As if responding to Vihera's words, Rei noticed an emotion directed from the forest's center toward their location... no, clearly aimed at him personally. It could be interpreted as hostility, hatred, or rejection.

(Wait, is this directed at me personally? Well, I can't say I'm surprised.)

Seeing the tree root doll burned to ashes by the magic Rei had unleashed had led to the current situation.

Thinking about it that way, it wasn't strange for such negative feelings to be directed at Rei personally rather than everyone present... in fact, it was only natural.

"...Looks like things are developing just the way Vihera likes them."

Holding the Death Scythe, he waited for the presence directing hostility toward him to reveal itself.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Soon, that roar echoed throughout the surroundings.

No, Rei and the others didn't realize it, but that roar hadn't just echoed throughout the entire Treant Forest. It had reached Gilm... and even as far away as Abuelo.

A roar powerful enough to reach that far would normally be enough to rupture the eardrums of those who heard it.

But perhaps because the roar itself was a voice imbued with magic, no one's eardrums burst upon hearing it.

...Instead, like monsters struck by the King of Set's overwhelming pressure, countless beings found themselves unable to move.

Still, the fact that only those near Treant Forest were immobilized... was less a matter of luck and more because the one they hated was right there.

"Damn, noisy bastard."

Muttering, Rei turned his gaze toward Treant Forest... or more accurately, toward the entity emerging from within it.

In a word, it was a giant turtle.

However, countless trees sprouted from its shell, it had three extended necks, and sharp fangs protruded from its mouth.

It had a total of six legs, and the tip of its tail bore something resembling a giant chestnut or sea urchin, like a morning star.

Despite the trees growing from its shell reaching about ten meters in height, the highest point of the giant turtle's shell surpassed the height of the trees.

From that lofty shell, even more trees grow.

Such a colossal turtle, truly worthy of the phrase "looking up to it," walks straight toward Rei and the others, snapping the trees of Treant Forest as it advances.

With each step, a slight tremor rippled through the surroundings, a testament to the sheer weight of this colossal creature.

It seemed to be the embodiment of Treant Forest, or perhaps its very essence itself. But was it acceptable for such an entity to walk through the forest, destroying it as it went? Rei thought this as he scanned his surroundings.

The roar unleashed from the giant turtle's mouth moments ago had rendered most of the adventurers present unable to move.

Considering that only adventurers of a certain rank or higher accepted this quest, it was clear just how powerful that roar must have been.

(Well, it seems that roar can't be used just like that, though.)

If it could still unleash a similar roar, it could do so repeatedly, not just once.

The fact that it hasn't happened suggests there's some reason behind it.

(Meaning, if we can somehow deal with these guys who are still reeling from that giant turtle's preemptive roar, they'd still be a viable fighting force. Besides, these aren't just greenhorns who just became adventurers. They've survived countless deadly situations to reach this rank.)

Of course, some might have risen in rank without any real hardship, purely by luck, but such individuals are extremely rare.

Given that, even if they're frozen now by the giant turtle's roar, once they can move again, they should be able to participate in combat without issue.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei paused to think... then let out a small sigh, resigned to the situation, and began to gather his magic power.

The first to react to Rei's actions was Runo, who possessed the magic eyes that could see magic power.

Runo herself, having survived more hellish situations as a solo adventurer than those in parties, wasn't immobilized by the giant turtle's roar.

However, Slesha, who was part of the party even if only temporarily, was still very inexperienced compared to the other adventurers present. Naturally, she was frozen in place by the giant turtle's roar.

Runo was trying to figure out how to help her partner when, the moment she saw the magic power enveloping Rei's entire body, she couldn't open her eyes, as if she'd been blinded by the sun.

Rei usually concealed his magic with the New Moon Ring, but now he was deliberately releasing it.

It was precisely because Runo possessed the magic-seeing magic eye that witnessing Rei's magic temporarily blinded her.

Moreover, Runo wasn't the only one who noticed Rei's state.

Marina, Vihera, and Set were certainly aware, as were those who could still move despite the giant turtle's roar.

...Though Byune, who was part of Rei's party, had been immobilized by the roar.

The giant turtle itself, while moving, must have also noticed something amiss with Rei.

After all, this giant turtle had appeared specifically to eliminate Rei, a threat to Treant Forest.

Given that purpose, it was impossible for it not to notice the change in Rei.

Yet its movement speed didn't increase significantly—perhaps due to the giant turtle's physical structure.

Using its six legs, it advanced toward Rei, shaking the ground with each step.

Keeping the giant turtle firmly in sight, Rei concentrated his magic power even further, compressing it.

Rei's immense magical power condensed to its absolute limit... until eventually, it compressed to a point where it became visible—a magical force normally imperceptible to all but those with special senses like Runo.

And then, in the next instant... Rei's magic power was dyed with his own attribute, becoming a Crimson magic power that wrapped around his body.

The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour... Rei’s ultimate skill.

It was precisely because of this skill that Rei could fight against Noiz, a Rank S adventurer.

And... when such a skill was suddenly unleashed right before their eyes, the adventurers couldn't help but react.

Even the adventurers, immobilized by the giant turtle's roar, couldn't help but shift their focus when something surpassing the paralysis caused by that roar erupted right beside them.

Seeing Rei standing where their gazes now fell, the shock of the sight blew away the paralysis from the roar.

That's how powerful the shock of seeing Rei activate the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour must have been.

Of course, Rei had intended that effect, and he wasn't about to let this opportunity slip away.

"Everyone, fall back from here for now! We can't move freely in the forest!"

"H-hold on a second! Are you planning to take on that monster!?"

One adventurer shouted at Rei's words.

Fighting a monster that gave the illusion of a mountain itself moving.

They couldn't possibly believe they were capable of such a thing.

They were confident in their skills, after all, being active in Gilm.

But... even so, if asked if they could take on such a colossal monster, they couldn't honestly nod in agreement.

"...Right. Then, only those who believe they can fight should come. The rest, report this matter to Gilm. Also, it would be helpful if you could bring reinforcements."

Saying this, Rei led only those he judged capable of fighting away from the forest.

Some suggested it might be better to fight while hiding among the trees of Treant Forest, but when told you never knew when one of those trees might become a Treant, they agreed to head outside the forest where they could focus solely on the giant turtle.

Among those left behind was Byune. Considering her strength, it was probably unavoidable.
