Though Bobbs' appearance took him by surprise, once Rei realized Bobbs had arrived, he didn't show any further shock.

"Hey, long time no see. It's been a while since we met face-to-face like this, but I've been hearing about your exploits."

Rei returned a smile to Bobbs's manly grin.

"I don't think it's anything that deserves that much praise."

"Come on, no one would believe you if you said that. ...Well, whatever. More importantly, we need to talk fast."

There was no need to guess what he was referring to.

After all, Rei and Vihera had come here specifically to discuss information about Ambris.

Rei understood that, so he nodded and opened his mouth.

"I'll get straight to the point. I saw Ambris with my own eyes. But he vanished right before us. He did it by burrowing into the ground."

"...Burrowed into the ground, huh? So it can move underground?"

"From what I saw, it certainly looked that way. In fact, I think that's precisely why we couldn't find it—because it was moving like that."

If it moves underground, naturally you can't see it there.

Of course, it doesn't mean Ambris always moves underground, but just knowing it has the ability to do so definitely makes tracking it down harder from now on.

Then again, since we don't actually have any abilities to probe underground, it's also true that searching for it won't really add much new hassle.

"So it really was black mist?"

"Yeah. No doubt about it. Just like the guild said, it was black mist. And magic stones or anything like that... weren't there, right?"

Vihera nodded at Rei's gaze.

"It was black mist, I'm certain of that. And I didn't sense any will either."

"...You didn't sense a will?"

At Lenora's words, which seemed to express incomprehension, Vihera nodded.

"Yes. When we discovered Ambris's form, Set let out a growl. But... Ambris showed us no reaction whatsoever before sinking back into the earth. If Ambris had any sort of self-awareness, wouldn't it have reacted to seeing Set?"

"That's... probably right. Living in Gilm, it's hard to grasp, but if you consider Set-chan a Rank A monster... no, a rare species, that would make him equivalent to a Rank S monster."

"Normally, a monster like that would show some kind of reaction if it were near you... but there was none, huh?"

Bobbs murmured as if continuing Lenora's words, and Rei nodded in agreement.

"Yeah. He had absolutely no interest in us... or rather, it was questionable whether he even recognized us as living creatures."

"That's troublesome. If it at least recognized us as living beings, we'd have some options."

The other three agreed with Bobbs's irritated mutter.

Whether it attacked or fled, if it showed any reaction at all, there were numerous options—traps, luring it out, and so on.

But if it was going to ignore them completely, they had no idea what kind of reaction to expect.

So far, there was no information that it had attacked Ambris—after all, Rei and the others were the ones who confirmed its existence—but that didn't mean it didn't possess any means of attack.

That would make it extremely difficult to counter.

"Even if someone finds it, they can't just carelessly attack it, right?"

"Mr. Bobbs, we were told from the start that physical attacks are ineffective against Ambris, and it possesses high magical defense. So, what would happen with a half-hearted attack..."

"Right. So, to attack effectively, we still need someone with a powerful offensive method. ...It's a shame Elk isn't here."

Elk's Axe of the Thunder God is an incredibly powerful magic item.

It's exactly the kind of thing that would undoubtedly be useful in a situation like this.

"I've never met him personally, but there are a few other adventurers with nicknames in Gilm, right? Then maybe we could get them to join us..."

"They already are. ...Or rather, you've never met any other nicknamed adventurers besides Elk?"

Bobbs looked slightly surprised, but Rei had never been particularly good with people.

He might have responded if approached, but he generally didn't make the first move to talk to someone without a reason.

"As for nicknamed adventurers I know besides Elk... Immovable? Heavenly Bow?"

"...Actually, I'm more surprised you know those two."

Bobbs faltered for a moment at the nicknames Rei mentioned, but what finally appeared on his face could only be described as a wry smile.

Immovable, Heavenly Bow. Those nicknames belonged to two of the world's three Rank S adventurers.

They weren't the kind of people you could just meet whenever you wanted.

"Well... I suppose it's because it's Rei. Yeah. I think it's best if I pretend I didn't hear any of that just now."

Lenora's gaze shifted away from Rei as she muttered, staring at the conference room wall.

"Even if Immovable is impossible, Heavenly Bow might be... No, probably not."

Bobbs' words trailed off, turning into a resigned sigh.

Fudo was a Rank S adventurer of the Bestir Empire; it was immediately clear he couldn't possibly be summoned to Gilm. So, perhaps a Rank S adventurer from the Mireana Kingdom...

That was his reasoning, but Rank S adventurers were under the control of the royalist faction.

To summon one from the capital to Gilm would require Daskar's intervention, and such a move would leave the Neutral Faction, to which Daskar belonged, deeply indebted to the royalist faction.

Of course, if things truly became desperate, Daskar wouldn't hesitate to bow to the royalist faction. But the reality was that Gilm wasn't yet cornered to that extent.

"Mr. Bobbs. Since I'm looking away, could we change the subject? Specifically, how can we defeat Ambris?"

"...Right. Using the power of the Heavenly Bow would defeat Ambris easily enough... but now is not the time to rely on that, is it?"

Bobbs' muttered words drew a sigh of relief from Lenora.

She was just a receptionist at the Adventurers' Guild; getting dragged into the squabbles between factions like the Neutral Faction and the royalists was something she'd rather avoid if possible.

And to avoid the need to summon the Heavenly Bow, Lenora hurriedly opened her mouth.

"Anyway, about defeating Ambris. According to the Guildmaster, physical attacks are basically useless. We have to use magic attacks, but Ambris's magic defense is extremely high. So far, so good?"

After laying out the premise, Lenora watched Bobbs, Rei, and Vihera's reactions.

Seeing the three nod in turn, Lenora continued.

"Since the only recorded sightings of Ambris date back three hundred years, it's not very reliable... but apparently, only powerful attacks using magic can actually damage it. The problem is, we don't know exactly how powerful those attacks need to be."

"We have records from three hundred years ago, right? Can't we make a rough estimate based on that?"

Lenora shook her head at the obvious question that came from Rei's mouth.

"Some information remains, but the details... Even though the Guildmaster is doing everything possible, gathering more information than we have now is a bit difficult."

"I see. So, it seems the only option is for someone capable of magical attacks to step in."

"I suppose so. And the prime suspect is Rei, right?"

"Yes. As Ms. Vihera said. In truth, those capable of attacks using magic power are by no means few."

"To be precise, we should say 'not few within Gilm,' shouldn't we?"

Bobbs murmured, catching Lenora's words.

Gilm attracts powerful adventurers from all over the Mireana Kingdom... sometimes even from abroad.

Naturally, there are more magic users here than in other towns or villages, and even those who can't use magic often utilize magic power as a skill.

"...But you mean few possess enough offensive power to take on Ambris?"

Vihera asked Bobbs, though she herself was using a magic-based skill—the Magic-Infused Palm.

But when asked if that Magic-Infused Palm could defeat Ambris, even he couldn't help but tilt his head in doubt.

"Yeah, that's how it is. ...Well, actually, since we don't know what level of magical power an attack needs to be effective, we'll just have to try it out."

"Looks like tomorrow's gonna be busy,"

Everyone present nodded at Rei's muttered words.

Now that Ambris's presence had been confirmed, it was only natural to efficiently deploy those capable of magic-based attacks starting tomorrow.

And with Set around, Rei and Vihera would naturally be tasked with surveying a wide area.

(Huh? But doesn't that mean it'll end up being no different from before?)

Suddenly, Rei wondered if that meant his actions wouldn't fundamentally change at all.

True, his range of action would be the same, but his actual actions—picking off Ambris the moment he was spotted with powerful magic attacks or whatever—meant it couldn't be exactly the same as before.

(Powerful magic, huh... If that's the case, it's gotta be the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.)

A skill that allowed him to concentrate his magic power to the point of making it visible.

That skill, now one of the most powerful within Rei himself, should undoubtedly be effective against Ambris.

No, rather, if an attack using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour didn't work, it would mean Rei had effectively run out of ways to attack Ambris.

"There are several others with titles, but honestly, Rei, who can fly through the skies riding Set, has the best chance of finding Ambris. It'll be a lot of trouble, but I'm counting on you."

Bobbs bowed deeply, but Rei shook his head dismissively.

"Gilm is like home to me too. If merchants stop coming because they fear the monster hordes, and Gilm ends up declining, that'd be a problem for me."

For Rei, his true homeland was, of course, Japan.

But now that he'd come to Elgin, he couldn't go back to Japan.

(Well, considering Takumu came here and the Kabajido incident, maybe there's a chance I could return to Japan after all...)

The two others confirmed to have come from Japan besides himself... Takumu of the Zepairu clan and Kabajido, the former Crown Prince of the Bestir Empire, flashed through Rei's mind.

Takumu seemed to have come to Elgin with his physical body intact, but Kabajido, like Rei, should have been a reincarnated being whose soul alone had come to this world.

Even considering all that, Rei didn't truly believe he could go to Japan.

Even if he did go to Japan in his current state, it was obvious it would cause a commotion.

Rei looked completely different from when he had been in Japan before, and above all, he now had a partner named Set.

Taking a griffin to Japan would undoubtedly cause a huge commotion.

That said, Rei's house was near the mountains.

He doesn't live in the city, so it's entirely possible he could avoid being noticed.

(Besides, my ethics and such have loosened up quite a bit since living in Elgin.)

Given that I no longer hesitate to kill people, if you ask whether I could live quietly in Japan, the answer is no.

Of course, I wouldn't kill someone who'd done nothing to me over some petty reason, but if someone tried to harm me, I wouldn't hesitate to fight back.

At the very least, I wouldn't agonize over attacking or killing someone like the novels and manga Rei read back in Japan.

(And what would happen if I brought someone like Vihera or Marina... or Elena (a woman) over to that world? Though Gilm doesn't have any.)

Even Rei, who'd seen beauties from around the world through magazines and TV, had no memory of ever seeing three or more beauties together, including Elena.

What would happen if such a person... or rather, three such people appeared together in Japan? It was obvious no matter how you looked at it.

Though, setting Elena and Vihera aside, the dark elf Marina probably isn't even in the realm of being judged as beautiful or not.

Rei thought it would be interesting if he could go to Japan someday.

...But the fact that he thinks of it as 'going' to Japan, not 'returning' to Japan, suggests that in Rei's mind, this Elgin is already where he belongs.
