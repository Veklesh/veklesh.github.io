"No luck again today... Dammit, it's almost Gamelion season already..."

The sun was about to set, and there wasn't much time left before the gates of Gilm closed... Elk stood alone, a gloomy expression on his face, joining the line of adventurers and merchants before the main gate.

As the line moved forward and he completed the procedures to enter the city, Elk headed for the guild.

He hadn't taken on any extermination requests, but he still had items like magic stones and proof-of-kill parts.

He didn't necessarily need to sell them today, but while magic stones were one thing, the proof of slaying parts and materials were pieces stripped from corpses.

Naturally, the longer he waited, the worse their freshness became, and the lower the selling price would be.

(Damn it, how long is this situation going to last?)

Elk inwardly seethed at his current situation... the fact that he still couldn't bring his only son back to consciousness.

"Hii!"

Unconsciously, that frustration must have shown on his face. A nearby adventurer, around twenty years old, let out a small scream.

"Whoops, sorry. I wasn't trying to scare you or anything."

The small cry snapped him back to reality, and he hurriedly offered an apology.

"N-no, it's... I mean... I'm not bothered by it, so it's no problem!"

For a Rank D adventurer like him, Elk, a Rank A adventurer with a notorious nickname, was an idolized figure.

He'd let out a cry at the anger radiating from Elk, but thanks to that, he'd been able to exchange words with Elk. You could call it luck.

But the Elk of the moment had no room to understand such feelings.

Deciding the matter was settled for now, he headed toward the guild counter.

(As for Min... I doubt I'll get any useful information from her either.)

As he spoke to mages and alchemists, the image of his wife, who had been acting independently, flashed through Elk's mind, but he couldn't place any hope in her actions.

It was only natural. He had consulted countless mages, alchemists, apothecaries, and all manner of people before, yet still found no clues.

Some had even tried to provide false information to extort a little money... but they ended up learning the hard way what happens when you incur the wrath of a Rank A adventurer.

"Ah, Elk-san! You finally made it!"

A fox-kin woman, clearly delighted to see Elk, called out cheerfully.

She was a woman with well-defined features, fitting for someone working as a receptionist.

Her fox ears, sprouting from her head, twitched as if expressing her joy.

Hearing her voice, Elk tilted his head.

"Did you need something? I don't recall accepting any requests from you."

"No, not at all."

Elk wondered if he had accepted a request and forgotten about it, but the receptionist shook her head in denial.

Normally, Min was there, so he wouldn't forget anything, but today he and Min were working separately.

That's why he wondered if maybe... But the receptionist's reassuring words that it wasn't the case made him breathe a sigh of relief, and he opened his mouth.

"So, what about me?"

"Yes. The Guildmaster instructed me to notify you immediately upon your arrival."

"The Guildmaster?"

Since Elk is also a Rank A adventurer, he's naturally fairly close with the Guildmaster.

Though, if anything, Min was closer to the Guildmaster than Elk was.

For Min, a skilled mage, he probably had a lot in common with Marina, a dark elf who used spirit magic.

Lately, with the Rodos business, they hadn't had time for that, but before that, Min and Marina would occasionally meet at taverns or restaurants to chat.

But judging by what the current receptionist said, it seemed this time the matter concerned him.

Though he felt some doubt about that, he couldn't very well ignore a summons from the Guildmaster just because he wasn't in the mood.

Well, if it had been any guildmaster other than Marina, he might have pulled such a stunt. But Marina was special.

She was the guildmaster of this frontier Gilm guild, a former skilled adventurer.

Yet she was caring and never unpleasant to deal with.

...Far from unpleasant, many adventurers were seriously smitten with Marina.

For ordinary people, the feminine allure Marina radiated was simply too intense.

It's even joked that when Marina makes an appearance, the brothel's earnings triple...

Only those connected to the brothel know whether that's a joke or not.

"How nice of you to come, Elk. Honestly, I was just thinking of contacting you, so it's a relief you came here instead."

Marina smiled, wearing her usual party dress with a plunging neckline that created a deep cleavage accentuated by her tanned skin.

Most men would have been so captivated by Marina's appearance that they'd be rendered speechless, but Elk was different.

His frequent encounters with Marina through his relationship with Min probably played a part.

Leaning back into the sofa, he spoke.

"So, you really need to talk to me? Not Min?"

"No, it could have been Min, actually. Or rather, it would have been best if Min had been here too."

Hearing that, Elk understood.

Not just me, not just Min, but wanting to see both of us.

With that, he could sort of understand where the conversation was going.

Probably, there was a request that needed their help.

"Sorry, but keep the requests short-term ones only. As you know, I'm pretty busy with various things right now."

"Huh?"

Marina tilted her head at the words coming from Elk's mouth.

The movement caused her silver hair to sway, catching Elk's eye. But right now, Elk was watching Marina with a puzzled expression.

"Huh? If you had business with me and Min, doesn't that mean you had a request?"

"Oh, you thought so? Well, not exactly. ...Well, I suppose if you look at it another way, I suppose you could call it a request?"

Elk looked taken aback by Marina's muttered response.

Honestly, when she said she wanted to call both him and Min, he'd assumed it was some kind of troublesome request.

But seeing her now, it hardly seemed that way.

It could be taken as a request. She said that, but it didn't make it a clear request.

"Sorry, but I'm pretty busy here. If it's not urgent, I'll have to take my leave."

He started to rise from the sofa he'd been sitting on...

"Oh? Are you sure? This might be your chance to bring your son, Rodos, back to consciousness!"

At those words from Marina, Elk's movement froze, as if time itself had stopped.

Several seconds passed in silence between them.

Finally, a voice squeezed from the depths of his gut emerged from Elk's mouth.

"What... do you mean? Unfortunately, jokes don't fly with me right now. Not even if it's coming from you, Guildmaster."

He could never forgive deception or lies, even if the offender was the Guildmaster.

Reaching for the handle of his beloved weapon, the Axe of the Thunder God, resting near the sofa, he asked.

Marina remained completely unperturbed, fully aware of the gaze directed at her, one that radiated a half-killing intent.

Marina specialized in spirit magic and the bow. Normally, facing a skilled fighter like Elk at this close range, with a gaze laced with murderous intent, would cause anyone to shrink back at least a little.

But Marina, facing such an Elk, had the composure to even smile.

For Marina, confrontations like this with Elk were nothing new; she'd experienced such tension countless times before. More than that, she had absolute confidence in her proposal.

...Though, if asked whether the magic imbued within the Silver Lion's heart could definitely restore Rhodes' consciousness, she couldn't say for certain.

Even so, Marina's proposal was undoubtedly far superior to searching for a way to restore Rhodes' consciousness with no clues whatsoever.

"Unfortunately... or perhaps fortunately, in this case. In any event, what I'm about to say is no joke."

Seeing Marina deliver this with such confident assurance, Elk must have realized she wasn't joking at all, that she meant every word.

Perhaps... just maybe, this could hold the key to restoring Rhodes' consciousness. Unconsciously swallowing hard, Elk opened his mouth.

"Tell me. What's your proposal?"

Seeing Elk ready to listen, Marina understood she'd cleared the first hurdle.

"First off, this isn't about Rhodes, but you know about Vihera, right?"

"Of course."

Elk had been involved, to no small extent, in the series of civil wars that began with the martial arts tournament in the Bestir Empire.

In fact, the very cause of Rhodes' unconscious state stemmed from that civil war.

Elk had met Vihera face-to-face and spoken with her.

"About that Vihera—you know she's unconscious now, just like Rhodes, because of what happened in Ambris this summer, right?"

"...Ah."

He wasn't very familiar with the details, but since he was based in Gilm, some information had reached him.

The reason I didn't know more was simple: most of my efforts were focused on finding a way to restore Rhodes' consciousness.

"So, Rei has also been searching for a way to restore Vihera's consciousness for a while now, just like Elk..."

Hearing that far, Elk understood what Marina was getting at.

She opened her mouth with a serious expression.

"Did you find it?"

"Yes."

"What exactly is it?"

Elk quietly asked Marina, who simply nodded at her own words.

He knew that if he hadn't been holding himself back, he would have shouted.

His words were quiet, but the force behind them could be said to contain Elk's entire being.

Marina paused before speaking in response to Elk's words.

"What I'm about to tell you... honestly, as an adventurer... no, within the Gilm... might contain information that could be fatal to your life in the Mireana Kingdom."

Even hearing this, Elk showed no sign of confusion whatsoever, waiting for Marina to continue.

Seeing Elk's expression, Marina spoke again.

"Understand? First, promise me this. Even if you choose to refuse after hearing what I'm about to say... this matter will remain a secret between you and Min alone. You must never reveal it to anyone else. ...My preamble got rather long, but this situation truly requires crossing that dangerous bridge."

"Understood. Even if I refuse what you're about to tell me, I won't reveal a single thing to anyone but Min. I'll make sure Min promises not to tell anyone else either."

Seeing no trace of deception in Elk's eyes—only his desperate determination to awaken Rodos—Marina finally nodded slightly and began her explanation.

It was the kind of story that would make any ordinary adventurer question their sanity.

"There's someone... someone? You could call a Rich Lord, someone Rei knows."

"..."

At those words from Marina, Elk urged her on without saying anything else.

Of course, he wasn't exactly unfazed.

In fact, when told Rei knew a lich lord, Elk's body froze for a moment.

But... precisely because of that, Elk sensed a possibility of restoring Rhodes' consciousness.

No matter how much they searched, they couldn't find a way to restore Rodos's consciousness, but if it was an undead...

"That Lich Lord... the one called Lord Grimm. They say if he uses the heart of a Rank S monster, there's a high chance he could restore consciousness."

"An S-rank monster? But that's—"

Rank S monsters are extremely rare beings.

You certainly couldn't just find one whenever you decided to look.

Seeing Elk's expression, Marina nodded in agreement.

"Yes, normally you wouldn't find an S-rank monster so easily. But right now... there's a place where we know exactly where one S-rank monster is."

"Huh!? That's right, the Silver Lion!"

Elk wasn't stupid by any means.

That's precisely why his thoughts immediately turned to the Rank S monster in the dungeon, the Silver Lion.

"That's right. ...However, to restore consciousness using this method requires a significant amount of magic power equivalent to the Silver Lion's heart. That's why we'll prioritize Vihera first, and if it's still possible afterward, we'll treat Rodos. ...If that's acceptable, would you like to join us in hunting the Silver Lion?"

After a moment's thought, Elk responded to Marina's words.

"I want to discuss it with Min."

"Yes. But we plan to leave Gilm tomorrow. If you're interested, you should start preparing immediately."

Elk nodded at her words and promptly rose from the sofa to consult Min.
