The next day, after deciding to keep an eye on Ajas for a little while longer... Rei was handling his work as usual.

"Oh my, Rei. That magic item called the Death Scythe... I must see it!"

"You never learn, do you? Forgotten how close you came to being crushed last time?"

The old wizard, Marz, shouted upon seeing Rei using the Death Scythe to smooth out the subtle distortions in the surrounding ground with terrain manipulation.

Rei's reply carried a hint of exasperation.

In fact, a few days earlier, when Marz had asked to see the Death Scythe more closely, Rei had granted permission... but the Death Scythe normally weighed around 100kg.

Even an exceptional wizard was physically just an ordinary wizard... and Marz was merely an old man. He simply couldn't lift that weight.

Seeing Rei hold the Death Scythe with one hand, seemingly unaffected by its weight, Marz probably hadn't realized just how heavy it truly was at first.

Even when Rei said it was heavy, Marz's interest in the Death Scythe was so strong he insisted he was fine... and while he did manage to escape before getting crushed, it felt like he'd barely escaped death.

Given that this was the same Marz who now asked to see the Death Scythe again, it was only natural for Rei to worry about him.

At first, Rei didn't know how to handle Marz, but he came to understand that Marz had no ulterior motives towards him, only pure respect for magic and magical items. That's why he started interacting with him more casually.

...Though he wasn't quite used to being treated with such reverence.

"It's fine. Last time it happened because I tried to catch the Death Scythe directly. If you just place it on the ground..."

"Well, fine then."

He pulled a cloth from his Misty Ring and spread it on the ground.

Placing the Death Scythe on top, Marz began examining it intently.

"Just so you know, we don't have much time."

"I understand!"

Responding to Rei's words while looking at the Death Scythe, Marz began examining it intently.

Glancing sideways at Marz's actions, Rei surveyed the surrounding situation.

Though still quite low, the new wall had already begun to spread.

Though to be precise, it wasn't spreading automatically; it was the craftsmen working hard to expand it.

Still, overall, it was far from complete.

After all, they were expanding the already massive Gilm by fifty percent.

(But then again, with a construction project of that magnitude, progressing bit by bit is probably necessary. It'd be nice if it could be completed in about a year... but is that even feasible?)

By Rei's standards, expanding a city by fifty percent was something he couldn't possibly imagine finishing in a single year.

But... this is the world of Elgin.

It's a world with magic.

Unlike the Earth Rei came from, there are no heavy machinery types, but magic exists to compensate for that.

It doesn't offer the same convenience as heavy machinery that anyone can use, but it's a power where some can accomplish tasks that would take heavy machinery ages in just seconds.

Of course, overall, only a few can use it, and it ceases to work when magic power runs out... so compared to heavy machinery, it has several inferior aspects.

However, the beastmen and dwarves working here possess raw strength incomparable to Earth's inhabitants, and the same goes for the adventurers.

Overall, Rei felt it was on par with Earth... or perhaps even surpassed it.

"Hm?"

Rei, who had been observing his surroundings, suddenly noticed a group of people gathered together.

He could tell it wasn't a hostile atmosphere, so they weren't fighting, but then why? He wondered.

"Marz, I'm going to check out that group over there... Have you heard anything?"

Even when Rei called out, Marz remained focused on the Death Scythe.

Watching this, Rei addressed someone nearby—not an adventurer, but a commoner hired for odd jobs.

"I'm going to check out that group over there. If Marz snaps out of it and I'm not back yet, tell him that."

"Ah, yes. Understood."

The man, who looked to be in his twenties and older than Rei, nodded without any particular resistance.

This man had been working here since the demolition of the old wall—since the very start of the expansion project—and had seen firsthand just how much of a force Rei was on this job.

That's precisely why he didn't feel any resistance to Rei's words and could nod in agreement without hesitation.

He understood that being slightly older held no real significance.

Leaving Marz with that man, Rei headed toward a spot a little further away where people were gathered.

As he drew closer, he could see four people—two men and two women—engaged in conversation.

As Rei had initially sensed, they weren't necessarily arguing or fighting...

They were all just talking with serious expressions.

"What's up? Did something get stuck during the construction or something?"

"Ah... Rei...?"

One of the two men and two women, a woman, noticed Rei and called out.

Her face bore a look of deep concern as she looked at Rei.

(Could it be... a fatal flaw in the construction... or something?)

The construction had already begun.

Although the new wall had only just begun to be built, many people had spent a long time meticulously calculating, coordinating, and preparing materials to carry it out.

Despite that, if a major flaw were discovered in the construction now... it would inflict enormous damage on Gilm, both financially and in terms of time.

And in the worst case, the entire project might have to be restarted from scratch.

With that thought, he turned his gaze toward her. The woman who had spoken to Rei seemed momentarily confused, as if unsure what had happened, but she quickly understood what had been said.

She shook her head frantically.

"No, it's nothing to do with this construction. It's just about our party..."

Her blue hair, reaching halfway down her back, swayed as she shook her head.

Having grown accustomed to Ilse's green hair, the word "traffic light" suddenly flashed through Rei's mind for some reason.

(Signals are green, yellow, red... but actually, it's green, yellow, red, right? ...But thinking about that now, here in Elgin, is pointless.)

Thinking this, Rei glanced at the others. Seeing the party consisted of a red-haired man and woman and a yellow-haired man only made him dwell on the traffic light analogy even more.

"Ah... right. Well, I guess it's good they're not involved in this construction."

Relieved they weren't connected to the construction, he still couldn't just leave the party alone in this situation.

Approaching them was probably Rei's mistake.

Normally, he might have walked away, but since they had been polite to Rei from the start, his goodwill toward them was fairly high.

Though it felt somewhat reluctant, Rei looked at the four people in front of him and spoke again.

"So, what happened? Telling me might not solve anything, but I'm willing to listen."

Hearing Rei's words, the blue-haired woman who had spoken first opened her mouth as if seeking support.

"Well, we're a Rank D party called Rain of Mercy. But one of our party members suddenly stopped showing up a few days ago..."

"Disappeared, huh? Without telling anyone?"

"Yes. After finishing work, she said she was going out to do some shopping, left the inn, and never came back. She's a woman, so we're worried about all sorts of things..."

"Just asking, but did that woman get hit on by some random guy passing by and run off with him... or is she still with that guy in some room somewhere?"

At Rei's words, the members of Mercy Rain all wore displeased expressions.

As if speaking for them all, the woman who had been talking with Rei spoke up again.

"She wouldn't do something like that. ...Of course, there's always the possibility of a fateful encounter or something like that..."

"There's no way Ryusha would do something like that!"

The man with yellow hair shouted.

His words carried a force that could only be described as full-throated.

The other three felt a sense of pain at the sight of him.

The feelings this man harbored for the missing woman were obvious to anyone watching... even to Rei.

"So, maybe she got caught up in some kind of incident?"

"That's possible. Or she might be helping someone. Ryusha was a kind soul, after all."

The other three nodded in agreement at the woman's words.

"Has this happened before?"

"Yes."

"...Then I don't think we need to worry that much. Maybe she's helping someone again?"

"I wish that were the case. ...But there are some strange rumors going around, so I can't help but worry."

"Strange rumors?"

"Yes. Haven't you heard? Apparently, more people are suddenly disappearing. ...Well, with this many people gathering, it's not surprising if someone vanishes for some reason."

"I suppose so."

Gilm had a considerable population to begin with, but the expansion work had increased that number even further.

Rei had never seen so many people gathered in a guild at Gilm before.

(Well, there were even more people in the Bestir Empire, though.)

Rei recalled the crowd at the martial arts tournament, but decided now wasn't the time for that and turned his gaze to the members of the Rain of Mercy.

"Where did this rumor about people disappearing even come from? I've never heard anything like it."

Though Rei moved between multiple locations in short order rather than working in one place, he hadn't heard that rumor.

Of course, he hadn't heard it from Marina or Vihera, who he ate dinner with every night.

Elena and Ara were basically holed up at Marina's house, and Byune was difficult to communicate with, so she didn't count in this case.

"It's not exactly a widespread rumor. More like a rumor-like rumor... I guess you could say."

"A rumor that's almost a rumor? Interesting way to put it. Anyway, what are you guys going to do now?"

"Luckily, today's work should be done by midday, so we were thinking of going out to look for it together this afternoon."

"I see. ...Rumors aside, all sorts of people are showing up in Gilm these days. Some of them might have strange ideas, so you should be careful about that."

"Yes, thank you."

The blue-haired woman bowed her head in response to Rei's words.

The other three also bowed toward Rei.

They were probably thinking they wanted Rei's help.

In truth, Rei's influence was no small thing... no, it was considerable.

But precisely because of that, if Rei were to casually lend a hand to someone, it was certain others would follow suit, demanding the same favor.

If the missing person had definitely been caught up in some kind of incident, they might have asked Rei for help even in that situation.

But right now, they knew nothing.

Maybe she's actually spending sweet time at the home of the man she fell for at first sight.

...Though that's probably the last thing the yellow-haired man, who harbors feelings for the missing Ryusha, wants to think about.

Rei considered saying something to the four of them, but ultimately decided that anything he said here would be meaningless.

"I see. Got it. If it was that Ryusha, right? If that woman gets seriously tangled up in some trouble, she should talk to the guards or the adventurers patrolling the streets. Especially the guards in Gilm are excellent. They deal with skilled adventurers practically every day."

A glimmer of hope flickered in the expressions of the four who heard Rei's words.

Leaving the four behind, Rei headed back toward where Marz was.

There, Marz was still examining the Death Scythe.

His childish excitement made it seem like he might collapse right then and there... even Rei thought so.

Indeed, several others nearby were keeping a close eye on Marz's state.

(Someone's gone missing... huh. Maybe I'll ask Vihera about it during dinner tonight. ...Could Ajas be involved somehow? I'm heading to Treant Forest later, so maybe I'll probe a little there.)

With such thoughts swirling, Rei began walking toward the base of the Death Scythe.
