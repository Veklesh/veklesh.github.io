"Yaaaaah!"

The halberd is swung with a battle cry.

Naturally, the blade is blunted, but this weapon—a combination of spear and axe—possesses enough power to easily kill a person with its sheer weight alone.

……Though, that’s assuming it actually hits.

“You’re being too optimistic.”

Dodging the halberd’s attack by a hair’s breadth, Vihera steps forward and swings her fist.

It wasn’t a particularly powerful blow, but it was still more than enough to knock the woman she was sparring with to the floor, which was Vihera.

“Next.”

It was a brief command, but upon hearing it, the man stood before Vihera, club in hand.

The club is a common weapon.

After all, even goblins often use clubs as weapons.

…Though, of course, the clubs goblins use are basically just broken branches they’ve snapped off nearby—not properly crafted, genuine clubs.

In that regard, the club held by the man who stepped in front of Vihera had been carefully shaped with its center of gravity in mind to make it easy to grip, and the handle was wrapped in leather to prevent slipping.

“Here I go!”

Shouting that, the man lunged forward and swung his club at Vihera.

“No matter how powerful it is, a wide swing won’t work.”

Unlike with the halberd earlier, Vihera dodged the blow and gently touched the club, redirecting its momentum.

The club’s trajectory was effortlessly altered, and it slammed into the floor.

“What!?”

The man let out a stunned cry—had he not expected his strike to be parried so effortlessly?—and before he knew it, his body was slammed onto the floor.

His hand holding the club had been grabbed, his legs swept out from under him, and he’d been slammed into the floor using that momentum—but he had no idea what had happened.

That said, since Vihera had pulled him up slightly just before he hit the ground, the man suffered almost no damage.

While Rei and Vihera were overwhelming their sparring partners, the remaining Byune was a slightly different story.

“Hmph!”

Byune dodged the spear with a crushed tip that was thrust toward her, then tried to use her speed to close the distance and get inside her opponent’s guard in one swift move. However, as a user of a polearm, she knew that she was vulnerable if an opponent managed to get inside her guard.

Furthermore, given that the petite Byune was wielding a dagger with a blunted blade, her options were limited… and that was precisely why he was able to counter her in advance.

“Hah!”

Facing Byune, who had slipped into his guard, the man closed the distance himself.

He closed the distance to a point where it was impossible for her to swing the dagger… and once that happened, the next moment would be a collision of their bodies.

And when bodies collide head-on, naturally, body weight becomes the most crucial factor.

If he possessed physical abilities that belied his appearance, like Rei, or had high-level evasion skills like Vihera, it might be different. But while Byune has improved considerably compared to before, he doesn’t possess that level of skill.

No, if she were fighting an amateur or someone who had just become an adventurer, Byune might be able to pull something like that off.

However, the person fighting Byune right now is an opponent of equal skill—no, slightly superior skill.

Under those circumstances, she couldn’t simply parry attacks so easily, and it was only natural that Byune would be blown away by the difference in weight when they collided head-on.

“Hmph!”

But as a member of the Crimson Wings, Byune trains rigorously every day.

Even as she was knocked back, she twisted her body in midair to regain her balance and landed safely on the floor.

Seeing Byune’s actions, gasps of admiration escaped from those watching nearby.

Of course, there were quite a few people present who could pull off a stunt like the one Byune just did.

But that is something they can only do right now.

If asked whether they could have pulled off such a feat when they were Byune’s age, very few would be able to nod in affirmation.

It was precisely because the onlookers understood this that they voiced their admiration for Byune’s exceptional ability.

“Hmm!”

“Come on!”

As Byune landed on the floor and let out a short shout, the man with the spear shouted back as if in response.

And so their battle resumed, but overall, it was a fact that the man was stronger than Byune.

Byune tried desperately to close the distance, but the man’s spearwork was too skillful to allow it.

Ultimately, the man spotted a gap in Byune’s movements, and his spear moved swiftly… thrusting the tip into Byune’s face, sealing the outcome of the battle.

...Of course, if this were a real battle, Byune would have used his long spear, or his weapon—the White Cloud, crafted from Silver Lion material—and the outcome likely wouldn’t have been the same.

That said, if Byune were to use her weapon in its true form, it would naturally follow that the man would also use his true weapon, so it was entirely unclear how things would have turned out in that scenario.

“Guh…”

While Vihera and Byune were engaged in repeated mock battles, Rei was also fighting one after another in mock battles.

After Edward, the first man he fought, lost, others lined up one after another to spar with him, and currently, the eighth man was kneeling on the floor after taking a blow from Rei.

“Hmm, I think you’ve got some potential. The moment you took my attack, you relaxed your strength and tried to parry that strike. However, your transition into that movement was slow. That’s why you couldn’t parry most of the force, and this is the result. If you can make that reflexive—rather than a conscious decision—you’ll be able to take it to the next level.”

Of course, to be able to parry attacks reflexively, you need to keep getting hit over and over again until you can perform that reflexive parry.

This really comes down to talent and instinct, so there’s a huge individual variation in how long it takes to master it completely.

For example, in Vihera’s case, he’s able to perform such movements almost entirely on instinct, with hardly any training.

However, if asked whether the man standing before him possesses as much talent as Vihera, Rei wouldn’t be able to nod in agreement so readily.

That said, everyone in this training ground possesses a certain level of talent.

…To be precise, it’s probably more accurate to say that applicants flooded in because of today’s mock battle with Rei, and Edvard selected the talented ones from among them.

It’s not as if Edward actually owns this training ground, but everyone accepts him as the one who keeps the users in line.

In fact, if everyone who wanted to had been allowed to participate in this mock battle, there would undoubtedly have been a huge crowd… and there’s no way everyone could have fought.

After all, there were even some who had never used this training ground before but wanted to participate just because they heard they could spar with Rei.

Rei is quite feared in Gilm, but at the same time, he isn’t seen as a man who resorts to violence without cause.

And since there’s no doubt Rei is actually strong, it was only natural that so many people would want to participate once they heard there was an opportunity to spar with him.

“Next up… Hmm, bare-handed? That’s unusual.”

The woman who stood before Rei next was wearing gauntlets that covered her arms up to her elbows.

She glanced at Vihera, who was energetically enjoying her mock battle, but naturally, the woman currently standing before Rei was not wearing light clothing like Vihera.

“Let’s go!”

With a short shout, the woman lunged forward.

The advantage of unarmed combat over opponents wielding weapons is, after all, that one’s own body becomes the weapon.

Unlike weapons such as longswords or spears, it is easier to intuitively judge the distance.

……Another characteristic is that they’re lighter than weapons, but the gauntlets the woman is wearing are quite sturdy, and they’re certainly heavier than a dagger, at the very least.

Given that, I didn’t think she could launch an attack with the same speed as a dagger.

And sure enough, while the fist she threw at Rei had speed, the thrust of a dagger… compared to the thrusts Byune unleashes, it fell somewhat short.

Rei dodged that strike and thrust the spear’s tip into her leg.

That said, it wasn’t so much a sweeping kick as it was positioning the spear’s tip where the woman would step, so it would be more accurate to say he hooked her leg with the spear.

“Yaaah!”

Since Rei had dodged the attack and passed by her side, the woman, who had frantically tried to turn around, fell to the floor the next moment and let out a scream.

From the woman’s perspective, she likely had absolutely no idea what had just happened to her.

And even as she screamed, when she managed to raise her upper body from the ground, what she saw before her was the tip of Rei’s spear.

Clearly, she was in a corner.

“I get that you want to prioritize hitting me, but when your opponent is wielding a long weapon like a spear, you’d better keep an eye on that too.”

“……Yes.”

The woman replied with a look of deep regret.

From her perspective, it was only natural—she’d charged in without actually engaging in any real sparring with Rei, only to have her foot caught by the spear and end up falling.

Perhaps feeling sorry for her, Rei spoke up as if to comfort her.

“I only have one more person left on my list, so once I’m done with them, I’ll fight you again.”

“R-Really!?”

“Yeah. So, think about your strategy so you can put up a better fight in the next sparring session.”

“U… okay.”

Although only the woman was granted the right to spar with Rei again, those who had sparred with him before her were the ones who felt resentful upon seeing this.

It was only natural. They had only been able to spar with Rei once, yet only the girl had been granted a second chance.

Even Edvard, who was leading the group, was casting envious glances at the girl.

“Ah, what can you do? This girl lost without really doing much of anything. The rest of you, at least, managed to fight me, didn’t you? So, don’t you think that’s enough?”

Even so, a few of them still shot Rei looks that were both dissatisfied… and yet full of expectation.

She hesitated for a moment, wondering what to do...

“Fine, all of you come at me together. But since it’s going to be a free-for-all, be prepared to get a few scrapes!”

Vihera’s voice echoed through the training ground.

When Rei turned her gaze toward the source of the voice, she saw a mock battle taking place—a many-against-one fight.

Yes, it was a battle pitting Vihera alone against ten adventurers.

Under normal circumstances, Vihera should have been overwhelmed by the sheer numbers… but Vihera’s individual strength easily surpassed that of the others.

As a result, while it was barely a fight, the ten were still being overwhelmingly pushed back.

The fact that it was even a contest at all was likely because Vihera was holding back to some extent.

Watching the battle, Rei spoke up.

“Right. Once the mock battle with the last person is over, if you still want to spar with me, shall we all fight you at the same time, just like that? Is that okay?”

“Please do.”

The first to speak up was Edvard.

The others nodded in agreement, as if drawn in by Edvard’s words.

Rei decided that since it would be over in one go rather than repeating one-on-one matches over and over, that would be better, so he accepted.

“Alright. Then, this will be the final one-on-one.”

At those words, the last man—wielding a weapon that was a cross between a dagger and a longsword… something one might call a “mid-sword”—stepped before Rei.

“Let’s go!”

With those words, perhaps aiming to strike first, he closed the distance to Rei in one swift move.

He intended to enter the range of Rei’s spear and settle the match within the range of his own weapon.

Naturally, Rei immediately understood his opponent’s intentions and swiftly thrust the spear—specifically the stone tip—sharply at him repeatedly.

No matter how hard he tried to get inside the spear’s range, he couldn’t find an opening amidst the relentless barrage of thrusts… and so, the mock battle ended with the man being struck repeatedly in the body by the spear’s pommel.

Glancing at the man, Rei turned his gaze back to Edvard and the others.

“Well then, as you wished, it’s me alone against all of you. As Vihera said, only those prepared to accept a few injuries should come at me.”

At those words, Edvard and the others—and even the man wielding the middle sword who had just been defeated—stood up.

And so began a one-against-many mock battle.

Naturally, from Rei’s perspective, they weren’t opponents who posed much of a challenge, but fighting against such a large group meant he was sometimes attacked from unexpected angles, making it decent training for him as well.

...That said, there was no way Rei intended to lose, and he emerged victorious even in the one-against-many scenario.

In the end, the mock battle resulted in Rei and Vihera winning every single match, while Byune ended up with four wins and six losses—a result that, in a sense, was exactly as expected.
