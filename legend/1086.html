News of the Twilight Spear spread like wildfire among Gilm's adventurers.

While it was fairly well known that Rei had a hobby of collecting magic items, this was likely the first time he'd so clearly displayed one in public besides the Death Scythe.

Rei did possess offensive magic items like the Thorn Spear and Nebula's Eye, but he had never before used one so ostentatiously in public.

Moreover, the sheer power of the Twilight Spear was now etched into the eyes of the other adventurers.

The result? Well... it was obvious without even thinking about it.

"Please, Rei-san! Sell me that magic spear!"

"No, if you're selling it, then to me! Please, I beg you!"

"You idiot! That magic spear is called the Spear of Twilight! Asking to buy it without even knowing its name? That's a bit cheeky, don't you think!?"

That's how it was. The number of people approaching Rei, begging him to sell them the magic spear, increased dramatically.

Even so, all those asking Rei to sell them the Twilight Spear were not merchants based in Gilm, but only those who had come to procure goods.

The merchants based in Gilm knew Rei was an extremely generous customer, but many also harbored the fear that carelessly upsetting him might lead to their entire shop being destroyed.

After all, there was the precedent of the Azoth Trading Company. ...Well, strictly speaking, the Azoth Trading Company wasn't destroyed, but it's true they suffered significant damage after angering Rei.

Knowing that Bornter, who led Azoth Trading Company at the time, was ultimately captured, most merchants in Gilm view Rei as a valuable customer, but one they absolutely cannot afford to anger.

Moreover, when it comes to magic lances, adventurers desire them far more than merchants... Yet, among the adventurers who had stayed in Gilm for any length of time, none even considered trying to take the Lance of Twilight from Rei.

There were adventurers who had just arrived in Gilm and happened to witness Rei using the Twilight Spear. Some, judging by Rei's appearance, thought he was unworthy of such a magical spear and tried to seize it... but they were stopped by other adventurers who knew better. Seeing him out and about with the griffin, Set, made it impossible for anyone to make a move.

For these reasons, Rei ended up being pursued by merchants who knew his nickname but nothing of his character.

Merchants who did know about Rei's personality, however, understood that forcing their way in like this would only backfire, so they refrained from acting.

As a result, it was the merchants of a lower caliber who swarmed around Rei.

Even so, the Dusk Wheat inn, where Rei stays regularly, charges higher lodging fees than ordinary inns, so the sort of people swarming around Rei now don't come to the inn itself.

...It should be noted that the Dusk Wheat inn is also famous for its cuisine, attracting many diners. The merchants, however, weren't quite that rude.

Then again, it was obvious just by watching him eat that Rei genuinely enjoyed his meals, and no one would dare disturb that.

(Still, having them gather like this isn't exactly pleasant.)

Watching the merchants desperately pleading around him to buy Twilight Spears, Rei wore an unpleasant expression...

"Grrrrrrrrr"

Noticing Rei's reaction, Set growled threateningly and shot the merchants a glare.

'Eek!'

All the merchants swarming around Rei screamed at Set's threatening growl and took several steps back.

Those from Gilm wouldn't fear Set this much, but to the merchants, who didn't know his friendly nature, a griffin was nothing but terror.

...Even so, their eyes blinded by the profit of the Twilight Spear, they gathered around Rei precisely because they had the guts of merchants.

Whether Rei would admit that was another matter.

(But, yeah. Maybe disappearing from Gilm for a while isn't so bad? Though, what about the goblin meat... Nah, I said I'd try aging it, so I guess I won't be needed for a bit.)

With these thoughts, Rei hurried toward the guild, almost as if fleeing from the merchants Set had shooed away.

He figured the guildmaster was the most appropriate person to consult at a time like this.

Incidentally, Vihera , who usually works alongside Rei, was absent because she was accompanying Byune back to her hometown in the Labyrinth City of Exil as her guardian.

Originally, they were supposed to return once during spring, but the World Tree incident had delayed things until now.

Admittedly, there was also the fact that Vihera couldn't accept leaving only Marina and Rei behind in Gilm...

Even so, sending Byune back was likely because they couldn't break a promise made before coming to Gilm.

As a result of what could be called a battle of wills between Vihera and Marina, Rei now finds himself in this situation.

"Ah, Rei! Long time no see!"

As usual, after parting with Set at the entrance, the first to call out to Rei as he entered the guild was, unsurprisingly, the guild receptionist, Kenny , who made no secret of her fondness for him.

Perhaps feeling a sense of urgency after seeing him act alongside Marina regarding the World Tree, her attempts to appeal to Rei were even stronger than before.

Still wiggling her ears with delight at Rei's arrival, she waved at him.

"It hasn't been that long, though. I was here just two days ago."

"Oh, Rei-kun. I feel disappointed if I don't see you even for a day... Ow!"

Kenny, who had been calling out to Rei, screamed as Lenora beside her struck her with a paper-ball weapon.

He didn't seem to feel as much pain as his words suggested. Kenny shot a displeased glance at Lenora beside him and opened his mouth.

"Hey, Lenora. What the hell was that for?"

"That's what I should be asking you. It's fine to talk to Rei-san, but you should at least finish your work first."

What came out of Lenora's mouth was undeniably the truth.

Kenny tried to retort, but when the guild official, their superior, cleared his throat, they immediately returned to work.

It was daytime now, not the guild's busiest hours like early morning or evening. The main tasks were creating and organizing documents.

Kenny hurriedly finished up such tasks.

His speed, unimaginable given his appearance and casual manner, was proof of Kenny's competence. ...Though a hint of ulterior motive was visible too – finishing quickly to make time to talk with Rei.

But despite Kenny's efforts, Rei approaches Lenora, who has finished her work and is taking a break.

"Could you put me through to Marina?"

"The Guildmaster?"

Hearing Rei and Lenora's exchange, Kenny abruptly stopped his swift work.

He knew from their recent interactions that the bond between Rei and the Guildmaster... Marina, had grown deeper.

Honestly, he envied it, but part of him also accepted that Marina was simply irresistible. ...The next moment, Kenny snapped himself back to reality, refusing to lose to her as a woman!

He understood his boss was glancing his way several times to check if he was working, so he pushed himself harder than ever to finish his tasks. He knew he couldn't talk to Rei until he was done.

Beside Kenny, Rei and Lenora exchanged words.

(I'm jealous.)

To chat with Rei, smiling, to dispel her envy of Lenora, Kenny worked relentlessly to finish her tasks.

Even to the man who had been Kenny's boss, the speed was clearly top gear. He sighed, wondering why Kenny couldn't work at this pace normally.

Even as this exchange happened, Rei and Lenora's conversation continued... until Rei stepped inside the counter and headed for the stairs at the back... the stairs leading to the Guildmaster's office.

By the time Kenny desperately finished a stretch of work, Rei was already up the stairs on the second floor... leaving him overcome with sorrow.

"Marina, are you there? I need to talk to you about something."

Just as Kenny finished his work and was overcome with grief, Rei was knocking on the door of the Guildmaster's office.

"Rei? Come in, please."

Rei opened the door at the voice from inside, and what met his eyes was a mountain of documents piled high on the desk.

"...Hasn't this pile grown since I was last here?"

Rei murmured at the sight before him.

Since returning to Gilm after resolving the World Tree incident, Rei had visited this office several times.

But every time he came here, the pile of papers on the desk seemed to have grown taller.

It wasn't that Rei was seeing things.

The piles of paperwork were, in fact, multiplying.

If this were a village in the countryside... or even just a town of some size, the paperwork wouldn't have piled up this high.

But this was Gilm... the only town in the remote frontier of the Mireana Kingdom.

Its size was expanding year by year, and it was said that at this rate, it might become a city within just a few more years.

And naturally, because of its frontier location, many of Gilm's residents are adventurers. Consequently, the guildmaster handles a lot of paperwork.

"Well, yes. It has increased a little, I suppose."

Facing the mountain of paperwork, Marina smiled as she said this.

She showed no sign of being overwhelmed.

In fact, even while talking with Rei, she picked up papers, glanced at them to quickly confirm their contents, and processed each one.

(Speed reading, huh? ...Pretty handy.)

The pile of documents on her desk diminished by the second.

"So, what brings you here? I'm glad you came to see me, though."

She must have finished organizing the papers for now. She lifted her face, turned her gaze toward Rei, and smiled radiantly.

"Well, I did come to see you, that's for sure."

"Heh heh, well, you are here, so that's obvious. But that's not what I meant."

Marina glanced sideways. Sitting down, Rei couldn't fully see what she was wearing, but he knew she was in one of her usual party dresses with a generously low neckline.

The white dress complemented Marina's tanned skin beautifully. If she wore such a dress to a party, she'd undoubtedly be asked to dance by many and receive countless advances.

Rei tilted his head at Marina's words, then stated the reason he'd come here.

"Actually, things have been getting a bit noisy around me lately. I came to consult you about whether there's any way to deal with it."

"Ah, I've heard about it. ...Seems you've acquired quite a powerful magic item."

"The branches and sap of the World Tree must have been the key. Well, that part itself was fine, but..."

"Can I take a look?"

Marina's expression, though her tone was smooth and alluring, held a serious gleam in her eyes.

It wasn't exactly an order, but still, if she wanted to see it, Rei would show her. Faced with Marina's gaze, carrying that kind of attitude, Rei couldn't refuse.

Marina wouldn't think of stealing the Twilight Spear, and showing it was fine. With that trust, he drew the spear from the Misty Ring.

What emerged was a crimson spear.

Its shape was extremely simple, even simpler than the thorn spear Marina had given Rei as payment for a previous request.

Yet, strangely enough, Rei couldn't take his eyes off the Twilight Spear. Perhaps it was because his magic power had become ingrained in it.

Marina, in particular, had once surrendered her entire being—both inside and out—to Rei's magic during the World Tree incident, so her affinity with the Twilight Spear was likely even stronger.

Whereas the Thorn Spear was a solid deep green, the Twilight Spear could be called the Crimson Spear, echoing Rei's own epithet.

However, perhaps thanks to the World Tree's materials, several green patterns exist, twining around the spear like vines, serving as an accent.

"Wow, this is incredible. Just looking at it, I can tell what a powerful magic item this is. It's so captivating..."

"Well, thanks to that, merchants keep pestering me to sell it."

"Oh? I heard some of the merchants hitting on you are women? If you think about it that way, maybe it's not just a nuisance?"

"...I'm not interested."

It was true that the female merchant had tried to use her charms, but with rare beauties like Marina, Vihera, and Elena right in front of him, Rei's deep connections to them meant the merchant's advances simply didn't work.

Seeing Rei like this, Marina flashed a satisfied smile and opened her mouth as if about to reveal a special prank.

"Hey, Rei. How about we pop over to that desert town in the neighboring country... Gosh?"
