"Jada?"

Rei's voice echoed through the room.

The being they had always called Ambris actually had a different name.

Hearing Grim say that from the other side of the Paired Orb surprised not only Rei, but Elena and Marina as well.

But among them, Marina immediately accepted Grimm's words and spoke up.

"That's right. The name 'Ambris' was given by the people who encountered her three hundred years ago. If that's the case, it wouldn't be strange if Jada... no, Jarda had appeared before that and been given that name. It's just strange that the name hasn't been passed down."

"My child, there's no need to forcefully use the name Jada that I employ. After all, this name too was merely a provisional designation given by those who encountered it, just like you all. If the name hasn't survived to the present day, then Ambris is perfectly acceptable."

Marina was startled when Grim suddenly responded to her mutterings.

She had seen him conversing amicably with Rei, but she hadn't expected him to be so friendly toward her as well...

"Um, is that okay?"

Marina asked Grim, who had lived far longer than she had.

Seeming to like Marina's attitude, Grimm let out another laugh that echoed around them.

"Ho ho ho. Not at all. You're quite important to Rei, aren't you? So, what about that Jarda... no, what about Ambris?"

"Look at this."

Rei moved the Paired Orbs, revealing Vihera, who lay unconscious on the bed, appearing to be asleep.

"Hmm?"

Whether it was due to years of experience or not, Grim seemed to grasp her condition just by looking at Vihera on the bed through the Paired Orbs.

He murmured, looking slightly intrigued.

"I cornered Ambris pretty badly, trying to finish him off... but then, Ambris suddenly teleported. He appeared beside Vihera... this woman sleeping in this bed, and then sank into her body like he does when evolving humanoid monsters."

"Hmm, so the Ambris Rei fought possessed the ability to evolve humanoid monsters?"

At Grim's muttered words, Marina suddenly opened her mouth as if she'd just realized something.

"Come to think of it, Lord Grimm, you mentioned earlier that it could evolve specific monsters... So, when it was called Jarda, it wasn't evolving humanoid monsters?"

Though Marina treated Daskar like a child, she clearly couldn't adopt such an attitude toward Grimm.

She asked in a polite tone.

Seemingly unconcerned by Marina's attitude, Grimm nodded and spoke.

"Hmm. To my knowledge, there are Ambris who only evolve beast-type monsters, Ambris who only evolve bird-type monsters, and Ambris who only evolve insect-type monsters... I've heard of such things."

"You know that much about Ambris?"

The Ambris that appeared in this incident was the first discovered in three hundred years.

Hearing that he knew of so many different types of Ambris was enough to cause confusion.

"Indeed. However, I haven't personally seen and confirmed all of them. Some are stories I've heard secondhand, so it might be dangerous to believe everything outright."

"...I see."

For Marina, the fact that he knew stories about multiple Ambris, even if secondhand, was nothing short of astonishing.

Still, being able to hear this information about Ambris was more than she could have hoped for.

(Though, if I spread the information I got here, people would ask how I know it... That part might be a bit tricky.)

Carelessly leaking information heard from Grimm could easily get her branded a criminal.

Communing with monsters wasn't that unusual in itself.

After all, tamers communicate with monsters to harness their power.

But... if that connection was with a Lich Lord... specifically the remnant of a necromancer from the era when Zepail, known as the Demon Lord, was alive... that was a different story entirely.

No matter how you looked at it, the reputation was far too damaging.

And if anyone harboring ill will toward Marina learned of it, they'd gleefully spread the scandal far and wide.

Marina had earned her share of grudges—albeit misplaced ones—during her days as an adventurer, and that remained true even now as Guildmaster.

But far more numerous than those were the nobles and heads of major merchant guilds captivated by Marina's beauty, scheming to make her their own.

For such individuals, possessing wealth and power, it was only natural that seeing Marina's beauty would make them want to possess her, even knowing it could spell their own ruin if they played their cards wrong.

Naturally, Marina had no intention of letting such men have their way with her.

That was precisely why information about Ambris couldn't be made public so easily.

There was also the matter of needing some degree of verification.

It wasn't that she thought Grimm was lying; it was simply that monsters' behavior could change depending on the time and place.

In fact, the Ambris Marina knew possessed the ability to evolve humanoid monsters, starting with goblins, including those from three hundred years ago. But according to Grimm's explanation, there were also Ambris with the ability to evolve other things.

"Setting aside individual differences among Ambris..."

Elena interjected between Marina and Grimm's words.

"So, Lord Grimm. As Rei said, the Ambris fused with Vihera's body... I'm not sure if that's the right term, but anyway, that's the situation we're in now. Right?"

Rei and Marina, met with Elena's questioning gaze, nodded immediately.

"Yes. We had both a healer and a doctor examine her, but despite showing no physical abnormalities whatsoever, she shows absolutely no sign of waking. It's been nearly two months since she lost consciousness."

"Hmm... As for possible explanations, did you say Ambris and Vihera? He must be trying to take over that woman's body. The reason she still hasn't woken up is likely because she's fighting Ambris."

Hearing Grim's words, everyone nodded in agreement.

This prediction had also been pointed out by doctors and healers.

"So, the next time she wakes up, we won't know if it's Vihera... or Ambris?"

"That's right."

"Even so... even so, isn't there some way we can help Vihera?"

The thought of just passively watching Vihera and Ambris fight was unbearable.

Hearing Rei's words, Elena and Marina both nodded in agreement.

Seeing Rei and the others like this, Grimm let out a slightly softer laugh... and then spoke.

"What you could do, if anything, would be to enter that woman's... Vihera, was it? Enter her mental world and try to help her... but honestly, it's going to be pretty tough, you know?"

"Difficult?"

"Aye. Entering another's mind naturally places a considerable burden. Not only on the one being entered, but also on the one doing the entering."

Grim's words carried a hint of concern for Rei.

To Grimm, Rei was precious in many ways.

Not only does he interact with him casually, but he also inherits the demon beast magic created by the Zepairu clan.

And this time, he'd also learned that the person by Rei's side wouldn't panic or make a fuss upon seeing him.

That's precisely why he didn't really recommend trying to enter someone else's mental world.

Of course, since it was Grimm who voiced that concern, it couldn't be undone now.

(Hmm?)

Is there no alternative plan?

Grimm's gaze turned to Marina as he pondered this.

More precisely, her ears, longer than a human's.

Those ears and her brown skin were proof that Marina was a dark elf.

And speaking of dark elves... As Grimm pondered this, a story he'd heard before suddenly came to mind.

"You're a dark elf, aren't you?"

"Huh? Oh, yes. That's right."

"Then your homeland has the World Tree, doesn't it?"

Why had the words "World Tree" come out of Grimm's mouth?

Though he felt a twinge of doubt about that, he couldn't deny it here.

Perhaps... that thought had crossed Marina's mind too.

Watching Marina's reaction, Grimm spoke again.

"Using the power of the World Tree can increase one's mental fortitude... meaning it could become a great strength for the woman who is currently unconscious to confront Ambris."

"...Does the World Tree possess such power?"

Though Marina came from a bloodline that could be called priestesses of the World Tree, she had never heard such a thing.

Coming from Grimm's mouth, it couldn't be a complete lie... yet, even thinking that, she still couldn't fully believe it.

Grim must have noticed Marina's hesitation. He nodded once before speaking.

"It's no wonder you find it hard to believe. Still, isn't it worth trying? Personally, I'd rather not send Rei to some place like the spiritual world. Given that, I see no point in lying to you now."

Watching Rei's reflection in the Paired Orb, Marina didn't feel like Grimm's words were a lie.

The same was true for Elena, who could only stare silently at Grimm's face.

A silence fell between Grimm and Marina.

It was broken by Rei's single sentence.

"The World Tree in Marina's homeland had a bit of a problem just a few months ago. Considering that, I don't want to push her too hard."

"But as far as I know, that's the quickest way to bring that woman's consciousness back, isn't it?"

"...Even so..."

Rei turned his gaze back to Marina beside him.

Marina frowned, her beautifully shaped brows knitted in worry.

Marina was also a Dark Elf, born of a bloodline connected to the World Tree.

Given that, even now, away from the settlement, she still held a strong connection to the World Tree.

The World Tree had undoubtedly been weakened by the turmoil that had occurred just this spring.

Marina honestly couldn't decide whether it was acceptable to borrow its power...

"Lord Grimm, is there no other way?"

Marina desperately wished there was another way.

Marina wished for it, but Grim, reflected in the Paired Orb, shook his skeletal head.

'If you ask if I know of anything else... I suppose I could say yes. But could you obtain a dragon's heart, a phoenix's tear, or the healing tail of a nine-tailed fox?'

"That..."

What Grimm had just mentioned were all incredibly rare items... the sort that appeared in legends and heroic tales.

They were not things one could simply go out and find.

What's more, every monster Grimm had just cited as examples were S-rank monsters.

They weren't the kind of foes you could defeat easily just by deciding to take them on.

"A dragon?"

Elena murmured, her expression tinged with slight unease.

She was likely worried about her familiar, Yellow, being a black dragon's child.

Noticing Elena's reaction, Grimm's gaze shifted to her from across the Paired Orb.

'Do you know something about dragons?'

"The familiar I created using dragon language magic is the child of a black dragon."

"Hmm, unfortunate. What we need is the heart of an Ancient Dragon... well, not quite that ancient, but a dragon that has lived a comparable span of years. A newly created familiar won't be of use this time."

At Grimm's words, Elena exhaled in relief.

Even though it was just a familiar... or perhaps precisely because of that, Yellow had become important to Elena.

(Besides, even if Vihera regained consciousness with Yellow's heart, he definitely wouldn't be happy about it.)

In fact, Rei could easily imagine Vhela flying into a rage.

"Isn't there anything else? Another monster's heart, or a magic stone..."

Rei shuddered at the thought of a furious Vihera and quickly dismissed the idea, turning to ask Grimm instead.

'Hmm, that's true. While not as dramatic as what I mentioned earlier, if it's the heart of an S-rank monster, I could manage it with a little help...'

Hearing this, everyone thought, Then why didn't Grim just do it from the start? But even as they spoke, Grim remained a monster.

Even if he was once human, he was now undead. There was no way to forcefully change Grim's thoughts.

In any case, when he heard "Rank S monster," the name that flashed through Rei's mind was Set—a rare species of Rank A monster considered equivalent to Rank S.

But sacrificing Set was out of the question.

(Anything else... anything else... Huh? Rank S? Wait a minute, could it be...?)

Grim, Elena—his gaze swept between them in turn before Rei spoke.

"The Silver Lion."
