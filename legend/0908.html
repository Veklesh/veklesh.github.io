The voice drifting over from the direction of Dusk Wheat inn sounded familiar to Rei, who left the snowman behind and headed toward it.

Set followed behind Rei as if it were only natural.

Aware of this, Rei continued on without seeming particularly bothered... until a carriage stopped in front of the Dusk Wheat inn came into view.

It was clearly no ordinary carriage, despite being one.

The carriage body was intricately carved with elaborate decorations, and the carriage parts themselves were crafted from carefully selected materials.

Rei couldn't identify the exact materials, but even at a glance, he understood it was no ordinary carriage.

(It resembles the magic item carriage Elena rides in. And the horses too...)

Rei shifted his gaze to the horses pulling the carriage.

They were undoubtedly horses, but not ordinary ones. They were clearly horse-shaped monsters.

And a fairly high-ranking one at that.

In fact, even Set beside Rei seemed wary but showed no outward sign of fear.

Several servants were gathered near the carriage, which seemed like undeniable proof that the voice Rei had heard earlier belonged to the person he had expected. And then...

"Hm, you were here? I thought you'd be inside the inn."

Emerging from the Dusk Wheat inn as he spoke was, indeed, the person Rei had anticipated.

Despite being a girl not yet ten years old, her eyes held a distinct spark of intelligence.

Barserlo, the heir to the Baron Sisne family, was also wise beyond his years, but the girl before them, despite being younger than Barserlo, possessed an even more evident high level of intelligence.

Her green hair flowing freely, the girl spotted Rei and Set and broke into a delighted smile.

"Hmm. I hear you two have been quite active. The royalist faction is rather lively these days... But it's good to see you're still full of energy."

"Maruka...?"

When the familiar name was spoken, Maruka nodded back with satisfaction.

"Indeed. It seems you haven't forgotten me, and that is most gratifying!"

This girl, whose speech belied her appearance, was Maruka Quent.

She is the only daughter of Duke Quent, a prominent figure within the royalist faction, and the next Duke Quent.

A precocious genius rumored to be the future pillar of magic for the Mireana Kingdom, she wields mastery over the three elements: wind, earth, and light.

She had visited Gilm before and had formed a friendship with Rei during that visit.

"Long time no see, Rei."

Emerging from behind Maruka was a man wearing a smile.

Even to Rei's eyes, he was someone whose strength was evident at a glance.

He was Koan, a former Rank A adventurer and magic swordsman.

He gave off an approachable vibe, but as Maruka's bodyguard, his actual skill was exceptionally high.

While his former rank as an A-rank adventurer contributed to this, he was utterly devoted to Maruka—a girl barely half his age... no, not even a third. He would face a dragon itself to protect her.

...Though, to be fair, Maruka herself possessed exceptionally high magical ability as a mage, so she didn't really need a bodyguard that often.

"Even Koan... What on earth is he doing here... Wait, that sounds a bit too contrived."

The only reason that came to Rei's mind for why the heir to Duke Quent, a key figure in the royalist faction, would come all the way to Gilm in the snow, was the duel with Zeros... more accurately, the duel between the Count of Eriel and the Baron of Sisne that had taken place a few weeks prior.

Although it was winter, Ranga had already set out for the Count of Eriell's domain, accompanied by knights and soldiers serving as his escort, to collect half of the property Keep had wagered on that duel.

Why Ranga, who was also the captain of the guard? Rei wondered, but when told Ranga was one of Daskar's confidants, he had no choice but to accept it.

"Hmm. Indeed. My visit is for the reason you suspect. ...Now then, I wish to discuss that matter. Would you mind?"

The innocent smile that had graced her face when reuniting with Rei and Set had vanished. What now appeared on Maruka's face was an expression befitting the next duke.

Rei nodded in response to that expression.

"Where should we talk? You probably don't want others overhearing. How about my room? ...It's not that big, though."

"Rei's room, huh. Hmm, any worries about eavesdroppers?"

"At this inn, you can generally assume that won't happen. And if someone is eavesdropping, it wouldn't be hard to detect. Besides, Coan will be present too, right?"

"Very well. In that case, it's settled. ...Have the carriage moved to a spot out of the way. I have matters to discuss with Rei for a while. Everyone except Coan should wait inside the inn. I hear the food in this inn's dining hall is delicious. You've all been exhausted since your forced march through winter to reach Gilm, then went straight to the lord's manor before coming here. Rest well."

At Maruka's words, the butler and attendants near the carriage bowed deeply.

Their eyes held both gratitude and concern, and they sent Koan a look filled with trust.

Even Rei understood what this meant.

It meant, "If danger threatens Maruka, we entrust her to you."

"So then, landlady. Please let my people rest in the dining hall for a while. This is to apologize for the disturbance and to cover the cost of their food and drink."

Maruka handed five gold coins from the cloth bag he'd received from Koan to Lana, who, despite the arrival of the duke's daughter, showed no surprise on her face, though her inner thoughts were unknown.

Five gold coins was completely unexpected, even for Lana. She opened her mouth to Maruka, flustered.

"Miss, this is too much."

But Maruka smiled at her and shook her head.

"This is compensation for the trouble. We caused you inconvenience by coming this time. Besides, as members of the Quent Duke's household, this much is only natural. Please accept it as saving my face."

Lana hesitated for a moment at Maruka's words, but she likely realized that refusing such an offer from a noble would create awkwardness.

She bowed deeply to Maruka.

"Understood. Then I shall have a meal prepared that will satisfy you all."

"Mm, thank you. ...Now, Rei. Please show them the way."

Prompted by Maruka, Rei patted Set's head, urging him back to the stables, and entered the inn.

Seeing Set return to the stables without being ordered, the men accompanying Maruka wore expressions of surprise.

(So, why the smug look on your face?)

Rei silently rolled her eyes at Mireine, who stood with her chest puffed out, her attitude practically shouting, "Isn't little Set amazing?"

The reason she didn't seem particularly surprised by the arrival of the duke's daughter wasn't because Mireine herself was accustomed to nobility... but rather because her attention was still focused on Set.

Glancing sideways as Lana approached Mireine, Rei guided Maruka and Koan to his own room.

"Well, well, so this is Rei's room. ...Somehow, it seems rather sparse and lonely, doesn't it?"

That was the first thing Maruka said after entering Rei's room and looking around.

Koan nodded in agreement.

"Well, I keep basically all my necessary belongings in the item box. It's more convenient that way, so I can move quickly if needed. It's just a room like this, but please sit down for now. It might be a bit rough for a duchess's daughter."

Rei gestured toward the chair beside the desk.

Truthfully, those words were clearly rude. If spoken to a noble with only pride, they would likely have flushed crimson and shown anger at the insult.

But Maruka showed no sign of being offended by Rei's words... no, quite the opposite. She sat down in the chair with an amused smile.

Behind Maruka, seated in the chair, stood Koan as her guard.

"For now, at least drink this."

What Rei offered was fruit water in a cup.

It was something purchased in summer and stored in the Misty Ring, something that normally couldn't be drunk at this time of year.

"Well now. This... Hmm. Item boxes are quite convenient, aren't they?"

Maruka smiled happily and brought the cup to his lips.

Normally, given Maruka's position, a taster would be required, but he showed no sign of concern.

Part of it was trust in Rei, but more significantly, it was because he possessed a magic item capable of detecting poison.

"Hmm, hmm, hmm. Mm, cold and sweet. It's a bit problematic to drink this at this time of year, but since it's warm inside this inn, it won't be an issue."

Savoring the sweet and sour taste, Maruka downed the entire contents of the cup in one gulp.

Exhaling deeply, her expression shifted from that of a young girl to that of a duke's daughter.

Koan faded into the background, standing behind her so as not to disturb Maruka.

"Now then, you understand why I've come to the frontier town of Gilm at this time of year, don't you?"

"Ah. ...It's about the duel, right?"

Maruka nodded in agreement at Rei's straightforward answer.

"I have come here as your father's representative. ...More precisely, as the representative of Duke Quent, a leading figure of the royalist faction."

She paused there, then turned her gaze toward Rei... but seeing no particular change in his expression, she let out a small sigh.

"You should be a bit more surprised that I've come as the Duke's representative, shouldn't you?"

"It's not like it's bad news, right? Then there's no need to be surprised."

"Oh? Why do you think that? The man you fought in the duel is the next head of the Eriell family. Frankly, I didn't care for him much, but he remains a fellow supporter of the King's faction. Such a man is about to lose half his family's fortune! If I came here under those circumstances, wouldn't you normally think it meant something unfavorable for you?"

But to Maruka's words, Rei shrugged slightly and brought the fruit juice to his mouth.

"Believe it or not, Lord Daskar holds me in considerable regard. If it were something truly fatal for me, I'd have been warned beforehand. Besides..."

Rei paused, continuing the thought silently in his mind.

(Lord Daskar saw firsthand just how strong I am during last spring's war. If something were to happen that reduced Gilm to ashes, he'd at least tell me to leave Gilm beforehand.)

Daskar had witnessed the vanguard of the Bestir Empire's army collapse completely last spring during the war, destroyed by a massive fiery tornado—a fire whirlwind. Rei couldn't imagine Daskar deliberately letting Rei run rampant within the Gilm.

"Huh? What's wrong with that?"

"Nothing. Anyway, all I did was act as a proxy in a duel. Considering Lord Daskar's character, I can't imagine him making me do anything to my disadvantage."

"Hmm? But I've said this before—my father is a duke. I mean, no offense, but he has the power and military might to deal with one or two border lords however he pleases. Couldn't you see him fearing that...?"

"...Don't test me. Believe it or not, I think I know Daskar reasonably well. Well, maybe it's just the surface, but even so, I can't imagine Daskar doing something like selling me out. Though..."

With a spiteful smile playing on his lips, Rei continued.

"Unless the so-called Eliel family is worth helping even if it means turning the neutral faction against us, that is."

Underlying those words was the implication: Does a man like Keep really hold that much value to the next head of the house?

If Maruka had claimed he actually was valuable, Rei would have been helpless.

Fortunately, however, Maruka smiled and nodded contentedly.

"Hmm, indeed. The Counts of Eriell certainly held considerable influence within the royalist faction, but it was only considerable. If one sought a replacement, there are plenty. ...However, it is also true that the royalist nobles suffered the utmost humiliation."

Sighing sadly, Maruka looked at the glass in his hand, then at Rei, then back at the glass.

Seeing this, Rei understood what Maruka was requesting. He took another fruit water from the Misty Ring and handed it to Maruka.

Maruka happily took a sip from the cup and continued speaking.

"However, the fault in this matter clearly lies with Lord Keep. Punishing you now would make the royalists look like fools. But... among the royalists, there are many fools who don't understand even that basic logic... no, who deliberately refuse to understand, yet shout the loudest. Therefore..."

Maruka, wearing a smile that suggested scheming, murmured the decisive words with that same expression.

"Rei, how about you try being an instructor for a while?"

"...Huh?"

Unable to grasp the meaning of Maruka's words, all Rei could manage was a dumbfounded gasp.
