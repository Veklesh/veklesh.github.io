"Huh? Rei?"

Rei, who had been sipping tea at Marina’s house while watching Set and Byune play in the garden, snapped back to reality at the sound of that voice.

Of course, Rei knew exactly whose voice it was.

The fact that the owner of that voice was there meant, naturally, that there was another person present as well.

“Rei? Marina too… oh, Yellow.”

Yellow, who had been in Elena’s arms, immediately headed toward the window—perhaps because he’d spotted Set outside.

Of course, since the window was closed, Ara, who had hurried over in a fluster, had to open it.

“I was bored staying at the inn anyway. Besides, I wanted to find out more about the kobold situation and other things, so I came here.”

“Hmm, I see. I’ve heard about the kobold situation from Lord Daskar myself. Though, it seems there’s no need for me to lend a hand. He even told me not to get involved.”

Perhaps because she didn’t take kindly to being told not to get involved, Elena muttered, looking slightly displeased.

Of course, Elena fully understood why she’d been told that.

Elena is here in Gilm as a chaperone for the nobles of the Noble Faction.

If someone like her were to be sent out to hunt kobolds, the Noble Faction would be displeased at the idea of using their symbol against such creatures, and rumors would spread that Gilm’s forces alone were insufficient to defeat the kobolds.

That would certainly not be good for Daskar, who leads the Neutral Faction, one of the three major factions.

If they actually became unable to defeat the kobolds and the adventurers of Gilm alone could no longer handle the situation, then they might have to turn to Elena, but for now, the adventurers of Gilm are more than sufficient.

Given that, it was an undeniable fact that there was no need to enlist Elena’s help in this matter.

“I suppose so. Considering Daskar’s position, he has no choice but to say that. …Anyway, welcome back.”

“...I’m back.”

“We’ll be counting on you again.”

Marina welcomed Elena and Ara while showing consideration for Daskar’s position, and the two, though looking a bit embarrassed by the welcome, replied in kind.

For the two of them, Marina’s house was probably a much more comfortable place to stay than any ordinary inn.

After all, thanks to Marina’s spirit magic, the house is always kept spotless, and the environment is warm in winter and cool in summer.

“Well, now that all the party members are gathered together for the first time in a while, shall we make tonight’s dinner a little more special?”

“Well, Ara and I aren’t actually members of the Crimson Wings…”

Elena says this with a slightly—just a tiny bit—disappointed look.

She must be envious of being in a party with Marina and Vihera, who are both her comrades and her romantic rivals.

But, given Elena’s position, she can’t exactly become an adventurer.

"Is that so? ...But we’re always together anyway, so we can just consider us associate members, right?"

Of course, there’s no such thing in the guild’s system.

That is something Marina, the former guild master, knows better than anyone.

Nevertheless, the main reason she said this was because she wanted Elena to feel even a little bit the same way they did.

In fact, when Elena heard the words “associate member,” she broke into a delighted smile.

“Associate member, huh? Heh, I see. …I see.”

The way she murmured thoughtfully, combined with Elena’s natural beauty, was so captivating it took one’s breath away.

Naturally, Rei found himself mesmerized by Elena’s appearance…

“Um, ahem. So, you mentioned we’d have a fancy meal today—what do you think? Should we cook it ourselves? Or go out to eat somewhere?”

“I think we should cook it ourselves. We can’t take Set into a restaurant.”

Marina replied to Vihera, who asked the question after clearing her throat as if to cover something up.

She probably felt that if this group were to have a meal together, Set and Yellow simply had to be there.

Rei sent Marina a grateful look in response to her thoughtfulness.

“In that case, what should we make? Should we bring out the oven?”

Until recently, whenever they ate at Marina’s house, they’d cook using the magic oven that Rei carried.

It hadn’t even been a month yet, but the incident in Anesis must have had that much of an impact.

“Yeah, you’re right. …Since we’re at it, let’s all use that oven to try making various things together. Luckily, we have the ingredients… Will Rei contribute some from her side too?”

“Ingredients? Oh, I’ve got plenty of those. I don’t mind contributing my share. …Come to think of it, we’ve still got some fish we caught when we all went to the beach near the end of this summer. It might be fun to make something with that. Though, the only thing I can cook is grilled fish with salt. Or maybe… soup?”

However, when I say “soup” in this case, I just mean using fish instead of dried meat; Rei wouldn’t be able to go so far as to adjust the soup specifically for the fish.

If you think of it as just throwing fish into the kind of soup you’d make on a camping trip, that would be a huge waste.

If you lived near the sea, cooking fish that way wouldn’t be a problem, but this is Gilm, where there’s no ocean in sight.

Of course, there are rivers, so it’s not as if we can’t eat fresh fish… but still, there are significant differences between river fish and sea fish in terms of taste, texture, size, and other factors.

Also, since processed seafood like salted, smoked, or dried fish does make its way here, it’s not as if we can’t eat any seafood at all.

...Even so, the sea fish that comes into Gilm has been processed in some way, and while I wouldn’t say it’s impossible to get the kind of raw fish Rei keeps in her Misty Ring... it would cost an amount that’s far beyond what an ordinary household could afford for a meal.

That is precisely why the idea of casually adding ocean fish to a simple soup like the one Rei can make would likely surprise, dismay, and perhaps even anger adventurers, cooks… and others who might find out.

That said, everyone here knows that Rei’s Misty Ring contains a large quantity of sea fish, so no one stops him for that reason.

They stop him for a different reason: they’d rather eat a delicious fish dish than the haphazard soup ingredients Rei has thrown together.

“I’ll cook the fish. Rei, you’d rather have a delicious meal, wouldn’t you?”

Rei readily nods in agreement to Marina’s suggestion.

Even though he wouldn’t go so far as to say his own cooking was bad, he understood better than anyone that it certainly wasn’t delicious.

Seeing Rei like that, Elena, Vihera, and Ara—who had been listening to the conversation—all wore happy expressions.

Getting to eat Rei’s home-cooked meals wasn’t a bad deal for the three of them either.

But if they were going to eat it anyway, it was only natural to want it to be delicious.

“Well then, we’ll have to let Byune know about this too. It’s a special celebration, after all. Let’s make the most of it. I’m itching to get cooking myself.”

If you were to ask who was the best cook in this group, everyone would probably answer Marina.

It’s not that the others are completely hopeless in the kitchen—they’re not the kind of terrible cooks who can only produce dishes that could be called hazardous—but there’s no doubt they can only make food that’s just barely edible.

That’s exactly why everyone looks so happy at Marina’s words.

“As for the ingredients, what should I bring? …Should I bring some high-rank monster meat?”

“Hmm, that’s fine, but if you ask me if I can really bring out the full flavor of ingredients like that with my cooking skills, it’s a bit difficult. For now, could you get me meat from a Rank B monster instead of a Rank S or A one?”

Rei nodded at Marina’s words, but normally… that is, anywhere other than Gilm, Rank B monster meat is usually priced out of reach for the average household.

Of course, for those with money to spare, buying Rank B monster meat isn’t all that difficult.

“Got it. In that case, I’ll… Oh, come to think of it, I have some meat that’s already been prepped, so I’ll just roast that in the cauldron.”

Saying that, Rei went out into the garden with Marina.

Immediately, they heard the delighted meows of Set and Yellow, and while Byune wasn’t exactly laughing out loud, there was no doubt she was radiating a happy vibe.

The two of them, along with Rei and Marina who had come out into the garden, and Elena and the others who followed, caught their attention. The dogs looked at them with puzzled expressions, as if to ask, “What’s going on?”

“Growl?”

As if speaking for the two dogs and one person, Set purred, “What’s going on?”

Rei stroked Set as he spoke.

“Today’s the day we’re back in Gilm, right? So, I thought we’d have a party in this garden tonight. …It’s nice and warm out here.”

What Rei murmured was certainly true.

Thanks to Marina’s spirit magic, the temperature inside this garden is maintained at a comfortable level, like spring or autumn.

Moreover, as you can see from Set and Byune running around, the ground isn’t wet and difficult to walk on due to the snowfall.

“Growl!?”

Hearing about the party, Set let out a delighted growl, as if to say, “Really!?”

Byune, who was standing a little ways off, didn’t change her expression, but she gave off an air of delight.

For Byune, who loves to eat, the thought of a party likely means she’ll get to enjoy delicious food.

Since Marina herself said she’d be cooking, that assumption isn’t wrong at all.

“So, we’re going to start getting ready now. That means no running around and playing in the garden for the time being. Got it?”

“Grr!”

Set let out a meow, as if to say, “If I get to eat delicious food, I can put up with that much!”

Yellow lands on Set’s back and meows as if to say he agrees with Set.

“I see. I’m glad you understand. Well then… let’s get right to it. First, let’s get the ingredients out. What should we start with? Meat? Fish? Vegetables?”

“Hmm, that’s true. Since we’re prepping, maybe fish first. …It’s so convenient that with Rei’s Misty Ring, we don’t have to worry about the ingredients spoiling.”

Normally, in winter, there’s basically no need to worry about ingredients spoiling.

Of course, if you left ingredients out in the open for days on end, they would spoil.

But with Rei’s Misty Ring, as long as you store the ingredients after prepping them, they won’t spoil. …That said, since time stands still inside the Misty Ring, it doesn’t allow for things like flavors to meld or eliminating the smell of meat and fish, so it’s not all advantages.

Still, since you can store everything in the Misty Ring after prepping it, making it ready to use whenever you need it, the benefits are significant.

“Alright, fish it is. …What kind of fish should I bring out?”

“Hmm, you know that fish with the sharp, pointed mouth that was a bit on the large side? Could you bring that one out?”

Hearing that, Rei recalled a fish that resembled a marlin.

It wasn’t exactly a marlin, but there was no doubt it looked similar.

“This one, then.”

The fish Rei pulled out was about a meter long.

That said, since its mouth alone was about 30 centimeters long, the actual body was only about 70 centimeters.

When Marina saw the fish, she nodded happily.

“Yes, yes, this fish. I tried a little bit of it when I was out at sea, and I think it would be delicious if you pan-fried it in butter. The flesh was tender, but it had a mild flavor.”

Butter-fried swordfish. Rei nodded in agreement, picturing the dish and thinking it would undoubtedly be delicious.

“If you drizzle it with the juice of a tart fruit before eating, it’d probably taste even better.”

“Huh? Really? If we’re frying it in butter, I think the acidity would just be an unnecessary flavor.”

Vihera countered Rei’s words.

I suppose it’s a matter of personal preference.

After all, when Rei was in Japan, he liked to squeeze lemon juice on his karaage.

(Vihera is probably the type who doesn’t put lemon juice on things like fried chicken or tonkatsu. Come to think of it, there were a lot of storylines in manga where that kind of thing caused a commotion.)

Thinking that, Rei decided that for now, if he were to drizzle juice over the butter-fried chicken, he’d limit it to just the portion he’d taken onto his own plate.
