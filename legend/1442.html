Even in summer, night inevitably brings darkness to the sky.

Of course, being summer, the sky darkens later than in winter, but even so, by around ten o'clock at night, the sun had naturally set completely.

With no clouds, the moonlight streaming down from the large moon brought enough brightness to the ground that even on such nights, one could make out the surroundings to some degree.

That moonlight, along with several magic item lights, illuminated the garden of Marina's house.

But that wasn't the only source of light.

The light leaking from the oven placed in the garden also illuminated the surroundings, albeit to a lesser extent.

Though, to be precise, pizza was baking inside that very oven.

"Rei seems to be getting quite used to it,"

Marina murmured thoughtfully, propping her elbows on the table brought from inside the house.

Her gaze was fixed on Rei, who stood before the oven.

While he hadn't used this oven that many times, perhaps practice made perfect—Rei moved the giant peel with ease, shifting the pizza inside the oven to ensure it cooked just right.

Especially today, having already baked pizzas multiple times, his movements were exceptionally smooth.

"Rei's cooking... Normally, you'd think it's pretty amazing, right?"

Vihera murmurs as she takes a bite of the salad generously topped with cheese.

'Well, of course it is. It's food made by an adventurer with a nickname, right? That's not something you get to eat easily.'

Responding to Vihera's words was Elena, reflected in the crystal... the Paired Orb on the table.

Vihera had suggested contacting Elena too, reasoning that since everyone from Crimson Wings was gathered for the banquet, it would be a shame not to invite her.

Of course, Elena and Rei's party weren't the same group.

Still, the Crimson Wings and Elena were bound by deep ties in many ways. Though not party members, they were recognized as comrades connected by profound bonds.

...Especially for Marina and Vihera, they saw her as a comrade who loved the same man.

For Elena too, interacting with Marina, Vihera, and Byune was a joy because she could be her natural self around them.

For this reason, the Elena reflected in the Paired Orb wore a smile of genuine happiness, making her beauty appear even more captivating to those around her.

Incidentally, while Elena is now reflected in the Paired Orb, until recently it was Yellow, the black dragon's child, who appeared there.

Meanwhile, from Rei and the others' perspective, Set was in front of the Paired Orb, engaging in their usual round-and-round, squeak-and-chirp interactions... Watching the two of them, Rei and the others each felt a warm, fuzzy feeling.

Yellow, even if only through the Paired Orb, must have been happy to see Set again after so long. He was quite excited, and now, perhaps from that exhaustion, he was asleep.

Set was undoubtedly happy to see Yellow too, but perhaps because he was more mature than Yellow, he didn't seem nearly as tired.

This might be influenced by the fact that he was created through demon beast magic.

In any case, Set was currently lying down in the garden, relaxing. He had his eyes closed, being stroked by Byune as she waited for the pizza to be ready.

"Hehe, looks like you're having fun over there. I'm jealous."

Glancing at the garden from the other side of the Paired Orb, she said this, then brought the teacup in her hand to her lips as if to hide her envy.

"Well, yes. It really is enjoyable, isn't it?"

Marina replied with a smile.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be strange for this to be taken as a dig at Elena, but in Marina and Elena's relationship, that simply couldn't be the case.

'I do envy you, but the bond between Rei and me? That's not something something like that can break.'

Even if she couldn't say it with Rei nearby, she could say it like this, among women.

"Oh, really? But Vihera and I are closer to Rei, aren't we? If that's the case, wouldn't affection grow between us?"

"I see. That's enviable."

Elena gave Marina a short reply, but her attitude belied her words, radiating confidence.

Was it because she believed in the connection between herself and Rei, or was there another reason?

Marina and Vihera, who had been listening nearby, exchanged puzzled looks.

But Elena merely smiled at them, showing no sign of responding.

Wondering about this, Vihera tried to say something, but...

"Pizza's ready!"

Rei shouts, pulling out a giant spatula.

The first to react to those words was, naturally, Byune.

Just moments ago, she had been petting Set, but in the next instant, she had already appeared before Rei.

For a moment, Rei's face showed surprise, but having someone look forward to the pizza he made wasn't a bad feeling at all.

...Though, strictly speaking, the dough was already prepared, and all Rei did was bake it.

But with pizza, the baking step naturally holds great significance.

If you bake it too long, it burns; if you don't bake it long enough, it's undercooked.

The pizza Rei baked wasn't quite at the level of a seasoned pro... well, that might be putting it a bit too generously, but it was perfectly cooked enough for regular eating.

Having already eaten several slices of Rei's pizza, Byune must have rushed over to Rei the moment she heard the pizza was done.

She cut portions for herself, Marina, and Vihera, gave the rest to Byune, and placed the other pizza still baking in the oven in front of Set.

"Gururu!"

Set purred happily at the pizza placed before him.

After petting Set, Rei headed toward the table set up in the garden.

(Spirit magic is amazing, huh?)

Observing his surroundings, Rei reflected deeply.

On a summer night, especially in a lit area, it wouldn't be strange for various insects, starting with mosquitoes, to come.

But thanks to Marina's spirit magic, no insects entered this garden.

That was precisely why they could enjoy the garden on a summer night without worrying about insects.

Returning to the table, Rei noticed Marina, Vihera, and Elena across the Paired Orb all staring at him with what seemed like reproachful looks.

"Wh-what's wrong? Look, the pizza's done, at least."

A few seconds of silence followed Rei's words... then Marina finally spoke.

"Well, I suppose we should eat. After all, Rei went to the trouble of baking it."

Marina's words dispelled the uneasy atmosphere that had been hanging around—at least for Rei—and he let out a slight sigh of relief.

"...How is it?"

"Mmm, I think it's delicious! The fact that Rei baked it makes a big difference."

Hearing Vihera's words, Rei looked pleased and reached for the pizza too.

The satisfying crunch, the rich, savory cheese, and the variety of vegetables, ham, and sausage delighted his taste buds.

"Yeah, it's tasty, especially since I made it. Well, maybe it just tastes extra good because I made it myself."

'Next time we meet, I'm having you make that dish for me too... pizza, was it? That dish.'

Watching Rei and the others eat, Elena looked envious from behind her Paired Orb.

"Yeah. Next time we meet, I'll treat you to my home cooking. ...Well, 'home cooking' just means I grilled it, but still."

"You promised. Next time we meet, absolutely."

Rei nodded, looking puzzled at Elena as she insisted on emphasizing it for some reason.

"Got it."

"Hmm, very well then. ...Now, changing the subject..."

Clearing her throat softly, Elena shifted the subject.

"Regarding the matter with Count Lerdact... it seems I caused you trouble. ...My apologies."

As Elena bowed her head, her golden hair swayed, reflecting the light from beyond the Paired Orb.

"That's remarkably fast information. I only returned to Gilm today."

"Hmm. The information came from Lord Daskar. ...I never imagined someone within the Noble Faction would commit such an act."

"Elena, you really shouldn't worry about it. It's only natural that there are all kinds of people within the Noble Faction."

Vihera said this, as if to encourage Elena.

...No, it wasn't really meant as encouragement; he'd probably just voiced his honest thoughts.

Perhaps realizing this, Elena, reflected in the Paired Orb, let a faint smile touch her lips.

'Hearing you say that is a relief. But... still, this incident is ultimately the Noble Faction's failure. Father must have already warned the nobles currently affiliated with the Faction not to make any unnecessary moves against Gilm.'

"Ugh."

It was Marina who muttered that.

As the former guildmaster, Marina naturally understood Kerebel's character.

Precisely because she was Marina, she could fully grasp how Duke Kerebel felt about this incident.

"But, all things considered, this incident should be the end of Gilm's... no, the Marquis of Larks' retaliation, right? So, it probably won't get too harsh, will it? ...Well, Lerdact was tragic in many ways, though."

"...Rei. It's awkward for me to say this, but please don't make it too flashy. This matter has been settled as Count Lerdact's own doing, but if handled poorly, there's a risk assassins could be sent from the Noble Faction."

"Huh? Actually, I'm a bit surprised it was settled as his own fault."

Rei looked taken aback by Elena's words.

He'd been certain the Noble Faction would definitely strike back again.

After all, he had severed both arms of a noble belonging to the Noble Faction at the shoulder.

'It was at the request of Count Lerdact. ...And apparently, the Lerdact estate is also going to review its tax rates and such. Something about deciding to listen to the people's opinions to show magnanimity as nobles.'

"Ah, I see."

With just those words, Rei could roughly guess what had happened.

First, as was only natural, the rebels hadn't gone so far as to kill Lerdact, just as Rei had told them.

And he could also guess that Count Lerdact's words about listening to the people's opinions were undoubtedly forced upon him.

(By 'the people,' he probably meant the rebels. ...And someone will need to succeed Count Lerdact, but I wonder what happened with that?)

From Rei's perspective, Lerdact was in no condition whatsoever to handle the duties of a lord.

Losing both arms might be manageable if he could find magical prosthetic hands... but given that one of the county's vital income sources, the mines, had collapsed, it was hard to imagine Count Lerdact's domain had the resources for that.

Moreover, after Rei stormed into Jawa—effectively the capital of the Lerdact domain—destroyed its barrier, and rampaged through the lord's manor, many soldiers fled from Lerdact's command.

Naturally, Rei couldn't imagine those who had abandoned their employer and master, to whom they had sworn at least nominal loyalty, being rehired if they simply returned.

In reality, Lerdact was in a state where his spirit had been completely broken by Rei, so there was a good chance he could have been hired back immediately upon returning... but the deserters had no way of knowing that. In the end, every single one vanished from Jawa.

They chose not to remain in Jawa because they feared retribution if found by Lerdact, who surely believed they had abandoned him and fled.

"Anyway, there won't be any trouble regarding the matter of the Lerdact County. ...At least not on the surface, though."

Hearing that, Rei felt relieved, but also wondered for a moment about the rebels. He decided it wasn't his concern and pushed the thought out of his mind for now.

Furthermore, though he had never met Kerebel directly, Rei knew he was a prominent figure.

As Elena's father and the one rallying the Noble Faction, it was easy to surmise he wasn't just another common noble, but the real deal.

"I see."

In the end, that was all that came out of Rei's mouth. After that, they changed the subject and the conversation grew lively.
