“...So? So you decided to take on that request after all?”

Although autumn was already drawing to a close, Rei and the others were still having dinner in Marina’s garden.

Of course, it would naturally be cold if they weren’t doing anything, so Marina was using wind spirit magic to keep the chilly night breeze from entering the garden.

This was something only Marina, a skilled spirit mage, could do; even for other spirit mages, it wasn’t something they could accomplish so easily.

In fact, if any other spirit mage were to witness this scene, they would likely lament what a waste of such skill it was.

They might think there must be some other way to have a meal with Set.

But from Marina’s perspective, since she can handle it with her own spirit magic, this is the quickest and easiest solution.

It wouldn’t be surprising if she thought, “Once it starts snowing and the ground gets covered, pulling a stunt like this will become difficult, but until then…”

That highly skilled spirit mage was casting a look of disbelief at Rei, who was sitting at the table eating stew packed with meat and vegetables.

With the work in Treant Forest nearly finished, Rei currently had no urgent tasks to attend to.

But since he’ll have to head to the Duchy of Kerebel with Elena soon, Marina’s honest question was, “Why waste time on something like that?”

No, it wasn’t just Marina; the others felt the same way.

The only one who looked on with anticipation upon hearing it was combat training was Vihera.

“Ah. To be honest, I didn’t really intend to accept that request… no, that favor.”

Although it was presented as a request that didn’t go through the guild, to Rei, it felt more like a personal favor than a formal commission.

“Then why did you accept it?”

Elena asked as she ate fish baked in a magic item kiln.

It was a fish resembling a sea bream, about 40 centimeters long, but the flesh, seared by the kiln’s heat, had a texture similar to that of a Pacific saury, and the flavor was more than satisfying even for Elena, who, as a noble, had experienced a wide variety of cuisines.

Furthermore, the fish paired exceptionally well with the sauce made from tart fruit, and it was the kind of dish that wouldn’t be out of place in a high-end restaurant.

“That guy who bought a meat bun from that stall—turns out he was actually born into a family that ran a diner. He told me that if I agreed to train him once, he’d be willing to teach the owner of that meat bun stall all sorts of things.”

That’s right; since the skinhead man was born and raised in a family that ran a diner, he actually had even deeper knowledge of cooking than Rei.

Since he’d been helping out at home since he was little, that kind of knowledge must have come naturally to him.

That said, he ultimately chose the path of an adventurer rather than that of a chef.

But just because he became an adventurer instead of a chef didn’t mean he’d lost the culinary knowledge and skills he’d acquired since childhood.

And that’s precisely why the bald man understood that Rei wanted to help out at the meat bun stall, if possible.

It was precisely because he saw through Rei’s intentions that he asked Rei to train him, in exchange for his own cooking skills and advice on making meat buns.

……Objectively speaking, Rei would be taking on the task of training the adventurer, and the meat bun stall owner would be the one receiving the payment.

In that sense, there was no direct benefit for Rei… yet, he decided to accept the request anyway.

For Rei, the satisfaction of good food holds immense significance.

Moreover, if a new endeavor ends in failure, it would likely discourage others from trying it as well.

To prevent that from happening, and so that Rei could eat delicious meat buns, he accepted the request.

When Rei explained this to Elena, she didn’t look exasperated… but rather, she gave him a look of understanding.

To those who had known Rei for a long time, it was clear just how much he valued the act of eating.

If anything, one could say that the man’s actions were very well-executed.

“I see. But while training is fine, we don’t have much time, you know?”

“I know. It’s just for tomorrow, so don’t worry.”

“Oh, well then… can I come too?”

It was, of course, Vihera who insisted on joining the training.

For Vihera, who relished combat and loved fighting powerful opponents above all else, there was simply no option to sit this one out.

Everyone present knew this even better than they knew that Rei had a weakness for food.

“That’s fine, but what about the patrol?”

Rei’s main job—logging in the Treant Forest—was already nearly finished, as winter was just around the corner.

That was precisely why Rei had the time to accompany the other adventurers in their combat training.

However… Vihera’s main job was essentially patrolling Gilm as an assistant to the guards.

The number of merchants had dwindled considerably, and many of those who had been working on expansion projects as much as possible had already finished their work and left Gilm.

Thanks to this, there was less commotion in Gilm compared to its peak season, but since there were still those who intended to spend the New Year in Gilm, the town’s population was larger than usual.

And with more residents, trouble naturally increased, keeping the guards busy.

When the guards are stretched too thin, that work naturally falls to the adventurers… and there was still a fair amount of patrol work to be done as an assistant to the guards.

“Is that work going well?” Rei asked, but Vihera smiled and said there was absolutely no problem.

“Compared to a little while ago, things have calmed down quite a bit. I don’t think there’ll be any particular problems even if I don’t join in.”

At Vihera’s words, Rei… no, all three of them present—Elena, Marina, and Ara—turned their gaze toward Byune, who was savoring her fifth bowl of stew.

Realizing she was the focus of all four of their gazes, Byune nodded silently.

Even while concentrating on eating her stew, she must have been listening carefully to what Rei and the others were saying.

With her demeanor, Byune indicated that what Vihera was saying was true.

Seeing this, Rei and the others finally believed Vihera’s words, but Vihera, who had been watching them as if it were only natural, took offense.

“Hey, didn’t you trust what I said?”

“Even if you say that, your usual behavior… you know?”

At Marina’s words, Rei, Elena, and Ara all nodded in unison.

If this had been some other matter—something completely unrelated to combat—they probably would have trusted Vihera’s words.

You wouldn’t guess it from her appearance, but Vihera actually takes her work seriously when it doesn’t involve combat.

But when it comes to combat, the story changes drastically.

Given their past experiences, it was only natural for Rei and the others to think that way.

Perhaps Vihera understood her own personality, for she could say nothing more in response to the others’ attitudes.

She said nothing, but she stared at Rei with a fixed gaze.

Noticing Vihera’s demeanor, Rei spoke up, hoping to lighten the mood.

“So, does that mean you’ll be joining us for that combat training, Vihera?”

“Yes. And Byune, too.”

“Huh?”

Startled by Vihera’s sudden remark, Byune, who had been focused on her stew, tilted her head in confusion.

“Yes. If you always spar with the same people, you inevitably get used to them, don’t you? So this is the perfect opportunity. Is that okay with you?”

“……Hmm.”

After hearing Vihera’s words, Byune thought for a moment before nodding in agreement.

Perhaps byune understood that combat training could never hurt.

The party led by Rei, known as the Crimson Wings, is made up of highly skilled fighters.

For that very reason, even though she was still young, Byune felt it was necessary to strengthen her own abilities.

That was why she nodded so readily at Vihera’s suggestion.

“So, that’s the plan—you’re okay with that, right?”

“Yeah, I think that’s fine too. The more people they have doing combat training, the better for them.”

While she was looking forward to combat training with Rei and the others, the fact that training with Vihera and Byune was also important shouldn’t be a bad thing for them either.

“If Vihera and Byune are coming, what about Elena and the others?”

“Why don’t we all go together?” Rei suggested, but Elena shook her head at his words.

“No, if I go out, it might cause a commotion. Besides, there’s no guarantee the Noble Faction won’t make a move. I’ll stay here.”

“I’ll be here, so I’ll let you know right away if anything happens…”

As far as Ara was concerned, it seemed safer for Elena to stay here than to go out into the streets of Gilm.

That said, since time spent with Elena had become precious lately, Ara was actually all for her staying put.

“What about Marina? Is she coming with us?”

“Hmm, I’m a little curious about that food stall, but I’ve actually been called away by the guild.”

“...Is there a problem? You don’t think another dungeon has appeared somewhere, do you?”

If that’s the case, I’d go there instead of the mock battle.

Rei implied as much, but Marina gave him a wry smile and shook her head.

“No, it’s not that. Oh, but it’s not a dungeon—though I suppose you could say it’s related to Rei.”

“What do you mean?” Rei asked, puzzled, and Marina shot him a look tinged with exasperation.

“Listen, Rei, you’re the one who left a huge pile of monster corpses in the guild’s warehouse, right? Have you already forgotten?”

“That’s true… but are you going to help with the dissection too, Marina? I heard that monster dissection is usually handled by unemployed adventurers or something.”

“That’s true. You’re not wrong. But my spirit magic—especially water spirit magic—is useful for butchering monsters, isn’t it? Plus, if I use wind spirit magic, I can deal with the smell in the warehouse too.”

Hearing that, Rei understood why Marina had been called to the butchering site.

It was thanks to Marina’s spirit magic that they were able to dine comfortably outside on a night like this, with winter just around the corner.

Anyone familiar with her abilities would naturally conclude that they’d be extremely effective for butchering monsters inside the storage hangar.

That said, it’s not like they could just casually call on Marina, the former guild master, for something like that.

“Well, as long as it’s not an emergency, that’s fine. …Sorry about that.”

“It’s fine, really. The expansion work has mostly been put on hold anyway, so I didn’t have anything else to do.”

“Still, there must be some parts of the construction that are moving forward, right?”

It’s not as if all the expansion work comes to a halt just because it’s winter.

Some work can be done even in winter, and there are areas that require specialized craftsmen.

Especially in the latter case—with specialized trades—since there are so few skilled workers, someone like Marina who can step in and help out when needed would be a huge help.

“That’s true. But Gilm has plenty of skilled workers to begin with. It’s not like they absolutely need me.”

“Ah… Now that you mention it, you’re right.”

As befits a frontier town, most of the adventurers affiliated with Gilm are skilled.

Some were born and raised in Gilm, others arrived with strong companions, and still others have come seeking work on Gilm’s expansion project… While not everyone is necessarily a skilled professional, they’re certainly no less capable than those in the royal capital or the Labyrinth City.

While there are few mages and spirit mages, there are enough of them to make a difference, and having healing magic available in case of an emergency is undoubtedly a stroke of luck.

And scaling back the scope of the expansion project means these individuals can fully demonstrate their abilities.

“Right? That’s why I can focus on dismantling Rei’s monsters.”

“Well, even so, I’m not sure I think it’s a good idea for Marina to go out of her way to help with that…”

As if to follow up on Rei’s words, Vihera interjected.

“Well, you know. Even if Guild staff are keeping watch, that doesn’t mean it’s completely foolproof, right? I don’t think there are any adventurers foolish enough to pull a stunt like that… but these days, lots of adventurers are flocking to Gilm from outside. Given that, we need to keep an eye on things, don’t we?”

Hearing Marina’s words, spoken with a seductive smile, everyone present prayed that there weren’t any thieves among the adventurers who had accepted the demolition job.
