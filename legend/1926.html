"Wow, this is handy..."

That was said by one of the adventurers tasked with guarding the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

When Rei first created the earthen wall, they had been amazed, wondering just how on earth he’d managed to make such a thing—but that amazement had been directed solely at the sheer power of Rei’s earth magic—which was actually a terrain manipulation skill.

But when the Kobolds actually arrived and were even slightly slowed down by that earthen wall, he truly realized just how useful it really was.

Kobolds are weak monsters; for adventurers tasked with guarding the group currently dismantling the structure, they are opponents they could easily defeat.

Even so, it was clear that being attacked by a large number of Kobolds would be a hassle.

However, simply having the earthen wall forced the Kobolds to move individually.

There was no doubt that this made things much easier for the adventurers.

“That makes sense. That’s Crimson Rei, after all. There’s no way an adventurer with a nickname like that would do anything wasteful.”

“Ah… well, I guess so. She really was impressive when we fought her this morning, too.”

The monster they fought that morning was a Snow Cyclops, a Rank B monster.

Even for an adventurer who could easily defeat a Kobold, things were a different story when it came to a high-rank Rank B monster.

Rei defeated such a high-rank monster effortlessly… without the slightest struggle.

As someone who works as an adventurer in Gilm, I naturally know just how skilled Rei is… but even so, this was the first time I’d ever seen him completely dominate a monster like that.

“To defeat a monster like that all by himself… well, even with Set by his side, to defeat it so easily? Honestly, I’m more inclined to think it’s unbelievable.”

“I can’t deny that. …Oh, look, while we’re talking, another Kobold’s coming over.”

Saying that, the few men who had been talking grabbed their weapons and ran toward the newly arrived Kobolds.

Watching the backs of those adventurer bodyguards, the group butchering the Giganto Turtle exchanged words without pausing their work.

“It’s amazing, really. …This giant turtle—was it called the Giganto Turtle? That guy Rei took it down too, right? Thanks to Suchi, we’ve been lucky from the start this year.”

“Haha. Uncle Garada’s absolutely right. We really have to thank Suchi. At least we can sleep somewhere without drafts at night.”

The two, who knew each other through Suchi, said this as they skinned the Giganto Turtle’s leg, and the others around them—all from the slums—nodded in agreement.

In reality, just getting the Kobold’s corpse was a huge help, but with this butchering job, they’d receive proper pay, get the Giganto Turtle’s meat, and on top of that, if they worked hard, there was even a chance they’d be hired by the guild starting in the spring.

Given that, it would have been impossible to tell the slum dwellers not to give it their all.

“Still… I wish I could’ve done that butchering job over there, too.”

Suddenly, one of the slum dwellers muttered, glancing over at the group butchering the Snow Cyclops.

It’s not that they dislike dismantling Giganto Turtles, but when you have to keep dismantling monsters this huge, it’s only natural to sometimes wish you could try dismantling a different kind of monster.

However, even though the Snow Cyclops is five meters tall and certainly massive, it’s still incomparably smaller than the Giganto Turtle.

Given that, it’s only natural that more people are assigned to the Giganto Turtle’s disassembly.

Moreover, unlike the Giganto Turtle, we know exactly where to find what materials in the Snow Cyclops, so it was only natural that the task would be entrusted to adventurers accustomed to dissection.

“Don’t be unreasonable. I asked around a bit, and apparently that monster is pretty strong. Way stronger than the Kobolds we’ve been butchering.”

“Well, of course. You saw it devour that Kobold this morning, didn’t you?”

Even as they talked like that… the fact that their hands never stopped moving was proof that everyone had gotten used to butchering.

“What did you say!?”

At the sudden voice, everyone present turned their gaze in that direction.

Standing where the voice came from was an incredibly beautiful woman who had been coming to this butchering site for the past few days.

She was clearly furious as she spoke to a guild employee.

Apart from that initial shout, her voice wasn’t particularly loud, so I couldn’t make out what she was saying, but it was clear she was angry.

“I wonder what happened.”

“……Huh?”

One of the women muttered, but the man—who had been captivated by the beauty of the woman speaking to the guild staff member, Vihera—was so mesmerized by her beauty that he didn’t immediately notice the voice.

“Huh.”

Seeing the man like that, the woman let out a deep sigh.

“Men are just like this…” she thought to herself, then repeated what she had said earlier.

“I just asked if something had happened.”

“Ah… um, yeah. I think someone heard that person talking about fighting a strong opponent or something, so if you think about it, it’s probably about the Snow Cyclops, right? …It’s such a waste.”

From the man’s perspective, he probably couldn’t understand why a beauty like Vihera would be so obsessed with fighting.

After all, it seemed to him that if she really wanted to, she could live a life of ease without any trouble at all.

“Hey, big brother, big sister! Don’t just talk—get your hands moving too!”

The man and woman who had been chatting were told this by a child nearby who was skinning the leg of a Giganto Turtle that had been cut up, and they hurriedly returned to their work.

……Meanwhile, the much-talked-about Vihera…

“Really. I’m so jealous of Rei. …So, is there a chance that monster, the Snow Cyclops, might show up early tomorrow morning too?”

“Uh, yes. If there was only one Snow Cyclops, that raises questions about the number of Kobolds, so of course, there’s a possibility that there just happened to be fewer Kobolds last night…”

I was talking with a guild staff member.

Just as Rei had predicted, Vihera arrived at the scene around 10:00 a.m. and, upon seeing the headless corpse of the Snow Cyclops lying there, felt a deep sense of regret.

Rei had clearly told her that monsters were gathering at the Giganto Turtle dissection site early in the morning, drawn by the smell of blood and the faint scent of flesh.

Despite that, she hadn’t paid it any mind since the only monsters present were Kobolds… but now she deeply regretted it, thinking that if she could have fought such a formidable foe, it would have been worth getting up early.

For Vihera, the only consolation—as the guild staff had mentioned—was the possibility that the Snow Cyclops Rei had defeated wasn’t the only one; there might be others out there.

“I’ll have to get up early tomorrow.”

As she said this, Vihera’s eyes were already lost in thoughts of tomorrow’s battle.

She turned her gaze toward the guild clerk, her eyes burning with a fierce fighting spirit.

Faced with that gaze, the guild clerk instinctively took a few steps back.

Working at the guild, she’d had plenty of opportunities to see hot-blooded adventurers… but while Vihera seemed similar to them, there was clearly something different about her.

It was likely this half-realization that prompted his reaction.

(Please come back soon...)

“I’m going to take a look at the earthen wall.”

With those words, Rei left the area, and the guild clerk could do nothing but pray that she would return as soon as possible.

“Hm?”

Suddenly feeling as if someone had called Rei's name, Rei looked around.

But all that was around him were materials for the expansion project set to begin once spring arrived; no one seemed to be calling him.

“Guruu?”

Seeing Rei like that, Set tilted his head and asked, “What’s wrong?”

Rei stroked Set and spoke.

“Nah, I just thought I heard someone calling me. Guess it was just my imagination.”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a soft “Hmm?” and looked around again.

Rei followed suit and scanned the surroundings as well.

Since Set has sharper senses than Rei, if someone had actually called out to Rei, it wouldn’t be strange for Set to have noticed it before Rei did.

However, since Set didn’t seem to have noticed anything of the sort, he concluded that the earlier incident must have been his imagination after all, and looked around once more.

Spread out before him were supplies for the expansion project.

It was no different from yesterday, but the one major difference from yesterday was… that there were no adventurers to be seen.

Well, it’s not that there are none at all.

He could make out a few adventurers even from a distance.

The reason the number of adventurers has clearly decreased compared to yesterday is likely the earthen wall Rei built.

Thanks to the earthen wall, the Kobolds can no longer enter Gilm.

...That said, it’s not entirely foolproof—Rei didn’t know how they got in, but there were signs of a fight in a few places.

The guild had also inspected the earthen wall and concluded that the likelihood of Kobolds getting in was low, so as a result, the number of people accepting Kobold extermination requests had plummeted starting today.

That said, since there were still a few skirmishes happening, it wasn’t as if Kobold intrusions had ceased entirely, so they still needed a certain number of adventurers.

(Ah, but if that’s the case, I wonder what’s happening with the bounties for Kobold extermination. I heard they used to keep the dismantling fees low to balance things out, but…)

While pondering this, they continued toward the earthen wall… and without encountering any Kobolds, Rei and Set arrived at the wall’s base.

However, as Rei spotted the earthen wall in the distance, he noticed several figures who appeared to be adventurers standing in front of it, and a question arose in his mind.

Still, since they’d come to check if there was anything wrong with the earthen wall, they couldn’t just stand there watching from afar, so they continued on.

Then, the adventurers in front of the earthen wall must have noticed Rei approaching, as they turned their gaze toward him.

(Huh?)

There were about ten people in front of the earthen wall, a mix of men and women.

Rei noticed that the gazes of some of them contained hostility—though not quite murderous intent—and this made him wonder.

Having been through a lot himself, Rei didn’t delude himself into thinking no one held a grudge against him; on the contrary, he understood that it wouldn’t be strange if there were hundreds, or even thousands, of people who wanted him dead.

That is precisely why, even when met with such hostile stares, he shows no particular concern as he approaches the earthen wall… and the gathered adventurers.

Since there were no familiar faces among them, Rei paid no mind to the adventurers’ stares and walked right past them…

“Wait a minute!”

Suddenly, a man standing in front of the earthen wall called out to Rei.

The voice belonged to one of the men who had been glaring at Rei with hostility.

However, since he didn’t make any move to draw a weapon, Rei didn’t brace himself either; he simply turned to look at him and spoke.

“What’s up?”

“Don’t ask me ‘what’s up’! You built this earthen wall, didn’t you?”

“Yeah. I didn’t want Kobolds getting into Gilm, so I had it built. Is there a problem with that?”

To be precise, the main reason was that he needed more manpower to dismantle the Giganto Turtle, but he decided there was no need to explain that to someone who was hostile toward him.

“It’s not ‘what’s the matter’! I’ve been making a living here by killing Kobolds. But because of this, the Kobolds aren’t coming in anymore. What are you going to do about it?”

“So, you’re saying that as long as you can make money, it’s fine for Kobolds to enter Gilm and cause damage in the town?”

“I never said that. In fact, until now, I’d always kill all the Kobolds before they got into town. So there shouldn’t have been a problem, right?”

There was confidence in the man’s words.

A fierce confidence that he would never lose to something as trivial as a Kobold.

Of course, since most adventurers in Gilm possess the skill to easily defeat Kobolds, that might be to be expected.

“You’d better not assume things will stay this way forever. What if rare or high-tier monsters appear… and what do you plan to do if such monsters make their way into town? Besides, if you just want to fight Kobolds, you could join the escort for the Giganto Turtle’s dissection—you’d get to fight Kobolds there. So why don’t you take that job?”

At Rei’s words, the man fell silent.
