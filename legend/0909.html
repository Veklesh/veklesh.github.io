Rei tilted her head at the words that came out of Maruka's mouth.

Thinking he might have misheard, he glanced at Koan standing behind Maruka, only to see him nod with a smile.

Meaning what came out of Maruka's mouth was neither a mishearing nor Rei's imagination, but genuine words.

"Um, sorry. I'm not quite following the thread here. The root problem is that I brought shame upon... more accurately, upon the Earl of Eriell family, my keep's family, right?"

He'd suffered the ultimate humiliation by wetting himself in front of so many people.

In this city of Gilm, there was no doubt that the name "Eriel" and "wet pants" had become synonymous.

And since so many people visited this place called Gilm, there was no stopping the information from spreading.

The only saving grace was that it was winter now.

By the time the snow melted and the flow of people and goods picked up again, perhaps... just maybe, the rumors might have died down already.

Though, whether news that the heir apparent of a noble house bearing the title of Count had wet himself in public would die down in just a few months was doubtful.

"That's true. No doubt about it."

"...So why am I the one becoming an instructor? Or rather, what kind of instructor am I supposed to be?"

"Hmm. In the city of Graciall, which is also the seat of the Quent Duke's household, there's an officer training academy for the Quent Duke's Knights. You'll be an instructor there."

"No, are you even listening to me? Why the hell would I need to go out of my way to teach at that academy? What exactly am I supposed to teach?"

"You were an apprentice wizard, weren't you? If so, I believe there are various things I could teach you."

Maruka's words made Rei recall his own backstory.

(Come to think of it... yeah, that was the setup. Damn it, I never expected this kind of offer. Wait, should I just teach them some random stuff from their world? ...No, that's not it.)

Rei realized he'd been thinking as if he already understood he'd be teaching. He sighed again and spoke up.

"So, that's not it. What I want to know is why the hell I need to leave Gilm and go to... Graciall, was it? Why do I need to go there?"

"...Hmph. Must I really say it?"

Maruka looked up at Rei with a gaze from below.

If one didn't know Maruka's personality, she'd be so adorable you'd almost nod in agreement.

But Rei knew Maruka's personality.

He sighed and spoke again.

"So, why?"

"Hmm... That's strange. I was told that if I asked like this, you'd grant my request immediately."

Who on earth gave him that useless advice? Suppressing the urge to retort, Rei urged him on with a glance.

Then, perhaps resigned to it, Maruka opened his mouth, though his expression looked slightly displeased.

"To put it simply, they've decided to use this incident to tighten discipline among the royalist faction."

Sitting in his chair, his feet dangling and swinging because they didn't reach the floor, Maruka looked almost like a doll, partly due to his own endearing charm.

Yet hearing the words "discipline and integrity" come from his mouth didn't feel out of place, likely because Rei knew something of Maruka's character.

"Disciplinary measures?"

Rei shifted his gaze to Koan standing behind Maruka and caught a small smile there.

Even if he didn't understand the reason for that smile, he urged Maruka to continue for now.

"Hmm. As you surely know, the Royalist faction is the largest power within the Kingdom of Mireana. And being the largest faction... meaning it has many members, there are all sorts of people within it. One particularly troublesome individual is Keep, the one who clashed with you this time."

Unlike earlier, he now addressed him simply as Keep. This change in tone likely stemmed from Keep's capture after attempting to attack the Baroness Sisne's residence.

He caused such a commotion that he brought the King's faction's prestige down as low as it could possibly go.

Had he at least quietly paid the price for losing the duel, he might still have remained within the royalist faction.

But the attempted attack on the Baron Sysne household that followed afterward was impossible to gloss over.

"Of course, as I just said, Keep is particularly egregious. But even without going that far, there are far too many nobles behaving in ways that make them unworthy of the title. Upon learning this, His Majesty the King ordered a crackdown to restore discipline within the royalist faction."

"I'd like to say 'good work,' but how does that lead to me becoming an instructor?"

"Just hear me out. The cause of this disciplinary crackdown is undoubtedly you. More precisely, it's the Sysne Baronial House and you. Naturally, the hatred of those targeted in this crackdown will be directed at you.However, to harm others out of such resentment... as your concubine... or rather, as a member of the royalist faction, my pride won't allow it. Though, of course, there's also the matter of public perception."

Maruka shrugged her shoulders slightly and looked at Rei to see if he understood.

When Rei nodded at that look, Maruka continued her explanation.

"Anyway, that's why you, Rei, are being targeted by resentment from nobles within the royalist faction who feel cornered."

"Muet... Baron Sisne? If this whole incident stems from various causes, doesn't that mean he's the one in real danger?"

The root cause of this whole affair was Keip setting his sights on Ashié, the maid serving the Baron de Sisne household.

Given that, the most endangered party was undoubtedly the Sysne household... specifically, its current head, Muet.

The faces of Muet, Baslero, and Ashié flashed through Rei's mind.

But Maruka shook her head at Rei's words, insisting there was no problem.

"I hear the Baroness Sisne's household has officially joined the neutral faction because of this incident. Once that happens, even the royalists can't touch them. If Lord Daskar were to lay a finger on a neutral faction member here in Gilm, he'd have no excuse. But you're a different matter."

Maruka's gaze shifted back to Rei.

His eyes reflected a look of concern for Rei.

"If you were Daskar's subordinate, acting as a member of the neutral faction, there would be no issue. But... you are close to Daskar and take many of his requests, yet you remain an adventurer, not a member of the neutral faction, correct?"

Maruka's words succinctly defined Rei's position.

He possessed abilities that set him apart from ordinary adventurers, commanding a griffin—a rank A monster—as his familiar. Yet, he was notoriously picky about who he liked, holding particularly unfavorable views toward the majority of nobles.

Under normal circumstances, such a person would be regarded as dangerous, warranting vigilance. It wouldn't be surprising if someone tried to frame him for something, leading to his arrest.

Yet Daskar tolerated Rei, partly because his talent was too valuable to waste. In fact, he even showed him various favors.

It's not strange for Ray to feel affection or gratitude toward Daskar.

Even so, Rei has never considered joining a faction. He refuses to be tied down, unable to act freely, or forced into actions against his will through orders.

"So that's the reason after all?"

"Yes. What happens if you truly anger Rei? There are those who don't understand that, those who understand but don't think it would be directed at them as nobles, and even those who believe all the rumors they hear are fabrications spread by Rei to make himself look important."

"Even those who actually saw me fight? Besides, last spring's war was led by the King's faction, wasn't it? Though many died when Theorem's strategy struck us from behind, survivors must have remained."

"The Flash, you say. That's undoubtedly true. Yet there are still those who can only believe their own opinions, or refuse to believe anything else. Such people might try to harm you. However, as long as you remain in Glacial, which is also my stronghold, they won't dare act recklessly."

"That's true, but if they come at me, I'm prepared to fight back."

"If we cause you trouble due to our circumstances, that would be utterly unacceptable for us as supporters of the King."

"Well, to begin with, asking me to come all the way to your stronghold is a bit of a hassle, isn't it?"

Maruka gasped at the words that came out of Rei's mouth.

Rei's confidence wasn't just talk. He genuinely possessed that level of combat prowess, and his track record proved it. That was precisely why...

"Lord Daskar likely wants to avoid having Rei rampage through Gilm. Of course, this isn't a demand. If you absolutely refuse, we won't force you. However... well, if you agree to be the instructor, I'm willing to pay in fire ore. Naturally, you could also make it a formal request through the guild."

Rei's movements halted at the words coming from Maruka's mouth.

That was the name of the magical ore Rei had found before with Fron and Blasso.

Well, to be precise, it was transformed into Flame Ore by the fire magic Rei had used.

It was an extremely rare material for blacksmiths and alchemists, and the same held true for Rei.

Mixing fire ore into Rei's secret weapon, the fire whirlwind, increased its power exponentially more than adding something like iron shards.

(Besides, it was winter now, and he really didn't have anything else to do.)

The very words "Flame Ore" had already tipped the scales in Rei's mind, leaning him halfway toward accepting Maruka's request.

He also considered that if it took the form of a designated request, it could serve as a credential to advance to a higher rank.

"Flame ore, huh? How much exactly can I get?"

"Seems you're getting enthusiastic. ...Hmm, how about one barrel about my size?"

Maruka, who delivered this offer, stood just over a meter tall.

A barrel of that size would hold a considerable amount.

It could be said to be worth as much as a platinum coin... no, even a light gold coin.

But after a moment's thought, Rei shook his head.

"Two. How about that?"

"Hmm... That's quite greedy of you."

"I got dragged into this whole royalist mess in the first place, so you could at least cut me some slack, couldn't you?"

"You're hitting a sore spot. ...Koan, what do you think?"

Maruka turned his gaze to Koan standing behind him and asked.

"Well, hiring someone of Rei-san's caliber certainly justifies that price. After all, he defeated an immovable Noiz of Rank S."

Rei failed to suppress the look of surprise that flashed across his face at Koan's words.

If he meant she lost to Noiz, he was clearly only referring to the tournament.

But if he won, that was a statement that could only come from someone who knew about the civil war and Rei's fight against Noiz.

"...You're quite the gossip."

At Rei's words, Maruka opened her mouth with a broad smile.

"The largest faction in the Mireana Kingdom isn't just for show, you know."

"Yet despite that, Keep seemed to know absolutely nothing about me?"

"Information, you see, is meaningless if it just sits there. One must put it to proper use. Especially for a house like the Counts of Eriell, which is so disliked, it inevitably leads to being out of the loop."

With that alone, Rei vaguely understood the situation.

(This disciplinary crackdown was probably planned for some time. I just happened to be the trigger.)

That's precisely why the course of action had already been decided. That's why, just weeks after that duel, he'd made the effort to come all the way to Gilm in the middle of winter, with snow falling.

What Rei, who had vaguely anticipated this, could do was...

"Just so you know, I didn't actually beat Noiz. He let me win. Essentially, he just spared me."

All he could do was tell the truth about that fight.

Those who knew the circumstances said Noiz had fled, so it counted as Rei's victory. But even so, Rei felt more strongly that he'd been let off.

If they fought again, this time he would truly show him he was the victor.

Hearing Rei's words, spoken with that resolve, Maruka and Koan both flashed small expressions of surprise.

"Hmm, well, that's up to your own perspective, I suppose. So, what do you say? Will you accept my request?"

Maruka's words brought up Noiz, reminding him of the Rank S adventurer in the Kingdom of Mireana.

"All right. Two barrels of fire ore, and I want to meet the Rank S adventurer in the Kingdom of Mireana. I'll take on the role of instructor. But I can only handle combat. No teaching theory—just practical training like mock battles."

"...Hmm, surprisingly greedy, aren't you? Well, fine. As for the Rank S adventurer, Lord Saldato, I can't guarantee it. How about 'to the best of my ability' as the condition?"

"Understood. What's the timeframe for the instructor role? I do have some plans tentatively scheduled for spring."

"That should be fine. That sort of thing should wrap up during the winter."

And so, Rei found himself saddled with the job of instructor—a role utterly unsuited to him.

"By the way, Rei. Your magic power..."

"Ah, this, huh?"

He ended up showing the ring and explaining its effects in response to a question Maruka casually brought up.
