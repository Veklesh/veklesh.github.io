A man wielding a spear—one might call it a trident—collapsed to the ground… and in the next instant, the announcer’s voice boomed throughout the arena.

“That’s enough! All mock battles are now over! An undefeated record! Rei, known by the alias ‘Crimson,’ has demonstrated strength beyond compare!”

“Waaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!”

The audience’s cries echoed around the stage.

Not all of the spectators who came to watch this mock battle were particularly interested in combat.

But even so, the fact that a single man named Rei had fought his way to victory against such overwhelming odds

That left a strong impression and shock on those watching.

……Of course, among those rejoicing were those who had bet on Rei winning every match, so it’s only natural that they would want to let out a roar of triumph.

Miranda, who is in charge of Rei, is certainly one of them.

Rei had given her a platinum coin, and she had placed it as a bet… but to be honest, until Rei actually won like this, her heart was pounding so hard it felt like it might burst.

And that’s only natural. After all, when she bet on Rei winning every match, the man running the bets even gave her a look that seemed to ask, “Are you serious?”

Incidentally, that “Are you serious?” was meant to ask both if she was serious about betting on Rei’s undefeated streak and if she was serious about betting the platinum coin… but Miranda hadn’t realized that.

“I won… really… Huh? Wait, why…?”

Miranda realized she had been crying without even noticing and quickly wiped her eyes with a handkerchief.

Having watched him train with the Kerebel Duke’s Knights, Miranda naturally knew that Rei was strong.

Even so, considering Rei’s small stature, it was easy to imagine just how exceptional it was for him to win every single match against such a large group.

At the very least, as far as Miranda knew, there weren’t many others capable of doing the same.

Only Elena and Philma.

……Though, the fact that those two know others who can do the same thing is strange in itself.

In any case, after confirming that the tears that had suddenly welled up had stopped, she began to worry about what to do with the money she’d won from the bet.

(I have to make sure no one steals it. It’s one thing if it’s just my share, but there’s also Rei’s share.)

At Rei’s suggestion, Miranda had also bet on Rei to win every match.

Miranda was born into nobility, but as the third daughter of a baron’s family based in Anesis, she didn’t have that much money to spend freely.

That said, this was only relative to other nobles; as was evident from the fact that she worked at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence and was entrusted with Rei’s duties—where she was treated as a guest—she was clearly trusted by her superiors.

Naturally, she received a commensurate salary and had wagered a considerable sum on this bet.

As a result, the total amount she would receive—including Rei’s share—was quite substantial… and in such a situation, if a maid—though she wasn’t wearing her maid’s uniform today—were to carry that much money, there was no guarantee she wouldn’t be targeted by people with ulterior motives.

No, given that this is the second-largest city in the Mireana Kingdom, it naturally attracts many unscrupulous individuals.

If such people were to see Miranda—who could undoubtedly be described as beautiful—receiving stacks of gold and platinum coins as her winnings, it goes without saying what they would do.

In fact, several men standing a short distance away from Miranda were exchanging meaningful glances, communicating silently.

But Miranda is neither accustomed to violence nor trained in it.

Naturally, she was completely unaware that she was the object of such stares…

“Miranda?”

“Huh?”

Startled by the sudden voice, Miranda turned around.

She was startled to hear her name called out of the blue, but since it was someone she knew, she immediately broke into a smile.

“Hello, Mr. Leodanis. So you came to watch, too?”

“Ah, yes. It was a good opportunity to objectively assess Rei’s abilities with my own eyes.”

“Oh? If you mean assessing his skills, he’s been doing mock battles with the Knights’ Order over and over again—don’t you already know what he’s capable of?”

Miranda hasn’t watched every single mock battle between Rei and the Knights, but she still knows that Rei has engaged in mock battles with them many times.

Given that, surely Leodanis fully understands Rei’s abilities by now?

It wouldn’t be strange for Miranda to think that.

After all, Leodanis had engaged in mock battles with Rei quite a few times himself, so he should have felt the extent of his abilities firsthand.

“The knights of the Duke of Kerebel’s Order are all highly skilled. But… that’s precisely why I wanted to see how strong Rei is when fighting opponents other than them.”

“Is that so? …I don’t really understand that kind of mindset men have.”

Miranda said this, but those around her who had been watching her closely—and trying to seize the winnings from the bet—stopped in their tracks upon hearing their conversation.

Living in Anesis, the men naturally knew the name of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights, and they also knew that they were an elite force led by a man named Philma.

It was fortunate for both sides that none of the men were hot-headed.

If they were to cause a disturbance with the knights of the Kerebel Duke’s Order or their associates, a severe counterattack would undoubtedly follow.

After all, they were a group capable of thinking that far ahead.

If there had been someone present who couldn’t grasp the situation at all, he would likely—no, he would undoubtedly have been blinded by greed and attacked Miranda.

That said, while it was clear to an outside observer that Miranda had bet on Rei to win every match and had won, it was unclear exactly how much money she had won.

Given that Miranda is dressed in ordinary clothes today, I can’t imagine she’d bet such a large sum.

Depending on the situation, it’s even possible she bet just a few copper coins…

No, actually, while it wasn’t exactly a long shot, she bet on Rei winning every match—which had fairly high odds—so I didn’t think she’d bet that much money.

...If they’d known that Miranda was receiving platinum coins from Rei, the men who’d been eyeing her might have changed their minds.

Fortunately, however, that wasn’t the case, and the men decided that getting involved with Miranda would only lead to trouble for them, so they simply walked away.

Miranda, completely unaware that she had narrowly escaped danger, continued her conversation with Leodanis.

That said, Leodanis didn’t actually know that Miranda had been targeted by thugs, so he had absolutely no sense that he had saved her from danger.

“So, what are you going to do now, Miranda? Um, if you’d like, would you like to go check out the food stalls with me?”

Leodanis asked Miranda, his cheeks flushing slightly as he rubbed the bridge of his nose.

As a maid of the Duke of Kerebel’s household… and as a woman worthy of being called a beauty, Miranda could easily imagine what Leodanis’s invitation meant.

She thought for a moment... then smiled and spoke.

“Well, since it’s an invitation from you, Leodanis, I’ll accept. But… I’ll need to hand over the money I won from Rei first. Is that okay?”

“So he was doing that? Well, if he won every single match, I suppose you can’t exactly call it cheating.”

If Rei had bet on which match he would lose, Leodanis would never have tolerated it as blatant cheating.

But in this case, since Rei has won every single match, it’s fair to assume he’s confident in his own strength—and that’s precisely why he bet on a clean sweep.

(Actually, the appearance of an adventurer with a nickname like “Ice Blade” was probably unexpected even for Rei.)

Thinking this, Leodanis recalled the battle between Ice Blade and Rei.

True to his nickname, he realized that Ice Blade was a far superior opponent.

He felt so strongly that if he were to fight Ice Blade, he would almost certainly… not stand a chance even if he fought a hundred times.

That said, given Leodanis’s personality, he wasn’t the type to give up just because he couldn’t win.

Even if it’s impossible now, he would surely win someday.

Thinking that...

“Oh? You invited me here, yet you’re daydreaming about something else? Isn’t that a bit rude?”

“Huh? Oh… uh, yeah. Sorry. I was just thinking about the fight between Hyoujin and Rei, and I got a little carried away. So, what do you say? Would you like to go around the shops with me?”

“...Alright. I was actually thinking I’d like to talk with you a bit, Mr. Leodanis.”

After a moment’s silence, Miranda nodded in response to Leodanis’s invitation.

That brief silence made Leodanis worry that Miranda might turn him down... but he was relieved when she ultimately accepted his invitation.

In fact, Leodanis didn’t seem to realize at all that that silence was actually part of Miranda’s subtle maneuvering.

“Um, well, let’s go then. Oh, first I need to cash in the money Rei asked me to get.”

“I suppose so. …Though it doesn’t look like there are many people around.”

After each mock battle ended, Leo Danis saw the spectators near Miranda lamenting their losses.

It was precisely because so few people had bet on Rei winning every match that the odds were so high.

Given that, Miranda predicted there wouldn’t be many people cashing out their winnings… and that turned out to be an undeniable fact.

This was proven by the small number of people present when they arrived at the payout counter.

“Oh, but there are more people here than I expected.”

“True. Well, if you know even a little about Rei’s abilities, it wouldn’t be strange to bet on her, so I don’t think it’s that surprising.”

In fact, for those who knew Rei’s true strength, the possibility of her winning every match in this mock battle wasn’t entirely out of the question.

The problem was that not that many people actually knew that.

(The reason there are so many people who look like adventurers is probably because they’d heard rumors about Rei’s abilities.)

Even those who aren’t adventurers must have heard various rumors about Rei, of course.

But while rumors like the one about him burning down a first-class inn were entertaining to those who enjoyed them, if asked whether they truly believed them, they couldn’t exactly nod in agreement so readily.

At the very least, there probably weren’t many who would go so far as to place a bet based on that.

Compared to those people, some of the adventurers—precisely because they were fellow adventurers—had gathered more information on Rei… and it was those very people who bet on him winning every match in this mock battle and came out on top.

That said, given that opponents in the mock battles included those with nicknames like “Ice Blade” and knights in the service of nobles, the possibility that Rei might lose partway through could never be entirely ruled out.

Of course, not everyone here is an adventurer.

Some bet simply because they had a hunch he’d go undefeated, while others, as merchants, had accurately memorized the rumors.

With people like that around, even when Leodanis and Miranda came to cash in their winnings, they didn’t stand out in any particular way.

“Hey, kid. Man, you’re something else. There were others who bet on Rei winning every match, but you’re the only one tough enough to put platinum coins on the line… I was sure we were going to make a killing.”

When she handed the betting slip to the man in charge of cashing out, he looked at Miranda and said, sounding surprised.

Since she’d gone so far as to bet a platinum coin, he must have remembered Miranda well.

That’s why, even as he looked at Miranda in surprise… he also seemed a little disappointed.

“Heh, thank you. Now, please take care of the payout.”

“Ah, got it. Man, you’re definitely the one who made the most money today.”

“Is that so? That’s nice to hear. …Leodanis, shall we go?”

“Yeah. …This place feels a little uncomfortable, you know.”

Part of it is probably because he’s a knight, but Leodanis says as much.

That said, the bet itself isn’t exactly illegal.

The fact that Leodanis feels uncomfortable is purely a matter of his own perception; in reality, the people around him aren’t really looking at him that way.

In any case, after cashing out their winnings, Miranda and Leodanis headed off to meet Rei to hand over the money they’d won from the bet.
