The monsters that had come in search of the lingering scent of yesterday’s butchering—or perhaps the scraps of meat that had fallen during the process—were wiped out in a matter of minutes.

The fact that there were no high-rank monsters among the gathered horde was probably a stroke of luck for those planning to perform the butchering... but for Rei and Set, it was a bit of a letdown.

After all, if a high-rank monster had appeared, they might have been able to secure an unknown magic stone.

In any case, the matter was quickly settled: whoever defeated a monster gained ownership of it.

...That said, since the adventurers here were from Gilm, they didn’t really feel the need to keep any monsters—except for the Orcs, which were literally delicious—so in the end, they decided to give them to Suu-chi and the other slum dwellers.

This was partly because disposing of them would have been a hassle, but also because they wanted the residents to improve their butchering skills even slightly, so that the dissection of the Giganto Turtle could proceed as smoothly as possible.

No one else knows exactly how skilled the slum dwellers are at butchering.

That’s precisely why I did it.

Of course, if the slum dwellers’ butchering skills improve, that means the Giganto Turtle’s butchering will be finished sooner, which ultimately means I’ll get less meat.

“So, what should we do? I never expected the effects of yesterday’s butchering to still linger even after a whole night has passed.”

Watching the workers who had already begun butchering the Giganto Turtle, a guild official asked Rei.

This guild official was the one in charge of the butchering site, and he was the one who had ordered those unable to fight to gather together when the monster attacked.

His split-second judgment was spot-on, as befitted someone entrusted with this job.

“Hmm, even if you say that… There’s nothing we can do about it, is there? We certainly can’t dismantle the Giganto Turtle right here in the Gilm.”

That would undoubtedly have been the best solution, but while it might have been possible with a Giganto Turtle whose body had shrunk further through disassembly, there was absolutely no way they could bring the current Giganto Turtle out into the city.

“You’re right. …Or maybe we could have Rei cut the Giganto Turtle’s body into several pieces and dismantle them inside the Gilm… Is that… difficult?”

“Probably. Besides, since we don’t even know which parts of the Giganto Turtle can be used as materials, I don’t want to do anything reckless.”

Rei’s honest feeling was that he’d rather not do anything like that, given the possibility that carelessly cutting off parts like the Giganto Turtle’s legs, body, or tail could ruin them as materials.

If there had been detailed reference materials on the Giganto Turtle, and if he had known specifically which parts yielded what kind of materials, Rei would likely have nodded in agreement with the guild official’s words.

But considering the possibility of ruining the materials, he’d rather not take that option if he could help it.

(In that sense, cutting off the head probably caused all sorts of problems too. …Though, it’s true that there weren’t many other ways to take down a creature of this size.)

If they’d tried to take their time and defeat it clumsily, they would undoubtedly have inflicted even more damage, which would have significantly reduced its value as a material.

“Hmm, it’s a tough call. Since there weren’t any monsters that powerful today, it wasn’t a problem… but there’s no guarantee tomorrow will be the same as today.”

“What should we do?” the guild clerk asked, turning his gaze toward me.

We’ve hired adventurers to act as escorts for the butchers, but taking down a group of monsters large enough to pose a threat while carrying the people we’re supposed to protect on our backs… I wouldn’t say it’s impossible, but it would be difficult.

Moreover, as the guild clerk said, today there were only weak monsters, but there’s no guarantee there won’t be high-rank monsters starting tomorrow.

After giving the guild clerk’s words some thought, Rei spoke up.

“You’re right. I’ll take that on. It’s a bit of a stretch to call it ‘lucky,’ but there are monsters that are tasty to me, like orcs. If I handle a certain number of monsters, then the goblins and kobolds… wait, one just came over from where I was talking.”

Perhaps drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood, a kobold appeared.

Watching the escorting adventurers immediately head toward the kobold, Rei muttered, “Speak of the devil.”

That said, as an adventurer in Gilm, there was no way he’d be taken down by something as trivial as a kobold, so Rei merely glanced in their direction before continuing his conversation with the guild staff.

“Anyway, that’s the deal—I’ll take on the high-rank monsters. …Even if there aren’t any high-rank monsters, I’m willing to take on a certain number of them. Is that okay?”

Even as he said this, Rei seemed somewhat reluctant.

Since Rei was generally not a morning person, getting up early every day under these circumstances—let alone in the middle of a quest—was actually quite a struggle.

Still, since Rei was the only one capable of storing the Giganto Turtle, it was a fact that they couldn’t begin dismantling it unless he showed up early in the morning.

“Yes, I’ll do that. …Having an Item Box is something I really envy, but it’s also a hassle in many ways.”

“That’s true. The fact that only the user can access it is convenient for security, but it causes all sorts of problems in situations like this. I wish the simplified version that’s become somewhat widespread had a larger capacity.”

“Don’t ask the impossible, please. I could probably manage something a little bigger, but trying to handle a whole Giganto Turtle that big…”

The guild clerk paused there and shook his head.

Rei, too, had said that knowing full well the limitations, so he didn’t seem particularly upset.

“You never know, right? Even if it’s impossible now, you can’t rule out the possibility that an item box could be created someday. Since something like this actually exists, it wouldn’t be strange if something similar—or in some cases, an even more powerful magic item—were to be created in the future.”

“Well… I suppose so.”

The guild clerk nodded at Rei’s words, but it was clear from his expression that he didn’t believe such an item box would be created anytime soon—certainly not within a few years.

That said, while an alchemist’s skill plays a role, the value of a magic item also varies greatly depending on the materials used.

Given that, if they could obtain materials from a rare monster like the Giganto Turtle, then perhaps—and I mean just perhaps—the possibility of creating an item box isn’t entirely out of the question.

Rei knew full well that the chances of that happening were quite slim, though.

“Anyway, that’s the plan from here on out.”

Deciding that feeling sleepy in the morning was simply a matter of taking a nap to make up for the lack of sleep, Rei stated this with some degree of forcefulness.

As a guild staff member, if Rei was willing to do that, he certainly had no complaints.

No, on the contrary, since he was being considerate of the guild, there was no way they could refuse.

“Yes, thank you very much. The Guild will do our best to live up to your expectations, Rei. …Though, judging by the way things are going, the dismantling might actually be finished sooner than expected.”

As he said this, the guild official’s gaze turned toward the people carrying out the demolition.

There, just as the people who had participated in the demolition yesterday were working with enthusiasm, the residents of the slums were working even harder.

At first, the slum dwellers seemed to struggle with the demolition due to the differences between the kobolds and the Giganto Turtles, but they gradually got the hang of it.

In this case, the fact that many of the newcomers were children whom Suu-chi trusted played a role.

Because they were children, they adapted to the situation quickly.

Furthermore, many of the adults who had come possessed demolition skills, and while not quite as quickly as the children, they were still getting the hang of it at a decent pace.

There are fifty people who can be considered immediately effective.

That is, of course, the manpower needed to increase the dismantling speed of the Giganto Turtles.

Above all, what Rei can look forward to is that, while there were only about fifty people today, Suu-chi mentioned that the numbers might increase starting tomorrow.

Many of them were supposed to come today, but had various errands to run or were hesitating to join because they didn’t trust the pay—at least from the perspective of slum dwellers—so they were waiting to see how things played out. However, Rei had heard that even those people might join the dismantling efforts starting tomorrow.

Naturally, since he’d made it clear that Suu-chi’s group would face penalties if those people pulled any funny business, Rei believed Suu-chi would only bring people she could trust.

(That said… if the slum dwellers start making an unusually large profit, it’s only natural that residents from other slums will ask where they earned it. Depending on the situation, there might even be some who try to steal the money Suu-chi and the others have earned. …Though I doubt they’d do something like that if they belong to a somewhat organized group.)

To the underground organizations operating in Gilm’s slums, Rei is nothing short of a plague.

So far, several organizations that have gotten involved with Rei have either been wiped out… or, if not that far, have suffered significant damage.

And as for who’s behind the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle—well, if you’re even slightly interested in gathering information, you could probably find that out.

After all, Rei isn’t exactly hiding his name when soliciting requests for dismantling jobs.

Under these circumstances, attacking Suu-chi and her companions involved in dismantling the Giganto Turtle would be interpreted as a declaration of war against Rei.

I can’t imagine anyone could win in a head-on battle against Rei, and if Rei were to resort to guerrilla-style tactics alongside Set, the situation would become even more unmanageable.

When weighing the risks and rewards, the risks are overwhelmingly greater.

Any high-risk, low-return move is something the underground organization would want to avoid at all costs.

Actually, when it comes to opposing Rei, it might not even be low return—it might be no return at all.

In any case, for those belonging to the underground organization, they would never do anything to antagonize Rei for such reasons.

……Even if they belong to an organization, low-ranking members might act recklessly without considering such circumstances.

(Come to think of it, the ones more likely to attack Suu-chi and the others are probably the ones who aren’t part of any organization and haven’t even bothered to gather proper intelligence. …I should probably warn them to be careful about that. A few of them seem to possess a fair amount of strength, though.)

Among the people Suu-chi brought along, there are likely some with backgrounds as former adventurers.

Rei could tell that much just from the way they moved.

But since there aren’t that many of them, I can’t possibly protect all of Suu-chi’s companions.

(Ideally, I’d like them to stay in one place so I can protect everyone, but with nearly fifty people… no, it’ll be even more than that, so there’s no place big enough to accommodate that many.)

Or perhaps, if this had been before the expansion work, it might have been manageable.

However, many of the rooms are currently occupied by people who came for the expansion work, so there’s no room to spare.

That said, since it’s winter, some people have left Gilm, so we could probably manage if it were just a small group.

“We could manage, you say?”

“Huh? Is something wrong?”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s mutter, a guild staff member approached him.

Rei explained the anxiety he was feeling to the guild staff member.

“Ah… I see. Now that you mention it, that does sound a bit difficult. Hmm… Should we try to make some arrangements here? With this many people… no, actually, I do know of a place that can accommodate even more. Of course, we can’t use it without the Guild Master’s permission.”

Hearing the guild clerk’s words, Rei immediately understood what he was referring to.

It’s probably the guild’s massive warehouse, he thought.
