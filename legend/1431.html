Rei kicked down the lower half of the door as he entered the room. What he saw was a man smashing the door down with a shield.

There were also men armed with longswords, daggers, battle-axes, and clubs, each fighting alone.

The man who had shot an arrow when Rei severed the door was present, but he had already lost his will to fight after the earlier exchange.

And behind the armed men, hiding as if to conceal himself, stood another man.

A nobleman of average height and build, his features reasonably well-proportioned as befitted his status.

But seeing the lustful glint clouding his eyes, it was impossible to feel any respect or admiration for him.

"Well, it seems everyone who remained is here. ...This was unexpected."

"Wh-What do you mean?! You scoundrel!"

The nobleman—Lerdact—heard Rei's mutter and shouted in frustration and anger.

From Lerdact's perspective, it was as if Rei had suddenly attacked him.

To make matters worse, the barrier protecting the town had been destroyed, and many of his hired mercenaries had fled in the face of Rei and... the adventurer known by the alias Crimson.

Once they'd encountered the intruder and fled, they'd never return to serve Lerdact again.

Even if they did return, Lerdact had no intention of re-hiring them.

In fact, they would likely impose some form of punishment for fleeing in the face of the intruders.

In any case, there was no doubt he harbored intense fury and hatred toward Rei for causing such a situation. But...

"Huh? What are you talking about? You were the one who started this, weren't you? Did you really think it was perfectly fine for you to attack, but you never expected to be attacked yourself? Surely you weren't thinking something that stupid?"

Lerdact's face twisted in rage at Rei's earnest question, flushing crimson.

Rei's earnest demeanor only fueled his rage further.

Seeing Lerdact like this, Rei slung the Death Scythe over his shoulder and sighed in exasperation.

"Seems you genuinely didn't expect retaliation. ...The Lerdact family, a minor noble house, picked a fight with Larks, the central figure of the Neutral Faction? I don't understand why you thought you'd get away with it."

"Shut up! What do I care about such a weak faction?! If anything, it's presumptuous of a minor faction like theirs to be entrusted with a crucial stronghold like Gilm!"

Irritation toward Daskar and panic from being attacked—the mixture of these emotions caused such a cry to escape Lerdact's mouth.

Shouting in such a situation meant he'd likely felt this way for a long time.

But until now, he hadn't been able to voice it.

Seeing Reldaxt like this, Rei opened his mouth in exasperation.

"The Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction are currently strengthening their ties. Do you really think someone like you—some nobly insignificant nobody within the Noble Faction—can get away with interfering? Honestly, even without me getting involved, I bet the Noble Faction's higher-ups would have already taken action."

"Don't talk nonsense!"

Lerdact shouted, unable to believe Rei's words.

"Well, if that's what you think, fine. ...But Gilm can only be governed because it's Daskar-sama, you understand? The Noble Faction... Honestly, even if you were put in charge of Gilm, you'd probably last less than a month."

Lerdact imposed such heavy taxes on his own territory.

If he were put in charge of Gilm... Gilm, the sole frontier of the Mireana Kingdom, precisely because of which vast sums of money flow in... Rei could easily imagine what actions Lerdact would take.

He'd raise taxes and squeeze every last drop out of adventurers and merchants.

If he did such a thing, it was obvious what the adventurers would do, regardless of the merchants.

If they simply grew weary of Gilm and left, that would be the better outcome.

But most of those gathered in Gilm were skilled adventurers.

Besides Rei, there were several others with nicknames, and the same went for Rank A adventurers.

Would such individuals, forced into submission from the top down, meekly obey or simply leave Gilm?

Even Rei knew the answer was no.

What would such people do...? The quickest solution would likely be force.

If things went badly, it wouldn't be surprising if the Gilm and the adventurers ended up opposing each other internally, plunging the Gilm into civil war.

And if civil war broke out, the future where Lerdact emerged victorious was practically nonexistent.

But that was something only Rei, who knew the Gilm's true nature, could understand. To Lerdact, who only knew the Gilm through hearsay, Rei's words were utterly unacceptable.

"Don't... you dare mock me! You think I couldn't even manage a town of that caliber?!"

"Ah. The problem is, you're fundamentally mistaken. The moment you start thinking of Gilm as an ordinary town, you're already beyond help."

"Huh!? ...Do it! Kill this man! Kill him!"

Unable to tolerate Rei's words any longer, Lerdact screamed, her face flushing redder than before.

But... as for those around her who heard those words, their will to fight had already been half-crushed.

Facing Rei, known by the Crimson epithet, they didn't believe they could do anything, no matter how confident they were in their own abilities.

If there had been someone present who knew nothing about Rei, the situation might have been different.

Unfortunately, everyone in this room possessed a certain level of skill and, naturally, had put considerable effort into gathering information.

Even if they were somewhat out of the loop, there was no one here who didn't know about the adventurer known as Crimson.

The only chance they had was a surprise attack the moment they entered the room... but that too was easily thwarted when their arrows were sliced away.

"Well then, your employer says this... What do you say? I'm perfectly fine with it if you want to go through with it."

Rei flashed a small smile as he turned his gaze toward them, but the guards averted their eyes, refusing to meet his stare.

Yet, they didn't move away from Lerdact, seeming to agonize over what to do.

They knew they couldn't win if they fought, but simply abandoning Lerdact to his fate would leave a bad taste in their mouths... or so it seemed.

Noticing this, Rei finally spoke, Death Scythe still slung over his shoulder.

"The client who commissioned this job ordered me not to kill Lerdact. At the very least, I guarantee he'll be kept alive."

Those words were the decisive blow.

A hollow echo reverberated around them.

Glancing in that direction, he saw the man holding the dagger release his grip on the weapon, letting it fall to the floor.

The meaning behind that action was clear to anyone watching.

And, as if it were only natural, Lerdact understood the meaning behind that action, his face flushing crimson as he shouted.

"Don't you dare! You lot are hired by me! No matter what enemy you face, fight! Fight and inflict even a single wound before you die! My life is worth more than yours, worth no more than a single copper coin!"

Irritation, anger, panic, and fear.

These emotions mingled, causing words that could only be called abuse to spill from Lerdact's mouth.

They were indeed abusive words, but they were also Lerdact's true feelings.

Those around him understood this... and soon, clang, clang, the sound echoed through the room one after another.

Following the first man, the others each dropped their weapons to the floor.

If they merely wished to show they weren't resisting, they could have simply sheathed their longswords. But this time, they needed to demonstrate their non-hostility toward Rei in an almost blatant manner.

Noticing the reactions of those around him, Lerdact's face flushed crimson as he opened his mouth to speak... and in the next instant, his eyes rolled back and he collapsed to the floor.

"Huh?"

Who uttered that sound?

No one could have predicted that someone would lose consciousness like this while angry.

Rei was just as surprised.

"Um... well, you know. You guys are surrendering, right? Well, if you want to fulfill your duty to Lerdact, I could fight you..."

Rei said this, but he asked it in a somewhat deflated state now.

He'd been thinking, "Let's do this!" but he certainly hadn't imagined he'd get so excited he'd just pass out.

"No, I didn't mean to... Hey?"

"Yeah, that's right."

Everyone nodded in response to the man with the longsword.

They absolutely refused to risk their lives fighting a powerful opponent like Rei for someone who had so clearly treated them like disposable pawns.

"I see. Then go. If you stay here, it'll only cause trouble."

Nodding in unison at his words, they picked up the weapons they'd dropped on the floor moments before and quickly exited the room.

(Well, if these guys were hired by Lerdact and doing whatever they pleased, they might get into serious trouble if the rebels found them... but that's none of my concern.)

However, from what Rei had seen, the people who had been here until just now didn't seem to have such tyrannical personalities.

Of course, he couldn't say they didn't feel bad about abandoning Lerdact so easily after being treated like disposable pawns... Still, they didn't strike him as the kind of people the rebels had described.

"Right then,"

muttering, Rei approached Lerdact, who had lost consciousness in a fit of rage, making a rather embarrassing spectacle of himself.

For a moment, he wondered if he was just pretending to be unconscious, but even to Rei, it looked like he'd genuinely passed out.

"Wake up, come on."

He called out, giving Lerdact a light kick in the side.

"Guh!"

He hadn't kicked that hard, but it was apparently enough pain for Lerdact. He opened his eyes, groaning.

He looked around... saw Rei, and remembered what had happened before he lost consciousness.

"What?! Where did you take the others?!"

"Well, where could they be? Regardless of the reason, there's no one else here but you now. Given that, I don't think the outcome awaiting you will change..."

"Guh!"

The blow, delivered with the stone tip of the Death Scythe scooping upward, easily sent Lerdact flying.

He crashed into a painting hanging on the wall, shattering it as he fell to the floor.

"Wh-what... What are you doing to me?!"

Normally, a strike from Rei... especially one using the Death Scythe, would have easily knocked Lerdact unconscious.

That it didn't was because Rei had held back.

Whether Lerdact realized this or not,

Regardless, he screamed in frustration at the unrelenting harm inflicted upon him.

If this were someone living in the Lerdact County, they would likely cower at Lerdact's angry shout.

But standing before Lerdact was Rei.

Lerdact knew that too. He knew it, but... all Lerdact could manage now was to shout, trying desperately not to let his fear show on his face.

Facing such a Relduct, Rei aimed the Death Scythe... this time not the pommel, but the blade itself.

A massive blade, likely reaching a meter in length.

Moreover, the Death Scythe was a scythe, a weapon so monstrous compared to a normal longsword that it could be called a weapon of the abnormal.

Precisely because it was monstrous, Lerdact, facing that blade, was terrified.

"What? If you think about what you did to Gilm, you should know what's coming next, shouldn't you?"

"What did I do?!"

"...You don't know what your mercenaries were doing?"

"Of course not! I know nothing! Besides, are you serious!? If you go through with this, you'll definitely regret it! I belong to the Noble Faction, after all!"

At those words, Rei let out a sigh of exasperation.

"Well, yes. You may belong to the Noble Faction."

"Then..."

"But so what?"

"...What?"

Rei muttered in exasperation, then in the next instant, he swung Death Scythe with a sharp flash.

And... with that single blow, Lerdact's right arm was severed at the shoulder, spinning through the air.

"Even if you belong to the Noble Faction, there's no way you could be of any use now, even if you survive."

Perhaps because the severed stump was so cleanly cut.

As Rei spoke, blood finally spurted from Lerdact's right shoulder.

"Gya... Gyaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!"

His right arm had been severed.

Leldact's scream, realizing this, echoed throughout the area.
