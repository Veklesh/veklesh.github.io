The moment Rei introduced himself, everyone present stirred with emotion.

Until now, they had been wary of him as an unexpected intruder, but they hadn't paid much attention to who he was.

But when Rei declared his name, they instantly recognized the figure before them as the adventurer known by the epithet Crimson.

And Rei was recognized as an adventurer specialized in combat.

...Indeed, he earned his nickname through his exploits in the war against the Bestir Empire, so that perception was not entirely mistaken.

Though, to be fair, he wasn't solely specialized in combat—he also possessed transport abilities using his item box and investigative skills utilizing Set's keen senses...

Regardless, the defenders of the lord's manor where Lerdact resided understood that the figure before them was utterly beyond their capabilities.

"Well then,"

Rei murmured briefly, shifting his gaze. Almost everyone present trembled, their faces turning pale.

They knew. They knew what would happen if they tried to interfere with Rei's actions.

Information about Rei... about Crimson, was, of course, known to many present.

It was said he wielded his power without mercy, even against nobles.

Given such a person as their opponent, it was only natural to expect that even soldiers serving nobles would face the merciless swing of the large scythe in his hand.

...After all, anyone who forcibly breaks through the city's barrier in the middle of the night to storm the lord's mansion clearly had no hesitation about doing so.

"The reason I came here, as I said before, is revenge against the person Lerdact attacked. ...So, only those who want to interfere and fight me should stay. Everyone else, get out of here. I won't chase you if you leave now."

No sooner had he spoken than most of those around him turned on their heels and fled.

They likely realized all too well what fate awaited them if they fought Rei.

Moreover, the fact that they were ultimately just hired by Lerdact and held no loyalty to him played a significant role.

Had this been in Gilm, even knowing they couldn't defeat him, many adventurers bearing titles would have stood their ground against the lord's mansion being attacked.

Compared to Gilm's soldiers, Lerdact's troops would retreat immediately if they felt they couldn't win.

This starkly revealed what might be called the difference in character between Daskar and Lerdact.

But the fact that so many fled meant, conversely, that some had stayed behind...

"Is it really necessary to risk your lives like this against Lerdact?"

At Rei's words, one of those who remained spoke up, sword drawn.

"That commoner is irrelevant. I simply don't want to miss the chance to spar with a strong opponent like you."

"Ah... I see. So you're that type."

Not someone with deep loyalty to Lerdact, but simply someone who wanted to fight a strong opponent.

The same sort as Vihera.

...Though, while Vihera also enjoys combat, that doesn't mean she'd be hired by someone like Lerdact. The answer to that is no.

(The others... are different in various ways, I suppose.)

I'd assumed everyone here loved combat, but of the three remaining, only one was glaring at Rei with a burning gaze.

Why the other two? Rei wondered for a moment, but seeing the collars around their necks, he understood immediately.

(Slaves?)

At first glance, they didn't seem like particularly strong opponents.

Well, they had physiques that could probably handle ordinary people, but to Rei's eyes, that just meant they were easy targets with large areas to aim for.

They probably knew they couldn't beat Rei themselves... but with those slave collars, they couldn't disobey their master's orders.

"Look at me, Crimson! Look at me!"

Shouting this, the man with the longsword lunges straight at Rei.

True to his reputation as a battle maniac, his lunge was formidable.

It was good enough to hold its own even among adventurers working in Gilm.

(If he loves combat so much, he'd never lack opponents in the frontier...)

Thinking this, Rei swung the Twilight Spear in his left hand to meet the charging man.

The sound of air being sliced through echoed, followed by the thud of the man being blown away.

His lunge was swift, and he possessed the skill to operate in Gilm... but conversely, that was all he had.

Opposing Rei was far beyond the man's capabilities.

Blown away, he slammed into a nearby tree and lost consciousness.

Glancing at Rei, Rei then turned his attention to the two slaves who were now aiming spears at him.

Bound by their collars to obey Lerdact's commands, the two slaves' eyes held fear, yet they couldn't move from their positions.

It was obvious from the exchange just now that there was no way they could possibly defeat Rei on their own.

The man Rei had just blown away was someone the two slaves couldn't even scratch, even if they attacked together.

What could they possibly do against Rei, who had literally kicked such an opponent aside?

"...Don't worry. I'll knock him unconscious right away."

Rei didn't particularly enjoy attacking weaker opponents for fun.

Well, he didn't dislike crushing overconfident opponents, but he didn't have a taste for attacking someone like the one before him now, someone whose eyes pleaded for mercy.

Whether they believed Rei's words or not, the two slaves let their guard down for an instant... and the next moment, the pommel of the Twilight Spear pierced their solar plexus, knocking them unconscious.

Having thus rendered all three remaining individuals unconscious, Rei stroked the approaching Set as a thought suddenly occurred to him.

"Ah, if I knocked everyone out, I won't be able to ask where they stashed the magic items."

"Gururuu"

Set let out a comforting growl, gently rubbing his face against Rei.

Moved by Set's thoughtfulness, Rei felt happy and stroked Set's body even more gently than before.

An adventurer playing with his familiar.

Just hearing that description, it would be a rather heartwarming scene. ...If the unconscious men weren't lying around, that is.

After playing with Set for several minutes, Rei shifted her focus and looked toward the lord's mansion.

Several people in the courtyard were watching Rei, but they were clearly civil officials.

Unlike the three currently unconscious, they showed no sign of attacking Rei.

"I could ask those guys over there..."

"Hii!"

The man must have sensed his gaze meet Rei's. He immediately ran off, and the others followed suit, scattering away.

(Would asking those bureaucrats have been quicker?)

Rei briefly considered it, but judging by their current state, he concluded they were too terrified to speak properly.

"Alright, Set. I'm going to meet with Lerdact. You wait here. Basically, don't let anyone inside. Ah, but if the rebels come, let them in."

"GRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set growled, "Leave it to me!"

Giving Set a light pat, Rei stowed his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear into the Misty Ring before entering the building.

He did so by forcefully breaking through a window.

Inside, Rei saw figures that appeared to be clerks running away.

"If only someone could guide me to Relduct's room, that'd be a big help. ...Guess I'll have to find it myself."

It was his first time here, but he had already confirmed the building's layout from above.

And someone with such a strong desire to amass wealth and such a strong need for self-aggrandizement would naturally arrange their office, private quarters, or whatever room they should normally occupy in the central part of the building.

Rei made his judgment and headed toward the center of the building.

"Halt! You're an intruder!"

Occasionally, someone shouting this would appear, only to be knocked unconscious by a punch from Rei, or to fall to the floor, moaning in pain from the arrowheads created by Nebra's eyes.

While Rei generally preferred long-range weapons, he was by no means inept at throwing or hand-to-hand combat using Nebula's Eye.

...Though it should be noted, Nebula's Eye is a magic item that creates projectiles using magic power.

Such attacks are effective at inflicting pain, but they lack the power to knock someone unconscious.

In that sense, it could be said that Rei's chosen method of attack was inappropriate for this situation.

Rei, laying waste to those who tried to stop her, knocking them unconscious one after another, eventually arrived before a single door.

It was visibly different from the other doors.

Both in size and appearance.

While it was several grades below Daskar's office door, it was undoubtedly the most luxurious door in the entire mansion.

(No guard, or gatekeeper? ...or anything like that. The escort... must be inside the room, I suppose.)

From within the room, he could sense a presence wary of him.

They likely judged it better to concentrate their forces on a single point rather than risk having them picked off one by one by deploying them carelessly.

Holding that thought... and simultaneously, if they understood that the intruder was Rei, they might have also considered that sending soldiers recklessly would only result in her escaping.

After all, many who recognized the opponent as Rei had fled, so that was understandable.

"Well. I can't stay like this forever... and keeping them waiting isn't exactly polite either. ...Though, come to think of it, since I'm barging in like this, there's no need to worry about politeness at all."

Muttering to himself, Rei knocked on the door before him.

"Reldact, if you're there, come out quietly. It'll be less painful that way."

He called out through the door, but as expected, there was no reply.

But considering the palpable anger emanating from the other side had intensified, it was clear Rei's words hadn't gone unheard.

"Looks like you have no intention of coming out. ...Then—"

He drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and swung it.

Given it was such an ornate door, it was naturally built sturdy... but without channeling magic, relying solely on the Death Scythe's weight, sharpness, and Rei's skill, the door was cleanly severed, revealing the room's interior.

"Whoa!"

The instant the room became visible, as if waiting for that exact moment, an arrow pierced the spot where the door had been mere seconds before.

It was a strike that seemed to exploit a split-second lapse in focus, but the arrow was easily deflected by the Death Scythe.

Though it was a massive weapon—its shaft two meters long and its blade one meter—Rei showed no sign of exerting any effort in wielding it.

In fact, one of the Death Scythe's abilities was to make its weight imperceptible to Rei and Set.

Thanks to that, Rei could easily wield the Death Scythe, which weighed around 100 kg.

"What!?"

Even though he knew the basic facts about Rei, he clearly hadn't grasped the details of his abilities... let alone the capabilities of the magic item he possessed.

A gasp of astonishment escaped the man who had just fired the arrow.

"I've confirmed hostile action. No need to hold back now."

Even as he said this, Rei had been explicitly instructed by Daskar to avoid killing anyone on this mission if possible.

To be precise, Daskar had specifically said not to kill Lerdact, but Rei understood that meant the others too.

That said, while Rei didn't hesitate to kill if justified, he didn't particularly relish it either.

If it could be avoided, that was fine by him.

"What!? Kill him! Kill that bastard! Whoever takes Rei down will get whatever reward they want!"

Even Rei knew whose voice that was coming from inside the room.

Even without having met them in person, he knew exactly who would say something like this.

(Not using my alias, Crimson, but specifically calling me by name... It's probably to make sure the ones inside aren't afraid of me, huh?)

Even so, those words seemed sufficient to boost the morale of those inside the room, as a roar like a battle cry erupted from within.

Only the man who had shot an arrow at Rei turned pale, his face etched with despair, utterly convinced he could never defeat her.

"Well then... time for the finishing touch."

Muttering, Rei kicked aside the remaining lower half of the door's wreckage and stepped into the room.
