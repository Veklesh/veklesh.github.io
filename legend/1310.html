After finishing the explanation of the incident at the security guard post, several guards immediately headed for Seralis's infirmary.

They were going to retrieve the victim of this incident, Azimov.

If he had truly been injured, they wouldn't have been able to move him.

Fortunately, however, Azimov's wounds had healed thanks to the potion Rei had given him, and he was at least no longer in a life-threatening condition.

It was precisely because they heard this that the guards went to retrieve Azimov as a precaution.

"So, Marina. Do you know where Rei went?"

According to Marina's explanation, he had gone after the scent with Set, Vihera, and Byune, but without knowing exactly where they went, there was nothing to be done.

Given Rei's current state of furious rage, if things went badly, part of Gilm could be... no, there was even a risk that all of Gilm could be destroyed.

As a guard, and as someone who loved Gilm, I absolutely could not allow that to happen.

I had to find a way to calm Rei down.

Driven by that thought, I asked Marina, but she silently shook her head.

"I'm sorry, but right after Rei and the others left, I took Azimov to the clinic. I don't know where they went."

At this point, the guards hadn't yet received the information that Rei had charged into the Noble District.

Well, to be precise, the guards in the Noble Quarter had received information from adventurers, but that information hadn't reached this guard post yet.

Had they known, they would have immediately gathered other guards and headed for the Noble Quarter.

...Though, considering it might cause them to become enemies with Rei, it wasn't a decision to be made lightly.

"I see. ...But I can't imagine anyone in this guild being reckless enough to deliberately steal Rei's magic item. ...Well, that's what I'd normally say."

The annual tradition of various troubles caused by new adventurers—new to Gilm, that is.

It wouldn't be surprising if some believed they could make easy money here in this remote Gilm.

And if someone foolish enough to entertain such thoughts happened to possess mediocre strength... well, the outcome was obvious.

Usually, they get a baptism by fire from Gilm's adventurers, but that's not always the case.

"But would an adventurer who just joined Gilm really target Azimov?"

He's a skilled alchemist, so they'd probably want to avoid messing with him unnecessarily.

"No, but since few people live near Azimov's residence, depending on the situation..."

Azimov's house, located in a sparsely populated area.

Thinking about it that way, it wouldn't be strange for someone new to Gilm, unaware of the circumstances, to attempt such a thing. It wouldn't be strange, but...

"Considering it a random crime, there are several inconsistencies. To begin with, Azimov rarely leaves his home, right? Why would anyone think such an Azimov is an alchemist?"

"Couldn't it just be a coincidence?"

"So they didn't attack Azimov because he was an alchemist, but rather, attacking Azimov just happened to make him an alchemist? ...Well, I suppose that possibility isn't entirely out of the question."

Even as she said this, it was clear Marina wasn't convinced by the guard's explanation.

By chance, someone found Azimov's house—a location perfectly suited for an attack—and when they attacked it, by chance Azimov was an alchemist, by chance he was in his lab surrounded by numerous magic items, and by chance they found Rei's Sleipnir Boots among them... and by chance, they noticed Rei entering the house and fled.

How many coincidences would have to align for that to happen?

One or two coincidences might be plausible, but when this many align, it's hard to call it mere coincidence anymore.

"You're right. Thinking about it, that might be true. ...Anyway, Marina, what are you going to do now?"

Marina paused, considering the guard's words, who had judged it difficult to dismiss the matter as mere coincidence.

She could wait at the guard post, but she preferred to investigate this matter herself if possible.

...Had she been in the position of Guildmaster, it might have been problematic, but fortunately, Marina was currently just a common adventurer.

Acting according to her own judgment posed no problem whatsoever.

But at the same time, it was also true that Rei was acting purely on impulse.

Probably... no, definitely, this incident would cause a commotion that would reach the guards.

"I'll look into it myself a little too. But if anything happens, could you let me know? If Rei gets out of hand, someone will need to calm him down, right?"

"That... yes."

Normally, it's the guards' job to handle problems when someone causes trouble.

In fact, since spring, the guards have suppressed many disturbances... most caused by adventurers newly arrived in Gilm.

...The reason I say "most" is because often, other adventurers had already quelled the disturbances before the guards got there.

That said, it wasn't just the newcomers; the adventurers who had been in Gilm from the start had caused their fair share of disturbances too.

In any case, the guards were a group capable of handling most adventurers. But that was only true for 'most' adventurers.

When it came to high-rank adventurers... especially someone like Rei, who bore a nickname, suppression was simply impossible.

If they truly intended to subdue Rei, they would need to gather other high-rank adventurers or those with nicknames.

Doing something like that would inevitably spark a disturbance involving the entire Gilm, putting the Gilm itself at risk of dissolution.

To prevent that, someone capable of calming Rei was needed.

In that regard, Marina was undoubtedly one of Gilm's most prominent figures.

...The top-tier person to calm Rei? The guard flashed a slightly awkward expression for a moment, but quickly suppressed it and bowed to Marina.

"I'll contact you immediately if I learn anything."

"Is that so? Well then..."

Marina murmured at the guard's words and chanted a short incantation.

The next instant, a mass of water appeared in the room where Marina and the others stood.

This mass of water was not much different from the one used to transport Azimov.

However, while the one used to transport Azimov was large enough to carry a person, this new mass was only fist-sized.

"Um, what's this...?"

The guard asked, looking confused at the suddenly appearing mass of water.

Still, he didn't treat the water mass with caution, likely because he trusted Marina.

Having served as Guildmaster for so many years was more than enough to inspire strong trust in Marina.

"It's something created with spirit magic. Once you know where Rei is, crush this water mass. That way, I'll receive the signal too."

"...This is quite convenient."

While it wasn't a direct means of communication between them, within Elgin's limited communication options, using water for signaling was likely deemed highly useful.

But Marina shook her head at the guard.

"Sorry, but it's not that convenient, you know? First, the range isn't very wide. It turns back into plain water after a few hours... And above all, you need to be quite skilled with spirit magic to create it."

Since there weren't that many people who could use spirit magic, it was impossible to create this water block on a constant basis.

"I see. That's unfortunate, but I suppose it can't be helped."

The guard, not in a position to press Marina further, quickly gave up.

...Still, while spirit mages were few, Marina wasn't the only one.

She had been thinking about whether she could somehow secure another spirit mage.

"Well then, I'll be counting on you."

"Ah, yes. Understood. I'll contact you immediately if I find out where Rei is."

After exchanging a few brief words, Marina left the guard post.

"...Right then. The problem is how to find clues."

A spring-like blue sky and warm air. Just looking at that, it wouldn't be strange if he was napping somewhere in the shade.

Set liked napping, and she'd seen him and Rei join in on those naps many times before.

But... even so, she couldn't imagine Rei doing something like that in the current situation.

Given how furious Rei had been, finding the person who attacked Azimov would undoubtedly mean they'd be made an example of.

(I just hope he doesn't kill them...)

The reason I think that is because I need someone to hear the details of this incident.

The fact that Marina wasn't that close to Azimov probably played a big part too.

I do like Azimov in his own way—he has almost no interest in anything besides alchemy and never hits on me, even when he sees me.

Thinking such thoughts, she walked through the city streets.

...Right now, Marina didn't seem to be searching for anything specific; she just looked like she was out for a pure stroll.

About ten minutes after leaving the station... After that much time had passed, Marina glanced around.

(It wouldn't be strange if something happened soon...)

Thinking this as she scanned her surroundings, Marina noticed someone watching her.

Her stunning beauty and the revealing party dress drew many eyes toward Marina.

Men and women, young and old alike.

But the gaze fixed on Marina now was entirely different from those.

Following the gaze back to its source, she found an ordinary man standing there, devoid of any distinguishing features.

He was the very embodiment of the words "ordinary" and "average."

So ordinary, in fact, that you might not even notice him if you passed him by. That's the kind of person he seemed.

Yet, this very man must have noticed Marina's gaze. He gave a slight bow, then meaningfully shifted his gaze away toward an empty space.

Naturally, Marina eyed him suspiciously for a moment, but she had no choice but to follow him.

Given Marina's striking beauty and flashy attire, those around her followed her with their eyes... but ultimately, they did nothing more. Marina simply continued walking toward the empty area.

"...Well, since you've shown me the way, that should be enough. Do you know anything about this matter?"

"I only relay the information my master has given me. Is that acceptable?"

The man at the end of Marina's gaze turned around and stated it in a flat tone... a voice devoid of any emotion whatsoever.

His features were utterly ordinary, average, and unremarkable, yet that very ordinariness made him stand out to Marina as a defining characteristic.

"Yes, that's fine. I need any clue I can get right now, and it's my job to verify whether that information is genuine."

"I see. ...Then, to get straight to the point. This incident was orchestrated by someone trying to pit Rei against Azoth Trading Company."

"...Azoth Trading Company?"

Given her position as Guildmaster, Marina naturally knew about the previous incident where Rei clashed with the Azoth Trading Company.

She also knew it was part of the Bestir Empire's scheme, resulting in the capture of Azoth Trading Company's chairman at the time, and that his younger brother, Galahad, was now the chairman.

And that Galahad becoming head of Azoth Trading Company had led to a restoration of his relationship with Rei.

"Who on earth would do such a thing? And does whoever plotted this even know what kind of man Rei is? If they think they can play him like a puppet, they'll regret it."

Marina murmured with a voice thick with conviction, but the man who heard her showed no particular sign of being shaken.

It was as if he didn't care either way how this matter turned out.

"Unfortunately, I haven't been briefed on the details either. But since it's someone who knows Rei, I suppose it's not a problem."

"...I hope so. And that's all the information you can share with us?"

"Yes. At the very least, this incident was not orchestrated by the Azoth Trading Company."

"You're strangely protective of Azoth Trading Company, aren't you?"

"I can't say anything more. What you think about it is entirely up to you."

With that, the man turned his back on Marina and walked away.

Marina considered calling out to his retreating back, but then she noticed the water block she'd left behind in the office had been crushed. She gave up on saying anything more.
