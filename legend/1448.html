"Heh heh, I can just picture Rei looking surprised."

The one who spoke was a woman with golden hair that looked as if the very sunlight of midsummer had transformed into strands.

Running her fingers through her vertically rolled hair, Elena smiled as she turned her gaze to the view outside the room's window.

Seeing Elena like this, the woman standing nearby opened her mouth with a troubled expression.

"Lady Elena, why didn't you contact Rei? If you had, I believe you could have met him immediately upon arriving in Gilm."

"Hmm, it seems you, Ara, are also unfamiliar with the subtleties between men and women."

The woman addressed so bluntly—Ara—touched the battle axe strapped to her back and wore a slightly sulky expression.

In truth, Ara understood herself that she was indeed clueless about the subtleties between men and women.

Elena had once been similarly clueless, but now she had Rei.

In that sense, it wouldn't be strange for Ara to have a steady partner soon too... No, in this world of Elgin where many married in their mid-teens, she was at an age where she could be called past her prime for marriage.

Especially since Ara held the position of Elena's confidante, yet was also the third daughter of the Scarley Count's household.

Despite that, not only was she unmarried, she didn't even have a fiancé.

...Not that this meant Ara was unpopular.

If anything, judging purely by popularity, it wouldn't have been strange for Ara to receive numerous marriage proposals from nobles.

The Scarley family held considerable influence within the Noble Faction.

Indeed, even the Count of Lerdact family, recently attacked by Rei, holds nowhere near the same level of influence despite sharing the same title.

Despite this, the fact that Ara still has no fiancé or lover of any sort is likely because she desires to be with Elena.

For Ara, who is utterly devoted to Elena, matters of her own romance... let alone marriage, hold some interest, but ultimately, that's the extent of it.

If Ara were the Scarley family's only child, or their eldest daughter, things might be somewhat different.

Fortunately, Ara is the third daughter.

With brothers ahead of her, inheriting the Scarley estate is not a concern, nor are political marriages a worry for the current Scarley family.

In fact, keeping Ara close to Elena served the Scarley family's interests far better... That alignment of father and daughter's interests had led to the current situation.

Of course, given that Ara holds the important position of Captain of Elena's Guard, she couldn't simply marry off easily.

In any case, while her lack of interest in romance was the primary reason, it was also an undeniable fact that Ara was unfamiliar with the subtleties of male-female relationships due to various other factors.

"And yet, Lady Elena, it seems your relationship with Rei hasn't progressed much either, despite all the talk?"

As if mounting a counterattack, Ara fired back at Elena.

That counterattack wasn't quite fatal for Elena... but it certainly dealt significant damage.

In truth, while she didn't speak with Rei every single day, she did converse with him fairly regularly through their Paired Orbs.

But ultimately, all she could do through the Paired Orb was talk... She couldn't touch the one she cherished.

Especially since, as far as Elena knew, there were women like Marina and Vihera around Rei.

They were discussing the three of them marrying Rei... but even so, the fact that she alone was separated from them meant she couldn't help but feel something.

"...That's why I came all the way to Gilm, isn't it?"

She stated this, reaching for the tea on the table, savoring a sip or two... Eventually, Elena turned her gaze toward the room's door.

Ara, who understood all too well what this meant, flipped a switch within herself. She shifted from being Elena's childhood friend to being the Captain of Elena's Guard Knights.

A dozen seconds later... a knock sounded at the door. Elena gestured for him to enter, and Daskar appeared.

Under normal circumstances, detecting Daskar's arrival this quickly would be impossible.

But for Elena, who had inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone and risen to a rank beyond that of a human, it was no difficult feat.

"My lady Elena, I apologize for keeping you waiting. I would have come sooner if possible... but I've had a lot of urgent matters to attend to."

Daskar certainly didn't look like someone skilled with paperwork—he was more the type you'd call a warrior or knight. Yet, as the lord of Gilm... and as a central figure in the Neutral Faction, he managed such tasks reasonably well.

In fact, Elena knew that despite appearances, he wasn't actually bad at that sort of work.

Even for Daskar, now that the expansion of Gilm had begun in earnest, it was becoming difficult to handle all his duties.

Elena understood this well, which was precisely why she shook her head, not particularly concerned.

"I understand Lord Daskar is busy. In fact, I feel rather bad about coming at this time."

"...No, we're absolutely delighted to have you. With Lady Elena at Gilm, there won't be anyone foolish enough to do something that would disgrace the Duke of Kerebel family."

The incident with the Count of Lerdact family had led to Elena's dispatch this time.

To be precise, when the discussion arose among the Noble Faction to send someone to apologize for the Lerdact incident and ensure other noble factions didn't meddle unnecessarily, Elena volunteered herself.

Indeed, several others had considered going to Gilm, but Elena's determination was so overwhelming that they all promptly withdrew their requests upon seeing her resolve.

Of course, Elena volunteered to go to Gilm in such a forceful manner partly to prevent the deterioration of relations between the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction, with whom she had recently been building a good relationship... but the bigger reason was undoubtedly Rei.

The relationship between Rei and Elena was already something close to an open secret even among the Noble Faction.

Many harbored bitter feelings about it, but Elena was a woman who had turned down countless suitors—dozens, hundreds, perhaps even over a thousand.

Among the nobles, there was a sense that even now, trying to bring themselves... or perhaps their brothers or sons closer to Elena would likely be futile.

Of course, that doesn't mean everyone accepts Rei and Elena's relationship.

In fact, among the Noble Faction, those who did approve were likely in the minority.

"Hearing you say that is a relief. ...This is a letter from my father."

Saying this, Elena handed Daskar a letter bearing the wax seal of the Duke of Kerebel.

Daskar took it, opened the envelope, and began reading the letter.

Several minutes passed. Finally, having finished reading, Daskar addressed Elena, unable to hide his rare look of surprise.

"Well... I've read the letter... Are you sure this is fine? I mean, of course, as the lord of Gilm, I should be delighted..."

"Absolutely. I came here of my own volition, so I have no complaints whatsoever."

In response to Daskar's words, Elena nodded firmly, indicating there was absolutely no issue.

"Fundamentally, the reason problems like this arose is because the aristocrats of the Noble Faction in Gilm weren't doing their jobs properly. So, to fix that, isn't it only natural that I take on that responsibility?"

"...As I just said, we have no complaints. If anyone dares to pull any funny business under the Princess General's watch, we might even praise them for it."

Currently, there are quite a few people loyal to the Noble Faction in Gilm.

Most of them, however, are either relatives or subordinates of the head of the household.

Given that, while gathering information might not be a problem, actually preventing someone who's gone rogue from acting... well, that's quite difficult in many ways.

Considering that, having Elena, the one with the nickname Princess General, is the ultimate deterrent.

"Well then, that's settled."

"...Understood. I'll leave it to you. Now, what about lodging? Ideally, I'd like to get you a room at the Dusk Wheat inn, but given the current situation, that's difficult. Would you mind staying here? Fortunately, we have plenty of spare rooms."

The fact he mentioned right away that securing a room at Dusk Wheat inn was impossible suggests Daskar also clearly understands Elena's primary purpose for coming to Gilm.

"Hmm... I see. Staying here would be clearer than staying at a mansion of the Noble Faction, as it would show cooperation with the Neutral Faction. ...Ara, what do you think?"

"I don't see any problem with it. However, if word gets out that Lady Elena is staying here, it might cause various inconveniences for Lord Daskar."

Daskar understood exactly what Ara was worried about.

But Daskar shook his head at Ara, dismissing her concerns.

"Even now, merchants and such are gathering here in large numbers. If nobles start gathering too, it won't make much difference, right? Actually, dealing with nobles might be easier than dealing with merchants."

Daskar said it jokingly, but his words were half-serious.

After all, they had already made arrangements with the absolutely essential merchants. The merchants currently arriving were those rejected by the upper echelons of the guild, including Daskar himself.

That being the case, they weren't strictly necessary... Yet Daskar still met with them, hoping to find capable merchants unknown to him, or perhaps receive unexpected proposals...

Dealing with such people was far more difficult than dealing with nobles.

Honestly, Daskar couldn't help but think so.

"So, Lady Elena. To get straight to the point... Unfortunately, I have no business requiring your assistance today. So, how about this? Since you've made the effort to come to Gilm, why not take a look around the city? Particularly... well, the site where they're currently building the wall is quite something to see."

"...Where they're building the wall?"

Not understanding why it was recommended, Elena tilted her head.

Beside Elena, Ara tilted her head in puzzlement too.

"Um, why the wall?"

To Ara's puzzled question, Daskar merely smiled without answering.

"Hmm, seems we'll understand once we get there... Ara, let's go. Lord Daskar, I don't know what you're scheming, but I'll enjoy seeing it."

"Ah, enjoy it thoroughly."

After exchanging these words, Elena rose from the sofa where she had been sitting.

Perhaps because she fundamentally saw herself as someone who stood on the battlefield alongside knights and warriors rather than nobility, Elena and Daskar got along well.

(If only all men were like this, things would be much easier for me.)

Recalling the faces of those who fawned over her, those who fawned over her, Elena let out a small sigh.

Still, she reminded herself, she was in Gilm now. Shifting her mood, she greeted Daskar and left the room with Ara.

"Shall we travel by carriage?"

"No, our carriage is too conspicuous. Besides, it's been a while since I've been to Gilm, so I'd like to walk."

The carriage Elena used, regardless of its contents, looked no different from an ordinary carriage at first glance.

But anyone with even a little discerning eye would quickly realize it wasn't an ordinary carriage, and above all, the horses pulling it were specially bred.

And standing before the lord's mansion were merchants.

That meant trouble was definitely coming.

Perhaps understanding this from Elena's words, Ara raised no objection and asked the maid to take them out through the back entrance instead of the front.

If they weren't traveling by carriage, slipping out the back door to avoid attention wouldn't be difficult.

That said, Elena was incredibly conspicuous even just walking normally, with hair like the sun itself transformed into gold, beauty worthy of the word "peerless"—or perhaps even surpassing it—and a body with curves that were exceptionally alluring for a woman.

Even so, perhaps influenced by the presence of Marina, Vihera, and many other beauties, Elena smiled at the scarcity of stares directed her way as she proceeded toward the new wall being constructed.
