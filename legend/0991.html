Mass production of Magic Swords that control their wielders.

Rei's bad feeling was confirmed when he gave up stripping the Cyclops and returned to Gilm after finishing stripping the Kobold's materials.

"I told you, there's no way he'd do something like that!"

It was still only a little past noon.

Rei and the others had returned to Gilm with two adventurers who had been chased by kobolds, but upon reaching the gate where entry procedures were conducted, they noticed some commotion.

They assumed it was people waiting to enter Gilm. Wondering why so many had gathered, Rei and the others headed toward the source of the noise.

There, a man who looked like an adventurer was being held down by several guards.

And not just restrained—he had been forcibly thrown to the ground, with guards pinning down each of his limbs in a rather severe manner.

The man pinned down didn't move at all, as if unconscious.

Even so, the surrounding guards showed no sign of letting their guard down as they held him down.

"What happened?"

"Huh? Oh, Rei?"

When I asked a man in his thirties who seemed to be a merchant nearby, he recognized Rei's face and didn't seem particularly surprised to see Set either.

...Though he was certainly captivated by Vihera's appearance.

Still, he quickly snapped back to attention, hurriedly shifting his gaze from Vihera to the guards... specifically, to the man who seemed to be the adventurer's companion, the one being pinned to the ground and making a commotion.

"That adventurer they're pinning down suddenly went berserk with a dagger. And his body was glowing strangely, looking really suspicious."

"...What?"

At the merchant's words, Rei hurriedly turned his gaze toward the man pinned to the ground.

But, as the merchant had said, there was absolutely no sign of light emanating from within his body.

Still, Rei kept his eyes fixed on the man, watching him carefully.

The only one making a fuss was the adventurer accompanying the man. The one being held down seemed drained of strength, limp and lifeless.

He certainly didn't look like he was about to start struggling.

(That dagger...)

Rei's gaze fell upon the dagger lying on the ground.

The moment he saw it, Rei felt it resembled the Cyclops's hammer or the kobold's Magic Sword.

In other words, the dagger now in Rei's line of sight was likely a magic item crafted by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire...

"Rest assured. I believe we can quickly prove he held no hostility toward us."

Ranga spoke up, trying to calm the excited, noisy adventurer.

The adventurer man addressed knew Ranga, of course.

He sighed, seeming to settle down from his earlier protesting tone.

"...Sorry. I got a little too worked up."

"No, don't worry about it. It's only natural to feel that way when your partner suddenly goes berserk. But, just to be clear, would it be okay if we took him into custody here? Of course, we'll let him go as soon as we've checked him over and found no issues."

"But... no, wait. Yeah. I get it. Go ahead."

The man started to protest to Ranga for a moment, but quickly held his tongue.

He understood that causing any more commotion here would only worsen his own and his comrades' standing.

Besides, he trusted Ranga.

Ranga, who commanded the guards, came out to the gate like this and worked alongside the soldiers despite his position.

And he did this after finishing his duties as captain of the guard.

Because of this, Ranga's character was well-known among the residents of Gilm. That was likely why the adventurer man didn't press the issue further and quietly backed down.

"I'm sorry about this. But rest assured, he won't be charged. Probably..."

Ranga paused, his gaze fixed on the dagger lying on the ground.

As one guard moved toward it...

"Halt!"

The sharpness of the command was unlike the usually mild-mannered Ranga.

The guard addressed by those words naturally halted in his tracks.

"Captain?"

"The cause of this commotion is likely that dagger."

Though he used the word "probably," Ranga seemed certain. His piercing gaze fixed on the dagger lying on the ground.

"But, um, what do we do? We can't just leave it like this, can we? Especially if this dagger is the reason."

"There's an alchemist in the knight order. I'm calling for them now. So, for now, don't touch that dagger. And make sure no one else touches it either. Keep the dagger surrounded."

"...Understood."

"Well, this matter is settled for now. Everyone, please return to your duties. We can't keep others waiting too long..."

Just as he said that, Ranga noticed Rei's presence.

The reason he hadn't noticed Rei until now was probably because Set was standing a little way off.

...Because Rei and Johanna doted on him.

Even without Set present, the fact that Ranga had noticed Rei spoke to his keen attentiveness.

Still, if he said anything here, some might start suspecting Rei had something to do with this whole affair.

Merchants and adventurers tend to be sharp in that regard. ...No, it's more accurate to say they have to be sharp to survive.

That's precisely why Ranga settled for a brief nod of acknowledgment before turning his gaze back to the man being held down.

He desperately needed to know how this man had acquired the dagger.

Of course, this man was merely a pawn and bore no guilt. Ranga intended to release him immediately after a physical examination, questioning, and a strict gag order.

With that thought, Ranga had the gathered crowd realign into orderly lines and resumed the process of entering Gilm.

About ten minutes later, several knights on horseback emerged from within the Gilm.

They must have ridden horses to get here from the lord's manor, as walking would have taken too long.

Behind one of the knights stood a slender figure who looked nothing like a knight. Ranga judged this must be the knightly order's alchemist.

"Are we late?"

One of the knights asked Ranga.

Knights naturally held higher rank than guards, but this wasn't just any guard—it was Ranga, the captain of the guard.

Moreover, few of Gilm's knights were the type to lord it over others.

...Not nonexistent, but few. Fortunately, the knight who had just addressed Ranga was not one of those rare exceptions.

"No, it's fine. More importantly..."

He answered the knight, shifting his gaze to the man being held down by the guards.

Though, having lost consciousness the moment he threw the dagger, he wasn't moving on his own.

"He... And what about that magic item?"

"Over there."

The guards were keeping a tight watch over the dagger lying a short distance from the man, ensuring no one touched it.

Even if someone tried to touch it, it would be impossible without somehow dealing with the guards.

And the guards remained vigilant, alert to anyone approaching, ready to stop them.

"Please."

The knight gave a brief order to the alchemist, who nodded slightly and dismounted.

Even though he belonged to the knight order, being an alchemist meant he probably didn't ride horses regularly. Even though he had been riding with the knight, his dismount looked somewhat unsteady.

Still, he managed to reach the ground without tumbling, took a small, deep breath as if to steady himself, and walked toward the dagger.

Seeing Ranga nod, the guard who had been protecting... and watching over the dagger yielded his spot to the alchemist.

"This... I see. Technically, it's not that... but then, the twist? But why not symmetrical left and right... If so, is this intentional?"

The alchemist muttered while staring at the dagger lying on the ground.

His muttering to himself, seemingly satisfied with his own reasoning, looked rather unsettling to an observer.

But the person murmuring seemed completely oblivious to their surroundings.

Rather, the knights watching his companion act this way seemed slightly embarrassed.

Still, unlike earlier, there were fewer onlookers around, so they could probably tolerate it.

The gazes directed at them were mainly from the guards, and those processing entry into Gilm were shielded from view by the guards acting as a wall.

But just because they could endure it didn't mean they wanted to endure those stares forever.

One of the knights, thinking this, called out to the alchemist.

"Hey, Wal. Come back already. If you're going to analyze that dagger, you don't have to do it here. Take it back to the lab."

"...But this magical metal should be pretty rare. You're just going to throw it away? But by using this magical metal... Huh? Ah, ah. Sorry. I just got carried away."

His companion's words finally snapped him back to reality. The alchemist called Wal immediately replied.

"Alright, now that you get it, get ready. We're taking it back right now."

"Yeah, got it."

At the knight's urging, Wal pulled a piece of cloth from his pocket.

Naturally, coming from an alchemist, it wasn't just any cloth.

It had been specially processed using magic, designed to offer some degree of protection against mental attacks directed at the wearer.

It was only to a certain extent, however. If the dagger possessed something like powerful mental domination, it couldn't prevent that. Even so, creating such a cloth required rare materials and wasn't something easily made.

Wrap the cloth around the dagger. Gently touch the dagger through the cloth and immediately withdraw your hand... Once you're certain there's no issue, touch the cloth with your fingers for several seconds. When you confirm there's still no ill effect, grasp the dagger through the cloth.

The surrounding knights watched him, holding their breath. When Wal, now gripping the dagger, gave a small nod, they finally exhaled in relief.

"Seems it's all right, then."

"Somehow. ...It seems this ability to dominate others isn't as powerful as I thought."

At Wol's words, the surrounding knights and guards breathed sighs of relief.

"Then let's get going right away."

Nodding at the knight's words, Wol reached for the dagger wrapped in cloth and tried to mount his horse... but realized his hand was restricted by the dagger. Still, with the knight who had brought him here lending a hand, he managed to swing himself onto the horse's back.

"Well then, Lord Ranga. We'll take our leave now. Please be extremely careful about that matter."

"Understood. As captain of the guard, I wouldn't dream of letting dangerous individuals into Gilm."

As long as the alchemist is scheming, Gilm remains the most convenient place to resupply food and other provisions.

Of course, the alchemist is likely on guard, but humans need to eat.

(Though if they have skilled companions, they might be eating monster or wild animal meat instead.)

Though he thought this, he couldn't afford to let his guard down. Ranga strengthened his vigilance to prevent the alchemist from entering Gilm.

"Don't cause any trouble. With the kobold incident, I figured there'd be more..."

In Rei's room at the Dusk Wheat inn, besides the room's owner, Vihera and Byune were also present.

They had returned to the guild with Mireine and Johanna, but considering the scene they witnessed at the gate, they couldn't bring themselves to celebrate the successful quest with a party, so they parted ways mid-journey.

The only bright news for Rei was that Johanna and the others had cooperated to carry the required proof of slaying parts and materials, so the Cyclops extermination request would likely be successfully completed.

"If mass production of magic items that can control monsters is possible, that would be troublesome, wouldn't it?"

"Hmm."

Vihera's words prompted Byune to nod in agreement.

Rei couldn't disagree with that statement either.

While the damage hadn't been significant yet, if this were to be done on a larger scale, Gilm and its surroundings would become dangerous in many ways.

But as if to dispel Rei's unease, Vihera continued.

"But for now, maybe we don't need to worry that much?"

"Huh? Why?"

"Because if they could truly control monsters completely, wouldn't there be a much bigger uproar by now?"

Hearing this, Rei recalled all the opponents he had defeated or encountered.

Cyclops, kobolds, and humans.

Though the only incidents he knew of involving the alchemist causing this current trouble were those three, he quickly understood what Vihera meant and agreed.

"Come to think of it... If you could completely control monsters or their users, you'd either proceed in secret or cause a huge commotion."

"Yes. Whether it was the Cyclops or the Kobold, it felt more like manipulating the user, or rather, pushing them close to a rampage state. I only saw the gate incident after they were subdued, but based on past experience, it was probably the same."

"So then... what about a massive attack on Gilm by monsters controlled by an alchemist?"

"Probably not, I don't think so."

Relief escaped Rei's lips at Vihera's words, easing his immediate concern for Gilm.
