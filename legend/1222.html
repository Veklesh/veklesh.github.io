Before they knew it, the surroundings had completely changed.

Those who experienced it initially couldn't comprehend what had happened. They looked around, only then realizing their surroundings were completely different from just seconds before, their faces contorting in shock.

"Well then, I must go and check on the experiment. I shall take my leave for now. Once you, Rei, have defeated the Silver Lion, call for me again."

With only that statement, Grimm's form faded away like mist or something.

Some wondered if he might help, since he seemed concerned about Rei.

A few thought that, but Grimm vanished as if to say such thoughts were completely irrelevant.

"Um... He seems like quite an impressive person? Person... Undead, right?"

Johanna murmured thoughtfully, and Rei nodded in response.

"Well, he lives... no, he's dead in a different time than us. Anyway, he spends his time like that, so there are bound to be differences. More importantly, everyone, get yourselves ready."

Realizing they were in a familiar place, Rei murmured.

A spot slightly removed from the core room of the dungeon guarded by the Silver Lion.

Grim had likely teleported them here because placing them too close to the Silver Lion's chamber would have overwhelmed them with its presence.

Grateful for Grimm's consideration, Rei, Elena, Marina, Ara, Elk, and Min each checked their weapons and equipment before joining the fight against the Silver Lion.

Vihera and Rhodes, who had been riding on Set's back, had already been dismounted by Johanna and the others.

Near Johanna and the others, Yellow, who also wouldn't be participating in this battle, stood quietly.

Unlike Set, Yellow wasn't particularly strong in combat.

While his defense is strong, it's unclear how effective it would be against the Silver Lion. Considering that, it's only natural he wasn't included in this battle.

Yellow seemed to understand his own limitations. He looked a little lonely, but remained quiet.

"Speaking of which, I heard the Silver Lion uses a powerful roar. Didn't you prepare earplugs or something to protect against it?"

Johanna asked while watching Rei and the others check their weapons, armor, and equipment.

Some time had passed since Johanna arrived at this dungeon.

With that much time, gathering information about the dungeon wasn't difficult.

And gathering information about the dungeon naturally meant gathering information about the Silver Lion residing in its lowest level.

Most adventurers who challenged the Silver Lion died, but many managed to escape.

The biggest reason for this was that the Silver Lion never left the room it guarded.

That is precisely why some who fought the Silver Lion managed to survive.

...Of course, these adventurers don't possess transfer magic like Grimm, nor do they have magical items for teleportation like the Bestir Empire.

Even if they managed to escape the Silver Lion by the skin of their teeth, they still had to find their way to the dungeon's exit on their own.

They had to fight their way through the many monsters that emerged from the dungeon while wounded by the Silver Lion.

Even if they survived the Silver Lion, very few managed to escape the dungeon alive.

(If there were something like the teleportation function in the dungeon in Exil, perhaps more survivors would have made it out alive.)

The name of the labyrinth city Rei once visited with Elena flashed through his mind.

Of course, if there had been a way to transfer directly to the desired level, the number of those who challenged the Silver Lion and were killed would undoubtedly have increased.

In any case, one piece of information about the Silver Lion gleaned from those rare survivors was that it used a powerful roar.

Of course, such details were known from legends about the Silver Lion's existence, but those who had actually seen that roar with their own eyes, heard it with their own ears, and experienced it firsthand...

It wasn't just sound; that roar possessed a physical attack power akin to some kind of ultrasonic wave. There was no doubt it would be the first major hurdle in fighting the Silver Lion.

The sheer intensity of the sound could rob its victims of their sense of balance.

If you get hit with such an attack right off the bat, and before you can even coordinate properly, the S-rank monster, the Silver Lion, will launch a direct assault.

It was simply beyond the capabilities of ordinary adventurers to handle.

To avoid taking the Silver Lion's attack, earplugs were essential to prevent the roar from overwhelming your ears.

Of course, earplugs alone couldn't completely block the roar, but they significantly reduced the damage compared to taking it head-on... That's what Johanna had heard.

But Rei shook his head at Johanna's words.

"Earplugs might block the roar, but you'd also lose your allies' voices. That would mess up our coordination."

"But it's better than being immobilized by the roar..."

Before even considering coordination, what happens if you can't act at all?

Johanna pointed this out, but Rei nodded without seeming particularly concerned.

"Fortunately, the Silver Lion can't leave the core room of the dungeon. So, if it uses its roar and we're truly helpless, we can just leave the room. Worst case, we can exit the room, let our ears recover, and then rejoin the fight."

"True. It's strange why none of the adventurers who challenged the Silver Lion before us ever used that method."

"...Well, normally, no one would be reckless enough to think of challenging an S-rank monster with such a small party."

Johanna muttered, sounding exasperated.

To begin with, reaching the lowest level is necessary to challenge the Silver Lion, meaning combat strength is already depleted by that point.

Of course, there are no monsters on this lowest level, so camping here to recover stamina shouldn't be too difficult.

But the tools depleted on the way here couldn't be recreated without an alchemist, and even hired porters could only carry a limited amount of luggage.

On top of that, preparing enough strength to fight the Silver Lion while going in and out of the room... that's usually difficult.

No, it's safe to say it's impossible.

The only reason it's possible is because such a gathering of skilled individuals—those with nicknames and high-ranking adventurers—is assembled here.

In truth, even if Johanna and the others joined this Silver Lion hunt, they'd only be a hindrance.

They'd be little more than sacrificial decoys or human shields.

Even Ara, who would be considered skilled by standard adventurer standards, would be... not exactly a liability here, but it's questionable whether she'd be a real asset to the fight.

Still, Ara was included because they likely counted on her brute strength.

Thanks to the Power Axe effect, there's no need to worry about her stamina running out.

On top of that, the weapon's unique nature—not slashing, not thrusting... more like smashing—might prove useful this time...

(You want me to fight alongside these people? What kind of impossible task are you asking of me?)

Holding the Power Axe, Ara sighed softly to herself.

As the leader of Elena's guard knights, she had confidence in her own skill.

But that confidence was only relative to ordinary people.

If asked whether she considered herself among those like the beings surrounding Ara—those who had stepped into the realm of the inhuman—she would answer an immediate no!

Still, she didn't volunteer to stop them because she wanted to be of some use to Elena... or rather, to Elena who was worried about Vihera.

"Everyone, warm up and get ready."

Rei, having finished his conversation with Johanna, addressed those participating in the Silver Lion battle.

Having been teleported straight to this lowest level upon entering the dungeon, their bodies weren't yet ready for combat.

Of course, against ordinary enemies, such meticulous preparation wouldn't be necessary.

But since the enemy they must defeat this time was the Silver Lion, there was no way they could neglect their preparations.

After addressing everyone, Rei also readied himself, holding the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left, preparing his usual dual-spear stance.

He gave the Death Scythe a light swing, and in perfect sync, swung the Twilight Spear.

Seeing Rei like this, Elk's eyes widened slightly in surprise.

It was fairly well-known that Rei had started using the dual-spear style, but Elk had been so preoccupied with the Rhodes incident lately that he hadn't had the mental bandwidth for other information.

"Rei, what's that?"

"Huh? This is how my fighting's been lately."

"...Two long weapons? Isn't that awkward to handle?"

"Yeah. It was pretty tough at first, but once you get the hang of it, it's actually pretty easy to fight with, and it gives you an advantage over enemies."

Using a scythe and a spear, weapons with wide ranges to begin with, meant that mastering them would indeed give him an edge in combat.

But that's only if you can master them.

Even a spear takes time to master.

And just how unwieldy a scythe like the Death Scythe is? Well, you only need to look at how uncommon scythes are as weapons to realize that.

"Well, it'd be powerful if you could master it... but can you?"

"I've gotten the hang of it lately."

It's true I was extremely confused when I first started the Two-Spear Style.

But it's been several months since I started using the Two-Spear Style.

Given Rei's combat instincts, it was only natural that with that much time spent training and testing it in actual combat, he'd start to get the hang of it.

Of course, he couldn't say he'd mastered the Two-Spear Style completely; he was still far from fully utilizing it.

"This is all I can manage, dammit!"

Elk murmured wistfully, holding the Axe of the Thunder God.

"Nah, you don't need to worry about it that much. You're using a weapon that suits you well."

Rei had seen Elk fight using the Axe of the Thunder God countless times before.

In fact, he'd even fought alongside him directly, though it had been under duress from the Bestir Empire.

The Axe of the Thunder God, a powerful magic item, suited Elk's fighting style perfectly.

No, rather, it was precisely because the Axe of the Thunder God and Elk's abilities were so perfectly matched that he was able to rise to become a Rank A adventurer with a nickname.

"Is that so? Hmm, well, I suppose so."

At Rei's words, Elk flashed a pleased smile.

Lately, the matter with Rodos had forced him to be serious, but Elk was essentially a grown-up version of a childhood bully.

He could usually sense danger lurking behind words with hidden meanings, but when Rei offered a pure compliment like this, he took it at face value.

"Rei, Elk too. Make sure you stretch properly."

Elena, who had been watching the two of them, offered a word of caution.

She didn't want them to take it too lightly, since they were about to challenge the Silver Lion.

Of course, that doesn't mean they should be tense either.

If they became so tense that their bodies froze during the actual fight... that would be nothing short of counterproductive.

Of course, both Rei and Elk understood this, so upon Elena's warning, they resumed lightly moving their bodies.

But it wasn't just a simple restart; they also watched each other's movements, checking for any flaws.

Elk, especially, had never seen a style combining a large scythe and two spears before, so his gaze was particularly sharp.

This wasn't merely out of curiosity.

During the battle against the Silver Lion, Elk needed to fight while keeping a close eye on Rei's actions.

In terms of unconventional weapons, Elena's linked sword, Mirage, was another example.

It could transform freely between a whip-like form and a longsword, allowing her to change her combat style at will.

However, Elk had fought Elena before, and that experience allowed him to read her movements to some extent.

But this is his first time fighting someone who employs a technique like the Two-Spear Style—especially one where one weapon is a large scythe, not a spear.

Elk was also a high-ranking adventurer known by his alias.

Given that, teaming up with most opponents posed no problem. But facing a dual-spear style he'd never seen before was another matter entirely.

Still, the fact that he could grasp the basics just by watching for a short while showed Elk was no ordinary figure himself.

After confirming everyone's bodies were sufficiently warmed up... Rei spoke up.

"Let's go."

At Rei's words, everyone nodded silently. Except for Johanna and the others, they began walking toward the Silver Lion's location.
