Three days after leaving Gilm... Rei and the others had already departed the Kingdom of Mireana and entered the Kingdom of Reburina.

Originally, the journey was supposed to take about two days, but it took an extra day because Rei had discovered a merchant caravan under attack by bandits along the way.

It wasn't that he didn't feel inclined to help the innocent caravan... but for Rei, the stronger impulse was that spotting bandits meant it was time to make some money.

If that were all, he could have simply raided the hideout the bandits revealed and taken the treasure and weapons there. However, the bandit gang wasn't just after valuables; they had captured many people to sell as slaves. Dealing with that situation took considerable time.

He couldn't possibly leave the prisoners behind in the bandits' hideout... especially one deep in the mountains. In the end, he had to escort the prisoners to a nearby village, which took up an entire day.

Fortunately, the village wasn't a remote hamlet; it was fairly large for a village, so communication went smoothly.

Of course, not all the captives were villagers, and Rei couldn't take responsibility for them all.

In the end, it was decided that the prisoners' personal belongings found in the bandit hideout would be returned to them, while the rest would be kept by Rei and her group. After that, they departed the village.

(If there had been fewer prisoners, I might have been able to transport them all at once using the Mirage Cage... but over twenty people is too many.)

Perched atop Set's back, Rei glanced toward the city beyond the horizon—Rossi, the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina—as these thoughts crossed his mind.

"Is that Rossi?"

"Hey, Rei. What does Rossi look like to you!?"

From inside the Mirage Cage, Vihera asked Rei above her.

The Mirage Cage had no windows to speak of, only a single door for entry and exit.

Given that, it was only natural to feel a certain sense of confinement.

This was also considered a point for improvement after this assignment was completed.

However, given that the Mirage Cage's defining feature was its ability to blend into the surrounding scenery using Murder Chameleon leather, simply adding windows wasn't feasible.

The ceiling section can be removed, which somewhat alleviates the claustrophobia. But looking up only reveals Set's body and wings, Rei's feet straddling Set, and the sky... so it doesn't completely eliminate the feeling of confinement.

That's precisely why the Vihera aboard the Mirage Cage had no choice but to ask Rei about the outside world.

"Hmm, well... For a capital city, it's not that big. From what I can see like this, it's smaller than Gilm."

"I suppose so."

It wasn't the first Vihera who asked, but Marina who answered Rei's words.

"Gilm is the only city in the frontier. And that's for the Kingdom of Mireana. The number of people drawn to such a place is different from that of a capital city, even if it's just the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina, a vassal state of Mireana."

Her slight pride likely stemmed from her experience working as a guildmaster, one of the major pillars supporting Gilm, who oversaw the adventurers.

She couldn't exactly claim to have built all of present-day Gilm herself... but her words still carried pride in having contributed to its development.

As Marina spoke, Rei watched Rossi gradually approach from atop Set's back.

Considering the purpose of their visit to Rossy, it would probably be better to act separately from a distance and enter Rossy one by one.

But to enter Rossy, identification was naturally required, starting with a guild card.

If he'd had more time, he could have used his authority as the Daskar lord to prepare fake guild cards, but this time, there was no room for such luxuries.

Taking more time risked reports of Ajax and the others being captured, and it was highly likely the Kingdom of Mireana would make their move first.

...Then again, the organization Ajax belonged to possessed abundant magic items. Given its scale, it was also highly likely connected to high-ranking officials of the Kingdom of Reburina.

If that were the case, it wouldn't be surprising if they possessed the Paired Orb.

Anyway, lack of time was one thing... but the biggest reason I didn't prepare a forged guild card was undoubtedly Set's presence.

Someone who has a griffin as a familiar? There might be only one such person in the entire world.

Furthermore, the nickname "Rei the Crimson" is widely known not only in the Mireana Kingdom and the Bestir Empire, but also in neighboring countries, precisely because it refers to the adventurer who has a griffin as a familiar.

Naturally, word had spread to the Kingdom of Reburina, a vassal state of the Kingdom of Mireana. Seeing the griffin would make it obvious that it was Rei, even without checking a guild card.

Elena, Marina, and Vihera are also beauties of a caliber one might see only once in a lifetime, making it difficult for them to conceal their identities.

Elena, with golden vertical curls that looked as if sunlight itself had become her hair.

Marina, the dark elf, who always wore party dresses and naturally radiated an intense feminine allure.

Vihera, clad in sheer garments so thin they seemed almost transparent, reminiscent of those worn by courtesans or dancers.

With three such women, it was impossible for them not to stand out.

Byune was also essentially expressionless, but her features themselves were well-defined. ...Yet the fact that she stood out less than the others only served to prove just how flashy Rei and the others were.

For that reason, they reasoned that since they'd inevitably draw attention trying to enter Rossi covertly anyway, they might as well go all out and stand out completely.

Of course, this would inevitably cause various unwanted disturbances, but it also meant there was a good chance clues related to this incident would come to them.

"Well then, Rossi is getting closer. Time to land."

Even as they spoke, Set continued flying, significantly closing the distance to Rossi.

Even so, the fact that no commotion arose about Set... or rather, the griffin attacking, was likely thanks to the Mirage Cage.

Set's wings were larger than the Mirage Cage, so anyone looking up would surely recognize them clearly. Fortunately, no one on the ground was watching that intently.

"We're ready whenever you are. Count on us!"

Hearing Elena's voice, Rei signaled to Set.

Set then flapped his wings and descended to a spot slightly off the road leading to Rossi.

If he had merely flown overhead, those walking below might not have noticed him. But descending to the ground was another matter entirely.

The sound of Set's flapping wings and the wind they stirred up were impossible to conceal. Those walking the road turned their gaze toward the source.

Though a small country, Rossi was the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina.

While not as large as present-day Gilm, the number of people heading toward the capital was considerable.

One or two might have gone unnoticed, but there was no way such a large group could fail to notice Set's presence.

"Hey, wait! Hey! What's that?! What is it?!"

"A griffin!? No way, a griffin... and there's a person riding it!?"

"No, it's carrying something, but what is that thing?"

"Hey, hey. Shouldn't someone go get the soldiers?"

"You idiot! If that really is a griffin, soldiers wouldn't stand a chance against it!"

Passersby spotted Set, Rei riding on his back, and the mirage cage Set was holding, and began to stir.

Seeing some actually trying to go get soldiers, Rei hurriedly spoke up.

"Wait! This Set... this griffin is my familiar. It won't harm anyone unless provoked, so don't worry! I'm Rei, a Rank B adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana!"

Though Rei declared this with a dignified air, his current appearance—short and seemingly frail beneath the hood of his Dragon Robe—made it understandable if he seemed unreliable.

Still, he succeeded in stopping those who had been about to flee upon seeing Set's form.

"Hey, who's that guy?"

"Well, he's an adventurer, right? ...But a Rank B adventurer? That little guy?"

"...Ah! I've heard of him! He's the adventurer who burned the entire Bestir Empire army during the war between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Bestir Empire!"

Hearing those words, Rei's expression grew slightly awkward.

Given that word-of-mouth and minstrels were the primary means of spreading information, it was only natural for tales to grow as they traveled. Still, he hadn't expected rumors claiming he'd burned the entire Bestir Empire Army to the ground to be circulating.

(At this rate, what if the rumor reaches even farther? ...It won't turn into something like 'I burned both nations' entire armies,' will it?)

It was slightly unsettling, but he decided dwelling on it now was pointless and opened his mouth.

"There are some differences, but yeah, it's safe to say that adventurer is me. So, I'm not some bandit or anything like that, so please don't worry."

"...But what? Why would such a famous adventurer be in this country?"

"Ah, I have a request. That's why I'm here."

As he replied to the woman who had recognized Rei as Crimson, before she could say anything else, the door of the mirage cage placed on the ground opened.

The people around them, clearly not expecting a door, instinctively turned their gaze that way.

And emerging from the open door were beauties, beauties, beauties... and a young beauty.

The three beauties were so utterly beyond this world that everyone present, men and women alike, could do nothing but stare in awe.

Some pinched their cheeks, wondering if this was a dream, but even managing that much movement was impressive.

(Looks like it would've been better to just have Elena and the others come out from the start, rather than me explaining anything.)

Seeing the reactions around him, Rei felt a twinge of embarrassment over having explained anything at all. He averted his gaze toward Rossi.

Then, he saw several men who appeared to be security guards approaching from that direction.

...Of course. Rei and the others weren't that far from Rossi.

So naturally, the guards would have seen Set come down. Even if they hadn't witnessed that, seeing so many passersby gathered would make it only logical for them to come investigate what was happening.

(Ah, this feels like trouble brewing. ...No, actually, in terms of making a strong impression that we're here, maybe it's convenient? That depends on how much information Ajax's organization has on Gilm, though.)

The intelligence gathered by the Intelligence Unit and guards from the other two besides Ajax had been passed on to Rei and the others.

But honestly, both of them were pretty tight-lipped, and we haven't gotten much useful information out of them.

"Hey, you two! What are you doing gathered in a place like that?!"

One of the guards shouted, pointing the tip of his spear at Rei, who seemed to be the ringleader of this incident.

...But despite his bold words, he clearly showed signs of tension, likely because he had spotted Set.

The other guards also pointed their spear tips at Rei, but there was absolutely no atmosphere suggesting they intended to fight.

"I'm Rei. An adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana. These are my party members. We came to the Kingdom of Reburina on a request."

"...Rei of Crimson, huh?"

As guards, they likely knew some information about the Kingdom of Mireana.

He murmured softly, looking at Rei.

"Yeah. So, our means of transportation is that basket over there and Set. If Set landed directly in front of the main gate, it would cause a commotion, so we had him land a little ways off."

"But... we can't just leave such a huge cage here like this. It's a problem."

Probably because Rei's identity had been confirmed. The guard's tone was softer than before, and the spear tip was already lowered.

Facing the guards, Rei shook his head reassuringly.

"I'll take care of this. It's fine."

"...Take it? This enormous cage?"

"Yeah."

The guard who had been speaking with Rei wore a puzzled expression, seemingly unaware of the Misty Ring.

Leaving the guard behind, Rei moved to where the Mirage Cage lay on the ground, gently touched it... and in the next instant, the Mirage Cage vanished.
