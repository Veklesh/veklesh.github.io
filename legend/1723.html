An entity believed to be the core of the dungeon, located at the bottom of the subterranean lake.

Under normal circumstances, one would never have been able to see it.

However, thanks to the moss glowing in various colors—red, blue, green, yellow—like a display of lights, and perhaps due to the unique water quality of this subterranean lake, the entity at the bottom of the lake, believed to be the core of the dungeon, could be clearly identified.

That said, there are some here who have never seen the dungeon’s core before.

“Is that the dungeon’s core? …Really? There’s no chance it’s just something that looks similar, right?”

“I don’t know. But… take a look.”

Skeptical—or rather, three parts belief and seven parts doubt—at Relieu’s words, Rei looked around this vast space once more.

At first, Relieu seemed unsure what Rei was getting at, but when he followed his gaze, he understood.

There were several paths leading to this subterranean lake, in addition to the one they had come through.

In other words, even if the paths they took along the way differed, they were all destined to reach this underground lake in the end.

“Ugh.”

Realizing this, a sound of disgust—genuine disgust—escaped from Relieu’s mouth.

She must have realized that no matter what they did, they would have ended up at this underground lake anyway.

(Come to think of it, since we got here without getting too lost, were we lucky in a way? Though, it’s also possible that we were able to arrive in a relatively short distance precisely because the path was littered with traps.)

That said, considering those traps were the kind that Byune—who, combat prowess aside, isn’t exactly a master even if she possesses a certain level of pure thieving skill—could disarm, maybe we were on the right path after all.

“I suppose there were probably several other staircases in that forest just like the one we found. And if you go up and down those stairs, you’d eventually end up here. Unless, of course, you hit a dead end.”

“...It’s frustrating that I can’t deny it, damn it.”

Relieu, too, must have come to understand that this subterranean lake was the deepest part of the dungeon.

He couldn’t say anything more; he was at a loss for words.

“So, it’s settled then—we’ll fight that snake, right?”

Vihera asked for confirmation, but no one could object.

As long as they were here with the goal of clearing this dungeon, there was no option but to defeat that giant snake.

But… while it’s true we have to defeat it, there’s also the question of how to do so.

“Since it’s a snake, we should probably assume it can move on land as well as in water. …Does anyone here, including Marina, have any information on that monster?”

At Elena’s words, everyone except Marina shook their heads, and their gazes naturally turned to Marina.

She was the one who had lived the longest among them, had the most extensive history as an adventurer, and held the key position of Guild Master.

Naturally, her knowledge was deep, and when it came to monsters, it surpassed even Rei and the others.

That’s precisely why I thought she might have some information about that giant snake… but the expression on Marina’s face was closer to confusion.

If she didn’t know, she could have simply said so; if she did know, she could have said so.

Yet, Marina said neither; she merely evaded the question.

Given Marina’s usual personality, this was highly unlikely.

“What’s wrong? Don’t you know about that snake?”

“No… well, I know a monster that looks similar. But the one I know isn’t nearly that big. It’s about the length of two grown men…”

“So, you mean it’s a higher-tier or rare variant of the monster you know?”

That was the first possibility that came to mind when told that even similar monsters could differ in appearance and abilities.

Watching the snake, which had already returned to the water... For a moment, I wondered anew why we hadn’t been attacked yet, but remembering the battle with the Silver Lion, I immediately understood.

(The Silver Lion couldn’t leave the room I was in either. If that’s the case… perhaps that snake can’t leave its designated space either? If so, what exactly is that designated space…?)

Rei’s gaze fell upon the subterranean lake where the giant snake was presumably swimming.

Rei realized that they would likely have to enter the underground lake before the battle with that snake could begin.

“A high-tier species or a rare species… Well, logically speaking, that makes sense. But it’s just a bit too big.”

Marina said this with a troubled expression, but since no one had any information on that serpent monster, there was no doubt that the information she possessed would serve as the standard.

That said, Marina, who was the focus of everyone’s hopeful gazes, was honestly hesitant to speak up, fearing that the information she provided might be wrong.

“Just tell me. It’s better to have some information—even if it might be slightly wrong—than to fight a monster like that with absolutely no information at all. If it turns out to be wrong, we can just act based on that assumption.”

Stared at intently by Rei, Marina seemed to hesitate for a moment, but eventually opened her mouth.

“All right. But keep in mind this is just for reference—listen with that in mind.”

Everyone nodded at Marina with serious expressions.

After confirming their understanding, Marina began to share the information she knew.

“First off, what I know is a Rank C monster called Garangiga. This monster mainly operates in water. Its fangs are poisonous, and it’s also capable of spitting venom.”

“A poison-type monster, huh? …That’s going to be a pain.”

Relieu muttered this with a look of distaste, likely because she knew just how potent it was.

Poisonous monsters are commonplace.

But it’s precisely because that poison is effective that it’s used so frequently, and as a result, it’s become a common means of attack.

Also, even though we call it “poison,” there are many different types.

There are paralyzing poisons that immobilize the opponent, and there are even potent poisons that can kill you just by touching them.

“Was it a Galangiga? I’d like to hear what kind of poison that monster uses.”

At Elena’s words, Marina nodded and spoke.

“First, its fangs contain a bleeding poison. It’s the kind that makes your blood flow uncontrollably. As for the venom, it seems to cause a burning pain upon contact. …But, as I’ve said before, this is information based on the Garangiga monster I know. I don’t know if these general rules apply to that monster, which is believed to be a rare or higher-tier species.”

“I understand. So we need to be careful of the poison. …Then, what other attack methods does it have besides poison?”

“There are sharp spines hidden between its scales, which it uses for ramming and wrapping attacks.”

“...That’s a nasty way to attack.”

A giant snake charging at you with countless spines protruding from the gaps in its scales.

Imagining that, Rei muttered with genuine disgust.

If such an attack were to hit you head-on without evasion or defense, it might not be fatal… but it would certainly result in a gruesome injury.

The others must have imagined the same thing as Rei, for they all wore expressions of disgust.

That said, the image Rei had in mind was of his body being scraped by a grater, so it was slightly different from what the others were picturing.

“That’s about the extent of its special attacks. Still… I can’t imagine the Garangiga in this subterranean lake possesses only the same attacks I’m familiar with.”

A rare species is called such precisely because it possesses abilities that the original species does not have, and a higher-tier species is an evolved form of that species, so naturally, its abilities are enhanced compared to its pre-evolution state.

Given that, it’s likely one of those two… or, in the worst case, both. Since there’s a possibility it’s a rare variant of a Galangiga’s higher-tier species, it was unclear what kind of attacks it would use.

Still, it was far better than fighting a Galangiga with absolutely no information.

“So, we need to watch out for venom from a distance, and at close range, attacks using its venomous fangs and the spines growing between its scales. Other than that, we can treat it just like a regular snake.”

“I suppose so. Though I have absolutely no idea if it’s okay to treat a snake that size the same as an ordinary snake.”

Everyone probably felt the same way Marina did. They let out a weary smile.

The laughter helped ease the tension, and once the atmosphere had settled somewhat, Rei moved on to the next topic.

“Now that we have a rough idea of what to expect from Garangiga’s abilities, let’s move on to the next topic. …Needless to say, I have absolutely no intention of fighting in the water—their territory. Just to be sure, is there anyone here who actually wants to fight Garangiga underwater?”

Rei asked, but considering Vihera’s personality, he couldn’t help but wonder if…

Fortunately, however, Vihera didn’t argue with Rei’s words and simply nodded in agreement.

This was likely because Vihera had her own thoughts on fighting underwater.

Basically, Vihera isn’t very good at fighting underwater.

Attacks using the Magic Palm or claws and blades created by magic might be somewhat effective, but underwater, above all, Vihera cannot fully utilize her physical combat skills—which are her true forte.

In other words, Vihera cannot utilize the very foundation of her combat style.

Naturally, fighting a powerful enemy under such circumstances offers Vihera no sense of satisfaction.

Given that, it’s only natural that she would want to fight on her home turf—the ground.

Moreover, Garangiga isn’t a monster that can’t fight on land.

As Marina explained, it was clear that it possesses sufficient combat power on land as well.

“I’m glad no one seems to be a suicide candidate. …That being the case, the biggest problem is how to lure Garangiga onto the ground.”

“It doesn’t seem to care at all that we’re nearby.”

Relieu must have realized that even if he said he didn’t want to fight any more, his opinion wouldn’t be taken into account.

He shifted his focus to figuring out how to defeat Rangiga safely.

If they were going to fight, he naturally wanted to ensure victory as much as possible—that was only natural for an adventurer with a nickname.

“If we go into the underground lake, it’ll probably react… Marina, how does the Garangiga usually react to things around here?”

“I know what you’re getting at, Vihera, but from what I know of Galangiga, it would attack immediately if there were prey nearby. The reason this Galangiga isn’t attacking us is probably because that underground lake serves as its so-called ‘boss room.’”

“That’s a pain.”

Given the nature of the Garangiga monster, it was undoubtedly troublesome, but what was even more troublesome was the underground lake itself.

“I can fly through the sky on Set… but Elena’s Sleipnir Boots aren’t powerful enough to let her roam the skies freely.”

“Hmm. Unfortunately, that’s how it is.”

“I have spirit magic and a bow, so I can manage my offensive options to some extent.”

“I’ve got Wind Slash… but that only works when I’m in the air.”

“I don’t have any long-range attacks to begin with. …Maybe I’ll just throw rocks like I did before.”

"Hmm!"

Each of them mentioned their own methods of attack, but in the end, it became clear that they simply couldn’t fight underwater.

“In that case, we’ll have to get Garangiga to come out of the underground lake… What do you think our options are?”

Marina, who had been looked at, thought for a moment... and then, prefaced by "I’m not sure about this," voiced the idea that had come to mind.

“First, we go into the underground lake… no, in this case, to the surface, I suppose. If we move there and attack Garangiga, I think it’ll recognize us as enemies. Then, if we move to where we are now—outside the underground lake—it might come after us… maybe. We won’t know if it’ll actually come out of the boss room until we try it, though.”

“‘Up’ means…”

The gazes of everyone present, including Rei, turn toward Set.

Set, with Yellow perched on his head, looked back at them with round eyes, as if to say, “What?”

They hesitated, wondering if it was really okay to send just Set alone up to the surface of the underground lake... but in the end, realizing there was no other choice, they decided to leave it up to him.
