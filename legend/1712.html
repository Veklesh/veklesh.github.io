In the end, Rei went upstairs and picked as many fruits as he could from the tree there.

As for what the others were doing during that time, they were either helping him pick the fruit or scouting the surrounding area.

After picking a fair amount of fruit, Rei asked, “Should I come help you guys now?” but… Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Byune all objected in unison.

They told Rei to focus on picking the fruit and storing it in the Misty Ring.

Even Byune, who was usually expressionless, wore a look on her face that could only be described as terrifying as she glared at Rei—which surely showed just how much the women in the group valued the fruit.

Since they’d started traveling with Rei, they’d never been short on money—though Elena and Marina hadn’t been short on money even before that—yet they likely believed they should pick as many fruits as possible while they could.

Rei thought of the saying that “there’s always room for dessert,” but wisely kept his mouth shut and concentrated on picking fruit in silence.

Moreover, the tree bearing various fruits wasn’t the only one Rei had found.

There were several others, and as they continued harvesting them… as a result, today ended up being what one might call a “fruit-picking day” rather than a dungeon exploration day.

That said, if you were to ask whether Rei minded, the answer would be no.

In any case, the sun was beginning to set in the forest on the second floor of the dungeon, as if to synchronize with the outside world… Seeing this, Rei and the others decided to head back to Golz for the day.

Since they had the Magic Tent, camping right there wouldn’t have been a problem, and they had Yellowbird and orc meat. If they searched the forest, they’d surely find wild vegetables, so camping there—though it was too luxurious to really call it “camping”—wouldn’t have been an issue, but in the end, they decided to return to Golz.

One reason was that they should probably explain the dungeon to the guild, but the bigger reason was that if they slept in the Magic Tent, Rei would have to sleep on the floor or the sofa.

If necessary, Rei wouldn’t hesitate to camp out, but since he was going to sleep anyway, he’d rather do so in a proper bed.

……Relieu was the only one who was in favor of camping, though.

The main reason Relieu was in favor of camping was that, under the pretext of keeping watch outside the magic tent, she could admire Set.

Although Relieu had long since stopped trying to hide her fondness for Set, her proposal was ultimately rejected.

In any case, having safely escaped the dungeon and returned to Golz, Rei and the others headed to the guild… and were immediately ushered into the guildmaster’s office.

“I’m sorry about that. I should have greeted you when you came yesterday… but I was in a meeting with the magistrate and didn’t get back until the middle of the night.”

Speaking to Rei and the others as they sat on the reception sofas in the Guild Master’s office was a woman in her forties.

As a former adventurer, her physique was unmistakably muscular.

“No, don’t worry about it. I was pretty busy myself yesterday.”

Rei, as the party leader, said this, though in reality, there wasn’t anything in particular keeping him busy.

He was simply trying to imply that they were just as busy as the Guild Master.

If she had approached them with a high-handed attitude, Rei would have responded in kind, but since she had been friendly this time, he had simply followed her lead.

The guild master probably understood that, but he didn’t say anything about it.

“That’s a relief to hear. Well, better late than never, but I’m Vanillas, the Guild Master of Golz. Though, just call me Guild Master—that’s fine.”

Vanillas said this with a smile, but his expression quickly turned serious.

“Now, I’d like to ask you right away… Was that on the cliff face definitely a dungeon?”

Although he asked the question, Vanillas’ expression betrayed his certainty that it was indeed a dungeon.

As an adventurer, he must have expected as much.

Since Rei had no intention of hiding the fact that it was a dungeon, he simply nodded.

“Yeah. I’m pretty sure it was a dungeon. The first floor had a simple layout with just one fork in the path, but the second floor was filled with a forest. It didn’t look like a dungeon that had just been created at all.”

“A forest…? You mean it wasn’t an illusion or anything like that—an actual forest with real trees growing there?”

Rei nods at Vanillas’ words and pulls a single fruit from his Misty Ring.

It was a fruit with yellow speckles on its skin, a common fruit found in any town. …Though it is a fruit that’s in season in the spring.

When she placed the fruit on the table, Vanillas picked it up, looking puzzled.

Since she knew Rei had an item box, she wasn’t particularly surprised to see a spring fruit still fresh and juicy here at this time of year.

“What’s this?”

“I picked that one today from a tree growing in the forest inside the dungeon. …As you can see, it’s the real thing, right? Well, even so, it doesn’t mean anything unless you believe me.”

“No, I believe you. Everyone over there is famous. You wouldn’t lie in a situation like that.”

People like Elena and Marina aren’t the type to lie about something like this.

I’ve spoken with Marina several times as the Guild Master, so I know her personality well.

The others, too, have their own circumstances, but many of them are well-known figures.

Considering all that, if they say there’s a forest, I can be certain it exists.

(I’d prefer to send someone to check it out in person… but that’s probably impossible for an adventurer from Golz. If anyone were to go, it would have to be me, but I don’t have the time for that either.)

The adventurers in Golz aren’t exactly strong.

After all, the strongest among them is a Rank C adventurer, so it goes without saying just how average the quality of the average adventurer is.

“But… if that’s the case, doesn’t that mean that dungeon was just created?”

“I don’t know. With dungeons, there’s probably more that hasn’t been figured out than what has. It wouldn’t be strange at all if it had been that kind of dungeon from the very beginning.”

Upon hearing Marina’s words, Vanillas frowned slightly as she pondered.

From Vanillas’ perspective, having a fully-fledged dungeon near Golz—where she serves as Guild Master—was far from ideal.

That was precisely why she’d used her connections to contact Walker and a few others right away, arranging for them to send adventurers here.

And yet, the fact that the dungeon has grown far beyond his expectations at this point is a complete miscalculation.

(Tomorrow… no, actually, later today. Anyway, I’ll have to go talk to the magistrate.)

Though it was a hassle, it was certain that this information needed to be relayed to the magistrate as soon as possible.

The magistrate, who was sent to govern Golz in place of the local noble, is competent enough—neither particularly good nor bad.

He’s a bit tight-fisted, which has been a headache for Vanilla, who serves as the guild master, but objectively speaking, there’s no doubt he’s a capable individual.

As for his personality, he was a noble through and through… but not the kind of noble who harbors the “chosen people” mentality typical of the Noble Faction.

Then again, since he was said to be the noble’s fifth son, perhaps he simply didn’t have the opportunity to develop such a mindset.

In any case, since he is by no means a bad person, it was certain that upon hearing the dungeon had actually grown, he would take appropriate action.

“So, were there any monsters besides the ones we already know about?”

“No, I didn’t see any new ones. Oh, but I did run into a Yellowbird yesterday. …It is a forest, after all, so it wouldn’t be strange if there were a lot of bird monsters.”

“Besides, it was just sitting there picking fruit right at the top of the stairs the whole time.”

At Vihera’s words, Rei tried to argue back… but since it was the truth, he couldn’t say a thing.

In fact, there was no doubt that they had been so focused on gathering fruit that, as a result, they had barely explored the second floor at all.

...That said, Vihera, who was now flashing a mischievous smile at Rei, had also agreed to pick the sweet fruit... no, she had actually been quite enthusiastic about it.

In that sense, you could call Vihera an accomplice too.

Whether she’d admit it or not is another matter.

“Well, that’s beside the point.”

Vanillas didn’t particularly blame Rei for focusing on gathering fruit, and steered the conversation back on track.

Since they weren’t originally exploring the dungeon on a guild commission, he wasn’t in a position to complain about Rei and the others proceeding at their own pace.

Of course, as the guildmaster of Golz, he would have preferred them to make more progress in exploring the dungeon, but he couldn’t push them that hard.

After all, since Rei and the others were the only ones capable of tackling the dungeon at the moment, he had no choice but to rely on them.

“So, are we going into the dungeon again tomorrow?”

“Unless something urgent comes up, that’s the plan. …We need to thoroughly investigate what kind of monsters are in that forest, after all.”

For Rei, searching for unknown monsters is extremely important from a magical beastcraft perspective.

Of course, the most important thing is the dungeon’s core, where he can learn terrain manipulation skills, but aside from that, it’s not uncommon to find unknown monsters in dungeons.

In fact, on the very day he arrived in Golz, he managed to find an unknown monster called the Yellowbird that had emerged from a dungeon.

Since that Yellowbird likely inhabits the forest on the second floor, it’s highly probable that there are other unknown monsters there as well.

For that reason, too, Rei wanted to press on with exploring the forest.

(It’s a bummer that we’re the first ones to tackle that dungeon. If someone had gone in before us, we’d probably have some kind of intel.)

Rei felt a sense of dread as he thought about the forest, but precisely because they were the first to attempt it, they would be able to claim all the undiscovered treasures in the dungeon for themselves.

“Oh, come to think of it…”

Speaking of “unknown things,” Rei recalled the items he’d obtained in the dungeon and pulled out the rock plant he’d found on the first floor from his Misty Ring.

It was cut off partway, but even so, it was clear just by looking at it that the plant was made of rock.

Upon seeing the plant, Vanillas’ face twisted in horror.

“C-could it be… that there’s a monster like a basilisk, one with the ability to turn people to stone?”

Hearing those words, asked with a look of trepidation, Rei realized what Vanillas was worried about and immediately denied it.

“No, it’s not like that. As you can see, it’s a plant made of rock, but it wasn’t using any petrification ability—it was just living in this rocky state. And if you get close, it seems to attack by flinging its rock-made leaves at you.”

Hearing Rei’s explanation, Vanillas felt a deep sense of relief.

Monsters with petrification abilities, like basilisks and cockatrices, are incredibly powerful.

In fact, she is convinced that the adventurers in Golz would be unable to defeat them.

If such a monster existed, it might even become necessary to relocate the town of Golz in the worst-case scenario.

Vanillas had thought that far in an instant, but Rei’s words made her realize she didn’t need to worry about that.

“So, a plant made of rock? Is it a monster?”

The most famous plant monster is, of course, the Treant.

Vanillas wondered if the plant in front of her was that kind of monster, but Rei shook his head.

“No, it didn’t have a magic stone, so it’s not a monster. I think it’s just a plant.”

“...It’s made of rock, right?”

Vanillas asked, looking incredulous, but Rei simply nodded in response.

“Given that this is a dungeon, I figured nothing would be too surprising… but this really took me by surprise. For now, could you have the guild look into it? If it’s valuable, I’d like to see if there are any others.”

At Rei’s request, Vanillas gasped softly before nodding.
