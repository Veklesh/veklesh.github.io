“...So? So, in the end, Rei came back without hearing anything?”

That evening, while dining in Marina’s garden as usual, Vihera shot Rei a look of exasperation.

Thanks to Marina’s spirit magic, it was comfortable enough to eat in the garden even though it was winter… but Rei, on the receiving end of Vihera’s exasperated gaze, didn’t feel quite so comfortable.

“Well, what can I say? She faints just from seeing my face, you know? If it were just once, that’d be one thing, but when it happens twice, three times in a row, it’s only natural they’d conclude there’s no way to get any information while I’m around.”

That’s right—the woman who had lost consciousness and was in the infirmary fainted the moment she saw Rei’s face when he entered the room.

If it had happened just once, it could have been dismissed as a coincidence, but when it happened two, three times in a row, it couldn’t be brushed off as mere chance.

As a result, the guard who was dealing with Rei told him to go home for the day… and seeing the woman faint every time she looked at his face, Rei couldn’t very well complain, so he ended up coming home just like that.

On top of that, since he had to wait for the woman to wake up, he ended up barely making any progress today in capturing the people wrapped in red cloth.

“Rei, just to be sure… You’re definitely coming with Elena and me to the Duchy of Kerebel tomorrow, right? You don’t want to stay here a little longer because of that red cloth business or anything…”

Ara asked Rei, looking slightly worried.

Having seen up close how much Rei was preoccupied with the matter of the people wrapped in red cloth, she probably thought, “Maybe…”

Elena, too, tried not to show that she was concerned about Rei’s demeanor, but she was watching him out of the corner of her eye.

Unaware that she was being watched, Rei nodded back without showing any hesitation or hesitation.

“Ah, that’s not a problem. The red cloth thing is a bit concerning, but Elena had the prior engagement, after all.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Elena felt relieved, though she tried not to let it show on her face.

Returning to the Duchy of Kerebel with Rei was something Elena had been looking forward to quite a bit.

If things went well this time, it wouldn’t mean there would be no one left to propose to her… though that was unlikely, the number would certainly drop dramatically.

Naturally, there would undoubtedly be some commotion at first.

Those who had participated in the Bestir Empire’s war understood that aristocratic authority held no sway over Rei. But for those who hadn’t participated, what they knew about Rei was merely hearsay.

Since they haven’t seen it with their own eyes, it’s certain that many will foolishly convince themselves they’ll be fine, even if they know Rei is that kind of person… no, precisely because they’ve heard of Rei’s reputation, it’s almost certain that some will emerge who want to assert their presence among the Noble Faction—or impress Elena—by trying to suppress it.

(Though, such people will surely come to regret their own short-sightedness.)

Savoring the seafood soup brimming with fish, Elena pondered.

“I see. That’s good to hear. …Though, coming from me, it might sound strange, but what are you going to do about those men in red? While Rei is away from Gilm, they might run wild and do as they please.”

“If they’re causing trouble specifically to target me, wouldn’t they figure there’s no point in causing trouble if I’m not there?”

“I wonder.”

At Rei’s words, Marina, who had been drinking wine, interjected.

Hearing this, Rei, Ara, and the others turned their gaze toward her. Marina set her wine glass back on the table before continuing.

“If Rei isn’t here, wouldn’t they just make even more of a commotion to get him to come back?”

“If that happens, Vihera… can you handle it?”

“Yes, leave it to me. I know a lot of the guards, so I get that kind of information a step ahead of everyone else. Who knows, maybe the issue with the Red Cloth gang will be resolved before Rei even gets back?”

Since Vihera had handled so many patrol requests, it was only natural that she interacted frequently with the guards.

...Though, given Vihera’s looks and style, it wasn’t uncommon for her to get hit on, so she was also close to the guards for that reason.

Given who Vihera was, saying she could resolve the matter with the Red Cloth people while Rei was away was by no means just her putting on a brave face.

She had the ability to do it, and with the information she could get from the guards, it was entirely feasible.

Upon hearing Vihera’s words, Rei wore a slightly frustrated expression.

This incident involving the “Red Cloth”—who had almost certainly been targeting him—was something he had wanted to resolve himself, if possible.

However, as long as he had a promise with Elena, he couldn’t do such a thing.

As they watched Rei’s face, the last night they would all eat together—at least for this year—slipped away.

The next morning, as winter had already set in, it was still dark outside even after the six o’clock bell rang.

And because it was winter, the number of people trying to leave Gilm had dwindled; aside from Rei, Elena, and Ara—who had joined them here—there were fewer than ten people going through the departure procedures.

Naturally, there were far fewer guards than there had been just a short while ago, and despite the small number of people, the procedures took a little longer than expected.

“Rei, how far do you plan to go today?”

“It’s hard to say exactly. I guess we’ll just go as far as we can.”

Watching Set and Yellow play together, Rei answered Elena’s question.

Naturally, Elena stood out in a place like this, especially since she wasn’t riding in a carriage, but because there were so few people around, it didn’t cause much of a stir.

“Rei, I did bring some packed lunches just in case…”

Ara said this, but she wasn’t carrying any luggage in her hands.

Well, she was carrying her Power Axe on her back, but that was all.

Seeing Ara like that, Rei concludes that the packed lunches must be inside Elena’s magic pouch.

“In that case, maybe we can eat those today. Though, since it’s winter, they probably won’t go bad right away.”

Unlike an item box, time still passes inside a magic pouch even when it’s stored away.

That’s why, in the summer, the food would go bad by the end of the day at worst, but since it’s winter now, there’s no need to worry about that.

“Hmm. Since Marina made it, I’m fine with that.”

“Oh, so it’s Marina’s cooking?”

Elena and Ara had been staying at Marina’s house until today.

That’s probably why Marina made lunch boxes for Elena and Ara.

Even as they were having this conversation, the procedures for leaving Gilm were moving forward, and eventually, Rei and the others finished their paperwork.

The guards were slightly surprised that Rei and Elena were leaving the city, but Rei had left Gilm countless times before.

They were surprised that Elena and Ara were with him, but they figured such things happen, and the formalities concluded smoothly without any particular issues.

Once they were outside Gilm, Rei took the Set Cage from the Misty Ring and simply walked away.

A few hours after Rei left Gilm... a heavy silence hung in the room.

“So, what happened to Cherish?”

At Carles’s question, a man spoke up.

“She’s at the guard post, so it’d be impossible with those guys in red robes.”

“You’re right. Those guys may be numerous, but they’re too weak. Hey, so can I go?”

A small man, a sharp dagger in hand, asks Carles.

But Carles thought for a moment at the man’s words, then shook his head.

“No, if this weren’t Gilm, it might have been fine, but this is Gilm. The guards are highly trained, so it’s best to hold off.”

“Whaaaat?! Wait a minute—are you saying you think I’d lose to some guards!?”

“I wouldn’t put it that way. But our primary objective is, after all, to investigate Crimson’s personality. If we accidentally cause harm to the guards here, it’ll make things much more difficult for us going forward.”

Hearing that, Carles couldn’t argue.

Seeing that the short man seemed convinced, Carles turned to another man and asked.

“So, what’s Crimson up to right now?”

“Ah… Apparently, she left Gilm early this morning. I heard the Princess General and her attendant were with her too.”

Even upon hearing this, Carles merely nodded without showing any particular reaction.

“I see. Judging by Crimson’s past behavior, she’s probably gone monster hunting. We’ve also heard she has a hobby of collecting magic stones.”

The reason Carles concluded that Rei would return soon was that there was no information suggesting he was traveling with heavy luggage.

Also, the fact that Elena—a symbol of the Noble Faction—wasn’t even using a carriage was a significant factor.

Naturally, since Carles had been investigating Rei, he was also aware of the Set Cage.

However, he never imagined that Elena, the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, would attempt to return to the Duchy of Kerebel without even using a carriage.

If word of that had been spreading around town, Carles might have realized the significance of Elena and her companions’ actions, but unfortunately, Elena’s return home hadn’t become the subject of gossip.

Elena had explained the situation to Daskar and the nobles she knew, but that information hadn’t reached Carles.

That is precisely why Carles concluded that Rei and the others had gone out to hunt monsters or something of the sort.

Carles’s misfortune was that Rei and the others were such outliers that they would take such actions.

Normally, someone in the position of a duke’s daughter would travel in a luxurious carriage.

Based on that common-sense assumption, Carles overlooked Rei and Elena’s actions… and ultimately, even as tonight turned into tomorrow, and the day after that, and the day after that, and beyond—Rei and Elena still did not return to Gilm. It was only then that he finally realized something was wrong, but that is a story for a little later.

Never imagining things had turned out that way, Carles assumed Rei would return as early as today.

Well, he did know that Rei had a magic tent, so he couldn’t completely rule out the possibility that he might be staying overnight, even in winter.

“So, what are we going to do now? Even if we’re going to investigate Crimson, we can’t do it without Rei…”

One of the men said this, but Carles shook his head silently.

“Even if we’re investigating Crimson, it’s not like she has to be here herself. No, in fact, being able to get an objective opinion is actually valuable.”

“So, does that mean we’ll be putting the guys who’ve been quietly gathering intel so far to work more actively?”

“Yes. In exchange, would it be difficult to have the Red Cloth people scale back their activities…?”

“That’d be difficult. To begin with, we aren’t actually controlling those guys; we’ve merely given them a little nudge to make them act that way. You of all people, Carles, should know that best, considering this whole situation.”

At those words, Carles let out a wry smile.

To be honest, there was no doubt that Carles had come up with the red cloth plan, but he never imagined it would go this smoothly.

Even those who couldn’t commit crimes on their own could do so if they banded together and acted as a group.

When such people gather, even more extreme crimes tend to occur.

He had anticipated that this would likely happen, and similar results had emerged when he’d used a similar tactic before.

But… even for Carles, this incident exceeded his expectations.

“I suppose so. I guess there were more hot-blooded individuals than I expected.”

“If this were the usual Gilm, things might have gone exactly as Carles planned. But I guess the expansion project brought in too many extra people.”

“If we hadn’t been doing the expansion work, there’s no doubt that even if we’d handed out the red cloth—the symbol of our fellowship—it wouldn’t have gone this smoothly. This is something that could only have been achieved because of the current situation.”

With Carles wrapping things up like that, no one voiced any further complaints.

That said, just because they weren’t voicing their complaints didn’t mean they had any intention of doing anything about the people wearing the red cloth—they were counting on the guards to handle that… or so was the honest sentiment of Carles and his group.

Besides, if the guards got busy dealing with the Red Cloth crowd, it would make it easier to rescue Cherish, who was being held at the guard post… or, worst-case scenario, to silence her.

Coming to this conclusion, Carles quietly gave instructions on the plan to gather information on Rei.
