Upon arriving beneath the World Tree, Rei and Set witnessed a scene completely beyond their expectations.

While heading here, they had seen the upper leaves of the World Tree withering, but what Rei actually saw upon arriving beneath it was a scene where many more leaves had withered beyond just the top. And above all else...

"What the hell is that...?"

At the end of his gaze, something enormous clung to the World Tree's trunk.

Its size was about twenty meters in length, and its width was nearly ten meters at its thickest points.

Though Rei uttered the question, he himself knew what it was... or rather, he knew of a similar existence.

Back when Rei was still in Japan, he had seen its form every year without fail.

And the enormous hole gouged at the base of the World Tree pointed to where this thing had come from, confirming Rei's suspicion.

It was a being that appeared every summer. It could be called a summer tradition, though it wasn't normally this enormous.

"...A cicada?"

Yes. What lay before Rei's gaze was undoubtedly a giant cicada.

It had ten legs—five on each side—and something resembling a tail, yet its original form was unmistakably that of a cicada... or rather, it was currently in the pupal stage, preparing to become a cicada, so strictly speaking, it wasn't quite one yet.

Of course, the creature before her eyes wasn't a genuine cicada. At least, to Rei's knowledge, no cicada twenty meters long existed.

Cicadas become vulnerable during metamorphosis, so they generally avoid emerging during daylight hours.

It's common for them to emerge in the evening or at night, when predators like ants and bees become inactive.

(Well, a cicada this size probably wouldn't be threatened by ants or bees anyway.)

Seeing the giant cicada pupa clinging to the tree trunk, Rei wondered what to do.

The enormous hole in the ground and the giant cicada pupa clinging to the tree trunk.

Seeing these two things made it crystal clear to Rei what had caused the World Tree's disease that had summoned him here in the first place.

After all, cicada nymphs grow by sucking sap from tree roots underground.

(Well, questions remain, like how it reached beneath the World Tree while the barrier was active...)

It looked like a cicada on the verge of emerging, but a cicada twenty meters long was simply impossible.

Rei's guess was that it was undoubtedly a monster.

(So, the reason the World Tree was losing its magic and growing weaker and weaker must be because this thing was sucking it dry from the roots. But... what to do? Killing it now probably wouldn't be that difficult, but doing so would also damage the World Tree.)

Holding the Death Scythe, he agonized over what to do.

The pupa clung tightly to the World Tree's trunk with five legs on each side. The bark was peeled away, and the tips of the five legs pierced deep into the trunk.

Worse still, despite being in its pupal state, it was even sucking sap flowing through the World Tree's veins from its legs.

Given how firmly it clung to the World Tree, attacking the pupa carelessly would undoubtedly damage the tree itself.

(The World Tree really is the most troublesome part, isn't it?)

Rei's gaze shifted to the World Tree, then to the glowing orb floating right beside him.

Its flickering light was gradually growing weaker, and to Rei, it felt like a sign that the World Tree was weakening.

(There's no time. The quickest way to attack would be from the inside...)

Thinking of an internal attack, the first spell that flashed through Rei's mind was 'Dancing Flame Serpent'.

A vicious spell that spawns a fiery serpent within the enemy, burning them from the inside out.

But... the snake was made of fire itself, possessing enough power to potentially inflict significant damage on the World Tree.

A weakened version might avoid that concern, but then it might struggle to inflict damage on a twenty-meter-long giant cicada pupa.

(Then...)

Next, Rei's gaze turned to Set, who was nearby, letting out a threatening cry at the giant cicada pupa.

A Power Crush, with its rock-shattering force, wouldn't risk spreading the flames. It wouldn't...

(Those ten legs the pupa has firmly embedded into the World Tree are a problem. If we carelessly blast that pupa away, the force would likely transfer directly to the legs piercing the World Tree, potentially causing significant damage to it. So then... would attacking immediately after it emerges be better?)

Even so, the World Tree's leaves continue to wither rapidly even now, while it remains in its cocoon state.

If I wait until it fully emerges, the World Tree will undoubtedly suffer major damage.

The light sphere floating beside Rei was also urging me to do something quickly! Even though its light had grown faint, it was still flickering violently.

Considering its desperation, this was no situation where waiting for the pupa to emerge was an option.

After a moment's thought, he realized his current position was directly in front of the pupa clinging to the World Tree.

"Right... If I can just inflict some damage from the side..."

Fortunately, among Rei's attack methods, there was spear throwing, capable of pinpoint strikes, and above all, the Flying Slash attack.

With its significantly increased power compared to before, the current Flying Slash might be able to cleave the giant cicada in two from the side while it was still in its pupa state.

Rei couldn't clearly see what was inside the chrysalis now, but if things went well, it might be possible to take out the giant cicada in one go before it could fully emerge as an adult.

Reaching this conclusion, Rei moved alongside the World Tree with Set and the light sphere... to a position where he could target the pupa from the side.

"In that case, it was fortunate it couldn't move while in its pupa state."

"Gururu!"

Set growled at Rei's words, while the light orb flickered, signaling its own intent.

The faint sounds of the Dark Elves fighting could be heard in the distance, but there was nothing they could do about it now.

All Rei could do now was kill the giant cicada as quickly as possible and rush to their aid.

"Phew... Alright."

Taking a small, deep breath, Rei's hand gripped a spear retrieved from the Misty Ring.

He hesitated slightly over whether to choose the Flying Slash or the spear throw, but ultimately opted for the spear throw, which allowed for a more precise strike.

"Set, cover me if anything happens."

"Guruu!"

Set growled in response to Rei's words, as if saying, Leave it to me!

Trusting him, Rei stroked his head with his free hand, then fixed a sharp gaze on the chrysalis.

He firmly checked the feel of the spear shaft in his hand, then took a few paces to build momentum, adding a twist to his body... and hurled it with all his might.

The spear sliced through the air at a speed that would be impossible for any ordinary person to track with their eyes.

True to Rei's aim, it headed for what could be called the pupa's flank... but the moment it closed to within about three meters, it was deflected as if hitting an invisible wall.

"...What?"

Rei muttered, still holding the spear he'd just thrown, but he understood perfectly well what had just happened.

In other words, his attack had been blocked by some kind of barrier.

"A barrier field, huh? ...No, it's no wonder it can use a barrier similar to the World Tree's, having grown by sucking its sap... but a barrier powerful enough to block my attack?"

Though he harbored doubts, seeing the light sphere flickering more violently than ever made it clear the World Tree had almost no time left.

He decided to save the thinking for later; attacking the pupa was the priority now. What he drew from the Misty Ring wasn't a new spear... but the Death Scythe.

Fortunately, after the series of battles in the forest last night, he had used the Flying Slash numerous times and had become somewhat proficient with it.

It wasn't perfect yet, merely somewhat proficient.

But given its barrier-like abilities, the Flying Slash was the optimal means to attack from a distance, even if it carried some risk.

(If this doesn't work, I'll have to switch to a direct attack instead of ranged... But since I don't know what abilities it possesses, I really hope this can damage it... Please work...)

He poured his will into the Death Scythe, assumed his stance... and swung down.

"Flying Slash!"

The flying slash released with that word streaked straight toward the giant cicada.

Its trajectory would miss the World Tree, yet strike the giant cicada.

If it's a Flying Slash with increased power... That's what Rei was thinking as he watched, but the next instant, a barrier identical to the one that had blocked the spear throw moments before materialized, deflecting the Flying Slash.

"No way."

He had considered the possibility of it being blocked, but he had been certain the Flying Slash would ultimately get through.

But in the end, the Flying Slash was blocked by the barrier's protective field, unable to inflict any damage on the pupa.

"Even if you say you're using a barrier spell, isn't this barrier way too hard?"

"Grrrr..."

Set growled in agreement with Rei's words.

The barrier shrouding the Dark Elf settlement seemed hard enough that Rei could probably break it with a bit of effort.

Indeed, the Flying Slash he just unleashed should have easily... well, not exactly easily, but he felt that if he fired a few more shots, he could probably manage something.

That's precisely why he couldn't understand why the same barrier-like ability had blocked him so completely.

"Could it be... that the giant cicada wields the barrier spell more skillfully than the World Tree?"

The moment the words slipped out, the light sphere hovering beside Rei began flickering more violently than ever before.

It felt almost like a protest directed at Rei.

Rei had been completely unaware, but the reason the giant cicada monster, which sucked the sap from the World Tree, had grown so powerful was due to Rei's magic.

Rei, who had been draining nutrients like sap and magic from the roots of the World Tree, had absorbed the magic power he poured into it. That influence had made it into such a formidable being.

In other words, without Rei's magic, it would never have attained such formidable power.

But the current Rei was completely oblivious to the light sphere's state, focused solely on defeating it now.

That said, there was no trace of despair in Rei's expression.

It was extremely disappointing that long-range attacks weren't working, but that didn't mean he had no options left.

"I'd rather avoid close combat if possible,"

The words slipped from Rei's lips as he gripped the Death Scythe.

Honestly, given the barrier's protective power, he'd preferred to finish it off with a long-range attack.

Still, the fact that there were ways to handle this meant the situation wasn't quite that desperate.

"...You stay back a bit. It might be dangerous if things keep going like this."

At Rei's words, the light sphere flickered several times before drifting away.

Watching it go, Rei's gaze shifted to Set.

"Set, sorry, but I need you to come with me."

"Gururuu, guruu, gurururuu!"

At Rei's words, Set shook his head.

Rei understood what that meant.

If close combat was inevitable, he was saying he'd do it instead of Rei.

Like Rei's Flying Slash, Set's Power Crush possessed tremendous destructive force.

Level-wise, it hadn't yet reached the level five where Flying Slash became exceptionally powerful, but thanks to Set's own physical prowess and the effect of the magic item, the Bracelet of Might, its raw destructive force surpassed Rei's Flying Slash.

Though, as the name implies, Flying Slash is a flying slash attack, so in terms of the destructive power of a single strike, it isn't that powerful.

"No. I'll handle it."

"Grrr..."

Set growled sadly at Rei's firm declaration.

Then, with his round eyes, he stared intently at Rei.

"......"

He held that gaze for several seconds. Finally, Rei sighed, patted Set's head, and spoke.

"Alright, alright. Then attack after me. Count on you for the follow-up strike."

"Guruu!"

Unable to resist Set's gaze and deciding it was pointless to waste more time here, Rei spoke. Set responded with a pleased growl.

"Alright, let's go!"

"GRRRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set growled bravely.

And so, Set followed after Rei as he kicked off the ground.

"Wooooooooooooooh!"

The pupa's form rapidly approached, kicking off the ground.

He activated Sleipnir's Boots, leaping one step, then two, into the air.

He channeled magic into the Death Scythe he gripped, fully prepared to unleash its vicious sharpness and the devastating weight of a single strike.

But... the moment he saw the rapidly approaching giant cicada pupa, Rei noticed a small, slit-like opening on its back.

(Dammit, this is way too early!)

He inwardly cursed, realizing it was already preparing to shed its skin... Yet he couldn't stop. Rei swung the Death Scythe down, aiming for the pupa.
