Rei and Ilse were brought by Dahl to a tavern.

The moment they realized it, Rei and Ilse couldn't help but think, "Another tavern..." but Dahl paid them no mind and walked straight inside.

Having come this far, Rei and Ilse knew there was no option to return without doing anything here.

After telling them to play nearby as usual, Set also entered the tavern.

Once inside the tavern, Dahl didn't sit at a nearby table among the few patrons present. Instead, he headed toward the back of the establishment.

Naturally, the two followed him.

(I see. This place is probably one of those guys' hideouts.)

Rei made this assumption as he followed into the back.

Indeed, several rooms at the back of the tavern contained faces Rei had seen before, confirming his suspicion.

"This is it, come in. ...It's a dirty place, though."

The room Dahl led him into was about six tatami mats in size.

A table and chairs stood in the center, with just a window in the wall... a rather stark room.

Dahl had called it dirty, but it wasn't covered in dust or spider webs.

"I'll go get us something to drink. Please wait here. There are a few others I need to explain things to, so I'll go get them."

With that, Dahl left the room.

Watching him leave, only Rei and Ilse remained in the room.

With nothing particular to do, they sat down on the chairs, figuring standing around was pointless.

"Rei. What do you think Ajax is thinking... no, what do you think he's planning?"

Perhaps because there was no other topic, Ilse asked Rei that question.

But even when asked, Rei had no words to answer.

"I wonder. Honestly, I'm not quite sure. Considering the intelligence unit is moving like this, it's certain he's plotting something... but what exactly that is, I don't have any information either."

For Rei, even if Ajax was plotting something, crushing the plot along with him would suffice.

That was his mindset.

But considering the intelligence unit was on the move, it was definitely more than just a minor misdeed...

As Rei and Ilse spoke, a knock sounded at the door. Darl appeared, carrying a tray with a glass of drink and a plate of bite-sized sandwiches.

Ilse, who had eaten and drunk a little at the tavern when she spoke with Melan, wasn't particularly tempted, but Rei reached for the sandwiches on the table without a second thought.

"Now then... I'd like to get straight to the point. Is that alright?"

At Darl's words, Rei and Ilse nodded in unison. ...Though Rei was eating his sandwich as he did so.

"Thank you. But, well... where should I start? Ah, right. The reason this whole thing became important was because we looked into that Ajax fellow."

"Well, that makes sense. If you weren't interested in the first place, you wouldn't have looked into it. And if you hadn't, it would have been difficult to discover what Ajax was planning, even if they were up to something."

"That's right. ...At first, there weren't any particular problems. Well, we're short-staffed right now, so we couldn't spare many people to handle it."

In reality, Egg only assigned one person to investigate Ajax.

But... it's also true that this one person produced significant results in this case.

There was no doubt that person was investigating the Ajax matter, but naturally, it was impossible for one person alone to investigate it perfectly.

That's precisely why the man decided to use an external organization.

Fortunately, the intelligence unit was a covert branch within the Daskar-led organization, and they maintained friendly relations with some external groups.

After Egg's subordinates had done their initial investigation, the follow-up was entrusted to that external organization... but... Since that external organization had its base in the slums, it detected suspicious activity from one of the other organizations also based in the same slums.

...Yes. They were forcibly abducting women who had wandered into the slums from among those drawn to Gilm by the expansion construction.

Worse still, they were even using people connected to the slums to deliberately lure women into the slums.

Of course, that organization knew they were walking a dangerous line.

But they had their reasons—a major deal was on the line. More importantly, to avoid arousing suspicion among Gilm's residents, they restricted their targets to only those who came to Gilm for the expansion work.

In the end, though, the organization was caught out anyway.

An organization friendly with the intelligence unit obtained that information and gathered more to sell to the intelligence unit at a high price... While there was some damage, connections to people like Ajax, Levjef, and Haston surfaced.

Upon learning of this incident, Egg naturally focused his efforts on investigating Ajax.

Admittedly, the intelligence unit was already stretched thin under current circumstances, so the investigation took some time... but regardless, it became clear Ajax was attempting to abduct numerous women, and that presented a dilemma.

Understandably so. Since the investigation into Ajax was originally requested by Marina—or more accurately, by Rei—and wasn't particularly hidden, the intelligence unit could easily gather information.

In other words, depending on the circumstances, it could potentially lead to antagonizing Rei.

It wouldn't be strange if someone thought that.

To prevent that, the intelligence unit decided to approach Rei.

Hearing the explanation from the man's mouth, Rei and Ilse each wore different expressions.

Rei looked satisfied... while Ilse looked at the man from the unit before her with sympathy.

"So, what Ajax is planning is to take the women away from Gilm. ...Make them slaves or something?"

"Yes, that seems highly likely. If it were just one or two, we might think Ajax and his men were acting on personal desire, but based on our intelligence, there are over twenty... no, closer to thirty."

"In that case, it's probably not just that Ajax and his men are simple womanizers..."

"No."

Dahl nodded in agreement with Rei's words.

Though only the two of them were having this exchange, Ilse listened to their conversation with an expression of displeasure.

It wasn't that she disliked Rei or Dahl.

As a woman, she couldn't hide her disgust at what the Ajax were doing.

"...I see. Well, that's fine. But why did you contact us? The fact that you might clash with us means you must have found some kind of clue in this matter, right?"

"Yes. Actually, thanks to you, Rei... yes, thanks to you, the three of us Ajax can no longer stay in Gilm indefinitely."

She probably wanted to say it wasn't thanks to Rei, but because of Rei.

Watching Darl's expression, Rei spoke up.

"I see. So it's because we contacted Ajax."

"Yes. If Ilse-san had been the only one we contacted, they wouldn't have moved so hastily. In fact, Ilse-san might have been targeted herself."

"That's probably right."

Ilse couldn't deny what Dahl said.

Beautiful, yet lacking in true strength.

Such a person, for whatever reason—or rather, for reasons she didn't want to know—was nothing but prime prey for Ajax.

Of course, it wasn't Ajax themselves who were directly gathering the women, but an organization they dealt with.

"Anyway, that's why Ajax and his crew are trying to speed up their next deal with the organization. ...No, it would be more accurate to say they were trying to speed it up."

Dahl seemed unaware of the information that Rei and Ilse had applied direct pressure to Ajax... but even so, as an intelligence operative, he vaguely understood the situation from the current circumstances.

"Ah... right. Ilse, what should we do?"

Rei asked if it was okay to tell Ilse about the situation.

Ilse considered Rei's words for a moment before nodding.

"Yes, it's fine."

Nodding, Ilse began to explain her situation.

Five years ago, her parents and older brother, who were traveling merchants, were killed by their escort, Ajax.

She became an adventurer to seek revenge.

...How she came to Gilm, where the expansion had brought new life and crowds, and found Ajax.

Hearing this, Dahl's expression showed understanding.

Why would someone like Ilse, who was clearly not cut out for rough work, be targeting someone like Ajax? Because there was a plausible reason for it.

"I see. ...In that case, if things go badly, she might try to escape from Gilm tonight."

"But escape... through somewhere other than the main gate? So, the expansion site?"

"Unfortunately, I don't know about that part either. But since it's an illegal organization, it wouldn't be surprising if they had some kind of connection, or even a loophole we don't know about."

"Isn't that impossible because of the barrier?"

"I agree, but nothing is absolute. There's a very real possibility they could find a way we never imagined..."

Seeing Dahl mutter with obvious irritation, Rei couldn't help but agree.

Such organizations probably have some means at their disposal.

"So, did we rush things a bit?"

He couldn't say he didn't think so, but they only learned what Ajax was specifically planning now.

Expecting us to foresee that beforehand was unreasonable.

No, someone more sharp-witted might have been able to foresee it.

But... unfortunately, such feats were beyond Rei's capabilities.

"I suppose so. But I suppose that can't be helped."

Dahl replied to Rei's words.

In truth, even the intelligence unit took time to uncover Ajax's true identity and discern his scheme.

If they had been abducting original Gilm residents, it would have become a topic of discussion much sooner.

But all those taken away were people who had come to Gilm after the expansion work began.

That's why word inevitably spread so slowly.

(Huh? Women going missing...)

Suddenly, he recalled a story he'd heard a few days ago from a party called Rain of Mercy.

One of the female members of that party had also gone missing.

That's what I'd been told.

"Ah... Mercy Rain... I forgot her name, but they said one of their women went missing too. Could it be her?"

"Rain of Mercy, you say? Hmm..."

Dahl thought for a moment at Rei's words before speaking.

"Ah, yes. There was a report filed with the security squad about a woman named Ryusha going missing. Based on our investigation, we believe it's highly likely she was caught up in this incident as well."

"I figured as much."

Hearing Dahl's words, he felt a little disappointed, precisely because he had expected it.

This incident would likely... no, definitely cause a major uproar.

If the commotion arose to prevent the actions of those involved, including Ajax, it was only natural that the abducted women could also suffer harm.

Even adventurers, after being captured for several days,

Their physical condition couldn't be considered optimal, and being held captive, they likely had no weapons.

Given that, even if Rei and the others tried to make a move, it would be difficult to capitalize on it.

...Though in reality, they were restrained by slave collars, preventing any resistance.

Considering the value of slave collars, neither Rei nor... nor Darl and the others had realized such items could be produced in large quantities.

Though Egg, who led the intelligence unit, and those around him hadn't dismissed the possibility.

Even so, preparing slave collars for the number of missing individuals would require a small fortune.

The profits gained from taking such a risk wouldn't be that great.

That's precisely why Egg and his group believed either no slave collars had been prepared, or if they had, only a handful at most...

This was likely where they misjudged the seriousness of whoever was giving orders to Ajax and the others.

"Anyway, it'd be problematic if Rei and the others were found right now. Could you leave the watch over there to us? Of course, we'll notify you immediately if they make any moves."

At those words, Rei looked at Ilse.

Ilse considered Rei's gaze for a few seconds—a gaze that implicitly conveyed "Leave it to us"—before finally nodding.
