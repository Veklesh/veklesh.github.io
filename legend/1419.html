Having visited the lord's manor many times before, Set seemed quite accustomed to the place as he parted ways with Rei and the others and headed toward the garden.

Meanwhile, Rei and the others were being led to the office by a maid serving Daskar.

"There are a lot of people here too."

The number of people walking the corridors was significantly different from when Rei had visited before.

(Probably because they're limiting the number of people inside the building at once, letting them out and replacing them with other merchants.)

As he walked down the hallway, casually observing his surroundings, the maid turned back to Rei, smiled, and spoke.

"These days, merchants come almost every day. Some are from guilds I've never even heard of, while others are the kind everyone knows by name..."

"I suppose so."

With so many people gathered, it was only natural that all sorts were coming—from large guilds to individual merchants working on their own.

(Still, the ones here are probably those who weren't originally chosen for this expansion project...)

As Rei pondered this, several merchants caught sight of Rei and her companions, their eyes widening in surprise.

If Set were here, the attention would likely be on him, but right now, it was focused solely on Marina and Vihera.

Rei, wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, and the child Byune did draw some glances... but they certainly weren't attracting that much attention.

(Well, considering how these two look, it's only natural.)

On one hand, a Dark Elf—a race so rare even in a Gilm where many gather, they wouldn't number more than a handful. On the other, a figure clad in thin garments that made them look like a prostitute or dancer.

And both were beauties the likes of which one might see only once in a lifetime. It was impossible for them not to draw attention.

That said, for Marina and Vihera, such stares were nothing new, so they didn't seem particularly bothered.

The maid guiding Rei and the others observed the pair, showing a hint of surprise and admiration as she led them toward the office.

Several merchants attempted to address Marina and Vihera, but the aura emanating from the pair prevented any of them from speaking.

And so, without anyone managing to speak to them, Rei and the others arrived at Daskar's office...

"Don't you dare, you fool! Do you really think such a plan will be approved?!"

Just as the maid reached out to knock on the door—a door worthy of being called a work of art—such a shout erupted from within the office.

(Something's up.)

Rei, Marina, and Vihera exchanged glances, each thinking the same thing.

...As for Byune, she remained expressionless as ever, silently staring at the door.

Less than ten seconds after the shout, a male merchant in his mid-twenties emerged from the office.

No, it would be more accurate to say he practically fled.

He flung the door open and sprinted past Rei and the others.

His expression was one of sheer desperation, as if he feared he would be killed if he stayed inside the office any longer... that thought was clearly written on his face.

But the maid seemed completely oblivious to the man's state, calling out toward the office.

"Excuse me, Master. I've brought Lord Rei and the others."

"...Ah, let them in."

Hearing that voice, the maid ushered Rei and the others into the office.

What Rei and the others saw upon entering the office was the figure of Daskar, his expression clearly displeased.

There was no doubt the cause of his displeasure was related to the shouting they'd heard earlier.

(I thought the people here were sharp-eyed merchants... but apparently, even sharp-eyed ones can say things that make others angry. ...Come to think of it, that's only natural.)

Watching Daskar, Rei thought this, careful not to let it show on his face.

"I heard they call you Daskar-sama at the guild?"

"Ah, yes. ...Sit down, anyway. We can't talk standing up. Hey, bring something to nibble on."

"Yes."

The maid bowed at Daskar's words and left the room.

Daskar then moved from his desk, piled high with documents, to the guest sofa.

"You seemed pretty angry, didn't you?"

Once everyone was seated on the sofa, Marina asked Daskar.

If he were one of Daskar's subordinates, he wouldn't have dared speak to him while he was this angry.

But to Marina, Daskar was like a troublesome younger brother.

Or perhaps a childhood friend she used to play with when they were little...

That's precisely why she could casually speak to him like this, even when Daskar, with his stern face, looked displeased.

Daskar, seeing Marina like this, didn't get particularly angry either. Still looking displeased, he opened his mouth.

"Yeah, they suggested lowering the quality of the materials for the extension by one... or maybe two grades."

"...That's enough to make anyone angry."

If this weren't the frontier, that proposal might have been worth considering.

Of course, even in ordinary villages or towns, one wouldn't cut corners on materials when expanding.

After all, monsters existed, even if not as densely as around Gilm, and above all, to guard against not monsters but people... thieves and the like, cutting corners in that area was simply not permitted.

Given that situation, it was only natural that Gilm paid considerable attention to the quality of materials used for extensions.

And yet, the merchant who had just stormed out of this office showed no concern whatsoever, suggesting they lower the quality of the materials.

This was utterly unacceptable to Daskar... and the result was the angry outburst Rei and the others had heard.

"Well, I can see his point. The materials being used for this expansion are top-grade... and among top-grade materials, they're particularly superior. They're of a quality rarely used even in the royal capital under normal circumstances, right? It's no wonder a merchant would set his sights on them."

"We're procuring those materials precisely because they're necessary. ...If we cut corners here, we'll be the ones crying about it later. Besides..."

Daskar stopped speaking, but the sound of knocking echoed through the room, interrupting him.

Daskar ordered them to enter, and the maid from earlier, along with several others, brought in tea and sandwiches for everyone.

The sandwiches appeared freshly made, the aroma of hot meat wafting from several fillings.

Sandwiches are typically meant to be eaten cold. The bread is usually left to rest with the fillings for a while to let the flavors meld... to mature, as it were.

But the sandwiches now on the table were warm, made with freshly cooked ingredients.

You could say they were made to be eaten immediately after preparation.

Delicious, but the fillings would taste bad once cooled... perhaps it should be described as a sandwich with a short shelf life for its deliciousness.

The maids left, Daskar told them to eat for now, and Rei and Byune reached for the sandwiches.

A moment later, Marina, Vihera, and Daskar also reached for their sandwiches.

The one Rei picked up was made with some kind of grilled meat and vegetables sandwiched between bread... a dish worthy of being called a steak sandwich.

Following Daskar's instruction to choose the most easily handheld option, all the sandwiches were bite-sized.

While eating these sandwiches, Daskar spoke again.

"Well, putting that aside for now. I called you all... or rather, Rei specifically, because I wanted to ask you for a favor."

Having eaten the sandwiches and changed his mood, he seemed somewhat calmer now.

Daskar set aside his earlier anger for the moment and spoke.

"For me?"

"Yeah. ...Originally, I only called Rei... Well, I did expect Marina and the others to come along."

He glanced briefly at Marina, but she merely responded with a seductive smile.

As if to say it was only natural she was here.

Vihera wore a similar smile, while Marina was engrossed in eating her sandwich.

Observing the members of Crimson Wings, Daskar sighed resignedly before speaking.

"Actually, I called Rei here because it relates to that recent material theft incident."

"Ah, that."

The mission was to recover the materials stolen by someone posing as bandits—likely someone's private soldiers—and apprehend the culprits.

Beyond that, the quantity of stolen materials exceeded what Bistor, the intensely eccentric merchant who commissioned Rei, had transported.

Since those materials wouldn't serve any particular purpose even if Rei kept them, they were sold off through the guild to Gilm... or more accurately, to Daskar.

As Daskar mentioned earlier, every single piece of material used in this expansion project is top-grade.

For that very reason, although payment hasn't been made yet, when Rei receives his reward for the expansion work, it will undoubtedly be a substantial sum.

"That's right. Since Rei and the others brought back nearly all of them as prisoners, interrogation was a breeze."

What kind of interrogation took place, even Rei doesn't know.

But it's safe to assume that many of those who underwent interrogation ended up in a miserable state.

(If they'd just spilled the beans, they probably wouldn't have been treated so badly.)

That sort of thing had nothing to do with Rei and the others who captured them, and above all, he had no interest in it.

Well, he was interested in the information extracted from the prisoners.

"So, who were they ultimately affiliated with? To maintain that many private soldiers, it must be a fairly large merchant guild... or perhaps only nobility could afford it."

"A noble."

Marina grimaced at the blunt answer.

Though she'd mentioned a major merchant house, Marina had probably already guessed as much.

"Right. I wonder if the Royal Faction is getting desperate too."

"No."

Daskar immediately shook his head at Marina's words.

"Huh? No... then, could it be—"

"Yeah. The Noble Faction is the one pulling the strings behind this whole thing."

"...Really? You mean it's not that the prisoners are manipulating the information like that?"

Could this be a scheme to pit the Neutral Faction against the Noble Faction, to drain their forces, funds, and supplies?

Daskar shook his head again at Marina's statement.

"No, I naturally suspected that too. But we interrogated each one individually, from different angles. If they could all give answers without contradiction... well, they wouldn't be wasting their time as private soldiers. They could be working somewhere with much better conditions."

"Right. But what were they thinking doing something like that? Honestly, I don't quite understand... well, not that I don't understand at all, but it looks like nothing but suicide."

"I agree, but it's not uncommon for even among the Noble Faction to have those who can't control their emotions with reason. There's a possibility of such a renegade."

Those words made sense to Rei too.

No, perhaps it was precisely because Rei had seen such nobles countless times before that he understood.

"I see. I understand the situation... but what do you want me to do now that you've called me?"

"I want you to take revenge."

"...Huh? Are you sure?"

Clear evidence had been secured in the form of a prisoner.

But even so, this faction is different from the Neutral Faction Daskar... It's larger than the Neutral Faction Daskar, and moreover, it's currently trying to build friendly relations.

Moreover, for Rei, the Noble Faction wasn't just someone else's problem—it was the faction Elena belonged to.

Yet, Daskar simply nodded in response to Rei's question.

"Fine. If you accept the request, I'll tell you who to retaliate against. You can handle it however you like, but please avoid killing anyone if possible—injuries aside."

"But the merchant who was attacked... people died. You still insist?"

"Even so."

Rei didn't believe in pretty ideals like all human lives being equal.

Even so, being told so bluntly like this wasn't exactly pleasant.

Daskar must have anticipated this reaction from Rei. With a manly grin, he continued.

"What I'm saying is, the absolute priority is avoiding casualties. Put another way, anything else is fair game. ...Though going too far is a problem."

Hearing Daskar's words, Rei nodded with a satisfied look and a mischievous grin.

At this point, Rei had already half decided to accept Daskar's request.
