Originally, it was thought that Rei and the others wouldn't return until the next day... or worse, might be gone for several days. But unexpectedly, they came back that very day and were naturally welcomed by everyone.

Setting aside the meals, sleeping in a carriage or on the ground was probably something they simply couldn't stand.

As if yielding to the expectant gazes, Rei produced the sand ship.

Having nearly a thousand people sleep inside the sand ship meant it was, of course, quite cramped.

Still, it was vastly more comfortable and far better for recovery than sleeping seated in a carriage or exposed to wind and rain on the ground without shelter.

Moreover, having slept in the sand ships throughout the journey so far, they were accustomed to them.

For that very reason, Rei's return was welcomed.

Of course, the welcome wasn't solely about the sand ships.

The presence of Set, who could easily be called the spiritual pillar; Rei, Elena, and Marina, each possessing strength that could rival Vihera; and the anticipation that tomorrow's breakfast, if nothing else, would be a delicious meal.

All these feelings combined made Rei and the others' return a huge celebration.

"I never imagined we'd be welcomed this warmly... though it seems most are just after the Sand Ship."

Women scrambled inside the sand ship, trying to secure even a decent place to sleep.

Women swarming around Set.

Watching them roughly split into these two groups, Rei murmured.

"Heh heh, it can't be helped. Just knowing that Rei wasn't here made the trip today feel pretty gloomy."

Seeing that Rei's busyness had settled down, Gime Kara smiled and spoke up.

"It wasn't me, it was Set, wasn't it?"

"That included, I think it was thanks to Rei-san's power. ...So, since you were able to return this quickly, does that mean you were able to meet with the members of the envoy?"

That seemed to be Gime Kara's main concern.

Ryutas seemed equally concerned about that point, waiting beside Gime Kara for Rei to speak.

"Ah, we managed to meet up without issue. We've also managed to borrow a few guards, though they're few in number. It should make things a bit easier."

"That makes sense."

Compared to the adventurers chosen to guard a small country like Reburina—chosen more for their temperament to avoid trouble than for strength—those active in the frontier region of Gilm within the great nation of Mireana were undoubtedly skilled.

In that sense, for Gimekara of the Zolge Commerce Guild, who was aiding this group, Rei's proposal couldn't have been more welcome.

"So, what about the envoy? Did you tell them about us?"

"Yeah, I told them. Though, since I've already briefed Lord Daskar on that sort of thing, I don't think it'll be a problem even if the delegation says something. Ah, but... just so you know, I did mention that once we join up, I'd like to hear all sorts of information from you guys."

"That's no problem. Right?"

"That's right. In fact, it's exactly what we want."

Gimekara and Ryutas exchanged glances and nodded in agreement.

(Huh. ...I don't think they were ever that bad to begin with, but it seems like they've gotten even closer today.)

Even when Rei was around, the two weren't exactly hostile toward each other.

But now, their relationship seemed even better than when Rei left this morning.

With just the two men alone in the carriage while Rei was gone, they'd probably been able to talk more deeply.

(Vihera and Byune were in the same carriage too, but maybe they just didn't click?)

Rei knew Vihera was incredibly charming, not just in appearance but in personality too.

But while she was charming, it was also undeniable that she had a terribly strong-willed personality.

The fact that she relishes battles against formidable foes alone makes her quite challenging for most men.

"At least the information exchange went smoothly. We can consider that a success, right?"

"Yeah. I explained everything about Ryutas properly, including to Daskar-sama, so I don't think you need to worry about that."

"...I see."

Ryutas relaxed at Rei's words.

He had expected things to go smoothly with Daskar, but he had only ever met Daskar through the Paired Orb. Meeting the members of the envoy delegation directly was different.

Could it be... just maybe... he'd feared being captured upon meeting them. Only now, hearing Rei's words, did he finally feel at ease.

The possibility that even Rei might deceive them, and that Ryutas would be captured the moment they joined forces, hadn't completely vanished.

But since Rei had said this, even if harm threatened him upon joining the delegation, at the very least, Rei would undoubtedly stand by them.

(Well, once I join that envoy and start acting with them, I can't completely rule out the possibility that after Rei and the others are gone... But if they're Gilm people, they should know how unprofitable it is to make an enemy of Rei, right?)

Ryutas half-told himself.

He knew that if the guards he'd brought from Gilm tried to harm him, his own guards alone would be utterly powerless to stop them.

He knew he was probably overthinking it, but that was the caution bred from growing up in Jaya, that dark organization... and as a potential successor.

"Anyway, we'll talk about that later... How was today's trip?"

"Ah... well, there were definitely some issues."

Gimekara answered, sounding a bit reluctant.

"Food issues again? Or worrying about where to sleep tonight?"

"That too, but... it seems quite a few people didn't trust my and Byune's abilities."

"No, Vihera. That's..."

"Ah, don't worry. I'm not upset about that. I know my fighting style is plain."

Rei murmured in utter bewilderment at the exchange between Gimechara and Vihera.

In truth, her form in battle—clad in a sheer garment evoking images of courtesans or dancers... or perhaps even more provocative—was an elegant fighting style, as if she were dancing.

It was absolutely not a fighting style that could be described as unassuming.

"Well, in my case, it's the clothes I wear that are the problem. Most of this group were originally slaves, controlled by collars and forced into prostitution, right? I suppose it brings back memories of that."

While Marina's feminine allure was of a different nature, Vihera's appearance, which more directly stimulated desires like carnal lust and sexual appetite, probably stirred up various feelings in the women who had been forced into prostitution.

Especially now, when most people wear ordinary clothes except for a few eccentric types.

That's precisely why Vihera inevitably stands out now, inevitably reminding them of the time they were forced into prostitution.

"Well, I'm wearing this outfit based on my own convictions, so if you dislike it, I won't blame you for that."

"Well, if that's how you feel, Vihera, I won't say anything more about it... But are you really going to be okay once we're gone?"

"Even if I'm not okay, I'll just have to make myself okay. ...If possible, I hope the guards sent with the envoy can handle things better."

"Probably... I think it'll be fine."

The reason he couldn't say it was definitely okay was probably because it still wasn't clear exactly who would be coming.

The embassy also wants to send the most capable people possible as escorts, but honestly, they also want to avoid drastically reducing their own combat strength.

(For now, Milein would likely volunteer, but Ronitas probably wouldn't approve. ...And even before that, Sulunin would likely reject it.)

Serving as the group's escort means acting alongside Rei and the others, even if only for a short time.

If asked whether Milein, a Set enthusiast, would pass up such a golden opportunity... Rei could only shake his head.

But Milein is one of the rising stars among Gilm's adventurers—exceptions like Rei aside. She might not have a nickname, but as a bodyguard's prestige, she's undoubtedly top-tier.

No matter how much Milein wanted to act alongside Rei and the others—or more accurately, alongside Set—Ronitas would never let her slip away.

What would happen if such people met...? It wouldn't be good if they ended up in a hostile relationship like Milein and Johanna, but having Milein become their subordinate wouldn't be interesting either.

(Honestly, considering the loss of guards when Ryutas leaves, even if the envoy delegation provided replacements, it would be a net loss... No, the guards coming are simply superior replacements for Ryutas's current ones, so it shouldn't be a problem... right? Though losing numbers hurts.)
