"An assassin...?"

Augusto responded to the explanation given by Daldora.

Less than an hour after parting ways with Rei , who had accompanied him to the research facility, Daldora had arrived at Augusto's residence again. The words that came from her mouth concerned an assassin.

He knew such entities existed, and even if Solrain was a small country compared to its neighbors, he didn't believe it alone was untouched by such matters.

But... he had always thought that was something specific to the capital city of Solrain.

After all, he had heard such stories before from merchants who had come from the capital on several occasions.

But those were stories from other places.

They weren't stories about where he lived.

That's precisely why, even hearing it from Daldora's mouth, I found it hard to believe immediately.

(Daldora had once set sand pirates upon me. If that's the case, then it wouldn't be strange to think that here, the sand pirates were fulfilling the role of assassins, would it?)

I wondered, but sand pirates and assassins, though both lurking in society's shadows, were vastly different.

Anyone could become a sand pirate if they wanted to, but assassins were truly sunk into the shadows of society.

Of course, this was purely Augusto's speculation, and he could well imagine the reality was quite different.

But... even so, he still couldn't grasp the idea that there was an assassin here in Gosh, one who was targeting them specifically.

"Why are you and Daldora being targeted in the first place? I don't quite grasp the logic behind that."

Augusto voiced his confusion, and both Zaruust and Rei, his escorts, nodded in agreement.

"Of course, as a merchant, I can't exactly claim I've never incurred anyone's grudge. But why would both Daldora and I be targeted together?"

Daldora could only shake her head at that question.

She understood that she herself was resented by many people, and she also understood that, in stark contrast to her, Augusto was well-liked by the townspeople.

Precisely because of that, he couldn't see the common thread that would make both of them targets at the same time.

"Maybe Augusto was targeted as a decoy, to distract your guards?"

Zalust's muttered voice echoed in the room, now growing dim as night approached, bathed in a redness that could only be called the remnants of the sunset.

It was the most plausible explanation given the current situation, and everyone nodded in agreement.

"Even if I'm the target, it doesn't change the fact that Augusto is also being targeted. If that's the case, there's a very real possibility they'll use Augusto as bait again to attack me..."

"And by peeling off or replacing facial skin, they could make the appearance completely different from the body, regardless of the physique... is that it?"

Augusto murmured, as if continuing Daldora's words, and both of them sighed in unison.

The fact that they'd brought up skin-stripping again to become someone else showed just how troublesome it was.

Even someone you know well could be indistinguishable if they wore the right skin. Thinking about it that way, it was probably accurate to say there was no way to guard against it.

"In that case, we'd want to avoid being on the defensive. Since we have no idea where they're hiding, it's more efficient for us to go on the offensive all at once."

Rei murmured, squinting at the faint red hue in the room, the remnants of the setting sun.

It was true, but... there was also a reason why it wasn't so simple.

"But Rei, we don't even know where the other assassins are, do we? So how exactly are we supposed to launch an attack?"

Augusto's words prompted nods of agreement from the others present.

Truthfully, there wasn't a single clue to be found.

All they had was the corpse of the assassin Rei had killed... or more accurately, the assassin who had committed suicide under Rei's blow. Expecting them to find the enemy's hideout from that was unreasonable.

Rei, who had been met with such skeptical glances, smiled as if to say he'd anticipated this from the start, and spoke.

"It's not exactly certain... but there is a way to pinpoint the base of the ones who sent the assassins this time."

When Rei said that, everyone present looked at him with expressions of disbelief.

It was only natural. Despite their discussions, they hadn't found a way to break the deadlock. All they could think of was waiting and dealing with any assassins who came next.

Amidst that, Rei suddenly mentioned a way to find the assassins' hideout.

There was no way anyone wouldn't be interested.

Representing the group, whose eagerness to learn the method was plain to see, Augusto spoke up.

"So, Rei. What exactly is this method?"

"It's not that complicated. We rely on Set."

"...Set, you say?"

At the words "rely on Set," everyone, including Augusto, was initially baffled.

Some of Daldora's guards didn't even know Set's name, adding to the confusion.

Seeing Daldora's guards like this, Augusto opened his mouth to explain.

"Set is the griffin that Rei has as his familiar. ...So, Rei, what are you going to do with Set?"

The guards showed a slight reaction at the word "griffin," but Rei nodded at Augusto's question and spoke.

"As you know, griffins are Rank A monsters."

Technically, Set was classified as a rare species and treated as an S-rank monster, but there was no need to mention that now.

Seeing everyone present nod at the mention of Rank A monsters, Rei continued his explanation.

"And, being such a monster, its senses are naturally far sharper than a human's. ...That includes its sense of smell. Now, Daldora has the assassin's corpse at her disposal. ...So, what do you think can be inferred from all this?"

Having said that much, many could arrive at the answer.

Most present were seasoned adventurers, and neither Augusto nor Daldora were slow thinkers.

"You mean... we can find the hideout by scent?"

"I won't say it's guaranteed, and since it involves following a scent, it might take some time. Still, it's better than having no clues at all, don't you think?"

At Rei's words, everyone present could only nod in agreement.

The truth was, as things stood, they could only wait for the other side to make their move.

That meant the initiative lay with the enemy.

Such a situation would be mentally draining.

"Given our failure in this attack, the possibility that they'll give up... is probably nonexistent, right?"

Everyone nodded in response to Daldora's words.

Truthfully, no one believed that a single failed assassination attempt would make them give up on killing Augusto and Daldora.

If this were a second- or third-rate assassin group, perhaps that possibility might exist.

But... there was no way anyone could make such an optimistic prediction about an assassin group that went so far as to peel off someone's facial skin.

"So, what do we do? We could go look for them now..."

"Wait, that's dangerous. As you can see, it's already evening. As night falls, the temperature will drop, making movement harder. Plus, night is the assassins' time, so I think we should wait until tomorrow to search."

"...No, Daldora. If night is the assassins' time, then giving them tonight as their opportunity seems rather unwise."

Augusto considered Daldora's words and wondered aloud if they shouldn't go searching now, but Daldora shook her head at that too.

"There are other reasons why tomorrow is better. ...Narsus, who is coordinating our guards, cannot be used as a combat asset right now. Well, if we pushed it, we could use him as one, but..."

Narsus was the most skilled among the guards Daldora had hired.

Having him as a combatant versus not having him made a huge difference.

Hearing Daldora's explanation, everyone, including Augusto, showed expressions of understanding.

...Of course, Daldora wanted Narsus to participate in this mission as a combatant partly to bolster their own strength and deal with the assassins as a group. But equally important was that if Narsus excelled, it would count as Daldora's achievement.

With Rei on Augusto's side, it was certain that the greatest credit would go to him.

However, ending this matter without achieving any credit at all would be a significant blow to Daldora.

Therefore, Daldora preferred Narsus to lead the assault on the assassins' hideout.

"Besides, if we're launching an assault on the assassins' hideout, we'll need to arrange things with the guard squad. Since we have to go through Ryubrand, it can't be done immediately..."

"True. If guards showed up while we're attacking the assassins' hideout, it would cause all sorts of trouble."

Augusto voiced his agreement with Daldora's opinion.

...The fact that neither of them counted the guards as combat assets for this raid probably meant their training was that inadequate.

Their words reflected the understanding that bringing guards along would only slow them down.

If the adventurers' skills were low, the guards might have been somewhat useful.

But, for better or worse, every adventurer here was exceptionally skilled by the standards of a town like Gosh.

"...Alright. Then let's do it tomorrow."

Rei, who had wanted to resolve the assassin issue today, reluctantly nodded.

The deciding factor was likely the time it would take to get the guard squad on board.

And once Rei agreed, the others didn't particularly object.

The real problem, though, was...

"Um, Rei. Are you really sure Set can find those assassins?"

"I don't know. It's just a possibility. Plus, the scent will naturally fade by tomorrow, so I think the chances are lower than if we pursue them now."

"Oh! ...Right, I suppose so. Since it's a scent, that makes sense."

Daldora tried to say something, but realized there was nothing to be done once the scent vanished, and quickly dropped the subject.

"So then, should we push through and finish it tonight, even if it's a stretch? Considering the risk of losing the scent tomorrow... we can't really bring up Narsus anymore."

"But what about notifying the guard squad in that case?"

When Augusto addressed Daldora, the only response was a shake of the head.

"As for that, we can handle it once we head to the lord's mansion. ...However, there's a chance the guards might not be fully informed, so they might stick their noses in."

What do you say? Daldora turned her gaze to Augusto.

If we want to fully prepare our forces, tomorrow. If we want to definitively pinpoint the assassins' hideout, today.

As the two merchants hesitated over this binary choice, Rei pulled a potion from the Misty Ring.

It wasn't as high-quality as the one used on Augusto, but it would undoubtedly provide several levels of superior effect compared to what was sold in Gosh.

Of course, Daldora knew exactly what that potion meant.

"Are you sure? Really?"

"Yes. More combat power is welcome on our end. So there's no need to hesitate here, right?"

"...Understood."

To be honest, Daldora naturally had potions in stock as well.

But... compared to those potions, the one right in front of him was clearly a high-quality item.

"This should cover most of it. So, we'll attack the assassins' hideout tonight, right? And naturally, only a few others besides me and Set will participate in the raid."

No one could say no to Rei's confirmation.

After all, by tomorrow, the scent would have faded, making discovery less likely.

Considering that, as someone targeted by bodyguards and assassins, he couldn't deny it.

And so... they would search for the hideout during the night—the assassins' prime time—and launch their assault.
