"Uh..."

Rei looked at the scene before him, unsure of what to say.

At the guild, Lenora and Kenny had warned him to watch out for thieves.

And it’s no mistake that he even thought it might be some kind of foreshadowing.

But... that was more of a half-joking thought, and he never imagined he’d actually see a thief.

That said, it wasn’t Rei—who was with Set—who was attacked, but an old woman walking a short distance away from him.

A man darted out from a side street and tried to snatch the bag—though calling it a “bag” was a bit of a stretch—that the woman was carrying and run off with… but what was truly unlucky for that man was that Set was right there.

And to make matters even worse, the old woman had fed Set many times before, and Set would never abandon someone who had been kind to him.

As a result, the moment he heard the old woman’s scream… no, the moment he saw the man snatch the bag, Set dashed forward and crushed the thief with his forepaws.

That said, perhaps because Set fully understood that this was Gilm—in the middle of town—he held back considerably.

The thief lost consciousness from Set’s single blow, but he hadn’t lost a single limb, his head wasn’t crushed, and his internal organs weren’t destroyed.

“Grrr…”

“Oh, thank you. Yes, you’re such a good boy.”

The old woman, whose bag had been stolen, stroked Set’s head while thanking him.

Set, too, didn’t seem to mind having his head stroked like that. He let the old woman’s hand rest on him and purred contentedly.

Meanwhile, Rei kept a watchful eye on the thief—though “robber” would be the more accurate term—who lay unconscious on the ground, making sure he didn’t try to escape.

He had a nagging feeling in the back of his mind that this might be a foreshadowing of something, but since there was no one around who would understand even if he said it, he didn’t bother voicing it.

As she waited for the guards to arrive... she suddenly noticed a red cloth tied around the man’s arm.

Seeing the red cloth, he wondered for a moment if it was binding an injured arm, but he quickly realized it wasn’t blood or anything like that—it was just a red cloth.

Relieved by this—not because I’d been worried about a robbery, but because I’d feared Set’s blow might have injured him—Rei took another look at the unconscious man.

He didn’t seem particularly concerned with fashion, so Rei found it even more puzzling that the man had a cloth tied around his arm.

Then again, Rei hadn’t really cared about fashion since he was in Japan, and that hadn’t changed since he’d come to Elgin.

……He usually wore the Dragon Robe, and other than that, he didn’t really make any particular effort to look stylish.

Well, since the Misty Ring on his right hand is shaped like a bracelet, you could say that’s the only element of style he has.

In any case, even someone like Rei finds it odd that the unconscious robber is so conspicuously wrapping a red cloth around his arm.

Even if he manages to escape this scene without being spotted by Set, the conspicuous red cloth wrapped around his arm would serve as a marker, making it easier to track him down.

(No, or is that actually the plan? After all, it’s just a cloth wrapped around his arm. If he escapes from here and unties it, there won’t be any evidence left. But… the fact that he’s thinking that far ahead is a bit concerning.)

Just as a faint sense of foreboding crept over him, and he felt a pang of displeasure at the thought that his own color—red, the color of his “Crimson” alias—was being used so blatantly for a crime, a guard emerged, pushing through the crowd.

There are quite a few guards in Gilm, but due to various past events, Rei is on familiar terms with many of them.

Fortunately, the guard who had just arrived was also someone Rei knew.

Even if the guard hadn’t known him personally, that would simply mean he wasn’t acquainted with Rei specifically; as long as he was in Gilm, he would know what kind of person Rei was.

Still, it’s only natural to think it’s better to explain the situation to someone you know.

“That must have been rough, Rei. Or should I say, Set?”

Perhaps having already heard the gist of the situation from the person who came to report it, the guard addressed Rei with those words.

The reason his attitude wasn’t all that serious was probably because the robber had already been knocked completely unconscious by Set’s single blow.

“Exactly. It’s started snowing, too, so I really didn’t want to get involved in this kind of trouble if I could help it. …Not that I’m saying the guards should have done their jobs better, though.”

“You’re saying exactly that.”

Rei interjected, looking as if he hadn’t meant to.

However, after teasing Rei like that, the guard bowed his head, looking somewhat apologetic.

“But I get what you’re saying, Rei. There’s been more of these guys around lately. Plus, with the expansion project this year, there are just so many people here. …Ah, I knew it. This guy too.”

After bowing to Rei, the guard looked at the man and muttered with an air of exasperation.

If the guard’s words and actions had been based on the man’s face, Rei probably wouldn’t have minded that much.

But what the guard had seen was the man’s right arm… yes, the spot where the red cloth Rei had noticed earlier was wrapped.

Seeing the guard’s reaction, Rei asked with a sense of foreboding.

“Hey, could it be… that the guys with this red cloth wrapped around them have been doing the same thing over and over again…?”

To anyone who knew the usual Rei, the way she asked—timidly, as if she couldn’t quite believe it—was surprising.

But the guard’s response to Rei was simply to gently look away.

The guard knew that Rei was nicknamed “Crimson.”

And yet, right in front of Rei, they had wrapped themselves in red cloth as if to flaunt it, then tried to forcibly snatch the old woman’s belongings.

There was no way that wouldn’t have caught Rei’s attention.

“Huh… While I’ve been busy with various things, quite a few amusing characters have started popping up in Gilm again, haven’t they?”

Rei doesn’t claim that red is his color alone.

Still… still, the nickname “Crimson” wasn’t something he earned on his own; it was a nickname he earned because of the people he’d fought against up until now.

Among those he’d fought, there were naturally some he didn’t care for, but there were also enemies worthy of respect.

For that very reason, hearing that a group of people wrapped in red cloth… and not just committing acts of banditry, but repeatedly committing what might be called petty crimes… Rei couldn’t possibly find it amusing.

Seeing Rei like this, the guard couldn’t help but look up at the sky.

A flake of snow drifted down and landed on the guard’s face, the coldness of it cooling his head, before he spoke again.

“Just calm down for now. After all, these guys may be wearing red cloth, but they’re just petty criminals. They’re the kind of scum who aren’t even a match for the bandits you usually take down out there, you know? They’re not the sort of people a high-ranking adventurer with a reputation should be worrying about that much.”

The guard spoke as if trying to placate Rei, but the one listening said nothing in particular.

……It wasn’t that he wasn’t going to argue with the guard. He was simply too engrossed in his own thoughts.

(Oh, man.)

Seeing Rei like that, the guard decided there was no point in trying to reason with her any further.

That said, there was no doubt that the guards considered the recent groups engaging in extortion and robbery to be a troublesome bunch.

If they had gone so far as to kill someone, the guards would have taken the investigation much more seriously.

But whether the group with red cloth wrapped around their arms was targeting people, lacked the nerve, or had some other reason—he couldn’t tell—they never did anything as extreme as killing anyone, nor did they target people of high standing.

The group with the red cloth invariably targets people like the elderly or the powerless, as was the case this time.

(There’s no doubt some organization is pulling the strings behind the scenes… but what possible benefit could they gain from going out of their way to provoke Rei?)

The guard harboured such doubts, but deciding that calming the situation was the priority for now, he called out to the old woman nearby who was petting Set.

“Old lady, could you come to the guardhouse with me for a moment?”

“Me? Sure, I suppose… but won’t Set-chan get in trouble for this?”

“Ah, it’s fine. All Set did was catch a criminal. He’ll get praised, not scolded.”

“Oh, that’s good to hear, Set.”

“Grr!”

Set let out a happy meow as the old woman stroked him.

The guard watched the pair, then turned to Rei, who still seemed deep in thought, and spoke to him.

"Come on, Rei. You need to snap out of it. We need to hear your side of the story, too."

At the guard’s words, Rei turns his gaze toward him with a sigh.

“I’ll ask, but why on earth are these guys running wild? Isn’t that the guards’ job?”

Rei isn’t actually angry.

Well, he was angry at the people wearing the red cloth, but he wasn’t angry at the security guard.

Still, perhaps feeling a sense of guilt—whether from the intensity of Rei’s gaze or their own incompetence in letting criminals run rampant—the guard quietly looked away.

“You know that in Gilm right now, the number of guards alone isn’t enough, don’t you?”

“Yeah. I know. But since that wasn’t enough, you were supposed to be hiring adventurers to patrol as backup for the guards, right?”

“……You’re right.”

“But why haven’t these guys been caught?”

“I suppose information about where we’re patrolling has gotten out to them.”

“I see. So there’s someone among the guards or adventurers who’s in cahoots with them.”

Even as he said this, Rei imagined that it was likely the adventurers, not the guards, who were connected to the Red Cloth gang.

There were clearly far more adventurers than guards, and due to the ongoing expansion project, many adventurers had gathered in Gilm.

It’s impossible for every single adventurer gathered here to be someone who doesn’t commit misdeeds.

Naturally, there are some unsavory characters among them, and it wouldn’t be surprising if they were leaking information to the Red Cloth gang for some reason—most likely money.

Whether it was just Rei who said this or the guard who was told it, the guard nodded in understanding.

“Seriously, what a bunch of people doing such a pathetic little money-making scheme.”

“……By the way, and I mean just by the way, there wouldn’t be any problem if I were to arrest these guys, would there?”

“Ah… Well, if you’re asking whether it’s a problem or not, it’s not a problem, but this isn’t the kind of job a high-ranking adventurer with a reputation should be doing, is it?”

“I know. Still, these guys are just a little… something.”

If they’d been wearing any color other than red, it might have been a different story, but the fact that they were flaunting those red cloths… and doing so right here in Gilm, Rei’s base of operations, somehow really got under his skin.

Perhaps sensing Rei’s thoughts, the guard said nothing more on the matter and turned his gaze back to the old woman admiring Set.

“Old lady, I’m heading to the guardhouse. Would you mind coming with me?”

“Oh, sure. …Is Set-chan coming too?”

Seeing the old woman ask this while petting Set, the guard felt a slight wave of relief wash over him.

Given that just a few seconds ago he’d been engaged in a tense exchange with Rei, he felt even more relaxed.

“I’m bringing Rei along, so of course Set will be coming too.”

“Is that so? Well then, shall we go? Set-chan.”

“Grr!”

At the old woman’s words, Set let out a growl and stood up.

While that was happening, several more guards arrived from behind. They exchanged a few words with the guard who had been talking to Rei just moments ago, then took the man—who was still unconscious—away.

There was no doubt that the somewhat rough way they were handling him stemmed from their frustration at being burdened with this trouble at such a busy time.

“Alright, Rei. You’re coming with us too. …And just so you know, it’s fine if you catch those guys in the red robes, but don’t forget that if you pull a stunt like that when you haven’t done anything wrong, I might end up having to arrest you myself.”

If, like this time, he’s arresting someone who’s committed a crime, there’s no problem.

But if Rei were to do something to the people with red cloths wrapped around their arms simply because they were there, it wouldn’t be surprising if he ended up getting arrested himself.

……That said, given that the people wearing the red cloth have something to hide, it was still a bit of a toss-up whether they’d actually report him to the guards.
