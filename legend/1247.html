The masked men had been attacking Rei and the others, but just as they defeated the masked assailants, the security guards appeared as if they had timed their arrival perfectly.

Of course, they hadn't actually timed their arrival that precisely, but still, showing up only after the situation had been resolved inevitably drew looks of exasperation from those around them.

That said, it was only because the commotion had been safely quelled without incident. Had some tragic event occurred, the security guards would have faced far more than just disbelief.

"Um... well... um... ah..."

The security soldiers seemed aware they were late, their words trailing off as they glanced around.

Still, they understood their duties clearly and moved toward the unconscious men to restrain them.

Naturally, they removed the masks in the process... but unsurprisingly, the faces revealed weren't particularly famous targets.

The guards who had hoped they might be bounty hunters, or at least criminals with some sort of warrant out for them, flashed a hint of disappointment.

A mask, naturally, is something one wears when they don't want their face seen.

Since he wore one, it meant he didn't want others to see his face—that was the reasoning behind his actions. The reason, they assumed, was that the face hidden beneath the mask was fairly well-known.

That's what the guards had thought, but they were completely off the mark.

"Does anyone know this guy's face?"

He asked his comrades, but everyone shook their heads.

The faces beneath the other masks were the same.

They tried asking not just their fellow guards but also the people gathered around, but none of them had seen that face before either.

"Well, no choice. We'll take him to headquarters. Once he regains consciousness, we'll interrogate him. ...Still, you, man, you really are, from the bottom of your heart, thoroughly, completely, utterly loved by trouble."

Rei and a security guard he knew opened his mouth, sounding somewhat exasperated.

But just as he reached to place a hand on Rei's shoulder... he finally noticed the others present.

The first to catch his eye was Marina, the guildmaster of this very guild, Gilm.

Next came Elena, nicknamed the Princess General, a symbol of the Noble Faction in the Kingdom of Mireana.

Lastly, he noticed Vihera, who had been part of Rei's party for a while and was famous for her provocative attire.

...Actually, Ara was also present, but the other members were so conspicuous that the guard Rei was talking to hadn't noticed her.

Then again, for Ara, this was a common occurrence when she was with Elena, and she didn't seem particularly bothered by it.

While Ara's features were certainly well-formed, she was likely aware she couldn't compare to Elena.

In any case, the guard widened his eyes upon seeing Elena and Marina present.

"Huh? Guildmaster Elena?"

"You're slow on the uptake. This carriage belongs to Elena to begin with, so you really should've noticed sooner."

"You should've said something sooner about this! ...Well, anyway, given the location, let's hurry to the guardhouse. We need to properly apprehend these attackers too."

"...Isn't your attitude a bit too obvious?"

Rei muttered, his voice tinged with slight exasperation, though this attitude was actually quite normal.

After all, they were a duke's daughter with a notorious nickname and a guildmaster.

Not many people could casually interact with such figures.

"You idiot! These people aren't the sort we can casually chat with, you know!?"

The other guards carrying away the men whose masks had been removed nodded in agreement at the guard's words.

Rei, who had known Elena and the others for a long time, wasn't particularly bothered, but it did make him realize their status all over again. ...Then again, Rei wasn't the type to change his attitude because of that, and Elena and the others probably wouldn't want him to act differently either.

"Anyway, we can't stay here forever. We're blocking the main street."

"...Ah, I see."

Looking around again, Rei noticed the crowd watching the carriage, them, and the attackers had blocked a significant portion of the main street.

It wasn't completely blocked, so the main street wasn't impassable.

Still, it was far from smooth sailing.

Considering that, he understood why the guards were urging Rei and the others on.

"Alright, then let's head to the guard post for now. ...Is that okay?"

"Yes, that's fine."

Marina nodded at Rei's words, and the others, seemingly without objection, nodded as well.

"That's a relief. Well then, let's go. We'll provide escort for the carriage."

The guard who had been talking to Rei earlier addressed Elena and Marina in a completely different tone.

Considering the other party's position, the change in tone was only natural.

Accustomed to such attitudes, the two nodded without changing their expressions.

"Ara, take the reins."

"Yes, Elena-sama. Now, please board the carriage."

At those words, Elena and the others boarded the carriage. Set, who had been playing with the children, saw this and returned to the carriage.

"Hey, Rei. Normally, I should ride with you in the carriage for protection... but do you need it?"

As Rei was about to board the carriage, the guard from earlier asked him formally.

Rei's response to the guard was simply to shake his head.

"No need. Actually, if anyone tries to attack us in this situation, it'll help us get clues about the assailants."

"...Yeah."

In fact, the weakest ones here weren't Rei and his group, but the guards themselves.

If asked whether they needed that guard as an escort, even the guard himself would likely tilt his head in confusion.

That's precisely why they accepted Rei's stance without complaint.

"Besides, you're having guards secure the front and rear of the carriage, right? Then doesn't that technically count as an escort too?"

"...If anything, it's more like protection to prevent unnecessary injuries to whoever attacks us."

While the security force held the authority to apprehend criminals, it was obvious they weren't allowed to injure them needlessly.

Rather, the main purpose of this escort was likely to restrain the criminals themselves, preventing them from sustaining unnecessary injuries if anyone dared to pick a fight with this group.

This wasn't to aid the criminals, but rather a judgment that having more potential sources of information was advantageous.

Though it was also true that anyone foolish enough to pick a fight with Rei and his crew would likely only inspire pity in the criminals.

In terms of pure combat strength, Rei's group could wipe out a village in under an hour. Even against an army, victory was assured as long as Rei was present.

To attack someone possessing such strength... or rather, power that borders on a natural disaster... isn't that just suicide?

"Well, whatever. As long as it's not bothersome. Lead the way."

Saying only that, Rei climbed into the carriage.

During the journey to headquarters, perhaps thanks to the guards accompanying them, there were no further attacks.

Even without the guards, Rei and his group possessed extraordinary combat strength. With the guards added, their force grew even stronger. Perhaps the attackers realized that the strength they had shown earlier was no match for this new force. Or maybe they simply decided that one attempt to attack Rei and his group was enough.

In any case, upon arriving at headquarters, they halted the carriage and discussed the next steps with the guards.

One guard voiced a theory: "Maybe those men attacked because they hid something in the carriage while Rei wasn't looking, and they came back to retrieve it?" Just to be absolutely sure, they asked Elena for permission to search the carriage...

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAH! Wh-what the hell is this!?"

The guard's scream echoed from inside the carriage.

What happened? The other guards rushed inside too, only to be met by similar screams echoing around them.

...It was only natural. They had assumed it was just a carriage, but what spread out before them was a proper room complete with a sofa, a table, and even basic cooking utensils.

Under normal circumstances, few would fail to be astonished by this sight.

Given that it was a magic item created by Duke Kerebel with no expense spared, it was impossible for a mere guard to comprehend it all.

"Ah."

Who had murmured that?

For Rei, having used this carriage many times, it had become familiar territory.

While Vihera and Marina hadn't ridden it as often as Rei, one was a former Rank A adventurer dark elf, and the other a former princess of the Bestir Empire.

While not exactly identical, they had used similar magic items before, so they weren't as startled as the guards.

"What do we do?"

Rei imagined the guards inside the carriage, likely stunned or frozen in place, and asked the carriage's owner.

But Elena, asked what to do, didn't know either.

This had never happened before, but she quickly composed herself and spoke.

"What do you mean, what should we do? To begin with, our carriage was securely stored at that inn in the dungeon, and we didn't encounter anyone else on the way to Gilm. I don't think it's possible for something to have been hidden inside the carriage."

As Elena spoke, brushing back her golden hair, Rei nodded, his gaze momentarily drawn to her.

"Unless someone from the inn was in on it, they couldn't have pulled something like that off."

"Right? And there was no one like that at that inn. ...Of course, we didn't thoroughly investigate every single employee, so I can't say it's absolutely impossible."

Everyone seemed to agree with Elena's assessment, returning her nod.

At that moment, guards dismounted from the carriage.

Though screams had been heard from inside earlier, they seemed to be guards, and their surprise had already faded.

Seeing this, Rei felt relieved they wouldn't be grilled with questions and spoke up.

"So, what did you find? Anything suspicious inside?"

"Ah... Sorry, but just looking at it, I couldn't understand it at all. I never imagined the inside was like a room... It's incredible, huh?"

The guard's final muttered remark drew nods of agreement from the others, each looking somewhat taken aback.

While Rei could understand their feelings, he knew they couldn't stay like this forever.

"So, what do we do now?"

"Well, I suppose we'll have to hear the details, but that should be over quickly. If you want to ask Rei anything, you can just go to the Dusk Wheat inn, right?"

The fact that Rei was staying at the Dusk Wheat inn, considered a high-class inn even in Gilm, was common knowledge among guards... no, anyone with even a little insight into the situation knew it.

"Yeah. It's not just me. Everyone except Marina—Elena and the others—are staying at the Dusk Wheat inn too. If you need anything, just contact them over there."

"Right. If you need anything from me, just contact the guild."

Marina followed suit, addressing the guard.

Hearing those words, Rei suddenly realized he didn't know where Marina lived.

Whenever he met Marina, it was always at the guild's office.

Given that, Rei didn't know where Marina lived.

(Well, now's not the time to ask. Besides, she said she'd be handing over the guildmaster position to a Walker. Once that happens, we won't be meeting at the office anymore, so I'll probably find out where Marina lives then.)

They were staying at the Dusk Wheat inn, but considering her position as Guildmaster, Marina undoubtedly had her own home.

"I'll take you up on that. ...Then let's head to the guardhouse. It's cold out here."

With no objection to the guard's words, Rei and the others entered the guardhouse.
