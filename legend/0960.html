The garden behind the inn where Rei and the others are staying.

No, judging by the overgrown vegetation and complete lack of maintenance, it couldn't really be called a garden.

Perhaps it should be called an abandoned space.

Still, perhaps because spring had only just arrived, weeds hadn't grown too thickly yet.

Instead, branches snapped by the weight of snow lay scattered about.

In this place that could hardly be called a garden, Rei and Magita faced each other.

A short distance away stood Vihera, Byune, and the two merchants.

Normally, Vihera and Byune aside, the two merchants hadn't wanted to accompany Rei. However, after Rei declared he wanted them to properly witness his true strength as payback for being saddled with Magita, this situation had unfolded.

"Well, thanks to your stubbornness, we're fighting like this... But let me ask again: isn't this a pain in the ass?"

"Huh? What do you mean, a hassle?"

"Well, I mean, there's no real need to go through with a fight I'm bound to win, is there?"

At Rei's statement that he'd only spoken the truth, veins bulged on Magita's forehead.

"Huh? You think I'll lose?"

His voice carried an intensity that would have made ordinary people freeze in fear, but it had no effect on Rei, who had faced Noiz.

"Yeah. No matter how we fight, I don't see any way I could lose."

If this were meant as provocation, that would be one thing, but Rei stated it naturally, without any particular intent.

Magita seemed to grasp this. The veins on his forehead stood out even more sharply.

"...Alright. If I lose, I'll obey any order you give. In return, if you lose, you have to obey one order of mine, anything at all. Deal?"

"Is that okay? Well, I don't really have anything specific I want... though having someone buy me food would be convenient. If it's something like that, I won't mind fighting either."

Magita's anger flared even more at Rei, who stated this as if victory were already assured.

Rei, certain of his victory, offered his chest. The atmosphere even suggested he was lending it.

"Ha, ha... It's been a long time since I've been mocked this much. Fine, I'll make you eat your words!"

With a cry of irritation, Magita kicked off the ground and closed the distance to Rei in one swift motion.

His speed was impressive, befitting someone confident in his own abilities.

Being a wolf beastman probably contributed to this speed. However...

"Slow."

To Rei's eyes, honed by countless battles, it was undeniably slow.

Even without the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, reading her movements was no challenge. He casually deflected the fist streaking straight for his face with his right hand.

It wasn't a fist deflecting a fist, but a palm striking and deflecting it, sending a sharp sound ringing through the air.

A sound unlike anything one would expect from flesh striking flesh.

Magita had likely anticipated being dodged or blocked, but being struck and deflected with a flat hand was undoubtedly unexpected.

Caught off guard, Magita recoiled in surprise at the pain in his fist.

As if in sync with that pulling-back motion, Rei stepped forward.

"What!?"

Magita, clearly caught completely off guard—he surely hadn't expected Rei to step forward on his own initiative, and especially not timed to coincide with him pulling back his fist—felt Rei's hand extend toward him. It touched the torso of his leather armor, which he wore prioritizing mobility, as if drawn to it.

"Hah!"

The blow, unleashed with a shout of determination, sent Magita flying about three meters through the air. He slammed his back into a nearby tree and collapsed to the ground.

"...No good."

Despite having knocked Magita away, the expression in Rei's eyes wasn't one of joy.

No, there was even frustration.

Why did he look frustrated instead of pleased, after managing to knock back an opponent larger than himself?

The two merchants looked at Rei curiously, but Vihera beside them was different.

"Oh. ...Still a long way to go, huh?"

"Hmm."

Vihera smiled amusedly as she critiqued the strike, and Byune, who had seen that skill several times since reuniting with Vihera, nodded slightly in agreement.

What Rei had done was an imitation of Vihera's skill, the Demon-Infused Palm.

However, the strike Rei unleashed was nowhere near what could be called Immersion Palm.

The skill was meant to use magic power to ignore leather armor, clothing, or skin, driving the impact directly into the body. What Rei had just done, however, was simply pushing with his palm to blow the opponent away.

(Well, just pushing alone to send a man considerably larger than himself flying like that is impressive enough, I suppose.)

If he could generate that much force purely through brute strength, what was the point of using the Demon-Infused Palm?

That's what Vihera thought, but the next moment, she saw Magita, who had been lying on the ground, get back up, and her expression turned to surprise.

"Again... She's not even a bear or boar beastman, yet she's surprisingly tough."

"Mm."

Byune nodded in agreement.

Byune's perspective, Magita was clearly no match for Rei.

If Rei had been serious, he would likely have knocked her unconscious with a single blow.

The gap in power between them was overwhelming, impossible to overcome.

Given that, even if Rei's attack was merely a failed attempt at the Immersion Palm... or even if it was just a straightforward shove, Buune couldn't imagine Magita getting back up after taking it.

"From what I heard from customers in the cafeteria, he was supposedly just looking for someone to fight in Subursta. I figured he was just some cocky fool who got a little too big for his britches... but even if he wasn't going all out, getting hit by Rei and still getting back up? Seriously?"

Vihera had fought Rei and lost.

Moreover, they had fought side by side on the same side during last year's civil war in the Bestir Empire.

That's precisely why he knew full well just how formidable Rei's strength truly was.

That's precisely why, even if he wasn't going all out, seeing him get back up after taking Rei's blow was a genuine surprise to Vihera.

And Rei, too, wore an expression of surprise as he turned his gaze toward Vihera.

"I don't think that was a light blow at all. But if you're getting back up, I guess you weren't just talking big."

"Heh, heh... I can't lose. I absolutely can't lose! Right now, I can't even beat an opponent like this, but I will never lose!"

Rei, referred to as "this kind of opponent," let out a small smile.

He hadn't expected someone who could barely get back up after taking such a hit to still talk about himself like that.

(He definitely doesn't know I have a nickname, but an opponent who gets back up like this is a bit refreshing.)

The blow Rei had delivered hadn't even fully become the Demon-Infused Palm... it had merely knocked him back.

Even so, the speed of the knockback and the impact against the tree must have been considerable.

"You won't lose to me, huh? Fine. Interesting. I'm starting to take a bit of an interest in you."

I couldn't tell what circumstances had driven the man trembling before me to stand up.

Still, it was certain he had piqued Rei's interest.

Whether that was fortunate or unfortunate remained unclear for now, but it would undoubtedly have some effect on the man.

"Let's see how long you can hold out... Show me a little more."

Muttering, Rei began walking toward Magita.

His pace was deliberately slow, nothing like the swift strides Magita had displayed earlier.

Even so, Magita couldn't bring himself to act against Rei as he drew nearer.

All she could do was watch the figure drawing nearer.

Partly because his legs trembled and refused to move, but more fundamentally, he felt paralyzed, as if overwhelmed by some inexplicable force emanating from the approaching Rei.

(What the hell? Why can't I move? What the hell is going on? Move! Come on, move, my legs! He's getting closer to me! Move, you fucking legs!)

I berate my unresponsive legs internally, but there's no sign of movement.

It was as if he believed moving meant death, leaving him utterly immobilized.

Rei, who had approached Magita in this state, gently reached out a hand.

"Can't move? ...Looks like this is as far as you go."

Seeing that Magita showed no sign of moving even when touched, Rei, thinking he might have misjudged the situation, touched the abdomen covered in leather armor.

"Sleep."

With that single word, his right hand swung.

But this blow was different from before—it wasn't meant to pierce.

He clenched his fist and struck her abdomen through the leather armor.

The blow sent Magita's insides reeling, and he collapsed easily to the ground.

"Guh, gah..."

He vomited the contents of his stomach onto the ground, but Rei had already lost interest in Magita.

"If you want a strong opponent, come to Gilm. There'll be plenty lying around who can take down someone like you. Well, since you got back up after that first hit, I'll let the whole 'orders' thing slide."

With only that, he left Magita, still vomiting, behind and approached Vihera and the others.

His gaze fell upon the merchant who could be said to have orchestrated this entire affair.

"You said you wanted to see my strength. Is this enough to satisfy you?"

"...Ah, yeah."

"I see. I'm glad you understand. Next time, I'd appreciate it if you avoided this kind of pointless charade as much as possible."

With only that said, Rei left the two merchants behind and returned inside the inn.

"Well, what can you do. ...Alright then, shall we go too?"

"Mm."

"...Huh? What's wrong?"

Vihera asked in confusion, wondering why the two merchants hadn't moved despite saying they'd follow. Only silence returned.

She waited a few seconds, but seeing no sign of movement, she concluded that since Magita's fighting spirit seemed crushed, leaving the two here wouldn't be a problem. She also figured they probably had things they wanted to discuss among themselves. So, taking Byune with her, Vihera headed back inside the inn.

A dozen seconds passed after Vihera and Byune disappeared. Finally, the more brusque of the merchants spoke.

"He was strong."

"Could it be that they were actually weak?"

"No way. Besides, did you even see how he moved?"

"Nah, nah. I'm a merchant. I can't read the movements of someone even slightly strong."

"True enough. ...Still, Crimson. Rumors tend to grow the more they spread, but with him, the rumors are spot-on... or maybe the rumors are actually toned down compared to reality."

"Yeah, you're right. But hey, if someone like that's offering to be our escort, isn't that fine?"

"...I suppose."

His hesitation was likely because he was still concerned about Vihera.

The more affable merchant had also harbored feelings for Vihera, but he seemed to have completely moved on now, showing no particular concern.

At least, he wasn't showing it outwardly.

They both silently turned their gaze toward Magita, who was crouched on the ground.

Toward Magita, who finally seemed to be calming down, perhaps having nothing left to vomit.

"...Shall we go?"

"Yeah, you're right. We haven't even finished eating yet. Tomorrow morning comes early. It's probably best to get a good night's sleep."

I suppose it'd be fine to stay here, but we might get hassled.

Considering how Magita had acted since they met, it was only natural to think that way.

Reaching that conclusion, the two merchants left the spot.

Besides, they were still in the middle of their meal. The thought that lingering here served no purpose, especially to satisfy their hunger, was strong.

The two left to continue their meal, never noticing Magita staring intently at their retreating backs as they headed back to the inn.

"I see... Tomorrow morning... Tomorrow morning... I see."

Magita uttered these words.

'Well, well, what a series of coincidences this has turned out to be.'

That voice echoed in Rei's room at the inn.

A clear, pure voice that brought joy just to hear.

Yet, that voice, possessing a solid presence, held a slightly complex hue today.

A voice that seemed to blend joy and jealousy.

The source of that voice opened her mouth, smiling near Rei.

"Hehe, it's been a while, Elena. I'm so glad to see you looking well. Byune was worried about you, you know?"

"Mm!"

Vihera, seated near Rei, and Byune, sitting on Vihera's lap.

She was happy to interact with the two of them, especially Byune, but still, Elena felt a strong pang of envy that Vihera had reunited with Rei before she had.

'It's good to see Byune looking well too. ...I'd like to head over there sooner myself, but I've got a lot on my plate. Looks like I'll be a bit late.'

"Is that so? Well, I'm happy because it means more time with Rei... but if you take too long, I might tempt Rei away, you know?"

Vihera declared, flashing a seductive smile that would almost certainly captivate any man.

Elena grumbled at Vihera, then softened her gaze at the thought of her long-awaited reunion with Byune.

"So, not only Rei, but Vihera was also attacked by someone believed to be an assassin from the Holy Light Church?"

"Yes. Though we let them escape, so we never learned their identity. Still, it was deemed too dangerous to leave Exil like this, so I decided to bring her with me to Gilm."

"...I wasn't attacked, though."

"Compared to adventurers or renegade royalty, I suppose I'm less of a target."

The night passed as we talked about the Holy Light Church and the events of this winter...

"Master! Take me as your disciple!"

Early the next morning, as Rei and the others prepared to depart from Saburusta, that voice called out to them.
