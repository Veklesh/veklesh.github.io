I need a bodyguard... When those words were spoken and the gaze fell upon him, it was Gifnan standing beside Precious.

Why me? Gifnan wondered, initially thinking perhaps there was some mistake.

To begin with, Gifnan had no relationship whatsoever with Rei.

He'd only met him once, back in the slums.

And it wasn't exactly a friendly chat; it was more accurate to say they had been threatened by Rei.

That was the extent of their relationship. No matter how favorably he thought about it, Gifnan couldn't consider his relationship with Rei to be good.

"Gifnan, you say? ...He's my bodyguard, so I can't just lend him out so easily when asked."

Before Gifnan could say anything, Precious replied to Rei, her words tinged with strong wariness.

It could be said those words came precisely because she had half-expected why Rei and the others had appeared here at this time.

The series of actions Precious had ordered Gifnan to carry out.

They knew about it... no, they must already have some kind of evidence.

In fact, that suspicion was far from unfounded.

For Rei, taking Gifnan to the guard post... and having him meet Keena, who was being hidden there—in a cell, but still—would provide solid proof.

For Precious, Gifnan was an extremely reliable bodyguard.

He's reasonably skilled, and Precious is absolutely certain he would never betray her.

Precisely for that reason, she couldn't hand Gifnan over to Rei here.

"Is that so? But... if you don't, your life will be forfeit. Even so?"

He drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and swung it wide.

A strike so sharp it felt like it sliced through the air... no, through space itself.

And the killing intent emanating from Rei's body was equal to... no, even greater than what it had been in the slums.

It was so intense that those inside the room, including Gifnan, who were guarding Precious, couldn't even think about doing anything about it.

It felt as if the killing intent itself was exerting a physical effect, freezing their movements.

The words Rei spoke: "Life vanishes."

That it was neither a lie nor a falsehood, but truly words from the heart, the absolute truth... Everyone present understood it completely.

Vihera seemed unfazed... no, her eyes were even glistening with lust. But Byune grabbed Vihera's thin robe, desperately trying to keep her from collapsing.

That killing intent didn't stay confined to the room; it was sensed by every guest staying at the inn who possessed a certain level of skill.

Those individuals wondered what on earth had happened, yet they couldn't move, fearing that acting now might bring harm upon themselves... but even Rei couldn't understand that.

"...So, what's it going to be? If you keep refusing like this, I'll just have to take you by force."

Rei turned his gaze to Precious and asked, but the one being questioned couldn't move.

She was overwhelmed by the presence of the Grim Reaper standing before her, wielding a large scythe.

"Hmm, then... I'll ask you. I came to you with this proposal in the first place. What do you say? Is being forcibly taken away your wish? In that case, there's no doubt significant damage will be inflicted on this room and the people inside it."

Gifnan, unable to move, pondered what he should do.

Resisting now wouldn't be wrong in the sense of not yielding to Rei.

But if he did that, it would undoubtedly harm not only himself, but his comrades... and even Precious.

If that happened, he wouldn't be able to protect Precious, which was something he needed to do before anything else.

The moment that thought struck him, Gifnan summoned his will and spoke.

"I'll... go."

Even though he said only that, Gifnan felt as if he had used up every last ounce of willpower and physical strength within him.

(Huh.)

But seeing Gifnan like that, Rei was impressed.

He hadn't expected Gifnan to be able to speak at all in this state.

After all, he had been radiating such intense killing intent that even a seasoned adventurer would likely be unable to move.

Still, Gifnan had managed to speak because he believed that if he didn't, Precious would be harmed.

"I see. Then... I'll stop being so reckless."

With those words, he reined in his killing intent and sheathed the Death Scythe into the Misty Ring.

"Zehahh, haa, haa..."

Gifnan finally calmed his killing intent, breathing heavily as he pressed both hands against the floor.

He wasn't the only one in this state. The other guards... and Precious too, were the same.

No, perhaps Precious, who didn't train regularly, had taken the biggest hit. Leaning her upper body against the desk, she struggled to steady her ragged breathing.

Glancing at Precious, Rei turned his gaze back to Gifnan and spoke.

"Well then, since we've mutually understood each other amicably... shall we go out together?"

At the word "amicably," Vihera shot Rei a look mixed with exasperation.

It was likely because the killing intent emanating from Rei had vanished. Vihera's gaze was no longer moist with lust.

That Rei didn't notice Vihera's change was one thing, but the others... Precious and the others either couldn't notice it, or even if they did, couldn't bring themselves to mention it. That was likely because the killing intent emanating from Rei had only just subsided, and they were far too preoccupied to think of anything else.

Seeing this situation as his chance, Rei turned his gaze to Gifnan.

"Well then, let's go."

"...Alright."

Though his legs trembled like those of a newborn fawn, Gifnan managed to take a step forward.

Satisfied with Gifnan's progress, Rei nodded. He then left the room, accompanied by Vihera and Byune, grabbing Gifnan's arm and practically dragging him along.

"Wait—"

Precious tried to say something from inside the room, but Rei deliberately ignored her and quickened his pace.

He understood exactly what Precious wanted to say.

Having swiftly deduced the reason Rei and the others had come here, handing Gifnan over to them was simply not an option for Precious.

The chances of them tracing it back to her were extremely high.

That was precisely why Precious had been trying to stop Rei somehow, but now, completely paralyzed by the killing intent Rei had unleashed, she couldn't move.

Leaving Precious behind, Rei and the others descended the stairs.

Rei ignored the pale-faced, frozen-in-place employee by the counter and the bodyguard who had collapsed onto the floor, leaving the inn.

Outside the inn, an adventurer watched the building with a slightly puzzled expression, perhaps sensing little of the killing intent. That was all.

"Gururu!"

Set, seeing Rei, growled as he approached.

The passersby who had been petting Set watched him go, their expressions regretful but not persistent.

"Business done. Let's head to the guard station."

"The... station...?"

Though Gifnan still couldn't speak properly, he didn't miss what Rei was saying.

Just as he was trying to grasp the situation, the words "security squad headquarters" came out of Rei's mouth.

Why was he being taken there? He wondered... Could it be that Keena's matter had been discovered? That thought finally occurred to him.

But he hadn't left any evidence linking him to Keena, and with Keena dead, he couldn't understand how they'd found him.

After all, he hadn't even given his name or said where he belonged.

At this point, Gifnan quite naturally assumed Kena was already dead.

But given that Kena was an assassin, that judgment was certainly not wrong.

No, under normal circumstances, it would be considered correct.

What was fatal for Gifnan was that even after being subjected to such a trick, he had come to believe Rei fit within the ordinary framework.

"Come on, walk properly. It hurts when you get pulled, doesn't it?"

Rei told Gifnan this while pulling his arm, but Gifnan probably wanted to scream, "Don't be ridiculous!"

Given that his current predicament was entirely due to Rei, it was only natural that Gifnan resented being told to walk properly by the very person responsible.

...Of course, there was no way Gifnan could actually complain to Rei right now.

Still, seeing Rei had already calmed his killing intent and was walking as if nothing was particularly bothering him, Gifnan felt his tension slowly... really, very slowly, begin to ease, allowing his body to move again.

He couldn't even think of trying to escape from Rei.

After all, Rei was dragging him along by his arm.

Trying to escape now would undoubtedly fail.

Moreover, if he tried to escape now, it would undoubtedly put Precious at a disadvantage, making it absolutely impossible for him to attempt it.

In the end, all he could do was obediently follow Rei and the others.

"Oh, Set. You're still so cute. Here, eat this."

"Eek! Set, let's play together!"

"Huh? Hey, who's that guy getting dragged along by Rei? He's not one of the Crimson Wings, is he?"

"Ah, I've never seen that face before. Could it be someone connected to a request?"

"Vihera-onee-sama..."

As they walked down the street like that, various people called out to Set or saw Rei and the others and started talking.

They must have gotten far enough from the inn. There was no longer anyone there who sensed the killing intent Rei had emitted.

They looked at Rei and the others and spoke as if it were perfectly normal.

Perhaps it was the touch of that everyday atmosphere that helped, but the tension gradually began to leave Gifnan's body too.

"...So, you're taking me to the station. Can I ask why?"

Once the tension eased, it was only natural to be curious about his current situation, so he asked Rei, who was holding his hand.

He wanted to gather even a little information, to be of use to Precious... that thought was probably there too.

Whether Rei sensed Gifnan's thoughts or simply found answering troublesome, he let the question go in one ear and out the other.

Still, he never loosened his grip on Gifnan's wrist, ensuring he couldn't escape.

"Hey, listen. Can you at least explain the situation a little? Otherwise, I don't know what I'm supposed to do."

"...What you're going to do, I'll tell you once we get to headquarters. So just stay put until then. Don't even think about trying to escape, okay? If you pull that stunt, Precious will get hurt too."

"I won't run. Besides, you're holding my arm, so even if I wanted to, I couldn't escape."

The words flew out almost reflexively as he spoke to Rei. The instant they left his lips, Gifnan remembered that the hand gripping his arm belonged to a monster, despite its appearance, and he gasped.

But, fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—Rei didn't seem the type to get angry over something like that.

"I see. Then you'll come with us quietly. Ultimately, it'll be for Precious's own good."

"Precious-sama...?"

Rei nodded back at Gifnan, who was looking at him suspiciously.

(Paying for the sins committed will undoubtedly benefit Precious... though first, you'll have to endure the punishment I'll give you.)

He attacked Azimov, stole Sleipnir Boots, and tried to align with the Azoth Trading Company.

Rei had not forgotten that matter.

Given that, proper retribution was necessary.

"That's right. If you pull any strange stunts, it only puts Precious at a disadvantage. Remember that."

Knowing Gifnan cherished Precious, Rei drove the point home.

Those words served as the sharpest warning for Gifnan, restraining him from any reckless actions.

Having been told that any suspicious behavior toward Rei would disadvantage Precious, Gifnan had no choice but to behave himself.

As Rei and the others walked along... eventually, the security guard station came into view ahead.
