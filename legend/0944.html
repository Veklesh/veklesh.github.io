Watching Vicente charge straight toward him, Rei manipulated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour to launch it.

A section of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour flew straight toward Vicente... and the moment it landed on its trajectory, immense flames surged up around it like a wall.

"What!?"

Vicente, who had charged forward, leapt diagonally forward in an instant.

Had he flown straight into the wall of flames, he would undoubtedly have suffered severe burns.

Normally, the metal armor worn by the Stigmata mitigates or nullifies most attacks through magical enchantments.

But this was an attack from Rei, who was still resisting even within the Sanctuary, a space that should have been absolutely advantageous for them. If he took it head-on, it was uncertain whether he would survive unscathed.

That judgment had been correct.

Had he charged into that wall of flames, he would have been incinerated along with his armor, which he relied on, by the flames created by Rei's compressed magical power.

It was likely a reflexive action, but it was as correct as it could possibly be.

But... even so, Vicente's struggle continued unabated.

As if anticipating his evasion, the next portion of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour was unleashed. Upon striking the ground, it transformed into viscous flames, spreading like a net to envelop Vicente's body.

"Guh!"

Realizing a direct charge would get him tangled in the fiery net, Vicente forcibly altered his direction once more, fully aware of the strain it would place on his body.

Under normal circumstances, such an action would surely injure his knees or ankles, but within the Sanctuary, it was not impossible.

"You stay put too!"

Glancing at Vicente as he pulled back, Rei then severed a section of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and hurled it toward the old man's location.

"Hehehe. Even knowing it's futile, you still choose to attack me?"

The old man declared with a smile, but the fragment of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour that Rei had launched exploded mid-air. In the next instant, it dispersed into countless tiny flames, scorching the entire area surrounding the old man.

(How about that?)

At the point where Rei's gaze was fixed, the old man vanished the instant the flames hit, as if it were only natural... only to reappear in a location far removed from the area now heavily scorched by the fire.

"Persistent!"

Without confirming the old man's location, Ray saw Vicente, having dodged the flaming net, making a wide detour towards him. Ray hurled another piece of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

As if to seize the initiative, the fragment of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour that struck the ground instantly flared into a massive inferno.

Expecting flames to surge toward him again, just as before, Vicente flinched in surprise, kicking off the ground to distance himself from the inferno.

"Damn it!"

The words spat out were likely straight from Vicente's heart.

The sheer variety of flames, impossible to believe they all stemmed from the same attack method.

If Noiz were here, he would undoubtedly nod in agreement.

This attack method was the skill derived from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour: Deep Flame.

It involved throwing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which compressed Rei's magical power, and then shaping the flames into whatever form Rei desired.

While its raw power isn't as great as the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour itself, the fact that the flames take whatever shape Rei imagines... that it depends entirely on her imagination, means it's extremely difficult for the target to counter.

Even Noiz, a Rank S adventurer who named this technique Deep Flame, found it troublesome. It's no wonder Vicente couldn't handle it.

In fact, the reason he hasn't taken a hit from Deep Flame's attack yet is precisely because of the Sanctuary, an innate skill of the Stigma deployed around him.

"You intend to keep me at bay? Well, I have my own plans."

Vicente spat out the words with obvious irritation, immediately assuming a fighting stance.

The distance between him and Rei wasn't just a few steps. Despite being over ten meters apart, Vicente clenched his fist tightly, as if to say such distance meant nothing.

What was poured into it wasn't magic power... but Vicente's own faith.

An intense devotion to the Goddess of Holy Light, usable only while this sanctuary was active.

That power, concentrated entirely within Vicente... the next instant, it erupted.

"Receive the sacred power!"

With a battle cry, he swung his fist forward in place. The next instant, a fist formed from light materialized and flew toward Rei, who had deployed his Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Sensing the immense power behind that strike, Rei's brow furrowed slightly.

(Can I take this head-on?)

Had this been ordinary space, he would have judged it no problem to withstand.

He had deep trust in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which had fought Noiz on equal terms.

But... the biggest problem was this place.

This entire area, filled with some strange, inexplicable force that even Rei couldn't comprehend, was clearly abnormal.

Indeed, the effect significantly reduced Rei's physical capabilities, leaving him barely able to move around properly and inevitably forcing him to play catch-up.

After a split-second hesitation, he ultimately chose evasion.

Despite wearing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, his movements still felt sluggish. To evade, Rei didn't make exaggerated motions; instead, he pulled half his body back into a half-body stance, creating space for the light fist to pass through.

Even that small movement felt as though dozens, perhaps hundreds of kilograms of weights were strapped to his entire body, making him sluggish.

Frustrated by his sluggishness, Rei nevertheless managed to steady himself. Realizing he couldn't predict what would happen if the Light Fist made contact with the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, he directed most of the armor's power as a Deep Flame and unleashed it toward the old man.

The fist of light passed directly beside Rei, and the deep flames struck the spot where the old man stood almost simultaneously.

"Guh!"

Despite evading it, the pain was palpable.

It transmitted an intense shock even through the Dragon Robe Rei wore.

While the Dragon Robe was strong against blades and magic, it was by no means invulnerable to techniques that delivered shock directly through it.

Vicente's Light Fist was precisely such a technique.

Still, he wasn't blown away by the fist's impact. He managed to endure the pain on the spot, likely because he had successfully dodged it without taking a direct hit.

What would have happened if it had connected? Considering that, Rei decided he couldn't leave Vicente free. And then...

BOOM!

That sound echoed throughout the area.

Rei didn't question what caused that sound.

It was his own attack, after all.

He turned his gaze toward the source of the sound to see the result. What met his eyes was a scene that could only be described as a sea of flames.

The deep flames he had just unleashed had spread rapidly the moment they struck the ground.

Compared to the attack he had unleashed just before, which had exploded in midair to cover a wide area, the range of this attack was dozens of times greater.

"Guh!"

And then came the agonized cry.

(Just as I thought.)

Realizing the cry came from a spot far removed from where the old man had been moments before, he forced a look of understanding onto his face despite the pain.

Yes, in this series of exchanges, Rei had taken damage, but the gains were far greater.

The old man with the Stigma who had been causing nothing but trouble since the battle began.

His movement, vanishing as if teleporting the instant he was attacked—what if it wasn't teleportation?

That thought had crossed Rei's mind during the fight, but after several attacks yielded no response, he began to doubt his own reasoning.

Holding onto that thought, he unleashed the Deep Flame while dodging Vicente's attack.

The resulting spread of flames, which could be called a zone suppression, was completely unexpected even for the old man.

He likely believed the flames wouldn't reach his position, but that calculation was wildly off.

As a result, he suffered significant burns.

...Yes, not the old man who had been standing where Rei had aimed the Deep Flame, but the old man who had been standing far away from that spot.

(An illusion, huh. And one that realistic? Impressive.)

Relieved that the mystery was finally solved, he poured magic back into the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which had been consumed as Deep Flame.

The next instant, a visible mass of compressed, concentrated crimson magic swirled around Rei once more.

Even so, Rei's brow furrowed slightly.

The magic power being poured into the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour consumed several times... perhaps even over ten times the usual amount.

Originally, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour didn't consume that much more magic power compared to the Overlord’s Armour it was based on.

Compared to the Overlord’s Armour, which even Rei couldn't maintain for long, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour significantly reduced mana consumption.

But that comparison was only relative to the Overlord’s Armour.

Even the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour would only last a mere ten to twenty seconds for an ordinary mage.

The reason Rei could wield the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which consumed that much magic power, was thanks to his immense reserves of magic power.

(Is this also the power of that so-called Sanctuary? ...He possesses a troublesome ability. It must be the power of that Stigma thing.)

Even so, with the magical power Rei possessed, using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour again was not difficult.

Thus, with the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour activated, Rei faced Vicente, who glared at him from afar with a stern expression.

"You... You actually dodged a strike imbued with my faith. I never imagined anyone could pull off such a feat within the Sanctuary. No, perhaps that's precisely why you're such a formidable foe."

"Your tone's changed quite a bit since the start. Is that your true self? ...Sacred domain, huh? How convenient you are with your words. To me, this isn't some sacred domain. It's more like a realm of a corrupted god, filled with miasma. That makes more sense to me."

"Huh!? You bastard!"

The greatest anger he had shown thus far.

His expression was truly worthy of being called demonic.

His normally handsome features only served to accentuate the sheer strangeness of it.

He must have felt his own faith defiled. Driven by that rage, he tried to close the distance to Rei in one swift move...

"Calm yourself, you fool! How can you fall for such a simple provocation? He intends to divide us and destroy us one by one!"

"I know that much myself! But I swear on my faith, I will bring divine punishment upon that man, our great enemy! Besides, without the gods' protection in this sanctuary, his movements are sluggish. Now is the perfect opportunity!"

"...That's why I keep telling you to calm down. Don't be so hasty. He remains my greatest enemy too. Given that, I understand your feelings perfectly well. ...Look at this arm—it trembles with the joy of piercing him through with this staff."

"Master..."

Vicente turned his gaze toward the old man, clearly moved. But for Rei, having Vicente calm down now was a problem.

Thanks to this mysterious entity called the Sanctuary, his physical abilities and magical power had all declined, while the two Stigmata users' powers had actually increased.

(I don't envy them just for that. If only I could use this skill too...)

Forcibly lowering the abilities of others while boosting one's own.

From Rei's perspective, who often operated solo and faced multiple opponents, such a skill was incredibly enviable.

But he immediately shook his head silently.

From their earlier words and actions, he understood this skill stemmed from the tattoos... the Stigmata.

This skill had a prerequisite of sorts, which Vicente suggested was likely faith in the Goddess of Holy Light.

Even if it wasn't quite that, it was more than likely a secret technique of the Holy Light Church.

If that was the case, then to gain access to the Sanctuary, one would have to become a follower of the Holy Light Church...

"No thanks,"

He muttered under his breath.

Rei didn't know what triggered it.

But it was certain that those seemingly innocuous words had fatally enraged Vicente.

"You bastard! I will never forgive you! In the name of the Holy Goddess of Light, I will bring down divine punishment upon you, my sworn enemy, punishment so severe it will wish you dead!"

Vicente began gathering light in his fist once more, while the old man beside him sighed.

Watching this, Rei... suddenly shifted his gaze behind Vicente and let out a small smile.

Because what he saw was...

"Guruu!"

...was because there stood Set, Rei's partner—a being who could even be called his other half.

With a short cry, Set vanished. His front paws must have plunged down from above. Though Vicente sensed Set's approach and managed to dodge, the next instant, a blow struck his body as if to say, "Here's your payback."
