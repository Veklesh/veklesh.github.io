That night, Set slept in the stable as usual.

However, he wasn't in a position ready to spring into action like when camping; instead, he curled up like a cat.

This sleeping posture stemmed from his full understanding that this was the stable, where no adversaries threatened him.

Not like a feline animal... but rather, he slept like a giant cat itself. The other horses in the stable made no fuss about it.

The other horses in the stable had initially feared Set, the griffin.

But once they understood Set would never attack them, their fear gradually faded.

Of course, they didn't fully trust him, but it was enough for them to feel a sense of relief that he wouldn't attack them without reason.

Admittedly, it did take them considerably longer to get used to him compared to the horses Set had been with before.

Still, since Set spent the entire day in the stable with them unless summoned by Rei, he naturally spent long periods alongside the stable horses. Thus, a sort of compromise-driven calm was gradually taking shape within the stable.

...Though, there had been that one time before when Set bolted from the stable, causing quite a commotion among the horses at the time.

Anyway, now that it was night, the stable naturally drifted into quiet hours.

That changed around the hour that could be called midnight.

Set, who had been sleeping curled up like a cat, suddenly twitched.

Lifting his head, Set looked around restlessly, as if something had caught his attention.

"Grrr?"

The other horses in the stable must have noticed Set, who was purring while looking around. The sleeping horses woke up, and the noise gradually grew louder.

Completely oblivious to the commotion around him, Set continued to observe his surroundings, guided by a sense of unease within him. Suddenly, he froze, his movements halting.

This reaction stemmed from his detection of Rei using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Only Set, magically connected to Rei, could sense it.

Even so, he didn't immediately bolt out of the stable.

Because the last time Rei used the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, nothing happened when he dashed out of the stable.

But... even so, Set hesitated.

It wasn't simply because he sensed the activation of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, like last time.

There was something else, a sense of unease within him, that caused his hesitation.

It took him a dozen seconds to make up his mind and decide to head toward Rei.

A voice inside him whispered that Rei might be in some kind of danger.

"Gurrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

He growled, rising from his crouched position.

In that instant, the air around Set shifted from ordinary to that of a battlefield.

The other horses in the stable, though they likely didn't fully understand what Set was doing, began to neigh persistently toward him.

Their neighs sounded almost like they were cheering Set on.

Of course, Set couldn't understand horse whinnies, so perhaps it was just his imagination.

But even so, to Set in that moment, it felt as if the other horses were cheering him on.

"Grrr... Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

He neighed loudly, then proceeded to destroy the door that kept the animals inside the stable from freely going outside, bursting out into the stable aisle.

He was well aware that such behavior would cause trouble for Rei.

In fact, he had witnessed the stable staff giving Rei a mild reprimand the last time he had bolted from the stable.

The door was designed to be bolted from the outside to keep the animals inside.

It wasn't a lock in the true sense, just a simple wooden bar to keep the door shut, but even so, opening it from the inside was difficult.

Well, maybe Set could manage it using his skills.

But he judged that such a maneuver would take too much time, so he chose to force his way through instead.

"GRRRRRR!"

Breaking through the door into the passageway, Set immediately ran out of the stable.

As if sending Set off, the horses inside the stable neighed loudly.

"Grrrr!"

Propelled by the horses' voices from behind, Set stepped outside the stable and immediately flapped his wings, soaring into the air.

Just like in Gilm, flying freely was forbidden in Glacial.

But Set instinctively realized this was no time for such rules, and flew through the sky as if to say, "Who cares about regulations?"

Flying was inherently a far superior means of travel compared to running on the ground.

And when Set was flying, his speed could be described as overwhelming.

That's precisely why, within mere seconds of taking flight, he arrived at the location where the battle raged below.

"GRRRR!"

Yet, Set ignored the battle unfolding below, simply soaring past it without intervention.

The battle between what appeared to be an elf and the owner of the armor reflecting the moonlight might normally have piqued his interest, but not now.

What mattered more than that was the change in his beloved Rei.

(Huh? That was a griffin, right? Ah, right, a griffin is Rei's familiar. If that's the case... I might not even need to go send reinforcements.)

Sardat, bow in hand, turned his gaze toward the man.

The man at the end of that gaze hadn't suffered a fatal wound, but areas not covered by his armor bore numerous cuts and gashes of varying sizes.

Moreover, every single one of the pawns he had prepared lay collapsed on the ground, either unconscious or dead.

Sardat offered a small smile to the man glaring at him with a bitter expression before speaking.

"It seems within this peculiar space, the strength of one's power fluctuates based on various factors... but once you get used to it, it's no big deal."

"You bastard..."

The man gritted his teeth at Sardat, who spoke as if it were the easiest thing in the world.

He hadn't thought he could win.

Still, he hadn't expected the matchup to be this disastrous.

His Stigma-created sanctuary was effortlessly seen through, then nullified.

Of course, knowing his opponent was a Rank S adventurer, Saldat the Heavenly Bow, he hadn't expected an easy victory.

Still, being pushed back this overwhelmingly was beyond his expectations.

Normally, a man's Sanctuary, where power fluctuates based on various factors, should have given me a one-sided advantage over my opponent.

But Saldat, though slightly flustered at first, quickly grasped the nature of this Sanctuary and countered it.

The man couldn't comprehend what kind of ability allowed such a thing.

He'd never seen anyone respond like this within his Sanctuary before, and it unsettled him.

Their innate power levels were already different to begin with. There was no way he could fight Saldat fairly in such a situation, and he gradually began to be pushed back.

"The Stigma I fought didn't use strange techniques like this... Is this your power? If so, we were poorly matched. Honestly, the Stigma I fought before had more potential. ...So, what now?"

"...Even if I can't win here, if I can delay you, that's enough. I knew from the start I couldn't defeat you."

"Ah, stalling for time. You were planning to attack Rei while I was distracted, weren't you? ...Poor thing."

"What?"

Sardat's unexpected reaction made the man frown in puzzlement.

He hadn't expected that kind of response here.

"Pity, you say? What do you mean? I admit I can't defeat you. But you should understand it's not impossible to fight a battle where I don't lose. That way, that man, our great enemy, would have to face both Stigmas..."

"Yeah, that possibility might have existed."

Before the man could finish, Sardat nodded.

Then why? Facing the man's questioning gaze, Saldat smiled and spoke.

"Actually, it'd be a lie to say I wasn't worried about that at first too. But... that worry just vanished a moment ago."

"...Just now?"

The man couldn't understand what Sardat was talking about, but the one who muttered it had no intention of answering.

There was no need to deliberately resolve the other man's confusion, and he understood that the information he'd just given was perfectly sufficient to leave the man thoroughly bewildered.

And then...

"So, sorry, but I'm going to have to make my decision now!"

With that declaration, he kicked off the rooftop floor beneath his feet.

The force rebounding from the floor due to the Sanctuary's effect was abnormally strong, but Sardat deflected that force, using only the appropriate amount as his own propulsion.

If the force returning was excessive, he could simply deflect it; if it was insufficient, he could apply more force.

It sounded simple enough, but how many people could actually do it on command? Especially in the heat of battle?

Then again, it's precisely because they can perform such feats that they hold the rank of S-rank adventurer, the pinnacle of their profession.

The man braced himself, ready to dodge the Saldat charging straight at him without hesitation, even within this sanctuary.

(The fact he can move normally even within this sanctuary proves he's worthy of being an S-rank adventurer. But... even so, since he can't exert his usual strength, he should be able to buy time. He said something meaningful earlier, but he can't let that unsettle him.)

Ultimately judging that Sardat's words were meant to unsettle him, the man took a firm stance.

As he had thought inwardly, within this domain, he should still be able to hold off the Saldat for a little while longer.

Trusting his own judgment.

"Gluuuh! ...Gluuuh?"

Completely unconcerned with the commotion on the ground that didn't involve Rei, Set, who had been flying through the air, sensed the presence he had been searching for below. He let out a momentary, pleased growl, but the next instant, he tilted his head in puzzlement.

Set's keen senses, and even his sixth sense, were detecting an anomaly on the ground.

Should he descend to the ground now? Set hesitated for an instant, but the moment he understood that Rei, clad in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, had glanced his way—even if only for a second—he grasped what was being demanded of him and immediately sprang into action.

"Guruu!"

With a short growl, Set vanished from sight.

This was the effect of Set's skill, optical camouflage.

It allowed him to become transparent for about twenty seconds—a skill that was a nightmare for anyone facing him. However, it also had a drawback: once used, it took thirty minutes to recharge.

Now invisible, Set dove straight down toward his target.

His target was the man radiating intense killing intent toward Rei.

He aimed for that man, Vicente, preparing to strike with his front paws while utilizing the speed of his dive.

A strike from the front paws of Set, over two meters in length, delivered from a hundred meters up.

Furthermore, Set wears the magic item known as the Bracelet of Might, amplifying his already formidable griffin strength.

Such a lethal strike, unleashed by Set in his invisible state, would normally end a life without a second thought. ...Yes, normally.

"Gluuuh!?"

Set, who had been descending toward the ground, suddenly sensed a strange phenomenon: the strength draining from his body once he reached a certain altitude.

Unable to comprehend what had happened for a moment, he let out a cry of confusion.

Naturally, neither Vicente nor the old man, the two Stigmata, failed to hear that cry.

Anyone who had gathered even the slightest information about the person named Rei would inevitably know of the griffin, Set, existing there.

Given that, it was only natural for them to remain prepared for the griffin to burst in at any moment, even while fighting Rei.

But... even Vicente and the others couldn't have imagined that Set would become invisible.

The instant Vicente heard Set's cry, he reflexively leapt backward, and the old man vanished on the spot.

Then, he sensed something fall where he had been standing just an instant before.

However, when Vicente, expecting a griffin to be there, took up an intercepting stance, what he saw was not a griffin, but something unidentifiable.

The snow had melted due to the effect of Rei’s Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, and while mud clung to its body from that, it was essentially something transparent.

Set, too, was bewildered by the sluggish sensation in his body... Both froze, momentarily unable to comprehend the situation. Yet, Set, still transparent, snapped back to his senses a split second faster.

"GRRRRRR!"

Rather than hesitate, he must save Rei first. With that thought, a transparent strike from his front paws was unleashed toward Vicente.
