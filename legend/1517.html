The sound of meat being pounded echoes through the alley.

"Guh... s-stop... please, I was wrong, I was wrong..."

A man in his twenties said this while bowing his head to the person he'd gotten into a fight with... Rei.

His face was already badly swollen from several punches.

Blood flowed from his nose and mouth, disrupting his breathing. Scattered around him, reflecting the moonlight and streetlights, were what must be his teeth.

The man, looking utterly battered and bruised, wanted to tell himself if he could go back a few minutes, to absolutely not pick a fight with the person in front of him.

If things had gone according to his plan, their positions should have been reversed right now.

He'd intended to take his regular prostitute, but for some reason, she was already gone from the brothel. Half out of frustration, he'd planned to tell Rei that it was still too early for him to be in this pleasure district...

But it was just plain bad luck, or rather, he'd picked the wrong person to mess with.

"Is that so? Personally, I'd be happy if you played along a bit longer. I haven't had much chance to move my body lately, so the feel of punching someone tends to get dull."

"H-hii! S-sorry! I'm sorry! I'll pay you back, anything! Please!"

"Well, well, that's odd. You were bragging about being a Rank E adventurer, weren't you? Yet you're making such a pathetic spectacle of yourself over something this trivial... Could it be... you're a fake or something?"

Facing Rei's beaming smile, the man could feel nothing but terror.

It was true the man was a Rank E adventurer, but only within the Kingdom of Reburina.

In other words, compared to the guilds in Gilm that Rei knew, the guilds in the small country of Reburina, where dangerous monsters weren't exactly rampant, inevitably had more lenient screening standards.

...Though, while powerful monsters weren't common, thieves and the like did roam the land instead.

(And then there's this massive entertainment district like Mejougo... deliberately located outside the capital, Rossi. Come to think of it, it's a prime target for thieves, isn't it?)

Then why aren't they ever attacked by thieves?

Rei pondered this and quickly reached a conclusion.

After all, if the organization that runs this Mejougo is the same one Ajax belonged to, it wouldn't be strange if they had arrangements with the surrounding bandits.

Generally, bandits establish a base somewhere and operate from there.

Of course, that's just the basic pattern; in reality, there are also types of bandits who roam around, repeatedly carrying out raids.

But if asked which type was more common, it was overwhelmingly the former.

Given that, overconfidence aside, it made sense that Mejougo wouldn't need to worry about bandits... Understanding this, Rei turned his gaze back to the man trembling before him.

(To extract information, it's best to break his will completely first.)

Thinking this, he smiled and opened his mouth.

"Well then, big brother. Since you're here, would you care to participate in my experiment?"

"...An experiment? What kind of experiment is that?"

Facing the man who asked timidly, Rei opened his mouth with a broad smile.

"It's an experiment to see how long a person can endure having their bones broken over and over... Don't worry, despite how I look, I'm quite confident in my skills. I'm sure you won't die or anything."

"Eek!"

The man must have realized that Rei was absolutely serious.

A stifled scream escaped the man's lips.

"I-I'm sorry! I really am sorry, please! Stop it already! W-w-what? Look, I'll give you all the money I have!"

As he spoke, the man held out a leather pouch containing his entire fortune toward Rei with trembling hands.

But Rei, still wearing a broad smile at the man's attitude... shook his head.

"No, no one said they wanted money, right? What I want is a subject that can really put up a fight. ...Don't worry. Believe me, I pride myself on being pretty good at breaking bones."

"No, no! That's not what I meant!"

"Fine? Ah, so you're happy to be my test subject? That's very kind of you."

As Rei took a step forward, deliberately twisting the meaning of "fine," the man—now completely stripped of the arrogant air he'd had when first accosting him—frantically shook his head.

"No! Stop it, that's not what I meant!"

"Japanese has its tricky parts, doesn't it..."

"Nihongo...? What the hell is that?"

Did he not understand the meaning of the word "Japanese," or was he implying he didn't know such a language existed?

Rei couldn't tell either, but in any case, he realized his slip of the tongue and frowned slightly... yet the next moment, he smiled again and addressed the man.

"Nothing at all. ...Well then, shall we get started? Are you ready? Though I have no idea what kind of preparation 'breaking bones' entails."

"Wait! Wait a minute! Please, I beg you. Look, I admit I was wrong to pick a fight with you. I've made amends, haven't I? So, just a little longer... I beg you!"

The man bowed deeply, his eyes filled with fear, as if trying to hide it.

Seeing the man like this, he must have judged his will was already sufficiently broken.

He stepped closer... and lightly placed his hand on the trembling man's shoulder, still bowed.

The man flinched at the touch, but Rei spoke to him with a smile.

"So, I have a few questions I'd like to ask. Will you answer them? If you do, I might let your brother go. ...But if you don't know anything, or worse, if you lie... you know what'll happen, right?"

"I-I understand. I'll tell you anything I know. So please, just let me go!"

Satisfied with the man's words and attitude, Rei nodded. After a few seconds of silence, meant to drive the point home, she spoke again.

"Then, could you tell me a little about this Mejougo?"

"About Mejougo...? Wh-what do you want to know? Recommended prostitutes or something like that?"

"Hmm... Unfortunately, it's a bit different. For example, running a place like this Mejougo requires all sorts of special exceptions, right?"

"...I don't know the details, but I suppose so."

There was no way asking an adventurer about city management or permits would yield a proper answer.

Well, not absolutely—there are smart adventurers too. But at least this adventurer trembling before Rei was the kind of rough-and-tumble guy you'd typically picture when you heard the word "adventurer."

Realizing he'd been foolish to ask such a difficult question of such a person, he opened his mouth again.

"Then, next question. There are a lot of prostitutes in this Mejougo. How do you think they gathered so many?"

"Huh? Even if you say that... didn't they become prostitutes because they couldn't make a living in villages or wanted to make easy money or something like that?"

The man responded to Rei's question with a look that said he didn't understand what she was talking about.

His question was pure curiosity; he genuinely seemed to believe the women had come to Mejougo of their own free will to become prostitutes.

The man's personality was rough, but precisely because of that, he seemed simple and not the type to be good at hiding things.

In a good way, that made him a source of information for Rei.

But precisely because of that, it made Rei realize the man probably didn't know much.

(Damn. I thought he'd be a convenient source of info since he approached me... but maybe it's too soon to give up. There might be other ways to get information.)

Feeling a little disappointed, Rei nevertheless turned to the man again and asked.

"Alright, next question. This Mejougo... As the name 'entertainment district' suggests, it's not an ordinary town, right? Like I mentioned earlier, places like this obviously have their own rulers, right? Beyond the nobles entrusted with managing this area, I mean."

He meant the kind of people you have to pay protection money or escort fees to.

Formally, some noble family probably holds the title of governing Mejougo, but Rei suspected that was likely a bluff... well, not quite a bluff, but just a facade.

And the organization that actually controls this Mejougo? That's probably the same one Ajax belonged to.

"Well... I know that, of course. But what do you intend to do with that information?"

Rei offered no reply to the man who asked timidly, merely returning a smile.

The instant he saw that look on Rei's face, a chill ran down the man's spine. Even though it was still summer and a tropical night where sweating was perfectly normal, he felt the sweat drain from him.

"No, nothing! It's nothing, so don't worry about it!"

As the man declared frantically, Rei kept smiling... but the quality of that smile changed as he opened his mouth.

"You're clever. ...If you'd shown that cleverness a little sooner, you might not have ended up like this. ...Well, I'm glad to have gotten some information. So, what's the name of the organization?"

"...It's an organization called Jāya."

Bingo.

Rei murmured inwardly.

He had, of course, heard that name before from Gilm's Intelligence Unit.

That very fact made the mention of Jaya here an entirely expected development for Rei.

But mentioning it carelessly would arouse suspicion, so he couldn't ask Kotonara, who was riding in the carriage with him.

"Huh... Jaya, huh? So that organization runs Mejougo?"

"Y-Yeah. But Jaya is an organization with considerable power in this country. Word is they have connections even to the upper echelons of the government. They're not an opponent you can mess with carelessly."

He stated it so firmly because he understood just how much power Jaya held within the Kingdom of Reburina.

(The guy who hit on me at that bar and gave me info about Mejougo didn't say anything about Jaya... Well, maybe he thought it went without saying? Or maybe it's known within Mejougo, but not that widely outside of it?)

Feeling a bit uneasy that the man who'd approached me was my source, I prompted him to continue.

"So, could you tell me where the headquarters of this organization called Jāya is?"

"Hell no! I don't know! Jaya's an underground organization, for crying out loud! It's got connections to the upper echelons of the government! How the hell would a lowly guy like me know something like that?!"

"...I see. Well, that's probably about what I expected. But you might know a place that isn't their headquarters, right?"

"W-Well... I do know some things... But it's a place anyone who knows even a little about Mejougo would know about!"

"Even though it's an underground organization, and even if it's not the main headquarters, isn't it a problem that so many people know where the hideout is?"

Though Rei sensed a hint of doubt in the man's tone, he nodded without hesitation and spoke.

"If something happens in Mejougo, that's where we're supposed to go. So..."

A police box? Rei wondered for a moment, but if the organization Jaya was effectively running Mejougo, then such a place would probably be necessary.

Causing trouble in Mejougo would be a slap in the face to Jaya.

Even knowing that, with taverns and prostitutes present, some people were bound to lose control.

What's more, people came to Mejougo from other countries besides Reburina.

Many probably wouldn't even recognize the name "Jaya" as an organization.

After that, Rei heard various bits of information from the man, but as he himself said, there wasn't anything particularly important... Still, he listened to all sorts of minor details.

When he was told that a particular favorite prostitute had vanished from the brothel without warning, causing the man to grow irritable and take it out on Rei... Rei could only sigh.
