A murmur rose. Those who saw Rei and Set began to murmur.

It was only natural. After all, just a short while ago, they had been staging a mock battle against a large crowd on stage.

For those who had been thoroughly excited by that mock battle, they likely never imagined they’d get to see Rei in a place like this.

But Rei and Set, despite being the focus of such stares, seemed completely unfazed as they headed toward a nearby food stall.

The food sold at that stall was skewered meat—a very simple, everyday dish.

That said, precisely because it is such a common dish, various techniques are employed in its preparation—from the cooking process and seasoning to the pre-processing of the meat and the way it is cut.

It’s likely that because it’s such a popular dish, it sells well and is described as commonplace.

In any case, Rei paid the shopkeeper—who was clearly intrigued by Set’s presence—and received a freshly grilled skewer.

Then, seemingly completely unconcerned about the stares from those around him, he brings the skewered meat to his mouth.

“Mmm!”

“It’s not fair that Rei gets all the fun!” Set growls, and Rei, smiling, offers the skewer he’s holding to Set.

Set deftly picks up the meat skewered on the stick with his beak and eats it.

The doneness and seasoning of the meat must have been more than satisfying for Set. Set chirps happily.

Hearing Set’s chirp, the stall owner smiled happily.

Judging by Set’s reaction, he must have concluded that Set was saying his skewered meat was delicious.

There was enough joy in Set’s chirp to make that clear.

The stall owner couldn’t help but smile at Set’s chirp… but before he knew it, Rei and Set had already vanished from in front of the stall, and the others who had been watching them from nearby were suddenly swamped with a crowd of customers lining up to try the skewered meat that had made Set chirp so happily.

Meanwhile, seeing the crowd swarming the skewered meat stall, Rei felt a little let down.

He had been convinced that, just as Leodanis had said, a crowd would come up to talk to him.

But so far, no one had actually spoken to him.

(Ah)

That was what Rei had thought, but upon spotting a man clearly heading straight for him, he muttered quietly to himself.

That said, since it was a man he knew, it didn’t look like things would turn out the way Leodanis had feared.

“Hey, Rei. That mock battle was amazing.”

With a pleased smile, Brewitt called out to Rei.

While watching the mock battle, perhaps mindful of the setting, he had been dressed properly in noble attire, but now he was dressed like an ordinary person, just as he had been when he first met Rei.

Many of those around them watched with surprised expressions as Brewitt spoke to Rei.

Some even shot jealous glances, wondering why he was the one to speak up first… but Rei, Set, and Brewitt—the two humans and the dog—didn’t seem to mind at all.

“You’ve changed your clothes quite a bit in such a short time. …Come to think of it, you weren’t there when I was invited to the noble section.”

“I’m not really fond of places like that.”

“I get it.”

Rei wholeheartedly agreed with Brewitt’s words, but even though they both disliked those kinds of places, there was a huge difference between Rei and Brewitt when it came to whether they could carry on a proper conversation in such a setting.

“Oh? So you get it too, Rei? I mean, what’s so fun about those stuffy gatherings anyway? Nobles flattering each other, exchanging insincere compliments and backhanded remarks… If there’d been some food, I might’ve gone just for that, but there wasn’t even that.”

“Huh? I did see a few people eating sandwiches and stuff like that, though.”

“I hear they do serve light snacks like that, but sandwiches alone are definitely not enough to fill you up.”

“...I can’t deny that.”

Brewitt has a build worthy of being called a giant, and while Rei is small in stature, he has a huge appetite. As for Set, well, that goes without saying.

If they were to serve light meals like sandwiches that would fully satisfy the appetites of those two humans and one beast, just how much food would they have to bring?

At that rate, the supplies prepared for this mock battle would be gone in no time.

“Well, that’s why I got the hell out of there as fast as I could and ended up eating all sorts of things like this. …Want some?”

Saying that, Brewitt handed Rei a sandwich made with a fist-sized piece of bread.

It was filled with dried meat and cheese, and looked absolutely appetizing.

(But if I put a hamburger patty in this, it’d make a hamburger. There are onions, tomatoes, and lettuce-like vegetables here. …Oh, is this what a bagel sandwich is supposed to be like?)

The reason the term “bagel sandwich” didn’t immediately come to mind was that I’d never actually eaten one before.

The bakery that came to the school Rei attended didn’t sell bagel sandwiches, and they weren’t sold at places like supermarkets or convenience stores either.

Well, to be precise, they might have sold them, but at least Rei had never seen them, and he had never bought one.

That’s why the only bagel sandwiches Rei knew about were the ones that appeared in manga and novels.

I remembered that they looked exactly like the bread Brewitt was holding.

“Is that okay?”

“Yeah. Thanks to you, I made a profit.”

“……You were betting too?”

Hearing the word “made a profit,” Rei realized what Brewitt had done and shot him a look mixed with a touch of exasperation.

That said, if Brewitt had known that Rei was looking at him that way—after all, Rei had done something like betting platinum coins on his own win—he would have said he didn’t want that.

Fortunately, Rei said nothing about it and simply kept giving him that exasperated look, but he took the sandwich, gave half to Set, and ate the other half himself.

When he took a bite, he was slightly surprised by the crisp texture.

He didn’t know how this bread had been made, but the texture in Rei’s mouth was vaguely reminiscent of a croissant.

She had been convinced it would be a much denser, more substantial bread, so in a good way, Rei’s expectations were proven wrong.

“Huh, this is… a bit unexpected. And it’s delicious, too.”

“Right? I was a little surprised too when I took my first bite.”

“Which food stall is it?”

“It’s a stall a little ways from here. They had all kinds of other bread too, so if you’re interested, why don’t you go check it out?”

Hearing that, Rei tried to head straight for the bakery, but...

"Grr!"

Set, who had grabbed the hem of the Dragon Robe with his beak, let out a growl.

“What are you doing?” Rei gave Set a look that seemed momentarily confused, but when Set growled again and turned his gaze toward another food stall, Rei more or less understood what Set was trying to say.

In other words, since there are so many stalls here, he wanted to stop by the other shops too. That’s what he was trying to say.

“Hmm, I get what you’re saying. I get it, but… with bread this delicious, there’s a good chance it’ll sell out if we linger too long. Are you sure you’re okay with that?”

“Growl? …Growl.”

At Rei’s words, Set looked at the stalls lining the street, thinking for a moment.

Soup, skewers, stews, stir-fries… Beyond that, there were even stalls selling roasted nuts and raw meat.

If asked whether he’d pass by all those stalls to go to a bread shop… even Rei had to give it some thought.

It wasn’t as if they couldn’t buy bread at one stall and then get food from the others.

But Rei and Set… along with Brewitt—the two of them and the dog—are drawing every single gaze in the vicinity.

It’s as if, the moment Rei moves, those eyes follow her… that’s the feeling.

If Rei were to wander back and forth around here, it could easily become a nuisance to ordinary passersby.

Coming to that conclusion, Rei decides to follow Set’s advice after all.

“You’re surprisingly weak, aren’t you?”

Watching Rei so easily give in to Set’s request, Brewitt remarked with a hint of surprise.

In response, Rei tilted his head and asked, “Is that so?”

Actually, thinking back on their exchange just now, he couldn’t help but wonder if that was really the case… but he decided it wasn’t that strange after all.

“Anyway, I did want to buy a bunch of stuff. Plus… just being with someone as conspicuous as you is bound to draw a lot of attention, so you should be a little more careful about that.”

“Is that so? I’m the one who stands out, after all. Ha ha ha.”

Watching Brewitt let out a forced laugh, Rei headed toward a nearby soup shop for the time being.

Even though it wasn’t snowing today, it was still cold enough for winter.

Perhaps because of that, a line of about five people had formed in front of the stall.

Rei, Set, and Brewitt—who apparently had decided to stick with Rei—quietly joined the line.

“Huh?”

Perhaps not expecting such a conspicuous group to line up behind him, the man at the back of the line let out a surprised gasp.

The startled man couldn’t help but wonder if he should let Rei and the others go ahead of him, but the line was moving quickly.

That made sense. All they had to do was have the soup poured into their bowls and pay the price.

The price is a bit high for street food, but that’s because it includes the cost of the bowl.

If you return the bowl and spoon after finishing, you get a refund, bringing the price down to about the same as other stalls.

Of course, if you bring your own utensils, it would be cheaper… but in the midst of this festive chaos, it’s unlikely anyone would bring utensils just to drink soup.

That said, in Rei’s case, since he has a Misty Ring, tableware usually fits inside it.

That said, Rei wouldn’t go out of his way to take out separate tableware here.

Because if you use dishes, you have to wash them.

In other words, if he uses his own dishes here, he’ll naturally have to wash them.

If he’s going to go through that trouble, it’s quicker to just use the dishes provided at the food stalls.

So the line moved forward, and soon it was Rei and the others’ turn.

“Three bowls of soup.”

“Sure thing. It’s a pleasure to have today’s hero try our soup.”

“Eat?” I momentarily questioned the shopkeeper’s choice of words, but when I saw the bowl he handed me, I understood exactly what he meant.

Soups packed with ingredients aren’t exactly rare, but… the soup he was handed clearly had far more ingredients than an ordinary one.

Potatoes, daikon radish, carrots, beans, and meat… Rei’s first impression upon seeing the soup was, “Is this tonjiru?”

This was likely influenced by the fact that tonjiru was the miso soup with the most ingredients that had left the strongest impression on Rei.

In reality, the soup contained wild boar meat and offal, and since it wasn’t seasoned with miso—as one might expect—it was a bit of a stretch to call it tonjiru… but even so, there was no doubt that the soup I was served was delicious.

“Gurru!”

Set happily drank and ate the soup placed on the ground.

Rei and Brewitt were savoring their soup right beside him, but Brewitt suddenly turned to Rei and spoke up.

“Are you sure about treating me? I’m not exactly short on cash, so I’ll pay for myself.”

“It’s fine, really. It’s just this much. I made some money from today’s mock battle, and Brewitt gave me a sandwich earlier, didn’t you? Just think of this as payback for that.”

“If you put it that way, I won’t hold back either.”

At Rei’s words, Brewitt says nothing more and continues to savor the soup.

The rich, savory flavor of wild boar had infused the soup, and with a fair amount of fat floating on top, it was perfect for warming the body.

Just as they were savoring the soup... suddenly, Rei stopped moving.

At the same time, Set lifted his face from his soup bowl and looked around.

“Hey, what’s wrong? Did something happen?”

Brewitt didn’t understand why Rei and Set had suddenly acted that way.

But since they wouldn’t do something like that for no reason, Brewitt figured there must be a reason for it and asked.

“...Nah, it’s nothing. It seems someone was watching me.”

In this context, “watching” clearly didn’t just mean observing Rei and the others out of curiosity; it had a different meaning.

Hearing Rei’s words, Brewitt also looked around cautiously.
