"This is Vihera, my lady. How wonderful that you've come all the way to this remote place. It's been since last autumn, hasn't it?"

"Yes, it's been about half a year, I suppose. We both had quite busy times last year, but it's wonderful to see you looking well."

As Vihera and Byune, guided by the maid, entered the reception room, Daskar, seated on the sofa, greeted them with a smile.

When Rei visited this mansion, their interactions usually took place in the study. However, even though the other party had fled, welcoming a princess of the Bestir Empire naturally demanded a certain level of formality and courtesy from the host.

Daskar's choice of words, not his usual blunt tone, was likely an adjustment for that reason.

After urging Vihera and Byune to sit on the sofa and having the maid place tea and cookies brought by the maid before leaving the reception room, Daskar finally spoke.

"Normally, someone of Lady Vihera's stature visiting this frontier region would warrant a grand ceremony to welcome you..."

As if to cut him off, Vihera shook her head.

"No, don't worry about it. As you know, I've already fled the Bestir Empire. Treat me just like any other adventurer, and that's fine. ...And this child too, of course."

"Mm."

Gently pushed forward by Vihera, Byune bowed deeply toward Daskar.

Though Byune hailed from the historic and prestigious Fraut family in Exil, there was a world of difference between an autonomous city's noble house and an active Marquis of the Frontier—especially Daskar, who ruled the only frontier of the Mireana Kingdom.

Moreover, the Fraut family had already fallen into decline, and Byune was generally perceived as more akin to common folk.

"This child has some circumstances that make speaking difficult, but her name is Byune Fraut."

"Oh? This child, you say?"

"Yes. She's like a dear sister to me. Naturally, she possesses a certain level of... no, considering her age, one could say she's quite skilled."

"I see. How intriguing. We'd be delighted to have a skilled adventurer come to Gilm in the frontier. ...However, Lady Vihera—"

"Please drop the '-sama'. And you don't need to force such formal speech either. Just speak as you normally would. I ran away partly because I hated all that stiffness."

Vihera declared, shrugging her shoulders slightly.

Naturally, her ample curves swayed, attempting to capture Daskar's gaze, but he forced himself to look away.

...If Daskar had no resistance whatsoever to such allure, he would have been irresistibly drawn to Vihera's figure.

But Daskar possessed a strong resistance to intense allure.

Fortunately for him, he had years of experience with Marina, a dark elf who seemed to embody feminine allure taken to its extreme.

His childhood proposal to Marina likely contributed to his immunity to such allure.

In any case, having been told it was fine to use his usual speech, Daskar paused briefly before speaking.

"Then, if you don't mind, I'll use my usual speech patterns, okay?"

"Yes, please do. After all, even back when I was in the Bestir Empire, quite a few people in my faction didn't use polite language."

An image flashed through Vihera's mind: a man wielding battleaxes in both hands, smiling.

Daskar, likely unaware of this connection, gave a wry smile and spoke.

"Talking casually to a princess... especially one from a great nation like the Bestir Empire? That takes some nerve."

"Is that so? You're speaking to me in your usual manner too, aren't you?"

Vihera said this while sipping her tea, but Daskar shook his head slightly.

"I'm talking to the current Lady Vihera. If we'd met by mistake back in the Bestir Empire and I'd been told to use broken language... well, I wonder how that would have gone."

Muttering to himself, Daskar offered a cookie from the table toward Byune.

Byune, eating the cookie with all her might like a squirrel or something, reminded him of a small animal. Suddenly, the image of Set flashed through Daskar's mind.

(Size-wise, he's definitely not what you'd call a small animal, but when he's eating, he does have that small animal vibe. ...No wonder he's so popular.)

Though he thought of Set, his gaze fell on Vihera sitting before him, and he hurriedly refocused his attention.

"Now then. Since you came willingly when Ranga called, I know you're not plotting anything strange against Gilm. But what's your purpose here? Is it about last year's civil war?"

Even as he asked, Daskar knew Vihera's character well enough to doubt she'd do such a thing.

When he'd been told during work that Ranga had an urgent report, he'd wondered what it could be, but as long as they were talking like this, he didn't seem to be plotting anything.

(If that's the case, was she interested in the frontier? ...Is that even plausible?)

The Bestir Empire certainly had its own frontiers, but it was also true that as a princess, Vihera couldn't just casually visit such places.

So it wouldn't be strange if she came to Gilm wanting to experience the frontier for herself.

Moreover, while the term "frontier" lumps them together, the monsters that appear differ depending on the specific frontier.

Considering Vihera's combative nature, it wouldn't be strange for her to have traveled around the frontiers of various nations.

But at Daskar's words, Vihera shook her head.

"I was originally planning to come to Gilm in spring to meet up with Rei. I just didn't know if I'd be bringing Byune along, but there was a little something that happened..."

"...May I ask what those circumstances were?"

"Yes. Have you heard of the Holy Light religion?"

"I've heard the name. Fortunately or unfortunately, Gilm's location on the frontier means the Holy Light Church hasn't penetrated here. But in the territories of nobles affiliated with the Neutral Faction, it's fairly well-known. ...Though I hear more people have grown wary since last year's incident."

From Daskar's position as a central figure in the neutral faction, the Holy Light Cult seemed like a very troublesome entity.

While there's no particular need for concern in Gilm itself, his position still requires him to warn the nobles affiliated with factions.

"I suppose so. They're troublesome in many ways. Byune and I were attacked in Exil by what seemed to be assassins from the Holy Light Church. That's why we decided it was too dangerous to leave Byune in Exil and came here to Gilm together."

"...More trouble."

If one believed Vihera's words, it felt like the Holy Light Church was targeting figures like Vihera and Byune.

Though he grumbled aloud, Daskar's gaze drifted to Byune, who was stuffing cookies into her cheeks like a small animal beside Vihera.

It was painful to think this girl might become a target for the Holy Light Church, but from Daskar's position, he wanted to avoid any troublesome situations arising in Gilm as much as possible.

But... before Daskar could say anything, Vihera spoke up.

"By the way, I heard Rei was also attacked by Holy Light assassins in Graciall?"

"......"

Hearing that, Daskar couldn't simply tell her to go out recklessly.

It meant he trusted Rei's abilities that much.

Indeed, Rei's actual strength was something Daskar found extremely reliable. Plus, having the griffin Set as his familiar meant his movement speed was incomparably faster than that of ordinary cavalry.

Furthermore, as seen during last spring's war against the Bestir Empire, he was also exceptionally skilled at transporting supplies using his item box.

Beyond pure combat strength, Rei was a dependable person.

While they hadn't known each other for years, nearly two years was still a considerable time, making him someone Gilmu trusted and relied upon.

Telling the person standing before him to leave because dealing with the Holy Light Church was troublesome meant Rei would also have to tell Gilm to leave.

Of course, given the length of their relationship, Rei, Vihera, and Byune couldn't necessarily be grouped together.

But if conflict with the Holy Light Church was unavoidable, having Vihera as a combat asset would be a significant advantage for Gilm.

Considering both the advantages and disadvantages of having Vihera in Gilm for a while...

"Understood. I want Lord Vihera to stay here in Gilm and relax."

In the end, they chose to accept Vihera.

"Is that so? Well, if it means I can stay with Rei, I'd be happier that way. With this child's situation, I'd prefer to stay in Gilm if possible."

"...Hm?"

Byune, her cheeks stuffed full of cookies, turned her gaze toward Vihera, as if to say, "Did you call?"

That look only reinforced the impression Daskar had just had—that she resembled a small animal.

"Heh heh, I see. Right. I suppose I can't very well chase a child like this away. Understood. I grant permission for Lady Vihera to operate in Gilm in my name. ...However, I would ask you to refrain from actions that might provoke men."

His gaze fell upon the sheer fabric draped over her alluring form.

"Oh? I accept any challenge. Though the woman I am belongs to Rei, the part of me that seeks battle is another matter entirely."

"...It's fine that you feel that way, Lady Vihera, but I can't have it disrupting Gilm's peace. This guild is already a gathering place for many adventurers. And adventurers tend to be hot-blooded. There's bound to be no shortage of those who'd rashly attack you."

"Those people will surely come to regret their rash actions."

"If you'd just stop wearing that outfit, maybe no one would lose their minds."

As Daskar sighed and spoke, Vihera flashed a provocative smile.

Seeing that smile, one couldn't help but understand that there was no way Vihera would ever change her current attire.

In the end, all Daskar could manage was to warn her that if she went too far, he'd have no choice but to punish her as he would any other adventurer.

"Oh, hello, Rei. It's been quite a while..."

Lana, the proprietress of the Dusk Wheat inn, murmured upon spotting Rei entering the inn.

Perhaps because Rei had been a frequent guest for so long, her tone felt more familial than that of a customer.

And that was only natural. The Dusk Wheat inn was one of the more upscale inns in Gilm.

Naturally, most guests stayed only briefly, and few, like Rei, remained for extended periods.

...Though the "rarely" did include high-earning adventurers like Rei.

That very reason meant that after staying at the inn for so long, even to Lana, he felt like family.

It seemed she was sitting at the counter, resting and minding the shop now that the daytime rush had died down. A glass containing some sort of drink sat by her hand.

"Well, it's been since I left in winter. So, I'd like to rent a room again. And please take care of Set too."

Rei approached her to book a room as he had done before, but what he got in return was Lana's puzzled expression.

"Book a room...? That young lady paid for six months up front, you know?"

"...Huh?"

For a moment, Rei didn't understand what she'd said. After a few seconds of silence, he let out a dumbfounded voice.

"So, six months' worth... well, actually, it's been two months since then, so it's about four months' worth left, but that lodging fee has already been paid in advance."

"...That Marka, pulling such a clever trick."

Rei smiled faintly, thinking of Marka who had prepared not only the fire ore but also the advance lodging fee as payment. But then he remembered that Vihera and Byune were also staying at this inn.

Even Marka probably wouldn't have paid for Vihera and Byune's lodging too, he thought.

"Two women should be arriving shortly. Please prepare rooms for them."

"Oh, could it be..."

Ignoring Lana's meaningful gaze, Rei continued.

"Whether they share a room or get two single rooms, I'll ask them when they arrive. Their names are Vihera and Byune, and they're pretty noticeable, so I don't think you'll mistake them for anyone else."

"How boring. Fine, I understand."

Though clearly bored that Rei wasn't engaging, Lana switched back into her innkeeper persona.

To that Lana, Rei reiterated her request regarding Set and headed for her own room.

...Later, when Lana saw Vihera and Byune enter the inn, she understood perfectly why Rei had said they stood out.
