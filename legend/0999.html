"What do you think?"

"...I don't know. Given Rei's instincts, it wouldn't surprise me if you were right. But judging from earlier, they definitely don't seem willing to tell us who they got it from, no matter what."

"Hmm."

"Guru?"

Vihera's words drew agreement from Byune, and Set, who had been being petted by Rei, tilted his head slightly, asking, "What's up?"

"Yeah. That means we're back to square one... What do we do?"

"What do you mean, 'what should we do'? Neither Byune nor I have been in Gilm for very long, you know? What exactly do you expect us to do?"

It was obvious when he put it that way, but strangely enough, Vihera and Byune did seem to have settled into Gilm quite well in Rei's eyes.

"Hmm... Should I talk to Ranga about it? Or report to Daskar-sama... but we barely have any clues. ...Huh? Clues?"

Muttering that far, Rei suddenly remembered the magic item incense burner he'd acquired on the way to the cave.

"Come to think of it, there was a clue. And there's an alchemist who might be able to examine it."

"Oh? Did you find something that might be a clue?"

"Yeah. Remember what I mentioned in the cafeteria? That place with all the monster corpses? I got a magic item there that was apparently set up to prevent the smell of blood and rot from spreading. If we examine it, we might find something."

"If only we had something like a golem's core or that jar from deep in the cave..." Rei muttered regretfully, but Vihera just gave him a somewhat exasperated look.

"You always get dragged into trouble, yet somehow you end up with things that might just solve it. What kind of luck is that?"

"Well, what can I say? Anyway, let's go see Azimov. That's fine, right?"

When Rei asked, Vihera and Byune naturally had no objections since there were no other leads anyway, and they immediately nodded in agreement.

"Gururuu"

Set growled in agreement, and so Rei and his companions headed for Azimov's workshop.

"It's surprisingly quiet around here. Even though it's almost evening."

As evening approached, the flow of people naturally increased.

Beyond the usual activities of townspeople—early dinners, shopping, returning from work—the outlying district of Gilm would normally see a significant increase in people as adventurers completed their quests and returned. Yet now, around Azimov's workshop-cum-home, there was scarcely a soul in sight.

Occasionally, a passerby would appear, but they would jog away as if desperate to leave the area as quickly as possible.

Watching the surroundings, Vihera murmurs to herself. Rei recalls asking Pamidor the same question before and answers her query.

"Well, considering Azimov's personality, it's not surprising, is it?"

"...Are you sure it's really okay? I've only met him once, so I can't quite trust him that much."

"Mm."

Byune nodded in agreement with Vihera's words, but Rei ignored it and lightly knocked on the door in front of him.

"Azimov, are you there? Can I talk to you for a moment?"

He called out, but there was no response from inside.

If this keeps up, will I have to rip the door off like Pamidor did? Thinking that, Rei knocked again.

"Hey, Azimov! You there? Get out here already!"

The sound of his fist pounding the door grew louder and louder. It was no longer pounding; it was more like punching. It was only a matter of time before the door was destroyed. At that moment.

"Shut up! Dammit, I was in the middle of something important! What the hell is this sudden commotion?!"

He clearly couldn't stand being interrupted during his research. With veins bulging on his forehead, Azimov appeared.

"Seems you heard me clearly today. Good."

"Huh? ...Rei? What do you want? I'm completely focused on your spear right now, and you, the client, are interrupting me. What do you think you're doing?"

"Sorry, I just had something I wanted you to take a look at. When I think of a skilled alchemist I know, you're the only one who comes to mind."

The compliment of being called skilled seemed to please him somewhat. The irritation on Azimov's face subsided a little.

"Ugh. Fine. Come up. I'll hear you out inside."

"...Sorry about that. Oh, and this is a little something. I figured you'd like it."

What he offered was hair that had fallen out while petting Set on the way here, and a feather.

For Rei, these were items he could produce in abundance, but for Azimov, they were extremely rare materials for alchemy.

Some residents of Gilm had grown so accustomed to Set that they no longer fully grasped his significance, but griffins were inherently extremely rare and precious Rank A monsters.

Countless people risked their lives, and lost them, trying to obtain such materials.

Given that such valuable materials could be obtained so readily here, Gilm was undoubtedly a highly beneficial place for its alchemists, blacksmiths, and apothecaries.

Being a frontier region, rare materials naturally gathered here, making Gilm an increasingly special place for the alchemists of the Mireana Kingdom.

"Are you sure?"

"Yeah. Make good use of it."

"...Well, fine. I was looking for a break anyway. Come on in."

With a wry smile at Azimov's sudden change in attitude, Rei, Vihera, and Byune entered the building.

As usual, Set lay outside keeping watch, waiting for Rei and the others to return.

"Now then. What brings you here at such a busy time? I've already heard from the lord's messenger about you spreading that rumor that the hammer was made by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire."

"No, not that. Actually, there's something I'd like you to take a look at."

"Oh?"

At Rei's words, his previously annoyed expression shifted completely, and he turned a look of genuine interest toward him.

Bringing out the hammer the Cyclops had last time must have made a big impression.

Azimov looked at Rei with the expectation that he might have brought something similar again. Rei handed him the censer he had taken out from the Misty Ring.

"This... isn't exactly a masterpiece of craftsmanship."

Asimov, who had been touching and examining the incense burner, muttered with a lackluster tone that seemed to ask what had become of his hopeful gaze just seconds ago.

"Is that so?"

"Yeah. The hammer you brought was quite elaborate, but for something made by the same person, this is rather crude. It's not that they lack skill; they probably made it in a hurry out of necessity."

"Wait."

Though Ajimov's words were muttered as if it were nothing, Rei noticed there was information in them he couldn't ignore and cut Ajimov off.

Rei wasn't the only one who noticed. Vihera also shot a sharp glance in Azimov's direction.

"Huh? What is it?"

"They say that incense burner was made by the same person who made the hammer. Are you sure about that? Could it be that even among the alchemists of the Bestir Empire, someone other than the hammer's maker created it?"

"No."

He declared it without hesitation, then opened the lid of the incense burner and showed it to Rei.

"This part here. See? They used the heart of a lizardman—specifically one where the magic stone was left intact—soaked it in magic-infused water, dried it, and ground it into powder to channel the magic. The technique is identical to the hammer. This is a fairly valuable piece, you know."

"If it's 'fairly valuable,' doesn't that mean others might use it too?"

"Probably. But the way the magic-flowing part is made is distinctive. It's a magic item using the same material, with similar characteristics. And I can't imagine many people deliberately messing with the left-right balance like that. Well, maybe not none at all, but the idea that such people would all be gathered near Gilm... it's just hard to fathom."

As he spoke, Azimov seemed to get carried away, listing several other common features shared by the hammers and censers.

Listening, Rei realized he found himself strangely convinced.

(So it really was that alchemist using monsters as test subjects in that place.)

He had suspected it, but he hadn't had proof. It had been based almost entirely on circumstantial evidence and intuition.

That very fact meant that obtaining confirmation meant he was getting closer to the alchemist, step by step, no doubt about it.

(If that's the case, next... Ideally, I'd like him to look into who made Seymour's magic item too. But wait, Azimov's in a good mood now. Could I ask him?)

Once he'd thought it through, Rei acted swiftly.

"Azimov, sorry to bother you, but could you spare some time? There's another magic item I want you to take a look at. If we can confirm that magic item was made by the same person who crafted that incense burner and hammer, it would be a huge step forward in this case."

At Rei's words, Azimov flashed a momentary look of displeasure.

Understandable. If possible, Asimov wanted to focus on his research... specifically, he wanted to make a spear for Rei.

But... his gaze drifted to a glass jar placed a short distance away. More precisely, it settled on the strands of Set's fur and feathers inside the jar.

If he refused now, he might not be able to obtain materials like griffin feathers and hair in the future, even if he could manage this time.

He decided it was better to go through the trouble now than regret it later for skimping on a little effort.

"Alright. So where do I go?"

Rei had been certain he'd refuse to go, so he was surprised by Azimov's unexpectedly compliant response. His astonished expression faded quickly as he snapped back to reality and stood up, ready to start moving immediately.

"Alright, let's get going then."

Following Rei, Vihera and Byune also stood up, and thus Rei and the others headed toward the location of Seymour's food stall.

"Hmm, I think it was around here somewhere..."

It was already evening.

Amidst the sunset casting a red glow over the surroundings, Rei, Vihera, Byune, Azimov, and Set—four people and one creature—walked on.

They had come to the spot where Seymour had been when Rei returned to Gilm, but they couldn't find his distinctive figure.

Rei had thought it wouldn't be hard to spot him—a muscular giant, yet not particularly intimidating-looking, a somewhat rare type—but he was completely wrong.

"Grrr!"

Set chirped, tugging lightly at Rei's Dragon Robe with his beak.

Rei turned his gaze toward Set and spotted Seymour where Set was looking, letting out a sigh of relief.

"There he is. Over there. ...Thanks, Set."

"Grrrrr!"

At Rei's words, Set, who had tracked Seymour by scent, purred happily.

Leading Set and Vihera, Rei headed toward Seymour's food stall, arriving just as a customer finished paying.

"Oh, Rei. What's up?"

"I was just looking for Seymour. This spot's different from where he was when I arrived, though?"

"Huh? Oh, this spot gets more foot traffic at this time, so sales are better here. ...It's handy that stalls can be moved around like this."

"Who's gonna break it, ugh!"

Vihera covered Ajimov's mouth before he could say something he shouldn't.

Of course, Seymour didn't miss that, but he said nothing more and simply stepped aside for Azimov.

Partly because the customers had settled down, and partly because Rei, while different in approach, shared his hobby of collecting magic items.

"Alright, I'll leave it to you."

"Hmph."

Once freed from Vihera, Rei asked Azimov to examine the magic item... Within a minute, the inspection was complete, and Azimov addressed Rei.

"Just as you suspected."
