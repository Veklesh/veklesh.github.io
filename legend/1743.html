“Well then, here is the payment for the slave.”

Saying that, a guard of somewhat higher rank paid Rei the ransom for the bandits.

The amount contained therein was slightly less than average.

Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by this.

If he had taken them to another town or put them up for auction, the bandits would likely have fetched a higher price.

However, Rei hadn’t intended to make a huge profit from selling the bandits in the first place, and since taking them to another town would have been a hassle, he settled for a reasonable price.

The result is the money currently in Rei’s possession.

And there was another reason Rei settled for this price...

“Regarding the women who were captured by the slave traders, I’m counting on you to take good care of them.”

“Yes, of course. For us, too, the idea that slave traders were operating near the town is utterly disgraceful. We’ll make sure to send everyone safely back to their hometowns.”

Yes, just as the man standing before Rei had said, the guards had firmly promised to escort each of the women who had been in the slave trader’s wagon back to their respective hometowns.

Perhaps it was a silver lining: most of the women who had been captured by the slave traders were from villages and towns in this very area.

The financial burden itself shouldn’t be too great.

If they had all been from places far away—places that would take months to reach by carriage—it would have been impossible to send them all back home.

“Oh, right, about the slave trader… it seems there’s no hidden agenda. After examining the slave trader’s body and belongings, it appears he was acting alone… or at most with a few accomplices.”

“I see.”

Rei felt a slight sense of relief at the guard’s words.

Since it involved slaves, he couldn’t help but think of the incident in the Kingdom of Reburina that had occurred not long ago.

He’d wondered if there might be a connection, but since that wasn’t the case, a weight was lifted from his shoulders.

“That completes all the formalities. Once again, thank you for helping us eliminate the bandits and the illegal slave trader.”

The guard bowed deeply to Rei.

This wasn’t a bow to authority figures like Rei, who had a notorious reputation, or Marina, the former guildmaster of Gilm who was with him, or even Elena, the famous Princess General who wasn’t part of their party. It was purely an expression of gratitude for defeating a threat that had been lurking near the town.

“No, don’t worry about it. It was mostly just a matter of circumstance for us, too… and we did get the bandits’ loot. Besides, I had a bit of a grudge against that bandit.”

“A grudge, you say? If you don’t mind, could you tell me about that grudge?”

When asked this, Rei decided there was no need to hide anything, so he told them about the special potion.

Upon hearing this, the guard frowned in displeasure.

It was only natural that the fact a special potion meant to treat an illness hadn’t reached the bandit wasn’t a pleasant development for the guard either.

“Well… I’m even more grateful that you went and took them down.”

“It was just a coincidence, though. I never would’ve guessed they were using this area as their base.”

“Haha. Come on, someone who’s even called a ‘Bandit Eater’ can’t possibly be acting by chance—that’s just not how it works!”

Seeing the guard laughing, Rei gave up, realizing there was no point in saying anything more.

Perhaps this was yet another downside of becoming famous for having a nickname.

With that thought in mind, he stepped out of the guardhouse to find Rei—and Relieu—waiting for him.

“Did I keep you waiting?”

“Grr!”

Seto let out a happy yelp at Rei’s words, but Relieu looked a little disappointed that her time with Set was over.

Seeing Relieu like that, Rei smiled slightly as he stroked the head of Set, who had come closer.

On the way from Gilm to Golz, Relieu had still been holding back, at least to some extent, from showing her affection for Set, trying to hide her fondness for him from Rei and the others.

Of course, there was no way she could hide it completely, and it became known all too soon.

Still, it hadn’t been quite so obvious at first… but now, they were on their way back to Gilm.

And once they arrived in Gilm, surrounded by Set’s admirers—Milein foremost among them—a man like Relieu who loved Set would inevitably feel out of place.

That’s probably why he’s thinking he wants to fully admire Set before they arrive in Gilm.

He had already stopped trying to hide the fact that he was admiring Set.

(Well, I get the feeling he couldn’t hide it anymore even on the way to Golz.)

Perhaps envious of his closeness with Set, a hint of envy mingled in Relieu’s gaze.

Aware of this, Rei asks.

“So, about the inn? What happened with that dark elf?”

The women who had been captured by the slave traders had already been handed over to the guards.

Just as she’d discussed with the guards earlier, they were to be returned to their hometowns.

However... the Dark Elf, who had been severely abused, was to be temporarily taken in by Marina.

I suppose her sense of kinship as a Dark Elf was just too strong.

“Her wounds have been treated, and she’s apparently sleeping now to recover her strength.”

“I see. …But, it’s fine that we took her in, but what do you think she plans to do?”

Rei asked as he walked toward the inn with Relieu.

Rei, Relieu… and Set naturally drew the attention of the townspeople.

Still, the fact that there wasn’t an over-the-top reaction like there had been in Somis was probably a stroke of luck for Rei and Set.

“Don’t ask me that. I’m only tagging along as your backup. I try not to get too involved in that sort of thing.”

Hearing Relieu’s words, Rei nodded in agreement… and soon, they arrived at their destination.

This was undoubtedly a high-class inn, even by the standards of this town.

It wasn’t that Rei and the others had chosen this inn because they wanted to splurge; it was selected purely because of the size of the stables available for Set.

They left Set in the care of the inn staff and went inside…

“Oh, Rei. You’re early. Have you finished the check-in already?”

Vihera, who had been sitting in a chair talking with Byune, noticed Rei and Relieu entering the inn.

“Is that so? I thought it took quite a while.”

“Considering how many people we had to hand over, and the slave trader situation, I think you were actually quite fast. So? How was the profit?”

“It’s decent, I suppose. But since I wasn’t really planning on making a profit off this deal, I don’t mind.”

“I see.”

Vihera neither gets angry nor offers praise in response to Rei’s words.

It probably wasn’t a topic she’d brought up out of any particular interest to begin with.

Rei understood Vihera’s attitude, so he didn’t seem particularly bothered and walked over to where Vihera and the others were.

……Naturally, Vihera stood out remarkably even within this inn.

Since it was already nearing evening, the guests staying at the inn—or those returning to it—were gasping at Vihera’s beauty and her voluptuous figure, clad in attire reminiscent of a courtesan or dancer. In fact, they were even sizing each other up, vying to be the one to speak to her.

Amidst all this, Rei ignored those men and spoke to Vihera—or, to be precise, Vihera spoke to him first—so to those who had been waiting for an opportunity to speak to Vihera, Rei was by no means an appealing prospect.

The only reason they didn’t try to pick a fight with Rei was likely because Relieu, standing by her side, clearly looked like a formidable adventurer.

Compared to Rei, whose true strength wasn’t apparent from her appearance, and Vihera, whose attire emphasized her femininity over her power, Relieu was someone who could truly be called a formidable fighter.

Among those who had intended to speak to Vihera, the adventurers hesitated to approach her the moment they saw Relieu, judging her to be overwhelmingly superior to themselves.

As for the merchants, seeing that even the guards they had hired and those who clearly looked like adventurers were cowering, they judged that it would be unwise to speak to her themselves and refrained from doing so.

As a result, nothing particularly chaotic happened.

That said, the people involved themselves didn’t seem to care at all about the atmosphere around them.

“How is that dark elf doing?”

“According to Marina, thanks to the effects of Rei’s potion, she’ll be fine by tonight.”

At first, Rei didn’t understand what Vihera was talking about.

But he soon remembered the potion he’d given Marina—the one he’d gotten from Golz’s magic item shop—half to placate her and half to avoid her bad mood directed at him.

“That potion wasn’t supposed to be that high-grade, though.”

In fact, the effects of the potion Rei had heard from the shopkeeper were supposed to be nothing more than average.

How on earth did it become so effective? For Rei, it was nothing but a mystery.

“Maybe it’s because of Marina’s spirit magic? After all, water spirit magic can heal, right?”

“So it’s a synergistic effect, I suppose. …But if that’s true, I’d think the word would have spread more by now.”

“Even if you ask me, I don’t know the details. Why don’t you go to her room and ask Marina directly? I was thinking of heading back myself anyway.”

Hearing Vihera say that, and feeling no particular need to stay here, Rei nodded readily.

“Got it. Well, let’s go then. I should probably ask Marina what they plan to do with that Dark Elf, too. …You’re not from the same hometown as Marina, are you?”

As Rei asked this, recalling the settlements where the World Tree existed, Vihera nodded in response.

“I hear that’s true. But since elves and dark elves are few in number, they’re said to have a strong sense of camaraderie—maybe that’s why?”

“Well, compared to humans, races with smaller populations naturally tend to have stronger bonds.”

Relieu, who had been listening to Rei and Vihera, muttered this, and Byune nodded expressionlessly in response... but Rei and Vihera felt slightly uncomfortable and looked away.

That’s because, within the Crimson Wings led by Rei, Byune is practically the only one who could be called a “normal human.”

Needless to say, Rei himself is essentially an android created by the Zepairu clan using the pinnacle of their technology, while Marina is a dark elf and Vihera is a former human who absorbed an Ambris.

Although she isn’t a member of Rei’s party, Elena can no longer be called human either, having inherited the magic stone of an Ancient Dragon.

In that sense, the only two among Rei’s companions who can truly be called human are Byune and Ara.

(The percentage of non-humans is surprisingly high, isn’t it? Well, not that it really matters.)

As far as Rei is concerned, since he’s come to a fantasy alternate world, he doesn’t really intend to worry that much about race.

For the rest of the group, that shouldn’t make much of a difference either.

With these thoughts in mind, Rei and the others climbed the stairs.

……A voice of lament came from behind them, expressing regret that Vihera—who was the ultimate eye candy—was no longer there, but Rei and the others didn’t seem to mind.

They arrived at the door, gave a light knock, and the door opened to reveal Marina.

The reason they weren’t allowed inside was likely because she didn’t want them to see the Dark Elf sleeping.

If it had been someone like Elena or Vihera, she would have invited them in without any hesitation.

“Oh, Rei. What’s up?”

Marina, who called out to him, seemed much calmer.

Of course, she wasn’t completely calm yet, but at least she wasn’t giving off the kind of pressure that those around her could feel… or doing anything like that.

Relieved by this, Rei spoke up.

“I was wondering how the Dark Elf who was captured is doing, and also, since we’re leaving tomorrow, I was wondering what we’re going to do.”

“Oh, that’s not a problem. I spoke with her a bit when she woke up, and she seems pretty strong, so she said she can make it back to her village on her own. …Though she did say she’d be careful not to get tricked again.”

“...Deceived?”

“Yes. Apparently, when she went into town, someone bought her a drink. She drank it, fell asleep, and when she woke up, she was already in the hands of a slave trader.”

“Ah… yeah. I don’t know what to say to that… my condolences?”

Saying that, she forced a smile, as if to hide her discomfort.
