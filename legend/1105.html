Before Rei lay the corpse of a coconut crab, its head crushed.

The coconut crab had suffered a terrible fate: its shell, which was also its home, destroyed; its body scorched by flames; and finally, its head crushed. But now, to Rei, it was nothing more than food.

(Well, I'll need to ask Goushu back at the house if it's actually edible.)

Looking back at the coconut crab, its claws stood out prominently.

Though the claws hadn't been able to attack Set, it was certain they possessed considerable power if they connected.

Moreover, the carapace, including the claws, was undoubtedly sturdy, making it seem perfectly usable as a material.

"The claws must be packed full of meat."

That was ultimately the first thing Rei thought of.

He'd happily eat coconut crabs, even though he wouldn't touch a sand serpent.

If someone like Auguste heard about it, they'd probably tilt their head in confusion.

"Grrr?"

What are we doing with this coconut crab? Set growled, and Rei immediately pulled out a butcher's knife.

"Whether it's edible or not, let's recover the magic stone now. ...I haven't decided yet whether Death Scythe or Set will absorb it."

He announced this as he examined the coconut crab's body, shattered by Set's single blow.

Soon, he discovered a magic stone in what appeared to be the heart.

"This is it."

"Gururuu"

"Hold on a sec."

As Set leaned in, demanding to see it, Rei drew the Waterfall Dagger from his Misty Ring and washed the magic stone.

"There. This is it."

It wasn't particularly large, but Set still showed an odd interest in it. Was it because he had defeated the monster alone?

Wondering slightly, Rei stroked Set's head and asked.

"Will you absorb this magic stone, Set?"

"Guru?"

Set tilted his head slightly, asking, "Is it okay?"

He probably thought it would be okay to let Death Scythe absorb it.

But the Death Scythe had already absorbed the Sand Serpent's magic stone, causing its corrosion to level up.

If it hadn't learned any skills after absorbing the Sand Serpent's magic stone, he might have wanted it to absorb the coconut crab's magic stone.

But now that the corrosion level had risen, even Rei couldn't bring himself to ask for the coconut crab's magic stone that Set had defeated alone.

(Well, I did take the initial action to draw the coconut crab's attention... but that was all I did.)

That action allowed Set to ambush the coconut crab, but Rei didn't consider it his doing.

That's precisely why he decided Set should absorb this coconut crab's magic stone.

"Here, take it."

Saying that, Rei tossed the magic stone toward Set.

As expected, Set caught it with his beak and swallowed it.

[Set has acquired the skill 'Earth Arrow Lv.1']

The announcement message echoed in Rei's mind.

"...Well, I kind of expected that."

Since he had blocked Set's attack with a wall made of sand, it was certain he could perform earth-based attacks.

Considering that, Earth Arrow seemed like a reasonable choice.

"So now we have Wind Arrow for wind, Ice Arrow for ice... water, and Earth Arrow for earth. Will we get Fire Arrow or something like that eventually?"

Rei wondered, though he vaguely felt that absorbing magic stones from fire-type monsters might raise the level of his Fire Breath.

"Anyway, could you try using it? Earth Arrow... An arrow made of earth should have decent mass, so I expect it to pack some punch."

"Grrrrrrr"

At Rei's words, Set immediately growled and used Earth Arrow.

Then, five arrows made of earth floated up beside Set.

"Five, huh... Then, why don't you try shooting them at that sand over there?"

"GRRRR!"

The earth arrows flew swiftly, though not as fast as Wind Arrows. Upon hitting the sand, each arrow sank completely into it.

"...Since it's sand, I can't gauge the power."

"Grrr..."

Set nodded in agreement with Rei's thoughtful mutter.

Indeed, given the target was sand, any attack exceeding a certain power level would inevitably create a scene like this.

(Still, using it against that coconut crab is a bit much.)

There was no doubt the shell could be used as material, and the meat was probably edible.

But to soil it with dirt was something even Rei couldn't condone.

Set looked at him apologetically with those round eyes, and Rei patted him down, telling him not to worry.

"It's not like we have to figure out Earth Arrow's abilities right now. We can find a monster tomorrow and try it out on that. I want to try using corrosion too."

Corrosion naturally only works when the target is equipped with weapons or armor.

It's basically ineffective against unarmed targets... like the dead coconut crab corpse lying before Rei now.

(Well, maybe if Corrosion reaches level five or higher, its performance might improve...)

For now, it only corrodes metal weapons and armor, but eventually, it might be able to corrode things like wood and leather too.

As Rei pondered this, Set rubbed his face against his, asking, "Shall we go already?"

"Yeah, you're right. If we get too late, we might not be able to get into Gosh. Plus, I want to ask about this coconut crab too. If Set can eat it, you'd want to eat it too, right?"

"Guruu!"

"Of course!" Set growled energetically. Rei nodded, approached the coconut crab, and stored it away in his Misty Ring.

Though Rei preferred shrimp over crab, that didn't mean he disliked crab.

Among the shattered shells the coconut crab had been carrying, the larger pieces were stored in the Misty Ring, while the surrounding area was littered with nothing but troublesome fragments and bits of flesh too small to bother collecting.

Deciding other monsters would likely dispose of these leftovers, Rei climbed onto Set's back and flew away.

Then, a few minutes after Rei and Set vanished... ants emerged from the nearby sand.

These ants were not monsters, but perfectly ordinary ants.

Even the largest ones were only about the size of Rei's fingertip, but the problem lay in their sheer numbers.

A few, a dozen, a few dozen, a few hundred, a few thousand, tens of thousands... Ants appeared one after another, instantly swarming the coconut crab's flesh fragments... and even the shell fragments.

Their bodies were brown, typical of desert-dwelling species. The crab meat and shell fragments were soon covered by ants, their brown bodies blending so well with the surrounding desert that they became nearly indistinguishable.

Soon, the ants vanished... and not a single fragment of the coconut crab's shell remained.

Had Rei witnessed this scene, a chill would have run down her spine.

Though merely ordinary insects, not monsters, their sheer numbers in the Ferris Desert made them vicious predators.

They were beings that could not be dismissed as mere ants.

"Um... huh? Wait? We should be arriving soon... where is it?"

"Grrr?"

On Set's back, Rei muttered in confusion.

Flying over a desert with no landmarks, it was only natural that Gosh's figure was nowhere to be seen.

No, there should be a landmark. Goshu was a city built around a massive oasis, after all.

But the crucial thing—the oasis itself—was nowhere to be found.

"...This is bad, huh?"

Though he muttered this, there was no sense of despair in Rei's voice, despite being lost in the desert.

Of course not. Inside the Misty Ring were a magic tent, a flowing water dagger, and a considerable amount of food.

It was impossible to take this situation seriously.

Still, knowing people were waiting for them back in Gosh meant they couldn't just stay lost.

The desert was an extremely harsh place, and it was common for people not to return within the planned timeframe.

Similarly, if they were several days over their scheduled time, it would be understandable if people assumed they had lost their lives in the desert.

In other words, if they were lost for days, Rei could easily be declared dead in the desert.

If that happened, word would naturally spread from Gosh's guild to Gilm's guild, worrying Marina.

"Elena has the Paired Orb, so she could signal she's safe... but I'd rather not resort to that if I can help it."

Wondering what to do, Rei flew through the sky atop Set.

He thought that since there was a huge oasis at the center of Gosh, he couldn't possibly lose sight of it from above...

"I can't see it anywhere. ...Well, what should I do?"

"Gurururuu"

Set, who had been listening to Rei's words, suddenly growled.

Had he found Gosh? Rei wondered, but Set was looking down at the ground.

And on the ground were what appeared to be four adventurers.

The four adventurers were running desperately.

It was a sprint worthy of the term "all-out dash."

In a desert, running speed naturally slows down, but this group was moving at a pace indistinguishable from running on regular ground.

"Huh... They're surprisingly fast. And what's chasing them from behind... Well, well, well."

Seeing the creature attacking the adventurers, Rei was slightly surprised.

Because what stood there was what appeared to be a lizardman.

However, it differed in many ways from the lizardmen Rei had seen before.

The lizardmen he had seen in the dungeon with the Altar of Succession had green scales.

In comparison, the lizardman in Rei's line of sight had skin the same brown color as the surrounding sand.

While Rei hadn't known about the coconut crab earlier, he was familiar with the lizardman now on the ground.

Sand Lizardman. True to its name, a lizardman adapted to the desert. While ranked as a D-rank monster, the same rank as a regular lizardman, its desert specialization made it tougher to deal with in the desert than a standard lizardman.

Like regular Lizardmen, they possess a certain level of intelligence, and it's not uncommon for them to attack in groups like they were doing now.

The Desert Lizardmen encountered in Exil are a similar yet distinct race.

"...Like that, huh?"

At the end of his gaze... several Sand Lizardmen could be seen running ahead of the adventurers.

Their scales were the same color as the sand, so the four adventurers desperately fleeing from the Sand Lizardmen behind them probably didn't notice.

The ambushing Sand Lizardmen understood their own exceptional stealth, which was why they lay in wait like this. It was only natural they went unnoticed.

The reason Rei noticed them was simply because he was above them.

"We can get Sand Lizardman materials and meat, plus magic stones. On top of that, we'll get a guide to Gosh. There's no choice but to help them here, right?"

While Rei felt repulsed by the idea of eating Sand Serpent meat, for some reason he had no issue with Sand Lizardman meat—a preference that, viewed objectively, was baffling.

But no one here pointed that out.

Set was somewhat puzzled, but he didn't press the issue.

"Alright, let's go help them right away. ...Set, you take care of the Sand Lizardman lying in wait. I'll deal with the one chasing those four."

"GRRRR!"

Set let out a sharp growl of agreement at Rei's words.

Lightly stroking the back of Set's neck, Rei effortlessly leapt down from the back he had been sitting on.

From a height of a hundred meters, he naturally began his descent.

But, just like with the coconut crab, he used Sleipnir's boots to slow his descent, landing right in front of the four escaping adventurers.

"Huh!? Enemy!?"

The man in his twenties leading the group reflexively shouted upon seeing Rei fall, but Rei paid him no mind. From Misty Ring, he drew not the Death Scythe... but the Crimson spear... the Twilight Spear.

While the Death Scythe was Rei's usual weapon, he judged the Twilight Spear would be more suitable for dealing with humanoid monsters.

The adventurers were momentarily transfixed by the Twilight Spear that appeared in Rei's hand from nowhere.

Even in this near-critical state, it captivated their gaze... The Twilight Spear possessed that kind of power.

"If you keep going straight, Sand Lizardmen are lying in wait. My partner's taking care of that side, so hang around a bit longer... Watch me take down the Sand Lizardmen chasing us here."

As he passed by the adventurers, uttering those words, Rei charged at the Sand Lizardman with the Twilight Spear in hand before they could even process his meaning.
