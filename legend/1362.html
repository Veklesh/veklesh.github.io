On the night Rei returned from cutting down trees in Treant Forest... several figures were present in the guild's warehouse.

One was Walker, the guildmaster; another was Daskar, the lord of Gilm; and there was also the alchemist, Zmos.

There were others present, but they were currently focused on examining the wood inside the warehouse.

"Looking at the magic distribution in this wood, using the red liquid from this Talis might be better..."

"No, even with Talis liquid, the blue liquid might be better suited for this case?"

"But there's the issue of stabilizing the magic power..."

Conversations incomprehensible to anyone but alchemists were happening everywhere around the wood.

Zumos, too, while reporting his findings to Daskar, seemed preoccupied with the wood. It was clear he wanted to examine it himself, likely an alchemist's instinct.

That's precisely why, once he'd heard the full explanation, Daskar spoke up.

"Go ahead. I'll hear the precise report once you've finished your investigation."

"Thank you!"

At Daskar's words, Zmos heads toward his fellow alchemist.

Watching his subordinate depart, Daskar wore a wry smile. Walker, smiling, spoke up.

"Well, it seems it should be perfectly usable as building material. That's good news."

"Yeah. ...But even if we expand Gilm, we're still short on materials. No matter how much lumber we have, we can't exactly make the walls out of wood."

"True."

Walker nodded in agreement with Daskar's words, finding no particular objection.

Indeed. That was correct.

If Gilm weren't located in the frontier, the wood obtained from Treant Forest would likely be sufficient for the expansion.

But even so, wood is still just wood.

Some monsters wield magic, others breathe fire.

...Yes, fire.

Flame magic is Rei's specialty, but it's not like Rei is the only one who can use it.

In fact, it's fair to say it's a common offensive tactic.

Precisely because of that, even if you built a barrier out of wood, there was a risk it could be burned away easily.

There was also the problem that wood rots easily.

While magic could handle that to some extent, it was only to a certain degree.

Beyond that, there were various other reasons, making it only natural that Gilm's expansion required numerous materials besides wood.

"So then, the question is where to get those materials..."

"Yeah. Which merchant guild we approach will definitely set off all sorts of reactions. If they're positive, that's fine, but if they're negative... well..."

"True. Just recently, Rei got caught up in some internal squabbling at the Spier Trading Company."

"Ah, and the Azott Trading Company got dragged into it too."

Given that Azott was one of the major guilds within Gilm, the report naturally reached Daskar as well.

After all, it was a matter where Marina came to see him personally. There was no way he could forget something like that.

"Right. For now, it's probably best not to involve her too much in this matter. If we ask her for something and then another problem arises, it'll cause us all sorts of trouble."

Asking them to procure materials, only for internal squabbles to delay delivery and stall the town's expansion... If this were a normal town expansion project, it wouldn't be a big deal. But for a frontier town like Gilm, it's a completely different story.

After all, it's a region where all manner of monsters become active at night.

That's precisely why if the town expansion stalls... it means monsters might get into the town.

Of course, the expansion doesn't finish in just one night.

It takes over a month... no, several months, and depending on the scale, it's not uncommon for it to take years.

Given that, it wouldn't be unreasonable to think that one merchant guild taking a little extra time to procure materials wouldn't be a problem... under normal circumstances.

But considering that even a slight delay could impact the entire expansion project in Gilm, such a delay could never be easily tolerated.

As a result, the Spier Trading Company might not be able to participate in Gilm's major public works project of expanding the city... and even if they did participate, they'd likely be assigned to areas where problems wouldn't significantly impact the project.

"Also, we'll need to gather a large number of people. Especially carpenters and the like."

"Yes, we'll handle the escort for the personnel."

Walker promptly offered this assurance, proving himself quite capable.

"We'll need more adventurers than usual... Is that manageable?"

Gilm has many adventurers... and they're all skilled ones.

Even so, if they were to fully cooperate with the city's expansion project, a shortage of manpower was inevitable.

Because even while the city expansion work was underway, regular requests kept coming in one after another.

If they reduced the manpower assigned to extermination requests, the number of monsters could increase, potentially leading to monster attacks on the expansion sites.

Furthermore, if they reduced the manpower for gathering requests, items like potions and magic items circulating in the Gilm would become scarce.

If we reduced the personnel assigned to merchant escorts, they might not be able to defend against monster or bandit attacks.

Normally, bandits don't appear near Gilm.

But with the town's expansion, it undoubtedly requires a large number of merchants and supplies, and thieves will also increase in search of those merchants.

...Though most of those increased bandits would likely operate near Abelo, along the route, rather than at Gilm itself.

"We should probably mobilize the Prairie Wolves too."

"That's... Daskar-sama's... no, it's the Gilm's intelligence unit, right?"

"Yes. Did Marina tell you about them?"

"That's part of it, and I've heard various rumors about them being an excellent unit. In any case, increasing the number of adventurers is essential. ...Though, that depends on the scale of the expansion, of course."

Daskar, met with a questioning look from Walker, opened his mouth with a smile on his rugged face.

"The size of Gilm... well, I'd like to increase it by about forty percent from its current state... ideally, around fifty percent."

"...That's quite..."

Walker was speechless at Daskar's words, which far exceeded his expectations.

They had discussed expansion, but he never imagined it would be on such a massive scale.

But for Daskar... no, for the lord of Gilm, expanding the town wasn't something to be undertaken lightly.

Given that, it was only natural to assume that once the decision was made, they would see it through to the end.

Of course, undertaking such extensive construction in a town in the middle of nowhere would likely result in people not gathering there, leading to further depopulation.

But Gilm, situated on the frontier, could attract people in droves.

To be precise, it was already stretched quite thin even in its current state.

After all, come spring, large numbers of adventurers arrived, so the population only increased.

Of course, deaths from illness, old age, accidents, incidents... and failures on quests as adventurers were by no means rare.

Even so, more people move in than leave—adventurers and merchants seeking to settle, plus the children born to existing Gilm residents.

Given that, it was only natural to want to expand Gilm as much as possible.

(Still, forty or fifty percent... Honestly, that seems a bit excessive, don't you think?)

Walker thought this, but judging by Daskar's expression, it certainly didn't seem like he was joking.

Which meant he was serious.

"I apologize for saying this from my position, but an expansion project of that scale is clearly beyond what Gilm's adventurers alone can handle."

"I suppose so. ...Therefore, if possible, approach other cities... specifically guilds in cities friendly with Walker. Request they dispatch adventurers to Gilm. Naturally, Gilm will cover the fees for those assignments."

Though it can't compare to cities on par with the royal capital, the frontier town of Gilm possesses an economic sphere worthy of being called a city.

Being the sole frontier region within the Mireana Kingdom carries significant value.

It possesses materials from monsters and plants found only in the frontier, along with various other sources of income.

"It seems even newly minted adventurers, not just seasoned ones, could find work here."

"Indeed. Beyond escort duties during work or nighttime protection, we can always use more hands for physical labor like hauling construction materials."

In fact, adventurers, who are more physically trained than the average person, are more useful when it comes to tasks like carrying heavy loads.

Walker understood this well, having run a guild branch near the dungeon.

He'd even seen adventurers who didn't venture into dungeons, instead helping carpenters.

Ultimately, that person quit adventuring and apprenticed as a carpenter.

Anyway, adventurers were strong, as expected from their training, and in that sense, they were incredibly useful.

"Understood. So, when exactly do you plan to start the expansion work?"

"...I don't know the details yet. I'm currently having my subordinates do all sorts of detailed calculations. I never imagined such convenient building materials would appear. And so casually, too."

"I see. I still don't understand the logic behind it, but the idea that the forest expands automatically..."

A report written by Lenora after receiving a report from Rei.

According to it, the forest had indeed expanded compared to yesterday.

In other words, Walker judged that the forest would definitely continue expanding daily, and considering its current size, there would be no problem accommodating Gilm's expansion work.

(Slesha's report mentioned the presence of a Treant... but I hear that even after Rei cut down this tree, no Treant appeared. I'm curious about how that works... but that should become clear with future investigations.)

For now, the exact nature of the Treant Forest remains unclear.

Of course, further investigation will be necessary later, but preparations still needed to be made first.

At the very least, assembling skilled members like Rei isn't exactly easy... Still, if we're going to expand Gilm, it's better to start moving on that sooner rather than later.

(Is it still Rei, then? But since Rei has the item box, I'd prefer him to focus on logging... Which means it's Rei's party members? No, I should probably go with a party other than Crimson Wings...)

Walker's thoughts were racing, but suddenly, he heard loud voices coming from the alchemists who had been examining the tree. He turned his gaze in their direction.

Not just Walker, but Daskar also turned his gaze toward the source of the voices.

"What should we do?"

"Let's go check. When someone's that engrossed, it'll delay the report coming in."

"...You're right. Can't argue with that."

Walker nodded quietly, a faint smile on his face at Daskar's words.

No matter how much they pondered here, it was undeniable they still lacked sufficient information.

Given that, he concluded it would be better to send adventurers other than the Crimson Wings... multiple ones, in fact, to gather whatever information they could.

"What's up? You seem pleased about something."

Zmous, addressed by Walker, opened his mouth, his eyes shining.

"Yes! Look at this! When exposed to a specific magical liquid, it reacts to that magic and increases in hardness!"

"But... didn't you say the same thing about the branch yesterday?"

"Yes. The phenomenon itself is the same as yesterday's. But the crucial difference is the hardness. With this, it might even be usable for the outer walls protecting the city."

"...What about its resistance to fire?"

Daskar, who had been listening to Walker and Zumous' conversation, interjected.

The question seemed unexpected even for Zomus, who faltered.

"W-well..."

Zumos's attitude itself answered Daskar's question.

"No matter how sturdy it is, if it can be burned by flames, it's useless for walls."

"...Yes."

Fire attacks are a common tactic when attacking.

Whether against monsters or humans.

Given that, it stands to reason that fire resistance is required for walls.

"But... that wood might have other uses besides walls. Let me know if you discover anything else."

As if to offer reassurance, Daskar told Zomos this.
