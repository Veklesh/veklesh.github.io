Once it was decided that nearly all of the Silver Lion would go to Rei, Rei and the others immediately set about dismantling it.

Elk and Min, naturally, were assisting with this.

Normally, they would find time later to dismantle the monster themselves or request the guild to do it as a commission... but this time, Elk's opinion was that it would be better not to do that.

First, disassembling this Silver Lion alone... or with Vihera, who was traveling with Rei, or even with all three if Byune had returned to the guild. But the fundamental problem was where to disassemble such an enormous monster.

Including its tail, it measured about six meters in total length. Even without the tail, it was still about four meters long. This Silver Lion was incomparably larger than any ordinary monster.

Dismantling such a colossal body naturally required a correspondingly spacious location.

Such a place is hardly common. Even the forest near Gilm where Rei usually does his butchering—a forest not too far from Gilm with a river running through it—would likely be difficult to use for dismantling a Silver Lion of this size due to space constraints.

There was also the butchering warehouse used for processing meat for the Full Belly Inn, but the silver lion's size would be a problem there too.

Moreover, the Gamelion season was approaching, and it felt wrong to disturb that.

Then there was the guild that had temporarily stored the large number of Gamelion carcasses we acquired last year... but that would naturally require selling a corresponding amount of materials to the guild.

Of course, Rei wasn't opposed to selling materials to the guild, but it was still true that having more options was preferable.

In the end, the safest option left was simply to dissect it right here.

Normally, dissecting a monster corpse would bring the issue of material deterioration, but with the Misty Ring, Rei didn't have to worry about that.

In fact, butchering it here meant no one outside those involved would see the Silver Lion being cut up. That meant he wouldn't have to worry about scavengers trying to get scraps.

And above all else...

'When cutting the Silver Lion's hide, you need to slice between the hairs.'

The advantage of having advice from Grimm, who had extensive experience dissecting S-rank monsters.

The highest-ranked adventurers in the group were Rank A Elk and Min, but even they had almost no experience skinning Rank S monsters.

Min had once seen methods for stripping S-rank monster materials in a book, but it went without saying that advice from Grimm, who had actual experience processing S-rank monster carcasses, was far superior to vague, secondhand knowledge.

Furthermore, while adventurers like Johanna and the others—at least compared to Rei and Elk—were unlikely to encounter many opportunities to dissect S-rank monsters or strip their materials in their future careers, the experience wouldn't hurt.

Following Grimm's instructions, everyone except Rodos, who couldn't move, began working on dismembering the Silver Lion.

Meanwhile, Set and Yellow kept watch, ensuring no one entered the room.

The Silver Lion was the only monster occupying the lowest level of this dungeon, and normally, few adventurers ventured this far down.

However, there had been those who challenged the Silver Lion to battle before, so it wasn't impossible that someone thinking of doing so now might arrive.

If that happened, it was completely unknown how they would react upon seeing Rei and the others dismantling the Silver Lion here.

Of course, given the disparity in strength, it wasn't likely many would attack Rei and the others to seize the Silver Lion's corpse. But it wasn't impossible either.

After all, they had come this far driven by the desire to defeat the Silver Lion themselves.

What's worse, there was no guarantee that some wouldn't demand a share of the Silver Lion's materials for themselves.

If they could obtain the materials without fighting the Silver Lion, it was entirely possible they would insist on it, even if it meant pushing things a bit too far.

...And above all else, the biggest reason is surely that they cannot risk Grim being seen.

Under normal circumstances, openly traveling alongside an undead monster like this is clearly abnormal.

"The claws can wait. Focus on stripping all the hide first. Pay special attention to the tail. It's extremely useful as whip material, so handle it carefully."

Everyone, including Rei, peeled the silver lion's hide carefully, avoiding any unnecessary damage.

The silver lion's fur possessed strong resistance to cutting and piercing, but fortunately, its belly had already been slashed open by Grimm.

By inserting blades through that opening, they could continue skinning the silver lion without significant difficulty.

...However, naturally, ordinary skinning knives like Johanna's were inadequate for the task. Only after Grimm enhanced their sharpness with magic could they finally begin dissecting the silver lion.

The sensation of slicing through skin and flesh was so sharp it felt nothing like the knives Johanna and the others were accustomed to using, driving home the sheer power of magic once more.

For Johanna and the others, magic inevitably carried a strong image of offensive spells, given their lives as adventurers.

Above all, having witnessed magic firsthand during the civil war in the Bestir Empire—especially Rei's fire whirlwind—had cemented that image firmly in their minds.

Of course, many also used magic for more peaceful purposes.

The most representative of these was probably healing magic.

But... should one say "as expected," or "inevitably"? When asked which leaves a stronger impression—fire whirls or healing magic—most people would answer fire whirls.

"What incredible sharpness. I bought this after coming to Gilm, so it's good quality, but even so, I was debating whether to get it sharpened again or buy a new one."

Johanna murmured thoughtfully as she slid the knife between the meat and skin.

The others murmured similarly, marveling at how easily the blade cut through the Silver Lion.

"This is truly incredible. Just being able to use this magic makes monster dissection so much easier."

"No, no—what if we used this magic on weapons? Couldn't we easily take down even monsters with high defense?"

"Fofofo. I'm afraid that's impossible. This magic was developed solely for monster dissection and material extraction. It assumes the target is dead, so using it on a living opponent would be practically meaningless."

Just as they were conversing among themselves, Grimm abruptly announced this.

Johanna and the others, still unaccustomed to Grimm's presence, froze momentarily at his words.

After a moment, Johanna was the one to speak up.

"Um, why such a condition? If it could be used on monsters that are still alive, I think it would be much more versatile."

Johanna forced herself to speak, desperately suppressing the tremor in her voice.

Understanding Johanna's state, Grimm continued speaking without seeming particularly concerned.

"Well, I can't really use weapons anyway. Magic is sufficient for attacking. By restricting its use to living targets, I've made it sharper."

"I-I see. Now that you mention it, that makes sense."

Even to Johanna's eyes, Grimm didn't give the impression of engaging in close combat.

Given that he carried a staff, the impression that Grimm relied on magic in combat was stronger.

(Getting hit with that staff would probably hurt quite a bit, though.)

Johanna thought that if an ordinary human were struck by the staff Grim carried, they would undoubtedly suffer serious injury.

Moreover, unlike ordinary skeletons, Grimm was a higher-tier undead.

Therefore, it was only natural to assume that the bones composing his body weren't ordinary... skeleton-like bones, but were reinforced with magic.

"Now then, rather than that, hurry up and butcher the Silver Lion. You lot don't have much time, do you?"

"It's not that we don't have time... it's just that we want to finish this before other adventurers arrive."

If anyone saw them dissecting the Silver Lion in a place like this, it could cause unnecessary trouble. Above all, they couldn't let anyone else see Grimm.

With these thoughts in mind, Johanna slid her knife between the skin and flesh.

"Looks like they're having their own troubles over there."

"Yeah. But if we mess up the processing here... Elk, no! The Silver Lion's stomach mustn't be mixed with the other organs."

Mid-sentence, Min quickly cautioned Elk.

Elk and Min were engaged in the task of removing and processing the Silver Lion's entrails.

More accurately, Elk was removing the organs under Min's instructions.

Min would have preferred to finish the job quickly with both of them working together, but these were silver lion entrails—he couldn't afford to handle them carelessly.

If he simply left it to Elk, there was a risk the precious silver lion's innards could be ruined.

That was precisely why Min had instructed Elk... this was the method chosen for removing the organs.

Since Grimm had already drained every last drop of blood from the silver lion's body, Elk and Min didn't have to worry about blood when removing the organs. That was probably fortunate for them.

Elk, who was extracting the organs from the gutted silver lion's body, benefited most from this.

...Though, while all the blood had been removed, other bodily fluids remained untouched.

Being the Silver Lion's bodily fluids, they naturally held considerable value.

But compared to other materials, their value was overwhelmingly low. Given that Rei had said he didn't mind that aspect either, and due to time constraints, Elk and Min decided not to worry about the bodily fluids either.

"Rei, can you hold his face steady?"

"Yeah, leave it to me."

"Elena, are you ready over there?"

"We're good here too."

"Marina?"

"No problem."

Rei gripped the Silver Lion's massive neck.

While Elk removed the internal organs and Johanna and the others skinned it, the Silver Lion's body still twitched somewhat.

Amidst this chaos, they're stripping materials—extracting the intact eyeball, pulling out teeth including the fangs...

"Ah, no good. This eyeball's completely destroyed. Can't use it as material."

Marina, who had been watching the silver lion's eyeball—the one pierced by Rei's Twilight Spear—sighed regretfully.

Each of her gestures carried an undeniable sensuality, though it wasn't something she consciously did; it was more of a half-unconscious occurrence.

"That can't be helped. If it had just been lightly poked with a finger, maybe... but it was pierced by the Twilight Spear. If it were usable after that, the Silver Lion wouldn't have lost an eye in the first place."

Elena, who had been checking the fangs, addressed Marina with those words.

"I understand... No, don't you think the Silver Lion might be different? After all, it moved around just fine even with Rei's Deep Flame on its face for a long time."

"...I'll just say I understand the sentiment."

Having witnessed the Silver Lion's defensive prowess in battle, Elena too had been struck by Marina's words, perhaps thinking 'What if...?', and thus deflected the conversation.

"For an alchemist, the Silver Lion's eyeball is an incredibly valuable material. So, I really hoped it would be safe, for Rei's sake too... but I guess not."

A small sigh escaped Marina's lips.

Even the feathers and body hair of a Rank A monster like Set are materials alchemists would kill for.

The value of materials from an S-rank monster goes without saying.

"Rei-san, the skinning is done! I'm starting to cut the meat, so please help me!"

Johanna's voice echoed through the room.

"Got it!"

Rei's reply carried a hint of excitement.

Like magic stones, silver lion meat was what Rei desired most.

Even with its massive size, being an S-rank monster meant it could likely be devoured in no time if one really wanted to.

Given that he was cutting up such meat, there was no way Rei wouldn't be delighted.

And so, he cheerfully set about carving the meat... It took less than two hours for the Silver Lion to be completely butchered and turned into ingredients. Considering its size, that was probably quite fast.
