A room within the Elema Trading Company. Daldora, the room's owner, slammed his fist onto the table, his irritation plain to see.

The impact made the spot where the arrow was lodged hurt, but the frustration inside Daldora ignored the pain and made him shout.

"Don't you know who attacked me yet?!"

Though usually nervous, Daldora's eyes scanned the surroundings with even greater unease than usual.

His gaze fell upon those working for the Elema Trading Company—individuals skilled in rough work.

They were also the ones who built the secret information network, the ones who could get wind of any rumor in Gosh the fastest.

Yet even such individuals couldn't offer words that would satisfy Daldora now.

A few paces away from the desk, several guards stood quietly, keeping watch.

Normally, Narsas, who commanded the guards, would be here, but he was absent, receiving treatment for injuries sustained during Daldora's attack.

"I'm sorry, Daldora-sama. But there shouldn't be anyone in Gosh capable of causing such a major disturbance right now..."

The man's words trailed off, but this did nothing to improve Daldora's mood; only his gaze grew sharper.

"This Gosh caused all that commotion!? And you tell me you have no information whatsoever!?"

"Yes. I'm sorry, but we haven't received any information about the attackers. Ah, but there is one piece of information that might be related to this incident..."

"...What is it?"

Under Daldora's gaze, desperate for any information, one of the men standing before the desk hesitated slightly before speaking.

What he was about to say was likely information related to this incident.

But... if this information were revealed, it could potentially bring about the worst possible outcome for the Elema Trading Company.

Knowing this, he would have preferred not to share it... Yet, as someone employed by Daldora, he could not conceal this information.

"When Daldora was attacked, another person was attacked in the same manner."

"...Your hesitation here means... could it be... Ryubrand!?"

Daldora declared with an urgent expression.

If his patron, Ryubrand, had been attacked and injured by someone, it was certain that things would turn sour in the future.

The Elema Trading Company held significant influence in Gosh largely because Ryubrand... the feudal lord, was backing them.

Of course, the Elema Trading Company didn't grow this large solely because of backing; Daldora's own pure strength was also a factor.

Still, having a patron made a world of difference.

"No, that's not correct. It wasn't the lord who was attacked."

A different person from the one who mentioned the information to Daldora immediately denied the attack on Ryubrand.

"Is that so? Then who exactly was attacked?"

Daldora asked with relief, and the man who had denied the attack on Ryubrand steeled himself and spoke.

"Augusto."

"...What did you say?"

For a moment, Daldora didn't understand what had been said, but she grasped it immediately.

Since both she and Augusto had been attacked, whoever targeted them must consider both of them obstacles.

"The attacker over there?"

"Seems like just one."

"I see. So, the one who planned this attack has me as their primary target?"

Daldora seemed to want to say that it was obvious the one targeting Augusto was the main target, but considering influence, that wasn't an unreasonable way of thinking.

"So, just to confirm, what about the damage over there?"

"I heard Augusto was seriously injured, but he recovered thanks to Rei's potion."

"...Huh? Rei was there, yet Augusto got injured? Against just one person? Even if that one person was exceptionally skilled, that's hard to believe."

I'd heard about Rei's abilities from the guards, including Narsus, and the intel I gathered backed up his skill.

That's precisely why it's utterly unimaginable that Augusto could be injured with Rei present.

It wasn't just Daldora. The others in the room as her guards felt the same.

They weren't as skilled as Narsus, but they were still hired by Daldora precisely because of their strength.

Even though he'd only met Rei once before, he fully understood her strength.

And it wasn't just Rei. Given that they had griffins as familiars, it was utterly unbelievable that they would be caught off guard by opponents like those who had attacked them.

Daldora and the others' doubts were resolved when the man explained the situation.

The man who harmed Augusto encountered Rei while fleeing, and since he was swinging a spear wildly, he was subdued.

"So that's how it was. ...Well, I suppose that makes sense."

Daldora nodded, but seeing the man who brought the news still hesitating as if he had more to say, she tilted her head.

"What is it? Is there something else?"

If this were reported, Daldora might launch a full-scale attack on Augusto.

The man harbored such thoughts, but under Daldora's gaze, he opened his mouth.

"Actually, according to the intel we have here, Rei knocked out the person who targeted Augusto and captured them."

"...What did you say?"

The moment those words reached her ears, Daldora's eyes flashed sharply.

Among the attackers who had assaulted them, the archer's position had been utterly incomprehensible—they hadn't even been able to confirm his presence, let alone capture him.

As for the first three attackers, they had been killed during the initial confrontation. However, after Daldora and the others had taken refuge inside the shop, the guards went outside to search their belongings for any clues. By then, the bodies had completely vanished.

(Narsus said it was probably the work of the archers... but there were many onlookers besides us there. How could they have pulled off such a trick in a place where so many eyes were watching?)

Anyway, having no sources of information whatsoever is extremely difficult.

That's precisely why I summoned these men before me, searching for any possible information.

But... now, that information has been obtained.

Understanding this, Daldora could only growl in frustration.

Had this been ten days ago, he would undoubtedly have led his guards straight to Augusto's shop.

But now, such a move was utterly impossible.

If he did, they would make Rei their enemy.

(But he must pay for targeting me... What should I do? A direct assault is out of the question.)

A direct assault... If I led my forces to Augusto's shop or mansion, I'd become Rei's enemy.

And the moment we oppose Rei, our own destruction is certain.

(Whatever I do, Rei is the problem, isn't he? Troublesome. If only he'd come to Gosh a little later. And for someone who supposedly came to Gosh by chance, he shows no sign of leaving yet. ...No, that's only natural, I suppose.)

Under normal circumstances, Rei might have left Gosh.

But considering today... considering that the owner of the mansion where I'm staying was nearly killed, I doubt I'd be so irresponsible as to just leave.

He's not that heartless, and if that's the case, Rei's presence becomes even more of a hindrance to Daldora.

"It's Rei, after all. ...If possible, I'd like to bring him over to our side."

At Daldora's muttered words, everyone present in the office nodded in agreement.

Having someone like Rei on their side would undoubtedly be a tremendous asset.

Daldora was confident he could utilize Rei's power more effectively than Augusto ever could.

"...No, there's no point dwelling on what's impossible now. What I can do now is..."

There was no point dwelling on wishful thinking forever.

Thinking this, Daldora immediately shifted her focus.

Who was the most dangerous threat in the current situation? How would they be targeted? What was the solution?

After pondering such matters, Daldora must have reached a conclusion. She let out a small sigh before speaking.

"The person to inform Ryubrand about this matter has already been dispatched, correct?"

"Yes. Along with instructions to provide thorough protection."

One of the guards spoke up, and Daldora breathed a sigh of relief.

Given that she and Augusto had been targeted, it stood to reason that Ryubrand, her patron, could also be a target.

Considering that, it was better to be cautious.

After confirming the messenger had been dispatched to Ryubrand, Daldora let out a small sigh before opening her mouth, though somewhat reluctantly.

"Well... shall we go?"

"Go? Where to? Without Narsus, our strength is diminished. Considering that, I'd rather you not wander around town too much."

As his guard, he didn't want to go out in the current state where their forces had been reduced by the earlier attack.

If the attackers were like the first three who came earlier, they could probably handle it with their current strength.

But the ones who shot arrows afterward? We couldn't even catch a glimpse of them.

That very uncertainty about their identity was unsettling, and precisely because it was unsettling, I didn't want to engage them carelessly.

"I understand your concerns. But making contact with Augusto now is an absolute necessity."

"...Are we going to meet with Augusto?"

Hearing Daldora's words, the escort wore a look of surprise, having assumed from their earlier conversation that they were heading to the lord's manor.

But Daldora simply nodded back at the guard.

"Yes, that's the plan. After all, the person who holds the key to their side is right under Augusto's nose. Given that, the only way to get information is for us to go to him. ...We certainly can't have him coming here."

"Well... I suppose."

Anyone serving as Daldora's escort couldn't help but realize how close his relationship with Augusto was to being absolutely terrible.

After all, it was precisely because their relationship had deteriorated that far that he tried to have her disposed of using the Sand Thieves.

"Come on, let's go. Get ready quickly."

"...Understood. I'll report to Narsus for the record."

Saying anything more would be pointless, and worse, it might even make Daldora dislike me.

If, as a bodyguard, you end up being disliked by your charge, it can only lead to the worst possible future.

That thought led the escort, ordered by Daldora, to choose to inform Narsus first.

"Right, I'll inform him. But even after using the potion, he won't be able to move immediately. ...More accurately, he won't be ready for combat right away. So tell Narsus to rest for a while."

"Understood."

Daldora and the guards left the shop, and several tens of minutes passed.

At first, Daldora had been anxious about enemy attacks, but as the uneventful time dragged on, she began to relax somewhat.

The guards knew that letting their guard down could mean death, so they remained vigilant. However, their charge, Daldora, was able to act as if her initial tension had already vanished.

"It seems there's no one attacking us for now."

"Perhaps. But perhaps not. We must remain vigilant regardless... It feels odd to ask now, but do you truly believe we can ally with Augusto?"

"Well, I wonder. Augusto is a sharp man in his own way. He's too kind to get results, but he's no fool. I was attacked, and he was attacked. Naturally, he'd notice a third party involved. Besides..."

Daldora tried to continue, but before she could finish, the carriage stopped.

Through the carriage window, Augusto's shop came into view.

...And merchants gathered in front of the shop, clearly wary of her.

"Augusto... doesn't seem to be here. At home, perhaps?"

Muttering, Daldora stepped out of the carriage.

Naturally, her guards followed her. Those who had been outside the carriage remained on alert, watching the merchants gathered in front of Augusto's shop.

Daldora, having disembarked, walked straight up to the shop front and addressed the visibly wary merchants.

"I wish to speak with Mr. Augusto. Could you put me through?"

Unlike how she spoke to her own subordinates, Daldora addressed Augusto by his full name with the honorific "san." One of the merchants, visibly tense, replied.

"What is this about?"

"I have something to discuss regarding today's attack. I'm sure he'd be interested."

"...Unfortunately, Mr. Augusto has already returned home."

Watching the merchants' expressions as they relayed this, Daldora concluded there was no further need to stay and began to return to the carriage.

But the merchant who had just answered her spoke up.

"I heard you were also attacked today, Daldora, and injured... Is that true?"

"Yes, it's true, though I suppose I should say unfortunately. And needless to say, I did not order the attack on Augusto."

With that, Daldora left the scene, leaving the merchant behind, who still seemed to have something to say.
