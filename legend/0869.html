A completely unexpected skill acquisition.

For a moment, he wondered if it had been an auditory illusion. Even as that thought crossed his mind, he dodged Gamerion's attack—a slash delivered by its ears aimed directly at him—and swung his Death Scythe in a flash.

Gamellion, his body sliced horizontally, spewed his entrails around him as he charged toward Rei's back, his body split in two.

Watching it go, I displayed the Death Scythe skill in my mind's eye to confirm.

But sure enough, the terrain manipulation level displayed there had changed from one to two—the level before entering this dungeon.

This meant that cutting the dungeon's core with the Death Scythe had indeed granted him the skill.

(What's going on? Acquiring a Beast Magic skill should only be possible once per monster type. My knowledge of Beast Magic confirms this, and I've never acquired a skill twice from the same monster before.)

Confused yet gathering his thoughts, Rei fixed a sharp gaze on the Gamerions surrounding him.

The Gamerions flinched involuntarily under that gaze.

Though Gamellion possessed a ferocious nature, being hunted down so relentlessly like this meant fear could easily outweigh that ferocity.

...And yet, they still showed no sign of fleeing from Rei. It was likely because they understood they couldn't escape from this hall.

All Gamellion could do now was refrain from attacking and wait for an opening to strike back with a single decisive blow.

(Are magic stones and dungeon cores different? No, of course they're different.They're fundamentally different things. ...So, does that mean you can learn skills from every dungeon core? So far, the only dungeon cores destroyed by Death Scythe are the one from the Demon Fever incident and this one. Considering you learned skills from both, it doesn't seem entirely wrong. Besides, unlike before, the dungeon cores aren't disappearing either...)

Glaring at Gamellion, I shift my gaze to where the dungeon core had been.

Right in the center of this hall stood something like a pedestal, where the dungeon core lay, split cleanly in two diagonally.

The lower half still rested on the pedestal, but the upper portion, severed by Rei, had been blown away by the impact and was now nowhere to be found.

Moreover, unlike the previous incident with the Demon Fever, the dungeon's core remained intact.

Whether this was because it hadn't fully become a dungeon yet was unclear, but still, the fact that the core remained undestroyed was surprising.

(Damn. I guess I need to take it as proof I cleared the dungeon... but searching for it is a pain. More importantly, won't this dungeon collapse? Since it's newly formed, I want to believe it's safe for now, but I should probably deal with it as soon as possible.)

Surveying the Gamerians surrounding me as I pondered... At that instant, one of the Gamerians locked eyes with Rei and lunged forward in a single bound.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

The Gamellion lunged, baring its fangs.

For an ordinary adventurer, even a warrior, this would be an attack requiring evasion or shield defense. But for Rei, clad in the Flame Emperor's Crimson Armor, it was no trouble to block it with one hand... no, not even that. Blocking it with a single finger was hardly difficult.

The fangs aimed to tear his neck apart were easily caught by Rei's hand.

"Hardly a Noise-level strike. Well, I suppose you could say it's a matter of rank difference."

With those words, his arm swung out. The sound of something hard snapping echoed around them as Gamerion was hurled away, colliding with his comrades and sending them all flying.

Watching this, Rei's hand held the very fang he had just hurled.

The fang, broken off at the base, had snapped clean through from the impact of Rei's throw.

"Fragile. ...This probably won't sell."

The fang he clutched was broken in the middle.

Even if the guild bought it, a fang broken like this would be appraised at a ridiculously low price.

Still, better than nothing, Rei thought as he stowed it in his Mistling. He glanced at the significantly reduced number of Gamerians surrounding him and thrust the pommel of his Death Scythe into the ground.

"Terrain Manipulation."

Visualizing the ground swelling upward as he activated the skill, the entire hall except for the area around Rei rose about fifty centimeters the next instant.

The sudden ground elevation startled even the excited Gamerions, halting their movements as they looked around.

Watching them, Rei opened her mouth, looking slightly surprised.

"...Huh. It's been significantly enhanced. The range is considerably wider now, and the height of the terrain you can manipulate is fifty centimeters? If that's the case... Terrain Manipulation."

He activated the skill again, keeping the tip of the Death Scythe thrust forward.

The next instant, the ground sank by about fifty centimeters compared to before using Terrain Manipulation.

When raised and when lowered. If both are fifty centimeters, the total difference becomes one meter.

Considering the significantly expanded range of manipulable terrain compared to before, this skill possessed enough effectiveness to be a viable weapon for Rei.

"GAAAAAAAAAAH!?"

"GAAAAAAAAAAH!"

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!!"

They clearly had no idea what was happening to them. The surviving Gamerions each let out confused cries.

"I see. Then... terrain manipulation."

Using terrain manipulation again, he restored it to its original state.

What was strange was that even after raising and lowering the terrain so drastically, once it returned to normal, there was absolutely no discernible difference.

The fact that it completely returned to the state before the terrain manipulation was applied was proof that this terrain manipulation skill was clearly different from the skills Death Size had acquired up until now.

(I thought the only real reward would be the dungeon core, but it turned out to be quite an interesting haul. Especially getting another skill from the core using Death Size was a pleasant surprise. This terrain manipulation isn't particularly powerful yet, but if it keeps growing like this, it could become incredibly potent.)

A vision flashed through Rei's mind: using terrain manipulation to instantly collapse an enemy army.

After all, if you turn the ground beneath them into a pit tens of meters deep, it doesn't matter if they're an army or anything else—they're helpless.

Most would likely die upon falling into the pit, though naturally, in this world, some could survive such a height.

Against such opponents, he could unleash his fire magic freely into the pits, wiping them out completely without worrying about the flames spreading.

Rei inwardly smiled at the practicality of the terrain manipulation skill.

But Rei was immediately snapped back to reality by Gamerion's growl.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

The surviving Gamerians charged at Rei all at once.

It was only natural.

They had experienced the bizarre sensation of the ground shifting up and down, and they had almost instinctively sensed that Rei was the one who had done it.

Kill Rei, no matter what.

If they didn't kill him, they would be killed.

Having reached this conclusion, the Gamerians, now in a near state of panic, disregarded their own danger and charged at Rei all at once.

Rei, holding the Deathscythe, waited with a smile for such a Gamellion.

He readied the Deathscythe to strike at Gamellion, who had charged at him first.

"Well, that's about it."

Rei muttered, scanning the surroundings.

Countless corpses of Gamerians lay scattered about.

And in the center of this hall stood the pedestal holding the core of the dungeon, its upper half missing.

Rei was the only one still alive here.

Surveying the hall, he released the Crimson Armor of the Flame Emperor.

The intensely concentrated crimson magic, visible to the eye, dissipated, and he let out a small breath.

"Well, that was quite exhausting. The Flame Emperor's Crimson Armor didn't use that much magic, though."

Leaning against the pedestal that held the dungeon's core, he took a cold fruit water from Mistyling and brought it to his mouth.

Outside, it was winter, but strangely, the temperature inside the dungeon hadn't dropped that much.

Temperature regulation varied between dungeons—some in Exil even had desert-like layers—but this one Rei had conquered seemed to maintain a constant internal climate.

You could call it a comfortable dungeon.

That said, the Dragon Robe possessed the ability to keep one cool in the heat and warm in the cold.

Even so, the reason Rei was drinking this fruit water he'd stockpiled during summer was probably because killing over fifty Gamerians had left him exhausted.

A faint sweetness and subtle tartness. That sweet-and-sour taste made Rei down the entire contents of his cup in one gulp.

After taking a breather, the next task isn't stripping the Gamellion's materials... but simply storing the Gamellion's corpse in the Mistling.

If Rei were to skin all these Gamerians alone, it would take days, maybe over ten days, or even longer.

In that case, it would be quicker to recruit people at the guild to strip the materials, as before.

...Though, this is the year Gamerians are said to be scarce. Bringing in this many Gamerians would undoubtedly attract a significant number of buyers eager to purchase them.

Furthermore, having destroyed the dungeon's core, it wouldn't be surprising if this place collapsed at any moment.

Touching the Gamellion corpses, I stored them away into the Mistling.

He continued this task for over ten minutes.

When about half the Gamellion corpses in the hall had vanished, it caught Rei's eye.

Something round, yet neatly cut off partway through, resembling a stone.

Rei's gaze shifted toward the pedestal at the center of the hall.

The cut surface of the object Rei now held should match the cut surface of the dungeon's core placed atop that pedestal.

"The dungeon's core... I finally found it. Found it, but... sigh. Well, I'm glad my terrain manipulation level went up, but I guess Set will be sad about this."

Even when apart, if one of them acquired a skill, the other could understand it.

Therefore, there was no doubt that Set also understood that Rei's terrain manipulation level had increased.

Thinking this, Rei recalled the promise he made to Set before entering the dungeon, and simultaneously, Set's disappointed expression flashed through his mind.

His heart ached at the sight of Set's sorrowful face, yet he reached down toward the lower half of the dungeon's core, fixed to its pedestal.

Whether it was already damaged or designed to come off with just a light pull, Rei couldn't tell. Either way, the dungeon core came off easily once he grasped it.

"...No use dwelling on the past. I hope Gamerion's flesh will cheer him up."

Stowing the dungeon core into Mistyling, he resumed picking up and stowing the Gamellion corpses one after another.

Having swung Deathscythe wildly through the center of the Gamellion horde, he found numerous corpses with severed limbs, heads, or tails.

There were also a fair number of corpses with intestines spilling out from slashed abdomens, and others whose fur had been singed by the Flame Emperor's crimson armor.

"I should have thought more about how to defeat them. Well, with that many of them, it couldn't be helped."

He should have considered the consequences more carefully before attacking. Thinking this, he continued storing the corpses one after another into Mistyring.

After a while, he finally finished storing all the Gamellion corpses in the Mistling and let out a deep breath.

"Well, that takes care of everything here. I never expected to find so many Gamerians gathered, but since I didn't have to go hunting for them, it's like killing two birds with one stone. Besides, the reason Gamerians were hard to find until now was because of the dungeon's core. If that's the case, now that this dungeon has been cleared, there shouldn't be any reason Gamerians can't be found anymore. ...Is there any need to sell these Gamerians on the market?"

Rei tilted his head, but still felt some needed to be sold.

That's how scarce Gamellion meat had become in Gilm right now.

"Well, we'll decide everything once we're back in Gilm. We have to report that we cleared the dungeon, after all."

He muttered, glancing one last time around the hall to confirm there was nothing else of note, then tilted his head.

"...During the Altar of Succession, I thought the Silver Lion guarded the dungeon's core. But in this dungeon, was Gamellion the guardian... the boss-like role? It's more a matter of quantity over quality."

Rei voiced this question aloud, but a horde of Rank C monsters with ferocious temperaments was, frankly, extraordinary for a newly formed dungeon.

Especially since the other monsters present—goblins, kobolds, or even ogres—were considerably smaller compared to the ogres Rei had seen before.

The fact that they'd cleared the dungeon so easily was likely because Rei himself was an anomaly.

Unaware of this realization, Rei left the chamber before the dungeon collapsed.
