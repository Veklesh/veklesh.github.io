"Lord Vihera, you arrived only yesterday, yet here you are again so soon."

In the office where he was guided by a maid, Daskar called out to Vihera as she entered the room, sorting through papers.

Since Vihera had requested to be treated as a mere adventurer rather than a princess, Daskar's tone wasn't overly formal.

"Yes. A rather troublesome situation has arisen. I thought I should report it. Is that alright?"

Daskar had expected a smile in return, but what came back from Vihera was a serious expression. The two beside her, Rei and Byune, showed no sign of joking either.

Noticing the three of them, Daskar felt a bad feeling coming on as he gestured for them to sit on the sofa. He ordered the maid who had escorted Vihera and the others this far to bring tea.

He placed the documents he had been reading on his desk, walked over to the sofa, and sat down facing Vihera and the others.

"So, what exactly is this about? Judging by your expressions, it seems rather troublesome."

"Well, troublesome... I suppose 'annoying' is a more accurate description."

Vihera sighed, her expression heavy with worry. Daskar found himself momentarily captivated.

He was captivated for a moment, but he snapped back to reality quickly, likely thanks to his immunity built up by Marina.

"Not troublesome, but a hassle, huh? ...To be honest, I'm not particularly fond of that sort of hassle. So? What kind of hassle has arisen?"

"There's a possibility that an alchemist from the Bestir Empire is planning something against Gilm."

"...The Bestir Empire?"

Daskar's brow furrowed at the words coming from Vihera's mouth.

He'd assumed the "trouble" meant something minor, like a commotion over Vihera's beauty involving Rei and other adventurers, nobles, or merchants.

But what actually came out of Vihera's mouth was the phrase "alchemists of the Bestir Empire."

It was unexpected, and he realized that when Vihera said "trouble," it was the absolute truth.

"That certainly is troublesome. ...But why would the Bestir Empire try to do something to Gilm now? No, I can guess the reason..."

Daskar's gaze shifted to Rei.

The civil war that erupted in the Bestir Empire last year. Many who were reasonably well-informed knew that Rei had been involved in it.

And it was also clear that the Third Prince's faction won the civil war because of Rei's cooperation.

"There are several possibilities, but the most likely one among them is revenge against Rei... I suppose."

Following Daskar, Vihera's gaze also turned toward Rei.

Though both pairs of eyes were fixed on Rei, he remained silent, saying nothing.

Byune also fell silent without saying anything in particular, and the office was enveloped in quiet.

This silence lasted for several tens of seconds... until it was broken not by someone inside the room, but by the sound of knocking heard from outside.

"Excuse me."

The maid who entered carried a tray laden with tea and sandwiches for everyone.

The sun had already set, and night had fully fallen. The maid's thoughtful gesture was likely in response to their likely hunger.

Daskar, understanding this, expressed his gratitude. The maid smiled, bowed, and left the office.

"...Well, sitting here with a grim face won't make any good ideas appear. Let's at least eat something. You're eating too, aren't you, Vihera?"

"No, I ate at the tavern before coming here..."

"I see. What about Rei and that girl over there?"

Daskar could understand how difficult it must be to maintain Vihera's physique.

From a woman's perspective, it was a figure that could only be described as enviable: curves in all the right places, yet almost no excess fat where it shouldn't be.

Of course, Daskar was a man, so he could only imagine how much ordinary women might envy a figure like Vihera's.

"Ah, well then, I'll have some."

"Mm!"

Rei, who was always a big eater, and Byune, who ate surprisingly much for her small frame, nodded at Daskar's words and reached for the sandwiches.

Vihera watched the two of them with a hint of envy while sipping her tea.

Eating the sandwiches lightened the mood in the office compared to before. Putting the alchemist aside for now, talk about today's cyclops hunt slipped out of Rei and Vihera's mouths.

"Seven cyclopes total, and rare or higher-tier ones at that... How did you manage to defeat such a group?"

"In my case, it was only the red cyclops, so it wasn't that difficult."

"Mine was a regular cyclops, but I guess the combat matchup was favorable. No matter how powerful a single blow is, if it's just flailing around, dodging it is easy enough."

The ease with which they stated this made it abundantly clear they hadn't struggled at all during the actual fight.

Daskar, noticing this, sighed in exasperation.

He understood she possessed that level of strength, but seeing her prove it like this, right before his eyes, was still astonishing.

Before ascending to the position of lord, Daskar had belonged to the knightly order in the royal capital, and he was confident in his own strength.

He possessed the kind of confidence that allowed him to believe he could defeat most common soldiers, adventurers, or knights he encountered.

Even now, alongside his duties as a lord, he never neglected his training. His skills were certainly no duller than they had been during his knightly days.

If the lands Daskar governed were more peaceful, he himself wouldn't need to be this strong.

But Gilm... and the Marquisate of Larks is a frontier. Its people demand a lord with the strength to fight through the frontier.

That is precisely why it is a house rule that the heir to the House of Larks enters the knightly order at a young age.

Even to Daskar's eyes, Vihera was strong.

He instinctively understood that even if he fought, he didn't know if he could win... no, he possessed such skill that Daskar felt he would almost certainly lose.

Having lived as a knight, he wasn't entirely free of bitter feelings.

But at the same time, as a lord, having someone as strong as Vihera in Gilm was an immense source of joy.

(My own frustration is less important than what's best for Gilm.)

Reaching that conclusion, the next thought was what to do about this matter.

(Protest to the Bestir Empire? No way. We've worked hard to build good relations; deliberately ruining that would just lead to war again. If that's the case, I need to contact them discreetly. ...Handling this matter ourselves could benefit us too. Securing the alchemist could mean gaining that technology, for instance.)

Daskar pondered while reaching for a sandwich.

Facing such a Daskar, Rei and Vihera remained silent, not opening their mouths, and reached for sandwiches and tea.

...Meanwhile, Byune, seemingly unaffected, ate her sandwich silently by herself.

Byune's actions, best described as munching away, could be called adorable from an outsider's perspective.

Her expressionless face, yet her earnest efforts to get the sandwich into her mouth, had a charm reminiscent of a small animal.

Daskar had been agonizing over how best to deal with the alchemist, but seeing Byune like this made his worries seem foolish.

(Right. Ultimately, I need to capture him alive. Well, killing him might be unavoidable in the worst case, but keeping him alive would benefit me in many ways. And to capture an alchemist alive, I'd need someone exceptionally skilled.)

As for combat strength, an alchemist alone isn't something to worry about too much.

After all, alchemists aren't necessarily suited for combat.

Of course, as experts in magic items, they understand them better than anyone and possess the skill to wield them effectively.

But the general consensus, the majority opinion, was that against a top-tier... no, even a top-tier-and-a-half warrior, they'd basically be helpless and lose.

(That is, for an ordinary alchemist.)

An alchemist who hands magic items to a Cyclops couldn't possibly be ordinary.

They must either be skilled in combat themselves or have companions adept at fighting.

Someone with the strength to somehow handle even a Cyclops.

Considering that, there weren't many people who could be entrusted with this matter.

...Yes, like the individuals currently before Daskar.

Individuals involved from the start of this matter, whose abilities are beyond reproach.

Furthermore, Rei had his familiar, Set, and Vihera possessed the strength to defeat five Cyclopses.

Even Byune, who was currently eating a sandwich with single-minded focus before Daskar, possessed undeniably high prowess as a thief—Daskar understood that much.

(Though facing an alchemist from the Bestir Empire might be a bit much for Vihera...)

Even thinking that, he also felt it wouldn't be unreasonable to ask Vihera, as someone involved, for help since the commotion was caused by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire.

"Lord Vihera, would you accept a request to apprehend Rei and that child over there named Byune...?"

"The request is to capture the alchemist who plotted this incident... I suppose?"

"That's right. Capture them alive if possible."

"...What do you think? My position makes it a bit difficult for me to refuse, but Rei doesn't really have to accept it, does he?"

Laying eyes on Vihera as she asked, Rei shook his head.

"No, I'll take it too. If it's an alchemist, we might be able to get our hands on some nice magic items."

"So that's your reason. Even though Rei is one of the main targets being hunted yourself?"

"Well, even so. If I'm going to be targeted anyway, might as well get the best reward possible, right?"

"You can do whatever you want with the magic items the captured alchemist has. Just don't cause any trouble with them, obviously. Oh, and if there are any magic items you don't want, I'd like to buy them from you."

Daskar probably said that because he knew the magic items Rei collected were generally practical.

Magic items themselves often held rare value.

It wouldn't be surprising if some items, even ones Rei didn't want, were extremely valuable from Daskar's perspective.

"Understood. It's better for us to have them bought than to be stuck with unwanted magic items."

While the Misty Ring existed, having any number of unwanted magic items wasn't truly a problem, but letting them sit unused was pointless. Thus, Rei agreed to Daskar's proposal.

"I'll contact the guild about the designated request, so don't worry. ...Vihera, and Byune too, I'll leave it to you. Also, it's best not to make this matter public. The fact that an alchemist from the Bestir Empire is involved must remain strictly confidential. I'll send someone to ensure the two who told Rei about it don't mention it to anyone else either."

Vihera nodded at Daskar's words, and Byune, eating his sandwich, nodded in agreement as well.

And so, the very next day after returning to Gilm, Rei found himself caught up in the Cyclops incident.

"Isn't Rei a little too popular with trouble?"

On the way back from the lord's mansion, Vihera murmured suddenly.

The words weren't wrong, so Rei had no rebuttal.

"Guess that's just the star he was born under."

"Guruu"

For some reason, Set growled as if agreeing with Rei's words.

"Hmm."

And then, Byune, walking beside Set, also murmured briefly in agreement.

"Anyway, wanna grab something to eat? Warm soup or something?"

Rei mumbled, trying to change the subject.

The surroundings were dark, and even though spring had just arrived, it wasn't particularly warm at night.

In that sense, Rei's suggestion wasn't wrong at all.

...However.

"You just ate all those sandwiches, and you want more? ...You too, Byune?"

"Mm!"

At Vihera's words, Byune nodded in agreement that she wanted to eat.

"...What on earth is going on with your bodies? Of course, Set will eat too, right?"

"Guruu!"

Set roared in agreement at Vihera's words... and reluctantly, Vihera decided to join the two humans and one beast.
