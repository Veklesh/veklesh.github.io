"Guh, guh, guh..."

Crushed by an iron ball dropped from above, with spears piercing his limbs and body, Resonance could do nothing but groan.

Considering he could barely move at all, the fact that he felt no pain probably didn't help much either.

(No, considering he can still speak despite the pain, it's not useless after all.)

Falling from the sky toward the ground, he slowed his descent mid-air using Rei Punil's shoes before landing on the ground.

Seeing how barely any sand stirred despite landing on the desert, one could tell Rei had landed with almost no impact.

"...Well then."

He glanced at Resonance, who was barely able to move, and pulled the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring, just in case.

Glancing around, the assassins who had attacked Set, Zarust, and Narsus were already gone.

Or rather, none were standing upright.

Most lay collapsed on the sand, either dead or unable to move.

Since they were opponents incapable of feeling pain, the only way to stop their movements was to physically immobilize them or take their lives.

The assassins of the Hundred-Faced One, while insensitive to pain, retained self-awareness and acted while still capable of judgment, making stopping their movements even more difficult.

(The one who attacked Daldora also felt no pain, just like these guys, but I thought it was said they were in a state of near-total loss of self-control. I wonder how that difference works out?)

A sudden question arose, but there were more pressing matters now. He refocused his gaze on Resonance.

As he approached, Resonance, though immobilized, must have recognized Rei's form.

Despite having most of its body crushed, it even managed a smile as it spoke.

"Ha ha ha... Splendid. Never imagined you'd use my own weapon against me like that."

Spears pierced throughout its body and crushed by iron balls, it addressed Rei as if none of it mattered.

"You knew I had an item box, didn't you? And you saw me pull out the Death Scythe too."

"Hmm. Yet, I must admit... I did harbor doubts. ...I concede. I am defeated. And though I cannot move to confirm everyone's status, I hear no sounds of conflict. It seems we have suffered a complete defeat?"

His gaze swept the surroundings... strictly within the range Resonance could currently perceive, as he asked Rei.

"Ah. From what I can see here, some are alive... but many are dead."

"Hmm, I see. ...Well, it can't be helped. We were beings who lived in the shadows of others from the start. We were naturally prepared for an end like this."

True to his words, despite having his comrades killed, Resonance harbors no resentment toward Rei.

This seemed strange to Rei as well. Surprise in his voice, he spoke.

"Even though your comrades were killed... you don't... resent me? Us?"

Technically, it wasn't Rei who killed Resonance's comrades, but since Set had aided in it, Rei couldn't ignore his part.

To Resonance, who faced the one who had killed his allies, this was an insensitive question. He said nothing, simply turning his gaze toward the moon floating in the night sky.

Several tens of seconds passed. Then, as if timing it perfectly to see Set, Zarust, and Narsus approaching their location, Resonance spoke.

"I believe I mentioned this earlier, but we are not the sort who can live with pride. Precisely because of that, I never expected to end my life peacefully."

"...Even so, wouldn't you feel something if a comrade died?"

"That is true. Normally, it would be so. But... for us, it is different. Hmm, that is right. Since we made a promise, I suppose we should speak properly."

Rei thought it was rather proper of him, but if he was going to volunteer the information, she had no complaints.

He settled into a listening posture, and so did Zarust and Narsus as they drew nearer.

Only Set remained vigilant, watching for monsters approaching... especially in the desert night, wary of any drawn by the scent of blood.

"Now then, where shall I begin... First, about what you've been wanting to know—why I targeted Daldora and Augusto. Shall I start with that? That's what interests you most, isn't it?"

"We know the Tira Tree is somehow involved, but I can't help wondering why you were so relentless about it..."

When Rei asked about the idea he'd just thought of, Resonance flashed an amused smile.

...Even though he felt no pain, it was hard to believe that half his body was crushed, given that smile.

"Indeed. It's no mistake that the tree you call the Tira Tree is involved."

Resonance readily admitted the Tira Tree's involvement.

But since Rei had already mentioned the Tira Tree during the earlier battle, he probably judged that anything he said now would be pointless.

"So, how exactly is the Tira Tree involved?"

"Hmm. As I mentioned before, it is precisely because of the Tira Tree that we have lived as assassins. Our ancestors, who were originally a wandering people, discovered the Tira Tree... and by using the blood of our clan to perform a special processing on that Tira Tree, we were able to create a certain medicine."

Medicine. Hearing that word, Rei... no, not just Rei, but Zarust and Narsus too, immediately understood what kind of effect this medicine possessed.

"A drug that numbs pain?"

Resonance nodded simply at the words that slipped from Rei's mouth.

"Yes, that's right. Of course, with such an effect, it's not without side effects. Continued use will cause various side effects, depending on the individual. In any case, with that medicine... and also with the technique passed down in our clan that allows us to transform into anyone... we were able to operate as assassins."

"Transform into anyone... huh. So stripping off facial skin is also a technique passed down through your clan?"

Resonance nodded without hesitation at Rei's questioning words.

I'd assumed that since it was a secret technique, you'd hide it... But then I remembered Resonance saying he'd tell me everything, and I understood.

"Indeed. We've long since lost track of when this technique was first passed down. Still, it's precisely because we possess this skill that we could... no, that we were able to thrive as assassins."

Of those who had been in the village, nearly all capable of combat were dead. And given the situation, even I, leading the active assassins, would likely not survive.

In other words, the survivors were limited to those in the village without combat ability... and a few who were currently away on jobs in other towns, villages, or even countries.

With numbers this depleted, maintaining the clan would be nearly impossible.

And as for the village survivors...

(Besides, the artifact was destroyed by that earlier strike. ...Regardless, it's impossible to continue living here from now on, regardless of the clan.)

This place, with its small oasis, had undoubtedly been a safe haven for our clan.

It was protected by a barrier created by the artifact passed down through our clan, but that very artifact has been destroyed.

Given that, it was already a hopeless situation.

"In any case, we couldn't allow research on the Tira tree—a key ingredient for our medicine—to proceed, nor could we let them take the Tira trees growing in the desert to plant around the town."

"...So then, why target Augusto?"

"We know that person had researched the Tira tree before. ...That's precisely why we intervened."

"So, you targeted anyone who researched... or is researching the Tira tree, almost indiscriminately?"

"Aye. It's a harsh way to put it, but we can't have too many people learning our secrets. I doubt someone who merely studied the Tira tree could make the medicine we use, and anyway, making the medicine requires our blood. But... what do you think happens when they realize that?"

Hearing that, Rei vaguely understood.

In other words, they might even hunt down the Resonance clan to gather the ingredients for that medicine.

But even so, Rei tilted his head.

There was a chance Resonance was right.

After all, a drug that prevents pain would be useful in many ways.

Beyond combat, it could even be used as an anesthetic.

Rei couldn't think of them all, but there were surely many other uses.

"Besides..."

Rei had been deep in thought, but Resonance's words drew his attention.

"And? Is there something else?"

"Hmm. ...I know this might sound strange, but doing this kind of work inevitably takes a mental toll. After all, it's the sort of job where failure means you probably won't make it back alive."

Even from what Rei could imagine, being an assassin seemed like an incredibly harsh environment.

After all, if you let your guard down even a little, you'd have to pay the price yourself.

(Thinking about it that way, adventurers are pretty privileged, huh? When they're not working, they don't have to stay on edge like that.)

Reflecting on the stark difference between his own life and that of the assassin—a true outcast—Rei found himself deeply agreeing with Resonance's words.

"So, you were planning to quit being an assassin soon anyway?"

"I wonder... If it were just me, maybe that would have been fine. But considering the others, I couldn't just nod and agree so easily. Anyway..."

Having said that much, Resonance suddenly pressed a hand to her mouth as if holding something back, but she couldn't suppress it and began coughing violently.

It was a cough that felt as though he were coughing up his very life.

The blood pouring from his mouth, not just the cough, was undoubtedly from the spears piercing his body and, above all, the iron ball dropped from above.

(If they'd stopped her movement with the thorn spears first, then dropped the iron ball... that sequence would have meant only one spear, so the damage would have been less... but that wasn't possible, was it?)

Since the iron ball was dropped from above, attempting such a maneuver would inevitably cause significant damage to the spears already piercing Resonance.

Indeed, several spears had snapped under the iron ball's impact. Had he carelessly used the thorn spears, there was a real risk the shafts would break midway.

If I broke the thorn spear Marina gave me like this, I'd be in for a world of trouble when I got back to Gilm.

"I understand your reasoning, but why didn't you attack the lord despite attacking Lord Daldora and Augusto?"

Narsus, who had been listening to Rei and Resonance's conversation until now, suddenly asked.

If researching the Tira Tree was something this clan couldn't tolerate, then wouldn't it make sense that Ryubrand, who had accepted Daldora's proposal regarding the Tira Tree and instructed them to research it, should have been attacked too?

This doubt wasn't just Narsus's; Zarust likely felt it too. He shot a look at Resonance, who was literally spitting blood, urging an answer.

"Cough... Well, yes, normally that would be the best course. But... how should I put this... If we were to kill that man, and given that his son is still young, the lord of Gosh would likely be replaced."

"...Well, it'd probably be a deputy or something like that."

Resonance nodded at Rei's mutter, continuing without caring that the blood flowing from his mouth fell onto the sand.

"Hmm. And as a deputy, it's highly likely a capable person would be sent."

Hearing that far, all three of them understood why Resonance and the others hadn't killed Ryubrand.

The only one who didn't understand... or rather, had no intention of understanding, was Set, who tuned out the conversation and kept watch on their surroundings.

"You don't mean... incompetent? I never thought he was overlooked because he wasn't competent."

Zalust and Narsus nodded at Rei's muttered words.

"...Well then, it seems my time has come. ...Everyone, I'll see you all on the other side."

He murmured. When Rei and the others turned their gaze toward Resonance, already his life had ended.
