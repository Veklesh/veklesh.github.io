The day after conducting an experiment using Fuma Ore on a fire whirlwind… Rei was seen walking through the streets of Anesis.

Since Rei didn’t have anything in particular to do until Elena finished her errands, he had planned to look for something unusual in Anesis—maybe some rare food, magic items, or souvenirs.

Or so he thought...

“I feel like I’m standing out like a sore thumb, though.”

He muttered this with a hint of dissatisfaction.

Normally, since he went out into town with Set, it wasn’t unusual for him to attract attention.

In that case, Rei was already used to it and didn’t really need to worry about standing out that much…

“Ah, I knew you’d think that. Hey, you guys. Rei and I don’t really mind you tagging along, but could you at least come by yourselves?”

Brewitt, standing next to Rei, addressed the two who were eating skewered meat from a street stall with curious expressions.

The two of them, Ranigus and Teres, returned puzzled glances as they ate the skewered meat from the food stall—a first for them.

It wasn’t that they were trying to hide anything; they genuinely had no idea why he was saying such a thing.

Realizing this, Brewitt sighed and spoke.

“We’re your bodyguards, guys. And your attendants, too. With nearly twenty of us traveling together like this, of course we’re going to stand out!”

“Even so… I always look like this when I go out in town. What about you, Teres?”

“Me too. Besides, I’m a delicate woman, so of course I need bodyguards, right? And I also need a female attendant to handle things I can’t ask a man to do.”

The two of them, the epitome of nobility, stated this as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

That said, if asked which of the two—Ranigus and Teres or Brewitt—embodies the true image of a noble, almost everyone would answer the former.

After all, Brewitt—a noble, and the heir apparent at that—walking through the streets without a single bodyguard is, in many ways, the exception.

“In that case, I should have come alone… no, with just one person and one dog.”

Watching the three of them interact, Rei murmured.

Normally, Set would prioritize being with Rei, but today he must have decided to play with Yellow, as he didn’t come out into the city with Rei.

Yellow usually spends a lot of time with Elena, but Elena has been busy with the Celestan incident and doesn’t have time to be with Yellow.

So, Rei had planned to explore Anesis alone today… but Brewitt came to visit him in the morning, and they ended up going into town together. Just as the two of them were about to leave the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion, they ran into Ranigus and Teres, and for some reason, the four of them ended up sightseeing in Anesis together.

(Brewitt aside, I haven’t really talked much with Ranigus and Teres. Besides, since they came to the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, they probably had business with Elena. So why on earth did they decide to tour Anesis with me? Was it because of yesterday’s fire whirlwind?)

I couldn’t help but think that might be the case, but I decided not to dwell on it for now.

No, actually, what bothered me more were the knights and attendants who had come along as their escorts.

“Come on, Rei, don’t worry about it so much. With this many people around, there’s practically no one who’d even think of doing anything stupid.”

Ranigus said that, and it was certainly true.

It would be strange if someone were to pick a fight in a situation where there were so many knights acting as our escorts.

That would mean they weren’t just randomly getting involved—they’d likely come looking for a fight with some specific purpose in mind from the start.

“Ah… I see. For now, let’s just go around with this group. I don’t have to check out Anesis all by myself today, after all.”

“...That’s surprising. I was sure that, being Rei, you’d just send the unnecessary people away.”

From Brewitt’s perspective, Rei’s words must have been completely unexpected. He wore an expression that suggested he’d truly been caught off guard.

“Is that so? Well, I just thought it might be nice to check things out with a big group for a change. You know, a change of pace.”

“A change of pace, huh. Well, if that’s what you say, Rei, I don’t really mind.”

In the end, since Rei didn’t seem to mind, Brewitt accepted it without saying anything further.

Although Brewitt usually went out into the city alone, he was still a noble, so he was usually surrounded by a large entourage.

That’s probably why, even in a situation like this, he doesn’t really mind it himself.

The fact that he seemed concerned here was largely out of consideration for Rei, who wasn’t used to this kind of situation…

That said, it was undeniable that he also found it a hassle to walk around town with such a large entourage.

“Look, next… hmm, how about we try some soup at that food stall over there? I’ve had soup there before, and it was actually pretty good.”

To change the mood, Rei pointed to a food stall selling soup a short distance away.

Naturally, since neither Ranigus nor Teres had ever tried soup from a street stall before, they headed toward it with keen interest.

As a stall owner, it wouldn’t be strange to think that he wouldn’t normally want to do business with such a group… but fortunately, he had a resolute personality, so he showed no signs of fear and welcomed the two of them—along with their guards and attendants.

Of course, a major factor in his ability to adopt such an attitude is the fact that Duke Kerebel rules over Anesis.

After all, if a noble were to act arrogantly here, it would be tantamount to picking a fight with Duke Kerebel.

That is precisely why, even though many nobles gather here—including those who do not regard commoners as human beings in their own territories—most of them do not behave arrogantly in Anesis.

...I say “most” here because, even in a place like Anesis, there are still a few who act as they please.

Naturally, such individuals will face the appropriate consequences from Duke Kerebel.

According to rumors heard by the residents of Anesis, there were even cases where several nobles had their titles stripped.

That said, these are merely rumors, and it is unclear whether they actually happened… Nevertheless, there is no doubt that the residents of Anesis felt that someone like Duke Kerebel’s Liberte would not be out of place engaging in such behavior.

In any case, for that reason, the residents of Anesis do not go so far as to fear the nobility excessively.

That said, of course, they don’t act arrogantly toward the nobility either.

“Welcome. I heard what you were saying. How many servings of soup do you need?”

“Well, I’ll take enough for everyone here. If the soup is as delicious as Rei says, it wouldn’t be strange for everyone to try some.”

At Ranigus’s words, the guards and attendants looked surprised yet pleased.

Even though it was daytime, given that it was winter, hot soup was naturally very welcome.

Once the soup was prepared, one of the attendants tasted it and, finding it safe, Ranigus and Teres drank it, followed by the guards and attendants.

Normally, this was the kind of soup commoners would drink when they were a little hungry or feeling cold—it was certainly not the sort of soup a noble would drink.

Even so, the moment Ranigus and Teres tasted it, they genuinely thought it was delicious.

It wasn’t that the soup had actually improved in taste; this was purely a matter of the surrounding atmosphere.

The food bought from festival stalls isn’t usually anything to write home about, but it still tastes good when you’re in the festive atmosphere.

The same thing must be happening to Ranigus and Teres.

(Well, unlike Brewitt, these two are proper nobles. Considering that, the idea of buying something to eat from a street stall like this... could this actually be their first time?)

Thinking that, Rei also bought some soup from a food stall and drank it.

It wasn’t exactly delicious, but the flavors of the vegetables and dried meat had infused into the soup, and there was no doubt it was a comforting taste.

“Is this because we’re in Anesis? Or do they serve soup like this in other places too…? What do you think?”

“Hmm. I’d like to think it’s because of Anesis. I hope they eat the same way back in my family’s territory.”

Ranigus nodded in agreement with Teres’s words.

...Seeing the two of them like that, several of the guards and attendants nearby smiled.

They were likely pleased to see the people they were tasked with protecting and supporting thinking so earnestly about their own family’s lands.

“...So, are you sure you don’t need to go in there?”

Rei asks Brewitt with a hint of teasing.

Although Ranigus and Teres come from noble families, they cannot become the heads of their respective houses.

In contrast, Brewitt has already been designated as the next head of the family.

Given that, Brewitt should naturally be on the same side as Ranigus and Teres, not on Rei’s side.

Despite that, Brewitt stands by Rei’s side, watching Ranigus and Teres as if they were some sort of endearing sight.

“That’s because he’s hardly ever been out in the city. I go out there pretty often, so I have a pretty good idea of what life in the city is like.”

At Brewitt’s words, the guards standing nearby—Ranigus and Teres—revealed a hint of surprise.

For those serving as noble guards, the idea that a noble would venture into town so frequently was likely completely unexpected.

Noticing the guards’ reactions, Rei said nothing in particular and headed toward the next shop.

The next stop was a shop that sold a variety of tableware and general sundries, chosen so that Ranigus and Teres could enjoy themselves as well.

For Rei and Brewitt, it wasn’t all that unusual a shop, but that was only because the two of them were accustomed to moving about in the city.

In that sense, a shop selling general sundries was a very rare sight for Ranigus and Teres.

“Hey, look at this. …I’ve never seen tableware like this before… I wonder what it’s used for?”

“Hmm… I’m not quite sure either. Wouldn’t it be quickest to just ask someone who works here?”

“Young master, that’s a tool used to tenderize meat when cooking.”

Hearing Ranigus’s words, an attendant who had been listening nearby spoke up.

“Tenderize… meat? Why on earth would anyone do that?”

“By tenderizing the meat with this, you can make it more tender, even out the thickness, and ensure it cooks through properly.”

“...Thickness aside, does just pounding the meat with this really make it tender?”

Ranigus looked at the small, hammer-like kitchen tool with a slightly skeptical expression.

Teres also gave the explanation a slightly skeptical look.

However, when the shop clerk explained that the attendant’s words were correct, they finally seemed convinced and shifted their interest to the next tool.

“Is it really that interesting?”

From Brewitt’s perspective, a store selling general sundries like this probably isn’t anything out of the ordinary.

However, that’s only because Brewitt often walks around town by himself.

For an ordinary noble, entering a shop frequented by commoners like this is extremely rare, so it’s only natural that he would be fascinated by everything.

“It’s only natural that it’s unusual. …Brewitt, when you first started wandering around town, weren’t you fascinated by all sorts of things too?”

“Well… I suppose so. I won’t deny it.”

Brewitt muttered as if in agreement, perhaps recalling the past.

Rather, he likely recalled that when he first started wandering the streets himself, so many things were novel to him, and he was moved by just about everything.

Back then, he must have been even more moved than Ranigus or Teres.

Thinking of that, he realized the scene unfolding before him wasn’t all that unusual… and Brewitt watched the interactions inside the general store.
