The moment something unidentifiable spread throughout the surrounding area, Rei landed on the ground while feeling a sense of unease about his own movements.

Using Sleipnir's boots, Rei had performed a series of aerial jumps—a movement pattern that could be called a triangle jump, a square jump, or even a pentagon jump—but an unexpected event caused him to halt his movement the instant he touched down.

"What... is this?"

"Don't you recognize it? This is divine punishment. Taste the power of the Goddess of Holy Light with your own body."

Vicente burst into laughter at Rei's expression.

The tattoo-like mark on his right cheek glowed, asserting itself fiercely.

(So it's a stigma. Meaning that's the sacred mark, huh. What a peculiar sacred mark it is. ...If that's the case, is the tattoo on that old man's hand also that stigma? And does that mean the effect making him vanish the instant my attacks hit him is also that stigma's effect? But, but, if that's the case...)

Thoughts raced through his mind, but Vicente saw no need to wait for an answer.

The enemy was a great foe, one who had insulted the one and only God.

He must deliver punishment fitting that sin.

"Well then, great enemy... Receive the retribution for your sins. I execute divine punishment."

At Vicente's declaration, the old man wiped the gentle smile usually on his face and nodded in earnest agreement.

Beside the old man, who held his staff like an estoc, Vicente clenched his fist... and in the next instant, he kicked off the ground.

Seeing Vicente's straight charge toward him, Rei readied the Death Scythe in his hand, preparing to meet the attack.

Seeing Rei like that, Vicente's lips curved into a smile.

The instant that smile touched his lips, Rei felt a fierce chill run down his spine, as if an icicle had been thrust into him.

"Huh!?"

As if driven by that chill, Rei didn't meet Vicente's attack head-on. Instead, he flew sideways.

It was not a conscious decision, but a reflexive movement.

But the moment he made that move, the sense of unease within Rei grew stronger than ever before.

(Slow!?)

Yes, a movement he could normally execute in an instant now took several times longer to complete.

Of course, it was still a remarkably swift movement for an ordinary human. Yet, to Rei's senses, it felt sluggish and cumbersome. Worst of all, it was slow enough to hinder his ability to intercept Vicente.

It felt as if the opponent was moving normally, yet only he was being left behind by the flow of time.

Defending like this wouldn't be fast enough.

Rei made a split-second decision. Instead of blocking Vicente's attack with Death Scythe, he tried to defend by rapidly increasing the heat of his own magic skill... the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

He hadn't yet fully mastered precise heat adjustments, but simply choosing to raise or lower the temperature wasn't difficult.

Infusing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour with magic to incinerate the fist aimed at him, the surrounding temperature began to rise exponentially.

"What!?"

The one who let out this cry of astonishment was Vicente.

His voice carried the strong conviction that such a thing was absolutely impossible.

"Vicente!"

The old man's voice snapped Vicente back to his senses, his mind blank from witnessing the utterly impossible phenomenon.

It was no longer the kindly old man's voice from moments before, but a sharp tone—sharp enough to convince anyone that he was indeed a member of the Stigma.

Snapping back to reality at that voice, Vicente abandoned his attack on Rei, leaping backward to create distance.

"Master, what on earth... Why, within a sanctuary where Stigma has been activated..."

Beside Vicente, who had leapt backward, stood the old man, now holding a staff.

Vicente turned to ask the old man, but the only response was a shake of the head.

"I don't know. Its movements are definitely slowed, so the sanctuary isn't ineffective. If that's the case... No, now is not the time to ponder such things."

Centered on Rei, the surrounding temperature rapidly began to rise.

It was winter... the dead of night, yet all the snow that had accumulated before the battle began had melted into water.

That water was now evaporating due to the heat Rei emitted, turning into steam that increased the surrounding humidity. On top of that, the temperature had risen to nearly thirty degrees Celsius.

Even Vicente and the others, who were away from Rei, felt this heat. What would happen if they got close to Rei?

Vicente, especially, had just tried to swing a punch at Rei. He shuddered, his spine going cold despite the surrounding heat, thinking that if he had followed through, his fist would undoubtedly have been scorched.

Of course, if it meant delivering divine punishment to a great enemy, he cared not what happened to his own body or life.

But he was a member of the Stigmata, chosen ones within the Holy Light Church itself.

If that Stigma could do nothing, and worse, suffered severe injury just for trying to attack, the Holy Light Church itself could become a laughingstock.

"Master..."

Please lend me your wisdom. What should I do?

Vicente turned his gaze to the old man standing beside him, and the old man, holding his staff, thought for a moment before speaking.

"Hmm. Then perhaps I should intervene a little."

As the old man spoke, his form vanished abruptly.

The next instant, he abruptly materialized right before Rei's eyes.

"What?!"

Hadn't he expected the old man to suddenly appear nearby in temperatures hot enough to incinerate anyone who approached? Rei let out a startled cry, but in the next instant, he swung his Death Scythe, channeling his magic through it.

Thanks to the effects of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the blade of the Death Scythe, swung in an instant, sliced through the air toward the old man at a speed that seemed to leave even sound behind, aiming to cleave his torso in two... but the moment the blade touched the old man again, his form vanished.

"Hmm, hmm. I see. Quick reflexes."

As the old man reappeared beside Vicente and murmured, Rei suddenly felt something was off.

(That just now... wasn't that strange? No, it was definitely strange. Could someone really teleport that frequently? Even the teleportation stones created by the Bestir Empire have all sorts of strict conditions... So then, maybe it wasn't teleportation?)

Perhaps the old man's ability wasn't teleportation at all.

Holding that doubt, Ray readied his Death Scythe while maintaining the heat of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, fixing his gaze on the old man ahead.

Even though he had just appeared right next to Rei moments ago, he didn't seem to have a single drop of sweat on his face. Wasn't that strange, even for someone like Stigma?

Once suspicion took root, questions kept bubbling up.

For instance, when he vanished after dodging Rei's Flying Slash, didn't he reappear in the exact same spot where he had been before vanishing, right after the Flying Slash tore through the wall instead of him?

Even using teleportation, appearing in an identical spot should be extremely difficult.

Of course, if someone claimed to possess that level of skill, one might have to accept it. Still, from Rei's current perspective, it leaves nothing but suspicion.

(But the attack he took when exiting the window was definitely real. ...Maybe I should test it a bit more. Ideally, if I could touch him directly with my hand just before he vanishes, that would prove it. But how... how do I even test it?)

While he was inwardly plotting how to defeat his opponent, the two Stigmata weren't just standing by silently watching.

But dealing with an opponent whose very approach inflicted damage on one's own body—a skill that fused offense and defense—was difficult even for Vicente.

And above all else...

(Why can they wield such power within the Sanctuary?)

Yes, that was Vicente's greatest question.

Normally, when this Sanctuary was activated, everyone within its domain should be unable to wield even half their power.

The sole exception to this rule were those bearing the Stigma.

For those bearing the Stigma specifically, they could wield greater power than usual within this sanctuary.

And yet, even under such circumstances, the formidable enemy before their eyes retained sufficient power... enough to still stand against them.

This was fundamentally impossible, and if handled poorly, it could even become an issue concerning the Holy Light Church's doctrine.

That was precisely why Vicente couldn't comprehend the being before him... and therefore couldn't tolerate it.

"You who insolently oppose the power of the Goddess of Holy Light... You truly are our great enemy!"

"Calm down, Vicente."

"But, Master!"

The old man interjected, trying to calm Vicente, but Vicente immediately tried to retort at the old man.

But in the next instant, the gaze the old man directed at Vicente sharpened to piercing intensity.

"I said calm down. Could you not understand those words?"

"That... Yes. I apologize."

Under that gaze, Vicente could say nothing more and simply calmed down.

Seeing Vicente like this, the old man returned to his usual kindly expression and smiled.

"Heh heh heh. It's good that you're serious, but you're too quick to lose your temper. I've told you this many times before."

"...Yes."

"Well, I understand why you care so much about him, but the problem with this matter probably isn't that complicated, you know."

The old man's words, spoken with half-conviction, were both astonishing and worthy of respect to Vicente.

Why was Rei displaying such combat prowess within the Sanctuary? Even though he himself had no clue, the old man had already solved that puzzle.

"Why is that?"

"What? It's not that difficult. There were several keys to solving the puzzle. For instance, his movements themselves are slowed by the sanctuary's blessings. You understand that, right?"

"Yes. His reaction speed to my attacks has definitely slowed. But..."

"Don't rush the answer. As I just said, his movements being slowed means the Sanctuary's effect is definitely working on him. Yet, there's no sign of attenuation from that red thing. ...At first glance, it seems that way."

"At first glance?"

At the old man's words, Vicente turned his gaze once more toward Rei, who had activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Even from this distance, he could feel the overwhelming heat.

Even if someone completely unaware were here, they would never believe it was winter now.

They would perceive it as spring... no, summer.

Had he attacked Rei just now, his fist would likely... no, undoubtedly have been burned to ashes.

"Hmm. To be blunt, his abilities are simply too high. Even with the effects of the Sanctuary dampening them, he still possesses that level of strength. That is my assessment."

"You fool!"

Even though the words came from someone he respected, the man reflexively denied it.

It was only natural. Even with the protection of the Holy Light Goddess suppressing the opponent's power to less than half, it still meant he possessed the ability to injure them.

That meant Rei's power surpassed even theirs, despite being under the protection of the Holy Light Church's goddess.

Unforgivable. Unbearable. Unacceptable. Impossible.

Such thoughts filled Vicente's heart... until finally, an absolute rage toward Rei filled not only his mind but his entire body.

"Calm yourself. This too is likely a trial bestowed upon us by the gods. Only by overcoming this trial can we demonstrate our sincere devotion to the divine. That the foe is a great enemy means we must defeat this one and show the power of our faith. That is surely the meaning."

At the old man's words, the fury that had clouded Vicente's expression instantly stilled.

If he defeated the great enemy before him through his faith, it would prove his devotion and simultaneously bring joy to God.

Hearing this, his fury from just moments before seemed nothing but his own immaturity.

"I understand, Master. I was foolish. ...I must bring divine punishment upon him, the great enemy. That is all I need to focus on, correct? Then, the gods will accept my faith, and I too shall be embraced by their love. ...To envelop all of this world in the love of the Goddess of Holy Light, creating days where all may live peacefully and happily."

"Hmm. I share your sentiment. Such a being must not exist. In the name of the Goddess of Holy Light, divine punishment shall befall the great enemy."

"Divine punishment!"

Shouting sharply, Vicente kicked off the ground and stepped forward.

Rei, thinking it better than letting the situation remain deadlocked, prepared to act, clad in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, awaiting Vicente.

Strangely enough, the decision to move the situation forward was made by both parties almost simultaneously.
