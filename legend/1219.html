Once Johanna and the others were hired on a temporary basis by Rei and his crew, there was nothing left to do until Marina returned, so they each began eating.

They ate the food they'd ordered, chatted animatedly, and drank—except for Rei, of course.

Naturally, the tension that had lingered around them just moments before vanished.

In fact, everyone laughed and chatted animatedly.

The most popular person, unsurprisingly, was Elena... or rather, Elk.

Naturally, some of Johanna's male companions wanted to talk to Elena.

But Elena, whose beauty rivaled even Vihera's, was not someone one could approach so easily.

Beyond her beauty, the fact that she hailed from the Bestir Empire and bore the nickname "Princess General" likely made many hesitant.

...And yet, they could talk normally to Elk, who also had a nickname like Rei and Elena.

Being men probably played a big part in that.

"Then, when a pack of werewolves attacked, Min used magic to wipe them all out."

Elk, pleased that Rodos's treatment was on track, continued cheerfully.

Seeing Elk so carefree, the others seemed to be having fun, their moods rising and falling with his.

"Looks like you're having quite the lively time."

The one who spoke to the group was Marina, who had finished her conversation with the Guildmaster and arrived at the tavern unnoticed.

She murmured with a hint of envy, thinking about how her own conversation had been purely business...

"Oh, Marina. You're back. Is the meeting over?"

Marina gave a small smile and nodded as she answered Rei, who was eating stew packed with meat.

"Yes. I settled the matter regarding this incident. I also got a thorough report on the recent dungeon activities. ...Things are going much better than I anticipated. It was an unexpected stroke of luck."

"...Huh? What do you mean?"

Rei looked slightly puzzled at Marina's meaningful words.

But Marina merely offered Rei a smile that hinted at feminine charm, saying nothing as she sat down in the chair beside him.

Elena on his right, Marina on his left. ...A scene more fitting for the phrase "a woman on each arm" would be hard to come by.

"Well, you see..."

Marina said, as if trying to gloss over something.

But Rei judged it must be related to guild secrets and didn't press further.

In contrast to Marina, Elena, who had been eating a vegetable and ham sandwich next to Rei, opened her mouth with a slightly puzzled look.

"You're not plotting something, are you?"

"Well, I'm always thinking about how to captivate Rei, you know?"

Marina flashed a smile that could charm even a woman.

That smile captivated not only Johanna's companions, who had been talking with Elk... and the other men watching Rei and the others from the tavern... but even the women were drawn in.

Unlike when they were discussing the Silver Lion, Johanna's companions now gave off no aura of rejection toward their surroundings.

That's precisely why they had the leisure to turn their gaze toward Marina now.

"Oh? What's the matter?"

Marina asked with an amused smile. Everyone around her flushed, not from drunkenness, and averted their eyes.

Normally, with beauties like Elena and Marina present, it wouldn't be surprising if someone tried to pick a fight... but among those in the tavern and guild, no one dared bother Rei and his group.

After all, there was no one present who didn't know all of them: Rei, Elena, Marina, and Elk.

If they'd all been eating at separate tables, maybe one or two strangers who didn't recognize them might have tried to pick a fight.

But with so many famous faces gathered together, they were bound to recognize someone.

Naturally, the one whose face was recognized most widely was Elk.

"Is this meat from the dungeon? This dish is delicious."

"Ah, yes. The dungeon has a forest layer too, and this is meat from the Water Monkeys that appear there."

Overhearing Elk and Johanna's conversation, Rei exchanged glances with Elena beside him.

A wry smile played on both their lips.

They must have recalled the time they were ambushed by a massive horde of Water Monkeys during their previous dungeon run.

"We had a rough time back then, didn't we?"

"Yeah. We managed thanks to Rei... But the fact the guild is serving water monkey meat dishes means there are adventurers out there fighting those swarms."

"Honestly, I don't want to fight those monsters again."

Rei reminisced deeply about that time, Elena admired the adventurers who hunted Water Monkey meat, and Ara recalled it all with a look of utter disgust.

Their previous dungeon descent had brought its share of hardships, but it wasn't defined solely by those trials. ...Whether the Water Monkeys fit that description is another matter.

Being attacked and chased by what could be described as countless Water Monkeys... While they managed to survive in the end, it wasn't exactly a pleasant memory.

Still, considering it was the quest that led to meeting Elena, it wasn't entirely bad.

...Though, to be fair, that quest ended on a major sour note.

"But didn't Rei bring back Water Monkey meat? If so, I don't think he'd be this surprised."

Elena asked, her gaze fixed on Rei's Misty Ring.

Understanding what Elena meant by that look, Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah, it's still in there. But... I tried a little bit before, and it wasn't that delicious. ...It certainly didn't turn out anything like this stew."

Despite being thoroughly stewed, it retained a satisfying chew that delighted the palate.

And yet, after chewing for a while, it melted in the mouth while still allowing the rich meat flavor to be savored.

This stew, offering a truly satisfying "This is meat!" feeling, was so good it wouldn't be strange to enjoy it on its own, rather than just as a bar snack with drinks.

"Ah, you think so too, Rei? Actually, we tried all sorts of things when we defeated the Water Monkeys in the dungeon... but we just couldn't get it to taste this good."

Johanna, who had been listening to Rei, muttered discontentedly.

However, that dissatisfaction wasn't just about the cooking; it also stemmed from her frustration at not being able to see Set.

Just as the conversation ended and the banquet was about to begin, Johanna tried to leave the guild.

Naturally, her destination was the stable where Rei and the others were staying.

But Rei stopped her, saying they could meet tomorrow.

Johanna wanted to see him today, even if she could see him tomorrow.

Completely oblivious to Johanna's attitude, Rei reached for the food and spoke up.

"This is purely down to the cook's skill, I suppose."

"...I suppose so. But why would such a skilled cook be in a place like this? I mean, it's different in big cities like Gilm."

It was Marina, who had been talking with Ara, who answered Johanna's tilted-head question.

Was it because she was the guildmaster that she knew why such a skilled cook was here, or had she simply heard it from someone else?

In any case, Marina addressed Johanna, who looked puzzled.

"Because this is a dungeon."

"Because it's a dungeon?"

"Yes. Precisely because it's a dungeon, all sorts of materials... well, in this case, I suppose we should call them ingredients. They're readily available. For someone training in cooking, this is the perfect place to hone their skills."

"...I see."

Now that she thought about it, it made perfect sense.

Being able to cook with rare ingredients obtained from the dungeon could be considered a privilege for chefs working in dungeon-filled areas.

"But even if you hone your skills here, the materials... ingredients found in dungeons probably can't be obtained elsewhere, so wouldn't it be pretty useless?"

One of Johanna's companions asked Marina.

...Though he asked seriously, the man kept glancing repeatedly at Marina's deeply plunging neckline.

Despite receiving such glances, Marina smiled and spoke.

"That might be true. But being able to prepare numerous dishes is a valuable skill for a cook. It might not be useful elsewhere, but it might just be useful."

"Is that... really the case?"

The man, who lived as an adventurer, didn't seem to fully grasp Marina's words.

Marina smiled at the tilting man and nodded in agreement.

He couldn't grasp it, but when told that's just how cooks are, he had to admit it made sense.

And so, they spent some time eating and drinking together... Eventually, as the food began to run out, Rei spoke up.

"Well, we don't want to make too much noise here and affect tomorrow, so I think we should call it a night around here. ...Johanna, come to the dungeon entrance around nine tomorrow morning when the bell rings."

"Huh? That's... quite a leisurely schedule. Reaching the lowest level where the Silver Lion is takes considerable time, doesn't it? ...Oh, wait, I suppose that goes without saying to you, Rei-san."

Rei had been to the lowest level before.

He should have known exactly how long it would take to get there.

But then why...? Johanna was confused.

"Well, I'll explain that tomorrow."

Rei had mentioned knowing Grimm, but he decided it wasn't wise to discuss Grimm's abilities right now.

When he'd brought up Grimm, Johanna's companions had been keeping watch around them, but now everyone was drinking and eating, and it wasn't the right time for secrets.

Still, meeting Johanna and the others at this tavern had genuinely provided a welcome change of pace.

"Tomorrow... once Vihera regains consciousness, we'll celebrate in style, shall we?"

"Ah, that sounds wonderful. Lady Vihera will surely be delighted. Um, and of course, Set-chan will be there too... right?"

"Yeah."

He probably hadn't been there when they came to the guild today. Facing Johanna's pleading gaze, Rei nodded as if it were obvious.

Since everyone would be celebrating, it was only natural that Set would be there too.

(But then, we'd have to rent out a whole place somewhere. And it'd have to be a pretty big one.)

At the very least, they'd need a place big enough for Set to fit inside, and whether they could find such a place was questionable.

If it were spring or summer... or just autumn, they could have a banquet outside, but it was already quite cold outside now.

It was still somewhat warm during the day, but holding a banquet outside around evening was practically impossible.

Well, Rei has his Dragon Robe, so he could hold a banquet outside even in winter without issue, and the same goes for Set.

But for most others, attempting a banquet outside could easily lead to freezing to death in the worst case.

Though, since most attendees are adventurers, worrying about freezing to death might be overkill.

"Ah, then I'll find a good restaurant. Believe it or not, I've been here quite a while now."

"Alright, I'll leave it to you then."

Rei had come here before to explore the dungeon, but back then, he'd had little time to look around the shops.

He'd come purely to explore the dungeon, completed the succession ritual there, and left immediately.

...Had betrayal not occurred, and the succession rite concluded without incident, he might have had the leisure to raise a celebratory toast at some shop or buy souvenirs upon returning to the surface.

Moreover, this place had developed tremendously since Rei's previous visit.

So much so that Rei wondered if it wasn't an entirely different place.

"Yes. Leave it to me. I'll plan a feast that will delight Set-chan... and astonish Lord Vihera, who has awakened after so long."

"...Alright, do that for me."

The fact that Set's name came before Vihera's was probably why Johanna was Johanna.

"Indeed. To surprise Vihera... Tomorrow absolutely cannot be a failure, you understand?"

Marina, who had been listening to Rei and Johanna's conversation, smiled... Her usual lustrous smile remained the same, but within that lustre, she murmured words filled with resolve. Rei and Johanna... and everyone else who had heard Marina's voice nodded in response.
