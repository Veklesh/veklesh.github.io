In these parts, it was proof that even if they were overconfident, they possessed a certain level of skill as adventurers.

In any case, each armed themselves and sprang into action.

"Now's not the time for that. We have to find Roakla, no matter what."

Slesha's words seemed to convince them.

Dagza nodded and shouted into the forest.

"Rokura! Where are you?! Answer me!"

Normally, shouting like this in the forest at night was extremely dangerous.

But it seemed an unavoidable decision to search for his comrades.

The others shouted into the surrounding darkness just as Dagza did, but there was no response from Roakla.

After searching for about thirty minutes... eventually, everyone gathered around Dagza.

The serious looks on everyone's faces meant that the complete lack of response, even after shouting like this, signaled a very low chance he was still alive.

"What do we do?"

What that meant was obvious.

After searching this hard and still seeing no sign of him, Roakla was probably no longer alive.

There was no doubt that Roakla was one of the most skilled warriors in Dagza's party.

Yet even he was killed so easily, in such a short time, without putting up much of a fight.

If the enemy shows up, do we stand a chance? It's only natural to think that.

"What do you mean, what should we do? It's obvious. We absolutely have to find Roakla or take revenge for him!"

Dugza's voice echoed through the forest.

Though Dagza was overconfident and forceful, he was also deeply loyal to his comrades, as befitting a party leader.

After all, if a man who was violent and didn't care about his comrades were the party leader, no one would follow him.

That's precisely why it was only natural for him to want to find Roakla, or avenge him if he had been killed.

'Huh!?'

Mid-sentence, a sudden scream erupted.

Dagza, Slesha, and Soknan reflexively turned toward the sound... their gazes fixed on the spot where their comrades should have been just seconds ago, each readying their weapons.

But there was no one there.

...Yes, just seconds ago, their comrades had definitely been there. Now, they had vanished completely.

As if their very presence there had been a mistake.

"Above!"

Amidst the chaos, Suresha was the first to shout.

No, judging by the angle his neck was bent at, his life was likely already gone.

"Vines!? Why vines...?"

"Huh!?"

Dagza's words made Slesha recall her search of the forest.

A quiet forest... one where only birds seemed to exist.

He had considered it a place where powerful monsters might exist, or had likely existed... but was that a complete misunderstanding?

Yes, they hadn't encountered one themselves, but they knew of such a creature existed.

Monsters that camouflage themselves as trees... Trents.

Trent itself is a Rank D monster, not particularly powerful.

But that's only if the enemy is a single one.

What if this place we're in... no, what if the entire forest is filled with Trents?

Wouldn't that mean we're right in the middle of the enemy's territory?

Slesha herself didn't know the exact size of this forest.

Even so, it was clear there weren't just ten or a hundred trees growing here.

"Danger, Slesha!"

As they contemplated their perilous situation, Dagza shouted and shoved Slesha's body.

No, it wasn't a gentle push; it was more accurate to describe it as shoving him with all his might.

Slasha, sent flying by the powerful impact, instinctively regained her balance to avoid falling and looked behind her.

"Guhhh!"

What Slesha saw when she turned was the sight of tree roots, stretching like they were crawling across the ground, piercing through Dagza's body from below.

And it wasn't just one or two roots. There were easily over ten.

That many roots pierced through Dagza's body... and spread roots throughout his entire body.

Like pulsing blood vessels, Slesha could see the roots spreading through Dagza's skin, permeating his entire body.

Slesha and Soknan, witnessing this, froze in shock.

They knew exactly what a Trent was, of course.

They didn't know every single detail about the Trent, but they thought they understood the basics.

But this? This was not part of the information Slesha and Soknan possessed.

"Run... away..."

It was likely the effect of being corrupted by Trent's roots. His speech was already broken and extremely difficult to understand.

But even so, Slesha and Soknan understood the feeling behind their leader's words. Almost reflexively, they kicked off the ground.

To escape this forest at any cost... likely a forest made of trents.

"Dagza... wait for us! We'll bring help, I promise!"

All Slesha could do was shout that.

For now, they had to escape this forest... this forest worthy of being called the Forest of Death. With that thought, they kept running through the woods.

Fortunately... or perhaps even that depended on the other side's circumstances, the trents forming the forest showed no sign of moving.

Then, she could escape the forest.

Thinking that, she kept running... until what came into view plunged both Slesha and Soknan into despair.

It was the place where they had camped.

No, that alone wouldn't be a problem.

But the fact that their campsite had been completely swallowed by the forest meant one of two things: either this forest was rapidly growing... or the trees themselves were moving.

And considering Trent was here, it was almost certainly the latter.

"Waaaaaah!"

"Soknan!?"

The shock of the campsite being swallowed by the forest froze them for a few seconds... but those few seconds were more than enough time for the two trapped within the forest—surrounded by trents—to be attacked.

"Run, Slesha! Tell Gilm about this quickly!"

Vines wrapped around his torso, legs, arms... every part of his body, trying to pull Soknan deeper into the forest.

Soknan tried desperately to resist by thrusting his dagger into the ground, but the force pulling him with the vines was stronger than he could have imagined.

He could only hold on for a few seconds with the dagger planted in the ground.

Slesha instinctively reached out to Soknan, trying desperately to pull his body free from the vines... but the instant she extended her hand, Soknan was dragged deeper into the forest.

(If only I'd stopped him more when Dagza said he was leaving the road... No, if only I'd stopped him when he said he was going to Gilm. Why... why...)

Tears fell from Slesha's eyes as she ran.

Of course, even an adventurer would tire from running nonstop.

She also felt she had to inform Gilm about this matter as soon as possible.

Though perhaps that thought stemmed less from a sense of duty and more from a calculation—a faint, perhaps even impossible hope—that she might be able to save Dagza and the others.

For Slesha, despite various issues, they were someone she could relax around.

Having survived countless life-threatening situations together, their bond was deep and unbreakable.
