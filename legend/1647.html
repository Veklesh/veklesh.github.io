The evening after a day spent playing in the river—or, to be precise, fishing—Rei, who was riding on Set’s back, finally spotted the group he was looking for.

He spotted a large number of carriages lined up near one of the entrances to a town along the highway.

With nearly a thousand people riding in those carriages, it was naturally difficult to fit them all inside the village or town.

Well, depending on the town’s size, it might have been possible, but the town currently in Rei’s line of sight wasn’t that large, so such a feat was likely impossible.

Naturally, simply leaving the carriages outside the town would likely cause people to get the wrong idea.

For that reason, several guards were stationed around the carriages and horses, also to tend to the animals.

(Monsters aside, it would be a real hassle if bandits or the like came after the horses.)

There’s no doubt that the monsters appearing in this area are basically low-rank monsters.

There was no doubt that thieves, who could use crude strategies, were far more troublesome than those weak or dim-witted monsters.

“I’ve spotted Sura and the others. We’re getting out!”

Squinting against the summer sunset, Rei called out to Set from inside the Set Cage, asking him to head toward the ground.

Set, who was lightly tapped on the back of the neck, immediately obeyed Rei’s command and began flapping his wings as he descended toward the ground.

Blending into the setting sun, Set’s descent went unnoticed for a moment by those who had been keeping watch around the carriage and horses.

Unfortunately, the first to spot Rei and Set were the bodyguards hired after Rei and Set had already separated from the group.

The sound of the Set Cage landing on the ground alerted them to something amiss, and when they looked up at the sky, there was a griffin.

Naturally, the guards had heard about Rei and Set, but there is a huge difference between hearing information from others and seeing it with one’s own eyes.

That’s why a sound—part scream, part roar—escaped the man’s mouth.

“U-uuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuu

And, naturally, when such a sound echoed through the area, those nearby grew tense, wondering what had happened.

They had naturally felt the impact when Set dropped the Set Cage, but upon hearing that even louder sound, their attention was completely drawn to it.

“Be… on… guard…?”

“Be on guard!” The male adventurer guard tried to shout, but unlike the one who had just screamed, he was a man hired as a guard by Gimekara’s arrangement, and had been there since Rei and the others arrived.

That was precisely why he had judged that it was Set descending from above.

It was dazzling in the setting sun, but even so, he didn’t miss Set’s silhouette.

...He likely also considered that if he mistook Set for an enemy and attacked him here, he would undoubtedly be subjected to a terrible fate at the hands of the women who were Set’s admirers—no, it wouldn’t be wrong to call them his followers.

In any case, the male adventurer, having recognized the descending figure as Set, shouted quickly.

“Cease fire! Don’t attack! It’s Set coming down! He’s one of us!”

At those words, those who had heard the earlier shout breathed a sigh of relief and lowered their weapons.

“What the hell? It’s Set? Seriously… Who was the one screaming like that?”

“Ah, you know. That guy who joined the escort after Rei and the others left.”

“Ah, I see. …Well, I guess it’s no wonder he was surprised to see Set.”

Several guards exchanged words with one another, visibly relieved.

Amidst this, Elena and the others emerged from the Set Cage.

Naturally, the guards find themselves captivated by the sight of Elena and the others.

Even while traveling with nearly a thousand women—all of whom had been abducted to be forced into prostitution—Elena and her companions possessed such striking beauty that they still managed to captivate everyone’s gaze.

Rei, having dismounted from Set’s back, calls out to the men, all of whom are captivated by the sight—whether they’re seeing them for the first time today or after a long absence.

“Looks like we came to the right place. It seems you’ve made quite a bit of progress while we were away—that’s a relief.”

“Rei-san!?”

Among Elena’s group, a man who had been gazing intently at Marina—who was dressed in a blue party dress with a particularly low neckline—finally snapped back to reality at Rei’s words.

As a former member of the Resistance, this man naturally recognized Rei’s face.

Rei seemed to remember the man’s face as well; he nodded before speaking.

“You were one of Sura’s men, weren’t you? I’m sorry to ask this, but could you bring Sura, Gimekara, and Rocks here?”

“Y-yes. Right away!”

Saying that, the bodyguard headed into the city.

The fact that he was able to clear the entry procedures so quickly meant there was no doubt the townspeople were giving him the necessary assistance.

To begin with, it was extremely rare for a group of nearly a thousand people to stop in such a small town.

That alone was certainly causing quite a commotion throughout the town.

That said, Sura and her group weren’t exactly bandits, and since they’d stopped in town, they’d be spending a fair amount of money—though that money would go to the Zolge Trading Company—so to the town, they weren’t a nuisance but rather a welcome sight.

Watching the man head into town, Rei approached a figure she recognized as well.

He didn’t remember her name, but she was a woman who had been hired as an adventurer bodyguard.

Originally, this group consisted almost entirely of women… and since they were brainwashed into working as prostitutes, the guards hired were primarily those who wouldn’t cause trouble with such individuals.

In that sense, it was only natural that they hired more female adventurers than male ones.

“Were there any major problems while we were away?”

“Huh? Well, a few of them split off in the villages and towns we stopped at along the way, but other than that, nothing in particular… Oh, but I did spot what looked like a bandit once.”

At the mention of bandits, a slight curve formed on Rei’s lips—perhaps because Rei, often called the “Bandit Eater,” had found new prey.

However, even that ferocious smile faded when the woman spoke, unconsciously taking a few steps back.

“It’s just… well… given how many of us there were, they ended up leaving without doing anything to us.”

“...I see.”

The slight hint of disappointment in Rei’s muttered reply was certainly not just the woman’s imagination.

“Anyway, I’m glad you’re safe. Were there any other problems?”

“Well…”

At Rei’s words, the woman gently turned her gaze toward Set.

Seeing her reaction, he must have vaguely understood what had happened. Rei gave a small smile and told her not to worry.

For those who had relied on Set as their emotional anchor, the idea of him disappearing from their lives was, by any measure, far from a positive development.

Given that, Rei knew full well that something had definitely happened on that front.

(I made the right call not bringing Johanna along, didn’t I?)

Rei had heard before that things had reached a breaking point between Milein and the women in this group.

If he’d brought Johanna—a Set enthusiast who seemed to rival Milein in her devotion—to that scene… it was easy to imagine the kind of commotion that would ensue.

Furthermore, naturally, when Rei and the others decided to return here and take several female adventurers along as escorts, Johanna—who had somehow heard about it—volunteered for the role.

However… considering Milein, there was no way they could hire Johanna as a bodyguard, especially since Johanna was the de facto leader of the former Guerrilla Unit.

Partly because of that, the plan to hire Johanna ultimately fell through… but the look on Johanna’s face at that moment was one that perfectly embodied the word “despair.”

She probably wanted to go with Rei and the others no matter what, but in the end, given her position, there was simply nothing she could do about it.

Rumor has it she lamented, “It’s always Milein…” or something to that effect.

That said, while Milein did manage to meet Set, since she was hired as a bodyguard for Ronitas, there was no way she could act alongside Rei, so their paths largely crossed without them actually meeting.

In fact, since Rei and the others were constantly going back and forth between this group and Gilm, Johanna probably had more opportunities to meet Set.

Even so, from Johanna’s perspective, the fact that Milein met Set before she did must have made her incredibly envious.

“Rei!”

I turned my gaze toward the voice I heard, and there stood three figures: Suura, Gimekara, and Rocks.

It was Gimekara who called out to her.

She must have been very happy that Rei had returned. A broad smile lit up her face.

“Gimekara, is it? Looks like you all made it back safely too. I’m a bit surprised at how far you’ve gotten in such a short time.”

“Haha. Things went more smoothly than we expected. Of course, there were various problems, but they were nothing we couldn’t handle here.”

Her words sounded like she genuinely, from the bottom of her heart, was happy that Rei had come.

Then again, he’s a sharp guy dispatched by the Zolgue Trading Company.

Naturally, his words and expression didn’t necessarily reflect his true feelings.

But still, given the current situation, there probably wasn’t much difference between his words and his true feelings.

“Hearing you say that is a relief to us, too. Still, I don’t think I’ll be able to stay here for very long this time either. Probably just a few days or so.”

“I see. Even so, having Rei and the others here is a huge help to us.”

For Gimekara, skilled individuals like Rei and his group—who, moreover, didn’t cause trouble—were exactly what he’d been desperate for.

...In Rei’s case, while he didn’t cause trouble with the women, there was certainly a good chance he’d cause trouble in other areas.

“Also, Rocks. I brought a few female adventurers from Gilm to serve as guards, so please put them to use.”

“That’s a big help. We’re definitely short on guards for this group.”

“I thought the number of guards had actually increased compared to before? I saw a few guys I hadn’t seen before.”

Rei asked this, recalling the man who had been startled by Set’s appearance, but Rocks shook his head at him.

“No, it’s not enough. To begin with, it’s crazy to expect this small group to guard a group of nearly a thousand people. When hiring guards, we prioritize personality over skill. …Of course, I understand the reason for doing it, though.”

Rocks was well aware that the women were watching him warily.

Still, the fact that their gazes are merely wary—and not filled with loathing—is likely due to Rocks’s behavior up to this point.

After all, as someone chosen to guard the diplomatic mission, Rocks is a man whose character—not just his skills—has been carefully considered.

“I’ll bring a few guards with me when I get back to Gilm, so please wait until then.”

“Yeah. But… please do it as soon as possible. Also, once we get close to Gilm, I’d like you to escort us all the way there if possible. What do you say?”

“Uh… I guess so.”

Rei agreed with Rox’s words.

Near Gilm… more precisely, around the towns of Abuero and Saburusta, which are close to Gilm, there are many bandits.

Bandits of all kinds gather there, targeting merchants heading to or returning from Gilm—a place that could be called a cash cow.

It’s as if their numbers never decrease, no matter how many are defeated.

While many of the bandits who gather there are weak, such bandits are weeded out by adventurers, merchant escorts, monsters, and conflicts among the bandits themselves.

As a result, the bandits who remain are those with considerable skill...

When passing through such a place, this party is clearly nothing but prime prey.

To prevent that, they need a deterrent that would make the bandits think twice about attacking.

“Got it. …But it might be better to just wipe out all the bandits in that area at once. For now, if I spot any suspicious-looking ones on the way back to Gilm, I’ll wipe them out.”

Of course, Rocks knew just how strong Rei was. Living in Gilm, that sort of information naturally came to his ears.
