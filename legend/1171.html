"...This is getting a bit tiresome."

After the adventurers left, Vihera muttered wearily.

Her gaze fell upon the bodies of lizardmen—bodies, bodies, bodies.

Except for the lizardman leader who had surrendered to Vihera, every single lizardman lay dead on the ground.

Naturally, the bodies of two lizardmen leaders were among them. All together, there were nearly thirty corpses.

...The initial number of lizardman corpses had been close to forty, but it had been reduced to nearly thirty because many corpses had been torn apart beyond recognition by Set's single strike, leaving only upper or lower halves.

If one absolutely needed lizardman materials, that would be another matter. But with nearly thirty intact corpses still lying there, he couldn't bring himself to collect such flesh fragments.

(If we're talking about bodies intact, Vihera's the best at that, huh?)

Rei pondered this as he looked at the corpses of the lizardmen he had slain.

Rei's attack method was fundamentally a two-spear style using the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

A single strike with the Death Scythe easily sliced through... no, severed the Lizardman's flesh.

In fact, many corpses lacked heads, limbs, tails, or other body parts.

The Twilight Spear's strikes were primarily thrusts, occasionally using the shaft for blows.

Neither technique caused significant damage to the lizardmen's bodies. ...Compared to the Death Scythe, that is.

In contrast, Vihera's attacks relied primarily on hand-to-hand combat. While lizardmen's bones might be broken, visible damage was minimal.

Of course, Vihera didn't defeat everything with hand-to-hand combat alone.

He also created magical claws on his gauntlets to slash through lizardmen bodies and used blades generated on the heels of his greaves.

Even so, the overwhelming majority of Lizardmen were defeated purely through hand-to-hand combat.

The most distinctive technique was undoubtedly Vihera's unique skill, the Demon-Infused Palm.

This attack method, which destroys the opponent from within, was extremely useful when one wished to leave a clean corpse.

...Though that only applied to the exterior, as internal organs were often destroyed, and in rare cases, valuable materials could be ruined depending on the target.

"Anyway, gather the corpses from each location into one spot. I'll handle storing them."

Normally, thirty Lizardmen and two Lizardman Leaders would represent a substantial income for adventurers.

But at the same time, ordinary adventurers would struggle to carry the lizardmen corpses back.

They'd need carts or similar vehicles, and considering the numbers, one or two carts wouldn't be enough to carry them all.

What made the impossible possible for ordinary adventurers was, needless to say, Rei's Misty Ring.

Starting with the ones closest to him, Vihera gathered the lizardmen he had slain and stored them in the Misty Ring. Next came Rei, then Set, and so on, until all the corpses were stored.

The entire process took about twenty minutes.

To Rei, it felt like an eternity, but if the adventurers attacked by this lizardman horde had witnessed this scene, they would have screamed, "Too fast!"

And so, after storing all the lizardmen—those still recognizable as such—before Rei lay the corpses of two lizardmen leaders and one goblin leader killed the previous day.

The goblin leader's corpse was something Rei had retrieved from the Misty Ring.

Why did she do that here? Simply because it was out of sight, and she wanted to absorb the goblin leader's magic stone.

"Lizardmen fetch a pretty good price for materials, and their meat is actually quite tasty. In that sense, I guess you could say this was a tasty job."

"Nah, that's because it's us, right? Rank A or B adventurers might be able to handle it, but ordinary adventurers getting ambushed by a pack this big of lizardmen? They wouldn't stand a chance."

While extracting magic stones from the goblin leader and lizardman leader's corpses, Rei conversed with Vihera.

...Meanwhile, Set kept watch over their surroundings while pecking at the badly damaged lizardman meat that hadn't been stored in the Misty Ring.

Since they weren't going to retrieve the remaining lizardman corpses anyway, neither Rei nor Vihera said anything about Set's actions.

They also judged that, since the bodies were so badly damaged, leaving them here wouldn't result in them becoming undead.

First, he extracted the magic stone from the goblin leader, then the one from the lizardman leader.

True to its status as a higher species of lizardman, its skin was tougher and more supple than that of ordinary lizardmen.

Even so, it couldn't resist Rei's brute strength and the sharp blade of his well-honed knife, allowing him to successfully extract the magic stone from its heart.

"Good. ...Is Set still eating his snack? Then I should absorb the Goblin Leader's magic stone with the Death Scythe."

Laying the extracted Goblin Leader's magic stone in his hand, Rei murmured.

Since he hadn't been able to acquire any skills from the kobold leader's magic stone, he couldn't help but wonder if he could acquire skills from the goblin leader's magic stone.

Still, it was a magic stone he hadn't absorbed yet, so not trying it here wasn't an option.

With Vihera watching with keen interest, he took the Goblin Leader's magic stone in his hand... and tossed it into the air. The Death Scythe flashed.

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Pain Burst Lv.3']

That announcement flashed through his mind.

The skill I learned increased the pain inflicted when attacking.

"...I see."

Rei wore a look of understanding at the skill's description.

The skill, which inflicted greater pain on the opponent for the same wound, made sense when he recalled the scene Pump, a former member of Hester's party, had endured.

Most of the skills he had acquired so far were fundamentally related to the monsters that possessed those magic stones.

Of course, exceptions existed. For instance, he had learned the Enhanced Sense of Smell skill from the Goblin Leader's magic stone Set had absorbed.

So while it wasn't guaranteed, he understood the general trend: the skills he could learn were often related to the abilities, characteristics, or personality of the monster that possessed the magic stone.

"How did it go? Did you learn any skills?"

"Yeah. I managed to enhance the Pain Burst skill."

He explained the effect of Pain Burst.

However, when Vihera asked what kind of effect the skill had, she didn't look particularly interested.

In fact, you could say the curiosity she'd shown when asking what skill I'd learned had almost completely vanished.

For Vihera, who enjoyed combat, the skill Pain Burst didn't seem to pique her interest at all.

"Hmm... I thought you'd learn a better skill. Like, say, that Flying Slash Rei uses all the time."

"Nah, what part of the Goblin Leader has Flying Slash elements?"

Even as he said this, considering everything that had happened so far, it wasn't entirely impossible.

After all, besides the example of enhanced sense of smell, he'd acquired several other skills that normally made you wonder, "Why would that monster have that skill?"

"Well, anyway. Never mind not getting any skills from the Kobold Leader. I'm glad I got one from the Goblin Leader. So, next..."

Rei's gaze fell upon the Lizardman Leader's magic stone.

This was arguably the main magic stone of this encounter.

"Who's going first, Set or Rei?"

"Not me, it's Death Scythe. ...Well, probably Death Scythe first."

Seeing Set pecking at the lizardman's flesh, Rei decided it would be better to go first.

(I'm sure Set heard that announcement message earlier too. Is lizardman meat really that tasty?)

Rei had eaten lizardman meat several times before.

It had a texture like springy chicken and a deep flavor that left an impression.

Still, it was more on the mild side, and in Rei's opinion, it couldn't quite match the intense umami of oak meat.

"Well, anyway... Now then, I hope I can learn some skill with this magic stone!"

He flung the lizardman leader's magic stone into the air and swung his Death Scythe in a flash.

The magic stone was cleaved in midair...

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Power Slash Lv.3']

That announcement flashed through Rei's mind.

"Power Slash, huh?"

Rei's muttered voice held a complex tone beyond mere joy.

Noticing Rei's state, Vihera spoke up curiously.

"What's wrong? Judging by your expression, you must have learned a new skill, right? If so, I think you should be happier about it."

"Yeah, I should be happy... but this Power Slash is tricky to handle."

Power Slash unleashes an attack that shatters rather than cuts, increasing the power of a single strike, but the strain on the user's hand is immense.

If you don't manage the shock properly, it can even injure your own wrist.

Even at Level 2, it's quite challenging to master. If my level goes up after this incident, it'll become even harder to handle properly.

(Then again, that also means its power increases significantly, so it could be a trump card in a pinch.)

Having gained a level, Rei had no intention of wasting that skill.

But the truth was, training in the Two-Spear Style was Rei's absolute top priority right now.

Considering that, it would likely be a while before she could truly master Power Slash.

(Though Set has mastered Power Crush, a similar skill, quite well.)

Even if she couldn't master it herself, Set could.

Thinking about it that way, he couldn't help but feel a bit pathetic, but he also thought that if he had to lose, losing to Set might not be so bad after all.

「Set！」

"Grr?"

Resolving to master Power Slash someday, Rei called out Set's name.

At Rei's words, Set lifted his head, leaving the Lizardman's flesh he'd been pecking at.

Blood and bits of flesh clung to his beak, proof he'd been pecking at the lizardman's flesh just moments before.

Wiping the beak of the approaching Set, Rei held out the magic stone he possessed toward Set.

Set stared intently at the magic stone resting in Ray's palm.

Then, extending his beak, he took the magic stone into his mouth and swallowed it whole.

[Set has acquired the skill 'Water Ball Lv.4']

The announcement flashed through his mind.

"Water Ball... Water Ball, huh. Well, considering he's a Lizardman, maybe it's not that surprising?"

Lizardmen are a race that naturally dwell in swamps and wetlands.

Of course, there are special cases like the Sand Lizardman Rei fought in Gosh, but those are exceptions.

No, considering even Sand Lizardmen utilize their own oases, one could say even they are closely connected to water.

Thinking that way, it's not at all strange for a Lizardman Leader, a higher-tier species than regular Lizardmen, to possess a water ball skill.

"Set, could you give it a try?"

Even without being explicitly told what to try, Set understood what Rei was asking.

"GRRRRRR!"

He let out a high-pitched cry at Rei's words... and in the next instant, water spheres materialized around Set.

Two of them.

Up to that point, it was no different from before.

However, the size of those water spheres was slightly larger than what Rei was familiar with.

He hadn't measured them precisely, but they were probably about fifty centimeters in diameter.

"...Just a bit bigger, huh. Well, once I level up again, they'll probably transform."

Rei himself had experienced with Flying Slash that reaching skill level five granted power that was a cut above anything before.

Of course, it was possible that this only applied to Flying Slash, but Rei instinctively sensed that wasn't the case.

"Right. Could you try shooting it at the ground over there?"

"GRRRR!"

Responding to Rei's words, it let out a growl and fired the water ball.

The water ball was slightly larger than before, but its speed remained unchanged.

No, it felt like it was moving slightly faster too, just like its size.

And so, the two water balls that struck the ground... swept up the surrounding earth, demonstrating slightly greater destructive power than before.
