Leaving the lord's mansion, Rei brushed off several merchants who tried to strike up conversations, then headed toward the guild with Set.

Along the way, a few people saw Set and tried to give him food or pet him, but it didn't take long for Rei and Set to arrive at the guild.

Just as at the lord's mansion earlier, Set immediately left them once they reached the guild entrance, without Rei needing to say a word.

Watching him go, Rei entered the guild.

It was daytime, after all. Few adventurers were inside the guild.

However, in the tavern section, despite the daytime hour, there were already people drinking.

Seeing the familiar scene, Rei couldn't help but smile as he headed towards the counter.

The receptionists noticed Rei's arrival, and one of them was the first to call out.

"Hey, Rei! You're back. When did you get back?"

Kenny, a female cat beastman, waved as she spoke.

...Seeing Kenny like that, Lenora, who had been organizing documents beside her, also looked up.

"Rei, you're back. Are you hurt anywhere?"

"Yeah. I'm fine. It was tough in many ways, but I made a decent profit... a profit... hmm..."

Rei started to say they'd made a profit, but stopped mid-sentence.

In the purest sense of profit, considering the magic items he'd seized, he could say he was completely in the black.

But for Rei, the real profit lay in the magic items he was collecting, or the magic stones from unknown monsters.

...Speaking of magic stones from unknown monsters, Rei suddenly remembered.

(Come to think of it, that Owl Rabbit we caught before heading to the Lerdact County... we never bled it out or anything. ...Well, that's unrelated to this matter though.)

I'll roast and eat it later with Set.

With that thought, Rei spoke again.

"Unfortunately, while I did manage to get my hands on some things that could be sold for money, there wasn't anything I really wanted."

"Hmm. Well, I figured you wouldn't have ended up in the red, Rei."

Kenny interrupted Rei and Lenora's conversation.

But while Lenora would normally scold Kenny, today she remained silent.

Because Kenny had already finished his own work.

(Could it be that he sensed Rei-san was coming today with his beastman intuition? ...It's hard to imagine normally, but if Kenny says so, I'd believe it.)

Of course, Lenora knew Kenny harbored romantic feelings for Rei.

Asked if she supported it, she felt a bit conflicted... Yet, hearing that his feelings for Rei had driven him to finish today's work through a woman's intuition somehow made it understandable.

"Anyway, please wait a moment. I need to report Rei's request to the Guild Master."

"Yeah, got it."

"Now, Lenora, you go report this right away. I'll keep Rei company while you're gone."

He must be pleased to unexpectedly get some time alone with Rei.

...Though, even if it's just the two of us, there are adventurers and guild staff inside the guild, so it's quite different from the kind of alone time Kenny might be imagining.

"Alright, alright. I'll be off then. Kenny, make sure you don't offend Rei."

With that, Lenora headed deeper into the guild.

After seeing her off, Kenny immediately addressed Rei.

"So, Rei. This job took you pretty far out, huh? Did you find any good food?"

He avoided asking about the quest itself because, judging from Rei and Lenora's earlier exchange, he figured it was better not to pry.

And choosing the topic of food was likely because he understood Rei enjoyed eating.

This was precisely why Kenny, having known Rei for several years, understood his preferences so well.

"Oh, actually, in a village I stopped at during the job, they served me a river fish hot pot. It was seriously delicious."

"River fish hot pot? Well, you can probably get that pretty much anywhere..."

"Unfortunately, the river fish used in that hot pot apparently only live in certain places. They're long, slender fish, but they were surprisingly tasty for river fish."

"...Huh. That sounds kind of interesting."

Being a cat beastman, Kenny naturally loved fish.

But naturally, Gilm isn't located by the sea, so the fish they eat are basically river fish.

Of course, salted or dried fish from the sea did occasionally make its way in, but being so far from the ocean meant the prices skyrocketed.

Given that, it's only natural that river fish are commonly eaten.

But at least for Kenny, it was undeniably true that sea fish tasted better than river fish.

Even if river fish have a mild flavor, they inevitably fall short of sea fish in terms of pure, unadulterated deliciousness.

That's precisely why Rei was naturally drawn to the river fish hot pot Rei had mentioned eating.

Kenny listened as Rei described the hot pot dish, licking his lips.

While such a gesture might often be seen as suggestive, Kenny's action now was not suggestive at all—it was purely a response to his stimulated appetite.

"Ah... wanna eat it later? I brought the whole pot with me, after all."

"Really!?"

For Kenny, the chance to try a fish hot pot he'd never eaten before... and to eat it with Rei... was incredibly exciting.

Eating hot pot in the summer wasn't exactly something he'd normally consider.

Still, sharing it with Rei made it quite enjoyable.

"Times like this, it really helps that you carry the item box, Rei."

Kenny said, and Rei nodded in agreement.

Typically, regional specialties and seasonal foods are only available in specific places and for limited periods.

But with the Misty Ring carried by Rei, they could enjoy them anytime, anywhere.

Being able to eat Gamelion meat, which only appeared in winter, fresh all year round was the prime example of this.

Of course, that would change if they ate everything stored inside the Misty Ring.

"Hearing you say that is a relief. ...Changing the subject, did anything unusual happen while I was gone?"

"Hmm. It seems there were quite a few difficulties without you, Rei, but they managed somehow. ...Though, it seems getting timber from Treant Forest is taking an awful lot of time."

"Ah... I figured."

Unlike Rei, who could fly from the Misty Ring to Gilm using Set, without him, they had no choice but to prepare a dedicated carriage and transport it with a large group.

High-rank adventurers could probably carry freshly cut logs with one hand, but Gilm simply didn't have the luxury of assigning high-rank adventurers to such tasks right now.

"But since Lady Marina is helping out with spirit magic, it's not as easy as when Rei carries it, but it is somewhat easier, right? Actually, having Lady Marina cooperate seems to be boosting the woodcutters' motivation too."

"Ah... I suppose so."

Though Rei uttered the exact same words as seconds before, the emotion behind them was entirely different.

There was a hint of exasperation mixed in.

Still, working alongside a bewitchingly beautiful woman like Marina, it made sense to Rei that the mostly male lumberjacks would be fired up.

"Oh, and... Rei, you can use earth-type magic, right?"

Seeing Rei's expression, Kenny suddenly changed the subject and asked that question.

Earth magic? Rei hesitated for a moment, but then he must have remembered that his skills were also considered magic. He nodded immediately.

Normally, Rei specialized solely in fire magic, possessing no aptitude whatsoever for other elements.

However, Death Scythe's skills were incredibly diverse.

To cover this up, Rei had decided that while not as proficient as with fire magic, he could still use other magic reasonably well. At least on the surface.

"I'm not exactly an expert, but I can manage."

"Oh, you're being modest. I hear you can control large areas of ground at will?"

Kenny's words made it immediately clear to Rei which skill she was expecting.

The terrain manipulation skill possessed by the Death Scythe.

A skill that allowed him to raise or lower the ground within a fifty-meter radius around himself by about one meter.

Even for earth-element mages, it wasn't a spell easily wielded, yet Rei could use it freely as a skill.

For mages, it wouldn't be strange to want to call Rei's existence cheating.

Regardless, despite that truth, Rei was sometimes regarded as someone who could wield earth magic on a fairly large scale.

"So, it's related to the expansion project, then?"

"Yes. Of course, it's not just Rei. We're gathering various others who can use similar magic. ...In that sense, it's rather fortunate you returned today."

"I kind of feel like it would've been better if he'd come back a little later."

Rei muttered that in response to Kenny's words.

"Don't say that. Once the wall's broken, we need to move as quickly as possible."

"Huh? Oh, so the preparations for that are finally ready?"

Until recently, various preparations had been underway—demolishing buildings near the wall, gathering materials needed for the expansion work.

And surely, there had been arguments among the many carpenters and craftsmen about how to proceed with the construction. Fortunately, while Rei was away from Gilm, those discussions must have been settled.

"Yes. ...Originally, there was a suggestion to have Rei handle the cleanup after the wall was destroyed too. But some felt relying on him too much might not be wise, and others worried it might take work away from other adventurers. So in the end, someone else will handle that cleanup."

"Oh? ...Well, I don't mind either way... Ah, but I'd like to keep the bigger wall fragments. Is that okay?"

"Huh? I think it should be fine, but I'll check with the higher-ups later. ...But what are you going to do with those?"

"They have various uses."

For Rei, who could fly, massive chunks of material—especially ones that didn't matter if they got broken—were incredibly powerful weapons.

Set always flew at an altitude of 100 meters.

What would happen if you dropped a huge wall fragment from that height...? You could call it a proper bombing raid.

Of course, the dropped wall fragments wouldn't explode, so strictly speaking, calling it a bombing might be inaccurate.

Against enemies like groups of monsters, an enemy nation's army, or even just a band of thieves, it becomes an extremely powerful and effective means of attack.

Or, something Rei excels at... no, something that has become synonymous with her: the fire whirlwind.

Mixing wall fragments into that would make it a vicious attack, tearing skin, gouging flesh, and shattering bones.

Kenny didn't seem to grasp Rei's exact thoughts, but after a moment's consideration, he nodded.

"Well, if you're willing to help out, Rei, I suppose it's fine. But since it's also part of the others' jobs, don't push yourself too hard, okay? Besides, I have something I want you to do after you break the wall."

"Ah, so basically just level the ground, right?"

"Yes. ...Of course others will help too, but when it comes to magic, it's gotta be Rei."

"Nah, you're overestimating me. I'm confident with fire magic, but honestly, I'm not that great with other kinds."

Kenny shot Rei a look of utter exasperation at her words.

Kenny had never actually seen exactly how much magic Rei could use.

But even so, from reports and rumors, it was easy to imagine what kind of thing it was.

It wasn't a joke or anything; there probably wasn't anyone who could use magic that surpassed Rei's.

That was practically accepted fact among adventurers in Gilm, though Ray himself probably didn't know it.

Then again, with more adventurers than usual flocking to Gilm these days, it wouldn't be surprising if some fool showed up who didn't know that and tried to mess with Rei.

"Anyway, I know this will place quite a burden on you, Rei, but the reward should be commensurate with it..."

Kenny started to continue, but seeing Lenora return, he sighed regretfully, realizing his alone time with Rei was over.
