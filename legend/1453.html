A tattoo of a snake coiled around an arm.

The moment Ilze saw someone with that tattoo, her mind turned crimson.

Her enemy... the one who killed her gentle parents and brother.

If she'd been in town, she might have been able to run to the guards or... if she was lucky, perhaps even the knights.

But her parents and brother, who were merchants, had been killed outside the city.

By adventurers they happened to meet.

Even now, Ilze dreams of that day.

Even though five years have passed since her parents and brother died, she is still plagued by nightmares every few days.

Unconsciously, her hand reaches for the hilt of her dagger...

"Whoa, you might want to hold off on drawing weapons inside the guild. At least for now."

The moment her hand touched the dagger's hilt, a hand rested on her shoulder, accompanied by those words.

"Huh!?"

Ilze gasped at the sudden voice from behind.

She hadn't noticed anyone was there until she was touched.

Of course, Ilze didn't possess the heightened skill to sense such a presence.

Still, Ilze, who could easily be called a beauty, had become sensitive to those near her in that regard.

If she hadn't developed that ability, she would have suffered all sorts of terrible things as a woman by now.

She spun around instinctively... yet the man's words lingered somewhere in her mind. Without drawing the dagger from its sheath, she looked at the figure.

There stood a small man wearing a hood despite the heat.

"...Who are you?"

"An adventurer, in this Gilm. ...Now, let me ask again, why did you try to draw your weapon?"

"Well..."

Ilze hesitated at the words of the man before her—his face hidden by the hood, yet his voice unmistakably male.

Seeing Ilze like this, the man let out a small sigh.

He could easily guess there was some reason behind it.

...Though even without a reason, suddenly drawing a dagger inside the guild could only be seen as dangerous behavior.

"Well, things happened. Don't worry about it."

"...Even if you say that..."

Even for the man, finding someone who suddenly drew a dagger inside the guild and then being told not to worry about it—he couldn't just nod and say "Yes, sir."

Fortunately, not many people had seen Ilze try to draw her dagger. ...Well, not many, that is.

Ilze hadn't noticed at all, but several adventurers inside the guild had seen her attempt to draw the dagger.

Ilze was fortunate that her enemy hadn't noticed her.

Anyway, Ilze now had to figure out how to cover up what just happened.

"Hey, Rei, what's up?"

A woman spoke to the man standing before Ilze.

The man... Rei... the moment he looked in the direction of the voice, Ilze swiftly spun around and dashed out of the guild.

(I'm saved.)

That was all that crossed Ilze's mind as she weaved through the crowd.

Or rather, she couldn't think of anything else...

She had found her enemy, yet she was relieved she hadn't been caught in front of them.

...If she had only drawn her dagger inside the guild, she probably wouldn't have been arrested or anything, but unfortunately, Ilze likely didn't understand the circumstances surrounding that.

Anyway, she needed to get away from the guild right now. Thinking that, she pushed through the crowd.

Eventually, she arrived at a place where there weren't many people.

Since she hated crowds, it was only natural... but that meant the place Ilze was now was one of the quieter spots in the current Gilm.

Naturally, such places attracted those who couldn't easily show their faces on the main streets...

"Hey, sis. What's your deal hanging out in a place like this? Nah, forget that. Just hang with me for a bit."

"Whoa! You sure move fast when you're hitting on someone, bro."

"Heh heh, right? Listen, it's like this: speed is everything, no matter what you're doing. Just getting moving fast carries huge weight. That's how I'll make this girl mine."

"...No way that's true."

The man declared it proudly, as if bragging to the guy beside him, but a calm voice cut through the air.

For a man in the middle of stating his personal theory, being suddenly interrupted was naturally unpleasant.

Especially when it was something that contradicted his argument.

"What the hell?!"

His good mood disrupted, the man turned his gaze toward the source of the voice and froze.

Ilze, who had followed the man's gaze in the same direction, also froze.

Ilze understood that she possessed a face of considerable value to men.

She certainly didn't have the arrogance to think she was the most beautiful woman in the world, but she did understand, as an objective opinion, that she was beautiful.

But... the person now in the line of Ilze's gaze was so stunningly beautiful that it made her feel ashamed for even thinking such a thing about herself.

"Huh?"

A sound of incomprehension slipped from Ilze's lips.

Her beauty was not calm, but rather active... and yet, dazzling.

She wore a sheer garment so thin it seemed almost transparent, and at first glance, she looked like nothing more than a dancer or a courtesan.

Moreover, her figure embodied such femininity that even Ilze found herself involuntarily swallowing hard.

Regardless of her facial features, for Ilze, who lacked confidence in her own bust size, this woman embodied the very definition of perfection.

Though her gaze lingered on the woman who had apparently saved her just seconds ago, Ilze snapped back to reality.

These were the men who had aggressively approached her.

If that was the case, wouldn't harm also come to this woman, who was clearly more beautiful than herself?

Yet, despite the woman having appeared several dozen seconds ago, the men showed no sign of aggressively pursuing her.

Why? Wondering, Ilze looked at the men and saw, for some reason, that they were trembling.

"Huh?"

The same voice escaped Ilze's lips again.

But the intent and emotion behind it were vastly different from before.

She had assumed they would treat the newly arrived woman the same way they had treated her—forcefully. Her expectation was completely wrong.

Because the men were trembling at the sight of the newly arrived woman.

Why? Ilze wondered.

If Ilze possessed even a modicum of ability to gauge an opponent's strength, she might have understood why the men were trembling.

Unfortunately, Ilze's combat talent was lacking, and she couldn't possibly grasp the immense power the figure before her possessed.

"W-w-what... Ms. Vihera? What are you doing in a place like this?"

"I saw that girl over there running this way. I thought maybe... and sure enough."

The men, met with a gaze tinged with amusement, couldn't focus their attention on either Vihera's beauty or her provocatively revealing attire. They could only tremble.

These men, too, had come to Gilm seeking work on the expansion.

But they hated the physical labor and had run away, often gathering around here near the slums now.

Precisely because of that, they knew Vihera well—she patrolled Gilm as an auxiliary guard. They had seen others blinded by her beauty, falling disgracefully—though not dead.

With such an opponent standing before them, it was no wonder the men trembled.

Vihera was indeed a flower of the highest caliber, but naturally, such a beautiful flower possessed thorns for self-defense.

Moreover, these thorns weren't merely defensive; they possessed a high degree of aggressiveness, actively seeking out their prey to strike.

Given that, only someone extremely confident in their own abilities would dare touch that flower.

And these men here, even if they were adventurers, were the sort who had run away from tough jobs.

Fighting the Vihera? There was no chance of winning.

The best they could do was try to resolve things peacefully.

"No, no, that's not it at all. I just tried to stop this young lady from going any further, since she'd end up in the slums."

The man said this with a smile.

Vihera watched him, then let out a small sigh before turning her gaze to Ilze.

"What do you want to do? If you want to make an issue of this, I'll go with you to the guard post."

"N-no. That won't be necessary."

As someone contemplating revenge, she wanted to avoid any involvement with the guards if possible.

Though he probably didn't understand Ilze's feelings, Vihera shifted his gaze from Ilze to the men and spoke.

"Well, that's good. No problem... But if something does become a problem, and I find out about it... You know what will happen, right?"

The men nodded frantically in response to Vihera's question, delivered with a broad smile.

Vihera understood there was such a vast difference in strength between them that fighting back was beyond their wildest thoughts.

"Now, let's leave this place. This isn't somewhere someone like you should be."

"Um... yes."

Honestly, Ilze didn't feel like going back into the crowd, but after Vihera had shown her this much concern, she couldn't refuse.

In the end, Ilze let Vihera lead her back toward the main street.

"Hmm?"

"Ah, sorry to keep you waiting."

Emerging from the passageway where Ilze and the others had been, a figure who could only be called a child was waiting for Vihera and Ilze.

Well, to be precise, only Vihera was waiting.

"Um...?"

"Ah, don't mind it."

Saying this, Vihera shot a disbelieving look at the child—Byune—stuffing dried meat into her mouth.

"Byune, how can you eat dried meat in this heat? Unless it's wine, I couldn't possibly bring myself to eat something like that."

"Hmph!"

Ilse, listening nearby, couldn't grasp the meaning behind that remark.

But that was only Ilse. Vihera understood perfectly well the meaning hidden within that single remark.

"Don't say that. Most people lose their appetite in summer, if you think about it."

"Um... You're Vihera, right? Can you understand what that girl is saying?"

Ilze asked Vihera, who was engaged in an exchange she couldn't comprehend, as if she couldn't help herself.

Vihera responded to Ilze with a smile and a nod.

"Yes. You might not understand normally, but after spending time together, you just start to get it somehow."

"...Is that how it works?"

By any normal standard, it was incredibly hard to believe.

But seeing it unfold before her eyes, she had no choice but to believe it.

"Anyway... what are you going to do now? From the looks of it, you're an adventurer... right?"

Vihera asked, guessing Ilze's identity from the leather armor made from monster hides and the dagger at her waist.

Ilze didn't particularly hide the fact that she was an adventurer, so she answered honestly.

"Yes. Though I am a lowly Rank F adventurer."

"...I'm surprised someone of that rank even thought to come to Gilm."

"Yes. The person I'm looking for is in Gilm..."

At Vihera's words, Ilze almost blurted out a response, but she caught herself, realizing what she was about to say, and her words caught in her throat.

Ilze was fortunate she'd used the phrase "someone I'm looking for" instead of "my enemy" or "my avenger." And above all, that Vihera didn't dwell on the details.

"...Hmm. Well, Gilm's pretty crowded these days, so I doubt you'll find whoever you're looking for that easily. Want to try posting a request at the guild or something?"

Vihera sensed there was more to the story, but didn't dwell on it, asking casually.

But of course, Ilze shook her head at Vihera's suggestion.

Because she had already found the person she was looking for... her enemy.

She didn't want to draw any attention, however slight, to herself here.

"Is that so? ...But if you cause any trouble in Gilm, I'll be the one to deal with you. Don't forget that."

Driving home that final sharp warning, Vihera departed from Ilze's presence alongside Byune.

Ilze could only watch their retreating figures in silence.

Unconsciously trembling at Vihera's gaze.
