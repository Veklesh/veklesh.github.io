As the setting sun dyed the highway red, Rei and the others departed Mejogo, heading first for Rossi.

That said, while they were heading for Rossi, they had no intention of actually entering the city.

...If nearly a thousand people tried to enter Rossi, the paperwork alone would undoubtedly cause a huge commotion.

To begin with, most of this group had been forcibly brought to this land by Jaya.

Naturally, few possessed identification like guild cards.

Even if they did have guild cards or similar documents, entering Rossi would not have been an option.

Rossi is the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina, and the kingdom's upper echelons were connected to Jaya.

That connection is now in the past tense, effective as of today. Had the Reburina upper echelons learned of Rei and the others, they would have sought to exploit them somehow... or, realizing they were known, would likely have silenced them.

Of course, Rei had no intention of letting them pull such a stunt, and if they tried, he intended to fight back.

But the truth was, if he hadn't gone to Rossi in the first place, such a situation would never have arisen.

Given that, the option for Rei to deliberately side with Rossi in a situation like this simply didn't exist.

"Hey, I'm tired! Give me a break!"

As they walked, that voice reached their ears.

Despite having departed Mejogo less than an hour ago, that voice had already reached them.

But... even hearing that voice, Rei didn't stop walking.

He understood that complaining like that after barely walking at all stemmed from being raised to be spoiled.

Of course, if someone was actually injured or sick and unable to move, Rei would have considered what to do.

But when Rei glanced at Sura and saw her shake her head, he felt certain he didn't need to stop.

"Hey, cut it out. We've barely walked at all. So why are you already talking about resting? Do you even understand what our situation is?"

A nearby Resistance man addressed the woman who was making a fuss.

But another woman near her retorted.

"What? We were living peacefully until the people of Reburina forced us to come here. So it's only fair that you Reburina people work for us now, right?"

"Listen... then let me ask you this: if the big shots in the country you lived in messed up, would you take responsibility for it?"

The resistance man muttered in exasperation, but the woman just looked at him with a puzzled, dissatisfied expression.

"Huh? What are you talking about? Of course not!"

"...Then that's your answer."

Hearing the resistance man's words, another woman nearby voiced her dissatisfaction again.

"What do you mean, 'that's the answer'? Anyway, you helped us, so you should take responsibility until the end, right?"

"...Hey, Sura. What about these people? Taking them along like this is impossible!"

Hearing that voice, Sura turned her gaze to Rei, asking what to do.

Rei was effectively the leader of this group.

They relied on Rei for supplies like food and water, and it was Rei who arranged for them to be accepted at Gilm.

Given that, it was only natural to prioritize Rei's opinion when making decisions.

"Don't worry about them. From here, getting back to Mejogo shouldn't be too hard. Just leave them behind."

But those were the words that came out of Rei's mouth.

Surprised by those words were Sura, but more so the women who had voiced their discontent.

They never imagined they'd be dumped like this all of a sudden.

"Hey, wait a minute! Why are you suddenly doing this?! We're the victims here!"

Ignoring the frantic words of the woman, Rei addressed the man who had been arguing with her.

"Get rid of the guys around that woman. We don't need them."

"Wait, wait, wait! Why are you doing this all of a sudden?!"

"Huh? Hey, are you sure about this?"

The Resistance man looked confused, but Rei nodded... only then did he turn his gaze to the woman and speak.

"Since you seem to have misunderstood, let me make this clear: I didn't ask this group to come to Gilm. I'm bringing them here precisely because I think, if they have nowhere to go, nowhere to earn a living, then Gilm can take them in."

Since he spoke while walking, no one stopped in their tracks.

In truth, Rei's voice didn't carry very far, so only those near the front where she stood could actually hear her.

"If you're whining about something this trivial, walking all the way to Gilm is out of the question. If you stick with us, you'll just cause trouble for everyone with your selfish actions. Go back to Mejogo and live there. At least in Mejogo, you won't have to worry about housing or anything."

Of course, if you stay in Mejogo, there's no telling how the people of Jaya would treat you if they found you. The same goes for if the army of the Kingdom of Reburina found you.

Leaving that part unsaid, Rei continued speaking to the women who were giving him fawning looks.

"Keeping you around forever isn't exactly fun for me either. So you don't need to come with us. Got it? Then get back there already."

"H-hold on! I was joking! I wasn't serious!"

From Rei's expression, they must have realized he was dead serious.

The woman who had first complained of exhaustion hurriedly protested.

Rei hesitated, looking at the woman who had spoken and the others around her.

To be honest, he felt like taking them along would probably lead to trouble.

As long as Rei was there, it would be fine.

But once Rei was gone, it was certain they'd start acting selfish again.

Still, this group had no leeway to indulge such whims.

Even if the women tried to use their bodies to lure men in, with over ninety percent of the group being women, there were very few potential partners.

...However, precisely because of that, some do think that if they could win over a man, they might get a sweet deal.

But for the women who'd been forced into prostitution, most felt disgust at the idea of using their bodies to win over men.

(Ideally, I should eliminate them here... but if I suddenly wields force now, the others might become intimidated later. If that happens, things might be fine while I'm here, but when I'm gone, it could get messy.)

After a moment's thought, Rei turned his gaze to Sura.

"I'll leave this decision to you. When I'm not here, Sura will be leading this group, so do it however you think best."

In the end, he decided to leave it to Sura.

Part of it was that he didn't want to deal with a troublesome woman, but more importantly, considering what would happen when he wasn't around, he thought it best to leave it to Rei's judgment.

Besides, when it came to managing a group, Sura, who had led the Resistance, was actually more experienced than Rei.

"Huh? Wait, me? Um... well..."

Suddenly addressed, Sura looked at the women in surprise.

The women, too, knew that depending on Sura's decision, they could be cut loose right here, so they couldn't afford to speak carelessly.

Sura voiced her dissatisfaction and turned her gaze to the women who agreed with her.

Age-wise, they were probably the same age... or perhaps Sura was younger.

Even so, it was clear Sura held the power in this situation.

Knowing this, the women couldn't afford to speak carelessly.

Eventually, Sura opened her mouth, as if lecturing them.

"I'll let it slide this time, but if any of you act selfish from now on, you'll be asked to leave. Of course, if you don't like that, you're free to leave me behind at any village or town we stop at along the way. Understood?"

The women silently nodded at Sura's words, which sounded like an ultimatum.

Truthfully, even Sura wondered if this was too harsh for an ultimatum.

But if she compromised here and now, wouldn't more people start causing similar disturbances later, forcing her to compromise each time?

That thought was what made her voice those words as an ultimatum.

"Is this acceptable?"

"It should be fine. Those with complaints will likely leave during the journey."

Though the group currently numbered nearly a thousand, Rei predicted that upon reaching Gilm, they wouldn't arrive at half that number... but still, around seven hundred would likely remain.

Those who had homes to return to would naturally part ways with Rei and the others before reaching Gilm.

It wouldn't be surprising if some grew fond of the villages or towns they passed through and decided to settle there.

Some might simply grow dissatisfied with Rei and Sura leading the group and leave.

Some might fall ill or get injured... and above all, the possibility of being attacked by bandits is by no means low.

As for monsters and animals, there's no worry of attack now that Set is with us.

But to bandits, this group is nothing but prime prey.

If they were protecting such a large number with a few guards, it would be impossible.

(If possible, it would be better to leave the Kingdom of Reburina and hire adventurer escorts in some village or town. No, villages have few adventurers, and their quality might not be that high?)

Watching Sura, Rei contemplated what lay ahead.

Where exactly is the envoy group that left before Rei and the others departed from Gilm?

Knowing that would likely reveal where they might have converged... but that was difficult.

If it were the Daskar in Gilm, they could use the Paired Orb to contact them, but that was an exception.

Ultimately, Rei and the others would have to find the envoy group and make contact themselves.

(If I could just know exactly how far they've come, I could pinpoint their approximate location...)

The envoy group doesn't need to take routes hidden from the Kingdom of Reburina; they can travel openly along the highways.

Of course, some delegations might prefer to avoid attention in certain situations, but that absolutely does not apply this time.

They can visit the Kingdom of Reburina openly and honestly.

Given that, if they followed the highway, Rei and the others should naturally be able to meet up with the delegation. ...That is, assuming the delegation hadn't made any strange detours.

Given that Daskar had personally dispatched this delegation, Rei couldn't imagine them doing anything like that.

"Grr."

While Rei pondered how they would meet up, Set, walking beside him, growled.

Following Set's gaze, they saw a red forest illuminated by the setting sun, appearing as if it were burning.

But from the sound of his growl, Rei understood it wasn't the sunset-drenched forest that had made Set growl.

"Enemy?"

Observing Rei and Set, Vihera, who was walking nearby, asked Rei.

"Yes. The problem is, which faction is it?"

"Possibilities are either pursuers from Jaya or the army of the Kingdom of Reburina."

"Surprisingly, it might even be bandits unrelated to either side."

Rei stated this, though he himself didn't believe it to be true.

After all, Rei had been attacked before by bandits not under Jaya's influence, so it wasn't entirely impossible.

But that incident itself was an anomaly. Such anomalies are called anomalies precisely because they're extremely rare.

"So? What are we gonna do? Wipe them out? Burn the place to the ground?"

"Look... why are you steering this conversation toward such violent solutions?"

Even as he said this, Rei did wonder if annihilation might be the quickest solution.

But using magic wasn't an option for Rei, since his specialty, fire magic, carried the risk of spreading the flames.

"Oh? But isn't it better if as little information as possible gets to them?"

"I won't deny that. ...Alright then. We'll go near that grove. If they still haven't moved, I'll leave the annihilation to you. But first, make sure to confirm which faction they belong to. There's a chance they're just hiding, startled by us."

Objectively speaking, it was a group of nearly a thousand, composed almost entirely of women.

And at their head was Set the Griffin.

If they knew nothing, it wouldn't be strange for them to judge it a dangerous group and take refuge in the nearby woods.
