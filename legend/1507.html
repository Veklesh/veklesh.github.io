The night they left Gilm... Rei and the others were naturally sleeping outdoors.

Though technically camping, the magic tent meant they could sleep in a room with a ceiling.

For dinner, Rei pulled freshly baked bread from the Misty Ring, along with hot stew, simmered dishes, vegetable salad, and bacon made from oak meat skewered on branches and roasted over the campfire—a hearty meal.

Gathered around the campfire, Rei and the others ate such food while chatting about various things.

"Hmm, being cooped up in that cage for so long really takes a toll on you physically, doesn't it?"

Elena felt tired despite not moving her body, but that fatigue was likely mental rather than physical.

Elena placed the bread she was holding on a plate and stretched her arms wide.

The others nodded in agreement with Elena.

It seemed sitting in that cage all day was indeed quite taxing in many ways.

Though Byune, perhaps because she'd been napping on Marina's lap, didn't seem particularly tired and was thoroughly enjoying the meal.

"Hang in there a little longer. We should reach the Kingdom of Reburina in just a few more days."

"...It takes over a month by carriage, but only a few days by Set, huh?"

"Grr?"

Marina's murmured voice of admiration must have reached him.

While eating a chunk of oak meat—probably over five kilograms, not raw but thoroughly wrapped in large leaves and steamed with herbs—Set looked at Marina and growled, "What?"

Seeing Set happily eating meat alongside Yellow, Marina shook her head, signaling it was nothing.

Seeing Marina like that, Set looked puzzled but returned his attention to his meal.

The sight of Set and Yellow eating their meal with such gusto had a soothing effect on anyone watching.

Considering Set was actually three meters long, one wouldn't normally expect to be able to watch him so closely without any worry like this.

The fact that she felt it was okay showed just how much trust she had in Set.

As they all ate together, they discussed various things about today.

The topic that drew the most attention was likely the river they had descended to for lunch.

Being a summer river, it was a very cool place, and fish were swimming in it.

Seeing summer river fish, Rei briefly wondered, "Ayu?" But it wasn't any of the typical river fish Rei knew—ayu, yamame, or iwana—it was a fish he'd never seen before.

Though, Set swiftly caught the river fish with his beak, swallowing them whole and devouring them in one gulp.

"It's a luxury to eat by a cool river precisely because it's so hot,"

No one could argue with Marina's words.

Indeed, lunch in this setting—the sound of flowing water, branches swaying in the breeze, dappled sunlight softening the summer sun—must have been a delightful break for Elena and the others, who had nothing to do during their journey.

Inside the cage, the women chatted among themselves, Marina used spirit magic to scout the surroundings, and Elena communicated with Ara using their Paired Orbs. Still, moving their bodies—in the sense of physical exercise—was fundamentally difficult.

"Well, given the size of the cage, there's not much we can do about it. ...Like Rei said at noon, it would be great if we could put a magic tent inside the cage."

"I understand what Vihera is saying, but..."

Saying this, Rei glanced toward the magic tent set up a short distance away.

"The magic tent is incredibly important for us as adventurers. Using it for the Mirage Cage... Well, if I could carry the Mirage Cage around normally, that might be fine."

Currently, the Mirage Cage could only blend into its surroundings using magic stones, but Daskar and Amaros had promised to modify it after this incident concluded.

Therefore, expanding it to fit the Magic Tent and enabling the Mirage Cage to be powered by magic instead of magic stones was practically a done deal.

(Like a wall around a house, the mirage cage would surround the magic tent. ...No, in this case, "enclose the magic tent within the mirage cage" might be a more accurate description?)

With the previous Magic Tent, it was immediately obvious from the outside that it was a tent.

But using the Mirage Cage would make it blend into the surrounding scenery, eliminating the worry of being spotted by monsters or thieves while camping.

Furthermore, while the mirage cage's flaw was that it matched the colors of the surrounding scenery from one minute prior, using it as a barrier for the magic tent eliminates the need for movement, thus solving that issue.

...Though, there's still a good chance we could be spotted by a campfire. And even if we were found, as long as Set is keeping watch around the Magic Tent, there's really no practical problem.

"If possible, I'd like another Magic Tent for the Mirage Cage. No, it doesn't need to be as well-equipped as the one Rei has. Just about the size of an empty room would be fine."

"Right. The main complaint is not being able to move around, so if there's nothing in it, we could just bring in furniture ourselves..."

Elena said this, but her expression didn't quite match the hope in her words.

Magic tents themselves were extremely rare items.

Of course, the Misty Ring is incomparable to Rei's Misty Ring... item box, but even so, crafting a magic tent is technically extremely difficult.

It wasn't just about having magic power; it required precise control over that power.

Even the slightest mistake meant not just failing to create the desired Magic Tent... but worse, it could potentially explode.

Given the need for such precise magical control, it was only natural that mass production wasn't feasible.

Since it wasn't something you could simply buy with money, even Rei and his group couldn't acquire it easily.

Using forceful means—like brute strength, pressuring the person or those around them... or outright theft—would solve the problem. But while thieves or enemies might resort to such tactics, there was no way they could use them on someone who wasn't an enemy.

Doing something like that would only make Rei and his crew look like bounty targets.

"So, I guess we'll just have to keep searching diligently for that kind of magic item, or find someone willing to part with one... Ajax's organization has made significant progress in magic item research, so maybe we should pin our hopes on them."

Rei's mutter was agreed to by the others.

The primary objective of this mission was to settle the score with the organization that had meddled with Gilm.

Naturally, that would deal a significant blow to the organization.

One reason Rei accepted this request was his strong interest in the magic items the organization possessed.

Even if they were cheap items with quite limited effects, they had prepared such a large quantity of slave collars.

It seemed certain that the organization possessed many magic items.

If that's the case, it wouldn't be surprising if they also possessed magic items like a Magic Tent.

"Right. Having magic items like that would be a big help to us."

Marina said with a smile.

While part of her felt relieved that they might have a magic item, Rei could also sense that she derived equal, if not greater, pleasure from the prospect of attacking an organization that had been meddling with Gilm.

Even though she had now relinquished the guildmaster position, Marina had long served as Gilm's guildmaster.

Because of that, her feelings toward Gilm were exceptionally strong.

What's more, Ajax and his group—or more accurately, the organization Ajax had been dealing with—had deemed even adventurers worthy of abduction. In fact, numerous adventurers had already had their freedom stolen, forced into slavery with collars around their necks.

Naturally, Marina was not pleased by this.

Watching Marina's reaction, Rei spoke up without seeming particularly concerned.

"If we could convert it into a surface ship, we wouldn't need magic tents or anything like that."

"...Well, that's..."

Vihera responded with a wry smile at Rei's words.

Of course, actually researching the sand ship to build a land vessel would undoubtedly be a project spanning several years.

And since success wasn't guaranteed, everyone—including Rei, who had brought up the land ship in the first place—agreed it was best to think of it as a lucky bonus if it happened.

As they continued discussing the Mirage Cage, Elena suddenly noticed something.

"Excuse me, it seems Ara is trying to contact us."

Saying this, Elena took out the Paired Orb.

'My Lady Elena, are you well? It seems you've been flying all day today. Is everything alright?'

"Ara, there's no need to worry that much. I'm fine, so please don't fret. Though, if possible, I wish the Mirage Cage were a bit more spacious."

'Understood. I'll relay that to Amaros.'

It was precisely because she had the Paired Orbs that she could immediately report any malfunctions or issues she noticed with the Mirage Cage.

But Elena shook her head at Ara's words.

"No, Amaros is busy with various things right now. You understand that too, don't you, Ara?"

"That... yes."

Even Ara could see the dark circles under Amaros's eyes; he was clearly exhausted.

If asked whether it was necessary to push such an Amaros now, she would say no.

Besides, even if they found a place to repair the Mirage Cage, the actual object was currently right here with Rei and the others.

Therefore, it was only natural that he should focus on the expansion work for now.

"So, just asking... anything unusual happen in Gilm today?"

'Nothing particularly noteworthy. The biggest talk was about Set carrying the Mirage Cage that Elena and the others rode in.'

"...That part was as expected, I suppose."

Of course, Elena knew there were various minor disturbances happening in Gilm too.

In fact, it was absolutely impossible for a day to pass without any problems at all in Gilm right now, with the expansion work underway.

Still, it was probably the fact that they... or more accurately, that Set carried the Mirage Cage that caused such a big stir.

To someone unaware of the situation, seeing Set carry something as enormous as a mirage cage would be impossible not to be startled by.

And if that mirage cage changes color to match its surroundings... if even bigger disturbances happen frequently, that would be a problem in itself.

Then again, this is the frontier town of Gilm. If you asked whether something bigger than that incident could absolutely never happen here, the answer would be no.

"Yes. But what about Elena-sama?"

"What do you mean, 'how'? As I said earlier, no particular problems have arisen. If anything, wouldn't the problems be more likely to occur over there? Even if it's Vihera's room, it's still an inn at the end of the day. Compared to Marina's house, I think it's bound to be more prone to various issues."

"Ah... yes, you're right."

Hearing Elena's words, Ara's voice trailed off.

That was likely proof someone had already come knocking on Ara's door.

Of course, anyone foolish enough to underestimate Ara as a woman and resort to force would surely regret it.

After all, the position of Knight Commander in Elena's guard wasn't held for show or anything like that; it was purely earned through genuine skill.

Elena seemed to understand this too. With a slightly exasperated look, she spoke.

"Now, now, don't get too rowdy."

"...Just to be sure, you didn't break anything in the room, did you?"

Since she was lending her own room, Vihera was understandably concerned about that.

For this mission, they'd brought all the necessary belongings for everyone inside Rei's Misty Ring, but that wasn't everything.

Items not immediately needed were left at places like the Dusk Wheat inn or Marina's house, so she was probably worried about whether those had been damaged.

"Ah, that's fine. No problem."

Vihera shot Ara a somewhat skeptical look as she said this.

In any case, the first night after leaving Gilm passed in this lively manner.
