After deciding to return to Gilm, Rei moved quickly.

Of course, since Kathy said she'd throw him a farewell party, he couldn't leave right away today.

What Rei was doing now was preparing to leave tomorrow.

"...Well, 'preparations' is a bit of an overstatement, really."

He hadn't even stayed at this mansion for half a month.

Given that, there wasn't much luggage to pack.

Just like at the Dusk Wheat inn, where Rei usually stays, he had the incredibly convenient magic item called Misty Ring.

That's precisely why, even items and tools that would normally be scattered about, weren't left anywhere in the room he was borrowing.

"Now then, how to kill time until nightfall. ...Should I have stayed in the village a little longer?"

Rei pondered, but since he'd abruptly decided to return to Gilm after seeing the village's state, going back now felt somehow off.

"I was kinda interested in that artifact that enveloped the village. Seems it broke, but maybe bringing back some debris would make someone very happy to study it."

Laying it all out, the reason he'd come to Gosh in the first place was because of the Twilight Spear that someone had made... Thinking that, and since he had nothing else to do anyway, Rei decided to practice his two-spear style.

"Haah!"

With that cry, the Twilight Spear held in his left hand sliced through the air.

Compared to a thrust delivered with both hands, its power was weaker, but that was only relative to Rei's own two-handed thrust.

Compared to an ordinary thrust... indeed, compared to the thrusts of any adventurer in Gosh, it was no less sharp... no, it possessed even greater sharpness.

He still couldn't coordinate the thrust with the Death Scythe in his right hand, but he felt confident that continued practice would eventually make it possible.

With that sense of satisfaction, he looked up at the sky. There lay a crimson-hued sky.

It was a sight truly worthy of being called a sunset.

"...Huh? What?"

Looking around in confusion, I saw Set lying on the ground a short distance away.

We'd decided to practice the Two-Spear Style, so I'd let Set lie down in the stable like before and trained him that way...

"Evening, you say?"

He muttered involuntarily.

Of course. Originally, Rei had decided to return to Gilm and come back to Augusto's mansion. He hadn't taken out his pocket watch, so he didn't know the exact time, but it was still around ten in the morning.

After talking with Augusto and the others, he returned to his room. Finding nothing else to do, he started practicing the Two-Spear Style. It hadn't been that long since he'd returned to Augusto's mansion.

And then... Rei pulled his pocket watch from his Misty Ring and looked at it. The hands showed it was already close to six in the afternoon.

Rei had no memory of eating lunch, nor, of course, of taking any sort of break.

He must have been so completely absorbed in his Nisoryu training that he lost track of time.

Realizing several hours had passed, his stomach naturally protested its hunger, and his throat demanded water.

He decided it would be better to skip eating anything now, since there was a farewell party planned for the evening, but he couldn't stand the thirst.

Rei's body was essentially the crystallization of the Zepairu clan's magical technology, but as a living being, replenishing fluids was essential.

(Wait, could she actually manage without drinking water?)

The thought flashed through his mind for an instant, but he had no intention of enduring his thirst just to find out.

Besides, he'd be drinking something at the farewell party tonight anyway.

He channeled magic into the flowing water dagger he'd taken from the Misty Ring, catching the water flowing from it directly in his mouth and drinking it down.

...If anyone had seen this scene, it would have looked like Rei was trying to swallow the dagger and commit suicide.

Still, this was near the stables within Augusto's mansion, and since only Augusto and Kathy lived here, it was extremely rare for anyone to come by.

Right. Rarely, yes, but that didn't mean absolutely no one would come.

"Yaaah! Rei, what are you doing!?"

And if that someone was Cathy, coming to call Rei for the party about to begin, it was obvious what her reaction would be upon seeing him trying to put the dagger's tip into his mouth.

"Gah!? Guh-guh-guh..."

Startled by the sudden shout, he nearly spit out the water in his mouth, but managed to swallow it down.

"Haah, haah, haah... I thought I was gonna die."

"...Oh?"

Seeing water dripping from the dagger tip she'd pulled from her mouth, Kathy finally realized it was the Waterfall Dagger.

Covering her mouth, she could only manage a wry smile at her own actions.

"Um, Rei. Are you okay? Did I interrupt something important?"

Rei waved her hand lightly at Kathy, who approached cautiously.

After all, it was clear that even if the dagger's tip had cut inside her mouth, the wound wouldn't be that deep.

"No problem. I was just a bit thirsty and drinking some water."

"Oh? Well, that's a relief..."

Her relief at Rei's words lasted only a moment. The next instant, Kathy looked at Rei with a gaze that was somehow accusatory.

...Not accusatory, but a completely accusatory gaze.

Kathy had once drunk water from the flowing dagger.

She could never forget the taste from that time—a flavor beyond compare, like heavenly nectar, something unimaginable for mere water.

That very reason made it unacceptable to her that Ray was now drinking the water from the flowing blade so carelessly.

Of course, that was purely Cathy's perception.

For Rei, the water flowing from the Sword of Flowing Water was something he could produce endlessly.

That's precisely why he was using it like water from any ordinary tap... relying on the sense he'd developed back in Japan.

"Well, that magic item belongs to Rei anyway, so I won't push it. Anyway, the party preparations are almost done. Could you come over?"

"Ah, yeah, got it."

Kathy seemed to decide that saying anything more to Rei about the Water-Flowing Dagger would be pointless, settling for just a small sigh.

...Though it might also be because lingering here any longer would just let the food get cold.

Also, since Rei and Ray would be parting ways tomorrow, she didn't want to waste time arguing here.

"Grrr..."

Set let out a slightly lonely purr at Rei.

He envied Rei, who was about to go to the party, and felt lonely at the thought of being left behind here alone while everyone else enjoyed themselves.

Even without words, that feeling radiated from Set.

"Why are you looking so sad? This party is a farewell party for Rei and Set-chan, so of course you're coming too, right?"

"Gururu!?"

Really!? Set let out a delighted growl.

Seeing Set like that, Kathy smiled and nodded.

"Yes, of course. You're an honored guest in our home, aren't you? Since you came with Rei, we wouldn't leave you out. Now, shall we go?"

Set happily started walking toward the smiling Kathy.

...And Rei felt gratitude toward Kathy for looking out for Set like that.

"Grrr!"

Set, who had walked a little ahead, growled at Rei, who hadn't moved.

What's wrong? Come on, let's hurry up! His expression seemed almost like a child delighted by a fun event.

...Then again, considering how old Set was since birth, that might not be so surprising.

Seeing Set like this, Kathy smiled and returned to the mansion with him.

Rei followed Cathy's lead and headed inside the mansion as well.

Rei thought they were heading to the living room as usual, but Kathy was actually going to the dining hall.

Of course, a mansion this size naturally had a dining room.

However, since only Kathy and Augusto lived in the mansion, they usually used the living room instead of bothering with the dining room.

"Huh? Not the living room, but that way?"

"Yes. The living room might be a bit cramped for you, Set-chan?"

"...Well, I suppose..."

Considering Set's size, he could probably fit in the living room.

But the living room itself isn't that spacious.

Given that, even though we're having a farewell party, we wouldn't be able to enjoy it fully.

To avoid that, they probably prepared the dining room so that even with Set, who's over two meters long, everyone could enjoy themselves.

"Grrr?"

Unaware that he'd caused trouble for Kathy, Set tilted his head slightly, his round eyes turning to Rei and Kathy as if asking, "What's wrong?"

Seeing Set like that, Rei tried to apologize to Kathy... but seeing Kathy's expression, he instantly realized that's not what she wanted.

"Thank you."

"Hehe, it's fine. I prepared all sorts of dishes, so you two absolutely must enjoy them to your heart's content."

Whether it was out of consideration for Rei, or simply because she naturally regarded Set as family...

Either way, as they walked down the hallway, Rei stroked Set's head and smiled.

Seeing Rei like that, Kathy also smiled... and so they arrived at the dining hall.

Even before opening the door, Rei—and Set, whose sense of smell was sharper than Rei's—caught the mouthwatering aroma wafting from inside the dining hall.

Having skipped lunch to practice the Niyari-ryu style, Rei was especially hungry.

Wiping away only a light sweat, the fact that he didn't smell sweaty was fortunate in that it wouldn't interfere with the aroma of the food.

"Welcome to the party venue!"

As the door opened with those words, an even more potent, appetite-whetting aroma drifted out than before.

True to Gosh's style, the dishes were generously seasoned with spices.

It was impossible to believe these lavish dishes had been planned and prepared only after deciding to hold the party today.

Even if daily life was frugal, he never spared expense on cooking.

Considering Kathy's culinary skills, these dishes were undoubtedly masterpieces.

"Hey, you're here."

"I know I'm the one throwing this party, but are you really leaving already? I think you could stay a little longer."

Waiting inside the room were Augusto and Günter.

Normally, Günter wouldn't have wanted to attend such a party, but when Augusto insisted he participate, he couldn't refuse.

Augusto would have liked to invite Zarust too, but since he was at the Village of the Hundred Faces, there was nothing to be done.

Well, if Set wanted to, he could fly through the sky and bring him here in minutes.

Augusto understood perfectly well from years of association that Zarust wouldn't abandon his work like that.

Daldora and her guards would probably come if summoned.

But his alliance with Daldora was strictly temporary, aimed solely at dealing with the Hundred-Faced One.

Once this matter was settled, Daldora would naturally intensify her interference with Augusto again.

With such an ally, enjoying the party was simply impossible.

Rei leaving meant they might never meet again.

Of course, if I truly wanted to see him, I could go to Gilm and find him.

But such a thing was far from easy.

For Set, coming to Gosh wouldn't be difficult, but considering Rei's capabilities as a person, that too would be difficult.

(That's precisely why Kathy is holding back her tears and trying to smile as she says goodbye.)

She let out a small sigh and quickly changed her mind.

He handed the cup sitting on the table to Rei.

He'd heard she didn't drink alcohol, so the cup contained tea instead.

Set had soup prepared instead of a drink, while Augusto, Kathy, and Günter each took an alcoholic beverage.

And, naturally, it was Augusto, the master of this house, who spoke up on behalf of those present.

Looking just a little... just a tiny bit lonely, he raised his glass and spoke.

"Then, to Rei and Set's new journey, and to the prosperity of everyone here at this party... Cheers!"

"Cheers!"

With those words, everyone raised their glasses and clinked them lightly.

...After drinking his tea, Rei immediately reached for the food.

Set, not to be outdone, savored his own dish.

Amidst this, they shared brief memories of each other... And so, Rei's final night in Gosh passed pleasantly, yet somehow tinged with a certain melancholy.
