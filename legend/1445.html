"Terrain Manipulation!"

As he shouted, the ground where he planned to build the wall sank about a meter.

"Good work. You used quite a bit of magic there—are you okay?"

The craftsman in charge of the area where Rei was currently using his Terrain Manipulation skill—a tanuki beastman—said this as he handed Rei a glass filled with water.

While Rei had ice-cold fruit water, regular water, and even a dagger made of flowing water inside his Misty Ring, he accepted the cup anyway, appreciating the gesture, and took a sip to wet his throat...

"Hm?"

He flashed a slight look of surprise at the water's coldness.

It wasn't as cold as the fruit water stored in his Misty Ring, but it was still sufficiently chilled.

It was closer to the chill of a mountain stream, you might say.

At the very least, it felt far too cold to have been prepared under this blazing sun.

"What's this?"

"Heh heh, surprised? Actually, they prepared a magic item for us up above. Probably to make our work a bit more comfortable and hurry up the wall construction. Well, it's also to make sure we don't get worn down by this heat, I suppose."

As he spoke, the tanuki beastman gazed up at the sky... a cloudless, boundlessly high blue expanse.

Against that very blue sky, the midsummer sun asserted itself, pouring intense sunlight down upon the earth.

Many workers continued their tasks, sweat beading on their foreheads from the heat and direct sunlight.

...Amidst this, thanks to his Dragon Robe's ability, akin to a portable air conditioner, Rei was able to complete his work without any hardship whatsoever, despite the blazing sun and the fact it was only morning.

"Well, the higher-ups probably have their reasons, but for us actually doing the work, better labor conditions are a good thing, right?"

"Yeah, you're right."

While terms like "heatstroke" weren't used, many knew from years of experience that moving around in the heat without hydrating was bad for the body.

Even while talking with that tanuki beastman craftsman, the mages assigned to this site used magic to dig deeper and expand the area created by Rei's terrain manipulation.

They couldn't manipulate the ground over such a wide area all at once like Rei could... or do it continuously like he did. Still, they possessed sufficient skill to follow behind Rei and dig the ground, even with his one-meter limitation.

Watching those working with sweat on their brows, Rei looked around once more.

Within Rei's visible range, numerous people were laboring.

Amidst them, the adventurers on guard duty against monsters looked bored.

Several hours had passed since the wall was breached, and it was nearing noon... yet not a single goblin had appeared. It was hardly surprising they'd let their guard down.

The veteran adventurers occasionally reminded them, but that only worked for a little while after they were warned.

(Well, the real guard duty is obviously at night anyway.)

The adventurers on daytime guard duty, aside from the leaders, aren't exactly the most skilled lot.

It made perfect sense for the guards to deploy their elite forces at night, when monsters were most active.

Observing the surroundings, Rei suddenly realized... truly, belatedly...

"Huh? Come to think of it, why did they destroy the old wall first? Wouldn't it have been better to build the new wall first, then tear down the old one?"

"...Now?"

The tanuki beastman must have heard Rei's mutter, because he turned to Rei with an exasperated look.

"Well, even if you say that, I'm not exactly an expert on construction or anything like that. Obviously, this is the first time I've been involved in expansion work for a town. Is this how it's done in other towns too?"

"No."

The tanuki beastman immediately shook his head in denial at Rei's words.

Even to Rei, that reaction speed seemed pretty impressive.

"No? But that's how it's actually done, right?"

"Well... How should I put this? In a normal town, they'd proceed like Rei said—building the new wall first, then tearing down the old one. ...But where are we?"

"Where? ...Gilm, right?"

"Right. Gilm... meaning, the frontier. And this Gilm is built to suit the frontier. The biggest thing is the barrier covering the entire city. Also, because it's the frontier, there's a lot of magic power flowing around it, and a lot flowing underground too."

"...Well, it is the frontier. If it were like a normal town or village in that regard, it wouldn't be called the frontier, would it?"

If the surrounding environment were the same as anywhere else, it wouldn't be called a frontier, but just ordinary countryside.

"So, it's a procedure specific to the frontier, then?"

"That's the gist of it. There are other minor reasons too, but broadly speaking, that's the main point. Initially, as Rei mentioned, someone proposed building a new wall first and then demolishing the old one... but the craftsmen of Gilm rejected that idea."

Hearing that, Rei could only agree.

After all, he had neither the knowledge nor the skills of a craftsman.

"So, the result is that we're tearing down the old wall first and then building the new one."

"That's how it is. Oh, and apparently the barrier thing is somehow related too... but that's outside my area of expertise, so I don't know the details."

"The barrier?"

From Rei's perspective, the barrier placed on Gilm was quite powerful.

At the very least, the barrier in the town where Lerdact lived, which Rei had visited just the other day, had been completely ineffective against Rei and Set.

It wasn't entirely useless, since it did alert them to Rei and Set's intrusion... but because Set had dove straight into the lord's mansion, even if the barrier detected Rei and Set's intrusion, the reaction to it was inevitably delayed.

As a result, while the barrier might have been effective against ordinary flying monsters, it proved completely useless against Rei and his companions.

"Ah. As I just said, I don't know the details. Someone higher up might know the circumstances surrounding that. Anyway, various factors led to this construction method. And..."

The tanuki beastman paused, then shot Rei a meaningful look.

"What is it?"

"Well, with Rei here, that part probably isn't a concern. But with the method of building the new wall first and then demolishing the old one, there's also the issue of transporting materials. If this weren't the frontier, we could probably gather the materials outside the town..."

If we carelessly leave materials outside Gilm, who knows what might happen in the frontier.

Rei looked at the speaker with a puzzled expression.

"Then why not just make an opening big enough for carts to pass through instead of demolishing the entire old wall?"

"If the walls were built with that purpose in mind from the start, that might be feasible. But when Gilm was constructed, such a scenario wasn't anticipated. Attempting to recklessly destroy part of the wall under these circumstances could potentially cause damage elsewhere."

"I see. That makes sense."

Rei had almost no knowledge in that area, so when told that by someone whose job it was, he had no choice but to accept it.

(You'd think they'd have considered what happens if the wall gets damaged... but is that different?)

Looking slightly intrigued, Rei was about to say something else when... before he could, a voice calling his name reached him from farther away.

"Hey, Rei! We need you to move on to other areas soon!"

Spending too much time in one place meant more work for the others.

Taking a short break was one thing, but a fair amount of time had passed since he started talking to the tanuki beastman.

Being hurried like this wasn't really that strange, was it?

"Got it, I'll be right there!"

Waving back at the person who called out to him, Rei returned the empty water cup to the tanuki beastman.

"Well, I'm heading out soon. I've got other places to check on too."

"Got it. Do your best. ...Having Rei around makes construction work easier, so we might get requests specifically for you from now on."

"Well... I'm not sure if I should be happy about that."

With a slightly troubled smile, Rei ran toward the man who had called him earlier.

It wasn't that far, so he reached the next site in under ten seconds.

"Sorry to rush you. But having Rei handle the work first really helps things move along over here too."

"I get it. We did take a bit too long of a break over here."

"You seemed to be talking seriously earlier. What was that about?"

"Nah, I was just curious why you went out of your way to tear down the old wall before building the new one. Just asking about that."

"Well, that's because this is Gilm, right?"

"Seems like it. I didn't know that. ...So, can we get started right away?"

Didn't you know about the place you live? Hearing the man's words, which seemed to ask that with his gaze, Rei asked as if trying to cover something up.

Whether he noticed Rei's unease or not, the man didn't seem particularly invested in the conversation anyway, and simply nodded.

"Alright, I'll do it."

Just as Rei was about to use his skill, one of the nearby mages suddenly approached him.

"Excuse me, but if it's alright, might I see you use your magic? I may look like this, but I'm quite confident in my own magic. However, I've heard rumors that your skill is exceptionally high. I'd very much like to see it up close."

Before Rei could respond to the old wizard's words, the craftsman in charge of this area spoke up.

"Hey, old man. Sorry, but don't bother Rei. He's the busiest one right now."

Even though he was told he was the busiest, Rei, having just been talking to the tanuki beastman, subtly averted his gaze.

"Well, don't be like that. ...So, what do you think?"

"Ah... I don't really care either way. Just don't do anything that'll mess with my concentration."

At Rei's words, the old man nodded, his expression unable to hide his curiosity as he stared intently at Rei's movements.

While Rei couldn't help but feel slightly troubled by the old man's demeanor, he decided to just get the job done for now and thrust the tip of the Death Scythe into the ground.

"Terrain Manipulation."

With that word, the ground where the wall was to be built sank about a meter.

The other mages and craftsmen watching nearby stared wide-eyed at the scene unfolding before them.

They knew from a distance what Rei was capable of, but seeing it up close was something else entirely.

It was only natural they wondered how he could unleash such powerful magic continuously, without showing any signs of fatigue.

"What!?"

Amidst the stunned silence, a strange voice broke the quiet. It came from an old man who had been watching intently right beside Rei.

His eyes were wide, his expression one of utter disbelief, as if he'd seen something unimaginable.

"What's wrong, old man? Why did you suddenly yell like that?"

The artisan in charge of the situation called out to the old man in a flustered voice.

He probably thought the heat might have caused some physical distress for the old man.

But the old man didn't seem to hear the craftsman's words. His expression still frozen in shock, he looked at Rei again.

He wasn't deliberately ignoring his surroundings... rather, he was purely focused on Rei, letting the attitudes of others pass right through him.

"You... you... who on earth are you!?"

Confronted by the old man, Rei tilted his head and opened his mouth.

"Well, what can I say... I'm a Rank B adventurer with a nickname?"

"That's not what I'm asking! You... no, that weapon you carry... what level of skill would it take to wield such a thing...?"

Hearing the old man's words, Rei's gaze sharpened as he looked at him.

Seemingly oblivious to Rei's gaze, the old man fixed a look on the Death Scythe that could only be described as fanatical.

"Tell me! Who made this magic item!? Whose work is it!? Please!"

"Even if you ask me, I don't know. I got this as a gift, so I don't know the details about its origins."

Unable to reveal it was created through Beast Magic, Rei managed to gloss over the truth.

Still, realizing his uniqueness hadn't been understood, he let a slight look of relief flicker across his face.
