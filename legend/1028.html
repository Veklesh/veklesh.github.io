"Wow... That forest is where the dark elves live? It's much larger than I imagined."

Inside the carriage, Vihera let out a gasp of awe.

Though they didn't say it aloud, Elena and Byune felt the same way. Even from a distance, the overwhelmingly imposing sight of the forest left them speechless, their eyes riveted.

Rei was also staring out the window at the forest in surprise, but not quite as intensely as the other three.

The main reason was undoubtedly that he had seen the Dark Forest—a forest even more vast than this one—with his own eyes... no, worse than that, he had actually traveled directly through it.

The Dark Elf Forest stretching before their eyes was magnificent. There was no doubt about that.

But even so, compared to the Demon Forest, it was a step... no, two or three steps down in class.

The sheer power emanating from it was similarly diminished.

"Hehe, I'm glad to hear you say that. Even after being away for so long, it seems one's homeland holds a special place in their heart."

Marina cast a delighted glance at the three of them... then suddenly noticed that Rei didn't seem nearly as surprised as the others.

"Oh? Rei doesn't seem particularly surprised."

Though asked by a woman whose sultry glance would undoubtedly make any ordinary man lunge forward, Rei opened his mouth, recalling his past background for the first time in ages.

"I grew up in the mountains too. It was a place covered in forests and mountains, so I'm somewhat used to it."

At Rei's words, Elena's lips curved into a brief smile.

Knowing exactly what kind of being Rei was, she probably found his words amusing.

"Come to think of it, I heard something like that before. Was it really that deep in the mountains?"

Marina leaned forward, seemingly intrigued by Rei's words.

Though not as revealing as her guild attire, her party dress still had a deep neckline. Leaning forward like that inevitably drew Rei's gaze to her ample cleavage.

"Hmm, ahem. Marina. Aren't you leaning forward a bit too much?"

"Oh, sorry. So, the mountain where Rei lived..."

"Before we talk about Rei, isn't there something we should do first?"

After Elena, it was Vihera who finally tore her gaze away from the window to address Marina.

Both Elena and Vihera regarded Marina as a formidable romantic rival.

She had felt this intensely throughout their journey from leaving Gilm until now.

Whether conscious or unconscious, she used her body—one that even they, as women, found undeniably attractive—to tempt them.

No, in terms of physique alone, Elena and Vihera were certainly not inferior to Marina.

But viewed purely as women, they had to admit they fell a step short of Marina.

Her restraint stemmed precisely from understanding that.

Whether or not she understood the inner thoughts of those two, Marina once again turned her gaze to the carriage window... toward the forest of her birthplace visible in the distance.

"Leaving aside Rei's origins, seeing it like this, the forest itself seems to have lost much of its vitality compared to before."

"...Is that so? From this vantage point, it looks like a truly magnificent forest... one you could even call a great forest."

As Elena said, the forest visible from the carriage seemed to overflow with vitality.

It was a sight that made it impossible to believe the World Tree was losing its power.

"It might look that way to someone seeing it for the first time, but to me, as someone from here, the whole forest seems to have lost a lot of its vitality."

As Marina said this, Audban and Jusla nodded in agreement...

"Lady Elena, may we speak with you for a moment?"

Suddenly, a voice came from the door connecting to the driver's seat.

It was the voice of Ara, the female coachman.

"Hmm? What is it? Is there a problem?"

At Elena's puzzled voice, Ara continued, sounding somewhat flustered.

"It's not so much a problem as... a group approaching us... Anyway, there's a group coming this way. Seeing them approach so openly, they probably don't mean harm, but... well..."

Hearing her hesitant words, Elena, Vihera, Byune, and Rei all tilted their heads in puzzlement.

In truth, Rei, who possessed the sharpest senses among them, detected no hint of hostility directed at them.

That meant the approaching group wasn't hostile, but then why had Ara been so vague?

Wearing out her patience, Vihera opened the door leading to the driver's compartment.

Then, they understood why Ara had been so vague. ...It had happened.

Approaching them was a group of three carriages.

The problem, however, was that these carriages were fitted with iron bars.

Fortunately, there was no one visible inside the barred carriages, but the nature of this group was clear just by looking at the carriages.

"Slave traders, huh. And what's worse, they're probably operating in a group."

At Vihera's words, Audban and Jusla both tensed, but Marina stroked their heads to calm them.

She reassured them that as long as they stayed inside the carriage, they wouldn't be seen from outside, so there was no need to worry.

"Rei, could you handle this? With just Vihera and Ara, there's a high chance something might go wrong."

"...I doubt my presence would make much difference."

Rei understood how his face appeared to others, but he decided it was better than causing unnecessary trouble and wasting time. He followed Vihera out to the driver's seat.

Just then, one of the approaching slave traders opened his mouth.

"Hey, where are you lot headed? Just saying, if you value your lives, you'd be wise to steer clear of the Dark Elf Forest."

Regardless of his tone, the fact that he didn't immediately attack earned Rei a slight increase in his regard for the slave trader.

Though for Rei, who knew the dark elves like Marina, a slight rise in favor meant almost nothing.

"Oh? Why?"

At Vihera's question, the man opened his mouth, his lips stretching into a smirk...

"Gluu?"

"Whoaah!?"

He screamed upon seeing Set emerge from behind the carriage.

It was only natural to react that way when a griffin suddenly appeared.

(Before I even stepped outside, wouldn't Set's presence alone have been enough to handle this?)

While Rei felt a long-forgotten emotion seeing Set react like this outside of Gilm, the slave traders who saw Set... who saw the griffin up close, weren't so fortunate.

The slave traders who had been talking with Vihera, and those in the other carriages, all froze in shock at the sight of Set.

Worse still, even the horses pulling the carriages had stopped moving.

In the worst case, it wouldn't have been strange for them to flee at full speed to get any distance from Set. In that sense, the slave traders could be considered lucky.

Watching the horses frozen in place under Set's gaze, as if turned to stone by Medusa, Rei spoke up.

"So, what exactly do you mean the forest is dangerous?"

"...Huh? Ah, um, well... ah, yeah. No, it's just..."

The slave trader was completely thrown off by Set's sudden appearance, but even so, being a slave trader... an illegal one at that, he had nerves of steel.

Realizing Set posed no immediate threat to them, he suppressed his inner turmoil and spoke, keeping his composure.

"So that's a griffin... You're not...?"

The gaze directed at Rei contained awe.

When he first saw Rei, he'd thought the opponent was oddly small, but knowing his true nature now, he couldn't possibly make the mistake of underestimating him.

"Well, maybe it's better if you don't know who I am? Now, let me ask again—what do you mean the forest is dangerous? Care to explain?"

Though phrased as a request, his tone was unmistakably that of a commander.

To begin with, the gaze Rei directed at the group before him was cold, given he was an illegal slave trader.

Even if his favorability had risen slightly, starting from a negative baseline meant it hadn't even reached zero.

Still, he hadn't made a move here because, while he knew they were illegal slave traders, they didn't seem to have captured a single Dark Elf yet.

"Ah, ah. Actually, various monsters have been appearing in the Dark Elf forest lately. Many are monsters I've never seen before, ones that never showed themselves before. And most of those monsters... how should I put it?"

The slave trader paused to think, but apparently couldn't find the right words.

Hesitating, he continued.

"Frenzied? Excited? Raging? Something like that. They're attacking anyone and everyone indiscriminately. Even other monsters, for that matter."

"Huh? Monsters fighting each other isn't that strange, is it? We often hear about strong monsters killing weaker ones for food."

"No, that's not it... They're killing each other, even monsters of the same kind. And, well, we fled the forest right away, so I can't say for sure, but they didn't eat the monsters they killed either. ...Yeah, it was like killing each other was the goal itself."

"...Killing each other? Monsters fighting among themselves?"

That statement surprised Rei too.

It's not that monsters never kill each other... but most monsters don't go around killing their own kind.

(Come to think of it, humans killing each other might be worse than monsters.)

The thought flashed through Rei's mind, but he quickly dismissed it.

Now wasn't the time for unnecessary thoughts; he needed to gather any information he could about the forest's disturbance.

"Specifically, what kind of monsters were killing each other? I mean, I've heard of ogres or goblins killing their own kind, but..."

Though Vihera's appearance and attire were undeniably alluring to the man, Rei's presence meant he couldn't look at her companion that way. He hurriedly glanced at Rei and continued.

"I've heard stories about ogres and goblins too, but what I saw were harpies."

"Harpies... Well, since they're monsters that act in groups, it's not like they wouldn't kill each other."

Rei recalled the harpy he had fought before.

They hadn't shown any signs of fighting among themselves, but that could be because Rei had ambushed them.

Without any experience fighting them head-on in a fair battle, it wasn't certain harpies didn't turn on each other.

Seeing Rei thinking like that, the informant probably decided that was enough information for now.

The thought that continuing to deal with Rei, known by the alias Crimson, would likely lead to nothing good was stronger.

If this had been a legitimate merchant with nothing to hide, things might have been different. But in this case, it was an illegal slave trader.

He wasn't carrying any slaves now, so he could wriggle out of anything, but he probably felt strongly about avoiding digging himself into a deeper hole by talking more.

(Besides, they say he's a Bandit Eater, showing no mercy to thieves. If that's true, when will he come after us... Wait. Bandit Eater? Could it be...?)

The faces of the bandits they dealt with flashed through the slave trader's mind.

He was a slave trader who bought humans captured by bandits at a discount, but considering that Rei was called the Bandit Eater, maybe...

That thought proved correct. When they stopped by the bandits' hideout on their way back, they found the bandits completely wiped out. Naturally, all the treasure and weapons stored in the hideout were gone. The prison cells where the bandits kept their captives still had their iron bars intact, but inside, there was no one to be seen.

What hurt the slave traders most, however, was that even the gold and platinum coins they had entrusted to the bandits to keep private had vanished without a trace.

Unaware of the future awaiting them, the slave traders kept their distance from Rei and departed.

After seeing off the group of carriages, Rei returned inside, informed Audban and Jusla that there were no particular problems, and began moving once more toward the Dark Elf forest visible in the distance.
