After rescuing Ryutas, who had been attacked by bandits in the woods, and interrogating the bandits to learn the location of their hideout, Rei naturally took whatever treasures he found there.

...Though, it was a small band of only five thieves. On top of that, they had only recently arrived in this area, so there was hardly anything worth calling treasure.

Still, he did manage to get a few gems and ornaments, some weapons, and a bit of cash... so it was a decent haul, at least.

"If they were thieves around there, then these ornaments were probably gifts to prostitutes, or maybe the prostitutes sold them... Ah, no. But because of those slave collars, the women were supposedly willingly working as prostitutes."

If that's the case, wouldn't they keep the jewelry they received instead of selling it?

That was the question Rei pondered, but for now, scouting Mejogo from the air took priority over dwelling on it.

Set already had the Set Cage with him when he took flight from the bandits' hideout.

Naturally, no one was inside the Set Cage, but he had loaded it with plenty of magic stones to prevent the magic from running out, so there should be no worry of running out mid-flight.

"Gururuu"

A few minutes after taking off from the bandit hideout, Mejogo came into view.

"Set, just to be safe, gain more altitude."

"Guru!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a growl and flapped his wings, aiming for a higher altitude.

They usually flew at around 100 meters, but now they were at 150... no, nearly 200 meters.

At this height, normal reconnaissance of the ground would be impossible.

Well, it's not that reconnaissance is impossible—he could still get a general sense of what lay where on the ground—but determining things like how many people were stationed where became difficult.

But since Rei had come this time to learn the general layout of Mejogo, and above all to confirm where the entrance to the underground facility was located, that was fine.

(They said the entrance to the underground facility was at the center of Mejogo... Is that it?)

When Rei turned his gaze toward the center of Mejogo, he saw a single building.

It wasn't particularly large in size, but several other buildings stood around it, positioned as if guarding the one Rei had noticed.

You might not notice it just walking around Mejogo, but viewed from above, it was impossible to hide.

(Well, they probably never even considered the possibility of aerial reconnaissance anyway.)

In this world where flight itself was extremely difficult, it seemed unlikely to Rei that a small country like Reburina would be so vigilant in Mejogo—not even its capital—against such a small number of people, including dragon knights.

(Anyway, that building is undoubtedly the entrance to the underground facility. Now, we just need to find the path leading there... Troublesome.)

They probably hadn't considered aerial reconnaissance, but it was clear to Rei that they were extremely vigilant against anyone attempting to infiltrate the facility from the ground.

Numerous buildings stood along the route to the structure housing the entrance to the underground facility. To reach the underground facility from the surface, they would need to deal with those buildings.

(Well, it's pointless in our case, though.)

All of Elena's group could fit inside the Set Cages.

Though few in number, those transported in the Set Cages—except for Byune—possessed combat prowess worthy of being called non-human.

In this world of Elgin, where the phrase "quality over quantity" holds no meaning once one possesses a certain level of power—indeed, where quality utterly annihilates quantity—Rei and the others would suddenly appear right in the enemy's midst using the Set Cages.

This method of attack would surely be enough to instill despair in any foe who stood against Rei and her companions.

After carefully examining the building, likely connected to the underground facility, Rei gently taps Set's back, signaling it's time to move on.

Without a word, Rei flapped his wings and departed.

Normally, he'd chirp happily when Rei stroked him, but knowing Set was currently scouting Mejogo, he remained silent.

Once they were sufficiently far from Mejogo, Rei stroked Set again.

"Well then, shall we stop somewhere and stow the Set Cage?"

"GRRRR!"

Hearing Set's cry, Rei looked down at the ground.

There was a grove there, very close to where they had rescued Ryutas from bandits while en route to Mejogo.

Unfortunately, Ryutas was nowhere to be seen, nor were the bandits.

(Probably those guys who approached back then have already moved on as his guards. ...Though, I still don't know if Ryutas is really a merchant or not.)

Though Ryutas had an expression suggesting good breeding, both the guards who had approached him stealthily and the aura Rei sensed from Ryutas himself led Rei to suspect he was likely not just a merchant.

He didn't have any definitive proof... but he had a feeling they would meet again somewhere soon.

While these thoughts occupied his mind, Set eventually descended into the woods.

Though, it was a different spot from where they'd encountered Ryutas.

"We're heading back to Rossi immediately."

"Grr."

Responding to Rei's words, Seto set the Set Cage he was holding down on the ground. ...Well, in this case, "dropped" might be a more accurate description.

Once on the ground, Rei stowed the Set Cage in the Misty Ring and immediately returned to Set's back.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set's joyful growl echoed around them.

Carrying the Set Cage wasn't particularly heavy—at least not by Set's standards—so that wasn't the issue. The problem was how bulky it was.

For Set, who loved flying freely through the sky, it was undoubtedly a source of stress.

It would have been different if someone had been riding inside the Set Cage, but this time it was being used purely for camouflage, so no one was aboard.

That's probably why it inevitably gets handled roughly.

(Well, it probably wouldn't break just from being handled a little roughly... right? If it broke that easily, it wouldn't be much use.)

Watching Rossi appear in the distance, Rei thought about it like that.

Originally, the distance between Rossi and Mejogo was about an hour one way.

At that distance, flying through the air at Set's speed meant you could literally get there in the blink of an eye.

(As a last resort, Set could fly through the air and attack Mejogo... but since Rossi is only a few minutes away, an alibi would hold up. Well, I doubt alibis are taken that seriously in this world anyway.)

He imagined taking a rock from Rossi, moving it to Mejogo, dropping it there, then returning to Rossi to retrieve another rock... But then he realized, if he was going to do that anyway, he could just put the rock into the Misty Ring held by Rei and drop it from above.

Rei knew he was thinking something trivial, but for some reason, pondering such nonsense felt pointlessly enjoyable right now.

The few minutes until they reached Rossi passed in a flash... and soon, Set descended toward the main gate.

A fair number of people were lined up to go through the procedures to enter Rossi, but naturally, many stiffened in surprise upon seeing Set.

It was only natural, really, when the griffin known as the Grim Reaper of the Skies suddenly descended from the sky.

The guard at the main gate was also startled by Set's appearance, but fortunately, the guard had seen Rei escorting Set out of Rossi with his own eyes.

That's why he didn't react with the same obvious shock as the others.

...Even so, since Set had landed a short distance from Rossi's main gate, those waiting to enter the compound didn't panic.

And once Set landed on the ground, many understood that the griffin was a familiar when Rei, who had been riding on its back, also dismounted.

Under the gaze of those people, Rei began preparing for the procedures to enter Rossi.

He glanced at the people lining up, wondering if Ryutas might be among them... but Ryutas was nowhere to be seen.

Not much time had passed since Rei parted ways with Ryutas in the woods.

If that were the case, it wouldn't be strange to meet Rei here... But as he was thinking this, one of the guards approached him for some reason.

"Rei, I'm sorry, but your presence here is making the others nervous. So, I'd like to process you first. Is that alright?"

The polite tone was partly due to Set's presence... but it was mostly because Rei was an adventurer with a notorious reputation.

Furthermore, rumors circulated that Rei showed no mercy, even to nobles. ...Which was true.

Given that, it was only natural for them to use polite language.

Of course, how they handled it depended on the guard's personality, but at the very least, this guard seemed to have decided it was best to get Rei into the city as quickly as possible.

In fact, that was absolutely the right call.

While everyone understood that Set was a familiar, the mere presence of a completely unfamiliar griffin nearby was reason enough to feel fear.

Had this been Gilm, many would already know of Rei and Set's existence and nature, and it likely wouldn't have caused such a commotion.

Rei seemed to understand the guard's reasoning and gave a small nod.

"If that's fine with you, it's exactly what I wanted. Handle the paperwork, please."

"Grrr."

Set growled in response to Rei's words. The guard, hearing this, paused for a moment but quickly began the procedures once Rei's consent was secured.

Thus, drawing attention from those around them, Rei finished the procedures, placed the familiar's collar on Set, and entered Rossi.

Even inside Rossi, the attention from those around them didn't change.

However, within Rossi, while Rei was one thing, Set seemed to be quite the topic of conversation. Some who had seen him once came out to see him again, so Set felt less lonely than when waiting outside.

Though, to be honest, being with Rei meant Set never felt lonely in the first place.

"What should we do, Set? Head straight back to the Silver Fruit Inn?"

"Grr? ...Grrrrr."

At Rei's words, Set hesitated slightly... then spotted a food stall a short distance away and let out a growl.

It was the same stall where Rei and the others had eaten Panyan when they first arrived in the Kingdom of Reburina... and in Rossi.

Though, while the dish called Panyan was the same, the stall itself was different from the one Rei had visited before.

"Yeah, let's grab some. Might as well. I want to stock up on good food while I can."

Speaking of things only possible because of the Misty Ring's existence, Rei approached the stall alongside Set.

"I..."

Then, upon seeing Rei, the stall owner was unable to utter another word.

Then again, considering he wasn't exactly used to seeing Set either, it was hardly surprising.

"Two Panyans."

As if to sample before buying in bulk, he bought two panyans and ate them with Set.

The seasoning was slightly different from what he'd eaten before, but that didn't mean it was bad.

It was simply that the panyan Rei had eaten before was his first, so he'd taken that taste as the standard for panyan.

"Gurururuu"

Set purred with satisfaction, and Rei nodded in agreement as he took a bite of the panyan.

It was slightly spicier than the ones he'd had before, and that spiciness enhanced the rich flavor of the generous meat filling.

Satisfied with the flavor of this panyan, Rei bought all the remaining panyan and left the stall.

The stall owner stared after Rei and Set, looking utterly stunned that his entire panyan stock had sold out.

But it was still afternoon.

The busiest part of the day, lunchtime, was over, but there were still several hours until the evening rush, which was almost as busy as midday.

To maximize today's earnings, the stall owner scrambles to run around and restock ingredients.

...And so, though Set was frightening, word gradually spread that Rei and Set bought large quantities of food from the stalls. As a result, Rei and Set came to be regarded as valued customers.
