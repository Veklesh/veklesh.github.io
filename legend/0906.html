By the time the sun had risen and it was nearly noon.

It was considerably later than his usual wake-up time, but it was only around then that Rei finally opened his eyes.

He sat up in bed and looked around. Realizing he was in his usual room at Dusk Wheat inn, he stretched widely.

"Waaaaaah..."

Still feeling groggy, he drifted in a haze, letting himself be carried along by the slow passage of time.

About five minutes passed like this. Finally, his mind began to work. He got ready while thinking about his plans for the day.

(Hmm, I suppose I need to make an appearance at the Baron Sisne household. I left the guards with Fron and the others, so that shouldn't be a worry. Actually, since I captured Keep and his men, I probably don't need to worry about an attack anymore.)

Finishing his preparations, he slipped on the Dragon Robe he'd taken from the Misty Ring and headed straight out of his room toward the dining hall.

Along the way, he passed several guests, but for some reason, many seemed unusually restless.

Feeling the lighthearted atmosphere flowing through the inn, he entered the dining hall. The moment he did, the gazes of the guests inside turned toward Rei.

It was probably because noon was approaching. There were guests trying to eat an early lunch, and others who seemed to be killing time in the dining hall because it was winter.

"Hey, it's Rei. Did you hear about yesterday?"

"Yeah. The guy who dueled at the festival and then attacked the Baron Sysne household, right?"

"Not exactly. It was the noble who hired that guy Rei fought in the duel, Zeros or whatever. Apparently, he didn't want to pay half the fortune he'd bet, so he took the hired men with him to slaughter the entire Cisne Baron family."

"Yeah, heard about that. So Rei read that and ambushed them with his crew, wiped them all out, right?"

"Seems like it. They say the noble had both arms and legs completely severed, you know?"

"Still, what was that red stuff that appeared around their bodies during the duel? Just seeing it made my body freeze up."

"Yeah, yeah. And there was a Rank B adventurer near me, but he couldn't move either. Sweat pouring down his face."

"...Even though he was the same Rank B? Man, Rei really is an outlier in every sense."

"Well, of course. His duel opponent was Rank B too, after all."

From the snippets I overheard, it was immediately clear they were talking about the duel and last night's attack.

Rei, unfazed by becoming the subject of gossip as usual, sat down at the table and ordered food for Lana.

(Keeping isn't with both hands and feet, just both hands. Rumors always get exaggerated so fast. Where did this info come from? The guards? Or the adventurers Fron brought? Well, whatever.)

To kill time while waiting for the food, Rei sipped his water and tuned out the rumors drifting his way. But the next moment, the cup he'd raised to his lips froze mid-motion.

"So the carriage with the treasure? It was connected to that noble too?"

"Yeah, that's what it seems like. They found a body, apparently. Pretty badly crushed by the carriage, though."

Rei tilted his head at the words and turned his gaze toward where the conversation had come from.

Not far from Rei's table.

The conversation belonged to two men who appeared to be adventurers.

Recognizing the faces, Rei stood up.

Despite being only Rank C adventurers, not high-ranking ones, they were known for their resourcefulness, making the Dusk Wheat inn their regular lodging. They had even come up in conversation with Kenny and Lenora.

One was human, the other a rat-like beastman, both men in their early twenties.

Approaching the table where these men sat, Rei addressed the two who were looking his way.

"Sorry, but could I hear a bit more of what you were talking about just now?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. Sure, no problem... Hey?"

"Ah, sure."

Though clearly older than Rei, their attitude toward him was that of superiors.

Not only was his rank higher, but it was also clear he possessed abilities far beyond even a rank B adventurer could hope to match.

Furthermore, Rei had risen to that position in less than two years after registering as an adventurer in Gilm.

Given that, it was no surprise many of the men harbored feelings bordering on admiration toward him.

...Though, given Rei's personality, there were also quite a few who disliked him.

Fortunately, these two were friendly toward Rei. They were surprised when he spoke to them, but quickly urged him to sit down.

What Rei learned from them was that late last night, Fultos had been found dead, crushed beneath an overturned carriage.

Furthermore, judging from the bloodstains left on the driver's seat, it was likely Furtos himself who had been driving the carriage. The carriage itself was loaded with valuables, including gold coins and even platinum coins.

"...So what? That Fultos guy was ordered by Keep to transport treasure?"

"No, I don't think so. If that were the case, they wouldn't have hired that Fultos guy—they'd have hired a professional coachman. And considering that man's build, he certainly didn't look like the type who'd willingly take on the job of a coachman, did he?"

At those words, Fultos' figure flashed through Rei's mind.

He was so fat he looked like he weighed twice, three times... maybe even more than twice or three times as much as himself.

(With that build, voluntarily wanting to be a driver... that's not something you'd normally think of.)

Rei nodded in understanding, but the conclusion he finally reached made him involuntarily frown.

"So, he used the master he served as bait to escape?"

"That's the common view. ...And there was talk that you had a hand in that guy's death, but it seems that's not the case."

Rei nodded in return, as if it were obvious.

Using one's own master as a sacrificial pawn was something Rei never could have imagined.

Knowing Fultos's character might have changed things, but Rei had only met him three times: when Asie was involved at the Baron Sysne's house, when he went to the lord's mansion about the duel, and during the duel itself.

If asked whether Rei himself had actually conversed with Fultos during those encounters, he could recall almost nothing.

For better or worse, it was Keep's personality that left the strongest impression.

"...Then who did it?"

Rei responded with a sarcastic smile at the other man's words.

"The one who couldn't leave their own disgrace unpunished, I suppose."

"Huh? You mean..."

Before the man who understood who that referred to could say anything, Rei stood up.

"Thanks for the intel. If anything comes up, let me know. I'll help out if I can, as a way of repaying this favor."

Surprised that Rei's words implied the king's faction was involved, the two adventurers exchanged glances before Rei called out to them and returned to his original table.

"Ah, Rei. Welcome back. You worked hard yesterday."

After finishing his meal at the cafeteria, Rei arrived at the Sysne Baron's residence.

Rei was greeted cheerfully by Ashie.

Of course she was. If it weren't for Rei, she would likely have been reduced to a noble's plaything.

She could never thank Rei enough for saving her and protecting her life with Muet and Basler.

"Don't worry about it. More importantly, has anything happened since then?"

"Yes. The guards stayed behind just in case, but there was no trouble."

"I see. That's good. ...So, is Muet here? I want to talk about a few things regarding this incident."

"Understood. I'll fetch her immediately. Please come inside."

Led by Ashié, Rei entered the reception room they had used before.

Herbal tea and dried fruit were promptly served.

She must have remembered how much Rei had enjoyed them last time, which is why she served the same.

He popped the sweet-and-sour dried fruit into his mouth, washing down the sweetness with herbal tea. His appetite and thirst were insatiable—hard to believe he'd just had a hearty meal at the Dusk Wheat inn and snacked from street stalls with Set all the way to the Noble District.

No, rather, for Rei, it was likely just a way to settle his stomach.

And just as she finished her herbal tea, Muet appeared, as if timing it perfectly.

He must have understood the conversation concerned this matter. Only Muet appeared in the reception room.

"Sorry to keep you waiting. So, today's business... it's about the duel, right?"

"Yeah. Not sure if you know, but that fat guy who was Keep's attendant? He's dead, apparently."

"Yes, the guards told me about that. Apparently, he was shot dead with an arrow."

"...Huh? No, from what I heard, it was run over by a carriage."

Muet shook his head at Rei, who was asking while recalling the information he'd heard at the Dusk Wheat inn.

"No, the direct cause of death seems to have been the arrow piercing his chest. The impact threw him to the ground, where he was run over by the carriage..."

"I see. So the culprit is still—"

"Yes. There's no proof, but it's likely a purge carried out by someone loyal to the king. Though, whether that purge was because he tried to steal the Earl of Eriell's property and flee, or if it was collateral damage for Lord Keep, who brought shame upon the royalists... it seems they can't tell which it was."

Hearing Muet's words, Rei surmised it was likely the latter.

There was no clear reason, but it was likely so.

"Well, at least there's no one left over there likely to cause trouble. This house is safe now. Oh, and regarding the reward for the escort... I'll leave that to you."

"Yes, of course. Without you and Fron, I, Basler, and Asie would likely be dead. I wouldn't dream of skimping on payment for those who saved our lives. ...Now, about the payment for Rei..."

He hesitated, his words trailing off awkwardly.

It was only natural. Rei had undoubtedly done the most work in this matter.

It was only natural that his reward would be higher.

Moreover, Rei was a high-ranking B-rank adventurer with a nickname.

"Ah, right. You don't have to push yourself now. We can wait until the inheritance from the Eriell family arrives."

"...I feel terribly bad about that, so please at least accept this much for now."

Muet said, handing the bag to Rei.

Inside the bag were three gold coins.

It was nowhere near enough to hire Rei, but it was still the maximum amount the Baroness Sysne household could offer at this moment.

Rei seemed to understand this. Without saying anything, he gave a small nod and stowed the bag inside the Misty Ring.

"Now that this matter is settled, you should be able to relax this winter."

"Yes, I suppose so. ...Honestly, I never imagined I'd be this busy. Even after the duel ended, I ended up having to write all sorts of documents. Like the contract with Lord Daskar."

"Ah, the fellow going to the Earl of Eriell's domain to claim his inheritance."

"Yes. Lord Daskar said there shouldn't be any particular issues since the report on this duel has already been delivered by the messenger birds summoned via spirit beasts to the capital and the Count of Eriell's household..."

Muet's words trailed off, and he didn't look particularly pleased.

He was undoubtedly anticipating some kind of trouble arising.

Yet, she still let out a faint sigh of relief. Probably because things would return to their usual routine from now on.

For quite some time now, Muet—who normally disliked drawing attention—had been very conspicuous.

Those days were over, and things would return to normal.

(Though, given all the commotion, the name of the Baron Sysne household was surely seared into the minds of Gilm's residents.)

Muet understood this well.

And there was no doubt that the name had also become known to the royalist faction to which the Count of Eriell's family belonged.

While they could expect quieter days from now on, it was undeniable that things would never be completely the same as before.

Just then, Rei's gaze suddenly shifted toward the door.

Simultaneously, the door opened, and Baslero appeared.

"Rei-san, I've thought of a special move! Could you take a look?"

Perhaps the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour displayed in the duel had made a strong impression; Baslero's eyes were filled with the hope that he too could create a special move.

(Well, at least he managed to keep that smile intact, I suppose.)

Seeing Asie waiting behind Baslero, Rei smiled and rose from the sofa where he had been sitting.

"I see, a special move. I'd love to see it. Is the courtyard okay?"

"Yes!"

With Basleru beaming happily, Rei exited the room.
