The day after the seafood party, Rei and Set were seen in the sky.

As expected, Set was carrying his Set Cage, and inside it were all of Elena and the others.

Although they were flying through the sky under these circumstances, Rei looked up at the sky with a slightly displeased expression.

This was because the sky was covered in rain clouds.

As the saying goes, “A woman’s heart and an autumn sky,” so Rei knew that the weather tends to change easily in the fall.

……Though, seasonally speaking, it was still the end of summer, so it wasn’t quite autumn yet.

Even so, during the nearly ten days Rei and the others had been enjoying their vacation at the beach, while it had been cloudy, it hadn’t rained.

That had been lucky for Rei and the others, but I suppose that luck couldn’t last forever.

“At this rate, it’s bound to start raining soon.”

Of course, just because Rei and Set got wet, it didn’t mean they’d catch a cold or fall ill.

Still, traveling in the rain would be unpleasant enough just from getting wet, so if possible, they wanted to find somewhere to shelter from the rain.

(If we use the Magic Tent, we can shelter from the rain comfortably... I guess I’ll look for a place where we can set it up.)

If it were just a matter of setting up the Magic Tent, that could be done just about anywhere.

But if they set it up in the wrong place, there was a chance they could get caught up in unwanted trouble.

Like, say, being attacked by bandits...

(Well, if it’s a bandit attack, I’d actually welcome it with open arms.)

I’m Rei, feared by bandits as the “Bandit Eater.”

Naturally, if bandits attacked, he would repel them, seize their treasure, and, depending on the situation, might even sell them to slave traders like the pirates of Galanka.

...Though if I can’t find a slave trader right away, the bandits will all end up as corpses.

“Hm? That area shouldn’t be too visible to others. Set, head toward that spot hidden behind the rock.”

“Grr? Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set descended toward the massive rock below.

He judged that if they could find a spot in the shadow of one of the several large boulders lined up there, they wouldn’t be easily spotted even if they used a magic tent to shelter from the rain.

So, as usual, she lowered the Set Cage to the ground first, then Seto stepped out onto the ground... and just as she finished setting up the Magic Tent, the rain began to fall.

“Oh my, it’s really coming down now.”

There was just enough space between the rocks to spread out the Magic Tent perfectly, and in that spot where no water dripped down, Marina murmured as she watched the rain falling from the entrance of the Magic Tent.

Since the Magic Tent is perfectly capable of sheltering them from the rain, they wouldn’t normally have had to squeeze into the gap between the rocks.

But since Set can’t fit inside the Magic Tent, they’d taken refuge in this space where they could shelter from the rain.

Fortunately, this gap was fairly large, and even with the Magic Tent set up, there was still enough room for Set to lie down.

……Though, strictly speaking, that’s just enough room to lie down; running around is out of the question.

“Kyuu! Kyu-kyu!”

“Grr…”

At that moment, Yellow was sitting on Set’s back, and the two of them were having a conversation.

Watching the two of them, Marina peered outside through the entrance of the magic tent and muttered, seemingly surprised by the intensity of the rain.

“Wouldn’t using spirit magic help in some way?”

It was Rei who asked this, filled with doubt.

It was a question born of his knowledge of just how skillfully Marina could wield spirit magic.

However, in response to Rei’s question, Marina shook her head.

“I can’t possibly manipulate the weather itself. That’s beyond the capabilities of a spirit mage of my caliber. Only a spirit mage far more skilled than I could do something like that.”

“A spirit mage more skilled than Marina…”

As far as Rei knew, there was no spirit mage more skilled than Marina.

No, in fact, spirit magic users themselves are extremely rare.

Even ordinary mages are quite rare, so mages capable of communicating with spirits are even fewer.

(Ah, there was someone in the Bestir Empire who used spirit magic.)

He recalled an elf who had a combat style involving the use of water spirits to form a water dragon.

Though, he couldn’t quite recall the elf’s name.

“Is there a spirit mage better than Marina?” Marina felt both pleased by Rei’s trust and a touch of exasperation as he looked at her with a puzzled expression.

“Look, there’s no doubt I’m pretty good at spirit magic, but that doesn’t mean I’m the best spirit mage out there. As far as I know, there are more spirit mages better than me than I can count on both hands.”

“...The world is a big place.”

Rei murmured thoughtfully in response to Marina’s words.

In fact, those words were certainly not wrong.

There are still countless things in this world that Rei doesn’t know about—things she couldn’t even imagine.

“That’s true. Though, when it comes to spirit mages who are better than me, you’re in the same boat, aren’t you, Rei?”

“Me?”

“Yes. Rei is undoubtedly strong. In fact, there’s no doubt you rank among the top in this country… no, in the world, right? But can you say for certain that there’s no one stronger than you?”

“I can’t say for sure.”

Rei answered without hesitation.

In his mind, the faces of two Rank S adventurers—one from the Bestir Empire and one from the Kingdom of Mireana—floated to the surface.

Furthermore, there are currently three Rank S adventurers in the world.

Given that, there must be one Rank S adventurer whom Rei has never even seen.

(Well, there’s such a thing as compatibility in battle, so depending on the situation, I might be able to win… but even so, there’s no doubt they’re not an opponent I can beat easily.)

Besides Rank S adventurers, there are also many adventurers with nicknames.

They aren’t opponents on the same extraordinary level as Rank S adventurers, but if asked whether Rei could fight them and win easily… Rei couldn’t immediately nod in agreement.

In fact, Rei has fought seriously against Elk, the "Axe of the Thunder God," who is the S-Rank adventurer he’s closest to. Even then, while he did win, it wasn’t exactly a walk in the park.

Given those circumstances, Rei understands why he can’t claim to be the strongest.

...That said, since Rei possesses combat power that ranks among the top in this world, to those common bandits, she’s nothing short of overwhelming firepower—nothing but overkill.

“That’s why I train every day, too. …It’s not like the mock battles Rei and the others do—it’s training to improve my affinity with spirits.”

“Technically, I should barely qualify as a mage… at least just by the tips of my fingers.”

Rei said this, but instead of Marina, even Vihera and Elena shot him exasperated glances.

In fact, considering that even Byune—who rarely shows any emotion—was giving Rei a cold stare, it was proof of just how foolish a thing he had said.

Faced with those looks, Rei hesitated for a moment, unsure how to respond... Eventually, he decided that saying anything in this situation would be pointless—in fact, it would only make him look even more foolish—so he looked away.

He understood that while he certainly used magic, he could never, under any circumstances, call himself a magician.

To be precise, he was a warrior who could also use magic—an existence that should be called a “magic warrior.”

“This rain just won’t stop, will it?”

“……Yeah.”

Rei blatantly changed the subject, but Marina, upon hearing this, didn’t press her on it either, simply letting it slide.

Normally, she would have wanted to call Rei out on all sorts of things, but she probably figured that anything she said to Rei about his strength would be pointless.

In fact, she wasn’t wrong about that.

Given Rei’s personality, she wouldn’t just bend her principles so easily.

Otherwise, she probably wouldn’t have gone so far as to develop a combat style like the “Two-Spear Style,” wielding the Death Scythe in her right hand and the Twilight Spear in her left.

“Hmm!”

Amidst all this, Byune, who had been eating dried fruit inside the magic tent, let out a small murmur.

It was just a single-word mutter, as usual, but this time, anyone who followed her gaze understood roughly what she meant.

“Eek, eek!”

A rather large owl let out that cry as it plunged toward Rei and the others from between the rocks.

“Didn’t it sense Set’s presence?” Rei thought for a moment as he watched the owl, but seeing its rain-soaked feathers stained red with blood, he could tell it hadn’t come here of its own accord—it must have been cornered or something and ended up here by accident.

(But first of all, isn’t it unusual for an owl to be active during the day?)

For Rei, the image of an owl was strongly associated with being a nocturnal bird.

Of course, what Rei knew was merely a general impression.

After all, he wasn’t exactly an expert on owls or birds…

The owl that landed—or rather, crashed—right next to Set stopped moving the moment it realized Set—and Yellow, for that matter—was right in front of it.

It froze so perfectly that if someone who didn’t know any better saw the scene, they might think time itself had stopped.

“Hey, what are you going to do? Shouldn’t we tend to it?”

Bistrel muttered, looking as if she’d like to tend to it if possible.

Regardless of her appearance, given her girlish nature, Bistol probably felt she couldn’t bear to leave the injured owl as it was.

"What are we going to do?" All eyes except Bistul’s turned to Rei.

Rei is the party leader of the Crimson Wings.

So, naturally, it’s up to Rei to make that call… that must be it.

Faced with those expectant gazes, Rei looked at the owl, which was still motionless, thought for a moment, and finally spoke.

“Alright. You can treat it. But, needless to say, don’t let it thrash around. …Though I don’t think it’ll thrash around.”

Seeing how it had stopped moving by Set’s side, he couldn’t imagine the owl acting out in a situation like this.

After all, if the owl were to act up here, Set would undoubtedly sense it.

That said, Set was merely watching the owl with an interested gaze.

If asked whether the owl would act out near Set, one would normally answer no.

“Leave it to me. I’m good at first aid, you know.”

At Rei’s words, Bistol happily emerged from the magic tent.

Normally, it wouldn’t be strange for the owl to go wild at the sight of Bistol, but perhaps because of Set’s presence, it remained calm. It let Bistol grab it and was led right into the Magic Tent.

“Eek!?”

Perhaps because he was inside the Magic Tent and had moved a little further away from Set, the owl let out a cry.

(An owl… it’s an owl, right? It doesn’t seem like a monster or anything.)

Even as he thought that, the main reason Rei couldn’t quite bring himself to recognize the owl struggling in Bistol’s hands as a bird was its cry.

The owls Rei knew made sounds like “Ho, ho.”

They certainly don’t make a “Gyaa, gyaa” sound like this owl.

Of course, the owls Rei knows are strictly Earth owls.

It’s not as if there aren’t owls in this world of Elgin that make bird-like sounds like the one right in front of him.

“Hey, stop struggling! Come on, look. I can’t see where you’re hurt!”

Bistol tried his best to check where the owl was injured, but the owl, perhaps sensing this as a hostile act, struggled to escape from Bistol’s hands.

“Is it running away because it’s scared of Bistol?”

Rei suddenly had that thought, but if asked whether an owl could distinguish a human’s appearance, he could only tilt his head in confusion.

“Hey, hold the owl down for a second.”

Perhaps unable to bear watching Bistoll struggle, Marina—who had conjured a ball of water into the air using spirit magic—called out to him.
