As the seasons passed, Gamelion’s presence finally vanished from the vicinity of Gilm.

As the daily temperatures continued to drop, and it seemed that snow would fall any day now... Rei was at the guild.

To be precise, in the guild’s warehouse.

There were currently a large number of monster corpses there.

This was the first phase of the Guild’s long-promised plan to butcher the monsters in Rei’s Misty Ring.

Since it had grown cold enough that snow could fall at any moment, expansion work had been scaled back to the bare minimum for the time being. This action was intended to allow adventurers who had lost their jobs to earn at least a little money.

……Furthermore, the rewards for the adventurers who accepted this dismemberment job were to be paid by the Guild and Daskar.

“Very well, I’ll take it. As promised, the monster materials…”

“Yeah, the Guild can buy back anything I don’t need.”

If Rei were an alchemist, the materials from the nearly one hundred monster corpses currently in the warehouse would be the perfect resource.

However, Rei cannot use alchemy or anything of the sort, and neither can his companions.

Given that, for now, they only need to keep the materials that seem valuable enough to use when crafting magic items.

Normally, monster materials need to be used before they spoil.

While items like internal organs or eyeballs can be preserved to some extent with proper processing, even that has its limits.

However, with Rei’s Misty Ring, the materials do not deteriorate like they would with ordinary food.

That is precisely why, even if he goes so far as to dissect them all at once, it poses no problem for Rei.

And above all...

“Don’t sell the meat—I’ll take it all. Is that okay?”

That’s right—even this much monster meat can be easily preserved with Rei’s Misty Ring.

(At least, I won’t have to worry about meat for a while.)

Rei pondered this while looking at the pile of monster corpses, though there was still plenty of meat stored in the Misty Ring.

Still, the reason he feels he won’t run out of meat is likely because, like Rei and Byune, Set is also a huge eater.

Rei and Byune can easily eat the equivalent of three normal meals in a single sitting, and Set can polish off a meal meant for about twenty people without breaking a sweat.

That’s precisely why, even though they currently have plenty of meat, they still feel compelled to stock up on large quantities.

“Thank you. Well then, once we’re done butchering the monsters in this warehouse, I’ll go let the inn know.”

“Yeah. But, as I’ve already told Guild Master Walker, I’ll be leaving Gilm soon. When that happens, please stop the butchering for now and start again when I return next year.”

“Understood. I’ll do as you say. …Still, looking at it like this, there’s an incredible amount. And this isn’t even all of it.”

“That’s true. Between one thing and another, they’ve been piling up ever since the incident in Treant Forest. Plus, there are monsters we’ve defeated in other places too. Though it looks like the Gigant Turtle won’t be making an appearance for a while yet.”

“With a creature that size, it’s certainly not going to be easy to butcher. In fact, if we start with the Giganto Turtle first thing next year… it wouldn’t be surprising if winter were over by the time we’re done.”

The guild clerk’s somber remark likely stems from having witnessed the Gigant Turtle during the commotion in the Treant Forest.

Its sheer size meant that, if things went wrong, it could have caused enough damage to wipe out Gilm.

That’s precisely why, based on past experience, he could predict just how long it would take to dismantle the Giganto Turtle.

“Right. I plan to have them do that once they return, so please make the necessary preparations.”

“Yes. I’ll go ahead and put the word out. Dismantling a monster that size will require a fair amount of manpower.”

If we let them know in advance, it’ll be easier to gather people.

Nodding at the guild staff member who said this, Rei left the warehouse.

“Growl?”

Having spotted Rei approaching him from outside the warehouse, Set growled softly while being petted by those around him.

Seeing Set like that, the others quietly left, realizing they’d be bothering him further.

Of course, a few of them looked reluctant to leave, but when Set made it clear with his body language that he wanted to go to Rei, they didn’t press the issue any further.

“Did they give you some love?”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set purred energetically in response.

Seeing Set like that, envious glances were directed at Rei from those around them.

While it would be no exaggeration to call Set the idol of Gilm, his true favorite was, after all, Rei.

For Set enthusiasts, Rei is someone to envy, someone to be jealous of, and someone to be grateful to.

After all, the residents of Gilm were only able to admire Set because Rei, who had Set as his familiar, came to Gilm.

Considering these circumstances, while they feel jealous of Rei for being on such good terms with Set, they also feel grateful, knowing that without Rei, they wouldn’t have been able to admire Set at all.

For Set enthusiasts, Rei was someone who evoked a complex mix of emotions.

In any case, Rei walks through Gilm, which is gradually turning into winter, alongside Set.

Along the way, they stop at a few diners, buying soup by the pot.

As they walk, they can really feel that, with winter having arrived, there are fewer people around than there were a short while ago.

“Hey, Rei. How’s it going? It’s cooked up nice and crispy—wanna grab some?”

As they walked down the main street, a street vendor called out to them.

Since Rei had been thinking about grabbing a bite to eat, he didn’t hesitate to buy some skewered meat.

“Give me about twenty skewers.”

“Thanks for your business. Man, it really makes my day when you buy from me, Rei. Plus, the fact that you’re eating them is proof that they’re delicious.”

The stall owner smiles happily.

In fact, since Rei buys such a large quantity, passersby can’t help but be drawn to the scene.

Seeing this, the passersby, watching Rei and Set eat so heartily, would get hungry themselves and buy some... and so on.

And if it’s delicious, they’ll keep coming back, so as a food stall, Rei was a most welcome customer.

Of course, that’s only true if the food is consistently good.

Among the stalls, there are some where—whether due to the cook’s skill, the owner’s personal taste, or simply because it doesn’t suit Rei’s preferences—Rei finds the food so unappealing that he never wants to eat there again.

That said, regardless of Rei’s personal preferences, those stalls ultimately fail to attract customers and can’t stay in business.

Street stalls can be set up for less money than a regular restaurant, but that’s exactly why the competition is so fierce.

“Speaking of which, you know what a meat bun is, right?”

They were seasoned only with salt, but the cooking was so perfect that Rei decided this was a winner as he ate his skewers, when suddenly the stall owner asked him that.

If you asked Rei if he knew what they were, of course Rei did.

After all, it was Rei who introduced meat buns to Gilm in the first place.

Naturally, meat buns, just like udon, have become fairly widespread in Gilm.

And just like with udon, it’s fairly well-known that Rei was the one who came up with the idea for meat buns… but it seemed this skewered meat vendor didn’t know that.

That said, compared to udon, meat buns are only really eaten during certain seasons.

In the summer, while udon has cold dishes like buckwheat noodles with cold broth, meat buns don’t taste good when they’re cold.

At the very least, Rei wouldn’t want to eat a cold meat bun, and considering that meat buns aren’t sold in the summer, that’s probably the general perception.

...In reality, this has a lot to do with what Rei said when she explained that meat buns are a dish typically eaten during the colder seasons, like autumn and winter.

“Oh, I know. But so what?”

“Well, actually, there are some food stalls that serve steamed buns now, and apparently they’re pretty popular.”

“I see. Well, a steamer doesn’t take up that much space, after all.”

Compared to an oven for baking bread, a steamer can be made of wood, and since the cooking method involves steaming, it doesn’t require particularly large or heavy equipment.

If that’s the case, cooking at a food stall shouldn’t be all that difficult.

(Besides, with meat buns, the bun itself, the filling, and the steaming time are what matter most. You can actually make the buns and the filling ahead of time at home, so all that’s left is steaming. If you specialize in that, you should be able to make a living running a street stall. Mind you, this is just my amateur opinion.)

Even as he thought that, it was only natural for Rei—who had a weakness for good food—to decide he’d give it a try if it was available at a food stall.

“Oh, looks like you’re interested. A friend of mine runs the place, so stop by the stall. It’s just straight down this street.”

The stall owner replied cheerfully to Rei’s words. Rei gave a quick wave in response, stowed the remaining skewers in his Misty Ring, and headed straight for the stall.

“Come to think of it, I really need to start getting ready to head to the Duchy of Kerebel.”

“Huh?”

Set, walking beside Rei, tilted his head at the mutter.

To Set, as long as Rei was there, it didn’t matter who else was around.

That said, since most people tend to be afraid of Set when visiting a place for the first time, it was a bit of a shame.

Seeing Set’s expression, Rei must have sensed a hint of unease. He gently stroked the head of Set, who was walking beside him.

“Don’t worry. I told you it’s the Duchy of Kerebel, right? That means Elena and Yellow will be there too.”

“Grrrr!”

Perhaps happy to hear that Elena and Yellow were there too, Set purred contentedly.

A passerby walking nearby was slightly startled by Set, but upon realizing it was him purring, they smiled warmly and went back to their business.

For most of Gilm’s residents, Set’s presence was already taken for granted.

Living in a place like Gilm, it must be especially disappointing for Set to be feared when he visits other towns.

That said, having his friend Yellow there is more than enough comfort for Set.

“Seriously, you’re so extreme.”

Watching Set wag his tail happily, Rei thought of a dog.

A griffin is a monster with the upper body of an eagle and the lower body of a lion; if you had to classify it as canine or feline, it would undoubtedly be feline.

But Set’s friendly personality is clearly closer to that of a dog than a free-spirited cat.

After all, he’s wagging his tail right now out of sheer joy.

Wagging his tail while walking down the street naturally bothered other passersby.

Knowing this, Rei tries to calm Set down as they make their way down the main street toward a food stall selling steamed buns.

Perhaps thanks to Rei’s actions, Set had calmed down considerably; while he remained in a good mood, his tail had stopped wagging so wildly.

They continued down the main street...

“Hmm, I thought it was supposed to be around here… but I don’t see any stalls that look like it.”

He looks around, but there are no stalls that appear to be using steamers.

He considered searching by smell, but with meat buns, the meat is wrapped inside the bun, so they don’t give off a particularly strong scent until you cut them open.

Of course, both Rei and Set had keen senses of smell... but the surrounding stalls were filled with a variety of appetizing aromas from grilling and stir-frying, and some even sold soups heavily seasoned with spices, so the various scents blended together into a single, overwhelming mix.

With all those smells mixed together, Rei couldn’t locate the stall selling meat buns, but Set, whose sense of smell was even sharper than Rei’s, eventually tugged at Rei’s Dragon Robe with his beak and led him this way, this way.

“That way…”

The place Set led Rei to had very few food stalls.

Of course, since it was a main street, it wasn’t as if there were no customers at all… but a smell like sweat was lingering in the air.

Steamed buns aren’t usually food with such a strong smell to begin with.

Given that, opening a stall in a place like this is undoubtedly a significant disadvantage for a street vendor.

“A specialized training ground? …Well, I guess since people who use this place might buy meat buns, it’s not exactly a disadvantageous location… Anyway, let’s go check out the shop.”

“Growl!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled happily.

It seems the smell of sweat doesn’t bother him as much as the prospect of eating a meat bun.

Feeling a bit envious of Set, Rei took a step forward.
