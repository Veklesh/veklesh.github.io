Before Rei's eyes, Gamelion collapsed to the ground.

His life had ended due to the attacks from Rent, Johanna, and the others.

The battle had progressed with Rent blocking Gamelion's attacks with his shield, creating openings for Johanna and the others to strike.

Having blocked nearly all of the attacks from fangs, ears, and tail, Rent's shield was already heavily scarred and even warped.

"Ugh. This needs repairing by a blacksmith... nah, buying a new one would be faster."

Rent muttered with a weary expression, staring at the metal shield.

"Damn, this is something else. When we fought those beast-fanged ones, we mostly dodged their attacks, but it turns out they actually pack this much punch."

The former guerrilla fighter remarked in astonishment.

Gamelion, nearly three meters tall, was solidly built with well-defined muscles.

The sheer force behind that single blow was evident from the damage it had inflicted on the metal shield.

"Thanks to Rent taking the hits, we barely took any damage over here. ...Thanks."

"Hah, I'm a tank by nature. This is nothing."

Lent replied to Johanna's words of thanks.

But a smile he couldn't quite hide played on his lips.

He had achieved his goal of showing Johanna his strengths.

For Rent, the purpose of this Gamelion hunt was more about showing Johanna his good side than just getting the meat.

Having accomplished that safely, it was only natural he'd be smiling.

"No casualties? That was some smooth teamwork."

Rei muttered as he approached Gamelion.

"Of course. Rent aside, the rest of us have fought together in the same unit, so we've had no choice but to hone our coordination."

"...Yeah, that's true. During training with Rei, if we didn't have any coordination at all, we'd just get hunted down one-sidedly."

"Honestly, I'm not sure about this idea that training requires more coordination than actual combat..."

"Heh heh, you might be right about that. Honestly, training was several times tougher than actual combat."

Johanna and the others each spoke their thoughts, and Rent's exasperated gaze turned toward Rei.

Despite the cold winter sky, steam rose from Rent's body as he moved around intensely, lending his gaze an unassuming yet compelling weight.

"Rei, what kind of training were you doing?"

"It wasn't that intense, really?"

'Bullshit!'

As Rei muttered while stowing Gamelion's corpse into the Misty Ring, Johanna and the others immediately voiced their dissent.

Their voices were perfectly in unison, perfectly reflecting Johanna and the others' feelings.

Still, Rei shrugged indifferently, seemingly unconcerned.

"Well, well. Never thought my former subordinates would say something like this."

"...If they say that training wasn't strict, I suppose it's understandable."

One of the former guerrilla fighters murmured thoughtfully.

"Rei, what the hell... what kind of training did you actually do?"

At Rent's words, Rei shook his head as if to say he had nothing to feel guilty about.

"Personally, I didn't think it was that harsh. Besides, don't you think it's a good thing that training is tougher than actual combat? It means you can survive in battle without having to push yourself to your absolute limit during training."

"Well... I won't deny that."

"Right? ...Anyway, that aside. That's the second one... well, technically one and a half Gamelions. Should we keep looking? I think we can hunt Gamelions now, so how many can one party hunt again?"

At Rei's words, Rent looked up at the sky.

Spreading out above them was a wintery overcast sky.

Clouds that could bring rain or snow at any moment.

"The limit is five, so we should probably hunt another two or three. But if we get caught in rain or snow on the way to Gilm, it'd be a real pain. What do you think?"

"I think Set-chan wouldn't mind snow or anything."

"No, I'm not talking about Set. I'm talking about us."

Johanna immediately steered the conversation back to Set, prompting a mutter from the former guerrilla fighter.

But Johanna's fondness for Set was well-known among the former guerrilla fighters, which was precisely why it was left at that.

"Then, should we look for Gamelion a little longer?"

"Please."

Rei bowed his head at the words coming from Rei's mouth, but Rei didn't seem particularly bothered. He shook his head sideways before speaking.

"Don't worry about it. As long as we get paid properly, we don't mind a little trouble."

Knowing Rei normally, those words would have been somewhat surprising.

That must mean the cheese Rent had fed him was truly delicious.

For the sake of eating that cheese, a little trouble meant nothing to Rei.

Rei had found that cheese to be worth declaring it so unequivocally.

(I don't think the way cheese is made changes much... but what could have made such a difference in the taste?)

Of course, Rei didn't know the details of cheese-making.

At most, he'd seen it on TV back in Japan, or made cottage cheese with lemon in milk during a school class.

That's precisely why he couldn't figure out how they achieved such a rich, lingering flavor.

(Could it be using monster ingredients? ...Yeah, that actually sounds plausible.)

He nodded to himself, satisfied with the thought that flashed through his mind.

"Alright, I'll head back to where Set is. Wait here for a bit."

"Ah, Rei-san. Should we wait here? Or head toward the carriage?"

"Ah... Yeah, then please head to the carriage. If you're riding Set, no one will be watching the carriage. You must be tired from the battle, so take your time coming. I don't think monsters will attack a place that smells like Set."

Saying this, Rei left Rent, Johanna, and the others behind and started running toward the carriage.

He deliberately avoided mentioning the near-attack by the adventurers.

"Whoa... That's just Rei for you. He's not just fast, he's something else."

The former guerrilla fighter muttered involuntarily as Rei vanished from sight in an instant.

Though none of those present voiced it, they all shared the same thought.

(Just knowing we won't have to fight against Rei-san if war breaks out makes coming from the Bestir Empire to the Mireana Kingdom worth it.)

The three former guerrilla fighters each came to the same conclusion, satisfied.

...In Johanna's case, since her primary reason for coming to Gilm was Set, she didn't feel quite as strongly about it.

"Well then, we can't stay here forever. Let's head back to the carriage."

At Rent's words, everyone nodded and left the site where the battle with Gamelion had taken place.

Nearby lay many goblin corpses devoured by Gamelion, the area permeated by a strong stench of blood.

If any monsters caught this scent, it wouldn't be surprising if they came here.

Though, monsters that actually enjoy eating goblins probably weren't that common.

Given the nature of the place, it was indeed best to leave as quickly as possible.

Unless you wanted to get dragged into another pointless fight.

"Hmm. Looking down from the sky like this, it's clear a lot of people are after Gamelion. Probably because the season's getting late, and they want to make as much as they can while they still can..."

Rei murmured as she looked down at the ground from atop Set's back.

Some adventurers were moving Gamelions loaded onto carts, while others had already started bleeding them out and butchering them on the spot.

And then there were adventurers currently locked in battle with Gamelion.

"This is definitely the peak time. Well, with this weather, it wouldn't be surprising if it started snowing heavily any moment now."

Rei murmurs, gazing up at the sky.

Unlike last year, the snow had started late this year.

Considering the Gamelion situation, that was fortunate.

If it started snowing, they'd have to fight Gamelion in the snow.

Even though it's grassland, what lies beneath is ground. Naturally, snow would turn it to mud, making it impossible to get a solid footing.

There was also the danger of wet grass tangling around his feet.

Fighting an opponent like Gamelion, capable of swift movement, in such terrain would clearly be a struggle unless there was a significant difference in skill.

Rei's expression briefly turned serious, but the next moment, he wiped that look clean off his face.

"Well, we've got enough Gamelion meat for ourselves... no need to get that desperate."

"GRRR!"

Set growled, his throat rumbling with hunger for Gamelion meat!

Running his hand over the back of Set's neck, Rei spoke.

"Yeah, the guild should have finished most of the butchering by now. Even if they haven't, it might be a good idea to pick up a bit of Gamelion meat for us to eat."

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set let out a growl, his voice dripping with delight at Rei's words.

Hearing that growl, several Gamelions sensed danger and immediately fled the scene... but Set and Rei, oblivious to that, were in high spirits thinking about Gamelions' meat.

It was delicious simply grilled as steak, and simmering it would enhance the flavor even more.

The sandwich made with Gamelion meat they'd eaten a little while ago was a prime example of that.

Despite being a simple dish of just meat between bread... no, precisely because it's simple, the flavor of the ingredient comes through directly.

Furthermore, steaming it to remove excess fat before serving it with a sweet-and-sour fruit sauce or a rich walnut sauce would undoubtedly make it absolutely exquisite.

"Oh, crap. I'm suddenly craving Gamelion meat like crazy."

The more I imagined Gamelion dishes, the louder my stomach growled in protest.

"Growl."

It wasn't just Rei; Set must have felt the same. His throat rumbled, saying he was hungry.

"For now, let's just eat this and hold off."

He pulled out some dried oak meat from his Misty Ring and handed it to Set, then shoved a piece into his own mouth.

The concentrated savory flavor of the meat spread through his mouth, somehow suppressing the demanding growls of his stomach.

But it only lasted a little while.

If anything, eating the jerky only made his appetite grow even stronger.

"Hmm, can't be helped. I'll just eat a little more."

"Gurrr!"

"Agreed!" Set growls energetically.

Seeing his companion like that, Rei let out a small smile.

Next, he pulled out a sandwich with a chunk of stewed meat sandwiched inside.

...Considering the size of the meat chunk and the thinness of the bread sandwiching it, the balance was clearly off.

But it was also true that the sheer size of the filling, so disproportionate it was unbalanced, only served to whet one's appetite even more.

In fact, since the freshly made sandwich had been stored inside the Misty Ring, the meat between the bread was still warm.

You could practically feel the warmth of the meat through the bread.

Of course, Set was flying at an altitude of about 100 meters. And it was winter, not exactly clear weather.

Given these conditions, the sandwich taken out of the Misty Ring cools rapidly.

"Well, well, well... The Dragon Robe's temperature control function is handy, but it feels a bit wasteful in a situation like this. If only it worked not just on me, but on the things I carry too."

He handed one to Set, then took a bite of his own sandwich, which held a large chunk of meat. Juicy flavor spread throughout his mouth.

Simultaneously, the freshly baked bread soaked up the juices, while the crisp leafy greens provided a delightful new texture.

"Mmm, delicious. ...Still, the bread feels a bit lacking on its own, but this is fine as it is."

"Growl!"

Though more than half of the sandwich remained in Rei's hand, Set, who had already finished eating, growled, demanding more!

Seeing Set like that, Rei was about to offer a new sandwich... when he noticed Gamelion walking on the ground.

Fortunately, the other adventurers hadn't spotted him yet. If they returned now, Johanna and Rent might even get there first.

"Sorry, Set. The sandwich will have to wait for now. Hold out with this."

He gave Set the last of his sandwich. Set happily purred, "That's fine!"

"Alright, let's head back to the carriage. We want to get our hands on it before the others do."

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set let out a high-pitched growl at Rei's words, then flapped his wings and headed straight back toward the carriage.
