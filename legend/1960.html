Looking at the earthen wall stretching out before her, Vihera spoke with admiration.

“I see. With this, the Kobolds won’t be able to just waltz into Gilm so easily. Though, as Rei said, it wouldn’t be that hard to climb over from the Gilm side.”

“Yeah. I never thought they’d actually try something like that. …Besides, even if we leave here, it’s winter, you know? Many of the monsters unique to winter are stronger than ordinary ones. Yet, if they tried something like that… it would be nothing short of suicide.”

"I suppose so. But if staying here in Gilm means getting captured, that changes things, doesn’t it?"

“That’s true.”

They intentionally summoned monsters to Gilm—specifically low-rank monsters like Kobolds—and sacrificed a human offering to those dangerously powerful tentacles for that very purpose.

If they were caught, they would almost certainly face the death penalty.

...No, there was even a possibility that the death penalty would be the lightest punishment.

Considering that, it wouldn’t be strange for them to take a gamble and climb over this earthen wall to get out of Gilm.

(Besides, there weren’t many Kobolds today. In other words, even if I escaped from here, the chances of being attacked by Kobolds like before would be low. It wouldn’t be strange to think that way.)

As I stood there thinking this, staring at the earthen wall, I suddenly heard the screech of a monster coming from the other side.

“Could it be that the Kobolds have arrived?”

Rei had just thought there were few Kobolds, but now they’ve appeared as if to prove him wrong.

Just as he was thinking that, ignoring Rei’s reaction, Vihera kicked off the ground and landed on top of the earthen wall.

Her movement, which seemed to defy gravity, was truly awe-inspiring.

“Oh, it’s not a Kobold—it’s a goblin.”

“Goblins, huh. They probably came out of curiosity because the Kobolds used to be here every day but are gone now.”

“So, the Kobold was here all this time because there was something tasty to be had, and now that the Kobold’s gone, they’re thinking, ‘This is our chance’…?”

“Probably. We can’t read a goblin’s mind, after all.”

In fact, it’s not uncommon for goblins to do things that make observers wonder, “Why on earth would they do that?”

Rei has heard countless stories of people getting curious about such goblins and ending up with unexpected injuries...

“So? What are we going to do? Are we going to deal with the goblins?”

“No way, that’s too much trouble.”

Vihera said this matter-of-factly, then kicked off the earthen wall with a movement that defied gravity and landed on the ground.

“I knew you’d say that. …Though things might have been different if there’d been a rare or high-ranking goblin around.”

“GYAAAAAAAAAAAAA!”

“Gya-gya-gyu!”

“Gyu-o-gyu-gyu-gyu!”

As if to interrupt Rei’s words, the goblins’ screams grew louder.

Why on earth? Rei wondered, but upon reflection, since Vihera was standing on top of the earthen wall, it was only natural that the goblins on the other side could see her… If the goblins saw Vihera, it was only natural that they would make a commotion like this.

After all, to the goblins, Vihera was the ultimate prey. …Whether or not they could actually take her down was another matter.

“You’re quite the popular one, aren’t you?”

At Rei’s slightly teasing remark, Vihera opened her mouth with a look of displeasure.

“I’m not exactly thrilled to be liked by goblins like that. If only they were stronger monsters, that might be different.”

“First a Kobold, and now a goblin. …Rather than being courted by any more strange creatures, shall we head back to town? We should probably go pick up Set, too.”

There was still some time before their agreed-upon meeting, but since Set had split off from them on the way here to play with the children, it would be best to go pick him up as soon as possible.

Since it was certain there would be a big commotion this afternoon, they also needed to eat beforehand.

Vihera thought for a moment at Rei’s words… and eventually nodded.

“You’re right. It’s better to look forward to the tough opponent this afternoon than the goblin right in front of us. Well then, let’s go. What are we having for lunch today?”

“I wonder. It is winter, after all, so I think something warm would be best.”

Rei said this, though thanks to the Dragon Robe, he didn’t feel the cold at all.

It wasn’t snowing right now, but since it was winter, the temperature was still extremely low.

...Still, it’s only natural to want to eat a hot meal in winter.

(Sukiyaki, maybe… but I don’t have the ingredients. I don’t even have soy sauce. And anyway, how do you make sukiyaki sauce? Sugar and… sake? Besides, I don’t have any of the other ingredients either.)

Just thinking about it made Rei crave sukiyaki, but since he couldn’t get the ingredients in the first place, there was nothing he could do.

Giving up on thinking about it any further, he and Vihera turned away from the earthen wall.

From beyond the earthen wall, the goblins’ excited cries echoed as usual, but Rei and Vihera ignored them.

“Come to think of it, since no Kobolds have shown up today, the guys who took on Kobold extermination requests are probably going to miss out on their earnings today.”

“That might be true. But wouldn’t it be a problem if there weren’t any of those people? Oh, but if the Kobolds aren’t showing up, maybe we don’t need to worry about that.”

“Hmm, if the Kobolds don’t show up at all from now on, that’d be fine. But if it’s because of the damage I dealt to that tentacle yesterday, I don’t know how long that’ll last.”

"True. If none of us have accepted a quest to hunt Kobolds and then, suddenly, Kobolds show up again... that would be a real disaster."

If no one is here specifically to hunt down the Kobolds, any Kobolds that manage to scale the earthen wall and enter Gilm would undoubtedly pass through the construction zone and end up in the crowded city center.

And if they encountered people who had no means to fight back… Rei could easily imagine what the outcome would be.

After all, Gilm is on the frontier, and there are many former adventurers here.

While there are more people capable of fighting here than in ordinary towns or villages, not everyone is like that, and if they were to encounter children, there’s no need to even think about it.

“Well, for that reason, some kind of guard is absolutely essential. Even before I built the earthen walls… even before the Kobolds started appearing in droves, we were already keeping watch to make sure monsters didn’t enter Gilm from the expansion site.”

As they talked, Rei and Vihera made their way past the construction site... and when they reached the town center, they spotted Set playing with the children.

Since it was near the construction site, there were many vacant lots in the surrounding area.

That’s exactly why it was a perfect spot for Set and the children to play.

“How heartwarming.”

Watching the children playing tag with Set, Vihera murmured.

Of course, those words were likely only possible because she knew who Set was.

If someone who knew nothing—someone seeing Rei and Set for the first time—were to witness this scene, they would almost certainly conclude that the children were being attacked by Set… by a griffin.

That said, there’s probably almost no one in Gilm who doesn’t know about Set.

“That’s right. I absolutely cannot let a Kobold attack ruin this scene where so many people look up to Set.”

For Rei, who considers this place his base—his home, so to speak—he could never tolerate the Kobolds running amok here… nor the pink tentacles summoning them.

That’s exactly why this incident had him feeling reasonably motivated.

...Though it was possible that the fact he’d expected the means of controlling the Kobolds to be a magic item, only to discover it was actually some kind of ritual—a completely different method—might have played a part in it.

“Grr? Grrrrrrr!”

He must have noticed Rei and Vihera talking.

Set, who had been playing with the children, looked over at them and purred happily.

And then. In stark contrast to Set’s joy, the children wore disappointed expressions.

Originally, the agreement had been that they could only play with Set while Rei was checking on the earthen wall.

Given that, everyone understood that since Rei had returned, their playtime with Set was over.

They wanted to play some more, but none of them would voice that desire or act selfishly.

They knew that if they said such a thing now, they wouldn’t be allowed to join in the next time they played with Set… and more than anything, they’d seen it happen before.

In the end, that child was forgiven after apologizing to Rei and Set, but the honest truth was that the other children didn’t want to make the same mistake.

“Sorry, guys, we’ve got things to do, so we’re going to head out. If you get the chance, please play with Set again.”

“Grr…”

As if in response to Rei’s words, Set growled.

The children waved goodbye, looking a little disappointed, and ran off from the spot to find something new to play.

Relieved that they weren’t heading toward the construction site, Rei stroked Set.

“Gurr, gurrr, gurrrr!”

Perhaps happy to be petted by Rei, Set purred contentedly.

From Set’s perspective, he’s happy to be petted by the children he’d been playing with earlier, or by anyone who pays him plenty of attention, but being petted by Rei is undoubtedly what makes him happiest.

Set rubbed his head against her as if to say, “Pet me some more.”

Rei smiles at the sight of Set and continues to pet him...

“Hey, this is nice, but shouldn’t we go eat soon? Things are going to get busy this afternoon, so I’d like to eat as early as possible if we can.”

Vihera says, sounding a little displeased.

It’s not that she’s jealous of Set, but seeing him being petted while she’s right there beside him does make her feel a certain way.

...Normally, one would call that jealousy, but Vihera herself didn’t seem to realize it.

And Rei, who had been petting—or rather, doting on—Set, didn’t seem to notice it either; she simply accepted it as a matter of course.

“Right. Well then, shall we go eat? …What do you feel like having?”

“I suppose so. It looks like we’ll be moving around quite a bit this afternoon, so I’d like a hearty meal.”

Normally, since they were about to engage in intense physical activity, they would have opted for a light meal… but for Vihera, it was different.

That said, Rei agreed with Vihera’s suggestion, and since Set also wanted to eat if he could, no one objected to Vihera’s idea.

“In that case, we’ll need a proper restaurant, not just sandwiches or skewers. It’s not even a matter of deciding what to eat…”

Rei, having been thinking about sukiyaki earlier, was in the mood for some kind of hot pot dish.

“Yeah. It’s winter, so how about a piping hot stew? One packed with ingredients—not the kind you just drink, but the kind you actually eat.”

“Gurrr!”

Perhaps reacting to the phrase “stew you can eat,” Set let out a happy purr.

It seemed that, for Set, the act of eating was preferable to drinking.

“Stew, huh? It’s cold out, so that sounds good. Besides the stew, I’d like to order some bread or maybe some other dishes to go with it.”

“Yeah. Maybe some stir-fried meat and vegetables with bread… or udon would be nice, too.”

“I’d love to have a meat bun for the first time in a while… but I guess that’s a bit of a stretch.”

The dishes developed with Rei’s help—starting with the meat buns—are quite popular in Gilm, partly because Rei naturally taught them that these were winter dishes.

However, the original shop—the one where Rei taught the recipes directly—naturally enjoys immense popularity.

To buy from that shop, you have to wait in line, and even then, you can’t buy enough to satisfy Rei and the others.

(If sukiyaki’s out of the question, maybe oden… Hmm, I wonder how they make the broth for oden?)

When Rei was in Japan, she used a pre-made oden stock mix.

That’s exactly why Rei doesn’t know the specifics of how to make oden broth.

(Fish cake, beef tendon, shirataki noodles, potatoes, daikon radish, eggs… and then, finally, pouring the oden broth over rice… Ah, I’m really starting to crave oden.)

While deeply longing for oden... in the end, just like with sukiyaki, she didn’t know how to make it, so she gave up with a heavy heart.
