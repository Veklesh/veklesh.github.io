After severing the dungeon’s core with the Death Scythe and leveling up his terrain manipulation skill, he stored the core inside the Misty Ring.

Then, with Rei on his back, Set immediately flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

A few seconds after rising above the water’s surface… Marina, having confirmed this, released her spirit magic, and water began to flow down where a path had existed just a moment ago.

Whether it was due to the presence of the dungeon core or because Garangiga had made it his home… the exact circumstances were unclear, but even for Marina—whose skill with spirit magic was first-rate… no, superlative—manipulating the water of this subterranean lake was extremely difficult.

If anything, it was precisely because she was Marina that she was able to control it.

If this had been just any ordinary spirit mage, they would never have been able to pull off such a feat.

...Though, of course, mages themselves are extremely rare, and among them, spirit mages are even rarer.

When they returned to where Elena and the others were waiting, they were greeted by Marina, who looked exhausted.

Controlling the water of the subterranean lake must have been that taxing.

“Sorry about that.”

“It’s fine. But, just to be sure… you did accomplish your objective, right? If you made me go through all this and then failed to accomplish it…”

“Yeah. Of course, that part’s all good.”

Without waiting for Marina to finish her sentence—or rather, unable to wait—he replied.

Hearing that, Marina broke into a beaming smile.

It wasn’t her usual seductive smile, the kind that radiates an intense feminine allure, but a smile that looked both happy and delighted.

It was the kind of smile that made even the person watching feel happy without even realizing it.

“Hey, Rei. So… does that mean we’ve cleared the dungeon? Is that okay to assume?”

Amidst all this, Relieu asked, looking as if she were treading on thin ice.

This demeanor, unimaginable given his usual attitude, was vastly different from the Relieu we knew.

But that was only natural.

For an adventurer, clearing a dungeon holds great significance.

Since the number of dungeons is limited, clearing one is no easy feat.

This holds true even for Relieu, a Rank A adventurer with a nickname.

And considering that Relieu is essentially a solo adventurer, it’s fair to say that clearing a dungeon is nearly impossible.

It was precisely because she understood this that she seemed almost unable to believe she had actually cleared a dungeon.

“Whoa… I really… I actually cleared a dungeon…”

“No, since you ended up taking on this dungeon with us, you don’t need to get that excited…”

Relieu, overjoyed from the bottom of his heart, didn’t seem to hear Rei’s words; he was simply basking in his own joy.

Seeing Relieu like that, Rei decided to let him bask in the happiness of having cleared the dungeon for now, and turned to Marina.

“Are you okay?”

“I’m a little tired, but it’s not a big deal. Besides, we can’t just stay here like this forever, can we? This dungeon was unique in many ways, and considering that its core has been destroyed, we have no idea what might happen next.”

As Marina spoke, looking around with concern, Elena approached her.

“You’re right, this dungeon is quite unique in many ways. …Though, the dungeon itself is unique, of course. In any case, I agree that we should get out of here as soon as possible.”

“...The battle with Garangiga wasn’t that intense, so I’m a little unsatisfied... but I guess it can’t be helped.”

Vihera also seems slightly dissatisfied, but she says she has no objection to leaving the dungeon.

The others also had no objections to leaving the dungeon as soon as possible...

“Relieu, it’s time to get out. If we stay here much longer, we can’t rule out the possibility of the dungeon collapsing or something.”

At Marina’s words, Relieu, who had been lost in the euphoria of having cleared the dungeon, snaps back to reality.

The fact that she could snap back to her usual self in an instant was a testament to Relieu’s strength and mental fortitude.

“Oh, sorry, Marina. I got a little carried away.”

“I understand how you feel, but right now we need to get out of here as soon as possible. I think we’ll probably be fine, but we also need to prepare for the worst-case scenario.”

Hearing the words “worst-case scenario,” Relieu’s expression hardened.

“Got it. Let’s get out of here as soon as possible. …Rei, we can just go back the way we came, right?”

Although phrased as a question, it was more of a confirmation.

In fact, considering the best way to escape the dungeon as quickly as possible, that was undoubtedly the best choice.

If they had to navigate a corridor they’d never traveled before in this situation, there was no telling how long it would take.

If that passage were rigged with as many traps as the one Rei and the others had just come through, there was no denying the possibility that something might go wrong in the dungeon while they were disarming them.

There was simply no time to go through the process of disarming traps and honing Byune’s skills as a thief, as they had done in the passageway Rei and the others had just come through.

This was a dungeon inside a cliff… and what’s more, the space inside was clearly distorted.

That much was certain, given the existence of the subterranean lake and the vast forest.

(Come to think of it, we’re calling it an underground lake… but this place isn’t actually underground. Ah, but since it was inside a dungeon—a cave—maybe calling it an underground lake is fine after all?)

Wondering belatedly if “underground lake” was the right term, Rei and the others immediately left the spot.

……If anyone other than Rei and her group had cleared this dungeon, it would have been absolutely impossible to carry out Galangiga’s corpse.

To carry out that entire massive body, they would have had to cut Garangiga into pieces or something similar and haul it out of the dungeon.

Since that’s not realistic, they’d probably just take the parts that could be sold as rare materials… or something like that.

In that sense, it’s fair to say that Rei, who possesses the Misty Ring, has an advantage that borders on being underhanded.

“We’re going to rush through this. Any objections?”

At Rei’s words, no one seemed inclined to speak up.

Given the current state of this dungeon, it wouldn’t be surprising if it collapsed in the worst-case scenario.

Given that, there was no way anyone would object to Rei’s suggestion to escape the dungeon as quickly as possible.

Although they were somewhat fatigued from the battle with Garangiga, Rei and the others quickly retraced their steps along the passageway they had come through.

On the way in, they’d spent a considerable amount of time discovering and disarming traps… but now that all the traps had been cleared, there was no need to slow down.

(Come to think of it, having Byune disarm the traps was the right call after all. Thanks to that, we can move forward without worrying about traps. …Well, since we don’t know when they might be reset, we can’t be completely at ease, but…)

Even so, Rei judged that the likelihood of traps being set in the passage again was low.

If this were after they’d camped by the underground lake for a night, they’d have had to worry about that, but it hadn’t been that long since Rei and the others had passed through this passageway.

So, how exactly were the traps in this dungeon set? Were they set by monsters like goblins, kobolds, and orcs, or were they triggered automatically by the power of the dungeon’s core?

He didn’t know the details, but Rei’s guess was that they’d probably be fine now… and so far, his prediction had been spot on.

“Oh, the exit!”

Relieu, running in the lead, shouted.

Just as she said, the area ahead—where a vast number of rock plants had once stood—came into view.

And as they exited the passageway...

“Huh!? Stop!”

In an instant, Relieu, who had been running in the lead, shouted.

Her voice was filled with tension.

Sensing that, everyone who had been running came to a halt.

What happened? I couldn’t help but wonder, but since Relieu was that tense, there was no doubt something had happened.

And what Rei and the others saw when they stopped...

“Ugh… No way.”

Just a moment ago… yes, just a moment ago, they had stored the rock plants in the Misty Ring, yet there they were again, growing in vast numbers.

Of course, they weren’t densely packed like when Rei and the others had defeated them earlier.

But even so, the rock plants, which had grown to a certain size, could only be described as eerie.

“Their regenerative ability is way too fast. I mean, I pulled most of them out by the roots, so how did they grow this much?”

Rei stared at the rock plants, puzzled, but right now, he needed to escape the dungeon as quickly as possible.

Given that, he had to find a way out of this situation…

“I guess I have no choice but to force my way through. I’ll just have to hope those rock plants don’t attack us…”

Rei’s gaze turned to Marina, but given how exhausted she was from using spirit magic on the underground lake, he couldn’t push her too hard here.

That said, being attacked from behind at will would be an absolute nightmare…

“I’ll straddle Set’s back facing backward and stay at the rear, either endlessly blocking attacks or launching attacks from here.”

“Is that okay?”

Elena turned to ask, but Rei nodded, indicating it was fine.

His stamina had been depleted to a considerable degree from the battle with Garangiga, but since they were escaping the dungeon, some degree of pushing himself was necessary.

...Given that a single hit from a Garangiga could be fatal, even if the actual damage was practically nonexistent, the mental strain was by no means small.

Even so, Rei would undoubtedly assert that, compared to the battle against the Silver Lion, this was definitely an easier fight.

The others seemed to have various things they wanted to say to Rei, but they understood that, for now, the priority was to escape the dungeon as quickly as possible.

In the end, they decided to press on without stopping, and Rei and the others plunged into a space overgrown with rock plants.

Only Rei was at the rear, riding on Set’s back in reverse—a slightly absurd sight—but considering the attacks from the rock plants… particularly their primary long-range attacks with rock leaves, it was a natural position. But…

“Hm?”

Although Rei was wielding the Twilight Spear—which was easier to handle than his usual Death Scythe—for some reason, the rock plants weren’t attacking him at all.

It seemed they were simply going to keep running through the area filled with rock plants—in a situation where there was truly no problem at all.

(What’s going on? Why aren’t they attacking? …Do the rock plants need to grow to a certain size before they can attack? No, if that were the case, I wouldn’t understand why the rock plants on the first floor attacked. The only other possibility is the influence of the dungeon’s core?)

Rei was puzzled, but the fact remained that if they weren’t attacking, that was certainly for the best.

“The rock plants aren’t attacking! Keep going straight toward the stairs!”

Straddling Set’s back to keep an eye on their rear, Rei shouted.

And so... in the end, without being attacked by the rock plants at all, Rei and the others safely reached the stairs.

Naturally, they didn’t slow down upon reaching the stairs; they simply kept climbing with the same momentum.

It wasn’t until they emerged into the forest that they finally slowed down.

Even so, they didn’t stop to rest but kept moving forward—likely because everyone understood that the current situation was fraught with danger.

Rei, who had been riding on Set’s back, had now dismounted and was moving through the forest.

“Why don’t we use the Set Cage?”

It had been a few minutes since they returned to the floor with the forest. Suddenly, Rei muttered.

In fact, this forest level is quite vast, and it takes a fair amount of time just to get to the stairs.

But considering Set’s speed, it wouldn’t take him long to get there.

That was the reasoning behind Rei’s suggestion… but, naturally, there were those who objected.

While riding in the Set Cage, only Rei and Set would be able to fight back if attacked.

Moreover, the Set Cage’s camouflage ability is ineffective against monsters flying in the air.

Given that, Relieu objected, saying it was too dangerous... but the others, who had complete faith in Rei and Set’s abilities, agreed with the idea, leaving Relieu with no choice but to go along.

And so, in the end, they used the Set Cage… moving all the way to the stairs in one go and quickly breaking through the cave-like area, allowing Rei and the others to safely escape the dungeon.
