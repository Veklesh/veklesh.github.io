"...I never imagined this one would be finished first."

Looking at the object before him, Rei voiced his surprise to the person who had made it... Azimov.

While staying at the inn, Azimov had come by and insisted on bringing him there, saying the magic item he'd been asked to make was finished. But what was there was something completely unexpected for Rei.

He'd assumed the Sleipnir Boots were finished, but what stood there was a kiln.

...Yes, it was the magic item Rei had asked Azimov to make for cooking during their camping trips.

Even though he'd requested it much later than the Sleipnir Boots, he never imagined this one would be finished first...

"Sleipnir Boots were already at a high level of completion to begin with. They won't be finished so easily. ...Of course, I have confidence in my own skill. I'll get them done by spring, so don't worry."

"Yeah, I believe you."

Among those Rei knew, Azimov was an alchemist of the highest caliber.

While his personality had its quirks, there was no doubt in Rei's mind that his alchemical skill placed him among the absolute best in Gilm.

After all, he had used numerous rare materials to create the Twilight Spear, a magic item of exceptionally high performance.

After seeing that, few could claim Azimov's skill was lacking.

"Seriously, whoever made your Sleipnir Boots is a real master craftsman, huh? While they don't have much expandability, they're built with incredibly intricate design. Honestly, they're so different from other Sleipnir Boots out there, even though they share the same name."

"That good?"

"Yeah. Honestly, even if I tried to make Sleipnir Boots from scratch, achieving that level of perfection would be nearly impossible."

His words betrayed a hint of regret, but more than that, they held an almost reverent admiration for the skill of the alchemist who made the Sleipnir Boots—Esta Nord.

(The fact that people don't recognize Esta's work... well, even if her name is known, her creations aren't exactly flooding the market.)

A member of the Zepairu lineage, she was an alchemist of unparalleled skill, even among the golden age when many were hailed as geniuses, prodigies, or exceptional talents.

Precisely because of this, while the name Esta Nord was known, magic items actually made by Esta were extremely rare.

...What kind of reaction would Azimov show if he knew that many of the magic items Rei possessed were handcrafted by Esta?

It crossed Rei's mind, but he decided voicing it would lead to all sorts of trouble and kept his mouth shut for now.

Instead, Rei refocused his gaze on the kiln before him.

It stood about one meter tall, shaped like a semi-circular hut, or perhaps a snow hut.

Its width exceeded two meters but fell short of three.

It was certainly not something portable.

It occupied a significant portion of the room, making it impossible to move it out the door.

It was clearly designed with Rei's Misty Ring in mind.

(Well, I suppose I'm the only one who'd request something like this, so it makes sense it's exclusively for me.)

Simple cooking utensils... well, groups like merchant caravans might carry them... no, even adventurers who care about cooking wouldn't be out of place with them.

In reality, few adventurers would carry around cooking gear that just gets in the way during quests. Still, Rei knew a few such peculiar... or rather, adventurers obsessed with cooking.

Even among them, though, you'd never find anyone carrying around a kiln this size.

(After all, a kiln this size isn't meant to be carried around; it's the sort of thing you install in a shop and use.)

While Rei gazed at the kiln with quiet admiration, Azimov continued explaining the Sleipnir Boots that Rei carried.

"So, you see. The Sleipnir Boots have a magic circulation rate and actual operational speed value that..." Hey, Rei. Are you listening?

"Nah, I'm not. Honestly, teaching me the structure of magic items is pointless... well, I won't say it's completely useless, but it's not exactly necessary, is it?"

"Hmph... You're such a dullard. You should appreciate the true value of the magic items you wield. The ones you possess are worth every bit of their worth."

Azimov looked slightly displeased, but he must have decided Rei had a point. He let out a small sigh before shifting his gaze away from the Sleipnir Boots and toward the kiln before him.

"This kiln... well, I haven't given it a specific name, and while it uses Silver Lion material, it's designed to hide that fact as much as possible. However, it was designed with Rei's use in mind."

"Designed with me in mind? Well, I did request it, so that's only natural, right?"

"Well, yes, but... This kiln is designed to run purely on magic power. You could use magic stones if you wanted, but it's inefficient. Only by powering it with magic can this kiln truly demonstrate its capabilities."

"...Why go to all that trouble?"

Of course, when Rei used this kiln himself, he intended to activate it using mana, not magic stones.

In that sense, it wasn't a problem. But just because Rei had it didn't mean he'd be the only one using the kiln.

To begin with, one reason Rei decided to build this kiln was because he planned to form a party starting in spring.

He thought it might be useful for cooking pizza or other dishes when camping with the party.

So why was it being designed with magic power activation as the default?

Rei is fine since he was always going to use it.

Marina the Dark Elf also has plenty of magic power.

Vihera also gained more magic power after the Silver Lion incident...

(Huh? Wait... Then isn't that fine? Byune needs to use magic stones, but honestly, we can't just leave the oven to Byune alone anyway.)

Of course, Rei didn't know if Azimov had fully considered all that when designing this kiln to rely on magic power. But looking only at the result, it was undeniably problem-free.

Moreover, if it was designed with magic-powered operation in mind, even if someone saw Rei's kiln and wanted it... most would likely give up in the end.

Then again, without an item box like Misty Ring, carrying such a kiln around would be impossible, so there's also the question of how many people would actually want one.

(If you had a carriage, you could probably load the kiln onto the cargo bed and use it as a mobile kiln... but considering the kiln's temperature, if you weren't careful, the carriage itself might be in danger. A cheaper version of the item box might make it possible, though.)

Rei couldn't tell if Azimov had thought everything through when he designed it this way.

Still, the fact remained that it meant the kiln wouldn't be targeted by those who might covet it, and that was a relief.

"Also, the reason it now runs solely on magic lies in this kiln's capabilities."

"...The kiln's capabilities? Does it do something besides baking food?"

"Yes. This kiln can automatically analyze the ingredients placed inside and bake them at the optimal temperature."

"...Well, that's quite the smart kiln."

Rei had assumed it was just an ordinary kiln, but then again, this was Azimov we're talking about—he'd probably given it some seriously over-the-top capabilities.

"Well, yeah. I got some help from the Manpukutei. But honestly, all I could manage was the standard dishes."

"Standard dishes?" Rei tilted his head in confusion.

"Yeah. Simply put, only the dishes the cooks at Manpukutei know how to make. So, if you want to cook something from another country or something, you'll have to carefully watch how long it needs to bake yourself. Plus, with my current skills, I can only manage standard cooking times."

"Not common dishes, but common cooking temperatures?"

At Rei's renewed confusion, Azimov nodded, looking slightly chagrinated.

"Unlike ordinary cooking, the heat control professional chefs use to make truly delicious dishes can be quite specialized. Well, I only learned about it myself while building this oven, so I'm not that detailed on it."

Having said that, Azimov explained what he'd seen demonstrated by the cook at Manpukutei... Dishot.

For standard cooking temperatures, this oven works perfectly fine.

But... to bring a dish to its absolute peak, the chef must judge the heat precisely for each specific dish.

Even with the same ingredients and size for the same dish, the oven temperature must be subtly adjusted based on the specific ingredients.

Moreover, achieving a state where the surface is thoroughly cooked while the inside is slightly more done than medium rare requires considerable skill.

In other words, this oven can produce dishes that are about average quality, but that's as far as it goes.

He meant that to create anything above average, you had to cook it yourself.

For Azimov, it must have been frustrating to be shown that his own skill wasn't sufficient.

Of course, there was also the issue that, after the incident with Sleipnir Boots, he couldn't focus solely on the kiln.

"Ah... well, I do want to eat delicious food, but even so, you can't just easily make the kind of dishes a professional chef creates, right?"

In stark contrast to Azimov's frustration, Rei accepted this so readily. For Rei, the kiln was merely a convenient tool to have around.

After all, if he wanted to eat dishes made by a professional chef like Dishot, he could simply buy freshly made food and put it in his Misty Ring.

Putting food directly into the Misty Ring meant extra costs like dishware and pot fees.

But the truth was, such costs didn't particularly bother Rei.

That's precisely why Rei doesn't feel nearly as frustrated as Azimov does.

Of course, he'd be happy to eat delicious food if he could.

"Just you wait, Rei. Someday I'll make a magic item oven with performance that'll astonish even professional chefs."

"Nah, this is fine for me. Just having it automatically adjust the oven temperature based on the dish is a huge help. Besides... it doesn't look like there's a place for firewood, so you don't need it, right?"

"Yeah, it generates heat directly using magic power or magic stones."

At those words, Rei's expression brightened just a little.

Of course, the Misty Ring contained a large amount of firewood.

But the amount of firewood needed for camping versus using in a kiln is obviously vastly different.

We're talking several times more... maybe even more than that.

Gathering or buying firewood wasn't particularly difficult, but if there was a way to avoid the hassle, it was only human nature to want to do so.

"That alone would be a huge help. ...And if you could get around to Sleipnir Boots as soon as possible, that would be great too."

"Of course. We both know that's the more important task. While I did get quite absorbed in this kiln, it was really more of a diversion."

"...Well, since it was a proper request, I'd appreciate it if you didn't call it a diversion."

Rei's words carried a hint of dissatisfaction, but he understood that given Azimov's personality, saying anything more would be futile.

Rei would prefer him to prioritize Sleipnir Boots... or so he thought.

But Azimov was fundamentally a free spirit, so Rei figured anything he said would be pointless anyway.

"Are you really okay with the Sleipnir Boots?"

"Yeah. I'll get them done by spring at the latest. That's for sure."

Hearing Rei say that much, Rei didn't press the matter further.

He knew Azimov was confident in his skills as an alchemist, and he knew Azimov was someone he could trust when it came to alchemy.

Though that also meant he couldn't be trusted for anything outside of alchemy.

In fact, without Pamidor's help, Azimov probably wouldn't have been able to lead a decent life.

...Considering Pamidor had settled in Gilm around the same time as Rei, it made one wonder what kind of life Azimov had led before that.

In any case, Rei thus succeeded in acquiring a high-performance magic item furnace.
