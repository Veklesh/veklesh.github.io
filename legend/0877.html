"So, was it okay? Leaving those two like that?"

After leaving the guild and traveling for some time, the knight finally asked Rei near the lord's mansion.

But Rei, the one being asked, simply stroked Set as he replied.

"Stopping Lenora in that state would be a bit difficult for me. Besides, if you felt that way, shouldn't you have said something sooner?"

"Sorry about that. Getting involved in a fight between women... no, a battle like that... nothing good ever comes of it."

The knight's correction from "quarrel" to "battle" hinted at his history.

Thinking that, Rei shook his head and spoke.

"Well, I feel the same way. Especially Lenora in that state—she's terrifying and impossible to handle."

"...I understand."

The knight murmured deeply, his words carrying a weight of profound understanding.

He found himself wondering what kind of life the knight walking beside him had led until now.

Perhaps sensing he was the subject of less-than-flattering curiosity, the knight changed the subject.

"Speaking of which, it's been the talk among the group—apparently there were Gamelions in the dungeon."

"Yeah, there were nearly fifty of them in the main chamber where the dungeon's core was."

"...Honestly, facing one or two Gamelions is one thing, but defeating fifty of them normally? That's astonishing. And Set wasn't there either, right?"

"The dungeon entrance was too small, so Set stayed outside to guard it, right?"

"Grrr..."

Set let out a disappointed growl at Rei's voice.

The knight couldn't help but smile at how adorable Set looked.

"What other impressions did you have?"

"What kind of impression, you say... Well, the only dungeons I've ever explored are ones far from here and the ones in the Labyrinth City of Exil. I don't think it'll be much help."

"No, no, most people don't explore multiple dungeons anyway."

Compared to when they first met at the guild, Rei felt that the knight seemed to have lost some of his usual dignity.

(Could this be his true self? Was he putting on a show at the guild?)

"Knights have it tough too..." Lay thought, recalling the knight's words about dungeons.

"Well... it was probably because it was newly opened, but there were a lot of goblins. If you count the enemies we defeated in the dungeon, I think there were more goblins than Gamelions."

"Goblins? I'd heard about that, but if you say it was that many, they must have been overwhelmingly numerous."

"For me, having an item box made it manageable, but for regular adventurers, weapon wear and tear must be brutal. There's the physical toll too."

The knight must have anticipated the difficulty of fighting alone. Hearing Rei's words, he nodded in understanding.

As they walked, discussing the dungeon, they eventually arrived at the lord's mansion.

Guards were stationed at the gate, but it was his fellow knight and acquaintance, Rei, who approached.

Perhaps because arrangements had been made beforehand, they exchanged brief greetings and entered the grounds without being made to wait.

"Grrrr..."

As usual, Set headed for the stables, while Rei and the knight, seemingly unfazed by his actions, entered the lord's manor.

For Set, this lord's mansion was like a second home... well, perhaps that was an exaggeration, but it was certainly a place he was very familiar with.

Indeed, the old stable manager showed no surprise at seeing Set... in fact, he even smiled as he ushered him into the stables.

For the old man entrusted with the stables, the horses inside were objects of affection, but also subjects requiring strict training.

Compared to them, Set was a presence entirely unrelated to the old man, someone he simply had to care for.

On top of that, with his round eyes and friendly demeanor, Set radiated pure adorableness, making the old man treat him almost like a grandchild.

Smiling, he greeted Set, gave him a few pieces of bread saved for when he got hungry, and stroked him.

"Gururuu"

"Mmm, mmm, is it tasty? Welcome back. You should stay a while today too."

Set generally warmed up to those who showed him affection.

He rubs his face against the old man, thoroughly enjoying the sensation of being stroked by those sinewy hands.

The feel varied slightly depending on whose hands stroked him, but the touch of this old man's hands didn't seem unpleasant to Set.

Of course, the hands that felt best to Set were Rei's.

Even so, the hands of this old man stroking him now, having handled animals for years, knew exactly where to press for the right spots. For Set, it was undoubtedly a pleasant time.

...Though it didn't take long before the old man's subordinate, who was in charge of this lodging, watched the two of them with a somewhat exasperated look, causing the embarrassed old man to snap back at him.

While Set and the old man were playing around, Rei was inside the lord's mansion.

It was the office he'd visited many times before and had grown accustomed to.

The only thing he still couldn't get used to was the sheer opulence of the door.

Sitting on a sofa in that very office, Rei faced Daskar.

Ranga was also present beside Rei.

The knight who had escorted Rei this far had already returned to his duties.

Tea and sandwiches prepared by a maid sat on the table, subtly indicating this was not an official meeting but Daskar's private quarters.

"So, the enemies that appeared in the dungeon were goblins, kobolds, ogres, orcs, and Gamelions—that's all of them?"

"That's correct. Though the ogre was only about half the size of the one we encountered before, and in terms of strength... well, saying it was weak might be a bit much, but it certainly wasn't strong."

"...Hmm. What do you think, Ranga?"

After taking a sip of tea, Daskar turned to Ranga in response to Rei's question.

"Well, I still think the biggest problem is that not much time has passed since the dungeon was created."

"You think that's also why the dungeon hasn't collapsed yet?"

"I'm not so sure about that. I do recall hearing a similar story before, about a dungeon core being brought out yet the dungeon itself remaining intact."

Daskar turned his gaze toward Ranga, who spoke as if recalling something.

Rei, who had been listening to the conversation, did the same.

"Really? Any details?"

"I'm afraid not. It was quite some time ago..."

"Hmm. But if the dungeon doesn't collapse, it's both troublesome and a relief."

"Both troublesome and helpful?"

Daskar nodded in response to Rei, who asked while reaching for a sandwich.

"If the dungeon remains intact, monsters or bandits might infiltrate the ruins and establish a base there. Well, it's unlikely anyone in these remote lands would have the guts to be a bandit, but still, the possibility isn't entirely zero."

"...I suppose so. We could station soldiers there, but maintaining the current scale indefinitely would be too much of a burden. On the other hand, if we reduce the numbers, someone might get caught off guard by monsters."

Listening to Daskar and Ranga, Rei nodded in agreement.

"Then what's the advantage?"

"This is the frontier. We can't rule out a massive monster outbreak. If that happens, this place could serve as a forward base."

"...That place?"

"Yes. Well, if we're going to use it for that purpose, we'll need to properly prepare the dungeon interior. As I mentioned, we'll also need to seal the entrance securely to prevent monsters from entering. As for thieves... well, we probably don't need to worry about them much in this frontier."

"As someone responsible for Gilm's security, I'd say thieves are just as dangerous as monsters, if not more so."

Ranga, who commanded Gilm's guards, muttered with a wry smile.

The fact he could get away with just a wry smile probably meant he understood that common thieves couldn't survive in this frontier.

Most bandits operate around places like Abero and Saburusta, which are also the entrances to the frontier.

Occasionally, some hot-blooded bandits venture near Gilm, but most end up as monster fodder. Even without that, Gilm is home to skilled adventurers. They get wiped out before long.

That's precisely why Ranga spoke those words.

If anything, Ranga should be wary of the bandits trying to infiltrate Gilm.

Gilm has its own slums, and such places tend to attract unsavory characters.

"Well, let's take a little more time to think about the dungeon. It's not like we have to decide right away. More importantly, Rei. You brought the core of the dungeon, right? Won't you show it to us?"

Daskar looked at him expectantly, but Rei shook his head at that gaze.

"I left the dungeon core with the alchemist, so I don't have it on me right now."

"The alchemist? Ah, right. You have a hobby of collecting magic items, don't you? Is that why?"

Daskar flashed a momentary look of disappointment, but a dungeon core was undoubtedly an extremely rare material, even if it was newly created.

"Are you going to use it to make some magic item?" Daskar asked. Rei shook his head again.

"Not making something new, but repairing... or rather, rebuilding? Something like that."

"Hmm, interesting. If you don't mind, tell me more."

Daskar set the teacup he'd been holding on the table and leaned forward, fixing his gaze on Rei.

"I don't think it's anything to get too worked up about. During the civil war in the Bestir Empire, when I fought the Noiz, I got my hands on a Magic Sword they used... but the blade was snapped clean in two. So I thought I'd have it reforged into a spear, a magic spear. I heard the dungeon's core might be usable for that, so I took it to an alchemist a blacksmith acquaintance introduced me to."

"...Don't go doing anything reckless again."

At Rei's words, Daskar muttered with a hint of exasperation.

Rei tilted his head at Daskar's reaction.

"Foolish, you say?"

"Yeah. Turning the blade of a regular longsword—not a magic item—into a spear isn't that unusual. But turning a Magic Sword into a magic spear... Well, it's not impossible, but without an exceptionally skilled alchemist and blacksmith, a top-tier Magic Sword could easily end up as a third- or fourth-rate magic spear. That's how difficult what you're proposing really is."

"...But the person in question was pretty enthusiastic about it."

Daskar's words suddenly made Rei feel uneasy.

Still, he didn't immediately try to return to Azimov's house because of one fact: Pamidor, who likely knew the ins and outs of the situation, had taken it upon himself to handle it.

He hadn't actually met Pamidor that many times.

But seeing the weapons he forged as a blacksmith, trusting the person who introduced him, witnessing how fondly he treated his son Kumito, and conversing with him himself, all reinforced Lay's belief that Pamidor was someone trustworthy.

Knowing Pamidor like that, he wouldn't claim to do something he couldn't possibly accomplish.

As for Azimov, I understood he was the sort of person you might call an alchemy fool, and precisely because of that, I knew he wouldn't take on something he couldn't do.

If he tried such a thing, he was certain Pamidor would met out severe punishment.

"Hmm, you're eager? Then you must be confident you can do it. May I ask what you asked the alchemist to do?"

"You said his name was Azimov, right?"

The moment Rei spoke those words, Daskar and Ranga, who had been listening with interest, widened their eyes slightly.

"I see, Azimov. ...Well, that alchemist could certainly manage something like that. Though getting him to accept the request might be tricky."

"Yes, that's right. Azimov is skilled, but... well..."

Watching the two exchange these words, Rei somehow understood what they meant.

Considering Azimov's personality, he probably wouldn't just take on a request that easily.

Being so moody, it must be difficult to get him in the right frame of mind.

"This time, it seems his interest in Set's feathers and fur, or the dungeon's core, was the deciding factor."

"...I see. In that case..."

Daskar muttered, and Ranga nodded in agreement.

It seemed these two also shared the view that Azimov was an alchemy nut.

After talking for a while longer, they learned that the group who had come with Rei to Gilm would be moving to the city tomorrow. They left their luggage stored in the Misty Ring and departed the lord's mansion.
