"Get away!" The guard who heard that shout never imagined it was a warning directed at him.

That's precisely why, when the shout came, he reflexively turned his gaze toward Daldora... toward the person he was supposed to protect.

Then he noticed Daldora looking at him, and only then did he realize the warning was meant for him.

Right beside him... that person who, just seconds ago, had looked like nothing more than a corpse, was now inexplicably right next to him, staring at him with clear murderous intent.

Why? He hadn't sensed a trace of their presence. Strange. Questionable.

Such fragmented thoughts flashed through his mind. Simultaneously, the corpse from seconds ago thrust the blade of the dagger it held toward the bodyguard's neck...

A strange, twanging sound echoed, and in the next instant, the figure who had been about to thrust the dagger at me vanished from before the guard's eyes.

"Huh!?"

Judging he was saved, he kicked off the floor almost reflexively, leaping backward from where he stood to create distance.

Only then did he grasp what had become of the man who had tried to kill him.

Numerous small, arrowhead-like objects were embedded in his body, bleeding profusely.

Despite bleeding from over a dozen wounds, including his face, the man in the white coat showed no sign of pain whatsoever. He stared intently at the man who was supposed to be his prey.

(What? He doesn't feel pain? So he's like those three who attacked today? No, but...)

Having finally regained his composure by creating distance, the man muttered inwardly as he watched the man in white at the end of his gaze.

By then, the other guards had already drawn their weapons and surrounded Daldora, while another guard positioned himself at the room's entrance, leaving the man in white with no escape route.

Yet despite this situation, the man pointed the dagger in his hand toward the guard he had failed to attack.

Seeing the man in white, Daldora and the other guards realized he was distinctly different from the three men who had attacked them earlier that day.

The three attackers who felt no pain had no light of will in their eyes.

But the man in white now standing before them possessed a firm, unyielding will in his eyes.

"...Who are you?"

Laying his hand on the Nebula Eye equipped beneath his Dragon Robe, Rei murmured.

To Rei, who didn't know the three men who had attacked Daldora, the man in white before him was simply an opponent who showed no sign of disturbance whatsoever, even after being struck by his attack...

(I've fought enemies immune to pain several times before... but I never imagined I'd face one in a place like this.)

The moment Rei released his grip from Nebra's eyes, his hand closed around the Twilight Spear he'd drawn from the Misty Ring.

The Death Scythe, designed primarily for slashing motions, was unsuitable for use within the somewhat spacious confines of the laboratory room.

He judged the Twilight Spear, which could focus on thrusting, would be more useful here.

If this were outdoors and collateral damage didn't matter, he would have drawn the Death Scythe.

Or perhaps dual spears... He thought this, then flashed a self-deprecating smile, knowing its refinement still wasn't high enough for actual combat.

Shifting focus from the fleeting thought, Rei, with the Twilight Spear in hand, stood beside the guard attacked by the man in white.

"Leave this to me. You take care of Daldora's escort."

"No, but..."

The guard couldn't immediately accept Rei's words. Despite being hired as a skilled adventurer, he hadn't noticed the white-robed man's attack at all.

The guard, likely wanting to redeem himself, hesitated. Rei, keeping the tip of the Twilight Spear pointed at the man in white who was slowly, inch by inch, moving closer, spoke.

"What is your true purpose? To defeat that man in white? Or... to protect Daldora, your charge?"

Faced with that question, the guard couldn't continue being stubborn.

He had been hired as Daldora's bodyguard, so naturally, her safety was his top priority.

"I understand. I'll leave it to you."

Muttering resentfully, the bodyguard turned back toward Daldora.

Confirming his retreat with a sideways glance, Rei relaxed and pointed the tip of his blade at the man in white.

He knew the guard possessed considerable strength.

But while he might be a threat outside, wielding the Twilight Spear in this lab—even if it was somewhat spacious—would only make him a hindrance.

...In the end, the reason Rei had mentioned Daldora's safety was precisely to ensure he wouldn't get in the way when she fought the man in the white coat.

The guard understood half of Rei's reasoning, but he also understood his own skills were nowhere near Rei's level, so he obediently went along with Rei's persuasion.

Though, admittedly, he was also eager to witness the true power of Rei, the one known as Crimson, with his own eyes.

"Now then, who are you? ...You could at least tell me that much, couldn't you?"

Rei spoke these words, Twilight Spear in hand, but the man in white said nothing.

His eyes held nothing but murderous intent as he fixed his gaze solely on Rei.

"Let me make this clear: I have no intention of letting you escape. If you calmly tell me why you attacked the lab, I'll treat you accordingly. It seems you lot were also behind the attacks on Augusto and Daldora today. Information is valuable, after all."

Rei declared this, though he was almost certain the man before him would never meekly obey his command.

Because within the eyes of the person before him, something unyielding like their own conviction shone.

This type of person rarely listens to what others say.

Proof of that was the fact they were ignoring Rei's words and still waiting silently for an opening.

"Damn it, no luck after all. ...Well, can't be helped. This might get a little rough, but you'll have to understand... okay!"

The moment the words left his mouth, a flash erupted from Rei's hand.

To Daldora, it appeared only as a flash of red light.

Even among the guards, almost none could clearly see the spear's movement with their own eyes.

Such was the speed of the strike.

The strike, truly deserving the name a crimson flash, headed straight for the man in white.

Of course, Rei had no intention of killing his opponent.

Given the urgent need for any information he could get, even if the man remained silent, this strike—aimed precisely at the man's hand... the hand gripping the dagger—might yield something.

"Guh!"

As an assassin who had remained at this location, the man in white likely had confidence in his own skill.

But... even so, he had unfortunately completely misjudged Rei's true strength.

The sharply thrust strike easily pierced the man's arm... no, thanks to the sharpness of the Twilight Spear and Rei's physical prowess, it cleanly severed the arm even without channeling magic.

The incision was so clean that for the first few seconds, the man in white didn't even realize his arm had been severed... and worse, not a drop of blood flowed.

"What!?"

A moment later, he realized his arm was gone, and simultaneously, blood gushed from the severed stump.

"Well, that takes care of your weapon. ...Next, your legs? Or your other hand? I'd rather avoid dealing with your head—I want to see what you do next."

"You... monster!"

The man in white screamed.

Rei ignored the scream and readied the Twilight Spear. Behind him, however, Daldora and the others gathered as her guards were nodding in agreement with the man in white's words for some reason.

That alone showed just how unbelievable the power, sharpness, and above all, the speed of the Twilight Spear's strike they'd just witnessed truly was.

"You've made that monster your enemy. You realize you have no chance of winning now, right? How about you surrender?"

"You fool! How could we possibly do such a thing?!"

Shouting, the man in white reached into his coat.

The instant he saw this, Rei stepped forward.

"Tch!"

What was unleashed wasn't a strike from the Twilight Spear, but a strike from Nebula's eyes.

Arrows, slicing through the air, were fired one after another at the man in white.

The arrows, launched with Rei's physical prowess, were impossible for the man in white to evade.

Multiple darts struck his remaining left arm, and the impact caused something to spill from the hand he kept tucked in his robe.

It looked like a glass bottle, yet strangely, it rolled across the floor without shattering.

Curious about the bottle's contents, Rei glanced in its direction for just an instant. The next moment, he swung Twilight Spear again.

This time, it wasn't a thrust, but a sweeping horizontal strike.

The swing, strangely enough, avoided destroying the desks and walls within the lab, heading straight for the man in the white coat.

The man in white seemed to grasp the blow's power and tried desperately to dodge... but even though it was a sweeping strike, not a thrust, its speed was far from easy to evade.

"Gah!"

Rei felt the sensation of shattering the man's ribs in his hand.

Blown away by that sensation, the man flew straight sideways... crashing into the wall, cracking it, before finally coming to a stop.

"W-wait... a human flew sideways... nearly several meters..."

A voice murmured from behind, one of Daldora's guards, but Rei paid it no mind as he approached the white-coated man who had slammed into the wall.

(Judging by that impact, he shouldn't be dead.)

He must have broken some ribs, but it shouldn't be a fatal wound.

He recognized this and observed the man, who showed no sign of movement... but the next moment, his expression shifted to one of utter incomprehension.

"Huh?"

Rei's utterly baffled voice must have puzzled Daldora's guards as well. Several of them approached Rei.

"What's wrong?"

"...Look at this."

At Rei's muttered words, one of the guards shifted his gaze toward the man in the white coat.

What they saw was the man vomiting a green liquid mixed with blood from his mouth.

His eyes were wide open, and he was clearly dead, no matter how you looked at it.

"He didn't die from Rei's strike... at least, that doesn't seem to be the case."

"Yeah."

Rei nodded immediately at the guard's words.

His strike possessed enough power to inflict severe injury on the opponent.

But it wasn't a blow lethal enough to take a life instantly.

"If he died from my blow, then what's that green liquid he's spewing from his mouth? ...And blood is flowing properly red from his mouth..."

"That... No, then what is this green liquid?"

"Well, don't ask me about that. I didn't do anything to him."

"You hit him with that spear, didn't you?"

That was the exchange between Rei and the others, but suddenly, something occurred to him.

Back in Japan, he'd seen in manga and novels that assassins would drink poison beforehand, then take an antidote if their mission succeeded.

(But even then, wouldn't they die too quickly? Then... suicide poison?)

He thought that far... and in the next instant, a slightly dazed voice escaped Rei's mouth, just like before.

"Ah..."

"Huh? What's wrong? Did you think of something?"

Naturally, such a sound couldn't go unnoticed by the guards nearby, prompting them to ask.

Rei looked troubled but opened his mouth.

"Could it be... just maybe... that he drank the suicide poison he had hidden in his back teeth? And not because he meant to drink it, but because the impact of my blow sending him crashing into the wall..."

"No, surely not... I can't imagine him dying from something so stupid, no matter what."

Swallowing the suicide poison from the impact of the collision, death.

From any angle, that would be nothing short of idiotic.

The guard said this, but when Rei's gaze shifted to the wall... the wall where the man in white had crashed into it, leaving a crack, he understood.

Normally, one wouldn't expect to endure an impact this severe.

Of course, not everyone would swallow poison if subjected to the same impact.

It was simply bad luck. That was the only way to put it.

"Setting that aside... so this guy attacked the lab after all? For that, it sure was a pretty anticlimactic end."

Well, since he's dead anyway, there's no point dwelling on it further.

As Rei muttered this to himself, the guard opened his mouth in exasperation.

"Listen, Rei, that's precisely why you could take him down so easily. Normally, he'd probably have given someone else a hard time."

"Is that so?"

"Yeah, exactly. ...You're already strong, Rei, and on top of that, you had such a powerful magic item. Honestly, I almost feel sorry for the enemy."

It was a remark devoid of any particular sentiment, considering his own faction's researchers had been slaughtered.

Rei laying eyes on him in question, the man shrugged slightly before speaking.

"I was only hired by Daldora-san recently. I hardly knew anyone here. ...More importantly, aren't you curious about that bottle this guy dropped?"

With that, the man walked over to the bottle lying on the floor.
