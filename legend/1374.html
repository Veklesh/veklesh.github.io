The sun had already set completely, and normally the gate would be closed at this hour.

However, thanks to the effect of the documents Kenny brought, the guard readily opened the main gate.

Of course, the fact that the documents bore not only Walker's signature but also Daskar's played a significant role. And the presence of Marina, the former guildmaster, along with Rei and Set—both notorious in their own ways—certainly contributed as well.

"Set, please."

"GRRRRRR!"

At Rei's command from his back, Set let out his usual growl, took a few running steps, flapped his wings, and soared upward.

After soaring into the sky, he descended again to pick up Marina and Vihera waiting below.

Normally, they would secure themselves with safety ropes, but this time, they wanted to reach Treant Forest as quickly as possible. More importantly, the travel time was only a few minutes, so they hadn't prepared any ropes.

As Set slowed his descent and flew close to the ground, Marina and Vihera timed their jumps perfectly, leaping onto his forelegs.

Set's balance faltered for just an instant, but he regained it the next moment and began flapping his wings.

Soaring high into the sky, Set flapped his wings toward Treant Forest.

Although the sun had fully set, it hadn't been that long since sunset.

Perhaps influenced by this, traces of the sunset lingered in the sky.

The night sky was cloudless, and gentle moonlight poured down upon the earth.

"What a beautiful view. If only nothing had happened... I wouldn't have anything to say."

Marina murmured, clinging to Set's leg with one hand.

She had changed into the clothes left in Vihera's room, wearing pants instead of her usual party dress.

Without a safety rope, she was clinging to Set's front legs with only the strength of her hands. If her grip slipped, death was certain.

Even knowing this, Marina showed no sign of tension.

"Yes. If possible, a date under the moonlight while watching the night view... I think that would be lovely."

Not only Marina, but Vihera also replied without showing any signs of tension.

There was not a trace of anxiety in either of them.

...No, perhaps because a battle awaited them, Vihera's eyes even held a hint of lust.

Lately, Vihera hadn't been able to enjoy a satisfying fight.

Of course, she had mock battles with Marina, combat training with Byune, and above all, mock battles with Rei.

But... those were only 'mock' battles.

While there was no fear of losing one's life like in a real battle, in exchange for that, one couldn't feel the genuine excitement and pleasure akin to actual combat.

As for Byune, it wasn't even a mock battle—it was merely combat training.

She certainly didn't dislike the time spent with Rei.

Even so, within Vihera burned a strong desire for combat.

She understood this mission was combat, albeit primarily as an escort. Yet the very notion of life-or-death fighting was enough to excite... no, arouse Vihera.

Rei, riding on Set's back, was naturally aware of this aspect of Vihera's nature, if you could call it that.

Yet Rei said nothing, because he understood that was part of who Vihera was.

Marina, having lived for a long time, has encountered many others with similar inclinations.

(Well, honestly, this opponent is a complete mystery. It probably won't be just Treant after all... ...Well, if it is Treant, then it is Treant, and we can get the magic stone, so that's fine.)

While these thoughts ran through her mind, Set continued forward, and soon a forest came into view on the ground below.

Bathed in moonlight, that forest was the Treant forest.

Though it had only been a few minutes of night flight, the moment Treant's forest came into view, Rei's eyes... and then Set, Marina, and Vihera's eyes too, were fixed on Treant's forest.

They immediately noticed the disturbance occurring within Treant's forest.

Several figures could be seen fighting near the outer edge of the Treant forest.

They were standing still before Treant.

It was clear they were the woodcutters led by Fekts, as they all carried axes.

Naturally, Set had spotted the situation even before Rei did, and he flapped his wings, descending toward the forest.

"Marina, Vihera, I've spotted what appear to be the woodcutters! Just as expected, they're under attack!"

At Rei's words, Marina and Vihera each wore different expressions as they turned their gaze toward the forest.

Time rewinds slightly.

Around the time Rei and the others took off from Gilm, Fekts was intercepting monsters alongside the woodcutters.

"Damn it all! What the hell are these things?!"

Shouting in frustration, Fekts swung his axe.

The axe severed the vines slithering like snakes at his feet.

The severed vine writhed like a snake, as if in its death throes... until it finally lost its strength and lay exposed on the ground.

"Fekts! What should we do?!"

"Just run! Run, dammit! Get some distance here and get out of the forest! If we do that, they probably won't chase us!"

At his comrade's voice, Fekts swung his axe at the vines closing in once more, shouting.

The axes Fekts and his companions wielded were, after all, logging axes.

They weren't battle axes modified for combat.

But... still, an axe is an axe, and it possessed a certain degree of offensive power.

Following Fekts' orders, the group ran desperately, trying to escape the forest.

Several factors worked in their favor: their background as lumberjacks meant they knew how to wield axes effectively against vegetation; unlike the party attacked within the forest, they hadn't been ambushed while sleeping; it hadn't been long since nightfall, so Fekts and his companions remained alert; and since their goal was to cut down trees, they were positioned near the forest's edge.

Thanks to these various reasons and sheer luck, fortunately, while some among Fekts and his companions were injured, none had lost their lives yet.

But the Fekts, fighting desperately, had no room for pride in that fact.

Now, they all had to cooperate and act solely to survive.

In this situation, it was fortunate for the woodcutters that Fekts, a central figure among the younger woodcutters, was present. ...Though, without Fekts, they probably wouldn't have made the mistake of coming to Treant Forest at night.

Regardless, for whatever reasons, it was undeniable that Fekts and his companions had survived.

But while they were all still alive now, no one knew how long that would last.

They should have been operating at the edge of the forest, yet that small distance refused to close.

Even in their current state, they were pushing close to their limits, and everyone was already injured to some degree. If things continued like this, casualties were certain not too far off.

Only now did they truly grasp how vital their escort adventurers had been... Understanding this, Fekts and the woodcutters still able to fight swung their axes.

Desperately... squeezing every last ounce of life from themselves, they kept running... until finally, the edge of the forest came into view.

We'll be saved.

That was the woodcutters' hope... but the next instant, the woodcutter leading the way stopped in his tracks.

Stopping abruptly after running at full speed naturally placed a tremendous strain on his body. His flesh, already fatigued from the battle, couldn't endure it, even for a brief moment. The man was pushed from behind and rolled across the ground.

The blade of the axe he held grazed his cheek, but he managed to get back up.

"Hey! What the hell are you doing?!"

"Fekts, no!"

Fekts shouted at his comrade who had suddenly stopped running ahead, but the response was desperate... a voice that sounded truly cornered.

But Fekts couldn't stop here either. Vines were slithering like snakes, closing in from behind... no, from all around.

One reason Fekts and the others had managed to survive was that the attacking entity... the monster... was only vines.

If the monsters that had killed adventurers before had appeared now, no matter how well things went, it would have been difficult for everyone to survive.

Regardless of the reason, for now, they had no choice but to flee from the vines closing in around them.

So when the front suddenly halted, it was no wonder Fekts, bringing up the rear, shouted.

But no matter what Fekts was thinking, the man in front couldn't just keep running straight through the forest. Because...

"SHAGYAAAAAA!"

Treant appeared, letting out a threatening roar.

Normally, trees couldn't move.

But Treant walked the ground, moving his roots like legs.

The man running ahead fixed his gaze on Treant, who had appeared while holding his axe... and his expression turned bitter.

Because one of the Treants had gouged a wound into the tree trunk.

The man recognized that wound.

Of course he did. That mark was the one he had carved into a tree as a marker.

Which meant this Treant was the very tree he had scarred back then.

"Damn it! You weren't a Treant back then!"

He shouted in frustration, facing the Treant with his axe in hand.

As a woodcutter, encounters with monsters like Treants were commonplace.

That's precisely why, for a woodcutter with a certain level of skill, distinguishing between a tree and a Treant by touch isn't particularly difficult.

It might be a different story with higher-level monsters... but at the very least, the man had the confidence to say he wouldn't mistake an ordinary tree for a Treant, and he actually possessed that level of skill.

That very confidence allowed him to be absolutely certain the tree he had wounded was not a Treant.

And yet, here it was, appearing as a Treant.

It was only natural he felt like screaming in disbelief.

But the Treant showed no concern for the man's state, swinging its branch.

A blow that felt like a punch delivered by a hand.

The man instinctively used his axe as a shield and managed to block the blow...

"Guh!"

In that instant, he took an impact from a different direction and was sent flying.

...Yes, the Treants that appeared to block Fekts' path were not just one.

There were over ten of them, forming a wall that blocked their path.

For adventurers, perhaps, but could mere woodcutters break through such a swarm of Treants? The answer was no.

But from the forest behind them, vines stretched endlessly, pursuing Fekts and the others.

A tiger at the front gate, a wolf at the back.

If Rei saw Fekts' current predicament, he wouldn't be wrong to describe it that way.

"Dammit, what do we do, Fekts!"

Despite the desperate crisis, the woodcutters still didn't blame Fekts.

That alone was proof of how much Fekts was cherished by everyone.

"Those with severe injuries to the center, those with minor injuries to the outside! Hold out somehow, then break through at one point when you see an opening!"

Following that order, they formed up in front of Treant.

Of course, unlike adventurers who regularly fought monsters, Fekts and his men had no formal combat training.

Of course, being lumberjacks, many had rough personalities and plenty had experience with brawls... but those were fights, not combat.

That's precisely why, even when they formed up according to Fekts's orders, the formation felt somehow lopsided.

Still, even if it was uneven, it was undeniably much easier to fight in formation than facing the Treant alone.

...Being woodcutters, all their weapons were axes; none carried bows or arrows.

Therefore, combat could only be carried out by those on the outside of the formation. The woodcutters inside couldn't provide supporting fire; they could only watch.

"WAAAAAAAH!"

Unable to bear it any longer, one of the woodcutters inside hurled his axe with all his might.

Though his arm was injured, he still had enough strength to throw the axe.

The axe flew through the air and embedded its blade deep into the trunk of the Treant...

"Dammit! How can it not even flinch?!"

The treant, still bearing the axe in its trunk, showed no sign of concern and prepared to attack...

"Not if I can help it!"

Suddenly, that voice echoed around them. The Treant, which had been about to attack the woodcutter, was cleaved cleanly in two.
