The day after Rei and the others headed to the slums and met Precious... Rei's figure was found in front of Azimov's house.

The security guards noticed him and kept him there for a day... but apparently, he was already back at his own house by the next day.

Rei hadn't known this himself, but upon encountering a security guard in town who told him so, he came to verify the truth.

From the building's exterior, nothing seemed particularly unusual.

Set had barged into Azimov's lab, but that wasn't visible from here.

That's precisely why, from the outside, nothing seemed out of the ordinary.

"...Well, it is Azimov we're talking about. He's probably got some countermeasure in place, of course."

Considering Azimov's personality, it was hard to imagine him repeating the same mistake after being attacked once.

As Rei muttered this thought, Vihera nodded beside him.

"Right. In fact, I'm pretty sure he's preparing some kind of counterattack."

"Probably. Still, since people like Pamidor come to this house too, I don't think they'd intercept us without warning... I hope."

The reason he couldn't say it with absolute certainty was, after all, because the target was Azimov.

Considering Azimov's personality, it's not entirely impossible that the door would explode the moment someone knocked...

(It'll be fine, right? ...Yeah, probably.)

Muttering to himself as if to convince himself, Rei finally confirmed he was properly wearing his Dragon Robe before knocking on the door.

"Azimov, are you there?"

Relieved that the door showed no signs of trouble, he called out from outside the house.

About a minute passed... then the door opened.

"Who is it? Oh, it's Rei."

It was Azimov, of course.

He didn't seem to have any particular abnormalities... no signs of lingering injuries or the like.

Relieved by this, Rei spoke up.

"I get that you're returning from the guard post, but isn't it reckless to be home alone? Why not hire some guards, even temporarily?"

"Hardly anyone's that eager. Besides, this time of year, there are plenty of untrustworthy adventurers around."

Even Azimov, who was often absorbed in his research, knew about such matters since he lived in Gilm.

(No. For an alchemist, it's only natural. If you hired a careless adventurer as a guard, the magic items you worked so hard to create might just vanish along with the guard... and that'd be the best-case scenario. Worse still, there's a high chance you'd be attacked by the guard adventurer themselves.)

Seeing Azimov's demeanor, Rei was impressed. He was actually quite level-headed...

But... even so, the idea of a targeted individual not hiring guards was downright dangerous.

"You don't necessarily have to pick newbies, right? If it's an adventurer who's been with Gilm for a while, you'd have some level of trust in them."

"...Even if you say that. I'm busy with research, so I hardly know any adventurers. Besides Rei and the others, I've only heard of a few names like Rank A adventurers or those with nicknames."

"You're a skilled fighter, after all... Don't adventurers ever come to you with requests or something?"

"They do come, but I mostly ignore them. Well, I've dealt with a few... but I don't remember most of their names. I might recognize them if I saw their faces, though."

"And yet, you remembered me?"

"With Rei... well, Set was there too. That guy from Pamidor brought him."

As an eccentric, Azimov's way of thinking was hard to grasp.

That was Rei's honest feeling.

Still, he knew saying it wouldn't change anything, so he kept quiet.

"Then how about Rei introduces someone to guard us? If it's someone Rei knows, they wouldn't try to steal anything from this house, right?"

At Vihera's words, Marina, Byune, and even Set, who hadn't yet headed for the courtyard, nodded in agreement.

"Someone I know?"

"Yes. What do you think?"

"Well, even if you say that..."

Rei faltered at Vihera's suggestion.

While not quite as well-connected as Azimov, Rei certainly wasn't exactly a social butterfly either.

Well, many adventurers knew Rei's face.

His face without the Dragon Robe hood was also fairly well-known.

But... that was merely that they knew Rei's face. It didn't mean Rei knew them in return.

It was partly due to Rei's strength, but more so his personality.

His nature of showing no mercy to those who oppose him may inspire awe, but it rarely inspires affection.

Of course, not everyone fears Rei and avoids him...

"Milein mentioned she has another request starting today."

Milein had been happily playing with Set yesterday, but as a rising star attracting attention, she often received direct requests this time of year.

Even without that, spring had just begun, and she was likely taking on every job she could to replenish the funds depleted over winter.

"If that's the case... Elk? Wait, Elk isn't at Gilm, is he?"

Due to the rehabilitation matter, Elk... no, the entire family of the Axe of the Thunder God had left Gilm.

"Hmm... this is tricky. Why don't we head to the guild and think it over? Maybe someone's there. Or perhaps..."

Rei stopped speaking there, turning his gaze toward Marina... the person who had been the guildmaster until just a little while ago.

She seemed to understand what opinion was being sought. Marina paused briefly before speaking.

"Hmm, I'm afraid it's best I don't show my face at the guild and start meddling in things right now. It would only lead to all sorts of unnecessary misunderstandings."

"Then what about asking here instead of at the guild?"

"...If you absolutely insist, I suppose it can't be helped... but it's still best to avoid it if possible."

"Why not?"

Vihera interjected into Rei and Marina's conversation.

"This is an action as a Crimson Wing, right? Considering what lies ahead, it's better not to rely too much on my abilities or authority. It might be fine within Gilm, but it'll cause problems when you're active in other towns or villages, won't it? It's better to get used to it a little now."

For a moment, Rei thought, Isn't it okay to wait? But Marina believed experience in these situations was crucial.

Leaving things to someone else when nothing's happening isn't that difficult.

But this time, there's no time... and what if other adventurers are busy and unreachable? How would you act then?

Of course, Marina intended to ask her adventurer acquaintances if push came to shove, but she had no intention of mentioning that now.

Anyway, since they were going to act as the Crimson Wings from now on, gaining experience as a party leader was something that needed to be done urgently.

"...Alright. Then, let's head to the guild for now. As for Azimov's escort..."

"Hmph!"

...Why?

That was Rei's honest reaction.

Why would Byune volunteer to guard Azimov?

Generally, Byune rarely took the initiative to act on her own. ...Except when it involved food.

Yet today, for some reason, this very Byune had volunteered to guard Azimov.

It was only natural for Rei to be puzzled.

No, Rei wasn't the only one wondering. Both Marina, who hadn't known Byune very long, and Vihera, who had known him for ages, were equally puzzled.

"What's up? That's unusual."

"Hmm."

Byune answered Vihera's question curtly.

But that seemed to be enough for Vihera to grasp Byune's intent. After a moment's thought, Vihera addressed Rei.

"She seems to think it's best for her to stay behind..."

"Byune? Wait, how did you figure that out from just now? ...Now of all times?"

Ray had long understood that Vihera and Byune, like himself and Set, could grasp each other's intentions without needing to put everything into words.

For a moment, Rei wondered if it was some kind of telepathy. But he quickly shook his head, dismissing the unnecessary thought.

Realistically, having Byune stay here as a guard wasn't a bad thing for Rei either.

Byune wasn't as strong as the other three, but her combat skills had improved significantly through the winter training compared to before.

At the very least, he seemed capable enough to serve as a bodyguard.

Plus, being a thief, he excelled at concealing his presence. For not interfering with Azimov, he was undoubtedly the best choice among them.

The biggest problem is that Azimov and Byune can't communicate properly.

Of course, they can manage minimal communication through nods and shakes of the head.

"...Sigh, I suppose I have no choice. Alright. I'll stay here too. Rei and Marina, go to the guild. As for Set..."

"Yeah, probably better to leave him here for now. That's fine, right, Set?"

"Grr."

Though Set had been looking sleepy, defeated by Rei's talk and the spring sunshine, he reacted immediately to Rei's words.

"Alright, that's settled... Let's go. Azimov, I plan to find someone at the guild to escort you. Is that okay?"

"If you don't interfere with my research, I don't mind. But is it really necessary to go that far?"

"Don't you realize you were this close to dying?"

At Azimov's words, Rei muttered with a hint of exasperation.

"Well, even so. I got attacked, passed out, and woke up at the guard post completely unharmed. Expecting me to feel threatened after that is a bit much, don't you think?"

"Ah... Yeah. Maybe."

For Azimov, being attacked and losing consciousness had been a stroke of luck, it seemed.

That's what Rei was thinking, but that's that, and this is this.

"Still, there's a chance you could be targeted again. Once you've been targeted, you can't assume you're safe anymore."

"Well, yeah, that's true."

"Got any spare cash? ...Nah, guess I don't need to ask."

Azimov had some personality issues, but he was undeniably a master alchemist, having crafted the Twilight Spear and modified the Sleipnir Boots. Given that, he fundamentally never struggled for money.

The reason I said "basically" is because that's only true under normal circumstances.

Alchemists primarily create magic items, but crafting these items requires various materials.

Most would be satisfied with a reasonable amount of materials... but in Azimov's case, precisely because he was so skilled, he could master all kinds of materials, leading him to constantly seek rarer materials, higher-performance materials...

Just consider how many materials he poured into the Twilight Spear, and you'll understand Azimov's obsession with materials.

But when Rei flashed a slightly worried look, Azimov nodded reassuringly.

"For now, I can cover up to about five gold coins immediately."

"...The reward isn't that high."

Of course, it depends on who you're asking, but even so, five gold coins for an escort is far too much.

"For a few days, at least... We'll see how it goes, and if more time is needed, we'll add to it. Any problem with that?"

"No problem. As long as you don't interfere with my business, that is."

"Alright then, that's settled. ...Vihera, I'm counting on you."

"Yeah, yeah, leave it to me. Rei, pick a good escort."

Waving lightly, Vihera pulled Byune and Azimov toward the house.

Set, looking a little disappointed to be parting ways with Rei, headed toward the courtyard.

Watching the three people and one animal leave, only Rei and Marina remained behind.

"Well then, shall we go? ...Come to think of it, it's a bit rare for it to be just the two of us, isn't it?"

"Is that so? ...Come to think of it, you're right. We spent time together during the winter, but just the two of us? Not that often, I suppose."

"Yes. So, believe it or not, I'm actually quite happy about this. Of course, I know it's because you need escorts for the Azimov matter, and we're going to find those escorts."

With Marina wearing a lustrous smile, they headed toward the guild.

With Marina wearing a party dress, it almost looked like he was escorting her on a date.

...If only Rei weren't clad in his Dragon Robe, and Marina, dressed in her party gown, weren't holding a bow and carrying a quiver on her back.

And so the two of them stepped out onto the main street, heading toward the guild...

"Ah! Rei!?"

Just as they reached the guild, that voice suddenly echoed around them.
