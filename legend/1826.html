The day after the mock battle… Rei was heading not to the Kerebel Duke’s Knights’ training grounds, as usual… but to the kitchen.

Naturally, it wasn’t just Rei; Miranda, the maid assigned to him, was with him as well.

They were heading to the kitchen, naturally, because Georgima had summoned them, but at the same time, Rei had something he wanted to ask about the soup—or, more precisely, the fish sauce—he’d had at dinner the night before.

For Rei, who was familiar with “shotturu”—a fish sauce packed with umami—Georgima’s summons this time was actually quite convenient.

(If they can make fish sauce, wouldn’t it make sense that they could also make regular… soy sauce made from soybeans? Well, I don’t actually know how regular soy sauce is made, though.)

The method for making fish sauce that Rei knew involved using sandfish, and even that was rather half-baked… He only had a vague memory of seeing it on a news special or something.

That’s exactly why, when it comes to fish sauce and soy sauce… even though the kanji are similar, I know almost nothing about how soy sauce is made.

All I remember about soy sauce is that I saw on the news or some TV show that it’s made with soybeans… that’s about it.

Considering I learned about both from the same TV program, there’s quite a difference between soy sauce and fish sauce… but that’s probably just a matter of which one Rei personally finds tastier.

Of course, it’s not that I thought regular soy sauce was bad; it’s just that shotturu happened to suit Rei’s taste buds that much better.

(Maybe… just maybe, Georgima actually wants to perfect the ramen broth. …The problem is the noodles, though.)

After all, if you don’t know what the essential alkaline water for ramen noodles is, you’ll end up with nothing but udon-like noodles.

Since I had absolutely no knowledge on this subject, all Rei could do was leave it up to Georgima.

Still, maybe… just maybe, the reason she called me was because she’d figured out the whole alkaline water thing.

With that thought in mind, Rei stepped into the kitchen.

“You idiot! Turn the heat down! If it’s too high, the ingredients will absorb too much heat, and it’ll ruin the texture when you eat it!”

“Yeeeees!”

The moment he stepped into the kitchen, Rei was slightly startled by the sudden shout.

That said, this wasn’t exactly uncommon in the kitchen.

Georgima was an excellent chef, but he was more like a traditional-minded craftsman who believed in the “learn by watching” approach.

For that very reason, yelling at his subordinates—or apprentices—whenever they made a mistake was, in a sense, just part of the daily routine in the kitchen.

That’s why the other chefs didn’t seem particularly intimidated even when they saw someone being yelled at.

Georgima’s shouting is scary. It’s scary, but… there’s definitely a reason for it.

Precisely because they understood this, those working alongside Georgima focused on what she was shouting while doing their own work, striving to improve their skills even slightly.

“Ah…”

As Rei, who had come to the kitchen at Georgima’s request, he wasn’t sure what to do in this situation.

Georgima is lecturing the chef she just yelled at on the precautions to take when cooking ingredients, and the other chefs are listening intently to her words.

He feels it would be rude to speak up in the middle of this, but simply standing there silently listening to Georgima’s lecture feels pointless.

Well, not that it’s completely pointless, but…

Normally, Rei was the one putting the finished dishes into the Misty Ring, but it wasn’t uncommon for him to cook alongside Marina.

Marina usually does the cooking, but Rei can help out a little.

That’s precisely why what Georgima was saying was somewhat interesting to Rei as well.

……That said, in the end, Rei was just an amateur cook—and one who could only help out.

Even if he heard about cooking techniques and tips from Georgima, a master chef, it wasn’t as if he could put them to good use.

In contrast to Rei, Miranda seemed to find Georgima’s words quite thought-provoking, nodding repeatedly as if in agreement.

Miranda was of noble birth, to be sure, but her family wasn’t particularly wealthy, nor was she in a position to inherit the family estate.

As for her lifestyle, it’s probably only slightly better off than that of a commoner—if that.

That’s precisely why she sometimes cooks for herself, and for someone like Miranda, Georgima’s explanation is quite educational.

Furthermore, one reason Georgima isn’t disliked by the maids, including Miranda, is that cooking skills have nothing to do with gender. No, cooking skills are everything, and the fact that she never makes any remarks or takes any actions that forbid women from entering the kitchen is a major factor.

As evidenced by the person Rei is glaring at—who seems to be yelling—there are indeed some who dislike her rough, old-school artisan temperament, but they are in the minority.

Above all, few people would go so far as to dislike someone who makes delicious food.

After all, everyone would rather eat delicious food than bad food. …Though there was certainly a limit to everything.

In any case, at the point where Rei’s gaze fell, Georgima had finished giving a detailed explanation of how to use the fire, and after about ten minutes, the lesson came to a close.

Seizing the moment, Rei addressed Georgima.

“Georgima, I heard you wanted to talk to me?”

At the sound of Rei’s voice, Georgima finally looked up… and wore a slightly surprised expression.

She probably never expected Rei to be there.

“You’re already here? That was quick. …You could’ve called out to me sooner, though.”

“Nah, I couldn’t very well interrupt you from doing your job. Anyway, that cook from earlier—I’ve never seen him before.”

Rei didn’t visit the kitchen all that often, but between explaining things about ramen and other matters, he did end up there fairly regularly.

Even to Rei, the cook who had just been scolded by Georgima was a face he’d never seen before.

“Hm? Oh, him? He just started yesterday. We’re going to be getting more cooks from now on.”

Hearing that, Rei could easily guess the reason.

“The New Year’s party?”

“That’s right. No matter what we’re doing, it’s always better to have more hands on deck.”

“Ah… I see. I heard quite a few people were coming. So it’s better to have extra hands.”

“Exactly. …Though, that doesn’t mean I want them sending over people who aren’t up to a certain standard.”

As Georgima said this, his gaze fell on the man who had just been yelled at about the heat of the fire.

Georgima was basically old-school, favoring a “watch and learn” teaching style, but of course, that wasn’t all there was to it.

No, ideally, that approach allows them to learn through trial and error and expand their skill set… but there are some people for whom that teaching style simply doesn’t work.

For those kinds of students, Georgima had decided to teach them to a certain extent—though, of course, not everything.

“You’re just that busy, aren’t you?”

Rei turned his gaze toward Miranda and asked.

Miranda, who had served the Duke of Kerebel’s household for quite some time, nodded in agreement at Rei’s words.

“Yes. Since this is a party hosted by the Duke of Kerebel, who leads the Noble Faction, people are coming from quite far away to make it in time. Ultimately, the number of attendees… if we include the heads of noble houses and their families, it will likely be around several hundred. Though, of course, that doesn’t mean everyone will be there.”

There are a few people whose estates are too far away to make it to the Duchy of Kerebel.

Of course, in such cases, they’ll send a letter or something of the sort.

“Hundreds of people? And all of them are connected to the nobility. …Honestly, it doesn’t sound like a party I’d particularly want to attend.”

Rei fully understood that there were nobles among them with whom he got along well or whom he respected.

But he also understood that, just as there were those he got along with… no, there were even more with whom his personalities clashed.

What would happen if Rei were to attend a party attended by hundreds of nobles?

That goes without saying.

“Being treated as a guest is a pain, too. I’d like to say I just have to cook… but every now and then, there’s someone who calls me over.”

Georgima let out a sigh, looking exasperated.

Watching Georgima like that, Rei was briefly reminded of a manga he’d seen while in Japan called *Call the Chef*.

It was a scene often seen in restaurants when eating delicious food—though strictly speaking, it was fiction, not reality—but he had never actually seen anyone do that in real life.

Then again, since Rei had lived in the countryside of Tohoku, it might have been a common sight in the city.

“But in those situations, you’re supposed to say something like, ‘That was delicious,’ right? So…”

Before Rei could finish, Georgima shook her head.

“Most of the time, that’s an invitation to come work for them once you quit this job. …I might consider it if the conditions were better than here, but there aren’t any places like that right now.”

“I suppose so.”

Rei also knew the terms under which Georgima was employed by Liberte.

She could get as many ingredients as she wanted and cook them however she pleased.

Naturally, there are limits, but considering Kerebel’s wealth, there couldn’t be many who could compete with that.

“So? Let’s get to the main point of today’s meeting. Did you call me here because of the seasoning you got through those connections? It was in yesterday’s soup.”

“……What? You knew?”

At the word “seasoning” that came out of Rei’s mouth, Georgima wore a slightly displeased expression.

“Yesterday’s dinner soup was different in a lot of ways. Still, you managed to get your hands on a rare seasoning like fish sauce.”

“Fish sauce? Hmm, now that you mention it, I think that’s what it was called.”

“Hey, did you really buy a seasoning without even knowing its name?”

“You used a seasoning like that?” Rei shot him a look of disbelief, but Georgima just smiled at his words.

“I know what you’re worried about, but don’t worry. It’s not some suspicious seasoning. I tasted it myself and had it checked out. That seasoning goes by different names depending on the region.”

“Huh? Is that even possible?”

“Yeah. In fact, just a few houses down, they might call it something else… But wait, fish sauce? That’s a good name. Alright, then, from now on, let’s call this seasoning ‘fish sauce.’”

Not sure exactly what it was about the word “fish sauce” that came out of Rei’s mouth that he liked so much, Georgima announced this with a beaming smile.

Seeing Georgima like that, Rei briefly thought he should have called it “shotturu” instead of “fish sauce,” but it was already too late.

Under Georgima’s orders, there was no doubt that the name “fish sauce” would soon spread throughout the kitchen.

“Ah, come on. Do whatever you want. So, did you want to brag about that fish sauce to me? Or did you think it would be good in ramen broth? I feel like it might go well with udon, too.”

In fact, when Rei was in Japan, he had sometimes eaten udon using the leftover broth from a shotturu hot pot.

Georgima was making ramen, but since that wasn’t possible, using it as udon broth would undoubtedly result in delicious udon.

But Georgima immediately shook her head at Rei’s suggestion.

“I ordered the fish sauce specifically to make ramen. Since that’s the case, I have no intention of using it for anything other than ramen.”

“……But didn’t you try using it in yesterday’s soup too?”

Even though she said she had no intention of using it for anything other than ramen, Rei—who had actually tasted it—knew better than anyone that she had definitely used it in the soup.

“That was a soup I made to test the characteristics of this fish sauce and see what kind of seasoning it is. Since I got a general sense of it from that soup, I can’t say for certain that I won’t use it in regular cooking from now on… but it should probably be quite rare.”

Normally, one might wonder why he’d serve his employer a soup made just to test a seasoning’s character, but the soup they drank last night was actually delicious, and Liberte and the others seemed satisfied.

The fact that even a soup made on a whim could impress the palates of aristocrats accustomed to fine dining was proof of just how skilled Georgima truly was.

In any case, after that, Rei and Georgima discussed various aspects of ramen, while Miranda prepared tea for the two of them.
