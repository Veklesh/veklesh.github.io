Rei was talking with Marz and starting work... Meanwhile, at Marina's house, Ara had finally woken up.

Having stayed up late with Ilse yesterday, she inevitably woke up later than usual.

Realizing this, Ara hurriedly got ready and left her room.

"S-sorry! I overslept!"

"Hm? Oh, good morning. You were up late with Ilse yesterday, weren't you? No need to worry about it."

The one who answered Ara's words was Elena, drinking tea at the table.

Elena, who previously couldn't brew tea, had learned the basics from Ara out of her single-minded desire to serve Rei delicious tea. Now, she could brew proper, tasty tea.

Of course, her pure skill still couldn't match Ara's.

"Ah..."

The moment Ara saw Elena was the only one in the living room, she collapsed completely.

She realized she had truly overslept.

(Comfortable living sure feels like a curse at times like this.)

Late at night... staying up until the wee hours, and Marina's spirit magic creating an environment that was neither too hot nor too cold, but perfectly comfortable. The combination of these two factors meant Ara had fallen into a deep, uninterrupted sleep.

Until literally everyone except Elena had left the house.

Ara was Elena's escort... well, more like an attendant, really. Still, since she was being taken care of in this house, Ara honestly wanted to wake up before Marina and the others left and at least see them off.

Seeing Ara like this, Elena spoke up again, trying not to make a big deal of it.

"Accompanying Ilse was a task only Ara could have done, wasn't it? Then, having fulfilled that duty, it's not strange for her to rest properly. No, rather, if Ara pushed herself too hard and fell ill, that would be the real problem."

"Elena-sama..."

Hearing Elena's words, Ara looked at her with a look of deep gratitude.

"Look, Marina has breakfast ready. Why don't you eat that and then go check on Ilse?"

"Yes, I'll do that."

With that, Ara sat down and reached for the food on the table.

Of course, since it had been prepared some time ago, the food was already cold.

But even so, Ara ate without particularly minding.

Having spent much time on the battlefield, she had no qualms about eating cold food, even as a noble lady.

Moreover, she was now sitting at the same table as Elena. That alone made the food taste incredibly delicious, even if it was cold.

"So, Elena-sama. What shall we do today?"

Ara, who was still eating the cold bread with apparent relish, asked Elena, who was watching Yellow flying outside the window while sipping her tea.

Incidentally, Yellow wasn't the only one in the garden; the horses pulling Elena's carriage were also let loose.

Under normal circumstances, such an act would cause the horses to bolt from the garden.

However, the horses Elena used had undergone extremely rigorous training and simultaneously recognized Elena as their master.

Elena understood this, so she felt no particular unease about letting them roam freely in Marina's garden.

Yellow, playing in the garden, was flying through the air, playfully interacting with those horses.

Normally, no horse would be able to casually interact with Yellow, a black dragon also known as a Black Dragon, even if it was a child... But these were horses that had shown little fear since their first meeting with the griffin, Set.

Even if it was a dragon, being a child like Yellow probably didn't scare him much.

In fact, he even played with him, gently nudging him with his nose or letting him land on his body.

After all, Elena always traveled by carriage.

This meant Yellow and the horse were constantly around each other.

There was no way they wouldn't be accustomed to each other's presence.

Looking at the courtyard scene, which seemed like a lie after last night's events, Elena answered Ara's question.

"Well... I don't have any particular plans today either... Ah, but perhaps I should head to the lord's manor and report yesterday's incident."

"You're going outside? If so, there's a chance those meddlesome people might come around again..."

Ara's concern was for those who might see this as an opportunity to get closer to Elena.

If it were only the nobles from the Noble Faction, that might be manageable, but there were also members of other factions... not to mention nobles not affiliated with the three major factions, or adventurers who had misunderstood something.

Moreover, due to the ongoing expansion work, numerous merchants were currently visiting. Merchants wanting to do business with the Duke of Kerebel's household were practically endless.

It was only natural that Ara found such individuals troublesome.

"I understand how you feel, Ara, but I think it would be best to inform Lord Daskar about last night's incident."

"Hmm. Honestly, I'd prefer to avoid that if possible. For Elena's safety."

"Oh? Do you think I'd be vulnerable to those people?"

"No, no. I don't think that. I don't, but I do think it would cause Lady Elena a lot of trouble, wouldn't it?"

"...Hmm. Well, if that's how you feel, Ara, I suppose I'll hold off. But honestly, won't you be bored staying at Marina's house like this?"

"Well..."

At Elena's words, Ara was at a loss for words.

Indeed, that was undeniably true.

Staying at Marina's house meant she wouldn't have to deal with troublesome people, but it also meant she'd have to fight boredom.

Of course, it wasn't as if there was nothing to do.

Preparing dinner was a given, and while the house was usually cleaned with spirit magic, that didn't mean she couldn't clean herself. There was also a fairly spacious courtyard, so she could practice combat training, or spend time with Yellow and the horses.

...If Rei had sensed Elena's current doubt, he would have retorted, "What kind of idle housewife are you?"

That said, being a housewife in this Elgin isn't exactly that leisurely.

While there are some magical items available, most tasks still require manual labor.

And if you're running a business or farming, you have to help with that too.

Truthfully, the kind of housewife Rei imagined was extremely rare in Elgin.

Perhaps only the wives of great merchants or nobles fit that description.

That said, even those women engage in activities like hosting parties and tea gatherings with their friends, building their own connections and factions.

"Good morning."

Ilse appeared, interrupting Elena and Ara's conversation, her usually beautiful face transformed by bloodshot eyes.

What those bloodshot eyes signified was something Ara, who had been with her last night, naturally knew, and Elena could easily imagine as well.

For that very reason, even upon seeing Ilse enter the living room, neither asked about that particular matter.

"Ah, good morning."

"Good morning. Breakfast... well, it's a bit late for that, but would you like to eat? There's some for Ilse too."

Elena gave a brief greeting, and Ara followed suit, greeting Ilse.

True to her words, besides what Ara was eating, a full breakfast for Ilse was also laid out on the table.

Ilse glanced at the breakfast spread, hesitated for a moment, then nodded immediately.

Normally, she wouldn't have the slightest appetite.

She'd just woken up, and she was sleep-deprived. But... above all else, the honest truth was that killing Ajax, the enemy she'd pursued for years, with her own hands had left her utterly devoid of motivation.

If Rei saw Ilse now, he would recall the term "burnout syndrome."

That phrase perfectly described her state.

Still, she had avenged the deaths of her father, mother, and brother—the three people she loved most.

Even if her symptoms resembled burnout syndrome, it wasn't entirely negative.

"Yes, I'll have some."

Ilse replied to Ara's words and brought the food placed on the table to her mouth.

Just as when Ara had eaten, every dish was already cold.

But Ilse still brought the food to her mouth... and murmured, "Delicious."

Seeing Ilse's faint smile, Ara's face relaxed into a relieved smile.

Elena, sipping her tea, watched Ilse's state and felt reassured.

(Things must be tough for her right now. But judging by this, she'll bounce back before too long.)

That was a happy thought for Elena too.

It wasn't that Elena had any particular feelings toward Ilse.

Of course, she understood Ilze's goal of avenging her family's murder, but that was essentially the extent of their relationship.

But... even so, Elena was glad to see Ilse doing better than she had expected.

(Thanks to Ara's efforts last night, I suppose.)

You never know how well personalities will mesh until you actually talk.

Ara, who was entrusted with Ilse's care as the one among Rei's group most like an ordinary person, had surprisingly good compatibility with her, and they had built a friendly relationship.

Of course, Ilse's near-exhaustion probably played a part in that too.

Even so, she was now exchanging words with Ara, smiling, albeit faintly.

Watching this exchange, Elena spoke up once breakfast had settled.

"Well then..."

That was all Elena said.

Nevertheless, that single word was enough to capture both Ara and Ilse's attention.

"Ilse. You avenged the family you pursued for so many years. So... have you decided what you'll do now?"

"That..."

Ilse faltered, unable to continue.

Truthfully, Ilse had lived her entire life solely for the purpose of avenging her family.

Now that she had achieved it, if asked what she could do or what she wanted to do now, she had no answer.

It felt like a gaping hole had opened up inside her chest.

When she was talking with Ara, she could enjoy herself reasonably well.

But she could easily imagine that once their conversation ended and she was alone again, that same sense of emptiness would likely overwhelm her once more.

To fill that void, she needs to channel the energy she'd previously focused on vengeance into some kind of action.

I understand that, but I just can't bring myself to do it.

...Then again, that's probably why it's called emptiness.

"I don't know. Honestly, I... I don't know what to do."

In the end, that was Ilse's honest feeling.

Elena and Ara looked at Ilse like that and said nothing.

Even though Ara had only just met her, seeing her friend in pain left her at a loss for words. Elena, meanwhile, felt a complex mix of understanding and confusion toward Ilse's feelings.

Both Elena and Ara had experienced the death of someone close to them and taken revenge.

Those enemies, too, had been subordinates or colleagues—people like that.

Precisely because of that, they couldn't help but have mixed feelings about the act of revenge.

Perhaps it was precisely because she had fought alongside Ver in the war against the Bestir Empire that she had become close with Ilse.

For a moment, Ara considered this.

"I see. But... leaving the feelings you're holding inside right now untouched isn't good for your mental health in many ways. If you just stay quiet and do nothing, it will end up eating away at Ilse's heart. Even if it's just putting on a brave face, it's better to take some kind of action."

"...You're right. Thank you. I'll think about it."

"Yeah, do that. If Ara's friend were to meet a tragic end... well, that wouldn't be a very interesting conclusion."

"E-Elena-sama!?"

Ara instinctively tried to say something at the words suddenly coming from Elena's mouth.

But in the end, the words stayed unspoken.

After all, there was no doubt Ara felt friendship toward Ilse.

Watching Ara's reaction with a smile, Elena continued.

"It might sound strange coming from me, but Ilse is still young. There will be plenty of joy ahead. There might be sadness and hardship too, but life is long. Live without regrets."

"...Yes."

Ilse must have found Elena's words meaningful, for she bowed deeply to her.
