Standing before the unconscious woman, Rei scans the area for any signs of movement.

Set joins Rei in scanning the area for any signs of life, but... there are none to be found.

Well, to be precise, there are plenty of signs of life from the residents of Gilm, but perhaps it would be more accurate to say that there are no longer any signs of hostility or intent to kill directed at them.

Holding his Death Scythe, he waits on the spot, ready to react the moment the woman is targeted again, when eventually, the sound of multiple footsteps reaches his ears.

That said, upon hearing those footsteps, Rei felt not tension, but rather relief.

If they were planning to target the unconscious woman again, they certainly wouldn’t have made a commotion like this, with so many people running over.

Given that, it was obvious without even thinking who those footsteps belonged to.

“Don’t move!”

A security guard shouted as soon as he entered the alley.

Yes, the approaching footsteps belonged to a guard, just as Rei had predicted.

The guard, too, must have been surprised to find Rei and Set there when he came running after hearing the screams of a woman being attacked.

The guard, still startled, spoke up.

“Rei… is that you? Was that scream just now about you?”

“Yeah, that’s right. Though, that voice was probably the work of the guy who tried to kill this woman by drawing my attention.”

As he said this, Rei sheathed the Death Scythe in the Misty Ring and turned his gaze to the remains of the dagger lying on the ground.

The dagger, its blade cleanly severed, was enough to make the guard’s expression harden.

“Could you give me a detailed explanation of what’s going on?”

“Well, I don’t mind explaining, but staying here any longer is dangerous for this woman’s safety. Why don’t we go to the guardhouse and talk? We’ll need to treat her injuries, after all.”

Although it was Rei who had knocked the woman unconscious, he made the suggestion without seeming the least bit concerned.

That’s why the guards assumed the dagger’s owner must have knocked the woman unconscious, and they led her out of the alley.

...It was only natural, given that several guards had gone into the alley, but when Rei emerged, he saw a large crowd of onlookers gathered, wondering what had happened.

(Maybe the guy who threw that dagger earlier is mixed in with this crowd… Could that be possible? Still, it’d be a pain to track him down. I guess I’ll just have to hope he slips up.)

Even as he thought this, Rei suspected that, given the current situation, the culprit probably wouldn’t try anything like that.

“Alright, Rei. I’m sorry to ask this, but I’d like to hear the details, so please come with me to the guardhouse.”

Rei, who had been surveying the people around him, nodded in response to the guard’s words.

However, seeing Rei like this, most of the onlookers weren’t surprised—they were just thinking, “Here we go again.”

That’s because, over the past few days, Rei had been caught up in incidents where he apprehended someone up to no good—it had happened time and time again.

That’s why, even as Rei headed toward the guardhouse with the guard, most people assumed the same thing had happened as before.

The only thing slightly different from usual was that a scream echoed through the area before the guards even arrived.

However, no one thought those screams were about Rei… perhaps because of his usual demeanor.

While Rei showed no mercy to those who provoked him or to criminals, he generally never laid a hand on law-abiding residents.

It’s precisely because the residents of Gilm know this that, even when they see Rei like this, they don’t think she’s committed any crime.

“Huh? Hey, wait a minute. Who on earth is that woman? She’s pretty… no, cute, actually.”

Several onlookers notice the woman being carried by the guards.

Once one person noticed her and spoke up, the others naturally took notice as well and began speculating about who she might be.

A woman whose appearance is more fitting to be called cute rather than beautiful.

...It’s unlikely many would guess that this woman was the one planning to turn Set into a taxidermy specimen.

That said, among the adventurers mingling with the onlookers, there were a few who saw that she was quite capable.

And… upon seeing Set emerge from the alley, several of them wore disappointed expressions.

Ideally, they would have liked to admire Set, but since Rei was heading to the guard post, Set would naturally follow, and they were disappointed that they wouldn’t be able to admire him.

Some were even thinking of admiring Set while he waited near the guard post.

In any case, Rei and the others headed toward the guard post while being met with all sorts of gazes.

Inside the guard post, Rei was talking to a guard he had seen many times over the past few days.

The scene was very peaceful; there was even a cup of tea sitting in front of Rei.

It was far from the scene one would imagine upon hearing the words “interrogation”; if anything, it would be more accurate to describe it as a friendly chat.

That said, while the atmosphere was friendly, the content of their conversation was by no means gentle.

“So, I blocked the dagger that was thrown at me. You recovered the remains of the dagger, right?”

“Yeah. But that dagger itself isn’t all that rare. It’s a dagger made for throwing, but that’s all it is. If it had been made by a skilled craftsman… then we might be able to pinpoint the place of manufacture to some extent, but…”

“Since it isn’t the work of a particularly skilled craftsman, it’s difficult to trace its origins.”

The guard continued.

“In that case, we’ll need to get information out of her once she wakes up… what do you think?”

The guard, who had been looking at Rei, shook his head.

“She shows no signs of waking up yet. As Rei said, we’ve tied her to the bed so she can’t move and have posted a guard…”

The reason he hesitated when mentioning the guard was likely because, after ordering his subordinate to keep a close watch on the woman’s sleeping face, he’d received a cold stare from another subordinate—one of the woman’s own subordinates.

Staring at a woman’s sleeping face was in poor taste.

That said, that female guard wasn’t particularly strong, and he didn’t think she’d be able to stop the woman if she woke up and started thrashing about in the infirmary bed.

In the end, he told the subordinate who had given the initial order to carry it out as is... but the cold glare from the woman’s subordinate pierced both the heart of the guard who had given the order and the heart of the guard who had received it like a sharp blade.

In any case, since she was unconscious, I couldn’t very well force her to wake up, so as a guard, I had no choice but to wait for her to wake up naturally.

“Still, I can’t believe there’s someone trying to mess with Rei in Gilm at this late hour. What kind of daredevil is that?”

“I think it’s probably got something to do with those guys in the red cloth.”

“Ah… the ones you’ve been looking for lately. Honestly, having Rei deal with them has been a huge help to us.”

Because more people are staying in Gilm than usual due to the expansion project, it’s only natural that there are more troubles than usual.

As a result, the security force is currently quite understaffed.

So much so that, even though most of the expansion work has currently come to a halt, they’re still hiring adventurers to assist with patrols.

Work has decreased, and a significant number of those who came to Gilm for the expansion project have already left.

Yet the city remains this busy precisely because people now fill the streets even during the day—a result of the lack of work.

For the guards, Rei—who, even if limited to those wearing red cloth, would track down and apprehend criminals—was a tremendous help.

Since Rei was acting on his own initiative rather than at the guild’s request, the fact that no reward was required was also a relief to the guards.

“I appreciate you saying that, but in the end, all we’re catching are small-time crooks.”

“Since the ones committing crimes are the low-level guys… the ‘small fry’ Rei talks about—of course, if you go after them, you’re only going to catch small fry.”

“I know that. That’s exactly why I have high hopes for that woman.”

It always comes back to that.

And since there’s nothing much to do until the woman regains consciousness, we’re just chatting like this… but suddenly, Rei realizes something.

“Wait, aren’t the guards supposed to be busy? Is it really okay for you to be hanging out with me like this?”

“Yeah. That’s why I’m the only one keeping you company. I left just one person at the infirmary and sent the rest back to work, didn’t I?”

“...So that’s why you’re taking your time with me like this.”

Rei says this with a hint of exasperation, but the guard responds by puffing out his chest and smiling.

“This is my job, after all. Besides…”

The guard was about to say something else, but before he could, there was a knock at the door.

“Excuse me for a moment. The woman in the infirmary has woken up. Romoro’s watching her right now, but she says she wants you to come as soon as possible.”

The speaker was a man in his forties.

He was the doctor in charge of the infirmary at this outpost.

And Romolo was the man who had been watching over the unconscious woman… and who was now being glared at by the woman’s subordinate, who wasn’t currently at the station.

“She’s awake, huh? Well, let’s go then. You’re coming too, right, Rei?”

“Of course.”

Saying that, Rei also stood up from the chair he had been sitting in.

To begin with, the only reason Rei had stayed here was to hear the woman’s side of the story.

What was she thinking, handing out red cloths and engaging in criminal behavior like that?

……Of course, there’s no physical evidence that she’s involved in the red cloth incident.

On the contrary, since the only thing she’s done is direct hostility toward Rei and Set, one couldn’t even say there’s enough circumstantial evidence.

After all, there’s no guarantee that no one in Gilm harbors ill will toward Rei.

Even the underground organizations wouldn’t dare to act recklessly against Rei, given her strength and her ruthless approach toward enemies, but that doesn’t mean they don’t harbor hostility toward her.

They’re weighing the pros and cons of taking action, and they’re holding back only because the cons overwhelmingly outweigh the pros.

Naturally, many members of the underground organization want to show off and be respected by those around them.

That is precisely why, setting aside the upper echelons of the underground organization, for the rank-and-file members, Rei’s existence is a major nuisance.

...In reality, there are even Set enthusiasts within the underground organization, so not everyone necessarily harbors hostility toward Rei.

In any case, the guard believed that the possibility that the woman currently in the infirmary was a member of such an underground organization could not be completely ruled out.

“...Stop making a fuss.”

As they neared the infirmary, the guard who had been dealing with Rei up until then muttered.

In fact, Rei had been hearing the woman’s voice coming from the infirmary for quite some time.

“Untie these ropes! Do you really think it’s okay to treat me like this?”

She was shouting things like that… and on top of that, he could hear her yelling at Romoro, saying things like, “That’s why you’re unpopular with women,” and various other remarks designed to crush Romoro’s spirit.

“Whoa…”

The guard must have understood what she was saying.

Realizing that if he went to the infirmary now, he too would be on the receiving end of those verbal barbs, the guard’s steps faltered slightly.

The stream of insults was so vile that it seemed the man in charge of the infirmary had come to fetch Romolo precisely because of this foul-mouthed behavior.

Since they came one after another without pause, there was no doubt that Romolo was suffering considerable psychological damage.

Rei and the others had made it to the front of the infirmary, but when the guard leading the way stopped, Rei and the doctor naturally stopped as well.

“Hey, aren’t you going to go in?”

Rei said this to the guard, who hadn’t moved even after several dozen seconds had passed in front of the door.

Of course, Rei didn’t want to face the foul-mouthed person he could hear from inside the infirmary, but he still needed to see them to get the information.

“Ugh… I get it. I really do. But still… you know? You understand, right?”

“I’m not saying I don’t get what you’re trying to say, but we can’t just stay like this forever. It’s about time you made up your mind and went inside.”

The guard shot Rei a resentful look, but what Rei was saying was also true.

And so, having finally made up his mind, the man opened the infirmary door...

“So, hurry up and get me in… Huh?”

The woman had been shouting something at Romolo, but when she noticed the door had opened, she turned her gaze that way... She started to say something to the guard, but the moment she saw Rei standing behind him, she lost consciousness again and collapsed onto the bed.
