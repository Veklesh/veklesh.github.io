"Rei, can I talk to you for a moment?"

After a dinner where they discussed revenge, only for the topic to somehow shift to romance midway—a dinner that left Rei feeling like his whole body was tingling... as if waiting for him, Ilze's figure stood near the Dusk Wheat inn.

No, not as if she were waiting for him—she was actually waiting for him.

Anyone even slightly familiar with Gilm knew that Rei made the Dusk Wheat inn his regular lodging.

After all, there weren't that many inns in Gilm with stables large enough to accommodate a Set.

Ask any resident of Gilm where Rei was staying, and they'd likely tell you right away.

That's precisely why, while it was somewhat surprising to Rei that Ilze was waiting for him like this, it wasn't exactly shocking.

"Rei, who's that girl? She looks familiar somehow..."

Vihera, who had taken a room with Rei at the Dusk Wheat inn, remarked while looking at Ilze.

"You know, I mentioned it at dinner. That's her."

"Ah, Ilze or something... Hmm."

Understanding who was before her thanks to Rei's words, Vihera nodded at Ilze.

But she quickly averted her gaze, as if losing interest.

Since there had been talk of revenge, she had assumed this person would be a worthy opponent... or so she had arrogantly believed.

But upon actually meeting her, he sensed almost no strength at all. For Vihera, who craved combat, it was undoubtedly a disappointment.

Unlike Vihera, Ilze didn't know what to say to the person who had suddenly looked at her.

No, she was actually captivated by Vihera's appearance.

She was aware that she herself possessed a face that men found attractive.

Even to Ilze, Vihera standing before her possessed an appearance that could only be described as fantastical.

Particularly, the way Vihera's thin robes blended with the darkness of night, the moonlight, and the glow of his magic items... all these elements combined were a major reason for the mesmerizing effect.

She also recognized him as the person she had saved before, but since he didn't seem to remember, she didn't bring it up now.

"Um, well...?"

"Ah, don't worry about it. Vihera's just... well, special in many ways."

"Hey, what do you mean 'special'? What's so special about her?"

Apparently dissatisfied with the word "special," Vihera addressed Rei.

But Rei ignored Vihera's words and turned to Ilze.

"So? Looks like you were waiting for me here. Why'd you do that?"

"No, well... I'd prefer to talk somewhere less crowded, if possible."

In front of the Dusk Wheat inn... and with Set present, it was only natural that passersby's gazes turned toward Rei and the others.

Rei and his companions were used to attracting attention, so they didn't particularly mind the stares. But for Ilze, who wanted to avoid standing out as much as possible, it was a different story.

If it weren't summer, she would have wanted to wear a hooded robe to hide her face.

But it was midsummer.

Doing something like that would inevitably draw attention.

The only ones who would do something like that at this time of year were magicians.

...She had considered disguising herself as a mage, but abandoned the idea, knowing that discovery would lead to all sorts of trouble.

Drawing the attention of the enemy I must avenge, being found... That was something I absolutely had to avoid.

"Ah... well. If we're talking about a place with no people, the cafeteria probably won't work either."

When she heard "dining hall," Byune reacted slightly, but ultimately wore a disappointed expression.

Lately, I've been eating at Marina's house, but the food prepared there inevitably pales in comparison to what's served at the Dusk Wheat inn.

They often served meals stored in Rei's Misty Ring, and sometimes used the magic item oven to bake pizza and other dishes.

But even so, it still couldn't match the food at Dusk Wheat inn.

...Though freshly baked pizza, with the texture and aroma of melted cheese, certainly stimulates the appetite quite a bit.

"Well, using Rei's room... would probably cause all sorts of problems. How about coming to our room instead?"

"That would be a great help."

Though captivated by Vihera's beauty, Ilze replied with a look of relief.

Even if Rei was a famous adventurer with a nickname, he was still a man.

Sure, he was younger than Ilze, who was over twenty, but that didn't mean she could just walk into a man's room. The answer was no.

(Besides, boys his age are usually starting to take an interest in girls.)

Even without knowing the word "puberty," she naturally understood such matters from similar experiences.

Moreover, from Ilze's perspective, Rei was also an adventurer of vastly superior rank. Considering that, going to Rei's room without knowing him well was simply not an option.

In that sense, being invited to Vihera's room was incredibly fortunate.

Considering the reason she'd come to ask for help this time, she wasn't entirely without reservations about it.

"Grrr?"

While stroking Set, who was growling as if to ask, "What are we doing?", Rei opened his mouth.

"Nothing. Just going to have a private talk. Set, go back to the stables."

"Grrr."

Seto growled, clearly disappointed at being separated from Rei. But since there was no way he could bring Set inside the room, sending him to the stable was only natural.

...Incidentally, Ilze had seen Set from a distance before, but seeing him up close like this surprised her a little.

Well, seeing a griffin up close was bound to be surprising, of course.

In any case, Ilze watched in a daze as Set obediently followed Rei's words and headed toward the back of the inn... toward the stables.

"What's wrong? Aren't you going to Vihera's room?"

Rei called out to Ilze, who was clearly startled, as if it were nothing unusual.

His attitude suggested he had no idea how incredible it was, by any normal standard, to have tamed a griffin.

Seeing Rei like this, Ilze was struck anew by the realization that the person before her was truly a formidable adventurer.

"Ah, yes. I'll go right away."

Precisely because he was Rei, she knew he would be a formidable ally if she could get him to help with her revenge.

Thinking this, Ilze trembled with sudden excitement as she entered the inn.

Upon entering the inn, she passed a party of adventurers, presumably just leaving the dining hall. They seemed to know Rei, though they didn't bother her in any way.

...Though, seeing an unfamiliar beauty with Rei, Vihera, and Byune did draw a few curious glances.

After all, the party Rei belonged to, the Crimson Wings, was one every man would envy—every member except Rei was female.

Moreover, the women in that party—whether Marina or Vihera—were beauties the likes of which most men might only see once in a lifetime.

Some even claimed Byune was the ultimate beauty, though they were a minority.

So, seeing an unseen beauty alongside the members of Crimson Wings was bound to draw attention.

However, the adventurers who knew of Rei but had never spoken to her simply left the inn quietly.

Unfazed by the stares, Rei and the others simply made their way up to the Dusk Wheat inn.

Once inside the room, Ilze finally breathed a sigh of relief.

If even half the rumors circulating about how powerful Rei and his group were true, it would be staggering.

That's precisely why she desperately needed their cooperation. But possessing such power inevitably drew attention.

"So, what business do you have with Rei?"

Watching Byune sit on the bed, shaking her head, perhaps from exhaustion, Vihera asked Ilze.

Urged to take a seat on the chair in the room, Ilze looked toward Rei, who was sitting on the floor with his back against the wall, and spoke.

"Well, I'd prefer to discuss this with just Rei-san..."

"Listen, don't be unreasonable about talking in my room. Besides, if you talk in this room, I'm obviously going to hear it, right?"

"...Right."

Hearing Vihera's words, Ilze probably realized it was pointless to insist further.

Eventually, though reluctantly, she began speaking to Rei.

"Well, as you probably understood from what happened today at lunch, I have someone I want to take revenge on."

The fact she said "will take revenge" rather than "wants revenge" revealed Ilze's intense determination.

Faced with such earnestness, Rei couldn't treat Ilze lightly.

Honestly, he found it a hassle... but he could at least manage the courtesy of not showing it here.

"Yeah, I figured. You're that serious about it, and I kind of understood from how you were acting earlier. ...So? From the look on your face, I guess your target is still in Gilm?"

"Yes. To be honest, I didn't know exactly where he was. It's been six... no, five years since that man killed my parents and brother."

Recalling the moment her parents and brother were killed, she sighed, trying to calm the anger welling up inside her.

After a few seconds, Ilze continued her explanation.

"I searched for that man, going to all sorts of places. I even wondered if he might have already left the Kingdom of Mireana for another country. Even so... even so, I couldn't bring myself to give up. That's when I heard about the expansion project in Gilm."

Many people gather at Gilm seeking work.

And so, perhaps... clinging to a sliver of hope, he came to Gilm, only to find the enemy he'd been searching for all these years.

Hearing this, Rei recalled the first time he met Ilze and nodded in understanding.

"I see. And when you found your enemy, your hand instinctively reached for the dagger, and that's when I made contact..."

"Yes. Well, better late than never, but thank you."

"Huh. So that's when the thanks come out. I thought you'd be saying something like, 'Why did you interfere?'"

Ilze shook her head at Rei's words.

"No, if I had attacked that man there, he would have easily countered. Unfortunately, I have no confidence in my combat abilities."

"I suppose so."

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Ilze's conversation, agreed with Ilze's statement about her lack of combat ability—almost ruthlessly so.

But Ilze understood just how little talent she possessed for combat.

That's precisely why, even upon hearing Vihera's words, she showed no particular dissatisfaction and simply nodded in agreement.

No, rather than being awkwardly glossed over, she found it easier to accept being told outright that she lacked the strength to fight.

"That's why we were truly grateful for Rei-san's help."

"I see. ...Hearing you say that helps me too. But..."

Rei hesitated slightly before speaking to Ilze.

Hearing the explanation just now, something had struck him as odd.

From what he'd heard, it seemed Ilze's family had been killed quite some time ago.

At the very least, Ilze herself had said it was five years ago.

Then...

"Are you absolutely certain the person you found is really your enemy? From what I heard, your family was killed five years ago, right? So, isn't it possible that the person you found this time might not actually be your enemy?"

Yes, that was the doubt Rei felt.

Even though her family was killed, it was someone she encountered only once, quite a while ago.

Could she really remember that face clearly...? Honestly, it was doubtful.

If there were cameras or something, it might be different, but Rei had never seen such devices on this Elgin.

Given that, it was entirely possible that the face in Ilze's imagination had changed over time.

When Rei implied she might have mistaken the person, Ilze immediately shook her head.

"If the face was the only distinguishing feature, I might have mistaken them. Their build could have changed over these past five years too."

"I suppose so."

Five years is long enough for someone thin to gain weight, or someone heavy to lose it.

Even so, what reason could there be for thinking the person she found was her enemy...?

Ilze opened her mouth with a confident look, meeting Rei's questioning gaze.

"The tattoo on his left arm. It's like the arm itself is a tree, with a snake coiled around it, climbing upward. There's no mistaking a tattoo like that."

She declared this with an air of absolute certainty.
