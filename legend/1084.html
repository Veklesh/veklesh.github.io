It had been about a month since Elena left Gilm. The season had already passed spring and turned to summer.

Even so, it wasn't yet the peak of summer's heat, so while everyone complained about the heat, many remained active.

Naturally, adventurers were also moving about energetically, sweating in the heat while completing quests posted on the request board.

Amidst this, as for Rei ...

"This one's no good either, huh?"

"That's right. The taste hasn't changed from before, and it just costs more."

After taking a bite of the dried meat, Rei muttered. The man in his late twenties shook his head as he replied.

Wiping sweat from his brow, the man pulled a chilled fruit drink from a nearby refrigerated magic item box and downed it in one gulp.

He was considerably taller than Rei, easily over 180 cm. But his width matched his height, and his weight was undoubtedly well over 100 kg.

This man, named Mayo, was the third-generation owner of Percy's Tool Shop, which sold various tools for adventurers. He was the person Rei had requested assistance from this time, introduced by Marina.

Percy's Tool Shop was a fairly large store even within Gilm, though not quite top-tier.

Nevertheless, it sold quality goods at fair prices, earning it a high reputation among Gilm's adventurers.

Sometimes even traveling merchants came to stock up on goods.

The third-generation owner of such a shop... well, technically, since his father was still the proprietor, calling him the next third-generation owner was more accurate. Mayo, as evident from his physique, had a strong interest in food.

It wasn't just that he wanted to eat delicious things himself; he also wanted others to eat tasty, cheap food. To an outsider, he seemed like someone who should be running a restaurant instead of a tool shop. But precisely because he was that kind of person, he was the perfect partner for Rei.

If the project Rei and the others were working on now succeeded, it wouldn't be a joke—it wouldn't be surprising if their names went down in the history of this world. Namely...

"Completely eliminating the goblin meat's gaminess is difficult, and it has a bitter, astringent taste. We also need to address the texture... Hmm, it's tricky."

Yes, they were thinking of ways to make goblin meat edible and delicious.

Of course, leaving a mark on history depends on success. Countless people—tens, hundreds—have attempted this challenge before, only to ultimately fail.

Goblins, needless to say, are incredibly prolific.

Even in the remote region of Gilm, they've managed to survive without being wiped out, so their reproductive capacity is truly a threat.

But when considered as a food source, that threat becomes an advantage.

While their strong reproductive capacity requires some control, it also meant that as a meat source, you could increase the meat supply as much as you wanted.

Moreover, goblins are omnivorous, eating anything... even things that are starting to rot. In fact, they eat rotten things too.

In other words, feeding them is never a problem.

The sole and greatest problem, however, is how awful their meat tastes.

"My Lord Mayo, isn't it rather impractical to use goblin meat for food?"

The one who voiced this concern was a male servant, one of Mayo's subordinates who had also been unlucky enough to be forced to eat goblin meat.

It had been about a week since Rei brought up the idea, Mayo got enthusiastic, and the research began. Having been forced to eat goblin meat numerous times during that period, the man pleaded for mercy.

"What are you talking about? You volunteered to help with this research, lured by the reward, didn't you? Then you must work hard enough to earn that reward. Besides, Rei and I have eaten just as much goblin meat as you. Complaining about it is pathetic."

The words that came out of Mayo's mouth were true.

Both Mayo and Rei had easily consumed over five kilograms of goblin meat so far.

Considering each sample was only a bite-sized portion, it was clear how much goblin meat had been wasted up to now.

...Though goblin meat was plentiful, and if one wanted it, there was no shortage.

That said, not all of that failed goblin meat went to waste.

They've been distributed as donations under the name of Percy's Tools to the slum districts, earning them a bit of a reputation.

For those living in the slums, many are happy to eat goblin meat.

Especially now in summer, when food spoils easily, their gratitude was particularly evident.

"That's true... but I never imagined it would be this awful."

It seemed this was the man's first time eating goblin meat, and no matter how good the pay was, he couldn't help but think participating as a taste tester was a mistake.

There was no one to listen to his complaints. Mayo and Rei continued discussing how they could make this goblin meat edible.

"What about using a lot more spices?"

That was the first thing that came to Rei's mind to mask the meat's odor, but Mayo immediately dismissed it.

"It might help somewhat, but spices are generally expensive. The biggest selling point of goblin meat is its cheapness. Using spices would defeat that purpose."

"...I suppose so."

"Yes. Besides, there are plenty of other meats that taste better with spices. If they cost about the same but taste significantly better... well, as adventurers, it's obvious which one we'd buy, isn't it?"

Mayo's words were undeniably true.

Spice-enhanced goblin meat at a premium price versus similarly priced monster meat. Asked which to buy, most would choose the latter.

"Ma'yo-sama, what about orcs? They have similarly high reproductive rates."

At the servant's words, Mayo... and then Rei opened his mouth as if to explain.

"Orcs are significantly smarter than goblins. Their individual abilities are incomparably higher than goblins', and gathering large numbers would be difficult. Precisely because they're intelligent, they don't carelessly reveal themselves like goblins do."

Both goblins and orcs could be called social monsters. But there's a huge difference in that sociality.

Orcs sometimes build settlements or even homes, but goblins simply don't possess that level of intelligence.

This vast difference between goblins and orcs made securing a steady supply of orc meat difficult.

Whereas goblins would likely continue their actions without a second thought even if their comrades were killed, orcs would normally retreat if they sensed something suspicious.

Rei nodded in understanding at the explanation he had given.

"I see. It's not that simple, is it?"

"Orc meat is delicious beyond compare, and if we could get a steady supply, it would definitely be a treat for us."

Mayo had eaten orc meat many times before.

Its flavor was undeniably superior to goblin meat, and even with simple seasoning, it was perfectly delicious.

(As for orc meat... there's quite a bit from the ones who attacked the dark elf settlement over the World Tree incident... but that meat isn't for sale. It's for me and Set to eat.)

Images of countless orc corpses flashed through Rei's mind.

Recalling the dark elf settlement triggered a chain reaction of memories about the giant cicada... causing him to flash a slightly bitter expression.

Since the giant cicada was a completely unknown monster, it had been handed over to the guild without being skinned, per their request.

Of course, it was a non-binding request. If Rei had insisted on claiming ownership and stripping it himself, they likely would have allowed it.

But when Marina suggested that examining it might unlock the mystery of how the giant cicada achieved such abnormal evolution through Rei's magic and the World Tree, Rei had no words to refuse.

It was already known that simply infusing large amounts of one's own magic wouldn't cause monsterization.

Rei's hypothesis was that the World Tree's sap was involved, and that it wouldn't become such a distinct monster unless it absorbed sap and magic power to the point of weakening the World Tree to the brink of death. This investigation would thoroughly examine that aspect.

"What's wrong?"

Mayo asked Rei curiously as he pondered the giant cicada.

"Nothing, just thinking I'd like some grilled orc meat skewers."

"Oh, that sounds nice."

"...You just ate goblin skewers, and you still want more?"

Though Mayo agreed with Rei's words, the servant muttered as if she had no desire for any more meat.

"It's getting hotter soon. You should eat a little to build up your stamina."

Rei muttered this, completely unaffected by the heat in the Dragon Robe.

Meanwhile, Mayo was sweating profusely while cooking, constantly taking chilled fruit water from the magic item refrigerator here to drink.

"I suppose so. Some people lose their appetite in summer."

"...Lord Mayo never experiences that, so I envy you."

"Ho ho ho. I have an appetite all year round."

Though the servant's words carried a hint of sarcasm, Mayo seemed to take it as a compliment, patting her large, rounded belly.

Seeing the servant like that, Rei said nothing in particular and turned his gaze back to the goblin meat.

"If using spices is out, and cooking it normally is out too... Should we ask another cook?"

The goblin meat dishes prepared besides the jerky were cooked by the cook at Percy's Tool Shop.

While most tool shops couldn't afford the luxury of a cook, this one sold preserved foods like jerky for adventurers, which was why they were hired. ...Though it also helped that Mayo's cousin happened to be a cook.

"Ideally, I'd prefer not to spread this information too widely. If our research succeeds and goblin meat becomes more palatable, it could bring significant profits to our shop."

"Personally, I'd be perfectly happy if goblin meat just became widely available and edible."

That said, when considering where to consult about this matter, Marina introduced me to Percy's Tools.

Since she introduced us and they're helping with the research, I understand we need to make sure Percy's Tools makes a profit.

"Meat... meat, huh? Improving the taste of the meat itself... Hmm?"

Not eating goblin meat through cooking methods, but enhancing the taste of the meat itself.

As that thought crossed Rei's mind, something suddenly flashed through his head.

It was a program he'd seen on TV back in Japan.

A technique that enhanced the flavor itself not by letting meat rot, but by aging it.

"Rei-san, what's wrong? Did you think of something?"

Rei reached out to Mayo, who had finished her fruit water and was now peeling a chilled fruit.

"...Just one, okay?"

He placed a fruit slightly smaller than a fist into Rei's hand, then handed one to the servant as well.

The three of them ate the chilled fruit and took a short break.

The servant, in particular, savored the happiness as the taste of goblin meat lingering in his mouth was overwritten... no, purified by the fruit.

"So, did you think of anything?"

Mayo asked while eating the fruit. Rei nodded and opened his mouth.

"There's a technique called aged meat."

"...Aged meat?"

"Yeah. But honestly, I don't know much about it either. I only saw a little about it in a book when I lived with my master before."

Recalling his usual backstory, Rei explained. Mayo gave him an interested look.

The fact that Rei had lived with a supremely skilled magician—or rather, a sorcerer—before coming to Gilm wasn't something Rei particularly hid, as he wanted to make use of that background. Consequently, it had spread among those in the know.

Rei wondered if a master wizard capable of such powerful magic might know some unknown way of eating meat. Rei explained.

"I believe leaving meat in a place with a certain level of cold for a few days enhances its flavor. ...That's roughly how I remember it."

"...Won't it rot?"

"No, at a temperature where it won't rot. I think it was around the temperature of midwinter."

Technically, there were other necessary steps to make aged meat, but that was all Rei remembered from watching TV.

Mayo tried her best to coax more information about aged meat out of Rei... but Rei, who only had that basic knowledge to begin with, had little else to offer.

Even so, Mayo researched and researched... and after several years, he would perfect the technique for making aged meat.
