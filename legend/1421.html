We had already traveled far from what could be called the frontier, to a place where it wouldn't be strange to take several days by carriage to reach Subursta.

The sun, shining with all its summer might, was sinking westward as if exhausted and drained from its daytime exertions.

Frowning at the last rays of the setting sun, Rei stowed the processed orc materials, magic stones, proof-of-kill parts... and most importantly, the meat, into the Misty Ring. He then headed towards where Set was waiting.

Set usually insisted on accompanying Rei, but this time he hadn't come along because he'd been asked to guard the woman who had been captured by the orc.

He could have dismembered the orc near the woman, but he knew that when she woke up—presumably after being carried off by the orc—seeing someone dissecting an orc corpse nearby would be a severe shock. That was his reasoning.

Admittedly, for Rei, who was accustomed to Set, this was the best choice, and in practice, it proved correct. However, for someone unaware, they would suddenly find a griffin right beside them.

Under normal circumstances, that would be far more shocking than encountering an orc.

But... whether it was fortunate or not, when Rei returned, the woman still showed no signs of waking.

...Even if she had been awake, discovering a griffin nearby might have caused her to simply continue feigning unconsciousness.

"Set, anything unusual?"

"Guruu!"

At Rei's words, Set, who had been lying on the grass, roared in response.

Truthfully, with Set exposing himself like this, any monster with a decent level of intelligence wouldn't approach on its own.

Except for exceptions like goblins.

"I see. Then I suppose I should prepare some food so he can eat something right away when he wakes up?"

"Gurururu!"

He must have been happy about the meal.

Set growled happily...

"Mmm... mmm..."

That sound from Set woke the unconscious woman.

Hearing the woman's voice, Rei turned his gaze toward her once more.

Rei knew it was rude to stare at someone sleeping.

But as long as he didn't know her true identity, Rei couldn't afford to let his guard down so easily.

(She could be an assassin who'll detonate explosives around her the moment she wakes up... or something like that. It's not like that possibility doesn't exist. ...Nah, probably not.)

He immediately dismissed his own thoughts.

After all, Rei's decision to pass through here today had been purely coincidental.

Had he not received Daskar's request for revenge against the Noble Faction, he wouldn't have come here at all. And depending on Set's flight path, there was a good chance he wouldn't have encountered the orcs either.

If that's the case, then this wasn't orchestrated; it was pure coincidence.

"Looks about twenty, maybe?"

That was the impression Rei got from his eyes.

Of course, it's by no means uncommon for people's actual age to differ significantly from their appearance.

Long-lived races like elves and dark elves are particularly notable examples of this.

In terms of perceived age, Rei was no exception.

Elena inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, extending her lifespan to an extraordinary length, and Vihera also became biologically superior to humans by absorbing Ambris.

(Huh? Does that mean the only normal human in Crimson Wings is actually Byune? ...Well, despite that tiny body, she eats as much as I do, so she's not exactly normal either.)

Rei also ate disproportionately for her size, and Byune was the same.

As I pondered this, the woman who had awakened looked around and sat up.

As she did, the sheet draped over her body fell away. Even in summer, it would be troublesome if she got cold.

Normally, one would cover her with a blanket, but it was summer now.

It wasn't midsummer yet, but the daytime temperatures were often hot enough to feel the heat.

Putting a blanket on her in this situation would be practically harassment.

That was precisely why Rei had used a thin sheet.

Of course, if he truly cared about her, he could have let her rest inside the magic tent... but Rei still didn't trust her that much yet.

"Um... I don't... know... why I was sleeping in a place like this..."

The woman seemed unaware of her current situation and tried to ask Rei, who was nearby.

But her words stopped mid-sentence, likely because she caught sight of the griffin lying down a short distance away.

"Don't worry. Set stays calm as long as you don't try to harm him."

"...Um, are you sure it's okay?"

"Yeah."

"Really? But... Oh, Griffin? Wait a minute. Could you be the Crimson Rei?"

"You knew?"

The words that came from the woman's mouth surprised Rei just a little.

To Rei's eyes, the woman wasn't wearing any armor... she was precisely the sort of person one would call an ordinary civilian.

Rei knew his name had spread since gaining his alias.

But that was only prominent among adventurers, soldiers, mercenaries—those who favored rough work. He hadn't expected someone from such a rural area to know him.

If this were in the Aburo or Saburusta regions near Gilm, it wouldn't have been strange for common folk to know him...

"Yes, the minstrels are singing about the war with the Bestir Empire."

"...Oh, I see."

Rei hadn't expected bards to be involved, let alone hear rumors spread by word of mouth. Still, with the source of the information clarified, he nodded in understanding, yet his expression remained subtly ambiguous.

Bards were those who sang songs in taverns, squares, dining halls... various places, earning their livelihood.

And these minstrels also served as a means of information dissemination in this era, turning various events into songs.

Since Paired Orbs weren't commonplace, minstrels played a major role among the means for those without significant status to obtain information about the world.

Of course, since minstrels lived by their songs, they had to earn money.

Consequently, it wasn't uncommon for the content of their songs to become more embellished than the original events.

Knowing this, Rei probably understood why the minstrel had heard her story, yet still wore a slightly awkward expression.

"Hey, hey. So it's true you defeated the entire Bestir Empire Army by yourself?"

"It's a lie."

I knew it.

Rei, who seemed to want to say that, sighed and denied the woman's words.

The woman started to speak again, but Rei cut her off.

"More importantly, why were you in a place like this? It's quite a distance from any nearby villages or towns."

"Huh? Now that you mention it...!? Oh, right, I remember! I went to gather nuts in the nearby woods, and then someone hit me... and when I came to, I was here."

"...I see. You could say you were lucky, or unlucky. It's a bit of a gray area."

She'd probably been so absorbed in gathering nuts that an orc knocked her unconscious.

Rei concluded as much, yet still shot the woman a somewhat exasperated look.

"Getting absorbed in gathering nuts is fine, but honestly, not noticing an orc? What's up with that?"

"Ugh... w-well... it can't be helped, right?! My friends always say I lose sight of everything else when I focus on one thing, but it's just my personality!"

"Well, if you know that, fix it. Or don't come alone—come with a group. The best solution is to hire adventurers as guards."

"Don't be ridiculous. Do you think I can hire guards every time I go nut-picking?"

"Well, that's probably impossible."

Rei knew that too.

But even so, venturing into forests and woods completely unprotected, alone, was nothing short of suicide.

Even if this wasn't the frontier, it wasn't exactly monster-free.

And while bandits weren't found in the frontier, it wouldn't be strange for them to be around here, even though it wasn't the frontier.

Given such dangers, going out alone to gather nuts was nothing short of suicide.

"In your case, it's a good thing you were captured by orcs. Well, considering orcs come in all sorts of personalities, I should say it's a good thing it was those particular orcs."

"...What do you mean?"

Rei murmured, looking as if she didn't understand what Rei was getting at.

"It's simple. If it had been bandits, they wouldn't have hesitated to lay hands on you right then and there. ...Considering that, it seems those orcs earlier weren't going to attack you on the spot. They probably intended to take you to their lair or somewhere before making their move."

Hearing this, the woman's face turned ashen.

She must have imagined what kind of specific act "making a move" might entail.

"Looks like you get it."

Asked to confirm her understanding, the woman gave a small nod.

Seeing her like that, Rei probably thought it best not to alarm her too much. He handed her a cup of fruit juice he'd taken out of the Misty Ring.

"Well, you made it out okay in the end. Consider yourself lucky this time. Be more careful next time. Here, drink this and calm down."

"Ah, thank you. ...Huh? This is cold!?"

The moment she touched her lips to the cup Rei handed her, the woman cried out in surprise.

It was only natural. If it were winter, it might be understandable, but it was summer now.

She probably never imagined she'd be able to drink something this cold in a place like this, let alone from a mountain stream.

Where did Rei get this from...? Completely unaware of the Misty Ring's presence, the woman looked at Rei again.

"Where did you get this!?"

"Well, for an adventurer with a nickname, this kind of thing should be no big deal, right?"

Laying it out casually, he knew that if any other famed adventurer—like Elk, the one with the nickname Axe of the Thunder God—heard that, they'd definitely get angry. He then took a sip of the cold fruit water himself and poured some into a deeper dish for Set.

Watching Set drink it happily, Rei turned back to the woman and spoke again.

"So, what are you going to do now?"

"What do you mean, 'what am I going to do'..."

The woman looked around, troubled.

Naturally, this wasn't the place where the woman had been captured by the orcs.

In this unfamiliar place, she looked at Rei with a troubled expression.

"Well, if it's okay... could you take me back to my village? I can't offer much in return..."

As if aware that hiring an adventurer required money, she pleaded with Rei, her voice somewhat weak.

Moreover, being a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname, hiring Rei undoubtedly required a considerable sum... far more than the woman could possibly afford.

Rei understood her feelings all too well.

Leaving her alone in this open field would likely mean she'd be attacked again, whether by monsters or bandits.

Unfortunately, the woman was reasonably attractive.

To thieves, she was the perfect target.

"Hmm... if your village has any delicious dishes..."

"Ah, yes! Well, I don't know if it's delicious, but we do have a famous dish from my village!"

"...Huh."

Hearing this unexpected information from the woman's mouth, Rei's expression softened slightly.

Normally, Rei needed to complete the request Daskar had asked of him.

But considering Set's speed, it was true that a slight detour wouldn't hurt.

It was already evening, and before long the sun would set completely, giving way to the moon.

Daskar surely wouldn't expect Rei to travel through the night.

Of course, Daskar wanted him to hurry as much as possible. Honestly, he probably wished Ray would travel through the night if feasible.

After all, as long as they didn't know what methods Count Lerdact, who was pulling the strings behind the Bystol incident, was using to gather information, they wanted them to hurry as much as possible.

If Rei truly needed to hurry... if it were a matter of life and death, he would travel through the night.

But he judged this matter didn't warrant that level of urgency... and the woman's invitation was just what Rei needed.

"Alright, I'll give you a ride for the sake of that famous dish. ...So, what's the name of your village?"

"The village is called Zakunya. And I'm Shira. It's only for a short while, but nice to meet you."

The woman... Shira said this to Rei with a smile on her face.
