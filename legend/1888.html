For Ranigus and Teres, exploring Anesis must have been unexpectedly fun.

In the end, they dragged Rei and Brewitt all over town the entire time they were out, popping into various shops.

And since it gets dark early in winter, once it started to get dim, they decided it was time to call it a day.

For a moment, Rei wondered if they should all just head back to Kerebel Duke’s mansion together. But the truth was, Rei was the only one staying at the Duke’s mansion; the others all had their own proper residences.

Given that, there was no real need for everyone to return to the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

Of course, it would be a different story if anyone had business with Elena or Liberte, but there was no one like that.

“Ah, wait a second. Before we go, there’s one more place I’d like to stop by. Is that okay?”

Just as we were about to call it a day... Ranigus suddenly spoke up.

Perhaps because, for Ranigus, if he missed this opportunity, he would rarely get to come out into the city like this again, there was a desperate tone to his voice.

That said, it’s not as if he’ll never be able to go out into town again.

Still, there’s no doubt he’d never be able to do something like this—walking around town with like-minded friends instead of traveling by carriage.

Perhaps sensing Ranigus’s feelings, Rei… and Brewitt, who—unlike Ranigus—was always out in town on his own, spoke up with an air of resignation.

“So? Which shop do you want to go to? Just so you know, we don’t have much time.”

“Sorry about that. …Actually, I’d like to stop by a weapons shop.”

Those words came as a surprise not only to Brewitt, but also to Rei, Teres, and everyone else around them.

The only exceptions were Ranigus’s bodyguards and attendants.

Since they knew Ranigus’s tastes, they didn’t seem particularly surprised.

“That’s surprising—that Ranigus would be interested in a weapons shop.”

“Is that so? Believe it or not, I’m actually pretty confident in my ability to judge weapons.”

Ranigus said this with a confident smile.

It wasn’t bravado or anything of the sort; he was letting everyone around him know that he truly believed it from the bottom of his heart.

“Alright. Then let’s get going. There’s not much point in standing here discussing it any longer. When you think about it, it’ll be quicker to just head straight to the weapon shop.”

With those words from Rei, the group’s final destination was decided.

“Huh… These are all fine weapons.”

“I see. I thought you were just a spoiled noble, but you know your stuff.”

The dwarf shopkeeper spoke with admiration in response to Ranigus’s muttered remark inside the weapon shop.

Under normal circumstances, speaking to a nobleman in such a manner would be tantamount to suicide.

However, the dwarf was well aware of this and did not alter his tone toward Ranigus.

This was likely due in large part to the fact that this dwarf had deep ties to the House of Duke Kerebel.

Although it was a weapons shop, it also served as a blacksmith’s workshop, and the weapons forged by this dwarf were on display for sale.

Naturally, Ranigus must have come to this shop knowing that.

With eyes shining brighter than Rei had ever seen before, he gazed at long swords, daggers, spears, halberds… and various other weapons, listening to the dwarf, saying something, and then talking to the dwarf again.

This was clearly unlike the Ranigus they usually knew, and the three of them—Rei, Brewitt, and Teres—stared at him in astonishment.

“Hey, just to be sure… Is that really Ranigus? Could I be seeing things? Or has he been replaced by someone else?”

“It’s definitely Ranigus, and he hasn’t swapped places with anyone. …To be honest, this is the first time I’ve seen Ranigus like this either, so I’m surprised too.”

Brewitt answered Teres’s question like that, but it had actually been less than a month since Brewitt had met Ranigus.

Moreover, given how few times they’d actually met, it wasn’t at all strange that Ranigus might have a side to him that Brewitt didn’t know about.

“Apparently, there are quite a few people who are interested in weapons…”

As Rei said, it wasn’t all that rare for nobles to have a hobby of collecting weapons.

That said, weapons naturally come with a hefty price tag, so even among the nobility, it’s difficult to pursue such a hobby unless one is fairly well-off.

…That said, when it comes to expensive hobbies, Rei’s hobby of collecting magic items costs far more than collecting weapons.

In that sense, Rei’s hobby is clearly the rarer one.

“Just by seeing how this longsword reflects light, you can tell how sharp it is. Is it a Tarshes-style technique?”

“You can tell that much? But to be precise, it’s not Tarshes—it’s Tarshel-style technology.”

“Tarshel-style? But isn’t that basically for spears…?”

“That’s right. But that doesn’t mean it can’t be applied to a longsword, does it?”

Technical terms are flying around, but Rei… no, none of them really understand exactly what they’re talking about.

Well, he can sort of grasp the general meaning from the tone, but he’s not sure if he’s getting it right.

“What should we do?”

“Even if you ask me that… given how things look, wouldn’t it be better to leave them alone for a while? I don’t think anything we say will make a difference.”

In response to Rei’s words, Teres said that.

Brewitt also nodded silently in agreement with Teres’ words.

Watching the two of them, Rei eventually decided it would be safer to leave them be and nodded as well.

“Hey, I’d like to ask you two something. What kind of weapon is best for self-defense?”

Teres suddenly asked this, turning her gaze toward Rei and Brewitt.

Why bring that up out of the blue? Rei wondered, but Brewitt nodded as if convinced by Teres’ words.

“Even if you have bodyguards, you should still carry a weapon just in case something happens. Not something flashy like a longsword, but something you can keep hidden.”

“...Is that really how it is?”

Rei knew quite a few nobles, but he hadn’t often seen any who carried weapons for self-defense.

Of course, that’s only because they don’t carry weapons in a way that draws attention; in reality, there are quite a few nobles who do carry weapons just in case.

“That’s just how it is. So, about self-defense weapons. Since you need to be able to conceal them, smaller items are generally better. To play it safe, I’d say a dagger or something like that.”

A weapon you can conceal.

When he heard that, the first thing that came to Rei’s mind was Nebula's Eye on his belt.

It could be easily concealed if she wore slightly loose-fitting clothes, and above all, with magic power, she could produce an endless supply of arrowheads.

Moreover, since those arrowheads revert to magical energy and vanish within a few minutes, they were the perfect weapon to conceal.

(Well, it consumes a massive amount of magic power, so I don’t think Teres could use it, and even if she could, this is a custom-made item, so I have no intention of giving it to her.)

If she were in the same party as me, like Byune, I wouldn’t have minded lending it to her, but Teres is different.

……That said, even Byune—who seemed better suited than Rei to make effective use of Nebula's Eye—was ultimately unable to use it due to a lack of magic power.

“A dagger, huh. …What do you think, Rei? I can’t quite trust Brewitt’s recommendation alone.”

“Hey!”

Brewitt looked displeased at Teres’ words.

From Brewitt’s perspective, the honest truth was that he’d gone out of his way to recommend it, so she really had no right to say that.

But as she watched Brewitt, Teres spoke up.

“I mean, Brewitt, you’re often in physical fights, right? Plus, there’s a big difference in our builds. If I buy something recommended by someone like you…”

“No, but I don’t think Brewitt’s recommendation is wrong.”

As if to cut off Teres’s words, Rei interjects.

In reality, if the purpose is limited to self-defense, the choices are inevitably few.

There’s no way she could possibly carry around a massive halberd just to protect herself… and above all, since Teres doesn’t seem particularly athletic, such a heavy weapon would likely be difficult for her to even lift.

Above all, considering he’s limited the choice to weapons he can conceal, the options naturally become even fewer.

“But, come to think of it. If we’re talking about something other than a dagger that she can conceal… maybe brass knuckles? Nah, we don’t have any here.”

“Hey, I don’t think it’d make any sense to give those to Teres.”

“...What kind of weapon is that?”

Perhaps because she didn’t like being told it wouldn’t do her any good, Teres shot Brewitt a stern glance before asking Rei.

The reason Brewitt knew about brass knuckles was probably because he’d gotten into a lot of fights in town.

“Well… to put it simply, it’s like a simplified version of a gauntlet. It has four slots where you slip your fingers in, and you grip it to punch with.”

At the direct word “punch,” Teres made a disgusted face.

Since she was familiar with gauntlets, Rei’s explanation gave her a general idea of what a knuckle was.

She understood, but... if asked whether she could actually use one herself, she would tilt her head in doubt.

“It’s not really my style.”

“Really? Since knuckles are made of metal, even without a blade, just jabbing the sharp metal parts into an opponent’s body packs quite a punch. And since they aren’t blades, they won’t kill someone with a single blow.”

“No, I’d rather not deal with anything that dangerous.”

“...I think they’re actually less dangerous than something with a blade, like a dagger. In that case, what else is there... maybe a short blowgun?”

If it were a blowgun about ten centimeters long, it wouldn’t be too difficult for Teres—who often wears dresses—to carry around.

Of course, a blow dart like that would be a one-time-use, disposable item… but if you think of it purely as a hidden weapon to protect yourself in an emergency, it shouldn’t be a bad idea at all.

“A blowgun… Hmm, a blowgun, huh. It might be better than a weapon like knuckles… but… hmm…”

To Teres, a blowgun seemed more suited to her than the knuckles Rei had described, but the fact remained that she couldn’t quite picture herself actually using one.

“Anything else… how about one with a weight attached to a thin chain? I don’t know if this counts as a type of morning star or not, though.”

“No, that’s not a morning star.”

Brewitt immediately dismissed the idea of it being a morning star, but if asked what other kind of weapon it might be...

“Then, just a chain and a weight? Anyway, if it’s a small chain and weight, there are plenty of places to hide it, and I think it’d be a pretty effective weapon in a pinch. Though, it looks pretty hard to handle, so I think you’d need training.”

Rei had never used that kind of weapon before, so he didn’t know exactly how difficult it was.

But seeing how Elena uses Mirage like a whip, it was easy to understand that a chain flail would likely be just as difficult to handle.

“I have a feeling they’d suit me better than blowguns or anything like that.”

“I think it’ll be hard to master… but well, if that’s what you want, why not give it a try? Unfortunately, it doesn’t look like they sell them here.”

The weapons sold in this shop—the ones displayed on the shelves—are almost all popular weapons like longswords and spears.

There are some hammer-like weapons, but they’re very few and far between.

Weapons with few users are, after all, much harder to sell.

Given that, if you wanted a weapon like a chain flail, you’d have to order one to be made rather than buying one off the shelf.

“...I’ll go with a dagger after all. It seems like that would be less of a hassle in the long run.”

Perhaps thinking it was too much trouble to have one made, Teres said this and, taking advice from Rei and Brewitt, purchased a dagger that looked easy to use from among the many on display.

Meanwhile, while Rei, Teres, and Brewitt were talking, Ranigus continued discussing weapons with the dwarf.
