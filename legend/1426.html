After finishing his exchange of information with Gale, Rei was already heading toward the Lerdact County with Set.

However, this time they weren't heading there openly; instead, they were infiltrating by flying through the air.

Given the nature of the retaliation mission Rei had been assigned, this was only natural.

Still, the covert operation was only until the act of revenge was completed.

(Destroying the mine was just a bonus... but surely no one would be at the mine at night, right?)

While Rei had no qualms about taking the life of an enemy, he still felt reluctant to kill someone who was merely working in the mines.

Therefore, the plan was to strike at night when the mine would be empty.

Of course, it wasn't guaranteed they'd all be gone, and a few might remain, so thorough reconnaissance would be necessary.

"Anyway, Jazz's brother... that Dostrite guy in Hazlue or whatever. Even if I go meet him, it'd be better after dark. ...Sneaking in should be easy enough."

In the frontier town of Gilm... well, it's hard to imagine anywhere else like it, but apparently, in the Earl of Lerdact's domain, the city walls are rather neglected.

This is apparently because the heavy taxes are barely enough to survive on, leaving no budget for repairs to walls or the like.

It wouldn't be surprising if bandit and monster raids were rampant, but perhaps Lerdact is mindful of that issue. Rei had heard from Gale that they actively encourage bandit suppression.

(If the taxes are that heavy, it wouldn't be surprising if people couldn't make ends meet and turned to banditry... But that isn't happening? That part is puzzling.)

As they flew through the sky surveying the surroundings, a relatively large town eventually came into view in the distance.

It was Hazlue, the destination Rei had been aiming for.

"Set, shall we kill time nearby until nightfall? ...Maybe grab a leisurely meal somewhere?"

"Guruu!"

At Rei's words, Set purred happily.

Even while Rei was talking with Gale, soldiers and maids who couldn't resist Set's cuteness had given him dried meat and roasted meat. Still, it was clearly never enough for Set.

Purring happily, he descended toward the ground.

Set's destination was a place quite a distance from Hazlue, where several large boulders lay scattered.

Thanks to his speed, he arrived in no time, but the distance from Hazlue to this rocky area would take over half a day to cover on foot.

For Rei and Set, who planned to sneak into Hazrui at night, it was the perfect hideout.

...Or so it seemed...

"Damn it, someone's already here?"

Several figures in his line of sight must have spotted Set descending. Even Rei could tell they abruptly shifted from resting to combat readiness, flustered.

(What should we do?)

For a moment, Rei wondered if it might be better to just fly away without approaching the rocky area. But then he realized that when it came to gathering information, the more sources, the better.

In that case, rather than flying away, wouldn't it be quicker to just overwhelm them all at once?

Making that judgment, he gave a small nod to Set, who was looking at him questioningly, and spoke.

"Go. Right now, I need every source of information I can get."

"Gurrrr!"

Set looked slightly disappointed, probably expecting something to eat, but he still understood Rei's words. He let out a growl and descended toward the ground.

As Rei and Set descended, several figures on the ground drew bows and fired arrows at them.

However, those arrows were easily sliced away by the Death Scythe Rei drew from the Misty Ring.

Arrows fired while Rei and Set were still in the air lost their momentum by the time they reached them, and likely wouldn't have pierced Set even if they had.

Still, Rei cut them away, just in case.

"If they're willing to go that far, we need to respond accordingly. ...Let's go, Set."

"GRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set flapped his wings and swiftly descended toward the ground.

Those on the rocky outcrop must have already judged that bows were useless. Those holding bows switched to spears, longswords, battle-axes, and other weapons.

"Huh."

A hint of amusement tinged Rei's voice as he spoke.

They might not know who he was, but seeing Set, they surely understood he was a powerful monster.

And yet, seeing them wait for Set to descend without even attempting to flee... it made him feel a little pleased, if only a little.

"Well, even so, I might need to go easy on him to get the information... right?"

Once he confirmed Set had descended to about ten meters above him, Rei jumped off Set's back.

Naturally, falling from a height of nearly ten meters would be fatal under normal circumstances.

But with Rei, it was different.

He activated the Sleipnir Boots mid-air, decelerating as he descended toward the rocky ground.

Even so, the speed he fell at would easily have broken a normal person's legs. But Rei's physical capabilities were vastly different from those of an ordinary human.

Just before hitting the rocks, he bent his knees... and with that single movement, he absorbed the entire impact of the fall, landing without even a sound.

"What!?"

Those who had attacked Rei and Set likely never imagined witnessing such a maneuver up close.

Everyone froze for an instant, their expressions stunned... That brief pause was more than enough time for Rei to make his next move.

"Hah!"

With a short exhalation, the Death Scythe swung.

He deliberately aimed the hilt, not the blade, likely because it was better to keep his interrogation subjects alive.

A sensation like crushing something filled the hand gripping the Death Scythe... and in the next instant, a man was sent flying.

"Ah."

Seeing the man's body slam into a nearby rock, a slight "oops" escaped Rei's lips. But the others, seeing their comrade blown away, snapped back to reality and immediately prepared to attack Rei...

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set flew right in front of the man who had raised his weapon.

He hadn't actually attacked; he'd simply flown by... But when a three-meter-tall Set flapped his wings and flew, it created a tremendous wind pressure all on its own.

At the very least, it was only natural that the men who had been about to attack Rei lost their balance from the unexpected attack coming from an unexpected direction.

"Whoa!"

"What!?"

"Dammit!"

Screaming like that, the men tried desperately to regain their balance... but Rei wouldn't let such an opening go to waste. In the next instant, the Death Scythe swung again, striking the others. Within minutes, nearly twenty men lay unconscious.

"...Phew. Somehow I managed not to kill them all. ...Right?"

After stowing the Death Scythe in Misty Ring, Rei turned his gaze to the first man he'd knocked flying.

The men who had attacked him later, half-crazed, had lost consciousness easily with a single blow from the Death Scythe. But the man he had blown away first had hit his body against the rock, so his injuries might be more severe than the others'.

"I know it feels wrong to use this on someone who attacked me... but then again, seeing Set out of nowhere would confuse anyone."

Muttering, she poured a potion taken from the Misty Ring onto the man lying a short distance away—the one Rei had blown away first.

That potion, however, was something sold in shops—the kind any adventurer in Gilm could buy and use without thinking twice.

At the very least, it wasn't some rare concoction made from World Tree ingredients.

"Grrr..."

Hearing Rei's mutter, Set, who had been watching over the unconscious men, let out a slightly sorrowful growl.

To that Set, Rei opened his mouth with an expression that said, "Well, what can you do?"

"These guys didn't know about Set. It can't be helped. If they knew Set well, they wouldn't be scared of him."

"Gurururu?"

Set tilted his head slightly, asking, "Really?" Without hesitation, Rei nodded.

In truth, many had been frightened by Set's appearance before, but once they actually interacted with him, most were charmed by his lovable nature.

Even if they didn't go that far, many softened their attitude toward Set.

So, as Rei encouraged Set not to be so downhearted, a groan finally escaped from the mouth of one of the unconscious men.

The groan came from the man Rei had knocked out first... the one he'd accidentally slammed into the rock.

Even someone like that... was it because Rei used a potion that he was the first to come to?

"Ugh... ugh..."

"Come on, wake up. I've got a lot I want to hear from you."

Ray gave the groaning man a light kick to the side to hasten his awakening.

And then, several dozen seconds later... the man Rei had blown away slowly opened his eyes.

"Me...?"

"Looks like you're awake. Remember me?"

"Huh? Wha... What the hell!? Ow!"

The man's gaze locked onto Rei's figure, and he frantically tried to stand up.

But the next moment, pain shot through his body, and a cry of agony escaped his lips.

Rei had used a potion, but it was still a cheap one.

It healed the scrapes and cuts on his skin from hitting the rock, but it was nowhere near enough to repair the damage inflicted by the Death Scythe's blow.

The man's condition was actually quite convenient for Rei at that moment.

"I'll ask again. Do you remember me?"

"You... who are you?"

It wasn't a feigned response; he genuinely didn't know who Rei was, deep down.

Was it the shock of the Death Scythe's blow? Or had he lost consciousness before even confirming Rei's form...? Whatever the reason, it was unexpected for Rei that the person before him didn't remember him.

"Ah... Then look over there. Do you recognize that?"

Rei pointed to Set, who was watching over the other unconscious men a short distance away.

"Huh!?"

Seeing Set, the man must have recalled what had happened just before he lost consciousness.

Glaring at Rei with an intensity that belied his injuries, he opened his mouth in frustration.

"Damn it, that bastard Lerdact... He mentioned hiring skilled fighters, but I never imagined he'd hire a monster like this too..."

"What?"

Rei murmured in surprise at the words escaping the man's mouth.

From Rei's perspective, he had assumed these men were simply bandits or the like.

But this mutter sounded different from what thieves would say.

Of course, even if they were thieves... no, precisely because they were thieves, it was only natural they'd resent Lerdact, who ruled this land, for possessing such formidable military strength.

But the words that came out of the man's mouth felt somehow different from what such people would utter.

No, the content was the same, but perhaps it was the atmosphere or impression that felt different.

"Who the hell are you? You don't seem like just some common thief."

"Thieves?! Why the hell should we be lumped in with those scum?! They're the ones who steal from people and kill!"

Seeing him shout with such disgust, Rei let out a small sigh.

It was clear that the being screaming before him was, at the very least, not his enemy.

Though calling him anything but an enemy felt a bit off to Rei, given how he'd attacked the flying Set without warning, he somehow realized the true nature of the being before him.

The Lerdact extorting heavy taxes from the residents, their high alertness, the attack on the flying Set, and now this man's words.

Considering all these points together, it wasn't too difficult for Rei to guess the true identities of the people before him.

No, given what he'd heard about Lerdact's reputation, it would be strange if such people weren't here. In other words...

"Rebels, or perhaps the Resistance?"

At the words that came from Rei's mouth, the man's eyes widened in shock, wondering how their true identities had been uncovered.
