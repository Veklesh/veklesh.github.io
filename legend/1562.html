In the end, only a few of the giant corpses were stored in Rei's Misty Ring.

That said, this was solely for the purpose of researching what kind of beings the giants were, not as materials or food, as Byune pointed out.

This arrangement represented the maximum concession Rei could make.

Leaving behind the fourth basement level, where numerous giant corpses remained, Rei and the others headed back down to the fifth basement level.

Unlike when they first arrived, however, not a single giant remained on the fifth basement level.

Rei's plan to lure all the giants to the fourth basement level and eliminate them had succeeded.

"...Looking at it like this, there doesn't seem to be anything particularly strange about that black crystal, does there?"

Vihera murmured as she looked at the black crystal from the stairs.

Indeed, even now, looking at the black crystal, there was nothing particularly unsettling about it.

Just as she said, it looked like nothing more than an ordinary black crystal.

But... Rei knew it was merely an illusion.

The black crystal's complete lack of movement was simply because the giants weren't present.

Originally, Rei and the others had lured the giants down to the fourth basement level precisely for this purpose, so this situation was as planned... but still.

"Anyway, let's head down the stairs. We don't know what they might do, so stay alert so we can respond immediately to anything."

If what awaited them on the fifth basement level were humans, or perhaps monsters, their actions might be somewhat predictable.

But the enemy was the Black Crystal—an unknown entity.

If we touched it carelessly and it caused that black snow Rei saw earlier to fall... Thinking that, Rei couldn't possibly act recklessly either.

Though, to be honest, even Rei didn't know what touching that black snow would actually do.

(I mean, I've got the Dragon Robe and all, so I should be fine unless something really bad happens. But still... honestly, I'd rather not risk it if I can help it.)

He thought this while staring at the black crystal, but he couldn't just stand there forever.

Eventually, Rei took a deep breath as if to steel himself, then began descending the stairs.

"Hey, what should we do?"

Rei called out from behind him, but Rei only glanced back briefly before speaking.

"We don't know what's out there, so just watch things from there for now. If you notice anything, just let us know."

"Got it. Be careful."

Marina told her so, and as the others also urged her to be careful, Rei descended the stairs.

Stepping onto the fifth basement floor, he looked around again.

"This is quite different."

That was Rei's honest reaction upon seeing his surroundings.

Up to the fourth basement level, everything was neatly maintained, with floors and walls indistinguishable from those above ground.

But this fifth basement level was literally just the ground dug up and left as is.

The scene, reminiscent of a cave, naturally raised questions in Rei's mind.

After all, the black crystal should have been extremely important to Jaya.

If this was the place where the Black Crystal was kept, it should have been properly maintained.

(So, there must be a reason this place is like this... right? Well, it would be weird if there wasn't. Still... Hmm.)

Still pondering, Rei looked around again.

There was nothing particularly noteworthy, and not a single survivor of the giants remained.

"For now... guess I'll just have to try it myself."

Since it was left in a dug-up state, the surrounding soil was naturally exposed, with numerous small stones scattered about.

The ground was clearly packed solid, likely from the giants repeatedly walking over it.

How many giants had come here?

And why, when Rei and the others first came to this fifth underground floor, were the giants huddled together in a circle around the black crystal?

(Seriously, why?)

Rei pondered the question, but he realized the answer probably lay in examining the black crystal itself.

He came to that conclusion and began slowly approaching the black crystal.

With each step forward, the sensation emanating from the black crystal grew stronger.

What it was, what it meant... Rei couldn't tell, but there was no doubt something was there.

Aware of this, Rei continued to close the distance between himself and the black crystal.

"Hey, Rei! Is it really safe to approach it so directly!?"

Marina's voice called out from behind him, but those words held no meaning for him now.

Overwhelmed by an emotion he couldn't even understand himself, he drew nearer to the black crystal...

"Rei, can you hear me!? Rei! Hey, can you hear me!?"

After Marina, Vihera's voice reached him.

Listening to that voice, Rei finally reached right beside the black crystal... and gently reached out her hand.

「Rei！」

He flinched.

At the last voice she heard—Elena's—Rei's movement froze for an instant.

But by the time he froze, Rei's hand had already touched the black crystal.

In that instant... as if some switch had been flipped, Rei completely lost all sense of where he was.

No, he didn't even know who he was anymore.

The scenery around him was no longer the cave-like basement from moments before, but an endless expanse of pure black.

(Space? ...No, what even is space? Who am I, anyway?)

Unaware that he couldn't even speak, he simply looked around.

He couldn't even remember touching the black crystal just seconds ago, nor could he comprehend why he was here.

He couldn't even remember who he was... a phenomenon that would be utterly incomprehensible under normal circumstances.

And yet, Rei's mind remained unshaken. In fact, he was strangely calm, even to himself.

(What the hell is this place...? Where am I...? Who the hell am I...? Huh?)

In this half-contradictory state of calm confusion, Rei suddenly noticed something.

The density of the darkness surrounding him was increasing.

Since it was a darkness so black it could only be called jet-black, whether its density or concentration increased, it wouldn't make much difference.

But somehow, Rei could understand that the darkness around him was becoming denser.

(What? What's happening? No, what even is this...?)

He didn't know who he was, where he was, or what kind of situation he was in.

In this state, the darkness around Rei grew even more concentrated... until the gaseous darkness became so dense it liquefied... and then, upon touching Rei's body, began to seep in.

Rei didn't understand what this specifically meant, but he grasped that it was an inconvenient phenomenon for him.

But even understanding that, she couldn't grasp the danger it posed as a tangible feeling.

This disconnect in sensation left her utterly lost, unsure what to do or how to react...

'GRRRRRRRRRR!'

Suddenly, that sound echoed through the space, seemingly coming from nowhere.

Even as the darkness consumed him, Rei still couldn't feel any sense of danger. He looked around, trying to figure out where the voice had come from.

But in a space filled only with liquid darkness, looking around offered no way to comprehend what might be there.

Was it just his imagination? Rei wondered, but the voice sounded again, seemingly from nowhere.

"Rei, what are you doing?! You are with me, aren't you?!"

The stern voice echoed around him once more.

The moment he heard it, Rei looked around again.

But, as expected, there was no sign of anyone.

Nothing but an impenetrable, pitch-black void stretched out around him.

Nothing. He understood that. Yet, hearing that voice now, he still felt as if something was there.

(What is this inside me? ...A warm, yet vivid golden light. Wait, warm? What is that? Gold? What is that? What am I thinking? What the hell is going on?)

Something inside me.

He should know, yet he doesn't. All Rei could do was feel bewildered by this thing.

But it was undoubtedly something important to him... something irreplaceable. Even so, he couldn't grasp what it meant to be important.

"Rei, what's wrong? We're having a party at my place tonight, right? Afterwards, I'll teach you how to spend a relaxing adult evening. Look forward to it."

The voice sounded again.

That rich, velvety voice brought a sense of calm just by hearing it... yet it also stirred conflicting feelings of unease.

But naturally, Rei couldn't understand what that feeling was either. All she could do was feel bewildered.

He understood that the feeling within him was irreplaceable, but he couldn't grasp the very essence of that irreplaceable feeling itself.

"Rei, I'm going to win today's mock battle! If I win, let's go to the river. But if you win, I'll take you to eat at the food stalls."

That voice, heard for the fourth time.

The spirited voice stirred a strange vitality within Rei.

Without knowing what that vitality was, Rei nonetheless let his thoughts drift to something welling up inside him.

(What is this... I, me, I... What is it?)

Only bewilderment was born within Rei, then vanished.

And as they vanished, they were born anew, only to vanish once more.

How many times this cycle repeated, Rei couldn't say.

No, he wasn't even aware of what he was doing.

A completely unconscious action.

But even so... the fact that she desired it meant it was something important to Rei... somewhere deep inside, she must have understood that.

Drawn by something within himself... no, it wasn't that simple; it was something that pulled him in.

Rei didn't understand that it existed. He didn't, yet somewhere deep inside... no, unconsciously, he understood it was irreplaceable to him.

He had no conscious awareness of it whatsoever, yet Rei still sought it... and the darkness surrounding him accelerated its pace of encroachment within him, refusing to be outdone by that something.

Rei remained completely unaware, merely staring silently at the dark space surrounding him. Yet within Rei, the light currently within him and the darkness surrounding him were locked in a stalemate, battling each other.

(Light... warm? What's that? Darkness? What's dark?)

Was he aware of what was happening within himself, or not?

Rei simply existed within the dark space.

Was she standing? Lying down? Or floating?

He didn't know what state he was in, but even in such a situation, he felt no confusion.

No, perhaps the very concept of confusion was beyond Rei's current understanding.

With a sensation akin to drifting through the darkness, Rei simply gazed intently at his surroundings.

How much time had passed in this state...? Rei didn't even comprehend the concept of time itself. Even if she had understood it, she wouldn't have known if it had been one minute, one hour, one day... or even one month, one year since she entered this dark space.

Rei simply drifted through the darkness, doing nothing.

Even then, a battle between light and darkness raged within Rei, though he himself was completely unaware of it.

But... in the midst of this, a voice echoed within Rei once more.

『Rei！』

The voices of many women overlapped, and among them, the sound of 'Guruu' somehow felt like it was calling out to Rei... And the instant that voice came from within himself, Rei instantly understood who he was and what situation he was in.

"I am..."

The first physical voice uttered while trapped within this dark space.

And the instant Rei voiced it from his own mouth, he finally grasped the darkness that had been trying to consume him.

Once he understood that darkness, Rei's actions were swift.

"Disappear... the darkness inside me... Disappear!"

Along with those words, an immense amount of magical power erupted from Rei.

The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the most powerful skill within Rei’s arsenal.

The skill activated... and in the next instant, the darkness within his own body, the darkness surrounding him, and the very space filled with darkness itself were consumed by flames.
