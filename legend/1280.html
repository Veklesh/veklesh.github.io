Kakou's shop, White Fragrance, wasn't particularly spacious.

Then again, considering Kakou ran it alone, it might be considered spacious enough.

Inside that shop, various cosmetics were crammed onto the shelves.

"...Is this okay?"

Other shops also boldly displayed their merchandise, but that was because they were confident they could handle theft themselves, had bodyguards, or were selling counterfeits.

But as far as Rei could tell, the cosmetics displayed here didn't look like fakes.

Given that situation, and the fact that no one was left to watch the shop while Kako was outside deep in conversation with Kenny, it was no wonder Rei felt that way.

"Ahaha. Well, this shop is frequented by guild staff and adventurers too. Quite a few people use perfumes blended by Kakou, you know? Do you really think those people would just let someone who harmed this shop go unpunished?"

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to agree.

Rei knew how much women spent on beauty.

Back in Japan, he'd seen his own mother buy countless cosmetics with his own eyes.

Watching Kenny shed his coat and layers, Rei asked about something that had puzzled him in the explanation.

"But then, doesn't that mean I'm not really needed?"

If guild staff, female adventurers, and even a few male adventurers bought cosmetics, sales wouldn't be a problem.

It wasn't Kenny who answered Rei's question, but Kako, who had been playing with Set.

"No way! I'd love for more people to enjoy perfume, you know? Besides, there's even perfume made for adventurers!"

"Perfume for adventurers?"

Hearing that, Rei thought of perfume meant to mask body odor.

When they went out on quests like monster hunts or gathering requests, some monsters and animals possessed keen senses of smell, though not quite as sharp as wolves or dogs.

In such cases, wouldn't they use perfume to become odorless... or perhaps apply the scent of some other monster or animal?

But he shook his head immediately.

Because she realized that wouldn't really be perfume anymore.

"Yeah, you know. Guys probably don't care much, but girls get bothered by all kinds of smells, you know? Plus, in the forest and stuff, there are bugs, right?"

"Ah, that sort of thing."

Rei vaguely remembered reading or hearing somewhere that perfume originally developed as a way to mask body odor from not bathing.

Of course, that was knowledge Rei had acquired in Japan.

It wouldn't be strange if things were different in Elgin.

"Yeah. But like I said before, I'd really like others to wear perfume too. Is there any way to make that happen?"

Kakou tilted her head and asked, but since Rei had almost no knowledge on the subject, he couldn't answer.

"Even if you say that... How about creating a perfume that smells incredibly foul as a means to attack monsters or animals with keen senses?"

What Rei imagined was tear gas spray.

Of course, foul odors don't necessarily make people cry, but even so, considering it as a means of attack, it might not be such a bad idea.

That's what he thought...

"Nooo! I want you to think of a more peaceful use!"

Kakou shouted, seemingly displeased with Rei's suggestion.

"Peaceful... Well, I guess perfume is usually used peacefully. But asking me how to do that..."

Rei pondered while recalling his time in Japan, but asking Rei—who had never even properly used perfume—to think of peaceful uses for it was unreasonable.

Still, seeing Kakou looking at him with eyes brimming with tears made Rei wonder if he really should do something about it.

"Please, do something about it—"

Staring at those teary eyes, Rei agonized over what to do...

Was there anything from his own experiences in Japan?

As this thought crossed Rei's mind, an idea flashed through her brain the next instant.

He hadn't thought of anything because he'd been thinking of it as perfume.

Perfume is about scent, so if it's related to that...

"Deodorant or something?"

"No, not the kind adventurers use, something more peaceful—"

"No. Not that. Room deodorizer."

"...Room deodorizer?"

While decorating with flowers to enjoy their scent and appearance was likely common, the concept of using deodorizer inside a room probably hadn't occurred to him.

Kakou shot Rei a look that said he was completely taken aback.

"Yeah. All kinds of smells get trapped inside a room. Like the stench of adventurers' sweat, cooking smells, and all sorts of other odors. What if we made a deodorizer that eliminates those smells... or overpowers them?"

When Rei visited a friend's house in Japan, he remembered the lavender scent that greeted him upon entering the room.

Rei wasn't particularly sensitive to smells, but that friend was quite particular about the scent of their own room.

Remembering that, he thought, "A room deodorizer might sell, right?"

"That... might work?"

Kako seemed to think Rei's idea wasn't bad at all. He looked at Rei with interest.

Seeing Kako's reaction, Rei spoke again.

"But we should keep it as cheap as possible. If it's expensive like real perfume, people won't want to buy it. ...Yeah, ideally just a few copper coins."

"Whaaat!? There's no way that's feasible at that price!"

Perfume, even the cheapest kind, costs several silver coins... and the expensive ones can run up to several platinum coins.

How could he possibly make such perfume cost just a few copper coins?

Even Kako, with his naturally straightforward personality, couldn't agree with Rei's suggestion. He looked at Rei with an expression that clearly said, "I disagree."

Kenny seemed to agree with Kakou, looking at Rei with a somewhat exasperated expression.

"But still. The perfume isn't selling because it's too expensive, right? So, if we make it a bit cheaper, and people start getting interested in perfume, wouldn't we make a profit in the end?"

He probably hadn't expected such strong opposition. He muttered this, slightly surprised.

For Rei, the price he'd quoted was based on what deodorant cost back in Japan, right?

Selling it cheaply as an entry point to perfume, to spark interest in perfume itself.

Kakou pondered Rei's explanation.

"Hmm... Thinking about it that way... But..."

"I think it's fine, you know? Besides, the room deodorant Rei's talking about is just for beginners, right? So the perfume doesn't need to be top-tier quality either."

"That's true, but... what about perfume for deodorizing? The kind adventurers use is only meant for specific parts, right?"

"Huh? Isn't just leaving a perfume bottle open enough?"

Images of spray-type deodorizers from TV commercials and deodorizers that just needed to be placed somewhere flashed through Rei's mind.

Thinking that might work the same way, Rei spoke up, but Kakou shook her head.

"No way—that'd make the perfume run out way too fast, and only the spot where you put it would smell super strong."

"...Is that so?"

"Yeah. If you did it Rei-kun's way, we'd need to make specialized equipment. Plus, like Kenny said, we'd have to mix in some cheaper perfume too."

"But if it works out, wouldn't this shop get a monopoly on the deodorant? I don't know how many shops in Gilm handle cosmetics... Well, I guess copycats would show up soon enough, so it probably wouldn't last that long."

"Hmm... What should we do? If we could proceed as Rei suggested, I think more people would use the perfume..."

Profit is almost guaranteed.

She could see that, but "almost" meant there was still a chance of failure... Kako didn't have the courage to take such a gamble alone.

"Then why not invite others? Gather a few people who handle perfume like you do, and pool your money. What you need is a device to keep the perfume from running out quickly, right?"

"But then you wouldn't get to keep all the profits for yourself," Kenny concluded.

Kenny's words seemed appealing to Kakou too.

He immediately started thinking about who to invite: this person, that person, and...

"Are you okay with the profits being smaller?"

Seeing Kakou readily choose the safer option, Rei asked Kenny.

"Yes. Kakou's always been the type who likes doing things with others. ...Of course, I also like doing things with you, Rei-kun?"

Kenny shot Rei a sidelong glance, but Rei seemed oblivious, his gaze fixed on Kakou.

(Being a dog beastman, does he really prefer being in a group?)

Dogs like to act together with their pack, while cats are individualists.

That was just Rei's impression, but even beastmen probably weren't entirely free from those tendencies.

"Hey, Rei-kun. Are you listening to me?"

"Hm? Oh, sorry. What was it?"

"...Sigh. Well, whatever. About the perfume—any other good ideas?"

"It's not like I can just come up with something on the spot. It took me a while to even think of the room deodorizer. ...Ah, but maybe insect repellent perfume would be handy if we had it."

Whether it was due to Rei's magic or the power of the griffin, Set.

He couldn't tell, but insects like mosquitoes never approached Rei or Set.

In that sense, insect repellent perfume wasn't strictly necessary, but she thought it might be handy when traveling with others.

"Oh! Well then, I'll give you this one, this one, and this one."

Upon hearing Rei's words, Kakou promptly handed Rei three bottles of perfume with insect-repelling effects.

"Is it okay to take these? Perfume is usually expensive, right?"

"Yeah, they are. But you taught me about perfume, Rei-kun."

Three bottles of perfume would cost more than silver coins could cover.

Rei was surprised that Kakou would so casually hand over something of such value, but the response he got back was Kakou's typical laid-back tone.

Uncertain whether he should really accept the perfume given to him, Rei's gaze shifted toward Kenny.

But Kenny, meeting his gaze, nodded without any sign of hesitation.

"Why not take it? Honestly, thanks to Rei, Kakou can try out a new way to use perfume."

"That's right. Thanks to Rei, more people will probably use perfume too. If you think of it as payment for that, it's not a bad thing at all."

Kakou affirmed that Kenny's words were correct.

"Is that so? ...Well, I didn't exactly accept a request, so I don't really need any payment or reward. But if you insist on giving it, I'll take it."

Deciding it would be rude to refuse after being offered that far, Rei stared intently at the insect repellent perfume.

Probably because it was made for adventurers. Compared to other perfumes, it had a rather rough-and-ready design.

Perfumes used by nobles, on the other hand, often featured containers that were works of art in themselves.

Beyond glass, they might use materials infused with magic, or even creations crafted by alchemists using precious stones.

But there was no need for adventurers' perfume to have such elaborate containers.

Considering it might break during combat, there was no need to carry such expensive items.

...Though, admittedly, some carried such perfume bottles not for the perfume itself, but as high-value items to exchange for gems.

This isn't particularly unusual.

It stands to reason that items crafted from processed gems would be more valuable than simple stones themselves.

It wasn't unusual to possess something of high monetary value, not just gemstone perfume bottles.

"Hmm. Now I can show everyone just how wonderful perfume is!"

Kakou smiled happily as Rei obediently handed over the perfume.

For Kako, business was important, but... above all, she wanted many people to know about and use the perfume she was passionate about.

That this naturally broadened his business opportunities was an undeniable truth.

After this, the novelty of the deodorant for room use caused it to become quite the trend within Gilm... The nobles of the royal capital learned of its existence, bought it up without regard for cost, and the deodorant became one of Gilm's famous products.

Naturally, merchants outside Gilm tried to create similar deodorizers, but they had to give up because several key ingredients could only be obtained in the frontier regions.
