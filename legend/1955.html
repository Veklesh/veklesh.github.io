It was almost as if Rei leaped onto the stairs and the tentacles struck the wall right next to them at the exact same moment.

That alone was proof of the speed and force with which the pink tentacles extending from the rift in space were attacking their target.

“...Phew.”

Rei sheathed the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, which had slightly slashed the wall of the staircase, into his Misty Ring and breathed a sigh of relief.

It seemed the tentacles wouldn’t attack him now that he was on the stairs.

“We’re safe, then. …But considering how long those tentacles are, I’d think they could have easily reached us here.”

One of the guards behind Rei muttered that.

In reality, no one knew exactly how long the tentacles extending from the spatial rift actually were.

Well, perhaps someone familiar with those tentacles—the one behind this Kobold incident—might know, but unfortunately, no one here, including Rei, had any idea.

“Just a wild guess, but maybe those tentacles can only move freely within the underground space, and if they step even a single step outside of it, they’re helpless?”

“Ah, that’s possible. Even the person behind this whole incident wouldn’t be so reckless as to let something that dangerous roam freely.”

One of the guards nodded in agreement with Rei’s words.

In fact, considering that the tip of the tentacle didn’t pierce the skin—no, that there wasn’t even a wound—and given that this was an utterly terrifying opponent capable of sucking out bodily fluids, internal organs, and flesh the moment it made contact, allowing such a dangerous creature to move about freely would be far too risky.

Given those circumstances, it makes sense to keep them from leaving the underground space.

“Why don’t we head back up for now? We’d probably be fine staying here, but that’s no guarantee. If our assumptions are wrong, I definitely don’t want to have to fight that tentacle in a cramped space like this.”

Whether it’s the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear, both are considered long-range weapons.

With ordinary long weapons, you’d close the distance to deal with them, but for someone with Rei’s skill, dealing with them even at close range isn’t difficult.

But… on stairs like these, it’s impossible to even swing the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear in the first place.

In fact, when Rei leaped onto these stairs earlier, his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear didn’t fit inside the space and ended up slashing the walls instead.

(Honestly, I wonder how it is that these are easier to cut through than those tentacles...)

Looking at the severed spot, Rei once again pondered the tentacles’ peculiar nature.

It wasn’t simply a matter of them being sturdy; those tentacles possessed the troublesome property of having strong elasticity, like rubber or something, while still being durable.

Although they’d managed to fight without any particular problems this time, what would happen if they faced a similar situation next time…? If they were attacked in a confined space like the one they were in now, it wasn’t that they couldn’t handle it, but it would undoubtedly be a struggle.

Perhaps agreeing with Rei’s assessment, the guards said nothing further and began climbing the stairs.

And, naturally, the conversation they had while climbing the stairs was about the tentacles they’d just encountered.

“So, do you think those tentacles are involved in the Kobold incident after all?”

The guards looked troubled as Rei spoke, keeping a watchful eye behind them just in case the tentacle might be following them.

“Hmm… I’m not sure. Logically, that makes sense, but… do you really think that tentacle is capable of controlling Kobolds or something like that? To begin with, what kind of connection do you think there is between that tentacle and the Kobolds?”

If what had emerged from the spatial rift had taken the form of a dog or a wolf, they might have been able to accept the connection to the Kobolds.

But what emerged from the spatial rift was a pink tentacle. It was drenched in some kind of liquid—the sort of tentacle that wouldn’t be out of place if called obscene.

There was no way Rei could believe those tentacles had any connection to the Kobold.

“Those tentacles, huh. Yeah. No matter how I look at it, I can’t see how they could possibly be related to the Kobolds. …Which makes me wonder—could it be that those tentacles actually have nothing to do with the Kobold situation at all?”

“No, the Kobold incident alone is a major issue. And yet, they’re simultaneously proceeding with what looks like a ritual to deal with such a dangerous entity? If you think about it logically, there’s no doubt those tentacles are connected to the Kobold incident.”

"Hmm... I can’t say either way. With the information we have now, it’s clearly not enough, no matter how you look at it. If that’s the case, I think we should gather more solid information before making a judgment."

Three different opinions.

It just goes to show that just because they’re all guards doesn’t mean their opinions will necessarily align.

While keeping an eye on his back, Rei found himself strangely impressed.

In the end, without being attacked by tentacles from behind again, they arrived at the room with the hidden staircase…

"Huh!?"

The guard who had gone ahead poked his head out, gasped for a moment, but immediately looked relieved.

The reason was the sight of the other guards who had gathered in this room while Rei and the others were in the underground space.

“Don’t give us such a scare. I was sure those guys were lying in wait for us over here.”

With those words, the guards behind him and Rei’s group must have realized who was in the room. They entered the room, looking relieved.

Inside the room were five security guards.

Among them was even the man who, perhaps by good timing, had informed Rei that he’d found that massive pile of skeletal remains.

“What do you mean, here?”

“I was searching around the mansion when I saw the wall in this room had been broken. I was wondering what to do when you guys walked by.”

One guard answered the other’s question, and the guard who had been told he’d just happened to be passing by nodded in response.

“I see. …We were really lucky we didn’t come down the stairs.”

“What happened?”

The guard shot a questioning glance at his colleague, who spoke as if murmuring to himself, with a sense of deep, heartfelt conviction.

As a colleague, he knew the other’s personality, so it was only natural to be puzzled by such a remark.

The guard who was asked hesitated, unsure how to describe what he had experienced... but as if to save him, Rei, who had been listening to the conversation beside him, spoke up.

“That corpse you found—reduced to nothing but skin and bones. I went and saw with my own eyes exactly how it was made.”

“That is…”

The guard who had found the skeleton-like corpse in this mansion was at a loss for words at Rei’s statement.

In fact, as someone who had no idea how such a corpse could be created, he was probably intrigued.

But seeing the guard who had come up the stairs with Rei, he hesitated, unsure if it was wise to ask such a question... Seeing this, the guard shook his head, telling him not to hold back, and proceeded to describe the scene he had witnessed in the basement.

An underground space that extends not only beneath this mansion but also into the grounds of the surrounding estates.

Furthermore, the staircase leading from the underground space likely connects to the surrounding mansions as well.

And that, via a different staircase from the one Rei and the others were on, the mindless Akabu had been brought up, bound with rope, by a single man.

They managed to capture the man who brought them and learned he belonged to the Royal Faction, but at that very moment, a rift opened in the center of the underground space.

Pink tentacles emerged from the rift, and the man from the Royal Faction who was touched by their tips had all his internal organs, bodily fluids, and muscles sucked out, leaving him nothing but skin and bones.

The mindless Red Cloth members were killed just like that man, and Rei and the guard managed to escape.

“In the end, there must be some kind of restriction preventing those tentacles from leaving the underground space—once we entered the stairwell, they didn’t pursue us any further.”

Having told the whole story, he concluded with that.

The guard who had been listening wore an expression of deep revulsion.

Partly because the guard who told the story was strangely eloquent, he could easily imagine the scene Rei and the others had witnessed.

……No, in some cases, a person’s imagination can provide a stronger sense of realism than seeing something with one’s own eyes.

In that sense, it wouldn’t be surprising if this gave the guards a sense of terror even stronger than the experience Rei and the others had felt firsthand.

“Well, it was a bit exaggerated in parts, but that’s generally how it was. Those tentacles were hard enough that even my Death Scythe met with some resistance… To be precise, they were elastic, so considering that, it’d be pretty tough for an ordinary adventurer to take them on.”

As Rei spoke up to continue the guard’s story, the guards’ expressions once again turned to one of distaste.

That said, their displeased expressions weren’t the kind of imagined reaction the men had felt earlier; rather, they were genuine concerns about what they would do if they actually encountered a tentacle that was difficult to sever even with Rei’s Death Scythe.

At the very least, it was certain that the guards present here, with their skills and weapons, would be unable to stand up to those tentacles.

“Is that true?”

One of the guards in the room asked, seemingly unable to help himself.

Knowing how strong Rei was, even coming from him, they couldn’t believe it… or rather, to be honest, they didn’t want to believe it.

But it wasn’t just Rei; the guard who had been with him also shook his head.

“It’s unfortunate, but what Rei’s saying is definitely true. For starters, the tentacles’ movement speed… or should I say flight speed? It’s incredibly fast, and just touching one of those tentacles would kill us. We’re no match for it.”

“...Troublesome.”

“The one silver lining is that it doesn’t kill you instantly upon contact with the tip of the tentacle; it feels more like it sucks various things out of your body. So maybe… just maybe, if you can somehow manage to pull away from the tentacle immediately after touching it, you might not die. …Though, I definitely wouldn’t want to try that myself.”

Having witnessed the tentacles kill people firsthand, the guard’s words carried a strong sense of conviction.

The guard who had been listening swallowed hard, then spoke again.

“So, what do we do now? If something that dangerous is down there, we can’t just leave it alone. I don’t know if it’s related to the Kobold incident or not, but…”

“For now, it’s almost certain that this mansion and the surrounding ones have staircases leading to the underground space. That means we’ve got a legitimate reason to mobilize a large number of guards, don’t we? Though we don’t know how long that rift in space will remain open.”

At Rei’s words, some of the guards wore bright expressions of agreement, while others looked disapproving.

“I think it’ll probably be fine. I think so… but still, since it’s not certain, I can’t say for sure.”

“But if we go down to the underground space, there will definitely be bodies left behind. Isn’t that alone reason enough for us to act as guards?”

“If there were any tentacles left behind, it would have been a sure thing…”

In response to Rei’s words, someone who believed it would be difficult to mobilize the guards on a large scale spoke up.

However, since the tentacles Rei had severed had vanished into dust, not even a single fragment remained.

If there had been any remains of the tentacles, they could have served as evidence…

(Could it be that the owner of that tentacle had thought of that too? ...No way. It didn’t seem to have any intelligence. Ah, but since there’s a high probability it’s connected to the Kobold incident, maybe it isn’t without intelligence after all. And besides...)

Although he is already dead, the man who brought the Red Cloths had mentioned that the tentacle was connected to the Kobold incident.

The problem, however, is that the man who gave that testimony was turned into a corpse—nothing but skin and bones—by the tentacles.

Because of that, the only ones who heard it were Rei and the guards, so it’s too weak to be considered evidence.

Deeply regretting that the tentacles had killed that man, Rei spoke up.

“Anyway, we found some documents, and there’s also the matter of the underground space. So, could you please ask the guards to take action? If necessary, I can even speak to Lord Daskar myself.”

“...No, I appreciate your concern, Rei, but it’s best to avoid that. If we do that, it could lead to all sorts of problems down the line.”

Hearing the guard’s words, Rei wondered, “Is that really how it works?” But since a professional guard had said so, he accepted it for the time being, and they agreed to report the matter regarding the underground space to their superiors.
