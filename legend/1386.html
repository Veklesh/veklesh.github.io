At first, when Rei and the others heard the commotion, they thought it might be some kind of quarrel among adventurers.

With so many adventurers gathered together, it was only natural that there would be people who didn't get along.

Moreover, everyone participating in this quest possessed a certain level of skill... meaning many were also stubborn individuals.

After all, skill and personality don't necessarily go hand in hand.

Well, there are some who do, but such individuals are extremely rare.

It was precisely because they were skilled that their egos grew larger, and thus it wasn't surprising if they grew to dislike each other's presence and started a fight.

But... it was Set who first realized this wasn't the kind of quarrel Rei had imagined.

He couldn't very well pull out his magic tent in a place with this many people... well, more accurately, he judged that if something happened, being inside the magic tent would put him at a disadvantage. In any case, he sat directly on the ground like the other adventurers.

...And yet, naturally, there was something different about him compared to the other adventurers.

Yes, the magic item cauldron currently near Rei and the others was the prime example.

A cauldron crafted by the alchemist Azimov, an acquaintance of Rei's.

Naturally, since it was made by an alchemist, it wasn't just an ordinary stove—it was a magic item powered by magic.

Inside that stove, Rei had taken out several types of meat he'd stored in his Misty Ring, seasoned them casually, and tossed them in.

The distinct cooking method, different from direct grilling over an open flame, filled the air with the aroma of roasting meat.

Waiting happily beside the stove for the meat to cook were Byune and... Set.

Suddenly, Set shifted his gaze from the stove toward the forest.

"Grrr..."

「Set？」

The first to notice Set's change in demeanor was Vihera, who had been watching Byune with an exasperated look.

Vihera called out to Set, but Set, the one in question, didn't respond to Vihera's call. He just stared fixedly at one spot... toward Treant Forest.

Or rather, it might be more accurate to say he was glaring.

Naturally, with Set acting like that, Rei couldn't help but notice. He paused his conversation with Marina about Treant Forest and followed Set's gaze.

"Looks like this isn't just some ordinary quarrel."

"I agree. A disturbance at this hour... well, it's obvious what it must be."

At Rei's words, Marina picked up the bow and quiver nearby.

Vihera, still wearing her gauntlets and greaves, didn't need to prepare anything special, and neither did Rei or Set.

Byune, seemingly shifting her mood, checked the presence of the scabbard holding her weapon, White Cloud.

"Wait here a moment."

Just before departure, Rei took the meat from the stove and stored it directly into Misty Ring.

"We'll eat properly once this mess is over."

Hearing Rei's words, Marina and Vihera sent him looks that were somewhere between exasperation and affection.

Only Byune nodded repeatedly toward Rei, her expression unreadable.

By then, the other adventurers around Rei and the others had also noticed the commotion they could hear.

Initially, they thought it was just a brawl, but as time passed, it became clear this wasn't a brawl at all... In fact, it was a battle against monsters actually emerging from Treant Forest. Realizing this, they immediately headed to provide backup.

"Anyway, considering how vast Treant Forest is, we have no idea how many monsters are pouring out. Our forces are limited too, so don't go wasting all your strength at once."

Marina, Vihera, and Byune nodded at Rei's words.

For Rei and Set, with their seemingly inexhaustible stamina, rampaging all night long against monsters of the caliber they faced last night in Treant would pose absolutely no problem.

But such feats were only possible because Rei and Set were exceptional, beyond the norm.

(Though, if it were Vihera, she'd probably get so engrossed in a fight against a strong enemy that she'd lose track of time.)

Though Vihera seemed bored during the fight with Treant, a smile now graced her face.

The reason for that smile was undoubtedly the sight of the sunflowers growing in Treant's forest, even larger than the trees, visible from where Rei and the others stood.

In any case, there's a real possibility Vihera could push herself too hard in the fight and reach her physical limit.

Marina, who was primarily a rear guard, was also prone to this, and it went without saying that the petite Byune would suffer from insufficient stamina.

Though Byune had gained considerable stamina through training during the winter.

Still, she was no match for Rei.

Rei's warning to not overexert themselves was met with nods from everyone, and they immediately began running toward the direction where the battle seemed to be taking place.

Although the survey team had positioned themselves in a fairly wide area, it still took them less than a few dozen seconds to reach the scene.

As the battle came into view, Rei charged toward the fray, thinking they should first attend to those under attack.

Already in hand, as usual, was the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear, retrieved from the Misty Ring, in his left.

Passing right beside an adventurer shouting "Cowards, stay back!" to those he'd warned to conserve their stamina like Rei, he slashed the Treant behind him with a single swing of the Death Scythe.

The Treant, sliced diagonally, collapsed to the ground, its upper body crumpling.

Watching this, Rei glanced at the adventurer who had been shouting about cowards and opened his mouth.

"Considering how unnatural this forest is, conserving strength over rushing into a quick fight isn't wrong, I think."

"Huh!? ...Rei?"

Naturally, as one of Gilm's adventurers, this man knew who Rei was.

Even so, seeing his strength up close like this still took him aback.

His bold personality, and the fact that his skills matched that personality, as evidenced by his participation in this quest.

But even to a man like him, Rei's strength was something unbelievable.

The speed with which she slipped past right beside him, the sharp, powerful attack that severed a tree trunk in one blow—even Treant couldn't have done that.

If that was the result of a strike delivered with all his might, the man might have been able to accept it.

But even to the man's eyes, Rei still seemed to have considerable reserves left.

(So this is... a high-rank adventurer, and one with a nickname...)

Leaving the stunned man behind, Rei shouted loudly to those engaged in combat.

"This monster doesn't have any magic stones! So strictly speaking, it might not even qualify as a monster! As for the materials, I don't know if the guild will even buy them! So forget about claiming credit for yourselves, just work together and fight!"

In truth, the corpse of the Treant Rei had handed over was said to be somewhat lacking in value as material, aside from the fact that it lacked the most crucial monster material: the magic stone.

It was perfectly usable as construction material, though...

As if hearing Rei's words, several adventurers began cooperating with each other to fight the Treant.

Perhaps they judged it a waste of stamina if the materials weren't profitable, or maybe they anticipated a long battle.

In any case, the arrival of Rei and the Crimson Wings calmed the chaos somewhat, and the adventurers began fighting together.

"Archers, gather over here!"

At Marina's call, those armed with bows gathered in that direction.

Some even wielded slingshots as weapons, but Marina accepted them without hesitation.

The priority was gathering anyone capable of ranged attacks and deploying them strategically.

They concentrated their support on adventurers fighting at a disadvantage or about to be ambushed.

...But against a Treant, bows weren't particularly effective weapons.

Of course, they weren't completely useless, but they could only serve as a deterrent at best.

Perhaps Marina understood this, for she herself did not use a bow, instead attacking with spirit magic.

Marina focused her attacks especially on the vines.

The vines, moving like snakes, crawl along the ground, making them difficult to spot.

Wind maidens, conjured by wind spirit magic, launched attack after attack.

Some adventurers found themselves captivated by the beautiful wind maidens. For those seeing the maidens' appearance and spirit magic for the first time, it was perhaps unavoidable.

Byune, however, did not believe her attacks alone could defeat the Treant and focused on keeping it at bay.

The weapon forged from the fangs of the silver lion, White Cloud, could easily slice through even a Treant.

...Still, due to the blade's length, a single strike wouldn't be enough for a fatal wound.

Not as a substitute, but branches like those of a Treant could be severed with ease.

Moreover, vine monsters were even easier to cut through than Treants.

However, the seeds occasionally falling from above were a significant nuisance for Byune.

Some were about the size of Byune's fist, while others were as large as Byune's head.

Since they fell from above on a whim, she also had to focus on dodging them.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Suddenly, a scream erupted nearby.

The adventurers nearby turned their gaze toward the source of the scream. There, an adventurer who had failed to dodge a seed attack had been struck on the arm by one.

...Well, that alone wouldn't be a problem, but if the seed had embedded itself in the body and begun growing roots inside, that was a different story.

The roots spread visibly before their eyes, visible even through the skin.

As the pulsating roots spread halfway up the body... suddenly, the adventurer stopped screaming abruptly, as if losing consciousness.

"H-hey...?"

The scene was too gruesome to bear.

An adventurer nearby, after blasting the Treant away with a hammer, cautiously called out.

But the man showed no sign of reacting to his comrade's voice... The next instant, branches burst forth suddenly, tearing through his eyes, nose, mouth, ears... even his skin itself.

...Yes, branches.

Branches, not unlike those growing on the Treant, sprouted one after another from his entire body... no, they grew out of him.

In less than ten seconds, the man who had touched the seed was transformed into a silent tree.

The only evidence that this tree had once been a man was the torn clothing and broken armor scattered on the ground, remnants of what had been ripped off when the branches erupted from his body.

"What the hell is this?! Hey, those seeds falling from above are dangerous! Don't touch them!"

The man shouted upon seeing his companion turn into a tree.

These adventurers possessed the strength required for this quest. Knowing the seeds were dangerous, avoiding them shouldn't be difficult.

And if avoidance was truly impossible, they could simply use weapons or shields to knock the seeds away.

But what's easy for adventurers is difficult for researchers who rarely move their bodies.

Researchers who do fieldwork and get some exercise might manage...

For that reason, the adventurers escorting the researchers also had to deflect the seeds raining down on them.

"Guh! ...Dammit, this is never-ending!"

The adventurer man, who had forcibly deflected a seed the size of a child's head with his shield, shouted irritably to no one in particular, his arm numb from the impact.

They knew there would be danger, given it was a rank-restricted request.

They'd also been warned beforehand that monsters, starting with the Treant, would attack once night fell.

Even so, this situation was beyond his expectations. And what's more...

"This... Could it be that the Treant Forest is expanding because of these seeds?"

"No, but people hardly ever come this far into the Treant Forest. There's no way it could spread this much..."

"No. Look. Trees are growing where the seeds fell. Humans aren't necessary for the seeds to germinate, after all."

While the adventurers struggled desperately, the researchers stood still, observing the Treant Forest and debating their opinions.

Of course, the researchers had come to the Treant Forest for this very purpose, so they were simply doing their jobs diligently.

Still... to the adventurers desperately fighting or deflecting the showering seeds, it couldn't have been interesting.
