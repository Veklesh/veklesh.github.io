When Rei and the others returned to Gilm, the surroundings were already completely bright.

It was only natural. They had fought a colossal monster like Giganto Turtle, leaving them utterly exhausted, both mentally and physically.

After resting to recover some of that fatigue, they all suddenly realized something.

Weren't there too few carriages?

The carriages they had ridden to Treant Forest had been sent back with those who hadn't participated in the battle against the Giganto Turtle, as they would have been a hindrance during the fight.

The adventurers who had come as reinforcements during the Giganto Turtle battle had arrived by carriage, and with them present, the number of people who could ride was naturally limited.

There was some leeway, so a few people could probably ride without issue, but it was absolutely impossible for everyone to fit.

That problem was solved, however, by those who had fought the Giganto Turtle boarding the carriages.

Most adventurers held goodwill toward those who had experienced the battle against the Giganto Turtle, so few were dissatisfied.

...Though that did mean a small number were dissatisfied.

Even so, they couldn't openly complain to Rei, and as a result, the reinforcements ended up walking back to Gilm.

"I've been waiting for you, adventurers! Well done resolving the matter in Treant Forest!"

Arriving at the main gate of Gilm, the adventurers disembarked from the carriage to greet them with that voice.

Those who heard the voice fell silent for a moment, then began to murmur.

It was only natural. After all, the one who had come out of the main gate to greet them was none other than Daskar Larks, the Marquis of the Frontier, who ruled this very Gilm guild.

Beside Daskar stood the current Guildmaster, Walker.

With such important figures of Gilm gathered, it was only natural that knights serving as their guards were also present.

(Even though it was morning, it wasn't quite time for the main gate to open yet.)

Due to the limited seating in the carriage, Rei rode back to Gilm on Set's back.

He glanced at his watch, curious about the time, and saw it was still before five in the morning.

Considering the distance to Gilm, it was probably still before six in the morning.

Having so many knights summoned at such an hour must have been a real nuisance for them.

It was only natural that everyone, including Rei, felt that way.

"Lord Daskar personally coming out to greet us... I must say, I'm a bit surprised,"

The one who said this was Marina, who, as a former guildmaster, was accustomed to this sort of negotiation.

Her tone was more formal than usual when dealing with Daskar, likely because she understood this was an official exchange, not a personal one.

Daskar seemed to grasp this too, his brow twitching briefly at Marina's words before he immediately smiled and spoke.

"The Treant Forest suddenly appearing near Gilm. Facing a giant monster that emerged from that forest... especially since its roar could be heard all the way in Gilm. There was an earthquake too."

"...Speaking of which, were you okay with the effects of that earthquake?"

"Yeah. Fortunately... though 'fortunately' might not be quite the right word. The shaking in Gilm was minimal. Compared to that incident when I was a kid..."

"Oh, right, that happened too. Quite a few houses collapsed back then."

Rei... and most others couldn't understand what the two were talking about, but a few veteran adventurers present seemed to grasp the situation.

They exchanged nods, each wearing a wry smile.

But Daskar must have noticed that many didn't understand their conversation. Clearing his throat softly, he spoke again.

"Anyway, everyone did well. Preparations are being made to heal your bodies. We'll treat the injured. Of course, there'll be food and drink too."

"Wooooooooooooo!"

The adventurers let out cries of joy at the unexpected hospitality Daskar had offered.

Even those who had been complaining about wanting to rest as soon as they arrived cheered enthusiastically at Daskar's offer.

"Well, you're quite well-prepared, aren't you?"

"...Hmph. I need to gather various pieces of information regarding the matter in Treant Forest. And, if possible, I'd prefer this conversation not spread too widely."

"But... didn't the roar of the Giganto Turtle reach here too? Isn't it impossible to hide that?"

"Of course we'll make an official announcement about that. But we can't have unnecessary information spreading pointlessly. We're sending the Knights and guards to Treant Forest too, and plan to close it off for a while."

"You can't possibly close off the entire forest? It's huge."

"I know. That's why I want to declare this incident resolved as soon as possible. By the way, is this Giganto Turtle the same giant turtle monster mentioned in the report?"

"Yes. Though it's not the official name; Rei just gave it that as a temporary label."

"Rei, huh. Should I praise him or reprimand him... No, in this case, it's my fault for not explaining how we'd use the Treant Forest incident."

Amidst the adventurers' cheers of joy, Daskar and Marina, no longer the center of attention, exchanged words in their usual manner.

The knight nearby could hear their exchange, but he didn't seem bothered, treating it as business as usual.

They probably figured the public exchanges had run their course.

And so, Rei and the others were all led directly to the lord's manor.

Some adventurers wondered, "Why the lord's mansion?" but they weren't told it was to prevent the Treant Forest matter from spreading too widely. Instead, they were told that at this early hour, there were only a limited number of places where banquet preparations could be made.

Of course, not everyone bought that explanation... well, most adventurers understood the situation, but none voiced their doubts.

For the adventurers dispatched as reinforcements, who arrived only to find the battle already over and ultimately had nothing to do, this feast probably served to soften their resentment at being forcibly roused from their sleep.

They hadn't complained either.

In fact, many were delighted at the prospect of free food and drink.

"Quite a few people out at this hour, huh?"

Despite it being early morning, a fair number of people were out on the streets.

Walking alongside Set, Rei muttered this to himself. Runo, who was walking nearby, opened his mouth in exasperation.

"Of course. Once it starts getting light... depending on the season, plenty of folks are already out and about before dawn. Besides, adventurers gather at the guild early in the morning anyway. ...Only a few, including Rei, go to the guild late just because they dislike crowds."

"In my case, I don't really need to force myself to take on requests anyway."

In Rei's case, he already had more money than he could possibly spend.

Even if he really wanted to earn money, he could hunt monsters and sell proof of slaying parts, magic stones, materials, and so on to make as much as he wanted.

Going to the guild is more about gathering information and meeting Lenora and Kenny.

Rei said things that would make ordinary adventurers jealous enough to go berserk, but everyone already knew that was just the kind of person Rei was.

As they talked like this, the group arrived at the lord's mansion.

Immediately, those hungry headed to the dining hall where food was prepared, while the injured went for treatment, and so on.

Rei had intended to eat too... but Marina naturally pulled him along, and all the members of Crimson Wings headed for the lord's office.

Only Set remained, lying down near the stables, relaxing and looking forward to the maid bringing him food.

(I'm jealous. I want to sleep and eat too.)

It was only natural for Rei to feel that way.

But having actually communicated with the tree-root puppet, the incarnation of Treant Forest, using the Image Language, Rei needed to hear the details from Daskar and Walker as well.

Treant Forest wasn't absolutely essential for Gilm's expansion... but it was undeniably a place that would be incredibly helpful to have.

Precisely because of that, they needed to discuss what would happen to Treant Forest from now on... and how they would be involved going forward.

Arriving at Daskar's office, they each took a seat on the sofa.

Daskar seemed to understand that Rei and the others had been up all night and hadn't eaten in a long time, instructing the maid to bring something suitable to eat.

Thus, only the members of Crimson Wings, Daskar, and Walker remained in the room.

"...Hmph. First, I suppose I should thank Rei. I never imagined such a being existed in Treant Forest. I'm grateful you found it and defeated it. Grateful, but..."

True to his words, Daskar was likely grateful to Rei.

But the complicated expression on his face stemmed from the fact that this matter was connected to the expansion of Gilm.

Of course, the expansion could be done even in the current state for Gilm.

After all, the frontier is a place where great wealth accumulates.

Still, the lower the cost, the better.

...Though cutting corners to save money defeats the purpose entirely.

After all, this is the frontier.

If corners are cut on the outer wall construction meant to protect the people living in this frontier, they could end up paying for it with their lives.

Daskar and Walker exchanged glances. Then Walker, seated beside Daskar, spoke up.

"Actually, Rei... We have plans to expand the Gilm soon."

"...Is that so?"

Rei had assumed they were here to discuss the matter of Treant Forest, and clearly hadn't expected this topic to come up.

He shot Walker a slightly surprised look.

"Yes. Naturally, expanding the town will require a great deal of materials."

Hearing that, Rei understood what Walker was getting at.

In other words, Walker had set his sights on Treant Forest as a source of materials for the expansion work.

And why go to the trouble of hiring woodcutters... no, why hire adventurers under the guise of bodyguards and have them do such things?

Also, regarding the felled timber Rei had brought into the Misty Ring, Rei could have transported it himself somewhere, but instead, only a select few were moving it. That must be related to the expansion work too.

"But... I hate to say it, but it's just lumber, right? I get it's for building materials, but shouldn't the town's outer walls be more important?"

"Transporting wood for building a single house isn't difficult. But increasing Gilm's size by fifty percent...?"

"Fifty percent!?"

Walker's unexpected words drew a look of genuine surprise from Rei.

If he'd been wearing a hood, it would have surely flown off at that level of shock.

But that reaction was understandable. Gilm was already an exceptionally large city by any standard.

It was so large, one could almost call it a city.

To expand a place as large as Gilm by another fifty percent... Even Rei understood just how massive a construction project that would be.

Of course, he didn't know the exact scale, but he could make a rough estimate.

Seeing Rei's eyes widen in astonishment, Walker continued his explanation.

"I hope you understand the scale of this project. Furthermore, we know that trees felled in Treant Forest, with some processing, can offer a degree of defense against magic. That's precisely why the timber from Treant Forest was so valuable in many ways..."

"You mean I defeated it?"

"No, no. To be honest, as Lord Daskar mentioned, defeating that giant turtle monster... Giganto Turtle, was it? That was a tremendous help. We never knew when such a monster might appear. And then..."

Walker paused when he heard a knock at the door.

Daskar then ordered him to come inside. A maid placed various dishes—likely from the banquet—on the table before leaving the room.

"Well, please eat for now. We'll talk after that."

Following Walker's words, Rei reached for the food on the table.
