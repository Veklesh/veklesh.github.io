"Is this all the lumber we're transporting?"

"Yes, thank you. I expect there'll be more by this afternoon, but that's all we have for now."

It was just past noon when Rei arrived at Treant Forest with Set.

Ever since the decision was made to expand Gilm, the woodcutters had been felling trees desperately, yet to Rei's eyes, the Treant forest's vastness seemed scarcely diminished.

From above, only the vast expanse left by the Giganto Turtle's movement was visible, and perhaps that very sight made it seem even more unchanged.

Normally, Set would be patrolling the skies above Gilm, watching for flying monsters. However, considering the immense difficulty of transporting timber from Treant Forest, that task had taken priority.

The fact that it only takes Set a few minutes to fly from Gilm to Treant Forest likely influenced that decision.

The timber from Treant Forest must be that crucial.

After exchanging brief words with the man handling the negotiations, Rei touched the fallen trees one after another, storing them away.

The woodcutter's hard work from the morning vanished in mere minutes.

The woodcutter who had just arrived at Gilm could only stare at Rei in utter disbelief.

Seeing the stunned woodcutters, the others—the woodcutters who had been in Gilm from the start—nodded as if to say they understood that feeling.

But among them, several woodcutters shot Rei a displeased look.

Leading the pack was Danz, who had fallen for Marina at first sight and challenged Rei to a fight only to be knocked out cold.

But he understood all too well that he was no match for Ray in raw strength.

If he couldn't beat him in a fight, Rei was simply not someone Danz could handle.

Normally, one might gather allies, but Danza's personality meant he never had many friends.

He had a few underlings, but they too had seen firsthand just how formidable Rei was, so there was nothing they could do.

As a result, all Danza could do was glare at Rei, filled with irritation.

Despite that glare, Rei swiftly stored the felled timber in the Misty Ring, mounted Set, and flew away from the spot.

"Tch."

Danza could only silently watch Rei depart, letting out an irritated click of his tongue.

The adventurers of Gilm, who knew what kind of being Rei was, watched Danzar with pitying eyes.

It was a look born of knowing full well what it meant to pick a fight with Rei.

"That's all of it, then."

"Yes, thank you very much. I'll see you again this afternoon."

Nodding at the craftsman's words, Rei turned his gaze once more to what lay behind him... the stacked wood.

Every single piece had been felled in Treant Forest. Only after the alchemist processed them in various ways would they finally become usable as building materials.

After completing the handover, Rei headed to a nearby food stall with Set.

Rei was about to take his lunch break.

He could head to the construction site, but if he did, Marz, who held a strong interest in Rei's Death Scythe and her magic, would surely come looking.

Maltz wasn't a bad guy, but being cornered by the old man wasn't exactly pleasant for Rei either.

(Come to think of it, he seemed quite interested in Feilma too... I hope he focuses on that instead.)

Wizards were a very rare breed to begin with.

Among them, those capable of using spirit magic are even fewer still.

With such an elemental mage present, it was only natural for Marz to focus his attention there.

Marina was also a spirit mage, after all. But Marutsu probably didn't have the courage to approach Marina.

After all, Marina is a well-known celebrity in Gilm.

"Gurururu!"

With Set growling his hunger, Rei bought a variety of food from a nearby stall... a lot of it.

Skewers, sandwiches, steamed buns, soup. And various other dishes besides.

Eating steamed buns in summer felt a bit odd to Rei... but still, good food is good food.

Amidst this, Rei and Set's feet stopped in front of a certain food stall.

"Huh."

It was Rei who exclaimed in admiration upon seeing the stall.

Not only were there crowds of customers surrounding the stall... but also, it was because of what those customers were eating.

The dish was udon.

...However, it wasn't the kind of udon Rei had popularized in this world, like kake udon or nibori udon... nor was it the kind of yakisoba-style udon the residents of Elgin had come up with themselves.

It was likely a dish born precisely because it was midsummer and hot.

What many people were eating was a dish that, in Rei's understanding, was close to bukkake udon: udon noodles in a bowl topped with boiled, water-chilled meat and vegetables as ingredients, and drizzled with cold sauce.

Of course, it differed from the bukkake udon Rei knew in several ways.

For instance, it lacked condiments like grated daikon radish, myoga ginger, shiso leaves, green onions, pickled plums, and bonito flakes. It used a thick sauce instead of dashi soy sauce, and it didn't have a raw egg yolk on top...

Then again, the bukkake udon Rei knew was based solely on what she'd eaten while living in Japan.

Being from a farming family meant they could freely use various condiments, like summer-harvested myoga ginger, whenever they wanted.

Vegetables unsuitable for market were eaten at home, and even then, some went to waste. Naturally, a wide variety of vegetables found their way into dishes.

Anyway, what was being served at the food stall differed in many ways from the bukkake udon Rei knew, but even so, by Rei's understanding, it was a dish that could only be called bukkake udon.

"What do you say? Eat?"

"Growl!"

At Rei's words, Set growled in agreement.

Seeing this, the customers around the stall quietly made room.

Most of the customers were adventurers, and seeing Set, they probably figured the one with him was Rei.

"Welcome."

"One for me and one for Set. Make Set's a five-person portion. Ah, and mine's a three-person portion."

"Got it."

The vendor was a man who usually ran his stall in Gilm.

Even though the order was larger than usual, he accepted it without any sign of confusion.

If someone asked for Set's portion too, most would be surprised... some might even refuse.

But for the people of Gilm, having Set eat their food meant attracting customers with his adorable appearance... and above all, seeing how deliciously Set ate caused orders to flood in.

Compared to the usual street food, it was a bit... no, quite a bit pricier for a bowl of udon, but even so, many in Gilm currently had extra money due to the expansion construction.

Given the circumstances, the shop owner welcomed Set eating his food wholeheartedly and never once considered refusing.

Most of the ingredients were already prepared; all that was left was to boil the udon, chill it, and plate it.

Of course, while three servings for Rei was manageable, five servings for Set was quite a lot, so the amount of noodles to boil was substantial.

After boiling the noodles, he rinsed them in water to remove the slime, plated them, and began adding the various toppings... Finally, he poured over the sauce chilled to just below freezing using a magic item.

This sauce, chilled by the magic item, was the very reason the food stall had become so popular.

...Though, using that magic item was also why the food was more expensive than usual.

"...Delicious."

Eating bukkake udon with a fork was still an unfamiliar situation for Rei, but even so, the bukkake udon was delicious.

Even Rei, wearing a Dragon Robe with built-in air conditioning, found the bukkake udon delicious eaten like this under the blazing sun. For those without such gear, it wouldn't be strange at all if they were willing to pay any price to eat this bukkake udon.

He had eaten bukkake udon before, at the Full Belly Inn.

But this bukkake udon was clearly more refined than what he'd had at Full Belly Inn.

Moreover, since the Full Belly Inn obviously didn't have any magic items, achieving this level of coldness was simply impossible.

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Next to Rei, Set slurped his bukkake udon, his throat making satisfying noises as he exclaimed, "Delicious!"

Seeing Set like that, several people who had been hesitating due to the high price placed orders... and that sparked a chain reaction, with more and more customers following suit.

Eating his bukkake udon while observing this, Rei looked back at his own plate.

(Bukkake udon is nice, but I'd also like some chilled Chinese noodles... But, udon aside, I have no idea how to make Chinese noodles.)

For Rei, cold noodles were more typical for summer than bukkake udon.

While udon was more popular than Chinese noodles in some parts of Japan, at least where Rei lived, cold Chinese noodles were the norm.

Udon, however, could be made with just flour, salt, and water. Fortunately, Rei had made it before, so he could manage that. But he had never even attempted to make Chinese noodles.

And even when she said she managed, Rei had only taught the general method for making udon. It was Dishot from Full Belly Inn who actually perfected the udon through repeated trial and error.

Compared to udon, Rei only had a vague memory of seeing Chinese noodles in some cooking manga or something.

(Egg... She mentioned something like egg noodles, so I guess they use eggs in Chinese noodles? Other than that... kansui? I think she added something called kansui, but what even is kansui? Special water? Special... How is it special?)

The more he thought about it, the more Rei agonized over how to make Chinese noodles.

Also, even if she made chilled Chinese noodles, there was the issue of the sauce.

Rei liked cold noodles with sesame sauce, but he didn't dislike the standard tangy sauce either.

But if asked how to make either, she didn't actually know the recipes.

Sourness? When Rei heard that, citrus fruits or vinegar came to mind.

(Ah, but I think I've seen something like lemon soy sauce or something written on cold noodle sauces... So maybe it's citrus, not vinegar?)

As Rei pondered the cold noodles, that thought suddenly occurred to her. But even if she figured out the source of the sourness, it couldn't possibly be just soy sauce and lemon.

(Meat, omelets, vegetables... those things can be made normally here too. Of course, there would be various differences.)

The meat wasn't boiled chicken breast or ham, but monster meat like oak meat, and the vegetables weren't the cucumbers and tomatoes Rei knew, but vegetables from Elgin.

"Grrr?"

As Rei pondered the cold noodles, he must have finished eating.

Set tilted his head, asking, "What's wrong?" Rei shook his head, taking the very last bite himself, as if to say it was nothing.

"Just a thing. I remembered a dish I want to eat, but I'm not quite sure how to make it. It's the kind of dish you crave on a hot day like this."

At Rei's words, the surrounding customers pricked up their ears.

Many who had been in Gilm for a while knew that Rei possessed deep knowledge about food.

Udon, pizza, meat buns—dishes attributed to Rei—had become huge hits throughout Gilm.

The udon he first created had already spread far beyond the surrounding areas, even reaching deep into the Kingdom of Mireana. The pizza and steamed buns he devised for winter had also spread throughout the region.

In other words, they understood that there was no such thing as a bad dish when Rei came up with it.

...After all, Rei's culinary knowledge was acquired while he was in Japan, so his taste preferences aren't that different from Japanese people's—meaning he doesn't enjoy things like rotten meat or extremely spicy, bitter, or sour foods—so for the inhabitants of this world, there are no "misses" in his cooking.

Of course, this is only because the dishes Rei knows are generally well-received. For instance, if asked whether many people would readily accept something like kusuya, a food with an intensely foul odor, the answer would likely be no.

(Hatahata sushi probably wouldn't be popular either... even though it's delicious.)

Rei recalled the hatahata sushi he pickled every winter—essentially a type of rice sushi.

For Rei, it was truly a dish that tasted like home.

Naturally, she helped make it every year, so she remembered the recipe unusually well.

(Hata-hata sushi... I'd love to make it, but I don't have any rice. Even if I could substitute some other fish for the hata-hata...)

It was a dish he craved, but with almost none of the ingredients available, Rei sadly had no choice but to give up.
