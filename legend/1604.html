Naturally, the theft of a large number of carriages from the royal castle in Rossi was discovered immediately.

The fact that Rei immediately took to the skies on Set without attempting to attack the royal castle... that too was likely the reason the carriage theft was discovered so quickly.

Normally, delaying the enemy to prevent pursuit would have been an effective tactic.

But Rei didn't do that... partly because he felt it was wrong to attack in a way that would involve innocent people, but above all... Rei himself strongly wanted them to pursue.

Whether the security chief or higher-ups would believe reports of someone flying on Set was something of a gamble for Rei. But given that the royal castle had been brazenly attacked, shaming the nation's leadership, Rei anticipated they couldn't simply let Rei and her group get away with such an act. It was highly likely they would order a pursuit.

And if they did order a pursuit, Rei also anticipated it would naturally be mounted cavalry, not infantry, due to their superior mobility.

Above all, something Rei hadn't realized: one of the carriages they'd taken... the one Rei had found oddly luxurious... was actually a carriage reserved for the royal family of the Kingdom of Reburina.

Since royal property had been stolen, leaving it at that was absolutely not an option.

"So, you see... I'd only hoped you might come, but you've hit the nail on the head."

"Oh dear, you're such a bad person."

As Rei muttered this from the rear of the resistance fighters and former prostitutes, Marina uttered words that seemed to scold him.

Though, contrary to her words, a smile played on Marina's face.

"Cavalry, huh... If it's the cavalry of the Kingdom of Reburina, I don't think we can expect much."

Vihera predicted the weakness of the approaching enemy and murmured regretfully.

While Vihera had no personal experience fighting the Reburina army—only the soldiers of Jaya—she had heard from Rei. Honestly, it didn't sound promising.

Rei had mentioned that the soldiers they fought in the forest were likely sent as sacrificial pawns and might not actually be that weak... but since that was purely speculation, Vihera wasn't exactly fired up by his words.

"Hmm."

On this side, Byune was deemed capable enough to handle soldiers of the Reburina Kingdom and was assigned to intercept them.

As night deepened, she rubbed her eyes, looking a bit sleepy.

(Well, if it comes to combat, she'll wake up whether she likes it or not, so it's fine.)

Despite everything, Rei trusted... no, relied on Byune to do her job properly.

"Hmm. So the target isn't the horse, but the soldier riding it... or perhaps a knight? Is that correct?"

Elena, holding her Mirage, asked from beside Rei.

Like the others, she showed no sign of tension despite the impending battle.

But that was only natural. Rei and the others, including Elena, had survived countless battles before.

There was no way they'd be nervous about a battle of this level now.

"Do that. We have a decent number of wagons, but the more horses to pull them, the better."

With Set, it should be possible to pull even the carriages Rei had seized if they were hitched together.

He might not be able to pull all of them, but pulling several would definitely be within his capabilities.

But Rei couldn't stay with this group until the end.

It was almost certain that Daskar would request Rei and his group to return to Gilm alone partway through.

Considering how much Gilm's expansion work was delayed because Rei and the others weren't there, that request was only natural.

And if Rei were to return to Gilm, it would naturally require traveling with Set and riding in the Set Cage.

Therefore, horses to pull the carriage were absolutely necessary.

"You there! Who are you lot?!"

The man at the head of the cavalry group shouted.

To the cavalry, moving in such numbers at night—especially when they couldn't even tell how many there were—looked extremely suspicious.

Even if they weren't specifically searching for intruders who had stormed the royal castle, they would undoubtedly have questioned the group about their circumstances.

"Why are you fleeing from Rossi? Explain yourselves. If you have something to hide... we won't show mercy!"

The sight of him thrusting the tip of his spear at Rei was filled with suspicion and irritation.

It was only natural. The night was just beginning... Many had planned to head out to Mejogo, or at the very least, to Rossi's tavern or the brothel. This sudden summons had disrupted their plans.

It was only natural for them to be irritated, their plans for the night ruined.

His words carried a hint of taking it out on others...

"Right. If you ask who I am... I'd say this. I'm your enemy."

As soon as Rei finished speaking, Elena and the others sprang into action.

The first to attack was Elena, who had transformed her Mirage into a whip-like form.

The blade extended to a length unimaginable for a longsword, slamming into the torso of the man who had been talking with Rei.

The reason the man didn't die wasn't because of his skill or high-quality armor...

Simply because Elena's strike hadn't connected with the blade itself.

(What I want is the horse, not the lives of these men.)

The range of the whip-like Mirage was extremely wide.

In fact, nearly half the cavalrymen present were within Elena's attack range.

And Elena wasn't the only one attacking.

Vihera, looking annoyed, closed the distance with the cavalry and swung his fist at the legs mounted on the horses. Byune used White Clouds to inflict wounds.

The ultimate blow, however, would undoubtedly be Marina's strike, wielding her mastery of spirit magic.

Not wind blades... but wind shockwaves dealing damage.

The cavalrymen had no idea what was happening... but they must have realized staying here was dangerous.

They turned their horses around, attempting to return to the castle.

Had they decided they were helpless and were going to call for reinforcements? Or had they simply become too frightened to stay and fled? Or was it some other reason?

Whatever the reason, the fatal mistake was that their horses refused to obey.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR

Set's cry echoed high into the summer night sky.

Only a handful present knew this was the skill known as the King's Intimidation.

The vast majority of the cavalrymen were stunned and confused when their mounts suddenly stopped moving or refused to obey commands.

The moment the cavalry arrived here and Set slipped behind them, their fate was already sealed.

The moment they failed to realize this, the cavalry's annihilation here was already decided.

...Though, even in their annihilation, not a single one of them died.

Of course, just because no one died doesn't mean there were no injuries.

In terms of injuries, those suffering serious wounds were not uncommon.

Unlike those soldiers, the horses, partly due to the intimidating presence of Set's king, were immobilized, which worked in their favor, meaning more horses remained uninjured.

"Alright. ...I'm taking the horses. Those familiar with handling horses, please help!"

At Rei's words, the resistance members watching from a short distance away... or simply women skilled with horses, each approached their respective mounts.

Near the horses, soldiers who were still conscious realized their mounts were being taken and tried desperately to resist, but their bodies, crippled by injury, could barely move.

Some managed to grab the legs of those trying to take their horses, but such individuals were quickly kicked away.

In the end, they acquired about thirty horses.

It was slightly insufficient to move all the wagons, but considering they could acquire more along the way in villages and towns, it was a sufficient number.

"Alright, let's hurry. If they see these guys don't come back, they'll send reinforcements, and that'll be a pain."

"Rei, wouldn't more horses be better? If we keep luring the enemy like this and taking their horses..."

One of the resistance fighters asked Rei.

Having witnessed Rei's abilities... and those of her companions up close, her words carried an almost reverent tone.

...For Rei, neither the battle with Jaya at Mejogo nor this fight against the cavalry had been fought with full seriousness.

"That might be a good idea. But if we spend too much time here, the risks will grow significantly when I leave."

In truth, the female resistance fighter's suggestion wasn't entirely bad.

Horses... especially horses trained to fight on the battlefield... were extremely valuable commodities.

Being a small country, the value of such horses was even higher for the Kingdom of Reburina than for the Kingdom of Mireana.

Moreover, horses possessed overwhelming mobility compared to people running, making them extremely useful creatures, not just in war.

In the sense of wearing down the Reburina Kingdom by acquiring such horses one after another, it wasn't entirely wrong as a strategy to weaken the enemy.

"But if we weaken Reburina too much here, we risk losing the battle against Jaya. If that happens, things will get messy going forward."

"Ah... I see. My apologies."

"It's fine. That's exactly why, when we're not around, you all need to discuss things and act accordingly. For that very reason, it's best to think carefully about what you would do in such situations."

Hearing Rei's words, the resistance woman looked at him with respect.

"Yes!"

Rei didn't think he had a personality worthy of respect, nor did he try to be that way.

At the very least, he wasn't as fair and just as Elena, nor did he do things like Marina, who helped people despite her complaints... Not that he never did, but it was certainly rare.

That's precisely why being looked at like this by the Resistance woman felt somewhat awkward.

"Let's go. We need to walk for a few more hours and get as far away from Rossi as possible."

Saying only that, Rei left the spot and headed to the front of the group.

"Heh heh."

Beside Rei, Marina smiled amusedly.

"What?"

"Nothing. Just thought it was an unusual attitude for Rei."

Rei considered retorting to Marina, but in the current situation, he couldn't imagine winning an argument with her, so he fell silent.

That choice was the right one.

Marina had been thinking that if Rei had said something back, she would have teased him a little.

Finding the current atmosphere unpleasant, Rei decided to change the subject for now.

...Though, normally, he should have brought that up first.

"So, when do you think the pursuit will come? Or rather, do you think they'll pursue us at all?"

"I wonder. This pursuit was largely because they didn't know who infiltrated the castle, right? If that's the case, they now know we're involved in this incident. So, for the Kingdom of Reburina, wanting to gain favor with the Kingdom of Mireana..."

...it's quite possible they won't pursue us at all.

Rei murmured this to himself, nodding in agreement.

In truth, Marina's words weren't mere fantasy or delusion; they were a very real possibility.

Therefore, for the Kingdom of Reburina, which wants to strike at Jaya alongside the Kingdom of Mireana, it wouldn't be able to act so easily.

Moreover, wasting forces here would only make their fight against Jaya more difficult.

With the military strength of the suzerain kingdom, Mireana, there was no way Reburina would lose.

Even so, to preserve some semblance of dignity, they needed to prepare adequate forces and simultaneously achieve tangible results.

To do that, they'd have to expend forces here... and it would be natural to avoid antagonizing Rei and his group, who were agents of the Kingdom of Mireana.

"Well, if you act purely on emotion without considering those circumstances, there's a chance they might pursue us."

Rei nodded in agreement at Marina's words.
