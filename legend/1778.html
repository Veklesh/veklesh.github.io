News of the commotion in the garden naturally reached Duke Kerebel immediately.

Just as he heard the report, Duke Kerebel was in the middle of drafting documents to arrange for food distribution to a village where starvation was expected due to food shortages... but he nearly ruined the documents he was working on.

Yet he demonstrated remarkable mental fortitude and managed to finish the documents properly—perhaps because he had held the title of duke for so many years.

“So? What happened with that commotion in the end?”

“Yes. Thanks to Lady Elena, it didn’t escalate into a major incident.”

“...I see. Then, make sure to properly entertain Rei and Set as guests.”

“Is that all right?”

At Kerebel’s words, the man who had brought the report showed a slight look of surprise.

Even if someone has a nickname, that alone doesn’t make them a guest of Duke Kerebel.

After all, even if Elena claimed Rei was a guest, whether Duke Kerebel, as the head of the household, would accept that was a different matter entirely.

That was precisely why the man who had brought the report had expected that Rei would be driven out of the mansion immediately.

Perhaps noticing his subordinate’s reaction, Duke Kerebel turned his gaze toward him without a word.

The subordinate who had brought the report noticed the gaze and bowed his head in a fluster.

“Yes! I’ll make the arrangements immediately.”

Glancing at the subordinate hurrying away, Duke Kerebel returned to his work.

However… a few minutes after the man left, he looked up from the documents he was reading and gazed out the window.

From his office, he couldn’t see the garden where Elena and the others were causing a commotion, but even so, as he gazed at the garden visible through the window, he felt as though he could see Elena there.

And at the same time, the figure of Rei, the Crimson-haired man who had come with her.

He had always wanted to meet Rei in person someday, but he couldn’t help but feel that this was hardly the right time.

“I can just picture those fools making a racket. …Really. They didn’t have to bring Rei here at a time like this.”

As the leader of the Noble Faction, Duke Kerebel naturally receives visits from numerous nobles to offer New Year’s greetings, and parties are held.

And since Elena had brought Rei along, she must naturally intend to have him accompany her to that party.

It goes without saying that attending a party with a member of the opposite sex who isn’t a relative implies that the two share a special relationship.

Or even if they aren’t explicitly in such a relationship, it still suggests they are closer than ordinary men and women.

And if Elena—who bears the nickname “Princess General” and is a symbolic figure even within the Noble Faction—were to do such a thing, it would naturally not sit well with the other nobles.

The fact that his daughter is being viewed in such a light is certainly not to Duke Kerebel’s liking either.

It’s not pleasant… but at the same time, considering the nature of the nobility, he also felt it was inevitable.

In any case, for the other nobles—especially those who had been eyeing a marriage with Elena—there is no doubt that Rei is an absolutely intolerable presence.

In the case of such nobles, many were deeply entrenched in a sense of entitlement… and in such a situation, it was practically impossible for a commotion not to arise.

And if a commotion did arise, Rei would show no mercy.

Well, he might hold back just enough to avoid killing them, but only to the extent that he wouldn’t be killed himself.

In fact, as far as Duke Kerebel knew, during the war with the Bestir Empire, Rei had severed the arms of nobles—not even nobles from the enemy Bestir Empire, but nobles from the Kingdom of Mireana.

Still, since it was the battlefield back then, it could have been interpreted any way.

But if something like that were to happen at a place like a party venue, it would undoubtedly cause a huge scandal.

(The worst-case scenario would be if Rei were to give up on the Kingdom of Mireana and leave because of this.)

Duke Kerebel, who is gathering information on Rei, is naturally aware of Rei’s power as well.

He has a Rank A—no, actually, a Rank S-equivalent griffin named Set as his familiar. He uses a variety of magic himself, but he specializes particularly in wide-area annihilation spells.

Furthermore, he possesses various magical items, starting with an item box.

If such a person were to leave the Kingdom of Mireana, the loss would undoubtedly be immense.

It was precisely because he knew this that Duke Kerebel was racking his brain.

“Anyway… I suppose I need to take care of this first.”

Knowing full well that this was merely putting it on the back burner, he returned to his paperwork.

In fact, there were certainly a fair number of urgent matters among the paperwork, and there was no doubt he needed to take care of those.

It was also an undeniable fact that, under the current circumstances, paperwork was the only thing that brought Duke Kerebel any peace of mind.

While his father sought solace in paperwork, Elena had returned to her room with Ara.

She felt a sense of nostalgia seeing her room again after so long, yet there was still something off about it.

(I guess that just shows how comfortable Marina’s house was.)

When she was at Marina’s house, she used to change her own clothes.

But now that she was back at this mansion, that was the maid’s job.

To be honest, as someone who often finds herself on the battlefield, Elena would like to say she can at least change her own clothes… but given her status as the daughter of a duke rather than a “Princess General,” having a maid help her change was only natural.

“My lady, how about this dress today?”

One of the maids brought her a blue dress.

Elena looked at the dress and thought for a moment.

The very word “dress” reminded her of Marina, who wore party dresses like that on a regular basis.

The dress the maid had brought was not the kind one would wear to a party.

It was the kind of dress that wouldn’t look out of place as everyday wear, but to Elena, who had grown accustomed to Marina’s style, it seemed rather plain.

(I wonder if Rei would be happy if I wore a dress like Marina’s?)

She wasn’t entirely opposed to the idea, but the mere thought of standing before Rei in a dress that revealed so much skin made her squirm with embarrassment.

If Rei weren’t around, she wouldn’t feel particularly embarrassed wearing a dress with a low neckline and an open back—the kind Marina loved to wear.

Still, part of me wanted Rei to see me like that... but Elena didn’t complain about the dress the maid brought and changed into it.

“Well, it suits you perfectly. Any gentleman would be captivated just by seeing you like this.”

“Is that so…?”

Since it came from the maid’s mouth, perhaps it was nothing but flattery.

Though she thought this, Elena asked anyway, but the maid was genuinely, from the bottom of her heart, praising Elena’s appearance in the dress.

Elena must have realized this too, as she finally felt a little relieved.

“How is Rei doing?”

“As for Rei, Ara is keeping him company. Um… are you really all right?”

The maid’s anxious remark wasn’t about Rei… but about Set, Rei’s familiar.

From the perspective of someone seeing Set for the first time, the maid’s concern was only natural.

But Elena smiled at the anxious maid and spoke.

“No problem. Set would never attack without good reason. …Though, if someone were to do something foolish, Set would likely take appropriate action.”

The maid seemed relieved by the first part of Elena’s words, but her face took on a look of terror again at the second part.

“Don’t worry. I’ve said this many times, but Set is basically friendly. In Gilm, he was treated like a pet, you know?”

“……Um, that’s a griffin, right? A Rank A monster, isn’t it?”

“No.”

“Huh?”

Perhaps she hadn’t expected to be told “no” at that moment; though her eyes were drawn to the movement of Elena’s blonde hair, the maid cast a puzzled glance at Elena.

Watching the maid’s reaction, Elena wondered if she shouldn’t say anything. Still, she decided the news would likely spread quickly anyway, so she spoke up.

“Set isn’t just any griffin—he’s a rare species. To be precise, he’s a monster equivalent to Rank S.”

“Yikes!”

Even a Rank A monster is a symbol of terror, so to hear it described as Rank S-equivalent was beyond belief...

Elena’s words must have been completely unexpected for the maid, as she gasped softly.

Seeing the maid’s reaction, Elena continued her explanation in a soothing tone.

“I understand why you’re scared, but Set is basically harmless. …Come to think of it, you haven’t actually seen Set yourself, have you? Aren’t you just judging him based on hearsay?”

“Well…”

The maid fell silent at Elena’s words.

In fact, since a griffin had arrived, she hadn’t gone to see it herself.

It was simply the fact that a griffin—a vicious Rank A monster… though in reality it was equivalent to Rank S—had arrived that had her frightened.

“Why don’t you go take a look at Rei playing with Ray? If you’re still scared after that, you just don’t have to go near him.”

Even as she said this, Elena was certain that once people got to know Set’s personality, he would become popular here as well.

In fact, although Set has always been feared in unfamiliar places, once people spend a certain amount of time with him, most of them end up growing fond of him.

Some even became Set enthusiasts rivaling Milein herself.

That’s why Elena was certain that even though Set might be feared at first while staying at this mansion for a while starting today, he would undoubtedly be accepted in the end.

“Kyuu?”

Perhaps hearing Elena’s conversation with the maid, Yellow, who had been eating dried meat in the room, looked up with a yelp.

Yellow, the black dragon’s child, was a popular figure even at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

That was precisely why Elena had judged that the groundwork was laid for Set to be accepted as well.

“Set gets along well with Yellow. So, if you become friends with Set, Yellow will be happy too.”

That single remark had a profound effect.

The maid, who had been afraid of Set just moments ago, now wore an expression of determination stronger than fear.

“I understand. Well, since I’m scared to go alone, I’m going to invite a few others and go to the griffin… or was it Set? I think I’ll go try to meet him.”

“I see. Oh, right. If you’re going to see Set, I think bringing him some food will help him warm up to you faster. Set loves to eat, you know.”

“Well… in that case, he’s not much different from Yellow, is he?”

“That’s true. Actually, the only difference is his size; you could probably say he’s just as friendly as Yellow.”

At the words “no different from Yellow,” the maid smiled.

She must have thought that meant she didn’t need to be afraid of him either.

That said, Set is currently over three meters tall, which is quite massive.

There probably aren’t many people who could approach such a massive body without hesitation.

(As long as we’re family, as long as I and Father make sure not to provoke Set in any strange ways, there shouldn’t be a problem. But… the real issue is, of course, the other nobles.)

Elena, too, comes to the same conclusion as her father, Duke Kerebel.

No, in Elena’s case, having been courted by nobles and asked to marry them on numerous occasions, her anxiety is even greater.

“It’s certain this will cause trouble for Rei, isn’t it? …Should I prepare some delicious food or perhaps a magic item of some sort?”

Since it’s bound to cause a commotion, it’s certain she’ll be putting Rei through a hassle.

Given that, it was only natural for Elena to want to prepare a gift or a meal that Rei would like—both as an apology for the trouble she was causing him and as a way to thank him for accompanying her to the party.

Delicious food and a magic item.

There was no doubt that Rei would be delighted to receive either of these.

That said, not just any magic item would do.

Rei collects the kind of magic items that are actually usable.

That much should be obvious when you consider the various magic items Rei already possesses, such as the Dagger of Flowing Water and the Twilight Spear.

(So, it’s got to be the food, then.)

Since this was the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, there was no shortage of ingredients for a party, and the chefs on staff were top-notch.

For that very reason, Elena was firmly convinced that they could serve a meal that would please Rei.
