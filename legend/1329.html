Rei and the others were making their way through the slums when they spotted a building in the distance matching the description they'd heard earlier and stopped moving.

"So, what do we do now? Wait for them to come out?"

Marina asked Rei while stroking Set, who was looking around with keen interest.

The usual Rei wouldn't hesitate to charge into the building right in front of them, Death Scythe or Twilight Spear in hand.

It was a two-story building, the kind you'd expect to find in the slums, seemingly abandoned for quite some time.

Yet, to Marina's eye, she could see it had been carefully reinforced in places.

At first glance, it seemed like a typical slum building, but the fact that it was actually well-maintained made it clear this was no ordinary slum structure.

What was even more decisive was the presence of figures beside the building who were clearly bodyguards.

They weren't openly drawing weapons, but they were still tense, watching their surroundings.

In fact, one might even worry they were being overly cautious.

The truth was that word had reached them that Rei and the others had entered the slums, and hearing that had made Slain's men jumpy.

"Well, what to do... If Precious were outside the building, I wouldn't have had to think this far."

"Well, Precious came to the slums for a reason, so of course she's inside the building, right? She must be meeting someone."

At Vihera's slightly exasperated mutter, Marina flashed a mischievous smile and spoke up.

"Maybe she came looking for something? As you can see, the slums are incredibly chaotic. There's hardly a better place to hide something than here."

"That could be. ...Ah."

Rei nodded at Marina's words, then suddenly felt eyes on him and looked over.

They had drawn many stares since entering the slums, but this gaze clearly recognized Rei as Rei... and simultaneously conveyed intense wariness, fear, and tension.

The source of the gaze was in the direction they had been looking... the guards stationed on the second floor of a building.

(Well, obviously. We do stand out in a lot of ways.)

They were still quite a distance from the building, and nothing obstructed their view.

With a nearly three-meter-tall Set present, it would have been strange if the people around him weren't noticeable.

And then there was Rei and Byune—even from a distance, they stood out for all the wrong reasons.

Marina, dressed in a party gown fit for an aristocratic gathering, carrying a bow and wearing a quiver on her back.

Vihera, wearing a thin, revealing dress like a courtesan or dancer would, yet equipped with gauntlets and greaves.

By any normal standard, it was impossible for them not to stand out.

"What are we going to do?"

Rei hesitated when Vihera turned her gaze to him and asked.

Originally, the plan was to intentionally show his face to Precious, to pressure her by making her feel he suspected her... but if they were spotted over there before he could do that, it would be hopeless.

(Then... I just need to change my approach.)

Within seconds, Rei revised his plan and spoke up.

"Since they've already spotted us, how about we just show ourselves openly and call out to them?"

"...Well, if that's what you want, Rei, I suppose it's fine. It might put some pressure on them too."

When Vihera said that, Marina and Byune nodded back, seemingly having no objections either.

Set, seemingly without much thought, cleared his throat.

The only hint of displeasure was likely due to their location in the slums.

As expected of a slum, it was unsanitary, meaning various foul odors lingered here compared to the main streets... or even the back alleys.

For Set, whose senses were already highly sensitive, these odors weren't unbearable, but they were still unpleasant.

"Since no one seems to object, let's proceed openly then."

Saying this, Rei proceeded down the road with Set, heading toward their target building.

Naturally, the guards protecting the building had to do something... but as Slain's subordinates, they were reasonably well-informed and knew exactly who was approaching them.

"Hey, wait! What are we supposed to do?! That's Rei, right!?"

"Yeah. And the entire Rank B party, Crimson Wings, is here. What are we supposed to do?"

"Anyway, we need to inform Slain..."

Before the guard could finish his sentence, Rei closed the distance to the building... and to the guards in one swift move.

Seeing Rei's movement, and above all, not knowing how to react to Rei suddenly appearing right before them... fearing that any wrong move might provoke an attack from Rei, the guards froze in place.

Before them, Rei stopped, took a deep breath, and spoke.

"Pure-sha-su-kun, let's play!"

That voice echoed throughout the area.

To the casual ear, it sounded like a child inviting a friend to play.

Indeed, it wasn't uncommon to hear children shouting such things on the main streets.

But... this was the slums, deep within the slums.

Naturally, few would utter words like Rei's just now here... and above all, given that the target of the call was a building owned by Slein, only someone seeking suicide would attempt such a thing.

But that was exactly what Rei could do.

He possessed overwhelming power that intimidated most, and precisely because of that, no one could easily lay a hand on him even if he acted like this.

After calling out to the building, Rei waited silently until there was a response from the other side.

The guards probably understood that speaking here would only put them at a disadvantage. They remained still.

Silence spread around them... and then, the sound of Marina and the others walking approached.

"So, what's the deal? Did you get any response from over there?"

"No, unfortunately, nothing yet. ...Could it be they can't hear us? Hey, just asking, but this building doesn't completely block out external sounds, does it?"

The guard who was asked nodded grimly.

Even as he wondered why he had to be the one facing Rei and exchanging words.

"Yes. There are no soundproofing measures. Voices from outside... if they're loud like Ms. Rei's earlier, they should definitely be audible."

The man answered carefully, choosing his words to avoid angering Rei even slightly.

Standing over two meters tall with a solidly built physique, he was truly a giant worthy of the name. His face bore numerous slash wounds, and in terms of sheer physical presence, he far outmatched Rei.

Yet now, this intimidating figure was speaking carefully, trying not to upset Rei in the slightest.

To anyone unaware of the situation, it would be laughable.

But... none of those present could afford to laugh.

They understood all too well what their leader, Slein, had done at Precious's request... and the true extent of Rei's capabilities.

Since they had no idea how much information Rei had on them, what would happen if they gave a careless reply here?

It was an outcome even a man who'd survived the underworld didn't want to imagine.

He didn't think he was seen that way... or rather, perhaps it was more accurate to say he didn't care. In any case, Rei nodded in agreement at the man's response and turned his gaze toward the building.

Of course, without knowing where Precious was, even entering the building wouldn't guarantee finding the target.

(Besides, this kind of situation usually means there's a hidden passageway somewhere.)

If they forced their way inside and the target sensed danger and escaped through a hidden passage, it would completely negate the purpose of coming all the way to the slums.

...Of course, while waiting outside like this, there was always the possibility Precious and the others could escape through a hidden passage.

"Hey, what kind of guy is your boss?"

"Yes, he's a respectable person."

"...Specifically?"

"He always looks out for us. If anything happens, he'll buy us drinks."

"I see."

Rei understood he was generous, but suddenly noticed Vihera giving him a look of exasperation.

"What's wrong?"

"Look... why are you even friends with those guys in the first place?"

"Well, I'm not exactly friends with them, am I?"

"I think you are."

Vihera said this, but Rei genuinely didn't think he was getting close to the man.

He'd just been talking about the building.

"...Hm."

Amidst this, Byune suddenly murmured.

For some reason, the words registered clearly, and everyone present turned their gaze toward Byune.

They noticed Byune was staring intently at one particular spot and followed her gaze.

What Byune was looking at was a single room on the second floor of the building.

When Rei looked there, she understood why Byune had been watching that room.

Because there, she saw Precious.

Presumably thinking they couldn't be spotted from where Rei stood, Precious, noticing Rei's gaze directed at the room, showed a look of surprise.

But soon, she must have decided that being spotted by Rei from this distance meant hiding was impossible. Rei could see Precious speaking to the people inside.

Then, a few minutes later... Precious emerged from the building, accompanied by her guards.

However, only Precious came out; Slain was nowhere to be seen.

Precious had hoped Slain would appear too... but Slain, having been ordered to harm an alchemist acquainted with Rei, could absolutely not show himself before Rei.

He knew all too well what would happen to him if he showed his face.

...Of course, he didn't think Rei knew who he was, so that was merely a possibility.

But even so, considering the remote possibility, he didn't want to make the mistake of appearing on his own initiative when he hadn't been summoned.

As a result, Precious and her escort... along with one stern-faced man appeared.

(Is that guy Precious's negotiator?)

Given that he came out with Precious, it was only natural for Rei to think that.

But in reality, that man was Slain, who had volunteered to appear as his stand-in... no, more accurately, as his shadow.

"So, Rei. I never expected to run into you in a place like this. What brings you here?"

"Well, I just happened to come to the slums, heard by chance that Precious and the others were here, and while walking down the street, I happened to pass a building nearby where Precious was, so I thought I'd drop by."

Three coincidences piling up like that just doesn't happen.

It doesn't... but Rei intended to pass it off as coincidence.

Of course, Precious understood that too. But if she said something clumsy here, it would likely come back to haunt her.

After all, she was here to get him to take action regarding Tris.

If by some mistake that became known... and from there the Azimov incident came to light, there was no need to think about what would happen to her.

"I see. I'm glad to see you too, Rei. ...Still, coming to see me after meeting just once before... Should I say I'm pleased in this situation?"

"Yeah, I'd appreciate it if you did say that. ...Ah, right. Actually, I heard a rumor that the culprit who went after someone I know might be in the slums... Do you know anything? Hm? Come to think of it..."

Rei cut himself off there, shifting his gaze to the man beside Precious... Slain's body double.

His attitude was far too deliberate, clearly indicating that Rei suspected that man... and Precious, who was with him.

But Precious, understanding Rei's intentions, smiled and shook her head.

"That's unfortunate. ...He's an old friend of mine. He came to visit me after all this time."

"I see."

At Precious's words, Rei responded with a broad smile beneath his hood.
