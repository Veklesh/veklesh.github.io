“Rei, good work today. I went to Treant Forest for the first time in a while—how was it?”

At the Guild of Gilm, Kenny, the cat-shape-shifter receptionist, called out to Rei as he approached the counter.

Normally, Rei’s usual attendant is Lenora, but at this time in the afternoon, Lenora was on her lunch break.

That’s why Kenny had happily called out to Rei.

...Normally, it wouldn’t be surprising if Lenora scolded her for not focusing on her work, but Kenny probably saw Lenora’s absence as a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

That said, despite her flashy appearance and carefree demeanor, Kenny is a woman who can actually do her job properly.

Otherwise, it would be difficult for her to make it as a receptionist at the Guild in Gilm.

Even so, the reason she gets scolded by Lenora is probably because, despite being competent, she often prioritizes her personal life.

“I’ve moved all the wood from over there to Gilm, so I don’t think we’ll have any trouble running out of supplies.”

“Right. That’s a relief for now. …It seems Rei really is the fastest at hauling wood, after all.”

“That’s because I have this. Compared to transporting them by carriage, this is definitely faster.”

Saying that, he shows Kenny the Misty Ring on his right hand.

Kenny understood what it was, so he nodded without showing any particular surprise.

“I see. I’m really envious. …By the way, I haven’t seen you around lately, Rei. Were you away somewhere?”

“Hm? Oh, right. I had to leave Gilm for a special request from Lord Daskar. I can’t go into details, though.”

“……Hmm.”

Kenny wanted to know everything about Rei, but when the name “Daskar”—the lord of Gilm—came up, she couldn’t press him any further.

Well, if this meant Rei was in any danger, that would be a different story, but as long as he was talking to Rei like this, he could conclude there was no such danger.

Therefore, he decided it would be best not to press the issue here and risk putting Rei in an awkward position.

Rei understood Kenny’s consideration and changed the subject.

“So, how are the others doing? Well, I don’t think there are any particular problems.”

Kenny flashed a momentary look of displeasure at that question—likely because he didn’t want her to mention other women in front of him.

Rei knew that those women were important comrades to him, and one of them was a former guild master whom she respected, despite everything.

But even so, as a woman who cared for Rei, she couldn’t help but think that way.

Still, the fact that she only showed that attitude for a split second was likely because Kenny was fully aware that she was just a receptionist.

“Yes, the others are working hard in their own ways, too. Especially Lady Marina—she’s doing an amazing job.”

“……I suppose so.”

The spirit magic Marina uses boasts overwhelming power and scale compared to the spirit magic used by others.

There are elves and a few humans in Gilm who use spirit magic.

However, even though they’re both spirit magic, it wouldn’t be an exaggeration to say that the spirit magic used by those people and the spirit magic used by Marina are almost entirely different.

That’s how skilled Marina is at wielding spirit magic.

Of course, that doesn’t mean Marina can use every type of spirit magic with complete freedom.

Due to the nature of the Dark Elf race, she has a particularly poor affinity with fire spirits.

This was only natural, given that she was a Dark Elf living in the forest.

Moreover, Marina is descended from the priestesses of the World Tree.

It was only natural that she would have a poor affinity with fire spirits.

That said, it remains to be seen whether she’ll have a chance to use fire spirit magic at Gilm, where construction is underway.

“Ms. Vihera and young Byune are patrolling the town just like before. I hear they’ve already captured a few people.”

“……Caught them?”

“Yes. Apparently, there were people drinking and causing a ruckus in broad daylight, others fighting at construction sites over disagreements, and men aggressively hitting on women… that sort of thing.”

“I see. With those two, I guess I don’t have to worry about that. Though I am a little worried that Vihera might make a big deal out of things.”

“Hahaha. You’re right. With Vihera, if she finds a strong opponent, she might challenge them to a fight.”

Even before Rei and the others went to the Kingdom of Reburina, they had been doing similar work within Gilm.

Back then, she caused a few incidents similar to the ones Kenny mentioned.

Of course, the fact that it never turned into a major problem was probably because Vihera handled it appropriately.

“Hey, more importantly, what are you going to do now, Rei? Are you going back to Treant Forest?”

“Hmm… I’m not sure. I brought all the trees that were left over there, so I don’t really need to go back right away. Though, that only applies to the trees.”

“...Just the wood? Was there something else?”

“Yeah. Actually, Set went and took down the orc that was in Treant Forest.”

At the mention of “orcs,” Kenny frowned slightly.

For Kenny, too, the very existence of orcs was something she couldn’t condone as a woman.

Then again, maybe that’s exactly why he has no qualms about eating orc meat.

“Right. I’d heard stories about monsters living in Treant Forest, but orcs…”

His serious expression likely stems from his worry that the orcs might establish a settlement in Treant Forest.

And once a settlement is established, defeating it would require a significant amount of manpower.

At a time when we’re already short-handed, I’d honestly rather avoid such a hassle—that’s probably the honest truth from my perspective as a receptionist… or, more accurately, as a guild employee.

“Could you send a few skilled adventurers to Treant Forest to hunt down the monsters? Set has been taking care of some of them while I’m there, but I’ll have to head out again soon.”

“Huh? Again?”

At the mention of “leaving,” Kenny shot Rei a surprised look.

She had understood from their earlier conversation that he had left Gilm on some errand, but she had assumed that errand was already finished.

And yet, to be told he was leaving again… it would be impossible not to be surprised.

Rei understood why Kenny was surprised, so he spoke to calm him down.

“It won’t be that long this time. I probably won’t be back the same day… but I think I’ll be back in about five days.”

Even as he said this, Rei thought to himself that if he couldn’t find them, it might take a little longer…

During the day, it would be hard to miss a group of nearly a thousand people on the move.

But if they’re inside a village or town, it’ll be a bit harder to spot them.

...That said, since there are nearly a thousand people moving, while it might be manageable in a city or a large town, in a village or a small town, they wouldn’t be able to bring all the carriages inside, so it should be possible to figure out where they are based on that.

“Um… so, are you free tonight? I’ll tell you about Gilm when you weren’t around, so how about we grab a bite to eat together? Look, there are more people around now, aren’t there? Thanks to that, more restaurants serving dishes from other regions have opened up.”

Hearing about cuisine from other regions, Rei naturally became interested.

For Rei, the idea of trying food he’d never eaten before held a tremendous appeal.

If he didn’t have any plans, Rei would have accepted Kenny’s invitation. But…

“Sorry, I’m supposed to have dinner at Marina’s house tonight.”

“Oh…”

“You know, I’ve just come back to Gilm after a long time, right? So I need to catch up on today’s events and other things. Like the party.”

When put that way, Kenny, as the receptionist, couldn’t argue.

After all, it was true that Rei and the others had been away from Gilm for quite some time.

If he said they needed a chance to catch up on what had happened during that time, she could understand. She could understand… but that didn’t mean Kenny was going to give up on having dinner with Rei.

“Oh, well then, when Lenora gets back, I’ll be on my lunch break, so how about we go to a restaurant together? Of course, since it’s this time of day, I don’t think we can go to a proper restaurant… What do you say?”

“Yeah, that’s fine. Though I have to go to Treant Forest later, so I can’t take too much time.”

“Ugh… I guess I can’t help it. But hey, since I get to eat with Rei, I won’t complain. …So, what kind of food do you want to eat? My recommendation is a bean dish made with tangy cheese.”

“Cheese with a strong tang? Are you sure that’s okay?”

Could it be that the cheese has gone bad?

Rei was worried about that, but Kenny smiled and spoke up.

“Of course it’s not spoiled. I heard they make it that way using a special process. Actually, it’s pretty tasty when you try it.”

Judging by Kenny’s demeanor, she didn’t seem to be trying to trick Rei.

(Well, even when I was in Japan, there were quite a few people who couldn’t stand natto, and I remember seeing on TV that some incredibly smelly cheeses were considered luxury items. When I think about it that way, maybe it’s not that strange after all?)

That said, Rei doesn’t actually dislike the taste of natto itself, but he just can’t stand how sticky it makes his mouth feel.

To avoid that, he can eat natto rolls, but he doesn’t really like eating natto on its own.

(Ah, natto. …I seem to recall reading somewhere that you can make natto by boiling soybeans and wrapping them in straw. Is this the right dish to teach the Zolgue Trading Company?)

Rei came up with the idea on his own, and he found himself thinking, “Actually, this might be a pretty good idea,” patting himself on the back.

A short distance from where Rei lived—a place that, on a national scale, could certainly be considered part of the same region—natto production was fairly widespread.

Thanks to that, Rei had more or less learned how to make natto… but if someone tried to make natto based solely on Rei’s explanation, it would be quite difficult without the natto bacteria.

Moreover, natto looks like nothing more than stringy beans.

To someone who knew nothing about it, they would look like nothing more than rotten beans.

Telling someone to eat it and taste it would be tantamount to telling them to get food poisoning.

Even if Rei were to teach the Zolgue Trading Company how to make natto through Gimekara, whether the Zolgue Trading Company would actually make it is, to be honest, a bit of a long shot.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“Nah, nothing. I was just thinking about that cheese you mentioned and wondered if I could make a new dish with it, but it didn’t work out.”

"Oh... Udon and the other dishes Rei comes up with are always so delicious. That’s too bad."

"Well, I didn't actually come up with the udon myself..."

"Wait, Rei?"

"I just taught him a recipe from a book my master had."

Just as Rei was about to say that, someone called out to him.

When he turned his gaze that way, there stood Lenora, the receptionist assigned to Rei.

She must have been surprised to see Rei there when she returned from her lunch break.

“Oh, Lenora’s back. So, I’m next for a break, right?”

“Huh? Yeah, I guess so… Hey, Kenny. Make sure you hand things over properly.”

“It’s fine. Nothing much happened. Rei’s matter is all taken care of, too. …Alright, Rei. I’m going to go change, so wait here for a moment.”

With a smile and a happy wink, Kenny left to change out of her guild uniform and into her street clothes.

Technically, she could go out to eat while still wearing her guild uniform, but since Kenny has feelings for Rei, she probably wants to go out in her regular clothes instead.

Some of the adventurers in the guild who harbored feelings for Kenny or looked up to her were casting jealous glances at Rei… but that was a common sight at the Guild in Gilm.

Among them were adventurers who had arrived in Gilm while Rei was away in the Kingdom of Reburina, and some of the more rough-and-tumble types among them considered trying to pick a fight with Rei. Fortunately, the other adventurers around them stopped them before they could actually act on it.

At first, they seemed dissatisfied, wondering why they were being stopped, but once they learned Rei’s true identity—that he was the adventurer known by the alias “Crimson”—they were grateful for being stopped, and none of them even considered picking a fight with Rei.

“Rei, did Kenny say anything rude?”

“No, not really. He just talked to me a little.”

“I see. Well, that’s good then…”

As Rei and Lenora were talking, Kenny, who had finished changing, came over, and the two of them headed out to eat.
