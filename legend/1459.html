"Hmm... Revenge, huh?"

While eating dinner in Marina's garden, Rei recounted the incident with Ilze from earlier that day, prompting Vihera to mutter that short phrase.

Normally, Rei and the others would have eaten in the dining hall of the Dusk Wheat inn at dusk most evenings. The reason they had started dining in Marina's garden instead was due to the presence of Elena and Ara.

The nobles of the Noble Faction in Gilm naturally had information about Elena being in Gilm.

After all, Elena came to Gilm specifically to prevent the Noble Faction from interfering with the expansion project.

Given that, it was only natural to make sure the Noble Faction in Gilm knew Elena had arrived.

That the Neutral Faction learned of Elena's situation here—in Gilm, the territory of Daskar, the central figure of the Neutral Faction—was only natural.

Among the three major factions, the Royal Faction was naturally the last to learn of Elena's arrival.

...Though, being the largest faction among the three, their influence was naturally strong in many ways.

Though they learned of Elena's arrival in Gilm last, it was only a matter of hours.

Moreover, beyond the nobles, many merchants gathered before the lord's mansion had naturally realized Elena's arrival, and information was flowing in from various other places as well.

Currently, a large number of adventurers had gathered in Gilm from all over, and naturally, some among them knew Elena.

In such circumstances, taking Elena out to the Dusk Wheat inn was out of the question.

If she were taken out, it would be impossible to have a calm meal.

The reason Elena could currently eat while chatting leisurely with Rei and the others was because this was Marina's mansion, protected by spirit magic.

(Even though we share the same title, the treatment we receive is quite different, isn't it?)

Marina watched Elena and Rei, finding the situation rather amusing.

The Princess General and Crimson.

Both are nicknames known throughout the surrounding nations, yet others treat them very differently.

Respect for the Princess General, dread for Crimson.

Of course, some felt dread toward the Princess General and respect toward Crimson.

But overall, the difference was decisive.

Thinking this, Marina opened her mouth.

"Revenge comes in many forms, you know. If possible, I'd prefer it not to cause too much of a commotion."

"Judging by your tone, Marina, you don't seem to have any particular feelings about revenge either?"

"Well, yes. When you've lived a long time, it's not unusual to see people seeking revenge. ...If the target of that revenge were someone close to me, that would be a different story. But if not, I think they should do as they please."

Marina replied casually to Rei's words.

"Lady Marina, isn't that a bit... as a former Guildmaster, rather irresponsible?"

Ara placed the spoon she'd been holding back into the soup bowl as she reproached her.

But Marina, taken to task, merely tilted her head slightly, as if to say, "Oh?"

As she did so, her brown skin, illuminated by the moonlight, created a scene that could be described as alluring.

Though not homosexual herself, Ara found Marina's appearance, as if embodying the very essence of feminine allure, causing her cheeks to flush involuntarily.

Realizing her reaction, Ara hastily shook her head.

Marina had been watching Ara's reaction with amusement, but as Ara's gaze grew more intense, she finally spoke.

"Well, I am, after all, the former Guildmaster. I'm not the current one, so there's no need to be so concerned. Especially since we're not exactly in a public place right now."

"If you speak that way casually in a place like this, I think you might just blurt it out in a public setting too,"

Ara's slightly reproachful remark prompted Marina to smile seductively and reply.

"It's fine. How long do you think I've been Guildmaster? I understand the situation perfectly well."

Given that, it was hard to argue with Ara, who had only been alive for about twenty years.

"Marina, please don't be too hard on Ara."

"Oh, really? Talking to her like this, she's just so adorable, I can't help myself... you know?"

Marina replied to Elena's words. Ara opened her mouth as if wanting to say something to Marina... but in the end, she couldn't bring herself to say more and reached for the food on the table.

"Still, revenge, huh? You've brought up quite a troublesome topic again."

Elena averted her gaze from Ara and naturally turned it to Rei, who had brought up this matter.

But Rei wasn't the one seeking revenge; he'd merely been asked what he thought about it.

"Well, even if you say that... By the way, Elena, judging by your current reaction, are you against revenge?"

"I can't really say anything about that."

"Huh?"

Expecting a clear "no" in response, Rei let out a flustered sound.

And it wasn't just Rei.

Marina, Vihera, and Byune all reacted the same way.

Only Ara seemed unfazed by Elena's words.

"What's wrong? Why so surprised?"

"No, I just assumed Elena would say revenge is a meaningless act."

"...I've been on countless battlefields and stained these hands with blood. Do you really think someone like me could say revenge is foolish? That would just be idle chatter."

Having fought on many battlefields, Elena had naturally taken many lives.

Counting not only those she took directly, but also those taken by her subordinates under her command, the number of lives would be far beyond mere tens or hundreds.

Given that, Elena believed it was only natural that those connected to the people she had killed, whether directly or indirectly, would come seeking revenge.

Everyone present nodded in understanding at Elena's explanation.

Most present here had experience taking many lives in battle.

The only exception was Byune. Due to her age, she had never participated in war, and even when she tackled dungeons alone, she tended to avoid combat whenever possible. Consequently, she hadn't taken many lives.

...Even for someone like Byune, however, it was merely "not that many"—not none at all. That was the burden borne by adventurers, knights, and soldiers.

"Well, I don't know exactly who this woman named Ilze wants revenge against, but..."

"The fact she came to ask me specifically... probably means... that's the case."

Rei replied to Elena's words.

She'd come all the way to the Gilm adventurers' guild to ask what they thought about revenge, so it was certain she intended to take revenge on someone currently in the guild.

(It can't be that she has someone to take revenge on a bandit at a time like this, right?)

Even normally, many adventurers gathered at Gilm.

Given that, Rei couldn't imagine the thieves would come near Gilm.

Of course, there was the possibility of Lerdact's private soldiers posing as thieves, so he couldn't say for certain they weren't there.

Or perhaps they'd been killed by monsters... but ultimately, he had to admit that possibility was slim.

"Hope nothing weird happens,"

He muttered this, sensing the possibility of a major disturbance.

That said, with adventurers currently gathering in Gilm, the guards were patrolling more frequently than usual.

Even so, they were stretched thin, to the point of requesting adventurers to assist with patrols through the guild.

In such a situation, if anyone tried to cause trouble, they'd almost certainly be caught, leading to a major uproar.

"So... did you ask him why he wants revenge?"

"No, I didn't. What I was asked was simply what I thought about revenge, you see."

"Right. ...I hope it's revenge without bloodshed, though... What do you think?"

"No way."

At Marina's words, Rei shook his head firmly in denial.

From the way she'd asked him what he thought about revenge... and more than that, from the way she'd spoken of envying and resenting his power... he couldn't possibly imagine the outcome wouldn't be a huge mess.

In fact, it was clear to Rei that Marina was aiming for revenge that would undoubtedly take the other person's life.

Seeing Rei's reaction, Marina nodded back, looking disappointed.

"Well then, all we can do is hope it ends as quietly as possible."

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Marina's conversation, muttered. Beside her, Byune, who was eating pizza, nodded in agreement.

These two were currently assigned to patrol Gilm.

Given that, there was a strong possibility they'd be called out if trouble erupted.

Though communication was difficult, Byune possessed average skills as a thief, making her well-suited for pursuing those who had caused trouble.

Vihera, with her strength, was suited for neutralizing those causing trouble.

Considering Gilm's current state, there was a high probability that those causing trouble were adventurers or hot-blooded craftsmen.

Naturally, subduing such individuals requires considerable skill.

Of course, Gilm's guards were fully capable of handling such individuals, as they did so routinely. But now, disturbances were erupting everywhere.

They simply couldn't handle it all... which is precisely why adventurers like Vihera were being hired.

In such circumstances, skilled individuals are naturally called upon more often, and Vihera is one of the most highly skilled individuals within Gilm.

If the opponent were truly formidable, Vihera might show some enthusiasm... but unfortunately, such opponents are extremely rare.

...The fact that they're rare, not nonexistent, probably highlights Gilm's peculiar nature.

After all, there have been several fights up until today where Vihera found the opponent somewhat enjoyable, even if only a little.

In any case, beyond that, Vihera was also the only one who could communicate perfectly with Byune, so he was sometimes called for that reason as well.

While Vihera and Byune generally acted together, there were times when they absolutely had to split up... and that's when Vihera would be called upon.

"A minor disturbance, huh. ...I really hope it ends that way..."

"What is it, Marina? You seem to be beating around the bush."

What's wrong? Elena asked. Marina, who had been watching Vihera and Byune, let out a small sigh.

"An expansion project of this scale? Normally, it wouldn't be strange for people to start meddling, right? After all, Count Lerdact already tried to interfere."

"True. That's why I ended up coming to Gilm. ...Though personally, I was quite happy to come to Gilm."

Originally, Elena could only meet with Rei through the Paired Orb.

Well, even so, it could be considered fortunate for this era.

Unlike when Rei was in Japan, here in Elgin, you couldn't casually chat with someone far away using the internet.

Even letters weren't sent in an environment where they'd reliably reach the recipient in a few days, like they could back in Japan.

The cheapest way to send a letter was to entrust it to a merchant caravan, but then there was no guarantee when it would arrive.

Worse still, there was even the possibility that the caravan could be attacked and destroyed by bandits or monsters.

There were other methods, like adventurers, summoning magic, or tamed monsters to carry letters, but those required sums ordinary people couldn't casually afford.

In that sense, long-distance relationships were extremely difficult to maintain here in Elgin.

...Though the fact that they weren't entirely impossible might just prove how strong human romantic feelings truly are.

In any case, compared to others, Rei and Elena, who possessed a Paired Orb, were exceptionally fortunate.

Well, initially they didn't have such orbs. It was their mutual desire to talk whenever they were apart that drove them to obtain the Paired Orbs themselves. So it wasn't just luck; it was because they possessed the strength to make it happen.

But... even so, for a young woman in love like Elena, it was only natural that she would want to speak to Rei directly, not through the Paired Orb.
