"Grrrrrrr!"

Spotting Rei appear at the harbor—though it was rather modest to call it that—Set let out a happy cry.

“Hey, Set. Looks like we managed to protect the ship after all. As for the pirates… well, as long as they’re not dead, I guess that’s fine.”

Glancing at the twenty or so pirates piled up a short distance away, Rei turns around to look at the men tied to the rope he’s pulling—the captured pirates.

There were about twenty of them as well.

In addition to the pirates Rei encountered where he first landed, these were the pirates he’d found by searching the island’s suspicious areas.

That said, even though the ships were quite old, forty men seemed a bit too few to man two vessels.

(Given the size of this island, there’s still a possibility that pirates are hiding somewhere we haven’t found yet… but then again, even if they stay hidden, they’ll be helpless once their ships are gone.)

The village closest to this island is the one Rei visited earlier, where he was asked to help defeat the pirates.

It’s about a ten-minute flight on Set’s Wings.

...If it takes ten minutes on Set’s Wings, it would take a considerable amount of time to travel by foot.

And if we have to swim across the sea, the travel time will increase even further.

Swimming for that long would be difficult, even for a pirate.

On top of that, the sea is home not only to fish but also to monsters.

Swimming through such a place would be nothing short of suicide.

In other words, if Rei were to seize both ships here, the pirates left on this island would be forced to live a truly deserted island life.

(Well, I can’t rule out the possibility that a boat or something like that might have been left behind, and in the worst-case scenario, they could build a raft and escape the island.)

Given that, I decided Rei wouldn’t go any further than that.

“What!? …G-Griffon!?”

The man walking at the front of the pirates being dragged by Rei let out a cry of shock.

It must have been completely unexpected for a Griffin to appear in a place like this.

Hearing the pirate’s words, the other pirates, chained together in a line, also wore expressions of disbelief.

Rei looked at them with a slightly surprised expression.

“What’s the matter? Didn’t you guys know who I was? I’m pretty famous among thieves, so I figured you pirates would know me too.”

Muttering this, Rei pulled the Death Scythe out of his Misty Ring once more.

“I thought seeing this would make it pretty obvious who I am.”

Rei said this, but the pirates looked at Set and finally seemed to realize who Rei was.

Many of them wore expressions of despair.

To drive the point home to the pirates, Rei gave the Death Scythe a light swing, demonstrating a slash that seemed to cut through the air itself, before speaking.

“I suppose it’s a shame for you lot not to know who captured you, so I’ll go ahead and introduce myself. I’m Rei. A Rank B adventurer, known by the alias ‘Crimson.’ …Since the thieves used to call me ‘Bandit Eater,’ that name might ring a bell for you guys.”

At the mention of “Bandit Eater,” several pirates winced.

Those who reacted that way must have had some detailed information.

That’s precisely why they despaired upon realizing they’d been targeted by someone they might as well call the Harbinger of Doom.

Even so, these pirates could still be considered fortunate, at least by the standards of their kind.

If they had been like the bandits around Sabrusta—especially those who called themselves “Immaculate White”—who inflicted all manner of harm on their captives, they would never have been captured alive like this.

The fact that not a single one of them had been taken prisoner was because these pirates had judged that kidnapping would only cause trouble… but the fact that this allowed them to survive was truly a stroke of luck.

“Well, for now…”

Muttering that, Rei tied the rope he was holding to a nearby tree.

“Everyone here. Is that correct?”

At Rei’s question, the pirates exchanged glances for a few seconds… and eventually nodded.

(That’s a lie.)

Judging by the pirates’ attitude, Rei instinctively concluded that claiming this was everyone was a lie.

After all, he had checked the areas that seemed somewhat suspicious, but that didn’t mean he had thoroughly searched every single spot.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange if someone were hiding somewhere… or if someone hadn’t noticed Rei’s presence.

(The possibility that they didn’t notice Set’s first cry… is probably nonexistent.)

It was highly unlikely that they hadn’t heard that cry.

I say “probably” because there are people in the world with incredibly high levels of concentration, and it’s not uncommon for such individuals to remain oblivious even if someone were to scream right in their ear.

I can’t imagine a pirate who’s set up a hideout in a backwater like this having that kind of focus, but just maybe—and I mean just maybe—there was a possibility such a person existed.

“Let me make this clear: if everyone here is all there is, then anyone we find from now on—whether pirate or not—will be treated as a suspicious individual and killed immediately. …Is that really okay with you?”

At Rei’s words, several of the pirates grimaced.

Seeing this, Rei was convinced his prediction had been correct. He drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring and brandished it ostentatiously.

It was a massive scythe—one that, under normal circumstances, would seem difficult for someone as petite as Rei to even hold.

Yet Rei held it with one hand, wielding the massive scythe as if it were nothing more than a twig.

Though he swung the Death Scythe casually, the speed and sharpness of the strike possessed such force that it seemed capable of slicing through not just the air, but space itself.

Knowing that Rei was the one known by the nickname “Crimson” and called the “Bandit Eater,” the pirates watching were naturally captivated by that single strike.

They felt as if ice had been poured down their spines, wondering if that very strike might have been aimed at them.

“Just to be sure, I’ll ask one last time. …Is everyone here really all of you? From now on, if I spot anyone on this island, I’ll cut them down as a suspicious person—is that okay with you?”

He asked this with a serious, earnest expression… or rather, with a smile on his face, Rei asked the pirates.

At Rei’s words, the pirates fell silent.

They absolutely did not want to do anything here that would betray their comrades who were still safe.

But if they didn’t say they still had comrades here, Rei would undoubtedly kill whoever he found as a suspicious person.

Anyone who had seen Rei’s behavior could easily imagine that, and for those who knew Rei—the so-called “Bandit Eater”—the fear was even stronger.

Eventually... one of the pirates who had been watching Rei spoke up.

“There are still… about ten of them here.”

Some of the other pirates shot reproachful glances at the one who spoke, while others looked at him with an air of resignation.

Though the pirate was met with such looks, he must have decided that once he’d spoken, there was no turning back. The pirate fixed a fierce gaze on Rei and spoke.

“I told you that not everyone is here yet. So, if we find someone else, you won’t kill them just because they look suspicious, right?”

If he lied now, I would never forgive him.

Facing the pirate’s intense gaze, Rei paused for a moment before speaking.

“Yeah, I suppose I could give that some serious thought.”

“……Consider? That’s not what we agreed on! Promise me!”

The pirate, who appeared to be in his twenties, shouted, accusing Rei of failing to keep his word.

But Rei, the target of that accusation, didn’t seem to think he was being criticized; rather, he looked at the pirate with an exasperated expression.

“Listen, think about what you guys have done so far. Do you really think we can trust you that easily?”

Hearing that, the pirates recalled their own past deeds and fell silent.

It was only natural, since few of those present here had nothing to hide.

Seeing the despair on the pirates’ faces, Rei spoke again.

“That said, it seems you aren’t exactly the most vicious pirates out there. So… well. For now, if you tell us where on this island you’ve hidden the treasure you’ve hoarded so far, I promise to capture you alive—as long as those we find don’t put up a futile fight or try to escape.”

Rei looked around inside the hut where the pirates had been living, but unfortunately, there was no sign of the treasure they were supposed to have hoarded.

In other words, the pirates must have hidden their hoard somewhere on this island.

(There’s a chance they’ve hidden it on another island, but if they’re worried someone might find it, they’d probably want to keep it close by.)

"Where is your treasure?" The pirates fell silent as Rei fixed her gaze on them.

“Hmm, where is your leader? Isn’t he among those we’ve captured?”

Rei asks this, having only just realized that none of the pirates speaking hold absolute authority.

At first, he’d assumed the one who’d been acting a bit arrogant was the pirates’ leader, but watching them talk like this, he realized that wasn’t quite the case.

For that reason, Rei wondered if perhaps the leader hadn’t been captured yet, and asked the question… but at those words, several of the pirates’ expressions changed.

“I see. It seems your leader isn’t among those we’ve captured. In that case, capturing the leader becomes our top priority… but now, what should we do?”

“Hey, wait! I’m the one leading these pirates!”

Rei had thought of the man as a slightly arrogant pirate and he shouted.

“Oh… so you’re the boss, huh? …For someone in that position, you don’t seem to be very well-liked by your men, though.”

When leading outlaws like pirates, it goes without saying that a leader must possess a certain degree of charisma, as well as strength.

The pirate claiming to be the boss seemed to lack both.

“That can’t be true. Believe it or not, I’m actually quite well-liked. It might not look like it, though.”

“...I see. I’ll ask you later if that’s really true. For now, there’s something else I need to do. You’d better learn exactly what kind of enemy you’ve been targeted by.”

Saying that, Rei left the pirates—their faces twisted in grimace—behind and headed toward the ship… but before he could, he noticed the pirates lying unconscious near Set. He tied them up with rope for the time being, then set off for the ship once more.

It wasn’t exactly a proper harbor; it was built with the mindset that as long as it was usable for the time being, that was good enough.

For that very reason, he walked through a harbor that would undoubtedly become unusable if a typhoon were to hit, making his way toward the ship.

Both ships are quite dilapidated, but he approaches the one that shows the most damage and signs of repair.

The pirates, unsure of what Rei was trying to do, watched his actions intently.

To the pirates, who know almost nothing about Rei, it probably looks like she’s just trying to search the ship.

"Is searching the ship—or should I say, ransacking it—the reason to get to know Rei?" the pirates wondered, looking puzzled, but Rei paid no heed to their stares and gently reached out toward the ship floating on the sea.

Whether this was designed so he could board immediately, or if it was built this way because they had set up a makeshift port, Rei didn’t know.

But for now, knowing that he could touch the ship simply by reaching out was enough.

The pirates couldn’t make any sense of Rei’s actions.

“What are you doing? What are you going to do? What do you think you’re doing?”

Despite being met with such stares, Rei proceeded to stow the ship away in the Misty Ring.

In an instant… literally, in the blink of an eye, the ship vanished from the sea.

The pirates, seeing this, could do nothing but stare blankly, completely baffled by what was happening.

“Well, on to the next one.”

Leaving the dazed pirates behind, Rei headed toward a ship anchored a short distance away...

“Hey, wait a minute! What the hell did you just do?!”

He could hear one of the pirates shouting, but Rei paid him no mind as he arrived beneath the other ship and touched it… but the next moment, he frowned.

“It won’t fit inside?!”
