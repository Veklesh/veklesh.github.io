From the throne made of tree roots, a doll made of tree roots rises.

The standing root doll immediately turns toward Rei before it... and swings its right arm down.

The arm, made of tree roots, lashes out toward Rei with a supple, whip-like motion.

But to Rei's eyes, that strike was by no means too fast to react to.

No, rather, it was a speed easily countered by Rei.

He dodged the blow aimed straight at him by shifting his body backward.

In the next instant, he stomped on the right arm that had struck the ground and swung the Death Scythe.

But while the Death Scythe usually sliced through with almost no resistance, at the instant it cut through the right arm, Rei felt a slight resistance.

At that, Rei flashed a hint of surprise across his face as he swung the Death Scythe through.

(Does it possess strong magical power?)

Rei had barely infused any magic into the Death Scythe for that strike.

Even so, under normal circumstances, the sheer sharpness of the Death Scythe itself, its weight of about 100kg, and the force of Rei's strike should have easily severed it.

The only plausible reason his strike had met even slight resistance was magic.

In the past, Rei's Death Scythe strikes had been blocked several times by weapons imbued with magic power.

Given that, if his strike was blocked, it made sense that it was due to magic.

"But that doesn't mean it can be completely blocked... What!?"

Rei, who had swung Death Scythe to sever the torso in the same motion that severed the arm, leapt from the spot at the sudden sound of wind cutting through the air.

Something passed through the spot where Rei's body had been just an instant before... and the next moment, it slammed into the ground.

Another sharp whistling sound followed.

Without time to see its source, he leapt swiftly from the spot.

Using Sleipnir Boots to kick off mid-air, he directed his gaze toward where the sound had come from... and his expression turned to shock.

Because staring back at him were multiple dolls made from tree roots, the very ones Rei had been trying to knock down.

Though fundamentally similar in shape, the details differed somewhat.

This was likely because tree roots weren't uniform.

Still, from a distance, recognizing them as identical entities wasn't difficult, given their similar shapes.

"Right? Since the dolls themselves are terminals, it's not surprising they could create multiple similar ones...!"

While still in midair, he infused the Twilight Spear with magic and hurled it with just a twist of his upper body.

That strike was even weaker than the one he had unleashed on the giant flower while riding Set.

When riding Set, he could at least partially stabilize his body using Set's back.

But floating in the air with Sleipnir Boots? Even if he could briefly step on the air with that ability, it was nowhere near the stability of sitting on Set's back.

Even so, the Twilight Spear pierced through several tree root dolls at once, vanishing them without a trace.

The next instant, the Twilight Spear was back in Rei's hand.

(With the Death Scythe, there was at least some resistance when cutting... but not with this.)

The difference in the magic power infused into them was the obvious explanation, but even so, to Rei's eyes, the later-appearing root dolls seemed weaker than the first one.

(Mass-produced? Could that be possible?)

The root dolls that had been waiting here for Rei and the others to arrive, and the root dolls hastily created when the fight began.

Even if their appearance was identical, Rei couldn't possibly believe they possessed the same capabilities.

If that was the case, then it was still an easier opponent to fight.

Thinking this, Rei glanced around for just an instant.

As expected, Vihera and Set were battling a giant Treant.

Even if it was larger than other Treants, it was still just a Treant.

That's what Rei thought, but the Treant Vihera and Set were fighting was actually different from the ones Rei and the others had fought before.

It swung branches like hands, hurled fruits from its branches, and lashed out with vines缠绕 its trunk... While its attack methods were fundamentally the same as other Treants, its sheer size meant its power was several levels above a normal Treant.

...That said, whether it could hold its own against Vihera or Set was an entirely different matter.

Being larger than ordinary Treants meant this one also possessed higher defensive capabilities.

But... for Vihera, a high-defense opponent was nothing more than easy prey.

For Vihera, who possessed the skill Immersion Palm—which sent a shock directly into the enemy's body, a shock accompanied by magic power—high defense was utterly meaningless.

"Hah!"

He gently touched it, then drove the shock into the Treant's body from there.

That magic-infused strike unleashed its full power within the Treant's body, causing it to burst apart the next instant as if an explosion had erupted from within.

Treant probably didn't even understand what had happened.

He froze for a moment, as if confused... and the next instant, he collapsed to the ground.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

While Vihera had defeated Treant using a technique... a skill, Set had overwhelmed the massive Treant solely with his griffin's physical prowess, enhanced by the magic item known as the Bracelet of Might.

Griffins and Treants were fundamentally different in their status as monsters.

Especially in Set's case, the influence of Beast Magic meant he possessed strength several levels above that of an ordinary griffin.

Against such a griffin, a monster like a Treant had no chance of resisting, no matter what it tried.

A single strike from his foreleg, unleashed with a mighty roar, easily shattered the Treant's trunk.

Though the Treant attacked with branches, vines, and nuts, evading all of them was no difficult feat for Set with his physical prowess.

With graceful footwork, he dodged the attacks while closing the distance to deliver a fatal blow.

Without using any of the skills he had mastered as a beast sorcerer, Set still dominated the Treants.

That he possessed the strength to trample the Treants was likely because he was Set.

"Whoa!"

Seizing a momentary opening in the fight between Vihera and Set, the tree root puppet swung its whip-like arm.

He dodged the horizontal strike by kicking off the air, activating the Sleipnir Boots...

"Whoa!"

Seeing a tree shoot up from the ground where he'd dodged, Rei instinctively swung Death Scythe.

The tree that had been reaching for him was instantly sliced away.

Landing on the ground, Rei saw a single tree root puppet burying its hands in the earth a short distance away.

Seeing such an opponent, Rei suddenly felt something was off.

Judging by the current situation, it appeared the tree root puppet had grown a tree from the ground to attack Rei.

But upon reflection, the root doll was merely a terminal within the existence of this Treant Forest.

If that was the case, it should have been able to attack Rei directly without going through the terminal, the tree root puppet.

(Then why? Why go through the trouble of attacking via a terminal? ...Dammit!)

As if to deny her time to focus on her thoughts, other tree root dolls launched attack after attack toward Rei.

Where were they getting the additional roots from? The length of the whips wielded by the root dolls had already exceeded five meters.

And yet, the size of the root dolls hadn't changed at all. It was clear beyond doubt that the roots were being replenished from somewhere.

Furthermore, as the battle dragged on, multiple tree root puppets began coordinating their attacks.

It was far from a dazzlingly beautiful coordination... it was clearly clumsy.

Still, coordination was coordination, and they launched attacks in sync with their comrades.

"Get out of the way!"

As he shouted, Rei kicked off the ground, passing right beside the root puppet and slicing its torso clean in two with the Death Scythe.

As expected, he felt no resistance when slicing through the torso.

In essence, what could be called the mass-produced version of the tree root doll Rei had mentioned earlier simply split into upper and lower halves, each collapsing to the ground... but in the next instant, roots sprouted from the severed points, the upper and lower halves intertwined, and it became one doll once more.

Watching these tree root dolls, Rei wore a displeased expression.

"We need to eliminate them, not just cut them down. ...Fortunately, this place is spacious. With this much open ground, the flames shouldn't spread to the surrounding area."

Even as Rei muttered, the tree root puppet's actions didn't stop.

Dodging the relentless barrage of whipping roots, he chanted his spell.

'Flames, obey my will and consume the enemy.'

With that incantation, a fireball about thirty centimeters in diameter formed at the tip of the Death Scythe.

'Fireball'

Closing the distance with the root puppet, he activated the magic as he passed by its side.

He hurls the created fireball at the tree root puppet.

In an instant, the tree root puppet is engulfed in flames from the fireball Rei unleashed.

Given that it was night, the flames were highly conspicuous.

While the trees growing in Treant Forest possess some degree of magical defense, such limited protection was utterly useless against Rei's spell.

The root doll, engulfed in flames, was reduced to charcoal in mere seconds, carried away by the night wind.

"...Phew. Looks like burning it all at once prevents the fire from spreading. I was worried since there's grass growing on the ground."

Rei murmured as he watched all the tree root dolls cease moving.

Did they not know what fire was...? That thought crossed Rei's mind, but he had heard from Slesha that they had bonfires at night.

Thinking about it that way, it was unlikely the Treant Forest knew absolutely nothing about fire.

(Then why?)

With that question lingering, Rei turned his gaze toward Vihera and Set.

There, one person and one beast were still engaged in combat against Treant.

...No, it was no longer a battle; it was more like a rout.

The moment Vihera's hand touched a Treant's trunk, its body shattered as if imploding from within. Set, swinging his forepaws as if playing, continued to destroy the Treants.

(Huh? Vihera's Demon-Infused Palm is a skill that channels shockwaves through the body. I don't think it could actually cause explosions inside someone...)

As far as Rei knew, it shouldn't have possessed such a dangerous power to cause an explosion inside the body.

(Come to think of it, didn't it explode inside Treant's body when I saw it earlier? ...Has it been enhanced?)

Watching the tree root puppet, Rei pondered, but his memory was correct.

Through fusion... no, absorption with Ambris, Vihera's abilities had been significantly enhanced.

One result of that was the attack currently being carried out inside Treant's body.

(What a monster.)

Watching another Treant explode inside its own body, Rei thought this with deep conviction.

Even without the explosion, the skill alone—ignoring armor, skin, body hair... all that high defense—and driving shockwaves into the body possessed vicious power. Now, the element of explosion had been added to that.

It had evolved into a more aggressive... no, a more vicious skill.

At the very least, Rei had no desire to fight an opponent wielding such a skill.

...The problem was that Vihera, who possessed that skill, often seemed eager to fight Rei.

That's what Rei was thinking when, perhaps triggered by one of his comrades being taken down, he noticed all the tree root puppets had stopped attacking and were staring intently at him.

"...What is it?"

The battle with Treant was still ongoing.

Despite that, the tree root puppets had completely stopped attacking and were staring intently at Rei.

Rei felt something eerie about their quiet stillness, but he still thought reducing their numbers was the priority for now. He tried to cast his spell again... At that very instant, the roots that made up the bodies of all the tree root dolls suddenly began to dissolve.

It was as if tightly knotted threads were being untangled without anyone touching them...

(What's going on? Well, I'm glad the enemy's gone... but there's definitely something fishy about this.)

The tree root dolls dissolved one after another before Rei's eyes.

Then, the fallen roots slithered into the ground like snakes.

Rei could only stare at the scene, unable to do anything.

And the very next moment, after all the roots had vanished into the ground... the forest of Treant itself began to tremble.
